# 以西结书 导读 / Ezekiel Guide

## 一、以西结的蒙召与审判预言（1-24章）

以西结书以圣经中最壮丽的异象之一开始——四活物与神的宝座车。四活物各有四个脸面（人、狮、牛、鹰），旁边有轮中套轮，"轮辋周围满有眼睛"（1:18）。这异象的核心是"在宝座以上有仿佛人的形状"（1:26）——神虽不可见，却以可见的荣耀临到被掳的百姓中间。以西结被这异象震倒在地，然后被差遣去做"守望者"。

以西结的蒙召独特之处在于他要吃一卷书——"他对我说：人子啊，要吃我所赐给你的这书卷，充满你的肚腹。我就吃了，口中觉得其甜如蜜"（3:3）。先知必须先消化神的话，才能传讲。

第四至二十四章用一系列象征行动和预言宣告耶路撒冷必被毁灭：以西结用砖画耶路撒冷被围困（4章），向左向右侧卧表示以色列和犹大受罚的年数（4:4-6），剃头发用火烧、用刀砍、任风吹散（5章）。这些戏剧性的行为是视觉化的预言——让百姓看见即将来临的审判。

第八至十一章记载了以西结书最令人心碎的异象——神的荣耀离开圣殿。先知被提到耶路撒冷，看见百姓在殿中拜偶像，然后耶和华的荣耀从基路伯之上升起，停在门槛上，最终离开圣殿，离开城市，停在城东的山上（11:23）。神的同在离开了——这比城墙被毁更可怕。

### I. Ezekiel's Calling and Prophecies of Judgment (Ch. 1–24)

Ezekiel opens with one of Scripture's most magnificent visions — the four living creatures and God's throne-chariot. Each creature had four faces (human, lion, ox, eagle), with wheels within wheels, "and their rings were full of eyes round about" (1:18). The vision's core: "upon the likeness of the throne was the likeness as the appearance of a man" (1:26) — though God is invisible, His visible glory came to the exiled people. Ezekiel fell on his face, then was commissioned as a "watchman."

Ezekiel's unique calling required eating a scroll — "Son of man, cause thy belly to eat, and fill thy bowels with this roll that I give thee. Then did I eat it; and it was in my mouth as honey for sweetness" (3:3). The prophet must first digest God's word before proclaiming it.

Chapters 4–24 use symbolic actions and oracles to announce Jerusalem's coming destruction: Ezekiel drew Jerusalem under siege on a brick (ch. 4), lay on his sides for the years of Israel's and Judah's punishment (4:4–6), shaved his head and divided the hair by fire, sword, and wind (ch. 5). These dramatic acts were visual prophecies — making the coming judgment visible.

Chapters 8–11 record Ezekiel's most heartbreaking vision — God's glory departing the temple. The prophet was transported to Jerusalem, saw the people worshiping idols in the temple, then the glory of the LORD rose from above the cherubim, paused at the threshold, and finally departed the temple and city, stopping on the mountain east of the city (11:23). God's presence left — more terrifying than walls being breached.

## 二、列国审判与牧人的应许（25-32章）

第二十五至三十二章是对列国的审判——亚扪、摩押、以东、非利士、推罗、西顿和埃及。推罗的审判（26-28章）特别值得注意：28:12-19对推罗王的哀歌，"你曾在伊甸神的园中"——许多解经家认为这段超越了人间的王，指向撒但的堕落。

第三十三章重申以西结作守望者的角色，然后全书基调从审判转向盼望。

第三十四章是牧人的宣告——神谴责以色列的牧人（领袖）只喂养自己不喂养羊群，然后宣告"我必亲自做我羊的牧人"（34:15概意）。"我必立一牧人照管他们，牧养他们，就是我的仆人大卫。他必牧养他们"（34:23）——这是弥赛亚的应许，耶稣说"我是好牧人"（约10:11）正是应验此预言。

第三十六章宣告内在更新的应许——"我也要赐给你们一个新心，将新灵放在你们里面，又从你们的肉体中除掉石心，赐给你们肉心。我必将我的灵放在你们里面"（36:26-27）。这不是外在的律法遵行，而是内心的彻底改变——与耶利米的新约应许（耶31:33）互相呼应。

第三十七章枯骨复苏的异象是以西结书最著名的场景。神带以西结到平原，那里满了枯干的骸骨。神问"这些骸骨能复活吗？"以西结回答"主耶和华啊，你是知道的"。然后骸骨在神的灵吹拂下长筋、长肉、有皮包裹，站起来成为极大的军队（37:1-10）。这异象首先预表以色列的民族复兴，更深层指向末日身体的复活。

### II. Judgment on Nations and the Shepherd's Promise (Ch. 25–37)

Chapters 25–32 pronounce judgment on surrounding nations — Ammon, Moab, Edom, Philistia, Tyre, Sidon, and Egypt. The Tyre oracles (ch. 26–28) are particularly notable: the lament over Tyre's king (28:12–19), "Thou hast been in Eden the garden of God" — many interpreters see this as transcending the human king, pointing to Satan's fall.

Chapter 33 reaffirms Ezekiel's watchman role, then the book's tone shifts from judgment to hope.

Chapter 34 is the shepherd oracle — God condemns Israel's shepherds (leaders) for feeding themselves rather than the flock, then declares "I will feed my flock, and I will cause them to lie down" (34:15). "I will set up one shepherd over them, and he shall feed them, even my servant David; he shall feed them" (34:23) — a messianic promise fulfilled when Jesus said "I am the good shepherd" (John 10:11).

Chapter 36 announces the promise of inner renewal — "A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh, and I will give you an heart of flesh. And I will put my spirit within you" (36:26–27). This is not outward legal observance but radical inner transformation — echoing Jeremiah's New Covenant promise (Jer 31:33).

Chapter 37's valley of dry bones is Ezekiel's most famous scene. God brought Ezekiel to a valley full of dry bones. God asked, "Can these bones live?" Ezekiel answered, "O Lord GOD, thou knowest." Then the bones, under God's Spirit, grew sinews, flesh, and skin, standing up as a vast army (37:1–10). This vision first foreshadows Israel's national restoration, and more deeply points to the bodily resurrection at the last day.

## 三、枯骨复活：以色列的复兴（33-37章）

以西结书37章的枯骨异象是全书最著名的段落。耶和华问："人子啊，这些骸骨能复活吗？"以西结回答："主耶和华啊，你是知道的"（37:3）。然后神的灵吹在枯骨上，它们长出筋、肉、皮，站起来成为极大的军队。

这异象的直接含义是被掳的以色列将要复国——"我必用我的灵吹入你们里面，你们就要活了。我将你们安置在本地"（37:14）。更深的含义指向属灵的复活——只有神的灵能使灵性死亡的人复活。

"牧人"的主题在34章展开：以色列的假牧人只顾自己，神要亲自牧养他的羊（34:11-16），并兴起大卫的后裔作永远的牧人（34:23-24）。这预言在耶稣——"好牧人"——身上完全应验（约翰福音10:11）。

### III. Valley of Dry Bones: Israel's Restoration (Ch. 33-37)

The valley of dry bones in Ezekiel 37 is the book's most famous passage. The LORD asked: "Son of man, can these bones live?" Ezekiel answered: "O Lord GOD, thou knowest" (37:3). Then God's Spirit breathed upon the bones; sinews, flesh, and skin covered them, and they stood up an exceeding great army.

The immediate meaning is that exiled Israel would be restored -- "I shall put my spirit in you, and ye shall live, and I shall place you in your own land" (37:14). The deeper meaning points to spiritual resurrection -- only God's Spirit can revive the spiritually dead.

The "shepherd" theme unfolds in chapter 34: Israel's false shepherds served themselves; God would shepherd His flock personally (34:11-16) and raise up a Davidic heir as eternal shepherd (34:23-24). This prophecy was perfectly fulfilled in Jesus -- the "good shepherd" (John 10:11).

## 四、歌革和玛各的末世争战（38-39章）

第三十八至三十九章预言歌革和玛各之战——一支来自北方的联军攻击复兴的以色列，但被神亲自用火与硫磺击败。这段预言在启示录20:8再次出现，指向末世最后的争战。改革宗解经多将歌革理解为象征性的末世势力，而非特定的历史国家。

第四十至四十八章是以西结书的高潮——新圣殿的异象。以西结被提到极高的山上，详细测量了一座理想圣殿的每一个尺寸。这段描述的精确程度令人惊叹——门廊、内院、外院、祭坛，每一个细节都有具体数字。

最重要的时刻在第四十三章：神的荣耀从东门回到圣殿——"以色列神的荣光从东而来……他的荣光充满了殿"（43:2-5）。在第十一章离开的荣耀，现在回来了！这是整卷书的高潮——审判不是终点，神的同在必将归回。

第四十七章描绘了从圣殿流出的水——起初只到脚踝，然后到膝盖，到腰，最后成为不可趟过的河（47:3-5）。这条河所到之处，死海的水都变甜，鱼极多，两岸的树"叶子不枯干，果子不断绝；每月必结新果子……其叶子乃为治病"（47:12）。启示录22:1-2的生命水河和生命树的异象直接呼应此处。

全书最后一句定义了这座新城的名字："耶和华的所在"（48:35）。整卷以西结书从神的荣耀离开走到神的荣耀归回——这就是救赎历史的轮廓。

### III. Gog's War and the New Temple (Ch. 38–48)

Chapters 38–39 prophesy the war of Gog and Magog — a northern coalition attacks restored Israel but is defeated by God Himself with fire and brimstone. This prophecy reappears in Revelation 20:8, pointing to the final eschatological battle. Reformed interpretation generally understands Gog as a symbolic end-times force rather than a specific historical nation.

Chapters 40–48 are Ezekiel's climax — the vision of a new temple. Ezekiel was taken to a very high mountain and measured an ideal temple in precise detail — porches, inner court, outer court, altar, every dimension with exact figures.

The crucial moment comes in chapter 43: God's glory returns to the temple through the east gate — "and, behold, the glory of the God of Israel came from the way of the east... and the house was filled with the glory of the LORD" (43:2, 5). The glory that departed in chapter 11 has returned! This is the book's climax — judgment is not the end; God's presence will return.

Chapter 47 depicts water flowing from the temple — first ankle-deep, then knee-deep, then waist-deep, then a river too deep to cross (47:3–5). Wherever this river goes, the Dead Sea's waters become fresh, teeming with fish, and trees on both banks whose "leaf thereof shall not fade, neither shall the fruit thereof be consumed: it shall bring forth new fruit according to his months... and the leaf thereof shall be for medicine" (47:12). Revelation 22:1–2's river of life and tree of life directly echo this.

The book's final sentence names the new city: "The LORD is there" (48:35). Ezekiel moves from God's glory departing to God's glory returning — this is the outline of redemption history.

## 五、新圣殿的异象：神永远同在（40-48章）

以西结书最后九章描绘了一座壮丽的新圣殿异象。天使带着量杆带领以西结详细测量圣殿的每一个部分（40-42章），然后"耶和华的荣耀从东门进入殿中"（43:4）——与先前荣耀离开的方向一致（10:18-19, 11:23），象征神的回归。

这座圣殿至今没有按字面建成，因为它指向更大的实体。新约启示我们：教会就是神的殿（以弗所书2:21-22），基督徒的身体也是圣灵的殿（哥林多前书6:19），最终启示录描绘的新耶路撒冷不再需要圣殿，"因主神全能者和羔羊为城的殿"（启示录21:22）。

从圣殿流出的河水越流越深、越流越广，所到之处万物得医治（47:1-12）——这是生命之水的预表，在基督里完全实现："人若渴了，可以到我这里来喝"（约翰福音7:37）。

### V. Vision of the New Temple: God's Eternal Presence (Ch. 40-48)

The final nine chapters of Ezekiel describe a magnificent new temple vision. An angel with a measuring rod led Ezekiel to measure every part of the temple in detail (ch. 40-42), and then "the glory of the LORD came into the house by the way of the gate whose prospect is toward the east" (43:4) -- the same direction from which the glory had departed (10:18-19, 11:23), symbolizing God's return.

This temple has never been literally built because it points to a greater reality. The New Testament reveals: the church is God's temple (Ephesians 2:21-22), the believer's body is the temple of the Holy Spirit (1 Corinthians 6:19), and ultimately the New Jerusalem needs no temple, "for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it" (Revelation 21:22).

The river flowing from the temple grows ever deeper and wider, bringing healing wherever it goes (47:1-12) -- a type of living water fully realized in Christ: "If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink" (John 7:37).
