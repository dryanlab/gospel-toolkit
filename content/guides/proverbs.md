# 箴言 导读 / Proverbs Guide

## 一、智慧的呼唤——敬畏耶和华是知识的开端（1-9章）

箴言前九章是一位父亲对儿子的教导，也是智慧本身向世人的呼唤。全书的钥匙在1:7——"敬畏耶和华是知识的开端；愚妄人藐视智慧和训诲。"这不是说信神之后才能学知识，而是说：离开了对神的敬畏，一切知识都没有正确的根基。

智慧在第八章被拟人化为一位在街头呼喊的女子。她宣告自己在创世之前就已存在："在耶和华造化的起头，在太初创造万物之先，就有了我"（8:22）。早期教父们常将这段经文与约翰福音1章的"道"（Logos）联系起来——基督就是神的智慧（林前1:24）。

与智慧女子对比的是愚昧的妇人和淫妇（2、5、7章）。箴言用两条路的意象贯穿始终：智慧之路通向生命，愚昧之路通向死亡。第七章对淫妇引诱的描写极其生动——"他却不知道有死亡的阴影在那里"（7:27概意）。箴言的警告不是禁欲主义，而是对罪的真实后果的清醒认识。

### I. Wisdom's Call — The Fear of the LORD Is the Beginning of Knowledge (Ch. 1–9)

The first nine chapters are a father's instruction to his son and Wisdom's own call to humanity. The key to the entire book is 1:7 — "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge: but fools despise wisdom and instruction." This does not mean one must believe in God before learning; rather, apart from reverence for God, all knowledge lacks its proper foundation.

In chapter 8, Wisdom is personified as a woman crying out in the streets. She declares her existence before creation: "The LORD possessed me in the beginning of his way, before his works of old" (8:22). Early church fathers often connected this passage with the "Word" (Logos) of John 1 — Christ is the wisdom of God (1 Cor 1:24).

Contrasting Lady Wisdom is the foolish woman and the adulteress (ch. 2, 5, 7). Proverbs uses the image of two paths throughout: the way of wisdom leads to life, the way of folly to death. Chapter 7's portrayal of the adulteress's seduction is vivid — the young man "knew not that it was for his life" (7:23, cf. 7:27). Proverbs' warnings are not asceticism but sober recognition of sin's real consequences.

## 二、所罗门的箴言——对比的智慧（10-22章）

箴言的主体（10-29章）是数百条独立的格言，涵盖了生活的方方面面：言语、财富、勤劳、友谊、家庭、公义、谦卑。这些格言大多采用对比式平行体——上半句说义人/智慧人如何，下半句说恶人/愚妄人如何。

关于言语："回答柔和，使怒消退；言语暴戾，触动怒气"（15:1）。"生死在舌头的权下，喜爱它的，必吃它所结的果子"（18:21）。箴言对言语的教导在社交媒体时代尤为宝贵。

关于勤劳与懒惰："懒惰人哪，你去察看蚂蚁的动作就可得智慧"（6:6）。"手懒的，要受贫穷；手勤的，却要富足"（10:4）。箴言不是成功学，而是对神所设立的因果秩序的观察。

关于管教："不忍用杖打儿子的，是恨恶他；疼爱儿子的，随时管教"（13:24）。"教养孩童，使他走当行的道，就是到老他也不偏离"（22:6）。

25-29章特别标注为"希西家的人所誊录的所罗门的箴言"——说明这些格言经过了约两百五十年的传承和编辑。智慧文学不是一蹴而就的，而是几代人的积累。

### II. Solomon's Proverbs — Wisdom for Daily Life (Ch. 10–29)

The body of Proverbs (ch. 10–29) contains hundreds of independent maxims covering every aspect of life: speech, wealth, diligence, friendship, family, justice, humility. Most employ antithetic parallelism — the first line describes the righteous/wise, the second the wicked/foolish.

On speech: "A soft answer turneth away wrath: but grievous words stir up anger" (15:1). "Death and life are in the power of the tongue: and they that love it shall eat the fruit thereof" (18:21). Proverbs' teaching on speech is especially precious in the age of social media.

On diligence and laziness: "Go to the ant, thou sluggard; consider her ways, and be wise" (6:6). "He becometh poor that dealeth with a slack hand: but the hand of the diligent maketh rich" (10:4). Proverbs is not a success manual but an observation of the cause-and-effect order God has established.

On discipline: "He that spareth his rod hateth his son: but he that loveth him chasteneth him betimes" (13:24). "Train up a child in the way he should go: and when he is old, he will not depart from it" (22:6).

Chapters 25–29 are specifically labeled as "proverbs of Solomon, which the men of Hezekiah king of Judah copied out" — indicating these maxims were transmitted and edited over roughly 250 years. Wisdom literature was not produced overnight but accumulated across generations.

## 三、更多箴言与各家格言（23-29章）

箴言10-22章是所罗门箴言的核心，以对比句的形式呈现智慧与愚昧、义人与恶人的两条道路。

关于言语："生死在舌头的权下"（18:21）。关于勤劳："手懒的，要受贫穷；手勤的，却要富足"（10:4）。关于谦卑："骄傲来，羞耻也来；谦逊人却有智慧"（11:2）。关于交友："滥交朋友的，自取败坏；但有一朋友比弟兄更亲密"（18:24）。

这些不是空洞的格言，而是建立在敬畏耶和华根基上的生活指南。"人心筹算自己的道路，惟耶和华指引他的脚步"（16:9）——最深的智慧是认识到人的有限，将一切交托给神。智慧不仅是聪明，更是在每一个具体处境中按神的旨意而行。

### III. Further Proverbs and Collected Sayings (Ch. 23-29)

Proverbs 10-22 form the core of Solomon's sayings, presenting the two ways of wisdom and folly, righteousness and wickedness, in antithetical parallelism.

On speech: "Death and life are in the power of the tongue" (18:21). On diligence: "He becometh poor that dealeth with a slack hand: but the hand of the diligent maketh rich" (10:4). On humility: "When pride cometh, then cometh shame: but with the lowly is wisdom" (11:2). On friendship: "A man that hath friends must shew himself friendly: and there is a friend that sticketh closer than a brother" (18:24).

These are not empty maxims but practical guidelines built on the fear of the LORD. "A man's heart deviseth his way: but the LORD directeth his steps" (16:9) -- the deepest wisdom is recognizing human limitation and entrusting all to God. Wisdom is not merely cleverness but living according to God's will in every concrete situation.

## 四、亚古珥与利慕伊勒（30章）

第三十章是亚古珥的言语——一位非以色列人的智者。他的开场白充满谦卑："我比众人更蠢笨，也没有人的聪明"（30:2）。他提出了一个深刻的神学问题："谁升天又降下来？谁聚风在掌握中？谁包水在衣服里？谁立定地的四极？他名叫什么？他儿子名叫什么？你知道吗？"（30:4）——这个问题指向的答案是神自己，而"他儿子名叫什么"在新约中得到了回答。

第三十一章前半段是利慕伊勒王的母亲教训他的话——关于节制、公义和为穷人发声。

全书以"才德的妇人"（31:10-31）作为高潮结束。这是一首按希伯来字母顺序排列的离合诗，描绘了一位智慧、勤劳、慈善、敬畏神的女性形象。"才德的妇人谁能得着呢？她的价值远胜过珍珠"（31:10）。

值得注意的是，这首诗的最后一句回到了全书的主题："艳丽是虚假的，美容是虚浮的，惟敬畏耶和华的妇女必得称赞"（31:30）。从1:7"敬畏耶和华是知识的开端"到31:30"敬畏耶和华的妇女必得称赞"——敬畏神是箴言的起点和终点。

### IV. Agur, Lemuel, and the Virtuous Woman (Ch. 30–31)

Chapter 30 contains the words of Agur — a non-Israelite sage. His opening is remarkably humble: "Surely I am more brutish than any man, and have not the understanding of a man" (30:2). He poses a profound theological question: "Who hath ascended up into heaven, or descended? who hath gathered the wind in his fists? who hath bound the waters in a garment? who hath established all the ends of the earth? what is his name, and what is his son's name, if thou canst tell?" (30:4) — the answer points to God Himself, and "what is his son's name?" receives its answer in the New Testament.

The first half of chapter 31 records the instruction of King Lemuel's mother — on temperance, justice, and speaking for the poor.

The book climaxes with the "virtuous woman" (31:10–31), an acrostic poem arranged by the Hebrew alphabet, portraying a woman of wisdom, diligence, charity, and fear of God. "Who can find a virtuous woman? for her price is far above rubies" (31:10).

Notably, the poem's final verse returns to the book's central theme: "Favour is deceitful, and beauty is vain: but a woman that feareth the LORD, she shall be praised" (31:30). From 1:7 — "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" — to 31:30 — "a woman that feareth the LORD, she shall be praised" — the fear of God is Proverbs' starting point and destination.

## 五、才德的妇人——智慧的化身（31章）

箴言31章以利慕伊勒王的母亲的教导开始（31:1-9），以"才德的妇人"结束（31:10-31）。这首字母诗（希伯来文22个字母依序排列）不仅描绘了理想的妻子，更是全书"智慧女子"形象的具体化。

她勤劳持家（31:13-19）、扶助贫穷（31:20）、口出智慧（31:26）、敬畏耶和华（31:30）。"艳丽是虚假的，美容是虚浮的；惟敬畏耶和华的妇女必得称赞"（31:30）——这句话呼应了全书的开头"敬畏耶和华是智慧的开端"（1:7）。

才德的妇人是箴言书的总结：真正的智慧不是抽象的哲学，而是活出来的信仰。从家庭到社区，从言语到行为，敬畏神的人在日常生活的每一个细节中彰显智慧。这就是箴言给每一位读者的呼召。

### V. The Virtuous Woman -- Wisdom Embodied (Ch. 31)

Proverbs 31 begins with King Lemuel's mother's instruction (31:1-9) and ends with the "virtuous woman" (31:10-31). This acrostic poem (each line beginning with a successive Hebrew letter) not only portrays the ideal wife but concretizes the "Lady Wisdom" figure from the entire book.

She is industrious (31:13-19), generous to the poor (31:20), speaks wisdom (31:26), and fears the LORD (31:30). "Favour is deceitful, and beauty is vain: but a woman that feareth the LORD, she shall be praised" (31:30) -- this echoes the book's opening: "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" (1:7).

The virtuous woman is Proverbs' summary: true wisdom is not abstract philosophy but lived-out faith. From home to community, from speech to action, the God-fearing person manifests wisdom in every detail of daily life. This is Proverbs' call to every reader.
