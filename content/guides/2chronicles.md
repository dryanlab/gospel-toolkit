# 历代志下 导读 / 2 Chronicles Guide

## 一、所罗门与圣殿（1-9章）

历代志下前九章聚焦所罗门建造圣殿的全过程。与列王纪上不同，历代志下几乎不提所罗门的堕落（仅一笔带过），而将重心完全放在圣殿——因为对被掳归回、正在重建第二圣殿的读者来说，这才是最需要学习的。

献殿礼（5-7章）是全书最辉煌的场景。当祭司们从至圣所出来，歌唱的利未人和一百二十个吹号的祭司一同发声赞美耶和华，"耶和华的荣光充满了神的殿"（5:14）。云彩充满殿到一个地步，祭司都不能站立供职。这是何等壮观的敬拜！

所罗门的献殿祷告后，神在夜间显现，说出了旧约中最著名的应许之一："这称为我名下的子民，若是自卑、祷告，寻求我的面，转离他们的恶行，我必从天上垂听，赦免他们的罪，医治他们的地"（7:14）。这节经文成为历代信徒复兴祷告的根基。

示巴女王来访（9章）证实了所罗门的荣耀。但读者知道，这一切辉煌将因背约而失去——第二圣殿的重建者更应珍惜与神立约的关系。

### I. Solomon and the Temple (Ch. 1–9)

The first nine chapters of 2 Chronicles focus on Solomon's construction of the temple. Unlike 1 Kings, 2 Chronicles barely mentions Solomon's apostasy (only in passing), placing the weight entirely on the temple — for readers who had returned from exile and were rebuilding the Second Temple, this was the essential lesson.

The dedication ceremony (ch. 5–7) is the most magnificent scene in the book. When the priests came out of the Holy of Holies, the Levite singers and 120 priests with trumpets raised their voices together praising the LORD, and "the glory of the LORD had filled the house of God" (5:14). The cloud so filled the house that the priests could not stand to minister. What spectacular worship!

After Solomon's dedicatory prayer, God appeared to him at night, speaking one of the Old Testament's most famous promises: "If my people, which are called by my name, shall humble themselves, and pray, and seek my face, and turn from their wicked ways; then will I hear from heaven, and will forgive their sin, and will heal their land" (7:14). This verse has become the foundation of revival prayers through the ages.

The Queen of Sheba's visit (ch. 9) confirmed Solomon's glory. But readers know all this splendor would be lost through covenant-breaking — the builders of the Second Temple should all the more cherish their covenant relationship with God.

## 二、犹大诸王——信仰的两极（10-28章）

王国分裂后，历代志下只关注南国犹大（北国以色列几乎不被提及）。犹大的二十个王中，敬虔与悖逆交替出现。

亚撒（14-16章）是早期的改革王，他向神祷告："耶和华啊，惟有你能帮助软弱的，胜过强盛的"（14:11），大胜古实百万军兵。但晚年他依靠亚兰而非信靠神，甚至在病中也不求耶和华（16:12）。

约沙法（17-20章）建立了教导律法的体系，差遣官员和利未人到各城教训百姓。面对摩押和亚扪的大军，他的祷告成为名句："我们无力抵挡这来攻击我们的大军，我们也不知道怎样行，我们的眼目单仰望你"（20:12）。

乌西雅（26章）大有能力，"名声传到远方"，却因骄傲而进殿烧香被神击打长了大麻风。"他既强盛，就心高气傲，以致行事邪僻"（26:16）——权力和成功是信心最大的考验。

希西家（29-32章）和约西亚（34-35章）是犹大最后的两位改革王。希西家在全国恢复逾越节，"自从以色列王所罗门的儿子罗波安的时候，在耶路撒冷没有行过这样的事"（30:26）。他的改革证明：无论多么荒凉，只要真心悔改归向神，复兴仍然可能。

### II. The Kings of Judah — Extremes of Faith (Ch. 10–28)

After the kingdom divided, 2 Chronicles focuses exclusively on southern Judah (northern Israel is barely mentioned). Among Judah's twenty kings, piety and rebellion alternated.

Asa (ch. 14–16) was an early reforming king who prayed: "LORD, it is nothing with thee to help, whether with many, or with them that have no power" (14:11), and won a great victory over a million Cushite troops. Yet in later years he relied on Aram instead of trusting God, and even in illness "sought not to the LORD" (16:12).

Jehoshaphat (ch. 17–20) established a system of teaching the law, sending officials and Levites throughout the cities to instruct the people. Facing a vast Moabite-Ammonite army, his prayer became iconic: "We have no might against this great company that cometh against us; neither know we what to do: but our eyes are upon thee" (20:12).

Uzziah (ch. 26) was greatly powerful and "his name spread far abroad," yet his pride led him to burn incense in the temple, for which God struck him with leprosy. "When he was strong, his heart was lifted up to his destruction" (26:16) — power and success are faith's greatest tests.

Hezekiah (ch. 29–32) and Josiah (ch. 34–35) were Judah's last two great reformers. Hezekiah restored Passover throughout the nation; "since the time of Solomon the son of David king of Israel there was not the like in Jerusalem" (30:26). His reform proves: no matter how desolate things become, true repentance toward God makes revival possible.

## 三、被掳与盼望——居鲁士的诏书（36章）

历代志下最后一章快速记载犹大最后四个王，然后是耶路撒冷的陷落和圣殿的被毁。"迦勒底人焚烧神的殿，拆毁耶路撒冷的城墙，用火烧了城里的宫殿，毁坏了城里一切宝贵的器皿"（36:19）。

然而历代志下不像列王纪那样以黑暗结束。最后两节是整卷书的神学高潮："波斯王居鲁士元年，耶和华为要应验借耶利米口所说的话，就激动波斯王居鲁士的心，使他下诏通告全国"（36:22）。居鲁士宣告：要在耶路撒冷为耶和华建造殿宇，凡属祂的子民都可以上去。

这个结尾充满盼望：（一）神的话必定成就——耶利米预言七十年被掳，一年不多一年不少；（二）神掌管万国万王——波斯王居鲁士不知不觉地成为神旨意的执行者；（三）归回和重建是可能的——被掳不是终点，而是新的开始。

历代志下以归回的盼望作结，正好与以斯拉记的开头无缝衔接——因为传统认为同一位作者（以斯拉）写了这三卷书。

### III. Exile and Hope — The Edict of Cyrus (Ch. 36)

The final chapter of 2 Chronicles rapidly chronicles Judah's last four kings, then the fall of Jerusalem and destruction of the temple. "And they burnt the house of God, and brake down the wall of Jerusalem, and burnt all the palaces thereof with fire, and destroyed all the goodly vessels thereof" (36:19).

Yet 2 Chronicles does not end in darkness like Kings. The final two verses are the theological climax of the entire book: "Now in the first year of Cyrus king of Persia, that the word of the LORD spoken by the mouth of Jeremiah might be accomplished, the LORD stirred up the spirit of Cyrus king of Persia" (36:22). Cyrus proclaimed: let a house be built for the LORD in Jerusalem, and let all His people go up.

This ending overflows with hope: (1) God's word will surely be fulfilled — Jeremiah prophesied seventy years of exile, not one year more or less; (2) God rules over all nations and kings — Cyrus of Persia unwittingly became the executor of God's purpose; (3) return and rebuilding are possible — exile is not the end but a new beginning.

2 Chronicles closes with the hope of return, seamlessly connecting to the opening of Ezra — for tradition holds that the same author (Ezra) wrote all three books.

## 四、约沙法到希西家：忠信与背叛的交替（17-32章）

历代志下中段记录了一系列王的兴衰。约沙法"寻求他父亲大卫初行的道"（17:3），设立教师走遍犹大教导律法书。然而他与恶王亚哈联姻，差点害死自己（18章）。先知对他说："你岂当帮助恶人，爱那恨耶和华的人呢？"（19:2）

乌西雅王在忠心时蒙福，但心高气傲要自己进圣殿烧香，当场长了大麻风（26:16-20）。成功是最危险的试探——让人忘记自己的位份。

希西家在国家最危急的时刻——亚述大军兵临城下——把西拿基立的信"展开在耶和华面前"（32:20, 参以赛亚书37:14）祷告。一夜之间天使击杀亚述军十八万五千人（32:21）。希西家的信心展示了一个真理：靠神的人永远不孤单。

### IV. Jehoshaphat to Hezekiah: Alternating Faithfulness and Betrayal (Ch. 17-32)

The middle section of 2 Chronicles records the rise and fall of successive kings. Jehoshaphat "sought the LORD God of his father, and walked in his first ways" (17:3), appointing teachers to instruct Judah in the Book of the Law. Yet his marriage alliance with wicked Ahab nearly cost his life (ch. 18). The prophet told him: "Shouldest thou help the ungodly, and love them that hate the LORD?" (19:2)

King Uzziah prospered while faithful but grew proud and entered the temple to burn incense himself, immediately struck with leprosy (26:16-20). Success is the most dangerous temptation -- it makes people forget their place.

Hezekiah, at the nation's most critical moment -- the Assyrian army at the gates -- spread Sennacherib's letter "before the LORD" (32:20, cf. Isaiah 37:14) in prayer. In one night the angel of the LORD struck down 185,000 Assyrian soldiers (32:21). Hezekiah's faith demonstrates a truth: those who rely on God are never alone.

## 五、约西亚的改革与耶路撒冷的陷落（33-36章）

玛拿西是犹大最恶的王（33:1-9），但被掳后竟然悔改了——"他在急难的时候，就恳求耶和华他的神，且在他列祖的神面前极其自卑"（33:12）。这个记载只有历代志有，列王纪没有。历代志强调：悔改的门永远不关。

约西亚八岁登基，十六岁开始寻求神，二十岁开始洁净全地（34:1-7）。在修理圣殿时发现了被遗忘的律法书（34:14），他"撕裂衣服"带领全民立约。约西亚是犹大最后一位好王——但他独自一人的敬虔无法挽回整个民族的堕落。

历代志下最后四节记录了耶路撒冷的陷落和被掳。但末句令人瞩目——古列王的诏令（36:23）："耶和华天上的神已将天下万国赐给我，又嘱咐我在犹大的耶路撒冷为他建造殿宇。你们中间凡作他子民的，可以上去。"毁灭不是结局，归回已在路上。历代志以盼望结束。

### V. Josiah's Reform and the Fall of Jerusalem (Ch. 33-36)

Manasseh was Judah's most wicked king (33:1-9), yet after captivity he repented -- "when he was in affliction, he besought the LORD his God, and humbled himself greatly before the God of his fathers" (33:12). This account appears only in Chronicles, not in Kings. Chronicles emphasizes: the door of repentance is never closed.

Josiah became king at eight, began seeking God at sixteen, and cleansing the land at twenty (34:1-7). While repairing the temple, the forgotten Book of the Law was discovered (34:14); he "rent his clothes" and led the people to renew the covenant. Josiah was Judah's last good king -- but one man's piety alone could not reverse an entire nation's decline.

The final four verses of 2 Chronicles record Jerusalem's fall and exile. But the closing note is remarkable -- Cyrus' decree (36:23): "The LORD God of heaven hath given me all the kingdoms of the earth; and he hath charged me to build him an house in Jerusalem. Who is there among you of all his people? let him go up." Destruction is not the end; return is already on the way. Chronicles ends with hope.
