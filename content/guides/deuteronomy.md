# 申命记 导读 / Deuteronomy Guide

## 一、回顾旷野历程（1-4章）

申命记是摩西对新一代以色列人的临终讲道。在摩押平原上，摩西知道自己不能过约旦河，便将四十年的经历和教训向即将进入应许之地的年轻一代重新宣讲。

第一至三章回顾了从何烈山到摩押平原的旅程：加低斯巴尼亚的惨败、旷野的漫长漂流、以东和摩押的绕行、最终在约旦河东的军事胜利。摩西的目的不是写历史，而是从历史中提炼教训——看哪，不信带来什么后果！看哪，神的信实如何贯穿始终！

第四章是一段热烈的劝勉："以色列人哪，现在我所教训你们的律例典章，你们要听从遵行，好叫你们存活"（4:1）。摩西警戒百姓不可拜偶像、不可忘记在何烈山所看见的，并预言如果百姓悖逆将被分散到列邦——但"你们若从那里寻求耶和华你的神，你尽心尽性去寻求他的时候，就必寻见"（4:29）。审判中仍有恢复的盼望。

### I. Review of the Wilderness Journey (Ch. 1–4)

Deuteronomy is Moses' farewell sermon to the new generation of Israel. On the plains of Moab, knowing he could not cross the Jordan, Moses proclaimed afresh the experiences and lessons of forty years to the young generation about to enter the Promised Land.

Chapters 1–3 review the journey from Horeb to Moab: the catastrophe at Kadesh-barnea, the long wilderness wandering, the detour around Edom and Moab, and the final military victories east of the Jordan. Moses' purpose was not to write history but to distill lessons from it — Behold what unbelief produces! Behold how God's faithfulness persists throughout!

Chapter 4 is a passionate exhortation: "Now therefore hearken, O Israel, unto the statutes and unto the judgments, which I teach you, for to do them, that ye may live" (4:1). Moses warned against idolatry, against forgetting what they had seen at Horeb, and prophesied that if the people rebelled they would be scattered among the nations — yet "if from thence thou shalt seek the LORD thy God, thou shalt find him, if thou seek him with all thy heart and with all thy soul" (4:29). Even within judgment, there remains hope of restoration.

## 二、重申律法——十诫与"示玛"（5-11章）

第五章重新颁布十诫。与出埃及记20章相比，申命记的十诫有微妙的差异：安息日的理由从"因为耶和华六日创造天地"变为"因为你们在埃及地作过奴仆"——同一诫命，双重根基：创造与救赎。

第六章4-5节是犹太信仰的核心宣告——"示玛"（Shema，意为"听"）："以色列啊，你要听！耶和华我们神是独一的主。你要尽心、尽性、尽力爱耶和华你的神。"耶稣称这是"第一且是最大的诫命"（太22:37-38）。紧接着是关于教导下一代的吩咐："也要殷勤教训你的儿女，无论你坐在家里、行在路上、躺下、起来，都要谈论"（6:7）——信仰的传承是在生活中进行的，不仅是安息日的功课。

第七至十一章反复强调以色列被拣选不是因为自己的义或人数众多，而是"因为耶和华爱你们"（7:8）。摩西提醒百姓在富足之后不要忘记神："你要记念耶和华你的神，因为得货财的力量是他给你的"（8:18）。"你们不可心里说，耶和华将我领进来得这地，是因我的义"（9:4）——连进入应许之地也不是出于功德，而是恩典。

### II. Restating the Law — The Decalogue and the Shema (Ch. 5–11)

Chapter 5 re-proclaims the Ten Commandments. Compared with Exodus 20, Deuteronomy's version has subtle differences: the reason for the Sabbath shifts from "for in six days the LORD made heaven and earth" to "remember that thou wast a servant in the land of Egypt" — the same commandment with a dual foundation: creation and redemption.

Chapter 6:4–5 is the central confession of Jewish faith — the Shema (meaning "hear"): "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD: And thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy might." Jesus called this "the first and great commandment" (Matt 22:37–38). Immediately following is the charge to teach the next generation: "And thou shalt teach them diligently unto thy children, and shalt talk of them when thou sittest in thine house, and when thou walkest by the way, and when thou liest down, and when thou risest up" (6:7) — the transmission of faith happens in daily life, not merely as a Sabbath lesson.

Chapters 7–11 repeatedly emphasize that Israel was chosen not because of their own righteousness or great numbers, but "because the LORD loved you" (7:8). Moses reminded them not to forget God in prosperity: "Thou shalt remember the LORD thy God: for it is he that giveth thee power to get wealth" (8:18). "Speak not thou in thine heart... saying, For my righteousness the LORD hath brought me in to possess this land" (9:4) — even entering the Promised Land was not by merit but by grace.

## 三、具体律法条例（12-26章）

申命记中段是律法的具体应用，涵盖宗教生活、社会公义、家庭伦理、战争规则等各个层面。

敬拜的集中化（12章）是申命记最重要的律法改革之一：以色列人进入迦南后，必须在神所选择的地方（后来确定为耶路撒冷）敬拜，不可在各处高地随意设立祭坛。这是为了防止迦南宗教的渗透和混合主义。

假先知的辨别标准（13章）令人警醒：即使有人行了神迹奇事，若引导百姓去事奉别神，就当被处死。神迹不是真理的最终标准，与神话语的一致性才是。

社会律法体现了深刻的公义关怀：安息年免除债务（15章）、保护寄居者和孤儿寡妇（24:17-22）、公正的审判（16:19"不可屈枉正直"）、禁止挪移地界（19:14）。"你们要追求至公至义"（16:20）不仅是司法原则，更是神性情的反映。

第十八章预言神将兴起一位"像我（摩西）的先知"（18:15-18），这在使徒行传3:22-26被明确应用于基督——基督就是那位终极的先知。

### III. Specific Legal Statutes (Ch. 12–26)

The middle section of Deuteronomy applies the law to specific areas: religious life, social justice, family ethics, rules of warfare, and more.

The centralization of worship (ch. 12) is one of Deuteronomy's most important legal reforms: after entering Canaan, Israel must worship at the place God chooses (later identified as Jerusalem), not set up altars on every high place. This was to prevent the infiltration and syncretism of Canaanite religion.

The standard for discerning false prophets (ch. 13) is sobering: even if someone performs signs and wonders, if they lead people to serve other gods, they must be put to death. Miracles are not the ultimate criterion of truth; consistency with God's word is.

Social laws reflect profound concern for justice: Sabbath-year debt release (ch. 15), protection of sojourners, orphans, and widows (24:17–22), just judgment (16:18–20, "thou shalt not wrest judgment"), prohibition of moving boundary markers (19:14). "That which is altogether just shalt thou follow" (16:20) is not merely a judicial principle but a reflection of God's own character.

Chapter 18 prophesies that God will raise up a Prophet "like unto me [Moses]" (18:15–18), explicitly applied to Christ in Acts 3:22–26 — Christ is that ultimate Prophet.

## 四、约的更新——祝福与咒诅（27-30章）

第二十七至二十八章是申命记最庄严的部分。摩西吩咐以色列人进入迦南后，在以巴路山和基利心山举行立约仪式：六个支派站在基利心山宣读祝福，六个支派站在以巴路山宣读咒诅。

第二十八章的祝福与咒诅形成鲜明对比。祝福篇幅较短（1-14节），咒诅却长得多（15-68节）——这不是神偏爱审判，而是先知性的预言：以色列终将悖逆，咒诅终将临到。第28:64-67精确预言了犹太人被分散到列邦的命运，这在公元70年和之后的历史中逐一应验。

第三十章是申命记中最感人的经文之一。在列出所有祝福与咒诅之后，神向百姓发出呼吁："我将生死祸福陈明在你面前，所以你要拣选生命，使你和你的后裔都得存活；且爱耶和华你的神，听从他的话，专靠他"（30:19-20）。这不是律法主义的威胁，而是父亲对儿女的深情呼唤。

更令人惊叹的是30:6的应许："耶和华你神必将你心里和你后裔心里的污秽除掉，好叫你尽心尽性爱耶和华你的神，使你可以存活。"这就是新约的预言——神亲自施行心灵的割礼，使人有新的心来遵行祂的律法。

### IV. Covenant Renewal — Blessings and Curses (Ch. 27–30)

Chapters 27–28 are the most solemn section of Deuteronomy. Moses commanded that upon entering Canaan, Israel would conduct a covenant ceremony on Mount Ebal and Mount Gerizim: six tribes on Gerizim to pronounce blessings, six on Ebal to pronounce curses.

Chapter 28's blessings and curses form a stark contrast. The blessings are relatively brief (vv. 1–14); the curses are far longer (vv. 15–68) — not because God delights in judgment, but as prophetic foresight: Israel would ultimately rebel, and the curses would come. Verses 28:64–67 precisely foretold the Jewish people's scattering among the nations, fulfilled from AD 70 onward.

Chapter 30 contains some of the most moving words in Deuteronomy. After listing all blessings and curses, God appeals to His people: "I have set before thee this day life and good, and death and evil... therefore choose life, that both thou and thy seed may live: That thou mayest love the LORD thy God, and that thou mayest obey his voice, and that thou mayest cleave unto him" (30:19–20). This is not legalistic threat but a father's heartfelt plea to his children.

Even more astonishing is the promise of 30:6: "And the LORD thy God will circumcise thine heart, and the heart of thy seed, to love the LORD thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, that thou mayest live." This is a prophecy of the new covenant — God Himself performing heart-circumcision, giving a new heart to obey His law.

## 五、摩西之歌与摩西之死（31-34章）

第三十一章，摩西一百二十岁，将领导权正式交给约书亚："耶和华必在你前面行；他必与你同在，必不撇下你，也不丢弃你。不要惧怕，也不要惊惶。"（31:8）摩西将律法书交给祭司保管，吩咐每七年在住棚节向全民宣读。

摩西之歌（32章）是一首宏大的诗篇，概括了以色列与神的关系：神的完全与信实（"他是磐石，他的作为完全"）、以色列的忘恩与悖逆、神的审判与最终的怜悯。这首歌被命令教给百姓，在他们悖逆时成为见证——你们不能说没有被警告。

摩西最后为十二支派逐一祝福（33章），呼应雅各在创世记49章的祝福。然后摩西登上尼波山，神让他看见应许之地的全貌——从基列到但、拿弗他利、以法莲、玛拿西、犹大直到西海、南地和棕树城耶利哥的平原。"耶和华对他说：这就是我向亚伯拉罕、以撒、雅各起誓应许之地。我必叫你眼睛看见，你却不得过到那里去。"

摩西死后，"以色列中再没有兴起先知像摩西的"（34:10）——直到那位"像摩西的先知"耶稣基督来到。摩西五经以仰望应许之地作结，整部旧约都在仰望那位将要来的基督。

### V. The Song of Moses and the Death of Moses (Ch. 31–34)

In chapter 31, Moses at 120 years old formally transferred leadership to Joshua: "Be strong and of a good courage!... The LORD, he it is that doth go before thee." Moses entrusted the Book of the Law to the priests, commanding it be read to the entire nation every seven years at the Feast of Tabernacles.

The Song of Moses (ch. 32) is a grand poem summarizing Israel's relationship with God: God's perfection and faithfulness ("He is the Rock, his work is perfect"), Israel's ingratitude and rebellion, God's judgment and ultimate mercy. This song was commanded to be taught to the people, serving as a witness when they rebelled — you cannot say you were not warned.

Moses' final blessing of the twelve tribes (ch. 33) echoes Jacob's blessing in Genesis 49. Then Moses ascended Mount Nebo, where God showed him the entire Promised Land — from Gilead to Dan, Naphtali, Ephraim, Manasseh, Judah to the Western Sea, the Negev, and the plain of Jericho, the city of palm trees. "And the LORD said unto him, This is the land which I sware unto Abraham, unto Isaac, and unto Jacob... I have caused thee to see it with thine eyes, but thou shalt not go over thither."

After Moses" death, "there arose not a prophet since in Israel like unto Moses" (34:10) — until that Prophet "like unto Moses," Jesus Christ, came. The Pentateuch ends gazing toward the Promised Land; the entire Old Testament gazes toward the Christ who was to come.
