## 正文（中文）

七日的第一日清早，天还黑的时候，抹大拉的马利亚来到坟墓那里，看见石头从坟墓挪开了。她跑来见我和西门彼得，对我们说："有人把主从坟墓里挪了去，我们不知道放在那里。"

我和彼得立刻出来，往坟墓那里去。我们两个人同跑，我比彼得跑得快，先到了坟墓，低头往里看，看见细麻布还放在那里，只是没有进去。西门彼得随后也到了，进坟墓里去，就看见细麻布还放在那里，又看见主的裹头巾没有和细麻布放在一处，是另在一处卷着。

那细麻布和裹头巾的摆放让我震惊——它们还保持着包裹身体的形状，却是空的！如果有人挪走主的身体，必定会把布一起拿走，或者至少会弄乱。但这里的情形仿佛主的身体从裹尸布中穿透出去了一样。那一刻，我相信了！

"这门徒（就是我）看见就信了。"我信主复活了！因为我们从圣经上还不明白主必要从死里复活的道理，所以先前虽然主多次预言祂要复活，我们却不明白。但如今我看见空墓和那叠放整齐的细麻布，圣灵在我心中光照，我知道主真的复活了！

我们就回自己的住处去了。马利亚却站在坟墓外面哭泣。她哭的时候，低头往坟墓里看，看见两个天使，穿着白衣，在安放主身体的地方坐着，一个在头，一个在脚。他们对她说:"妇人，你为什么哭？"她说："因为有人把我主挪了去，我不知道放在那里。"

说了这话，她转过身来，看见主站在那里，却不知道是主。主对她说："妇人，为什么哭？你找谁呢？"马利亚以为是看园的，就对祂说："先生，若是你把祂移了去，请告诉我，你把祂放在那里，我便去取祂。"

主说："马利亚！"就一个名字，主用祂熟悉的声音叫她的名字，她立刻认出来了。她转过来，用希伯来话对祂说："拉波尼！"（就是夫子的意思）。她要抓住主，但主说："不要摸我，因我还没有升上去见我的父。你往我弟兄那里去，告诉他们说，我要升上去见我的父，也是你们的父，见我的神，也是你们的神。"

抹大拉的马利亚就去告诉门徒说："我已经看见了主。"她又将主对她说的这话告诉他们。主第一个向谁显现？不是彼得，不是我，而是一个曾被七个鬼附着的女人（路8:2）。这显明主的恩典何其丰富——祂拣选最不配的人作祂复活的第一个见证人。

那日（就是七日的第一日）晚上，门徒所在的地方，因怕犹太人，门都关了。主来，站在当中，对他们说："愿你们平安！"说了这话，就把手和肋旁指给他们看。门徒看见主，就喜乐了。主又对他们说："愿你们平安！父怎样差遣了我，我也照样差遣你们。"说了这话，就向他们吹一口气，说："你们受圣灵！你们赦免谁的罪，谁的罪就赦免了；你们留下谁的罪，谁的罪就留下了。"

但那十二个门徒中，有称为低土马的多马，主来的时候，他没在那里。其他门徒对他说："我们已经看见主了。"多马说："我非看见他手上的钉痕，用指头探入那钉痕，又用手探入他的肋旁，我总不信。"

过了八日，门徒又在屋里，多马也和他们同在。门都关了。主来，站在当中说："愿你们平安！"然后对多马说："伸过你的指头来，摸我的手；伸出你的手来，探入我的肋旁。不要疑惑，总要信！"

多马说："我的主！我的神！"（ὁ κύριός μου καὶ ὁ θεός μου）这是新约中对主的神性最清楚的宣认之一。主没有责备他僭妄，反而接受了这敬拜——因为祂配得敬拜，祂真是神！

主对他说："你因看见了我才信；那没有看见就信的有福了。"这话是对我们这些后来之人说的。我们没有像多马那样亲手摸过主复活的身体，但我们因着使徒的见证、因着圣灵的工作而相信，我们是有福的！

我写这本福音书，记录了许多神迹。主在门徒面前另外行了许多神迹，没有记在这书上。但记这些事要叫你们信耶稣是基督，是神的儿子，并且叫你们信了，就可以因祂的名得生命。

这就是我写这本书的目的：不是为了展示我的文笔，不是为了记录历史，而是为了让你们信——信耶稣是基督（那位受膏者，旧约应许的弥赛亚），是神的儿子（与父同质同荣的第二位格），并且因信得生命（不仅是肉身的生命，而是永恒的生命——认识父神和主耶稣基督的生命）。

我亲眼看见主复活的身体，我亲手摸过祂的钉痕，我亲耳听见祂说话。祂真的复活了！这不是幻觉，不是神话，而是历史的事实。因为祂活着，我们也要活着。因为祂得胜，我们也必得胜。

## Content (English)

On the first day of the week, early in the morning, while it was still dark, Mary Magdalene came to the tomb and saw that the stone had been taken away from the tomb. She ran and came to me and to Simon Peter and said to us, "They have taken the Lord out of the tomb, and we do not know where they have laid him."

Peter and I immediately went out and were going toward the tomb. Both of us were running together, but I outran Peter and reached the tomb first. Stooping to look in, I saw the linen cloths lying there, but I did not go in. Then Simon Peter came following me and went into the tomb. He saw the linen cloths lying there, and the face cloth, which had been on the Lord's head, not lying with the linen cloths but folded up in a place by itself.

The arrangement of the linen cloths and face cloth astonished me—they still maintained the shape of wrapping a body, yet were empty! If someone had moved the Lord's body, they would have taken the cloths too, or at least would have disturbed them. But the scene here was as if the Lord's body had passed through the burial cloths. In that moment, I believed!

"This disciple (that is, me) saw and believed." I believed the Lord had risen! Because we did not yet understand from Scripture that the Lord must rise from the dead, though He had prophesied His resurrection many times, we did not understand. But now I saw the empty tomb and the neatly arranged linen cloths, the Holy Spirit illuminated my heart, and I knew the Lord had truly risen!

We went back to our homes. But Mary stood weeping outside the tomb. As she wept she stooped to look into the tomb and saw two angels in white, sitting where the body of the Lord had lain, one at the head and one at the feet. They said to her, "Woman, why are you weeping?" She said to them, "They have taken away my Lord, and I do not know where they have laid him."

Having said this, she turned around and saw the Lord standing, but she did not know it was the Lord. The Lord said to her, "Woman, why are you weeping? Whom are you seeking?" Supposing him to be the gardener, she said to him, "Sir, if you have carried him away, tell me where you have laid him, and I will take him away."

The Lord said, "Mary!" Just one name, the Lord calling her name in His familiar voice, and she immediately recognized Him. She turned and said to Him in Aramaic, "Rabboni!" (which means Teacher). She wanted to hold on to the Lord, but He said, "Do not cling to me, for I have not yet ascended to the Father; but go to my brothers and say to them, 'I am ascending to my Father and your Father, to my God and your God.'"

Mary Magdalene went and announced to the disciples, "I have seen the Lord"—and she told them these things He had said to her. To whom did the Lord first appear? Not to Peter, not to me, but to a woman who had been possessed by seven demons (Luke 8:2). This reveals how abundant the Lord's grace is—He chose the least deserving person to be the first witness of His resurrection.

On that same day (the first day of the week) in the evening, the doors being locked where the disciples were for fear of the Jews, the Lord came and stood among them and said to them, "Peace be with you!" Having said this, He showed them His hands and His side. Then the disciples were glad when they saw the Lord. The Lord said to them again, "Peace be with you. As the Father has sent me, even so I am sending you." And when He had said this, He breathed on them and said to them, "Receive the Holy Spirit. If you forgive the sins of any, they are forgiven them; if you withhold forgiveness from any, it is withheld."

But Thomas, one of the twelve, called the Twin, was not with them when the Lord came. So the other disciples told him, "We have seen the Lord." But he said to them, "Unless I see in his hands the mark of the nails, and place my finger into the mark of the nails, and place my hand into his side, I will never believe."

Eight days later, the disciples were inside again, and Thomas was with them. The doors were locked, but the Lord came and stood among them and said, "Peace be with you!" Then He said to Thomas, "Put your finger here, and see my hands; and put out your hand, and place it in my side. Do not disbelieve, but believe."

Thomas said, "My Lord and my God!" (ὁ κύριός μου καὶ ὁ θεός μου) This is one of the clearest confessions of the Lord's deity in the New Testament. The Lord did not rebuke him for presumption, but accepted this worship—because He is worthy of worship, He truly is God!

The Lord said to him, "Have you believed because you have seen me? Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed." These words are for us who come later. We have not, like Thomas, physically touched the Lord's risen body, but we believe through apostolic testimony and the work of the Holy Spirit—we are blessed!

I wrote this Gospel and recorded many signs. The Lord did many other signs in the presence of the disciples that are not written in this book. But these are written so that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that by believing you may have life in his name.

This is the purpose of my writing this book: not to display my literary skill, not to record history, but so that you may believe—believe that Jesus is the Christ (the Anointed One, the Messiah promised in the Old Testament), the Son of God (the second person, of the same substance and glory as the Father), and have life through believing (not merely physical life, but eternal life—the life of knowing God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ).

I saw with my own eyes the Lord's risen body, I touched with my own hands His nail marks, I heard with my own ears His voice. He truly rose! This is not a hallucination, not a myth, but a historical fact. Because He lives, we too shall live. Because He conquered, we too shall conquer.

## 历史背景（中文）

第20章记录主复活当日和八日后的显现。"七日的第一日"（1、19节）就是星期日，成为基督徒聚会敬拜的日子（徒20:7；林前16:2；启1:10"主日"）。

**抹大拉的马利亚**：来自加利利海边的抹大拉城，曾被七个鬼附着，被主医治后跟随主（路8:2）。她是十字架下和复活清晨最忠心的见证人。在当时的文化中，妇女的见证不被法庭采纳，但主拣选她作第一个见证人，显明福音的革命性。

**空坟墓**：约瑟夫（犹太历史学家）和罗马档案都没有否认空坟墓的事实。犹太领袖编造"门徒偷走了身体"的谎言（太28:11-15），反而证明坟墓确实是空的。如果主没有复活，犹太人只需展示尸体就能驳倒基督教，但他们做不到。

**复活的身体**：主的复活不是"灵魂不灭"或"精神永存"的希腊哲学概念，而是身体的复活。主能吃东西（路24:42-43；约21:12-13），能被触摸（约20:27），身上有钉痕（20节）——但又能穿过关闭的门（19、26节），不受物理限制。这是"荣耀的身体"（腓3:21），是我们将来复活身体的样式（林前15:42-44）。

**使徒的见证**：保罗在哥林多前书15:3-8列出复活的见证人：彼得、十二使徒、五百多弟兄、雅各、众使徒、保罗自己。这些人大部分在保罗写信时还活着（"其中一大半到如今还在"），可以被查验。复活不是几个人的幻觉，而是数百人的集体见证。

**"八日后"**（26节）：即下一个星期日。这成为基督徒每周聚会的模式。从此，教会不再守旧约的安息日（星期六），而是守"主日"（星期日）——纪念主复活的日子。

## Historical Context (English)

Chapter 20 records the Lord's appearances on the day of resurrection and eight days later. "The first day of the week" (vv.1, 19) is Sunday, which became the day Christians gather for worship (Acts 20:7; 1 Cor 16:2; Rev 1:10 "Lord's Day").

**Mary Magdalene**: From the town of Magdala by the Sea of Galilee, she had been possessed by seven demons and was healed by the Lord, then followed Him (Luke 8:2). She was the most faithful witness at the cross and on resurrection morning. In the culture of that time, women's testimony was not accepted in court, but the Lord chose her as the first witness, revealing the revolutionary nature of the gospel.

**The Empty Tomb**: Josephus (Jewish historian) and Roman records did not deny the fact of the empty tomb. Jewish leaders fabricated the lie "Now when they were going, behold, some of the watch came into the city, and shewed unto the chief priests all the things that were done. And when they were assembled with the elders, and had taken counsel, they gave large money unto the soldiers, Saying, Say ye, His disciples came by night, and stole him away while we slept. And if this come to the governor’s ears, we will persuade him, and secure you. So they took the money, and did as they were taught: and this saying is commonly reported among the Jews until this day." (Matt 28:11-15), which actually proves the tomb was indeed empty. If the Lord had not risen, the Jews could have refuted Christianity by simply displaying the corpse, but they could not.

**The Resurrection Body**: The Lord's resurrection was not the Greek philosophical concept of "immortality of the soul" or "eternal spirit," but bodily resurrection. The Lord could eat (Luke 24:42-43; John 21:12-13), could be touched (John 20:27), had nail marks on His body (v.20)—but could also pass through locked doors (vv.19, 26), not bound by physical limitations. This is a "Who shall change our vile body, that it may be fashioned like unto his glorious body, according to the working whereby he is able even to subdue all things unto himself." (Phil 3:21), the pattern for our future resurrection bodies (1 Cor 15:42-44).

**Apostolic Witness**: Paul in 1 Corinthians 15:3-8 lists witnesses to the resurrection: Peter, the twelve apostles, more than five hundred brothers, James, all the apostles, and Paul himself. Most of these people were still alive when Paul wrote ("most of whom are still alive"), and could be verified. The resurrection was not a hallucination of a few people, but the collective testimony of hundreds.

**"Eight Days Later"** (v.26): That is, the next Sunday. This became the pattern for Christians' weekly gatherings. From then on, the church no longer kept the Old Testament Sabbath (Saturday), but kept the "Lord's Day" (Sunday)—commemorating the day of the Lord's resurrection.

## 经文结构（中文）

本章可分为四个场景：

**一、空坟墓（20:1-10）**
抹大拉的马利亚发现空墓（1节）。彼得和约翰跑到坟墓（2-8节）。约翰看见细麻布的摆放就信了（8节）。两位使徒回去（10节）。这部分强调空墓的见证——主的身体不见了，但不是被偷走，而是复活了。

**二、向马利亚显现（20:11-18）**
马利亚在坟墓外哭泣，看见天使（11-13节）。主向她显现，叫她的名字（14-16节）。主差遣她去报信（17-18节）。这部分强调主的声音——"好牧人...羊也认得他的声音"（约10:3-4）。

**三、向门徒显现（20:19-23）**
主在当日晚上向门徒显现（19节）。主显示钉痕和肋旁的伤（20节）。主差遣门徒，赐圣灵（21-23节）。这部分强调主的差遣和圣灵的恩赐。

**四、向多马显现（20:24-31）**
多马不信其他门徒的见证（24-25节）。八日后主向多马显现（26-27节）。多马认信"我的主！我的神！"（28节）。主论"没有看见就信"的人有福（29节）。约翰说明写书的目的（30-31节）。这部分强调信心的本质——不是凭眼见，而是凭见证和圣灵的工作。

整章结构呈现：空墓的事实 → 主的显现 → 使徒的使命 → 信心的呼召。从历史事实到生命回应。

## Structure (English)

This chapter can be divided into four scenes:

**I. The Empty Tomb (20:1-10)**
Mary Magdalene discovers the empty tomb (v.1). Peter and John run to the tomb (vv.2-8). John sees the arrangement of the linen cloths and believes (v.8). The two apostles go back (v.10). This section emphasizes the testimony of the empty tomb—the Lord's body is gone, not stolen, but risen.

**II. Appearance to Mary (20:11-18)**
Mary weeps outside the tomb, sees angels (vv.11-13). The Lord appears to her, calls her name (vv.14-16). The Lord sends her to tell the news (vv.17-18). This section emphasizes the Lord's voice—"To him the porter openeth; and the sheep hear his voice: and he calleth his own sheep by name, and leadeth them out. And when he putteth forth his own sheep, he goeth before them, and the sheep follow him: for they know his voice." (John 10:3-4).

**III. Appearance to the Disciples (20:19-23)**
The Lord appears to the disciples that evening (v.19). The Lord shows His nail marks and wounded side (v.20). The Lord sends the disciples, gives the Holy Spirit (vv.21-23). This section emphasizes the Lord's commission and the gift of the Spirit.

**IV. Appearance to Thomas (20:24-31)**
Thomas does not believe the other disciples' testimony (vv.24-25). Eight days later the Lord appears to Thomas (vv.26-27). Thomas confesses "My Lord and my God!" (v.28). The Lord speaks about those who believe "without seeing" being blessed (v.29). John explains the purpose of writing the book (vv.30-31). This section emphasizes the nature of faith—not by sight, but by testimony and the Spirit's work.

The structure of the entire chapter presents: the fact of the empty tomb → the Lord's appearances → the apostles' mission → the call to faith. From historical fact to life response.

## 神学要点（中文）

**复活的历史性**
改革宗神学坚持基督复活是客观的历史事实，不是主观的宗教经验。空坟墓、多次显现、门徒的转变（从惊恐到勇敢）、教会的诞生——这些都是复活的证据。保罗说："基督若没有复活，我们所传的便是枉然，你们所信的也是枉然"（林前15:14）。复活不是可有可无的附加信念，而是基督教信仰的基石。

**复活的身体**
主的复活显明信徒将来复活的样式。改革宗神学反对诺斯底主义（贬低身体）和物质主义（只看重身体）。我们相信"身体复活"（韦敏斯德信条32章）：灵魂与身体将要重新结合，身体要变化成为"荣耀的身体"（林前15:42-44；腓3:21）。这身体既有延续性（是同一个身体，有钉痕），又有更新性（不朽坏、荣耀、强壮、属灵）。

**基督的神性**
多马的认信"我的主！我的神！"（28节）清楚宣告主的神性。这不是感叹语（如"我的天！"），而是对主的敬拜——主若不是神，多马就是亵渎。但主接受了这敬拜，证明祂确实是神。约翰福音从1:1"道就是神"开始，到20:28"我的主！我的神！"结束，首尾呼应，清楚宣告基督的神性。

**使徒职分**
主说："父怎样差遣了我，我也照样差遣你们"（21节）。使徒是主特别差遣的见证人，有特殊的权柄（赦罪的钥匙权，23节）。改革宗神学区分"使徒职分"（一次性的、奠基性的）和"牧师职分"（持续的、建造性的）。使徒的教训成为教会的根基（弗2:20），记录在新约圣经中，对教会有永久的权威。

**信心的本质**
"那没有看见就信的有福了"（29节）。真信心不是凭眼见，而是凭神的话和圣灵的工作。希伯来书11:1说："信就是所望之事的实底，是未见之事的确据。"我们不需要看见、摸到主的身体才能信；我们因使徒的见证（约翰写的这本书）、因圣灵的光照而信，我们是有福的。

**约翰的目的宣言**
"记这些事要叫你们信耶稣是基督，是神的儿子，并且叫你们信了，就可以因祂的名得生命"（31节）。这不仅是本章的结束，也是整本约翰福音的主题宣言。约翰的目的是传福音（叫人信）和造就信徒（叫信的人得生命）。这两者不可分割：信心带来生命，生命来自于信心。

## Theology (English)

**Historicity of the Resurrection**
Reformed theology insists that Christ's resurrection is an objective historical fact, not a subjective religious experience. The empty tomb, multiple appearances, the transformation of the disciples (from fear to courage), the birth of the church—these are all evidence of the resurrection. Paul says: "And if Christ be not risen, then is our preaching vain, and your faith is also vain." (1 Cor 15:14). The resurrection is not an optional add-on belief, but the cornerstone of Christian faith.

**The Resurrection Body**
The Lord's resurrection reveals the pattern of believers' future resurrection. Reformed theology rejects Gnosticism (demeaning the body) and materialism (only valuing the body). We believe in "bodily resurrection" (Westminster Confession 32): soul and body will be reunited, and the body will be transformed into a "glorious body" (1 Cor 15:42-44; Phil 3:21). This body has both continuity (it is the same body, with nail marks) and renewal (imperishable, glorious, powerful, spiritual).

**Christ's Deity**
Thomas's confession "My Lord and my God!" (v.28) clearly declares the Lord's deity. This is not an exclamation (like "Oh my God!"), but worship of the Lord—if the Lord were not God, Thomas would be blaspheming. But the Lord accepted this worship, proving He indeed is God. John's Gospel begins with 1:1 "the Word was God" and ends with 20:28 "My Lord and my God!", bookending the clear declaration of Christ's deity.

**Apostolic Office**
The Lord said: "As the Father has sent me, even so I am sending you" (v.21). The apostles were witnesses specially commissioned by the Lord, with special authority (the power of the keys for forgiveness, v.23). Reformed theology distinguishes between "apostolic office" (one-time, foundational) and "pastoral office" (ongoing, building). Apostolic teaching becomes the foundation of the church (Eph 2:20), recorded in the New Testament, with permanent authority over the church.

**The Nature of Faith**
"Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed" (v.29). True faith is not by sight, but by God's Word and the Spirit's work. Hebrews 11:1 says: "Now faith is the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen." We do not need to see or touch the Lord's body to believe; we believe through apostolic testimony (this book John wrote) and the Spirit's illumination—we are blessed.

**John's Purpose Statement**
"These are written so that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that by believing you may have life in his name" (v.31). This is not only the conclusion of this chapter, but the thematic declaration of the entire Gospel of John. John's purpose is evangelism (that people may believe) and edification of believers (that believers may have life). These two are inseparable: faith brings life, life comes from faith.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**第三日复活**
何西阿书6:2预言："过两天他必使我们苏醒，第三日他必使我们兴起，我们就在他面前得以存活。"这预言在主身上应验——第三日从死里复活。"第三日"在旧约中常是拯救临到的日子：亚伯拉罕第三日看见献以撒的地方（创22:4），约瑟第三日释放哥哥们（创42:18），以色列人第三日要预备好见神（出19:11）。主在第三日复活，成就了所有这些预表。

**初熟的果子**
哥林多前书15:20说："基督已经从死里复活，成为睡了之人初熟的果子。"利未记23:10-11规定，以色列人要在安息日的次日（七日的第一日）将初熟的庄稼献给神。主正是在这一天复活，应验了初熟果子的预表。祂的复活保证我们也要复活——正如初熟的果子保证整个收成必要来到。

**新造的开始**
"七日的第一日"（1、19节）不仅纪念创造（创1:1-5第一日造光），更是新造的开始。主的复活开启新时代：旧事已过，都变成新的了（林后5:17）。从此，教会以"主日"取代"安息日"——不是守律法的结束（旧造的第七日），而是庆祝救恩的成就（新造的第一日）。

**好牧人的声音**
主叫"马利亚"（16节），她就认出来了。这应验了约翰福音10:3："羊也听他的声音。他按着名叫自己的羊，把羊领出来。"以西结书34:11-16神应许："我必亲自寻找我的羊...失丧的，我必寻找。"主复活后寻找马利亚，叫她的名字，这正是大牧人寻回迷失羊的画面。

**赐下圣灵**
主向门徒"吹一口气"（22节），使他们受圣灵。这预表五旬节的圣灵降临，也回应创世记2:7："耶和华神...将生气吹在他鼻孔里，他就成了有灵的活人。"正如神吹气给亚当生命，复活的主吹气给门徒属灵的生命。以西结书37:9-10枯骨复活的异象也在此应验——圣灵的气使死人活过来。

**钉痕的记号**
主复活的身体仍保留钉痕和枪伤（20、27节）。这些伤痕在永恒中成为祂受苦和得胜的记号。启示录5:6看见宝座中有羔羊，"像是被杀过的"——即使在天上的荣耀里，主仍带着为我们受死的记号。这是何等的爱！祂愿意永远带着这些伤痕，提醒我们祂为我们付出的代价。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Risen on the Third Day**
Hosea 6:2 prophesies: "After two days he will revive us; on the third day he will raise us up, that we may live before him." This prophecy was fulfilled in the Lord—risen from the dead on the third day. "The third day" in the Old Testament is often the day deliverance comes: Abraham on the third day saw the place to offer Isaac (Gen 22:4), Joseph on the third day released his brothers (Gen 42:18), Israel on the third day was to prepare to meet God (Exod 19:11). The Lord rose on the third day, fulfilling all these types.

**The Firstfruits**
1 Corinthians 15:20 says: "But in fact Christ has been raised from the dead, the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep." Leviticus 23:10-11 prescribed that Israel was to offer the firstfruits of the harvest to God the day after the Sabbath (the first day of the week). The Lord rose on this very day, fulfilling the type of the firstfruits. His resurrection guarantees we too will rise—just as the firstfruits guarantee the entire harvest will come.

**Beginning of New Creation**
"The first day of the week" (vv.1, 19) not only commemorates creation (Gen 1:1-5, the first day creating light), but is the beginning of new creation. The Lord's resurrection inaugurated a new era: the old has passed away, all has become new (2 Cor 5:17). From then on, the church replaced the "Sabbath" with the "Lord's Day"—not keeping the end of the law (the seventh day of old creation), but celebrating the accomplishment of salvation (the first day of new creation).

**The Good Shepherd's Voice**
The Lord called "Mary" (v.16), and she recognized Him. This fulfilled John 10:3: "The sheep hear his voice, and he calls his own sheep by name and leads them out." Ezekiel 34:11-16 God promised: "I myself will search for my sheep...the lost I will seek." After His resurrection, the Lord sought Mary and called her name—this is the picture of the Great Shepherd finding the lost sheep.

**Giving the Spirit**
The Lord "breathed on" the disciples (v.22), making them receive the Holy Spirit. This prefigured the descent of the Spirit at Pentecost, and echoed Genesis 2:7: "The LORD God...breathed into his nostrils the breath of life, and the man became a living creature." Just as God breathed life into Adam, the risen Lord breathed spiritual life into the disciples. Ezekiel 37:9-10, the vision of the valley of dry bones coming to life, was also fulfilled here—the Spirit's breath makes the dead live.

**The Mark of Nail Scars**
The Lord's risen body still bore the nail marks and spear wound (vv.20, 27). These scars in eternity become the marks of His suffering and victory. Revelation 5:6 sees in the throne a Lamb "standing, as though it had been slain"—even in the glory of heaven, the Lord still bears the marks of dying for us. What love! He is willing to bear these scars forever, reminding us of the price He paid for us.
