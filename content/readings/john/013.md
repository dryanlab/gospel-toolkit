## 正文（中文）

我约翰永远忘不了那最后晚餐的夜晚。逾越节以前，主知道自己离世归父的时候到了。"祂既然爱世间属自己的人，就爱他们到底。"（13:1）这句话何等宝贵——主对我们的爱不是半途而废，乃是爱到底、爱到完全。

吃晚饭的时候，魔鬼已将卖主的意思放在加略人犹大心里。主知道父已将万有交在祂手里，且知道自己是从神出来的，又要归到神那里去。就在这时候——祂最荣耀的时刻前夕——祂做了一件最卑微的事。

主离席站起来，脱了衣服，拿一条手巾束腰，把水倒在盆里，就洗门徒的脚，并用自己所束的手巾擦干。洗脚是奴仆的工作，是最低贱的服侍。但主——神的儿子、万有的主——竟俯身洗我们的脚！

挨到我的哥哥西门彼得，彼得对主说："主啊，你洗我的脚吗？"主回答说："我所做的，你如今不知道，后来必明白。"彼得说："你永不可洗我的脚！"主说："我若不洗你，你就与我无分了。"彼得立刻说："主啊，不但我的脚，连手和头也要洗。"

主说："凡洗过澡的人，只要把脚一洗，全身就干净了。你们是干净的，然而不都是干净的。"主原知道要卖祂的是谁，所以说"你们不都是干净的"。

洗完了脚，主穿上衣服又坐下，问我们："我向你们所做的，你们明白吗？你们称呼我夫子，称呼我主，你们说的不错，我本来是。我是你们的主，你们的夫子，尚且洗你们的脚，你们也当彼此洗脚。我给你们做了榜样，叫你们照着我向你们所做的去做。"（13:12-15）

弟兄姊妹，这不仅是服侍的榜样，更是福音的图画！主洗我们的脚，象征祂洁净我们的罪。我们本是污秽的，祂用祂的宝血洗净我们。彼得说"你永不可洗我的脚"，显出人的骄傲——不愿接受恩典、不愿承认需要洁净。但主说"我若不洗你，你就与我无分"——若不接受基督的洁净，就不能与祂有份。

然后主说："我这话不是指着你们众人说的，我知道我所拣选的是谁...同我吃饭的人用脚踢我。"（13:18,引诗41:9）主心里忧愁，就明说："我实实在在地告诉你们，你们中间有一个人要卖我了。"

我们门徒彼此对看，猜不透所说的是谁。我侧身挨近主的怀里（那是最亲密的位置），彼得点头对我说："你告诉我们，主是指着谁说的。"我便就势靠着主的胸膛，问祂："主啊，是谁呢？"主回答说："我蘸一点饼给谁，就是谁。"主就蘸了一点饼，递给加略人西门的儿子犹大。

犹大吃了以后，撒但就入了他的心。主便对他说："你所做的，快做吧！"同席的人没有一个知道是为什么对他说这话。犹大受了那点饼，立刻就出去。那时候是夜间了。

"那时候是夜间了"——这句话何等深沉！不仅是时间上的夜晚，更是灵性上的黑暗。犹大离开光（主）,走向黑暗（撒但）；他离开生命，走向死亡。

犹大既出去，主就说："如今人子得了荣耀，神在人子身上也得了荣耀。神要因自己荣耀人子，并且要快快地荣耀他。"（13:31-32）十架的时刻近了，但主不说"我要受苦了"，而说"人子得荣耀了"！十架在人看是羞辱，在神看却是荣耀的高峰。

然后主说："小子们，我还有不多的时候与你们同在...我赐给你们一条新命令，乃是叫你们彼此相爱；我怎样爱你们，你们也要怎样相爱。你们若有彼此相爱的心，众人因此就认出你们是我的门徒了。"（13:33-35）

这新命令的新，不在于"爱"是新的，乃在于标准是新的——"我怎样爱你们，你们也要怎样相爱"。主怎样爱我们？祂为我们舍命（15:13）、洗我们的脚、爱我们到底。这是何等高的标准！不是人本性能做到的，乃是唯有蒙恩的人、有圣灵内住的人才能活出的爱。

彼得问："主往哪里去？"主回答："我所去的地方，你现在不能跟我去，后来却要跟我去。"彼得说："主啊，我为什么现在不能跟你去？我愿意为你舍命！"主说："你愿意为我舍命吗？我实实在在地告诉你，鸡叫以先，你要三次不认我。"（13:36-38）

这预言何等精准！彼得以为自己忠心，主却知道他的软弱。但主仍爱他、仍为他祷告、仍在复活后恢复他。我们的失败不能改变主的爱，因为祂爱我们到底。

## Content (English)

I, John, will never forget that night of the Last Supper. Before the Passover feast, the Lord knew that His hour had come to depart out of this world to the Father. "Having loved his own who were in the world, he loved them to the end." (13:1) What a precious statement—the Lord's love for us is not halfway but to the end, to completion.

During supper, the devil had already put it into the heart of Judas Iscariot to betray Him. The Lord, knowing that the Father had given all things into His hands, and that He had come from God and was going back to God—at this moment, on the eve of His most glorious hour—He did the most humble thing.

The Lord rose from supper, laid aside His outer garments, took a towel and tied it around His waist. He poured water into a basin and began to wash the disciples' feet and to wipe them with the towel. Foot washing was a slave's work, the most menial service. But the Lord—the Son of God, Lord of all—stooped to wash our feet!

When He came to my brother Simon Peter, Peter said to Him, "Lord, do you wash my feet?" The Lord answered, "What I am doing you do not understand now, but afterward you will understand." Peter said, "You shall never wash my feet!" The Lord said, "If I do not wash you, you have no share with me." Peter immediately said, "Lord, not my feet only but also my hands and my head!"

The Lord said: "The one who has bathed does not need to wash, except for his feet, but is completely clean. And you are clean, but not every one of you." The Lord knew who was to betray Him; that was why He said, "Not all of you are clean."

After washing their feet, the Lord put on His outer garments, reclined again, and asked us: "Do you understand what I have done to you? You call me Teacher and Lord, and you are right, for so I am. If I then, your Lord and Teacher, have washed your feet, you also ought to wash one another's feet. For I have given you an example, that you also should do just as I have done to you." (13:12-15)

Brothers and sisters, this is not merely an example of service but a picture of the gospel! The Lord washing our feet symbolizes Him cleansing us from sin. We are filthy; He cleanses us with His precious blood. Peter saying "You shall never wash my feet" shows human pride—unwilling to accept grace, unwilling to admit the need for cleansing. But the Lord said "If I do not wash you, you have no share with me"—without accepting Christ's cleansing, one cannot have part with Him.

Then the Lord said: "I am not speaking of all of you; I know whom I have chosen... He who ate my bread has lifted his heel against me." (13:18, quoting Ps 41:9) The Lord was troubled in spirit and testified: "Truly, truly, I say to you, one of you will betray me."

We disciples looked at one another, uncertain of whom He spoke. I was reclining close to the Lord's side (the most intimate position). Peter motioned to me to ask, "Tell us who it is." So I leaned back against the Lord's chest and asked, "Lord, who is it?" The Lord answered, "It is he to whom I will give this morsel of bread when I have dipped it." So when He had dipped the morsel, He gave it to Judas, the son of Simon Iscariot.

After Judas took the morsel, Satan entered into him. The Lord said to him, "What you are going to do, do quickly." No one at the table knew why He said this to him. After receiving the morsel, Judas immediately went out. And it was night.

"And it was night"—how profound this statement! Not merely nighttime but spiritual darkness. Judas left the Light (the Lord) and walked toward darkness (Satan); he left life and walked toward death.

When Judas had gone out, the Lord said: "Now is the Son of Man glorified, and God is glorified in him. If God is glorified in him, God will also glorify him in himself, and glorify him at once." (13:31-32) The hour of the cross was near, but the Lord did not say "I will suffer" but "the Son of Man is glorified!" The cross to humans is shame, to God it is the pinnacle of glory.

Then the Lord said: "Little children, yet a little while I am with you... A new commandment I give to you, that you love one another: just as I have loved you, you also are to love one another. By this all people will know that you are my disciples, if you have love for one another." (13:33-35)

The newness of this new commandment is not that "love" is new, but that the standard is new—"just as I have loved you, you also are to love one another." How did the Lord love us? He laid down His life for us (15:13), washed our feet, loved us to the end. What a high standard! Not what human nature can achieve, but love that only those who have received grace, who have the Spirit dwelling within, can live out.

Peter asked, "Lord, where are you going?" The Lord answered, "Where I am going you cannot follow me now, but you will follow afterward." Peter said, "Lord, why can I not follow you now? I will lay down my life for you!" The Lord said, "Will you lay down your life for me? Truly, truly, I say to you, the rooster will not crow till you have denied me three times." (13:36-38)

How precise this prophecy! Peter thought himself loyal; the Lord knew his weakness. But the Lord still loved him, still prayed for him, still restored him after resurrection. Our failures cannot change the Lord's love, because He loves us to the end.

## 历史背景（中文）

第13章开始"逾越节以前"，但14:31"起来,我们走吧"之后才离开楼房。约翰福音13-17章（"楼房讲论"）都发生在最后晚餐期间,对应符类福音中的设立圣餐和客西马尼园之前的教导。

洗脚在第一世纪的中东是必要的礼节。道路满是尘土，人穿凉鞋，脚容易脏。通常由最低等的仆人（甚至外邦奴隶）负责为客人洗脚。主人亲自洗客人的脚是闻所未闻的降卑。主这么做，震撼了门徒的世界观。

犹大"侧身挨近主的怀里"（13:23,25）——古代近东宴席是斜倚在矮榻上，呈U字形围绕矮桌。最靠近主人胸前的位置是最尊贵的位置（通常给最亲密的朋友）。约翰称自己是"主所爱的门徒"（13:23），不是骄傲，乃是谦卑地强调主的恩典——他深知自己不配,却蒙主特别的爱。

"蘸饼"（13:26）是古代宴席中主人给尊贵客人的礼遇——把饼蘸在共同的盘子里(苦菜、酱汁等),递给对方,是友谊和尊重的标志。主给犹大蘸饼，是最后一次呼召他悔改的机会。但犹大拒绝了，"撒但就入了他的心"（13:27）。

"那时候是夜间了"（13:30）——约翰善用象征手法。夜间不仅指时间，也指灵性状态。整卷约翰福音以光与暗对比为主线（1:5"光照在黑暗里"，3:19"光来到世间，世人...不爱光倒爱黑暗"，8:12"我是世界的光"）。犹大离开主（光）,进入夜间（黑暗），象征他彻底离弃真理，走向灭亡。

## Historical Context (English)

Chapter 13 begins "before the Feast of the Passover," but they did not leave the upper room until 14:31 "Rise, let us go from here." John chapters 13-17 (the "Upper Room Discourse") all occur during the Last Supper, corresponding to the institution of the Lord's Supper and pre-Gethsemane teachings in the Synoptic Gospels.

Foot washing was a necessary courtesy in the first-century Middle East. Roads were dusty, people wore sandals, feet easily got dirty. Usually the lowest servant (even a Gentile slave) was responsible for washing guests' feet. A master personally washing guests' feet was unheard-of condescension. The Lord doing this shook the disciples' worldview.

"Reclining at table close to the breast of Jesus" (13:23,25)—ancient Near Eastern banquets involved reclining on low couches in a U-shape around a low table. The position closest to the host's chest was the most honored (usually given to the closest friend). John calling himself "the disciple whom Jesus loved" (13:23) is not pride but humble emphasis on the Lord's grace—he deeply knew his unworthiness yet received the Lord's special love.

"Dipping the morsel" (13:26) was a courtesy at ancient banquets where the host gave to an honored guest—dipping bread in the common dish (bitter herbs, sauce, etc.) and handing it over was a sign of friendship and respect. The Lord giving the dipped morsel to Judas was a final opportunity to call him to repentance. But Judas refused, and "Satan entered into him" (13:27).

"And it was night" (13:30)—John is skilled at symbolism. Night refers not only to time but also to spiritual state. The entire Gospel of John uses the contrast of light and darkness as its main thread (1:5 "The light shines in the darkness," 3:19 "the light has come into the world, and people loved the darkness," 8:12 "I am the light of the world"). Judas leaving the Lord (light) and entering the night (darkness) symbolizes his complete abandonment of truth, heading toward perdition.

## 经文结构（中文）

**一、洗脚的服侍（13:1-20）** — 主知道自己的时候到了，"既然爱世间属自己的人，就爱他们到底"。主起来洗门徒的脚。彼得拒绝，主说"我若不洗你，你就与我无分"。洗完后主解释："我给你们做了榜样...仆人不能大于主人。"这不仅是服侍的榜样，更是救赎的图画——唯有基督能洁净我们，我们必须接受祂的洁净才能与祂有份。

**二、预言犹大卖主（13:21-30）** — 主心里忧愁，明说"你们中间有一个人要卖我"。门徒彼此对看，不知所指。约翰靠主胸前问是谁，主蘸饼递给犹大。撒但入了犹大的心，主说"你所做的，快做吧"。犹大就出去了，"那时候是夜间了"——灵性的黑暗降临。

**三、新命令（13:31-35）** — 犹大出去后，主说"如今人子得了荣耀"。然后赐下新命令："我怎样爱你们，你们也要怎样相爱。"这爱的标准是基督的爱——舍己、牺牲、无条件。"你们若有彼此相爱的心，众人因此就认出你们是我的门徒了。"

**四、预言彼得不认主（13:36-38）** — 彼得问"主往哪里去"，主说"我所去的地方，你现在不能跟我去"。彼得说"我愿意为你舍命"，主预言"鸡叫以先，你要三次不认我"。这显明人的软弱——我们自以为刚强，主却知道我们会跌倒。但祂仍爱我们，正如祂后来恢复了彼得。

全章围绕"爱到底"的主题：主洗脚（服侍的爱）、主洁净（救赎的爱）、主赐新命令（彼此相爱）、主知道门徒会跌倒却仍爱他们（坚忍的爱）。

## Structure (English)

**I. Service of Foot Washing (13:1-20)** — The Lord knew His hour had come, "having loved his own who were in the world, he loved them to the end." The Lord rose and washed the disciples' feet. Peter refused; the Lord said "If I do not wash you, you have no share with me." After washing, the Lord explained: "I have given you an example... a servant is not greater than his master." This is not merely an example of service but a picture of redemption—only Christ can cleanse us; we must accept His cleansing to have part with Him.

**II. Prophecy of Judas' Betrayal (13:21-30)** — The Lord was troubled in spirit and testified "one of you will betray me." Disciples looked at one another, not knowing whom He spoke of. John leaning on the Lord's chest asked who it was; the Lord dipped a morsel and gave it to Judas. Satan entered Judas' heart; the Lord said "What you are going to do, do quickly." Judas went out, "and it was night"—spiritual darkness descended.

**III. New Commandment (13:31-35)** — After Judas left, the Lord said "Now is the Son of Man glorified." Then He gave the new commandment: "Just as I have loved you, you also are to love one another." The standard of this love is Christ's love—self-sacrifice, unconditional. "By this all people will know that you are my disciples, if you have love for one another."

**IV. Prophecy of Peter's Denial (13:36-38)** — Peter asked "Lord, where are you going?" The Lord said "Where I am going you cannot follow me now." Peter said "I will lay down my life for you"; the Lord prophesied "the rooster will not crow till you have denied me three times." This shows human weakness—we think ourselves strong; the Lord knows we will fall. But He still loves us, just as He later restored Peter.

The whole chapter revolves around the theme of "love to the end": the Lord washed feet (love of service), the Lord cleanses (love of redemption), the Lord gave the new commandment (love one another), the Lord knew disciples would fall yet still loved them (persevering love).

## 神学要点（中文）

**救赎的本质——接受基督的洁净：** "我若不洗你，你就与我无分"（13:8）——这句话是救恩论的核心。我们不能靠自己洁净自己；唯有接受基督宝血的洗净，才能与神有份。彼得说"你永不可洗我的脚"，代表人的骄傲和自义——不愿承认自己污秽、需要救主。但主说"我若不洗你，你就与我无分"——拒绝恩典就是拒绝救恩。改革宗强调：唯独恩典（Sola Gratia）——我们得救全然是神的恩典，不是靠自己的行为或意志。

**圣徒的坚忍与神的保守：** "我既然爱世间属自己的人，就爱他们到底"（13:1）——"到底"（eis telos）意味着完全、彻底、到终点。主对属祂之人的爱不会中途放弃。彼得即将三次不认主，但主仍爱他；犹大将要卖主，但主仍给他悔改机会。改革宗强调：圣徒的坚忍不是靠人的努力，乃是神的保守（约10:28-29"谁也不能从我手里把他们夺去"）。真信徒可能跌倒，但不会彻底失落，因为主爱他们到底。

**拣选与遗弃：** "我知道我所拣选的是谁"（13:18）——主拣选了十二使徒，包括犹大。但犹大从未真正属于主（6:70"你们中间有一个是魔鬼"）。主引用诗篇41:9"同我吃饭的人用脚踢我"，显明犹大的背叛应验了圣经预言。改革宗区分"外在呼召"（external call）与"有效恩召"（effectual call）：犹大听见福音、跟随耶稣、甚至行神迹（太10:1-4），但他从未被圣灵重生，所以至终背叛。真拣选的记号不是外在的宗教活动，乃是内在的重生和对基督的爱。

**新命令——成圣的标准：** "我怎样爱你们，你们也要怎样相爱"（13:34）——这命令之"新"，在于标准的高度：基督舍己的爱。旧约也有"爱人如己"（利19:18），但新约的标准是"爱人如基督爱你"——为罪人死、洗门徒的脚、爱到底。这不是律法主义的要求，乃是福音的果子：蒙恩的人自然流露出爱，因为"我们爱，因为神先爱我们"（约壹4:19）。改革宗强调：成圣是称义的必然结果；真信徒必然结出圣灵的果子（加5:22-23），其中最大的就是爱（林前13:13）。

**基督的人性——心里忧愁：** "耶稣心里忧愁"（13:21）——这显明基督完全的人性。祂不是没有感情的神，乃是真正的人，会为门徒的背叛而忧伤。希伯来书4:15"他也曾凡事受过试探，与我们一样，只是他没有犯罪。"祂知道犹大要卖祂、彼得要否认祂、众门徒要逃散，心中忧愁；但祂仍然爱他们到底。改革宗迦克墩信经（Chalcedonian Creed）坚持：基督是真神真人，二性不混淆、不改变、不分离、不分割。

## Theology (English)

**Essence of Redemption—Accepting Christ's Cleansing:** "If I do not wash you, you have no share with me" (13:8)—this statement is the core of soteriology. We cannot cleanse ourselves; only by accepting the cleansing of Christ's blood can we have part with God. Peter saying "You shall never wash my feet" represents human pride and self-righteousness—unwilling to admit one's filth, needing a Savior. But the Lord said "If I do not wash you, you have no share with me"—rejecting grace is rejecting salvation. Reformed theology emphasizes: Sola Gratia (Grace Alone)—we are saved entirely by God's grace, not by our own works or will.

**Perseverance of Saints and God's Preservation:** "Having loved his own who were in the world, he loved them to the end" (13:1)—"to the end" (eis telos) means completely, thoroughly, to the finish. The Lord's love for those who belong to Him will not abandon them midway. Peter was about to deny Him three times, yet the Lord still loved him; Judas was about to betray Him, yet the Lord still gave him opportunity to repent. Reformed theology emphasizes: Perseverance of the saints is not by human effort but by God's preservation (John 10:28-29 "no one will snatch them out of my hand"). True believers may stumble but will not fall away completely, because the Lord loves them to the end.

**Election and Reprobation:** "I know whom I have chosen" (13:18)—the Lord chose the twelve apostles, including Judas. But Judas never truly belonged to the Lord (6:70 "one of you is a devil"). The Lord quotes Psalm 41:9 "he who ate my bread has lifted his heel against me," showing Judas' betrayal fulfilled Scripture prophecy. Reformed theology distinguishes "external call" from "effectual call": Judas heard the gospel, followed Jesus, even performed miracles (Matt 10:1-4), but he was never regenerated by the Spirit, so ultimately betrayed. The mark of true election is not external religious activity but internal regeneration and love for Christ.

**New Commandment—Standard of Sanctification:** "Just as I have loved you, you also are to love one another" (13:34)—the "newness" of this commandment lies in the height of the standard: Christ's self-sacrificial love. The Old Testament also has "Thou shalt not avenge, nor bear any grudge against the children of thy people, but thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself: I am the LORD." (Lev 19:18), but the New Testament standard is "love others as Christ loved you"—dying for sinners, washing disciples' feet, loving to the end. This is not a legalistic demand but the fruit of the gospel: those who have received grace naturally overflow with love, because "We love him, because he first loved us." (1 John 4:19). Reformed theology emphasizes: Sanctification is the necessary result of justification; true believers will inevitably bear the fruit of the Spirit (Gal 5:22-23), the greatest being love (1 Cor 13:13).

**Christ's Humanity—Troubled in Spirit:** "Jesus was troubled in spirit" (13:21)—this shows Christ's full humanity. He is not an emotionless God but a true human who would grieve over disciples' betrayal. Hebrews 4:15 "in every respect has been tempted as we are, yet without sin." He knew Judas would betray Him, Peter would deny Him, all disciples would scatter, and His heart was troubled; yet He still loved them to the end. The Reformed Chalcedonian Creed insists: Christ is true God and true man, two natures not confused, not changed, not separated, not divided.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**逾越节羔羊的应验：** 第13章开始"逾越节以前"，主在最后晚餐设立圣餐，直接应验逾越节的预表。出埃及记12章，以色列家家户户宰杀羔羊、把血涂在门楣上、吃羔羊的肉。主说"这是我的身体...这是我立约的血"（太26:26-28），宣告祂自己是真正的逾越节羔羊（林前5:7）。祂的血使信祂的人免于神的忿怒，祂的身体（肉）成为我们的粮。

**洗脚与洁净礼：** 旧约祭司在会幕事奉前必须洗手洗脚（出30:19-21），象征洁净。基督洗门徒的脚，预表祂用宝血洗净我们（启1:5"用自己的血使我们脱离罪恶"，多3:5"藉着重生的洗和圣灵的更新"）。"凡洗过澡的人，只要把脚一洗，全身就干净了"（13:10）——这区分重生的洗（一次性，"洗过澡"）与成圣的洗（持续的，"洗脚"）。信徒已因信称义（全身洁净），但在世界行走仍会沾染污秽，需要持续悔改、认罪（洗脚），靠主宝血不断洁净（约壹1:9）。

**受苦的仆人（以赛亚书53章）：** 主洗门徒的脚，应验以赛亚书53:12"他倾倒自己的性命...担当多人的罪"。祂是那位"取了奴仆的形像"（腓2:7）的主，"不是要受人的服事，乃是要服事人，并且要舍命作多人的赎价"（可10:45）。祂降卑到极点——万王之王洗罪人的脚——为要高举我们到极点（使我们与祂同坐天上，弗2:6）。

**犹大与该隐、以扫：** 犹大跟随主、听道、行神迹，却最终背叛，应验了"卖主的"预表。该隐献祭不蒙悦纳，杀了兄弟亚伯（创4章）；以扫轻看长子名分，贪恋世俗（来12:16-17）。犹大贪爱钱财，为三十块钱卖主——他是挂名信徒、假弟兄的典型，警戒我们：外在的宗教活动不能代替内在的重生（太7:21-23"我从来不认识你们"）。

**彼得与人性的软弱：** 彼得说"我愿意为你舍命"（13:37），几小时后却三次不认主。这应验人类堕落后的软弱——"立志为善由得我，只是行出来由不得我"（罗7:18）。但主提前知道他会跌倒，仍为他祷告（路22:32"我已经为你祈求，叫你不至于失了信心"），复活后恢复他（约21:15-17三次问"你爱我吗"）。彼得是我们的代表——软弱会跌倒，但蒙主保守不至失落。

**新命令与新约：** "我赐给你们一条新命令"（13:34）呼应耶利米书31:31-34的新约预言："我要与以色列家另立新约...我要将我的律法放在他们里面，写在他们心上。"新约的标志是内在的更新（圣灵内住）和爱的诫命（彼此相爱）。主在最后晚餐设立圣餐、赐下新命令，宣告新约时代的来临——不再靠外在律法，乃靠内在的圣灵和基督的爱。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Fulfillment of Passover Lamb:** Chapter 13 begins "before the Feast of the Passover"; the Lord instituted the Lord's Supper at the Last Supper, directly fulfilling the Passover type. Exodus 12, every Israelite household slaughtered a lamb, put blood on the doorposts, ate the lamb's flesh. The Lord said "And as they were eating, Jesus took bread, and blessed it, and brake it, and gave it to the disciples, and said, Take, eat; this is my body. And he took the cup, and gave thanks, and gave it to them, saying, Drink ye all of it; For this is my blood of the new testament, which is shed for many for the remission of sins." (Matt 26:26-28), proclaiming Himself the true Passover Lamb (1 Cor 5:7). His blood spares believers from God's wrath; His body (flesh) becomes our bread.

**Foot Washing and Purification Rite:** Old Testament priests had to wash hands and feet before serving in the tabernacle (Exod 30:19-21), symbolizing cleansing. Christ washing disciples' feet prefigures His cleansing us with His blood (Rev 1:5 "freed us from our sins by his blood," Titus 3:5 "by the washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit"). "The one who has bathed does not need to wash, except for his feet, but is completely clean" (13:10)—this distinguishes the bath of regeneration (once, "bathed") from the washing of sanctification (ongoing, "washing feet"). Believers are already justified by faith (wholly clean), but walking in the world still gets dirty, needing continual repentance, confession (washing feet), continuously cleansed by the Lord's blood (1 John 1:9).

**Suffering Servant (Isaiah 53):** The Lord washing disciples' feet fulfills Isaiah 53:12 "he poured out his soul to death... bore the sin of many." He is the One who "But made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant, and was made in the likeness of men:" (Phil 2:7), "For even the Son of man came not to be ministered unto, but to minister, and to give his life a ransom for many." (Mark 10:45). He humbled Himself to the extreme—King of Kings washing sinners' feet—to exalt us to the extreme (make us sit with Him in heavenly places, Eph 2:6).

**Judas and Cain, Esau:** Judas followed the Lord, heard teachings, performed miracles, yet ultimately betrayed, fulfilling the type of "betrayer." Cain's sacrifice was not accepted, killed his brother Abel (Gen 4); Esau despised his birthright, craving the profane (Heb 12:16-17). Judas loved money, sold the Lord for thirty pieces of silver—he is the典型 of nominal believers, false brothers, warning us: external religious activity cannot replace internal regeneration (Matt 7:21-23 "I never knew you").

**Peter and Human Weakness:** Peter said "I will lay down my life for you" (13:37); hours later he denied the Lord three times. This fulfills post-Fall human weakness—"For I know that in me (that is, in my flesh,) dwelleth no good thing: for to will is present with me; but how to perform that which is good I find not." (Rom 7:18). But the Lord knew beforehand he would fall, still prayed for him (Luke 22:32 "I have prayed for you that your faith may not fail"), restored him after resurrection (John 21:15-17 three times asking "Do you love me?"). Peter represents us—weak, will stumble, but preserved by the Lord to not fall away.

**New Commandment and New Covenant:** "A new commandment I give to you" (13:34) echoes Jeremiah 31:31-34's new covenant prophecy: "I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel... I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts." The mark of the new covenant is internal renewal (Holy Spirit indwelling) and the commandment of love (love one another). The Lord at the Last Supper instituted the Lord's Supper, gave the new commandment, proclaiming the arrival of the new covenant era—no longer by external law but by the internal Spirit and Christ's love.
