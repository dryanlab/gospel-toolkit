## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是约翰。第七章记载了住棚节期间主在耶路撒冷引起的巨大争论。群众分裂了，宗教领袖愤怒了，但在这一切纷乱之中，主站起来发出了震撼天地的邀请。

一、兄弟的不信（7:1-9）

住棚节到了，主的兄弟对他说："你离开这里上犹太去罢，叫你的门徒也看见你所行的事"（约7:3）。他们的意思是：你既然要出名，就去耶路撒冷大展身手吧。约翰特别注明——"因为连他的弟兄说这话，是因为不信他"（约7:5）。

主的亲兄弟尚且不信——这何等令人心痛！但主回答说："我的时候还没有到，你们的时候常是方便的。世人不能恨你们，却是恨我，因为我指证他们所做的事是恶的"（约7:6-7）。世人恨基督，因为基督是光，光照出黑暗中的罪。

二、在圣殿教训人（7:10-36）

主暗暗地上了耶路撒冷。到了节期中间，他上殿里去教训人。犹太人奇怪说："这个人没有学过，怎么明白书呢？"（约7:15）。

主回答："我的教训不是我自己的，乃是那差我来者的。人若立志遵着他的旨意行，就必晓得这教训或是出于神，或是我凭着自己说的"（约7:16-17）。**真理的检验方法不是学术辩论，而是顺服——你若愿意遵行神的旨意，你就必然知道这道是从神来的。**

众人中间有许多议论。有人说他是好人，有人说他不是，他是迷惑人的。有人想要捉拿他，却没有人下手——"因为他的时候还没有到"（约7:30）。弟兄姐妹，没有任何人能在神预定的时间之前动基督一根汗毛。

三、活水的江河（7:37-39）

到了节期的最后一天，也是最大的日子。住棚节有一个传统——祭司每天从西罗亚池子取水，浇在祭坛上，纪念旷野中磐石出水。到了第七天（有人说第八天），这个仪式达到高潮。

就在这个时刻——**"耶稣站着高声说：人若渴了，可以到我这里来喝。信我的人就如经上所说：从他腹中要流出活水的江河来"（约7:37-38）。**

在住棚节最庄严的仪式中，在祭司浇水的那一刻，主站起来高声宣告：不要看那些水——**我就是活水的源头！** 不是西罗亚池子的水，不是旧约磐石出的水，而是基督自己——从他里面要涌出永不干涸的活水。

约翰解释说："耶稣这话是指着信他之人要受圣灵说的"（约7:39）。活水就是圣灵。五旬节以后，圣灵浇灌在信主的人身上，成为他们里面永不枯竭的泉源。

四、更深的分裂（7:40-52）

群众分成了几派。有人说他是先知，有人说他是基督，有人质疑他的出身——"基督岂是从加利利出来的么？"（约7:41）。他们不知道主虽然在加利利长大，却是在伯利恒出生的——正应验了弥迦书5:2的预言。

法利赛人派差役去捉拿主，差役空手回来说了一句令人难忘的话："从来没有像他这样说话的！"（约7:46）。连来抓他的人都被他的话语征服了。

尼哥底母——就是第三章在夜间来见主的那位——说了一句公道话："不先听本人的口供，不知道他所做的事，难道我们的律法还定他的罪么？"（约7:51）。他们嘲笑他："你也是出于加利利么？你且去查考，就可知道加利利没有出过先知"（约7:52）。

弟兄姐妹，这一章让我们看见：**同一个基督，有人渴慕，有人怀疑，有人仇恨。** 但他的邀请永远在那里——"人若渴了，可以到我这里来喝。"你渴吗？来吧。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am John. Chapter seven records the great controversy the Lord caused in Jerusalem during the Feast of Tabernacles. The crowds were divided, the religious leaders were furious, yet amid all this turmoil, the Lord stood and issued an earth-shaking invitation.

I. The Brothers' Unbelief (7:1-9)

The Feast of Tabernacles was near. The Lord's brothers said to Him, "His brethren therefore said unto him, Depart hence, and go into Judaea, that thy disciples also may see the works that thou doest." (John 7:3). Their meaning: if you want fame, go to Jerusalem and show yourself. John specifically notes: "For neither did his brethren believe in him." (John 7:5).

The Lord's own brothers did not believe—how heartbreaking! But the Lord answered: "Then Jesus said unto them, My time is not yet come: but your time is alway ready. The world cannot hate you; but me it hateth, because I testify of it, that the works thereof are evil." (John 7:6-7). The world hates Christ because Christ is light, and light exposes sin in the darkness.

II. Teaching in the Temple (7:10-36)

The Lord went to Jerusalem secretly. Midway through the feast, He went to the temple and taught. The Jews marveled: "And the Jews marvelled, saying, How knoweth this man letters, having never learned?" (John 7:15).

The Lord answered: "Jesus answered them, and said, My doctrine is not mine, but his that sent me. If any man will do his will, he shall know of the doctrine, whether it be of God, or whether I speak of myself." (John 7:16-17). **The test of truth is not academic debate but obedience—if you are willing to do God's will, you will know this teaching comes from God.**

There was much murmuring among the people. Some said He was a good man; others said He was deceiving them. Some sought to arrest Him, but no one laid hands on Him—"Then they sought to take him: but no man laid hands on him, because his hour was not yet come." (John 7:30). Brothers and sisters, no one can touch a hair on Christ's head before God's appointed time.

III. Rivers of Living Water (7:37-39)

On the last day of the feast, the great day, there was a Tabernacles tradition—priests drew water daily from the Pool of Siloam and poured it on the altar, commemorating water from the rock in the wilderness. On the seventh day (some say the eighth), this ceremony reached its climax.

At that very moment—**"Jesus stood up and cried out, 'If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink. Whoever believes in me, as the Scripture has said, "Out of his heart will flow rivers of living water"'" (John 7:37-38).**

In the most solemn ceremony of Tabernacles, at the moment the priests poured water, the Lord stood and proclaimed aloud: Do not look at that water—**I am the source of living water!** Not the water of Siloam's pool, not the water from the Old Testament rock, but Christ Himself—from Him flows water that never runs dry.

John explains: "(But this spake he of the Spirit, which they that believe on him should receive: for the Holy Ghost was not yet given; because that Jesus was not yet glorified.)" (John 7:39). The living water is the Holy Spirit. After Pentecost, the Spirit was poured out on all who believe, becoming an inexhaustible spring within them.

IV. Deepening Division (7:40-52)

The crowd split into factions. Some said He was the Prophet; others, the Christ; still others questioned His origins—"Others said, This is the Christ. But some said, Shall Christ come out of Galilee?" (John 7:41). They did not know that though the Lord grew up in Galilee, He was born in Bethlehem—fulfilling the prophecy of Micah 5:2.

The Pharisees sent officers to arrest Him. They returned empty-handed with an unforgettable response: "The officers answered, Never man spake like this man." (John 7:46). Even those sent to seize Him were conquered by His words.

Nicodemus—the one who came to the Lord by night in chapter 3—spoke a word of fairness: "Doth our law judge any man, before it hear him, and know what he doeth?" (John 7:51). They mocked him: "They answered and said unto him, Art thou also of Galilee? Search, and look: for out of Galilee ariseth no prophet." (John 7:52).

Brothers and sisters, this chapter shows us: **the same Christ draws thirsting hearts, provokes doubt, and incites hatred.** But His invitation stands forever—"If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink." Are you thirsty? Come.

## 历史背景（中文）

**住棚节**（Sukkot）是以色列三大朝圣节期之一，持续七天（加第八天），纪念以色列人在旷野四十年住帐棚。**水礼仪式**是住棚节最重要的仪式之一——祭司从西罗亚池取水，在利未人的诗歌伴奏中浇在祭坛上，纪念旷野磐石出水（出17章、民20章）。每天一圈，第七天绕祭坛七圈（类似耶利哥），达到高潮。主选在这个仪式中宣告"到我这里来喝"，具有极强的象征意义。尼哥底母为主辩护（7:50-51）暗示他的信心在成长——从夜间秘访到公开发声。

## Historical Context (English)

The **Feast of Tabernacles** (Sukkot) was one of Israel's three pilgrimage festivals, lasting seven days (plus an eighth), commemorating Israel's forty years of dwelling in tents in the wilderness. The **water-pouring ceremony** was among the feast's most significant rituals—priests drew water from the Pool of Siloam and poured it on the altar to Levitical singing, recalling water from the rock (Exod 17; Num 20). Each day they circled the altar once; on the seventh day, seven times (like Jericho), reaching a crescendo. The Lord's declaration "come to me and drink" during this ceremony carried immense symbolic power. Nicodemus' defense of the Lord (7:50-51) suggests his faith was growing—from secret nighttime visit to public advocacy.

## 经文结构（中文）

全章按**时间线**展开：**（一）节前——私下的对话**（7:1-9）——主与兄弟的交流，时候未到；**（二）节中——殿里的教训**（7:10-36）——公开教导、群众议论、领袖仇恨；**（三）节末——活水的宣告**（7:37-39）——全章高潮，主在水礼仪式中发出邀请；**（四）节后——更深的分裂**（7:40-52）——群众分派、差役被震撼、尼哥底母发声。全章的张力从私密（与兄弟）到公开（与群众），从隐藏（暗暗上去）到高潮（站着高声说），形成一个不断升温的叙事弧。

## Structure (English)

The chapter unfolds along a **timeline**: **(1) Before the feast—private conversation** (7:1-9)—the Lord and His brothers; the time has not come; **(2) Mid-feast—temple teaching** (7:10-36)—public instruction, crowd murmuring, leaders' hostility; **(3) Last day—the living water declaration** (7:37-39)—the chapter's climax; the Lord cries out during the water ceremony; **(4) After the feast—deeper division** (7:40-52)—factions form, officers are awed, Nicodemus speaks up. The tension builds from private (with brothers) to public (with crowds), from hiddenness (going secretly) to crescendo (standing and crying out), forming an ever-intensifying narrative arc.

## 神学要点（中文）

本章的核心神学是**基督是圣灵的赐予者**。"从他腹中要流出活水的江河来"（7:38）——这活水指圣灵（7:39）。改革宗神学强调圣灵的工作是**使基督的救赎应用在信徒身上**——不是信徒自己抓住恩典，是圣灵将恩典浇灌在信徒心中。韦敏斯德信条第十章论有效恩召：圣灵……使选民的意志更新，决定他们行善。此外，"人若立志遵着他的旨意行，就必晓得这教训或是出于神"（7:17）——这是**信仰与顺服的辩证关系**：不是先完全理解才顺服，而是在顺服中逐渐明白。信心先于理解，正如亚伯拉罕先离开吾珥，然后才逐渐明白神的计划。

## Theology (English)

The central theology is **Christ as the giver of the Holy Spirit**. "Out of his heart will flow rivers of living water" (7:38)—this living water is the Spirit (7:39). Reformed theology emphasizes that the Spirit's work is **applying Christ's redemption to believers**—believers do not seize grace themselves; the Spirit pours grace into believers' hearts. The Westminster Confession (ch. 34): The Holy Spirit...renews the will of the elect and determines them to do good. Furthermore, "If anyone's will is to do God's will, he will know whether the teaching is from God" (7:17)—this presents the **dialectic of faith and obedience**: not understanding fully before obeying, but understanding gradually through obedience. Faith precedes understanding, just as Abraham first left Ur and then gradually grasped God's plan.

## 基督的影子（中文）

住棚节的水礼仪式直接指向基督。旷野中摩西击打磐石出水（出17:6），保罗说"那磐石就是基督"（林前10:4）。在住棚节，祭司从西罗亚（"奉差遣"之意）池取水——而基督自己就是"奉差遣者"，是真正的磐石、真正的活水源头。以赛亚曾预言："你们必从救恩的泉源欢然取水"（赛12:3）——这"救恩的泉源"就是基督。此外，住棚节纪念旷野的帐棚生活——约翰福音1:14说道成肉身是"住（支搭帐棚）在我们中间"。基督自己就是最终的帐棚——神与人同在的实体。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

The Tabernacles water ceremony points directly to Christ. In the wilderness, Moses struck the rock and water flowed (Exod 17:6); Paul wrote, "And did all drink the same spiritual drink: for they drank of that spiritual Rock that followed them: and that Rock was Christ." (1 Cor 10:4). At Tabernacles, priests drew water from the Pool of Siloam (meaning "sent")—and Christ Himself is the "Sent One," the true Rock, the true source of living water. Isaiah prophesied: "Therefore with joy shall ye draw water out of the wells of salvation." (Isa 12:3)—that "well of salvation" is Christ. Moreover, Tabernacles commemorated wilderness tent-dwelling—John 1:14 says the Word became flesh and "dwelt (tabernacled) among us." Christ Himself is the ultimate tabernacle—the reality of God dwelling with man.
