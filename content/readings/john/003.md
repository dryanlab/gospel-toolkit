## 正文（中文）

有一个法利赛人,名叫尼哥底母,是犹太人的官。这人夜里来见耶稣,说:"拉比,我们知道你是由神那里来作师傅的;因为你所行的神迹,若没有神同在,无人能行。"主耶稣回答说:"我实实在在地告诉你:人若不重生,就不能见神的国。"

我作为约翰,主所爱的门徒,必须告诉你们,这段对话是何等重要。尼哥底母不是普通人——他是法利赛人,是犹太公会的成员,是以色列人的师傅。他来"夜里"见主,可能是怕被同僚看见,也可能是想要有安静不被打扰的深谈。他称主为"拉比",承认主的神迹证明神与祂同在,这对一个法利赛人来说已是很大的让步。

但主的回答直指核心:"人若不重生,就不能见神的国。"这话让尼哥底母困惑:"人已经老了,如何能重生呢?岂能再进母腹生出来吗?"主继续说:"我实实在在地告诉你:人若不是从水和圣灵生的,就不能进神的国。从肉身生的,就是肉身;从灵生的,就是灵。我说'你们必须重生',你不要以为希奇。风随着意思吹,你听见风的响声,却不晓得从哪里来,往哪里去;凡从圣灵生的,也是如此。"

尼哥底母问:"怎能有这事呢?"主回答说:"你是以色列人的师傅,还不明白这事吗?"这不是责备,而是提醒——旧约早已预言了这新约的应许。以西结书36:25-27说:"我必用清水洒在你们身上⋯⋯我也要赐给你们一个新心,将新灵放在你们里面。"重生不是人的努力,而是圣灵主权的工作。

主继续说:"我们所说的,是我们知道的;我们所见证的,是我们见过的;你们却不领受我们的见证。我对你们说地上的事,你们尚且不信;若说天上的事,如何能信呢?除了从天降下仍旧在天的人子,没有人升过天。摩西在旷野怎样举蛇,人子也必照样被举起来,叫一切信祂的都得永生。"

这里主预言了祂自己的十字架。民数记21章记载,以色列人在旷野因发怨言被火蛇所咬,摩西举起铜蛇,凡仰望的就得医治。铜蛇做成蛇的形状,却没有蛇的毒性,正如基督"成为罪身的形状"(罗8:3),却没有罪。仰望铜蛇的人得肉体的医治,仰望十字架上基督的人得灵魂的拯救。

然后是那句最著名的经文,我永远不会忘记主说这话时的语气:"神爱世人,甚至将祂的独生子赐给他们,叫一切信祂的,不至灭亡,反得永生。因为神差祂的儿子降世,不是要定世人的罪,乃是要叫世人因祂得救。信祂的人,不被定罪;不信的人,罪已经定了,因为他不信神独生子的名。"

这是福音的核心!神的爱不是抽象的情感,而是具体的行动——"将祂的独生子赐给他们"。"独生子"(μονογενής)强调基督的独特性和宝贵性,祂是父神唯一的、独一无二的儿子。父神舍弃祂,为要救赎我们这些罪人,这是何等的爱!

主继续解释审判的原理:"光来到世间,世人因自己的行为是恶的,不爱光倒爱黑暗,定他们的罪就是在此。凡作恶的便恨光,并不来就光,恐怕他的行为受责备;但行真理的必来就光,要显明他所行的是靠神而行。"

这事以后,主和我们到了犹太地,在那里居住施洗。那时约翰还没有下在监里,他也在靠近撒冷的哀嫩施洗,因为那里水多,众人都去受洗。约翰的门徒和一个犹太人辩论洁净的礼,就来见约翰说:"拉比,从前同你在约旦河外,你所见证的那位,现在施洗,众人都往祂那里去了。"

施洗约翰回答说:"若不是从天上赐的,人就不能得什么。我曾说'我不是基督,是奉差遣在祂前面的',你们自己可以给我作见证。娶新妇的就是新郎,新郎的朋友站着听见新郎的声音就甚喜乐;故此我这喜乐满足了。祂必兴旺,我必衰微。"

这是何等美好的见证!约翰不嫉妒、不争竞,他清楚自己的身份和使命。他是新郎的朋友(好像伴郎),不是新郎。听见新郎的声音、看见新妇归向新郎,这就是他最大的喜乐。"祂必兴旺,我必衰微"——这应该是每一个传道人、每一个基督徒的心志。

约翰继续说:"从天上来的是在万有之上⋯⋯父爱子,已将万有交在祂手里。信子的人有永生;不信子的人得不着永生,神的震怒常在他身上。"这最后的见证总结了整章的主题:基督从天而来,父神将万有交在祂手里,信祂的人有永生,不信的人面对神的震怒。

## Content (English)

There was a man of the Pharisees named Nicodemus, a ruler of the Jews. This man came to Jesus by night and said to Him, "Rabbi, we know that you are a teacher come from God, for no one can do these signs that you do unless God is with him." Jesus answered him, "Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born again he cannot see the kingdom of God."

I, John, the disciple whom Jesus loved, must tell you how profoundly important this conversation is. Nicodemus was no ordinary man—he was a Pharisee, a member of the Jewish Sanhedrin, a teacher of Israel. He came to the Lord "by night," perhaps fearing to be seen by his colleagues, or perhaps seeking quiet, uninterrupted conversation. He called the Lord "Rabbi" and acknowledged that His miracles proved God was with Him—for a Pharisee, this was already a significant concession.

But the Lord's response went straight to the heart: "Unless one is born again he cannot see the kingdom of God." This puzzled Nicodemus: "How can a man be born when he is old? Can he enter a second time into his mother's womb and be born?" The Lord continued: "Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God. That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit. Do not marvel that I said to you, 'You must be born again.' The wind blows where it wishes, and you hear its sound, but you do not know where it comes from or where it goes. So it is with everyone who is born of the Spirit."

Nicodemus asked, "How can these things be?" The Lord answered, "Are you the teacher of Israel and yet you do not understand these things?" This was not a rebuke but a reminder—the Old Testament had already prophesied this New Covenant promise. Ezekiel 36:25-27 says, "I will sprinkle clean water on you... And I will give you a new heart, and a new spirit I will put within you." Regeneration is not human effort but the sovereign work of the Holy Spirit.

The Lord continued: "We speak of what we know, and bear witness to what we have seen, but you do not receive our testimony. If I have told you earthly things and you do not believe, how can you believe if I tell you heavenly things? No one has ascended into heaven except He who descended from heaven, the Son of Man. And as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up, that whoever believes in Him may have eternal life."

Here the Lord prophesied His own cross. Numbers 21 records that when the Israelites in the wilderness complained and were bitten by fiery serpents, Moses lifted up a bronze serpent, and all who looked at it were healed. The bronze serpent was made in the form of a serpent but had no venom, just as Christ was made "For what the law could not do, in that it was weak through the flesh, God sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh, and for sin, condemned sin in the flesh:" (Rom 8:3) yet without sin. Those who looked at the bronze serpent received physical healing; those who look to Christ on the cross receive salvation of the soul.

Then came that most famous verse; I will never forget the tone in the Lord's voice when He said it: "For God so loved the world, that He gave His only Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have eternal life. For God did not send His Son into the world to condemn the world, but in order that the world might be saved through Him. Whoever believes in Him is not condemned, but whoever does not believe is condemned already, because he has not believed in the name of the only Son of God."

This is the core of the gospel! God's love is not an abstract emotion but concrete action—"He gave His only Son." "Only Son" (μονογενής) emphasizes Christ's uniqueness and preciousness; He is the Father's only, one-and-only Son. The Father gave Him up to redeem us sinners—what love this is!

The Lord continued explaining the principle of judgment: "Light has come into the world, and people loved the darkness rather than the light because their works were evil. For everyone who does wicked things hates the light and does not come to the light, lest his works should be exposed. But whoever does what is true comes to the light, so that it may be clearly seen that his works have been carried out in God."

After this, the Lord and we went into the Judean countryside, and there He remained and baptized. John also was baptizing at Aenon near Salim, because water was plentiful there, and people were coming and being baptized (for John had not yet been put in prison). Now a discussion arose between some of John's disciples and a Jew over purification. And they came to John and said to him, "Rabbi, He who was with you across the Jordan, to whom you bore witness—look, He is baptizing, and all are going to Him."

John the Baptist answered, "A person cannot receive even one thing unless it is given him from heaven. You yourselves bear me witness that I said, 'I am not the Christ, but I have been sent before Him.' The one who has the bride is the bridegroom. The friend of the bridegroom, who stands and hears him, rejoices greatly at the bridegroom's voice. Therefore this joy of mine is now complete. He must increase, but I must decrease."

What a beautiful testimony! John was not jealous or competitive; he knew clearly his identity and mission. He was the friend of the bridegroom (like a best man), not the bridegroom himself. To hear the bridegroom's voice and see the bride come to the bridegroom—this was his greatest joy. "He must increase, but I must decrease"—this should be the aspiration of every preacher, every Christian.

John continued: "He who comes from heaven is above all... The Father loves the Son and has given all things into His hand. Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life; whoever does not obey the Son shall not see life, but the wrath of God remains on him." This final testimony summarizes the theme of the entire chapter: Christ came from heaven, the Father has given all things into His hand, those who believe in Him have eternal life, those who do not believe face the wrath of God.

## 历史背景（中文）

第3章发生在主公开事工的早期,地点先在耶路撒冷(尼哥底母夜访),后到犹太地的乡村(主和门徒施洗)。尼哥底母是法利赛人和犹太公会成员,公会有71位成员,是犹太人最高的宗教和司法机构。作为"以色列人的师傅",他精通旧约律法和传统,但对"重生"的教导感到困惑,这显示了当时法利赛人对救恩的理解局限在外在遵守律法和血统身份上。

"夜里"来见主可能有几个原因:避免公开与主接触引起同僚反对;夜晚更安静,适合深入对谈;也可能象征他此时仍在属灵的"黑暗"中,尚未完全认识真光。有趣的是,约翰福音后来记载尼哥底母两次为主说话:一次在公会为主辩护(7:50-51),一次在主受死后带着香料来膏主的身体(19:39-40)。这显示他的信心在成长,从夜里来见主,到公开表明立场。

"从水和圣灵生"的说法,改革宗传统理解为圣灵借着神的道(水象征道)使人重生。以西结书36:25-27的应许("我必用清水洒在你们身上⋯⋯将新灵放在你们里面")是背景经文。这不是指洗礼本身有重生的功效,而是圣灵主权的工作,用神的道洁净人、更新人。

施洗约翰与主同时施洗的这段时期是过渡期。约翰的使命是预备道路,当主显现后,他的事工必然衰微。约翰称自己是"新郎的朋友",这是犹太婚礼中重要的角色,类似今天的伴郎,负责安排婚礼事宜,把新妇带到新郎面前。约翰明白自己的位置,不抢夺新郎的荣耀。

## Historical Context (English)

Chapter 3 occurs in the early period of the Lord's public ministry, first in Jerusalem (Nicodemus's night visit), then in the Judean countryside (the Lord and His disciples baptizing). Nicodemus was a Pharisee and a member of the Jewish Sanhedrin, which had 71 members and was the highest religious and judicial body of the Jews. As "the teacher of Israel," he was well-versed in Old Testament law and tradition, yet he was puzzled by the teaching on "new birth," revealing that the Pharisees' understanding of salvation was limited to external law-keeping and ethnic identity.

Coming to the Lord "by night" may have had several reasons: to avoid public contact with the Lord that might provoke opposition from colleagues; nighttime was quieter, suitable for in-depth conversation; it may also symbolize that he was still in spiritual "darkness," not yet fully knowing the true light. Interestingly, John's Gospel later records Nicodemus speaking up for the Lord twice: once defending Him in the Sanhedrin (7:50-51), and once bringing spices to anoint the Lord's body after His death (19:39-40). This shows his faith was growing, from coming to the Lord by night to publicly taking a stand.

The phrase "born of water and the Spirit" is understood in the Reformed tradition as the Holy Spirit regenerating a person through the Word of God (water symbolizing the Word). The background text is the promise in Ezekiel 36:25-27 ("I will sprinkle clean water on you... and a new spirit I will put within you"). This does not mean baptism itself has regenerating power, but rather that it is the sovereign work of the Holy Spirit, cleansing and renewing people through God's Word.

The period when John the Baptist and the Lord were both baptizing was a transitional phase. John's mission was to prepare the way; when the Lord appeared, his ministry necessarily decreased. John called himself "the friend of the bridegroom," an important role in Jewish weddings similar to today's best man, responsible for arranging wedding matters and bringing the bride to the bridegroom. John understood his position and did not usurp the bridegroom's glory.

## 经文结构（中文）

第3章分为两个主要对话和一段叙述:

**尼哥底母的夜访(1-21节)**
这段对话结构严密:开场白(尼哥底母的称赞)→ 主的核心宣告(重生的必要)→ 尼哥底母的困惑 → 主的解释(从水和圣灵生)→ 风的比喻(圣灵的主权)→ 天上的事与地上的事 → 铜蛇的预表(十字架)→ 福音的核心(神爱世人)→ 光与暗的对比(审判的原理)。

从"重生"到"举蛇"到"神爱世人",主一步步展开救恩的奥秘:人需要重生→重生靠圣灵主权工作→基督必须被举起(十字架)→这是神的爱的显明→信祂的得永生。整个对话是福音神学的浓缩。

**施洗约翰最后的见证(22-36节)**
分两部分:22-30节是约翰对门徒的回应,31-36节是约翰(或可能是福音书作者)的神学总结。约翰的见证包含几个关键主题:从天上赐的(主权恩典)→新郎与朋友的比喻(基督与约翰)→"祂必兴旺,我必衰微"(事工的本质)→从天上来的(基督的超越性)→信子得永生(救恩的唯一途径)。

整章的主题是"重生与信心"——人如何能进神的国?唯有重生。如何重生?唯有圣灵。圣灵如何重生?藉着仰望被举起的人子。这一切的根基是什么?神的爱。

## Structure (English)

Chapter 3 is divided into two main dialogues and one narrative section:

**Nicodemus's Night Visit (vv. 1-21)**
This dialogue is tightly structured: opening (Nicodemus's commendation) → the Lord's core declaration (necessity of new birth) → Nicodemus's confusion → the Lord's explanation (born of water and Spirit) → the wind analogy (sovereignty of the Spirit) → heavenly things vs. earthly things → the bronze serpent type (the cross) → the core of the gospel (God so loved the world) → light vs. darkness contrast (principle of judgment).

From "new birth" to "lifted up serpent" to "God so loved," the Lord unfolds the mystery of salvation step by step: people need new birth → new birth depends on the sovereign work of the Spirit → Christ must be lifted up (the cross) → this is the manifestation of God's love → those who believe in Him have eternal life. The entire dialogue is a condensation of gospel theology.

**John the Baptist's Final Testimony (vv. 22-36)**
Divided into two parts: verses 22-30 are John's response to his disciples; verses 31-36 are John's (or possibly the Gospel writer's) theological summary. John's testimony contains several key themes: given from heaven (sovereign grace) → the analogy of bridegroom and friend (Christ and John) → "He must increase, I must decrease" (the nature of ministry) → He who comes from heaven (Christ's transcendence) → whoever believes in the Son has eternal life (the only way of salvation).

The theme of the entire chapter is "new birth and faith"—how can a person enter God's kingdom? Only through new birth. How is one born again? Only by the Spirit. How does the Spirit regenerate? By looking to the lifted-up Son of Man. What is the foundation of all this? God's love.

## 神学要点（中文）

**改革宗重生观:第3章是改革宗"圣灵重生"教义的核心经文。主明确说"从肉身生的就是肉身,从灵生的就是灵",表明重生不是人的决志或努力,而完全是圣灵主权的工作。"风随着意思吹"强调圣灵的自由和主权,不受人控制。改革宗神学的"不可抗拒的恩典"(Irresistible Grace)正基于此——圣灵在拣选的人心中有效地工作,使他们愿意且能够相信。

**唯独信心:** "信子的人有永生"(36节),"叫一切信祂的都得永生"(15节)——救恩的管道是"唯独信心"。不是靠守律法(尼哥底母这样的法利赛人最擅长的),不是靠血统(犹太人身份),也不是靠善行或宗教仪式,而唯独因信基督。改革宗的"因信称义"教义在此得到主亲口的确认。

**神的主权与人的责任:** 第3章呈现了这个张力:"你们必须重生"是命令(人的责任),"风随着意思吹"是圣灵主权(神的主权)。改革宗神学不回避这个张力,而是持守两者都真实:神主权拣选和重生,人必须悔改和相信。这不是矛盾,而是奥秘。

**替代性救赎:** "摩西在旷野怎样举蛇,人子也必照样被举起来"(14节)。铜蛇的预表清楚显示"替代"的概念——铜蛇没有毒性却做成蛇的形状,代替以色列人承受审判;基督无罪却"成为罪",代替我们承受神的忿怒。改革宗强调"刑罚性替代"(Penal Substitution)——基督在十字架上代替我们承受了我们应得的刑罚,满足了神公义的要求。

**神的爱与忿怒:** 第16节"神爱世人"与第36节"神的震怒常在他身上"形成对比。神的爱不是普遍宽容,而是通过基督的十字架显明。不信的人面对神的震怒不是将来才发生,而是"常在"(现在式)——这显示人堕落的状态本已在神的忿怒之下,唯有信基督才脱离忿怒、进入恩典。

## Theology (English)

**Reformed Doctrine of Regeneration:** Chapter 3 is a core text for the Reformed doctrine of "regeneration by the Holy Spirit." The Lord clearly states, "That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit," indicating that regeneration is not human decision or effort but entirely the sovereign work of the Holy Spirit. "The wind blows where it wishes" emphasizes the Spirit's freedom and sovereignty, not subject to human control. The Reformed doctrine of "Irresistible Grace" is based on this—the Holy Spirit works effectually in the hearts of the elect, making them willing and able to believe.

**Sola Fide (Faith Alone):** "Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life" (v. 36), "that whoever believes in Him may have eternal life" (v. 15)—the instrument of salvation is "faith alone." Not by keeping the law (what Pharisees like Nicodemus excelled at), not by ethnic descent (Jewish identity), not by good works or religious ceremonies, but only through faith in Christ. The Reformed doctrine of "justification by faith alone" is confirmed here by the Lord's own words.

**God's Sovereignty and Human Responsibility:** Chapter 3 presents this tension: "You must be born again" is a command (human responsibility), "the wind blows where it wishes" is the Spirit's sovereignty (God's sovereignty). Reformed theology does not avoid this tension but holds that both are true: God sovereignly elects and regenerates, and people must repent and believe. This is not a contradiction but a mystery.

**Substitutionary Atonement:** "As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up" (v. 14). The bronze serpent type clearly shows the concept of "substitution"—the bronze serpent had no venom but was made in the form of a serpent, bearing judgment in place of the Israelites; Christ was sinless yet "became sin," bearing God's wrath in our place. Reformed theology emphasizes "Penal Substitution"—Christ on the cross bore the punishment we deserved, satisfying the demands of God's justice.

**God's Love and Wrath:** Verse 16 "God so loved the world" contrasts with verse 36 "the wrath of God remains on him." God's love is not universal tolerance but is manifested through Christ's cross. The wrath of God on those who do not believe is not something that will happen in the future but "remains" (present tense)—this shows that fallen humanity is already under God's wrath; only through faith in Christ can one escape wrath and enter grace.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**举起的铜蛇预表十字架:** 民数记21:4-9记载以色列人在旷野发怨言,神降火蛇审判他们,后来神吩咐摩西举起铜蛇,凡仰望的就活了。主亲自解释这是祂十字架的预表:"人子也必照样被举起来"。铜蛇的几个要素都预表基督:(1)蛇的形状——基督"成为罪身的形状"(罗8:3),却没有罪;(2)被举起来——基督被钉在十字架上,被举起来给万人看;(3)仰望就得救——不是靠行为,只要仰望(信靠)就得永生;(4)这是神的怜悯和救赎方法——出于神的恩典,不是人的功劳。

**独生子的献上:** "神爱世人,甚至将祂的独生子赐给他们"(16节)直接呼应创世记22章亚伯拉罕献以撒的故事。神对亚伯拉罕说:"你带着你的儿子,就是你独生的儿子,你所爱的以撒⋯⋯把他献为燔祭"(创22:2)。亚伯拉罕最终没有杀以撒,因为神预备了代替的公羊。但两千年后,天父真的将祂的独生子、祂所爱的献上,为我们作了挽回祭。创世记22:8亚伯拉罕说"神必自己预备作燔祭的羊羔",这预言指向神的羔羊——基督。

**光来到世间:** 主说"光来到世间,世人因自己的行为是恶的,不爱光倒爱黑暗"(19节)。这呼应第1章"那光是真光,照亮一切生在世上的人。祂在世界⋯⋯世界却不认识祂"(1:9-10)。基督就是那真光,祂来不是定世人的罪,而是要拯救。但人因为罪恶而恨光、躲避光。以赛亚书9:2预言"在黑暗中行走的百姓看见了大光",这光就是弥赛亚。

**新郎与新妇:** 施洗约翰称主为"新郎",这是旧约一贯的主题。以赛亚书54:5"因为造你的是你的丈夫,万军之耶和华是祂的名",耶利米书2:2神说"你幼年的恩爱,婚姻的爱情",何西阿书2:19-20"我必聘你永远归我为妻"。主耶稣就是那位神圣的新郎,教会是祂的新妇。启示录19:7"羔羊婚娶的时候到了,新妇也自己预备好了"——从创世记到启示录,圣经用婚姻的盟约预表基督与教会的关系。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**The Lifted-Up Bronze Serpent as Type of the Cross:** Numbers 21:4-9 records that the Israelites complained in the wilderness, God sent fiery serpents as judgment, and later God commanded Moses to lift up a bronze serpent—all who looked at it lived. The Lord personally explained this as a type of His cross: "So must the Son of Man be lifted up." Several elements of the bronze serpent typify Christ: (1) the form of a serpent—Christ was made "For what the law could not do, in that it was weak through the flesh, God sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh, and for sin, condemned sin in the flesh:" (Rom 8:3) yet without sin; (2) lifted up—Christ was crucified, lifted up for all to see; (3) look and be saved—not by works, but simply by looking (trusting) to receive eternal life; (4) this is God's mercy and method of redemption—by God's grace, not human merit.

**The Giving of the Only Son:** "God so loved the world, that He gave His only Son" (v. 16) directly echoes the story of Abraham offering Isaac in Genesis 22. God said to Abraham, "And he said, Take now thy son, thine only son Isaac, whom thou lovest, and get thee into the land of Moriah; and offer him there for a burnt offering upon one of the mountains which I will tell thee of." (Gen 22:2). Abraham did not ultimately kill Isaac because God provided a substitutionary ram. But two thousand years later, the heavenly Father truly gave up His only Son, whom He loved, as a propitiation for us. In Genesis 22:8, Abraham said, "God will provide for Himself the lamb for a burnt offering"—this prophecy points to the Lamb of God, Christ.

**Light Has Come into the World:** The Lord said, "Light has come into the world, and people loved the darkness rather than the light because their works were evil" (v. 19). This echoes Chapter 1: "The true light... was coming into the world. He was in the world... yet the world did not know Him" (1:9-10). Christ is that true light; He came not to condemn the world but to save it. But people, because of sin, hate the light and avoid it. Isaiah 9:2 prophesies, "The people who walked in darkness have seen a great light"—that light is the Messiah.

**Bridegroom and Bride:** John the Baptist calls the Lord the "bridegroom," a consistent Old Testament theme. Isaiah 54:5, "For your Maker is your husband, the LORD of hosts is His name"; Jeremiah 2:2, God speaks of "the devotion of your youth, your love as a bride"; Hosea 2:19-20, "I will betroth you to me forever." The Lord Jesus is that divine bridegroom, and the church is His bride. Revelation 19:7, "The marriage of the Lamb has come, and His bride has made herself ready"—from Genesis to Revelation, Scripture uses the covenant of marriage to typify the relationship between Christ and the church.
