## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是约翰。今天我要讲述的事发生在耶路撒冷的毕士大池旁——一个瘫痪了三十八年的人遇见了生命的主。

一、毕士大池旁的瘫子（5:1-9）

耶路撒冷靠近羊门有一个池子，希伯来话叫做毕士大，旁边有五个廊子。"里面躺着瞎眼的、瘸腿的、血气枯干的许多病人"（约5:3）。这些人等候池水动的时候，谁先下去就能得医治——至少他们这样相信。

主走到一个瘫子面前，知道他已经病了三十八年，就问他："你要痊愈么？"（约5:6）。

这个问题看似多余，实则直击灵魂。三十八年——当等待成为习惯，当绝望成为常态，人还敢盼望吗？瘫子没有直接说"要"，而是解释他的困境："先生，水动的时候，没有人把我放在池子里；我正去的时候，就有别人比我先下去"（约5:7）。他的回答里没有盼望，只有借口和无奈。

主没有继续问，也没有等水动。他说了一句话："起来，拿你的褥子走罢！"（约5:8）。那人立刻痊愈了。三十八年的瘫痪，一句话就解决了。

弟兄姐妹，注意——**不是池水医治了他，是主的话语医治了他。** 他不需要等水动，不需要有人帮忙，不需要做任何事——只需要听从主的话，站起来。这就是恩典：不是你做什么，是神为你做了一切。

二、安息日的争论（5:10-18）

那天是安息日。犹太人看见瘫子拿着褥子走路，就质问他违反了安息日。瘫子说："那使我痊愈的对我说：拿你的褥子走罢"（约5:11）。

他们不关心一个瘫了三十八年的人得了医治，只关心安息日的规条。**宗教主义的可怕之处在于：规条比人重要，传统比生命重要。** 主后来对他们说："我父做事直到如今，我也做事"（约5:17）。这句话更加激怒了他们——因为主把神称为"我父"，将自己与神同等。

三、子与父的关系（5:19-30）

接下来是约翰福音中最深刻的基督论讲论之一。主说："子凭着自己不能做甚么，惟有看见父所做的，子才能做；父所做的事，子也照样做"（约5:19）。这不是说子软弱或缺乏能力，而是说父与子的旨意**完全合一**——子所做的就是父所做的，父所做的子也照样做。

"父怎样叫死人起来，使他们活着，子也照样随自己的意思使人活着"（约5:21）。这是何等大的宣告——子有使死人复活的权柄！

然后主说了这段话中最核心的经文："我实实在在的告诉你们，那听我话、又信差我来者的，就有永生；不至于定罪，是已经出死入生了"（约5:24）。

> **"已经出死入生了"**——注意时态，不是将来，是**已经**。信的那一刻，永生就开始了。这不是等到死后才兑现的支票，是现在就生效的恩典。

四、四个见证（5:31-47）

主提出四个为他作见证的：施洗约翰的见证（约翰是"点着的明灯"）、主自己所做的事工、父神亲自的见证、以及圣经。

最后主说了一句令法利赛人无法接受的话："你们如果信摩西，也必信我，因为他书上有指着我写的话。你们若不信他的书，怎能信我的话呢？"（约5:46-47）。**摩西所写的每一个字，都是指向基督的。** 整本旧约就是一封指向基督的长信。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am John. What I recount today happened beside the pool of Bethesda in Jerusalem—a man paralyzed for thirty-eight years met the Lord of life.

I. The Paralytic at Bethesda (5:1-9)

In Jerusalem by the Sheep Gate there is a pool called Bethesda in Aramaic, which has five roofed colonnades. "In these lay a great multitude of impotent folk, of blind, halt, withered, waiting for the moving of the water." (John 5:3). They waited for the water to stir, believing the first one in would be healed.

The Lord approached a paralytic, knowing he had been ill thirty-eight years, and asked: "When Jesus saw him lie, and knew that he had been now a long time in that case, he saith unto him, Wilt thou be made whole?" (John 5:6).

The question seems unnecessary yet strikes at the soul. Thirty-eight years—when waiting becomes habit, when despair becomes normal, does one dare hope? The man did not simply say "yes" but explained his predicament: "The impotent man answered him, Sir, I have no man, when the water is troubled, to put me into the pool: but while I am coming, another steppeth down before me." (John 5:7). His answer held no hope, only excuses and resignation.

The Lord did not wait for the water to stir. He spoke one sentence: "Jesus saith unto him, Rise, take up thy bed, and walk." (John 5:8). Immediately the man was healed. Thirty-eight years of paralysis, resolved by one word.

Brothers and sisters, notice—**it was not the pool water that healed him but the Lord's word.** He needed no stirring water, no helper, no action of his own—only to hear the Lord's command and stand. This is grace: not what you do, but what God has done for you.

II. The Sabbath Controversy (5:10-18)

It was the Sabbath. The Jews saw the healed man carrying his mat and accused him of breaking the Sabbath. He replied, "He answered them, He that made me whole, the same said unto me, Take up thy bed, and walk." (John 5:11).

They cared nothing that a man paralyzed for thirty-eight years had been healed; they only cared about Sabbath regulations. **The horror of legalism: rules matter more than people, tradition more than life.** The Lord later told them, "But Jesus answered them, My Father worketh hitherto, and I work." (John 5:17). This enraged them further—He called God "my Father," making Himself equal with God.

III. The Relationship of Father and Son (5:19-30)

What follows is one of the most profound Christological discourses in John's Gospel. The Lord said: "Then answered Jesus and said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, The Son can do nothing of himself, but what he seeth the Father do: for what things soever he doeth, these also doeth the Son likewise." (John 5:19). This does not mean the Son is weak or lacks power; it means the Father and Son are **perfectly united in will**—what the Son does is what the Father does.

"For as the Father raiseth up the dead, and quickeneth them; even so the Son quickeneth whom he will." (John 5:21). What an astounding declaration—the Son has authority to raise the dead!

Then the Lord spoke the most pivotal verse of this passage: "Verily, verily, I say unto you, He that heareth my word, and believeth on him that sent me, hath everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life." (John 5:24).

> **"Has passed from death to life"**—notice the tense: not future, but **already**. The moment one believes, eternal life begins. This is not a check to be cashed after death; it is grace effective now.

IV. Four Witnesses (5:31-47)

The Lord presented four witnesses on His behalf: John the Baptist ("a burning and shining lamp"), the works the Lord Himself performed, the Father's own testimony, and Scripture.

Finally, the Lord spoke words the Pharisees could not accept: "For had ye believed Moses, ye would have believed me: for he wrote of me. But if ye believe not his writings, how shall ye believe my words?" (John 5:46-47). **Every word Moses wrote points to Christ.** The entire Old Testament is one long letter pointing to Him.

## 历史背景（中文）

毕士大池位于耶路撒冷城北羊门附近，考古发掘证实确实有一个带五个廊子的双池结构。"三十八年"可能有象征意义——以色列人在旷野漂流也是三十八年（申2:14）。犹太人的安息日规条极其严格，"拿褥子"被视为违反安息日禁止携带物品的规定（参尼13:15-22）。主将自己与"我父"等同，在犹太一神论的背景下是极大的宣称——正因如此犹太人要杀他（5:18）。

## Historical Context (English)

The Pool of Bethesda was located near the Sheep Gate in northern Jerusalem; archaeological excavations have confirmed a double-pool structure with five colonnades. "Thirty-eight years" may carry symbolic significance—Israel also wandered in the wilderness for thirty-eight years (Deut 2:14). Jewish Sabbath regulations were extremely strict; "carrying a mat" was considered a violation of the Sabbath prohibition against bearing burdens (cf. Neh 13:15-22). The Lord's equation of Himself with "my Father" was an extraordinary claim in the context of Jewish monotheism—precisely why the Jews sought to kill Him (5:18).

## 经文结构（中文）

本章分为**叙事**（5:1-18）和**讲论**（5:19-47）两大部分。叙事以医治瘫子为核心，引出安息日争论和"子等于父"的宣告。讲论部分是主对犹太人的辩护，结构为：**（一）子与父的关系**（5:19-23）——子做父所做的一切；**（二）生命与审判的权柄**（5:24-30）——信的人已经出死入生；**（三）四个见证**（5:31-40）——约翰、神迹、父神、圣经；**（四）对犹太人的责备**（5:41-47）——你们不信摩西，怎能信我？

## Structure (English)

The chapter divides into **narrative** (5:1-18) and **discourse** (5:19-47). The narrative centers on healing the paralytic, leading to the Sabbath controversy and the declaration of Son-Father equality. The discourse is the Lord's defense before the Jews, structured as: **(1) The Son-Father relationship** (5:19-23)—the Son does all the Father does; **(2) Authority over life and judgment** (5:24-30)—the believer has already passed from death to life; **(3) Four witnesses** (5:31-40)—John, miracles, the Father, Scripture; **(4) Rebuke of the Jews** (5:41-47)—if you believed Moses, you would believe me.

## 神学要点（中文）

本章的核心神学是**基督的神性和审判权柄**。主宣称与父同等地做工（5:17）、赐生命（5:21）、施行审判（5:22）——这些都是唯独属于神的权柄。韦敏斯德信条第八章论基督为中保教导：子在永恒里由父所生，是真实的神和真实的人。"已经出死入生"（5:24）体现了改革宗**有效呼召**的教义——不是人选择信，是神的话语有效地呼召，使听见的人从死里活过来。正如瘫子自己无法站起来，是主的话语赐给他力量——重生同样不是人的决定，而是神主权恩典的作为。

## Theology (English)

The central theology is **Christ's deity and authority to judge**. The Lord claimed to work equally with the Father (5:17), give life (5:21), and execute judgment (5:22)—all prerogatives belonging to God alone. The Westminster Confession (ch. 8): "The Son of God, the second person of the Trinity, being very and eternal God, of one substance and equal with the Father." "Has passed from death to life" (5:24) embodies the Reformed doctrine of **effectual calling**—it is not man choosing to believe, but God's word effectively calling, bringing the hearer from death to life. Just as the paralytic could not rise on his own and it was the Lord's word that empowered him—so regeneration is not human decision but the sovereign act of God's grace.

## 基督的影子（中文）

三十八年的瘫痪在毕士大池旁得医治，与以色列人在旷野漂流三十八年后终于进入应许之地（申2:14）形成类比。**瘫子代表了人类在罪中的无能——不能自救，不能走向神，只能等待。** 而基督主动来到他面前，用一句话改变了一切。这正是救恩的模式：不是人走向神，是神来到人中间。约翰福音5:25说"死人要听见神儿子的声音，听见的人就要活了"——这指向的不只是肉体的复活，更是灵性的重生：基督的话语有创造的大能，能叫死在罪中的人活过来（弗2:1-5）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

The healing of a thirty-eight-year paralysis at Bethesda parallels Israel's thirty-eight years of wilderness wandering before finally entering the promised land (Deut 2:14). **The paralytic represents humanity's helplessness in sin—unable to save himself, unable to walk toward God, only able to wait.** Christ came to him proactively and changed everything with one word. This is the pattern of salvation: not man walking toward God, but God coming to man. John 5:25 says, "the dead will hear the voice of the Son of God, and those who hear will live"—this points not only to physical resurrection but to spiritual rebirth: Christ's word has creative power, able to bring to life those dead in sin (Eph 2:1-5).
