## 正文（中文）

当下彼拉多将主鞭打了。那是罗马刑罚中极其残酷的鞭打——用皮鞭，上面嵌着碎骨头和金属片，一鞭下去,皮开肉绽。主的背，我不忍去看。

兵丁用荆棘编作冠冕戴在主头上，给祂穿上紫袍，又挨近祂，说："恭喜，犹太人的王阿！"他们就用手掌打祂。这是何等的羞辱——天地的主宰，被罪人戏弄、殴打。但主一句话也没说，祂像羊在剪毛的人手下无声（赛53:7）。

彼拉多又出来对众人说："我带他出来见你们，叫你们知道我查不出他有什么罪来。"主戴着荆棘冠冕，穿着紫袍出来。彼拉多对他们说："你们看这个人！"（ἴδε ὁ ἄνθρωπος）这话何等讽刺——这不仅是"一个人"，这是"那人"，是神应许要来拯救我们的那位（创3:15）。

祭司长和差役看见主，就喊着说："钉他十字架！钉他十字架！"彼拉多说："你们自己把他钉十字架吧！我查不出他有什么罪来。"犹太人回答说："我们有律法，按那律法，他是该死的，因他以自己为神的儿子。"

彼拉多听见这话,越发害怕，又进衙门，对主说："你是哪里来的？"主却不回答。彼拉多说："你不对我说话吗？你岂不知我有权柄释放你，也有权柄把你钉十字架吗？"主回答说："若不是从上头赐给你的，你就毫无权柄办我。所以，把我交给你的那人罪更重了。"即使在这时刻，主仍显明祂的主权——没有人能杀祂，除非祂自己甘愿。

从此，彼拉多想要释放主。但犹太人喊着说："你若释放这个人，就不是凯撒的忠臣。凡以自己为王的，就是背叛凯撒了。"这话触动了彼拉多的软肋。他害怕被控告不忠于罗马，于是带主出来，到了一个地方，叫"铺华石处"，希伯来话叫厄巴大，就在那里坐堂。那日是预备逾越节的日子，约有午正。彼拉多对犹太人说："看哪，这是你们的王！"他们喊着说："除掉他！除掉他！钉他在十字架上！"彼拉多说："我可以把你们的王钉十字架吗？"祭司长回答说："除了凯撒，我们没有王。"

这话何等可悲！神曾应许亚伯拉罕的后裔作王直到永远，如今他们的祭司长却说"除了凯撒，我们没有王"。他们宁可认罗马的异教皇帝为王，也不认神的儿子为王。于是彼拉多将主交给他们去钉十字架。

他们就把主带了去。主背着自己的十字架出来，到了一个地方，名叫"髑髅地"，希伯来话叫各各他。他们在那里钉祂在十字架上，还有两个人和祂一同钉着，一边一个，主在中间。彼拉多又用牌子写了一个名号，安在十字架上，写的是："犹太人的王，拿撒勒人耶稣。"有许多犹太人念这名号，因为主钉十字架的地方与城相近，并且是用希伯来、罗马、希腊三样文字写的。

祭司长对彼拉多说："不要写'犹太人的王'，要写'他自己说：我是犹太人的王'。"彼拉多说："我所写的，我已经写上了。"这是彼拉多少有的一次坚持——他不知道，自己竟在十字架上宣告了真理：这真是犹太人的王，是万王之王。

兵丁既然将主钉在十字架上,就拿祂的衣服分为四份,每兵一份；又拿祂的里衣，这件里衣原来没有缝儿，是上下一片织成的。他们就彼此说："我们不要撕开，只要拈阄，看谁得着。"这要应验经上的话说："他们分了我的外衣，为我的里衣拈阄。"（诗22:18）兵丁果然做了这事。

站在主十字架旁边的，有祂母亲与祂母亲的姊妹，并革罗罢的妻子马利亚和抹大拉的马利亚。主见母亲和祂所爱的那门徒（就是我）站在旁边，就对母亲说："母亲，看，你的儿子！"又对我说："看，你的母亲！"从那时候起，我就接她到我家里去了。即使在十字架上，主仍顾念祂的母亲,托付给我照顾。

这事以后，主知道各样的事已经成了，为要使经上的话应验，就说："我渴了。"有一个器皿盛满了醋，放在那里；他们就拿海绒蘸满了醋，绑在牛膝草上，送到祂口。主尝了那醋，说："成了！"就低下头，将灵魂交付神了（παρέδωκεν τὸ πνεῦμα）。

"成了！"（τετέλεσται）这是我听过最伟大的宣告。不是"完了"，而是"成就了"——父所托付的救赎大工，在这一刻完全成就了。人类的罪债，在这一刻完全付清了。神的公义得到满足，神的慈爱得到彰显。这一个字，震动天地。

犹太人因这日是预备日，又因那安息日是个大日，就求彼拉多叫人打断他们的腿，把他们拿去，免得尸首当安息日留在十字架上。于是兵丁来，把头一个人的腿，并与主同钉第二个人的腿，都打断了。只是来到主那里，见祂已经死了，就不打断祂的腿。惟有一个兵拿枪扎祂的肋旁，随即有血和水流出来。

"骨头一根也不可折断"（出12:46）——逾越节羔羊的规定，在主身上应验了。"他们要仰望自己所扎的人"（亚12:10）——先知的预言，也在此应验。我看见这些事，我作见证——我记录这一切，是要你们信耶稣是基督，是神的儿子，并且叫你们信了，就可以因祂的名得生命（约20:31）。

这事以后，有亚利马太人约瑟，是主的门徒，只因怕犹太人，就暗暗地作门徒。他来求彼拉多，要把主的身体领去。彼拉多允准，他就把主的身体领去了。又有尼哥底母，就是先前夜里去见主的，带着没药和沉香约有一百斤前来。他们就照犹太人殡葬的规矩，把主的身体用细麻布加上香料裹好了。在主钉十字架的地方有一个园子，园子里有一座新坟墓，是从来没有葬过人的。只因是犹太人的预备日，又因那坟墓近，他们就把主安放在那里。

我看着他们把主放进坟墓，滚石封门。我的心破碎了。一切都结束了吗？我们跟随了三年半的主，真的就这样死了吗？那夜，我彻夜未眠，泪水浸湿了枕头。但我不知道，第三日的清晨，一切都要改变。

## Content (English)

Then Pilate took the Lord and flogged Him. It was one of the most brutal punishments in Roman law—a whip embedded with broken bones and metal fragments, each lash tearing flesh from bone. I could not bear to look at the Lord's back.

The soldiers twisted together a crown of thorns and put it on His head and arrayed Him in a purple robe. They came up to Him, saying, "Hail, King of the Jews!" and struck Him with their hands. What humiliation—the Lord of heaven and earth, mocked and beaten by sinners. But the Lord said not a word; He was like a sheep silent before its shearers (Isa 53:7).

Pilate went out again and said to them, "See, I am bringing him out to you that you may know that I find no guilt in him." The Lord came out, wearing the crown of thorns and the purple robe. Pilate said to them, "Behold the man!" (ἴδε ὁ ἄνθρωπος) What irony in these words—this is not merely "a man," this is "the Man," the one God promised would come to save us (Gen 3:15).

When the chief priests and officers saw the Lord, they cried out, "Crucify him, crucify him!" Pilate said to them, "Take him yourselves and crucify him, for I find no guilt in him." The Jews answered him, "We have a law, and according to that law he ought to die because he has made himself the Son of God."

When Pilate heard this statement, he was even more afraid. He entered his headquarters again and said to the Lord, "Where are you from?" But the Lord gave no answer. Pilate said to Him, "You will not speak to me? Do you not know that I have authority to release you and authority to crucify you?" The Lord answered him, "You would have no authority over me at all unless it had been given you from above. Therefore he who delivered me over to you has the greater sin." Even at this moment, the Lord revealed His sovereignty—no one could kill Him unless He willingly submitted.

From then on Pilate sought to release the Lord. But the Jews cried out, "If you release this man, you are not Caesar's friend. Everyone who makes himself a king opposes Caesar." These words struck Pilate's weakness. Fearing accusation of disloyalty to Rome, he brought the Lord out and sat down on the judgment seat at a place called The Stone Pavement, in Aramaic, Gabbatha. It was the day of Preparation for the Passover, about the sixth hour. He said to the Jews, "Behold your King!" They cried out, "Away with him, away with him, crucify him!" Pilate said to them, "Shall I crucify your King?" The chief priests answered, "We have no king but Caesar."

How tragic these words! God had promised that Abraham's descendants would reign forever, yet now their chief priests said, "We have no king but Caesar." They would rather recognize the pagan Roman emperor as king than the Son of God. So Pilate delivered the Lord over to them to be crucified.

They took the Lord. He went out, bearing His own cross, to the place called The Place of a Skull, which in Aramaic is called Golgotha. There they crucified Him, and with Him two others, one on either side, and the Lord between them. Pilate also wrote an inscription and put it on the cross. It read, "Jesus of Nazareth, the King of the Jews." Many of the Jews read this inscription, for the place where the Lord was crucified was near the city, and it was written in Aramaic, in Latin, and in Greek.

The chief priests said to Pilate, "Do not write, 'The King of the Jews,' but rather, 'This man said, I am King of the Jews.'" Pilate answered, "What I have written I have written." This was one of Pilate's rare moments of resolve—he did not know he was proclaiming truth on the cross: this truly is the King of the Jews, the King of kings.

When the soldiers had crucified the Lord, they took His garments and divided them into four parts, one part for each soldier; also His tunic. But the tunic was seamless, woven in one piece from top to bottom, so they said to one another, "Let us not tear it, but cast lots for it to see whose it shall be." This was to fulfill the Scripture: "They part my garments among them, and cast lots upon my vesture." (Ps 22:18). So the soldiers did these things.

Standing by the cross of the Lord were His mother and His mother's sister, Mary the wife of Clopas, and Mary Magdalene. When the Lord saw His mother and the disciple whom He loved (that is, me) standing nearby, He said to His mother, "Woman, behold, your son!" Then He said to me, "Behold, your mother!" And from that hour I took her to my own home. Even on the cross, the Lord cared for His mother and entrusted her to my care.

After this, knowing that all was now finished, that the Scripture might be fulfilled, the Lord said, "I thirst." A jar full of sour wine stood there, so they put a sponge full of the sour wine on a hyssop branch and held it to His mouth. When the Lord had received the sour wine, He said, "It is finished!" (τετέλεσται) and bowed His head and gave up His spirit (παρέδωκεν τὸ πνεῦμα).

"It is finished!" This is the greatest declaration I have ever heard. Not "It's over," but "It is accomplished"—the great work of redemption the Father entrusted to Him was fully accomplished in this moment. Humanity's debt of sin was fully paid in this moment. God's justice was satisfied, God's love was manifested. This one word shook heaven and earth.

Since it was the day of Preparation, and so that the bodies would not remain on the cross on the Sabbath (for that Sabbath was a high day), the Jews asked Pilate that their legs might be broken and that they might be taken away. So the soldiers came and broke the legs of the first, and of the other who had been crucified with Him. But when they came to the Lord and saw that He was already dead, they did not break His legs. But one of the soldiers pierced His side with a spear, and at once there came out blood and water.

"In one house shall it be eaten; thou shalt not carry forth ought of the flesh abroad out of the house; neither shall ye break a bone thereof." (Exod 12:46)—the regulation for the Passover lamb was fulfilled in the Lord. "And I will pour upon the house of David, and upon the inhabitants of Jerusalem, the spirit of grace and of supplications: and they shall look upon me whom they have pierced, and they shall mourn for him, as one mourneth for his only son, and shall be in bitterness for him, as one that is in bitterness for his firstborn." (Zech 12:10)—the prophet's prophecy was also fulfilled here. I saw these things, I bear witness—I record all this so that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that by believing you may have life in His name (John 20:31).

After these things Joseph of Arimathea, who was a disciple of the Lord but secretly for fear of the Jews, asked Pilate that he might take away the body of the Lord, and Pilate gave him permission. So he came and took away His body. Nicodemus also, who earlier had come to the Lord by night, came bringing a mixture of myrrh and aloes, about seventy-five pounds. They took the body of the Lord and bound it in linen cloths with the spices, as is the burial custom of the Jews. Now in the place where He was crucified there was a garden, and in the garden a new tomb in which no one had yet been laid. So because of the Jewish day of Preparation, since the tomb was close at hand, they laid the Lord there.

I watched them place the Lord in the tomb and roll the stone to seal it. My heart was broken. Was it all over? Did the Lord we had followed for three and a half years truly die like this? That night I could not sleep, tears soaking my pillow. But I did not know that on the third day morning, everything would change.

## 历史背景（中文）

第19章记录主受难和埋葬的整个过程，从鞭打（1节）到埋入坟墓（42节）。这发生在逾越节预备日（14、31、42节），即星期五。

**罗马鞭刑**：被称为"半死的刑罚"（flagellum），用皮鞭嵌碎骨和金属，常使囚犯在钉十字架前就死亡。以赛亚书50:6和53:5预言弥赛亚要受鞭打："因他受的鞭伤，我们得医治。"

**荆棘冠冕和紫袍**：士兵的戏弄（2-3节）讽刺地成了真理的宣告——祂真是王。紫色是王室的颜色，荆棘冠冕虽是羞辱的记号，却预表祂承担了人类因罪而受的咒诅（创3:17-18，地长出荆棘和蒺藜）。

**"看这个人"**（Ecce Homo）：彼拉多这话成为艺术史上著名的主题。在拉丁文传统中称为"Ecce Homo"，这短语不仅指向受苦的主，也指向祂是"末后的亚当"（林前15:45），是神应许的那位"女人的后裔"（创3:15）。

**午正时分**（14节）：约翰说"约有午正"（第六时），马可说主被钉十字架是"巳初"（第三时，可15:25）。这可能是因为：（1）约翰用罗马计时法（从午夜算起），马可用犹太计时法（从日出算起）；（2）或者约翰指彼拉多定案的时间，马可指实际钉十字架的时间。重要的是，主在逾越节羔羊被杀的时刻（下午三点左右）断气（出12:6）。

**"成了"**（Τετέλεσται）：希腊文完成时态，表示一个行动完全成就并持续有效。这词在商业文件中用来表示"债务已付清"。主在十字架上付清了我们的罪债。

**血和水**（34节）：医学上表明主真的死了（心脏破裂导致血清和红血球分离）。神学上，约翰一书5:6说"这借着水和血而来的，就是耶稣基督；不是单用水，乃是用水又用血"——指向洗礼和圣餐，教会的两大圣礼。

## Historical Context (English)

Chapter 19 records the entire process of the Lord's passion and burial, from flogging (v.1) to being placed in the tomb (v.42). This occurred on the day of Preparation for the Passover (vv.14, 31, 42), that is, Friday.

**Roman Flogging**: Called the "halfway death" (flagellum), using a whip embedded with broken bones and metal, often causing prisoners to die before crucifixion. Isaiah 50:6 and 53:5 prophesied the Messiah would be flogged: "By his stripes we are healed."

**Crown of Thorns and Purple Robe**: The soldiers' mockery (vv.2-3) ironically became a declaration of truth—He truly is King. Purple was the color of royalty, and the crown of thorns, though a sign of humiliation, prefigured Him bearing the curse humanity received because of sin (Gen 3:17-18, the ground producing thorns and thistles).

**"Behold the Man"** (Ecce Homo): Pilate's words became a famous theme in art history. In Latin tradition called "Ecce Homo," this phrase not only points to the suffering Lord, but also to Him as the "last Adam" (1 Cor 15:45), the "offspring of the woman" God promised (Gen 3:15).

**The Sixth Hour** (v.14): John says "about the sixth hour," while Mark says the Lord was crucified at "And it was the third hour, and they crucified him." (Mark 15:25). This may be because: (1) John used Roman timekeeping (from midnight), Mark used Jewish timekeeping (from sunrise); (2) or John referred to the time of Pilate's verdict, Mark to the actual crucifixion time. Importantly, the Lord died around the time the Passover lamb was killed (around 3 PM, Exod 12:6).

**"It Is Finished"** (Τετέλεσται): Greek perfect tense, indicating an action fully accomplished and continuing in effect. This word was used in commercial documents to mean "debt paid in full." The Lord paid our debt of sin on the cross.

**Blood and Water** (v.34): Medically indicates the Lord truly died (heart rupture causing separation of serum and red blood cells). Theologically, 1 John 5:6 says "This is he who came by water and blood—Jesus Christ; not by the water only but by the water and the blood"—pointing to baptism and the Lord's Supper, the two great sacraments of the church.

## 经文结构（中文）

本章可分为四个场景：

**一、主被羞辱（19:1-16a）**
鞭打、戏弄、"看这个人"（1-5节）。彼拉多与犹太人、与主的对话（6-12节）。最终彼拉多屈服，"你们的王"被交给百姓钉十字架（13-16节）。这部分强调主受的羞辱和彼拉多的软弱。

**二、主被钉十字架（19:16b-27）**
主背十字架到各各他（16-17节）。钉十字架，两边各有一人（18节）。罪状牌"犹太人的王"（19-22节）。兵丁分主的衣服（23-24节）。主将母亲托付给约翰（25-27节）。这部分充满旧约应验的细节。

**三、主断气（19:28-37）**
主说"我渴了"（28-29节）。主说"成了"，断气（30节）。兵丁扎主的肋旁（31-37节）。约翰强调他亲眼见证这一切，并引用两处旧约经文的应验。

**四、主被安葬（19:38-42）**
约瑟和尼哥底母领主的身体，按犹太规矩安葬（38-40节）。主被安放在新坟墓（41-42节）。这部分显明主真的死了，被真实地埋葬——为第20章的复活做预备。

整章结构呈现：羞辱→受刑→死亡→埋葬。这是人类历史上最黑暗的时刻，却也是神救恩计划的高潮。

## Structure (English)

This chapter can be divided into four scenes:

**I. The Lord Humiliated (19:1-16a)**
Flogging, mockery, "behold the man" (vv.1-5). Pilate's dialogue with the Jews and with the Lord (vv.6-12). Finally Pilate yields, and "your King" is delivered to the people to be crucified (vv.13-16). This section emphasizes the Lord's humiliation and Pilate's weakness.

**II. The Lord Crucified (19:16b-27)**
The Lord carries His cross to Golgotha (vv.16-17). Crucifixion, with one person on each side (v.18). The title "King of the Jews" (vv.19-22). Soldiers divide the Lord's garments (vv.23-24). The Lord entrusts His mother to John (vv.25-27). This section is filled with details fulfilling the Old Testament.

**III. The Lord's Death (19:28-37)**
The Lord says "I thirst" (vv.28-29). The Lord says "It is finished" and dies (v.30). The soldier pierces the Lord's side (vv.31-37). John emphasizes he witnessed all this with his own eyes and cites two Old Testament passages fulfilled.

**IV. The Lord's Burial (19:38-42)**
Joseph and Nicodemus take the Lord's body and bury Him according to Jewish custom (vv.38-40). The Lord is placed in a new tomb (vv.41-42). This section shows the Lord truly died and was truly buried—preparing for the resurrection in chapter 20.

The structure of the entire chapter presents: humiliation → suffering → death → burial. This is the darkest moment in human history, yet also the climax of God's plan of salvation.

## 神学要点（中文）

**代赎的成就**
"成了"（30节）宣告救赎大工的完成。改革宗神学坚持基督的赎罪是"充分的"（sufficient）和"有效的"（efficient）。充分的，意味着基督的一次献祭完全满足了神的公义要求，不需要重复或补充（来10:10-14）。有效的，意味着这赎罪真实地拯救父所拣选的人，不仅是"可能"的拯救。

**刑罚性代替**（Penal Substitution）
基督在十字架上不是仅仅作榜样或道德影响，而是真实地承担了我们该受的刑罚。祂替我们成为罪（林后5:21），替我们受咒诅（加3:13）。改革宗神学强调，基督的受苦不仅是肉体的痛苦，更是承受父神忿怒的属灵痛苦——这是"地狱的痛苦"的浓缩版本。

**圣礼的基础**
血和水（34节）预表圣礼：血指向圣餐（主的宝血），水指向洗礼（重生和洁净）。改革宗神学视圣礼为"看得见的话语"（visible words），是神恩典的记号和印证，但圣礼本身不能拯救——唯独信心能领受圣礼所印证的恩典。

**基督降卑的最低点**
"成了"之后，主"将灵魂交付神"（παρέδωκεν τὸ πνεῦμα）。祂真的死了，不是假死。腓立比书2:8说祂"自己卑微，存心顺服，以至于死，且死在十字架上。"十字架是当时最羞辱的死刑方式。基督降卑到最低点，为要把我们升高到最高点。

**旧约的应验**
本章引用或暗指多处旧约经文：分衣服（诗22:18），骨头不折断（出12:46；诗34:20），被扎（亚12:10），口渴（诗22:15；69:21）。这些应验证明：（1）十字架不是意外，而是神预定的计划；（2）旧约的一切都指向基督。

## Theology (English)

**Accomplishment of Atonement**
"It is finished" (v.30) declares the completion of the great work of redemption. Reformed theology insists that Christ's atonement is both "sufficient" and "efficient." Sufficient means Christ's one sacrifice fully satisfied God's demands of justice, needing no repetition or addition (Heb 10:10-14). Efficient means this atonement truly saves those the Father has chosen, not merely a "possible" salvation.

**Penal Substitution**
Christ on the cross was not merely an example or moral influence, but truly bore the punishment we deserved. He became sin for us (2 Cor 5:21), became a curse for us (Gal 3:13). Reformed theology emphasizes that Christ's suffering was not only physical pain, but spiritual suffering of bearing the Father's wrath—this was a concentrated version of "the pains of hell."

**Foundation of the Sacraments**
Blood and water (v.34) prefigure the sacraments: blood points to the Lord's Supper (the Lord's blood), water points to baptism (regeneration and cleansing). Reformed theology views sacraments as "visible words," signs and seals of God's grace, but the sacraments themselves cannot save—only faith can receive the grace the sacraments seal.

**The Lowest Point of Christ's Humiliation**
After "It is finished," the Lord "gave up his spirit" (παρέδωκεν τὸ πνεῦμα). He truly died, not a fake death. Philippians 2:8 says He "humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross." Crucifixion was the most humiliating form of execution at that time. Christ descended to the lowest point to raise us to the highest point.

**Fulfillment of the Old Testament**
This chapter quotes or alludes to multiple Old Testament passages: dividing garments (Ps 22:18), bones not broken (Exod 12:46; Ps 34:20), being pierced (Zech 12:10), thirst (Ps 22:15; 69:21). These fulfillments prove: (1) the cross was not an accident, but God's ordained plan; (2) all of the Old Testament points to Christ.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**逾越节的羔羊**
"那安息日是个大日"（31节）——逾越节的安息日。主在逾越节被杀，应验了祂作为"神的羔羊"（约1:29）的身份。出埃及记12章的逾越节羔羊的每个细节都在主身上应验：羔羊无残疾（出12:5）→ 彼拉多三次宣告主无罪；羔羊在黄昏被杀（出12:6）→ 主在下午三点左右断气；羔羊的骨头一根不可折断（出12:46）→ 兵丁没有打断主的腿（33节）。保罗总结："我们逾越节的羔羊基督已经被杀献祭了"（林前5:7）。

**铜蛇的预表**
约翰福音3:14主自己说："摩西在旷野怎样举蛇，人子也必照样被举起来。"民数记21:8-9，以色列人被火蛇咬伤，神吩咐摩西造一条铜蛇挂在杆上，凡仰望的就得活。主在十字架上"被举起来"，凡仰望祂、信靠祂的就得永生。铜蛇是审判的象征（蛇代表罪和咒诅），主在十字架上成为罪，好叫我们在祂里面成为神的义。

**受苦的仆人**
以赛亚书53章的预言在本章完全应验："他被藐视，被人厌弃...他被欺压，在受苦的时候却不开口...他像羊羔被牵到宰杀之地"（赛53:3、7）。主在十字架上"担当我们的忧患，背负我们的痛苦...因他受的鞭伤，我们得医治...耶和华使我们众人的罪孽都归在他身上"（赛53:4-6）。

**女人的后裔**
创世记3:15神应许女人的后裔要"伤蛇的头"，蛇要"伤他的脚跟"。十字架是这预言的应验：主在十字架上"败坏了那掌死权的，就是魔鬼"（来2:14）。主受了"脚跟"的伤（受死），却给撒但致命的"头"伤（败坏死亡权势）。

**大祭司的献祭**
利未记16章赎罪日，大祭司要献上赎罪祭。但旧约大祭司每年要重复献祭，因为那些祭物不能真正除罪（来10:1-4）。基督是更美的大祭司，祂献上自己作祭物，一次献上就永远有效（来7:27；9:12）。"成了"宣告：不需要再献祭了，救赎已经完成。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**The Passover Lamb**
"That Sabbath was a high day" (v.31)—the Sabbath of Passover. The Lord was killed at Passover, fulfilling His identity as "The next day John seeth Jesus coming unto him, and saith, Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world." (John 1:29). Every detail of the Passover lamb in Exodus 12 was fulfilled in the Lord: the lamb without blemish (Exod 12:5) → Pilate three times declared the Lord innocent; the lamb killed at twilight (Exod 12:6) → the Lord died around 3 PM; not a bone of the lamb shall be broken (Exod 12:46) → the soldiers did not break the Lord's legs (v.33). Paul summarizes: "Purge out therefore the old leaven, that ye may be a new lump, as ye are unleavened. For even Christ our passover is sacrificed for us:" (1 Cor 5:7).

**The Bronze Serpent Prefigured**
In John 3:14 the Lord Himself said: "As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up." In Numbers 21:8-9, when Israelites were bitten by fiery serpents, God commanded Moses to make a bronze serpent and put it on a pole; whoever looked at it would live. The Lord was "lifted up" on the cross; whoever looks to Him and trusts Him receives eternal life. The bronze serpent was a symbol of judgment (the serpent representing sin and curse); the Lord became sin on the cross, so that we might become the righteousness of God in Him.

**The Suffering Servant**
The prophecy of Isaiah 53 was completely fulfilled in this chapter: "He was despised and rejected by men...He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth...like a lamb that is led to the slaughter" (Isa 53:3, 7). The Lord on the cross "Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted. But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed. All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all." (Isa 53:4-6).

**The Offspring of the Woman**
In Genesis 3:15 God promised that the offspring of the woman would "bruise the serpent's head," and the serpent would "bruise his heel." The cross is the fulfillment of this prophecy: the Lord on the cross "Forasmuch then as the children are partakers of flesh and blood, he also himself likewise took part of the same; that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil;" (Heb 2:14). The Lord received a "heel" wound (death), but gave Satan a fatal "head" wound (destroying the power of death).

**The High Priest's Sacrifice**
In Leviticus 16 on the Day of Atonement, the high priest had to offer a sin offering. But Old Testament high priests had to repeat the sacrifice every year because those sacrifices could not truly remove sin (Heb 10:1-4). Christ is a better high priest; He offered Himself as the sacrifice, once for all, eternally effective (Heb 7:27; 9:12). "It is finished" declares: no more sacrifice is needed, redemption is complete.
