## 正文（中文）

我约翰永远忘不了那一天——主让死了四天的拉撒路从坟墓里走出来。这神迹彻底改变了一切，既使许多人信主，也促使公会下定决心要杀祂。

拉撒路病了。他的姐姐马大和马利亚打发人去见主说："主啊，你所爱的人病了。"主听见却说:"这病不至于死，乃是为神的荣耀，叫神的儿子因此得荣耀。"然后祂仍住了两天，直到拉撒路死了才动身往犹太去。我们门徒都很困惑——主既然爱拉撒路一家，为什么不立刻去医治他？后来我才明白：主要行更大的神迹，不是医治病人，乃是叫死人复活！

到了伯大尼，拉撒路在坟墓里已经四天了。马大一听见主来，就出去迎接，对主说："主啊，你若早在这里，我兄弟必不死。就是现在，我也知道，你无论向神求什么，神也必赐给你。"主说："你兄弟必然复活。"马大说："我知道在末日复活的时候，他必复活。"

然后主说出了那震撼千古的话："复活在我，生命也在我。信我的人虽然死了，也必复活，凡活着信我的人必永远不死。你信这话吗？"（11:25-26）

弟兄姊妹，这不仅是安慰，更是宣告！主不是说"我知道复活在哪里"，而是说"复活在我"——祂自己就是复活，祂自己就是生命。复活不是一个未来的事件，乃是一个位格，就是基督！生命不是一种状态，乃是一个人，就是耶稣！

马大回答："主啊，是的，我信你是基督，是神的儿子，就是那要临到世界的。"这是何等的信心宣告！即使在绝望中，她仍认信基督的身份。

马利亚也出来，俯伏在主脚前，说了同样的话："主啊，你若早在这里，我兄弟必不死。"我看见主看到她哭，同她来的犹太人也哭，"就心里悲叹，又甚忧愁"（11:33）。然后记载了圣经中最短的一节经文："耶稣哭了。"（11:35）

主哭了。祂不是冷漠的神，不是远离人间疾苦的哲学家。祂是与我们一同哀哭的主，是体恤我们软弱的大祭司（来4:15）。祂知道要叫拉撒路复活，但祂仍然为死亡、为罪带来的痛苦而哀哭。这是神对人类堕落状况的哀叹，也是祂爱的明证。

主来到坟墓前——那是个洞，有石头挡着。主说："你们把石头挪开。"马大说："主啊，他现在必是臭了，因为他死了已经四天了。"但主坚持。他们就把石头挪开。

主举目望天，祷告说："父啊，我感谢你，因为你已经听我。我也知道你常听我，但我说这话是为周围站着的众人，叫他们信是你差了我来。"然后祂大声呼叫："拉撒路出来！"

那死人就出来了！手脚裹着布，脸上包着手巾。主说："解开，叫他走！"许多犹太人就信了祂。

但有些人去见法利赛人，报告了这事。祭司长和法利赛人聚集公会说："这人行好些神迹，我们怎么办呢？若这样由着他，人人都要信他，罗马人也要来夺我们的地土和我们的百姓。"大祭司该亚法说："你们不知道什么。独不想一个人替百姓死，免得通国灭亡，就是你们的益处。"

约翰在此加上注释："他这话不是出于自己，是因他本年作大祭司，所以预言耶稣将要替这一国死；也不但替这一国死，并要将神四散的子民都聚集归一。"从那日起，他们就商议要杀耶稣。

弟兄姊妹，拉撒路的复活预示基督自己的复活，也预示末日所有信徒的复活。死亡不是终点，乃是通向荣耀的门。我们的身体会朽坏、会埋葬，但主一声呼唤，我们都要复活！因为复活在祂，生命也在祂。

## Content (English)

I, John, will never forget that day—when the Lord brought Lazarus, dead for four days, out of the tomb. This miracle changed everything, leading many to believe while also prompting the Sanhedrin to determine to kill Him.

Lazarus fell ill. His sisters Martha and Mary sent word to the Lord saying, "Lord, he whom you love is ill." But the Lord said: "This illness does not lead to death. It is for the glory of God, so that the Son of God may be glorified through it." Then He stayed two more days until Lazarus died before heading to Judea. We disciples were all confused—since the Lord loved the Lazarus family, why didn't He go immediately to heal him? Later I understood: the Lord wanted to perform a greater miracle—not healing the sick, but raising the dead!

When He arrived at Bethany, Lazarus had been in the tomb four days. When Martha heard the Lord was coming, she went out to meet Him, saying: "Lord, if you had been here, my brother would not have died. But even now I know that whatever you ask from God, God will give you." The Lord said: "Your brother will rise again." Martha said, "I know that he will rise again in the resurrection on the last day."

Then the Lord spoke those words that shake through the ages: "I am the resurrection and the life. Whoever believes in me, though he die, yet shall he live, and everyone who lives and believes in me shall never die. Do you believe this?" (11:25-26)

Brothers and sisters, this is not merely comfort but proclamation! The Lord did not say "I know where resurrection is," but "I am the resurrection"—He Himself is the resurrection, He Himself is the life. Resurrection is not a future event but a person: Christ! Life is not a state but a person: Jesus!

Martha answered: "Yes, Lord; I believe that you are the Christ, the Son of God, who is coming into the world." What a confession of faith! Even in despair, she still confessed Christ's identity.

Mary also came out, fell at the Lord's feet, saying the same words: "Lord, if you had been here, my brother would not have died." I saw the Lord, when He saw her weeping and the Jews with her weeping, "deeply moved in spirit and greatly troubled" (11:33). Then Scripture records its shortest verse: "Jesus wept." (11:35)

The Lord wept. He is not a cold God, not a philosopher distant from human suffering. He is the Lord who weeps with us, the High Priest who sympathizes with our weaknesses (Heb 4:15). He knew He would raise Lazarus, yet He still wept for death, for the pain sin brings. This is God's lament over humanity's fallen state and evidence of His love.

The Lord came to the tomb—it was a cave with a stone laid against it. The Lord said, "Take away the stone." Martha said, "Lord, by this time there will be an odor, for he has been dead four days." But the Lord insisted. So they took away the stone.

The Lord lifted up His eyes and prayed: "Father, I thank you that you have heard me. I knew that you always hear me, but I said this on account of the people standing around, that they may believe that you sent me." Then He cried out with a loud voice, "Lazarus, come out!"

The dead man came out! His hands and feet bound with linen strips, face wrapped with a cloth. The Lord said, "Unbind him, and let him go." Many Jews believed in Him.

But some went to the Pharisees and reported what happened. The chief priests and Pharisees gathered the council saying: "What are we to do? For this man performs many signs. If we let him go on like this, everyone will believe in him, and the Romans will come and take away both our place and our nation." Caiaphas the high priest said: "You know nothing. It is better for you that one man should die for the people, not that the whole nation should perish."

John adds this note: "He did not say this of his own accord, but being high priest that year he prophesied that Jesus would die for the nation, and not for the nation only, but also to gather into one the children of God who are scattered abroad." From that day on they made plans to put Jesus to death.

Brothers and sisters, Lazarus' resurrection foreshadows Christ's own resurrection and the resurrection of all believers at the last day. Death is not the endpoint but the door to glory. Our bodies will decay and be buried, but at the Lord's call, we will all rise! Because resurrection is in Him, life is also in Him.

## 历史背景（中文）

伯大尼是耶路撒冷以东约3公里（"六里路",11:18）的小村庄，在橄榄山东坡。这是主常住的地方，马大、马利亚、拉撒路一家接待过祂多次（路10:38-42）。从伯大尼到耶路撒冷的距离，意味着消息能很快传到城里，也解释了为何"有好些犹太人来看马大和马利亚"（11:19）——这些是从耶路撒冷来吊丧的人。

犹太人的丧葬习俗包括专业哀哭者、七日丧期、坟墓探访等。认为人死后灵魂在尸体附近徘徊三天，第四天才彻底离去——这解释了为何特别强调"在坟墓里已经四天了"（11:17,39）：拉撒路毫无疑问是真的死了，腐烂已经开始，不可能只是昏迷或假死。这使神迹更无可辩驳。

大祭司该亚法（主后18-36年在任）是撒都该派，以政治手腕和与罗马妥协著称。他的岳父亚那也曾任大祭司。他说"一个人替百姓死...免得通国灭亡"（11:50）本是出于政治考量——他怕耶稣运动引发罗马镇压——但神使用他的话成为无意的预言：基督确实要一人替众人死，成就救赎。

公会（Sanhedrin）的正式决议杀耶稣（11:53）标志着事态的决定性转折。此前虽有敌意和冲突，但从此刻起，祂的死成为既定计划，只是时间问题。主也因此"不再显然行在犹太人中间"（11:54），退到旷野附近的以法莲城，直到逾越节前六日才回到伯大尼（12:1）。

## Historical Context (English)

Bethany was a small village about 3 km ("six furlongs," 11:18) east of Jerusalem, on the eastern slope of the Mount of Olives. This was a place the Lord often stayed; the family of Martha, Mary, and Lazarus had hosted Him multiple times (Luke 10:38-42). The distance from Bethany to Jerusalem meant news could quickly reach the city, explaining why "many of the Jews had come to Martha and Mary" (11:19)—these were mourners from Jerusalem.

Jewish burial customs included professional mourners, seven-day mourning period, tomb visitation, etc. It was believed the soul lingered near the body for three days, departing completely on the fourth day—this explains the special emphasis on "he has been in the tomb four days" (11:17,39): Lazarus was undoubtedly truly dead, decay had begun, not possibly just unconscious or feigning death. This made the miracle irrefutable.

High Priest Caiaphas (served 18-36 AD) was a Sadducee, known for political maneuvering and compromise with Rome. His father-in-law Annas had also been high priest. His words "it is better for you that one man should die for the people... rather than that the whole nation should perish" (11:50) were politically motivated—he feared the Jesus movement would provoke Roman suppression—but God used his words to become an unwitting prophecy: Christ would indeed die alone for the many, accomplishing redemption.

The Sanhedrin's formal decision to kill Jesus (11:53) marked a decisive turning point. Though there had been hostility and conflict before, from this moment His death became a settled plan, only a matter of timing. The Lord therefore "no longer walked openly among the Jews" (11:54), withdrew to a town called Ephraim near the wilderness, until six days before Passover when He returned to Bethany (12:1).

## 经文结构（中文）

**一、延迟的爱（11:1-16）** — 拉撒路病了，姐妹打发人去告诉主。主说这病不至于死，却仍住了两天。门徒怕回犹太（"犹太人近来要拿石头打你"），但多马说"我们也去和他同死吧"——虽然多马后来怀疑复活，但此时他愿与主同死，这勇气可嘉。

**二、复活与生命的宣告（11:17-27）** — 主到了，拉撒路已死四天。马大出来见主，说"你若早在这里，我兄弟必不死"。主说"你兄弟必然复活"。马大说"我知道在末日复活的时候"。主纠正她："复活在我，生命也在我...你信这话吗？"马大认信："主啊，是的，我信你是基督，是神的儿子，就是那要临到世界的。"——这是全章神学核心。

**三、耶稣的哀哭（11:28-37）** — 马利亚出来，俯伏主脚前哭泣。主看见她和犹太人哭，"心里悲叹，又甚忧愁"，然后"耶稣哭了"。这显明主的人性——祂与哀哭的人同哭（罗12:15）。犹太人说"你看他爱这人是何等恳切"，也有人质疑"他既然开了瞎子的眼睛，岂不能叫这人不死吗？"

**四、死人复活的神迹（11:38-44）** — 主来到坟墓（一个洞，有石头挡着），说"把石头挪开"。马大提醒"他现在必是臭了"。主说"你若信，就必看见神的荣耀"。石头挪开后，主祷告感谢父（为周围的人），然后大声呼叫"拉撒路出来！"那死人就出来了。主说"解开，叫他走"。

**五、公会的阴谋（11:45-57）** — 许多人信了，但有些人去报告法利赛人。公会聚集商议，怕罗马人镇压。该亚法说"一个人替百姓死...免得通国灭亡"——这话无意中成为关于基督代赎的预言。从那日起商议要杀耶稣。主退到以法莲城。逾越节近了，众人寻找耶稣。

全章结构紧凑，从爱的延迟到神迹的显现，从生命的宣告到死亡的阴谋，贯穿的主题是：基督是复活和生命，祂的死不是失败，乃是神救赎计划的核心。

## Structure (English)

**I. Delayed Love (11:1-16)** — Lazarus fell ill, sisters sent word to the Lord. The Lord said this illness does not lead to death, yet stayed two days. Disciples feared returning to Judea ("the Jews were just now seeking to stone you"), but Thomas said "Let us also go, that we may die with him"—though Thomas later doubted the resurrection, at this moment he was willing to die with the Lord, commendable courage.

**II. Proclamation of Resurrection and Life (11:17-27)** — The Lord arrived, Lazarus had been dead four days. Martha came out to meet the Lord, saying "if you had been here, my brother would not have died." The Lord said "Your brother will rise again." Martha said "I know that he will rise again in the resurrection on the last day." The Lord corrected her: "I am the resurrection and the life... Do you believe this?" Martha confessed: "Yes, Lord; I believe that you are the Christ, the Son of God, who is coming into the world."—This is the theological core of the chapter.

**III. Jesus' Weeping (11:28-37)** — Mary came out, fell at the Lord's feet weeping. The Lord saw her and the Jews weeping, "deeply moved in spirit and greatly troubled," then "Jesus wept." This shows the Lord's humanity—He weeps with those who weep (Rom 12:15). Jews said "See how he loved him," and some questioned "Could not he who opened the eyes of the blind man also have kept this man from dying?"

**IV. Miracle of Raising the Dead (11:38-44)** — The Lord came to the tomb (a cave with a stone laid against it), said "Take away the stone." Martha reminded "by this time there will be an odor." The Lord said "If you believed, you would see the glory of God." After the stone was removed, the Lord prayed thanking the Father (for the people around), then cried with a loud voice "Lazarus, come out!" The dead man came out. The Lord said "Unbind him, and let him go."

**V. The Sanhedrin's Plot (11:45-57)** — Many believed, but some reported to the Pharisees. The council gathered to deliberate, fearing Roman suppression. Caiaphas said "it is better for you that one man should die for the people... rather than that the whole nation should perish"—these words unwittingly became a prophecy about Christ's substitution. From that day they made plans to put Jesus to death. The Lord withdrew to Ephraim. Passover was near, people sought Jesus.

The chapter structure is compact, from delayed love to miracle's appearance, from life's proclamation to death's conspiracy. The pervasive theme: Christ is resurrection and life; His death is not failure but the core of God's redemptive plan.

## 神学要点（中文）

**基督的神性——自有生命：** "复活在我，生命也在我"（11:25）——这是"我是"（ego eimi）系列宣告之一。基督不仅赐生命（祂使死人复活），祂自己就是生命的源头。约翰福音1:4"生命在他里头，这生命就是人的光"；5:26"父怎样在自己有生命，就赐给他儿子也照样在自己有生命"。祂不是从别处领受生命，乃是自有生命——这是神的属性。改革宗神学强调：基督的神性与父同等，祂有独立的生命权能（aseity）。

**末世论——已然未然（Already-Not Yet）：** 主说"信我的人虽然死了，也必复活；凡活着信我的人必永远不死"（11:25-26）。复活有两个层面：(1)已然——信徒已经出死入生（约5:24），灵性上已复活；(2)未然——身体的复活在末日（11:24）。拉撒路的复活是末日复活的预尝、记号，但不是最终的复活（他后来仍要再死）。基督的复活才是"初熟的果子"（林前15:20），保证所有在祂里面的人也必复活。

**神的主权与人的痛苦：** 主爱拉撒路一家（11:5），却任他死了。这不是冷漠，乃是主权的安排——为叫神的儿子得荣耀（11:4）。改革宗坚持：神的主权包括苦难。罗马书8:28"万事都互相效力，叫爱神的人得益处"。拉撒路的死不是意外，乃是神荣耀自己、坚固门徒信心的舞台。同时，主的哀哭（11:35）显明祂不是无情的主宰，而是满有怜悯的救主——祂恨恶罪和死亡带来的痛苦。

**代赎教义的预言：** 该亚法说"一个人替百姓死，免得通国灭亡"（11:50），约翰解释这是预言——耶稣将要替百姓死。这不仅指犹太民族，也指"神四散的子民"（外邦选民）。这是代赎神学的经典表达：一人替众人死（林后5:14）；义的代替不义的（彼前3:18）。改革宗强调：基督的死是特定的救赎（particular redemption）——祂为羊舍命（约10:15），不是为全世界每一个人，乃是为父所赐给祂的人（约17:9）。

**祷告与神迹：** 主在行神迹前祷告（11:41-42），不是因为祂需要求父帮助，乃是为教导众人：(1)认识父差子来；(2)明白父子同工。改革宗强调：神迹不是破坏自然律，乃是神主权的直接彰显；不是违背神的常规护理，乃是神特别的护理作为。

## Theology (English)

**Deity of Christ—Self-Existent Life:** "I am the resurrection and the life" (11:25)—this is one of the "I am" (ego eimi) declarations. Christ not only gives life (He raises the dead), He Himself is the source of life. John 1:4 "In him was life, and the life was the light of men"; 5:26 "For as the Father has life in himself, so he has granted the Son also to have life in himself." He does not receive life from elsewhere but is self-existent life—this is an attribute of God. Reformed theology emphasizes: Christ's deity is equal with the Father; He has independent life-power (aseity).

**Eschatology—Already-Not Yet:** The Lord said "Whoever believes in me, though he die, yet shall he live, and everyone who lives and believes in me shall never die" (11:25-26). Resurrection has two dimensions: (1) Already—believers have passed from death to life (John 5:24), spiritually already resurrected; (2) Not yet—bodily resurrection on the last day (11:24). Lazarus' resurrection is a foretaste and sign of the last-day resurrection, but not the final resurrection (he would die again later). Christ's resurrection is the "But now is Christ risen from the dead, and become the firstfruits of them that slept." (1 Cor 15:20), guaranteeing all in Him will also rise.

**God's Sovereignty and Human Suffering:** The Lord loved the Lazarus family (11:5) yet allowed him to die. This is not coldness but sovereign arrangement—so that the Son of God may be glorified (11:4). Reformed theology insists: God's sovereignty includes suffering. Romans 8:28 "And we know that for those who love God all things work together for good." Lazarus' death was not an accident but a stage for God to glorify Himself and strengthen disciples' faith. Meanwhile, the Lord's weeping (11:35) shows He is not an unfeeling Sovereign but a compassionate Savior—He hates the pain sin and death bring.

**Prophetic Declaration of Substitutionary Atonement:** Caiaphas said "it is better for you that one man should die for the people, not that the whole nation should perish" (11:50); John explains this was prophecy—Jesus would die for the people. This refers not only to the Jewish nation but also "the children of God who are scattered abroad" (Gentile elect). This is a classic expression of substitutionary theology: one died for all (2 Cor 5:14); the righteous for the unrighteous (1 Pet 3:18). Reformed theology emphasizes: Christ's death is particular redemption—He laid down His life for the sheep (John 10:15), not for every single person in the world but for those given Him by the Father (John 17:9).

**Prayer and Miracles:** The Lord prayed before performing the miracle (11:41-42), not because He needed to ask the Father for help, but to teach the people: (1) recognize the Father sent the Son; (2) understand Father and Son work together. Reformed theology emphasizes: miracles are not violations of natural law but direct manifestations of God's sovereignty; not contrary to God's ordinary providence but God's special providence at work.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督复活的预表：** 拉撒路死了四天后复活，预示基督第三日从死里复活。但拉撒路只是恢复到原来的生命（后来仍要再死），基督却复活得荣耀的身体，永不再死（罗6:9）。拉撒路被动地听从主的呼召出来，基督主动有权柄取回自己的生命（约10:18）。拉撒路的复活是记号，基督的复活是成全。

**以利亚、以利沙使死人复活的预表：** 旧约中以利亚使撒勒法寡妇的儿子复活（王上17:17-24），以利沙使书念妇人的儿子复活（王下4:18-37）。这些神迹都指向基督——祂是更大的先知，有更大的权能。那些先知要俯伏在死人身上、多次祷告才能使人活过来，但主只需一声呼唤："拉撒路出来！"创造宇宙的道（约1:1-3），用话语使无变有、使死人复活。

**以西结枯骨复活的异象（结37章）：** 神问以西结："这些骸骨能复活吗？"然后命令他向骸骨说预言，骨头就连络、长筋、长肉、有气息，成为军队站起来。这是关于以色列民族复兴的预言，但也预表基督末日叫死人复活的权能。拉撒路复活显明：神的话有使死人活过来的大能，基督一发命令（"拉撒路出来"），死亡都要听命。

**末日复活的保证：** 帖撒罗尼迦前书4:16"主必亲自从天降临...那在基督里死了的人必先复活。"哥林多前书15:52"就在一霎时，眨眼之间，号筒末次吹响的时候...死人要复活成为不朽坏的。"拉撒路复活证明主有叫死人复活的权柄和能力，保证末日所有信徒都要复活。我们不是相信抽象的复活教义，乃是相信那位自己就是"复活和生命"的基督。

**该亚法的无意预言：** 旧约大祭司有预言的职分（如乌陵土明）。该亚法说"一个人替百姓死"本是政治算计，神却使用他的话成为福音预言——基督要一人替众人死。这应验以赛亚书53:5-6"他为我们的过犯受害,为我们的罪孽压伤...耶和华使我们众人的罪孽都归在他身上。"也呼应约翰福音1:29"看哪，神的羔羊，除去世人罪孽的！"

**主的眼泪：** 主哭了（11:35），预示祂在客西马尼园的痛苦（路22:44"汗珠如大血点滴在地上"），也显明祂完全的人性。希伯来书5:7"基督在肉体的时候,既大声哀哭，流泪祷告...就因他的虔诚蒙了应允。"祂不是不能受试探的神，乃是凡事受过试探与我们一样、只是没有犯罪的大祭司（来4:15）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Type of Christ's Resurrection:** Lazarus rose after four days of death, foreshadowing Christ's resurrection on the third day. But Lazarus only returned to his former life (would die again later); Christ rose with a glorified body, never to die again (Rom 6:9). Lazarus passively obeyed the Lord's call to come out; Christ actively had authority to take back His own life (John 10:18). Lazarus' resurrection is a sign; Christ's resurrection is fulfillment.

**Type of Elijah and Elisha Raising the Dead:** In the Old Testament, Elijah raised the widow of Zarephath's son (1 Kgs 17:17-24), Elisha raised the Shunammite woman's son (2 Kgs 4:18-37). These miracles all point to Christ—He is the greater prophet with greater power. Those prophets had to stretch themselves upon the dead and pray repeatedly to bring people back to life, but the Lord needed only one call: "Lazarus, come out!" The Word who created the universe (John 1:1-3) speaks and brings being from non-being, raises the dead.

**Vision of Ezekiel's Dry Bones (Ezek 37):** God asked Ezekiel: "Can these bones live?" Then commanded him to prophesy to the bones; bones came together, grew sinews, flesh, breath, and stood as an army. This is prophecy about Israel's national revival, but also prefigures Christ's power to raise the dead on the last day. Lazarus' resurrection shows: God's word has power to make the dead come alive; Christ gives one command ("Lazarus, come out"), and death must obey.

**Guarantee of Last-Day Resurrection:** 1 Thessalonians 4:16 "For the Lord himself will descend from heaven... And the dead in Christ will rise first." 1 Corinthians 15:52 "in a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trumpet... the dead will be raised imperishable." Lazarus' resurrection proves the Lord has authority and power to raise the dead, guaranteeing all believers will rise on the last day. We do not believe in an abstract resurrection doctrine but in the Christ who Himself is "the resurrection and the life."

**Caiaphas' Unwitting Prophecy:** Old Testament high priests had prophetic function (e.g., Urim and Thummim). Caiaphas' words "it is better that one man should die for the people" were political calculation, but God used his words to become gospel prophecy—Christ would die alone for the many. This fulfills Isaiah 53:5-6 "But he was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities... and the LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all." Also echoes John 1:29 "Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!"

**The Lord's Tears:** The Lord wept (11:35), foreshadowing His agony in Gethsemane (Luke 22:44 "his sweat became like great drops of blood"), also showing His full humanity. Hebrews 5:7 "In the days of his flesh, Jesus offered up prayers and supplications, with loud cries and tears... and he was heard because of his reverence." He is not a God who cannot be tempted, but a High Priest who in every respect has been tempted as we are, yet without sin (Heb 4:15).
