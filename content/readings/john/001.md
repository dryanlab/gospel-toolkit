## 正文（中文）

我是约翰，西庇太的儿子，那位主曾经把我搁在祂胸前的门徒。如今我已经高龄，住在以弗所，是十二使徒里最后的一位。当我回头看那三年半的日子，回头看耶路撒冷、加利利海边、客西马尼、各各他、复活清晨那座空坟，我必须坐下来写。马太已写了，马可也写了，路加也写了，但有一件事我若不写，就要带进坟墓里去。我必须把那位"道"的奥秘写下来。

我不是要把弥赛亚介绍给从未听过的人。我是要写给那些已经信了，却还没看见祂全部荣耀的弟兄姐妹。我要把那位"道"、那位"神的羔羊"、那位"真葡萄树"、那位"好牧人"，重新摆在你们眼前。

所以我从永恒开始写起。"太初有道"。希腊文里我用 ἦν 这个字（持续地是），不是 ἐγένετο（开始是）。道不是某一刻才出现的，祂一直都在。祂与神同在（πρὸς τὸν θεόν，"面对面地在父怀里"），祂就是神。万物都是藉着祂造的，没有一样不是藉着祂造的。这位道，就是我跟随了三年半的那位耶稣。我看见祂饿、看见祂累、看见祂为拉撒路哭，但祂一直就是创造宇宙的那一位。这是我要你们明白的第一件事，也是诺斯底人最不愿承认的第一件事。

第二件事，是道与人的关系。光照进黑暗，黑暗却没有胜过光（οὐ κατέλαβεν 也可译为"没有领悟"）。世界是祂造的，世界却不认识祂；以色列是祂自己的百姓，自己人却不接待祂。但那些接待祂、信祂名的人，就被赐下作神儿女的权柄。这种"作神儿女"，不是从血气、不是从情欲、不是从人意，**乃是从神生的**。我把这一节摆在序言里，不是凑数。我要你们记住：你能称神为父，是因为神先把你生了。这是我们改革宗弟兄今天还在传讲的"唯独恩典"，五十年前我跟在主身边时，还没有这个名词，但那个真理已经在这里了。

第三件事，是道与肉身的联合。那位永恒的道，σὰρξ ἐγένετο（成了肉身）。希腊文 σάρξ 不是"身体"那种轻巧的词，是有血、有汗、有疲倦、会饿、会渴、会哭的"肉"。诺斯底人坚决不接受这个，他们说神不可能进入污秽的物质。但我亲手摸过祂、亲耳听过祂、亲眼看过祂（这就是我在《约翰一书》开头写的那段）。祂"支搭帐幕在我们中间"（ἐσκήNωσεν ἐν ἡμῖν，意思就是"住进了一个会幕"）。出埃及之后摩西支搭的那个会幕，神荣耀降临的那个会幕，原来都是预表这一刻：永恒的道支搭祂自己的会幕，住到我们中间来。摩西远远看不见神，只看见祂的背（出 33:23）；但我们看见了祂的荣光，那是父独生子的荣光（μονογενής，独一所生，不是被造），充充满满地有恩典与真理。律法是借摩西传的，恩典与真理是从耶稣基督来的。从来没有人见过神，唯有那位在父怀里的独生子，将祂"解释出来"（ἐξηγήσατο，希腊文"注释"的词，今天我们说 exegete）。基督不只是把神说出来，**祂本身就是神的解经**。

接下来我必须写到施洗约翰。我必须写他，因为到我落笔的这一年，仍然有人以为他是基督。他亲口三次否认。他说自己只是"在旷野有人声喊着说，预备主的道"，正是以赛亚 40 章那一段。他指着耶稣说："看哪，神的羔羊，除去世人罪孽的。"我多次回想这一句。羔羊，从亚伯的祭物到亚伯拉罕在摩利亚山的公羊，从逾越节抹门楣的羔羊到以赛亚 53 章那位被牵到宰杀之地的仆人，每一只都是预言。施洗约翰不是用诗意的称谓，他是在告诉以色列：一千五百年的献祭，终结于此。**祂就是那只羔羊**。"除去"这个动词（αἴρω）用现在式，意思是持续地、有效地、彻底地担当走了。

我永远忘不了那一天。我和安得烈站在施洗约翰身旁，看见耶稣经过。施洗约翰再说一遍："看哪，这是神的羔羊。"我们就跟去了。耶稣转身问："你们要什么？"我们答不上来，只问祂："拉比，在哪里住？"祂说："你们来看。"那是申初（ὥρα ἦν ὡς δεκάτη，第十时辰，按犹太人的计时大约下午四点）。一个老人五十年后还记得他生命被改变那一天的钟点，这不是寓言，这是历史。

安得烈先去找彼得。腓力次日找到拿但业。拿但业起初质疑："拿撒勒还能出什么好的吗？"但当他见到主，主一句话就揭穿了他的内心："腓力还没有招呼你，你在无花果树底下，我就看见你了。"拿但业立刻俯伏："拉比，你是神的儿子，你是以色列的王。"主回答："你将要看见比这更大的事。我实实在在地告诉你们，你们将要看见天开了，神的使者上去下来在人子身上。"

这一句话我一辈子在默想。雅各曾在伯特利梦见天梯，使者上去下来，他说"这不是别的，乃是神的殿，也是天的门"。但真正的天梯不是石头，是一个位格。基督就是那位连接天与地的"中保"（μεσίτης）。这就是为什么我在 1 章末了用"人子"（υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου）这个但以理 7 章的称号收尾。**祂是人，也是神，是从天降下又升上的中保**，是神与人之间唯一的通道。

这就是序言。我把整本福音书的钥匙都摆在这一章里。后面 20 章只不过是这一章的展开。你们读下去，就要看见祂在迦拿、在井旁、在毕士大、在加利利海上、在伯大尼、在客西马尼、在十字架、在复活清晨、在提比哩亚海边，每一处都是这一章里那位"道"的彰显。

## 正文（英文）

I am John, the son of Zebedee, the disciple whom the Lord once let lean against His chest. I am old now, living in Ephesus, the last of the Twelve apostles. When I look back over those three and a half years, when I look back to Jerusalem, the shore of Galilee, Gethsemane, Golgotha, the empty tomb on resurrection morning, I have to sit down and write. Matthew has already written. Mark has written. Luke has written. But there is one thing that, if I do not write it, I will carry into the grave. I must write down the mystery of the Word.

I am not introducing the Messiah to those who have never heard. I am writing for brothers and sisters who have already believed but have not yet seen the full weight of His glory. I am setting before your eyes again that Word, that Lamb of God, that True Vine, that Good Shepherd.

So I begin from eternity. "In the beginning was the Word." In Greek I use the verb ἦν (was, continuously), not ἐγένετο (came into being). The Word did not appear at some moment. He has always been. He was with God (πρὸς τὸν θεόν, literally "facing toward the Father"), and He was God. All things were made through Him, and apart from Him not one thing was made. This Word is the Jesus I followed for three and a half years. I saw Him hungry, I saw Him weary, I saw Him weep at the tomb of Lazarus, but He was, the entire time, the One through whom the universe was made. This is the first thing I want you to grasp, and it is the very thing the Gnostics will not accept.

The second thing is the Word and humanity. The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it (οὐ κατέλαβεν, which can also mean "has not understood it"). The world He made did not recognize Him. Israel, His own people, did not receive Him. Yet to those who did receive Him, who believed in His name, He gave the right to become children of God. This being made children of God is not from natural descent, not from human will, not from any man's decision, **but born of God**. I do not place this verse in the prologue casually. I want you to remember: you can call God Father only because God first gave you new birth. This is what our Reformed brethren today preach as "grace alone" (sola gratia). Fifty years ago when I walked with the Lord we did not yet have that phrase, but the truth was already here.

The third thing is the union of the Word with flesh. The eternal Word, σὰρξ ἐγένετο (became flesh). The Greek σάρξ is not a polite word for "body." It is meat, with blood, sweat, fatigue, hunger, thirst, tears. The Gnostics will refuse this absolutely. They will say God could never enter polluted matter. But I have touched Him with my hands, heard Him with my ears, looked at Him with my eyes (this is exactly what I open my first letter with). He "tabernacled among us" (ἐσκήνωσεν ἐν ἡμῖν, literally "pitched His tent"). The tabernacle Moses pitched after Egypt, where God's glory descended, was a foreshadowing of this very moment: the eternal Word pitches His own tabernacle and moves in among us. Moses could only see God's back from afar (Ex 33:23), but we beheld His glory, the glory of the Father's only-begotten (μονογενής, uniquely begotten, not made), full of grace and truth. The law was given through Moses; grace and truth came through Jesus Christ. No one has ever seen God; only the only-begotten Son, who is in the bosom of the Father, has "exegeted" Him (ἐξηγήσατο, the Greek root of our word "exegesis"). Christ does not merely speak about God. **He is the exegesis of God**.

Then I must write about John the Baptist. I must, because even in this year I am writing, there are still people who think he was the Christ. He denied it three times to their faces. He said he was only "a voice crying in the wilderness, prepare the way of the Lord," precisely the line from Isaiah 40. He pointed at Jesus and said, "Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world." I have turned this sentence over in my mind countless times. The lamb, from Abel's offering to Abraham's ram on Moriah, from the Passover lamb whose blood marked the doorposts to the Servant of Isaiah 53 led like a lamb to the slaughter, every one of them was a prophecy. John the Baptist was not paying Jesus a poetic compliment. He was telling Israel: fifteen hundred years of sacrifice end here. **He is that Lamb**. The verb "takes away" (αἴρω) is in the present tense, meaning continuously, effectively, completely lifts the sin away.

I will never forget that day. Andrew and I were standing beside John the Baptist when Jesus walked past. John said it again: "Behold, the Lamb of God." We followed. Jesus turned and asked, "What do you want?" We could not answer. We only said, "Rabbi, where are you staying?" He said, "Come and see." It was the tenth hour (ὥρα ἦν ὡς δεκάτη, by Jewish reckoning about four in the afternoon). An old man remembers, fifty years later, the very hour his life was changed. This is not allegory. This is history.

Andrew went first to find Peter. The next day Philip found Nathanael. Nathanael at first scoffed, "Can anything good come out of Nazareth?" But the moment he met the Lord, the Lord exposed his heart with a single sentence: "Before Philip called you, when you were under the fig tree, I saw you." Nathanael bowed instantly: "Rabbi, you are the Son of God; you are the King of Israel." Jesus answered, "You shall see greater things than these. Truly, truly I say to you, you shall see heaven opened, and the angels of God ascending and descending on the Son of Man."

I have meditated on this sentence my whole life. Jacob once dreamed at Bethel of a ladder with angels going up and down, and he said, "This is none other than the house of God, this is the gate of heaven." But the true ladder is not stone. It is a Person. Christ Himself is the Mediator (μεσίτης) connecting heaven and earth. That is why I close chapter 1 with "Son of Man" (υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου), the title from Daniel 7. **He is man and He is God, the Mediator who came down from heaven and went up again**, the only passage between God and man.

This is the prologue. I have placed the key to the entire Gospel in this one chapter. The twenty chapters that follow are only the unfolding of this chapter. Read on, and you will see Him at Cana, by the well, at Bethesda, on the sea of Galilee, at Bethany, at Gethsemane, at the cross, on resurrection morning, on the shore of Tiberias. Each scene is the manifestation of the Word who is in this chapter.

## 历史背景（中文）

约翰福音写于主后 85 年至 95 年之间，约翰已经是高龄使徒，在以弗所牧养亚细亚省的众教会。十二使徒中其余的弟兄已经全部殉道（彼得、保罗都已在罗马为主死了二十多年），约翰是最后一位见证人。这本福音书是新约中最后完成的福音书，也是约翰留给教会的"遗产"。

约翰落笔的时代，初代教会面临三重挑战，这本福音书针对每一重都给出回应。

**第一重：诺斯底主义的初期形态**已经在亚细亚省蔓延。诺斯底（γνωστικός，"有知识者"）相信物质本质邪恶，所以神不可能真的进入肉身。他们提出"幻影说"（docetism），主张耶稣只是看起来像人，并不真是人。这种异端要拆毁福音的根基，因为如果基督不是真正的人，祂就不能代我们死，我们就没有救恩。约翰福音 1:14"道成了肉身"是直接的反击，约翰一书开头"我们亲手摸过的"也是。

**第二重：犹太人对耶稣是弥赛亚的拒绝**仍是主要张力。主后 70 年圣殿被毁后，拉比犹太教转向严格的口传律法主义，"约翰式群体"被逐出会堂（约 9:22, 12:42, 16:2 三次提到）。约翰福音通篇用希腊文，但深植犹太背景：节期（逾越节、住棚节、修殿节）、会堂、犹太法庭程序、旧约引用频繁。约翰要让犹太人在他们自己的传统里看见耶稣就是那位被预言的弥赛亚。

**第三重：第一代信徒的离世与第二代的成长**。当亲眼见过主的人都走了，下一代如何稳固在真道上？约翰福音 20:31 写明目的："但记这些事要叫你们信耶稣是基督，是神的儿子，并且叫你们信了祂，就可以因祂的名得生命。"这是一卷训练教会神学根基的福音书，不是简单复述故事。

约翰也读过马太、马可、路加。他刻意补充前三福音没有详记的内容：耶稣在犹太地的事工（前三福音多记加利利）、深奥的"我是…"讲论、十四到十七章的告别讲论、复活后向多马的显现。前三福音重叙事，约翰福音重神学揭示。

第 1 章里"道"（Λόγος）这个字，对希腊与犹太双重背景的读者都有共鸣。希腊哲学（赫拉克利特、斯多葛派、亚历山太的斐罗）用"逻各斯"指宇宙的理性原则、神性的中介；犹太传统则强调"神的话"（dabar）有创造（诗 33:6）与启示（耶 1:4）的能力。约翰把两条传统熔铸在一起，宣告：那位希腊人在哲学中模糊向往、犹太人在律法中等候已久的"道"，已经道成肉身，住在我们中间。

约翰对时间细节的执念（"申初"、"明日"、"再次日"、"过了三天"）是目击证人的特征。改革宗神学坚守圣经的历史可靠性。约翰不是写神话，不是写寓言，是写历史中真实发生过的事，并以圣灵的光照揭示其神学意义。

## Historical Context (English)

The Gospel of John was written between AD 85 and 95, when John was an aged apostle shepherding the churches of Asia from Ephesus. The other Twelve had already been martyred (Peter and Paul had died in Rome more than twenty years earlier). John was the last living eyewitness. This is the last of the four Gospels to be completed, John's bequest to the church.

In the era when John took up his pen, the early church faced three challenges, and this Gospel answers all three.

**First, the early forms of Gnosticism** were spreading in Asia. The Gnostics (γνωστικός, "those who possess knowledge") believed matter was inherently evil, so God could never truly enter flesh. They proposed Docetism, the teaching that Jesus only seemed to be human. This heresy would destroy the foundation of the gospel: if Christ was not truly human, He could not die for us as our representative, and there is no salvation. John 1:14, "the Word became flesh," is a direct rebuttal. So is the opening of 1 John, "what we have touched with our hands."

**Second, Jewish rejection of Jesus as Messiah** remained the main tension. After the destruction of the temple in AD 70, rabbinic Judaism turned toward a stricter oral-law observance, and "Johannine communities" were being expelled from the synagogues (mentioned three times in John 9:22, 12:42, 16:2). The whole Gospel is in Greek but rooted in Jewish soil: the feasts (Passover, Tabernacles, Dedication), the synagogue, Jewish legal procedure, frequent Old Testament quotation. John wants Jews to see, within their own tradition, that Jesus is the prophesied Messiah.

**Third, the death of the first generation and the maturing of the second**. With every eyewitness gone, how does the next generation stand firm in the truth? John spells out his purpose in 20:31: "These things are written that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that believing you may have life in His name." This is a Gospel for theological grounding, not a retelling of the story.

John has also read Matthew, Mark, and Luke. He deliberately fills in what the Synoptics did not detail: Jesus's Judean ministry (the Synoptics focus on Galilee), the profound "I am" discourses, the farewell discourse of chapters 14 to 17, and the post-resurrection appearance to Thomas. The Synoptics emphasize narrative; John emphasizes theological disclosure.

The word "Word" (Λόγος) in chapter 1 resonates with both audiences. Greek philosophy (Heraclitus, the Stoics, Philo of Alexandria) used "Logos" for the rational principle of the universe, the divine mediator. Jewish tradition emphasized that God's word (dabar) has creating (Ps 33:6) and revealing (Jer 1:4) power. John fuses both traditions and proclaims: the Word that the Greek dimly longed for in philosophy, that the Jew waited for under the law, has become flesh and dwelt among us.

John's obsession with time (the tenth hour, the next day, the day after, after three days) marks him as eyewitness. Reformed theology is committed to the historical reliability of Scripture. John is not writing myth or allegory; he is writing what really happened, and under the Spirit's illumination he discloses its theological meaning.

## 结构（中文）

第 1 章共 51 节，是整本约翰福音的"序曲 + 字典 + 钥匙"。可以分为四大段，每段在文学上都自成单元，神学上又层层递进。

**一、序言：道的本质与道成肉身（1 至 18 节）**

这 18 节是整卷福音书的神学浓缩，是诗体而非散文。结构上呈"对仗式"递进：

- 1 至 5 节：道的永恒、与神的关系、创造、生命与光
- 6 至 8 节：施洗约翰的预备性见证（插入式打断诗体节奏）
- 9 至 13 节：道进入世界，世界拒绝，但少数接待者得新生
- 14 至 18 节：道成肉身的事实，恩典与真理的彰显，独生子的解经

第 14 节 "道成了肉身" 是整段诗的高潮（climax），前 13 节都在为它铺垫，后 4 节都在发挥它的意义。整个序言用了 "光"、"暗"、"接待"、"信"、"生"、"荣光"、"恩典"、"真理" 等八个核心词，全都是后面 20 章反复展开的主题。

**二、施洗约翰的双重见证（19 至 34 节）**

分两段，呈"否定 + 肯定"对偶：

- 19 至 28 节（否定式见证）：当祭司和利未人来盘问"你是谁"，约翰三次否认（不是基督、不是以利亚、不是那先知），只承认自己是以赛亚 40 章的"声音"。这是清楚划界，他绝不僭越基督。
- 29 至 34 节（肯定式见证）：次日他指认耶稣是"神的羔羊"和"用圣灵施洗的"。这是积极宣告，把整个旧约献祭体系的指针指向基督。

**三、首批门徒的呼召（35 至 51 节前半）**

记录了三个连续的"次日"，主呼召六位门徒（安得烈、约翰、彼得、腓力、拿但业，以及隐含的约翰的兄弟雅各）：

- 第一组（35 至 42）：约翰的两个门徒被引到耶稣面前，包括安得烈与作者本人；安得烈带哥哥彼得来见主
- 第二组（43 至 45）：腓力被主直接呼召，再带拿但业来
- 第三组（46 至 49）：拿但业的怀疑与降服

这一段的递进是门徒对主的称呼：拉比 → 弥赛亚 → 摩西所写的那位 → 神的儿子，以色列的王。从教师称谓一路升到弥赛亚 + 神的儿子的双重宣告。

**四、天梯的应许（51 节）**

最后一节单独成段。主对拿但业说出整本福音书的"展开公式"：你们将要看见天开了，神的使者上去下来在人子身上。这一句把约翰福音以下所有的标志（神迹）、所有的"我是…"讲论、所有的医治与死而复活、最终十字架与复活，都预告为"基督就是连接天地的天梯"的不同面向。

## Structure (English)

Chapter 1 contains 51 verses and serves as the prologue, the dictionary, and the master key for the entire Gospel of John. It divides into four major movements, each a literary unit on its own and theologically building one upon another.

**I. Prologue: The Nature of the Word and the Incarnation (vv. 1-18)**

These eighteen verses are the theological compression of the whole Gospel, written as poetry rather than prose. The structure is a chiastic progression:

- vv. 1-5: the eternity of the Word, His relation to God, creation, life and light
- vv. 6-8: the preparatory witness of John the Baptist (an interruption that breaks the poetic meter)
- vv. 9-13: the Word enters the world, the world rejects, but a few who receive Him are reborn
- vv. 14-18: the fact of the incarnation, the manifestation of grace and truth, the only-begotten Son's exegesis of the Father

Verse 14, "the Word became flesh," is the climax of the entire prologue. The first thirteen verses build toward it; the last four unfold its meaning. The prologue deploys eight core terms (light, darkness, receive, believe, born, glory, grace, truth), every one of which becomes a recurring theme through the next twenty chapters.

**II. The Twofold Testimony of John the Baptist (vv. 19-34)**

Two sections, in a "negation then affirmation" pair:

- vv. 19-28 (negative testimony): when priests and Levites come to interrogate him, "Who are you?", John denies three times (not the Christ, not Elijah, not the Prophet) and confesses only that he is the "voice" of Isaiah 40. He draws the line clearly. He will not usurp the place of Christ.
- vv. 29-34 (positive testimony): the next day he identifies Jesus as "the Lamb of God" and "the one who baptizes with the Holy Spirit." This is the active proclamation, pointing the entire Old Testament sacrificial system at Christ.

**III. The Calling of the First Disciples (vv. 35-51, first part)**

The text records three consecutive "next days" in which the Lord calls six disciples (Andrew, John, Peter, Philip, Nathanael, with John's brother James implied):

- First group (vv. 35-42): John the Baptist's two disciples are led to Jesus, including Andrew and the author himself; Andrew brings his brother Peter
- Second group (vv. 43-45): Philip is called directly by the Lord, then brings Nathanael
- Third group (vv. 46-49): Nathanael's doubt and surrender

The progression in this section is in the disciples' titles for the Lord: Rabbi, then Messiah, then "Him of whom Moses wrote," then "Son of God, King of Israel." From teacher-title rising all the way to the dual confession of Messiah and Son of God.

**IV. The Promise of the Heavenly Ladder (v. 51)**

The final verse stands alone. The Lord speaks to Nathanael the "unfolding formula" of the entire Gospel: "You shall see heaven opened, and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of Man." This sentence forecasts every "sign" (miracle) that follows, every "I am" discourse, every healing and raising from the dead, finally the cross and the resurrection, all as different facets of the truth that Christ Himself is the ladder connecting heaven and earth.

## 神学（中文）

约翰福音 1 章是整本圣经神学密度最高的章节之一，每一节都承载几个关键教义。改革宗神学在这一章里看到至少五个重要立场。

**一、圣子的完全神性与永恒先在性。** 第 1 节"太初有道，道与神同在，道就是神"，希腊文用三个动词都是 ἦν（未完成式，"持续地是"），不是 ἐγένετο（不定过去式，"变成"）。这是有意的语法选择：道不是被造，不是某一刻被生出来。祂"一直就是"。这一节直接堵住了所有否认基督神性的异端：阿里乌（基督是受造的高级被造物）、耶和华见证人（基督只是首生的天使）、摩门教（天父和耶稣是分开的物理身体）。改革宗严格遵守尼西亚信经"与父同质"（ὁμοούσιος）的教义，三位一体不是后来教会捏造的概念，约翰福音 1 章已经把它全部宣告出来。

**二、位格联合（hypostatic union）与道成肉身的奥秘。** 第 14 节"道成了肉身"。希腊文 σὰρξ ἐγένετο 是震撼的并置：永恒的 λόγος 与会朽坏的 σάρξ。卡尔西顿信经（主后 451 年）用四个否定描述这个联合：不混淆（与一性论对立）、不变化、不分开（与景教对立）、不分隔。基督是一个位格，里面有完全的神性与完全的人性。这个教义不是经院派的繁琐辩论，是救恩的根基：祂必须是真神，所以祂的代死有无限的功劳；祂必须是真人，所以祂能合法地代替人。如果剥掉任何一面，福音就破产了。约翰用 σάρξ 这个粗砺的词，正是要钉死那些想把基督的人性"灵化"的诺斯底人。

**三、唯独恩典与神主权的重生。** 第 12 至 13 节是改革宗救恩论（soteriology）的关键经文。"凡接待祂的，就是信祂名的人，祂就赐他们权柄作神的儿女。这等人不是从血气生的，不是从情欲生的，也不是从人意生的，乃是从神生的。"这里的次序极重要：**接待 + 信 在外面是人的回应，但底层是"从神生的"**。改革宗反对阿米念主义把"信"当作人主动选择神、神被动等候人决志的次序。约翰福音 1:13 与约翰福音 6:44（"若不是差我来的父吸引人，就没有能到我这里来的"）、罗马书 9 章共同支撑"无条件拣选"（unconditional election）的教义。重生是神主权的工作，发生在信心之先，是因，不是果。

**四、基督的中保职分与三重职分（munus triplex）。** 第 51 节"你们将要看见天开了，神的使者上去下来在人子身上"，主自比为雅各梦中的天梯。提摩太前书 2:5 直接说："只有一位神，在神和人中间，只有一位中保，乃是降世为人的基督耶稣。"改革宗强调基督的中保职分包含三重职分（munus triplex）：先知（启示父神，约 1:18 "将祂表明出来"）、祭司（自己作羔羊献祭，约 1:29）、君王（拿但业称祂"以色列的王"，约 1:49）。这一章已经把三重职分都宣告出来。罗马天主教传统把马利亚、圣徒列为"中保（mediatrix）"，改革宗坚决反对：基督的中保职分是排他的、足够的、永远的，无人能添加，无人能分享。

**三、（纠正编号）基督作为父神的"解经者"。** 第 18 节希腊文 ἐξηγήσατο（exegete，注释、解释）是一个极有意识的动词。从来没有人见过神，唯有那位在父怀里的独生子，把祂"注释出来"。这是改革宗"圣经唯一最高权威"（sola Scriptura）背后更深的真理：神最完整的自我启示不是文字的圣经，是道成肉身的基督；圣经之所以是最高权威，是因为它无误地见证了那位最高启示者基督。希伯来书 1:1-2 同样说：神古时藉先知多次多方晓谕列祖，末后藉著祂儿子晓谕我们。改革宗的解经原则"以经解经"和"基督中心解经"都源于此：每一节经文最终都指向那位永远的"解经者"。

**五、光与黑暗的二元对立与拣选论。** 第 4 至 5 节"生命在祂里头，这生命就是人的光。光照在黑暗里，黑暗却不接受光。"约翰福音通篇有一组强烈对比：光与暗、真理与谎言、属上的与属下的、信的与不信的。这不是希腊式的二元论（认为善恶是平等的两个原则），是圣经的二元论：**光是绝对的，黑暗只是光的缺席**。但谁能从黑暗进入光？不是凭自己。3 章 19 至 21 节明白说："光来到世间，世人因自己的行为是恶的，不爱光倒爱黑暗。"重生改变人的"爱"，从爱黑暗变成爱光。这正是改革宗"全然败坏"（total depravity）与"不可抗拒的恩典"（irresistible grace）的根据。

## Theology (English)

John 1 is one of the most theologically dense chapters in the entire Bible. Every verse carries multiple core doctrines. Reformed theology recognizes at least five major positions in this chapter.

**1. The Full Deity and Eternal Pre-existence of the Son.** Verse 1, "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." All three verbs in Greek are ἦν (imperfect, "was continuously"), not ἐγένετο (aorist, "came to be"). This is a deliberate grammatical choice: the Word is not created, not begotten at some moment in time. He always was. This verse seals every door against denials of Christ's deity: Arius (Christ as a high creature), Jehovah's Witnesses (Christ as the firstborn angel), Mormonism (the Father and Jesus as separate physical bodies). Reformed theology rigorously holds the Nicene confession "of one substance with the Father" (ὁμοούσιος). The Trinity is not a later ecclesiastical invention. John 1 has already proclaimed it whole.

**2. The Hypostatic Union and the Mystery of the Incarnation.** Verse 14, "the Word became flesh." The Greek σὰρξ ἐγένετο is a stunning juxtaposition: the eternal Λόγος united with corruptible σάρξ. The Council of Chalcedon (AD 451) described this union with four negations: without confusion (against Monophysitism), without change, without division (against Nestorianism), without separation. Christ is one Person in whom complete deity and complete humanity are united. This doctrine is not scholastic hairsplitting. It is the foundation of salvation. He must be true God so that His substitutionary death has infinite merit; He must be true man so that He can lawfully substitute for man. Strip away either side and the gospel collapses. John deliberately uses the coarse word σάρξ to nail shut the door against any Gnostic attempt to "spiritualize" Christ's humanity.

**3. Sola Gratia and Sovereign Regeneration.** Verses 12 to 13 are key texts of Reformed soteriology. "To all who did receive Him, who believed in His name, He gave the right to become children of God, who were born not of blood, nor of the will of the flesh, nor of the will of man, but of God." The order is critical: **receiving and believing are outwardly human responses, but underneath lies "born of God"**. Reformed theology rejects the Arminian ordering that treats "faith" as a man's free choice and God as waiting passively for human decision. John 1:13, together with John 6:44 ("no one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws him") and Romans 9, undergird the doctrine of unconditional election. Regeneration is God's sovereign work; it precedes faith, as cause precedes effect.

**4. Christ as Mediator and the Threefold Office (munus triplex).** Verse 51, "You shall see heaven opened, and the angels of God ascending and descending on the Son of Man," casts the Lord as Jacob's ladder. 1 Timothy 2:5 states it directly: "There is one God, and there is one Mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus." Reformed theology emphasizes that Christ's mediatorial office encompasses three offices (munus triplex): Prophet (revealing the Father, John 1:18, "He has made Him known"), Priest (offering Himself as the Lamb, John 1:29), and King (Nathanael calls Him "the King of Israel," John 1:49). All three offices are already proclaimed in this chapter. Roman Catholic tradition includes Mary and the saints as "mediatrix." Reformed theology firmly rejects this. Christ's mediatorial office is exclusive, sufficient, and eternal. No one can add to it. No one can share it.

**5. Christ as the Father's Exegete.** Verse 18, the Greek verb ἐξηγήσατο (to exegete, to interpret, to make known), is exceptionally deliberate. No one has ever seen God; only the only-begotten Son, who is in the Father's bosom, has "exegeted" Him. This is the deeper truth behind the Reformed principle of sola Scriptura: God's most complete self-revelation is not the written word but the incarnate Christ. The Bible is the supreme authority because it inerrantly testifies to that supreme revealer, Christ. Hebrews 1:1 to 2 parallels this: "God spoke to our fathers by the prophets, but in these last days he has spoken to us by His Son." The Reformed exegetical principles, "Scripture interprets Scripture" and "Christocentric exegesis," both derive from here. Every verse ultimately points to that eternal exegete.

**6. The Light-Darkness Antithesis and Election.** Verses 4 to 5, "In Him was life, and the life was the light of men. The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it." Throughout John's Gospel runs a strong antithesis: light and darkness, truth and falsehood, from above and from below, those who believe and those who do not. This is not Greek dualism (good and evil as two equal principles). This is biblical dualism: **light is absolute, darkness is only the absence of light**. But who can move from darkness into light? Not by oneself. John 3:19 to 21 says plainly, "Light has come into the world, and people loved the darkness rather than the light because their works were evil." Regeneration changes a person's loves, from loving darkness to loving light. This is the foundation of the Reformed doctrines of total depravity and irresistible grace.

## 基督预表（中文）

在新约里，"基督预表"这个字段的语义需要从旧约的"影子"（来 10:1 的 σκιά）转换为"基督的彰显"（西 2:17 "形体却是基督"的 σῶμα）。约翰福音 1 章不是预表基督，是直接彰显基督。但旧约的预表与新约的彰显并非脱节，约翰刻意用旧约的预表语言来揭示基督的本相。

**一、神的羔羊（v. 29，与逾越节、以赛亚 53 章的彰显）。** 施洗约翰指着耶稣说"看哪，神的羔羊"。羔羊是旧约最深远的预表系列：亚伯所献的羊（创 4:4）、亚伯拉罕摩利亚山的公羊（创 22:13）、出埃及前夜抹门楣的羔羊（出 12）、利未记每日早晚的常献祭（民 28:3-4）、以赛亚 53:7 那位"像羊羔被牵到宰杀之地却不开口"的受苦仆人。这一千五百年所有的羔羊，都不是为自己的血而死，是为指向那位"神的羔羊"。改革宗神学坚守基督的死是真实的"挽回祭"（propitiation, ἱλαστήριον，罗 3:25），不是仅仅的"和好"（reconciliation，过于温和）也不是仅仅的"道德感化"（exemplarist 的错谬）。基督的血真实地满足了神公义的要求，担当了选民的罪。约 1:29 的 αἴρω（"除去"）用现在式，意思是这除去是持续有效的。

**二、独生子作为父的解经者（v. 18，与摩西、亚伯拉罕的彰显）。** "从来没有人看见神，只有在父怀里的独生子将祂表明出来。"这一节回应出 33:20"人见我面不能存活"。摩西在西奈山只看见神的背，亚伯拉罕只听见神的声音，以赛亚只看见神的衣裳垂下。但在道成肉身里，整个神性的丰盛有形有体地居住在基督里面（西 2:9）。哥林多后书 4:6 说："那吩咐光从黑暗里照出来的神，已经照在我们心里，叫我们得知神荣耀的光显在耶稣基督的面上。"基督就是父神的"完整解经"。改革宗"基督中心解经法"由此立定：旧约一切关于神的启示，都在基督身上得到完全彰显与解释。

**三、新的会幕与新的伯特利（v. 14, 51，与出埃及记、雅各的彰显）。** 第 14 节 ἐσκήνωσεν（支搭帐幕）直接呼应出埃及记 25 至 40 章的会幕。摩西的会幕是"模型"，基督的肉身是"原型"。出 40:34 "云彩遮盖会幕，耶和华的荣光就充满了帐幕"，到了约 1:14 成了"我们也见过祂的荣光"。同样，第 51 节"天开了，神的使者上去下来在人子身上"，是创 28 章雅各在伯特利所见的天梯。雅各称那地为"伯特利"（神的殿）和"天的门"，但真正的伯特利不是地点，是位格。基督就是那唯一的天梯。改革宗反对天主教的圣徒中保论、马利亚共救赎论：神与人之间的中保只有一位（提前 2:5），不容增减。

**四、真以色列与神的儿子（v. 47, 49，与出埃及记 4 章的彰显）。** 主称拿但业为"真以色列人，他心里是没有诡诈的"，暗示基督自己才是真以色列。"以色列"在创 32 章雅各被神改名时意思是"与神较力的"。整个旧约里，以色列国作为"神的儿子"（出 4:22 "以色列是我的儿子，我的长子"）一次次失败：出埃及后拜金牛犊、旷野悖逆、入迦南拜偶像、被掳归回后还是悖逆。但基督，那位真以色列、真神的儿子，从受洗到旷野受试探到客西马尼到十字架，每一步都完全顺服父神，成就了以色列国未能成就的使命。马太福音 2:15 引用何西阿 11:1 "我从埃及召出我的儿子来"，本指以色列出埃及，但马太说这话应验在基督身上。约翰在 1 章末了用"以色列的王 + 人子"双重称号，宣告基督既是真以色列，也是但以理 7 章那位从天而来要得国度的人子。

**五、改革宗对"基督彰显"的应用。** 旧约一切预表都指向基督，新约一切叙述都彰显基督。这是改革宗解经的核心：圣经不是一本"道德格言集"，也不是"宗教经验录"。圣经是神在历史中救赎选民、最终在基督里成全的整本叙事。每一处旧约的预表，在新约里都不是"被废除"，是"被成全"（πληρόω，太 5:17）。读约翰福音 1 章的弟兄姐妹，要带着旧约的整个救赎历史来读，才能看见这一章的厚度：祂就是创世记 1:1 那位创造的"道"，祂就是出埃及记 12 章的"羔羊"，祂就是创 28 章的"天梯"，祂就是赛 53 章的"受苦仆人"，祂就是但 7 章的"人子"。读约翰 1 章而不带着旧约来读，就只读到一半。

## Christ Shadow (English)

In the New Testament, this field shifts in meaning from the Old Testament's "shadow" (σκιά in Heb 10:1) to the "manifestation of Christ" (Col 2:17, "the substance is Christ," σῶμα). John 1 does not foreshadow Christ. It directly manifests Him. Yet the foreshadowing of the Old Testament and the manifestation of the New are not disconnected. John deliberately uses the language of Old Testament types to disclose Christ's reality.

**1. The Lamb of God (v. 29, the manifestation of the Passover and Isaiah 53).** John the Baptist points to Jesus and says, "Behold, the Lamb of God." The lamb is the most far-reaching typological series in the Old Testament: Abel's offering (Gen 4:4), Abraham's ram on Moriah (Gen 22:13), the Passover lamb whose blood marked the doorposts (Ex 12), the daily morning and evening sacrifice in Leviticus (Num 28:3-4), the Suffering Servant of Isaiah 53:7 "led like a lamb to the slaughter, who opened not His mouth." Fifteen hundred years of lambs did not die for their own blood. They died to point to that one "Lamb of God." Reformed theology firmly holds that Christ's death is true propitiation (ἱλαστήριον, Rom 3:25), not merely reconciliation (too mild) nor merely moral influence (the exemplarist error). Christ's blood truly satisfies the demands of God's justice and bears the sin of the elect. The verb αἴρω ("takes away") in John 1:29 is in the present tense, meaning the bearing-away is continuous and effective.

**2. The Only-Begotten Son as the Father's Exegete (v. 18, the manifestation of Moses and Abraham).** "No one has ever seen God; the only-begotten Son, who is in the Father's bosom, has made Him known." This verse answers Exodus 33:20, "No one can see Me and live." Moses on Sinai saw only God's back. Abraham heard only God's voice. Isaiah saw only the train of God's robe. But in the incarnation, the whole fullness of deity dwells bodily in Christ (Col 2:9). 2 Corinthians 4:6 says, "God, who said, 'Let light shine out of darkness,' has shone in our hearts to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ." Christ is the Father's full exegesis. From this the Reformed Christocentric hermeneutic stands: every Old Testament revelation about God finds its complete manifestation and interpretation in Christ.

**3. The New Tabernacle and the New Bethel (vv. 14, 51, the manifestation of Exodus and Jacob).** Verse 14 uses ἐσκήνωσεν (pitched His tent), directly echoing the tabernacle of Exodus 25 to 40. Moses's tabernacle was the model; Christ's flesh is the prototype. Exodus 40:34, "The cloud covered the tent of meeting, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle," becomes John 1:14, "We have seen His glory." Likewise verse 51, "Heaven opened, and the angels of God ascending and descending on the Son of Man," is Jacob's vision at Bethel in Genesis 28. Jacob called the place "Bethel" (house of God) and "the gate of heaven." But the true Bethel is not a place; it is a Person. Christ is the one true ladder. Reformed theology rejects the Roman Catholic doctrines of saintly mediation and Mary as co-redemptrix. There is one Mediator between God and man (1 Tim 2:5), neither addition nor subtraction allowed.

**4. The True Israel and the Son of God (vv. 47, 49, the manifestation of Exodus 4).** The Lord calls Nathanael "an Israelite indeed, in whom there is no deceit," implying that Christ Himself is the true Israel. "Israel" in Genesis 32, when Jacob is renamed by God, means "one who struggles with God." Throughout the Old Testament, the nation Israel as "God's son" (Ex 4:22, "Israel is my son, my firstborn") fails again and again: the golden calf after the exodus, rebellion in the wilderness, idolatry in Canaan, continued rebellion after the return from exile. But Christ, the true Israel, the true Son of God, from His baptism through the wilderness temptation to Gethsemane to the cross, obeys the Father at every step, accomplishing the mission Israel could not. Matthew 2:15 quotes Hosea 11:1, "Out of Egypt I called my son," which originally referred to Israel coming out of Egypt, but Matthew says this is fulfilled in Christ. John closes chapter 1 with the dual title "King of Israel" plus "Son of Man," declaring that Christ is both the true Israel and the heavenly figure of Daniel 7 who comes to receive the Kingdom.

**5. The Reformed Application of "Christ Manifested."** Every Old Testament foreshadowing points to Christ; every New Testament narrative manifests Christ. This is the heart of Reformed exegesis: the Bible is not a collection of moral aphorisms, nor a record of religious experiences. The Bible is the unified narrative of God redeeming His elect through history, finally consummated in Christ. Every Old Testament type, in the New Testament, is not "abolished" but "fulfilled" (πληρόω, Matt 5:17). The brother or sister reading John 1 must read with the entire Old Testament redemptive history in view, otherwise the chapter loses its depth: He is the creating "Word" of Genesis 1:1, He is the "Lamb" of Exodus 12, He is the "ladder" of Genesis 28, He is the "Suffering Servant" of Isaiah 53, He is the "Son of Man" of Daniel 7. To read John 1 without the Old Testament behind it is to read only half.
