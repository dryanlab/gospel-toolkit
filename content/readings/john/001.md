## 正文（中文）

我是约翰，主所爱的门徒，我将自己所见所闻的记录下来，为要叫你们信耶稣是基督，是神的儿子，并且叫你们信了祂，就可以因祂的名得生命。

太初，在万物被造之前，道就已经存在。这"道"（Λόγος）不是一个抽象的概念，而是有位格的神自己——祂与父神同在，祂就是神。我用"道"这个词，是因为祂是神完美的自我表达，是神旨意的执行者。万物都是藉着祂造的，没有一样不是藉着祂造的。生命在祂里头，这生命就是人的光。

我必须先提到另一个约翰——施洗约翰。他是从神那里差来的，为要作见证，为光作见证。我清楚地记得，很多人以为他就是基督，但他明确地否认了："我不是基督，我是在祂前面预备道路的。"那光是真光，要照亮一切生在世上的人。祂来到自己所造的世界，世界却不认识祂；祂来到以色列，自己的百姓竟不接待祂。

但凡接待祂的，就是信祂名的人，祂就赐他们权柄作神的儿女。这不是靠血统、不是靠人的意志、不是靠肉体的生育，而是从神生的。这是何等的特权！我们这些渔夫、税吏、罪人，竟然可以成为神的儿女！

然后发生了宇宙历史上最震撼的事件——道成了肉身，住在我们中间。"住"这个字（ἐσκήνωσεν）原意是"支搭帐幕"，让我想起旧约中神的荣耀充满会幕的情景。我们真的看见了祂的荣光，那是父独生子的荣光，充充满满地有恩典、有真理。律法是藉摩西传的，但恩典和真理却是由耶稣基督来的。从来没有人看见神，只有在父怀里的独生子将祂表明出来。

施洗约翰继续为主作见证。当祭司和利未人从耶路撒冷来问他："你是谁？"他直截了当地承认："我不是基督。"他们又问："那你是以利亚吗？是那先知吗？"他都否认了。他只说自己是"在旷野有人声喊着说：'修直主的道路！'"——正如先知以赛亚所说的。

次日，约翰看见耶稣来，就说："看哪，神的羔羊，除去世人罪孽的！"这个称呼意义深远——羔羊指向逾越节的羔羊，也指向以赛亚书53章那位代替我们受苦的仆人。约翰见证说，他看见圣灵仿佛鸽子从天降下，住在主的身上。那差他来用水施洗的对他说："你看见圣灵降下来，住在谁的身上，谁就是用圣灵施洗的。"约翰见证这就是神的儿子。

再次日，约翰和他的两个门徒站在那里。当耶稣经过时，约翰注目看祂，说："看哪，这是神的羔羊！"那两个门徒听见他的话，就跟从了耶稣。我就是其中一个。耶稣转过身来，看见我们跟着，就问："你们要什么？"我们说："拉比，在哪里住？"祂说："你们来看。"我永远不会忘记那一天——那是申初（下午四点左右），我们和祂同住了一整个晚上。

另一个门徒是安得烈，他是西门彼得的兄弟。他先找到自己的哥哥西门，对他说："我们遇见弥赛亚了！"（弥赛亚翻出来就是基督）。他领西门去见耶稣，耶稣看着他说："你是约翰的儿子西门，你要称为矶法。"（矶法翻出来就是彼得，意思是"磐石"）

又次日，耶稣想要往加利利去，遇见腓力，就对他说："来跟从我吧！"腓力找着拿但业，对他说："摩西在律法上所写的和众先知所记的那一位，我们遇见了，就是约瑟的儿子拿撒勒人耶稣。"拿但业质疑："拿撒勒还能出什么好的吗？"但当他见到耶稣，主说："看哪，这是个真以色列人，他心里是没有诡诈的。"拿但业惊讶地问："你从哪里知道我呢？"耶稣回答："腓力还没有招呼你，你在无花果树底下，我就看见你了。"拿但业立刻宣告："拉比，你是神的儿子，你是以色列的王！"

耶稣说："因为我说在无花果树底下看见你，你就信吗？你将要看见比这更大的事。"然后祂说了一句话："我实实在在地告诉你们：你们将要看见天开了，神的使者上去下来在人子身上。"这让我想起雅各在伯特利的异象——天梯连接天地。但现在，基督自己就是那天梯，是神与人之间唯一的中保。

这就是起初的日子。道成肉身，住在我们中间，我们跟从了祂。这不是结束，而是开始。

## Content (English)

I am John, the disciple whom Jesus loved, and I record what I have seen and heard, so that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that by believing you may have life in His name.

In the beginning, before all creation, the Word already existed. This "Word" (Λόγος) is not an abstract concept but the personal God Himself—He was with God the Father, and He was God. I use the term "Word" because He is God's perfect self-expression, the executor of God's will. All things were made through Him, and without Him nothing was made that has been made. In Him was life, and that life was the light of men.

I must first mention another John—John the Baptist. He was sent from God as a witness to testify about the light. Many thought he was the Christ, but he clearly denied it: "I am not the Christ; I am the one preparing the way before Him." The true light that gives light to everyone was coming into the world. He came to the world He had made, yet the world did not recognize Him. He came to His own people Israel, and His own did not receive Him.

Yet to all who did receive Him, to those who believed in His name, He gave the right to become children of God—children born not of natural descent, nor of human decision or a husband's will, but born of God. What an extraordinary privilege! We fishermen, tax collectors, and sinners could become children of God!

Then the most astounding event in cosmic history occurred—the Word became flesh and made His dwelling among us. The word "dwelling" (ἐσκήνωσεν) literally means "to tabernacle," reminding me of how God's glory filled the tabernacle in the Old Testament. We truly beheld His glory, the glory of the One and Only who came from the Father, full of grace and truth. The law was given through Moses, but grace and truth came through Jesus Christ. No one has ever seen God, but the One and Only Son, who is Himself God and is in closest relationship with the Father, has made Him known.

John the Baptist continued to testify about the Lord. When priests and Levites came from Jerusalem to ask him, "Who are you?" he confessed frankly, "I am not the Christ." They asked, "Are you Elijah? Are you the Prophet?" He denied both. He simply said he was "a voice of one calling in the wilderness, 'Make straight the way for the Lord'"—as the prophet Isaiah said.

The next day, John saw Jesus coming and said, "Look, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!" This title is profoundly significant—the Lamb points to the Passover lamb and to the suffering Servant of Isaiah 53 who bore our sins. John testified that he saw the Spirit descend like a dove from heaven and remain on Him. The One who sent him to baptize with water had told him, "The one on whom you see the Spirit descend and remain is the one who will baptize with the Holy Spirit." John testified that this was the Son of God.

The next day again, John was standing with two of his disciples. When Jesus passed by, John looked at Him intently and said, "Look, the Lamb of God!" The two disciples heard him and followed Jesus. I was one of them. Jesus turned and saw us following and asked, "What do you want?" We said, "Rabbi, where are you staying?" He said, "Come and see." I will never forget that day—it was about four in the afternoon, and we spent the whole evening with Him.

The other disciple was Andrew, Simon Peter's brother. He first found his own brother Simon and told him, "We have found the Messiah!" (which means Christ). He brought Simon to Jesus, who looked at him and said, "You are Simon son of John. You will be called Cephas" (which means Peter or "rock").

The next day, Jesus decided to go to Galilee. He found Philip and said to him, "Follow me." Philip found Nathanael and told him, "We have found the one Moses wrote about in the Law, and about whom the prophets also wrote—Jesus of Nazareth, the son of Joseph." Nathanael questioned, "Can anything good come from Nazareth?" But when he met Jesus, the Lord said, "Here truly is an Israelite in whom there is no deceit." Nathanael asked in amazement, "How do you know me?" Jesus answered, "Before Philip called you, when you were under the fig tree, I saw you." Nathanael immediately declared, "Rabbi, you are the Son of God; you are the King of Israel!"

Jesus said, "You believe because I told you I saw you under the fig tree? You will see greater things than that." Then He said something remarkable: "Very truly I tell you, you will see heaven open, and the angels of God ascending and descending on the Son of Man." This reminds me of Jacob's vision at Bethel—the ladder connecting heaven and earth. But now, Christ Himself is that ladder, the only mediator between God and humanity.

These were the early days. The Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we followed Him. This was not the end, but the beginning.

## 历史背景（中文）

约翰福音写于主后85-95年间，当时使徒约翰已是高龄，在以弗所牧养教会。这是四福音书中最后完成的一本，约翰已经读过马太、马可和路加的福音书，因此他特意补充了许多前三卷书没有详细记载的内容，特别是主在犹太地的事工和祂深奥的教导。

此时教会面临几个挑战：第一，诺斯底主义开始兴起，否认基督真实的人性，认为物质是邪恶的；第二，有犹太人仍不承认耶稣是弥赛亚；第三，初代信徒渐渐离世，教会需要更深厚的神学根基。约翰福音正是在这样的背景下写成，强调基督的神性与人性都是真实的——祂是"道成肉身"。

第1章的"道"（Λόγος）这个概念，对希腊哲学背景的读者和犹太读者都有深刻意义。希腊哲学用"逻各斯"指宇宙的理性原则，犹太传统则强调神的话语（dabar）有创造和启示的大能（如诗篇33:6）。约翰将这两个传统融合，宣告这位"道"就是成为肉身的基督。

约翰记载门徒跟随主的过程时，特别注明了时间细节（"申初"，下午四点），这是目击证人的记号。改革宗神学强调圣经的可靠性与历史真实性，约翰作为见证人，他的记载不是神话或寓言，而是真实发生的历史事件。

## Historical Context (English)

The Gospel of John was written between AD 85-95, when the apostle John was advanced in age and shepherding the church in Ephesus. This is the last of the four Gospels to be completed. John had already read the Gospels of Matthew, Mark, and Luke, so he deliberately supplemented many details not thoroughly covered in the Synoptic Gospels, especially Jesus's ministry in Judea and His profound teachings.

At this time, the church faced several challenges: First, Gnosticism was emerging, denying the true humanity of Christ and considering matter to be evil; second, many Jews still refused to acknowledge Jesus as the Messiah; third, as the first generation of believers gradually passed away, the church needed a deeper theological foundation. John's Gospel was written in this context, emphasizing that both Christ's divinity and humanity are real—He is "the Word made flesh."

The concept of "Word" (Λόγος) in Chapter 1 carries profound meaning for both Greek philosophical readers and Jewish readers. Greek philosophy used "Logos" to refer to the rational principle of the universe, while Jewish tradition emphasized that God's word (dabar) has creative and revelatory power (e.g., Psalm 33:6). John synthesizes these two traditions, proclaiming that this "Word" is Christ who became flesh.

When John records the process of the disciples following the Lord, he specifically notes time details ("about the tenth hour," four in the afternoon), which is the mark of an eyewitness. Reformed theology emphasizes the reliability and historical truthfulness of Scripture. As a witness, John's account is not myth or allegory but real historical events.

## 经文结构（中文）

第1章可分为四个部分：

**序言：道的本质与道成肉身（1-18节）**
这是整卷福音书的神学基础。约翰从永恒的视角开始："太初有道"，直接呼应创世记1:1"起初神"。道的三重身份：与神同在、就是神、创造万有。接着对比"光与暗"、"接受与拒绝"，最后宣告道成肉身的事实和意义。这18节经文浓缩了整本福音书的核心信息。

**施洗约翰的见证（19-34节）**
分为两段：19-28节记载约翰对祭司和利未人的回答，强调自己不是基督，只是预备道路的声音；29-34节记载约翰看见耶稣时的宣告："神的羔羊"和"用圣灵施洗的"。约翰的见证是整卷福音书的重要主题——我们都是见证人。

**首批门徒的呼召（35-42节）**
记录了三天内主呼召门徒的过程：第一批是安得烈和约翰（作者自己），第二位是彼得，接着是腓力和拿但业。每次呼召都有不同的方式，但结果相同——认出祂是弥赛亚。约翰记录了他们对主的称呼从"拉比"到"神的儿子、以色列的王"的渐进过程。

**天梯的应许（43-51节）**
拿但业的故事和主的预言："你们将要看见天开了，神的使者上去下来在人子身上"。这个应许贯穿整本福音书——基督是神与人之间唯一的中保和通道。

## Structure (English)

Chapter 1 can be divided into four parts:

**Prologue: The Nature of the Word and Incarnation (vv. 1-18)**
This is the theological foundation of the entire Gospel. John begins from an eternal perspective: "In the beginning was the Word," directly echoing Genesis 1:1, "In the beginning God." The threefold identity of the Word: He was with God, He was God, and through Him all things were made. Then comes the contrast of "light and darkness," "acceptance and rejection," and finally the declaration of the fact and meaning of the incarnation. These 18 verses condense the core message of the entire Gospel.

**The Testimony of John the Baptist (vv. 19-34)**
Divided into two sections: verses 19-28 record John's answers to the priests and Levites, emphasizing that he is not the Christ but only a voice preparing the way; verses 29-34 record John's proclamation when he saw Jesus: "the Lamb of God" and "the one who baptizes with the Holy Spirit." John's testimony is a major theme throughout the Gospel—we are all witnesses.

**The Call of the First Disciples (vv. 35-42)**
Records the Lord's calling of disciples over three days: the first were Andrew and John (the author himself), the second was Peter, followed by Philip and Nathanael. Each calling had a different approach, but the same result—they recognized Him as the Messiah. John records the progressive development of their titles for the Lord from "Rabbi" to "Son of God, King of Israel."

**The Promise of the Ladder (vv. 43-51)**
Nathanael's story and the Lord's prophecy: "You will see heaven opened and the angels of God ascending and descending on the Son of Man." This promise runs throughout the entire Gospel—Christ is the only mediator and pathway between God and humanity.

## 神学要点（中文）

**道的神性与先存性：** "太初有道"（ἐν ἀρχῇ ἦν ὁ λόγος）用的是未完成式动词（ἦν），表示持续的存在状态，不是"开始存在"而是"一直存在"。这明确宣告基督的永恒性和神性。改革宗神学强调三位一体中圣子的完全神性，祂不是被造物，而是与父同等、同荣、同永恒的神。

**道成肉身的奥秘：** "道成了肉身"（ὁ λόγος σὰρξ ἐγένετο）是整本圣经最震撼的宣告之一。σάρξ（肉身）强调真实的人性，不是幻影。这驳斥了诺斯底主义，也是基督救赎工作的前提——祂必须成为真实的人，才能作我们的代表和替代者。改革宗教义中的"位格联合"（hypostatic union）教导：基督有完全的神性和完全的人性，在一个位格中联合，互不混淆、不改变、不分离、不分割。

**神拣选的恩典：** 第12-13节强调重生完全是神的工作，不是"从血气生的，不是从情欲生的，也不是从人意生的，乃是从神生的"。这是改革宗"唯独恩典"的核心教导——得救不是人的决定或努力，而是神主权的拣选和重生的工作。

**基督为中保：** 拿但业的故事中，主应许"天梯"的异象，宣告祂自己就是神与人之间的中保。改革宗神学强调基督的中保职分（munus triplex）包括先知、祭司和君王三重职分，祂是唯一的道路、真理、生命，除祂以外别无拯救。

## Theology (English)

**The Deity and Pre-existence of the Word:** "In the beginning was the Word" (ἐν ἀρχῇ ἦν ὁ λόγος) uses the imperfect tense verb (ἦν), indicating a continuous state of existence—not "began to exist" but "always existed." This clearly declares Christ's eternality and deity. Reformed theology emphasizes the full deity of the Son in the Trinity; He is not a created being but God equal, co-glorious, and co-eternal with the Father.

**The Mystery of the Incarnation:** "The Word became flesh" (ὁ λόγος σὰρξ ἐγένετο) is one of the most astounding declarations in the entire Bible. σάρξ (flesh) emphasizes true humanity, not a phantom. This refutes Gnosticism and is the premise of Christ's redemptive work—He had to become truly human to be our representative and substitute. The Reformed doctrine of the "hypostatic union" teaches that Christ has complete deity and complete humanity united in one person, without confusion, change, division, or separation.

**God's Electing Grace:** Verses 12-13 emphasize that regeneration is entirely God's work, "born not of natural descent, nor of human decision or a husband's will, but born of God." This is the core teaching of Reformed "sola gratia"—salvation is not human decision or effort but God's sovereign election and regenerating work.

**Christ as Mediator:** In Nathanael's story, the Lord promises the vision of the "heavenly ladder," declaring that He Himself is the mediator between God and humanity. Reformed theology emphasizes Christ's mediatorial office (munus triplex) includes the threefold office of prophet, priest, and king. He is the only way, truth, and life; there is no salvation apart from Him.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**神的羔羊：** 施洗约翰的宣告"看哪，神的羔羊，除去世人罪孽的"（v.29），将整个旧约献祭系统指向基督。从亚伯的羊羔、亚伯拉罕在摩利亚山的公羊、逾越节的羔羊、到以赛亚书53章的受苦仆人，所有这些都是预表，指向那位真正的"神的羔羊"——基督在十字架上的代赎。改革宗神学强调基督的死是"挽回祭"（propitiation），祂的血真实地除去了神选民的罪，满足了神公义的要求。

**天梯的应许：** 主对拿但业说的话（v.51）直接引用创世记28章雅各的异象。在旧约中，雅各在逃亡途中看见天梯和上帝的使者，他称那地为"伯特利"（神的殿）。但真正的"伯特利"、真正的天梯就是基督自己。祂是神与人之间唯一的通道，是"唯一的中保"（提前2:5）。改革宗传统反对任何其他中保（如马利亚或圣徒），因为唯有基督是那连接天地的天梯。

**道成肉身的羔羊：** 整个第1章将"道"（创造的主）和"羔羊"（献祭的受害者）这两个身份结合在一起。创造万有的道，竟然成为被杀的羔羊——这是福音的核心奥秘。以赛亚书53:7说："他像羊羔被牵到宰杀之地"，预言了这位受苦的仆人。启示录5:6则看见"羔羊站立，像是被杀过的"。从创世记到启示录，整本圣经都见证这位道成肉身的羔羊——祂是配得一切敬拜和荣耀的。

**真以色列人：** 主对拿但业的称赞"这是个真以色列人，他心里是没有诡诈的"（v.47），暗示基督自己就是真以色列。"以色列"意思是"与神较力"，但只有基督完全顺服父神，成就了以色列国作为"神的儿子"（出4:22-23）却未能完成的使命。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**The Lamb of God:** John the Baptist's declaration "Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world" (v. 29) points the entire Old Testament sacrificial system toward Christ. From Abel's lamb, Abraham's ram at Moriah, the Passover lamb, to the Suffering Servant of Isaiah 53, all these are types pointing to the true "Lamb of God"—Christ's substitutionary atonement on the cross. Reformed theology emphasizes that Christ's death is propitiation; His blood truly removes the sins of God's elect and satisfies the demands of God's justice.

**The Promise of the Ladder:** The Lord's words to Nathanael (v. 51) directly reference Jacob's vision in Genesis 28. In the Old Testament, Jacob saw the heavenly ladder and the angels of God during his flight, and he called that place "Bethel" (house of God). But the true "Bethel," the true heavenly ladder is Christ Himself. He is the only pathway between God and humanity, "For there is one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus;" (1 Tim 2:5). Reformed tradition rejects any other mediators (such as Mary or the saints) because only Christ is that ladder connecting heaven and earth.

**The Incarnate Lamb:** Chapter 1 combines these two identities—the "Word" (Creator) and the "Lamb" (sacrificial victim). The Word who created all things became the Lamb to be slain—this is the core mystery of the gospel. Isaiah 53:7 says, "He was led like a lamb to the slaughter," prophesying this Suffering Servant. Revelation 5:6 sees "a Lamb standing, as if slain." From Genesis to Revelation, the entire Bible testifies to this incarnate Lamb—He is worthy of all worship and glory.

**The True Israelite:** The Lord's commendation of Nathanael, "Here truly is an Israelite in whom there is no deceit" (v. 47), hints that Christ Himself is the true Israel. "Israel" means "one who struggles with God," but only Christ fully obeyed the Father, accomplishing the mission that the nation of Israel as "God's son" (Ex 4:22-23) failed to complete.
