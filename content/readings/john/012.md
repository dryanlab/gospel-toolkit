## 正文（中文）

我约翰记得逾越节前六日，主回到伯大尼，就是祂叫拉撒路从死里复活之处。有人在那里给主预备筵席，马大伺候，拉撒路也在那同主坐席的人中。那时发生了一件美事，却也引发了争议。

马利亚拿着一斤极贵的真哪哒香膏，抹主的脚，又用自己的头发去擦，屋里就满了膏的香气。这香膏值三十两银子——相当于一个工人一年的工资！这是何等奢侈的奉献！她不是倒在主头上（虽然那也是尊荣），而是跪下抹主的脚，用头发擦拭。这是极深的谦卑和爱的表达。

犹大立刻质疑："这香膏为什么不卖三十两银子周济穷人呢？"约翰加上注释："他说这话，并不是挂念穷人，乃因他是个贼，又带着钱囊，常取其中所存的。"（12:6）这提醒我们：表面敬虔的话语可能出自假冒的心。犹大后来以三十块钱卖了主——他看重的始终是钱，不是主。

主却为马利亚辩护："由她吧！她是为我安葬之日存留的。因为常有穷人和你们同在，只是你们不常有我。"（12:7-8）主知道自己的时候近了，马利亚这膏抹是为祂的死预备的。她可能不完全明白，但她的爱使她做了最合宜的事。

第二天，许多上来过节的人听见主将到耶路撒冷，就拿着棕树枝出去迎接祂，喊着说："和散那！奉主名来的以色列王是应当称颂的！"主得了一个驴驹骑上，应验了撒迦利亚书9:9的预言："锡安的民哪，不要惧怕！你的王骑着驴驹来了。"

这场面何等荣耀——万众欢呼，夹道迎接，仿佛君王凯旋。但主骑的不是战马，乃是驴驹；祂来不是作征服者，乃是作和平之君。法利赛人彼此说："看哪，你们是徒劳无益，世人都随从祂去了。"他们怕失去权力和地位，却不知道几天后同样的群众会喊"钉祂十字架！"

有几个希腊人（外邦人）上来过节礼拜，他们来见腓力说："先生，我们愿意见耶稣。"主听见了，就说："人子得荣耀的时候到了。"然后祂说出那深刻的比喻："我实实在在地告诉你们，一粒麦子不落在地里死了，仍旧是一粒，若是死了，就结出许多子粒来。"（12:24）

弟兄姊妹，这是福音的核心！基督必须死，好使许多人得生命。祂是那一粒麦子，落在地里死了，结出无数子粒——我们这些因祂得救的人。祂不是被迫牺牲，乃是心甘情愿："我现在心里忧愁，我说什么才好呢？父啊，救我脱离这时候；但我原是为这时候来的。父啊，愿你荣耀你的名！"（12:27-28）

天上有声音回应："我已经荣耀了我的名，还要再荣耀。"众人听见，有的说打雷，有的说有天使对祂说话。主说："这声音不是为我，是为你们来的。现在这世界受审判，这世界的王要被赶出去。我若从地上被举起来，就要吸引万人来归我。"（12:30-32）"被举起"既指被钉十字架，也指被高举得荣耀——十架是祂得荣耀的道路。

但以色列人不明白。虽然主在他们面前行了许多神迹，他们还是不信祂。这应验了以赛亚的预言："主啊，我们所传的有谁信呢？主的膀臂向谁显露呢？"（赛53:1）又说："主叫他们瞎了眼，硬了心，免得他们眼睛看见，心里明白，回转过来，我就医治他们。"（赛6:10）

约翰解释："以赛亚因为看见他的荣耀，就指着祂说这话。"（12:41）以赛亚在异象中看见坐宝座的主（赛6章），那位就是道成肉身之前的基督！

虽然如此，官长中却有好些信祂的，只因法利赛人的缘故，就不承认，恐怕被赶出会堂。"这是因他们爱人的荣耀过于爱神的荣耀。"（12:43）这是何等可悲的光景——明明知道真理，却因怕人而不认。

主大声说："信我的，不是信我，乃是信那差我来的。人看见我，就是看见那差我来的。我到世上来，乃是光，叫凡信我的，不住在黑暗里。"（12:44-46）这是祂公开传道的最后宣告——祂是光，是父的启示，是通向永生的唯一道路。

弟兄姊妹，第12章是转折点——从加利利的传道到耶路撒冷的受难，从群众的欢呼到十架的羞辱。但主知道祂的使命："我原是为这时候来的。"十架不是意外，乃是计划；不是失败，乃是得荣耀。一粒麦子死了，结出许多子粒——这就是福音。

## Content (English)

I, John, remember six days before Passover, the Lord returned to Bethany where He had raised Lazarus from the dead. Someone prepared a dinner there for the Lord, with Martha serving and Lazarus among those reclining with Him. Then a beautiful act occurred, yet also sparked controversy.

Mary took a pound of expensive ointment of pure nard and anointed the Lord's feet and wiped His feet with her hair, and the house was filled with the fragrance of the perfume. This ointment was worth three hundred denarii—equivalent to a year's wages for a worker! What an extravagant offering! She did not pour it on His head (though that would also be honor), but knelt to anoint His feet and wiped them with her hair. This was an expression of deep humility and love.

Judas immediately questioned: "Why was this ointment not sold for three hundred denarii and given to the poor?" John adds this note: "He said this, not because he cared about the poor, but because he was a thief, and having charge of the moneybag he used to help himself to what was put into it." (12:6) This reminds us: outwardly pious words may come from a hypocritical heart. Judas later sold the Lord for thirty pieces of silver—he always valued money, not the Lord.

But the Lord defended Mary: "Leave her alone, so that she may keep it for the day of my burial. For the poor you always have with you, but you do not always have me." (12:7-8) The Lord knew His time was near; Mary's anointing prepared for His death. She may not have fully understood, but her love led her to do the most appropriate thing.

The next day, many who had come for the feast heard the Lord was coming to Jerusalem. They took palm branches and went out to meet Him, crying out: "Hosanna! Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord, even the King of Israel!" The Lord found a young donkey and sat on it, fulfilling the prophecy of Zechariah 9:9: "Fear not, daughter of Zion; behold, your king is coming, sitting on a donkey's colt!"

What a glorious scene—crowds cheering, lining the way, as if a king triumphant. But the Lord rode not a warhorse but a donkey's colt; He came not as conqueror but as Prince of Peace. The Pharisees said to one another, "You see that you are gaining nothing. Look, the world has gone after him." They feared losing power and status, not knowing in a few days the same crowds would shout "Crucify him!"

Some Greeks (Gentiles) came up to worship at the feast. They came to Philip saying, "Sir, we wish to see Jesus." The Lord heard and said: "The hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified." Then He spoke that profound parable: "Truly, truly, I say to you, unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit." (12:24)

Brothers and sisters, this is the core of the gospel! Christ must die so that many may have life. He is that grain of wheat, fallen into the earth and died, producing countless grains—we who are saved through Him. He was not forced to sacrifice but willing: "Now is my soul troubled. And what shall I say? 'Father, save me from this hour'? But for this purpose I have come to this hour. Father, glorify your name!" (12:27-28)

A voice from heaven responded: "I have glorified it, and I will glorify it again." The crowd heard; some said it thundered, others said an angel spoke to Him. The Lord said: "This voice has come for your sake, not mine. Now is the judgment of this world; now will the ruler of this world be cast out. And I, when I am lifted up from the earth, will draw all people to myself." (12:30-32) "Lifted up" refers both to crucifixion and exaltation to glory—the cross is His path to glory.

But Israel did not understand. Though the Lord performed many signs before them, they still did not believe. This fulfilled Isaiah's prophecy: "Who hath believed our report? and to whom is the arm of the LORD revealed?" (Isa 53:1) And: "Make the heart of this people fat, and make their ears heavy, and shut their eyes; lest they see with their eyes, and hear with their ears, and understand with their heart, and convert, and be healed." (Isa 6:10)

John explains: "Isaiah said these things because he saw his glory and spoke of him." (12:41) Isaiah in his vision saw the Lord seated on the throne (Isa 6)—that was Christ before incarnation!

Nevertheless, many even of the authorities believed in Him, but for fear of the Pharisees they did not confess it, so that they would not be put out of the synagogue. "For they loved the glory that comes from man more than the glory that comes from God." (12:43) What a tragic state—knowing the truth yet not confessing for fear of man.

The Lord cried out: "Whoever believes in me, believes not in me but in him who sent me. And whoever sees me sees him who sent me. I have come into the world as light, so that whoever believes in me may not remain in darkness." (12:44-46) This is His final public proclamation—He is light, the Father's revelation, the only way to eternal life.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 12 is the turning point—from Galilean ministry to Jerusalem's passion, from crowds' cheers to the cross's shame. But the Lord knew His mission: "For this purpose I have come to this hour." The cross was not accident but plan; not failure but glorification. A grain of wheat died and bore much fruit—this is the gospel.

## 历史背景（中文）

"逾越节前六日"（12:1）推算，若主在星期五被钉十字架，这膏抹发生在星期六日落后（犹太人的新一天从日落开始）。马大伺候、拉撒路同席、马利亚膏主——这一家三人都在场，显明他们对主深厚的爱和接待。

真哪哒香膏产自印度，极其昂贵。三十两银子（三百得拿利）相当于一个普通工人一年的工资。马可福音14:3说这玉瓶是"打破"的，意味着整瓶倾倒，无保留地献上。这可能是马利亚一家最贵重的财产——她为主倾尽所有。

棕树枝在犹太文化中象征胜利和节日欢庆（利23:40，尼8:15）。马加比起义后，犹太人用棕枝庆祝收复圣殿（主前164年）。群众用棕枝迎接主，显明他们期待祂作政治性的弥赛亚——推翻罗马、复兴以色列国。但主骑驴进城（而非战马），显明祂来建立的是属灵的国度，不是地上的政权。

撒迦利亚书9:9"看哪，你的王来到你这里！他是公义的，并且施行拯救，谦谦和和地骑着驴，就是骑着驴的驹子。"这预言弥赛亚是和平之君，不是军事征服者。主刻意应验这预言，宣告祂是那位要来的王，却是以和平、谦卑的方式来。

"希腊人"（12:20）指说希腊语的外邦人，可能是"敬畏神的人"（God-fearers）——虽非正式归信犹太教，却敬拜独一真神、参加犹太节期的外邦人。他们来"愿意见耶稣"，预示福音将传向外邦（徒1:8"直到地极"）。

## Historical Context (English)

"Six days before Passover" (12:1) calculates that if the Lord was crucified on Friday, this anointing occurred after sunset on Saturday (a new Jewish day begins at sunset). Martha serving, Lazarus reclining, Mary anointing—all three siblings were present, showing their deep love and hospitality toward the Lord.

Pure nard ointment came from India, extremely expensive. Three hundred denarii was equivalent to a year's wages for an ordinary worker. Mark 14:3 says the alabaster flask was "broken," meaning the entire bottle was poured out, offered without reserve. This was likely the family's most valuable possession—Mary gave her all for the Lord.

Palm branches in Jewish culture symbolized victory and festival celebration (Lev 23:40, Neh 8:15). After the Maccabean revolt, Jews celebrated the temple's recapture (164 BC) with palm branches. The crowd welcoming the Lord with palm branches showed they expected Him to be a political Messiah—overthrowing Rome, restoring Israel's kingdom. But the Lord's entry on a donkey (not a warhorse) showed He came to establish a spiritual kingdom, not an earthly regime.

Zechariah 9:9 "Behold, your king is coming to you; righteous and having salvation is he, humble and mounted on a donkey, on a colt, the foal of a donkey." This prophesied the Messiah as Prince of Peace, not a military conqueror. The Lord deliberately fulfilled this prophecy, proclaiming He is the coming King, yet in a peaceful, humble manner.

"Greeks" (12:20) refers to Greek-speaking Gentiles, possibly "God-fearers"—though not formally converted to Judaism, they worshiped the one true God and participated in Jewish festivals. Their coming to "see Jesus" foreshadows the gospel spreading to the Gentiles (Acts 1:8 "to the end of the earth").

## 经文结构（中文）

**一、马利亚的膏抹（12:1-11）** — 逾越节前六日，伯大尼的筵席。马利亚用极贵真哪哒香膏抹主的脚，用头发擦。犹大质疑"为何不卖周济穷人"（实则因他是贼）。主说"由她吧，她是为我安葬之日存留的"。许多犹太人来看拉撒路，祭司长商议连拉撒路也要杀了（因许多人为他的缘故信了主）。

**二、荣耀进圣城（12:12-19）** — 第二天，群众拿棕树枝迎接主，喊"和散那！奉主名来的以色列王是应当称颂的！"主骑驴驹进城，应验撒迦利亚书9:9。门徒起先不明白，等主得荣耀后才想起。法利赛人说"世人都随从祂去了"。

**三、一粒麦子的比喻（12:20-36）** — 希腊人要见主。主说"人子得荣耀的时候到了"，然后讲"一粒麦子"的比喻——若不死就仍是一粒，若死了就结出许多子粒。"爱惜生命的就失丧生命，恨恶生命的就保守生命到永生。"主宣告"我现在心里忧愁...父啊，愿你荣耀你的名！"天上有声音回应："我已经荣耀了，还要再荣耀。"主说："我若从地上被举起来，就要吸引万人来归我。"——这指祂将被钉十字架。

**四、不信的悲剧（12:37-43）** — 主行了许多神迹，他们还是不信，应验以赛亚书53:1和6:10。以赛亚看见主的荣耀（赛6章），就指着祂说这话。虽然如此，官长中有好些信祂的，但因怕被赶出会堂就不承认——"他们爱人的荣耀过于爱神的荣耀"。

**五、最后的宣告（12:44-50）** — 主大声说："信我的，不是信我，乃是信那差我来的。人看见我，就是看见那差我来的。我到世上来，乃是光，叫凡信我的不住在黑暗里。"祂不定人的罪（第一次来是要拯救），但弃绝祂的话的人，在末日有审判他的——就是主所讲的道。"我没有凭着自己讲，惟有差我来的父已经给我命令，叫我说什么，讲什么...我所讲的话正是照着父对我所说的。"

全章从膏抹（为死预备）到进城（荣耀的顶峰），再到宣告死的必要性（一粒麦子），最后是不信与信的对比。主题清晰：十架是荣耀的道路，死亡是生命的源头。

## Structure (English)

**I. Mary's Anointing (12:1-11)** — Six days before Passover, dinner at Bethany. Mary anointed the Lord's feet with expensive pure nard, wiped with her hair. Judas questioned "why not sell and give to the poor" (actually because he was a thief). The Lord said "Leave her alone, so that she may keep it for the day of my burial." Many Jews came to see Lazarus; chief priests plotted to kill Lazarus too (because many believed in the Lord on account of him).

**II. Triumphal Entry (12:12-19)** — The next day, crowds took palm branches to welcome the Lord, shouting "Hosanna! Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord, even the King of Israel!" The Lord rode a donkey's colt into the city, fulfilling Zechariah 9:9. Disciples did not understand at first; after the Lord was glorified they remembered. Pharisees said "the world has gone after him."

**III. Parable of the Grain of Wheat (12:20-36)** — Greeks wanted to see the Lord. The Lord said "The hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified," then spoke the parable of "a grain of wheat"—if it does not die it remains alone, if it dies it bears much fruit. "Whoever loves his life loses it, and whoever hates his life in this world will keep it for eternal life." The Lord proclaimed "Now is my soul troubled... Father, glorify your name!" A voice from heaven responded: "I have glorified it, and I will glorify it again." The Lord said: "And I, when I am lifted up from the earth, will draw all people to myself."—This refers to His crucifixion.

**IV. Tragedy of Unbelief (12:37-43)** — Though He performed many signs, they still did not believe, fulfilling Isaiah 53:1 and 6:10. Isaiah saw the Lord's glory (Isa 6) and spoke of Him. Nevertheless, many even of the authorities believed in Him, but for fear of being put out of the synagogue did not confess—"for they loved the glory that comes from man more than the glory that comes from God."

**V. Final Proclamation (12:44-50)** — The Lord cried out: "Whoever believes in me, believes not in me but in him who sent me. And whoever sees me sees him who sent me. I have come into the world as light, so that whoever believes in me may not remain in darkness." He does not judge (first coming is to save), but those who reject His word have a judge on the last day—the word He has spoken. "For I have not spoken on my own authority, but the Father who sent me has himself given me a commandment—what to say and what to speak... What I say, therefore, I say as the Father has told me."

The chapter moves from anointing (preparation for death) to entry (pinnacle of glory), to declaration of death's necessity (grain of wheat), finally contrasting unbelief and belief. Clear theme: the cross is the path to glory, death is the source of life.

## 神学要点（中文）

**基督的受难——主动的顺服：** "我现在心里忧愁，我说什么才好呢？父啊，救我脱离这时候；但我原是为这时候来的。"（12:27）这显明主的受苦不是被迫，乃是心甘情愿。祂有能力逃避十架（太26:53"你想我不能求我父...差遣十二营多天使来吗？"），但祂选择顺服。改革宗神学强调：基督的顺服包括主动的顺服（active obedience，一生守全律法）和被动的顺服（passive obedience，十架上承受刑罚）。两者都是我们称义的根基。

**代赎的必然性——一粒麦子：** "一粒麦子不落在地里死了，仍旧是一粒，若是死了，就结出许多子粒来。"（12:24）这比喻揭示救赎的逻辑：基督必须死，因为只有透过祂的死，多人才能得生。改革宗坚持：救赎不是备用方案，乃是永恒计划（弗1:4"创立世界以前，在基督里拣选了我们"）。十架不是神失败后的补救，乃是神智慧的高峰。

**三一神的荣耀：** 主祷告"父啊，愿你荣耀你的名"，父回应"我已经荣耀了，还要再荣耀"（12:28）。父在子的受难中得荣耀——这不是说神需要荣耀（祂自足完全），乃是说神的属性（公义、慈爱、智慧、权能）在十架上完全彰显。改革宗强调：神的终极目的是祂自己的荣耀（soli Deo gloria），救赎也是为神的荣耀，不仅为人的益处。

**撒但的失败：** "现在这世界受审判，这世界的王要被赶出去。"（12:31）基督的死看似失败，实则是撒但的彻底失败。十架上，基督解除掌死权的魔鬼的权势（来2:14），掳掠了仇敌（西2:15）。改革宗末世论强调：基督已经得胜（already），最终得胜将在末日完全显明（not yet）。撒但已被判决，只是等候执行。

**拣选与硬心：** 以赛亚书6:10"主叫他们瞎了眼，硬了心"——这是神主权拣选的另一面。神不仅拣选一些人得救（积极的拣选），也任凭一些人硬心（消极的遗弃）。改革宗强调：神的遗弃（reprobation）不是主动使人犯罪，乃是撤回恩典、任凭人在罪中。祂不欠任何人救恩；祂拯救一些人是恩典，任凭一些人是公义。

## Theology (English)

**Christ's Passion—Active Obedience:** "Now is my soul troubled. And what shall I say? 'Father, save me from this hour'? But for this purpose I have come to this hour." (12:27) This shows the Lord's suffering was not forced but willing. He had power to avoid the cross (Matt 26:53 "Do you think that I cannot appeal to my Father... and he will send me more than twelve legions of angels?"), but He chose obedience. Reformed theology emphasizes: Christ's obedience includes active obedience (lifelong perfect law-keeping) and passive obedience (bearing punishment on the cross). Both are the foundation of our justification.

**Necessity of Substitution—Grain of Wheat:** "Unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit." (12:24) This parable reveals redemption's logic: Christ must die, because only through His death can many have life. Reformed theology insists: redemption is not a backup plan but an eternal plan (Eph 1:4 "he chose us in him before the foundation of the world"). The cross is not God's remedy after failure but the pinnacle of God's wisdom.

**Glory of the Triune God:** The Lord prayed "Father, glorify your name," the Father responded "I have glorified it, and I will glorify it again" (12:28). The Father is glorified in the Son's passion—not that God needs glory (He is self-sufficient and perfect), but that God's attributes (justice, love, wisdom, power) are fully displayed on the cross. Reformed theology emphasizes: God's ultimate purpose is His own glory (soli Deo gloria); redemption is also for God's glory, not merely for human benefit.

**Satan's Defeat:** "Now is the judgment of this world; now will the ruler of this world be cast out." (12:31) Christ's death seemed like defeat but was actually Satan's total defeat. On the cross, Christ destroyed the one who has the power of death, the devil (Heb 2:14), disarmed the enemy (Col 2:15). Reformed eschatology emphasizes: Christ has already won (already), final victory will be fully revealed on the last day (not yet). Satan is already sentenced, only awaiting execution.

**Election and Hardening:** Isaiah 6:10 "He has blinded their eyes and hardened their heart"—this is the other side of God's sovereign election. God not only elects some to salvation (positive election) but also permits some to harden (negative reprobation). Reformed theology emphasizes: God's reprobation is not actively causing sin but withdrawing grace, permitting people in sin. He owes no one salvation; saving some is grace, leaving some is justice.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**逾越节羔羊：** "逾越节前六日"（12:1）——整章发生在逾越节前夕。逾越节羔羊要在尼散月14日黄昏被杀（出12:6），主也在逾越节那天被钉十架（约19:14"那日是预备逾越节的日子"）。祂是真正的逾越节羔羊（林前5:7"我们逾越节的羔羊基督已经被杀献祭了"），祂的血使信祂的人免于神的忿怒。

**膏抹君王与祭司：** 旧约君王、祭司、先知受膏时，用橄榄油或香膏倒在头上（撒上10:1,16:13；出29:7）。马利亚膏主，预表祂是受膏者（Messiah / Christ）——是君王（骑驴进城）、是祭司（为百姓献祭）、是先知（宣告神的话）。但她膏的是主的脚，为安葬预备，预示这位君王要先经历死亡。

**撒迦利亚书9:9的应验：** "锡安的民哪，应当大大喜乐...看哪，你的王来到你这里！他是公义的，并且施行拯救，谦谦和和地骑着驴。"这预言弥赛亚来临的方式。主骑驴进城，不是偶然，乃是刻意应验预言，宣告祂是那位要来的王。但这王不是骑战马征服仇敌，乃是骑驴来拯救百姓——透过自己的死。

**一粒麦子与复活初熟的果子：** "一粒麦子...落在地里死了，就结出许多子粒来"——这预表基督的死和复活。林前15:20"基督已经从死里复活，成为睡了之人初熟的果子。"基督是第一粒复活的麦子，我们是随后的收成。罗马书8:29"预先所知道的人，就预先定下效法他儿子的模样，使他儿子在许多弟兄中作长子。"

**被举起的铜蛇：** "我若从地上被举起来，就要吸引万人来归我"（12:32）——呼应约翰福音3:14"摩西在旷野怎样举蛇，人子也必照样被举起来。"铜蛇被举在杆上，以色列人仰望就得医治（民21:9）；基督被举在十架上，世人仰望祂就得救。"被举起"有双重含义：钉十架（羞辱）与升天得荣耀（高举）——这两者是一体的。

**以赛亚看见基督的荣耀：** 约翰说"以赛亚因为看见祂的荣耀，就指着祂说这话"（12:41），指的是以赛亚书6章异象——以赛亚看见主坐在高高的宝座上，撒拉弗呼喊"圣哉！圣哉！圣哉！"。那位荣耀的主就是道成肉身之前的基督。这证明旧约中的"耶和华显现"（theophanies）常是道成肉身之前的子神（pre-incarnate Son）向人显现。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Passover Lamb:** "Six days before Passover" (12:1)—the entire chapter occurs on the eve of Passover. The Passover lamb was to be killed on the 14th of Nisan at twilight (Exod 12:6); the Lord was also crucified on Passover day (John 19:14 "Now it was the day of Preparation of the Passover"). He is the true Passover Lamb (1 Cor 5:7 "For Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed"), His blood sparing believers from God's wrath.

**Anointing King and Priest:** In the Old Testament, kings, priests, and prophets were anointed with olive oil or ointment poured on their heads (1 Sam 10:1, 16:13; Exod 29:7). Mary's anointing of the Lord prefigures Him as the Anointed One (Messiah / Christ)—King (riding into the city), Priest (offering sacrifice for the people), Prophet (proclaiming God's word). But she anointed His feet, preparing for burial, foreshadowing this King would first experience death.

**Fulfillment of Zechariah 9:9:** "Rejoice greatly, O daughter of Zion!... Behold, your king is coming to you; righteous and having salvation is he, humble and mounted on a donkey." This prophesied the Messiah's manner of coming. The Lord's entry on a donkey was not accidental but deliberate fulfillment of prophecy, proclaiming He is the coming King. But this King rides not a warhorse to conquer enemies but a donkey to save His people—through His own death.

**Grain of Wheat and Firstfruits of Resurrection:** "Unless a grain of wheat... falls into the earth and dies, it bears much fruit"—this prefigures Christ's death and resurrection. 1 Cor 15:20 "But in fact Christ has been raised from the dead, the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep." Christ is the first grain of resurrection, we are the subsequent harvest. Romans 8:29 "For those whom he foreknew he also predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son, in order that he might be the firstborn among many brothers."

**Lifted-Up Bronze Serpent:** "And I, when I am lifted up from the earth, will draw all people to myself" (12:32)—echoes John 3:14 "And as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up." The bronze serpent was lifted on a pole, Israelites looked and were healed (Num 21:9); Christ was lifted on the cross, people of the world look to Him and are saved. "Lifted up" has double meaning: crucifixion (shame) and ascension to glory (exaltation)—these two are one.

**Isaiah Saw Christ's Glory:** John says "Isaiah said these things because he saw his glory and spoke of him" (12:41), referring to the Isaiah 6 vision—Isaiah saw the Lord seated on a high throne, seraphim calling "Holy, holy, holy!". That glorious Lord was the pre-incarnate Christ. This proves Old Testament "theophanies" (appearances of Yahweh) were often the pre-incarnate Son appearing to humanity.
