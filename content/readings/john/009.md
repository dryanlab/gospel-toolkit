## 正文（中文）

我约翰见证了主行的许多神迹，但第9章这个生来瞎眼之人得医治的神迹，却特别深刻地揭示了人心的盲目与刚硬。

那人生来瞎眼。门徒问主："拉比，这人生来是瞎眼的，是谁犯了罪？是这人呢？是他父母呢？"弟兄姐妹，这是当时普遍的观念——苦难必定是罪的刑罚。但主的回答打破了这种简单的因果论："也不是这人犯了罪，也不是他父母犯了罪，是要在他身上显出神的作为来。"弟兄姐妹，苦难不总是某个具体罪的后果——有时苦难是神荣耀自己的舞台。弟兄姐妹，当你看见别人的苦难——不要急着问"谁的错"——而要问"神要在这里做什么"。

主接着说了一句话："趁着白日，我们必须做那差我来者的工；黑夜将到，就没有人能做工了。我在世上的时候，是世界的光。"弟兄姐妹，"趁着白日"——主知道祂在世的时间有限——祂要抓紧每一刻做父的工。弟兄姐妹，你的"白日"也是有限的——趁着还有今天——做你该做的。

主吐唾沫在地上，和泥抹在瞎子眼睛上，叫他往西罗亚池子去洗。弟兄姐妹，为什么用这种方式？主可以只说一句话就医治他——但祂选择了和泥抹眼睛。弟兄姐妹，创世记里神用泥土造了亚当——这里耶稣也用泥重新"创造"了瞎子的眼睛。弟兄姐妹，这是"新创造"的象征——基督不只是修复——祂是重新创造。那人去一洗，回头就看见了！

但这事发生在安息日，于是引发了一场激烈的冲突。法利赛人坚持："这个人不是从神来的，因为他不守安息日。"弟兄姐妹，他们宁愿否认神迹的事实——也要坚持他们对律法的狭隘理解。弟兄姐妹，当神学体系与活生生的神迹冲突——人往往选择维护体系而否认神的作为。弟兄姐妹，这是何等的悲剧——为了守规矩而拒绝规矩的制定者。

那得医治的人却用最朴实的逻辑反驳："从创世以来，未曾听见有人把生来是瞎子的眼睛开了。这人若不是从神来的，什么也不能做。"弟兄姐妹，这简单的见证胜过一切诡辩——果子证明树！一个没有学问的乞丐——竟然在辩论中胜过了受过专业训练的法利赛人。弟兄姐妹，亲身经历过恩典的人——他的见证是无法反驳的。

但法利赛人不能接受这见证，就把他赶出会堂。弟兄姐妹，"赶出会堂"在当时意味着社会性的死亡——被排斥在犹太社区之外。弟兄姐妹，这个人为了真理付出了巨大的代价。但主听说了，就亲自来找他——问："你信神的儿子吗？"那人说："主啊，谁是神的儿子，叫我信他呢？"主说："你已经看见他，现在和你说话的就是他。"那人说："主啊，我信！"就拜主。

弟兄姐妹，当世界把你赶出去——基督亲自来找你。当你为真理失去一切——基督把祂自己给你。这个人失去了会堂的席位——却得到了宇宙之主亲自的启示和同在。弟兄姐妹，这是何等的交换。

主随即说出一句令人震撼的话："我为审判到这世上来，叫不能看见的可以看见；能看见的反瞎了眼。"法利赛人问："难道我们也瞎了眼吗？"主回答："你们若瞎了眼，就没有罪了；但如今你们说'我们能看见'，所以你们的罪还在。"

弟兄姐妹，这章的讽刺何等深刻！生来瞎眼的人得了医治——不仅肉眼看见——灵眼也被开启——认出主是神的儿子就敬拜祂。但那些自称"能看见"的法利赛人——律法的教师、神话语的守护者——却因骄傲和刚硬——看不见站在他们面前的就是神自己。弟兄姐妹，罪人最大的危险不是知道自己瞎了——而是自以为能看见。

## Content (English)

I, John, witnessed many miracles performed by the Lord, but the healing of the man born blind in chapter 9 especially profoundly reveals the blindness and hardness of human hearts.

The man was born blind. The disciples asked the Lord: "Rabbi, who sinned, this man or his parents, that he was born blind?" This was the common belief of the time—suffering must be punishment for sin. But the Lord's answer shattered this simple causality: "It was not that this man sinned, or his parents, but that the works of God might be displayed in him." Suffering is not always the consequence of a specific sin; sometimes it is the stage for God to glorify Himself.

The Lord spat on the ground, made mud with the saliva, and anointed the blind man's eyes, telling him to wash in the pool of Siloam. The man went, washed, and came back seeing! What a wonderful miracle—not just physical sight, but a prefiguration of spiritual sight. But this happened on the Sabbath, triggering a fierce conflict.

The Pharisees insisted: "This man is not from God, for he does not keep the Sabbath." They would rather deny the miracle's reality than abandon their narrow interpretation of the law. The healed man, however, used the simplest logic to refute them: "Never since the world began has it been heard that anyone opened the eyes of a man born blind. If this man were not from God, he could do nothing." This simple testimony triumphs over all sophistry—the fruit proves the tree!

But the Pharisees could not accept this testimony, so they cast him out of the synagogue. The Lord heard of it, found him, and asked: "Do you believe in the Son of Man?" The man said, "Lord, who is he, that I may believe in him?" The Lord said, "You have seen him, and it is he who is speaking to you." The man said, "Lord, I believe!" and worshiped Him.

The Lord then spoke a shocking statement: "For judgment I came into this world, that those who do not see may see, and those who see may become blind." Those Pharisees asked, "Are we also blind?" The Lord answered: "If you were blind, you would have no guilt; but now that you say, 'We see,' your guilt remains."

Brothers and sisters, how profound the irony of this chapter! The man born blind was healed, not only gaining physical sight but also spiritual vision, recognizing the Lord as the Son of God and worshiping Him. But those Pharisees who claimed to "see"—teachers of the law, guardians of God's Word—because of pride and hardness, could not see that standing before them was God Himself. They studied Scripture yet did not recognize the One Scripture testified to; they kept the law yet violated its essence—loving God and neighbor. Though physically sighted, they were spiritually totally blind.

This is the mystery of the gospel: God chooses the weak, foolish, and lowly of the world to shame those who consider themselves strong, wise, and noble. The blind man had nothing, could only cry for mercy; the Pharisees had status, learning, religious tradition, yet rejected grace. The greatest danger for sinners is not knowing they are blind, but thinking they can see.

## 历史背景（中文）

第9章的背景仍在住棚节期间或刚过后（7:2提到节期，9:1"耶稣过去的时候"延续叙事）。西罗亚池在耶路撒冷城南，是希西家王时期修建的蓄水池（王下20:20），用于供应城内用水。约翰解释"西罗亚翻出来就是奉差遣"（9:7），暗示基督被父差遣而来的身份。

会堂在第二圣殿时期是犹太社会的核心机构，不仅是敬拜场所，也是教育、司法、社区生活的中心。"赶出会堂"（9:22,34）是极其严重的惩罚——意味着与犹太群体隔绝，失去社会身份、经济来往、宗教生活的一切权利。这显示公会（Sanhedrin）的权力和对耶稣追随者的逼迫已经制度化。

安息日的争论反映了当时两大拉比学派的分歧。严格派（Shammai学派）禁止安息日的一切"工作"，包括医病；自由派（Hillel学派）相对宽松，但仍有界限。耶稣的医治挑战了他们对安息日的整个神学理解——安息日是为人设立的（可2:27），神的怜悯不受人为规条限制。

这一章也是约翰福音"渐进启示"手法的典范：那得医治的人对耶稣身份的认识逐步加深——从"那人名叫耶稣"（9:11），到"是个先知"（9:17），到"从神来的"（9:33），最后认信"主啊，我信"并敬拜祂（9:38）。这反映真门徒认识基督的过程。

## Historical Context (English)

Chapter 9's background remains during or shortly after the Feast of Tabernacles (7:2 mentions the feast; 9:1 "as he passed by" continues the narrative). The Pool of Siloam was in southern Jerusalem, a reservoir built during King Hezekiah's time (2 Kgs 20:20) to supply water to the city. John explains "Siloam (which means Sent)" (9:7), hinting at Christ's identity as sent by the Father.

The synagogue in the Second Temple period was a core institution of Jewish society—not only a place of worship, but also a center for education, justice, and community life. "Cast out of the synagogue" (9:22,34) was an extremely severe punishment—meaning isolation from the Jewish community, loss of social identity, economic intercourse, and all rights to religious life. This shows the Sanhedrin's power and the institutionalized persecution of Jesus' followers.

The Sabbath controversy reflects the disagreement between two major rabbinic schools of the time. The strict school (School of Shammai) prohibited all "work" on the Sabbath, including healing; the liberal school (School of Hillel) was relatively lenient but still had boundaries. Jesus' healing challenged their entire theological understanding of the Sabbath—the Sabbath was made for man (Mark 2:27), and God's mercy is not limited by human regulations.

This chapter is also an exemplar of John's Gospel's "progressive revelation" technique: the healed man's understanding of Jesus' identity deepens gradually—from "the man called Jesus" (9:11), to "a prophet" (9:17), to "from God" (9:33), and finally confessing "Lord, I believe" and worshiping Him (9:38). This reflects the process of true disciples coming to know Christ.

## 经文结构（中文）

**一、神迹的施行（9:1-7）** — 生来瞎眼之人，门徒的神学问题（罪与苦难），耶稣的回答（为显神的荣耀），医治的方式（和泥、抹眼、去洗），神迹的发生（得看见）。

**二、群众的困惑（9:8-12）** — 邻舍认不出他（"是他""不是"的争论），那人的见证（"有一个人名叫耶稣...我去一洗，就看见了"），简单但有力。

**三、宗教审问（9:13-34）** — 这一段是全章核心，显示真理与虚伪的对抗：
- **第一次审问（9:13-17）**：法利赛人分裂——有人说"不守安息日，不是从神来的"，有人说"罪人怎能行神迹？"那人说："是个先知。"
- **第二次审问（9:18-23）**：不信神迹事实，叫他父母来。父母怕被赶出会堂，不敢明确表态："他已经成了人，你们问他吧。"
- **第三次审问（9:24-34）**：法利赛人命令"将荣耀归给神，我们知道这人是个罪人。"那人反驳："他是个罪人不是，我不知道；有一件事我知道，从前我是眼瞎的，如今能看见了。"这见证无可辩驳！法利赛人恼羞成怒："你全然生在罪孽中，还要教训我们吗？"就把他赶出去。

**四、信心的告白（9:35-38）** — 耶稣找到被赶出会堂的他，引导他认信："你信神的儿子吗？""谁是神的儿子，叫我信他呢？""你已经看见他，现在和你说话的就是他。""主啊，我信！"就拜耶稣。这是全章高潮——从瞎眼到看见，从被赶出犹太会堂到进入神的国度。

**五、审判的宣告（9:39-41）** — "我为审判到这世上来，叫不能看见的可以看见,能看见的反瞎了眼。"法利赛人问"难道我们也瞎了眼吗？"主回答："你们若瞎了眼，就没有罪了；但如今你们说我们能看见，所以你们的罪还在。"——自以为义者的罪无法赦免，因他们拒绝承认需要拯救。

## Structure (English)

**I. Performance of the Miracle (9:1-7)** — The man born blind, disciples' theological question (sin and suffering), Jesus' answer (to display God's glory), method of healing (mud, anointing, washing), occurrence of miracle (gained sight).

**II. Crowd's Confusion (9:8-12)** — Neighbors couldn't recognize him (debate "it is he" "not him"), the man's testimony ("A man called Jesus... I went and washed and received my sight"), simple yet powerful.

**III. Religious Interrogation (9:13-34)** — This section is the chapter's core, showing the confrontation between truth and hypocrisy:
- **First interrogation (9:13-17)**: Pharisees divided—some said "not from God, does not keep Sabbath," others said "how can a sinner do such signs?" The man said: "He is a prophet."
- **Second interrogation (9:18-23)**: Refused to believe the miracle, called his parents. Parents feared being cast out of synagogue, dared not confirm: "He is of age; ask him."
- **Third interrogation (9:24-34)**: Pharisees commanded "Give glory to God. We know that this man is a sinner." The man refuted: "Whether he is a sinner I do not know. One thing I do know, that though I was blind, now I see." This testimony is irrefutable! Pharisees, furious: "You were born in utter sin, and would you teach us?" and cast him out.

**IV. Confession of Faith (9:35-38)** — Jesus found him after being cast out, guided him to confession: "Do you believe in the Son of Man?" "Who is he, sir, that I may believe in him?" "You have seen him, and it is he who is speaking to you." "Lord, I believe!" and worshiped Jesus. This is the chapter's climax—from blindness to sight, from being cast out of the Jewish synagogue to entering God's kingdom.

**V. Declaration of Judgment (9:39-41)** — "For judgment I came into this world, that those who do not see may see, and those who see may become blind." Pharisees asked "Are we also blind?" The Lord answered: "If you were blind, you would have no guilt; but now that you say, 'We see,' your guilt remains."—The self-righteous's sin cannot be forgiven because they refuse to admit they need salvation.

## 神学要点（中文）

**全然败坏与无能为力：** 生来瞎眼象征人类堕落后的属灵状态——不是后天变瞎，而是生来就瞎，根本没有"看见"的能力。罪人不能凭自己认识神、寻求神（罗3:10-11），如同瞎子不能自己医治眼睛。只有神主动施恩——耶稣主动找到他、医治他，他才能看见。

**有效恩召的不可抗拒：** 瞎子得医治的过程显示恩典的有效性——主说"去洗"，他就去，一洗就看见。他没有质疑"和泥抹眼"这奇怪的方法，只是简单顺服。这预表神的恩召临到选民时，圣灵使人心柔软，使死在罪中的人活过来（弗2:1-5）。那些法利赛人听到同样的话、看到同样的神迹，心却刚硬拒绝；但这瞎子蒙了光照，心眼被开启。

**渐进成圣：** 那人对基督认识的渐进（耶稣→先知→从神来的→神的儿子），显示成圣是一个过程。重生是瞬间的（得医治），但认识基督的丰富是一生的（从"我从前眼瞎，如今看见"到"主啊，我信"）。改革宗强调"蒙恩之道"（means of grace）——神使用祂的话、圣礼、团契来使信徒生命成长。

**圣徒的坚忍：** 那人被赶出会堂——失去社会地位、宗教群体、经济来源，却不否认基督。他宁愿失去一切，也不妥协真理："有一件事我知道，从前我是眼瞎的，如今能看见了。"这是真信心的标志：逼迫不能摧毁，威胁不能动摇。真信徒必坚持到底（perseverance of the saints），因神保守他们到底（约6:39）。

**律法与福音：** 法利赛人代表律法主义——徒有外表敬虔，却没有生命；注重条文规定，却失去了律法的精意（爱、怜悯、公义）。他们守安息日，却拒绝安息日的主；他们查考圣经，却不认识圣经见证的基督。福音则彰显在那瞎子身上——一无所有，只能领受恩典；看见自己的无助，就仰望基督。

## Theology (English)

**Total Depravity and Inability:** Born blind symbolizes humanity's spiritual state after the Fall—not becoming blind later, but born blind, fundamentally lacking the ability to "see." Sinners cannot by themselves know God or seek God (Rom 3:10-11), just as a blind man cannot heal his own eyes. Only God's initiative in grace—Jesus actively finding him, healing him—enabled him to see.

**Irresistible Grace of Effectual Calling:** The blind man's healing process shows the efficacy of grace—the Lord said "go wash," he went, washed, and saw. He did not question the strange method of "mud on eyes," only simply obeyed. This prefigures when God's call comes to the elect, the Holy Spirit softens hearts, making those dead in sin come alive (Eph 2:1-5). Those Pharisees heard the same words, saw the same miracle, yet their hearts remained hardened in rejection; but this blind man was enlightened, his spiritual eyes opened.

**Progressive Sanctification:** The man's progressive recognition of Christ (Jesus → prophet → from God → Son of God) shows sanctification is a process. Regeneration is instantaneous (healing), but knowing Christ's riches is lifelong (from "I was blind, now I see" to "Lord, I believe"). Reformed theology emphasizes "means of grace"—God uses His Word, sacraments, fellowship to grow believers' lives.

**Perseverance of the Saints:** The man was cast out of the synagogue—losing social status, religious community, economic livelihood, yet did not deny Christ. He would rather lose everything than compromise truth: "One thing I do know, that though I was blind, now I see." This is the mark of true faith: persecution cannot destroy it, threats cannot shake it. True believers must persevere to the end (perseverance of the saints) because God preserves them to the end (John 6:39).

**Law and Gospel:** Pharisees represent legalism—having the appearance of godliness but no life; focusing on letter regulations yet losing the law's essence (love, mercy, justice). They kept the Sabbath yet rejected the Lord of the Sabbath; they searched Scripture yet did not recognize the Christ Scripture testified to. The gospel is manifested in the blind man—having nothing, could only receive grace; seeing his own helplessness, he looked to Christ.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**创造的再现：** 神用地上的尘土造人（创2:7），耶稣用地上的泥开瞎子的眼睛。这神迹是新创造的预表——基督来不仅医治身体，更是要重造人心。"若有人在基督里,他就是新造的人，旧事已过，都变成新的了"（林后5:17）。和泥抹眼，象征基督的道和圣灵的工作使死人活过来。

**西罗亚池——奉差遣者：** 约翰特意解释"西罗亚翻出来就是奉差遣"（9:7）。基督是父所差遣到世上来的（3:17，5:36，6:38等），祂差遣瞎子去洗，象征信徒因顺服基督而得洁净。旧约中以利沙命乃缦去约旦河洗七次得洁净（王下5:10-14），也是预表顺服神的话带来医治。

**光照瞎眼的以赛亚预言：** 以赛亚书多次预言弥赛亚要"开瞎子的眼"（赛29:18,35:5,42:7）。当耶稣医治瞎子，不仅应验这些预言，更彰显祂就是以赛亚所说那位要来的"公义的苗裔"、"神的仆人"。路加福音4章，耶稣在拿撒勒会堂宣读以赛亚书61:1-2后宣告："今天这经应验在你们耳中了。"

**安息日与真安息：** 法利赛人执着于安息日的规条，却不认识安息日的主（太12:8）。基督来要赐下真安息——不是外在的停止工作，乃是心灵从罪的重担中得释放（太11:28-30）。希伯来书4章说，神的百姓仍有一安息日的安息为我们存留——那是因基督的工作完成，信徒进入神的安息。医治瞎子正显示：基督的工作不受人为规条限制，祂的怜悯是安息日真正的精义。

**属灵的瞎眼与看见：** 以赛亚书6:9-10预言以色列人"听是要听见，却不明白；看是要看见,却不晓得"。主在9:39引用这主题："叫不能看见的可以看见，能看见的反瞎了眼。"这应验了神主权的拣选——有些人被光照，心眼开启；有些人因骄傲自义，越发刚硬。正如保罗在罗马书9-11章所论述的，以色列人整体的硬心是神预定的计划，为使外邦人得救，最终全以色列也要得救。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Re-creation:** God formed man from the dust of the ground (Gen 2:7); Jesus used mud from the ground to open the blind man's eyes. This miracle prefigures new creation—Christ came not only to heal bodies but to recreate hearts. "Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new." (2 Cor 5:17). Mud on eyes symbolizes Christ's Word and the Spirit's work making dead people come alive.

**Pool of Siloam—The Sent One:** John specifically explains "Siloam (which means Sent)" (9:7). Christ is the One sent by the Father into the world (3:17, 5:36, 6:38, etc.). He sent the blind man to wash, symbolizing believers being cleansed through obedience to Christ. In the Old Testament, Elisha commanded Naaman to wash seven times in the Jordan River for cleansing (2 Kgs 5:10-14), also prefiguring healing through obedience to God's word.

**Isaiah's Prophecy of Opening Blind Eyes:** Isaiah repeatedly prophesied the Messiah would "open the eyes of the blind" (Isa 29:18, 35:5, 42:7). When Jesus healed the blind man, He not only fulfilled these prophecies but manifested Himself as the "righteous Branch," the "Servant of God" Isaiah spoke of. In Luke 4, after reading Isaiah 61:1-2 in the Nazareth synagogue, Jesus declared: "Today this Scripture has been fulfilled in your hearing."

**Sabbath and True Rest:** Pharisees clung to Sabbath regulations yet did not recognize the Lord of the Sabbath (Matt 12:8). Christ came to give true rest—not external cessation of work, but the soul's liberation from sin's burden (Matt 11:28-30). Hebrews 4 says a Sabbath rest still remains for God's people—that is, because Christ's work is complete, believers enter God's rest. Healing the blind man shows: Christ's work is not limited by human regulations; His mercy is the true essence of the Sabbath.

**Spiritual Blindness and Sight:** Isaiah 6:9-10 prophesied Israel would "keep on hearing, but not understand; keep on seeing, but not perceive." The Lord in 9:39 references this theme: "that those who do not see may see, and those who see may become blind." This fulfills God's sovereign election—some are enlightened, spiritual eyes opened; some because of pride and self-righteousness, become increasingly hardened. As Paul discusses in Romans 9-11, Israel's overall hardness is part of God's predetermined plan, so that Gentiles may be saved, and ultimately all Israel will be saved.
