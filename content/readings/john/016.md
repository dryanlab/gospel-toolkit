## 正文（中文）

主继续对我们说话，祂知道我们心中充满忧愁。祂告诉我们这些事，是要我们不至于跌倒。祂预言我们将被赶出会堂，甚至有人会以为杀我们就是事奉神。这话在后来的年日里一次次应验——我看见司提反被石头打死，雅各被希律的刀杀害，无数弟兄姊妹为主的名受逼迫。

但主说："然而我将真情告诉你们，我去是与你们有益的；我若不去，保惠师就不到你们这里来；我若去，就差他来。"那时我们无法理解，主离开怎么会"有益"呢？我们只想祂永远与我们同在。但如今回顾，我完全明白了：主在肉身时，只能在一地一时与我们同在；但圣灵来了，就住在我们每个人里面，无论何时何地都与我们同在。主的去，是为了更大的同在。

主详细告诉我们圣灵来了要做的三件事："祂既来了，就要叫世人为罪、为义、为审判，自己责备自己。"这是何等重要的教导！圣灵不是来安抚世人的，而是来光照他们的。为罪，因他们不信主；为义，因主往父那里去，证明祂的义；为审判，因这世界的王已经受了审判。五旬节那天，当彼得讲道，听众"觉得扎心"（徒2:37），这正是圣灵的工作。

主又说："只等真理的圣灵来了，他要引导你们明白一切的真理。"这应许太宝贵了。主在地上三年半的教导，我们听得似懂非懂。但圣灵来了，就把主所说的一切都"想起来"（约14:26），并引导我们进入更深的领悟。我写这本福音书，正是圣灵引导的结果。那些我曾听过却不明白的话，如今在圣灵的光照下，向我完全敞开了。

主接着告诉我们一个谜一般的话："等不多时，你们就不得见我；再等不多时，你们还要见我。"我们彼此对望，困惑不已。主知道我们想问，就解释说："我实实在在地告诉你们，你们将要痛哭、哀号，世人倒要喜乐；你们将要忧愁，然而你们的忧愁要变为喜乐。"

主用妇人生产的比喻来说明：产难的痛苦虽然剧烈，但婴孩一生下来，喜乐就取代了痛苦，甚至让人不再记得那苦楚。主的死让我们经历了人生中最深的痛苦——我们的希望破碎了，我们的主被钉在十字架上。但第三天，当我和彼得跑到坟墓，看见空墓和细麻布，当主向我们显现——那喜乐真是无法言喻！那痛苦的记忆不是被抹去，而是被喜乐的荣光照亮，成为见证主死而复活大能的记号。

"到那日，你们什么也就不问我了。"主说。这话在复活后逐渐应验。当圣灵来了，我们开始明白主所说的一切。我们不再困惑，不再满心疑问，因为圣灵将主的心意向我们启示。主又应许："你们奉我的名，无论向父求什么，他就赐给你们。"这不是空白支票，而是在主的名里——在祂的旨意和权柄里——祈求，就必蒙应允。

主说："向来你们没有奉我的名求什么，如今你们求，就必得着，叫你们的喜乐可以满足。"那时我们确实不懂什么叫"奉主的名"祈求。但圣灵来了之后，我们学会了在主里祷告，我们的祷告不再是为自己的私欲，而是为神的国度和荣耀。这样的祷告，满有能力。

主最后说了一句震撼人心的话："在世上，你们有苦难；但你们可以放心，我已经胜了世界。"这话成为历代圣徒在患难中的安慰。是的，在世上我们有苦难——这是主明说的，不是意外。但主已经胜了世界！十字架看似失败，实则是得胜；主的死看似终结，实则是新生命的开始。因为主已经胜了，我们也必得胜。

那一夜，我们听着这些话，心中仍然忧愁。但当我经历了主的死、主的复活、圣灵的降临之后，我才明白：主所说的每一句话,都带着永恒的智慧和无限的慈爱。

## Content (English)

The Lord continued to speak to us, knowing our hearts were filled with sorrow. He told us these things so that we would not fall away. He prophesied that we would be expelled from the synagogues, and that some would even think killing us was service to God. These words were fulfilled again and again in the years that followed—I saw Stephen stoned to death, James killed by Herod's sword, countless brothers and sisters persecuted for the Lord's name.

But the Lord said, "Nevertheless, I tell you the truth: it is to your advantage that I go away, for if I do not go away, the Helper will not come to you. But if I go, I will send him to you." At that time we could not understand how the Lord's departure could be "to our advantage." We only wanted Him to be with us forever. But looking back now, I fully understand: when the Lord was in the flesh, He could only be with us in one place at one time; but when the Spirit came, He dwells in each of us, with us at all times and in all places. The Lord's going was for a greater presence.

The Lord told us in detail three things the Spirit would do when He came: "And when he comes, he will convict the world concerning sin and righteousness and judgment." What crucial teaching! The Spirit did not come to comfort the world, but to illuminate it. Concerning sin, because they do not believe in the Lord; concerning righteousness, because the Lord goes to the Father, proving His righteousness; concerning judgment, because the ruler of this world has been judged. On the day of Pentecost, when Peter preached and the listeners were "Now when they heard this, they were pricked in their heart, and said unto Peter and to the rest of the apostles, Men and brethren, what shall we do?" (Acts 2:37), this was precisely the Spirit's work.

The Lord also said, "When the Spirit of truth comes, he will guide you into all the truth." This promise is so precious. During the three and a half years the Lord taught on earth, we heard but only half-understood. But when the Spirit came, He "But the Comforter, which is the Holy Ghost, whom the Father will send in my name, he shall teach you all things, and bring all things to your remembrance, whatsoever I have said unto you." (John 14:26) everything the Lord said, and guided us into deeper understanding. My writing of this Gospel is the result of the Spirit's guidance. Words I had heard but not understood were now fully opened to me under the Spirit's illumination.

The Lord then told us a puzzling statement: "A little while, and you will see me no longer; and again a little while, and you will see me." We looked at each other in confusion. Knowing we wanted to ask, the Lord explained: "Truly, truly, I say to you, you will weep and lament, but the world will rejoice. You will be sorrowful, but your sorrow will turn into joy."

The Lord used the analogy of a woman in labor: though the pain of childbirth is intense, when the baby is born, joy replaces the pain, even making one forget the suffering. The Lord's death brought us the deepest pain of our lives—our hope was shattered, our Lord crucified on a cross. But on the third day, when Peter and I ran to the tomb and saw the empty grave and linen cloths, when the Lord appeared to us—that joy was truly inexpressible! The memory of that pain was not erased, but illuminated by the glory of joy, becoming a testimony to the power of the Lord's death and resurrection.

"In that day you will ask me no questions." This was gradually fulfilled after the resurrection. When the Spirit came, we began to understand everything the Lord said. We were no longer confused, no longer full of questions, because the Spirit revealed the Lord's will to us. The Lord also promised, "Whatever you ask of the Father in my name, he will give it to you." This is not a blank check, but asking in the Lord's name—in His will and authority—and it will be answered.

The Lord said, "Until now you have asked nothing in my name. Ask, and you will receive, that your joy may be full." At that time we truly did not understand what it meant to ask "in the Lord's name." But after the Spirit came, we learned to pray in the Lord, and our prayers were no longer for our own selfish desires, but for God's kingdom and glory. Such prayers have great power.

Finally, the Lord said a heart-stirring statement: "In the world you will have tribulation. But take heart; I have overcome the world." These words have been the comfort of saints through the ages in suffering. Yes, in this world we have tribulation—the Lord said this plainly, it's not an accident. But the Lord has overcome the world! The cross seemed like defeat, but was actually victory; the Lord's death seemed like an end, but was the beginning of new life. Because the Lord has overcome, we too shall overcome.

That night, as we listened to these words, our hearts were still sorrowful. But after I experienced the Lord's death, the Lord's resurrection, and the descent of the Holy Spirit, I understood: every word the Lord spoke carried eternal wisdom and infinite love.

## 历史背景（中文）

第16章延续第15章的"楼上讲论"，主题聚焦在圣灵的工作和门徒即将经历的苦难与喜乐转换。历史背景上，这仍是逾越节晚上，距离主被捕只有几个小时。

"被赶出会堂"（2节）在第一世纪犹太背景下是极严重的惩罚。会堂不仅是宗教场所，也是社区中心。被逐出会堂意味着与犹太社群断绝关系，经济、社会、家庭关系都会受到影响。约翰福音9:22和12:42已经提到这一威胁。到了约翰写福音书的时代（约公元90年代），这已成为现实：犹太会堂加入了"米宁祷文"（Birkat ha-Minim），正式诅咒基督徒为异端。

"以为杀你们就是事奉神"（2节）预言了扫罗（后来的保罗）那样的热心犹太人对基督徒的逼迫（徒7:58；8:1-3；26:9-11）。保罗自己后来承认，他当时"以为"自己在事奉神，实际上是在抵挡神（提前1:13）。

主关于"等不多时"的话（16-22节）首先指向祂的死与复活：门徒将在十字架前失去祂（"不得见我"），三天后复活时又见到祂（"还要见我"）。但这话也有更深层的意义，指向主升天与再来之间的教会时代。

圣灵的应许是这章的核心。主将要离开，但会差遣"另一位保惠师"（παράκλητος，14:16）——不是"代替品"，而是以另一种方式继续主自己的工作。五旬节的实现（徒2章）证明这应许的真实。

## Historical Context (English)

Chapter 16 continues the "Upper Room Discourse" from chapter 15, focusing on the work of the Holy Spirit and the transition from suffering to joy that the disciples would soon experience. Historically, this was still the night of Passover, only hours before the Lord's arrest.

Being "put out of the synagogues" (v.2) was an extremely serious punishment in first-century Jewish context. The synagogue was not only a religious place but also a community center. Expulsion from the synagogue meant severance from the Jewish community, affecting economic, social, and family relationships. John 9:22 and 12:42 already mentioned this threat. By the time John wrote his Gospel (around the 90s AD), this had become reality: Jewish synagogues added the "Birkat ha-Minim" (Blessing Against Heretics), formally cursing Christians as heretics.

"Whoever kills you will think he is offering service to God" (v.2) prophesied the persecution of Christians by zealous Jews like Saul (later Paul) (Acts 7:58; 8:1-3; 26:9-11). Paul himself later admitted that he "thought" he was serving God at the time, when he was actually opposing God (1 Tim 1:13).

The Lord's words about "a little while" (vv.16-22) first pointed to His death and resurrection: the disciples would lose Him at the cross ("you will see me no longer"), and see Him again three days later at the resurrection ("you will see me"). But these words also have deeper meaning, pointing to the church age between the Lord's ascension and second coming.

The promise of the Spirit is the core of this chapter. The Lord would leave, but would send "another Helper" (παράκλητος, 14:16)—not a "replacement," but one who would continue the Lord's own work in another way. The fulfillment at Pentecost (Acts 2) proved the reality of this promise.

## 经文结构（中文）

本章围绕"圣灵的来临"展开，可分为四个部分：

**一、逼迫的预警（16:1-4）**
主预告门徒将被赶出会堂，甚至被杀害。这不是为了让他们恐惧，而是让他们"不至于跌倒"（σκανδαλίζω）。主说这话是在离别前为门徒预备，使他们在遭遇患难时记得主的话，知道这一切都在神的计划之中。

**二、圣灵的工作（16:5-15）**
这是本章的核心。主详述圣灵的三重工作：（1）对世界的工作——使世人为罪、为义、为审判自责（8-11节）；（2）对门徒的工作——引导他们进入真理，荣耀基督（12-15节）。圣灵不是说自己的话，而是将属基督的事指示门徒。圣灵与圣子、圣父同工，三位一体的团契在此显明。

**三、忧愁变为喜乐（16:16-24）**
"等不多时"的话让门徒困惑不已。主用生产的比喻解释：痛苦是真实的，但喜乐将要取代痛苦。关键词是"到那日"（20、23、26节）——指主复活后、圣灵降临后的新时代。在那日，门徒将奉主的名祷告，喜乐得以满足。

**四、我已胜了世界（16:25-33）**
主总结祂的教导，宣告祂即将回到父那里。门徒以为自己明白了，主却预言他们将要分散。但主最后的话给予极大安慰："在世上你们有苦难；但你们可以放心，我已经胜了世界。"这是整章的高潮，也是历代圣徒的盼望之源。

## Structure (English)

This chapter revolves around "the coming of the Spirit" and can be divided into four sections:

**I. Warning of Persecution (16:1-4)**
The Lord foretells that the disciples will be expelled from synagogues and even killed. This is not to frighten them, but to keep them from "falling away" (σκανδαλίζω). The Lord spoke these words before His departure to prepare the disciples, so that when they encounter tribulation, they will remember His words and know that all this is within God's plan.

**II. The Work of the Spirit (16:5-15)**
This is the core of the chapter. The Lord details the Spirit's threefold work: (1) His work toward the world—convicting the world concerning sin, righteousness, and judgment (vv.8-11); (2) His work toward the disciples—guiding them into truth and glorifying Christ (vv.12-15). The Spirit does not speak on His own but declares what belongs to Christ to the disciples. The Spirit works with the Son and Father, revealing the fellowship of the Trinity.

**III. Sorrow Turned to Joy (16:16-24)**
The words "a little while" greatly confused the disciples. The Lord used the analogy of childbirth to explain: the pain is real, but joy will replace the pain. The key phrase is "in that day" (vv.20, 23, 26)—referring to the new era after the Lord's resurrection and the Spirit's descent. In that day, the disciples will pray in the Lord's name, and their joy will be complete.

**IV. I Have Overcome the World (16:25-33)**
The Lord summarizes His teaching and declares He will soon return to the Father. The disciples think they understand, but the Lord prophesies they will scatter. Yet the Lord's final words give great comfort: "In the world you will have tribulation. But take heart; I have overcome the world." This is the climax of the entire chapter and the source of hope for saints through the ages.

## 神学要点（中文）

**圣灵论**
本章是圣经中关于圣灵工作最系统的教导之一。圣灵被称为"保惠师"（παράκλητος），意为"辩护者、安慰者、帮助者"。改革宗神学强调圣灵的神性和位格：祂不是一股力量，而是三一神的第三位格，与父、子同尊同荣。圣灵的工作包括：（1）使人知罪（8节）——这是归正的第一步；（2）引导进入真理（13节）——这是成圣的过程；（3）荣耀基督（14节）——这是圣灵工作的目标。任何声称出于圣灵却不荣耀基督的，都值得怀疑。

**有效恩召**
圣灵使世人"为罪、为义、为审判，自己责备自己"（8节），这是有效恩召的描述。人凭自己无法认识到自己的罪、基督的义和将来的审判，唯有圣灵光照，人才能真正悔改。这不是人的理性说服，而是圣灵超自然的工作。

**代祷与祈祷**
"到那日，你们要奉我的名祈求"（23、26节）。改革宗神学强调，我们能到神面前，唯独借着基督的中保工作。"奉主的名"不是祷告结尾的公式，而是承认我们靠自己不配祷告，唯有在基督里才能坦然无惧地来到施恩座前（来4:16）。主在父面前为我们代求（罗8:34），圣灵也在我们里面为我们代祷（罗8:26-27）。

**基督的得胜**
"我已经胜了世界"（33节）是完成时态（νενίκηκα）——在十字架之前,主已宣告祂的得胜。这得胜不是将来要争取的，而是已经成就的事实。改革宗神学的"已然未然"（already-not yet）概念在此彰显：基督已经在十字架上得胜，但这得胜要到祂再来时才完全彰显。我们活在这"已然未然"之间,靠着主的得胜,我们也必得胜。

## Theology (English)

**Pneumatology (Doctrine of the Holy Spirit)**
This chapter is one of the most systematic teachings on the work of the Holy Spirit in Scripture. The Spirit is called "Helper" (παράκλητος), meaning "advocate, comforter, helper." Reformed theology emphasizes the deity and personhood of the Spirit: He is not a force, but the third person of the Trinity, equal in honor and glory with the Father and Son. The Spirit's work includes: (1) convicting of sin (v.8)—this is the first step of conversion; (2) guiding into truth (v.13)—this is the process of sanctification; (3) glorifying Christ (v.14)—this is the goal of the Spirit's work. Anything claiming to be of the Spirit that does not glorify Christ is suspect.

**Effectual Calling**
The Spirit causes the world to "convict... concerning sin and righteousness and judgment" (v.8), which is a description of effectual calling. People cannot recognize their sin, Christ's righteousness, and coming judgment on their own; only through the Spirit's illumination can people truly repent. This is not rational persuasion, but the Spirit's supernatural work.

**Intercession and Prayer**
"In that day you will ask in my name" (vv.23, 26). Reformed theology emphasizes that we can approach God only through Christ's mediatorial work. "In the Lord's name" is not a formula to end prayers, but an acknowledgment that we are unworthy to pray on our own, and only in Christ can we boldly approach the throne of grace (Heb 4:16). The Lord intercedes for us before the Father (Rom 8:34), and the Spirit also intercedes for us within us (Rom 8:26-27).

**Christ's Victory**
"I have overcome the world" (v.33) is in the perfect tense (νενίκηκα)—before the cross, the Lord already declared His victory. This victory is not something to be fought for in the future, but an accomplished fact. The Reformed concept of "already-not yet" is manifested here: Christ has already conquered at the cross, but this victory will be fully manifested at His return. We live in this "already-not yet," and through the Lord's victory, we too shall overcome.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**圣灵荣耀圣子**
"他要荣耀我，因为他要将受于我的告诉你们"（14节）。圣灵不是来高举自己,而是来荣耀基督。这是三一神团契的美好见证：圣父爱圣子，圣子荣耀圣父，圣灵荣耀圣子。任何强调圣灵却模糊基督的教导，都偏离了圣经的启示。

**生产的痛苦与喜乐**
主用妇人生产来比喻祂的死与复活（21节）。这比喻在旧约中常指向弥赛亚国度的来临（赛26:17-19；66:7-9；弥4:9-10）。主的死如同产难，痛苦而剧烈；但复活是新生命的诞生，带来无法夺去的喜乐。教会是从基督的死与复活中"生出来"的新群体。

**基督的中保工作**
"我从父出来，到了世界；我又离开世界，往父那里去"（28节）。这短短一句话概括了基督道成肉身的全程：永恒→道成肉身→受死复活→升天。主是神与人之间唯一的中保（提前2:5），祂从父到世界，又从世界回到父那里，完成了救赎的工作。

**我已经胜了世界**
主在十字架前宣告"我已经胜了世界"（33节）。对门徒来说，主即将被捕、受审、钉十字架，怎么看都是失败。但主看到的是十字架背后的得胜：祂将要借着死败坏掌死权的魔鬼（来2:14），祂将要从死里复活，证明祂是神的儿子（罗1:4）。十字架是通往荣耀的道路，是得胜的方式，而非失败的标记。我们在患难中的盼望,就建立在这已经成就的得胜之上。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**The Spirit Glorifies the Son**
"He will glorify me, for he will take what is mine and declare it to you" (v.14). The Spirit does not come to exalt Himself, but to glorify Christ. This is a beautiful testimony to the fellowship of the Trinity: the Father loves the Son, the Son glorifies the Father, the Spirit glorifies the Son. Any teaching that emphasizes the Spirit but obscures Christ deviates from biblical revelation.

**The Pain and Joy of Childbirth**
The Lord used a woman in labor to illustrate His death and resurrection (v.21). This metaphor in the Old Testament often pointed to the coming of the Messianic kingdom (Isa 26:17-19; 66:7-9; Mic 4:9-10). The Lord's death is like labor pains, painful and intense; but the resurrection is the birth of new life, bringing joy that cannot be taken away. The church is the new community "born" from Christ's death and resurrection.

**Christ's Mediatorial Work**
"I came from the Father and have come into the world, and now I am leaving the world and going to the Father" (v.28). This brief sentence summarizes the entire journey of Christ's incarnation: eternity → incarnation → death and resurrection → ascension. The Lord is the only mediator between God and man (1 Tim 2:5); He came from the Father to the world, and from the world back to the Father, completing the work of redemption.

**I Have Overcome the World**
The Lord declared "I have overcome the world" (v.33) before the cross. To the disciples, the Lord was about to be arrested, tried, and crucified—it seemed like defeat no matter how you looked at it. But the Lord saw the victory behind the cross: He would destroy the devil who had the power of death through His death (Heb 2:14), He would rise from the dead, proving He is the Son of God (Rom 1:4). The cross is the path to glory, the way of victory, not the mark of defeat. Our hope in tribulation is built on this already-accomplished victory.
