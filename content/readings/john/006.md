## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是约翰。第六章记载了主在地上事工中最壮观的神迹之一——五饼二鱼喂饱五千人——以及紧随其后最令人震惊的宣告："我就是生命的粮。"

一、五饼二鱼（6:1-15）

逾越节近了。大批群众跟随主，因为他们看见了主医病的神迹。主举目看见许多人，就对腓力说："我们从哪里买饼叫这些人吃呢？"（约6:5）。约翰特别注明——"他说这话是要试验腓力，他自己原知道要怎样行"（约6:6）。

腓力算了一笔账："就是二百银币的饼也不够"。安得烈则找到了一个小孩："在这里有一个孩童，带着五个大麦饼、两条鱼，只是分给这许多人还算甚么呢？"（约6:9）。

五个饼，两条鱼，五千个男人加上妇女孩子。在人看来，这完全不可能。但主拿起饼来，祝谢了，就分给坐着的人，鱼也照样分了。众人都吃饱了。门徒收拾零碎，"装满了十二个篮子"（约6:13）。

弟兄姐妹，**在基督手中，不够的变成有余的。** 你觉得自己的才能太少、资源太穷、力量太弱？把它交给基督。他不嫌少——五个饼两条鱼他都用了——他嫌的是你不肯交出来。

二、海面行走（6:16-21）

天黑了，门徒上船渡海。忽然狂风大作，海浪翻腾。"约摇橹行了十里多路，看见耶稣在海面上走，渐渐近了船"（约6:19）。门徒都害怕了。主说："是我，不要怕！"（约6:20）。

"是我"——原文又是ἐγώ εἰμι（我是）。在狂风巨浪中，主说的不只是"别怕，是我约翰的朋友"，而是**"我是"——那位在起初创造天地、在旷野开道路的神，现在踏浪而来。**

三、生命的粮（6:22-59）

第二天，群众追过海来找主。但主知道他们的动机："你们找我，并不是因见了神迹，乃是因吃饼得饱"（约6:26）。他们要的是免费的午餐，不是生命的救主。

主说："不要为那必坏的食物劳力，要为那存到永生的食物劳力，就是人子要赐给你们的"（约6:27）。

然后主作出了约翰福音七个"我是"宣告中的第一个：

> **"我就是生命的粮。到我这里来的，必定不饿；信我的，永远不渴"（约6:35）。**

这个宣告呼应了旷野中的吗哪。以色列人在旷野饥饿时，神从天降下吗哪喂养他们。但吃了吗哪的人最终还是死了。基督说："我是从天上降下来生命的粮；人若吃这粮，就必永远活着"（约6:51）。**吗哪维持肉体的生命，基督赐下永恒的生命。** 基督不只是给你饼吃——他**自己就是那饼。**

四、门徒的分裂（6:60-71）

主的话太难了。"从此，他门徒中多有退去的，不再和他同行"（约6:66）。

主转向十二个门徒说："你们也要去么？"（约6:67）。

彼得——我亲爱的弟兄彼得——说了一句至今令我感动的话：**"主阿，你有永生之道，我们还归从谁呢？我们已经信了，又知道你是神的圣者"（约6:68-69）。**

弟兄姐妹，当世界让你失望、当信仰的道路艰难、当你想要离开时，请记住彼得的话：**我们还归从谁呢？** 除了基督，谁有永生之道？除了他，谁能满足你灵魂的饥渴？

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am John. Chapter six records one of the most spectacular miracles of the Lord's earthly ministry—feeding five thousand with five loaves and two fish—followed by His most astonishing declaration: "I am the bread of life."

I. Five Loaves and Two Fish (6:1-15)

The Passover was near. Great crowds followed the Lord because they had seen His healing miracles. The Lord looked up and saw the multitude, then said to Philip, "When Jesus then lifted up his eyes, and saw a great company come unto him, he saith unto Philip, Whence shall we buy bread, that these may eat?" (John 6:5). John specifically notes, "And this he said to prove him: for he himself knew what he would do." (John 6:6).

Philip calculated: "Two hundred denarii worth of bread would not be enough." Andrew found a boy: "There is a lad here, which hath five barley loaves, and two small fishes: but what are they among so many?" (John 6:9).

Five loaves, two fish, five thousand men plus women and children. Humanly impossible. But the Lord took the loaves, gave thanks, and distributed them. Everyone ate their fill. The disciples gathered the leftovers—"Therefore they gathered them together, and filled twelve baskets with the fragments of the five barley loaves, which remained over and above unto them that had eaten." (John 6:13).

Brothers and sisters, **in Christ's hands, not enough becomes more than enough.** Do you feel your talents are too few, your resources too poor, your strength too weak? Give them to Christ. He does not despise smallness—He used five loaves and two fish—what He minds is your refusal to hand them over.

II. Walking on the Sea (6:16-21)

Darkness fell; the disciples sailed across. Suddenly a great wind arose and the sea surged. "So when they had rowed about five and twenty or thirty furlongs, they see Jesus walking on the sea, and drawing nigh unto the ship: and they were afraid." (John 6:19). They were terrified. The Lord said, "But he saith unto them, It is I; be not afraid." (John 6:20).

"It is I"—the Greek is again ἐγώ εἰμι (I AM). Amid raging waves, the Lord's words meant more than "Don't worry, it's your friend"—they declared **"I AM"—the God who created the heavens and earth in the beginning, who made a path through the wilderness, now walks upon the waves.**

III. The Bread of Life (6:22-59)

The next day, the crowd crossed the sea seeking the Lord. But He knew their motive: "Jesus answered them and said, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Ye seek me, not because ye saw the miracles, but because ye did eat of the loaves, and were filled." (John 6:26). They wanted free lunch, not the Savior of life.

The Lord said: "Labour not for the meat which perisheth, but for that meat which endureth unto everlasting life, which the Son of man shall give unto you: for him hath God the Father sealed." (John 6:27).

Then the Lord made the first of John's seven "I AM" declarations:

> **"And Jesus said unto them, I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger; and he that believeth on me shall never thirst." (John 6:35).**

This echoes the manna in the wilderness. When Israel hungered, God rained manna from heaven. But those who ate manna eventually died. Christ said, "I am the living bread which came down from heaven: if any man eat of this bread, he shall live for ever: and the bread that I will give is my flesh, which I will give for the life of the world." (John 6:51). **Manna sustained physical life; Christ gives eternal life.** Christ does not merely give you bread—He **is** the bread.

IV. The Division Among Disciples (6:60-71)

The Lord's words were too hard. "From that time many of his disciples went back, and walked no more with him." (John 6:66).

The Lord turned to the Twelve: "Then said Jesus unto the twelve, Will ye also go away?" (John 6:67).

Peter—my dear brother Peter—spoke words that move me to this day: **"Then Simon Peter answered him, Lord, to whom shall we go? thou hast the words of eternal life. And we believe and are sure that thou art that Christ, the Son of the living God." (John 6:68-69).**

Brothers and sisters, when the world disappoints, when the path of faith is hard, when you want to walk away, remember Peter's words: **To whom shall we go?** Apart from Christ, who has the words of eternal life? Apart from Him, who can satisfy the hunger of your soul?

## 历史背景（中文）

约翰福音第六章的事件发生在加利利海东岸，时值逾越节前夕（约6:4）。**五饼二鱼**的神迹是四福音书唯一共同记载的神迹（太14、可6、路9、约6），说明其在早期教会传统中的重要性。"大麦饼"是穷人的食物，暗示那个孩童来自贫困家庭。"十二篮子"的"篮子"（κόφινος）是犹太人特有的随身篮，象征十二支派都得饱足。海面行走的地点在加利利海中央，当时的风浪可以非常凶险，渔民深知其危险。迦百农会堂（6:59）的考古遗址至今仍可参观。

## Historical Context (English)

The events of John 6 occurred on the eastern shore of the Sea of Galilee, near Passover (John 6:4). The **feeding of the five thousand** is the only miracle recorded in all four Gospels (Matt 14, Mark 6, Luke 9, John 6), indicating its importance in early church tradition. "Barley loaves" were food of the poor, suggesting the boy came from a humble family. The "twelve baskets" uses κόφινος, a distinctly Jewish hand-basket, symbolizing all twelve tribes satisfied. The sea-walking occurred in the middle of the Sea of Galilee, where storms can be extremely dangerous—fishermen knew the peril well. The synagogue at Capernaum (6:59) can still be visited as an archaeological site today.

## 经文结构（中文）

全章按**三个场景**展开：**（一）山上的神迹**（6:1-15）——五饼二鱼，群众要强逼他作王；**（二）海上的显现**（6:16-21）——ἐγώ εἰμι在风浪中；**（三）会堂的讲论**（6:22-71）——"我是生命的粮"的系统阐述。讲论部分有清晰的推进：从物质的饼→天上的吗哪→基督自己就是粮→"吃我肉、喝我血"的终极宣告。每一步都加深，每一步都筛掉一部分跟随者，直到最后只剩十二个人。

## Structure (English)

The chapter unfolds in **three scenes**: **(1) The miracle on the mountain** (6:1-15)—five loaves and two fish; the crowd tries to make Him king; **(2) The epiphany on the sea** (6:16-21)—ἐγώ εἰμι amid the storm; **(3) The synagogue discourse** (6:22-71)—systematic exposition of "I am the bread of life." The discourse progresses clearly: physical bread → heavenly manna → Christ Himself as the bread → the ultimate declaration to "eat my flesh and drink my blood." Each step deepens, each step sifts out more followers, until only the Twelve remain.

## 神学要点（中文）

本章核心神学是**基督作为生命之粮的充足性**和**神主权的拣选**。"到我这里来的，我总不丢弃他"（6:37）——这是无条件恩典的应许。但紧接着，"若不是差我来的父吸引人，就没有能到我这里来的"（6:44）——这是有效呼召的教义。**不是人选择来到基督面前，是父吸引他来。** 韦敏斯德信条第十章论有效呼召："凡神所预定得生命的人，且就只有这些人，祂乐意在祂指定与看为合宜的时候，借着祂的圣道与圣灵，有效地呼召他们。"第六章也明确教导**圣徒的坚忍**——"差我来者的意思就是：他所赐给我的，叫我一个也不失落"（6:39）。神所拣选的，一个也不会丢。

## Theology (English)

The central theology is **Christ's sufficiency as the bread of life** and **God's sovereign election**. "All that the Father giveth me shall come to me; and him that cometh to me I will in no wise cast out" (6:37)—an unconditional promise of grace. But immediately: "No man can come to me, except the Father which hath sent me draw him: and I will raise him up at the last day" (6:44)—the doctrine of effectual calling. **It is not man choosing to come to Christ, but the Father drawing him.** The Westminster Confession (ch. 10) on effectual calling: "All those whom God has predestined unto life...He is pleased, in His appointed time, effectually to call, by His Word and Spirit." Chapter 6 also clearly teaches the **perseverance of the saints**—"And this is the Father\'s will which hath sent me, that of all which he hath given me I should lose nothing, but should raise it up again at the last day" (6:39). Those whom God has chosen, not one will be lost.

## 基督的影子（中文）

五饼二鱼的神迹与旧约的吗哪形成完美的预表对应。以色列人在旷野吃天降的吗哪（出16章），基督在山上喂饱五千人——但基督说"你们的祖宗在旷野吃过吗哪，还是死了"（约6:49），而"我是从天上降下来生命的粮；人若吃这粮，就必永远活着"（约6:51）。吗哪是影子，基督是实体。此外，这事发生在逾越节前（6:4）——逾越节的羔羊预表基督的牺牲，而"生命的粮"讲论指向圣餐的意义：基督的身体为我们擘开，基督的血为我们流出。约翰福音没有记载设立圣餐的叙事，但第六章就是约翰版的圣餐神学。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

The feeding miracle forms a perfect typological correspondence with Old Testament manna. Israel ate heaven-sent manna in the wilderness (Exod 16), and Christ fed five thousand on the mountain—but Christ said, "Your fathers did eat manna in the wilderness, and are dead." (John 6:49), while "I am the living bread which came down from heaven: if any man eat of this bread, he shall live for ever: and the bread that I will give is my flesh, which I will give for the life of the world." (John 6:51). Manna was the shadow; Christ is the substance. Moreover, this occurred near Passover (6:4)—the Passover lamb foreshadowed Christ's sacrifice, and the "bread of life" discourse points to the meaning of the Lord's Supper: Christ's body broken for us, Christ's blood poured out for us. John's Gospel does not narrate the institution of the Supper, but chapter 6 is John's eucharistic theology.
