## 正文（中文）

这些事以后，主在提比哩亚海边又向门徒显现。那时，我们几个门徒都回到加利利。西门彼得说："我打鱼去。"我们说："我们也和你同去。"我们就出去，上了船；那一夜并没有打着什么。

我们整夜劳力，却一无所获。这场景何等熟悉——三年前，主第一次呼召我们时，也是这样（路5:1-11）。那时我们整夜劳力，一无所获，主上了我们的船，吩咐我们把网撒在水深之处，结果网满了鱼。如今，主复活后，祂要再一次提醒我们：离了祂，我们不能做什么（约15:5）。

天将亮的时候，主站在岸上，但我们不知道是主。主就对我们说："小子！你们有吃的没有？"我们回答说："没有。"主说："你们把网撒在船的右边，就必得着。"我们便撒下网去，竟拉不上来了，因为鱼甚多。

我首先认出来——"是主！"那时西门彼得赤着身子，一听见是主，就束上一件外衣，跳在海里，向岸边游去。我们其余的人坐小船拖着那网鱼，也来到岸边。我们上了岸，看见那里有炭火，上面有鱼，又有饼。

主说："把刚才打的鱼拿几条来。"西门彼得就去，把网拉到岸上。那网满了大鱼，共一百五十三条；鱼虽这样多，网却没有破。这一百五十三条鱼，我们数得清清楚楚——这不是偶然的数字，有人说它代表当时已知的所有鱼类种类，象征福音要临到万族万民。无论如何，那天的神迹让我们永志不忘：主掌管一切，连鱼的数目也在祂手中。

主说："你们来吃早饭。"我们都不敢问祂"你是谁"，因为知道是主。主就来拿饼递给我们，又照样拿鱼递给我们。这已经是主第三次向门徒显现了。

吃完了早饭，主对西门彼得说："约翰的儿子西门，你爱我比这些更深吗？"彼得说："主啊，是的，你知道我爱你。"主对他说："你喂养我的小羊。"

主又第二次对他说："约翰的儿子西门，你爱我吗？"彼得说："主啊，是的，你知道我爱你。"主说："你牧养我的羊。"

第三次主对他说："约翰的儿子西门，你爱我吗？"彼得因为主第三次问他"你爱我吗"，就忧愁，对主说："主啊，你是无所不知的；你知道我爱你。"主说："你喂养我的羊。"

我站在旁边，看着这一幕，泪水盈眶。主三次问彼得，正对应彼得三次不认主（18:17、25-27）。主不是要羞辱彼得，而是要恢复他。每一次的询问，都是一次医治；每一次的托付，都是一次恢复。主没有因彼得的跌倒而弃绝他，反而把牧养群羊的重任交给他——这是何等的恩典！

主接着说："我实实在在地告诉你，你年少的时候，自己束上带子，随意往来；但年老的时候，你要伸出手来，别人要把你束上，带你到不愿意去的地方。"主说这话是指着彼得要怎样死,荣耀神。说了这话，就对他说："你跟从我吧！"

彼得转过来，看见我跟着，就问主说："主啊，这人将来如何？"主对他说："我若要他等到我来的时候，与你何干？你跟从我吧！"于是这话传在弟兄中间，说那门徒不死。其实，主不是说他不死，乃是说："我若要他等到我来的时候，与你何干？"

我就是这门徒，为这些事作见证，并且记载这些事。我们也知道他的见证是真的。主所行的事还有许多，若是一一地都写出来，我想，所写的书就是世界也容不下了。

我写完这本福音书，放下笔，回想这一切。从主呼召我离开渔船的那一天，到如今已经过了多少年。我看见过主登山变像的荣耀，看见过祂在客西马尼园的痛苦，看见过祂被钉十字架，看见过祂复活的荣耀。我亲耳听见祂说"成了"，亲眼看见祂升天。

主托付我照顾祂的母亲，我照做了。主说我要"等到祂来"，我不知道这是什么意思，但我愿意等候。我现在已经很老了，但主的话仍然清晰地回响在我心中："你跟从我吧！"

是的，主，我跟从你。从那天在加利利海边，到如今在以弗所写这卷书，我一直跟从你。我见证你是基督，是神的儿子。我见证你的爱——你爱世人，甚至将独生子赐给他们，叫一切信祂的，不至灭亡，反得永生（约3:16）。

这就是我要告诉世人的：神爱你们，基督为你们死了，祂复活了，祂活着。你们要信祂，跟从祂，就必得生命——不是将来才有的生命,而是现在就开始的、直到永恒的生命。

我的见证就到此为止。但主的故事永不终结。祂说："我就常与你们同在，直到世界的末了"（太28:20）。祂今天仍活着，仍爱祂的羊，仍在呼召人来跟从祂。你听见祂的声音了吗？

## Content (English)

After these things the Lord revealed Himself again to the disciples by the Sea of Tiberias. At that time, several of us disciples had returned to Galilee. Simon Peter said, "I am going fishing." We said, "We will go with you." We went out and got into the boat, but that night we caught nothing.

We toiled all night and caught nothing. This scene was so familiar—three years ago, when the Lord first called us, it was the same (Luke 5:1-11). At that time we had toiled all night and caught nothing, the Lord got into our boat and told us to let down the nets into deep water, and our nets were filled with fish. Now, after His resurrection, He would remind us again: apart from Him, we can do nothing (John 15:5).

Just as day was breaking, the Lord stood on the shore, but we did not know it was the Lord. The Lord said to us, "Children, do you have any fish?" We answered Him, "No." He said to us, "Cast the net on the right side of the boat, and you will find some." So we cast it, and now we were not able to haul it in, because of the quantity of fish.

I was the first to recognize—"It is the Lord!" Simon Peter, who was stripped for work, when he heard that it was the Lord, put on his outer garment and threw himself into the sea, swimming toward the shore. The rest of us came in the small boat, dragging the net full of fish, also arriving at the shore. When we got out on land, we saw a charcoal fire in place, with fish laid on it, and bread.

The Lord said, "Bring some of the fish that you have just caught." So Simon Peter went aboard and hauled the net ashore, full of large fish, 153 of them. And although there were so many, the net was not torn. We counted those 153 fish clearly—this was not a random number; some say it represents all known species of fish at that time, symbolizing the gospel reaching all peoples and nations. In any case, that day's miracle was unforgettable: the Lord controls all things, even the number of fish is in His hands.

The Lord said, "Come and have breakfast." Now none of us dared ask Him, "Who are you?" knowing it was the Lord. The Lord came and took the bread and gave it to us, and so with the fish. This was now the third time that the Lord was revealed to the disciples.

When they had finished breakfast, the Lord said to Simon Peter, "Simon, son of John, do you love me more than these?" He said to Him, "Yes, Lord; you know that I love you." He said to him, "Feed my lambs."

A second time He said to him, "Simon, son of John, do you love me?" He said to Him, "Yes, Lord; you know that I love you." He said to him, "Tend my sheep."

He said to him the third time, "Simon, son of John, do you love me?" Peter was grieved because He said to him the third time, "Do you love me?" and he said to Him, "Lord, you know everything; you know that I love you." The Lord said to him, "Feed my sheep."

I stood nearby watching this scene, tears welling in my eyes. The Lord asked Peter three times, corresponding to Peter's three denials (18:17, 25-27). The Lord was not trying to humiliate Peter, but to restore him. Each inquiry was a healing; each commission was a restoration. The Lord did not reject Peter because of his fall, but instead entrusted him with the heavy responsibility of shepherding the flock—what grace!

The Lord went on to say, "Truly, truly, I say to you, when you were young, you used to dress yourself and walk wherever you wanted, but when you are old, you will stretch out your hands, and another will dress you and carry you where you do not want to go." The Lord said this to show by what kind of death Peter was to glorify God. After saying this, He said to him, "Follow me."

Peter turned and saw me following, and said to the Lord, "Lord, what about this man?" The Lord said to him, "If it is my will that he remain until I come, what is that to you? You follow me!" So the saying spread abroad among the brothers that this disciple was not to die; yet the Lord did not say to him that he was not to die, but, "If it is my will that he remain until I come, what is that to you?"

This is the disciple who is bearing witness about these things, and who has written these things, and we know that his testimony is true. Now there are also many other things that the Lord did. Were every one of them to be written, I suppose that the world itself could not contain the books that would be written.

I finished writing this Gospel, put down my pen, and reflected on all this. From the day the Lord called me to leave the fishing boat until now, how many years have passed. I saw the glory of the Lord's transfiguration, saw His agony in Gethsemane, saw Him crucified on the cross, saw the glory of His resurrection. I heard with my own ears Him say "It is finished," saw with my own eyes Him ascend to heaven.

The Lord entrusted His mother to my care, and I did so. The Lord said I would "remain until He comes"; I do not know what this means, but I am willing to wait. I am very old now, but the Lord's words still ring clearly in my heart: "Follow me."

Yes, Lord, I follow you. From that day by the Sea of Galilee to now writing this book in Ephesus, I have always followed you. I testify that you are the Christ, the Son of God. I testify to your love—you loved the world so much that you gave your only Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have eternal life (John 3:16).

This is what I want to tell the world: God loves you, Christ died for you, He rose, He lives. You must believe in Him, follow Him, and you will have life—not life that only begins in the future, but life that begins now and continues to eternity.

My testimony ends here. But the Lord's story never ends. He said, "Teaching them to observe all things whatsoever I have commanded you: and, lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world. Amen." (Matt 28:20). He is still alive today, still loves His sheep, still calls people to follow Him. Do you hear His voice?

## 历史背景（中文）

第21章是约翰福音的"附录"（epilogue），许多学者认为本书原本在20:31结束，第21章是后来附加的。但早期手稿都包含第21章，且写作风格与前20章一致，很可能是约翰本人所加。

**提比哩亚海**：即加利利海的另一个名称，因海边的提比哩亚城而得名。门徒回到加利利，应验了主在马可福音14:28的预言："我复活以后，要在你们以先往加利利去。"

**重回打鱼**：彼得说"我打鱼去"，其他门徒跟从。这可能显示门徒一度想回到旧生活——主死了，运动失败了，我们还能做什么呢？但主的显现扭转了这一切：祂呼召他们不是去打鱼，而是去"得人如得鱼"（太4:19）。

**153条鱼**：这个具体数字引发许多解释。早期教父耶柔米（Jerome）说当时已知的鱼类共153种，象征福音要临到万族。奥古斯丁从数字意义解释（10诫+7灵=17，1+2+3...+17=153）。无论如何，这数字的精确性证明这是真实的历史事件，不是编造的寓言。

**炭火**：18:18彼得在"炭火"（ἀνθρακιάν）旁不认主，如今21:9主在"炭火"旁恢复彼得。这不是巧合——主刻意用同样的场景（炭火、三次询问）来医治彼得三次否认的伤痛。

**彼得的殉道**：主预言彼得要"伸出手来"（18节），这是钉十字架的姿势。根据教会传统，彼得约在公元64-67年间在罗马殉道，要求倒钉十字架，因自觉不配与主同样方式受死。优西比乌（Eusebius）记载此事。

**约翰的寿命**：主的话"我若要他等到我来"（22节）被误解为约翰不会死。事实上，约翰是十二使徒中唯一善终的（其他都殉道）。根据传统，约翰活到公元90年代后期，在以弗所写了福音书、三封书信和启示录。

## Historical Context (English)

Chapter 21 is the "epilogue" of John's Gospel. Many scholars believe the book originally ended at 20:31, with chapter 21 added later. However, early manuscripts all include chapter 21, and the writing style is consistent with the first 20 chapters, so it was likely added by John himself.

**Sea of Tiberias**: Another name for the Sea of Galilee, named after the city of Tiberias by the sea. The disciples returned to Galilee, fulfilling the Lord's prophecy in Mark 14:28: "But after I am raised up, I will go before you to Galilee."

**Returning to Fishing**: Peter said "I am going fishing," and the other disciples followed. This may show the disciples briefly wanting to return to their old life—the Lord died, the movement failed, what else could we do? But the Lord's appearance changed everything: He called them not to fish, but to "And he saith unto them, Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men." (Matt 4:19).

**153 Fish**: This specific number has prompted many interpretations. Early church father Jerome said there were 153 known species of fish at the time, symbolizing the gospel reaching all peoples. Augustine interpreted from numerical significance (10 commandments + 7 spirits = 17, 1+2+3...+17=153). In any case, the precision of this number proves this was a real historical event, not a fabricated allegory.

**Charcoal Fire**: At 18:18 Peter denied the Lord by a "charcoal fire" (ἀνθρακιάν); now at 21:9 the Lord restores Peter by a "charcoal fire." This is not coincidence—the Lord deliberately used the same setting (charcoal fire, three questions) to heal the pain of Peter's three denials.

**Peter's Martyrdom**: The Lord prophesied Peter would "stretch out your hands" (v.18), the posture of crucifixion. According to church tradition, Peter was martyred in Rome around AD 64-67, requesting to be crucified upside down because he felt unworthy to die in the same manner as the Lord. Eusebius recorded this.

**John's Lifespan**: The Lord's words "if it is my will that he remain until I come" (v.22) were misunderstood to mean John would not die. In fact, John was the only one of the twelve apostles to die a natural death (the others were all martyred). According to tradition, John lived until the late 90s AD and wrote the Gospel, three epistles, and Revelation in Ephesus.

## 经文结构（中文）

本章可分为三个场景：

**一、主向门徒显现（21:1-14）**
门徒打鱼一夜无获（1-3节）。主显现，指示他们撒网（4-6节）。约翰认出是主，彼得跳海游向主（7-8节）。岸上早餐，153条鱼（9-14节）。这部分强调主对门徒生活的掌管和供应。

**二、主恢复彼得（21:15-19）**
主三次问彼得"你爱我吗？"（15-17节）。主三次托付彼得"喂养我的羊"（15-17节）。主预言彼得将来的殉道（18-19节）。这部分强调主的恢复和托付——跌倒的人可以重新站立，软弱的人可以被坚固。

**三、关于约翰的对话（21:20-25）**
彼得问约翰将来如何（20-21节）。主说"与你何干？你跟从我吧"（22-23节）。约翰的见证和本书的结语（24-25节）。这部分强调个人对主的跟从——不要与人比较，专心跟从主。

整章结构呈现：主的供应（鱼）→ 主的托付（牧养）→ 主的呼召（跟从）。从经历主的恩典，到承担主的使命，到一生跟从主。

## Structure (English)

This chapter can be divided into three scenes:

**I. The Lord Appears to the Disciples (21:1-14)**
The disciples fish all night and catch nothing (vv.1-3). The Lord appears and directs them to cast the net (vv.4-6). John recognizes it is the Lord, Peter jumps into the sea and swims to Him (vv.7-8). Breakfast on shore, 153 fish (vv.9-14). This section emphasizes the Lord's control over and provision for the disciples' lives.

**II. The Lord Restores Peter (21:15-19)**
The Lord asks Peter three times "Do you love me?" (vv.15-17). The Lord commissions Peter three times "Feed my sheep" (vv.15-17). The Lord prophesies Peter's future martyrdom (vv.18-19). This section emphasizes the Lord's restoration and commission—those who fall can stand again, the weak can be strengthened.

**III. Dialogue About John (21:20-25)**
Peter asks what will happen to John (vv.20-21). The Lord says "What is that to you? You follow me" (vv.22-23). John's testimony and the conclusion of the book (vv.24-25). This section emphasizes personal following of the Lord—don't compare with others, follow the Lord wholeheartedly.

The structure of the entire chapter presents: the Lord's provision (fish) → the Lord's commission (shepherding) → the Lord's call (following). From experiencing the Lord's grace, to undertaking the Lord's mission, to following the Lord for life.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神的主权与人的责任**
门徒整夜打鱼一无所获，主一句话就网满了鱼。这显明神的主权——成功在于神的祝福，不在于人的努力。但这不是说人可以懒惰——门徒仍要撒网、拉网、数鱼。改革宗神学坚持：神的主权和人的责任不矛盾。神预定结果，也预定手段。我们要尽人的本分（撒网），同时依靠神的恩典（"主若愿意"，雅4:15）。

**恢复的恩典**
彼得三次不认主，主三次问他"你爱我吗？"并三次托付他牧养的责任。这是恢复的福音：我们的失败不是终点，神的恩典大过我们的软弱。改革宗神学强调"圣徒坚忍"（perseverance of the saints）：真信徒可能跌倒，但神必扶持他站起来。彼得的恢复证明神保守属祂的人到底（腓1:6）。

**教牧神学**
主三次托付彼得："喂养我的小羊...牧养我的羊...喂养我的羊"（15-17节）。这是教牧事工的根基：（1）羊不是牧者的，而是主的——"我的羊"；（2）牧养是主的托付，是神圣的职分；（3）牧养的动机必须是爱主——"你爱我吗？"改革宗强调牧者的职分包括喂养（教导神的道）和牧养（关怀和治理）。

**殉道的荣耀**
主预言彼得将要为主殉道（18节），并说"用什么死荣耀神"（19节）。殉道不是悲剧，而是荣耀——以生命见证对主的忠诚。改革宗教会历史充满殉道者的血：胡斯、威克里夫、苏格兰的长老会信徒，他们"虽至于死，也不爱惜性命"（启12:11）。

**个人的呼召**
彼得问约翰将来如何，主说："与你何干？你跟从我吧！"（22节）这提醒我们：不要与人比较，不要为别人的道路分心，专心回应主对你个人的呼召。改革宗神学强调"呼召"（calling）的个别性——神对每个人有独特的旨意和计划，我们要寻求并跟随祂对我们个人的带领。

**见证的权威**
"这门徒...为这些事作见证，并且记载这些事。我们也知道他的见证是真的"（24节）。约翰强调他的见证是亲眼所见（约一1:1-3），是真实可靠的。改革宗神学坚持圣经无误：使徒的见证在圣灵的默示下成为圣经，对教会有永久的、无上的权威。

## Theology (English)

**God's Sovereignty and Human Responsibility**
The disciples fished all night and caught nothing; with one word from the Lord, the net was full of fish. This reveals God's sovereignty—success depends on God's blessing, not human effort. But this does not mean people can be lazy—the disciples still had to cast the net, haul the net, count the fish. Reformed theology insists: God's sovereignty and human responsibility do not contradict. God ordains the ends and also the means. We must do our part (cast the net) while relying on God's grace ("if the Lord wills," James 4:15).

**The Grace of Restoration**
Peter denied the Lord three times; the Lord asked him three times "Do you love me?" and three times commissioned him with shepherding responsibility. This is the gospel of restoration: our failures are not the end, God's grace is greater than our weakness. Reformed theology emphasizes "perseverance of the saints": true believers may fall, but God will uphold them to stand again. Peter's restoration proves God keeps those who belong to Him to the end (Phil 1:6).

**Pastoral Theology**
The Lord commissioned Peter three times: "Feed my lambs...Tend my sheep...Feed my sheep" (vv.15-17). This is the foundation of pastoral ministry: (1) The sheep do not belong to the shepherd, but to the Lord—"my sheep"; (2) Shepherding is the Lord's commission, a sacred office; (3) The motive for shepherding must be love for the Lord—"Do you love me?" Reformed theology emphasizes the pastoral office includes feeding (teaching God's Word) and shepherding (care and governance).

**The Glory of Martyrdom**
The Lord prophesied Peter would be martyred for Him (v.18), and said "by what kind of death he was to glorify God" (v.19). Martyrdom is not tragedy, but glory—witnessing loyalty to the Lord with one's life. Reformed church history is filled with martyrs' blood: Hus, Wycliffe, Scottish Covenanters—they "And they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony; and they loved not their lives unto the death." (Rev 12:11).

**Individual Calling**
Peter asked what would happen to John; the Lord said, "What is that to you? You follow me!" (v.22) This reminds us: don't compare with others, don't be distracted by others' paths, focus on responding to the Lord's call to you personally. Reformed theology emphasizes the individuality of "calling"—God has a unique will and plan for each person; we must seek and follow His personal leading for us.

**Authority of Testimony**
"This is the disciple...who is bearing witness about these things, and who has written these things, and we know that his testimony is true" (v.24). John emphasizes his testimony is eyewitness (1 John 1:1-3), true and reliable. Reformed theology insists on biblical inerrancy: apostolic testimony under the Spirit's inspiration became Scripture, with permanent, supreme authority over the church.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**第二次呼召**
主在加利利海边第一次呼召门徒（路5:1-11），如今复活后在同一地方再次呼召。这不是重复，而是更新的呼召——门徒已经经历了主的死和复活，如今要以新的生命开始新的使命。这预表每个信徒的经历：我们因信得救（第一次呼召），但需要不断更新奉献，回应主持续的呼召。

**早餐的团契**
主在岸上为门徒预备早餐（9-13节）。这画面充满温柔——复活的主不是高高在上的君王，而是服事门徒的仆人。这预示新天新地的筵席："看哪，我站在门外叩门，若有听见我声音就开门的，我要进到他那里去，我与他，他与我一同坐席"（启3:20）。

**牧人与羊群**
主托付彼得"喂养我的羊"（15-17节），应验了约翰福音10章"好牧人"的预言。主是大牧人（来13:20），祂将牧养的职分托付给祂的仆人。以西结书34:23预言："我必立一牧人照管他们，牧养他们，就是我的仆人大卫。"主耶稣是那位真正的大卫之子，祂兴起彼得和众使徒作祂的"副牧人"（彼前5:4）。

**伸出手来**
主说彼得要"伸出手来"（18节），预言他将要钉十字架。创世记22:10亚伯拉罕"伸手拿刀"要杀以撒，神为以撒预备了代替的羔羊。如今彼得要"伸出手"钉十字架，却没有代替品——因为那代替他的羔羊（主耶稣）已经献上了。彼得的殉道不是为赎罪，而是为见证那已经完成的救赎。

**你跟从我吧**
主两次对彼得说："你跟从我吧！"（19、22节）。这是全书的最后呼召，也是对每个读者的呼召。创世记12:1神对亚伯拉罕说："你要...往我所要指示你的地去。"如今复活的主说："你跟从我"——不问前路如何，不与人比较，单单跟从主。这是信心的道路，也是得福的道路。

**见证人**
约翰说："这门徒...为这些事作见证"（24节）。申命记19:15说："要凭两三个人的口作见证，才可定准。"约翰是那"看见又作见证"的（约19:35），彼得也是（彼后1:16），保罗也是（林前15:8）。他们的见证一致：主真的复活了。这不是神话，而是"我们曾听见、曾看见、亲眼看过、亲手摸过"的实在（约一1:1）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**The Second Calling**
The Lord first called the disciples by the Sea of Galilee (Luke 5:1-11); now after His resurrection He calls them again in the same place. This is not repetition, but renewed calling—the disciples have experienced the Lord's death and resurrection and now begin a new mission with new life. This prefigures every believer's experience: we are saved by faith (first calling), but need continual renewal and dedication, responding to the Lord's ongoing call.

**The Fellowship of Breakfast**
The Lord prepared breakfast for the disciples on shore (vv.9-13). This scene is full of tenderness—the risen Lord is not a lofty king, but a servant serving the disciples. This foreshadows the feast of the new heaven and new earth: "Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me." (Rev 3:20).

**Shepherd and Flock**
The Lord commissioned Peter to "feed my sheep" (vv.15-17), fulfilling the prophecy of the "good shepherd" in John 10. The Lord is the great shepherd (Heb 13:20); He entrusts the office of shepherding to His servants. Ezekiel 34:23 prophesies: "And I will set up over them one shepherd, my servant David, and he shall feed them." The Lord Jesus is that true Son of David; He raises up Peter and the apostles as His "And when the chief Shepherd shall appear, ye shall receive a crown of glory that fadeth not away." (1 Pet 5:4).

**Stretching Out Hands**
The Lord said Peter would "stretch out your hands" (v.18), prophesying his crucifixion. Genesis 22:10 Abraham "reached out his hand and took the knife" to slay Isaac; God provided a substitute lamb for Isaac. Now Peter would "stretch out his hands" for crucifixion with no substitute—because the Lamb who substituted for him (the Lord Jesus) had already been offered. Peter's martyrdom was not for atonement, but to witness to the already-completed redemption.

**You Follow Me**
The Lord said to Peter twice: "You follow me!" (vv.19, 22). This is the final call of the entire book, and also a call to every reader. Genesis 12:1 God said to Abraham: "Go...to the land that I will show you." Now the risen Lord says: "You follow me"—without asking about the future, without comparing with others, simply following the Lord. This is the path of faith and the path of blessing.

**Witnesses**
John says: "This is the disciple...who is bearing witness about these things" (v.24). Deuteronomy 19:15 says: "Only on the evidence of two witnesses or of three witnesses shall a charge be established." John is the one who "And he that saw it bare record, and his record is true: and he knoweth that he saith true, that ye might believe." (John 19:35), Peter also (2 Pet 1:16), and Paul also (1 Cor 15:8). Their testimony is unanimous: the Lord truly rose. This is not myth, but reality "That which was from the beginning, which we have heard, which we have seen with our eyes, which we have looked upon, and our hands have handled, of the Word of life;" (1 John 1:1).
