## 正文（中文）

那是逾越节的晚上，主耶稣刚刚为我们洗了脚，犹大已经离席而去。我们这些留下的门徒心中忐忑不安，主却在这最后的时刻，向我们启示了一个极其美丽而深刻的真理——祂是真葡萄树，我们是枝子。

我至今仍能回想起主说这话时的神情。祂说："我是真葡萄树，我父是栽培的人。"这不是随意的比喻，而是关乎我们生命存续的根本真理。在旧约中，以色列常被比作葡萄园，但那葡萄园却让神失望，结出的是野葡萄。如今，主自己成为那"真"葡萄树——唯一能结出真果子的源头。

主的教导是如此清晰：枝子若不常在葡萄树上，就不能结果子；我们若不常在祂里面，也是如此。这个"常在"（μένω）的概念，我在这封书信中反复使用了四十多次。它不是一次性的决定，而是持续的、活泼的连结。正如枝子的生命完全依赖于树干的汁液，我们的属灵生命也完全依赖于与基督的联合。

主又说："凡属我不结果子的枝子，他就剪去；凡结果子的，他就修理干净，使枝子结果子更多。"这话让我们震惊，也让我们明白：真实的信心必然结果子。那些看似在基督里，却从未真正与祂联合的人，终将显明他们只是虚假的连结。但对于我们这些真枝子，父神并不因我们结果子就满足，祂要修剪我们，使我们结果子"更多"。修剪是痛苦的，却是必需的——祂剪去那些消耗养分却无益的枝叶，好让生命的汁液集中到能结果子的部分。

"你们若常在我里面，我的话也常在你们里面，凡你们所愿意的，祈求，就给你们成就。"这是何等宝贵的应许！但这应许有前提：常在祂里面，祂的话也常在我们里面。当我们的意志被祂的话语塑造，我们所愿意的就是祂所愿意的，我们的祈求就与祂的旨意一致。这样的祈祷，怎能不蒙应允呢？

主接着向我们颁布了一条新命令："你们要彼此相爱，像我爱你们一样。"这爱不是人本性中所能有的，而是从葡萄树流向枝子的生命汁液的必然表现。主说："人为朋友舍命，人的爱心没有比这个大的。"那时我们还不完全明白，几个小时后，祂就要在十字架上为我们——这些软弱、小信、即将四散的门徒——舍命。

主又警告我们："世人若恨你们，你们知道，恨你们以先已经恨我了。"这预言很快就应验了。世界恨我们，因为我们不属世界。正如枝子与树干同享生命，也必与树干同受逼迫。主说这话，不是要我们逃避，而是要我们预备好，并知道这逼迫证明我们真的属于祂。

"但我要从父那里差保惠师来，就是从父出来真理的圣灵；他来了，就要为我作见证。你们也要作见证，因为你们从起头就与我同在。"这是主对我们的托付。五旬节那天，当圣灵降临，我才完全明白这话的意义。圣灵在我们里面为基督作见证，我们也因圣灵的能力向世界作见证。

那一夜，我们在楼上听着主的话，心中充满困惑和不安。但如今回顾，我看见主在那最黑暗的时刻，却向我们启示了最光明的真理：我们在祂里面，祂在我们里面，这联合是如此真实，以至于我们结果子，父神就因此得荣耀。我们爱弟兄，世人就认出我们是祂的门徒。我们被世界恨恶，却有圣灵作我们的保惠师和见证人。

常在祂里面，这就是一切。

## Content (English)

It was the night of Passover. The Lord Jesus had just washed our feet, and Judas had left. We remaining disciples were anxious and uncertain, yet in these final hours, the Lord revealed to us a beautiful and profound truth—He is the true vine, and we are the branches.

I can still recall the Lord's expression as He spoke these words. "I am the true vine, and my Father is the vinedresser." This was not a casual metaphor, but a fundamental truth concerning the very existence of our lives. In the Old Testament, Israel was often compared to a vineyard, but that vineyard disappointed God, producing wild grapes. Now, the Lord Himself became the "true" vine—the only source that could bear true fruit.

The Lord's teaching was crystal clear: a branch cannot bear fruit by itself unless it abides in the vine; neither can we, unless we abide in Him. This concept of "abiding" (μένω) I have used more than forty times throughout my epistle. It is not a one-time decision, but a continuous, living connection. Just as a branch's life depends entirely on the sap from the trunk, our spiritual life depends entirely on our union with Christ.

The Lord continued: "Every branch in me that does not bear fruit he takes away, and every branch that does bear fruit he prunes, that it may bear more fruit." These words shocked us, and helped us understand: genuine faith inevitably bears fruit. Those who appear to be in Christ but have never truly been united with Him will ultimately be shown to have only a false connection. But for us who are true branches, God the Father is not satisfied with our bearing fruit—He prunes us so that we may bear "more" fruit. Pruning is painful, yet necessary—He cuts away the shoots that consume nutrients but yield nothing, so that the life-giving sap may concentrate on the fruit-bearing parts.

"If you abide in me, and my words abide in you, ask whatever you wish, and it will be done for you." What a precious promise! But this promise has a condition: abide in Him, and His words abide in us. When our will is shaped by His word, what we wish becomes what He wishes, and our prayers align with His will. How could such prayers not be answered?

The Lord then gave us a new commandment: "Love one another as I have loved you." This love is not something inherent in human nature, but the necessary expression of the life-sap flowing from the vine to the branches. The Lord said, "Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends." At that moment we did not fully understand, but within hours, He would lay down His life on the cross for us—weak, doubting disciples who were about to scatter.

The Lord also warned us: "If the world hates you, know that it has hated me before it hated you." This prophecy was soon fulfilled. The world hates us because we do not belong to the world. Just as branches share life with the trunk, they must also share in the trunk's persecution. The Lord said this not to make us flee, but to prepare us, and to let us know that this persecution proves we truly belong to Him.

"But when the Helper comes, whom I will send to you from the Father, the Spirit of truth, who proceeds from the Father, he will bear witness about me. And you also will bear witness, because you have been with me from the beginning." This was the Lord's commission to us. On the day of Pentecost, when the Holy Spirit descended, I fully understood the meaning of these words. The Spirit bears witness to Christ within us, and we bear witness to the world through the Spirit's power.

That night, sitting in the upper room listening to the Lord's words, our hearts were filled with confusion and anxiety. But looking back now, I see that in that darkest hour, the Lord revealed to us the most luminous truth: we are in Him, He is in us, and this union is so real that when we bear fruit, the Father is glorified. When we love the brothers, the world recognizes we are His disciples. When we are hated by the world, we have the Spirit as our Helper and witness.

Abide in Him—this is everything.

## 历史背景（中文）

这段话语发生在逾越节晚餐后，犹大离席之后、主被捕之前的那几个小时。门徒们刚刚经历了洗脚礼和主关于背叛的预言，心中充满困惑。约翰福音13-17章（常被称为"楼上讲论"）记录了主在被钉十字架前最后的教导。

葡萄树的意象在旧约中有深厚根基。诗篇80篇、以赛亚书5章、耶利米书2章、以西结书15章、19章都用葡萄园或葡萄树来比喻以色列。但这些经文几乎都是审判的信息——神栽种的葡萄园结出野葡萄，神培育的葡萄树变得无用。在这个背景下，耶稣宣称"我是真葡萄树"意义重大：祂成就了以色列未能成就的，祂是神唯一喜悦的"葡萄树"。

这段教导也为即将到来的门徒经历作准备：主的被捕、受审、钉十字架将使门徒们四散。主预先告诉他们，要"常在"祂里面，不要因为即将发生的事而信心崩溃。同时，主也预告了圣灵保惠师的来临（将在16章详述），为五旬节铺路。

关于世界的恨恶，这也是对初代教会处境的预言。约翰写这福音书时（约公元90年代），基督徒已经开始遭受犹太会堂的逐出（约9:22已提及）和罗马帝国的零星逼迫。主的话语成为受苦教会的安慰：这逼迫不是意外，而是证明他们属于基督。

## Historical Context (English)

This discourse took place in the hours after the Passover meal, after Judas had left but before the Lord's arrest. The disciples had just experienced the foot washing and the Lord's prophecy about betrayal, and their hearts were filled with confusion. John 13-17 (often called the "Upper Room Discourse") records the Lord's final teachings before His crucifixion.

The imagery of the vine has deep roots in the Old Testament. Psalm 80, Isaiah 5, Jeremiah 2, Ezekiel 15 and 19 all use the vineyard or vine as a metaphor for Israel. But these passages are almost all messages of judgment—the vineyard God planted produced wild grapes, the vine God cultivated became useless. Against this background, Jesus' declaration "I am the true vine" is profoundly significant: He accomplished what Israel failed to accomplish; He is the only "vine" in whom God delights.

This teaching also prepared the disciples for what was about to happen: the Lord's arrest, trial, and crucifixion would cause the disciples to scatter. The Lord told them beforehand to "abide" in Him, not to let their faith collapse because of what was about to happen. At the same time, the Lord foretold the coming of the Holy Spirit as Helper (to be detailed in chapter 16), paving the way for Pentecost.

Regarding the hatred of the world, this was also a prophecy about the situation of the early church. When John wrote this Gospel (around the 90s AD), Christians were already experiencing expulsion from Jewish synagogues (mentioned in 9:22) and sporadic persecution from the Roman Empire. The Lord's words became comfort for the suffering church: this persecution was not an accident, but proof that they belonged to Christ.

## 经文结构（中文）

本章可分为三个部分：

**一、葡萄树与枝子的比喻（15:1-8）**
主自我启示为"真葡萄树"，父神是栽培的人。核心教导是"常在"（μένω）——这个词在1-10节出现了十次。结构递进：不常在就不能结果子（4节）→ 不常在就被剪除（6节）→ 常在就多结果子，父神得荣耀（8节）。修剪（2节）不是审判，而是管教和成圣的过程。

**二、爱的命令（15:9-17）**
从"常在"延伸到"爱"。主的爱是我们彼此相爱的模式和源头（9-10节）。遵守命令与住在爱中是同义的（10节）。主以朋友称呼门徒（15节），这是何等的恩典——祂拣选了我们，分派我们去结果子（16节）。

**三、世界的恨恶（15:18-27）**
与第二部分形成对比：门徒在内部经历爱，在外部遭受恨。世界恨门徒，因为先恨基督（18节）。这恨没有缘由（25节，引用诗35:19；69:4），却应验了圣经。但门徒不是孤单的：圣灵将来为基督作见证（26节），门徒也要作见证（27节）。

整章结构呈现：内在生命（与基督联合）→ 群体生活（彼此相爱）→ 外在使命（在逼迫中作见证）。这三者环环相扣，缺一不可。

## Structure (English)

This chapter can be divided into three sections:

**I. The Parable of the Vine and Branches (15:1-8)**
The Lord reveals Himself as the "true vine," with God the Father as the vinedresser. The core teaching is "abiding" (μένω)—this word appears ten times in verses 1-10. The structure progresses: without abiding, no fruit-bearing (v.4) → without abiding, being cut off (v.6) → with abiding, bearing much fruit, and the Father is glorified (v.8). Pruning (v.2) is not judgment, but the process of discipline and sanctification.

**II. The Commandment of Love (15:9-17)**
From "abiding" extends to "love." The Lord's love is the pattern and source of our love for one another (vv.9-10). Keeping His commandments and abiding in His love are synonymous (v.10). The Lord calls the disciples friends (v.15)—what grace! He chose us and appointed us to bear fruit (v.16).

**III. The Hatred of the World (15:18-27)**
This forms a contrast with the second section: disciples experience love internally and hatred externally. The world hates the disciples because it first hated Christ (v.18). This hatred is without cause (v.25, quoting Ps 35:19; 69:4), yet it fulfills Scripture. But the disciples are not alone: the Spirit will come to bear witness to Christ (v.26), and the disciples will also bear witness (v.27).

The structure of the entire chapter presents: inner life (union with Christ) → community life (loving one another) → outward mission (bearing witness amid persecution). These three are interconnected and indispensable.

## 神学要点（中文）

**与基督联合的教义**
"常在我里面"（μένω ἐν ἐμοί）是改革宗神学中"与基督联合"（unio cum Christo）教义的核心经文之一。加尔文强调，与基督联合是我们领受一切救恩益处的方式——称义、成圣、得荣耀都源于此。我们不是孤立地接受这些恩典，而是在与基督的联合中接受。这联合是圣灵的工作，是真实的、不可分割的。

**预定与拣选**
"不是你们拣选了我，是我拣选了你们"（16节）清楚显明拣选的主权在神。这拣选不是基于我们的配得或预见的信心，而完全出于神的主权恩典。神拣选我们，分派我们去结果子——这果子是预定要存留的。这与罗马书8:29-30的"金链条"（预知-预定-呼召-称义-得荣耀）完全一致。

**蒙恩管道**
"你们若常在我里面，我的话也常在你们里面"（7节）显明神话语的中心地位。我们常在基督里，不是神秘主义的冥想，而是通过祂的话语。改革宗强调圣道与圣灵并行：圣灵借着道运行，道借着圣灵成为我们的生命。

**圣徒坚忍**
真枝子会被修剪，却不会被剪除。假枝子最终显明从未真正连于树干。这教导了"一次得救永远得救"的真理，但不是廉价恩典——真信心必然结果子，必然遵守主的命令（10节）。没有果子的"信心"证明它从未是真信心。

## Theology (English)

**The Doctrine of Union with Christ**
"Abide in me" (μένω ἐν ἐμοί) is one of the central texts for the Reformed doctrine of "union with Christ" (unio cum Christo). Calvin emphasized that union with Christ is the way we receive all the benefits of salvation—justification, sanctification, and glorification all flow from this union. We do not receive these graces in isolation, but in union with Christ. This union is the work of the Holy Spirit, real and indivisible.

**Predestination and Election**
"You did not choose me, but I chose you" (v.16) clearly demonstrates that the sovereignty of election belongs to God. This election is not based on our merit or foreseen faith, but entirely on God's sovereign grace. God chose us and appointed us to bear fruit—fruit that is ordained to remain. This is fully consistent with the "golden chain" of Romans 8:29-30 (foreknowledge-predestination-calling-justification-glorification).

**Means of Grace**
"If you abide in me, and my words abide in you" (v.7) reveals the centrality of God's Word. Our abiding in Christ is not mystical meditation, but through His Word. Reformed theology emphasizes Word and Spirit working together: the Spirit works through the Word, and the Word becomes our life through the Spirit.

**Perseverance of the Saints**
True branches will be pruned, but not cut off. False branches ultimately reveal they were never truly connected to the trunk. This teaches the truth of "once saved, always saved," but not cheap grace—genuine faith necessarily bears fruit and keeps the Lord's commandments (v.10). Faith without fruit proves it was never true faith.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**真葡萄树与旧约以色列**
以色列在旧约中被称为神的葡萄树（诗80:8-16），却结出野葡萄让神失望（赛5:1-7）。基督是"真"葡萄树，祂成就了以色列的呼召，祂是神唯一喜悦的。这应验了以色列的失败指向那位真以色列人——耶稣基督。

**舍命的朋友**
"人为朋友舍命，人的爱心没有比这个大的"（13节）。几个小时后，这话就在十字架上应验。但更惊人的是，主为我们舍命时，我们还不是祂的朋友，而是祂的仇敌（罗5:10）。这是何等的爱！主称门徒为朋友（15节），这友谊不是我们赚得的，而是祂用宝血买来的。

**无故的恨**
"他们无故地恨我"（25节）引用诗篇35:19和69:4——大卫诗篇中受苦义人的呼声。这些诗篇预表基督的受苦。祂是无罪的，世界却恨祂；祂来施行拯救,世界却拒绝祂。十字架是这无故之恨的顶峰，也是神之爱的顶峰。

**真理的圣灵**
26节预言圣灵的来临。这位保惠师"从父出来"，与子同等、同荣。三一神的团契在这里显明：父栽培，子是葡萄树,圣灵使我们常在子里面。五旬节圣灵的降临,成就了主复活升天后的应许，使门徒从软弱变为刚强，从隐藏变为勇敢作见证。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**The True Vine and Old Testament Israel**
Israel was called God's vine in the Old Testament (Ps 80:8-16), but produced wild grapes that disappointed God (Isa 5:1-7). Christ is the "true" vine; He fulfilled Israel's calling and is the only one in whom God delights. This fulfills how Israel's failure pointed to the true Israelite—Jesus Christ.

**The Friend Who Lays Down His Life**
"Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends" (v.13). Within hours, these words were fulfilled on the cross. But even more astonishing is that when the Lord laid down His life for us, we were not yet His friends, but His enemies (Rom 5:10). What love! The Lord calls His disciples friends (v.15)—this friendship was not earned by us, but purchased by His blood.

**Hated Without Cause**
"They hated me without a cause" (v.25) quotes Psalms 35:19 and 69:4—the cry of the suffering righteous one in David's psalms. These psalms prefigure Christ's suffering. He was sinless, yet the world hated Him; He came to bring salvation, yet the world rejected Him. The cross is the culmination of this causeless hatred, and also the culmination of God's love.

**The Spirit of Truth**
Verse 26 prophesies the coming of the Holy Spirit. This Helper "proceeds from the Father," equal with the Son in glory. The fellowship of the Trinity is revealed here: the Father cultivates, the Son is the vine, the Holy Spirit enables us to abide in the Son. The descent of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost fulfilled the promise after the Lord's resurrection and ascension, transforming the disciples from weakness to strength, from hiding to boldly bearing witness.
