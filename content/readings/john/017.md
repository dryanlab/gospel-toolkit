## 正文（中文）

主说了这话，就举目望天。那一刻，整个房间仿佛充满了神圣的静默。我从未听过这样的祷告，也不会再听到了——这是神的儿子向父的祷告，是大祭司为自己、为我们这些祂的门徒、为历世历代一切信祂之人的代求。

"父阿，时候到了，愿你荣耀你的儿子，使儿子也荣耀你。"主这样开始祂的祷告。那"时候"已经到了——就是祂从创世之前就预定的时候，就是父所命定的时候。主不是求父救祂脱离十字架，而是求父在十字架上荣耀祂，使祂也能荣耀父。在世人看来，十字架是羞辱；但在天父看来，十字架是荣耀的舞台。

主接着说出了永生的定义："认识你—独一的真神，并且认识你所差来的耶稣基督，这就是永生。"永生不是时间的无限延长，而是关系的恢复——认识神，认识基督。这"认识"（γινώσκω）不是头脑的知识，而是生命的相交，是亲密的、经历性的认识。

"我在地上已经荣耀你，你所托付我的事，我已成全了。"主这样宣告。但此时，十字架还没有来临！然而在主的眼中，十字架的工作已如成就的事实一样确定。父所托付的使命，主完全成就了——一点也没有失落，一刻也没有偏离。

主又祈求："父阿，现在求你使我同你享荣耀，就是未有世界以先，我同你所有的荣耀。"这话显明主的先存性和神性。祂不是在地上被造，而是在万有之先就与父同享荣耀。道成肉身是祂荣耀的"隐藏"，复活升天是祂荣耀的"显现"——回到祂本来就有的荣耀。

然后，主转向为我们祷告。"我为他们祈求，不为世人祈求，却为你所赐给我的人祈求，因他们本是你的。"这话让我深深震撼：神的儿子竟然为我们祈求！祂不是泛泛地为所有人祷告，而是特别为父所赐给祂的人——就是我们这些因信与祂联合的人。

主求父保守我们："圣父阿，求你因你所赐给我的名保守他们，叫他们合而为一像我们一样。"这"合一"不是组织的合一，而是生命的合一。正如父与子在爱中完全合一，我们也要在基督里合一。主为这合一祷告，因为祂知道魔鬼会千方百计要分裂我们。

主说："我与他们同在的时候，因你所赐给我的名保守了他们，我也护卫了他们；其中除了那灭亡之子，没有一个灭亡的，好叫经上的话得应验。"犹大的背叛不是意外，而是圣经的应验（诗41:9）。但这不减轻犹大的责任——他选择了灭亡的道路。其他的门徒，主一个也没有失落。

"我不求你叫他们离开世界，只求你保守他们脱离那恶者。"主没有求父把我们从世界中取走，而是求父保守我们在世界中不被恶者夺去。我们是"在世界"却"不属世界"的人——这是何等微妙而重要的身份！

"求你用真理使他们成圣；你的道就是真理。"成圣不是靠人的努力，而是靠神的真理——就是神的道。我写这本福音书，正是要把主的道记录下来，使后来的人因这道成圣。

主又为那些"因他们的话信我的人"祷告——这包括了我们，也包括了历世历代一切因使徒的见证而信主的人！主在两千年前就为我们祷告了！祂为我们求什么呢？"使他们都合而为一。正如你父在我里面，我在你里面，使他们也在我们里面，叫世人可以信你差了我来。"

教会的合一是福音见证的基础。当世人看见基督徒彼此相爱、合而为一，他们就知道我们真是主的门徒（13:35）。这合一不是人造的，而是因我们都在父里面、在子里面的生命合一。

主又说："你所赐给我的荣耀，我已赐给他们，使他们合而为一，像我们合而为一。我在他们里面,你在我里面，使他们完完全全地合而为一，叫世人知道你差了我来，也知道你爱他们如同爱我一样。"这是何等惊人的话！父爱我们，如同爱祂的独生子一样！

主祷告的最后，是为我们将来的荣耀："父阿，我在那里，愿你所赐给我的人也同我在那里，叫他们看见你所赐给我的荣耀。"这是主的心愿——祂不仅要我们得救，更要我们与祂同在，看见祂的荣耀。那荣耀,我们有一天必要亲眼看见。

"我已将你的名指示他们，还要指示他们，使你所爱我的爱在他们里面，我也在他们里面。"这是主最后的宣告。祂已经把父的名——就是父的本性、父的爱——向我们显明了。祂还要继续显明，直到那爱充满我们,直到基督完全在我们里面成形。

我俯伏听着这祷告，泪流满面。这是圣子向圣父的代求，这是大祭司为祂百姓的祈祷。两千年过去了，这祷告仍在父面前为我们说话。主已经升天,但祂仍为我们代求（来7:25）。这是我们永远的安慰。

## Content (English)

After the Lord spoke these words, He lifted up His eyes to heaven. In that moment, the entire room seemed filled with holy silence. I had never heard such a prayer, nor would I ever hear one again—this was the prayer of the Son of God to the Father, the high priest's intercession for Himself, for us His disciples, and for all who would believe in Him through all generations.

"Father, the hour has come; glorify your Son that the Son may glorify you." The Lord began His prayer this way. That "hour" had come—the hour ordained before the foundation of the world, the hour appointed by the Father. The Lord did not ask the Father to save Him from the cross, but to glorify Him on the cross, so that He might glorify the Father. In the world's eyes, the cross was shame; but in the Father's eyes, the cross was the stage of glory.

The Lord then gave the definition of eternal life: "And this is eternal life, that they know you, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom you have sent." Eternal life is not the infinite extension of time, but the restoration of relationship—knowing God, knowing Christ. This "knowing" (γινώσκω) is not intellectual knowledge, but life's communion, intimate, experiential knowing.

"I glorified you on earth, having accomplished the work that you gave me to do." The Lord declared this. But at this moment, the cross had not yet come! Yet in the Lord's eyes, the work of the cross was as certain as an accomplished fact. The mission the Father entrusted, the Lord fully accomplished—not one detail lost, not one moment deviated.

The Lord also prayed: "And now, Father, glorify me in your own presence with the glory that I had with you before the world existed." These words reveal the Lord's pre-existence and deity. He was not created on earth, but before all things shared glory with the Father. The incarnation was the "veiling" of His glory; the resurrection and ascension was the "manifestation" of His glory—returning to the glory He originally had.

Then the Lord turned to praying for us. "I am praying for them. I am not praying for the world but for those whom you have given me, for they are yours." These words deeply shocked me: the Son of God actually intercedes for us! He does not pray generally for all people, but specifically for those the Father gave Him—us who are united with Him through faith.

The Lord asked the Father to keep us: "Holy Father, keep them in your name, which you have given me, that they may be one, even as we are one." This "oneness" is not organizational unity, but life unity. Just as Father and Son are completely one in love, we are also to be one in Christ. The Lord prayed for this unity because He knew the devil would try every means to divide us.

The Lord said: "While I was with them, I kept them in your name, which you have given me. I have guarded them, and not one of them has been lost except the son of destruction, that the Scripture might be fulfilled." Judas' betrayal was not an accident, but the fulfillment of Scripture (Ps 41:9). But this does not diminish Judas' responsibility—he chose the path of destruction. Of the other disciples, the Lord lost not one.

"I do not ask that you take them out of the world, but that you keep them from the evil one." The Lord did not ask the Father to take us out of the world, but to keep us from being snatched by the evil one while in the world. We are people who are "in the world" yet "not of the world"—what a subtle yet crucial identity!

"Sanctify them in the truth; your word is truth." Sanctification is not by human effort, but by God's truth—God's Word. I wrote this Gospel precisely to record the Lord's words, so that those who come after might be sanctified by this Word.

The Lord also prayed for those who "will believe in me through their word"—this includes us, and includes all who believe in the Lord through apostolic testimony through all generations! The Lord prayed for us two thousand years ago! What did He ask for us? "That they may all be one. Just as you, Father, are in me, and I in you, that they also may be in us, so that the world may believe that you have sent me."

The unity of the church is the foundation of gospel witness. When the world sees Christians loving one another and being one, they know we are truly the Lord's disciples (13:35). This unity is not man-made, but life unity because we are all in the Father and in the Son.

The Lord also said: "The glory that you have given me I have given to them, that they may be one even as we are one, I in them and you in me, that they may become perfectly one, so that the world may know that you sent me and loved them even as you loved me." What astonishing words! The Father loves us as He loves His only Son!

At the end of the Lord's prayer is our future glory: "Father, I desire that they also, whom you have given me, may be with me where I am, to see my glory that you have given me." This is the Lord's desire—He not only wants us to be saved, but to be with Him and see His glory. That glory, one day we will see with our own eyes.

"I made known to them your name, and I will continue to make it known, that the love with which you have loved me may be in them, and I in them." This is the Lord's final declaration. He has already revealed the Father's name—the Father's nature, the Father's love—to us. He will continue to reveal it, until that love fills us, until Christ is fully formed in us.

I bowed down listening to this prayer, tears streaming down my face. This was the Son's intercession to the Father, this was the high priest's prayer for His people. Two thousand years have passed, and this prayer still speaks for us before the Father. The Lord has ascended, but He still intercedes for us (Heb 7:25). This is our eternal comfort.

## 历史背景（中文）

约翰福音17章被称为"大祭司的祷告"（High Priestly Prayer），是主在客西马尼园被捕之前的最后祷告。不同于客西马尼园那痛苦的"杯"的祷告（符类福音记载），这是主在楼上讲论结束后、前往园子路上或到达园中时的祷告。

这章是整本圣经中最神圣的祷告之一，我们仿佛能听见天上的对话——圣子与圣父的交通。在希伯来书的神学框架下，基督是更美之约的中保，是升入高天的大祭司（来4:14-16；7:25-28）。这祷告预示了祂永远的祭司职分。

祷告的三个对象清晰可见：（1）为自己（1-5节）——求父荣耀子；（2）为门徒（6-19节）——求父保守和成圣他们；（3）为未来的信徒（20-26节）——求父使教会合一，叫世人相信。这结构显明主心意的优先次序：首先是父的荣耀，其次是门徒的保守，最后是教会的见证。

"合一"（ἕν ὦσιν）是这章的关键主题，出现四次（11、21、22、23节）。初代教会面临内部分裂的危机（林前1:10-13；3:1-9；腓2:1-4），约翰记录这祷告，提醒教会回到主的心意——合一不是可选项，而是主的命令和心愿。

关于"父所赐给我的人"，这短语在本章出现多次（2、6、9、11、24节），强调拣选的主权和救恩的确据。主不是为"可能得救的人"祷告，而是为"父所赐给祂的人"祷告——这些人必定得救,必定被保守到底。

## Historical Context (English)

John 17 is known as the "High Priestly Prayer," the Lord's final prayer before His arrest in Gethsemane. Unlike the anguished prayer about the "cup" in Gethsemane (recorded in the Synoptic Gospels), this was the Lord's prayer after the Upper Room Discourse concluded, either on the way to the garden or upon arriving there.

This chapter is one of the most sacred prayers in all Scripture; we seem to hear the conversation of heaven—the communion of Son with Father. Within the theological framework of Hebrews, Christ is the mediator of a better covenant, the high priest who has ascended to heaven (Heb 4:14-16; 7:25-28). This prayer foreshadows His eternal priesthood.

The three objects of prayer are clearly visible: (1) For Himself (vv.1-5)—asking the Father to glorify the Son; (2) For the disciples (vv.6-19)—asking the Father to keep and sanctify them; (3) For future believers (vv.20-26)—asking the Father to make the church one, so the world may believe. This structure reveals the Lord's priorities: first the Father's glory, second the disciples' keeping, finally the church's witness.

"Unity" (ἕν ὦσιν) is the key theme of this chapter, appearing four times (vv.11, 21, 22, 23). The early church faced the crisis of internal division (1 Cor 1:10-13; 3:1-9; Phil 2:1-4). John recorded this prayer to remind the church to return to the Lord's will—unity is not optional, but the Lord's command and desire.

Regarding "those whom you have given me," this phrase appears multiple times in this chapter (vv.2, 6, 9, 11, 24), emphasizing the sovereignty of election and the assurance of salvation. The Lord does not pray for "those who might be saved," but for "those the Father has given Him"—these people will certainly be saved and certainly be kept to the end.

## 经文结构（中文）

这章可以按祷告对象分为三个部分：

**一、为自己祷告（17:1-5）**
主求父在十字架上荣耀祂（1节）。这荣耀包括：（1）赐永生给父所赐祂的人（2-3节）；（2）完成父所托付的工作（4节）；（3）回到与父同享的荣耀（5节）。主的祷告显明祂的先存性、神性和使命。

**二、为门徒祷告（17:6-19）**
主首先数算门徒的特征：他们是父所赐的，他们遵守了神的道，他们知道主从父而来（6-8节）。然后主为他们代求：（1）求父保守他们合一（11节）；（2）求父保守他们脱离恶者（15节）；（3）求父用真理使他们成圣（17节）。主还说明祂差遣门徒的使命，正如父差遣子一样（18节）。主为门徒自洁，作他们的榜样（19节）。

**三、为未来信徒祷告（17:20-26）**
主的祷告跨越时空，为"那些因他们的话信我的人"祷告。主求：（1）他们合而为一（21-23节）——这合一是三一合一的反映；（2）他们将来与主同在，看见主的荣耀（24节）；（3）主的爱在他们里面（26节）。这部分的高潮是："父爱他们如同爱我一样"（23节）——何等惊人的宣告！

整章祷告的结构呈现递进：从主自己→门徒→普世教会。主心中装着所有属祂的人，一个也不失落。

## Structure (English)

This chapter can be divided into three sections according to the objects of prayer:

**I. Prayer for Himself (17:1-5)**
The Lord asks the Father to glorify Him on the cross (v.1). This glory includes: (1) giving eternal life to those the Father has given Him (vv.2-3); (2) completing the work the Father entrusted to Him (v.4); (3) returning to the glory He shared with the Father (v.5). The Lord's prayer reveals His pre-existence, deity, and mission.

**II. Prayer for the Disciples (17:6-19)**
The Lord first counts the disciples' characteristics: they were given by the Father, they kept God's Word, they knew the Lord came from the Father (vv.6-8). Then the Lord intercedes for them: (1) asking the Father to keep them in unity (v.11); (2) asking the Father to keep them from the evil one (v.15); (3) asking the Father to sanctify them in truth (v.17). The Lord also explains His sending of the disciples on mission, just as the Father sent the Son (v.18). The Lord sanctifies Himself for them, setting an example (v.19).

**III. Prayer for Future Believers (17:20-26)**
The Lord's prayer transcends time and space, praying for "those who will believe in me through their word." The Lord asks: (1) that they be one (vv.21-23)—this unity is a reflection of Trinitarian unity; (2) that they be with the Lord in the future and see His glory (v.24); (3) that the Lord's love be in them (v.26). The climax of this section is: "You loved them even as you loved me" (v.23)—what an astonishing declaration!

The structure of the entire prayer presents progression: from the Lord Himself → the disciples → the universal church. The Lord's heart holds all who belong to Him, losing not one.

## 神学要点（中文）

**基督的祭司职分**
希伯来书详述基督是照着麦基洗德的等次永远为祭司（来5:6；7:17）。约翰福音17章是这祭司职分的生动展现：基督为自己和百姓代求，进入至圣所（天上）,将自己献上为祭（十字架）,并永远为我们代求（来7:25）。这祷告不是一次性的,而是持续的——主今天仍在父右边为我们代求（罗8:34）。

**拣选与预定**
"父所赐给我的人"（2、6、9、11、24节）清楚教导拣选的教义。父在创世之前就拣选了这些人（弗1:4），将他们赐给子。子保守他们，一个也不失落（除了灭亡之子，12节）。这不是条件性的，而是无条件的拣选。改革宗神学的"双重预定"在此得到支持：父拣选一些人得救（拣选之恩），而让另一些人按他们的罪灭亡（公义的审判）。

**救恩的确据**
"我与他们同在的时候，因你所赐给我的名保守了他们...其中除了那灭亡之子，没有一个灭亡的"（12节）。主保守属祂的人到底。那"灭亡之子"犹大从未真正属于基督（约6:64；13:10-11）。真信徒可能软弱、跌倒，但不会至于灭亡，因主为我们代求，圣灵保守我们到底（彼前1:5）。

**圣化论**
"求你用真理使他们成圣；你的道就是真理"（17节）。成圣（ἁγιασμός）是神的工作，借着真理——即神的道——来进行。改革宗神学强调成圣是"渐进的"（progressive），借着神话语的持续作用和圣灵的工作。主"为他们的缘故，自己分别为圣"（19节），作我们成圣的榜样和根基。

**教会的本质**
主为教会合一的祷告（21-23节）显明教会不是人的组织，而是神的家（弗2:19）、基督的身体（弗1:22-23）、圣灵的殿（林前3:16）。教会的合一不是靠人的努力制造的，而是在基督里本来就有的生命合一。我们的责任是"竭力保守圣灵所赐合而为一的心"（弗4:3）。

## Theology (English)

**Christ's Priestly Office**
The book of Hebrews details that Christ is a priest forever according to the order of Melchizedek (Heb 5:6; 7:17). John 17 is a vivid display of this priestly office: Christ intercedes for Himself and His people, enters the Most Holy Place (heaven), offers Himself as a sacrifice (the cross), and intercedes for us forever (Heb 7:25). This prayer is not one-time, but continuous—the Lord today still intercedes for us at the Father's right hand (Rom 8:34).

**Election and Predestination**
"Those whom you have given me" (vv.2, 6, 9, 11, 24) clearly teaches the doctrine of election. The Father chose these people before the foundation of the world (Eph 1:4) and gave them to the Son. The Son keeps them, losing not one (except the son of destruction, v.12). This is not conditional, but unconditional election. The Reformed doctrine of "double predestination" is supported here: the Father elects some for salvation (electing grace), while allowing others to perish according to their sins (righteous judgment).

**Assurance of Salvation**
"While I was with them, I kept them in your name...and not one of them has been lost except the son of destruction" (v.12). The Lord keeps those who belong to Him to the end. That "son of destruction" Judas never truly belonged to Christ (John 6:64; 13:10-11). True believers may be weak and stumble, but will not perish, because the Lord intercedes for us and the Spirit keeps us to the end (1 Pet 1:5).

**Doctrine of Sanctification**
"Sanctify them in the truth; your word is truth" (v.17). Sanctification (ἁγιασμός) is God's work, carried out through truth—God's Word. Reformed theology emphasizes that sanctification is "progressive," through the continuous action of God's Word and the work of the Spirit. The Lord "sanctifies himself for their sake" (v.19), becoming our example and foundation for sanctification.

**The Nature of the Church**
The Lord's prayer for the church's unity (vv.21-23) reveals that the church is not a human organization, but God's household (Eph 2:19), Christ's body (Eph 1:22-23), and the Spirit's temple (1 Cor 3:16). The church's unity is not manufactured by human effort, but life unity that already exists in Christ. Our responsibility is to "Endeavouring to keep the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace." (Eph 4:3).

## 基督的影子（中文）

**永生的定义**
"认识你—独一的真神，并且认识你所差来的耶稣基督，这就是永生"（3节）。旧约圣徒盼望永生，却不知永生的确切内容。基督在此启示：永生就是认识神、认识基督。这"认识"应验了耶利米书31:34新约的预言："他们各人不再教导自己的邻舍和自己的弟兄说：'你该认识耶和华'，因为他们从最小的到至大的都必认识我。"

**未有世界以先的荣耀**
"父阿，现在求你使我同你享荣耀，就是未有世界以先，我同你所有的荣耀"（5节）。这话启示基督的先存性。箴言8:22-31智慧的自述（"在耶和华造化的起头，在太初创造万物之先，就有了我"）预表了道的先存。约翰福音1:1-3更清楚宣告："太初有道，道与神同在，道就是神。"基督不是被造物，而是造物主；不是在时间里开始，而是从永恒就存在。

**灭亡之子**
"其中除了那灭亡之子，没有一个灭亡的"（12节）。"灭亡之子"（ὁ υἱὸς τῆς ἀπωλείας）这称呼在新约只出现两次：犹大（这里）和敌基督（帖后2:3）。这暗示犹大的背叛预表末世敌基督的背叛。诗篇41:9大卫被密友出卖的经历预表基督被犹大出卖："连我知己的朋友，我所倚靠、吃过我饭的也用脚踢我。"

**成圣的根基**
"我为他们的缘故，自己分别为圣"（19节）。旧约大祭司进入至圣所前要先洁净自己（利16章）。基督是无罪的，却"分别为圣"——不是从罪中分别出来，而是为了完成救赎使命而分别自己。祂的成圣是我们成圣的根基：我们之所以能成圣，是因祂先为我们成圣（来10:10）。

**父爱子的爱**
"你爱他们如同爱我一样"（23节）。这是整本圣经最震撼的启示之一。父对子的爱是无限的、永恒的（约3:35；5:20）。而主说,父爱我们就像爱祂一样！这只可能是因为我们"在基督里"——因着与基督的联合,父看我们如同看基督。这是何等的恩典！

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**The Definition of Eternal Life**
"And this is eternal life, that they know you, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom you have sent" (v.3). Old Testament saints hoped for eternal life but did not know its exact content. Christ reveals here: eternal life is knowing God, knowing Christ. This "knowing" fulfills the prophecy of the new covenant in Jeremiah 31:34: "And no longer shall each one teach his neighbor and each his brother, saying, 'Know the Lord,' for they shall all know me, from the least of them to the greatest."

**Glory Before the World Existed**
"And now, Father, glorify me in your own presence with the glory that I had with you before the world existed" (v.5). These words reveal Christ's pre-existence. Proverbs 8:22-31, wisdom's self-description ("The Lord possessed me at the beginning of his work, the first of his acts of old"), prefigures the pre-existence of the Word. John 1:1-3 declares even more clearly: "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." Christ is not a creature, but the Creator; He did not begin in time, but has existed from eternity.

**The Son of Destruction**
"Not one of them has been lost except the son of destruction" (v.12). "Son of destruction" (ὁ υἱὸς τῆς ἀπωλείας) appears only twice in the New Testament: Judas (here) and the antichrist (2 Thess 2:3). This suggests Judas' betrayal prefigures the end-time antichrist's betrayal. Psalm 41:9, David's experience of being betrayed by a close friend, prefigures Christ being betrayed by Judas: "Even my close friend in whom I trusted, who ate my bread, has lifted his heel against me."

**The Foundation of Sanctification**
"And for their sake I consecrate myself" (v.19). The Old Testament high priest had to first cleanse himself before entering the Most Holy Place (Leviticus 16). Christ is sinless, yet "consecrates Himself"—not separating from sin, but setting Himself apart to complete the mission of redemption. His sanctification is the foundation of our sanctification: we can be sanctified because He first sanctified Himself for us (Heb 10:10).

**The Father's Love for the Son**
"You loved them even as you loved me" (v.23). This is one of the most shocking revelations in all Scripture. The Father's love for the Son is infinite and eternal (John 3:35; 5:20). And the Lord says the Father loves us as He loves Him! This is only possible because we are "in Christ"—through union with Christ, the Father sees us as He sees Christ. What grace!
