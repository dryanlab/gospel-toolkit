## 正文（中文）

我约翰永远记得主耶稣说"我是好牧人"时那温柔又坚定的声音。第9章那瞎子被赶出会堂，主就用羊圈和牧人的比喻来安慰他，也向所有跟随祂的人显明祂的心肠。

主说："我实实在在地告诉你们,人进羊圈，不从门进去，倒从别处爬进去，那人就是贼，就是强盗。从门进去的，才是羊的牧人。"祂指的是谁呢？那些假教师、假牧人——他们不是神差来的,乃是为自己的利益来剥削羊群。他们爬墙进入，不是为了牧养，乃是为了偷窃、杀害、毁坏。

"我就是门；凡从我进来的,必然得救，并且出入得草吃。"（10:9）主是唯一的门——不是众门之一，乃是唯一进入救恩的道路。这话何等排他！但也何等确定——从祂进来的必然得救，没有人能从父手里把他们夺去。

"我是好牧人；好牧人为羊舍命。"（10:11）这与雇工不同。雇工不过是为工价来的，看见狼来就逃走，留下羊被撕裂。但好牧人认识祂的羊,羊也认识祂——这认识不是头脑知识,乃是亲密的关系，如同父认识子、子认识父那样深刻。

主说："我另外有羊，不是这圈里的；我必须领他们来,他们也要听我的声音，并且要合成一群，归一个牧人了。"（10:16）这话震撼我心！主不仅来拯救犹太人,也要拯救外邦人——我们这些散居在地极的人，也要被祂领进羊圈,与犹太信徒合成一群。不再分犹太人、希腊人，奴仆、自主的，都在基督里成为一了（加3:28）。

"我父爱我；因我将命舍去,好再取回来。没有人夺我的命去，是我自己舍的。我有权柄舍了,也有权柄取回来。"（10:17-18）主的死不是被动的、无奈的，乃是主动的、心甘情愿的。祂有权柄舍命，也有权柄取回——这是神的权能！祂从死里复活,不是因为死亡拘禁不住祂（徒2:24），乃是因为祂自己有复活的权柄。

到了修殿节（冬天），犹太人围着主说："你叫我们犹疑不定到几时呢？你若是基督，就明明地告诉我们。"主回答说："我已经告诉你们,你们不信。我奉我父之名所行的事可以为我作见证；只是你们不信,因为你们不是我的羊。我的羊听我的声音，我也认识他们，他们也跟着我。我又赐给他们永生；他们永不灭亡，谁也不能从我手里把他们夺去。"（10:25-28）

这是何等宝贵的应许！属主的羊有永生，永不灭亡，无人能夺去——这是救恩的确据，是圣徒坚忍的根基。不是我们紧紧抓住神，乃是神紧紧抓住我们！我们软弱会失脚，但主的手永不松开。

然后主说出了那震撼人心的话："我与父原为一。"（10:30）犹太人立刻明白这话的意思，拿起石头要打祂："你是个人，反将自己当作神。"主引用诗篇82:6("我曾说你们是神")说：若那些承受神道的人尚且称为神，何况父所分别为圣又差到世间来的，祂自称是神的儿子,你们还向祂说僭妄的话吗？

弟兄姊妹，第10章让我看见主对羊群的爱——祂认识每一只羊,叫他们的名；祂为羊舍命，使他们得永生；祂保守他们，无人能从祂手中夺去。那些假牧人、雇工只顾自己，但好牧人把羊看得比自己的命还宝贵。我们这些卑微、软弱、常常跌倒的罪人，竟是祂用宝血重价买来的，是祂日夜看顾、永不撇弃的。何等的爱！何等的恩典！

## Content (English)

I, John, will forever remember the gentle yet firm voice of the Lord Jesus when He said "I am the good shepherd." After the blind man in chapter 9 was cast out of the synagogue, the Lord used the parable of the sheepfold and shepherd to comfort him and reveal His heart to all who follow Him.

The Lord said: "Truly, truly, I say to you, he who does not enter the sheepfold by the door but climbs in by another way, that man is a thief and a robber. But he who enters by the door is the shepherd of the sheep." Whom was He referring to? Those false teachers, false shepherds—they were not sent by God but came for their own profit to exploit the flock. They climb over the wall, not to shepherd but to steal, kill, and destroy.

"I am the door. If anyone enters by me, he will be saved and will go in and out and find pasture." (10:9) The Lord is the only door—not one door among many, but the only way into salvation. How exclusive these words! But also how certain—those who enter through Him will surely be saved; no one can snatch them from the Father's hand.

"I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep." (10:11) This is different from hired hands. Hired hands come only for wages; when they see the wolf coming, they flee, leaving the sheep to be torn apart. But the good shepherd knows His sheep, and the sheep know Him—this knowledge is not mere intellectual information but intimate relationship, as deep as the Father knowing the Son and the Son knowing the Father.

The Lord said: "And I have other sheep that are not of this fold. I must bring them also, and they will listen to my voice. So there will be one flock, one shepherd." (10:16) These words shook my heart! The Lord came not only to save Jews but also Gentiles—we who are scattered to the ends of the earth will also be brought into the sheepfold, forming one flock with Jewish believers. No longer Jew or Greek, slave or free—all are one in Christ (Gal 3:28).

"For this reason the Father loves me, because I lay down my life that I may take it up again. No one takes it from me, but I lay it down of my own accord. I have authority to lay it down, and I have authority to take it up again." (10:17-18) The Lord's death was not passive or helpless but active and willing. He has authority to lay down His life and authority to take it up again—this is divine power! He rose from the dead not because death could not hold Him (Acts 2:24), but because He Himself had authority to resurrect.

At the Feast of Dedication (in winter), the Jews surrounded the Lord saying: "How long will you keep us in suspense? If you are the Christ, tell us plainly." The Lord answered: "I told you, and you do not believe. The works that I do in my Father's name bear witness about me, but you do not believe because you are not among my sheep. My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me. I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand." (10:25-28)

What a precious promise! The sheep who belong to the Lord have eternal life, will never perish, and no one can snatch them away—this is assurance of salvation, the foundation of perseverance of the saints. It is not we who tightly grasp God, but God who tightly grasps us! We are weak and may stumble, but the Lord's hand will never let go.

Then the Lord spoke those shocking words: "I and the Father are one." (10:30) The Jews immediately understood the meaning, picking up stones to stone Him: "You, being a man, make yourself God." The Lord quoted Psalm 82:6 ("I said, you are gods") saying: If those who received the word of God were called gods, how much more the One whom the Father consecrated and sent into the world—should He call Himself the Son of God, do you accuse Him of blasphemy?

Brothers and sisters, chapter 10 shows me the Lord's love for His flock—He knows every sheep, calls them by name; He lays down His life for the sheep, giving them eternal life; He preserves them, and no one can snatch them from His hand. Those false shepherds and hired hands care only for themselves, but the good shepherd values the sheep more than His own life. We, these lowly, weak, often-stumbling sinners, are those He bought with His precious blood, whom He watches over day and night and will never forsake. What love! What grace!

## 历史背景（中文）

第10章发生在住棚节之后、修殿节之前的时间段（10:22"那时,在耶路撒冷有修殿节,是冬天的时候"）。修殿节（Hanukkah / Feast of Dedication）庆祝主前164年犹大·马加比（Judas Maccabeus）洁净圣殿、恢复敬拜的事迹——这是在安提阿古四世（Antiochus IV Epiphanes）亵渎圣殿、强迫犹太人敬拜希腊神后的恢复。

羊圈和牧人的比喻在旧约有深厚根源。以西结书34章严厉批评以色列的假牧人："祸哉！以色列的牧人只知牧养自己...瘦弱的,你们没有养壮；有病的,你们没有医治...失丧的,你们没有寻找"（结34:2-4）,然后神应许："我必立一牧人照管他们,牧养他们,就是我的仆人大卫"（结34:23）。耶稣宣称"我是好牧人",直接应验这弥赛亚预言。

"所罗门的廊下"（10:23）是圣殿外院的柱廊,是犹太人常聚集辩论的地方。这里成为主与犹太宗教领袖最后公开对质的场景之一——祂宣称与父原为一,他们控告祂僭妄。这为11章拉撒路复活后公会决议杀祂（11:53）以及受难周的审判埋下伏笔。

第一世纪的巴勒斯坦，牧羊是常见职业。牧人常住在野外,与羊群同吃同住，认识每一只羊的性情、习性。羊也认识牧人的声音——这不是理论,乃是日常生活的现实。主用这比喻,把抽象的神学真理（拣选、保守、救赎）用百姓熟悉的图像表达出来。

## Historical Context (English)

Chapter 10 takes place between the Feast of Tabernacles and the Feast of Dedication (10:22 "It was the Feast of Dedication at Jerusalem, and it was winter"). The Feast of Dedication (Hanukkah) commemorates Judas Maccabeus' cleansing of the temple and restoration of worship in 164 BC—after Antiochus IV Epiphanes desecrated the temple and forced Jews to worship Greek gods.

The parable of the sheepfold and shepherd has deep Old Testament roots. Ezekiel 34 severely criticizes Israel's false shepherds: "Son of man, prophesy against the shepherds of Israel, prophesy, and say unto them, Thus saith the Lord GOD unto the shepherds; Woe be to the shepherds of Israel that do feed themselves! should not the shepherds feed the flocks? Ye eat the fat, and ye clothe you with the wool, ye kill them that are fed: but ye feed not the flock. The diseased have ye not strengthened, neither have ye healed that which was sick, neither have ye bound up that which was broken, neither have ye brought again that which was driven away, neither have ye sought that which was lost; but with force and with cruelty have ye ruled them." (Ezek 34:2-4), then God promises: "And I will set up one shepherd over them, and he shall feed them, even my servant David; he shall feed them, and he shall be their shepherd." (Ezek 34:23). Jesus' declaration "I am the good shepherd" directly fulfills this Messianic prophecy.

"Solomon's Portico" (10:23) was a colonnade in the outer court of the temple, a common gathering place for Jewish debate. This became one of the last public confrontations between the Lord and Jewish religious leaders—He claimed to be one with the Father, they accused Him of blasphemy. This lays groundwork for the Sanhedrin's decision to kill Him after Lazarus' resurrection in chapter 11 (11:53) and the trials during Passion Week.

In first-century Palestine, shepherding was a common occupation. Shepherds often lived outdoors, eating and dwelling with their flocks, knowing every sheep's temperament and habits. The sheep also recognized the shepherd's voice—this was not theory but daily reality. The Lord used this parable to express abstract theological truths (election, preservation, redemption) through images familiar to the people.

## 经文结构（中文）

**一、羊圈与牧人的比喻（10:1-6）** — 贼与强盗从别处爬进去;真牧人从门进入。羊认识牧人的声音,跟着他；不认识生人的声音,必要逃跑。门徒当时不明白这比喻的意思。

**二、"我是门"的宣告（10:7-10）** — 主解释：祂是羊的门,凡在祂以先来的都是贼、强盗（指假弥赛亚、假教师）。凡从祂进来的必然得救,出入得草吃。盗贼来无非要偷窃、杀害、毁坏;祂来了是要叫羊得生命,并且得的更丰盛。

**三、"我是好牧人"的宣告（10:11-18）** — 好牧人为羊舍命；雇工看见狼来就逃走。祂认识羊,羊也认识祂,正如父认识子、子认识父。祂另外有羊（外邦人），要领他们来,合成一群归一个牧人。祂有权柄舍命,也有权柄取回——这是父给祂的命令。

**四、犹太人的分争（10:19-21）** — 有人说"他是被鬼附着,而且疯了";有人说"这不是鬼附之人所说的话,鬼岂能叫瞎子的眼睛开了呢？"——回应第9章的神迹。

**五、修殿节的对质（10:22-30）** — 犹太人要祂明说是否基督。主回答:已经说了,你们不信,因你们不是我的羊。我的羊听我的声音,我赐他们永生,他们永不灭亡,谁也不能从我手里、从我父手里把他们夺去。"我与父原为一。"

**六、最后的争论（10:31-42）** — 犹太人拿石头要打祂,控告祂僭妄（"你是个人,反将自己当作神"）。主引用诗篇82:6辩护,并说:"我若不行我父的事,你们就不必信我；我若行了,你们纵然不信我,也当信这些事,叫你们又知道又明白父在我里面,我也在父里面。"他们又要拿祂,祂却逃出他们的手,往约旦河外去——那里有许多人信了祂。

全章结构清晰：从比喻到解释,从"我是门"到"我是好牧人",最后以"我与父原为一"达到高潮。主题一贯：基督是唯一的救主、真牧人,祂与父同等、同荣、同权。

## Structure (English)

**I. Parable of Sheepfold and Shepherd (10:1-6)** — Thieves and robbers climb in by another way; the true shepherd enters by the door. Sheep recognize the shepherd's voice and follow him; do not recognize a stranger's voice and flee. The disciples did not understand the parable at that time.

**II. "I Am the Door" Declaration (10:7-10)** — The Lord explains: He is the door of the sheep; all who came before Him are thieves and robbers (referring to false messiahs, false teachers). Whoever enters through Him will be saved, go in and out and find pasture. Thieves come to steal, kill, and destroy; He came that they may have life and have it abundantly.

**III. "I Am the Good Shepherd" Declaration (10:11-18)** — The good shepherd lays down His life for the sheep; hired hands flee when they see the wolf coming. He knows the sheep, and the sheep know Him, just as the Father knows the Son and the Son knows the Father. He has other sheep (Gentiles) to bring, forming one flock under one shepherd. He has authority to lay down His life and authority to take it up again—this is the Father's command.

**IV. Division Among Jews (10:19-21)** — Some said "He is demon-possessed and insane"; others said "These are not the words of one oppressed by a demon. Can a demon open the eyes of the blind?"—responding to chapter 9's miracle.

**V. Confrontation at Feast of Dedication (10:22-30)** — Jews wanted Him to tell plainly if He is the Christ. The Lord answered: already told you, you don't believe because you are not my sheep. My sheep hear my voice, I give them eternal life, they will never perish, no one can snatch them from my hand or from my Father's hand. "I and the Father are one."

**VI. Final Dispute (10:31-42)** — Jews picked up stones to stone Him, accusing Him of blasphemy ("You, being a man, make yourself God"). The Lord quoted Psalm 82:6 in defense, and said: "If I am not doing the works of my Father, then do not believe me; but if I do them, even though you do not believe me, believe the works, that you may know and understand that the Father is in me and I am in the Father." They again sought to arrest Him, but He escaped from their hands and went beyond the Jordan—many believed in Him there.

Clear chapter structure: from parable to explanation, from "I am the door" to "I am the good shepherd," finally climaxing with "I and the Father are one." Consistent theme: Christ is the only Savior, the true shepherd, equal, co-glorious, and co-authoritative with the Father.

## 神学要点（中文）

**拣选教义（Particular Redemption）：** "我的羊听我的声音...你们不信,因为你们不是我的羊"（10:26-27）——这清楚显示救恩是特定的、有限的（limited atonement）。不是所有人都是主的羊,只有父所赐给子的人是祂的羊。改革宗"郁金香"（TULIP）五要义中的"L"（Limited Atonement / Particular Redemption）不是说基督的死功效有限,乃是说救赎的对象是特定的——祂为羊舍命,不是为山羊；为新妇,不是为仇敌。

**救恩的确据（Assurance of Salvation）：** "我又赐给他们永生;他们永不灭亡,谁也不能从我手里把他们夺去。我父把羊赐给我,他比万有都大,谁也不能从我父手里把他们夺去。"（10:28-29）这是圣经中关于救恩永恒保障最明确的经文之一。真信徒不会失落,不是因为自己够坚强,乃是因为父和子的双重保守——我们在子的手里,子在父的手里,双重保险！改革宗强调：圣徒的坚忍（Perseverance）不是靠人的努力,乃是神的保守。

**基督的神性与三一论：** "我与父原为一"（10:30）——希腊文"hen"（一）是中性单数,指本质的合一,不是位格的混同。父与子是两个位格,但同一本质、同一神性。这是三一论的核心教义。亚他那修信经说："我们敬拜三位一体的独一神,在三一中独一,在独一中三一;既不混淆位格,也不分裂本质。"主耶稣宣称与父"原为一",不是说祂是父,乃是说祂与父同等、同荣、同权。

**基督的代赎（Substitutionary Atonement）：** "好牧人为羊舍命"（10:11,15,17-18）——这"为"（hyper）字有代替、代表的含义。基督的死不仅是榜样、不仅是道德感化,乃是代赎性的牺牲——祂替羊死,使羊免于死。改革宗坚持刑罚代赎论（Penal Substitution）：基督在十架上承受了选民当受的刑罚,满足了神公义的要求,成就了救赎。祂是主动舍命（"我有权柄舍了"），不是被动受害；祂是心甘情愿（"父爱我,因我将命舍去"），不是无奈牺牲。

## Theology (English)

**Doctrine of Election (Particular Redemption):** "My sheep hear my voice... but you do not believe because you are not among my sheep" (10:26-27)—this clearly shows salvation is specific and limited (limited atonement). Not all people are the Lord's sheep; only those given by the Father to the Son are His sheep. The "L" (Limited Atonement / Particular Redemption) in Reformed "TULIP" does not mean Christ's death is limited in efficacy, but that the object of redemption is specific—He laid down His life for the sheep, not goats; for the bride, not enemies.

**Assurance of Salvation:** "I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand. My Father, who has given them to me, is greater than all, and no one is able to snatch them out of the Father's hand." (10:28-29) This is one of Scripture's clearest passages on eternal security of salvation. True believers will not be lost, not because they are strong enough, but because of the dual preservation by Father and Son—we are in the Son's hand, the Son is in the Father's hand, double insurance! Reformed theology emphasizes: Perseverance of the saints is not by human effort but by God's preservation.

**Deity of Christ and Trinity:** "I and the Father are one" (10:30)—the Greek "hen" (one) is neuter singular, referring to unity of essence, not confusion of persons. Father and Son are two persons but same essence, same deity. This is the core doctrine of the Trinity. The Athanasian Creed says: "We worship one God in Trinity and Trinity in Unity, neither confusing the persons nor dividing the substance." The Lord Jesus' claim to be "one" with the Father does not mean He is the Father, but that He is equal, co-glorious, and co-authoritative with the Father.

**Christ's Substitutionary Atonement:** "The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep" (10:11,15,17-18)—this "for" (hyper) has connotations of substitution and representation. Christ's death is not merely example or moral influence, but substitutionary sacrifice—He died for the sheep, so the sheep are spared death. Reformed theology insists on penal substitution: Christ on the cross bore the punishment the elect deserved, satisfied God's justice, and accomplished redemption. He actively laid down His life ("I have authority to lay it down"), not passively suffered; He was willing ("For this reason the Father loves me, because I lay down my life"), not reluctantly sacrificed.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**大卫的预表：** 大卫本是牧童,被膏为王后仍以牧人自称："耶和华是我的牧者"（诗23:1）。神应许大卫："我必立一牧人照管他们,牧养他们,就是我的仆人大卫"（结34:23）——这不是指历史上的大卫复活,乃是指大卫的子孙、弥赛亚基督。祂是真大卫,永远的君王,也是好牧人。

**摩西的预表：** 摩西在旷野牧羊四十年,然后被神呼召带领以色列"羊群"出埃及（诗77:20"你曾藉摩西和亚伦的手引导你的百姓,好像羊群一般"）。但摩西只能带百姓到旷野,不能使他们进入安息；基督却要领祂的羊进入永远的安息、天上的迦南。

**逾越节羔羊与赎罪祭：** "好牧人为羊舍命"——牧人为羊死,这在旧约献祭制度中是颠倒的（本该羊为人死）。这显明基督的代赎何等超越：不是我们为祂献祭,乃是祂为我们献祭；不是羊死代人,乃是牧人死代羊。祂既是祭司,又是祭物；既是牧人,又是羔羊。

**以色列的牧人耶和华：** 诗篇80:1"领约瑟如领羊群之以色列的牧者啊,求你留心听！"以赛亚书40:11"他必像牧人牧养自己的羊群,用膀臂聚集羊羔抱在怀中,慢慢引导那乳养小羊的。"旧约多次称耶和华为以色列的牧者。当耶稣说"我是好牧人",祂宣称自己就是那位旧约的耶和华、以色列的神、雅各的牧者。

**会幕/圣殿的门：** 旧约会幕和圣殿只有一个门进入（出26:36,王上6:8）。"我就是门"呼应这预表——进入神面前、得救恩,只有一条路,就是基督。使徒行传4:12"除他以外,别无拯救；因为在天下人间,没有赐下别的名,我们可以靠着得救。"基督是独一的中保（提前2:5）,独一的门。

**"一群一牧人"与新约教会：** 以西结书37:22-24预言："我要使他们在那地...成为一国...永不再分为二国...我的仆人大卫必作他们的王,众民必归一个牧人。"这在教会中应验——犹太人和外邦人在基督里合而为一（弗2:14-16），不再是两个民族,乃是一个新人、一个身体、一个羊群、一个牧人。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**David as Type:** David was a shepherd boy, and after being anointed king still identified as a shepherd: "The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want." (Ps 23:1). God promised David: "And I will set up one shepherd over them, and he shall feed them, even my servant David; he shall feed them, and he shall be their shepherd." (Ezek 34:23)—this does not refer to historical David resurrected, but to David's descendant, Messiah Christ. He is the true David, eternal King, and also the good shepherd.

**Moses as Type:** Moses shepherded sheep in the wilderness for forty years, then was called by God to lead Israel's "flock" out of Egypt (Ps 77:20 "You led your people like a flock by the hand of Moses and Aaron"). But Moses could only bring the people to the wilderness, not into rest; Christ will lead His sheep into eternal rest, heavenly Canaan.

**Passover Lamb and Sin Offering:** "The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep"—the shepherd dying for sheep reverses the Old Testament sacrificial system (sheep should die for man). This shows how Christ's substitution transcends: not we offering sacrifices for Him, but He offering sacrifice for us; not sheep dying for man, but shepherd dying for sheep. He is both priest and offering; both shepherd and lamb.

**Yahweh, Shepherd of Israel:** Psalm 80:1 "Give ear, O Shepherd of Israel, you who lead Joseph like a flock!" Isaiah 40:11 "He will tend his flock like a shepherd; he will gather the lambs in his arms; he will carry them in his bosom, and gently lead those that are with young." The Old Testament repeatedly calls Yahweh the shepherd of Israel. When Jesus said "I am the good shepherd," He claimed to be that Yahweh of the Old Testament, the God of Israel, the Shepherd of Jacob.

**Door of Tabernacle/Temple:** The Old Testament tabernacle and temple had only one door for entry (Exod 26:36, 1 Kgs 6:8). "I am the door" echoes this type—there is only one way to enter God's presence and obtain salvation: Christ. Acts 4:12 "And there is salvation in no one else, for there is no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved." Christ is the sole mediator (1 Tim 2:5), the sole door.

**"One Flock, One Shepherd" and the New Testament Church:** Ezekiel 37:22-24 prophesied: "I will make them one nation in the land... and they shall never again be two nations... My servant David shall be king over them, and they shall all have one shepherd." This is fulfilled in the church—Jews and Gentiles are one in Christ (Eph 2:14-16), no longer two peoples but one new man, one body, one flock, one shepherd.
