## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是约翰，主所爱的门徒。今天我要讲述一件令所有门徒都震惊的事——主耶稣与一个撒玛利亚妇人的对话。

一、必须经过撒玛利亚（4:1-6）

我们从犹太地往加利利去，主说"必须经过撒玛利亚"（约4:4）。你们要知道，犹太人和撒玛利亚人素来没有往来。大多数犹太人宁可绕道约旦河东岸，也不愿踏入撒玛利亚的土地。但主说"必须"——这不是地理上的必须，而是救恩计划的必须。在雅各井旁，有一个灵魂等着他。

主走路疲乏了，就坐在雅各井旁。那时约有午正。一个撒玛利亚妇人来打水——午正打水很不寻常，其他妇人都是清晨来的。她选在最热的时候来，是为了避开人群。她的过去让她不敢见人。

二、活水的对话（4:7-26）

"耶稣对他说：请你给我水喝"（约4:7）。多么简单的一句话，却打破了多少堵墙——种族的墙、性别的墙、宗教的墙。犹太拉比不会单独与妇人说话，更不会向撒玛利亚人要水。

妇人惊讶地问："你既是犹太人，怎么向我一个撒玛利亚妇人要水喝呢？"（约4:9）。

主的回答开启了一场改变她一生的对话："你若知道神的恩赐，和对你说'给我水喝'的是谁，你必早求他，他也必早给了你活水"（约4:10）。

弟兄姐妹，主用了一个奇妙的方法——从**她的需要**（水）引向**他的供应**（活水）。妇人关心的是打水的辛苦，主关心的是她灵魂的干渴。

"凡喝这水的还要再渴；人若喝我所赐的水就永远不渴。我所赐的水要在他里头成为泉源，直涌到永生"（约4:13-14）。

世上一切的满足都是暂时的——金钱、名誉、感情、享乐——喝了还要再渴。唯有基督所赐的活水，能永远满足人心最深处的渴望。

然后主说了一句话，直击她的痛处："你去叫你丈夫也到这里来"（约4:16）。妇人说："我没有丈夫。"主说："你说没有丈夫是不错的。你已经有五个丈夫，你现在有的并不是你的丈夫"（约4:17-18）。

她有过五个丈夫，现在和第六个男人同居。五次婚姻，五次寻找爱与安全感，五次失望。她的生命就像那口井——打了又打，喝了还渴。**主不是要揭她的伤疤，而是要指出：你一直在错误的泉源里找水。**

妇人想转移话题，谈起敬拜的地点——撒玛利亚人在基利心山，犹太人在耶路撒冷。主说了一句石破天惊的话："时候将到，如今就是了，那真正拜父的，要用心灵和诚实拜他"（约4:23）。敬拜不在乎地点，在乎的是心灵。

最后，妇人说："我知道弥赛亚要来。"主说："这和你说话的就是他！"（约4:25-26）。在整本约翰福音中，这是主最清楚、最直接的自我启示之一——而他选择向一个被社会遗弃的撒玛利亚妇人说出来。

三、撒玛利亚人的信（4:27-42）

妇人留下水罐子，跑进城去，对众人说："你们来看！有一个人将我素来所行的一切事都给我说出来了，莫非这就是基督么？"（约4:29）。

她留下了水罐子——因为她找到了活水，地上的水罐子不再重要了。许多撒玛利亚人信了。他们对妇人说："现在我们信，不是因为你的话，是我们亲自听见了，知道这真是救世主"（约4:42）。

四、大臣之子得医治（4:46-54）

回到加利利后，有一个大臣来求主医治他将死的儿子。主说了一句话："回去罢，你的儿子活了"（约4:50）。大臣**信了**主的话就回去了——还没有看见，就信了。回到家时，仆人告诉他：你的儿子昨日午后一时热就退了——正是主说那句话的时刻。

这是约翰福音记载的第二个记号。第一个记号（迦拿变水为酒）是在喜乐中显明荣耀；这第二个记号是在绝望中彰显权能。**信心不需要先看见才相信——"你的儿子活了"，信了就走，这就是信心。**

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am John, the disciple whom the Lord loved. Today I will recount something that astonished all of us disciples—the Lord Jesus' conversation with a Samaritan woman.

I. He Had to Pass Through Samaria (4:1-6)

We were traveling from Judea to Galilee when the Lord said He "And he must needs go through Samaria." (John 4:4). You must understand that Jews had no dealings with Samaritans. Most Jews would rather detour east of the Jordan than set foot on Samaritan soil. But the Lord said "had to"—this was not a geographical necessity but a redemptive one. At Jacob's well, a soul was waiting for Him.

The Lord, wearied from the journey, sat down by Jacob's well. It was about the sixth hour. A Samaritan woman came to draw water—drawing at noon was unusual; other women came at dawn. She chose the hottest hour to avoid the crowd. Her past made her afraid to face people.

II. The Living Water Conversation (4:7-26)

"There cometh a woman of Samaria to draw water: Jesus saith unto her, Give me to drink." (John 4:7). Such a simple request, yet it shattered so many walls—racial, gender, and religious. A Jewish rabbi would not speak alone with a woman, let alone ask a Samaritan for water.

The woman was astonished: "Then saith the woman of Samaria unto him, How is it that thou, being a Jew, askest drink of me, which am a woman of Samaria? for the Jews have no dealings with the Samaritans." (John 4:9).

The Lord's reply began a life-changing conversation: "Jesus answered and said unto her, If thou knewest the gift of God, and who it is that saith to thee, Give me to drink; thou wouldest have asked of him, and he would have given thee living water." (John 4:10).

Brothers and sisters, the Lord used a wonderful method—moving from **her need** (water) to **His provision** (living water). The woman was concerned about the labor of drawing water; the Lord was concerned about the thirst of her soul.

"Jesus answered and said unto her, Whosoever drinketh of this water shall thirst again: But whosoever drinketh of the water that I shall give him shall never thirst; but the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well of water springing up into everlasting life." (John 4:13-14).

Every earthly satisfaction is temporary—money, fame, relationships, pleasure—drink and you will thirst again. Only the living water Christ gives can eternally satisfy the deepest longing of the human heart.

Then the Lord spoke a word that struck at her deepest wound: "Jesus saith unto her, Go, call thy husband, and come hither." (John 4:16). The woman said, "I have no husband." The Lord replied, "The woman answered and said, I have no husband. Jesus said unto her, Thou hast well said, I have no husband: For thou hast had five husbands; and he whom thou now hast is not thy husband: in that saidst thou truly." (John 4:17-18).

Five marriages, five searches for love and security, five disappointments. Her life was like that well—drawing and drawing, drinking yet always thirsting. **The Lord was not exposing her scars but pointing out: you have been looking for water in the wrong wells.**

The woman tried to change the subject, raising the question of worship locations—Samaritans at Mount Gerizim, Jews in Jerusalem. The Lord gave a stunning answer: "But the hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth: for the Father seeketh such to worship him." (John 4:23). Worship is not about location but about the heart.

Finally, the woman said, "I know that Messiah is coming." The Lord said, "The woman saith unto him, I know that Messias cometh, which is called Christ: when he is come, he will tell us all things. Jesus saith unto her, I that speak unto thee am he." (John 4:25-26). In all of John's Gospel, this is one of the Lord's clearest, most direct self-revelations—and He chose to speak it to a socially outcast Samaritan woman.

III. The Samaritans Believe (4:27-42)

The woman left her water jar, ran into the city, and told the people: "Come, see a man, which told me all things that ever I did: is not this the Christ?" (John 4:29).

She left her water jar behind—she had found living water; the earthly jar no longer mattered. Many Samaritans believed. They told the woman: "And said unto the woman, Now we believe, not because of thy saying: for we have heard him ourselves, and know that this is indeed the Christ, the Saviour of the world." (John 4:42).

IV. The Official's Son Healed (4:46-54)

Back in Galilee, an official came begging the Lord to heal his dying son. The Lord spoke one sentence: "Jesus saith unto him, Go thy way; thy son liveth. And the man believed the word that Jesus had spoken unto him, and he went his way." (John 4:50). The official **believed** the Lord's word and went home—he believed before seeing. When he arrived, his servants told him the fever left at the seventh hour yesterday—the very moment the Lord had spoken.

This was the second sign recorded in John's Gospel. The first sign (water into wine at Cana) revealed glory in joy; this second sign displayed power in despair. **Faith does not need to see before believing—"your son will live"; he believed and went. That is faith.**

## 历史背景（中文）

约翰福音第四章的历史背景涉及犹太人与撒玛利亚人数百年的仇恨。公元前722年，亚述灭北国以色列后，将外族迁入撒玛利亚，与残留的以色列人通婚。犹太人视撒玛利亚人为混血的背道者。**雅各井**位于示剑附近（今纳布卢斯），深约30米，至今仍存。"午正"即中午十二点（罗马计时）或下午六点（犹太计时），学者多取前者。**大臣**（βασιλικός）可能是希律安提帕宫廷的官员，从迦百农到迦拿约30公里，步行需一天。

## Historical Context (English)

The historical background of John 4 involves centuries of enmity between Jews and Samaritans. After Assyria destroyed the northern kingdom in 722 BC, foreigners were settled in Samaria and intermarried with remaining Israelites. Jews regarded Samaritans as mixed-blood apostates. **Jacob's well** is near Shechem (modern Nablus), about 30 meters deep, and still exists today. "The sixth hour" is noon (Roman reckoning) or 6 PM (Jewish reckoning); most scholars favor the former. The **official** (βασιλικός) was likely a courtier of Herod Antipas; the journey from Capernaum to Cana was about 30 kilometers, a day's walk.

## 经文结构（中文）

本章分为两大叙事单元：**（一）撒玛利亚妇人**（4:1-42）——包含三个层次的推进：耶稣与妇人的私人对话（4:7-26）→妇人向城中人作见证（4:27-30）→撒玛利亚人亲自信主（4:39-42）。从个人到群体，从听闻到亲历。**（二）大臣之子得医治**（4:43-54）——第二个记号（σημεῖον），与第一个记号（2:1-11）形成对比：迦拿变水为酒是在喜宴中，大臣之子是在绝望中。两个记号框架了约翰福音的"记号神学"——神迹不只是奇事，更是指向基督身份的**记号**。

## Structure (English)

The chapter divides into two narrative units: **(1) The Samaritan woman** (4:1-42)—with three progressive layers: Jesus' private conversation with the woman (4:7-26) → the woman witnesses to the townspeople (4:27-30) → the Samaritans believe personally (4:39-42). From individual to community, from hearsay to firsthand faith. **(2) The official's son healed** (4:43-54)—the second sign (σημεῖον), contrasting the first (2:1-11): water to wine occurred at a joyful feast; the official's son at the brink of death. These two signs frame John's "sign theology"—miracles are not just wonders but **signs** pointing to Christ's identity.

## 神学要点（中文）

本章的核心神学主题是**基督是活水的赐予者**。改革宗神学强调**恩典的主动性**——不是妇人来找基督，而是基督"必须"经过撒玛利亚来找她。这正是有效呼召的写照：神在永恒中拣选，在历史中寻找。其次，**心灵和诚实的敬拜**（4:23-24）打破了一切外在形式主义——敬拜不在乎地点、仪式、传统，在乎的是被圣灵更新的心灵和合乎真理的信仰。韦敏斯德信条第二十一章教导：可蒙悦纳的敬拜神的方式，乃是神自己所设立的。第三，大臣之子的医治彰显了**唯独信心**——他没有看见就信了主的话，这是约翰福音所赞扬的信心模式（参20:29）。

## Theology (English)

The central theological theme is **Christ as the giver of living water**. Reformed theology emphasizes **the initiative of grace**—the woman did not come seeking Christ; Christ "had to" pass through Samaria to find her. This is a portrait of effectual calling: God elects in eternity, seeks in history. Second, **worship in spirit and truth** (4:23-24) shatters all external formalism—worship is not about location, ritual, or tradition, but about a heart renewed by the Spirit and faith grounded in truth. The Westminster Confession (ch. 21): "The acceptable way of worshipping God is instituted by Himself, and so limited by His own revealed will." Third, the healing of the official's son displays **faith alone**—he believed the Lord's word without seeing, the very model of faith John commends (cf. 20:29).

## 基督的影子（中文）

本章的基督论集中在主耶稣的"我是"宣告上。当撒玛利亚妇人说"我知道弥赛亚要来"，主回答"这和你说话的就是他"——原文直译是ἐγώ εἰμι（我是），与出埃及记3:14神向摩西启示的名字"我是自有永有的"同一表达。此外，**活水**的意象在旧约中指向神自己——耶利米书2:13称神为"活水的泉源"。主耶稣宣称自己能赐活水，就是宣称自己是神。撒玛利亚人最后的宣告"这真是救世主"（σωτὴρ τοῦ κόσμου）超越了犹太弥赛亚的民族性概念——基督不只是以色列的救主，更是全世界的救主。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

The Christology centers on Jesus' "I am" declaration. When the Samaritan woman said "I know Messiah is coming," the Lord replied "I who speak to you am he"—the Greek is literally ἐγώ εἰμι (I am), the same expression God used to reveal His name to Moses in Exodus 3:14, "I AM WHO I AM." Furthermore, the image of **living water** in the Old Testament points to God Himself—Jeremiah 2:13 calls God "the fountain of living waters." When Jesus claimed to give living water, He was claiming to be God. The Samaritans' final confession, "this is indeed the Savior of the world" (σωτὴρ τοῦ κόσμου), transcends the ethnic concept of a Jewish Messiah—Christ is not only Israel's Savior but the Savior of the entire world.
