## 正文（中文）

我约翰——主所爱的门徒，至今仍记得那清晨在圣殿的一幕。文士和法利赛人带来一个行淫时被拿的妇人，想要试探我们的主。按摩西律法，她该用石头打死。但耶稣弯腰在地上画字，然后直起身说："你们中间谁是没有罪的，谁就可以先拿石头打她。"那些控告者从老到少，一个个都离开了。主对那妇人说："我也不定你的罪。去吧，从此不要再犯罪了！"

弟兄姐妹，这就是恩典与真理的完美结合——祂不轻忽罪，却赐下赦免和新生命的机会。弟兄姐妹，注意主没有说"你没有罪"——祂说"不要再犯罪了"。恩典不是纵容——恩典是给你一个重新开始的机会——但这个机会要求你改变。弟兄姐妹，那些控告者一个个走了——为什么从老到少？因为年长的人更知道自己的罪。弟兄姐妹，越认识自己的人越不敢审判别人。

随后主宣告说："我是世界的光。跟从我的，就不在黑暗里走，必要得着生命的光。"弟兄姐妹，这是约翰福音七个"我是"宣告中的第二个。祂不是众光之一，乃是唯一真光。弟兄姐妹，住棚节期间圣殿院子里会点燃巨大的金灯台——照亮整个耶路撒冷——而耶稣就在这个场景中宣告"我是世界的光"。弟兄姐妹，圣殿的灯会熄灭——但基督的光永不熄灭。

法利赛人质疑祂的见证，但祂说："我虽然为自己作见证，我的见证还是真的；因我知道我从哪里来，往哪里去。"弟兄姐妹，人的见证需要外在的确认——但神的见证是自证的——因为没有比神更高的权威可以为祂作证。祂与父原为一——祂的见证有父为祂作证。

弟兄姐妹，接下来的对话越来越紧张。那些犹太人以为自己是亚伯拉罕的子孙就必得救。主直言不讳："你们若是亚伯拉罕的儿子，就必行亚伯拉罕所行的事。"弟兄姐妹，肉身的血统不等于信心的传承。亚伯拉罕信神——但他的后裔却要杀神的儿子。弟兄姐妹，信仰不遗传——每一代人都要自己面对基督——自己做出回应。

主更严厉地说："你们是出于你们的父魔鬼，你们父的私欲你们偏要行。"弟兄姐妹，这话何等严厉！但这是真理——人若不接受基督——仍活在罪中——就证明自己属于那说谎者、杀人者的父。弟兄姐妹，世界上只有两个"父"——天父和魔鬼——没有中间地带。你不属于这个就属于那个。

而那最震撼人心的宣告——当犹太人说"你还没有五十岁，岂见过亚伯拉罕呢？"主回答说："我实实在在地告诉你们，还没有亚伯拉罕就有了我。"弟兄姐妹，这是何等的宣告！祂用"我是"（ego eimi）这个神圣的名——呼应出埃及记3:14神对摩西说的"我是自有永有的"——宣告自己就是那位从永恒中存在的神！

犹太人完全明白这话的意思——祂宣称自己是神！于是他们拿起石头要打祂。弟兄姐妹，如果耶稣只是一个好老师——一个道德楷模——他们不会拿石头。石头是对亵渎神的人准备的。弟兄姐妹，耶稣要么是祂所宣称的——与父同等的永恒之神——要么是历史上最大的骗子。没有中间选项。

弟兄姐妹，这一章让我们看见：罪人在祂面前蒙怜悯——却也看见人心刚硬拒绝真光的悲剧。祂是世界的光——但黑暗不接受光。祂是自有永有的神——但人却要用石头打祂。然而祂的使命不会因人的拒绝而改变——祂来是要释放一切被罪捆绑的人——"天父的儿子若叫你们自由，你们就真自由了。"

## Content (English)

I, John—the disciple whom the Lord loved—still remember that morning scene in the temple. The scribes and Pharisees brought a woman caught in adultery, seeking to test our Lord. According to Moses' law, she should be stoned to death. But Jesus bent down and wrote on the ground, then stood up and said: "Let him who is without sin among you be the first to throw a stone at her." Those accusers departed one by one, from the oldest to the youngest. The Lord said to the woman, "Neither do I condemn you; go, and from now on sin no more."

This is the perfect union of grace and truth—He does not make light of sin, yet grants forgiveness and the opportunity for new life. He came not to condemn the world, but to save it.

Then the Lord proclaimed: "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness, but will have the light of life." He is not one light among many, but the only true light that illuminates all who sit in darkness and the shadow of death. The Pharisees questioned His testimony, but He said, "Even if I do bear witness about myself, my testimony is true, for I know where I came from and where I am going." He and the Father are one; His testimony is witnessed by the Father.

Those Jews did not understand. They thought being Abraham's descendants would save them, not knowing that physical lineage cannot make one a child of God. The Lord spoke plainly: "You are of your father the devil, and your will is to do your father's desires." How severe these words! If one does not receive Christ and remains in sin, it proves they belong to the father of lies and murder.

And the most shocking declaration came when the Jews said, "You are not yet fifty years old, and have you seen Abraham?" The Lord answered: "Truly, truly, I say to you, before Abraham was, I AM." What a declaration! He used "I AM"—that sacred name—proclaiming Himself to be the self-existent God, the very LORD who appeared to Moses in the burning bush!

The Jews understood what this meant—He claimed to be God! So they picked up stones to throw at Him, but His hour had not yet come, and He left the temple. We who followed Him were both amazed and fearful, for we heard with our own ears this carpenter's son claim to be the One who existed before Abraham, the Creator of the universe, the God of Israel.

Brothers and sisters, this chapter shows us: sinners receive mercy in His presence, yet we also see the tragedy of hardened hearts rejecting the true light. He is the light of the world, but darkness does not receive the light. He is the Son of God, yet people sought to stone Him. But His mission will not change because of human rejection—He came to set free all who are bound by sin, that they may have freedom, truth, and life.

## 历史背景（中文）

约翰福音第8章发生在主后30年左右的耶路撒冷，正值住棚节期间（7:2）。这是犹太三大节期之一，庆祝旷野漂流时神的同在和供应。节期中有点灯的仪式——在妇女院中点燃巨大的金灯台，象征神在旷野的火柱引导以色列。正是在这背景下，耶稣宣告"我是世界的光"，直接宣称自己是神同在的实体应验。

行淫妇人的叙事反映了第二圣殿时期犹太宗教领袖的双重标准：他们热衷于控告别人，却对自己的罪视而不见；他们强调摩西律法，却选择性执行（律法要求奸夫淫妇都要处死，申22:22,但他们只带来妇人）。这种宗教虚伪正是耶稣常常批评的。

"还没有亚伯拉罕就有了我"（8:58）这句话使用了希腊文"ego eimi"（我是），直接呼应出埃及记3:14神向摩西启示的圣名"我是自有永有的"（I AM WHO I AM）。犹太人立刻明白这是宣称神性的话，因此拿石头要打祂——按利未记24:16,亵渎神名者当被石头打死。

这一章也显示了耶稣与犹太宗教当局之间张力的升级，为后面11章拉撒路复活后公会正式决议杀祂（11:53）埋下伏笔。

## Historical Context (English)

John chapter 8 takes place in Jerusalem around 30 AD, during the Feast of Tabernacles (7:2). This was one of the three major Jewish festivals, celebrating God's presence and provision during the wilderness wandering. The feast included a lamp-lighting ceremony—lighting massive golden lampstands in the Court of Women, symbolizing God's pillar of fire guiding Israel in the wilderness. It is against this background that Jesus declared "I am the light of the world," directly claiming to be the fulfillment of God's presence.

The narrative of the woman caught in adultery reflects the double standard of Jewish religious leaders in the Second Temple period: they were zealous to accuse others while blind to their own sins; they emphasized Mosaic law yet selectively enforced it (the law required both adulterer and adulteress to be put to death, Deut 22:22, but they only brought the woman). This religious hypocrisy was what Jesus often criticized.

The statement "before Abraham was, I AM" (8:58) uses the Greek "ego eimi" (I AM), directly echoing Exodus 3:14 where God revealed His sacred name to Moses as "I AM WHO I AM." The Jews immediately understood this as a claim to deity, hence they took up stones to stone Him—according to Leviticus 24:16, blasphemers of God's name were to be stoned to death.

This chapter also shows the escalating tension between Jesus and the Jewish religious authorities, laying groundwork for the Sanhedrin's formal decision to kill Him after the raising of Lazarus in chapter 11 (11:53).

## 经文结构（中文）

第8章结构围绕"光明与黑暗"、"真理与谎言"、"自由与奴役"的对比展开：

**一、恩典的光照（8:1-11）** — 行淫妇人的故事：控告者离去，耶稣不定罪却命令"不要再犯罪"。这是福音的缩影：罪人蒙赦免，不是为继续犯罪,乃是得新生命的能力。

**二、真光的宣告（8:12-20）** — "我是世界的光"。法利赛人质疑祂的见证，耶稣指出祂的见证有父为祂作证（两个见证人原则，申19:15）,且祂知道自己从哪里来往哪里去——祂从父那里来,又要回到父那里去。

**三、真理的自由（8:21-30）** — "你们要死在罪中"与"真理必叫你们得以自由"的对比。人若不信基督，必死在罪中；但信祂的人要晓得真理，真理使人得自由。许多人因这话信了祂。

**四、真与假的父（8:31-47）** — 最尖锐的对质：犹太人自称是亚伯拉罕的子孙、神的儿女，但耶稣指出他们行为显明他们的父是魔鬼——说谎者、杀人者。真儿女听父的话并行父的事；他们想杀耶稣，证明他们不属神。

**五、"我是"的启示（8:48-59）** — 高潮：当犹太人质疑祂的年纪时，祂宣告"还没有亚伯拉罕就有了我"（Before Abraham was, I AM）。这不仅是时间上的先在，更是本质上的自有永有，是神圣名号的宣称（出3:14）。

全章从赦罪开始（妇人蒙恩），到宣告神性结束（自有永有的神），贯穿的主题是：耶稣基督是真光、真理、真自由的唯一源头，拒绝祂就是选择黑暗、谎言和奴役。

## Structure (English)

Chapter 8 is structured around the contrasts of "light and darkness," "truth and falsehood," "freedom and bondage":

**I. Light of Grace (8:1-11)** — The story of the woman caught in adultery: accusers depart, Jesus does not condemn but commands "sin no more." This is the gospel in miniature: sinners are forgiven not to continue in sin, but to receive power for new life.

**II. Proclamation of True Light (8:12-20)** — "I am the light of the world." Pharisees questioned His testimony; Jesus pointed out His testimony is witnessed by the Father (the two-witness principle, Deut 19:15), and He knows where He came from and where He is going—from the Father and returning to the Father.

**III. Freedom of Truth (8:21-30)** — The contrast between "you will die in your sins" and "the truth will set you free." If one does not believe in Christ, they will die in their sins; but those who believe will know the truth, and truth will make them free. Many believed in Him because of these words.

**IV. True and False Fathers (8:31-47)** — The sharpest confrontation: Jews claimed to be Abraham's descendants, children of God, but Jesus pointed out their actions reveal their father is the devil—the liar and murderer. True children hear their Father's words and do their Father's works; they wanted to kill Jesus, proving they do not belong to God.

**V. The "I AM" Revelation (8:48-59)** — Climax: when the Jews questioned His age, He declared "before Abraham was, I AM." This is not merely temporal pre-existence, but essential self-existence, a claim to the divine name (Exod 3:14).

The chapter begins with forgiveness of sin (the woman receives grace) and ends with the proclamation of deity (the self-existent God). The pervasive theme: Jesus Christ is the sole source of true light, truth, and freedom; to reject Him is to choose darkness, falsehood, and bondage.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神的主权拣选：** "所有犯罪的就是罪的奴仆"（8:34）——这是人类堕落后的普遍状态。人不能凭自己意志从罪中自拔。"若不是蒙我父的恩赐，没有人能到我这里来"（6:65的延续）。犹太人拒绝基督，不是信息不清楚，乃是因他们"不是出于神"（8:47），心里没有神的爱（8:42）。这显明救恩全然出于神的恩典和主权拣选，非出于人的行为或血统。

**基督的神性：** 第8章是四福音中最明确的基督神性宣告之一。"还没有亚伯拉罕就有了我"（8:58）直接使用"ego eimi"（我是）这一神圣名号，宣称自己是自有永有的耶和华。祂与父"同在"（8:16,29），做父所做的事（8:28-29），认识父如同父认识祂（8:55）。改革宗神学强调：基督不是被造的第一个受造物（阿里乌异端），乃是与父同永恒、同本质、同荣耀的三一神第二位格。

**因信称义：** "你们若不信我是基督，必要死在罪中"（8:24）——这节经文总结了唯独因信称义的核心：称义不是靠行为、血统、宗教礼仪，唯独借着对基督的信心。人在神面前被接纳，只有一条路：承认自己全然败坏，完全仰赖基督的宝血和义。那些自以为义的法利赛人（"我们从来没有作过谁的奴仆"8:33），恰恰证明他们不认识自己罪的深度，因此拒绝基督。

**圣化与成圣：** "你们若常常遵守我的道，就真是我的门徒；你们必晓得真理，真理必叫你们得以自由"（8:31-32）。这不是说遵守道才得救，而是说真门徒的标志是持续遵守主道，这是成圣过程的必然结果。自由不是放纵，乃是从罪的奴役中被释放，得以自由地顺服神、行义。改革宗强调：称义瞬间完成，成圣一生持续，得荣耀在将来完全——这三者不可分割。

## Theology (English)

**Divine Sovereign Election:** "Everyone who practices sin is a slave to sin" (8:34)—this is the universal state of humanity after the Fall. Humans cannot free themselves from sin by their own will. "No one can come to me unless it is granted him by the Father" (continuation of 6:65). The Jews' rejection of Christ was not due to unclear message, but because they were "not of God" (8:47), having no love of God in them (8:42). This reveals salvation is entirely of God's grace and sovereign election, not of human works or lineage.

**Deity of Christ:** Chapter 8 contains one of the clearest declarations of Christ's deity in the four Gospels. "Before Abraham was, I AM" (8:58) directly uses "ego eimi" (I AM), the divine name, claiming to be the self-existent Yahweh. He is "with" the Father (8:16,29), does what the Father does (8:28-29), knows the Father as the Father knows Him (8:55). Reformed theology emphasizes: Christ is not the first created being (Arian heresy), but the second person of the Trinity, co-eternal, co-essential, and co-glorious with the Father.

**Justification by Faith Alone:** "Unless you believe that I am he you will die in your sins" (8:24)—this verse summarizes the core of justification by faith alone: justification is not by works, lineage, or religious rituals, but solely through faith in Christ. There is only one way for man to be accepted before God: acknowledge total depravity and wholly depend on Christ's blood and righteousness. Those self-righteous Pharisees ("We have never been enslaved to anyone" 8:33) proved they did not know the depth of their sin, hence rejecting Christ.

**Sanctification:** "If you abide in my word, you are truly my disciples, and you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free" (8:31-32). This does not mean obeying the word saves us, but that the mark of true disciples is continued obedience to the Lord's word—the inevitable result of sanctification. Freedom is not license, but liberation from slavery to sin, free to obey God and do righteousness. Reformed theology emphasizes: justification is instantaneous, sanctification is progressive throughout life, glorification is complete in the future—these three are inseparable.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**逾越节羔羊的应验：** 行淫妇人本该死（摩西律法的要求），但耶稣说"我也不定你的罪"。这预表基督自己要承担罪的刑罚——那无罪的替代有罪的，使罪人得赦免。正如逾越节羔羊的血涂在门楣上使以色列人免于死亡，基督的宝血使信祂的人免于神的忿怒。

**铜蛇的预表（民21:8-9）：** 当耶稣说"你们举起人子以后，必知道我是基督"（8:28），这呼应祂在3:14所说"摩西在旷野怎样举蛇，人子也必照样被举起来"。以色列人被火蛇咬伤，仰望铜蛇就得医治；罪人被罪毒侵蚀，仰望钉十字架的基督就得拯救。举起不仅指被钉十架，也指被高举、得荣耀——死亡成为祂得荣耀的道路。

**会幕中的灯台：** "我是世界的光"对应会幕/圣殿中的金灯台（出25:31-40）——那灯台在至圣所外照亮圣所,预表基督是真光,照亮神百姓的道路。住棚节的点灯仪式纪念旷野的火柱；耶稣宣告自己是那火柱的实体，是神同在的真光。旧约的光只是影子，基督是实体。

**"我是"的启示：** 出埃及记3:14,神在荆棘中向摩西启示祂的名"I AM WHO I AM"（我是自有永有的）。约翰福音记载耶稣七次"我是"宣告（我是生命的粮、世界的光、羊的门、好牧人、复活和生命、道路真理生命、真葡萄树），每一次都指向祂神性的宣称。第8章的"Before Abraham was, I AM"尤为直接——祂不说"我在"（I was），而是"我是"（I AM），宣告自己超越时间、自有永有，就是那位荆棘中的神，是带领以色列出埃及、立约、赐律法的耶和华。

基督不仅是大卫的子孙（肉身），更是大卫的主（神性）；不仅是亚伯拉罕的后裔，更是亚伯拉罕之前就有的神。旧约所有的预表——羔羊、铜蛇、灯台、会幕——都指向祂，在祂里面得着完全的应验。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Fulfillment of Passover Lamb:** The woman caught in adultery should have died (requirement of Mosaic law), but Jesus said "Neither do I condemn you." This foreshadows Christ bearing the penalty of sin Himself—the sinless substituting for the sinful, enabling forgiveness for sinners. Just as the blood of the Passover lamb on the doorposts saved Israelites from death, Christ's precious blood saves believers from God's wrath.

**The Bronze Serpent (Num 21:8-9):** When Jesus said "when you have lifted up the Son of Man, then you will know that I am he" (8:28), this echoes what He said in 3:14: "as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up." Israelites bitten by fiery serpents looked to the bronze serpent and were healed; sinners poisoned by sin look to Christ crucified and are saved. Lifting up refers not only to crucifixion, but also to exaltation and glorification—death becomes the path to His glory.

**The Lampstand in the Tabernacle:** "I am the light of the world" corresponds to the golden lampstand in the tabernacle/temple (Exod 25:31-40)—the lampstand outside the Holy of Holies illuminated the Holy Place, prefiguring Christ as the true light illuminating the path of God's people. The lamp-lighting ceremony during the Feast of Tabernacles commemorated the pillar of fire in the wilderness; Jesus declared Himself the reality of that pillar, the true light of God's presence. Old Testament lights were shadows; Christ is the substance.

**The "I AM" Revelation:** In Exodus 3:14, God revealed His name to Moses from the burning bush as "I AM WHO I AM" (the self-existent One). John's Gospel records Jesus' seven "I AM" declarations (I am the bread of life, light of the world, door of the sheep, good shepherd, resurrection and life, way truth and life, true vine), each pointing to His claim to deity. Chapter 8's "Before Abraham was, I AM" is especially direct—He did not say "I was," but "I AM," declaring Himself transcendent over time, self-existent, the very God in the burning bush, the Yahweh who led Israel out of Egypt, made covenant, and gave the law.

Christ is not only David's son (in flesh), but also David's Lord (in deity); not only Abraham's descendant, but the God who existed before Abraham. All Old Testament types—lamb, bronze serpent, lampstand, tabernacle—point to Him and find complete fulfillment in Him.
