## 正文（中文）

第三日，在加利利的迦拿有娶亲的筵席，我的母亲马利亚在那里。我和我的门徒也被请去赴席。这是我见证的第一个神迹——主将水变为酒的记号。

酒用尽了，母亲马利亚对主耶稣说："他们没有酒了。"主回答说："母亲，我与你有什么相干？我的时候还没有到。"虽然主的话语看似严厉，但祂母亲信任祂，对用人说："他告诉你们什么，你们就做什么。"那里有六口石缸，是犹太人洁净的规矩要用的，每口可以盛两三桶水。主耶稣对用人说："把缸倒满了水。"他们就倒满了，直到缸口。主又说："现在可以舀出来，送给管筵席的。"他们就送了去。

管筵席的尝了那水变的酒，并不知道是哪里来的（只有舀水的用人知道），便叫新郎来，对他说："人都是先摆上好酒，等客喝足了，才摆上次的；你倒把好酒留到如今！"这是主耶稣所行的头一件神迹，是在加利利的迦拿行的，显出了祂的荣耀来，祂的门徒就信祂了。

我要说明，约翰福音称神迹为"记号"（σημεῖον），不只是奇妙的作为，更是指向属灵真理的标记。水变酒这个记号有多重含义：洁净的水代表旧约的礼仪律法，酒则代表新约的喜乐和丰盛；这也预示主将在十字架上流出的宝血，带来真正的喜乐和生命。主说"我的时候还没有到"，这"时候"（ὥρα）在约翰福音中特指祂受难、复活、得荣耀的时刻。

这事以后，主和祂的母亲、弟兄、并我们门徒都下迦百农去，在那里住了不多几日。犹太人的逾越节近了，主就上耶路撒冷去。祂看见殿里有卖牛、羊、鸽子的，并有兑换银钱的人坐在那里，就拿绳子做成鞭子，把牛羊都赶出殿去，倒出兑换银钱之人的银钱，推翻他们的桌子。又对卖鸽子的说："把这些东西拿去！不要将我父的殿当作买卖的地方。"

那时，我们门徒想起经上记着说："我为你的殿心里焦急，如同火烧。"这是诗篇69:9的话，是弥赛亚受苦的诗篇。因此犹太人问主说："你既做这些事，还显什么神迹给我们看呢？"主回答说："你们拆毁这殿，我三日内要再建立起来。"犹太人便说："这殿是四十六年才造成的，你三日内就再建立起来吗？"但主所说的殿，是指祂的身体。

所以到祂从死里复活以后，我们门徒才想起祂说过这话，就信了圣经和主所说的。当主在耶路撒冷过逾越节的时候，有许多人看见祂所行的神迹，就信了祂的名。耶稣却不将自己交托他们，因为祂知道万人；也用不着谁见证人怎样，因祂知道人心里所存的。

迦拿婚宴和洁净圣殿这两件事，看似相反——一个充满喜乐，一个充满义怒——却都显明主的身份和使命。祂来是要带来新约的喜乐，也要洁净祂的教会。祂有权柄称圣殿为"我父的殿"，因为祂就是神的儿子。祂的身体就是真正的圣殿，神与人相会的所在。三天后从死里复活，祂要建立一个不是人手所造的殿——就是祂的教会，我们这些信祂的人。

## Content (English)

On the third day, there was a wedding at Cana in Galilee, and my mother Mary was there. Jesus and we His disciples had also been invited to the wedding. This is the first miracle I witnessed—the sign of the Lord turning water into wine.

When the wine ran out, Mary the mother of Jesus said to Him, "They have no more wine." Jesus replied, "Woman, what does this have to do with me? My hour has not yet come." Though the Lord's words seemed stern, His mother trusted Him and said to the servants, "Do whatever He tells you." Now there were six stone water jars there for the Jewish rites of purification, each holding twenty or thirty gallons. Jesus said to the servants, "Fill the jars with water." And they filled them up to the brim. Then He said, "Now draw some out and take it to the master of the feast." So they took it.

When the master of the feast tasted the water now become wine and did not know where it came from (though the servants who had drawn the water knew), he called the bridegroom and said to him, "Everyone serves the good wine first, and when people have drunk freely, then the poor wine. But you have kept the good wine until now." This, the first of His signs, Jesus did at Cana in Galilee, and manifested His glory. And we His disciples believed in Him.

I must explain that John's Gospel calls miracles "signs" (σημεῖον)—not merely wonderful acts but markers pointing to spiritual truth. This sign of water into wine has multiple meanings: the purification water represents the ceremonial law of the Old Testament, while wine represents the joy and abundance of the New Covenant; it also foreshadows the precious blood the Lord would shed on the cross, bringing true joy and life. When the Lord said "My hour has not yet come," this "hour" (ὥρα) in John's Gospel specifically refers to the moment of His suffering, resurrection, and glorification.

After this, He went down to Capernaum with His mother, brothers, and us disciples, and there we stayed for a few days. The Passover of the Jews was at hand, and Jesus went up to Jerusalem. In the temple He found those who were selling oxen and sheep and pigeons, and the money-changers sitting there. Making a whip of cords, He drove them all out of the temple, with the sheep and oxen. He poured out the coins of the money-changers and overturned their tables. And He told those who sold the pigeons, "Take these things away; do not make my Father's house a house of trade."

At that time, we disciples remembered that it was written, "Zeal for your house will consume me." This is from Psalm 69:9, a messianic psalm of suffering. So the Jews said to Him, "What sign do you show us for doing these things?" Jesus answered them, "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up." The Jews then said, "It has taken forty-six years to build this temple, and will you raise it up in three days?" But He was speaking about the temple of His body.

When therefore He was raised from the dead, we disciples remembered that He had said this, and we believed the Scripture and the word that Jesus had spoken. Now when He was in Jerusalem at the Passover Feast, many believed in His name when they saw the signs that He was doing. But Jesus on His part did not entrust Himself to them, because He knew all people and needed no one to bear witness about man, for He Himself knew what was in man.

The wedding at Cana and the cleansing of the temple—these two events seem opposite, one full of joy and the other full of righteous anger—yet both reveal the Lord's identity and mission. He came to bring the joy of the New Covenant and to cleanse His church. He had the authority to call the temple "my Father's house" because He is the Son of God. His body is the true temple, the place where God meets humanity. Rising from the dead after three days, He would build a temple not made with hands—His church, we who believe in Him.

## 历史背景（中文）

第2章记载的事件发生在主公开事工的最开始阶段。迦拿距离拿撒勒约15公里，是加利利一个小村庄。婚宴在犹太文化中是极其重要的社交场合，通常持续七天。酒用尽是主人家的极大羞耻，会被视为待客不周。主的母亲马利亚在场，且主动提醒主这个问题，可能表示这家与他们有亲属关系。

"六口石缸"是犹太人洁净礼仪所用，每口可盛约80-120升水。犹太人在饭前和礼仪中都要洗手、洗脚，这些石缸就是为此准备的。主选择用这些礼仪用的水缸来行神迹，有特别的神学含义——祂来不是废除律法，而是成全律法，将旧约的洁净礼仪转化为新约的生命和喜乐。

洁净圣殿的事件发生在逾越节期间。当时的圣殿被称为"希律的殿"，是希律大王在主前20年开始重建的，到主后64年才完全竣工（所以犹太人说"四十六年才造成"是指到主传道时已建了46年）。外邦人院（Court of Gentiles）被商人占据，卖祭牲、兑换圣殿银币（因为圣殿不接受有凯撒肖像的罗马钱币）。这本是方便远道而来的朝圣者，但却变成了剥削的工具，祭司家族从中获取暴利。

约翰将这事件放在主事工的开始（符类福音则记载在最后一周），可能是为了从一开始就表明主的身份——祂有权柄洁净圣殿，因为祂是殿的主人，是神的儿子。

## Historical Context (English)

The events recorded in Chapter 2 occurred at the very beginning of the Lord's public ministry. Cana was a small village in Galilee, about 15 kilometers from Nazareth. Weddings in Jewish culture were extremely important social occasions, usually lasting seven days. Running out of wine would be a great shame for the host family and be seen as poor hospitality. The presence of Mary, the Lord's mother, and her proactive alertness to this problem may indicate that this family had kinship ties with them.

The "six stone jars" were used for Jewish purification rituals, each holding about 80-120 liters of water. Jews washed their hands and feet before meals and during rituals, and these stone jars were prepared for this purpose. The Lord's choice to use these ritual water jars for His miracle has special theological significance—He came not to abolish the Law but to fulfill it, transforming Old Testament purification rituals into New Covenant life and joy.

The temple cleansing event occurred during Passover. The temple at that time was called "Herod's Temple," rebuilt by Herod the Great starting in 20 BC and not fully completed until AD 64 (so when the Jews said "forty-six years to build" they meant it had been under construction for 46 years up to the time of Jesus's ministry). The Court of the Gentiles was occupied by merchants selling sacrificial animals and exchanging temple currency (since the temple did not accept Roman coins bearing Caesar's image). This was originally to facilitate pilgrims who had traveled far, but it had become a tool of exploitation, with priestly families reaping enormous profits.

John places this event at the beginning of the Lord's ministry (while the Synoptic Gospels record it in the final week), likely to establish from the outset the Lord's identity—He has authority to cleanse the temple because He is the temple's master, the Son of God.

## 经文结构（中文）

第2章包含两个主要事件，形成对比与呼应：

**迦拿婚宴：第一个记号（1-12节）**
结构精巧：问题出现（酒用尽）→ 母亲的请求 → 主的回应（时候未到）→ 母亲的信心（吩咐仆人顺服）→ 神迹的施行（水变酒）→ 神迹的证实（管筵席者的见证）→ 总结（显出荣耀，门徒信祂）。整个叙事突出"记号"的本质：不只是解决实际问题，更是"显出祂的荣耀"，引人信靠。

**洁净圣殿（13-22节）**
同样精心安排：背景（逾越节上耶路撒冷）→ 问题（圣殿被商业化）→ 主的行动（赶出商人）→ 主的宣告（不要将我父的殿当作买卖的地方）→ 门徒的回应（想起圣经）→ 犹太人的挑战（要神迹证明）→ 主的预言（三日内重建）→ 解释（指祂身体）→ 后来的理解（复活后想起）。

**结语：不信靠人的见证（23-25节）**
这三节经文是过渡，承上启下。许多人因神迹信主的名，但主"不将自己交托他们"——因为祂知道人心。这为第3章尼哥底母的故事做铺垫，也暗示真信心与表面信心的区别。

两个事件的对比：婚宴是私人场合，圣殿是公共场合；婚宴显出恩典和喜乐，圣殿显出公义和审判；但都指向同一位基督——祂既是新郎（带来喜乐），也是洁净者（炼净教会）。

## Structure (English)

Chapter 2 contains two main events forming contrast and correspondence:

**Wedding at Cana: The First Sign (vv. 1-12)**
Finely structured: problem arises (wine runs out) → mother's request → Lord's response (hour not yet come) → mother's faith (instructs servants to obey) → miracle performed (water to wine) → miracle confirmed (testimony of master of feast) → summary (manifested glory, disciples believed). The entire narrative highlights the nature of the "sign": not just solving a practical problem, but "manifesting His glory" and leading to faith.

**Cleansing the Temple (vv. 13-22)**
Equally carefully arranged: setting (going up to Jerusalem for Passover) → problem (temple commercialized) → Lord's action (driving out merchants) → Lord's declaration (do not make my Father's house a house of trade) → disciples' response (remembering Scripture) → Jews' challenge (demanding a sign) → Lord's prophecy (rebuild in three days) → explanation (referring to His body) → later understanding (remembered after resurrection).

**Conclusion: Not Entrusting Himself (vv. 23-25)**
These three verses are transitional, connecting what comes before and after. Many believed in His name because of signs, but the Lord "did not entrust Himself to them"—because He knew what was in man. This sets the stage for the story of Nicodemus in Chapter 3 and hints at the difference between true faith and superficial faith.

Contrast between the two events: the wedding is a private occasion, the temple a public one; the wedding displays grace and joy, the temple displays righteousness and judgment; yet both point to the same Christ—He is both the bridegroom (bringing joy) and the purifier (refining His church).

## 神学要点（中文）

**记号的神学：** 约翰不用"神迹"（δύναμις，能力）或"奇事"（τέρας，惊奇），而用"记号"（σημεῖον），强调这些作为不只是展示能力，更是指向属灵真理的标记。改革宗神学认为，神的所有作为都是"启示性"的，不是任意的炫耀，而是要启示祂自己的属性和救赎计划。水变酒记号指向基督带来的新约丰盛，远超旧约的洁净礼仪。

**基督的"时候"：** 主对母亲说"我的时候还没有到"（v.4），这"时候"（ὥρα）在约翰福音中是一个关键神学主题，特指基督受难、复活、升天得荣耀的时刻（7:30, 8:20, 12:23, 13:1, 17:1）。改革宗强调神的主权和预定，基督的救赎工作都在"所定的日期满足"的时候成就（加4:4）。祂的每一步都按照父神的时间表进行，不受人的催促或拦阻。

**圣殿神学：** 主称圣殿为"我父的殿"，直接宣告祂与父神独特的关系。旧约圣殿是神同在的象征，但主说"你们拆毁这殿，我三日内要再建立起来"，将圣殿的意义转移到祂自己的身体。改革宗神学教导"基督论的圣殿神学"——基督就是真正的圣殿（约2:21），教会作为基督的身体也是圣灵的殿（林前3:16-17，弗2:21-22）。实体圣殿的时代已经过去，在新约时代，我们在基督里敬拜父神。

**审判从神的家开始：** 主洁净圣殿不只是纠正商业化，更是弥赛亚审判的预演。玛拉基书3:1-3预言："你们所寻求的主，必忽然进入祂的殿⋯⋯祂来的日子，谁能当得起呢？⋯⋯祂必洁净利未人"。改革宗强调教会的圣洁和纪律，基督爱教会，所以洁净教会，"审判要从神的家起首"（彼前4:17）。

## Theology (English)

**Theology of Signs:** John does not use "miracle" (δύναμις, power) or "wonder" (τέρας, marvel), but "sign" (σημεῖον), emphasizing that these acts are not merely displays of power but markers pointing to spiritual truth. Reformed theology holds that all of God's works are "revelatory"—not arbitrary displays but meant to reveal His attributes and redemptive plan. The sign of water into wine points to the New Covenant abundance Christ brings, far surpassing Old Testament purification rituals.

**Christ's "Hour":** When the Lord said to His mother "My hour has not yet come" (v. 4), this "hour" (ὥρα) is a key theological theme in John's Gospel, specifically referring to the moment of Christ's suffering, resurrection, and ascension to glory (7:30, 8:20, 12:23, 13:1, 17:1). Reformed theology emphasizes God's sovereignty and predestination; Christ's redemptive work was accomplished "But when the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son, made of a woman, made under the law," (Gal 4:4). His every step proceeded according to the Father's timetable, not subject to human urging or hindrance.

**Temple Theology:** The Lord called the temple "my Father's house," directly declaring His unique relationship with God the Father. The Old Testament temple was a symbol of God's presence, but when the Lord said "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up," He transferred the meaning of the temple to His own body. Reformed theology teaches "Christological temple theology"—Christ is the true temple (John 2:21), and the church as Christ's body is also the temple of the Holy Spirit (1 Cor 3:16-17, Eph 2:21-22). The era of the physical temple has passed; in the New Covenant age, we worship the Father in Christ.

**Judgment Begins with God's House:** The Lord's cleansing of the temple was not merely correcting commercialization but a preview of messianic judgment. Malachi 3:1-3 prophesies: "The Lord whom you seek will suddenly come to His temple... But who can endure the day of His coming?... He will purify the Levites." Reformed theology emphasizes the holiness and discipline of the church; Christ loves the church and therefore cleanses the church, for "For the time is come that judgment must begin at the house of God: and if it first begin at us, what shall the end be of them that obey not the gospel of God?" (1 Pet 4:17).

## 基督的影子（中文）

**新郎基督：** 婚宴背景本身就是基督论预表。旧约常用婚姻关系比喻神与以色列的关系（以赛亚54:5，何西阿2:19-20）。主在婚宴行第一个神迹，暗示祂就是真正的新郎。祂来要娶新妇——教会，并为她预备丰盛的筵席。启示录19:7-9说到"羔羊的婚宴"，主在迦拿所行的是预尝那末世的喜乐。水变为酒象征祂将律法的字句转化为福音的恩典，将旧约的仪文转化为新约的生命。

**圣殿就是基督的身体：** 主明确说"你们拆毁这殿，我三日内要再建立起来⋯⋯但主所说的殿，是指祂的身体"（v.19-21）。这是整本圣经最清楚的基督论宣告之一。所罗门的圣殿、后来的第二圣殿，都只是影子，基督才是实体。出埃及记25:8记载神说："又当为我造圣所，使我可以住在他们中间"，真正的应验就是道成肉身——基督的身体就是神居住的殿，是神与人相会的所在。

**三日复活的预言：** 主说"三日内要再建立起来"，这是祂首次公开预言自己的死与复活。旧约中约拿在鱼腹三日三夜（约拿书1:17），预表基督在地里三日后复活（太12:40）。"三日"在圣经中是神拯救的时间——第三日亚伯拉罕看见代替以撒的羔羊，第三日以色列人在西奈山预备迎见神，第三日以斯帖冒死进宫救赎族人，第三日基督从死里复活。这是神信实应许的记号。

**热心与鞭子：** 主"拿绳子做成鞭子"洁净圣殿，应验了诗篇69:9"我为你的殿心里焦急，如同火烧"。这位温柔的好牧人，同时也是公义的审判者。祂对罪恶和虚伪的愤怒是圣洁的愤怒，对神荣耀的热心促使祂不能容忍圣殿被玷污。这也预表祂末日再来时的审判——祂要洁净祂的教会，炼净属祂的人。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ the Bridegroom:** The wedding setting itself is a Christological type. The Old Testament often uses the marriage relationship to depict God's relationship with Israel (Isaiah 54:5, Hosea 2:19-20). The Lord performed His first miracle at a wedding, hinting that He is the true bridegroom. He came to take a bride—the church—and prepare a lavish feast for her. Revelation 19:7-9 speaks of "the wedding feast of the Lamb"; what the Lord did at Cana is a foretaste of that eschatological joy. Water turned into wine symbolizes His transformation of the letter of the law into the grace of the gospel, the ceremonial of the Old Covenant into the life of the New.

**The Temple Is Christ's Body:** The Lord explicitly stated, "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up... But He was speaking about the temple of His body" (vv. 19-21). This is one of the clearest Christological declarations in the entire Bible. Solomon's temple and the later Second Temple were only shadows; Christ is the substance. Exodus 25:8 records God saying, "Let them make me a sanctuary, that I may dwell in their midst"—the true fulfillment is the incarnation: Christ's body is the temple where God dwells, the place where God meets humanity.

**Prophecy of Resurrection on the Third Day:** When the Lord said "in three days I will raise it up," this was His first public prophecy of His death and resurrection. In the Old Testament, Jonah was in the belly of the fish three days and three nights (Jonah 1:17), typifying Christ's resurrection after three days in the earth (Matt 12:40). "Three days" in Scripture is God's time of deliverance—on the third day Abraham saw the ram to substitute for Isaac, on the third day Israel prepared to meet God at Sinai, on the third day Esther risked her life entering the palace to redeem her people, on the third day Christ rose from the dead. This is the sign of God's faithful promise.

**Zeal and Whip:** The Lord "made a whip of cords" to cleanse the temple, fulfilling Psalm 69:9, "Zeal for your house will consume me." This gentle Good Shepherd is also the righteous Judge. His anger against sin and hypocrisy is holy anger; His zeal for God's glory compels Him not to tolerate defilement of the temple. This also prefigures His judgment at His second coming—He will cleanse His church and refine those who belong to Him.
