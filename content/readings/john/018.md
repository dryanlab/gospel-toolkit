## 正文（中文）

主说了这话，就同我们出去，过了汲沦溪。在那里有一个园子——客西马尼园，我们常到那里去。那天晚上，月光下的橄榄树投下斑驳的影子，我心中有种说不出的不安。

犹大也知道那地方。他领了一队兵——不是几个人，而是一队罗马兵丁——和祭司长并法利赛人的差役，拿着灯笼、火把、兵器，浩浩荡荡来到园里。他们以为要对付一个危险的叛乱分子，却不知道他们来捉拿的，是生命的主。

主知道将要临到自己的一切事，却没有逃跑，反而主动走上前去，问他们："你们找谁？"他们回答："找拿撒勒人耶稣。"主说："我就是。"（ἐγώ εἰμι）就在主说这话的瞬间，那些人都退后倒在地上！主只说了一句"我是"（Yahweh的自称），那些武装的士兵就仆倒了。如果主愿意，祂可以不被捉拿；但祂甘愿被捉拿，因为这是父的旨意。

主再问一次："你们找谁？"确认他们只要祂，不要门徒。主说："我已经告诉你们，我就是；你们若找我，就让这些人去吧。"即使在被捕的时刻，主仍在保护我们。祂对父说："你所赐给我的人，我没有失落一个"（17:12），如今祂用自己的身体来履行这话。

彼得有刀，拔出来要保护主，削掉了大祭司仆人马勒古的右耳。但主制止了他："收刀入鞘吧！我父所给我的那杯，我岂可不喝呢？"主不需要我们用武力保护，祂要喝的是父所给的苦杯——代替罪人承受神的忿怒的杯。

他们把主捆绑了，先带到亚那面前。亚那是本年的大祭司该亚法的岳父。该亚法就是从前向犹太人建议说"一个人替百姓死是有益的"那位——他说这话时不知道,自己竟说了预言（11:49-52）。

主在大祭司面前受审问。他们问祂的教训和门徒。主回答："我从来是明明地对世人说话...你为什么问我呢？可以问那听见的人，我对他们说的是什么。"主没有隐藏什么，祂的教训是公开的。结果一个差役打了主一巴掌，说:"你这样回答大祭司吗？"主温和地回答："我若说得不是，你可以指证那不是；我若说得是，你为什么打我呢？"

就在主受审的同时，彼得在外面院子里烤火。这个曾经说"我就是必须和你同死，也总不能不认你"的彼得（太26:35），如今被一个使女一问："你不也是这人的门徒吗？"他就说："我不是。"我站在旁边，心如刀割。主早已预言彼得会三次不认主（13:38），如今一一应验。第三次鸡叫的时候，彼得想起主的话，出去痛哭。我看着彼得的背影消失在夜色中，泪水模糊了我的眼睛。

天亮了，他们把主从该亚法那里押往总督府。犹太人自己却不进去,因为怕染了污秽,不能吃逾越节的筵席。何等讽刺——他们为了礼仪的洁净，却正在谋杀神的儿子！

彼拉多出来问："你们告他什么事呢？"他们不能说出具体罪名,只说："这人若不是作恶的，我们就不把他交给你。"彼拉多说："你们自己带他去，按着你们的律法审问他吧。"犹太人说："我们没有杀人的权柄。"这应验了主自己的话，祂要怎样死——不是被犹太人用石头打死,而是被罗马人钉十字架（12:32-33）。

彼拉多进衙门，叫主来，问祂："你是犹太人的王吗？"主反问："这话是你自己说的，还是别人论我对你说的？"主这样问，是要彼拉多思考：他真的关心真理吗？彼拉多说："我岂是犹太人呢？你本国的人和祭司长把你交给我。你做了什么事呢？"

主回答说："我的国不属这世界；我的国若属这世界，我的臣仆必要争战，使我不至于被交给犹太人。只是我的国不属这世界。"这话何等重要！主的国度不是靠刀剑建立的，而是靠真理；不是属地的政权,而是属天的权柄。

彼拉多问："这样，你是王吗？"主说："你说我是王。我为此而生,也为此来到世间，特为给真理作见证。凡属真理的人就听我的话。"彼拉多讥诮地问："真理是什么呢？"说了这话就出去，对犹太人说："我查不出他有什么罪来。"

但犹太人不肯罢休。彼拉多想释放主，提出按照逾越节释放一个囚犯的惯例。他问："你们要我给你们释放犹太人的王吗？"他们喊着说："不要这人，要巴拉巴！"这巴拉巴是个强盗——何等讽刺，他们宁要强盗，不要救主。

我站在那里,看着这一切。主安静地站在彼拉多面前，像羊在剪毛的人手下无声（赛53:7）。祂本可以呼叫天父，差遣十二营多的天使来救祂（太26:53），但祂没有。祂甘愿被捉拿、被审问、被羞辱，因为这是父的旨意，是我们得救的道路。

## Content (English)

After the Lord spoke these words, He went out with us and crossed the Kidron Valley. There was a garden there—Gethsemane—where we often went. That night, the olive trees cast mottled shadows in the moonlight, and I felt an indescribable unease in my heart.

Judas also knew the place. He led a band of soldiers—not just a few men, but a cohort of Roman soldiers—along with officers from the chief priests and Pharisees, carrying lanterns, torches, and weapons, arriving at the garden in force. They thought they were dealing with a dangerous rebel, not knowing that the one they came to arrest was the Lord of life.

The Lord, knowing all that would happen to Him, did not flee but stepped forward and asked them, "Whom do you seek?" They answered, "Jesus of Nazareth." The Lord said, "I am he" (ἐγώ εἰμι). At the instant the Lord spoke these words, those people all drew back and fell to the ground! The Lord merely said "I am" (Yahweh's self-designation), and those armed soldiers fell prostrate. If the Lord had wished, He could have avoided arrest; but He willingly submitted to arrest because it was the Father's will.

The Lord asked again, "Whom do you seek?" confirming they only wanted Him, not the disciples. The Lord said, "I told you that I am he. So, if you seek me, let these men go." Even at the moment of arrest, the Lord was still protecting us. He had said to the Father, "Of those whom you gave me I have lost not one" (17:12), and now He fulfilled these words with His own body.

Peter had a sword and drew it to protect the Lord, cutting off the right ear of Malchus, the high priest's servant. But the Lord stopped him: "Put your sword into its sheath; shall I not drink the cup that the Father has given me?" The Lord did not need our force to protect Him. He would drink the cup the Father gave Him—the cup of bearing God's wrath in place of sinners.

They bound the Lord and led Him first to Annas. Annas was the father-in-law of Caiaphas, who was high priest that year. Caiaphas was the one who had previously advised the Jews that "it is better that one man should die for the people"—when he said this, he did not know he was speaking prophecy (11:49-52).

The Lord was questioned before the high priest. They asked Him about His teaching and His disciples. The Lord answered, "I have spoken openly to the world... Why do you ask me? Ask those who have heard me what I said to them." The Lord had hidden nothing; His teaching was public. As a result, one of the officers struck the Lord, saying, "Is that how you answer the high priest?" The Lord gently replied, "If what I said is wrong, bear witness about the wrong; but if what I said is right, why do you strike me?"

While the Lord was being tried, Peter was in the courtyard outside warming himself. This Peter who had said "Peter said unto him, Though I should die with thee, yet will I not deny thee. Likewise also said all the disciples." (Matt 26:35), when questioned by a servant girl, "You also are not one of this man's disciples, are you?" said, "I am not." I stood nearby, my heart pierced. The Lord had already prophesied that Peter would deny Him three times (13:38), and now it was being fulfilled one by one. When the rooster crowed the third time, Peter remembered the Lord's words and went out and wept bitterly. I watched Peter's figure disappear into the night, tears blurring my eyes.

At dawn, they led the Lord from Caiaphas to the governor's headquarters. The Jews themselves did not enter, so that they would not be defiled and could eat the Passover. What irony—for the sake of ceremonial cleanness, they were murdering the Son of God!

Pilate came out and asked, "What accusation do you bring against this man?" They could not state a specific charge, only saying, "If this man were not doing evil, we would not have delivered him over to you." Pilate said, "Take him yourselves and judge him by your own law." The Jews said, "It is not lawful for us to put anyone to death." This fulfilled the Lord's own words about how He would die—not stoned by the Jews, but crucified by the Romans (12:32-33).

Pilate entered his headquarters, called the Lord, and asked Him, "Are you the King of the Jews?" The Lord asked back, "Do you say this of your own accord, or did others say it to you about me?" The Lord asked this to make Pilate think: did he truly care about truth? Pilate said, "Am I a Jew? Your own nation and the chief priests have delivered you over to me. What have you done?"

The Lord answered, "My kingdom is not of this world. If my kingdom were of this world, my servants would have been fighting, that I might not be delivered over to the Jews. But my kingdom is not from the world." How important these words! The Lord's kingdom is not established by the sword, but by truth; not an earthly regime, but heavenly authority.

Pilate asked, "So you are a king?" The Lord said, "You say that I am a king. For this purpose I was born and for this purpose I have come into the world—to bear witness to the truth. Everyone who is of the truth listens to my voice." Pilate asked cynically, "What is truth?" After saying this, he went out and told the Jews, "I find no guilt in him."

But the Jews would not relent. Pilate wanted to release the Lord and proposed the custom of releasing one prisoner at Passover. He asked, "Do you want me to release to you the King of the Jews?" They cried out, "Not this man, but Barabbas!" This Barabbas was a robber—what irony, they would rather have a robber than the Savior.

I stood there watching it all. The Lord stood quietly before Pilate, like a sheep silent before its shearers (Isa 53:7). He could have called on the Father to send more than twelve legions of angels to rescue Him (Matt 26:53), but He did not. He willingly submitted to arrest, questioning, and humiliation, because it was the Father's will, the way of our salvation.

## 历史背景（中文）

第18章记录主被捕和受审的经过，从客西马尼园（18:1）到彼拉多总督府（18:28）。这些事发生在逾越节当晚到次日清晨。

**汲沦溪和客西马尼园**：汲沦溪（Kidron）在耶路撒冷城东，分隔圣殿区和橄榄山。客西马尼园（意为"榨油之地"）在橄榄山脚下，是主和门徒常去的地方。具有讽刺意义的是，大卫逃避押沙龙叛乱时也曾过汲沦溪（撒下15:23）——如今大卫的子孙也经历背叛和受苦。

**罗马兵丁和差役**："一队兵"（σπεῖρα）是罗马军事术语，指一个营（cohort），约600人。祭司长如此大动干戈，显示他们把主当作武装叛乱分子对待。但当主说"我是"（ἐγώ εἰμι）时,这些人都倒下了（6节）——主的神性和权柄在此显明。

**犹太审判程序**：犹太律法要求审判在白天进行，不能在节期，需要多个证人一致。但主的审判违反了所有这些规定——夜间审判（13-24节）、逾越节期间、证人不一致（可14:55-59）。这不是公义的审判，而是预谋的谋杀。

**罗马审判程序**：犹太公会没有死刑权（31节），这是罗马统治下的限制。因此他们必须把主交给彼拉多，让罗马政府执行死刑。彼拉多三次宣告主无罪（38节；19:4、6节），但最终屈服于犹太人的压力（19:12-16）。

**巴拉巴**：名字意为"父之子"（bar-abba），是个强盗和叛乱分子（可15:7）。百姓选择释放巴拉巴、钉死耶稣，正是人类悖逆的写照：宁要罪犯，不要救主。这也成为"代替"（substitution）教义的图像：无罪的耶稣代替有罪的罪人受死。

## Historical Context (English)

Chapter 18 records the Lord's arrest and trials, from the Garden of Gethsemane (18:1) to Pilate's headquarters (18:28). These events occurred from the night of Passover to the following morning.

**Kidron Valley and Gethsemane**: The Kidron Valley is east of Jerusalem, separating the Temple area from the Mount of Olives. Gethsemane (meaning "oil press") was at the foot of the Mount of Olives, a place the Lord and disciples frequently visited. Ironically, David also crossed the Kidron when fleeing Absalom's rebellion (2 Sam 15:23)—now David's descendant also experienced betrayal and suffering.

**Roman Soldiers and Officers**: "A band of soldiers" (σπεῖρα) is a Roman military term referring to a cohort, about 600 men. The chief priests mobilized such a large force, showing they treated the Lord as an armed rebel. But when the Lord said "I am" (ἐγώ εἰμι), these people all fell down (v.6)—the Lord's deity and authority were revealed here.

**Jewish Trial Procedure**: Jewish law required trials to be conducted during the day, not during a feast, and needed consistent witnesses. But the Lord's trial violated all these regulations—nighttime trial (vv.13-24), during Passover, inconsistent witnesses (Mark 14:55-59). This was not a just trial, but premeditated murder.

**Roman Trial Procedure**: The Jewish council lacked authority to execute (v.31), a limitation under Roman rule. Therefore they had to deliver the Lord to Pilate for the Roman government to execute the death penalty. Pilate declared the Lord innocent three times (v.38; 19:4, 6), but ultimately yielded to Jewish pressure (19:12-16).

**Barabbas**: His name means "son of the father" (bar-abba); he was a robber and rebel (Mark 15:7). The people's choice to release Barabbas and crucify Jesus epitomized human rebellion: preferring a criminal to the Savior. This also became an image of the doctrine of "substitution": the sinless Jesus died in place of guilty sinners.

## 经文结构（中文）

本章可分为两大部分——主的被捕和受审：

**一、在客西马尼园被捕（18:1-11）**
主主动迎向捉拿祂的人（4节）。当主说"我是"时，士兵们倒地（6节），显明主若不甘愿，无人能捉拿祂。彼得动刀被主制止（10-11节），主宣告祂要喝父所给的杯。这部分强调主的主权和甘愿受苦。

**二、在犹太人面前受审（18:12-27）**
主被带到亚那和该亚法面前（13-24节）。主宣告祂的教训是公开的（20-21节），却被打（22节）。与此同时，彼得三次不认主（15-18、25-27节），应验了主的预言。这部分交叉叙事（主受审 → 彼得不认主 → 主受审 → 彼得不认主），形成鲜明对比：主坚定承认自己的身份，彼得却否认与主的关系。

**三、在彼拉多面前受审（18:28-40）**
犹太人把主交给罗马总督（28节）。彼拉多与主的对话是本章的高潮：关于王权（33-37节）和真理（37-38节）的对话。主宣告"我的国不属这世界"（36节）和"我来...为真理作见证"（37节）。彼拉多问"真理是什么"（38节），却不等答案。最后，百姓选择释放强盗巴拉巴（39-40节）。

整章结构呈现：主的主权（被捕时）→ 主的坚定（犹太审判中）→ 主的国度（罗马审判中）。主不是被动的受害者，而是主动完成父旨意的救主。

## Structure (English)

This chapter can be divided into two main sections—the Lord's arrest and trials:

**I. Arrest in Gethsemane (18:1-11)**
The Lord actively met those who came to arrest Him (v.4). When the Lord said "I am," the soldiers fell down (v.6), showing that if the Lord did not willingly submit, no one could arrest Him. Peter drew his sword and was stopped by the Lord (vv.10-11), who declared He would drink the cup the Father gave Him. This section emphasizes the Lord's sovereignty and willing suffering.

**II. Trial Before the Jews (18:12-27)**
The Lord was brought before Annas and Caiaphas (vv.13-24). The Lord declared His teaching was public (vv.20-21), yet was struck (v.22). Meanwhile, Peter denied the Lord three times (vv.15-18, 25-27), fulfilling the Lord's prophecy. This section uses intercalated narrative (Lord's trial → Peter's denial → Lord's trial → Peter's denial), forming a stark contrast: the Lord firmly acknowledged His identity, while Peter denied his relationship with the Lord.

**III. Trial Before Pilate (18:28-40)**
The Jews delivered the Lord to the Roman governor (v.28). Pilate's dialogue with the Lord is the climax of this chapter: a conversation about kingship (vv.33-37) and truth (vv.37-38). The Lord declared "My kingdom is not of this world" (v.36) and "I came...to bear witness to the truth" (v.37). Pilate asked "What is truth" (v.38), but did not wait for an answer. Finally, the people chose to release the robber Barabbas (vv.39-40).

The structure of the entire chapter presents: the Lord's sovereignty (at arrest) → the Lord's steadfastness (in Jewish trial) → the Lord's kingdom (in Roman trial). The Lord was not a passive victim, but the Savior actively fulfilling the Father's will.

## 神学要点（中文）

**基督的神性**
"我是"（ἐγώ εἰμι，6节）是神在旧约中的自称（出3:14），主用这话证明祂的神性。当主说这话时，武装士兵倒地——这是神临在的反应（结1:28；但10:9）。改革宗神学坚持基督的完全神性：祂不仅是伟大的教师或先知，而是"神的儿子，第二位格，从永恒被父所生，与父同质，同等同尊，同为永恒"（韦敏斯德小要理问答 Q6）。

**基督的主权**
主不是被动地被捉拿，而是主动喝"父所给的杯"（11节）。改革宗神学强调神的主权贯穿整个救赎计划——包括基督的受死。没有人能夺主的命去，是主自己甘愿舍命（约10:18）。彼拉多以为自己有权柄，主却说："若不是从上头赐给你的，你就毫无权柄办我"（19:11）。即使在十字架上，主仍掌权。

**代赎的教义**
巴拉巴被释放，主被钉十字架（40节），这正是代赎（substitution）的图画。巴拉巴是罪犯，配得死刑；主是无罪的，却代替他受死。这正是福音的核心："神使那无罪的，替我们成为罪，好叫我们在他里面成为神的义"（林后5:21）。改革宗神学的"限定救赎"（particular redemption）在此彰显：主不是为所有人的罪受死（否则所有人都会得救），而是为父所赐给祂的人——就是那些信祂的人——受死（17:9）。

**人的全然败坏**
彼得的三次不认主显明人性的软弱。这个曾经信誓旦旦"必死不认主"的彼得，如今被一个使女一问就跌倒了。这显明人的意志和决心在神的恩典之外是靠不住的。改革宗神学的"全然败坏"（total depravity）不是说人坏到极点，而是说罪影响人的全部——意志、情感、理智。没有神的恩典，最强的人也会跌倒。

**真理的本质**
彼拉多的问题"真理是什么？"（38节）反映了后现代的相对主义。但主已经回答："我...为真理作见证。凡属真理的人就听我的话"（37节）。真理不是抽象概念，而是位格性的——就是基督自己（约14:6）。改革宗神学坚持绝对真理的存在，这真理在圣经中向我们启示，在基督里向我们显现。

## Theology (English)

**Christ's Deity**
"I am" (ἐγώ εἰμι, v.6) is God's self-designation in the Old Testament (Exod 3:14); the Lord used these words to prove His deity. When the Lord spoke these words, armed soldiers fell down—this is the response to God's presence (Ezek 1:28; Dan 10:9). Reformed theology insists on Christ's full deity: He is not merely a great teacher or prophet, but "the Son of God, the second person in the Trinity, eternally begotten of the Father, of the same substance and equal with the Father in power and glory" (Westminster Shorter Catechism Q6).

**Christ's Sovereignty**
The Lord was not passively arrested, but actively drank "the cup that the Father has given" (v.11). Reformed theology emphasizes God's sovereignty throughout the entire plan of redemption—including Christ's death. No one could take the Lord's life from Him; He willingly laid it down (John 10:18). Pilate thought he had authority, but the Lord said, "You would have no authority over me at all unless it had been given you from above" (19:11). Even on the cross, the Lord was in control.

**The Doctrine of Substitution**
Barabbas was released and the Lord was crucified (v.40)—this is the picture of substitution. Barabbas was a criminal, deserving death; the Lord was sinless, yet died in his place. This is the core of the gospel: "For he hath made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in him." (2 Cor 5:21). The Reformed doctrine of "particular redemption" (limited atonement) is manifested here: the Lord did not die for the sins of all people (otherwise all would be saved), but for those the Father gave Him—those who believe in Him (17:9).

**Total Depravity**
Peter's three denials reveal human weakness. This Peter who had sworn "I will never deny you" now stumbled when questioned by a servant girl. This shows that human will and determination apart from God's grace are unreliable. The Reformed doctrine of "total depravity" does not mean people are as bad as they could be, but that sin affects all of a person—will, emotions, intellect. Without God's grace, even the strongest person will fall.

**The Nature of Truth**
Pilate's question "What is truth?" (v.38) reflects postmodern relativism. But the Lord had already answered: "I came...to bear witness to the truth. Everyone who is of the truth listens to my voice" (v.37). Truth is not an abstract concept, but personal—it is Christ Himself (John 14:6). Reformed theology insists on the existence of absolute truth, revealed to us in Scripture and manifested to us in Christ.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**羊与牧人**
主被捉拿时说："你们若找我，就让这些人去吧"（8节）。这应验了主在约翰福音10章的话："好牧人为羊舍命"（10:11）。以西结书34章预言神自己要来作祂百姓的牧人，如今这预言应验了——主用自己的身体保护属祂的羊。

**父的杯**
"我父所给我的那杯，我岂可不喝呢？"（11节）这杯在旧约中常指神的忿怒（诗75:8；赛51:17；耶25:15）。主要喝的不是殉道者的杯，而是代替罪人承受神忿怒的杯。客西马尼园的祷告"父阿，倘若可行，求你叫这杯离开我"（太26:39）显明这杯何等可怕。但主甘愿喝这杯，因为不喝这杯，我们就要永远喝神忿怒的杯。

**逾越节的羔羊**
犹太人为了不染污秽、能吃逾越节（28节），却把真正的逾越节羔羊送上十字架。出埃及记12章的逾越节羔羊预表基督："你们的羊羔当是无残疾的"（出12:5）——彼拉多三次宣告"我查不出他有什么罪来"。羔羊的血使以色列人免受死亡——基督的血使我们免受永死。保罗明说："我们逾越节的羔羊基督已经被杀献祭了"（林前5:7）。

**沉默的羔羊**
主在受审时的沉默应验了以赛亚书53:7："他被欺压，在受苦的时候却不开口；他像羊羔被牵到宰杀之地，又像羊在剪毛的人手下无声，他也是这样不开口。"主不是因为无话可说，而是甘愿承受不义的审判。

**真理作见证**
主对彼拉多说："我...来到世间，特为给真理作见证"（37节）。这应验了以赛亚书42:1-4的"仆人之歌"："看哪，我的仆人...他不喧嚷，不扬声...他凭真实将公理传开。"主的一生就是真理的见证——祂的教训、神迹、受死、复活，都指向那唯一的真理：神爱世人，赐下独生子作挽回祭。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Sheep and Shepherd**
When the Lord was arrested, He said, "If you seek me, let these men go" (v.8). This fulfilled the Lord's words in John 10: "The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep" (10:11). Ezekiel 34 prophesied that God Himself would come to be the shepherd of His people; now this prophecy was fulfilled—the Lord protected His sheep with His own body.

**The Father's Cup**
"Shall I not drink the cup that the Father has given me?" (v.11) This cup in the Old Testament often refers to God's wrath (Ps 75:8; Isa 51:17; Jer 25:15). The Lord would drink not the cup of a martyr, but the cup of bearing God's wrath in place of sinners. The prayer in Gethsemane "And he went a little farther, and fell on his face, and prayed, saying, O my Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me: nevertheless not as I will, but as thou wilt." (Matt 26:39) reveals how terrible this cup was. But the Lord willingly drank this cup, because if He did not drink it, we would have to drink the cup of God's wrath forever.

**The Passover Lamb**
The Jews, in order to avoid defilement and eat the Passover (v.28), sent the true Passover Lamb to the cross. The Passover lamb of Exodus 12 prefigured Christ: "Your lamb shall be without blemish, a male of the first year: ye shall take it out from the sheep, or from the goats:" (Exod 12:5)—Pilate three times declared "I find no guilt in him." The lamb's blood spared Israel from death—Christ's blood spares us from eternal death. Paul states explicitly: "Purge out therefore the old leaven, that ye may be a new lump, as ye are unleavened. For even Christ our passover is sacrificed for us:" (1 Cor 5:7).

**The Silent Lamb**
The Lord's silence during His trial fulfilled Isaiah 53:7: "He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth; like a lamb that is led to the slaughter, and like a sheep that before its shearers is silent, so he opened not his mouth." The Lord was not silent because He had nothing to say, but willingly endured unjust judgment.

**Bearing Witness to Truth**
The Lord told Pilate, "I came...to bear witness to the truth" (v.37). This fulfilled the "Servant Song" of Isaiah 42:1-4: "Behold my servant...He will not cry aloud or lift up his voice...he will faithfully bring forth justice." The Lord's entire life was a witness to truth—His teaching, miracles, death, and resurrection all point to that one truth: God loved the world and gave His only Son as a propitiation.
