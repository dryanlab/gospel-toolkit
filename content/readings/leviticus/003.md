## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第三章介绍的是平安祭（shelamim，来自shalom，平安）——五大祭中唯一可以让献祭者自己吃的祭。如果燔祭是完全的奉献（全烧），素祭是劳动的奉献（面粉），那么平安祭是喜乐的分享——神、祭司和献祭者一同坐席。

> "人献供物为平安祭，若是从牛群中献，无论是公的是母的，必用没有残疾的献在耶和华面前。"

弟兄姐妹，注意平安祭与燔祭的两个关键差异。第一，燔祭只能献公的——平安祭可以献公的也可以献母的。限制更少、门槛更低、参与更广。第二，燔祭全部烧掉——平安祭只烧脂油，其余的肉分给祭司和献祭者吃。

平安祭的流程与燔祭相似——按手、宰杀、洒血——但之后的处理完全不同。只有脂油被烧在坛上——"盖脏的脂油和脏上所有的脂油，并两个腰子和腰子上的脂油……一概取下"烧在坛上。

为什么只烧脂油？弟兄姐妹，脂油在古代代表最好的部分——"奶油和蜂蜜"的"脂肪"。利未记3:16说"脂油都是耶和华的"——最好的部分属于神。你可以吃肉——但最肥美的部分要烧给神。这是一个优先顺序的教导：神得到最好的，人得到剩余的。

平安祭有三种动物来源——牛、绵羊羔、山羊。三种都有同样的原则：按手、宰杀、洒血、脂油烧坛。但绵羊的描述多了一个细节："整肥尾巴都要在靠近脊骨处取下"烧给神。中东的肥尾羊（fat-tailed sheep）的尾巴是最肥美的部分——可以重达十几斤。这是整只羊最好的脂肪——归给神。

弟兄姐妹，平安祭的核心神学是什么？它的名字告诉了一切——shalom，平安。平安祭不是为了赎罪（那是赎罪祭的功能），不是为了表达完全的奉献（那是燔祭的功能）——平安祭是在赎罪已经完成之后的感恩和团契。

平安祭的肉被分为三份：脂油归神（烧在坛上）、胸和右腿归祭司（利7:31-34会详细说明）、其余的肉归献祭者和他的家人朋友。弟兄姐妹，三方分享同一只牲畜的肉——神（脂油的烟）、祭司（胸和腿）、百姓（其余的肉）——这是一场盟约的宴席。三方共同享用同一个牺牲——这意味着神、祭司和百姓在同一张桌子上。

在古代近东，共同吃饭是最深的关系表达——一起吃饭意味着你不是我的敌人，我们之间有平安。平安祭就是与神一起吃饭——经过赎罪之后，罪人和圣洁的神坐在同一张桌子前，分享同一只牲畜的肉。这是关系的恢复、友谊的确认、团契的欢庆。

弟兄姐妹，利未记的五大祭有一个内在的逻辑顺序：先赎罪（赎罪祭和赎愆祭，第4-5章），然后完全献身（燔祭，第1章），然后日常劳动归主（素祭，第2章），最后与神欢喜团契（平安祭，第3章）。从解决罪的问题到享受与神的关系——这是信仰生活的完整画面。你不能跳过赎罪直接享受团契——先要解决罪的隔绝，然后才有平安的宴席。

最后一节经文包含了一个永久的禁令："脂油和血都不可吃；这要成为你们世世代代永远的定例。"

不可吃脂油——因为脂油属于神。不可吃血——因为"血就是生命"（利17:11）。生命属于神——你不能消耗它来满足自己。这个禁令在新约中被使徒会议重申——使徒行传15:29"禁戒偶像的污秽和奸淫，并勒死的牲畜和血"——血的禁令是旧约中少数被新约教会继续遵守的律例之一。

弟兄姐妹，平安祭是利未记中最温暖、最欢乐的祭——因为它的目的不是处理罪（那是严肃的），而是庆祝与神的关系（那是喜乐的）。在所有的罪被处理之后，在完全的献身之后，在日常的劳动归主之后——最终的画面是一个家庭围坐在一起，吃着与神共享的肉，在感恩和喜乐中享受shalom——那完全的、没有隔绝的、与神和好的平安。

这就是福音的最终图景——不是永远跪在祭坛前认罪（虽然这是必要的起点），而是最终坐在宴席上与神同乐。路加福音15:23浪子归来后，父亲说"把那肥牛犊牵来宰了，我们可以吃喝快乐"——那是平安祭的终极实现。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 3 introduces the peace offering (shelamim, from shalom, peace)—the only one of the five major offerings that the offerer himself could eat. If the burnt offering was complete surrender (entirely burned), and the grain offering was labor's surrender (flour), the peace offering was joyful sharing—God, priest, and offerer dining together.

> "If his offering is a sacrifice of peace offering, if he offers an animal from the herd, male or female, he shall offer it without blemish before the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, note two key differences between the peace offering and the burnt offering. First, the burnt offering was male only—the peace offering could be male or female. Fewer restrictions, lower threshold, broader participation. Second, the burnt offering was entirely burned—the peace offering burned only the fat; the remaining meat was shared between priest and offerer.

The peace offering's procedure resembled the burnt offering—laying on hands, slaughter, blood sprinkling—but afterward the handling was entirely different. Only the fat was burned on the altar—"the fat that covers the inner parts, all the fat that is on the inner parts, the two kidneys with the fat on them" removed and burned on the altar.

Why burn only the fat? Brothers and sisters, fat in antiquity represented the best portion—the "richest" part. Leviticus 3:16 says "All fat is the LORD's"—the best belongs to God. You may eat the meat—but the richest portion burns for God. This was a lesson in priority: God receives the best; humans receive the remainder.

The peace offering had three animal sources—cattle, lamb, and goat. All three followed the same principle: laying on of hands, slaughter, blood sprinkling, fat burned on the altar. But the lamb's description added one detail: "the whole fat tail cut off close to the backbone" burned for God. Middle Eastern fat-tailed sheep had tails weighing up to several kilograms—the sheep's finest fat—given to God.

Brothers and sisters, what was the peace offering's core theology? Its name tells everything—shalom, peace. The peace offering was not for atonement (that was the sin offering's function), not for expressing complete surrender (that was the burnt offering's function)—the peace offering was thanksgiving and fellowship after atonement was already complete.

The peace offering's meat was divided three ways: fat to God (burned on the altar), breast and right thigh to the priest (Leviticus 7:31-34 details this), remaining meat to the offerer and his family and friends. Brothers and sisters, three parties sharing one animal's meat—God (the fat's smoke), priest (breast and thigh), people (the rest)—this was a covenant feast. Three parties enjoying the same sacrifice meant God, priest, and people at the same table.

In the ancient Near East, eating together was the deepest relational expression—sharing a meal meant you are not my enemy; there is peace between us. The peace offering was dining with God—after atonement, the sinner and the holy God sitting at the same table, sharing the same animal's meat. This was relationship restored, friendship confirmed, fellowship celebrated.

Brothers and sisters, Leviticus's five offerings follow an internal logical sequence: first atonement (sin and guilt offerings, chapters 4-5), then complete surrender (burnt offering, chapter 1), then daily labor consecrated to the Lord (grain offering, chapter 2), finally joyful fellowship with God (peace offering, chapter 3). From resolving sin's problem to enjoying relationship with God—this is the complete picture of the life of faith. You cannot skip atonement and jump to fellowship—first resolve sin's separation, then the peace feast.

The final verse contained a permanent prohibition: "It shall be a statute forever throughout your generations, in all your dwelling places, that you eat neither fat nor blood."

No eating fat—because fat belongs to God. No eating blood—because "For the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul." (Leviticus 17:11). Life belongs to God—you cannot consume it for self-satisfaction. This prohibition was reaffirmed by the apostolic council in the New Testament—Acts 15:29: "Abstain from what has been sacrificed to idols, and from blood, and from what has been strangled"—the blood prohibition is among the few Old Testament regulations that the New Testament church continued to observe.

Brothers and sisters, the peace offering was Leviticus's warmest, most joyful offering—because its purpose was not addressing sin (which is solemn) but celebrating relationship with God (which is joyful). After all sin was addressed, after complete dedication, after daily labor consecrated—the final picture was a family seated together eating meat shared with God, in gratitude and joy enjoying shalom—that complete, unobstructed, reconciled peace with God.

This is the gospel's ultimate picture—not forever kneeling at the altar confessing sin (though this is the necessary starting point), but ultimately seated at a feast rejoicing with God. Luke 15:23, after the prodigal's return, the father said "Bring the fattened calf and kill it, and let us eat and celebrate"—that was the peace offering's ultimate fulfillment.

## 历史背景（中文）

平安祭（shelamim）的词根shalom在古代近东的外交文书中广泛使用——阿马尔纳书信中（约公元前14世纪）附庸国写信给法老时常以"shulmu"（平安）问候开始。平安祭在以色列历史中有几个重要时刻：撒母耳记上11:15扫罗在吉甲被立为王时"在耶和华面前献平安祭"；撒母耳记下6:17大卫迎约柜回来时"在耶和华面前献燔祭和平安祭"；列王纪上8:63所罗门献殿时"用牛二万二千，羊十二万为平安祭"。肥尾羊（Ovis laticaudata）是中东特有品种，尾巴的脂肪是储存能量的重要部位——这种羊至今仍在中东和北非饲养。"脂油都是耶和华的"（v.16）——在古代近东其他文化中，最好的部分通常归祭司或国王——以色列独特地将最好的部分归给神自己。

## Historical Context (English)

The peace offering (shelamim) root word shalom was widely used in ancient Near Eastern diplomatic correspondence—the Amarna Letters (c. 14th century BC) show vassal states writing to Pharaoh often beginning with "shulmu" (peace). The peace offering marked several important moments in Israel's history: 1 Samuel 11:15, Saul crowned king at Gilgal "offered peace offerings before the LORD"; 2 Samuel 6:17, David bringing back the ark "offered burnt offerings and peace offerings before the LORD"; 1 Kings 8:63, Solomon's temple dedication with "22,000 oxen and 120,000 sheep as peace offerings." The fat-tailed sheep (Ovis laticaudata) is a breed unique to the Middle East, the tail's fat serving as an important energy reserve—this breed is still raised in the Middle East and North Africa today. "All fat is the LORD's" (v.16)—in other ancient Near Eastern cultures, the best portion typically went to priests or kings—Israel uniquely gave the best portion to God Himself.

## 经文结构（中文）

**牛的平安祭（1-5节）**——公或母、没有残疾、按手宰杀洒血、脂油腰子肝网子取下烧坛上、馨香火祭。

**绵羊羔的平安祭（6-11节）**——公或母、按手宰杀洒血、整肥尾巴靠脊骨处取下、脂油腰子烧坛上、"献给耶和华为食物的火祭"。

**山羊的平安祭（12-16节）**——按手宰杀洒血、脂油腰子肝网子取下烧坛上、"脂油都是耶和华的"。

**永远的禁令（17节）**——脂油和血都不可吃、世世代代永远的定例。

## Structure (English)

**Cattle Peace Offering (vv. 1-5)**—Male or female, without blemish, laying hands slaughter blood sprinkling, fat kidneys liver covering removed and burned on altar, a pleasing aroma.

**Lamb Peace Offering (vv. 6-11)**—Male or female, laying hands slaughter blood sprinkling, entire fat tail cut off near backbone, fat kidneys burned on altar, "a food offering to the LORD."

**Goat Peace Offering (vv. 12-16)**—Laying hands slaughter blood sprinkling, fat kidneys liver covering removed and burned on altar, "All fat is the LORD's."

**Permanent Prohibition (v. 17)**—No eating fat or blood, a statute forever throughout all generations.

## 神学要点（中文）

**与神同席——团契的神学**
平安祭的核心是三方共享一个牺牲——改革宗圣餐神学在这里找到旧约的根基。加尔文特别强调圣餐是"与基督真实的灵性相交"——不只是纪念，而是真正的团契。韦敏斯德信条第29章论主的圣餐说信徒"灵性地接受并喂养于被钉十字架的基督"——圣餐是平安祭的新约实现：信徒与基督同席，分享祂牺牲的成果。

**脂油归神——最好的献给神**
"脂油都是耶和华的"——改革宗传统教导：我们最好的——最好的时间、精力、才能、财物——应该先献给神。箴言3:9"你要以财物和一切初熟的土产尊荣耶和华。"不是把剩余的给神，而是把最好的给神，自己享用剩余的。

**先赎罪后团契——次序不可颠倒**
平安祭建立在赎罪祭的基础上——你不能在罪未解决的情况下与神同席。改革宗传统强调称义先于成圣——必须先解决罪的法律问题（称义），然后才能进入与神同行的关系（成圣和团契）。以赛亚书59:2"你们的罪孽使你们与神隔绝。"罪造成隔绝——赎罪解除隔绝——然后平安祭的团契才可能。

## Theology (English)

**Dining with God—The Theology of Fellowship**
The peace offering's core was three parties sharing one sacrifice—Reformed eucharistic theology finds its Old Testament foundation here. Calvin especially emphasized the Lord's Supper as "genuine spiritual communion with Christ"—not merely memorial but true fellowship. Westminster Confession chapter 29 on the Lord's Supper says believers "spiritually receive and feed upon Christ crucified"—the Lord's Supper is the peace offering's new covenant fulfillment: believers dining with Christ, sharing His sacrifice's fruit.

**Fat Belongs to God—The Best Given to God**
"All fat is the LORD's"—the Reformed tradition teaches: our best—best time, energy, talents, resources—should be given to God first. Proverbs 3:9: "Honor the LORD with your wealth and with the firstfruits of all your produce." Not giving God the leftovers but giving God the best, ourselves enjoying the remainder.

**Atonement Before Fellowship—The Order Cannot Be Reversed**
The peace offering was built on the sin offering's foundation—you cannot dine with God while sin remains unresolved. The Reformed tradition emphasizes justification precedes sanctification—sin's legal problem must be resolved first (justification) before entering a relationship of walking with God (sanctification and fellowship). Isaiah 59:2: "Your iniquities have made a separation between you and your God." Sin creates separation—atonement removes separation—then the peace offering's fellowship becomes possible.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第三章的平安祭是基督带来的"与神和好"最美的旧约预表。

"平安"（shalom）——以弗所书2:14"因他使我们和睦（平安），将两下合而为一，拆毁了中间隔断的墙。"基督就是我们的平安——不只是带来平安的使者，而是平安本身。歌罗西书1:20"既然藉着他在十字架上所流的血成就了和平，便藉着他叫万有……都与自己和好了。"

三方同席——基督的十字架使三方和解成为可能：神与人和好（纵向的和平）、人与人和好（横向的和平）。以弗所书2:16"既在十字架上灭了冤仇，便藉这十字架使两下归为一体，与神和好了。"平安祭的三方分享（神、祭司、百姓）预表了基督在十字架上成就的全方位和平。

"脂油都是耶和华的"——基督把最好的——祂自己的生命——献给了父。约翰福音17:4"我在地上已经荣耀你，你所托付我的事，我已成全了。"基督完全的顺服是献给父的"脂油"——最好的、最肥美的、毫无保留的。

路加福音15:23的"肥牛犊"——浪子回头后父亲宰肥牛犊庆祝——这是平安祭的福音图画。罪人归回、罪得赦免、关系恢复——然后是宴席。启示录19:9"凡被请赴羔羊之婚筵的有福了！"——这是终极的平安祭宴席。从利未记的平安祭到浪子的肥牛犊到羔羊的婚宴——神与人同席的主题贯穿整部圣经。

不可吃血——基督的血不是被"消费"的——而是被"接受"的。约翰福音6:53-54耶稣说"你们若不吃人子的肉，不喝人子的血，就没有生命在你们里面。"这不是字面的吃血（利未记禁止），而是凭信心接受基督牺牲的功效。旧约禁止吃物质的血——新约邀请人凭信心接受基督属灵的血（赎罪的功效）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 3's peace offering is the most beautiful Old Testament prefiguration of the reconciliation Christ brings.

"Peace" (shalom)—Ephesians 2:14: "For he himself is our peace, who has made us both one and has broken down in his flesh the dividing wall of hostility." Christ is our peace—not merely a messenger bringing peace but peace itself. Colossians 1:20: "And through him to reconcile to himself all things...making peace by the blood of his cross."

Three parties dining together—Christ's cross makes three-way reconciliation possible: God and humanity reconciled (vertical peace), humans with each other reconciled (horizontal peace). Ephesians 2:16: "And might reconcile us both to God in one body through the cross, thereby killing the hostility." The peace offering's three-way sharing (God, priest, people) prefigured the comprehensive peace Christ accomplished on the cross.

"All fat is the LORD's"—Christ gave the best—His own life—to the Father. John 17:4: "I glorified you on earth, having accomplished the work that you gave me to do." Christ's complete obedience was the "fat" offered to the Father—the best, the richest, without reserve.

Luke 15:23's "fattened calf"—after the prodigal's return the father slaughtered the fattened calf to celebrate—this is the peace offering's gospel picture. Sinner returns, sin forgiven, relationship restored—then the feast. Revelation 19:9: "Blessed are those who are invited to the marriage supper of the Lamb!"—this is the ultimate peace offering feast. From Leviticus's peace offering to the prodigal's fattened calf to the Lamb's wedding banquet—God dining with humanity threads through the entire Bible.

The blood prohibition—Christ's blood is not "consumed"—it is "received." John 6:53-54: Jesus said "Unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink his blood, you have no life in you." This was not literal blood-drinking (Leviticus forbids it) but receiving by faith the efficacy of Christ's sacrifice. The Old Testament prohibited drinking physical blood—the New Testament invites receiving by faith Christ's spiritual blood (atonement's efficacy).
