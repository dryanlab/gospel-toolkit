## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十三章是利未记中最欢乐的一章——节期日历。前面几章充满了"不可""治死""不洁净"——第二十三章终于进入了"欢乐""圣会""安息"的领域。弟兄姐妹，信仰不只有严肃和敬畏——也有喜乐和庆典。神不只命令以色列人守规矩——也命令他们过节。

"耶和华的节期，你们要宣告为圣会的节期。"——弟兄姐妹，这些不是以色列人发明的节日——这些是"耶和华的节期"。神设立了节日——祂要祂的百姓定期停下来，聚在一起庆祝祂的作为。

第二十三章按照时间顺序列出了七个节期——从春天到秋天，构成了以色列的神圣年历：

第一个：安息日（3节）——每周一次。"六日要做工，第七日是圣安息日。"弟兄姐妹，安息日不是年度节期，但它是所有节期的基础——每七天一次的停工、安息、敬拜。它提醒以色列人：你的生命不是由工作定义的——是由安息和敬拜定义的。

第二个：逾越节和无酵节（5-8节）——正月十四日至二十一日。逾越节纪念出埃及那夜——羔羊的血涂在门框上，灭命的天使越过。无酵节紧接着——七天吃无酵饼，象征离开埃及的急促（来不及发面）和除去旧酵（罪）。

第三个：初熟节（9-14节）——无酵节期间安息日的次日。"要将初熟的庄稼一捆带给祭司……在耶和华面前摇一摇。"弟兄姐妹，初熟的庄稼是收成的第一把——你把最先收获的献给神，承认所有的收成都来自祂。在你吃自己的食物之前——先把最好的给神。

第四个：五旬节/七七节（15-22节）——初熟节后五十天。"到第七个安息日的次日，共计五十天。"五旬节是麦子收割结束的感恩节——两个加酵的饼（注意：有酵的！这是唯一用有酵饼的献祭），代表了日常生活中的食物被带到神面前。

弟兄姐妹，春天的四个节期（逾越节、无酵节、初熟节、五旬节）集中在正月到三月——从出埃及到进入应许之地的庄稼收割。然后有大约四个月的空白——从三月到七月——以色列人在田间劳作。

第五个：吹角节（23-25节）——七月初一。"要吹角作纪念，当有圣会。"弟兄姐妹，号角声在旷野中是集合的信号——吹角节宣告了秋季节期的开始，也是一个严肃的召唤：赎罪日即将来临，准备好你的心。

第六个：赎罪日（26-32节）——七月初十。"要刻苦己心……甚么工都不可做。"弟兄姐妹，赎罪日是节期日历中最严肃的一天——不是庆典而是忏悔，不是欢乐而是刻苦己心。这一天的详细仪式在第十六章已经论述——大祭司进入至圣所为全民赎罪。

第七个：住棚节（33-44节）——七月十五日起七天。"你们要住在棚里七日……好叫你们世世代代知道，我领以色列人出埃及地的时候曾使他们住在棚里。"

弟兄姐妹，住棚节是一年中最欢乐的节期——以色列人搭临时的棚子住在里面七天。"第一日要拿美好树上的果子和棕树上的枝子，与茂密树的枝条并河旁的柳枝，在耶和华你们的神面前欢乐七日。"

住棚节的目的是"纪念"——纪念旷野四十年的漂泊、神的供应、帐棚中的生活。弟兄姐妹，以色列人已经住在迦南地的石头房子里了——但每年七天，他们离开舒适的房子住进脆弱的棚子——提醒自己：你在这世上是寄居的。你的安全不在于房子的坚固——而在于神的同在。以色列在旷野住棚四十年并没有缺乏——因为神与他们同在。

弟兄姐妹，七个节期构成了一个完整的属灵年历：从逾越节（救赎的开始）到住棚节（与神同住的喜乐）——从被拯救到进入安息——从十字架到天堂。整个救赎历史被浓缩在一年的节期循环中。

"第八日也为圣安息"——住棚节有一个"第八日"，超出了七天的节期。弟兄姐妹，七是完全的数字——第八日是超越完全的、新创造的开始。住棚节的第八日指向一个终极的安息——不是暂时的节期而是永恒的同在。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 23 is Leviticus's most joyful chapter—the festival calendar. Previous chapters were filled with "you shall not," "put to death," "unclean"—chapter 23 finally entered the realm of "rejoice," "holy assembly," "rest." Brothers and sisters, faith was not only solemnity and reverence—but also joy and celebration. God did not only command Israel to follow rules—He commanded them to feast.

"These are the appointed feasts of the LORD that you shall proclaim as holy convocations"—Brothers and sisters, these were not Israel's invented holidays—these were "the LORD's appointed feasts." God established festivals—He wanted His people to regularly stop, gather, and celebrate His works.

Chapter 23 listed seven festivals chronologically—spring to autumn, forming Israel's sacred calendar:

First: the Sabbath (v. 3)—weekly. "Six days shall work be done, but on the seventh day is a Sabbath of solemn rest." Brothers and sisters, the Sabbath was not an annual festival, but was all festivals' foundation—every seven days ceasing work, resting, worshipping. It reminded Israel: your life is not defined by work—but by rest and worship.

Second: Passover and Unleavened Bread (vv. 5-8)—the first month, fourteenth to twenty-first. Passover commemorated the exodus night—lamb's blood on doorframes, the destroying angel passing over. Unleavened Bread immediately followed—seven days eating unleavened bread, symbolizing departure's haste (no time for dough to rise) and removing old leaven (sin).

Third: Firstfruits (vv. 9-14)—the day after the Sabbath during Unleavened Bread. "You shall bring the sheaf of the firstfruits of your harvest to the priest...He shall wave the sheaf before the LORD." Brothers and sisters, firstfruits were harvest's first sheaf—you gave the first gathered to God, acknowledging all harvest came from Him. Before eating your own food—give God the best first.

Fourth: Pentecost/Feast of Weeks (vv. 15-22)—fifty days after Firstfruits. "Counting fifty days to the day after the seventh Sabbath." Pentecost was the wheat harvest thanksgiving—two leavened loaves (note: leavened! the only offering using leavened bread), representing daily life's food brought before God.

Brothers and sisters, spring's four festivals (Passover, Unleavened Bread, Firstfruits, Pentecost) concentrated in the first through third months—from exodus to promised land harvest. Then approximately four months' gap—third to seventh month—Israel laboring in fields.

Fifth: Feast of Trumpets (vv. 23-25)—seventh month, first day. "A memorial proclaimed with blast of trumpets, a holy convocation." Brothers and sisters, trumpet blasts were the wilderness gathering signal—Trumpets announced autumn festivals' beginning and a solemn summons: the Day of Atonement approaches, prepare your heart.

Sixth: Day of Atonement (vv. 26-32)—seventh month, tenth day. "You shall afflict yourselves...you shall not do any work." Brothers and sisters, the Day of Atonement was the calendar's most solemn day—not celebration but repentance, not joy but self-affliction. This day's detailed ritual was covered in chapter 16—the high priest entering the Most Holy Place atoning for all the people.

Seventh: Feast of Tabernacles/Booths (vv. 33-44)—seventh month, fifteenth day, for seven days. "You shall dwell in booths for seven days...that your generations may know that I made the people of Israel dwell in booths when I brought them out of the land of Egypt."

Brothers and sisters, Tabernacles was the year's most joyful festival—Israelites built temporary shelters and lived in them for seven days. "On the first day you shall take the fruit of splendid trees, branches of palm trees, and boughs of leafy trees and willows of the brook, and you shall rejoice before the LORD your God seven days."

Tabernacles' purpose was "remembrance"—remembering forty years' wilderness wandering, God's provision, tent-dwelling life. Brothers and sisters, Israelites were living in Canaan's stone houses—but every year for seven days they left comfortable homes for fragile booths—reminding themselves: you are sojourners in this world. Your security lies not in your house's strength—but in God's presence. Israel dwelt in wilderness booths forty years without lack—because God was with them.

Brothers and sisters, seven festivals formed a complete spiritual calendar: from Passover (redemption's beginning) to Tabernacles (joy of dwelling with God)—from being saved to entering rest—from the cross to heaven. All redemptive history was condensed into one year's festival cycle.

"On the eighth day...a solemn rest"—Tabernacles had an "eighth day" beyond the seven-day festival. Brothers and sisters, seven was the number of completeness—the eighth day was the beginning beyond completeness, a new creation. Tabernacles' eighth day pointed to an ultimate rest—not a temporary festival but eternal presence.

## 历史背景（中文）

以色列的节期日历在古代近东独一无二——虽然所有农业文化都有收获节，但没有哪个文化像以色列一样把节期系统性地与救赎历史挂钩。逾越节纪念出埃及、住棚节纪念旷野漂泊——节期不只是庆祝丰收，而是重新经历神的救赎作为。"五旬节"（pentēkostē）这个名字来自希腊文"第五十"——犹太传统后来把五旬节与西奈山颁布律法联系起来（出19:1暗示以色列在第三个月到达西奈）。住棚节在第二圣殿时期成为最盛大的节期——约瑟夫说它是"犹太人最神圣、最伟大的节日"。住棚节期间有水的浇灌礼（nisukh ha-mayim）——祭司从西罗亚池打水浇在坛上，约翰福音7:37-38记载耶稣在住棚节的最后一天站起来大声说"人若渴了，可以到我这里来喝"。吹角节后来成为犹太新年（Rosh Hashanah），但利未记23章并没有把它称为新年。

## Historical Context (English)

Israel's festival calendar was unique in the ancient Near East—though all agricultural cultures had harvest festivals, none systematically linked festivals to redemptive history like Israel. Passover commemorated the exodus, Tabernacles the wilderness wandering—festivals were not merely celebrating harvest but re-experiencing God's redemptive acts. "Pentecost" (pentēkostē) came from Greek "fiftieth"—Jewish tradition later connected Pentecost with Sinai's law-giving (Exod 19:1 suggests Israel reached Sinai in the third month). Tabernacles became the Second Temple period's grandest festival—Josephus called it "the holiest and greatest feast among the Jews." During Tabernacles, a water-pouring ceremony (nisukh ha-mayim) occurred—priests drew water from the Pool of Siloam and poured it on the altar; John 7:37-38 records Jesus standing on Tabernacles' last day crying out "If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink." Trumpets later became Jewish New Year (Rosh Hashanah), though Leviticus 23 did not call it that.

## 经文结构（中文）

**安息日（3节）**——每周第七日、圣安息日、不做工。

**逾越节+无酵节（4-8节）**——正月14日逾越节、15-21日无酵节七天吃无酵饼、首尾两日圣会。

**初熟节（9-14节）**——安息日次日、摇初熟庄稼一捆、献燔祭素祭奠祭。

**五旬节/七七节（15-22节）**——初熟节后五十天、两个加酵饼+七只羊羔+公牛犊+公绵羊、田角落穗留穷人。

**吹角节（23-25节）**——七月初一、吹角纪念、圣会不做工。

**赎罪日（26-32节）**——七月初十、刻苦己心、甚么工都不做、不刻苦者被剪除。

**住棚节（33-44节）**——七月15-21日住棚七天+第八日严肃会、拿果子棕枝柳枝欢乐、纪念旷野住棚。

## Structure (English)

**Sabbath (v. 3)**—Every seventh day, solemn rest, no work.

**Passover + Unleavened Bread (vv. 4-8)**—1st month day 14 Passover, 15-21 Unleavened Bread seven days no leaven, first and last days holy assembly.

**Firstfruits (vv. 9-14)**—Day after Sabbath, wave sheaf of firstfruits, burnt grain drink offerings.

**Pentecost/Weeks (vv. 15-22)**—Fifty days after Firstfruits, two leavened loaves + seven lambs + bull + two rams, field corners for poor.

**Trumpets (vv. 23-25)**—7th month day 1, memorial trumpet blast, holy assembly no work.

**Day of Atonement (vv. 26-32)**—7th month day 10, afflict yourselves, absolutely no work, failure to afflict means cut off.

**Tabernacles (vv. 33-44)**—7th month days 15-21 seven days in booths + 8th day solemn assembly, take fruit palm willow branches rejoice, remember wilderness booths.

## 神学要点（中文）

**安息日——创造的冠冕和救赎的预尝**
改革宗传统把安息日视为创造秩序（神在第七日安息）和救赎秩序（在基督里的属灵安息）的交汇。韦敏斯德信条第二十一章论安息日教导：神从创造之初就指定七天中的一天为圣安息日。安息日不只是休息——它是对创造的感恩和对终极安息的盼望。

**节期是神学的戏剧——重新经历救赎**
每个节期不只是"纪念"过去——而是"重新经历"神的作为。改革宗传统把圣礼（洗礼和圣餐）也理解为"可见的话语"——节期就是旧约的"可见的话语"，用身体的行动（吃无酵饼、住棚子、禁食）来"演出"救赎历史。

**七个节期的完整性——救赎历史的缩影**
从逾越节（十字架）到住棚节（新天新地）——七个节期涵盖了整个救赎计划。改革宗传统教导救赎历史有一个"从创造到新创造"的完整弧线——利未记23章的节期日历就是这个弧线的旧约版本。

## Theology (English)

**Sabbath—Creation's Crown and Redemption's Foretaste**
The Reformed tradition views the Sabbath as the intersection of creation order (God rested on the seventh day) and redemption order (spiritual rest in Christ). Westminster Confession chapter 21 on the Sabbath: God has in His Word, by a positive, moral, and perpetual commandment binding all men in all ages, appointed one day in seven for a Sabbath. Sabbath was not merely rest—it was gratitude for creation and hope for ultimate rest.

**Festivals as Theological Drama—Re-Experiencing Redemption**
Each festival was not merely "commemorating" the past—but "re-experiencing" God's acts. The Reformed tradition understands sacraments (baptism and Lord's Supper) as "visible words"—festivals were the Old Testament's "visible words," using bodily actions (eating unleavened bread, dwelling in booths, fasting) to "dramatize" redemptive history.

**Seven Festivals' Completeness—Redemptive History in Miniature**
From Passover (the cross) to Tabernacles (new heaven and earth)—seven festivals encompassed the entire redemptive plan. The Reformed tradition teaches redemptive history has a complete arc "from creation to new creation"—Leviticus 23's festival calendar is this arc's Old Testament version.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第二十三章的七个节期每一个都在基督里找到了成全——这是旧约预表论中最完美的对应之一。

逾越节——基督是逾越节的羔羊。哥林多前书5:7"因为我们逾越节的羔羊基督已经被杀献祭了。"基督在逾越节期间被钉十字架——完美地应验了时间。

无酵节——基督是无罪的（无酵的）。哥林多前书5:8"所以，我们守这节不可用旧酵……只用诚实真正的无酵饼。"除酵象征除去罪——基督使我们脱离罪的辖制。

初熟节——基督是"睡了之人初熟的果子"。哥林多前书15:20"但基督已经从死里复活，成为睡了之人初熟的果子。"基督在安息日的次日（七日的第一日）复活——正是初熟节的日子。

五旬节——圣灵在五旬节降临。使徒行传2:1-4"五旬节到了，门徒都聚集在一处。忽然……他们就都被圣灵充满。"初熟节后五十天——复活后五十天——圣灵降下。旧约的五旬节献两个加酵的饼——犹太人和外邦人，两个群体被圣灵合为一个教会。

吹角节——基督再来的号角。帖撒罗尼迦前书4:16"因为主必亲自从天降临，有呼叫的声音和天使长的声音，又有神的号吹响。"

赎罪日——基督一次永远的赎罪。希伯来书9:12"乃用自己的血，只一次进入圣所，成了永远赎罪的事。"赎罪日是一年一次——基督是一次永远。

住棚节——神与人永远同住。启示录21:3"看哪，神的帐幕在人间。他要与人同住，他们要作他的子民。"住棚节的棚子是暂时的——新天新地是永恒的。住棚节的第八日——超越完全的新创造——启示录21:5"看哪，我将一切都更新了。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Every one of Leviticus 23's seven festivals found fulfillment in Christ—one of the Old Testament's most perfect typological correspondences.

Passover—Christ is the Passover Lamb. 1 Corinthians 5:7: "For Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed." Christ was crucified during Passover—perfectly fulfilling the timing.

Unleavened Bread—Christ is sinless (unleavened). 1 Corinthians 5:8: "Let us therefore celebrate the festival, not with the old leaven...but with the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth." Removing leaven symbolized removing sin—Christ sets us free from sin's dominion.

Firstfruits—Christ is "the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep." 1 Corinthians 15:20: "But in fact Christ has been raised from the dead, the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep." Christ rose on the day after Sabbath (the first day of the week)—exactly Firstfruits day.

Pentecost—the Holy Spirit descended at Pentecost. Acts 2:1-4: "When the day of Pentecost arrived, they were all together...And they were all filled with the Holy Spirit." Fifty days after Firstfruits—fifty days after resurrection—the Spirit descended. Old Testament Pentecost offered two leavened loaves—Jew and Gentile, two groups united by the Spirit into one church.

Trumpets—Christ's return trumpet. 1 Thessalonians 4:16: "The Lord himself will descend from heaven with a cry of command, with the voice of an archangel, and with the sound of the trumpet of God."

Day of Atonement—Christ's once-for-all atonement. Hebrews 9:12: "He entered once for all into the holy places...by means of his own blood, thus securing an eternal redemption." Atonement Day was annual—Christ was once for eternity.

Tabernacles—God dwelling with humanity forever. Revelation 21:3: "Behold, the dwelling place of God is with man. He will dwell with them, and they will be his people." Tabernacles' booths were temporary—new heaven and earth are eternal. Tabernacles' eighth day—new creation beyond completeness—Revelation 21:5: "Behold, I am making all things new."
