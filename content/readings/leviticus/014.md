## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。如果说第十三章是关于诊断和隔离（坏消息），第十四章就是关于洁净和恢复（好消息）。第十三章把人逐出营外——第十四章把人带回来。这是利未记中最完整的"恢复程序"——从营外到营内、从不洁到洁净、从死亡到新生。

洁净礼分为三个阶段，跨越八天以上。

第一阶段：营外的初步洁净（1-7节）——两只活鸟的仪式。

"祭司要出到营外察看"——弟兄姐妹，祭司主动走出营外。不是麻风病人自己走进来——是祭司出去找他。那个被隔离的人无权进营——必须有人出去把他带回来。

然后是一个极其独特的仪式：两只洁净的活鸟、香柏木、朱红色线、牛膝草。一只鸟被宰在盛有活水的瓦器上方，血流入水中；另一只活鸟连同香柏木、朱红色线和牛膝草一同蘸在血水中，然后用这血水在求洁净的人身上洒七次。最后——那只活鸟被放飞到田野里。

弟兄姐妹，两只鸟——一只死了，一只活着。死的那只代表了赎罪的代价——血流出来了，生命被倾倒了。活的那只带着死鸟的血飞向自由——代表了罪被带走了、不洁净被移除了。这个双鸟仪式与赎罪日（第16章）的两只山羊遥相呼应——一只被杀献祭，另一只带着罪被放逐到旷野。死亡和释放——惩罚和自由——这是赎罪的完整图画。

第二阶段：七天的过渡期（8-9节）。

求洁净的人洗衣服、剃去全身毛发、用水洗澡——然后可以进营，但要在帐棚外住七天。第七天再次剃去所有毛发——头发、胡须、眉毛、全身的毛。弟兄姐妹，剃光全身的毛发意味着什么？这是一个"归零"——像新生婴儿一样光洁。你旧的身份（不洁净的麻风病人）被彻底剥除——你在象征性地重新出生。

第三阶段：第八天的献祭（10-20节）——这是洁净礼的高潮。

"第八天"——又是第八天！第八天在利未记中反复出现（割礼在第八天，亚伦就职后第八天正式献祭）。第八天是新开始的日子。

求洁净的人要献四种祭——赎愆祭（公羊羔+油）、赎罪祭、燔祭、素祭。弟兄姐妹，注意这里献了赎愆祭——因为麻风病人的不洁净造成了"亏欠"：他不能参与崇拜、不能为社区服务——这些"损失"需要赔偿。

然后是最令人惊叹的仪式——与第八章祭司就职礼完全平行：

> "祭司要取些赎愆祭牲的血，抹在求洁净人的右耳垂上和右手的大拇指上，并右脚的大拇指上。"

弟兄姐妹，右耳、右手大拇指、右脚大拇指——这和祭司就职礼中亚伦被抹血的部位完全一样（利8:23-24）！洁净后的麻风病人接受了与祭司相同的仪式——这意味着什么？这意味着从不洁净中恢复过来的人，不只是"恢复正常"——他被"重新圣别"了。他的经历不是一个减法（去掉不洁净）——而是一个加法（获得新的圣洁身份）。

然后祭司把油抹在同样的三个部位——血上面再加油。血代表赎罪（洁净），油代表圣灵（成圣）。先血后油——先赎罪后成圣。"祭司手里所剩的油要抹在那求洁净人的头上"——和膏油倒在亚伦头上一样。弟兄姐妹，被恢复的麻风病人经历了一次"重新膏立"——好像被重新设立为以色列的一员。

穷人的替代方案（21-32节）同样完整——一只羊羔加两只鸟代替三只羊羔，但仪式的每一步都不省略。恩典的可及性再次被强调——穷人也能被完全洁净。

第十四章后半部分（33-53节）处理房屋上的tsara'at（墙壁上的霉斑或腐蚀）——诊断和洁净程序与人的麻风类似，包括同样的两只鸟仪式。弟兄姐妹，不洁净不仅影响人——也影响人所住的地方。罪的后果渗透到我们的环境中——需要从里到外的洁净。

利未记第十四章是整卷书中最完整的恢复叙事——从营外的隔绝到圣所面前的献祭，从不洁净的哀号到被血和油重新圣别。这不只是"去掉坏东西"——这是"赐予新身份"。洁净的麻风病人不只是"不再不洁净"——他是一个被重新膏立的、重新属于神百姓的人。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. If chapter 13 was about diagnosis and isolation (bad news), chapter 14 is about cleansing and restoration (good news). Chapter 13 expelled people from camp—chapter 14 brings them back. This is Leviticus's most complete "restoration procedure"—from outside camp to inside, from unclean to clean, from death to new life.

The purification rite had three stages, spanning eight or more days.

Stage one: initial cleansing outside the camp (vv. 1-7)—the two-bird ceremony.

"The priest shall go out of the camp"—Brothers and sisters, the priest actively went outside the camp. The afflicted person didn't walk in—the priest went out to find them. The isolated person had no right to enter—someone had to go out and bring them back.

Then an extraordinarily unique ritual: two clean living birds, cedarwood, scarlet yarn, and hyssop. One bird was slaughtered over an earthen vessel containing fresh water, its blood flowing into the water; the other living bird along with the cedarwood, scarlet yarn, and hyssop were dipped in the blood-water mixture, then this blood-water was sprinkled seven times on the person seeking cleansing. Finally—the living bird was released into the open field.

Brothers and sisters, two birds—one died, one lived. The dead one represented atonement's cost—blood shed, life poured out. The living one, bearing the dead bird's blood, flew to freedom—representing sin carried away, uncleanness removed. This two-bird ceremony echoed the Day of Atonement's two goats (chapter 16)—one killed as sacrifice, the other carrying sins into the wilderness. Death and release—punishment and freedom—this is atonement's complete picture.

Stage two: seven-day transition (vv. 8-9).

The person washed clothes, shaved all body hair, bathed—then could enter camp but lived outside their tent for seven days. On the seventh day, all hair shaved again—head, beard, eyebrows, entire body. Brothers and sisters, what did shaving all hair mean? A "reset to zero"—smooth as a newborn infant. Your old identity (unclean leper) was thoroughly stripped away—you were symbolically being reborn.

Stage three: eighth-day sacrifices (vv. 10-20)—the purification's climax.

"The eighth day"—the eighth day again! It recurs throughout Leviticus (circumcision on eighth day, Aaron's first official sacrifice on eighth day). The eighth day was the day of new beginnings.

The person offered four sacrifices—guilt offering (lamb + oil), sin offering, burnt offering, grain offering. Brothers and sisters, note the guilt offering—because the afflicted person's uncleanness created a "debt": unable to worship, unable to serve the community—these "losses" required restitution.

Then the most astonishing ritual—perfectly paralleling chapter 8's priestly ordination:

> "The priest shall take some of the blood of the guilt offering, and the priest shall put it on the lobe of the right ear of him who is to be cleansed and on the thumb of his right hand and on the big toe of his right foot."

Brothers and sisters, right earlobe, right thumb, right big toe—exactly the same locations where Aaron was anointed with blood during ordination (Lev 8:23-24)! The cleansed person received the same ceremony as the priest—what did this mean? It meant the person restored from uncleanness was not merely "returned to normal"—they were "re-consecrated." Their experience was not subtraction (removing uncleanness)—but addition (receiving a new holy identity).

Then the priest applied oil to the same three locations—oil on top of blood. Blood represented atonement (cleansing), oil represented the Spirit (sanctification). Blood first, then oil—atonement first, then sanctification. "The rest of the oil...the priest shall put on the head of him who is to be cleansed"—just as anointing oil was poured on Aaron's head. Brothers and sisters, the restored person experienced a "re-anointing"—as if being reinstalled as a member of Israel.

The poor person's alternative (vv. 21-32) was equally complete—one lamb plus two birds replacing three lambs, but no ceremonial step was omitted. Grace's accessibility was again emphasized—the poor could also be fully cleansed.

The second half (vv. 33-53) addressed house tsara'at (wall mold or decay)—diagnostic and cleansing procedures similar to human affliction, including the same two-bird ceremony. Brothers and sisters, uncleanness affected not only people—but their dwellings. Sin's consequences permeated our environments—requiring inside-out cleansing.

Leviticus 14 is the book's most complete restoration narrative—from camp's isolation to sacrifice before the sanctuary, from uncleanness's wailing to re-consecration with blood and oil. This was not merely "removing bad things"—it was "bestowing new identity." The cleansed person was not just "no longer unclean"—they were re-anointed, re-belonging to God's people.

## 历史背景（中文）

两只鸟的仪式在古代近东没有明确平行——这是以色列独有的洁净仪式。香柏木代表持久和高贵（所罗门殿用香柏木建造），牛膝草代表谦卑和洁净（出12:22用牛膝草涂逾越节羔羊的血），朱红色线代表血和生命。"活水"（mayim chayyim，字面"活着的水"）是流动的水而非静止的水——在利未记的洁净体系中，流水比死水有更强的洁净力。"剃去全身毛发"——在古代埃及，祭司每天都要剃去全身毛发以保持仪式洁净。以色列的求洁净者经历了类似的"完全重置"。第八天的四种祭加上血和油的仪式，构成了利未记中最复杂的个人恢复礼——总共涉及三只羊羔（或穷人的一只羊羔加两只鸟）、油、素祭、两只鸟——费用高昂，但社区的完整性值得这个代价。

## Historical Context (English)

The two-bird ceremony had no clear ancient Near Eastern parallel—it was uniquely Israelite. Cedarwood represented endurance and nobility (Solomon's temple was built with cedar), hyssop represented humility and cleansing (Exod 12:22 used hyssop to apply Passover lamb's blood), scarlet yarn represented blood and life. "Running water" (mayim chayyim, literally "living water") was flowing water not standing—in Leviticus's purity system, flowing water had greater cleansing power than still water. "Shaving all body hair"—in ancient Egypt, priests shaved all body hair daily to maintain ritual purity. Israel's purification candidate underwent a similar "complete reset." The eighth day's four offerings plus blood and oil rituals constituted Leviticus's most complex personal restoration rite—involving three lambs (or for the poor, one lamb plus two birds), oil, grain offering, and two birds—costly, but community integrity was worth the price.

## 经文结构（中文）

**第一阶段：营外两只鸟（1-7节）**——祭司出到营外、两只活鸟+香柏木朱红线牛膝草、一只宰在活水上、另一只蘸血水洒七次后放飞、定为洁净。

**第二阶段：七天过渡（8-9节）**——洗衣剃毛洗澡进营、帐棚外住七天、第七天再剃全身毛发。

**第三阶段：第八天献祭（10-20节）**——赎愆祭公羊羔+油摇祭、血抹右耳垂右大拇指右脚趾、油抹同三处再抹头上、赎罪祭+燔祭+素祭、"他就洁净了"。

**穷人的替代（21-32节）**——一只公羊羔+两只鸟、仪式步骤完全相同。

**房屋的麻风（33-53节）**——墙壁发绿发红→祭司察看→关锁七天→刮去灰泥换新石→仍发散则拆房→痊愈则两只鸟仪式洁净。

**总结（54-57节）**——各类麻风的条例总结。

## Structure (English)

**Stage One: Two Birds Outside Camp (vv. 1-7)**—Priest goes outside camp, two living birds + cedarwood scarlet hyssop, one killed over running water, other dipped in blood-water sprinkled seven times then released, declared clean.

**Stage Two: Seven-Day Transition (vv. 8-9)**—Wash clothes shave bathe enter camp, live outside tent seven days, seventh day shave all body hair again.

**Stage Three: Eighth-Day Offerings (vv. 10-20)**—Guilt offering lamb + oil wave offering, blood on right earlobe thumb toe, oil on same three places then on head, sin + burnt + grain offerings, "he shall be clean."

**Poor Person's Alternative (vv. 21-32)**—One lamb + two birds, ceremonial steps identical.

**House Leprosy (vv. 33-53)**—Greenish reddish walls → priest examines → quarantine seven days → scrape plaster replace stones → still spreading then demolish → healed then two-bird purification ceremony.

**Summary (vv. 54-57)**—Comprehensive summary of all leprosy regulations.

## 神学要点（中文）

**祭司出到营外——恩典的主动性**
祭司主动出去寻找麻风病人——改革宗传统强调恩典的先行性（prevenient grace的改革宗版本）。韦敏斯德信条第十章论有效恩召教导："这有效的恩召，是唯独出于神白白所赐的特别的恩典。"不是罪人先找到神——是神先找到罪人。路加福音15:4"你们中间谁有一百只羊失去一只，不把这九十九只撇在旷野、去找那失去的羊，直到找着呢？"

**血和油——称义与成圣不可分离**
先抹血后抹油——改革宗传统教导称义（血——赎罪）和成圣（油——圣灵）虽然在逻辑上可区分，但在实际中不可分离。韦敏斯德小要理问答第35问"成圣是什么？"答"成圣是我们从神白白领受恩典的工作，祂按自己的形像，更新我们的全人，使我们愈来愈能向罪死，并向义而活。"血洁净了罪——油赋予了新生命。

**与祭司就职相同的仪式——恢复即重新圣别**
麻风洁净礼与祭司就职礼的平行——改革宗传统教导"信徒皆祭司"（彼前2:9"你们是有君尊的祭司"）。每一个从罪中被恢复的信徒都经历了"重新圣别"——不只是回到原来的状态，而是被赋予新的事奉身份。

## Theology (English)

**The Priest Goes Outside Camp—Grace's Initiative**
The priest actively went out to find the afflicted—the Reformed tradition emphasizes grace's priority. Westminster Confession chapter 10 on effectual calling: This is entirely of God's free and special grace. The sinner did not first find God—God first found the sinner. Luke 15:4: "What man of you, having a hundred sheep, if he has lost one of them, does not leave the ninety-nine in the open country, and go after the one that is lost?"

**Blood and Oil—Justification and Sanctification Inseparable**
Blood applied first, then oil—the Reformed tradition teaches justification (blood—atonement) and sanctification (oil—Spirit) are logically distinguishable but practically inseparable. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q35: "What is sanctification?" "Sanctification is the work of God's free grace...more and more dying unto sin and living unto righteousness." Blood cleansed sin—oil bestowed new life.

**Same Ceremony as Priestly Ordination—Restoration as Re-Consecration**
The leprosy purification paralleling priestly ordination—the Reformed tradition teaches "the priesthood of all believers" (1 Pet 2:9: "You are a royal priesthood"). Every believer restored from sin experiences "re-consecration"—not merely returning to previous status but receiving a new ministry identity.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第十四章是旧约中最完整的基督预表之一——几乎每一个仪式细节都指向十字架和复活。

两只鸟——一只死了，一只活着带血飞向自由。基督死了（赎罪的代价），又复活了（罪被带走的确据）。罗马书4:25"耶稣被交给人，是为我们的过犯；复活，是为叫我们称义。"死鸟是十字架（基督为我们的罪死），活鸟是复活（基督胜过了死亡飞向自由）。

"祭司要出到营外"——基督出到营外来寻找我们。路加福音19:10"人子来，为要寻找、拯救失丧的人。"我们是营外的麻风病人——基督是出来寻找我们的大祭司。

血抹右耳、右手、右脚——基督恢复了我们全人的功能。我们的耳朵重新能听见神的话（罗10:17"信道是从听道来的"），我们的手重新能为神做工（弗2:10"我们原是他的工作，在基督耶稣里造成的，为要叫我们行善"），我们的脚重新能走在神的路上（弗2:10"就是神所预备叫我们行的"）。

血上加油——在基督里我们不仅被赦免（血），还被圣灵充满（油）。以弗所书1:13"你们既听见真理的道……信了基督，既然信他，就受了所应许的圣灵为印记。"先信后受印——先血后油。

穷人也能被完全洁净——基督的救赎不分贫富。雅各书2:5"神岂不是拣选了世上的贫穷人，叫他们在信上富足？"一只羊羔加两只鸟和三只羊羔在神面前有同样的洁净功效——因为洁净的能力不在于祭物的价格，而在于神的恩典。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 14 is one of the Old Testament's most complete Christological types—nearly every ritual detail points to the cross and resurrection.

Two birds—one died, one lived and flew to freedom bearing blood. Christ died (atonement's cost) and rose again (assurance that sin was carried away). Romans 4:25: "Who was delivered up for our trespasses and raised for our justification." The dead bird was the cross (Christ died for our sins); the living bird was the resurrection (Christ overcame death and flew to freedom).

"The priest shall go out of the camp"—Christ came outside the camp to find us. Luke 19:10: "The Son of Man came to seek and to save the lost." We were lepers outside the camp—Christ was the High Priest who came out to find us.

Blood on right ear, hand, foot—Christ restored our whole-person function. Our ears can again hear God's word (Rom 10:17: "Faith comes from hearing"), our hands can again work for God (Eph 2:10: "We are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works"), our feet can again walk God's path (Eph 2:10: "Which God prepared beforehand, that we should walk in them").

Blood then oil—in Christ we are not only forgiven (blood) but filled with the Spirit (oil). Ephesians 1:13: "In him you also, when you heard the word of truth...and believed in him, were sealed with the promised Holy Spirit." First faith then seal—first blood then oil.

The poor could also be fully cleansed—Christ's redemption knows no economic class. James 2:5: "Has not God chosen those who are poor in the world to be rich in faith?" One lamb plus two birds had the same cleansing efficacy before God as three lambs—because cleansing power lay not in the offering's price but in God's grace.
