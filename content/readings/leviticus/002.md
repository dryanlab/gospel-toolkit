## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二章介绍的是素祭（minchah，字面意思是"礼物"或"贡品"）——五大祭中唯一不涉及动物血的祭。如果燔祭是生命的完全奉献，素祭就是劳动成果的奉献——把日常生活中最基本的食物（面粉、油、乳香）献给神。

> "若有人献素祭为供物给耶和华，要用细面浇上油，加上乳香。"

弟兄姐妹，注意素祭的材料——细面、油、乳香。这三样是日常生活的产品：面粉来自田间的麦子（劳动的成果），油来自橄榄的压榨（加工的心血），乳香是昂贵的香料（最好的奉献）。素祭不是献一条牛的生命——而是献你一天劳动的果实。每一粒被磨成细面的麦子都代表了以色列人辛劳的耕种、收割、打麦、磨面的全过程。

素祭有四种形式——生面粉（最简单）、炉烤饼、铁鏊做的薄饼、煎盘做的饼。四种制作方式代表了从最简到最精的加工程度。弟兄姐妹，无论你的厨艺多好或多差——无论是一把生面粉还是精心烤制的饼——神都接受。

祭司从素祭中取出一把来，"作为纪念"烧在坛上。

"纪念"（azkarah）——不是神需要被提醒（祂不会忘记任何事），而是这把被烧的面成为了一个标记：这个人来过了，他把自己劳动的成果献在神面前了。神看到那把面上升为烟，就"纪念"了献祭者——不是记住他存在过，而是恩慈地接纳他的奉献。

> "素祭所剩的要归给亚伦和他的子孙。"

弟兄姐妹，素祭只有一把被烧在坛上——剩下的全部归祭司。这与燔祭形成鲜明对比：燔祭全烧，什么都不留；素祭只烧一把，其余供养祭司。神用百姓的素祭来喂养祭司——祭司因此可以全时间服事神，不需要自己种地。素祭是古代的"牧师薪水"——百姓的奉献直接维持了圣职人员的生活。

然后是三个严格的禁令和一个必须遵守的命令——这些规定揭示了深刻的神学：

第一，"不可有酵"。"凡献给耶和华的素祭都不可有酵。"在圣经中，酵几乎总是与罪、败坏联系在一起。基督在马太福音16:6说"你们要谨慎，防备法利赛人和撒都该人的酵"——酵代表虚假的教导和虚伪的敬虔。献给神的东西不能有罪的酵——奉献必须是纯洁的。

第二，"不可有蜜"。蜜代表自然的甜美和自然的愉悦。弟兄姐妹，蜜被禁止不是因为甜是坏的——而是因为献给神的奉献不能依赖自然的魅力或感官的愉悦。敬拜不是为了"让人感觉好"——它是为了"让神得到荣耀"。改革宗传统在敬拜中警惕"属灵娱乐化"——用感官的刺激替代真正的敬拜。

第三，"一切的供物都要配盐而献"。"在素祭上不可缺了你神立约的盐。"

弟兄姐妹，"立约的盐"——盐在古代近东代表持久和信实。盐不会腐败——它防止腐败。历代志下13:5说"耶和华……用盐约将以色列国永远赐给大卫和他的子孙。""盐约"——不可废除的、永久的约。素祭中的盐提醒以色列：你与神之间的约是"加了盐的"——是持久的、不会腐烂的。

最后是初熟之物的素祭——"要献上烘了的禾穗子，就是轧了的新穗子"。初熟的庄稼代表全部收成的"预告"——先把第一批给神，然后才享用其余的。哥林多前书15:20说"基督已经从死里复活，成为睡了之人初熟的果子"——基督的复活是全体信徒复活的"初熟"。

弟兄姐妹，素祭是利未记五大祭中最"日常"的一个——不涉及血和死亡，只是面粉、油和盐。但正是这个"日常性"使它格外珍贵：神不只要你生命中戏剧性的时刻（燔祭的全然献身），祂也要你每天磨面、做饼、劳动的普通日子。敬拜不只发生在圣所——也发生在厨房和麦田里。你每天的劳动，经过奉献的心志和信心的调和，就成为"馨香的火祭"。

第二章教导我们：真正的敬拜包含了生命的全部——不只是戏剧性的牺牲，也包括日常的、普通的、用双手劳动所得的奉献。改革宗传统说：在神的国度里，种田和讲道一样圣洁；做饭和祷告一样是敬拜。只要你把它献上——用盐（信实）调和，不加酵（罪）和蜜（自我愉悦）——你最普通的面粉就是神面前的馨香。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 2 introduces the grain offering (minchah, literally "gift" or "tribute")—the only one of the five major offerings not involving animal blood. If the burnt offering was complete surrender of life, the grain offering was surrender of labor's fruit—offering life's most basic food (flour, oil, frankincense) to God.

> "When anyone brings a grain offering as an offering to the LORD, his offering shall be of fine flour. He shall pour oil on it and put frankincense on it."

Brothers and sisters, note the grain offering's materials—fine flour, oil, frankincense. These three were products of daily life: flour from field wheat (labor's fruit), oil from pressed olives (processing's care), frankincense an expensive spice (the best offering). The grain offering was not surrendering an animal's life—but surrendering a day's labor. Every grain ground into fine flour represented the Israelite's entire process of plowing, harvesting, threshing, and milling.

The grain offering had four forms—raw flour (simplest), oven-baked bread, griddle-made flatbread, and pan-cooked cakes. Four preparations representing simplest to finest processing. Brothers and sisters, regardless of culinary skill—whether a handful of raw flour or carefully baked bread—God accepted it.

The priest took a handful from the grain offering, burning it on the altar "as a memorial portion."

"Memorial" (azkarah)—not that God needed reminding (He forgets nothing), but this burned handful became a marker: this person came, and offered their labor's fruit before God. God seeing that handful ascending as smoke "remembered" the offerer—not recalling their existence but graciously accepting their offering.

> "The rest of the grain offering shall be for Aaron and his sons."

Brothers and sisters, only a handful was burned on the altar—the remainder went entirely to the priests. In stark contrast to the burnt offering: burnt offering entirely consumed, nothing retained; grain offering burned only a handful, the rest sustaining the priests. God used the people's grain offerings to feed the priests—enabling priests to serve God full-time without farming. The grain offering was the ancient "pastor's salary"—the people's offerings directly sustaining sacred ministers' lives.

Then three strict prohibitions and one mandatory command—these regulations reveal deep theology:

First, "no leaven." "No grain offering that you bring to the LORD shall be made with leaven." In Scripture, leaven almost always associates with sin and corruption. Christ in Matthew 16:6: "Beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees"—leaven representing false teaching and hypocritical piety. What was offered to God could not contain sin's leaven—offerings must be pure.

Second, "no honey." Honey represented natural sweetness and natural pleasure. Brothers and sisters, honey was forbidden not because sweetness was bad—but because offerings to God could not rely on natural charm or sensory pleasure. Worship was not for "making people feel good"—it was for "giving God glory." The Reformed tradition in worship warns against "spiritual entertainment"—replacing genuine worship with sensory stimulation.

Third, "every offering of yours you shall season with salt." "You shall not let the salt of the covenant with your God be missing from your grain offering."

Brothers and sisters, "the salt of the covenant"—salt in the ancient Near East represented permanence and faithfulness. Salt does not decay—it prevents decay. 2 Chronicles 13:5: "The LORD...gave the kingship over Israel forever to David...by a covenant of salt." "Salt covenant"—irrevocable, permanent. Salt in the grain offering reminded Israel: your covenant with God is "salted"—enduring, incorruptible.

Finally the grain offering of firstfruits—"You shall offer for the grain offering of your firstfruits fresh ears, roasted with fire, crushed new grain." Firstfruits represented the entire harvest's "preview"—giving God the first batch before enjoying the rest. 1 Corinthians 15:20: "Christ has been raised from the dead, the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep"—Christ's resurrection is the "firstfruits" of all believers' resurrection.

Brothers and sisters, the grain offering was the most "everyday" of Leviticus's five offerings—no blood and no death, just flour, oil, and salt. But this "everydayness" made it especially precious: God does not only want life's dramatic moments (the burnt offering's total surrender), He also wants your ordinary days of grinding flour, baking bread, and laboring. Worship does not happen only in the sanctuary—it happens in the kitchen and the wheat field. Your daily labor, mixed with a willing heart and seasoned with faith, becomes "a pleasing aroma."

Chapter 2 teaches: true worship encompasses all of life—not only dramatic sacrifice but also ordinary, daily, hands-worn offerings. The Reformed tradition says: in God's kingdom, farming is as holy as preaching; cooking is as much worship as praying. As long as you offer it—seasoned with salt (faithfulness), without leaven (sin) or honey (self-gratification)—your most ordinary flour is a pleasing aroma before God.

## 历史背景（中文）

"素祭"（minchah）在创世记4:3-4该隐和亚伯的献祭中就出现了——该隐献的"地里的出产"就是minchah。在古代近东，minchah也用于描述附庸国向宗主国进贡的贡品（士3:15以笏带minchah给摩押王以矶伦）。"立约的盐"在古代近东文化中广泛使用——阿拉伯传统中共同吃盐意味着建立友谊和盟约关系；希罗多德记载波斯人用盐来确认条约。乳香（levonah）来自乳香树（Boswellia sacra），主要产于也门南部和索马里。它在古代是极其昂贵的商品——以赛亚书60:6提到示巴的乳香和黄金一同被带到耶路撒冷。"细面"（soleth）是经过多次筛选的最精细的面粉，代表了最精致的加工产品。

## Historical Context (English)

"Grain offering" (minchah) appears as early as Cain and Abel's offerings in Genesis 4:3-4—Cain's "offering of the fruit of the ground" was a minchah. In the ancient Near East, minchah also described tribute from vassal states to suzerains (Judges 3:15: Ehud bringing minchah to Moabite King Eglon). "Covenant of salt" was widely used in ancient Near Eastern cultures—in Arabic tradition, sharing salt meant establishing friendship and covenant relationship; Herodotus recorded Persians using salt to confirm treaties. Frankincense (levonah) came from the frankincense tree (Boswellia sacra), mainly from southern Yemen and Somalia. It was an extremely expensive commodity in antiquity—Isaiah 60:6 mentions Sheba's frankincense brought alongside gold to Jerusalem. "Fine flour" (soleth) was the finest flour produced through multiple siftings, representing the most refined processed product.

## 经文结构（中文）

**生面粉的素祭（1-3节）**——细面浇油加乳香、祭司取一把作为纪念烧在坛上、馨香火祭、剩余归祭司为至圣。

**烤制的素祭（4-10节）**——炉烤无酵调油饼、铁鏊无酵调油面分块浇油、煎盘用油与细面做、取一把纪念烧坛上、剩余归祭司至圣。

**酵蜜盐的禁令与命令（11-13节）**——不可有酵不可有蜜、初熟供物可献但不在坛上烧、一切供物配盐"不可缺了你神立约的盐"。

**初熟之物的素祭（14-16节）**——烘了的禾穗子轧了的新穗子、抹油加乳香、祭司烧其中一部分为纪念、火祭。

## Structure (English)

**Raw Flour Grain Offering (vv. 1-3)**—Fine flour with oil and frankincense, priest takes a handful as memorial portion to burn on altar, a pleasing aroma, remainder to priests as most holy.

**Prepared Grain Offerings (vv. 4-10)**—Oven-baked unleavened cakes with oil, griddle-made unleavened pieces with oil, pan-made with oil and fine flour, handful burned as memorial, remainder to priests as most holy.

**Prohibitions on Leaven/Honey and Salt Command (vv. 11-13)**—No leaven no honey, firstfruits may be offered but not burned on altar for aroma, every offering seasoned with salt "you shall not let the salt of the covenant with your God be missing."

**Firstfruits Grain Offering (vv. 14-16)**—Roasted fresh ears of crushed new grain, with oil and frankincense, priest burns memorial portion, a food offering.

## 神学要点（中文）

**日常劳动的圣洁化——万事归主**
素祭把日常的食物提升为圣洁的献祭——改革宗传统的"万事归主"（Soli Deo Gloria）原则在此找到旧约的根基。歌罗西书3:23"无论做甚么，都要从心里做，像是给主做的，不是给人做的。"一把面粉——经过劳动获得、经过奉献的心调和、经过火的检验——就成为了"馨香的火祭"。

**不可有酵、不可有蜜——纯正的敬拜**
酵（罪的渗透）和蜜（自然的甜蜜/自我愉悦）都被排除——改革宗敬拜原则要求纯正的、以神为中心的、不掺杂人的发明和自我满足的敬拜。韦敏斯德信条第二十一章教导：可蒙悦纳的敬拜神的方式，乃是神自己所设立的，不可按着人的想象和捏造来敬拜神。无酵——除去罪的污染；无蜜——除去以人的感受为中心的倾向。

**盐约——恩典的永恒性**
"立约的盐"——改革宗传统坚信恩典之约是不可废除的。韦敏斯德信条第7章"恩典之约"——"在这约中他白白地将生命和拯救赐给罪人。"盐防止腐败——恩典的约永远不会腐烂过期。

## Theology (English)

**Sanctification of Daily Labor—All Things to the Lord**
The grain offering elevated daily food to holy sacrifice—the Reformed "Soli Deo Gloria" (to God alone be glory) principle finds its Old Testament foundation here. Colossians 3:23: "Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men." A handful of flour—obtained through labor, mixed with a willing heart, tested by fire—became "a pleasing aroma."

**No Leaven, No Honey—Pure Worship**
Leaven (sin's permeation) and honey (natural sweetness/self-gratification) were both excluded—the Reformed worship principle demands pure, God-centered worship, unmixed with human invention and self-satisfaction. Westminster Confession chapter 21: God may not be worshipped according to the imaginations and devices of men. No leaven—removing sin's contamination; no honey—removing human-feeling-centered tendencies.

**Salt Covenant—Grace's Eternity**
"The salt of the covenant"—the Reformed tradition firmly believes the covenant of grace is irrevocable. Westminster Confession chapter 7 on: The Covenant of Grace—"In which he freely offers unto sinners life and salvation." Salt prevents decay—the grace covenant will never decay or expire.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第二章的素祭在几个层面预表基督。

"细面"——基督的完美人性。细面经过反复筛选——基督的人性是完全纯洁的、没有杂质的。希伯来书2:17"他凡事该与他的弟兄相同"——基督有真实的人性，但这人性是"细面"般完美的，没有罪的"粗糙颗粒"。

"浇上油"——圣灵的膏抹。基督被"油"（圣灵）完全浸透——约翰福音3:34"神赐圣灵给他是没有限量的。"素祭的面粉浇上油——基督的人性被圣灵完全充满。

"加上乳香"——基督的祷告是馨香的。启示录8:3-4"另有一位天使，拿着金香炉来，站在祭坛旁边。有许多香赐给他，要和众圣徒的祈祷一同献在宝座前的金坛上。那香的烟和众圣徒的祈祷从天使的手中一同升到神面前。"基督的代求就是加在信徒祷告上的"乳香"。

"不可有酵"——基督完全无罪。哥林多前书5:7-8"你们既是无酵的面，应当把旧酵除净……我们守这节不可用旧酵，也不可用恶毒、邪恶的酵，只用诚实真正的无酵饼。"基督是那真正无酵的饼——完全没有罪的渗透。

"配盐而献"——基督的约是永远的。希伯来书13:20"但愿赐平安的神，就是那凭永约之血、使群羊的大牧人——我主耶稣从死里复活的神。""永约"——基督的血立的约是永远不腐败的、加了盐的。

初熟之物——哥林多前书15:20"基督已经从死里复活，成为睡了之人初熟的果子。"基督的复活是第一粒收获——保证了全部庄稼（所有信徒）的复活收割。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 2's grain offering prefigures Christ on several levels.

"Fine flour"—Christ's perfect humanity. Fine flour was repeatedly sifted—Christ's humanity was entirely pure, without impurity. Hebrews 2:17: "He had to be made like his brothers in every respect"—Christ had genuine humanity, but this humanity was "fine flour" perfect, without sin's "coarse particles."

"Pour oil on it"—the Spirit's anointing. Christ was completely saturated with "oil" (the Spirit)—John 3:34: "He gives the Spirit without measure." The grain offering's flour was poured with oil—Christ's humanity was completely filled with the Spirit.

"Put frankincense on it"—Christ's prayer is a fragrant aroma. Revelation 8:3-4: "Another angel came and stood at the altar with a golden censer, and he was given much incense to offer with the prayers of all the saints on the golden altar before the throne. And the smoke of the incense, with the prayers of the saints, rose before God." Christ's intercession is the "frankincense" added to believers' prayers.

"No leaven"—Christ was completely sinless. 1 Corinthians 5:7-8: "Cleanse out the old leaven that you may be a new lump, as you really are unleavened...Let us therefore celebrate the festival, not with the old leaven, the leaven of malice and evil, but with the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth." Christ is the truly unleavened bread—completely without sin's permeation.

"Season with salt"—Christ's covenant is eternal. Hebrews 13:20: "Now may the God of peace who brought again from the dead our Lord Jesus, the great shepherd of the sheep, by the blood of the eternal covenant." "Eternal covenant"—the covenant established by Christ's blood never decays, forever salted.

Firstfruits—1 Corinthians 15:20: "Christ has been raised from the dead, the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep." Christ's resurrection is the first sheaf harvested—guaranteeing the entire harvest (all believers) will be reaped.
