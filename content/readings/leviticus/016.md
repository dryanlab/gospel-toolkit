## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十六章是整卷利未记的核心和高潮——赎罪日（Yom Kippur）。如果利未记是一座山，第十六章就是山顶。前十五章都在向这里攀登——献祭制度（1-7章）、祭司就职（8-10章）、洁净条例（11-15章）——一切都为了这一天：一年一次，大祭司独自进入至圣所，在施恩座前为全以色列赎罪。

第十六章以第十章的悲剧开头——"亚伦的两个儿子近到耶和华面前死了。"弟兄姐妹，赎罪日的规定直接源于拿答和亚比户的死亡——那个悲剧证明了一件事：随便进到神面前会死。所以赎罪日的第一条规定就是："不可随时进圣所的幔子内、到柜上的施恩座前，免得他死亡。"不是任何时候、不是任何人——只有一天、只有一个人——一年一次、只有大祭司。

大祭司在赎罪日的服装也不同——不穿平日的华丽圣衣（以弗得、胸牌、金牌），而是穿简朴的细麻布衣服。弟兄姐妹，在神面前的至圣所里，不需要华丽——只需要洁净。白色的细麻布代表纯洁——赤裸裸地、没有任何装饰地站在神面前。

赎罪日的核心仪式分为三个部分：

第一部分：大祭司为自己赎罪（3-6节，11-14节）。亚伦先献一只公牛为自己和本家的赎罪祭。他拿着香炉和香料进入至圣所——"使香的烟云遮掩法柜上的施恩座，免得他死亡。"弟兄姐妹，香的烟云遮掩了施恩座——大祭司不能直接看见施恩座上神的荣光（看见就会死）。烟云是一层保护的屏障。然后他把公牛的血弹在施恩座上面和前面——七次。

弟兄姐妹，这是全以色列一年中最紧张的时刻。大祭司独自一人在至圣所里——"会幕里不可有人"——整个以色列在外面等待。如果大祭司做错了任何一步——他就会像拿答和亚比户一样死在至圣所里。传说后来的大祭司进至圣所时，脚上绑着一根绳子——万一他死在里面，可以把他拖出来（因为没有人敢进去救他）。

第二部分：为百姓赎罪——两只山羊（7-10节，15-22节）。这是赎罪日最独特、最深刻的仪式。

两只公山羊被带到会幕门口。拈阄——一只归耶和华（被宰杀），一只归"阿撒泻勒"（被送到旷野）。

归耶和华的羊被宰杀——血被带入至圣所，弹在施恩座上和前面，"好像弹公牛的血一样。"弟兄姐妹，这只羊的血处理了罪的惩罚——它代替以色列人承受了死刑。血洒在施恩座上——施恩座（kapporet，来自"遮盖/赎罪"的词根kapar）就是约柜的盖子。约柜里面是什么？十诫的石版——神的律法。律法宣告了罪人的死刑——但施恩座上的血"遮盖"了律法的定罪。弟兄姐妹，血洒在律法的上面——恩典覆盖了审判。

然后是归阿撒泻勒的羊——活着的那只。亚伦"两手按在羊头上，承认以色列人诸般的罪孽过犯，就是他们一切的罪愆，把这罪都归在羊的头上"——然后把这只羊送到旷野的无人之地。

弟兄姐妹，两只山羊展现了赎罪的两个维度：第一只处理了罪的惩罚（替代死亡——血洒在施恩座上）；第二只处理了罪的存在（罪被带走——送到旷野的无人之地再也回不来）。一只死了——解决了罪的刑罚；一只活着带着罪离开——解决了罪的污染。

"阿撒泻勒"（Azazel）的意思在学术界有争议——可能是"完全移除"或一个旷野恶魔的名字。但无论其确切含义，功能是明确的：罪被永远地、不可逆转地带走了。诗篇103:12"东离西有多远，他叫我们的过犯离我们也有多远。"阿撒泻勒的羊把罪带到"无人之地"——永远不再回来。

第三部分：为圣所赎罪（16-19节）。"他因以色列人诸般的污秽、过犯……当这样在圣所行赎罪之礼。"弟兄姐妹，不仅人需要赎罪——连圣所本身也需要被洁净。以色列人一年来的罪污染了会幕——赎罪日是一年一度的"大扫除"，用血洁净至圣所、会幕和坛。

赎罪日的最后吩咐："这日你们要守为圣安息日，要刻苦己心。"全以色列在赎罪日什么都不做——不工作、不吃饭（"刻苦己心"传统理解为禁食）——只是安静地等候大祭司从至圣所出来。

> "这要作你们永远的定例——就是因以色列人一切的罪，要一年一次为他们赎罪。"

弟兄姐妹，"一年一次"——每年重复。这既是恩典也是局限。恩典——神每年都为以色列预备了赎罪的途径。局限——它需要每年重复，说明去年的赎罪不能覆盖今年的罪。旧约的赎罪是暂时的、重复的、不完全的。它需要一个终极的、一次性的、永远有效的赎罪——而这正是基督在十字架上所成就的。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 16 is all of Leviticus's core and climax—the Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur). If Leviticus is a mountain, chapter 16 is the summit. The first fifteen chapters climbed toward this point—the sacrificial system (chs. 1-7), priestly ordination (chs. 8-10), purity regulations (chs. 11-15)—all for this one day: once a year, the high priest alone entered the Most Holy Place, before the mercy seat, atoning for all Israel.

Chapter 16 opens with chapter 10's tragedy—"After the death of the two sons of Aaron when they drew near before the LORD and died." Brothers and sisters, the Day of Atonement regulations directly arose from Nadab and Abihu's deaths—that tragedy proved one thing: casually approaching God means death. So the first regulation: "He shall not come at any time into the Holy Place inside the veil, before the mercy seat that is on the ark, so that he may not die." Not any time, not anyone—only one day, only one person—once a year, only the high priest.

The high priest's clothing on this day was different—not the usual ornate vestments (ephod, breastpiece, gold plate), but simple white linen garments. Brothers and sisters, in God's Most Holy presence, no ornamentation was needed—only purity. White linen represented cleanness—standing before God stripped bare, without adornment.

The Day of Atonement's core ritual had three parts:

Part one: the high priest's self-atonement (vv. 3-6, 11-14). Aaron first offered a bull as sin offering for himself and his household. He carried a censer with incense into the Most Holy Place—"so that the cloud of the incense may cover the mercy seat...so that he does not die." Brothers and sisters, the incense cloud veiled the mercy seat—the high priest could not directly see God's glory on the mercy seat (seeing it would kill him). The cloud was a protective screen. Then he sprinkled the bull's blood on and before the mercy seat—seven times.

Brothers and sisters, this was all Israel's most tense moment each year. The high priest was alone in the Most Holy Place—"no one may be in the tent of meeting"—all Israel waited outside. If the high priest made a single misstep—he would die inside the Most Holy Place like Nadab and Abihu. Tradition says later high priests entered with a rope tied to their ankle—in case they died inside, they could be pulled out (no one dared enter to rescue them).

Part two: atonement for the people—two goats (vv. 7-10, 15-22). The Day of Atonement's most unique, most profound ritual.

Two male goats were brought to the tent's entrance. Lots were cast—one for the LORD (to be slaughtered), one for "Azazel" (sent to the wilderness).

The LORD's goat was slaughtered—blood brought into the Most Holy Place, sprinkled on and before the mercy seat, "as he did with the blood of the bull." Brothers and sisters, this goat's blood addressed sin's penalty—it bore Israel's death sentence as substitute. Blood on the mercy seat (kapporet, from the root kapar meaning "to cover/atone")—the mercy seat was the ark's lid. What was inside the ark? The stone tablets of the Ten Commandments—God's law. The law pronounced the death sentence on sinners—but the mercy seat's blood "covered" the law's condemnation. Brothers and sisters, blood sprinkled on top of the law—grace covered judgment.

Then the Azazel goat—the living one. Aaron "shall lay both his hands on the head of the live goat, and confess over it all the iniquities of the people of Israel, and all their transgressions, all their sins. And he shall put them on the head of the goat and send it away into the wilderness."

Brothers and sisters, two goats displayed atonement's two dimensions: the first addressed sin's penalty (substitutionary death—blood on the mercy seat); the second addressed sin's presence (sin carried away—sent to the uninhabited wilderness, never returning). One died—resolving sin's punishment; one lived, carrying sin away—resolving sin's contamination.

"Azazel"'s meaning is debated academically—possibly "complete removal" or a wilderness demon's name. Regardless of exact meaning, its function was clear: sin was permanently, irreversibly carried away. Psalm 103:12: "As far as the east is from the west, so far does he remove our transgressions from us." The Azazel goat carried sin to "a remote area"—never returning.

Part three: sanctuary atonement (vv. 16-19). "Thus he shall make atonement for the Holy Place, because of the uncleannesses of the people of Israel." Brothers and sisters, not only people needed atonement—even the sanctuary itself needed cleansing. Israel's year-long sins contaminated the tabernacle—the Day of Atonement was an annual "deep cleaning," purifying the Most Holy Place, tabernacle, and altar with blood.

The final instruction: "It shall be a statute to you forever that...you shall afflict yourselves." All Israel did nothing on the Day of Atonement—no work, no eating ("afflict yourselves" was traditionally understood as fasting)—simply waiting quietly for the high priest to emerge from the Most Holy Place.

> "This shall be a statute forever for you, that atonement may be made for the people of Israel once in the year because of all their sins."

Brothers and sisters, "once in the year"—annually repeated. This was both grace and limitation. Grace—God annually provided Israel a way of atonement. Limitation—annual repetition meant last year's atonement could not cover this year's sins. Old Testament atonement was temporary, repetitive, incomplete. It needed an ultimate, once-for-all, eternally effective atonement—which is precisely what Christ accomplished on the cross.

## 历史背景（中文）

赎罪日（Yom Kippur）是犹太历中最神圣的日子——至今犹太人仍在提斯利月（约公元历9-10月）十日守赎罪日。现代以色列在赎罪日全国停工——连电视台都停播、飞机不飞、公路上几乎没有车。"阿撒泻勒"的解释在学术界争论已久——(1)一个旷野恶魔/堕落天使的名字（以诺一书中有Azazel）；(2)"完全移除"（az-azel=强力移除）；(3)地名（一个悬崖的名字）。米示拿（Yoma 6:6）记载后来的传统是把阿撒泻勒的羊从悬崖推下去摔死——确保罪永远不会回来。"施恩座"（kapporet）在七十士译本中被译为hilasterion——罗马书3:25用同一个词描述基督："神设立耶稣作hilasterion（挽回祭）。"保罗直接把基督比作赎罪日的施恩座。第二圣殿时期（公元前516年至公元70年），至圣所里已经没有约柜了——大祭司把血洒在约柜本来应该在的地方。

## Historical Context (English)

The Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur) is the holiest day in the Jewish calendar—Jews still observe it on the 10th of Tishri (approximately September-October). Modern Israel shuts down entirely on Yom Kippur—even television stations cease broadcasting, planes don't fly, roads are virtually empty. "Azazel" has long been debated: (1) a wilderness demon/fallen angel's name (1 Enoch mentions Azazel); (2) "complete removal" (az-azel = strong removal); (3) a place name (a cliff). The Mishnah (Yoma 6:6) records the later tradition of pushing the Azazel goat off a cliff to ensure sin never returned. "Mercy seat" (kapporet) was translated in the Septuagint as hilasterion—Romans 3:25 uses the same word for Christ: "God put forward [Jesus] as a hilasterion (propitiation)." Paul directly compared Christ to the Day of Atonement's mercy seat. During the Second Temple period (516 BC–AD 70), the ark was no longer in the Most Holy Place—the high priest sprinkled blood where the ark should have been.

## 经文结构（中文）

**为自己赎罪（6，11-14节）**——公牛赎罪祭、香炉香料入至圣所、香云遮掩施恩座、血弹施恩座上面和前面七次。

**两只山羊（7-10，15-22节）**——拈阄一归耶和华一归阿撒泻勒、归耶和华的宰杀血弹施恩座、归阿撒泻勒的按手承认罪孽送到旷野无人之地。

**为圣所赎罪（16-19节）**——因以色列的污秽为至圣所会幕坛行赎罪礼、血弹坛上七次使之洁净。

**赎罪日的命令（29-34节）**——七月初十、圣安息日、刻苦己心不做工、永远的定例、一年一次为全以色列赎罪。

## Structure (English)

**Day of Atonement Background and Restrictions (vv. 1-5)**—After Nadab Abihu's death, may not enter Most Holy anytime lest die, wear linen garments, prepare bull (self) + two goats + ram (people).

**Self-Atonement (vv. 6, 11-14)**—Bull sin offering, censer incense into Most Holy, cloud covers mercy seat, blood sprinkled on and before mercy seat seven times.

**Two Goats (vv. 7-10, 15-22)**—Lots cast one for LORD one for Azazel, LORD's goat slaughtered blood on mercy seat, Azazel's goat hands laid confessing sins sent to wilderness uninhabited land.

**Sanctuary Atonement (vv. 16-19)**—Because of Israel's uncleanness atone for Most Holy tabernacle altar, blood sprinkled on altar seven times to cleanse.

**Day of Atonement Commands (vv. 29-34)**—Tenth of seventh month, holy sabbath, afflict yourselves no work, eternal statute, once yearly atone for all Israel.

## 神学要点（中文）

**施恩座——公义与恩典的交汇**
血洒在律法石版上方的施恩座上——改革宗传统将此视为称义教义的核心图画。韦敏斯德信条第十一章论称义教导："不是将义注入他们里面……乃是把基督的顺服和所成就的义归算给他们。"施恩座上的血不改变律法（律法仍在柜里）——它"满足"了律法的要求。公义没有被取消——它被满足了。恩典不违反公义——恩典通过替代性赎罪满足了公义。

**两只山羊——赎罪的双重维度**
一只被杀（罪的惩罚被承担）一只被放逐（罪被带走）——改革宗传统用此解释基督十字架的双重功效。韦敏斯德信条第八章论中保的工作：主耶稣的灵魂直接忍受了最剧烈的折磨，身体承受了最疼痛的苦楚（第一只羊）和"使他们与神和好"（第二只羊）。基督不仅为罪受了刑罚——祂还把罪永远带走了。

**一年一次——暂时性指向永恒**
每年重复说明旧约赎罪的不完全——改革宗传统强调基督"一次献祭"的终极性。韦敏斯德信条第29章论基督的献祭"一次献上"（希伯来书9:28）。旧约的年年赎罪是影子——基督的一次赎罪是实体。

## Theology (English)

**The Mercy Seat—Where Justice and Grace Meet**
Blood sprinkled on the mercy seat above the law tablets—the Reformed tradition views this as justification's core picture. Westminster Confession chapter 11 on justification: Not by infusing righteousness into them, but by...imputing the obedience and satisfaction of Christ unto them. Blood on the mercy seat did not change the law (the law remained in the ark)—it "satisfied" the law's demands. Justice was not cancelled—it was satisfied. Grace did not violate justice—grace satisfied justice through substitutionary atonement.

**Two Goats—Atonement's Dual Dimension**
One killed (sin's penalty borne), one banished (sin carried away)—the Reformed tradition uses this to explain Christ's cross dual efficacy. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on the Mediator's work includes "bearing and suffering God's most dreadful wrath" (first goat) and "reconciling them to God" (second goat). Christ not only bore sin's penalty—He also carried sin away forever.

**Once a Year—Temporariness Pointing to Eternity**
Annual repetition showed Old Testament atonement's incompleteness—the Reformed tradition emphasizes Christ's "once for all" offering's finality. Westminster Confession chapter 29 on Christ's sacrifice: once offered (Hebrews 9:28). The Old Testament's annual atonement was shadow—Christ's once-for-all atonement is substance.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第十六章是旧约中指向基督最直接、最完整的预表——希伯来书第九章几乎逐条对应地解释了赎罪日如何在基督里成全。

大祭司独自进入至圣所——基督独自进入天上的至圣所。希伯来书9:11-12"基督已经来到，作了将来美事的大祭司，经过那更大更全备的帐幕，不是人手所造，也不是属乎这世界的；并且不用山羊和牛犊的血，乃用自己的血，只一次进入圣所，成了永远赎罪的事。"亚伦用牛的血每年进一次——基督用自己的血一次进入永远有效。

两只山羊——基督的十字架同时完成了两只羊的功能。第一只羊被杀，血洒在施恩座上（罪的惩罚被承担）——基督在十字架上承受了我们罪的刑罚。罗马书3:25"神设立耶稣作挽回祭（hilasterion——施恩座），是凭着耶稣的血，藉着人的信。"基督就是施恩座——祂自己的血洒在祂自己身上。

第二只羊——阿撒泻勒的羊——带着罪被送到旷野永远不回来。约翰福音1:29施洗约翰看见耶稣说"看哪，神的羔羊，除去世人罪孽的！""除去"（airōn）——不只是"遮盖"（旧约的赎罪）而是"带走、移除"（新约的赎罪）。基督像阿撒泻勒的羊一样把我们的罪"带走"了——送到无人之地，永远不再回来。弥迦书7:19"将我们的一切罪投于深海。"

亚伦先为自己赎罪——基督不需要。希伯来书7:27"他不像那些大祭司，每日必须先为自己的罪……献祭。"亚伦是有罪的中保——基督是无罪的中保。

"一年一次"——基督"一次"。希伯来书9:25-28"也不是多次将自己献上……但如今在这末世显现一次，把自己献为祭，好除掉罪。"不需要每年重复——因为基督的赎罪是完全的、终极的、永远有效的。

"刻苦己心"——基督在十字架上为我们刻苦了自己。以赛亚书53:4-5"他诚然担当我们的忧患，背负我们的痛苦……他为我们的过犯受害，为我们的罪孽压伤。"我们不再需要"刻苦己心"来赎罪——因为基督已经为我们受了一切的苦。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 16 is the Old Testament's most direct, most complete prefiguration of Christ—Hebrews 9 explains nearly point-by-point how the Day of Atonement was fulfilled in Christ.

The high priest alone entering the Most Holy Place—Christ alone entered the heavenly Most Holy Place. Hebrews 9:11-12: "When Christ appeared as a high priest of the good things that have come, then through the greater and more perfect tent (not made with hands)...he entered once for all into the holy places, not by means of the blood of goats and calves but by means of his own blood, thus securing an eternal redemption." Aaron entered annually with bull's blood—Christ entered once with His own blood, eternally effective.

Two goats—Christ's cross simultaneously fulfilled both goats' functions. The first goat was killed, blood on the mercy seat (sin's penalty borne)—Christ on the cross bore our sin's punishment. Romans 3:25: "God put forward [Jesus] as a propitiation (hilasterion—mercy seat) by his blood, to be received by faith." Christ is the mercy seat—His own blood sprinkled on Himself.

The second goat—Azazel's goat—carried sins to the wilderness, never returning. John 1:29, John the Baptist seeing Jesus: "Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!" "Takes away" (airōn)—not merely "covering" (Old Testament atonement) but "carrying away, removing" (New Testament atonement). Like Azazel's goat, Christ "carried away" our sin—sent to the uninhabited land, never returning. Micah 7:19: "You will cast all our sins into the depths of the sea."

Aaron first atoned for himself—Christ needed none. Hebrews 7:27: "He has no need, like those high priests, to offer sacrifices daily, first for his own sins." Aaron was a sinful mediator—Christ a sinless one.

"Once a year"—Christ "once." Hebrews 9:25-28: "Nor was it to offer himself repeatedly...But as it is, he has appeared once for all at the end of the ages to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself." No annual repetition needed—because Christ's atonement is complete, ultimate, eternally effective.

"Afflict yourselves"—Christ on the cross afflicted Himself for us. Isaiah 53:4-5: "Surely he has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows...He was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities." We no longer need to "afflict ourselves" for atonement—because Christ already suffered everything for us.
