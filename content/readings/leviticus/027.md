## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十七章是利未记的附录——在第二十六章那个宏大的盟约宣告之后，神补充了关于许愿和奉献估价的实际规定。这一章看起来像行政条例，但它背后有一个深刻的神学原则：许给神的话必须算数，分别为圣的必须是真实的，奉献不可以随便反悔。

第一部分：许愿奉献人的估价（2-8节）。

"人若特许的愿，被许的人要按你所估的价值归给耶和华。"弟兄姐妹，以色列人有时在祷告中许愿："耶和华啊，如果你……我就把自己（或家人）奉献给你。"但他们实际上不能住在圣所里——所以可以用金钱来"赎回"自己的许愿，把金钱献给圣所。

估价按照年龄和性别分级：
- 20-60岁男性：50舍客勒；女性：30舍客勒
- 5-20岁男性：20舍客勒；女性：10舍客勒
- 1月-5岁男性：5舍客勒；女性：3舍客勒
- 60岁以上男性：15舍客勒；女性：10舍客勒

弟兄姐妹，这个估价表按照劳动力价值设定——男性比女性高、青壮年比老幼高。这不是关于人的价值（在神面前人人平等），而是关于劳动力的经济价值，用于实际的金钱换算。

"他若贫穷，不能照你所估定的价，就要把他带到祭司面前，祭司要按许愿人的力量估定他的价。"弟兄姐妹，穷人也可以履行许愿——标准会按照他的能力调整。恩典的大门永远不因贫穷而关闭。

第二部分：许愿奉献牲畜（9-13节）。

> "凡这一类献给耶和华的，都要成为圣。"

一旦你把一只动物许给神——它就成圣了，不可以再换回来做别的用途。"人不可改换，也不可更换，或是好的换坏的，或是坏的换好的。"

这里有一个人性的陷阱——你许愿献给神一只好羊，但等到真正要献的时候，舍不得，想换一只差的。"不可！"经文说。许给神的必须是你真正许的——不可以事后打折扣。

第三部分：许愿奉献房屋和土地（14-25节）。

房屋和土地可以被许给圣所——祭司估价，若想赎回需加五分之一。土地的估价与禧年相连——离禧年越近，价格越低（因为使用年限越短）。

第四部分：不可赎回的永献物（26-29节）。

> "一切永献的，就是人从他所有永献给耶和华的……都不可卖，也不可赎。凡永献的是归给耶和华为至圣。"

弟兄姐妹，普通的许愿是可以赎回的（加五分之一）——但"永献"是绝对的、不可反悔的。长子（属于神）不可"永献"——因为他已经是神的了（既然他本来就是神的，就不能再"献"一次）；但凡专门"永献"给神的，就永远不能收回。

第五部分：十一奉献（30-33节）。

> "地上所有的，无论是地上的种子是树上的果子，十分之一是耶和华的，是归给耶和华为圣的。"

弟兄姐妹，利未记以十一奉献作为结束——提醒以色列：你所有的一切，十分之一原本就是神的。十一奉献不是你把神的东西给神——是你承认神对所有一切的主权。你保留了十分之九——但这十分之九也是神的恩赐，只是神允许你使用。

"牛群羊群中，一切从杖下经过的，每第十只要归给耶和华为圣。不可问是好是坏，也不可更换。"弟兄姐妹，每第十只——随机的那只——归给神。你不可以挑选——不可以把好的留下把坏的给神。这个随机性提醒以色列：你不能"管理"你对神的奉献，让神只得到你认为够用的那份。奉献是信心的行动——你交出第十只，不管它是好是坏。

弟兄姐妹，第二十七章以"这就是耶和华在西奈山为以色列人所吩咐摩西的命令"作为利未记的结束——把整卷书锚定在西奈山的启示中。利未记不是人发明的宗教规则——它是从神而来的启示，是在圣山上神亲自赐下的。

弟兄姐妹，利未记从第一章的燔祭到最后一章的十一奉献——整卷书的主题始终如一：一切都归耶和华。你的献祭归耶和华，你的生命归耶和华，你的洁净归耶和华，你的节期归耶和华，你的土地归耶和华，你的收成归耶和华。"我是耶和华你们的神"——这句话在利未记中回响了将近五十次。一切都始于神，一切都归于神。愿荣耀归给祂，直到永远。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 27 is Leviticus's appendix—after chapter 26's grand covenant declaration, God supplemented practical regulations about vows and dedication valuations. This chapter looks like administrative rules, but behind it lies a profound theological principle: words pledged to God must count, what is consecrated must be genuine, offerings cannot be casually revoked.

Part one: valuing persons dedicated by vow (vv. 2-8).

"When a person makes a special vow of persons to the LORD at your valuation." Brothers and sisters, Israelites sometimes vowed in prayer: "LORD, if you...I will dedicate myself (or family members) to you." But they could not literally live in the sanctuary—so they could redeem their vow with silver, donating money to the sanctuary.

Valuations by age and gender:
- Ages 20-60, male: 50 shekels; female: 30 shekels
- Ages 5-20, male: 20 shekels; female: 10 shekels
- Ages 1 month to 5 years, male: 5 shekels; female: 3 shekels
- Age 60+, male: 15 shekels; female: 10 shekels

Brothers and sisters, this valuation table was set according to labor value—male higher than female, prime working age higher than elderly and young. This was not about human value (before God all persons are equal), but about economic labor value for practical monetary conversion.

"If the person is too poor to pay your valuation, then he shall be made to stand before the priest, and the priest shall value him according to what the vower can afford." Brothers and sisters, the poor could also fulfill vows—standards adjusted according to ability. Grace's door never closes because of poverty.

Part two: dedicating animals by vow (vv. 9-13).

> "If his gift is an animal that may be offered as an offering to the LORD, all of it that he gives to the LORD is holy." Brothers and sisters, once you dedicated an animal to God—it became holy, could not be reclaimed for other use. "He shall not exchange or substitute for it, good for bad or bad for good."

Here lurked a human temptation—you vowed to give God a fine sheep, but when actually giving it, unwilling, wanting to substitute an inferior one. "No!" the text says. What was vowed to God must be what was truly vowed—no subsequent discounting allowed.

Part three: dedicating houses and land (vv. 14-25).

Houses and land could be dedicated to the sanctuary—priest estimates value; redeeming required adding one-fifth. Land valuation connected to Jubilee—the closer to Jubilee, the lower the price (shorter remaining use term).

Part four: irrevocable devotions (vv. 26-29).

> "No devoted thing that a man devotes to the LORD, of anything that he has...may be sold or redeemed; every devoted thing is most holy to the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, ordinary vows could be redeemed (adding one-fifth)—but "devoted things" were absolute, irrevocable. Firstborn (belonging to God) could not be "devoted"—they already belonged to God (since already His, could not be "given" again); but whatever was specifically "devoted" to God could never be taken back.

Part five: tithes (vv. 30-33).

> "Every tithe of the land, whether of the seed of the land or of the fruit of the trees, is the LORD's; it is holy to the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, Leviticus concluded with the tithe—reminding Israel: of everything you have, one-tenth already belongs to God. Tithing was not you giving God what belongs to you—it was acknowledging God's sovereignty over everything. You kept nine-tenths—but those nine-tenths were also God's gift, merely permitted for your use.

"Every tithe of herds and flocks...every tenth animal that passes under the herdsman's staff shall be holy to the LORD. One shall not differentiate between good or bad, neither shall he make a substitute for it." Brothers and sisters, every tenth—the random one—belonged to God. You could not choose—could not keep good ones and give God bad ones. This randomness reminded Israel: you cannot "manage" your offerings to God, giving only what you deemed sufficient. Offering was an act of faith—surrendering the tenth, whatever it was.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 27 concluded Leviticus with "These are the commandments that the LORD commanded Moses for the people of Israel on Mount Sinai"—anchoring the entire book in Sinai's revelation. Leviticus was not humanly invented religious rules—it was revelation from God, personally given by God on the holy mountain.

Brothers and sisters, from chapter 1's burnt offering to the final chapter's tithe—the entire book's theme remained constant: everything belongs to the LORD. Your sacrifice belongs to the LORD, your life belongs to the LORD, your purity belongs to the LORD, your festivals belong to the LORD, your land belongs to the LORD, your harvest belongs to the LORD. "I am the LORD your God"—this phrase echoed nearly fifty times throughout Leviticus. Everything begins with God; everything returns to God. May all glory be to Him, forever and ever.

## 历史背景（中文）

利未记27章的估价表对研究旧约经济史有重要价值——50舍客勒是当时一个成年男性劳动力约三年的工资（按民22:7的"银子"标准）。这些估价反映的是劳动力市场价值，不是人的内在价值。"许愿"（neder）在以色列历史中有重要例子——拿西利人的愿（民6章），撒母耳的诞生（撒上1章，哈拿的愿），耶弗他的鲁莽誓言（士11章）。十一奉献在创世记14:20（亚伯拉罕给麦基洗德）和28:22（雅各的誓言）中已经出现——利未记27章把它制度化为律法的要求。"从杖下经过"的牛羊计数方式在古代近东也有文献记录——牧羊人用杖逐一清点羊群。第二十七章的结束语"这就是耶和华在西奈山……所吩咐摩西的命令"与利未记26:46是第二个结语——可能暗示第27章原本是一个独立的附录，后来被加入利未记正文。

## Historical Context (English)

Leviticus 27's valuation table is important for Old Testament economic history research—50 shekels represented approximately three years' wages for an adult male laborer (by Numbers 22:7's silver standard). These valuations reflected labor market value, not intrinsic human worth. "Vows" (neder) had important Israelite historical examples—Nazirite vows (Num 6), Samuel's birth (1 Sam 1, Hannah's vow), Jephthah's rash oath (Judg 11). Tithing appeared in Genesis 14:20 (Abraham to Melchizedek) and 28:22 (Jacob's vow)—Leviticus 27 institutionalized it as legal requirement. "Passing under the staff" animal counting is documented in ancient Near Eastern texts—shepherds counted flocks one by one under a staff. Chapter 27's closing formula "These are the commandments that the LORD commanded Moses for the people of Israel on Mount Sinai" was a second conclusion following Leviticus 26:46—possibly suggesting chapter 27 was originally an independent appendix later incorporated into Leviticus's main text.

## 经文结构（中文）

**人的估价（2-8节）**——按年龄性别分级估价（60岁以上男15女10→20-60岁男50女30→5-20岁男20女10→1月-5岁男5女3）、贫穷者按力量估定。

**牲畜的奉献（9-13节）**——洁净牲畜一许即为圣不可更换、不洁净牲畜按估价赎回。

**房屋和土地（14-25节）**——许给圣所可赎（加五分之一）、土地估价按禧年剩余年数、卖后不赎到禧年则归祭司。

**永献之物（26-29节）**——长子不可再献（已属神）、永献之物为至圣不可赎回不可卖、当灭之人必被治死。

**十一奉献（30-33节）**——土地果子的十分之一归耶和华为圣（可赎加五分之一）、牛羊每第十只归耶和华不可挑选不可更换。

**结语（34节）**——这是耶和华在西奈山为以色列人所吩咐摩西的命令。

## Structure (English)

**Human Valuations (vv. 2-8)**—Age-gender tiered valuations (60+ male 15 female 10 → 20-60 male 50 female 30 → 5-20 male 20 female 10 → 1mo-5yr male 5 female 3), poor assessed by ability.

**Animal Dedications (vv. 9-13)**—Clean animal once vowed becomes holy cannot be substituted, unclean animal redeemable at valuation.

**Houses and Land (vv. 14-25)**—Dedicated to sanctuary redeemable (+one-fifth), land valuation based on years remaining to Jubilee, if sold and not redeemed goes to priests at Jubilee.

**Devoted Things (vv. 26-29)**—Firstborn cannot be re-dedicated (already God's), devoted things are most holy cannot be redeemed or sold, devoted persons must be put to death.

**Tithes (vv. 30-33)**—Land and fruit tithe is the LORD's (redeemable +one-fifth), every tenth animal from herds and flocks belongs to LORD cannot be selected or substituted.

**Closing (v. 34)**—These are the commandments the LORD commanded Moses for Israel at Mount Sinai.

## 神学要点（中文）

**十一奉献——承认神的主权**
改革宗传统把十一奉献理解为承认神对万物主权的行为，而不是一种"换取神祝福"的交易。韦敏斯德大要理问答第108问论第二诫的要求包括承认神是独一的真神，我们的神，我们因此要崇拜、尊荣、荣耀神。十一奉献不是你帮助神——是你承认神不需要你的帮助，但祂要求你信靠祂、把一切归祂所有。

**许愿的严肃性——信实的操练**
"人若向耶和华许愿……不可食言，必要按口所出的一切话去行"（民30:2）——改革宗传统把守约作为圣洁生活的组成部分。韦敏斯德大要理问答第113问论第三诫的要求包括按誓言和许愿所承诺的去履行。言出必行——这是对神信实的回应，也是对邻舍的正直。

**"归给耶和华为圣"——奉献的不可逆性**
一旦分别为圣的不可收回——改革宗传统把这理解为呼召的神圣性。韦敏斯德大要理问答第108问论第二诫。你献给神的——你的信仰、你的事奉、你的人生——是不可以轻易收回的神圣委托。

## Theology (English)

**Tithing—Acknowledging God's Sovereignty**
The Reformed tradition understands tithing as acknowledging God's sovereignty over all things, not a transaction "purchasing God's blessing." Westminster Larger Catechism Q108 on the second commandment's requirements includes "acknowledging and adoring him as the only true God, and our God; and to worship and glorify him accordingly." Tithing was not helping God—it was acknowledging God needed no help but required trust, all things belonging to Him.

**Vows' Seriousness—Practicing Faithfulness**
"If a man vow a vow unto the LORD, or swear an oath to bind his soul with a bond; he shall not break his word, he shall do according to all that proceedeth out of his mouth." (Num 30:2)—the Reformed tradition includes covenant-keeping as part of holy living. Westminster Larger Catechism Q113 on the third commandment's requirements includes performing what we swear and vow. Speaking and doing—this was response to God's faithfulness and integrity toward neighbor.

**"Holy to the LORD"—Dedication's Irreversibility**
Once consecrated, cannot be reclaimed—the Reformed tradition understands this as calling's sacredness. What you gave to God—your faith, your service, your life—was a sacred commission not to be lightly withdrawn.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第二十七章虽是利未记的附录，却以奉献和献身的主题收结整卷书——这指向基督最终极的奉献。

十一奉献——"十分之一是耶和华的"。基督不只献上十分之一——祂献上了全部。腓立比书2:7-8"反倒虚己，取了奴仆的形象，成为人的样式；既有人的样子，就自己卑微，存心顺服，以至于死，且死在十字架上。"基督的奉献不是部分的——是全部的、彻底的、无保留的。

估价和赎回——基督用自己的血"赎回"了我们。哥林多前书6:20"因为你们是重价买来的。"彼得前书1:18-19"知道你们得赎，脱去你们祖宗所传流虚妄的行为，不是凭着能坏的金银等物，乃是凭着基督的宝血，如同无瑕疵无玷污的羔羊之血。"旧约的赎回需要加五分之一——基督的赎回付出了无价的生命。

"永献之物不可赎"——基督是神"永献"给世人的礼物。约翰福音3:16"神爱世人，甚至将他的独生子赐给他们。""赐"——神把儿子给了我们。约翰福音10:28"我又赐给他们永生，他们永不灭亡，谁也不能从我手里把他们夺去。"弟兄姐妹，神把我们赐给基督（约17:6"你从世上赐给我的人"），这个恩赐是"永献的"——永远不可撤回。

"这就是耶和华在西奈山为以色列人所吩咐摩西的命令"——利未记就这样结束了。整本书开始于神在会幕中呼叫摩西（1:1），结束于西奈山的权威。弟兄姐妹，利未记是一部启示——从神那里来，关于神，最终指向神——那位道成肉身的神，祂是我们的大祭司（来4:14）、我们的赎罪祭（约壹2:2）、我们的逾越节羔羊（林前5:7）、我们的禧年（路4:18）、我们生命的光（约8:12）。愿利未记中每一滴血、每一缕香烟、每一个节期，都指引我们仰望那位一切的成全者——耶稣基督。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 27, though Leviticus's appendix, concluded the entire book with the theme of offering and consecration—pointing to Christ's most ultimate sacrifice.

Tithes—"The tithe is the LORD's." Christ did not offer only one-tenth—He offered everything. Philippians 2:7-8: "He emptied himself, by taking the form of a servant, being born in the likeness of men. And being found in human form, he humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross." Christ's offering was not partial—it was total, complete, without reservation.

Valuation and redemption—Christ "redeemed" us with His own blood. 1 Corinthians 6:20: "You were bought with a price." 1 Peter 1:18-19: "You were ransomed...not with perishable things such as silver or gold, but with the precious blood of Christ, like that of a lamb without blemish or spot." Old Testament redemption required adding one-fifth—Christ's redemption paid the priceless cost of life.

"Devoted things shall not be redeemed"—Christ was God's "devoted gift" to humanity. John 3:16: "God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son." "Gave"—God gave us His Son. John 10:28: "I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand." Brothers and sisters, God gave us to Christ (John 17:6: "The people whom you gave me out of the world")—this gift was "devoted"—irrevocably given.

"These are the commandments that the LORD commanded Moses for the people of Israel on Mount Sinai"—Leviticus thus concluded. The entire book began with God calling Moses from the tabernacle (1:1), ending with Sinai's authority. Brothers and sisters, Leviticus was a revelation—coming from God, about God, ultimately pointing to God—the God who became flesh, who is our great High Priest (Heb 4:14), our propitiation (1 John 2:2), our Passover Lamb (1 Cor 5:7), our Jubilee (Luke 4:18), our light of life (John 8:12). May every drop of blood, every wisp of incense smoke, every festival in Leviticus lead us to look up to the One who fulfilled them all—Jesus Christ.
