## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。从第十一章开始，利未记进入了一个全新的主题——洁净与不洁净。前十章讲的是如何通过献祭来到神面前；第十一章开始讲的是如何在日常生活中保持圣洁。从圣所的祭坛走进厨房和饭桌——神不只关心你怎么敬拜，也关心你怎么吃饭。

这一章把动物分为"洁净的"（可以吃的）和"不洁净的"（不可以吃的），涵盖了四个领域——陆地走兽、水中活物、飞鸟和地上爬虫。

陆地走兽的标准（2-8节）——两个条件必须同时满足：分蹄且倒嚼。"凡蹄分两瓣、倒嚼的走兽，你们都可以吃。"牛、绵羊、山羊——分蹄又倒嚼，是洁净的。然后列出四个"几乎合格但差一点"的例子：骆驼（倒嚼但不分蹄）、沙番（倒嚼但不分蹄）、兔子（倒嚼但不分蹄）、猪（分蹄但不倒嚼）。

弟兄姐妹，猪在这个清单中最引人注目——因为它是"看起来洁净但实际不洁净"的代表。猪的蹄是分的（外表合格），但它不倒嚼（内在不合格）。外表的合格不能掩盖内在的缺陷。主耶稣后来说"你们这假冒为善的文士和法利赛人有祸了！因为你们好像粉饰的坟墓，外面好看，里面却装满了死人的骨头和一切的污秽"（太23:27）。猪就是旧约的"粉饰的坟墓"——外表分蹄，里面不倒嚼。

水中活物的标准（9-12节）——必须有翅有鳞。鱼类一般都是洁净的。没有翅和鳞的水中活物——虾、蟹、贝壳类、鳗鱼——都是不洁净的。经文用了很强的措辞——"你们都当以为可憎"。

飞鸟（13-19节）——没有给出分类标准，而是直接列出不可吃的清单：鹰、雕、鹞鹰、鸵鸟、猫头鹰、蝙蝠等。弟兄姐妹，不洁净的鸟有一个共同特征——它们大多是食腐动物或猛禽。它们以死亡为食——以吃其他动物的尸体或血为生。生命和死亡在利未记的世界观中是最根本的分界线——洁净与生命相关，不洁净与死亡相关。

地上爬虫（29-43节）——老鼠、壁虎、蜥蜴等都是不洁净的。"凡用肚子行走的和用四足行走的，或是有许多足的，就是一切爬在地上的，你们都不可吃。"

弟兄姐妹，为什么？为什么这些动物不能吃？在整个第十一章中，神只给了一个理由——不是健康理由（虽然这些规定确实有卫生上的智慧），不是生态理由——而是神学理由：

> "我是耶和华你们的神；所以你们要成为圣洁，因为我是圣洁的。"

这句话是整个利未记的核心宣言，也是第十一章的神学基础。你不吃猪肉不是因为猪肉不健康——你不吃猪肉是因为"我是耶和华，我是圣洁的，你们也要圣洁。"饮食律法是一种日常的、具体的、每顿饭都要操练的分别为圣。

弟兄姐妹，想象一下——以色列人每天吃饭时都要做一个选择：这个可以吃，那个不可以吃。每一顿饭都是一次小小的"信仰告白"——我属于耶和华，我按照祂的方式生活，连吃饭都要不同。当周围的迦南人吃猪肉、吃虾蟹的时候，以色列人不吃——这个日常的差异不断地提醒他们：你是不同的，你是被分别出来的民族。

> "要把洁净的和不洁净的，可吃的与不可吃的活物，都分别出来。"

弟兄姐妹，"分别出来"——这是利未记的关键动词。圣洁（qadosh）的本意就是"分别"。神把光和暗分别（创1:4），把海和旱地分别（创1:9-10），把以色列和列国分别（利20:26），现在又把洁净和不洁净的食物分别。分别——这是神创造和治理世界的基本方式。混淆界限——把圣的当俗的、把洁净的当不洁净的——就是对神秩序的破坏。

第十一章最后把这一切的饮食律法锚定在出埃及的事件上："我是把你们从埃及地领出来的耶和华。"弟兄姐妹，饮食律法不是抽象的规定——它扎根在历史事件中。神拯救了你——所以你要成为圣洁。恩典先于律法——不是你守了律法神才爱你，而是神已经爱你救了你，所以你要用圣洁的生活来回应。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Beginning from chapter 11, Leviticus enters an entirely new theme—clean and unclean. The first ten chapters addressed how to approach God through sacrifice; chapter 11 addresses how to maintain holiness in daily life. From the sanctuary altar into the kitchen and dining table—God cares not only about how you worship but also how you eat.

This chapter divides animals into "clean" (edible) and "unclean" (inedible), covering four domains—land animals, water creatures, birds, and ground-dwelling insects.

The land animal standard (vv. 2-8)—two conditions must both be met: split hooves and chewing cud. "Whatever parts the hoof and is cloven-footed and chews the cud, among the animals, you may eat." Cattle, sheep, goats—split hooves and cud-chewing, clean. Then four "almost qualifying but falling short" examples: camel (chews cud but unsplit hooves), rock badger (chews cud but unsplit hooves), hare (chews cud but unsplit hooves), pig (split hooves but does not chew cud).

Brothers and sisters, the pig is the most striking on this list—because it represents "appearing clean but actually unclean." The pig's hooves are split (externally qualified), but it does not chew cud (internally disqualified). External qualification cannot cover internal deficiency. The Lord Jesus later said: "Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! for ye are like unto whited sepulchres, which indeed appear beautiful outward, but are within full of dead men’s bones, and of all uncleanness." (Matt 23:27). The pig was the Old Testament's "whitewashed tomb"—outwardly split-hoofed, inwardly not cud-chewing.

Water creatures' standard (vv. 9-12)—must have fins and scales. Fish were generally clean. Water creatures without fins and scales—shrimp, crab, shellfish, eels—were all unclean. Scripture used strong language—"you shall regard them as detestable."

Birds (vv. 13-19)—no classification criteria given, just a direct list of forbidden species: eagle, vulture, hawk, ostrich, owl, bat, etc. Brothers and sisters, unclean birds shared a common trait—most were carrion-eaters or raptors. They fed on death—living by eating other animals' corpses or blood. Life and death in Leviticus's worldview formed the most fundamental dividing line—cleanness related to life, uncleanness to death.

Ground creatures (vv. 29-43)—mice, geckos, lizards, all unclean. "Everything that goes on its belly, and everything that goes on all fours, or whatever has many feet, any swarming thing that swarms on the ground, you shall not eat."

Brothers and sisters, why? Why could these animals not be eaten? Throughout chapter 11, God gave only one reason—not health (though these regulations indeed contained hygienic wisdom), not ecological—but theological:

> "For I am the LORD your God. Consecrate yourselves therefore, and be holy, for I am holy."

This statement is Leviticus's core declaration and chapter 11's theological foundation. You avoided pork not because it was unhealthy—you avoided pork because "I am the LORD, I am holy, and you shall be holy." Dietary law was a daily, concrete, every-meal practice of consecration.

Brothers and sisters, imagine—every meal Israelites made a choice: this is edible, that is not. Every meal was a small "confession of faith"—I belong to the LORD, I live according to His ways, even my eating is different. When surrounding Canaanites ate pork and shellfish, Israelites did not—this daily difference constantly reminded them: you are different, you are a set-apart people.

> "To make a distinction between the unclean and the clean and between the living creature that may be eaten and the living creature that may not be eaten."

Brothers and sisters, "make a distinction"—this is Leviticus's key verb. Holiness (qadosh) fundamentally means "set apart." God separated light from darkness (Gen 1:4), sea from dry land (Gen 1:9-10), Israel from the nations (Lev 20:26), and now clean from unclean food. Separation—this is God's fundamental way of creating and governing the world. Confusing boundaries—treating holy as common, clean as unclean—destroys God's order.

Chapter 11 finally anchors all dietary law in the exodus event: "I am the LORD who brought you up out of the land of Egypt." Brothers and sisters, dietary law was not abstract regulation—it was rooted in historical event. God saved you—therefore be holy. Grace preceded law—not that God loved you because you kept the law, but God already loved and saved you, so you respond with a holy life.

## 历史背景（中文）

以色列的饮食律法在古代近东是独特的——虽然埃及和美索不达米亚也有某些饮食禁忌（如埃及祭司不吃鱼），但没有任何文化有如此系统化的、涵盖所有动物种类的分类体系。"分蹄倒嚼"的双重标准——一些现代人类学家（如Mary Douglas在《洁净与危险》中）认为，不洁净的动物是"分类异常"的——它们不完全属于任何一个类别（如猪有分蹄但不倒嚼，蝙蝠有翅膀但不是鸟）。这种"边界模糊"的动物被视为不洁净，因为它们代表了对神创造秩序的混淆。"我是耶和华你们的神"（v.44）这个身份宣告在利未记中出现了约50次——它是所有律法的终极根基：不是因为这些规定本身有什么魔力，而是因为"我是耶和华"。犹太人的饮食律法（kashrut）至今仍被遵守——现代犹太教的kosher认证体系就建立在利未记11章的基础上。

## Historical Context (English)

Israel's dietary laws were unique in the ancient Near East—though Egypt and Mesopotamia had certain dietary restrictions (e.g., Egyptian priests avoided fish), no culture had such a systematic classification system covering all animal categories. The dual "split hoof and cud-chewing" standard—some modern anthropologists (such as Mary Douglas in Purity and Danger) argue unclean animals were "classificatory anomalies"—not fully belonging to any single category (pig has split hooves but doesn't chew cud; bat has wings but isn't a bird). Such "boundary-blurring" animals were deemed unclean because they represented confusion of God's created order. "I am the LORD your God" (v.44) appears approximately 50 times in Leviticus—it is all laws' ultimate foundation: not because the regulations possessed inherent power, but because "I am the LORD." Jewish dietary law (kashrut) is still observed today—modern Judaism's kosher certification system is built on Leviticus 11.

## 经文结构（中文）

**陆地走兽（2-8节）**——分蹄倒嚼可吃、骆驼沙番兔子猪为不洁（各缺一个条件）、不可吃不可摸。

**水中活物（9-12节）**——有翅有鳞可吃、无翅无鳞"以为可憎"。

**飞鸟（13-19节）**——列出不可吃的鸟类清单（鹰雕鹞鹰猫头鹰蝙蝠等）。

**有翅膀的昆虫（20-23节）**——四足爬虫可憎、但有腿在足以上能跳的可吃（蝗虫蚂蚱蟋蟀等）。

**接触不洁的规定（24-40节）**——摸死兽不洁到晚上、锅瓦器打碎、泉源蓄水池洁净。

**地上爬虫禁令（41-43节）**——凡爬行的不可吃。

**圣洁的宣告（44-47节）**——"你们要圣洁因为我是圣洁的"、我是领你们出埃及的耶和华、分别洁净与不洁净。

## Structure (English)

**Land Animals (vv. 2-8)**—Split hoof and cud-chewing edible; camel rock badger hare pig unclean (each lacking one condition); not to eat or touch.

**Water Creatures (vv. 9-12)**—Fins and scales edible; without fins and scales "detestable."

**Birds (vv. 13-19)**—List of forbidden birds (eagle vulture hawk owl bat etc.).

**Winged Insects (vv. 20-23)**—Four-footed insects detestable; but those with jointed legs above feet for hopping edible (locust cricket grasshopper).

**Contact with Unclean (vv. 24-40)**—Touching dead animals unclean until evening; earthen vessels broken; springs and cisterns remain clean.

**Ground Creature Prohibition (vv. 41-43)**—Nothing that swarms on ground to be eaten.

**Holiness Declaration (vv. 44-47)**—"Be holy for I am holy"; I am the LORD who brought you out of Egypt; distinguish clean from unclean.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"你们要圣洁因为我是圣洁的"——圣洁的根基**
这句话是整部利未记的核心。改革宗传统教导圣洁不是人自己追求的道德完美——而是对神圣洁的回应。韦敏斯德小要理问答第35问论成圣说成圣是"我们从神白白领受恩典的工作"——圣洁的源头是神，不是人。彼得前书1:15-16引用利未记说"那召你们的既是圣洁，你们在一切所行的事上也要圣洁。"圣洁是呼召，不是自力更生。

**日常生活的圣洁化——万事归主**
饮食律法把圣洁从圣所延伸到饭桌——改革宗传统强调信仰不只在教会，也在日常生活的每个角落。哥林多前书10:31"你们或吃或喝，无论做甚么，都要为荣耀神而行。"加尔文说"世界是神的剧场"——每一顿饭、每一个选择都是敬拜。

**分别——圣洁的本质**
"分别洁净的和不洁净的"——圣洁的核心是分别（separation）。改革宗传统教导教会在世界中但不属于世界——约翰福音17:14-16基督的大祭司祷告："他们不属世界……求你保守他们。"以色列的饮食分别预表了教会在世界中的道德分别。

## Theology (English)

**"Be Holy for I Am Holy"—The Foundation of Holiness**
This statement is all of Leviticus's core. The Reformed tradition teaches holiness is not self-pursued moral perfection—but response to God's holiness. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q35 on sanctification says it is "the work of God's free grace"—holiness's source is God, not humans. 1 Peter 1:15-16 quotes Leviticus: "As he who called you is holy, you also be holy in all your conduct." Holiness is a calling, not self-effort.

**Sanctifying Daily Life—All Things to the Lord**
Dietary law extended holiness from sanctuary to dining table—the Reformed tradition emphasizes faith belongs not only in church but in every corner of daily life. 1 Corinthians 10:31: "Whether you eat or drink, or whatever you do, do all to the glory of God." Calvin said "the world is God's theater"—every meal, every choice is worship.

**Separation—Holiness's Essence**
"Distinguish clean from unclean"—holiness's core is separation. The Reformed tradition teaches the church is in the world but not of it—John 17:14-16 Christ's high priestly prayer: "They are not of the world...keep them." Israel's dietary separation prefigured the church's moral distinction in the world.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第十一章的饮食律法在新约中经历了根本性的转变——基督宣告了一切食物都是洁净的。

马可福音7:18-19"耶稣对他们说：你们也是这样不明白么？岂不晓得凡从外面进入的，不能污秽人……耶稣这是说，各样的食物都是洁净的。"基督废除了旧约的饮食分类——不是因为利未记11章错了，而是因为它的教导已经在基督里成全了。

使徒行传10:9-15彼得在约帕看见异象——"有一块大布，系着四角，缒在地上；里面有地上各样四足的走兽和昆虫，并天上的飞鸟。又有声音向他说：彼得，起来，宰了吃！彼得却说：主啊，这是不可的！凡俗物和不洁净的物，我从来没有吃过。第二次有声音向他说：神所洁净的，你不可当作俗物。"

弟兄姐妹，这个异象不只是关于食物——它是关于外邦人。犹太人把外邦人视为"不洁净的"——正如不洁净的动物不可吃，不洁净的外邦人不可来往。但基督的十字架拆除了这堵隔断的墙。以弗所书2:14"因他使我们和睦，将两下合而为一，拆毁了中间隔断的墙。"旧约用饮食律法把以色列和列国分开——新约用十字架把犹太人和外邦人合为一体。

"我是耶和华你们的神……你们要圣洁因为我是圣洁的"——在基督里，圣洁的标准不再是外在的饮食规定，而是内在的心灵更新。罗马书14:17"因为神的国不在乎吃喝，只在乎公义、和平，并圣灵中的喜乐。"食物不再定义圣洁——基督定义圣洁。哥罗西书2:16-17"不拘在饮食上……都不可让人论断你们。这些原是后事的影儿，那形体却是基督。"利未记11章是影子——基督是实体。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 11's dietary laws underwent fundamental transformation in the New Testament—Christ declared all foods clean.

Mark 7:18-19: "Do you not see that whatever goes into a person from outside cannot defile him...Thus he declared all foods clean." Christ abolished the Old Testament dietary classifications—not because Leviticus 11 was wrong, but because its teaching was fulfilled in Christ.

Acts 10:9-15, Peter's vision in Joppa—"something like a great sheet descending, being let down by its four corners upon the earth. In it were all kinds of animals and reptiles and birds of the air. And there came a voice to him: 'Rise, Peter; kill and eat.' But Peter said, 'By no means, Lord; for I have never eaten anything that is common or unclean.' And the voice came to him again: 'What God has made clean, do not call common.'"

Brothers and sisters, this vision was not merely about food—it was about Gentiles. Jews viewed Gentiles as "unclean"—just as unclean animals could not be eaten, unclean Gentiles could not be associated with. But Christ's cross demolished this dividing wall. Ephesians 2:14: "For he himself is our peace, who has made us both one and has broken down in his flesh the dividing wall of hostility." The Old Testament used dietary law to separate Israel from the nations—the New Testament uses the cross to unite Jew and Gentile into one body.

"I am the LORD your God...be holy for I am holy"—in Christ, holiness's standard is no longer external dietary regulations but internal heart renewal. Romans 14:17: "For the kingdom of God is not a matter of eating and drinking but of righteousness and peace and joy in the Holy Spirit." Food no longer defines holiness—Christ defines holiness. Colossians 2:16-17: "Therefore let no one pass judgment on you in questions of food and drink...These are a shadow of the things to come, but the substance belongs to Christ." Leviticus 11 was the shadow—Christ is the substance.
