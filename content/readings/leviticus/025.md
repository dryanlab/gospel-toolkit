## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十五章是利未记中最革命性的一章——安息年和禧年。如果第十九章是"旧约的登山宝训"，第二十五章就是"旧约的社会革命宣言"。这一章的制度设计远远超前于任何古代文明——它要求每七年让土地休息一年，每五十年重新分配所有土地和释放所有奴仆。

第一部分：安息年（1-7节）。

> "六年要耕种田地……第七年，地要守圣安息，就是向耶和华守的安息，不可耕种田地。"

弟兄姐妹，安息日是每七天休息一天——安息年是每七年休息一年。但这次安息的不是人——而是土地本身。"地要向耶和华守安息"——弟兄姐妹，土地有"安息"的权利！人不是土地的绝对主人——土地属于神（23节"地不可永卖，因为地是我的"），人只是管家。即使是土地，也有被过度使用的时候——它需要安息来恢复。

安息年的土产怎么办？"地在安息年所出的，要给你和你的仆人、婢女、雇工人，并寄居的外人当食物。"弟兄姐妹，安息年的自然产出归所有人——主人、仆人、外人——共同享用。在安息年，社会等级被暂时模糊了——穷人和富人吃同样的食物，都是地里自然长出来的。

第二部分：禧年（8-17节）——利未记中最惊人的制度。

> "你要计算七个安息年，就是七七年……第五十年，你们要当作圣年，在遍地给一切的居民宣告自由。这年必为你们的禧年，各人要归自己的产业，各归本家。"

弟兄姐妹，禧年（Jubilee，来自希伯来文yovel，"公羊角"——因为禧年以赎罪日吹角开始）每五十年一次，它有三个惊天动地的规定：

第一——所有土地归还原主。"这禧年，你们各人要归自己的地业。"弟兄姐妹，这意味着在以色列，土地不可能被永久转让。你可以"卖"土地——但实际上你只是卖"使用权"，而且期限到禧年为止。"你若卖甚么给邻舍……你要按禧年以后的年数向邻舍买"——离禧年越近，土地的价格越低（因为使用年限越短）。这个制度从根本上防止了土地的永久集中——没有人可以通过买地来无限扩大自己的财富，因为五十年后一切归零。

第二——所有以色列奴仆被释放。"到了禧年，他和他儿女要离开你，一同出去归回本家。"弟兄姐妹，以色列人不可以永远做奴仆——因为"他们是我的仆人，是我从埃及地领出来的"。你的弟兄因为贫穷卖身给你——但他的身份不是"你的奴隶"而是"神的仆人"。他的终极主人是神——不是你。禧年提醒以色列：你不拥有任何人——神拥有所有人。

第三——所有债务被免除（与安息年的申15:1-2呼应）。

弟兄姐妹，禧年的三项规定加在一起构成了人类历史上最早的系统性反贫困制度：(1)防止土地永久集中在少数人手里；(2)防止穷人永远沦为奴隶；(3)定期清零社会的经济不平等。

第二十五章还有一系列保护穷人的具体规定——"不可向他取利，也不可向他多要"（36节）。弟兄姐妹，以色列人不可以向贫穷的同胞收利息——这不是普通的经济政策，这是信仰的要求。你不可以从弟兄的困境中获利。

"因为以色列人都是我的仆人，是我从埃及地领出来的。我是耶和华你们的神。"弟兄姐妹，禧年的终极根基是出埃及事件——你曾经是埃及的奴隶，神释放了你。所以你不可以把别人当成永久的奴隶。你经历过的恩典应该成为你对待别人的方式。

"地不可永卖，因为地是我的；你们在我面前是客旅，是寄居的。"弟兄姐妹，这是第二十五章最核心的神学宣告——土地不属于你，土地属于神。你是"客旅和寄居的"。这不只是关于土地——这是关于你在这世上的身份。你拥有的一切——土地、房屋、金钱、甚至你自己的生命——都不真正属于你。一切属于神。禧年每五十年提醒一次：你不是主人——你是管家。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 25 is Leviticus's most revolutionary chapter—the Sabbath year and Jubilee. If chapter 19 was "the Old Testament's Sermon on the Mount," chapter 25 was "the Old Testament's social revolution manifesto." This chapter's institutional design far surpassed any ancient civilization—requiring land rest every seven years, and every fifty years redistributing all land and freeing all servants.

Part one: the Sabbath year (vv. 1-7).

> "Six years you shall sow your field...but in the seventh year there shall be a Sabbath of solemn rest for the land, a Sabbath to the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, the Sabbath was every seventh day's rest—the Sabbath year was every seventh year's rest. But this time it was not people resting—but the land itself. "The land shall keep a Sabbath to the LORD"—Brothers and sisters, the land had the "right" to rest! Humans were not the land's absolute masters—the land belonged to God (v. 23: "The land shall not be sold in perpetuity, for the land is mine"), humans were merely stewards. Even land could be overworked—it needed Sabbath to recover.

What about the Sabbath year's produce? "What the land yields during the Sabbath year shall be food for you, for yourself and for your male and female slaves and for your hired worker and the sojourner who lives with you." Brothers and sisters, Sabbath year's natural produce belonged to everyone—masters, servants, foreigners—shared alike. During the Sabbath year, social hierarchy was temporarily blurred—poor and rich ate the same food, all naturally grown.

Part two: the Jubilee (vv. 8-17)—Leviticus's most astonishing institution.

> "You shall count seven weeks of years, seven times seven years...And you shall consecrate the fiftieth year, and proclaim liberty throughout the land to all its inhabitants. It shall be a jubilee for you, when each of you shall return to his property and each of you shall return to his clan."

Brothers and sisters, the Jubilee (from Hebrew yovel, "ram's horn"—because Jubilee began with trumpet blasts on the Day of Atonement) occurred every fifty years with three earth-shaking provisions:

First—all land returned to original owners. "In this year of jubilee each of you shall return to his property." Brothers and sisters, this meant in Israel, land could never be permanently transferred. You could "sell" land—but actually sold only "usage rights" with a Jubilee expiration. "If you make a sale to your neighbor...you shall pay him according to the number of years after the jubilee"—the closer to Jubilee, the lower the price (shorter usage term). This system fundamentally prevented permanent land concentration—no one could endlessly expand wealth through land purchases, because every fifty years everything reset.

Second—all Israelite servants freed. "In the year of jubilee he and his children with him shall be released and go back to his own clan." Brothers and sisters, Israelites could not be permanent servants—because "they are my servants, whom I brought out of the land of Egypt." Your brother sold himself to you out of poverty—but his identity was not "your slave" but "God's servant." His ultimate master was God—not you. Jubilee reminded Israel: you own no one—God owns everyone.

Third—all debts canceled (echoing the Sabbath year in Deut 15:1-2).

Brothers and sisters, Jubilee's three provisions together constituted human history's earliest systematic anti-poverty institution: (1) preventing permanent land concentration among the few; (2) preventing the poor from becoming permanent slaves; (3) periodically resetting society's economic inequality.

Chapter 25 also contained specific poverty protections—"Take no interest from him or profit" (v. 36). Brothers and sisters, Israelites could not charge interest to poor countrymen—not ordinary economic policy but a faith requirement. You must not profit from your brother's distress.

"For they are my servants, whom I brought out of the land of Egypt. I am the LORD your God." Brothers and sisters, Jubilee's ultimate foundation was the exodus—you were once Egypt's slave, God freed you. So you must not make others permanent slaves. The grace you experienced should shape how you treat others.

"The land shall not be sold in perpetuity, for the land is mine. For you are strangers and sojourners with me." Brothers and sisters, this was chapter 25's core theological declaration—the land does not belong to you; the land belongs to God. You are "strangers and sojourners." This was not merely about land—it was about your identity in this world. Everything you possess—land, houses, money, even your own life—does not truly belong to you. Everything belongs to God. Jubilee reminded every fifty years: you are not the owner—you are the steward.

## 历史背景（中文）

禧年制度在古代近东没有真正的平行——虽然美索不达米亚有国王在登基时宣告"misharum"（公义法令/债务豁免），但那些是一次性的政治行为，不是制度化的循环。以色列的禧年是固定的、预先设定的、不依赖国王意愿的——这在古代世界是独一无二的。历史上，以色列是否真正实行过禧年是有争议的——先知们反复谴责土地集中和压榨穷人（赛5:8"祸哉！那些以房接房、以地连地的"），暗示禧年制度可能从未被完全执行。"第五十年"的计算也有争议——是第49年（第七个安息年）之后的额外一年，还是就是第49年本身？如果是额外的第50年，那意味着连续两年不耕种（第49年安息年+第50年禧年），这对农业社会是极大的信心考验。路加福音4:18-19耶稣在拿撒勒会堂宣读以赛亚书61:1-2"主的灵在我身上……叫那受压制的得自由，报告神悦纳人的禧年"——耶稣明确把自己的使命与禧年挂钩。

## Historical Context (English)

The Jubilee institution had no true ancient Near Eastern parallel—though Mesopotamian kings proclaimed "misharum" (justice decrees/debt releases) at enthronement, those were one-time political acts, not institutionalized cycles. Israel's Jubilee was fixed, predetermined, independent of royal will—unique in the ancient world. Whether Israel ever truly practiced the Jubilee is debated—prophets repeatedly condemned land concentration and oppression (Isa 5:8: "Woe to those who join house to house, who add field to field"), suggesting the Jubilee system may never have been fully implemented. "Fiftieth year" calculation is also debated—an extra year after the 49th (seventh Sabbath year), or the 49th year itself? If an extra 50th year, it meant two consecutive fallow years (49th Sabbath year + 50th Jubilee), an enormous faith test for an agricultural society. Luke 4:18-19: Jesus in Nazareth's synagogue read Isaiah 61:1-2: "The Spirit of the Lord is upon me...to set at liberty those who are oppressed, to proclaim the year of the Lord's favor"—Jesus explicitly linked His mission to the Jubilee.

## 经文结构（中文）

**安息年（1-7节）**——六年耕种第七年地守安息、不可耕种收割、自长的归所有人（主仆外人牲畜）共享。

**禧年宣告（8-12节）**——七个七年=四十九年、第五十年赎罪日吹角、宣告自由、各归产业各归本家、禧年不耕种。

**土地买卖规则（13-17节）**——按禧年后年数定价、离禧年近则便宜、不可亏负。

**安息年的信心（18-22节）**——若问第七年吃什么、神赐第六年三年的出产。

**土地赎回权（23-34节）**——地不可永卖因地是神的、穷弟兄卖地则至近亲属赎回、城内房屋一年内可赎。

**保护穷人（35-38节）**——不可取利不可多要、敬畏你的神。

**以色列人不为永久奴仆（39-46节）**——穷弟兄卖身只到禧年、如雇工待他、不可严辖、因他们是神的仆人。

**外邦奴仆与赎回权（47-55节）**——外邦主人买了以色列人→亲属可赎→禧年必释放、因以色列人是神的仆人。

## Structure (English)

**Sabbath Year (vv. 1-7)**—Six years cultivate, seventh year land rests, no sowing reaping, volunteer growth shared by all (master servant foreigner livestock).

**Jubilee Proclamation (vv. 8-12)**—Seven sevens = forty-nine years, fiftieth year trumpet on Day of Atonement, proclaim liberty, each returns to property and clan, no sowing in Jubilee.

**Land Sale Rules (vv. 13-17)**—Price based on years after Jubilee, closer to Jubilee means cheaper, no wronging each other.

**Sabbath Year Faith (vv. 18-22)**—If asked what to eat in seventh year, God commands sixth year blessing for three years.

**Land Redemption Rights (vv. 23-34)**—Land not sold permanently for it is God's, poor brother's sold land redeemed by nearest kin, city houses redeemable within one year.

**Protecting the Poor (vv. 35-38)**—No interest no profit, fear your God.

**Israelites Not Permanent Slaves (vv. 39-46)**—Poor brother sells himself only until Jubilee, treat as hired worker, no harsh rule, they are God's servants.

**Foreign Masters and Redemption (vv. 47-55)**—Foreigner buys Israelite → kin may redeem → Jubilee must release, for Israel are God's servants.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"地是我的"——万物属于神的管家观**
改革宗传统强调万物属于神、人是管家。韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问"人一生的首要目的是什么？"答"（一）荣耀神；（二）以祂为乐，直到永远。"禧年的土地归还制度教导人：你拥有的一切是暂时托管的——终极的所有权属于神。加尔文教导信徒"把我们所拥有的一切都看作是神托付给我们的"。

**禧年——结构性恩典**
禧年不是个人善行而是制度性安排——改革宗传统不只关注个人道德，也关注社会公义的结构。韦敏斯德大要理问答第141问论第八诫的责任：尽力使别人的外在财富和境况得到保全和促进。禧年是"结构性恩典"——通过制度来保证穷人不被永久边缘化。

**"你们在我面前是客旅是寄居的"——在世客旅的身份**
改革宗传统教导信徒在世上是"客旅和寄居的"——希伯来书11:13"承认自己在世上是客旅，是寄居的。"这不是悲观主义——而是认识到我们的真正家乡在天上。禧年每五十年一次提醒以色列人：这片土地不是你的终极归宿。

## Theology (English)

**"The Land Is Mine"—Stewardship of All Things Belonging to God**
The Reformed tradition emphasizes all things belong to God; humans are stewards. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "What is the chief end of man?" "Man's chief end is to glorify God." Jubilee's land-return system taught: everything you possess is temporarily entrusted—ultimate ownership belongs to God. Calvin taught believers to "regard everything we possess as committed to us by God."

**Jubilee—Structural Grace**
Jubilee was not individual charity but institutional arrangement—the Reformed tradition concerns not only personal morality but social justice's structures. Westminster Larger Catechism Q141 on eighth commandment duties: "endeavoring...to procure, preserve, and further the wealth and outward estate of others." Jubilee was "structural grace"—institutionally ensuring the poor were not permanently marginalized.

**"You Are Strangers and Sojourners with Me"—Pilgrim Identity**
The Reformed tradition teaches believers are "strangers and sojourners" in this world—Hebrews 11:13: "They acknowledged that they were strangers and exiles on the earth." This is not pessimism—but recognizing our true home is in heaven. Jubilee every fifty years reminded Israel: this land is not your ultimate destination.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第二十五章的禧年是旧约中指向基督使命最直接的社会制度。

耶稣在路加福音4:18-19宣告自己的使命时，直接引用了与禧年相关的以赛亚书61:1-2："主的灵在我身上，因为他用膏膏我，叫我传福音给贫穷的人；差遣我报告：被掳的得释放，瞎眼的得看见，叫那受压制的得自由，报告神悦纳人的禧年。"弟兄姐妹，"神悦纳人的禧年"——基督宣告祂自己就是终极的禧年！

禧年释放奴仆——基督释放罪的奴仆。约翰福音8:36"所以天父的儿子若叫你们自由，你们就真自由了。"利未记25章的释放是每五十年一次——基督的释放是一次永远的。

禧年归还产业——基督恢复我们失去的一切。使徒行传3:21"天必留他，等到万物复兴的时候。"弟兄姐妹，亚当在伊甸园失去的"产业"——与神的关系、治理大地的权柄、永恒的生命——在基督里被归还了。基督是终极的禧年——一切被罪夺走的都将被恢复。

禧年免除债务——基督免除了我们罪的债。歌罗西书2:13-14"神赦免了你们一切过犯，便将那有碍于我们的字据撤去，钉在十字架上。"弟兄姐妹，我们的罪债不是每五十年免一次——基督在十字架上一次永远地撕毁了我们的罪债字据。

"地不可永卖，因为地是我的"——哥林多前书6:19-20"你们不是自己的人，因为你们是重价买来的。"弟兄姐妹，禧年说土地属于神——新约说你的身体就是圣灵的殿，属于神。基督用自己的血"买"了我们——我们是祂的"产业"。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 25's Jubilee is the Old Testament social institution most directly pointing to Christ's mission.

When Jesus announced His mission in Luke 4:18-19, He directly quoted Jubilee-related Isaiah 61:1-2: "The Spirit of the Lord is upon me, because he has anointed me to proclaim good news to the poor. He has sent me to proclaim liberty to the captives and recovering of sight to the blind, to set at liberty those who are oppressed, to proclaim the year of the Lord's favor." Brothers and sisters, "the year of the Lord's favor"—Christ declared Himself the ultimate Jubilee!

Jubilee freed servants—Christ frees sin's slaves. John 8:36: "So if the Son sets you free, you will be free indeed." Leviticus 25's freedom came every fifty years—Christ's freedom is once and forever.

Jubilee restored property—Christ restores everything we lost. Acts 3:21: "whom heaven must receive until the time for restoring all the things." Brothers and sisters, the "property" Adam lost in Eden—relationship with God, authority over earth, eternal life—is returned in Christ. Christ is the ultimate Jubilee—everything stolen by sin will be restored.

Jubilee canceled debts—Christ canceled our sin-debt. Colossians 2:13-14: "Having forgiven us all our trespasses, by canceling the record of debt that stood against us...nailing it to the cross." Brothers and sisters, our sin-debt is not forgiven every fifty years—Christ on the cross permanently destroyed our debt record.

"The land shall not be sold in perpetuity, for the land is mine"—1 Corinthians 6:19-20: "You are not your own, for you were bought with a price." Brothers and sisters, Jubilee said land belongs to God—the New Testament says your body is the Spirit's temple, belonging to God. Christ "bought" us with His own blood—we are His "property."
