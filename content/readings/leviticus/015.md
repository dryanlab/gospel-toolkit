## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十五章处理的是人体分泌物导致的不洁净——这是利未记中最"私密"的一章。它涉及男性的漏症（慢性分泌物和梦遗）以及女性的月经和血漏。弟兄姐妹，这些话题在任何文化中都是敏感的——但神不回避它们。祂把人体的每一个功能都纳入圣洁的范畴——连最隐秘的生理现象也在祂的关注之下。

第十五章按照四种情况分类——前两种是严重的（需要献祭），后两种是轻微的（只需等到晚上）：

第一种：男性的慢性漏症（2-15节）。"人若身患漏症，他因这漏症就不洁净了。"这里的"漏症"（zav）可能指慢性尿道感染或其他持续性的异常分泌。这是一种疾病状态——不是正常的生理功能。不洁净的程度很严重——他的床、他坐过的东西、摸他的人、被他吐过唾沫的人——全部不洁净。弟兄姐妹，不洁净像一圈圈涟漪一样向外扩散——从身体到衣物，从衣物到家具，从家具到接触的人。

痊愈后需要七天的洁净期，第八天献两只鸟——一只赎罪祭，一只燔祭。

第二种：女性的慢性血漏（25-30节）。"女人若在经期以外患多日的血漏，或是经期过长"——这也是疾病状态，不是正常月经。处理方式与男性漏症相同——七天洁净期加第八天的献祭。

弟兄姐妹，男女在这里完全平等——同样的不洁净规定，同样的恢复程序，同样的献祭。利未记不歧视性别——男性和女性的身体分泌物受同样的律法管辖。

第三种：男性的梦遗（16-17节）。"人若梦遗，他必不洁净到晚上，并要用水洗全身。"这是轻微的不洁净——不需要献祭，只需要洗澡，到晚上就自然恢复洁净。弟兄姐妹，梦遗不是罪——这是正常的生理现象。但即使是正常的、非自愿的生理事件也会导致暂时的不洁净——因为它涉及了"生命力"（精液是生命的载体）的流出。

第四种：女性的正常月经（19-24节）。"女人行经，必污秽七天。"同样，月经不是罪——但它导致七天的不洁净。与月经期的女性同房则双方都不洁净到晚上。

弟兄姐妹，为什么正常的生理功能会导致不洁净？因为利未记的不洁净不等于罪——它是"礼仪上的不洁净"（ritual impurity），不是"道德上的罪"（moral sin）。月经和梦遗不需要赎罪祭——因为它们不是罪。它们导致的不洁净是暂时的、自然结束的。

但这些规定背后有一个深层的神学原则：在利未记的世界观中，"生命"和"死亡"是最根本的分类。血是生命（利17:11"因为活物的生命是在血中"），精液是生命的种子。当这些"生命物质"从身体中流出——无论是血还是精液——它们代表了"生命力的流失"，是一种微小的"死亡"。在一个圣洁的神住在百姓中间的社区里，任何与"死亡"相关的事物都暂时地与"生命的神"不兼容。

第十五章的结尾给出了所有这些规定的终极理由：

> "你们要这样使以色列人与他们的污秽隔绝，免得他们玷污我的帐幕，就因自己的污秽死亡。"

弟兄姐妹，终极关切是帐幕——神的居所。不洁净的人如果接触帐幕会"死亡"。这不是迷信——这是"圣洁的神和不洁净的人不能同处一室"的神学原则。利未记的整个洁净体系都是为了保护百姓——使他们不会因为不小心带着不洁净进入神的同在而丧命。

弟兄姐妹，第十五章教导我们：(1)身体的每一个功能都在神的关注之下——没有什么是太"私密"以至于神不关心的；(2)不洁净不等于罪——但不洁净也是真实的、需要处理的；(3)生命力的流出提醒我们：我们是有限的、会衰败的、需要不断被更新的。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 15 addresses impurity caused by bodily discharges—Leviticus's most "private" chapter. It covers male discharges (chronic conditions and nocturnal emissions) and female menstruation and abnormal bleeding. Brothers and sisters, these topics are sensitive in any culture—but God did not avoid them. He brought every bodily function under holiness's purview—even the most intimate physiological phenomena were under His concern.

Chapter 15 categorized four situations—the first two were severe (requiring sacrifice), the latter two mild (requiring only waiting until evening):

First: male chronic discharge (vv. 2-15). "When any man has a discharge from his body, his discharge is unclean." This "discharge" (zav) likely referred to chronic urethral infection or other persistent abnormal secretion. This was a disease state—not normal physiology. The impurity was severe—his bed, everything he sat on, anyone touching him, anyone he spat on—all became unclean. Brothers and sisters, uncleanness spread outward like ripples—from body to clothing, clothing to furniture, furniture to people contacted.

After healing: seven-day purification period, eighth day two birds offered—one sin offering, one burnt offering.

Second: female chronic blood discharge (vv. 25-30). "If a woman has a discharge of blood for many days, not at the time of her menstrual impurity, or if she has a discharge beyond the time of her impurity"—also a disease state, not normal menstruation. Treatment identical to male discharge—seven-day purification plus eighth-day sacrifice.

Brothers and sisters, male and female were completely equal here—same impurity regulations, same restoration procedure, same sacrifices. Leviticus did not discriminate by gender—male and female bodily discharges were governed by the same law.

Third: male nocturnal emission (vv. 16-17). "If a man has an emission of semen, he shall bathe his whole body in water and be unclean until the evening." Minor impurity—no sacrifice needed, just bathing, cleanness restored by evening. Brothers and sisters, nocturnal emission was not sin—it was normal physiology. But even normal, involuntary physical events caused temporary impurity—because they involved "life force" (semen as life's carrier) leaving the body.

Fourth: normal menstruation (vv. 19-24). "When a woman has her regular discharge of blood...she shall be in her menstrual impurity for seven days." Likewise, menstruation was not sin—but it caused seven days' impurity.

Brothers and sisters, why did normal physiological functions cause impurity? Because Leviticus's "uncleanness" did not equal sin—it was "ritual impurity," not "moral sin." Menstruation and nocturnal emissions required no sin offerings—because they were not sins. The resulting impurity was temporary and naturally resolved.

But beneath these regulations lay a deep theological principle: in Leviticus's worldview, "life" and "death" were the most fundamental categories. Blood was life (Lev 17:11: "The life of the flesh is in the blood"), semen was life's seed. When these "life substances" flowed from the body—whether blood or semen—they represented "life force loss," a micro-"death." In a community where the holy God dwelt among the people, anything associated with "death" was temporarily incompatible with "the God of life."

Chapter 15's conclusion gave the ultimate reason for all these regulations:

> "Thus you shall keep the people of Israel separate from their uncleanness, lest they die in their uncleanness by defiling my tabernacle that is in their midst."

Brothers and sisters, the ultimate concern was the tabernacle—God's dwelling. Unclean persons contacting the tabernacle would "die." This was not superstition—it was the theological principle that "the holy God and unclean people cannot share the same space." Leviticus's entire purity system protected the people—preventing them from accidentally entering God's presence while unclean and dying.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 15 teaches us: (1) every bodily function is under God's concern—nothing is too "private" for God to care about; (2) uncleanness does not equal sin—but uncleanness is real and must be addressed; (3) life force leaving the body reminds us: we are finite, subject to decay, needing continual renewal.

## 历史背景（中文）

古代近东文化中普遍存在与身体分泌物相关的禁忌——美索不达米亚、埃及和赫人文化都有月经隔离的规定。但以色列的独特之处在于：(1)不洁净是暂时的、有明确的恢复程序；(2)男女受同样的律法管辖（而不是只限制女性）；(3)不洁净的根基是神学的（保护帐幕）而非迷信的。"血漏女人"在新约马可福音5:25-34中出现——"有一个女人，患了十二年的血漏"。按照利未记15:25的规定，她十二年来一直是不洁净的——不能进圣殿、不能触摸人。当她触摸耶稣的衣裳时，按律法她使耶稣也不洁净了——但结果恰恰相反：她被治愈了。拉比文献（米示拿Niddah篇）后来详细发展了月经不洁净的律法，比利未记本身更加严格。

## Historical Context (English)

Bodily discharge taboos were widespread in the ancient Near East—Mesopotamian, Egyptian, and Hittite cultures all had menstrual isolation regulations. But Israel's uniqueness lay in: (1) impurity was temporary with clear restoration procedures; (2) male and female were governed by the same law (not only restricting women); (3) impurity's foundation was theological (protecting the tabernacle) not superstitious. The "woman with a blood discharge" appears in Mark 5:25-34—"a woman who had had a discharge of blood for twelve years." Per Leviticus 15:25, she had been unclean for twelve years—unable to enter the temple, unable to touch people. When she touched Jesus' garment, by law she made Jesus unclean—but the opposite happened: she was healed. Rabbinic literature (Mishnah Niddah) later extensively developed menstrual impurity law, becoming stricter than Leviticus itself.

## 经文结构（中文）

**男性慢性漏症（2-15节）**——不洁净扩散到床座衣物人、骑过的鞍不洁净、触摸他的人洗衣洗身不洁净到晚上、痊愈后七天+第八天两只鸟赎罪。

**男性梦遗（16-17节）**——不洁净到晚上、洗全身、衣服皮子水洗。

**男女同房（18节）**——两人不洁净到晚上、水洗。

**女性正常月经（19-24节）**——污秽七天、床座不洁、摸她的人不洁到晚上、同房者不洁七天。

**女性慢性血漏（25-30节）**——经期外或过长、如月经不洁一样、痊愈后七天+第八天两只鸟赎罪。

**总结警告（31-33节）**——使以色列人与污秽隔绝、免得玷污帐幕死亡。

## Structure (English)

**Male Chronic Discharge (vv. 2-15)**—Impurity spreads to bed seat clothing people, saddle unclean, touching him wash clothes bathe unclean until evening, healed then seven days + eighth day two birds atonement.

**Male Nocturnal Emission (vv. 16-17)**—Unclean until evening, bathe whole body, clothing leather washed.

**Sexual Intercourse (v. 18)**—Both unclean until evening, bathe.

**Normal Menstruation (vv. 19-24)**—Impure seven days, bed seat unclean, touching her unclean until evening, intercourse during period unclean seven days.

**Female Chronic Blood Discharge (vv. 25-30)**—Outside period or prolonged, same as menstrual impurity, healed then seven days + eighth day two birds atonement.

**Summary Warning (vv. 31-33)**—Keep Israel separate from uncleanness, lest they defile tabernacle and die.

## 神学要点（中文）

**不洁净不等于罪——仪式与道德的区分**
月经和梦遗导致不洁净但不需要赎罪祭——改革宗传统区分"仪式律"和"道德律"。韦敏斯德信条第19章论神的律法：仪式律"在新约时已被废除"，但道德律"永远有效"。利未记15章的洁净规定属于仪式律——在基督里已被成全和废除。但它们所指向的原则（圣洁、分别、对生命的尊重）是永恒的。

**生命力的流出——有限性的提醒**
血和精液的流出代表生命力的暂时丧失——改革宗传统教导人的有限性。韦敏斯德小要理问答第17问论堕落的后果："始祖堕落将世人陷入罪恶和苦境中。"身体的衰退和排泄是堕落后人类有限性的日常提醒。

**"免得玷污帐幕死亡"——圣洁不可轻慢**
不洁者接近帐幕会死——改革宗传统强调神圣洁的严肃性。希伯来书10:31"落在永生神的手里，真是可怕的！"恩典不意味着可以轻慢圣洁——相反，恩典使我们更加尊重圣洁。

## Theology (English)

**Uncleanness Does Not Equal Sin—Ritual vs. Moral Distinction**
Menstruation and emissions caused impurity but required no sin offering—the Reformed tradition distinguishes "ceremonial law" from "moral law." Westminster Confession chapter 19 on God's law: ceremonial laws "are now abrogated under the New Testament," but moral law "forever binds." Leviticus 15's purity regulations were ceremonial—fulfilled and abolished in Christ. But the principles they pointed to (holiness, separation, respect for life) are eternal.

**Life Force Leaving—Reminder of Finitude**
Blood and semen flowing out represented temporary life force loss—the Reformed tradition teaches human finitude. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q17 on the fall's consequences: "All mankind...fell into...the miseries of this life...and everlasting death." Bodily decay and discharge are daily reminders of post-fall human limitation.

**"Lest They Defile the Tabernacle and Die"—Holiness Not to Be Trifled With**
Unclean persons approaching the tabernacle would die—the Reformed tradition emphasizes holiness's seriousness. Hebrews 10:31: "It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God!" Grace does not mean holiness can be trifled with—rather, grace makes us respect holiness more.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第十五章的不洁净条例在新约中因基督的到来而被根本性地超越。

血漏女人（可5:25-34）——十二年的血漏意味着十二年的不洁净、十二年的隔离、十二年不能进圣殿。按照利未记15章，她触摸任何人都会使对方不洁净。当她触摸耶稣的衣裳——按律法耶稣应该不洁净了。但发生了恰恰相反的事："她身上的血源立刻干了。"不是她的不洁净传给了基督——是基督的洁净传给了她。

弟兄姐妹，这就是福音的逻辑——在旧约中，不洁净是会传染的（摸了不洁净的东西你也不洁净）；但在基督里，洁净是会"传染"的（基督的圣洁胜过了我们的不洁净）。利未记的体系是"不洁净感染洁净"——基督翻转了这个方向："洁净治愈不洁净"。

"免得他们玷污我的帐幕"——在基督里，我们的身体就是圣灵的殿。哥林多前书6:19"岂不知你们的身子就是圣灵的殿么？"利未记15章保护的是石头和布幔做的帐幕——新约保护的是活的帐幕（信徒的身体）。但保护的原则相同："你们的身子就是圣灵的殿……要在你们的身子上荣耀神"（林前6:19-20）。

第八天的两只鸟——与第十四章的洁净礼呼应——再次指向基督的死和复活。一只死了（赎罪），一只活着（新生命）。在基督里，一切的不洁净——无论是疾病的、仪式的、道德的——都被一次性地彻底洁净。希伯来书9:14"何况基督藉着永远的灵，将自己无瑕无疵献给神，他的血岂不更能洗净你们的心，除去你们的死行，使你们事奉那永生神么？"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 15's impurity regulations were fundamentally transcended by Christ's coming in the New Testament.

The woman with blood discharge (Mark 5:25-34)—twelve years of bleeding meant twelve years of impurity, twelve years of isolation, twelve years barred from the temple. Per Leviticus 15, her touching anyone made them unclean. When she touched Jesus' garment—by law Jesus should have become unclean. But the opposite occurred: "Immediately the flow of blood dried up." Her uncleanness was not transmitted to Christ—Christ's cleanness was transmitted to her.

Brothers and sisters, this is the gospel's logic—in the Old Testament, uncleanness was contagious (touching unclean things made you unclean); but in Christ, cleanness is "contagious" (Christ's holiness overcomes our uncleanness). Leviticus's system operated on "uncleanness infects cleanness"—Christ reversed this: "cleanness heals uncleanness."

"Lest they defile my tabernacle"—in Christ, our bodies are the Spirit's temple. 1 Corinthians 6:19: "Do you not know that your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit?" Leviticus 15 protected a tabernacle of stone and curtains—the New Testament protects living tabernacles (believers' bodies). But the protective principle remained: "What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you, which ye have of God, and ye are not your own? For ye are bought with a price: therefore glorify God in your body, and in your spirit, which are God’s." (1 Cor 6:19-20).

The eighth day's two birds—echoing chapter 14's purification—again pointed to Christ's death and resurrection. One died (atonement), one lived (new life). In Christ, all impurity—whether from disease, ritual, or moral—is thoroughly cleansed once for all. Hebrews 9:14: "How much more will the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without blemish to God, purify our conscience from dead works to serve the living God?"
