## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第四章介绍的是赎罪祭（chattat，来自chata'，意思是"错过目标"）——这是五大祭中最严肃的祭之一。燔祭和平安祭是自愿的，赎罪祭是必须的——当你犯了罪，你必须献赎罪祭，这不是可选的。

> "若有人在耶和华所吩咐不可行的甚么事上误犯了一件。"

弟兄姐妹，注意"误犯"——赎罪祭处理的是无心之过（sin of inadvertence），不是故意的反叛。你不知道某件事是罪、或者你知道但一时失控犯了——这些"误犯"需要赎罪祭。民数记15:30-31说"若有人擅敢行事"（故意犯罪），那人"要从民中剪除"——故意的罪在旧约祭祀制度中没有对应的祭可以赎。弟兄姐妹，这令人战兢——故意犯罪在旧约中几乎无药可救。希伯来书10:26后来说"我们得知真道以后，若故意犯罪，赎罪的祭就再没有了。"

赎罪祭最独特的特征是——它按照犯罪者的身份分为四个等级：

第一等级：受膏的祭司犯罪——"使百姓陷在罪里"。大祭司犯罪的后果最严重——因为他是中保，他的罪连累全体百姓。所需的赎罪祭也最昂贵——没有残疾的公牛犊。血的处理也最复杂：血被带进圣所，在幔子前弹血七次，抹在香坛四角上，然后倒在燔祭坛脚。公牛的身体被搬到营外焚烧。

弟兄姐妹，大祭司的赎罪祭中，血被带进圣所——直到幔子前。这是唯一一种将血带入圣所内部的赎罪祭（普通人的血只洒在外面的燔祭坛上）。罪的严重程度决定了血需要被带到多深的地方——大祭司的罪太严重，血必须进到最靠近至圣所的地方。

第二等级：全会众犯罪——"以色列全会众若行了耶和华所吩咐不可行的甚么事，误犯了罪，是隐而未现"。集体的罪、不知不觉犯的罪——一个社会可以集体地、系统性地犯罪而毫不察觉。长老们代表全体百姓按手在公牛头上——程序与大祭司的赎罪祭完全相同。

第三等级：官长犯罪——献一只公山羊。血不进入圣所——只抹在外面燔祭坛的四角上。

第四等级：平民犯罪——献一只母山羊或母绵羊羔。血同样只在外面的坛上处理。

弟兄姐妹，四个等级揭示了一个残酷的现实：地位越高的人，犯罪的后果越严重。大祭司犯罪需要一头公牛——平民只需要一只母山羊。为什么？因为大祭司的罪影响了全体百姓——他是代表，他的失败就是全体的失败。责任越大，罪的代价越大。"多给谁，就向谁多取"（路12:48）——这是圣经一贯的原则。

弟兄姐妹，让我指出赎罪祭中几个令人深思的细节：

"弹血七次"——七是完全的数字。弹血七次意味着赎罪是完全的——不是局部的修补，而是彻底的清洗。

> "公牛搬到营外烧了"

赎罪祭的身体不在坛上烧（坛是馨香的地方），而是在营外烧（不洁之地）。希伯来书13:11-12直接应用于基督："所以，耶稣要用自己的血叫百姓成圣，也就在城门外受苦。"

"他必蒙赦免"——这句话在第四章中反复出现（vv.20, 26, 31, 35）。每一次赎罪祭之后，经文都确认：他必蒙赦免。弟兄姐妹，赦免不是模糊的期望——它是确定的应许。你照着神的方式献了赎罪祭，赦免就是确定的。

第四章的赎罪祭体系揭示了罪的严重性和赎罪的必要性——但它也揭示了旧约体系的局限性。赎罪祭只能处理"误犯"——故意的罪怎么办？动物的血真的能替代人的罪吗？希伯来书10:4给出了答案："因为公牛和山羊的血，断不能除罪。"旧约的赎罪祭是影子——它指向那真正能除罪的、那位在城门外受苦的。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 4 introduces the sin offering (chattat, from chata', meaning "to miss the mark")—one of the five offerings' most solemn. The burnt and peace offerings were voluntary; the sin offering was mandatory—when you sinned, you had to bring a sin offering. This was not optional.

> "If anyone sins unintentionally in any of the LORD's commandments about things not to be done."

Brothers and sisters, note "unintentionally"—the sin offering addressed inadvertent sins, not deliberate rebellion. You didn't know something was sin, or you knew but momentarily lost control—these "unintentional sins" required a sin offering. Numbers 15:30-31 says one who acts "with a high hand" (deliberately sinning) "shall be cut off"—deliberate sin had no corresponding sacrifice in the Old Testament system. Brothers and sisters, this is terrifying—deliberate sin in the Old Testament was virtually irredeemable. Hebrews 10:26 later says: "If we go on sinning deliberately after receiving the knowledge of the truth, there no longer remains a sacrifice for sins."

The sin offering's most distinctive feature—it was divided into four tiers based on the sinner's identity:

First tier: the anointed priest sins—"bringing guilt on the people." The high priest's sin had the gravest consequences—as mediator, his sin implicated all the people. The required sin offering was also the most costly—an unblemished young bull. Blood handling was also most complex: blood was brought inside the Holy Place, sprinkled seven times before the veil, applied to the incense altar's four horns, then poured at the burnt offering altar's base. The bull's body was carried outside the camp and burned.

Brothers and sisters, in the priest's sin offering, blood was brought inside the Holy Place—right to the veil. This was the only sin offering whose blood entered the Holy Place's interior (ordinary people's blood was only sprinkled on the outer altar). Sin's severity determined how deep the blood needed to go—the high priest's sin was so grave that blood had to reach the place nearest the Most Holy.

Second tier: the whole congregation sins—"If the whole congregation of Israel sins unintentionally...the sin is hidden from them." Collective sin, unconscious sin—a society can sin collectively and systemically without realizing it. Elders representing the people laid hands on the bull—the procedure was identical to the priest's sin offering.

Third tier: a leader sins—offering a male goat. Blood did not enter the Holy Place—only applied to the outer altar's four horns.

Fourth tier: a common person sins—offering a female goat or female lamb. Blood was similarly handled only at the outer altar.

Brothers and sisters, four tiers revealed a sobering reality: the higher one's position, the graver one's sin. The high priest's sin required a bull—a commoner needed only a female goat. Why? Because the high priest's sin affected all the people—as representative, his failure was collective failure. Greater responsibility meant greater sin's cost. "But he that knew not, and did commit things worthy of stripes, shall be beaten with few stripes. For unto whomsoever much is given, of him shall be much required: and to whom men have committed much, of him they will ask the more." (Luke 12:48)—Scripture's consistent principle.

Brothers and sisters, let me highlight several thought-provoking details in the sin offering:

"Sprinkle the blood seven times"—seven is the number of completeness. Seven sprinklings meant atonement was complete—not partial repair but thorough cleansing.

> "The bull carried outside the camp and burned"—the sin offering's body was not burned on the altar (the place of pleasing aroma) but outside the camp (the place of uncleanness). Hebrews 13:11-12 directly applies this to Christ: "So Jesus also suffered outside the gate in order to sanctify the people through his own blood."

"He shall be forgiven"—this phrase repeats throughout chapter 4 (vv. 20, 26, 31, 35). After every sin offering, Scripture confirmed: he shall be forgiven. Brothers and sisters, forgiveness was not a vague hope—it was a definite promise. If you brought the sin offering according to God's way, forgiveness was certain.

Chapter 4's sin offering system revealed sin's gravity and atonement's necessity—but it also revealed the Old Testament system's limitation. The sin offering handled only "unintentional" sins—what about deliberate sins? Could animal blood truly substitute for human sin? Hebrews 10:4 answers: "For it is impossible for the blood of bulls and goats to take away sins." The Old Testament sin offering was a shadow—pointing to the One who truly removes sin, the One who suffered outside the gate.

## 历史背景（中文）

赎罪祭（chattat）的分级制度在古代近东法律中有间接平行——汉谟拉比法典中，伤害高阶层人士的惩罚比伤害低阶层的更重。但以色列的赎罪祭独特之处在于：不仅受害者的地位重要，犯罪者的地位也影响赎罪的代价——大祭司犯罪比平民犯罪需要更昂贵的赎罪祭。"弹血七次"——七在古代近东文化中普遍代表完全和神圣——乌加里特文献中也有七次洒血的仪式。"搬到营外烧"——"营外"在以色列传统中是不洁的地方：麻风病人被隔离在营外（民5:2-3），污秽之物被带到营外处理。赎罪祭的牲畜承担了罪的不洁——因此必须在不洁之地被销毁。利未记4:3的"使百姓陷在罪里"——拉比传统解释为：大祭司如果在献祭中犯了仪式性的错误，整个赎罪就无效——因此全民都因他的过失而"陷在罪里"。

## Historical Context (English)

The sin offering's (chattat) tiered system had indirect parallels in ancient Near Eastern law—the Code of Hammurabi imposed heavier penalties for injuring higher-status persons than lower-status ones. But Israel's sin offering was unique: not only the victim's status mattered, the offender's status also determined atonement's cost—the high priest sinning required a more costly offering than a commoner sinning. "Sprinkle blood seven times"—seven universally represented completeness and sacredness in ancient Near Eastern culture—Ugaritic texts also contain seven-fold blood-sprinkling rituals. "Carried outside the camp to burn"—"outside the camp" in Israelite tradition was the unclean place: lepers were isolated there (Numbers 5:2-3), impure things were disposed of there. The sin offering animal bore sin's uncleanness—thus it had to be destroyed in the unclean place. Leviticus 4:3's "bringing guilt on the people"—rabbinic tradition explains: if the high priest made a ritual error during sacrifice, the entire atonement was invalid—thus all the people were "brought into guilt" through his mistake.

## 经文结构（中文）

**赎罪祭的总则（1-2节）**——耶和华对摩西说、若有人误犯了一件。

**大祭司的赎罪祭（3-12节）**——公牛犊、按手宰杀、血带入圣所弹七次、抹香坛四角、倒燔祭坛脚、脂油烧坛上、公牛身体搬营外烧。

**全会众的赎罪祭（13-21节）**——隐而未现的集体罪、公牛犊、长老按手、程序同大祭司赎罪祭、搬营外烧、"他们必蒙赦免"。

**官长的赎罪祭（22-26节）**——公山羊、按手宰杀、血抹燔祭坛四角倒坛脚、脂油烧坛上、"他必蒙赦免"。

**平民的赎罪祭（27-35节）**——母山羊或母绵羊羔、按手宰杀、血抹坛角倒坛脚、脂油烧坛上、"他必蒙赦免"。

## Structure (English)

**Sin Offering General Principle (vv. 1-2)**—The LORD to Moses, if anyone sins unintentionally.

**High Priest's Sin Offering (vv. 3-12)**—Young bull, laying hands and slaughter, blood brought into Holy Place sprinkled seven times, applied to incense altar horns, poured at burnt altar base, fat burned on altar, bull's body carried outside camp and burned.

**Whole Congregation's Sin Offering (vv. 13-21)**—Hidden collective sin, young bull, elders laying hands, procedure same as high priest's, carried outside camp, "they shall be forgiven."

**Leader's Sin Offering (vv. 22-26)**—Male goat, laying hands and slaughter, blood on burnt altar horns poured at base, fat burned on altar, "he shall be forgiven."

**Common Person's Sin Offering (vv. 27-35)**—Female goat or female lamb, laying hands and slaughter, blood on altar horns poured at base, fat burned on altar, "he shall be forgiven."

## 神学要点（中文）

**罪的分级——责任决定后果**
大祭司犯罪需要公牛，平民只需母山羊——改革宗传统教导"牧者和教师承担更大的责任"。雅各书3:1"我的弟兄们，不要多人作师傅，因为晓得我们要受更重的判断。"教会领袖的罪比普通信徒的罪有更严重的后果——因为他们的影响面更大。韦敏斯德大要理问答第151问在论罪的加重因素时提到冒犯者的身份、地位和职分——同样的罪，位高者犯的比位低者犯的更严重。

**误犯的罪也是罪——罪的客观性**
"误犯了罪"——即使不知道，罪仍然存在。改革宗传统强调罪的客观性：罪不取决于你是否知道它是罪——取决于神的标准。韦敏斯德小要理问答第14问"罪是什么？"答"罪就是不遵从或违背神的律法。"不知道偷东西是罪，偷了仍然是罪。无知不能免罪——但神的恩典提供了赎罪的途径。

**"他必蒙赦免"——赦免的确定性**
四次"他必蒙赦免"——改革宗传统强调赦免的确定性。韦敏斯德信条第十一章论称义教导：神白白称信徒为义，不是将义注入他们里面，而是把基督的顺服和所成就的义归算给他们。当赎罪祭被正确献上，赦免就是确定的——不是"可能"或"希望"，而是"必蒙"。

## Theology (English)

**Graded Sin—Responsibility Determines Consequence**
The high priest sinning required a bull; a commoner needed only a female goat—the Reformed tradition teaches "pastors and teachers bear greater responsibility." James 3:1: "Not many of you should become teachers, my brothers, for you know that we who teach will be judged with greater strictness." Church leaders' sin has graver consequences than ordinary believers'—because their influence is wider. Westminster Larger Catechism Q151 on sin's aggravating factors mentions the offender's qualities, position, and office—the same sin committed by those in higher position is more grave.

**Unintentional Sin Is Still Sin—Sin's Objectivity**
"Sins unintentionally"—even unknowingly, sin still existed. Reformed tradition emphasizes sin's objectivity: sin does not depend on whether you know it is sin—it depends on God's standard. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q14: "What is sin?" "Sin is any want of conformity unto, or transgression of, the law of God." Not knowing stealing is sin does not make stealing not sin. Ignorance does not exempt—but God's grace provides a way of atonement.

**"He Shall Be Forgiven"—Forgiveness's Certainty**
Four times "he shall be forgiven"—the Reformed tradition emphasizes forgiveness's certainty. Westminster Confession chapter 11 on justification says believers "are justified...not for anything wrought in them" but "Christ's righteousness imputed to them." When the sin offering was correctly brought, forgiveness was certain—not "maybe" or "hopefully" but "shall be."

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第四章的赎罪祭是对基督十字架最直接的旧约预表。

"营外烧了"——希伯来书13:11-13："原来牲畜的血被大祭司带入圣所作赎罪祭，牲畜的身子被烧在营外。所以，耶稣要用自己的血叫百姓成圣，也就在城门外受苦。这样，我们也当出到营外，就了他去，忍受他所受的凌辱。"基督被钉在耶路撒冷城外（各各他）——正如赎罪祭被烧在营外。祂承担了罪的不洁，被带到不洁之地。

四个等级的赎罪祭都不能赎故意的罪——但基督一次献祭，连故意的罪都赎了。希伯来书9:12"不是用山羊和牛犊的血，乃是用自己的血，只一次进入圣所，成了永远赎罪的事。"旧约的赎罪祭有限制（只赎误犯）——基督的赎罪祭没有限制（一次赎了一切的罪）。

"弹血七次"——基督的血完全洁净了所有的罪。约翰一书1:7"他儿子耶稣的血也洗净我们一切的罪。""一切"——不是一些，不是大部分，而是一切。七次弹血的完全性在基督里得到了终极的实现。

"他必蒙赦免"——罗马书8:1"如今，那些在基督耶稣里的就不定罪了。"在旧约中，赦免是每一次献赎罪祭后确认的；在基督里，赦免是一次性、永远有效的——"不再定罪了"。

大祭司的血被带进圣所——基督的血被带进天上的至圣所。希伯来书9:24"基督并不是进了人手所造的圣所（这不过是真圣所的影像），乃是进了天堂，如今为我们显在神面前。"旧约的大祭司把血带进地上的圣所——基督把祂自己的血带进了天上的圣所，一次永远。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 4's sin offering is the most direct Old Testament prefiguration of Christ's cross.

"Burned outside the camp"—Hebrews 13:11-13: "For the bodies of those animals whose blood is brought into the holy places by the high priest as a sacrifice for sin are burned outside the camp. So Jesus also suffered outside the gate in order to sanctify the people through his own blood. Therefore let us go to him outside the camp and bear the reproach he endured." Christ was crucified outside Jerusalem (Golgotha)—just as the sin offering was burned outside the camp. He bore sin's uncleanness, taken to the unclean place.

All four tiers could not atone for deliberate sin—but Christ's single offering atoned even for deliberate sins. Hebrews 9:12: "He entered once for all into the holy places, not by means of the blood of goats and calves but by means of his own blood, thus securing an eternal redemption." The Old Testament sin offering had limitations (only inadvertent sins)—Christ's sin offering had none (once for all sins).

"Sprinkle blood seven times"—Christ's blood completely cleanses all sin. 1 John 1:7: "The blood of Jesus his Son cleanses us from all sin." "All"—not some, not most, but all. The seven sprinklings' completeness found ultimate realization in Christ.

"He shall be forgiven"—Romans 8:1: "There is therefore now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus." In the Old Testament, forgiveness was confirmed after each sin offering; in Christ, forgiveness is once-for-all, eternally effective—"no condemnation."

The high priest's blood was brought inside the Holy Place—Christ's blood was brought into heaven's Most Holy Place. Hebrews 9:24: "For Christ has entered, not into holy places made with hands, which are copies of the true things, but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God on our behalf." The Old Testament high priest brought blood into the earthly sanctuary—Christ brought His own blood into the heavenly sanctuary, once and forever.
