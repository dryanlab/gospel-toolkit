## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十章是利未记中最令人震惊的一章——紧接着第九章天火降下的欢呼和荣耀，两个新就职的祭司在同一天被神的火烧死。从极大的喜乐到极大的悲剧，只隔了几节经文。

> "亚伦的儿子拿答、亚比户各拿自己的香炉，盛上火，加上香，在耶和华面前献上凡火，是耶和华没有吩咐他们的。就有火从耶和华面前出来，把他们烧灭，他们就死在耶和华面前。"

弟兄姐妹，同样的"火从耶和华面前出来"——在第九章烧尽了祭物，百姓欢呼；在第十章烧灭了拿答和亚比户，全民震惊。同一把火——悦纳的火也是审判的火。希伯来书12:29"因为我们的神乃是烈火。"

他们的罪是什么？"献上凡火，是耶和华没有吩咐他们的。""凡火"（esh zarah，字面意思是"陌生的火"或"外来的火"）——不是从坛上取的火、不是按规定的方式献的、不是在规定的时间献的。经文没有详细说明他们具体做错了什么——但关键短语是"耶和华没有吩咐他们的"。弟兄姐妹，这正是第八章反复强调的"照耶和华所吩咐摩西的"的反面。

第八章七次说"照耶和华所吩咐的"——一切照规矩来，荣光显现，火降悦纳。
第十章说"耶和华没有吩咐他们的"——自作主张，火降审判，两人烧死。

弟兄姐妹，拿答和亚比户不是外邦人——他们是亚伦的长子和次子，刚刚被设立为祭司，刚刚经历了第八章的洁净、膏抹和七天的分别为圣。他们不是不知道规矩——他们选择不遵守。他们以为自己可以"创新"——用自己的方式来到神面前。但神的回应是即刻的死亡。

> "于是摩西对亚伦说：这就是耶和华所说：我在亲近我的人中要显为圣；在众民面前，我要得荣耀。亚伦就默默不言。"

弟兄姐妹，"亚伦就默默不言"——这五个字是整本圣经中最沉重的沉默之一。亚伦失去了两个儿子——在同一天、在同一个地方、在他自己的面前。但他不能哀哭——因为神说的是对的。他不能争辩——因为拿答和亚比户确实犯了罪。他的沉默不是冷漠——是在极大的痛苦中对神公义的顺服。

接下来的命令更加令人心碎：

> "不可蓬头散发，也不可撕裂衣裳，免得你们死亡。"

蓬头散发和撕裂衣裳是以色列人哀悼的方式——但亚伦和他剩下的儿子被禁止哀悼。为什么？因为他们仍然是正在任职的祭司——他们的职分高于他们的悲伤。他们必须在圣所继续服事——不能离开。"你们也不可出会幕的门，恐怕你们死亡。"

弟兄姐妹，想象一下亚伦的处境——两个儿子的尸体被抬出去，他看着，但不能哭，不能撕衣服，不能离开会幕，还要继续献祭、继续服事。这是何等残酷的考验——但这也是何等崇高的职分。祭司的使命超越了个人的悲欢——你为整个民族站在神面前，你不能因为个人的痛苦而停止服事。

然后是一个意味深长的命令（8-11节）：

> "你和你儿子进会幕的时候，清酒、浓酒都不可喝，免得你们死亡。"

弟兄姐妹，为什么在拿答和亚比户事件之后立刻插入禁酒令？很多解经家由此推测拿答和亚比户可能是在醉酒状态下献了凡火——酒精使他们失去了判断力，做出了不该做的事。无论这个推测是否正确，禁酒令的目的很清楚："使你们可以将圣的、俗的，洁净的、不洁净的，分别出来。"酒精模糊了界限——而祭司的工作恰恰是分辨界限。

第十章的最后一幕（16-20节）是一个出人意料的转折——摩西发现赎罪祭的公山羊被烧了而不是被祭司吃掉（按照利未记6:26的规定，赎罪祭的肉应该由祭司在圣处吃）。摩西向以利亚撒和以他玛发怒。

亚伦为他的儿子们辩护——这是他在两个儿子死后第一次开口："今天他们在耶和华面前献上赎罪祭和燔祭，我又遇见这样的灾，若今天吃了赎罪祭，耶和华岂能看为美呢？"

弟兄姐妹，亚伦的意思是：我今天经历了如此大的灾难——在这种悲伤和惊恐中，我的儿子们怎么可能像平常一样吃赎罪祭的肉呢？那不是形式上的不顺服——那是心灵深处的敬畏：在如此沉重的审判之后，我们不配享用圣物。

> "摩西听见这话，便以为美。"

弟兄姐妹，摩西同意了。亚伦的判断是对的——有时候，形式上的完美遵守不如内心真实的敬畏。规则说"吃"——但敬畏说"在今天这样的日子，我不配吃"。神接受了亚伦的敬畏，胜过对条文的机械遵守。这是律法中的恩典——也是利未记中少有的温暖时刻。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 10 is Leviticus's most shocking chapter—immediately following chapter 9's heavenly fire, shouting, and glory, two newly ordained priests were killed by God's fire on the same day. From greatest joy to greatest tragedy, only a few verses apart.

> "Now Nadab and Abihu, the sons of Aaron, each took his censer and put fire in it and laid incense on it and offered unauthorized fire before the LORD, which he had not commanded them. And fire came out from before the LORD and consumed them, and they died before the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, the same "fire came out from before the LORD"—in chapter 9 it consumed offerings and the people shouted; in chapter 10 it consumed Nadab and Abihu and the nation was stunned. The same fire—the fire of acceptance was also the fire of judgment. Hebrews 12:29: "For our God is a consuming fire."

What was their sin? "Offered unauthorized fire before the LORD, which he had not commanded them." "Unauthorized fire" (esh zarah, literally "strange fire" or "foreign fire")—not taken from the altar, not offered in the prescribed manner, not at the prescribed time. Scripture does not detail exactly what they did wrong—but the key phrase is "which he had not commanded them." Brothers and sisters, this was precisely the opposite of chapter 8's repeated "as the LORD commanded Moses."

Chapter 8 said "as the LORD commanded" seven times—everything by the rules, glory appeared, fire fell in acceptance.
Chapter 10 says "which he had not commanded them"—acting on their own initiative, fire fell in judgment, two men burned to death.

Brothers and sisters, Nadab and Abihu were not foreigners—they were Aaron's eldest and second sons, just ordained as priests, having just experienced chapter 8's cleansing, anointing, and seven days of consecration. They did not lack knowledge of the rules—they chose not to follow them. They thought they could "innovate"—approach God in their own way. But God's response was instant death.

> "Then Moses said to Aaron, 'This is what the LORD has said: Among those who are near me I will be sanctified, and before all the people I will be glorified.' And Aaron held his peace."

Brothers and sisters, "Aaron held his peace"—these words form one of Scripture's heaviest silences. Aaron lost two sons—on the same day, in the same place, before his own eyes. But he could not weep—because what God said was right. He could not argue—because Nadab and Abihu had indeed sinned. His silence was not indifference—it was submission to God's justice amid agonizing pain.

The next commands were even more heartbreaking:

"Do not let the hair of your heads hang loose, and do not tear your garments, lest you die." Disheveled hair and torn garments were Israelite mourning customs—but Aaron and his remaining sons were forbidden to mourn. Why? Because they were still serving priests—their office superseded their grief. They must continue serving in the sanctuary—they could not leave. "Do not go outside the entrance of the tent of meeting, lest you die."

Brothers and sisters, imagine Aaron's situation—his two sons' bodies carried out, watching, but unable to weep, unable to tear his clothes, unable to leave the tabernacle, still having to sacrifice, still having to serve. What a cruel test—yet what an exalted office. The priest's mission transcended personal sorrow—you stood before God for the entire nation; you could not stop serving because of personal pain.

Then a suggestive command (vv. 8-11):

> "Drink no wine or strong drink, you or your sons with you, when you go into the tent of meeting, lest you die."

Brothers and sisters, why insert a prohibition against alcohol immediately after the Nadab and Abihu incident? Many commentators infer Nadab and Abihu may have offered unauthorized fire while intoxicated—alcohol impaired their judgment, leading them to do what they should not. Whether this inference is correct, the prohibition's purpose is clear: "so that you may distinguish between the holy and the common, and between the unclean and the clean." Alcohol blurs boundaries—and the priest's work was precisely discerning boundaries.

Chapter 10's final scene (vv. 16-20) provides an unexpected turn—Moses discovered the sin offering goat had been burned rather than eaten by the priests (per Leviticus 6:26, sin offering meat should be eaten by priests in a holy place). Moses was angry with Eleazar and Ithamar.

Aaron defended his sons—his first words after his two sons' deaths: "Behold, today they have offered their sin offering and their burnt offering before the LORD, and yet such things as these have happened to me! If I had eaten the sin offering today, would the LORD have approved?"

Brothers and sisters, Aaron's meaning: I experienced such catastrophe today—in such grief and terror, how could my sons eat the sin offering meat as if everything were normal? This was not formal disobedience—it was deep reverence: after such heavy judgment, we are unworthy to partake of holy things.

> "And when Moses heard that, he approved."

Brothers and sisters, Moses agreed. Aaron's judgment was correct—sometimes heartfelt reverence surpasses formal compliance. The rule said "eat"—but reverence said "on a day like this, I am unworthy to eat." God accepted Aaron's reverence over mechanical obedience to regulations. This was grace within the law—and one of Leviticus's rare moments of warmth.

## 历史背景（中文）

"凡火"（esh zarah）的确切性质是犹太解经史上争论最多的问题之一。拉比传统提出多种可能：(1)他们使用了不是从坛上取的火；(2)他们在不该献香的时间献了香；(3)他们在醉酒状态下进入圣所；(4)他们没有穿正确的圣衣；(5)他们试图在至圣所献香（不是普通的香坛）。最早的拉比文献《西弗拉》（Sifra，约3世纪）记载拉比以实玛利认为他们是因为醉酒——紧接着的禁酒令支持了这个解释。"亚伦默默不言"——塔古姆翻译（Targum Onkelos）将此翻译为"亚伦被安慰了"，暗示他从摩西的神学解释中得到了某种安慰。拿答和亚比户在出埃及记24:1,9中曾与摩西和亚伦一同上西乃山见过神——他们的地位极高，也许因此而骄傲自大。

## Historical Context (English)

The exact nature of "unauthorized fire" (esh zarah) is one of the most debated questions in Jewish exegetical history. Rabbinic tradition proposes multiple possibilities: (1) they used fire not taken from the altar; (2) they offered incense at an unauthorized time; (3) they entered the sanctuary while intoxicated; (4) they wore incorrect vestments; (5) they attempted to offer incense in the Most Holy Place (not the regular incense altar). The earliest rabbinic text Sifra (c. 3rd century) records Rabbi Ishmael's view that they were intoxicated—the immediately following alcohol prohibition supports this interpretation. "Aaron held his peace"—the Targum Onkelos translates this as "Aaron was comforted," suggesting he found some comfort in Moses's theological explanation. Nadab and Abihu had ascended Sinai with Moses and Aaron in Exodus 24:1,9 to see God—their exalted status may have fostered pride.

## 经文结构（中文）

**凡火与即刻审判（1-3节）**——拿答亚比户各拿香炉献凡火（耶和华没有吩咐的）、火从耶和华出来烧灭他们、摩西引用神的话"亲近我的人中显为圣"、亚伦默默不言。

**善后处理（4-7节）**——米沙利以利撒反抬尸体到营外、禁止亚伦和剩余儿子蓬头散发撕衣、不可出会幕门"恐怕你们死亡"、全家为耶和华的火哀哭。

**禁酒令与分辨圣俗（8-11节）**——进会幕不可喝酒"免得死亡"、要分别圣的俗的洁净的不洁净的、教训以色列人一切律例。

**赎罪祭肉的争议（12-20节）**——摩西吩咐吃素祭和平安祭的份、发现赎罪祭被烧而非被吃、摩西发怒、亚伦辩护"遇见这样的灾岂能吃赎罪祭"、摩西以为美。

## Structure (English)

**Unauthorized Fire and Instant Judgment (vv. 1-3)**—Nadab Abihu each take censers offer unauthorized fire (not commanded), fire from LORD consumes them, Moses quotes God "among those near me I will be sanctified," Aaron holds his peace.

**Aftermath (vv. 4-7)**—Mishael Elzaphan carry bodies outside camp, Aaron and remaining sons forbidden to dishevel hair or tear clothes, may not leave tent entrance "lest you die," whole house mourns the LORD's fire.

**Alcohol Prohibition and Discerning Holy/Common (vv. 8-11)**—No wine or strong drink when entering tent "lest you die," distinguish holy from common clean from unclean, teach Israel all statutes.

**Sin Offering Meat Dispute (vv. 12-20)**—Moses commands eating grain and peace offering portions, discovers sin offering burned not eaten, Moses angry, Aaron defends "after such disaster should I eat sin offering," Moses approves.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"耶和华没有吩咐的"——限定性敬拜原则的反面教材**
拿答和亚比户的凡火是改革宗限定性敬拜原则最严厉的反面教材。韦敏斯德信条第二十一章教导：不可按照人的想象和捏造来敬拜神。他们的"创新"不被视为虔诚的热情——而是对神主权的侵犯。加尔文在注释此段时说："神要被照祂自己所命定的方式来敬拜——不是照我们的幻想。"

**亚伦的沉默——在苦难中顺服神的公义**
"亚伦默默不言"——改革宗传统将此视为信心的典范之一。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理说：在各种忧愁、试炼、管教中……顺服祂的旨意。约伯记1:21"赏赐的是耶和华，收取的也是耶和华。耶和华的名是应当称颂的。"亚伦的沉默不是冷漠——是在极大的痛苦中对神主权的俯伏。

**规则与敬畏——律法中的恩典**
摩西接受亚伦不吃赎罪祭的理由——改革宗传统教导律法的目的不是死板的字面主义，而是心灵的真实回应。基督在马太福音12:7引用何西阿书说"我喜爱怜恤，不喜爱祭祀"——神看重的是心中的敬畏，胜过外在的形式完美。

## Theology (English)

**"Which He Had Not Commanded"—The Regulative Principle's Negative Case Study**
Nadab and Abihu's unauthorized fire is the Reformed Regulative Principle's most severe negative example. Westminster Confession chapter 21: God may not be worshiped according to the imaginations and devices of men. Their "innovation" was not viewed as pious zeal—but as violation of God's sovereignty. Calvin commented: "God desires to be worshiped as He Himself has ordained—not according to our fantasies."

**Aaron's Silence—Submitting to God's Justice in Suffering**
"Aaron held his peace"—the Reformed tradition views this as an exemplary act of faith. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence: In various afflictions, trials, and discipline...submitting to His will. Job 1:21: "The LORD gave, and the LORD has taken away; blessed be the name of the LORD." Aaron's silence was not coldness—it was prostration before God's sovereignty amid excruciating pain.

**Rules and Reverence—Grace Within the Law**
Moses accepted Aaron's reason for not eating the sin offering—the Reformed tradition teaches the law's purpose is not rigid literalism but the heart's genuine response. Christ in Matthew 12:7 quotes Hosea: "I desire mercy, and not sacrifice"—God values heartfelt reverence over formally perfect compliance.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第十章的悲剧从反面预表了基督的完美祭司职分。

"耶和华没有吩咐他们的"——基督完全相反。约翰福音12:49"因为我没有凭着自己讲，惟有差我来的父已经给我命令，叫我说甚么，讲甚么。"基督从不"自作主张"——祂所做的每一件事都是父吩咐的。约翰福音5:19"子凭着自己不能做甚么，惟有看见父所做的，子才能做。"拿答和亚比户擅自行动被烧死——基督完全顺服父的旨意而复活升天。

拿答和亚比户献凡火被审判——基督献上自己为"馨香的火祭"被悦纳。以弗所书5:2"基督爱我们，为我们舍了自己，当作馨香的供物和祭物，献与神。"人自己发明的敬拜方式（凡火）招来审判——基督按照父的旨意献上的祭（十字架）带来救赎。

"亚伦默默不言"——基督在审判面前也沉默。以赛亚书53:7"他被欺压，在受苦的时候却不开口；他像羊羔被牵到宰杀之地，又像羊在剪毛的人手下无声，他也是这样不开口。"亚伦的沉默是因为神的审判是公义的；基督的沉默是因为祂甘心承受本不属于祂的审判——为了我们。

亚伦不能为自己死去的儿子哀哭——基督却为罪人哭泣。路加福音19:41"耶稣快到耶路撒冷，看见城，就为它哀哭。"亚伦被禁止表达悲伤——基督自由地为失丧的人流泪。旧约的大祭司受限于律法——新约的大祭司超越了律法，用怜悯取代了冷漠。

禁酒令——基督在十字架上拒绝了醋和苦胆的混合物（太27:34"他尝了，就不肯喝"）——祂选择在完全清醒的状态下承受十字架的痛苦，不借助任何麻醉。这是终极的清醒——完全的顺服需要完全的意识。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 10's tragedy prefigures Christ's perfect priestly ministry by negative contrast.

"Which he had not commanded them"—Christ was the complete opposite. John 12:49: "For I have not spoken on my own authority, but the Father who sent me has himself given me a commandment—what to say and what to speak." Christ never "acted on His own initiative"—everything He did was the Father's command. John 5:19: "The Son can do nothing of his own accord, but only what he sees the Father doing." Nadab and Abihu acted presumptuously and were burned—Christ obeyed the Father perfectly and rose to ascend.

Nadab and Abihu offered unauthorized fire and were judged—Christ offered Himself as "a fragrant offering" and was accepted. Ephesians 5:2: "Christ loved us and gave himself up for us, a fragrant offering and sacrifice to God." Humanly devised worship (unauthorized fire) brought judgment—Christ's sacrifice according to the Father's will (the cross) brought redemption.

"Aaron held his peace"—Christ was also silent before judgment. Isaiah 53:7: "He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth; like a lamb that is led to the slaughter, and like a sheep that before its shearers is silent, so he opened not his mouth." Aaron's silence was because God's judgment was just; Christ's silence was because He willingly bore judgment not His own—for our sake.

Aaron could not mourn his dead sons—but Christ wept for sinners. Luke 19:41: "When he drew near and saw the city, he wept over it." Aaron was forbidden to express grief—Christ freely shed tears for the lost. The Old Testament high priest was constrained by law—the New Testament High Priest transcended law, replacing coldness with compassion.

The alcohol prohibition—Christ on the cross refused the wine mixed with gall (Matt 27:34: "He tasted it but would not drink it")—He chose to endure the cross's agony in full consciousness, without any anesthetic. This was the ultimate sobriety—complete obedience required complete awareness.
