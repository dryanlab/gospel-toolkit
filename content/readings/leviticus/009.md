## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第九章是利未记最激动人心的一章——七天就职期满之后的"第八天"，亚伦第一次以大祭司的身份正式献祭，而耶和华的荣光向全以色列显现，火从天降烧尽了坛上的祭物。

"到了第八天"——弟兄姐妹，"第八天"在圣经中有特殊的意义。七天是一个完整的周期（创造周的七天），第八天是新的开始。割礼在第八天（创17:12），亚伦就职后的第一次正式献祭也在第八天。第八天是旧周期的结束和新时代的开端——祭司就职的七天"旧时代"过去了，第八天，新的敬拜时代正式开始。

> "对亚伦说：你当取牛群中的一只公牛犊作赎罪祭，一只公绵羊作燔祭……你也要对以色列人说：你们当取一只公山羊作赎罪祭。"

弟兄姐妹，注意这里有两轮献祭——先为亚伦自己献，再为百姓献。大祭司不能未处理自己的罪就去替百姓赎罪。次序不可颠倒——先自己的赎罪祭和燔祭，然后才是百姓的赎罪祭、燔祭、素祭和平安祭。

然后摩西给了一个令人屏息的预告："因为今天耶和华要向你们显现。"弟兄姐妹，从出埃及记末尾荣光充满会幕以来，耶和华的荣光一直"住在"会幕里面——但百姓没有亲眼看见。今天——第八天——他们要看见了。

"全会众都近前来，站在耶和华面前。"整个以色列——不只是祭司——都来了。这不是祭司的私人仪式——这是全民的事件。弟兄姐妹，敬拜从来不只是"牧师的工作"——敬拜是全会众的参与。

亚伦按照规定一步一步地献祭——先为自己献赎罪祭公牛犊（v.8），再献燔祭公绵羊（v.12）；然后为百姓献赎罪祭公山羊（v.15），燔祭牛犊和绵羊羔（v.16），素祭取一把烧坛上（v.17），最后是平安祭的公牛和公绵羊（v.18-21）。

弟兄姐妹，第九章的献祭包含了前七章教导的所有五种祭——赎罪祭、燔祭、素祭、平安祭（还有之前的赎愆祭）。就像一个学生在考试中把所学的全部应用出来——亚伦在第八天"交了考卷"。

"亚伦向百姓举手，为他们祝福，就下来了。"这是亚伦作为大祭司第一次为百姓祝福——祭司不仅代替百姓向神献祭，也代替神向百姓宣告祝福。祭司是双向的中保：向上代表人到神面前，向下代表神到人面前。

然后是高潮——

> "摩西、亚伦进入会幕，又出来为百姓祝福，耶和华的荣光就向众民显现。有火从耶和华面前出来，在坛上烧尽燔祭和脂油。"

弟兄姐妹，这是何等壮观的场面！火从天降——不是自然的火，是从"耶和华面前"出来的超自然之火——落在坛上，烧尽了坛上的祭物。这是神的亲自回应：你们的祭我悦纳了。

这个场景在以色列历史上还会再出现几次：列王纪上18:38以利亚在迦密山上向巴力的先知挑战时"耶和华降下火来，烧尽燔祭"；历代志下7:1所罗门献殿祷告时"有火从天上降下来，烧尽燔祭和别的祭"。每一次天火降下，都是神在关键时刻宣告"我在这里，我悦纳了"。

> "众民一见，就都欢呼，俯伏在地。"

弟兄姐妹，欢呼和俯伏——这两个反应完美地表达了人在神的显现面前应有的双重态度：喜乐（欢呼）和敬畏（俯伏）。不是只有喜乐——那会变成轻浮；不是只有敬畏——那会变成恐惧。两者同时存在——极大的喜乐中有极深的敬畏。这就是真正的敬拜：在神的荣耀面前既欢呼又俯伏。

弟兄姐妹，第九章是利未记的"五旬节"——前七章是预备（律例的教导），第八章是分别为圣（就职），第九章是荣耀的显现。一切的预备都指向这一刻：神亲自从天降火，确认祂悦纳了祂百姓的敬拜。但这个荣耀的时刻几乎立即被第十章的悲剧打断——两个被设立的祭司在同一天被神的火烧死。从极大的喜乐到极大的审判，只有一步之遥。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 9 is Leviticus's most exhilarating chapter—after the seven-day ordination period, on the "eighth day," Aaron for the first time officially sacrificed as high priest, and the LORD's glory appeared to all Israel as fire from heaven consumed the altar's offerings.

"On the eighth day"—Brothers and sisters, the "eighth day" holds special biblical significance. Seven days is a complete cycle (creation week's seven days); the eighth day is a new beginning. Circumcision was on the eighth day (Gen 17:12); Aaron's first official sacrifice after ordination was also on the eighth day. The eighth day marked the old cycle's end and a new era's start—the seven-day "old age" of priestly ordination was past; on the eighth day, the new age of worship officially began.

> "Take for yourself a bull calf for a sin offering, and a ram for a burnt offering...And say to the people of Israel, 'Take a male goat for a sin offering.'"

Brothers and sisters, note two rounds of sacrifice—first for Aaron himself, then for the people. The high priest could not atone for the people before addressing his own sin. The order was irreversible—first his own sin and burnt offerings, then the people's sin, burnt, grain, and peace offerings.

Then Moses gave a breathtaking announcement: "For today the LORD will appear to you." Brothers and sisters, since Exodus's end when glory filled the tabernacle, the LORD's glory had been "dwelling" inside—but the people had not seen it with their own eyes. Today—the eighth day—they would see.

"All the congregation drew near and stood before the LORD." All Israel—not just priests—came. This was not a private priestly ritual—it was a national event. Brothers and sisters, worship was never just "the pastor's job"—worship is the whole congregation's participation.

Aaron sacrificed step by step—first his own sin offering calf (v.8), then burnt offering ram (v.12); then the people's sin offering goat (v.15), burnt offering calf and lamb (v.16), grain offering handful burned on altar (v.17), finally the peace offering bull and ram (vv.18-21).

Brothers and sisters, chapter 9's sacrifices included all five offerings taught in chapters 1-7—sin, burnt, grain, peace (plus the earlier guilt offering). Like a student applying everything learned on exam day—Aaron "submitted his test" on the eighth day.

"Aaron lifted up his hands toward the people and blessed them, and he came down." This was Aaron's first blessing as high priest—the priest not only offered sacrifice to God on the people's behalf, but also pronounced blessing from God to the people. The priest was a two-way mediator: upward representing humans before God, downward representing God before humans.

Then the climax—

> "Moses and Aaron went into the tent of meeting, and when they came out they blessed the people, and the glory of the LORD appeared to all the people. And fire came out from before the LORD and consumed the burnt offering and the pieces of fat on the altar."

Brothers and sisters, what a spectacular scene! Fire from heaven—not natural fire but supernatural fire from "before the LORD"—falling on the altar, consuming the offerings. This was God's personal response: your sacrifices I have accepted.

This scene would recur several times in Israel's history: 1 Kings 18:38, Elijah's challenge to Baal's prophets on Carmel when "the fire of the LORD fell and consumed the burnt offering"; 2 Chronicles 7:1, Solomon's temple dedication prayer when "fire came down from heaven and consumed the burnt offering." Each time heavenly fire fell, God was declaring at a crucial moment: "I am here; I have accepted."

> "When all the people saw it, they shouted and fell on their faces."

Brothers and sisters, shouting and falling prostrate—these two responses perfectly expressed the dual attitude proper before God's appearing: joy (shouting) and awe (prostration). Not joy alone—that becomes flippancy; not awe alone—that becomes terror. Both simultaneously—deepest awe within greatest joy. This is true worship: both shouting and falling before God's glory.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 9 is Leviticus's "Pentecost"—chapters 1-7 were preparation (teaching regulations), chapter 8 was consecration (ordination), chapter 9 was glory's appearing. All preparation pointed to this moment: God Himself sending fire from heaven, confirming His acceptance of His people's worship. But this glorious moment was almost immediately shattered by chapter 10's tragedy—two ordained priests killed by God's fire on the same day. From greatest joy to severest judgment, only one step apart.

## 历史背景（中文）

"第八天"在犹太传统中有特殊的末世论意义——七天代表现今的世界，第八天代表弥赛亚时代（olam haba，来世）。"火从耶和华面前出来"——列王纪上18:38以利亚在迦密山的经历，历代志下7:1所罗门献殿的经历，士师记6:21基甸的祭物被天火烧尽——天火在以色列传统中是神悦纳祭物的最高确认。亚伦"向百姓举手为他们祝福"——后来民数记6:24-26的亚伦祝福词（"愿耶和华赐福给你，保护你"）成为以色列崇拜中最重要的祝福语之一，至今犹太会堂和基督教会仍然使用。"众民一见就都欢呼俯伏在地"——希伯来文的"欢呼"（vayaronu）用的是joyful shouting的词根，而"俯伏"（vayippelu）是完全脸朝地的姿态——喜乐和敬畏的双重反应。

## Historical Context (English)

The "eighth day" holds special eschatological significance in Jewish tradition—seven days represent the present world, the eighth day represents the Messianic age (olam haba, the world to come). "Fire came out from before the LORD"—1 Kings 18:38 Elijah's experience on Carmel, 2 Chronicles 7:1 Solomon's temple dedication, Judges 6:21 Gideon's offering consumed by heavenly fire—divine fire in Israelite tradition was the highest confirmation of God accepting an offering. Aaron "lifting his hands toward the people and blessing them"—the later Aaronic benediction of Numbers 6:24-26 ("The LORD bless you and keep you") became one of the most important blessing formulas in Israelite worship, still used today in synagogues and Christian churches. "When the people saw it they shouted and fell on their faces"—the Hebrew "shouted" (vayaronu) uses the root for joyful shouting, while "fell" (vayippelu) describes a completely face-down posture—the dual response of joy and reverence.

## 经文结构（中文）

**第八天的吩咐（1-7节）**——摩西召亚伦和长老、吩咐亚伦自己的赎罪祭燔祭、吩咐百姓的赎罪祭燔祭平安祭素祭、"今天耶和华要向你们显现"。

**亚伦为自己献祭（8-14节）**——赎罪祭公牛犊（血抹坛角脂油烧坛皮肉粪营外烧）+燔祭公绵羊（切块洗腿全烧坛上）。

**亚伦为百姓献祭（15-21节）**——赎罪祭公山羊+燔祭牛犊绵羊羔+素祭取一把烧坛上+平安祭公牛公绵羊（摇祭胸和右腿）。

**祝福与荣光显现（22-24节）**——亚伦举手为百姓祝福下来、摩西亚伦进入会幕又出来再次祝福、耶和华荣光向众民显现、火从耶和华面前出来烧尽坛上祭物、众民欢呼俯伏在地。

## Structure (English)

**The Eighth Day's Commands (vv. 1-7)**—Moses summons Aaron and elders, commands Aaron's own sin and burnt offerings, commands people's sin burnt peace and grain offerings, "today the LORD will appear to you."

**Aaron Sacrifices for Himself (vv. 8-14)**—Sin offering calf (blood on altar horns, fat burned, body burned outside) + burnt offering ram (cut pieces washed burned entirely).

**Aaron Sacrifices for the People (vv. 15-21)**—Sin offering goat + burnt offering calf and lamb + grain offering handful burned + peace offering bull and ram (wave offering breast and thigh).

**Blessing and Glory Appearing (vv. 22-24)**—Aaron lifts hands blesses people and comes down, Moses and Aaron enter tent then emerge to bless again, LORD's glory appears to all people, fire from the LORD consumes offerings on altar, people shout and fall prostrate.

## 神学要点（中文）

**先为自己赎罪再为百姓赎罪——中保的次序**
亚伦先处理自己的罪——改革宗传统用此教导教会领袖的自省。韦敏斯德大要理问答第159问在论有效讲道时说牧者当殷勤地……在自己内心首先经历他所要教导的真理。你不能把自己没有经历的东西传给别人——先解决自己的罪，再服事他人。

**天火——神的悦纳与主权**
火从天降烧尽祭物——改革宗传统强调救恩完全出于神的主动。韦敏斯德信条第10章论有效恩召：完全出于神白白的恩典。不是人的献祭"感动"了神——是神主动降火表示悦纳。人做了自己该做的（按规矩献祭），但最终的接纳完全取决于神的恩典。

**欢呼与俯伏——敬拜的双重维度**
喜乐与敬畏同时存在——改革宗传统反对两个极端：一种是过度随意的"娱乐化敬拜"（只有欢呼），另一种是过度严肃的"恐惧式敬拜"（只有俯伏）。诗篇2:11"当存畏惧事奉耶和华，又当存战兢而快乐。"真正的敬拜既有庄严的敬畏又有真实的喜乐。

## Theology (English)

**Atone for Self Before Atoning for People—The Mediator's Order**
Aaron first addressed his own sin—the Reformed tradition teaches church leaders' self-examination. Westminster Larger Catechism Q159 on effective preaching says pastors should diligently...first experience in their own hearts the truths they are to teach. You cannot transmit what you have not experienced—first resolve your own sin, then serve others.

**Heavenly Fire—God's Acceptance and Sovereignty**
Fire from heaven consuming offerings—the Reformed tradition emphasizes salvation is entirely from God's initiative. Westminster Confession chapter 10 on effectual calling: entirely of God's free grace. Human sacrifice did not "move" God—God actively sent fire indicating acceptance. Humans did their part (offering according to regulations), but ultimate acceptance depended entirely on God's grace.

**Shouting and Prostration—Worship's Dual Dimension**
Joy and awe coexisting—the Reformed tradition opposes two extremes: overly casual "entertainment worship" (only shouting) and overly solemn "fear worship" (only prostrating). Psalm 2:11: "Serve the LORD with fear, and rejoice with trembling." True worship has both solemn awe and genuine joy.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第九章的"第八天"和"天火降下"是基督复活和五旬节的双重预表。

"第八天"——基督在第一日（七天周期后的第八天/新一周的第一天）复活。旧的周期结束，新的时代开始。约翰福音20:1"七日的第一日清早……到了坟墓那里。"基督的复活是终极的"第八天"——旧约的祭祀时代结束，新约的恩典时代开始。

"火从耶和华面前出来"——使徒行传2:3"又有舌头如火焰显现出来，分开落在他们各人头上。"五旬节圣灵降临如同天火——利未记第九章的天火确认了旧约的祭祀被悦纳，五旬节的天火确认了基督的献祭被悦纳。天火从坛上转移到了人的头上——神不再住在帐幕里，而是住在信徒心中。

"众民欢呼俯伏"——使徒行传2:11"我们听见他们用我们的乡谈，讲说神的大作为。"五旬节的众人也是欢呼和惊叹。从利未记九章到使徒行传二章——从天火降在祭坛到天火降在信徒——从欢呼俯伏到说方言赞美——旧约的"第八天"在五旬节得到了终极的成全。

亚伦为百姓祝福——基督升天前也为门徒祝福。路加福音24:50-51"耶稣领他们到伯大尼的对面，就举手给他们祝福。正祝福的时候，他就离开他们，被带到天上去了。"亚伦祝福后天火降下——基督祝福升天后圣灵降下。

两轮献祭（先为自己再为百姓）——基督只需一轮。希伯来书9:12"不是用山羊和牛犊的血，乃是用自己的血，只一次进入圣所，成了永远赎罪的事。"不需要先为自己赎罪——因为祂无罪。不需要多轮——因为一次就够了。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 9's "eighth day" and "heavenly fire" doubly prefigure Christ's resurrection and Pentecost.

"The eighth day"—Christ rose on the first day (the eighth day after the seven-day cycle / the first day of the new week). The old cycle ended; a new era began. John 20:1: "On the first day of the week...came to the tomb." Christ's resurrection is the ultimate "eighth day"—the Old Testament sacrificial era ended; the New Testament age of grace began.

"Fire came out from before the LORD"—Acts 2:3: "Divided tongues as of fire appeared to them and rested on each one of them." The Pentecost Spirit descended like heavenly fire—Leviticus 9's fire confirmed the Old Testament sacrifices were accepted; Pentecost's fire confirmed Christ's sacrifice was accepted. Heavenly fire moved from the altar to people's heads—God no longer dwelt in the tabernacle but in believers' hearts.

"The people shouted and fell prostrate"—Acts 2:11: "We hear them telling in our own tongues the mighty works of God." Pentecost's crowds also exclaimed in wonder. From Leviticus 9 to Acts 2—from fire on the altar to fire on believers—from shouting prostrate to speaking in tongues praising—the Old Testament's "eighth day" found ultimate fulfillment at Pentecost.

Aaron blessed the people—Christ also blessed the disciples before ascending. Luke 24:50-51: "He led them out as far as Bethany, and lifting up his hands he blessed them. While he blessed them, he parted from them and was carried up into heaven." After Aaron's blessing, heavenly fire fell—after Christ's blessing and ascension, the Spirit descended.

Two rounds of sacrifice (first for himself, then for people)—Christ needed only one round. Hebrews 9:12: "He entered once for all into the holy places, not by means of the blood of goats and calves but by means of his own blood, thus securing an eternal redemption." No need to first atone for Himself—because He was sinless. No need for multiple rounds—because once was sufficient.
