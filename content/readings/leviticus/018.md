## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十八章进入了利未记中一个极为严肃的领域——性伦理。这一章列出了一系列被禁止的性关系，涵盖了乱伦、通奸、同性行为、兽交和献子与摩洛。这些规定不是文化性的偏好——它们是神对人类性行为的绝对道德界限。

第十八章的开头和结尾构成了一个完整的框架：

开头（2-5节）："我是耶和华你们的神。你们从前住的埃及地，那里人的行为，你们不可效法，我要领你们到的迦南地，那里人的行为也不可效法。"

弟兄姐妹，以色列被夹在两个堕落的文化之间——埃及（你来的地方）和迦南（你去的地方）。这两个文化都有严重的性堕落。以色列不能效法任何一方——你不能说"埃及人都这样做"或"迦南人都这样做"。文化多数不是道德标准——"我是耶和华"才是道德标准。

然后神说了一句极其重要的话："人若遵行，就必因此活着。我是耶和华。"弟兄姐妹，"就必因此活着"——遵行神的律法不是限制而是生命之路。性伦理的界限不是压抑——而是保护生命。破坏性伦理的边界导致的不是自由——而是死亡（个人的、家庭的、社会的、最终是属灵的死亡）。

第十八章的核心（6-23节）列出了被禁止的性关系：

乱伦的禁令（6-18节）——不可与母亲、继母、姐妹、同父异母或同母异父的姐妹、孙女、继姐妹、姑母、伯母叔母、儿媳、弟兄之妻、母女同娶、姐妹同娶发生性关系。弟兄姐妹，这个清单极其详细——几乎穷尽了所有可能的亲属性关系。"不可露骨肉之亲的下体"——"露下体"是性关系的委婉说法。这些禁令保护的是家庭的边界和秩序——当家庭内部的性界限被打破时，家庭就瓦解了。信任变成了背叛、保护变成了侵犯、安全变成了创伤。

月经期的禁令（19节）——"女人行经不洁净的时候，不可露她的下体，与她亲近。"这条规定与第十五章的洁净律呼应。

通奸（20节）——"不可与邻舍的妻行淫，玷污自己。"

献子与摩洛（21节）——"不可使你的儿女经火归与摩洛。"弟兄姐妹，摩洛崇拜是迦南最可怕的偶像崇拜之一——把自己的孩子烧在火中献给这个异教神明。这不只是性伦理的问题——这是对人类生命最极端的亵渎。经文把它放在性伦理的清单中——因为性（生育的来源）和献子（杀害所生的）构成了一个可怕的反转：性本应创造生命，摩洛崇拜却把性的果实（孩子）投入死亡。

同性行为（22节）——"不可与男人苟合，像与女人一样；这本是可憎恶的。"

兽交（23节）——"不可与兽淫合……这本是逆性的事。"

弟兄姐妹，第十八章的结尾（24-30节）极其有力——它用"地吐出居民"的意象来警告以色列：

> "在这一切的事上，你们都不可玷污自己；因为我在你们面前所逐出的列邦，在这一切的事上玷污了自己；连地也玷污了，所以我追讨那地的罪孽，那地也吐出它的居民。"

弟兄姐妹，这里有一个极其深刻的神学原则——罪不只影响犯罪的人，罪影响他们脚下的土地。当一个社会普遍地破坏了神的性伦理界限——迦南人的乱伦、兽交、献子——那片土地本身都被"玷污"了。土地像一个有灵性的存在一样"呕吐"出那些居民——就是说，那些人最终无法在那片土地上继续生存。

弟兄姐妹，这不只是对迦南人的判决——这也是对以色列的警告："免得你们玷污那地的时候，地就把你们吐出，像吐出在你们以先的国民一样。"弟兄姐妹，这个警告后来在以色列历史中不幸地应验了——公元前586年巴比伦掳掠就是"那地吐出居民"的历史实现。当以色列走上了迦南人同样的道德堕落之路时——他们也遭受了同样的命运。

"所以你们要守我所吩咐的……我是耶和华你们的神。"——"我是耶和华"在这一章中出现了多次——提醒以色列：这些不是文化规范，不是人的意见——这是神自己的命令，根基在祂的本性——圣洁。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 18 enters an extremely solemn area of Leviticus—sexual ethics. This chapter lists prohibited sexual relationships, covering incest, adultery, same-sex acts, bestiality, and child sacrifice to Molech. These regulations were not cultural preferences—they were God's absolute moral boundaries for human sexual behavior.

Chapter 18's opening and closing formed a complete framework:

Opening (vv. 2-5): "I am the LORD your God. You shall not do as they do in the land of Egypt, where you lived, and you shall not do as they do in the land of Canaan, to which I am bringing you."

Brothers and sisters, Israel was sandwiched between two fallen cultures—Egypt (where you came from) and Canaan (where you were going). Both had severe sexual corruption. Israel could follow neither—you could not say "Egyptians all do this" or "Canaanites all do this." Cultural majority was not the moral standard—"I am the LORD" was the moral standard.

Then God spoke a crucially important word: "If a person does them, he shall live by them: I am the LORD." Brothers and sisters, "he shall live"—obeying God's law was not restriction but life's path. Sexual ethical boundaries were not repression—but life's protection. Destroying sexual ethical boundaries leads not to freedom—but to death (personal, familial, societal, and ultimately spiritual death).

Chapter 18's core (vv. 6-23) listed prohibited sexual relationships:

Incest prohibitions (vv. 6-18)—no sexual relations with mother, stepmother, sister, half-sister, granddaughter, stepsister, aunt, uncle's wife, daughter-in-law, brother's wife, mother and daughter together, sisters together. Brothers and sisters, this list was extremely detailed—nearly exhausting every possible kinship sexual combination. "Uncover nakedness"—a euphemism for sexual relations. These prohibitions protected family boundaries and order—when internal family sexual boundaries were broken, families collapsed. Trust became betrayal, protection became violation, safety became trauma.

Menstrual prohibition (v. 19)—connecting to chapter 15's purity law.

Adultery (v. 20)—"You shall not lie sexually with your neighbor's wife and so make yourself unclean with her."

Child sacrifice to Molech (v. 21)—"You shall not give any of your children to offer them to Molech." Brothers and sisters, Molech worship was one of Canaan's most horrific idolatries—burning one's own children in fire as offerings to this pagan deity. This was not merely a sexual ethics issue—it was the most extreme desecration of human life. Scripture placed it within the sexual ethics list—because sex (life's source) and child sacrifice (killing what was born) formed a terrible reversal: sex should create life; Molech worship cast sex's fruit (children) into death.

Same-sex acts (v. 22)—"You shall not lie with a male as with a woman; it is an abomination."

Bestiality (v. 23)—"You shall not lie with any animal...it is perversion."

Brothers and sisters, chapter 18's conclusion (vv. 24-30) was extraordinarily powerful—using the image of "the land vomiting out its inhabitants":

> "Do not make yourselves unclean by any of these things, for by all these the nations I am driving out before you have become unclean, and the land became unclean, so that I punished its iniquity, and the land vomited out its inhabitants."

Brothers and sisters, here was a profoundly deep theological principle—sin did not only affect sinners; sin affected the land beneath their feet. When a society universally violated God's sexual ethical boundaries—Canaan's incest, bestiality, child sacrifice—the land itself became "defiled." The land like a sentient being "vomited out" those inhabitants—meaning those people ultimately could not continue existing on that land.

Brothers and sisters, this was not only judgment on Canaanites—it was warning to Israel: "lest the land vomit you out when you make it unclean, as it vomited out the nation that was before you." This warning was tragically fulfilled in Israelite history—the 586 BC Babylonian exile was the historical realization of "the land vomiting out its inhabitants." When Israel followed the Canaanites' same path of moral corruption—they suffered the same fate.

"So keep my charge...I am the LORD your God."—"I am the LORD" appeared multiple times in this chapter—reminding Israel: these are not cultural norms, not human opinions—these are God's own commands, grounded in His nature—holiness.

## 历史背景（中文）

迦南文化的性堕落有考古证据支持——乌加里特（现叙利亚拉斯沙姆拉）出土的文献描述了巴力和亚拿特等神明之间的性关系，包括乱伦（巴力与其姐妹亚拿特）和兽交。庙妓制度在迦南宗教中广泛存在。摩洛崇拜的考古证据在腓尼基和迦太基的tophet（儿童献祭场所）中被发现——大量被焚烧的婴儿骸骨证实了旧约的记载。"地吐出居民"的意象在古代近东是独特的——没有其他文化把土地描述为有能力对居民的道德行为做出反应的实体。这个概念把以色列的道德律与土地（应许之地）直接挂钩——你对这片土地有道德责任。利未记18章在犹太传统中极其重要——在赎罪日的下午崇拜中被诵读，提醒百姓性伦理的严肃性。

## Historical Context (English)

Canaanite sexual corruption has archaeological support—Ugaritic texts (modern Ras Shamra, Syria) described sexual relations among deities like Baal and Anat, including incest (Baal with his sister Anat) and bestiality. Temple prostitution was widespread in Canaanite religion. Molech worship archaeological evidence was found in Phoenician and Carthaginian tophets (child sacrifice sites)—abundant burned infant remains confirmed Old Testament accounts. "The land vomiting out inhabitants" was unique in the ancient Near East—no other culture described land as an entity capable of responding to inhabitants' moral behavior. This concept directly linked Israel's moral law to the land (Promised Land)—you bore moral responsibility toward this land. Leviticus 18 holds extreme importance in Jewish tradition—read during Yom Kippur afternoon worship, reminding people of sexual ethics' gravity.

## 经文结构（中文）

**序言：不可效法埃及迦南（1-5节）**——我是耶和华你们的神、不效法埃及迦南的行为、遵行就必活着。

**乱伦禁令（6-18节）**——母亲、继母、姐妹/同父异母姐妹、孙女、继姐妹、姑母、伯叔母、儿媳、弟兄之妻、母女同娶、姐妹同娶——都不可露下体。

**其他性伦理禁令（19-23节）**——月经期禁令、通奸、献子与摩洛、同性行为（"可憎恶的"）、兽交（"逆性的事"）。

**警告：地吐出居民（24-30节）**——迦南人因此被逐、地也玷污了、以色列若同行则同遭命运、行可憎之事者被剪除。

## Structure (English)

**Prologue: Do Not Follow Egypt or Canaan (vv. 1-5)**—I am the LORD your God, do not follow Egyptian/Canaanite practices, obey and live.

**Incest Prohibitions (vv. 6-18)**—Mother, stepmother, sister/half-sister, granddaughter, stepsister, aunt, uncle's wife, daughter-in-law, brother's wife, mother-daughter, sisters together—all nakedness forbidden.

**Other Sexual Ethics Prohibitions (vv. 19-23)**—Menstrual prohibition, adultery, child sacrifice to Molech, same-sex acts ("abomination"), bestiality ("perversion").

**Warning: Land Vomits Out Inhabitants (vv. 24-30)**—Canaanites expelled for these, land defiled, Israel faces same fate if following same path, offenders cut off.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"人若遵行就必因此活着"——律法与生命**
改革宗传统区分律法的三重用途：(1)镜子（显明罪）、(2)约束（维持社会秩序）、(3)引导（指引信徒的生活）。利未记18:5"人若遵行就必因此活着"被保罗在加拉太书3:12和罗马书10:5引用——说明没有人能靠律法称义（因为没有人能完全遵行）。但律法本身不是死亡的工具——律法显明了生命之路。基督完全遵行了律法——祂是唯一"因此活着"的那位。

**"我是耶和华"——道德律的绝对根基**
性伦理的根基不是文化、不是传统、不是多数人的意见——而是"我是耶和华"。改革宗传统教导道德律反映了神不变的本性——韦敏斯德信条第19章"道德律永远约束所有的人"。文化会变、民意会变——但"我是耶和华"不变。

**"地吐出居民"——罪的社会和环境后果**
改革宗传统教导罪不只是个人行为——它有公共后果。韦敏斯德大要理问答第150问论罪的加重情形包括使别人的身体和灵魂受害。一个社会的道德堕落最终会导致该社会的崩溃——"地吐出居民"。

## Theology (English)

**"If a Person Does Them, He Shall Live"—Law and Life**
The Reformed tradition distinguishes law's threefold use: (1) mirror (revealing sin), (2) restraint (maintaining social order), (3) guide (directing believers' lives). Leviticus 18:5 "if a person does them, he shall live" was cited by Paul in Galatians 3:12 and Romans 10:5—showing no one can be justified by law (no one can fully obey). But law itself was not death's instrument—law revealed life's path. Christ perfectly obeyed the law—He is the only one who "lived by them."

**"I Am the LORD"—Moral Law's Absolute Foundation**
Sexual ethics' foundation was not culture, not tradition, not majority opinion—but "I am the LORD." The Reformed tradition teaches moral law reflects God's unchanging nature—Westminster Confession chapter 19: The moral law does forever bind all. Cultures change, public opinion changes—but "I am the LORD" does not change.

**"The Land Vomits Out Inhabitants"—Sin's Social and Environmental Consequences**
The Reformed tradition teaches sin is not merely individual—it has public consequences. Westminster Larger Catechism Q150 on aggravations of sin includes hurting others' bodies and souls. A society's moral corruption ultimately leads to that society's collapse—"the land vomits out its inhabitants."

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第十八章的性伦理律法在基督里得到了确认和深化——而非废除。

基督确认了创造秩序中的婚姻——马太福音19:4-6"那起初造人的，是造男造女，并且说：因此，人要离开父母，与妻子连合，二人成为一体……所以，神配合的，人不可分开。"基督把婚姻的标准追溯到创造——一男一女、终身盟约——这与利未记18章的性伦理界限完全一致。

基督深化了性伦理——不仅是外在行为，更是内心："你们听见有话说：不可奸淫。只是我告诉你们，凡看见妇女就动淫念的，这人心里已经与她犯奸淫了"（太5:27-28）。利未记18章禁止的是行为——基督连内心的欲念也一同审判。律法触及了身体——福音触及了灵魂。

"地吐出居民"——罗马书8:19-22保罗说"受造之物切望等候神的众子显出来……一同叹息、劳苦"。弟兄姐妹，利未记的"地也玷污了"在保罗那里被扩展到整个受造界——不只是迦南的土地，整个受造物都在人类的罪之下"叹息劳苦"。但在基督里有盼望——受造界也要"脱离败坏的辖制"（罗8:21）。基督的救赎不只恢复人——也恢复天地。

"人若遵行就必因此活着"——基督替我们遵行了。罗马书8:3-4"神就差遣自己的儿子……在肉体中定了罪案，使律法的义成就在我们这不随从肉体、只随从圣灵的人身上。"我们不是靠自己遵行18章的圣洁标准——我们靠圣灵的大能活出基督已经为我们赢得的义。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 18's sexual ethics law was confirmed and deepened in Christ—not abolished.

Christ affirmed creation-order marriage—Matthew 19:4-6: "He who created them from the beginning made them male and female, and said, 'Therefore a man shall leave his father and his mother and hold fast to his wife, and the two shall become one flesh'...What therefore God has joined together, let not man separate." Christ traced marriage's standard to creation—one man and one woman, lifelong covenant—fully consistent with Leviticus 18's sexual ethical boundaries.

Christ deepened sexual ethics—not merely external behavior but the heart: "Ye have heard that it was said by them of old time, Thou shalt not commit adultery: But I say unto you, That whosoever looketh on a woman to lust after her hath committed adultery with her already in his heart." (Matt 5:27-28). Leviticus 18 prohibited actions—Christ judged even inner desire. The law reached the body—the gospel reached the soul.

"The land vomits out inhabitants"—Romans 8:19-22, Paul said "the creation waits with eager longing for the revealing of the sons of God...the whole creation has been groaning together." Brothers and sisters, Leviticus's "the land became unclean" was expanded by Paul to all creation—not just Canaan's land; all creation "groans" under human sin. But in Christ there is hope—creation too will be "Because the creature itself also shall be delivered from the bondage of corruption into the glorious liberty of the children of God." (Rom 8:21). Christ's redemption restores not only people—but heaven and earth.

"If a person does them, he shall live"—Christ obeyed for us. Romans 8:3-4: "God has done what the law...could not do. By sending his own Son...in order that the righteous requirement of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not according to the flesh but according to the Spirit." We do not meet chapter 18's holiness standard by our own strength—we live out through the Spirit's power the righteousness Christ has already won for us.
