## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第八章是利未记的转折点——从献祭的"条例"（第1-7章）转入献祭的"执行"。前七章是理论，第八章开始实践。这一章记载了以色列历史上第一次祭司就职典礼——亚伦和他的儿子们被设立为祭司。

> "你将亚伦和他儿子一同带来，并将圣衣、膏油，与赎罪祭的一只公牛、两只公绵羊、一筐无酵饼都带来，又招聚会众到会幕门口。"

弟兄姐妹，这是公开的仪式——"招聚会众"。祭司的就职不是私下进行的秘密仪式——全以色列都要看见。为什么？因为祭司是代表全体百姓站在神面前的——百姓需要亲眼确认这些人确实是神所设立的中保。

仪式分为七个步骤——每一步都充满了神学意义：

第一步：水洗（v.6）。"摩西带了亚伦和他儿子来，用水洗了他们。"在任何事情之前——穿衣之前、膏抹之前、献祭之前——先洗净。洁净是一切服事的前提。弟兄姐妹，你不能脏着身子穿圣衣——外在的洁净象征内在的圣洁。

第二步：穿圣衣（vv.7-9）。这是出埃及记28章那套精心设计的大祭司圣衣——内袍、腰带、外袍、以弗得、胸牌（上面有乌陵和土明）、冠冕、金牌（刻着"归耶和华为圣"）。弟兄姐妹，穿上圣衣的那一刻，亚伦不再只是亚伦——他是大祭司了。衣服改变了他的身份。当他穿上胸牌时，以色列十二支派的名字就在他的心上——每次他进入圣所，他就"带着"全以色列进到神面前。

第三步：膏油（vv.10-12）。"摩西用膏油抹帐幕和其中所有的，使它成圣。"先膏帐幕和器皿，然后"把膏油倒在亚伦的头上膏他，使他成圣。"弟兄姐妹，膏油不是轻轻抹一点——是"倒在头上"，油从头流到胡须，从胡须流到衣裳的边——诗篇133:2描绘了这个场景："好比那贵重的油浇在亚伦的头上，流到胡须，又流到他的衣襟。"膏油的倾倒代表圣灵的完全浇灌——不是一点点，而是丰盛的、溢出的、充满的。

第四步：赎罪祭（vv.14-17）。在亚伦开始作祭司之前，他自己先需要赎罪。"亚伦和他儿子按手在赎罪祭公牛的头上"——弟兄姐妹，中保自己首先是罪人。亚伦不能凭自己的义站在神面前——他需要公牛的血来洁净自己。这是旧约祭司制度的根本局限：中保本身也需要中保。

第五步：燔祭（vv.18-21）。赎罪之后是奉献——公绵羊全烧在坛上。亚伦和他的儿子按手、宰杀、完全焚烧。赎罪祭解决了罪的问题，燔祭表达了完全的委身——我的生命全部献给你。

第六步：承接圣职之礼的羊（vv.22-29）——这是整个仪式中最独特的部分。

> "摩西把些血抹在亚伦的右耳垂上和右手的大拇指上，并右脚的大拇指上。"

弟兄姐妹，右耳、右手大拇指、右脚大拇指——三个身体部位代表了祭司生命的三个维度：耳朵——听从神的话语（顺服）；手——执行神的命令（服事）；脚——行走在神的道路上（生活）。血抹在这三处意味着：你的听、你的做、你的行——全部都被赎罪的血覆盖和分别为圣了。从今以后，你听到的应该是神的话，你做的应该是神的事，你走的应该是神的路。

然后是摇祭——脂油、右腿和三种饼放在亚伦和他儿子的手上"摇一摇"。这个动作的意思是：这些东西先献给神（向前摇），然后神把它赐回给祭司（向后摇）。一切的恩赐都来自神——祭司的权柄和供应都是神给的。

第七步：膏油和血弹在衣服上（v.30）。"摩西取点膏油和坛上的血，弹在亚伦和他的衣服上。"弟兄姐妹，膏油（圣灵）和血（赎罪）一同弹在祭司身上——圣洁和赦免不可分离。你不能有圣灵的膏抹而没有血的洁净，也不能有血的洁净而没有圣灵的充满。

最后是七天的分别："七天你们要昼夜住在会幕门口……免得你们死亡。"弟兄姐妹，七天——创造的完整周期。祭司就职需要七天来完成——这不是人的急躁可以缩短的。而"免得你们死亡"——服事神不是儿戏，不按规矩做就会死。第十章拿答和亚比户的悲剧很快就会证明这个警告的严肃性。

弟兄姐妹，第八章从头到尾重复了一个短语——"照耶和华所吩咐摩西的"（出现七次以上）。每一步都不是人的发明——每一步都是神的命令。人不能自己设计敬拜的方式——敬拜的每一个细节都由神来定。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 8 is Leviticus's turning point—from sacrificial "regulations" (chapters 1-7) to sacrificial "execution." The first seven chapters were theory; chapter 8 begins practice. This chapter records Israel's first-ever priestly ordination—Aaron and his sons being installed as priests.

> "Take Aaron and his sons with him, and the garments and the anointing oil and the bull of the sin offering and the two rams and the basket of unleavened bread. And assemble all the congregation at the entrance of the tent of meeting."

Brothers and sisters, this was a public ceremony—"assemble all the congregation." Priestly ordination was not a private, secret ritual—all Israel was to witness it. Why? Because priests stood before God representing all the people—the people needed to personally confirm these were indeed God's appointed mediators.

The ceremony had seven steps—each laden with theological meaning:

Step one: washing with water (v.6). "Moses brought Aaron and his sons and washed them with water." Before anything—before dressing, before anointing, before sacrificing—first cleansing. Purity was the prerequisite for all service. Brothers and sisters, you cannot put on holy garments while still dirty—external cleansing symbolized internal holiness.

Step two: the holy garments (vv.7-9). This was the elaborate high priestly outfit designed in Exodus 28—tunic, sash, robe, ephod, breastpiece (containing Urim and Thummim), turban, golden plate (inscribed "Holy to the LORD"). Brothers and sisters, the moment Aaron put on the holy garments, he was no longer just Aaron—he was the high priest. The clothing changed his identity. When he wore the breastpiece, the twelve tribes' names rested over his heart—every time he entered the sanctuary, he "carried" all Israel before God.

Step three: anointing oil (vv.10-12). "Moses took the anointing oil and anointed the tabernacle and everything in it." First the tabernacle and vessels, then "poured the anointing oil on Aaron's head and anointed him to consecrate him." Brothers and sisters, the oil was not lightly dabbed—it was "poured on his head," flowing from head to beard, from beard to garment's hem—Psalm 133:2 describes this scene: "Like the precious oil on the head, running down on the beard, on the beard of Aaron, running down on the collar of his robes." The oil's pouring represented the Spirit's complete outpouring—not a little, but abundant, overflowing, filling.

Step four: sin offering (vv.14-17). Before Aaron began serving as priest, he himself needed atonement. "Aaron and his sons laid their hands on the head of the bull of the sin offering"—Brothers and sisters, the mediator himself was first a sinner. Aaron could not stand before God on his own righteousness—he needed the bull's blood to cleanse himself. This was the Old Testament priesthood's fundamental limitation: the mediator himself needed a mediator.

Step five: burnt offering (vv.18-21). After atonement came dedication—the ram entirely burned on the altar. Aaron and his sons laying hands, slaughtering, completely burning. The sin offering resolved the sin problem; the burnt offering expressed total commitment—my entire life offered to You.

Step six: the ordination ram (vv.22-29)—the ceremony's most unique part.

> "Moses took some of its blood and put it on the lobe of Aaron's right ear and on the thumb of his right hand and on the big toe of his right foot."

Brothers and sisters, right ear, right thumb, right big toe—three body parts representing three dimensions of priestly life: ear—hearing God's word (obedience); hand—executing God's commands (service); foot—walking in God's ways (conduct). Blood on these three meant: your hearing, your doing, your walking—all covered and consecrated by atoning blood. Henceforth, what you hear should be God's word, what you do should be God's work, where you walk should be God's path.

Then the wave offering—fat, right thigh, and three breads placed on Aaron and sons' hands and "waved." This gesture meant: these things were first offered to God (waving forward), then God gave them back to the priest (waving back). All gifts come from God—priestly authority and provision were both God-given.

Step seven: oil and blood sprinkled on garments (v.30). "Moses took some of the anointing oil and of the blood that was on the altar and sprinkled it on Aaron and his garments." Brothers and sisters, anointing oil (the Spirit) and blood (atonement) sprinkled together on the priest—holiness and forgiveness are inseparable. You cannot have the Spirit's anointing without blood's cleansing, nor blood's cleansing without the Spirit's filling.

Finally the seven days of separation: "For seven days you shall not depart from the entrance of the tent of meeting...lest you die." Brothers and sisters, seven days—creation's complete cycle. Priestly ordination required seven days—human impatience could not shorten this. And "lest you die"—serving God was no game; doing it wrong meant death. Chapter 10's tragedy of Nadab and Abihu would soon prove this warning's gravity.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 8 repeats one phrase throughout—"as the LORD commanded Moses" (appearing over seven times). Every step was not human invention—every step was God's command. Humans cannot design their own worship methods—every worship detail is determined by God.

## 历史背景（中文）

祭司就职礼（承接圣职，希伯来文millu'im，字面意思是"充满"——把祭司的手"充满"祭物）在古代近东有广泛平行。埃及的祭司就职也需要洁净仪式和穿戴特殊服饰——但以色列的仪式独特在于强调赎罪（祭司自己先需要赎罪）。"乌陵和土明"是古代以色列求问神旨意的器具——其确切形态已经失传，可能是两块石头或骰子，通过某种方式给出"是"或"否"的答案。"膏油"的配方在出埃及记30:22-25中详细记载——没药、肉桂、菖蒲、桂皮和橄榄油混合。这种配方是专用的，禁止任何人用同样的配方制作普通用途的油（出30:32-33）。血抹右耳垂、右手大拇指、右脚大拇指——类似的仪式也出现在利未记14章麻风病洁净礼中，表明祭司就职和洁净礼之间有神学上的联系。

## Historical Context (English)

The priestly ordination (millu'im in Hebrew, literally "filling"—"filling" the priest's hands with offerings) had widespread ancient Near Eastern parallels. Egyptian priestly ordination also required purification rites and special vestments—but Israel's ceremony was unique in emphasizing atonement (priests themselves first needed atonement). "Urim and Thummim" were ancient Israelite instruments for inquiring God's will—their exact form is lost, possibly two stones or lots that gave "yes" or "no" answers. The "anointing oil" recipe is detailed in Exodus 30:22-25—myrrh, cinnamon, calamus, cassia, and olive oil. This formula was exclusive; anyone making the same formula for ordinary use was forbidden (Exod 30:32-33). Blood on right earlobe, right thumb, right big toe—a similar ritual appears in Leviticus 14's leprosy cleansing ceremony, indicating theological connection between priestly ordination and purification rites.

## 经文结构（中文）

**预备和招聚（1-5节）**——耶和华吩咐摩西带圣衣膏油祭物、招聚全会众到会幕门口。

**水洗和穿圣衣（6-9节）**——水洗亚伦和儿子、穿内袍外袍以弗得胸牌冠冕金牌、"照耶和华所吩咐摩西的"。

**膏油圣别（10-13节）**——膏帐幕和器皿、坛上弹油七次、膏油倒在亚伦头上、儿子们穿内袍束腰带。

**三个祭（14-29节）**——赎罪祭公牛（亚伦按手、血抹坛角、皮肉粪营外烧）→燔祭公绵羊（按手宰杀、全烧在坛上）→承接圣职羊（血抹右耳垂右大拇指右脚趾、脂油右腿饼作摇祭）。

**膏油血弹衣服+七天分别（30-36节）**——膏油和血弹在亚伦和儿子衣服上成圣、七天昼夜住会幕门口"免得你们死亡"。

## Structure (English)

**Preparation and Assembly (vv. 1-5)**—LORD commands Moses to bring garments oil offerings, assemble congregation at tent entrance.

**Washing and Vesting (vv. 6-9)**—Wash Aaron and sons, dress in tunic robe ephod breastpiece turban golden plate, "as the LORD commanded Moses."

**Anointing Consecration (vv. 10-13)**—Anoint tabernacle and vessels, sprinkle oil on altar seven times, pour oil on Aaron's head, sons dressed in tunics and sashes.

**Three Sacrifices (vv. 14-29)**—Sin offering bull (laying hands, blood on altar horns, body burned outside camp) → burnt offering ram (laying hands, entirely burned) → ordination ram (blood on right ear thumb toe, fat thigh bread as wave offering).

**Oil and Blood on Garments + Seven Days (vv. 30-36)**—Anointing oil and blood sprinkled on Aaron and sons' garments for consecration, seven days and nights at tent entrance "lest you die."

## 神学要点（中文）

**祭司自己先需赎罪——中保的局限性**
亚伦为自己献赎罪祭——改革宗传统用此对比基督的优越性。韦敏斯德信条第八章论中保教导基督是真实的神和真实的人，两个完全的本性合于一个位格中。希伯来书7:26-27"像这样圣洁、无邪恶、无玷污、远离罪人、高过诸天的大祭司，原是与我们合宜的。他不像那些大祭司，每日必须先为自己的罪……献祭。"亚伦需要赎罪祭——基督不需要。

**"照耶和华所吩咐摩西的"——限定性敬拜原则**
七次以上重复此短语——改革宗的"限定性敬拜原则"（Regulative Principle of Worship）在此有最强的旧约根基。韦敏斯德信条第二十一章教导：可蒙悦纳的敬拜神的方式，乃是神自己所设立的，不可按着人的想象和捏造来敬拜神。人不可自行发明敬拜的方式——每一步都必须是"照耶和华所吩咐的"。

**七天——完全的分别为圣**
七天承接圣职——改革宗传统强调牧职准备的严肃性。提摩太前书5:22"给人行按手的礼，不可急促。"按立牧师不可草率——正如祭司就职需要七天（完全的周期），牧者的装备和确认也需要完整的预备过程。

## Theology (English)

**The Priest Himself Needed Atonement—The Mediator's Limitation**
Aaron offered a sin offering for himself—the Reformed tradition uses this to contrast Christ's superiority. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on the Mediator: Christ: being very and eternal God and very man—in one person...without sin. Hebrews 7:26-27: "For it was indeed fitting that we should have such a high priest, holy, innocent, unstained, separated from sinners, exalted above the heavens. He has no need, like those high priests, to offer sacrifices daily, first for his own sins." Aaron needed atonement—Christ did not.

**"As the LORD Commanded Moses"—The Regulative Principle of Worship**
This phrase repeated over seven times—the Reformed "Regulative Principle of Worship" finds its strongest Old Testament foundation here. Westminster Confession chapter 21: "The acceptable way of worshiping the true God is instituted by Himself...God may not be worshiped according to the imaginations and devices of men." Humans may not invent worship methods—every step must be "as the LORD commanded."

**Seven Days—Complete Consecration**
Seven days of ordination—the Reformed tradition emphasizes the seriousness of ministerial preparation. 1 Timothy 5:22: "Do not be hasty in the laying on of hands." Ordaining pastors must not be rushed—just as priestly ordination required seven days (a complete cycle), pastoral preparation and confirmation requires a thorough process.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第八章的祭司就职礼是基督大祭司职分最完整的旧约预表之一。

水洗——基督受洗。马太福音3:15耶稣受约翰的洗"尽诸般的义"。基督不需要洁净（祂无罪），但祂受洗是"认同"罪人——祂站在罪人的位置上，经过了洁净的仪式。

穿圣衣——基督的完美人性和神性。以赛亚书61:10"他以拯救为衣给我穿上，以公义为袍给我披上。"基督的"圣衣"不是外在的以弗得和胸牌——而是祂完美的义和完全的圣洁。

膏油——圣灵的充满。使徒行传10:38"神怎样以圣灵和能力膏拿撒勒人耶稣。"基督受洗时"圣灵仿佛鸽子降在他身上"（太3:16）——不是一点点油，而是圣灵完全的降临。"基督"（Christos）本身就是"受膏者"的意思。

血抹右耳、右手、右脚——基督的全人献祭。基督的耳朵完全听从父（约5:30"我不求自己的意思，只求那差我来者的意思"），基督的手完全执行父的旨意（约17:4"你所托付我的事，我已成全了"），基督的脚完全行走在义路上（彼前2:22"他并没有犯罪，口里也没有诡诈"）。

亚伦先为自己献赎罪祭——基督不需要。希伯来书7:27"他不像那些大祭司，每日必须先为自己的罪……献祭；因为他只一次将自己献上，就把这事成全了。"这是基督超越亚伦的关键：亚伦是被赎的中保，基督是施赎的中保。亚伦需要牛的血——基督献的是自己的血。

七天的就职——基督在地上三十三年的"就职期"，最终在十字架上"成了"（约19:30）——祂的大祭司就职在复活升天时完全成就。希伯来书5:9"他既得以完全，就为凡顺从他的人成了永远得救的根源。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 8's priestly ordination is one of the most complete Old Testament prefigurations of Christ's high priestly office.

Washing—Christ's baptism. Matthew 3:15: Jesus was baptized by John "to fulfill all righteousness." Christ needed no cleansing (He was sinless), but His baptism "identified" with sinners—He stood in sinners' place, undergoing the purification rite.

Holy garments—Christ's perfect humanity and divinity. Isaiah 61:10: "He has clothed me with the garments of salvation; he has covered me with the robe of righteousness." Christ's "holy garments" were not an external ephod and breastpiece—but His perfect righteousness and complete holiness.

Anointing oil—the Spirit's filling. Acts 10:38: "God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Spirit and with power." At Christ's baptism "And Jesus, when he was baptized, went up straightway out of the water: and, lo, the heavens were opened unto him, and he saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove, and lighting upon him:" (Matt 3:16)—not a little oil but the Spirit's complete descent. "Christ" (Christos) itself means "the Anointed One."

Blood on right ear, hand, foot—Christ's whole-person sacrifice. Christ's ears fully obeyed the Father (John 5:30: "I seek not my own will but the will of him who sent me"), Christ's hands fully executed the Father's will (John 17:4: "I glorified you on earth, having accomplished the work that you gave me to do"), Christ's feet fully walked in righteousness (1 Peter 2:22: "He committed no sin, neither was deceit found in his mouth").

Aaron first offered sin offering for himself—Christ needed none. Hebrews 7:27: "He has no need, like those high priests, to offer sacrifices daily, first for his own sins...since he did this once for all when he offered up himself." This is Christ's key superiority over Aaron: Aaron was a redeemed mediator; Christ is the redeeming Mediator. Aaron needed bull's blood—Christ offered His own blood.

Seven days of ordination—Christ's thirty-three years on earth as His "ordination period," ultimately on the cross declaring "When Jesus therefore had received the vinegar, he said, It is finished: and he bowed his head, and gave up the ghost." (John 19:30)—His high priestly ordination was fully accomplished in resurrection and ascension. Hebrews 5:9: "And being made perfect, he became the source of eternal salvation to all who obey him."
