## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第七章是五大祭条例的总结篇——先补充了赎愆祭和平安祭的细则，最后以庄严的总结收束整个祭祀制度的第一大段落。

赎愆祭条例的补充（1-10节）很简短——"赎罪祭怎样，赎愆祭也是怎样"。两个祭的处理方式相同：脂油烧坛上，肉归祭司在圣处吃。然后补充了一条重要规定：为人献燔祭的祭司可以得燔祭牲的皮（v.8），素祭归献祭的祭司或所有祭司共享（v.9-10）。弟兄姐妹，神不仅设立了祭祀制度来处理罪——祂也通过这个制度养活了整个祭司阶层。祭司没有土地、不种田——他们的"收入"全部来自百姓的祭物。

然后是第七章的核心——平安祭的三种类型（11-21节）：

第一种：感谢祭（todah）。"他若为感谢献上，就要用调油的无酵饼和抹油的无酵薄饼，并用油调匀细面做的饼，与感谢祭一同献上。"弟兄姐妹，感谢祭是最丰富的平安祭——除了牲畜，还要带三种不同的饼。而且要"用有酵的饼"与无酵饼一同献上。有酵的饼！在素祭中酵是被禁止的——但在感谢祭中酵被允许了。为什么？因为感谢祭的饼不烧在坛上——它是给人吃的。坛上不可有酵（献给神的必须纯洁），但人吃的可以有酵（日常的面包本来就有酵）。

感谢祭的肉有严格的时间限制："要在献的日子吃，一点不可留到早晨。"当天必须吃完。弟兄姐妹，为什么这么急？因为感谢是即时的——你对神的感恩应该是热烈的、当下的、不拖延的。你不能"存着感谢慢慢吃"——感恩的火热不应该冷却。而且，在没有冰箱的古代中东，肉类过夜就开始腐败——神保护百姓不吃变质的食物，这也是卫生上的智慧。

第二种：还愿祭。"若所献的是为还愿……必在献祭的日子吃，所剩下的第二天也可以吃。"还愿祭比感谢祭宽松一天——可以吃两天。为什么？可能因为还愿祭往往涉及更多的宾客（你向很多人宣布了你的誓愿），需要更多时间分享。

第三种：甘心祭（自愿祭）。与还愿祭相同，可以吃两天。

> "但所剩下的祭肉，到第三天要用火焚烧。"

第三天是绝对的底线——无论哪种平安祭，第三天的肉都必须烧掉。为什么不能第三天吃？经文给出了严厉的警告："第三天若吃了平安祭的肉，这祭必不蒙悦纳，人所献的也不算为祭，反为可憎嫌的；吃这祭肉的，就必担当他的罪孽。"

弟兄姐妹，这不只是卫生问题——这是神学问题。你把"与神同席"的神圣宴席变成了"囤积食物"的日常行为。你在第三天吃的已经不是"圣洁的平安祭"——它已经退化为普通的剩菜。神圣的东西不能被世俗化、不能被拖延、不能被敷衍。

然后是洁净条例（19-21节）——这是平安祭中最严厉的规定：

> "人若不洁净而吃了，这人必从民中剪除。"
> "有人摸了甚么不洁净的物……吃了献与耶和华平安祭的肉，这人必从民中剪除。"

弟兄姐妹，"从民中剪除"——这是以色列律法中最严重的惩罚之一，意味着被逐出盟约群体。在不洁净的状态下吃平安祭，等于亵渎了"与神同席"的特权。你带着不洁净来到神的桌前——这不是蒙恩，而是定罪。哥林多前书11:27-29保罗关于主的圣餐也发出同样的警告："无论何人，不按理吃主的饼、喝主的杯，就是干犯主的身、主的血了。"

最后是平安祭的分配（28-36节）——祭司从平安祭中获得的份额：

"好把胸在耶和华面前作摇祭，摇一摇。"摇祭——祭司把胸前后摇动，象征献给神然后神把它赐回给祭司。"你们要从平安祭中把右腿作举祭，奉给祭司。"举祭——把右腿向上举起，象征举向天上的神。胸和右腿——这是平安祭中祭司永得的分。

第七章以庄严的总结结束："这就是燔祭、素祭、赎罪祭、赎愆祭，和平安祭的条例，并承接圣职的礼，都是耶和华在西乃山所吩咐摩西的。"

弟兄姐妹，七章的祭祀条例可以浓缩为一句话：圣洁的神为罪人开辟了一条来到祂面前的道路——通过血、通过火、通过祭司的中保、通过严格的规程——罪人可以赎罪、可以奉献、可以与神同席。这整个体系复杂而精密——但它也是暂时的、有限的、等候那最终的祭的。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 7 is the summary chapter of the five offerings' regulations—first supplementing guilt offering and peace offering details, then concluding the entire sacrificial system's first major section with a solemn summary.

The guilt offering supplement (vv. 1-10) is brief—"the guilt offering is just like the sin offering." Both were handled identically: fat burned on altar, meat to priests eaten in a holy place. Then an important provision: the priest who offers someone's burnt offering receives the skin (v.8); grain offerings go to the officiating priest or are shared among all priests (vv.9-10). Brothers and sisters, God not only established the sacrificial system to address sin—He also sustained the entire priestly class through it. Priests had no land, did not farm—their "income" came entirely from the people's offerings.

Then chapter 7's core—three types of peace offering (vv. 11-21):

First: thanksgiving offering (todah). "If he offers it for a thanksgiving, then he shall offer with the thanksgiving sacrifice unleavened loaves mixed with oil, unleavened wafers smeared with oil, and loaves of fine flour well mixed with oil." Brothers and sisters, the thanksgiving offering was the richest peace offering—beyond the animal, three different types of bread. And "leavened bread" was offered alongside unleavened. Leavened bread! In the grain offering leaven was forbidden—but in the thanksgiving offering leaven was permitted. Why? Because thanksgiving bread was not burned on the altar—it was for people to eat. No leaven on the altar (what was given to God must be pure), but people's food could have leaven (daily bread naturally contains leaven).

Thanksgiving meat had strict time limits: "The flesh of the sacrifice of his peace offerings for thanksgiving shall be eaten on the day of his offering. He shall not leave any of it until the morning." Must be consumed that day. Brothers and sisters, why such urgency? Because gratitude is immediate—your thanksgiving to God should be fervent, present, undelayed. You cannot "save up thanksgiving to eat slowly"—gratitude's fervor should not cool. Moreover, in the ancient Middle East without refrigeration, meat began spoiling overnight—God protected the people from eating spoiled food, showing hygienic wisdom as well.

Second: vow offering. "If the sacrifice of his offering is a vow...it shall be eaten on the day that he offers his sacrifice, and on the next day." One day more lenient than thanksgiving—two days permitted. Why? Perhaps because vow offerings often involved more guests (you had publicly declared your vow to many), requiring more time for sharing.

Third: freewill offering. Same as the vow offering—two days permitted.

"But what remains of the flesh of the sacrifice on the third day shall be burned up with fire." Brothers and sisters, the third day was the absolute deadline—regardless of peace offering type, third-day meat must be burned. Why not eat on the third day? Scripture gives a severe warning: "If any of the flesh of the sacrifice of his peace offering is eaten on the third day, he who offers it shall not be accepted, neither shall it be credited to him. It is tainted, and he who eats of it shall bear his iniquity."

Brothers and sisters, this was not merely hygienic—it was theological. You would have turned the sacred feast of "dining with God" into the mundane act of "hoarding food." What you ate on the third day was no longer "holy peace offering"—it had degraded into ordinary leftovers. Sacred things cannot be secularized, delayed, or treated carelessly.

Then the cleanness regulations (vv. 19-21)—the peace offering's strictest provisions:

> "The person who is unclean and eats...shall be cut off from his people."
> "If anyone touches an unclean thing...and then eats of the flesh of the sacrifice of the LORD's peace offerings, that person shall be cut off from his people."

Brothers and sisters, "cut off from his people"—one of Israelite law's severest penalties, meaning expulsion from the covenant community. Eating the peace offering while unclean desecrated the privilege of "dining with God." Approaching God's table while unclean brought not blessing but condemnation. 1 Corinthians 11:27-29, Paul issues the same warning about the Lord's Supper: "Whoever, therefore, eats the bread or drinks the cup of the Lord in an unworthy manner will be guilty concerning the body and blood of the Lord."

Finally the peace offering distribution (vv. 28-36)—the priests' portion:

"The breast that is waved...as a wave offering before the LORD." Wave offering—the priest waved the breast back and forth, symbolizing offering to God then God giving it back to the priest. "The right thigh you shall give to the priest as a contribution." Heave offering—lifting the right thigh upward, symbolizing lifting toward God in heaven. Breast and right thigh—the priests' perpetual share from peace offerings.

Chapter 7 concludes with a solemn summary: "This is the law of the burnt offering, of the grain offering, of the sin offering, of the guilt offering, of the ordination offering, and of the peace offering, which the LORD commanded Moses at Mount Sinai."

Brothers and sisters, seven chapters of sacrificial regulations can be distilled into one sentence: the holy God opened a way for sinners to come before Him—through blood, through fire, through priestly mediation, through strict protocols—sinners could atone, could offer, could dine with God. This entire system was complex and precise—but it was also temporary, limited, awaiting the final Sacrifice.

## 历史背景（中文）

感谢祭（todah）在以色列后期崇拜中成为最重要的祭之一——诗篇中多次提到todah（诗50:14"你们要以感谢为祭献与神"；诗100:4"当称谢进入他的门"）。拉比传统认为在弥赛亚时代所有的祭都会停止，唯有感谢祭（todah）永存——因为那时不再有罪需要赎，但感恩永远不会停止。"摇祭"（tenufah）和"举祭"（terumah）是以色列献祭中独特的仪式动作——在古代近东其他文化中没有明确的平行。摇祭的动作方向是水平的（前后），举祭是垂直的（上下）——一些学者认为这组合象征了献物在神面前的全方位呈现。"从民中剪除"（karet）的确切含义在拉比传统中有争议——可能是社会驱逐、也可能是神直接的惩罚（早死或断子绝孙）。

## Historical Context (English)

The thanksgiving offering (todah) became one of the most important sacrifices in later Israelite worship—the Psalms frequently mention todah (Ps 50:14: "Offer to God a sacrifice of thanksgiving"; Ps 100:4: "Enter his gates with thanksgiving"). Rabbinic tradition held that in the Messianic age all sacrifices would cease except the todah—because there would be no more sin requiring atonement, but gratitude would never end. "Wave offering" (tenufah) and "heave offering" (terumah) were ritual actions unique to Israelite sacrifice—without clear parallels in other ancient Near Eastern cultures. The wave offering's motion was horizontal (back and forth), the heave offering vertical (up and down)—some scholars suggest this combination symbolized presenting the offering before God in all directions. "Cut off from his people" (karet) had a debated precise meaning in rabbinic tradition—possibly social expulsion, or possibly God's direct punishment (early death or childlessness).

## 经文结构（中文）

**赎愆祭条例补充（1-10节）**——与赎罪祭同样处理、至圣的、祭司在圣处吃、献燔祭祭司得牲皮、素祭归祭司。

**平安祭三种类型（11-21节）**——感谢祭（三种无酵饼+有酵饼、当天吃完）、还愿祭（可吃两天）、甘心祭（可吃两天）、第三天必烧、不洁吃平安祭必从民中剪除。

**脂油和血的永久禁令（22-27节）**——脂油不可吃、血不可吃、违者从民中剪除。

**平安祭的祭司分配（28-36节）**——胸为摇祭归祭司、右腿为举祭归祭司、永远的定例。

**总结（37-38节）**——五大祭+承接圣职的条例、耶和华在西乃山吩咐。

## Structure (English)

**Guilt Offering Supplement (vv. 1-10)**—Handled like sin offering, most holy, priests eat in holy place, burnt offering priest receives skin, grain offerings to priests.

**Three Types of Peace Offering (vv. 11-21)**—Thanksgiving (three unleavened breads + leavened, eat same day), vow (two days), freewill (two days), third day must burn, eating unclean cut off from people.

**Permanent Fat and Blood Prohibition (vv. 22-27)**—No eating fat, no eating blood, violators cut off from people.

**Peace Offering Priestly Portions (vv. 28-36)**—Breast as wave offering to priests, right thigh as heave offering to priests, perpetual statute.

**Summary (vv. 37-38)**—Five offerings + ordination regulations, the LORD commanded Moses at Mount Sinai.

## 神学要点（中文）

**感谢祭——最高形式的敬拜**
拉比传统说弥赛亚时代只有todah（感谢祭）留存——改革宗传统同样把感恩视为信仰生活的核心。韦敏斯德大要理问答第104问在解释第一诫应尽的本分时提到"为所拥有的一切感谢祂"。海德堡要理问答第116问将基督徒的全部生活总结为"感谢"——感恩不是可选的附加品，而是基督徒生命的本质。

**第三天必烧——不可世俗化圣物**
平安祭的肉不能存到第三天——改革宗传统强调圣洁的事物必须被尊重地对待。韦敏斯德信条第21章论安息日要求分别为圣——不能把圣洁的崇拜变成世俗的娱乐。同样，平安祭的肉不能从"与神同席的圣宴"退化为"冰箱里的剩菜"。

**不洁者吃平安祭必被剪除——圣餐的警告**
不洁之人吃平安祭被剪除——改革宗传统将此应用于圣餐的自省。韦敏斯德大要理问答第171问论接受圣餐前的预备说信徒当"省察自己"。哥林多前书11:28"人应当自己省察，然后吃这饼、喝这杯。"不配的领受不是祝福，而是审判。

## Theology (English)

**Thanksgiving Offering—The Highest Form of Worship**
Rabbinic tradition says only the todah (thanksgiving offering) survives into the Messianic age—the Reformed tradition similarly views gratitude as the core of the life of faith. Westminster Larger Catechism Q104 on duties of the first commandment mentions being thankful for all He has. The Heidelberg Catechism Q116 summarizes the entire Christian life as "gratitude"—thankfulness is not an optional addition but the essence of Christian life.

**Third Day Must Burn—Do Not Secularize Sacred Things**
Peace offering meat could not be kept until the third day—the Reformed tradition emphasizes sacred things must be treated with reverence. Westminster Confession chapter 21 on the Sabbath requires setting apart as holy—sacred worship cannot become secular entertainment. Likewise, peace offering meat could not degrade from "holy feast with God" to "leftovers in the refrigerator."

**Unclean Eating Peace Offering Cut Off—The Lord's Supper Warning**
Unclean persons eating peace offerings were cut off—the Reformed tradition applies this to self-examination before communion. Westminster Larger Catechism Q171 on preparation before receiving the Lord's Supper says believers should examine themselves. 1 Corinthians 11:28: "Let a person examine himself, then, and so eat of the bread and drink of the cup." Unworthy reception is not blessing but judgment.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第七章的基督预表集中在三个方面。

感谢祭（todah）——基督设立圣餐时说"你们要如此行，为的是记念我"（路22:19）。"记念"——eucharistia，感恩。圣餐本质上就是新约的感谢祭。路德、加尔文和茨温利虽然在圣餐的神学理解上有分歧，但都同意一点：圣餐是感恩的筵席。每次领圣餐，我们都在献todah——感谢基督的身体为我们擘开、血为我们流出。

"当天吃完"——基督的恩典是"日用的"。主祷文说"我们日用的饮食，今日赐给我们"（太6:11）——恩典不是可以囤积的。你不能说"我上周已经感恩了，这周不用了"——每一天都需要新鲜的、热烈的、即时的感恩。

"不洁者从民中剪除"——哥林多前书11:29-30"因为人吃喝，若不分辨是主的身体，就是吃喝自己的罪了。因此，在你们中间有好些软弱的与患病的，死的也不少。"不配地领受圣餐的人面临属灵的"剪除"——不是说他们失去救恩，而是说他们招来神的管教。

摇祭（胸）和举祭（右腿）——胸代表爱（心的位置），右腿代表力量（行走和服事的力量）。基督把祂的爱（胸）和祂的力量（腿）都给了祂的教会。约翰福音13:1"他既然爱世间属自己的人，就爱他们到底。"基督把最好的——祂全部的爱和能力——"摇"给了我们。

"这就是……都是耶和华在西乃山所吩咐摩西的"——希伯来书1:1-2"神既在古时藉着众先知多次多方地晓谕列祖，就在这末世藉着他儿子晓谕我们。"西乃山的吩咐最终指向基督——所有的祭、所有的条例、所有的规程，都在十字架上找到终极的成全。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 7's Christological typology centers on three aspects.

The thanksgiving offering (todah)—Christ establishing the Lord's Supper said "And he took bread, and gave thanks, and brake it, and gave unto them, saying, This is my body which is given for you: this do in remembrance of me." (Luke 22:19). "Remembrance"—eucharistia, thanksgiving. The Lord's Supper is essentially the new covenant's thanksgiving offering. Luther, Calvin, and Zwingli, though differing in eucharistic theology, agreed on one point: the Supper was a feast of thanksgiving. Each time we receive communion, we offer a todah—thanksgiving for Christ's body broken and blood shed for us.

"Eaten the same day"—Christ's grace is "daily." The Lord's Prayer says "Give us this day our daily bread" (Matt 6:11)—grace cannot be stockpiled. You cannot say "I was grateful last week, no need this week"—every day requires fresh, fervent, immediate gratitude.

"Unclean persons cut off"—1 Corinthians 11:29-30: "For anyone who eats and drinks without discerning the body eats and drinks judgment on himself. That is why many of you are weak and ill, and some have died." Those unworthily receiving communion face spiritual "cutting off"—not losing salvation, but incurring God's discipline.

Wave offering (breast) and heave offering (right thigh)—the breast represents love (the heart's location), the right thigh represents strength (power for walking and serving). Christ gave His love (breast) and His strength (thigh) to His church. John 13:1: "Having loved his own who were in the world, he loved them to the end." Christ gave the best—His complete love and power—"waved" to us.

"This is the law...which the LORD commanded Moses on Mount Sinai"—Hebrews 1:1-2: "Long ago, at many times and in many ways, God spoke to our fathers by the prophets, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son." Sinai's commands ultimately pointed to Christ—all sacrifices, all regulations, all procedures find their ultimate fulfillment at the cross.
