## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十二章是利未记中最短的一章——只有八节经文——但它触及了人类生命最深层的神学问题：为什么生育——这个本应是祝福和喜乐的事件——会导致礼仪上的不洁净？

> "若有妇人怀孕生男孩，她就不洁净七天，像在月经污秽的日子不洁净一样。第八天，要给婴孩行割礼。妇人在产血不洁之中，要家居三十三天。"

弟兄姐妹，生育在创世记1:28是神最初的祝福——"要生养众多，遍满地面。"但堕落之后，生育也沾染了罪的后果。创世记3:16"我必多多加增你怀胎的苦楚，你生产儿女必多受苦楚。"生育的疼痛是堕落的结果。而每一个被生出来的孩子都是在亚当里出生的——诗篇51:5大卫说"我是在罪孽里生的，在我母亲怀胎的时候就有了罪。"

产妇的不洁净分为两个阶段：第一阶段——生男孩七天不洁净，生女孩十四天不洁净。第二阶段——继续家居三十三天（男）或六十六天（女），总计四十天或八十天。

弟兄姐妹，为什么生女孩的不洁净期是男孩的两倍？这是利未记中最令人困惑的规定之一。经文没有给出解释。一些解释认为：女婴将来也会经历月经和生育——她身上携带了"加倍的"生育能力，因此不洁净期加倍。另一些解释认为这与古代近东对血的理解有关——但无论如何，这个规定不意味着女性比男性更"有罪"或更低等。创世记1:27"神就照着自己的形像造人，乃是照着他的形像造男造女。"男女同样按神的形象被造。

然后是洁净的献祭——这是本章最温暖的部分：

> "满了洁净的日子……她要把一岁的羊羔为燔祭，一只雏鸽或是一只斑鸠为赎罪祭，带到会幕门口交给祭司。"

弟兄姐妹，产妇需要献两种祭——燔祭（奉献）和赎罪祭（洁净）。燔祭表达感恩——感谢神赐予新生命。赎罪祭处理不洁——恢复她的礼仪洁净状态，使她可以重新进入圣所和触摸圣物。

然后是穷人的替代方案："她的力量不够献一只羊羔，她就要取两只斑鸠或是两只雏鸽，一只为燔祭，一只为赎罪祭。"

弟兄姐妹，这个替代方案在新约中有一个极其重要的回应——路加福音2:22-24记载约瑟和马利亚在耶稣出生后带他上圣殿："又要照主的律法上所说，或用一对斑鸠，或用两只雏鸽献祭。"

弟兄姐妹，马利亚献的是穷人的祭物——两只鸽子而不是羊羔。这意味着耶稣的家庭买不起一只羊羔。神的儿子——万有的创造者——出生在一个需要献穷人祭物的家庭里。弟兄姐妹，这就是道成肉身的深度——不是降生在王宫里献公牛的家庭，而是降生在马槽里献鸽子的家庭。腓立比书2:7"反倒虚己，取了奴仆的形象。"虚己到什么程度？虚己到连羊羔都买不起的穷人家庭。

第十二章虽然只有八节，但它的神学脉络是深远的。它把我们带回创世记的堕落——每一次生育都提醒我们：我们是在罪中出生的。它也把我们带向福音的盼望——每一次洁净的献祭都指向那位能彻底洁净我们的。而穷人的两只鸽子在路加福音中成为了道成肉身最感人的见证——神不是降临在富足中，而是降临在贫穷中；不是降临在荣耀中，而是降临在卑微中。

"祭司要为她赎罪，她就洁净了。"弟兄姐妹，无论你经历了什么样的不洁净——身体的、礼仪的、道德的——神都提供了洁净的道路。从一只羊羔到两只鸽子——恩典的大门对每个人都是敞开的。

弟兄姐妹，第十二章虽然只有八节，却承载了极深的神学意义。每一次生育都是恩典——新生命是神的赐予。产妇的洁净礼不是惩罚——而是在神面前正式承认：生命来自神，新生命需要被带到神面前献上。感恩的燔祭和洁净的赎罪祭共同宣告：这个孩子是神赐的礼物，这位母亲是蒙恩的器皿。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 12 is Leviticus's shortest—only eight verses—yet it touches the deepest theological question of human life: why does childbirth—an event that should be blessing and joy—result in ritual impurity?

> "If a woman conceives and bears a male child, then she shall be unclean seven days. As at the time of her menstruation, she shall be unclean. And on the eighth day the flesh of his foreskin shall be circumcised. Then she shall continue for thirty-three days in the blood of her purifying."

Brothers and sisters, childbearing in Genesis 1:28 was God's original blessing—"Be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth." But after the fall, childbearing was also tainted by sin's consequences. Genesis 3:16: "I will surely multiply your pain in childbearing; in pain you shall bring forth children." Birth pain was the fall's result. And every child born enters the world in Adam—Psalm 51:5, David said: "Behold, I was brought forth in iniquity, and in sin did my mother conceive me."

Maternal impurity had two phases: First phase—seven days for a boy, fourteen days for a girl. Second phase—continuing thirty-three days (boy) or sixty-six days (girl), totaling forty or eighty days.

Brothers and sisters, why was the impurity period for a girl double that for a boy? This is one of Leviticus's most puzzling regulations. Scripture gives no explanation. Some explanations suggest: a female infant will herself experience menstruation and childbearing—she carries "doubled" reproductive capacity, thus doubled impurity. Others relate it to ancient Near Eastern understandings of blood—but regardless, this regulation did not mean females were more "sinful" or inferior. Genesis 1:27: "So God created man in his own image...male and female he created them." Both were equally created in God's image.

Then the purification offering—the chapter's warmest section:

> "When the days of her purifying are completed...she shall bring to the priest at the entrance of the tent of meeting a lamb a year old for a burnt offering, and a young pigeon or a turtledove for a sin offering."

Brothers and sisters, the mother needed two offerings—burnt offering (dedication) and sin offering (purification). The burnt offering expressed gratitude—thanksgiving for God granting new life. The sin offering addressed impurity—restoring her ritual purity so she could reenter the sanctuary and touch holy things.

Then the provision for the poor: "And if she cannot afford a lamb, then she shall take two turtledoves or two young pigeons, one for a burnt offering and the other for a sin offering."

Brothers and sisters, this provision has an extraordinarily important New Testament echo—Luke 2:22-24 records Joseph and Mary bringing Jesus to the temple after his birth: "And to offer a sacrifice according to what is said in the Law of the Lord, 'a pair of turtledoves, or two young pigeons.'"

Brothers and sisters, Mary offered the poor person's sacrifice—two pigeons, not a lamb. This meant Jesus' family could not afford a lamb. God's Son—Creator of all things—was born into a family needing to offer the poor person's sacrifice. Brothers and sisters, this is the incarnation's depth—not born in a palace offering bulls, but born in a manger to a family offering pigeons. Philippians 2:7: "He emptied himself, by taking the form of a servant." Emptied to what degree? Emptied into a family too poor to afford a lamb.

Chapter 12's eight verses have far-reaching theological significance. They take us back to Genesis's fall—every birth reminds us: we are born in sin. They also point forward to the gospel's hope—every purification offering pointed to the One who can thoroughly cleanse us. And the poor person's two pigeons became in Luke's Gospel the incarnation's most moving testimony—God came not in wealth but in poverty; not in glory but in humility.

"The priest shall make atonement for her, and she shall be clean." Brothers and sisters, whatever impurity you have experienced—physical, ritual, moral—God provides a path to cleansing. From a lamb to two pigeons—grace's door is open to everyone.

## 历史背景（中文）

产后洁净礼在古代近东有广泛平行——埃及、美索不达米亚和赫人文化中都有产后隔离期的规定。但以色列的独特之处在于：(1)不洁净不是永久的——有明确的期限和恢复方式；(2)恢复通过献祭完成——把洁净礼锚定在神学而非迷信中。"四十天"——在圣经中四十是重要的数字：洪水四十天（创7:12），摩西在山上四十天（出24:18），以色列旷野四十年（民14:33），耶稣旷野四十天（太4:2）。男孩的总洁净期恰好是四十天。希腊医学传统（亚里士多德和希波克拉底）也认为男胎在四十天成形，女胎在八十天成形——虽然利未记的期限不一定基于胚胎学，但这个巧合值得注意。路加福音2:22-24马利亚献鸽子的记载是路加特有的——只有医生路加注意到了这个"穷人的祭物"的细节。

## Historical Context (English)

Postpartum purification rites had widespread ancient Near Eastern parallels—Egypt, Mesopotamia, and Hittite cultures all had postpartum isolation periods. But Israel's uniqueness lay in: (1) impurity was not permanent—it had definite duration and restoration methods; (2) restoration was through sacrifice—anchoring purification rites in theology rather than superstition. "Forty days"—forty is an important biblical number: the flood lasted forty days (Gen 7:12), Moses on the mountain forty days (Exod 24:18), Israel in the wilderness forty years (Num 14:33), Jesus in the wilderness forty days (Matt 4:2). The total purification period for a boy was exactly forty days. Greek medical tradition (Aristotle and Hippocrates) also held that male fetuses formed in forty days, female in eighty—though Leviticus's periods may not have been based on embryology, the coincidence is noteworthy. Luke 2:22-24's record of Mary offering pigeons is unique to Luke—only the physician Luke noted the "poor person's sacrifice" detail.

## 经文结构（中文）

**生男孩的不洁净期（1-4节）**——七天不洁如月经、第八天行割礼、再家居三十三天（共四十天）、不可摸圣物不可进圣所。

**生女孩的不洁净期（5节）**——两个七天（十四天）不洁、再家居六十六天（共八十天）。

**洁净的献祭（6-7节）**——一岁羊羔为燔祭、雏鸽或斑鸠为赎罪祭、祭司赎罪使血源洁净。

**穷人的替代方案（8节）**——两只斑鸠或两只雏鸽（一燔祭一赎罪祭）、祭司赎罪她就洁净了。

## Structure (English)

**Male Child Impurity Period (vv. 1-4)**—Seven days unclean as during menstruation, eighth day circumcision, then thirty-three more days homebound (forty total), may not touch holy things or enter sanctuary.

**Female Child Impurity Period (v. 5)**—Two sevens (fourteen days) unclean, then sixty-six more days homebound (eighty total).

**Purification Offerings (vv. 6-7)**—Year-old lamb for burnt offering, pigeon or turtledove for sin offering, priest makes atonement and blood source is cleansed.

**Provision for the Poor (v. 8)**—Two turtledoves or two pigeons (one burnt one sin offering), priest makes atonement and she is clean.

## 神学要点（中文）

**生育与堕落——原罪的传递**
产后不洁净指向原罪的代际传递——改革宗传统强调原罪不是个人行为的结果，而是从亚当传到每一代的。韦敏斯德信条第六章论人的堕落与罪："始祖既然是全人类的根源，这罪孽就归算给他们的后裔，并且罪所引起的死亡和败坏的人性，借着生育传给了后代所有的子孙。"诗篇51:5"我是在罪孽里生的"——每一次生育都是一个提醒：罪通过自然出生传递。

**第八天割礼——盟约的记号**
生男孩第八天行割礼——改革宗传统把割礼理解为恩典之约的记号和印证，其新约对应物是洗礼。韦敏斯德信条第28章论洗礼。歌罗西书2:11-12把割礼和洗礼联系起来："你们在他里面也受了不是人手所行的割礼，乃是基督使你们脱去肉体情欲的割礼。你们既受洗与他一同埋葬。"第八天——新的开始——在盟约的记号中开始新生命。

**穷人的恩典——福音不分贫富**
两只鸽子替代一只羊羔——改革宗传统反复强调恩典不设经济门槛。雅各书2:5"神岂不是拣选了世上的贫穷人，叫他们在信上富足。"马利亚献鸽子——基督从社会底层来。

## Theology (English)

**Childbirth and the Fall—Original Sin's Transmission**
Postpartum impurity pointed to original sin's generational transmission—the Reformed tradition emphasizes original sin is not the result of personal actions but is transmitted from Adam to every generation. Westminster Confession chapter 6 on the fall and sin: They being the root of all mankind, the guilt of this sin was imputed, and the same death in sin and corrupted nature conveyed, to all their posterity descending from them by ordinary generation. Psalm 51:5: "I was brought forth in iniquity"—every birth was a reminder: sin is transmitted through natural birth.

**Eighth Day Circumcision—The Covenant Sign**
Male circumcision on the eighth day—the Reformed tradition understands circumcision as the sign and seal of the covenant of grace, whose New Testament counterpart is baptism. Westminster Confession chapter 28 on baptism. Colossians 2:11-12 connects circumcision and baptism: "In him also you were circumcised with a circumcision made without hands...having been buried with him in baptism." The eighth day—a new beginning—new life begins in the covenant's sign.

**Grace for the Poor—The Gospel Knows No Economic Class**
Two pigeons substituting for a lamb—the Reformed tradition repeatedly emphasizes grace sets no economic threshold. James 2:5: "Has not God chosen those who are poor in the world to be rich in faith?" Mary offered pigeons—Christ came from society's lowest rung.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第十二章最直接的基督预表就是路加福音2:22-24——马利亚的洁净礼。

马利亚献了穷人的祭物——两只鸽子——这是道成肉身最卑微的见证。全能的创造者选择降生在一个连羊羔都买不起的家庭。哥林多后书8:9"你们知道我们主耶稣基督的恩典：他本来富足，却为你们成了贫穷，叫你们因他的贫穷，可以成为富足。"基督的贫穷不是象征性的——是真实的、具体的、连献祭用的羊羔都买不起的贫穷。

"第八天行割礼"——路加福音2:21"满了八天，就给孩子行割礼，与他起名叫耶稣。"基督受了割礼——祂甘愿站在律法之下。加拉太书4:4"及至时候满足，神就差遣他的儿子，为女子所生，且生在律法以下。"祂不需要割礼（祂无罪），但祂接受割礼——为要"尽诸般的义"（太3:15），完全遵守律法，好叫律法在祂里面得到成全。

"四十天"——基督在出生后四十天被带到圣殿，后来祂在旷野受试探四十天（太4:2），复活后在地上显现四十天（徒1:3）。四十是预备和转变的数字——利未记的四十天洁净期在基督的四十天旷野试探中得到了终极的属灵对应：旧约的产妇四十天后恢复洁净，新约的基督四十天后开始公开事工。

"祭司要为她赎罪，她就洁净了"——基督是我们终极的赎罪祭。希伯来书1:3"他洗净了人的罪。"不需要鸽子、不需要羊羔——基督的血一次洗净一切。约翰一书1:7"他儿子耶稣的血也洗净我们一切的罪。"利未记12章的洁净是暂时的（下一次生育又会不洁净）——基督的洁净是永远的。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 12's most direct Christological connection is Luke 2:22-24—Mary's purification.

Mary offered the poor person's sacrifice—two pigeons—the humblest testimony of the incarnation. The almighty Creator chose to be born into a family that could not afford a lamb. 2 Corinthians 8:9: "For you know the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, that though he was rich, yet for your sake he became poor, so that you by his poverty might become rich." Christ's poverty was not symbolic—it was real, concrete, too-poor-to-afford-a-sacrificial-lamb poverty.

"On the eighth day...circumcised"—Luke 2:21: "At the end of eight days, when he was circumcised, he was called Jesus." Christ was circumcised—He willingly placed Himself under the law. Galatians 4:4: "When the fullness of time had come, God sent forth his Son, born of woman, born under the law." He did not need circumcision (He was sinless), but He accepted it—"And Jesus answering said unto him, Suffer it to be so now: for thus it becometh us to fulfil all righteousness. Then he suffered him." (Matt 3:15), fully observing the law so it might be fulfilled in Him.

"Forty days"—Christ was brought to the temple forty days after birth; later He was tempted in the wilderness for forty days (Matt 4:2); after resurrection He appeared on earth for forty days (Acts 1:3). Forty is the number of preparation and transition—Leviticus's forty-day purification period found its ultimate spiritual counterpart in Christ's forty-day wilderness temptation: the Old Testament mother was restored to purity after forty days; the New Testament Christ began public ministry after forty days.

"The priest shall make atonement for her, and she shall be clean"—Christ is our ultimate sin offering. Hebrews 1:3: "After making purification for sins." No pigeons needed, no lambs—Christ's blood cleanses once for all. 1 John 1:7: "The blood of Jesus his Son cleanses us from all sin." Leviticus 12's cleansing was temporary (the next childbirth would bring impurity again)—Christ's cleansing is eternal.
