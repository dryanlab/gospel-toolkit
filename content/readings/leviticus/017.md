## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十七章只有十六节经文，却是利未记下半卷（17-27章，常被称为"圣洁法典"）的开篇。如果说前十六章的重心是"怎样到神面前来"（献祭和洁净），从第十七章开始的重心是"怎样在日常生活中活出圣洁"。

第十七章处理两个核心问题：(1)宰杀动物必须在会幕门口进行；(2)绝对不可吃血。这两条看似简单的规定，背后是利未记中最深刻的神学原则之一——血的神学。

第一部分（3-9节）：所有的宰杀都必须带到会幕。

> "凡以色列家中的人宰公牛，或是绵羊羔，或是山羊，不拘宰于营内营外，若未曾牵到会幕门口、耶和华的帐幕前献给耶和华为供物，流血的罪必归到那人身上。"

弟兄姐妹，这条规定极其严格——在旷野时期，以色列人不能在自家帐棚前杀一头羊来吃晚饭。每一次宰杀都必须带到会幕门口——每一次流血都必须在祭司面前、在耶和华的坛前进行。为什么？经文给出了两个理由：

第一个理由（5-6节）——把宰杀转化为敬拜。"这是为要使以色列人把他们在田野里所献的祭带到会幕门口。"弟兄姐妹，当你杀一只羊不只是"吃肉"而是"在耶和华面前献平安祭"时，每一顿饭都变成了一次敬拜。血洒在坛上、脂油焚烧给耶和华——你吃剩下的肉，但最好的部分（血和脂油）归给神。每一次进食都是一个提醒：生命来自神，食物是神的恩赐，连吃饭都要向神感恩。

第二个理由（7节）——防止偶像崇拜。"他们不可再献祭给他们行邪淫所随从的鬼魔（原文作公山羊）。"弟兄姐妹，以色列人在埃及四百年间沾染了埃及的偶像崇拜——包括在田野里向"公山羊"（se'irim，可能指埃及的山羊神或旷野中被想象为恶魔的存在）献祭。如果允许人在田野里自由宰杀——谁知道他们是在宰羊吃饭还是在向鬼魔献祭？把所有宰杀集中到会幕门口，就杜绝了这种暗中的偶像崇拜。

第二部分（10-16节）：绝对禁止吃血——利未记中最核心的神学宣告之一。

> "凡以色列家中的人，或是寄居在他们中间的外人，若吃甚么血，我必向那吃血的人变脸，把他从民中剪除。"

弟兄姐妹，"我必向那吃血的人变脸"——这是极其强烈的措辞。"变脸"意味着神亲自与那个人为敌。为什么？下一节给出了利未记中最重要的神学声明：

> "因为活物的生命是在血中。我把这血赐给你们，可以在坛上为你们的生命赎罪；因血里有生命，所以能赎罪。"

弟兄姐妹，这节经文（11节）是整个旧约献祭神学的基石。它回答了一个根本问题：为什么杀一只动物可以赎人的罪？答案是——因为动物的生命在它的血中。当血流出来时，一个生命被交出来了。这个生命代替了你的生命——"在坛上为你们的生命赎罪"。替代——这是赎罪的核心机制。不是血液本身有什么魔力——而是血代表了一个被交出的生命。

因为血有这样神圣的功能——赎罪——所以它不可以被用来做普通的事（比如吃）。血是神赐给以色列人用来赎罪的——不是用来吃的。它属于坛，不属于饭桌。把血吃进肚子里就是亵渎了血的神圣功能——这就像把圣餐的酒杯拿来当普通酒杯用一样。

这条禁令也适用于猎物——"若打猎得了可吃的禽兽，必放出它的血来，用土掩盖。"猎物不需要带到会幕（猎物是野生的，不是家畜），但血仍然必须被尊重——放出来、用土掩盖。血不能被吃——即使在田野里、即使没有祭司在场——因为"一切活物的血就是它的生命"。

弟兄姐妹，第十七章教导我们一个深刻的真理：生命是神圣的。血代表生命——所以血是神圣的。每一次流血都应该指向赎罪——而不是随意被消费。每一次杀生都应该在神面前进行——而不是在暗处献给偶像。生命不是我们的——它属于那位赐生命的神。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 17 has only sixteen verses, yet it opens Leviticus's second half (chs. 17-27, often called the "Holiness Code"). If the first sixteen chapters focused on "how to approach God" (sacrifice and purity), from chapter 17 the focus shifts to "how to live out holiness in daily life."

Chapter 17 addresses two core issues: (1) all animal slaughter must occur at the tabernacle entrance; (2) blood must never be eaten. These two seemingly simple regulations rest on one of Leviticus's most profound theological principles—the theology of blood.

Part one (vv. 3-9): all slaughter must be brought to the tabernacle.

> "If any one of the house of Israel kills an ox or a lamb or a goat in the camp, or kills it outside the camp, and does not bring it to the entrance of the tent of meeting to offer it as a gift to the LORD...bloodguilt shall be imputed to that man."

Brothers and sisters, this regulation was extremely strict—during the wilderness period, Israelites could not slaughter a sheep at their tent for dinner. Every slaughter had to be brought to the tabernacle entrance—every bloodshedding had to occur before the priest, at the LORD's altar. Why? Scripture gave two reasons:

First reason (vv. 5-6)—transforming slaughter into worship. "This is to the end that the people of Israel may bring their sacrifices...to the priest at the entrance of the tent of meeting." Brothers and sisters, when killing a sheep was not merely "eating meat" but "offering a peace offering before the LORD," every meal became worship. Blood sprinkled on the altar, fat burned for the LORD—you ate the remaining meat, but the best portions (blood and fat) belonged to God. Every meal reminded: life comes from God, food is God's gift, even eating should be offered in gratitude to God.

Second reason (v. 7)—preventing idolatry. "So they shall no more sacrifice their sacrifices to goat demons, after whom they whore." Brothers and sisters, Israel during four hundred years in Egypt had been contaminated by Egyptian idolatry—including field sacrifices to "goat demons" (se'irim, possibly referring to Egyptian goat deities or wilderness entities imagined as demons). If people could freely slaughter in the field—who could tell whether they were butchering sheep for dinner or sacrificing to demons? Centralizing all slaughter at the tabernacle entrance eliminated such covert idolatry.

Part two (vv. 10-16): the absolute prohibition against eating blood—one of Leviticus's most central theological declarations.

> "If any one of the house of Israel or of the strangers who sojourn among them eats any blood, I will set my face against that person...and will cut him off from among his people."

Brothers and sisters, "I will set my face against"—extraordinarily strong language. "Setting face against" meant God personally became that person's enemy. Why? The next verse provided Leviticus's most important theological statement:

> "For the life of the flesh is in the blood, and I have given it for you on the altar to make atonement for your souls, for it is the blood that makes atonement by the life."

Brothers and sisters, this verse (v. 11) is the entire Old Testament sacrificial theology's cornerstone. It answers a fundamental question: why could killing an animal atone for human sin? The answer—because the animal's life was in its blood. When blood flowed out, a life was surrendered. This life substituted for yours—"on the altar to make atonement for your souls." Substitution—this was atonement's core mechanism. Blood itself had no magical power—blood represented a surrendered life.

Because blood served this sacred function—atonement—it could not be used for ordinary purposes (like eating). Blood was God's gift to Israel for atonement—not for consumption. It belonged to the altar, not the table. Consuming blood desecrated its sacred function—like using communion cups as ordinary drinking glasses.

This prohibition extended to game—"When any one...takes in hunting any beast or bird that may be eaten, he shall pour out its blood and cover it with earth." Game need not be brought to the tabernacle (game was wild, not livestock), but blood still had to be respected—poured out and covered with earth. Blood could not be eaten—even in the field, even without a priest present—because "the life of every creature is its blood."

Brothers and sisters, chapter 17 teaches a profound truth: life is sacred. Blood represents life—therefore blood is sacred. Every bloodshedding should point toward atonement—not be casually consumed. Every killing should occur before God—not secretly offered to idols. Life is not ours—it belongs to the life-giving God.

## 历史背景（中文）

"公山羊"（se'irim，字面"毛茸茸的"）在以赛亚书13:21和34:14中指旷野中的恶魔或鬼怪。以色列人在埃及期间可能染上了向这些存在献祭的习惯——第十七章的集中宰杀规定就是为了断绝这种做法。关于吃血的禁令早于利未记——创世记9:4挪亚之约已经说"惟独肉带着血，那就是它的生命，你们不可吃"。利未记17:11把这个禁令深化为赎罪神学。犹太教至今严格遵守禁血令——kosher屠宰法（shechita）要求一刀切断颈部动脉使血尽量流尽，然后用盐处理肉以提取残余血液。早期教会在耶路撒冷会议（徒15:20，29）也保留了禁吃血的规定——"禁戒……血"——这是少数从旧约延续到新约外邦教会的仪式律。

## Historical Context (English)

"Goat demons" (se'irim, literally "hairy ones") in Isaiah 13:21 and 34:14 referred to wilderness demons or specters. Israel during their Egyptian sojourn likely adopted the practice of sacrificing to such beings—chapter 17's centralized slaughter regulation was designed to eliminate this practice. The blood-eating prohibition predated Leviticus—Genesis 9:4 in the Noahic covenant already stated: "But you shall not eat flesh with its life, that is, its blood." Leviticus 17:11 deepened this prohibition into atonement theology. Judaism to this day strictly observes the blood prohibition—kosher slaughter law (shechita) requires a single cut severing the neck arteries to drain blood as completely as possible, then salting the meat to extract residual blood. The early church at the Jerusalem Council (Acts 15:20, 29) also retained the blood-eating prohibition—"abstain from...blood"—one of the few ceremonial laws continued from the Old Testament into the Gentile church.

## 经文结构（中文）

**所有宰杀必须在会幕（3-7节）**——营内营外宰杀未带到会幕门口则流血的罪归他、要从民中剪除、目的：献给耶和华为平安祭、不可再献给鬼魔。

**燔祭平安祭也必须在会幕（8-9节）**——本地人和外人同样适用、不带到会幕则被剪除。

**绝对禁止吃血（10-12节）**——吃血者神必变脸并剪除、因活物生命在血中、血在坛上赎罪、以色列人和外人都不可吃。

**猎物的血处理（13-14节）**——放出血来用土掩盖、一切活物的血就是它的生命。

**吃自死物的规定（15-16节）**——吃了自死或被撕裂的不洁净到晚上、洗衣洗身、不洗则担罪。

## Structure (English)

**All Slaughter Must Be at Tabernacle (vv. 3-7)**—Killing in or outside camp without bringing to tabernacle entrance incurs bloodguilt, cut off; purpose: offer to LORD as peace offerings, no more sacrificing to goat demons.

**Burnt and Peace Offerings Also at Tabernacle (vv. 8-9)**—Applies to native and sojourner alike, failure to bring means cut off.

**Absolute Blood-Eating Prohibition (vv. 10-12)**—God sets face against blood-eater and cuts off, life of flesh is in blood, blood given for altar atonement, neither Israelite nor sojourner may eat.

**Game Blood Handling (vv. 13-14)**—Pour out blood cover with earth, all creature life is its blood.

**Found Dead Animals (vv. 15-16)**—Eating naturally dead or torn animals makes unclean until evening, must wash clothes and bathe, failure to wash bears iniquity.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"活物的生命是在血中"——替代赎罪的神学基础**
利未记17:11是旧约赎罪神学的基石——改革宗传统将替代性赎罪（penal substitutionary atonement）视为福音的核心。韦敏斯德信条第八章论基督为中保教导：主耶稣极其乐意担任这职分，祂完全成全了律法，充分地满足了父的公义。动物的血代替人的生命献在坛上——预表基督的血代替罪人献给神。生命换生命——这是恩典之约的核心逻辑。

**集中崇拜——敬拜的规范性原则**
所有宰杀必须在会幕——改革宗传统的"敬拜规范性原则"（Regulative Principle）在此有根基：敬拜必须按照神所吩咐的方式进行，不可自行发明。韦敏斯德信条第21章论敬拜：可蒙悦纳的敬拜神的方法，乃是神自己所设立的。不能在田野里私自献祭——必须在神所指定的地方、按神所定的方式。

**血的神圣性——不可被世俗化**
血不可吃因为它被分别归为赎罪之用——改革宗传统教导圣物不可世俗化。犹太教的kosher传统保持了这一原则至今。血属于坛、属于神——不属于人的肚子。

## Theology (English)

**"The Life of the Flesh Is in the Blood"—Substitutionary Atonement's Foundation**
Leviticus 17:11 is Old Testament atonement theology's cornerstone—the Reformed tradition views penal substitutionary atonement as the gospel's core. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ as Mediator: "The Lord Jesus, by His perfect obedience and sacrifice of Himself...fully satisfied the justice of His Father." Animal blood substituting for human life on the altar—prefiguring Christ's blood substituting for sinners before God. Life for life—this is the covenant of grace's core logic.

**Centralized Worship—The Regulative Principle**
All slaughter must be at the tabernacle—the Reformed tradition's Regulative Principle of worship finds foundation here: worship must follow God's prescribed methods, not human invention. Westminster Confession chapter 21 on worship: "The acceptable way of worshipping the true God is instituted by Himself." No private field sacrifices—must be at God's designated place, in God's prescribed manner.

**Blood's Sacredness—Must Not Be Secularized**
Blood is inedible because it was set apart for atonement—the Reformed tradition teaches holy things must not be secularized. Judaism's kosher tradition has maintained this principle to this day. Blood belongs to the altar, to God—not to the human stomach.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记17:11"因为活物的生命是在血中……因血里有生命，所以能赎罪"——这节经文是理解十字架的钥匙。

基督的血是终极的赎罪之血。希伯来书9:22"若不流血，罪就不得赦免了。"旧约的动物之血年年流出——只能暂时遮盖罪。基督的血一次流出——永远除去罪。希伯来书9:12"乃用自己的血，只一次进入圣所，成了永远赎罪的事。"

"我把这血赐给你们"——弟兄姐妹，血是神"赐给"以色列的。赎罪不是人想出来的办法——是神主动提供的恩典。约翰福音3:16"神爱世人，甚至将他的独生子赐给他们。"旧约神赐牛羊的血——新约神赐自己儿子的血。恩典从来都是由上而下的礼物。

不可吃血——但基督设立圣餐时说"这杯是用我的血所立的新约，你们每逢喝的时候，为的是记念我"（林前11:25）。弟兄姐妹，旧约禁止吃血因为血是用来赎罪的；新约的圣餐杯正是因为基督的血已经完成了赎罪——所以我们"喝"这杯来记念祂。圣餐不是违反了利未记17章——圣餐是利未记17章的终极成全。

集中宰杀在会幕——基督就是最终的"会幕"（约1:14"道成了肉身，住在我们中间"，"住"字原文就是"支搭帐幕"）。一切真正的敬拜都必须通过基督——祂是唯一的道路（约14:6"我就是道路、真理、生命；若不藉着我，没有人能到父那里去"）。不能绕过基督自行到神面前——正如不能绕过会幕在田野里私自献祭。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 17:11—"For the life of the flesh is in the blood...it is the blood that makes atonement by the life"—this verse is the key to understanding the cross.

Christ's blood is the ultimate atoning blood. Hebrews 9:22: "Without the shedding of blood there is no forgiveness of sins." Old Testament animal blood flowed annually—only temporarily covering sin. Christ's blood flowed once—permanently removing sin. Hebrews 9:12: "He entered once for all into the holy places...by means of his own blood, thus securing an eternal redemption."

"I have given it for you"—Brothers and sisters, blood was God's "gift" to Israel. Atonement was not a human invention—it was grace God proactively provided. John 3:16: "God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son." In the Old Testament God gave cattle and sheep's blood—in the New Testament God gave His own Son's blood. Grace has always been a top-down gift.

Blood must not be eaten—yet when Christ instituted the Lord's Supper He said: "After the same manner also he took the cup, when he had supped, saying, This cup is the new testament in my blood: this do ye, as oft as ye drink it, in remembrance of me." (1 Cor 11:25). Brothers and sisters, the Old Testament prohibited eating blood because blood was for atonement; the New Testament communion cup exists precisely because Christ's blood has completed atonement—so we "drink" this cup remembering Him. The Lord's Supper does not violate Leviticus 17—the Lord's Supper is Leviticus 17's ultimate fulfillment.

Centralized slaughter at the tabernacle—Christ is the ultimate "tabernacle" (John 1:14: "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us"—"dwelt" literally means "tabernacled"). All true worship must go through Christ—He is the only way (John 14:6: "I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me"). One cannot bypass Christ to approach God independently—just as one could not bypass the tabernacle to sacrifice privately in the field.
