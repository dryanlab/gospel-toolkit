## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十一章专门论到祭司的圣洁标准——比普通以色列人更高、更严格的标准。如果说第十九章的圣洁要求是对所有以色列人的，第二十一章的圣洁要求是特别对祭司的。祭司站在神和百姓之间——他越靠近神，对他的要求就越高。

第二十一章分为三个层次——普通祭司（1-9节）、大祭司（10-15节）、有残疾的祭司（16-24节）——圣洁的标准层层递进。

第一层：普通祭司的限制（1-9节）。

关于接触死人——"祭司不可为民中的死人沾染自己。"弟兄姐妹，在利未记的世界观中，死亡是不洁净的终极来源——接触死尸导致最严重的不洁净（民19章详述）。普通以色列人接触死尸后经过七天的洁净礼可以恢复——但祭司要尽量避免接触死亡。

例外是至亲——父母、儿女、弟兄和未嫁的姐妹。弟兄姐妹，这个例外显示了律法的人性——神不要求祭司在父母去世时无动于衷。你可以为至亲哀悼——这是人之常情，神不否认它。但范围严格限定——亲戚、朋友、邻居的葬礼不可以参加。

关于哀悼方式——"不可使头光秃；不可剃除胡须的周围，也不可用刀划身。"弟兄姐妹，剃光头、剃胡须、用刀划身——这些是迦南人的异教哀悼仪式。以色列的祭司不可以用异教的方式表达哀伤——你的悲痛是真实的，但表达悲痛的方式必须与你圣洁的身份一致。

关于婚姻——"不可娶妓女或被污的女人为妻，也不可娶被休的妇人为妻。"弟兄姐妹，普通以色列人可以娶被休的妇人（申24:1-4规定了离婚的程序），但祭司不可以。祭司的婚姻标准更高——因为他的家庭也是"归神为圣"的。

第二层：大祭司的限制（10-15节）——比普通祭司更加严格。

"不可挨近死尸，也不可为父母沾染自己。"弟兄姐妹，普通祭司可以为至亲的死沾染自己——大祭司连父母去世都不可以。大祭司在世上没有任何一个人的死亡可以使他停下圣所的事奉。他的职分高于他的至亲关系——因为"神膏油的冠冕在他头上"。

"不可蓬头散发，也不可撕裂衣服"——这些是哀悼的标准动作（参利13:45），但大祭司不可以做。他必须时刻保持庄严——因为他身上的圣衣代表了以色列在神面前的身份。

大祭司的婚姻限制更严——"只可娶本民中的处女为妻"。不可娶寡妇、被休的妇人、妓女。弟兄姐妹，大祭司的妻子将来会生出下一代祭司——祭司家族的血脉必须无可指摘。

第三层：有残疾的祭司（16-24节）——这是第二十一章中最令现代人困惑的段落。

"凡有残疾的，都不可近前来献他神的食物。"然后列出了一长串残疾——瞎眼、瘸腿、塌鼻子、肢体有余、折脚折手、驼背、矮矬、眼有毛病、长癣、长疥、损坏肾子。

弟兄姐妹，这条规定不意味着残疾人在神面前的价值更低。经文明确说："神的食物，无论是圣的、至圣的，他都可以吃。"有残疾的祭司仍然是祭司——他仍然享有祭司的一切权利和福利（吃圣物）。他只是不能在坛前执行献祭的仪式——不能进入幔子前。

为什么？因为献祭的祭物必须是"没有残疾的"（利1:3），献祭物的人也必须在身体上"完整"——这是一个象征性的要求：在神面前呈献的一切都必须是"完整的""完美的"。不是因为残疾是罪——而是因为献祭的仪式要求象征性的完整，指向那位真正完美的大祭司。

弟兄姐妹，第二十一章的核心原则是：越靠近神，标准越高。普通人有普通人的圣洁标准（19章），祭司有更高的标准（21章），大祭司有最高的标准。这不是因为神偏心——而是因为"多给谁就向谁多取"（路12:48）。祭司的特权（吃圣物、在圣所事奉、作百姓和神之间的中保）与他的限制成正比——权利越大，责任越大。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 21 specifically addressed priestly holiness standards—higher, stricter than those for ordinary Israelites. If chapter 19's holiness requirements were for all Israel, chapter 21's were specifically for priests. Priests stood between God and the people—the closer to God, the higher the demands.

Chapter 21 had three tiers—ordinary priests (vv. 1-9), high priest (vv. 10-15), priests with physical defects (vv. 16-24)—holiness standards progressively ascending.

First tier: ordinary priests' restrictions (vv. 1-9).

Regarding contact with the dead—"No one shall make himself unclean for the dead among his people." Brothers and sisters, in Leviticus's worldview, death was the ultimate source of impurity—corpse contact caused the most severe uncleanness (Numbers 19 details). Ordinary Israelites could restore purity through seven-day purification after corpse contact—but priests were to avoid death contact as much as possible.

Exceptions were immediate family—parents, children, brothers, and unmarried sisters. Brothers and sisters, this exception showed the law's humanity—God did not require priests to be unmoved by parents' death. You could mourn for immediate family—this was human nature, and God did not deny it. But the scope was strictly limited—relatives', friends', neighbors' funerals were off-limits.

Regarding mourning practices—"They shall not make bald patches on their heads, nor shave off the edges of their beards, nor make any cuts on their body." Brothers and sisters, head-shaving, beard-shaving, body-cutting—these were Canaanite pagan mourning rites. Israel's priests could not express grief in pagan ways—your sorrow was real, but sorrow's expression had to align with your holy identity.

Regarding marriage—"They shall not marry a prostitute or a woman who has been defiled, nor shall they marry a woman divorced from her husband." Brothers and sisters, ordinary Israelites could marry divorcees (Deut 24:1-4 established divorce procedures), but priests could not. Priestly marriage standards were higher—because their families were also "holy to God."

Second tier: high priest's restrictions (vv. 10-15)—stricter than ordinary priests.

> "He shall not go in to any dead bodies nor make himself unclean, even for his father or for his mother." Brothers and sisters, ordinary priests could become unclean for immediate family's death—the high priest could not even for parents. No person's death in the world could halt the high priest's sanctuary service. His office transcended kinship—because "the anointing oil of his God is upon him."

"He shall not let the hair of his head hang loose or tear his clothes"—standard mourning actions (cf. Lev 13:45), but forbidden for the high priest. He must maintain dignity always—because his holy garments represented Israel's identity before God.

High priestly marriage was even more restricted—"He shall take as his wife a virgin from his own people." No widows, divorcees, prostitutes. Brothers and sisters, the high priest's wife would bear the next generation of priests—the priestly line had to be beyond reproach.

Third tier: priests with physical defects (vv. 16-24)—chapter 21's most perplexing section for moderns.

"No man...who has a blemish shall draw near to offer the LORD's food offerings." Then a long list of defects—blind, lame, mutilated face, limb too long, broken foot or hand, hunchbacked, dwarfed, eye defect, itching disease, scabs, crushed testicles.

Brothers and sisters, this regulation did not mean disabled persons had less value before God. Scripture explicitly stated: "He may eat the bread of his God, both of the most holy and of the holy." Defective priests remained priests—retaining all priestly rights and benefits (eating holy food). They simply could not perform sacrificial rituals at the altar—could not approach the veil.

Why? Because sacrificial animals had to be "If his offering be a burnt sacrifice of the herd, let him offer a male without blemish: he shall offer it of his own voluntary will at the door of the tabernacle of the congregation before the LORD." (Lev 1:3), and the one presenting them had to be physically "complete"—a symbolic requirement: everything presented before God had to be "whole" and "perfect." Not because disability was sin—but because sacrificial ritual required symbolic completeness, pointing to the truly perfect High Priest.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 21's core principle: the closer to God, the higher the standard. Ordinary people had their holiness standard (ch. 19), priests had a higher one (ch. 21), the high priest had the highest. This was not God playing favorites—but "But he that knew not, and did commit things worthy of stripes, shall be beaten with few stripes. For unto whomsoever much is given, of him shall be much required: and to whom men have committed much, of him they will ask the more." (Luke 12:48). Priestly privileges (eating holy food, serving in the sanctuary, mediating between people and God) were proportional to restrictions—greater rights, greater responsibility.

## 历史背景（中文）

古代近东其他文化的祭司也有类似的限制——巴比伦和埃及的祭司在执行仪式期间必须保持洁净，避免接触死尸。但以色列的祭司限制有一个独特的维度：它们是终身性的（不只是仪式期间），因为以色列的祭司是终身圣职。对残疾祭司的限制在古代并不罕见——美索不达米亚的divination priests（占卜祭司）也必须身体完整。大祭司不为父母哀悼的规定在犹太传统中引起了很多讨论——米示拿规定如果大祭司遇到无人认领的尸体（met mitzvah），他仍有义务安葬（因为无人安葬是更大的亵渎）。"不可用刀划身"——这种异教哀悼方式在列王纪上18:28中被巴力先知使用（"他们就按着自己的规矩，用刀枪自割"）。以利亚对抗巴力先知的迦密山事件展示了以色列祭司和异教祭司之间的根本差异。

## Historical Context (English)

Ancient Near Eastern priests in other cultures had similar restrictions—Babylonian and Egyptian priests had to maintain purity during ritual performance, avoiding corpse contact. But Israel's priestly restrictions had a unique dimension: they were lifelong (not just during ceremonies), because Israel's priesthood was a lifelong sacred office. Restrictions on defective priests were not uncommon in antiquity—Mesopotamian divination priests also required physical completeness. The high priest's prohibition against mourning for parents generated much rabbinic discussion—the Mishnah ruled that if the high priest encountered an unattended corpse (met mitzvah), he was still obligated to bury it (since non-burial was a greater desecration). "No cuts on the body"—this pagan mourning practice was used by Baal prophets in 1 Kings 18:28 ("they cut themselves after their custom with swords and lances"). Elijah's Mount Carmel confrontation demonstrated the fundamental difference between Israelite and pagan priests.

## 经文结构（中文）

**普通祭司的限制（1-9节）**——不可为死人沾染（至亲例外）、不可剃头剃胡刀划身、不可娶妓女被污被休妇、祭司女儿行淫则火烧。

**大祭司的限制（10-15节）**——不可蓬头散发撕衣、不可挨近任何死尸（连父母也不可）、不可出圣所、只可娶本民处女。

**有残疾的祭司（16-24节）**——瞎瘸塌鼻残肢折手脚驼背矮矬眼疾癣疥损肾都不可近前献祭、但可以吃圣物。

## Structure (English)

**Ordinary Priests' Restrictions (vv. 1-9)**—No corpse defilement (immediate family excepted), no head-shaving beard-shaving body-cutting, no marrying prostitute/defiled/divorced women, priest's daughter who prostitutes herself burned.

**High Priest's Restrictions (vv. 10-15)**—No disheveled hair or torn clothes, no approaching any corpse (not even parents), must not leave sanctuary, may marry only virgin from own people.

**Priests with Defects (vv. 16-24)**—Blind lame mutilated-face extra-limbed broken-hand-foot hunchbacked dwarfed eye-defective scabbed crushed-testicle all may not approach to offer; but may eat holy food.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"多给谁就向谁多取"——特权与责任的平衡**
祭司比普通人有更多限制——改革宗传统教导教会领袖负有更高的道德责任。雅各书3:1"我的弟兄们，不要多人作师傅，因为晓得我们要受更重的判断。"提摩太前书3:1-7保罗对监督（长老）的资格要求也回应了利未记21章——"无可指责……有节制……不因酒滋事"。

**象征性的完整——指向完美的大祭司**
祭物必须无残疾、祭司也必须身体完整——改革宗传统理解这为"影子"，指向基督这位完美的大祭司。韦敏斯德信条第8章论基督为中保"完全的顺服"。旧约的象征性完整是暂时的和外在的——基督的完美是实质的和内在的。

**大祭司不为父母哀悼——完全奉献的代价**
大祭司的职分超越至亲关系——改革宗传统理解这指向基督的完全顺服和奉献。马太福音12:48-50耶稣说"谁是我的母亲？谁是我的弟兄？"——基督的事奉超越了自然家庭关系。

## Theology (English)

**"To Whom Much Is Given, Much Is Required"—Privilege and Responsibility Balanced**
Priests had more restrictions than ordinary people—the Reformed tradition teaches church leaders bear higher moral responsibility. James 3:1: "Not many of you should become teachers, for you know that we who teach will be judged with greater strictness." 1 Timothy 3:1-7's qualifications for overseers (elders) echo Leviticus 21—"above reproach...sober-minded...not a drunkard."

**Symbolic Completeness—Pointing to the Perfect High Priest**
Offerings must be unblemished, priests must be physically complete—the Reformed tradition understands these as "shadows" pointing to Christ the perfect High Priest. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ as Mediator's: perfect obedience. Old Testament symbolic completeness was temporary and external—Christ's perfection is substantial and internal.

**High Priest Cannot Mourn Parents—Total Consecration's Cost**
The high priestly office transcended kinship—the Reformed tradition understands this as pointing to Christ's total obedience and consecration. Matthew 12:48-50, Jesus said "Who is my mother, and who are my brothers?"—Christ's ministry transcended natural family ties.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第二十一章的祭司标准在基督身上得到了完美的成全——祂是那位真正完美的、完全奉献的大祭司。

基督是没有残疾的大祭司——希伯来书7:26"像这样圣洁、无邪恶、无玷污、远离罪人、高过诸天的大祭司，原是与我们合宜的。"利未记21章要求祭司身体完整是一个象征——基督在道德和属灵上完全无瑕无疵。旧约的祭司有身体的限制——基督没有任何限制。

基督超越了至亲关系——大祭司不能为父母哀悼，因为职分高于亲情。基督在十字架上对母亲说"妇人，看你的儿子！"（约19:26-27）——祂在执行大祭司的终极职务（为全人类赎罪）的同时，仍然照顾了母亲。祂比旧约的大祭司更完美——不是因为忽略亲情来完成职分，而是在完成职分的同时成全了亲情。

有残疾的祭司"可以吃圣物但不可献祭"——基督接纳了所有有"残疾"的人进入祂的国度。马太福音15:30"有许多人到他那里，带着瘸子、瞎子、哑巴、有残疾的，和好些别的病人。"利未记21章把有残疾的人排除在祭坛之外——基督把他们带到祂面前医治。旧约看外表——基督看内心（撒上16:7"人是看外貌；耶和华是看内心"）。

基督的婚姻——大祭司只娶处女。以弗所书5:25-27"基督爱教会，为教会舍己。要用水借着道把教会洗净，成为圣洁，可以献给自己，作个荣耀的教会，毫无玷污、皱纹等类的病。"基督的"新娘"（教会）被祂的血洗净——成为圣洁无瑕疵的"处女"。不是因为教会本身完美——而是因为基督使她完美。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 21's priestly standards found perfect fulfillment in Christ—the truly perfect, fully consecrated High Priest.

Christ is the unblemished High Priest—Hebrews 7:26: "For it was indeed fitting that we should have such a high priest, holy, innocent, unstained, separated from sinners, and exalted above the heavens." Leviticus 21's physical completeness requirement was symbolic—Christ was morally and spiritually flawless. Old Testament priests had physical limitations—Christ had none.

Christ transcended kinship—the high priest could not mourn parents because office exceeded family. Christ on the cross said to His mother "When Jesus therefore saw his mother, and the disciple standing by, whom he loved, he saith unto his mother, Woman, behold thy son! Then saith he to the disciple, Behold thy mother! And from that hour that disciple took her unto his own home." (John 19:26-27)—while performing the ultimate high priestly duty (atoning for all humanity), He still cared for His mother. He surpassed the Old Testament high priest—not by neglecting family to complete His office, but by fulfilling family duty while completing His office.

Defective priests "may eat holy food but may not offer sacrifice"—Christ welcomed all "defective" persons into His kingdom. Matthew 15:30: "Great crowds came to him, bringing with them the lame, the blind, the mute, the crippled, and many others." Leviticus 21 excluded the defective from the altar—Christ brought them before Himself for healing. The Old Testament looked at appearance—Christ looked at the heart (1 Sam 16:7: "Man looks on the outward appearance, but the LORD looks on the heart").

Christ's marriage—the high priest married only a virgin. Ephesians 5:25-27: "Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her, that he might sanctify her, having cleansed her by the washing of water with the word, so that he might present the church to himself in splendor, without spot or wrinkle." Christ's "bride" (the church) was washed clean by His blood—made a holy, spotless "virgin." Not because the church was inherently perfect—but because Christ made her perfect.
