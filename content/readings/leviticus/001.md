## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。出埃及记以耶和华的荣光充满会幕结束——利未记以耶和华从会幕中呼叫我开始。出埃及记的最后一节说我不能进入会幕（因为荣光太满了）；利未记的第一节说神从会幕中向我说话。不能进去——但神的声音从里面出来。弟兄姐妹，这就是利未记的起点：神已经住在以色列中间了，现在的问题是——罪人怎样才能靠近这位圣洁的神？

利未记的答案是：通过献祭。第一章介绍的是最基本的祭——燔祭（olah，意思是"上升"，指全部烧成烟升上去）。

> "你们中间若有人献供物给耶和华，要从牛群羊群中献牲畜为供物。"

弟兄姐妹，注意"若有人"——燔祭是自愿的（第四章的赎罪祭是必须的）。这是一个人发自内心地想要亲近神、向神表达奉献和感恩时所献的祭。

燔祭有三个等级——公牛（最贵）、绵羊或山羊（中等）、鸟——斑鸠或雏鸽（最便宜）。弟兄姐妹，这三个等级不是说神偏爱富人——恰恰相反，这是神的怜悯：无论你多穷，你都可以献燔祭。即使买不起牛羊，两只鸽子也可以。每个人——从最富到最穷——都有资格亲近神。

让我走过燔祭的整个流程——以公牛为例：

第一步："他要按手在燔祭牲的头上，燔祭便蒙悦纳，为他赎罪。"

按手——semikah。这不是轻轻触碰，而是把全身重量压在牲畜头上。按手的意义是：我的罪转移到这只牲畜上，它代替我死。这是替代的核心行为——我本该死，但这只无辜的公牛替我死。弟兄姐妹，每一次按手都是一个无声的宣告："我是罪人，它替我受罚。"

第二步："他要在耶和华面前宰公牛。"

"他要宰"——不是祭司宰，而是献祭的人自己宰。弟兄姐妹，这意味着每一个以色列人都必须亲手杀死那替代自己的牲畜。你不能站在远处、让别人替你完成这个肮脏的工作。你必须亲手拿刀、亲手宰杀、亲眼看到血流出来。这是要让你深刻地感受到：罪的代价是死亡，而这个死亡本来属于你。

第三步："亚伦子孙作祭司的，要奉上血，把血洒在会幕门口、坛的周围。"

祭司负责处理血——把血洒在坛的周围。血是生命（利17:11"因为活物的生命是在血中"），洒血意味着生命被倾倒出来——替代的生命代替了罪人的生命。

第四步：剥皮、切块、洗脏腑和腿，然后全部烧在坛上。

"全烧在坛上"——这是燔祭的独特之处。其他祭（素祭、平安祭）祭司和献祭者可以分享一部分——但燔祭是全部烧掉的。没有任何部分留给人。一切都化为烟升上去——"馨香的火祭"。

弟兄姐妹，"馨香"——re'ach nichoach，字面意思是"安息的气味"或"使人满意的气味"。不是说神喜欢闻烤肉的味道——而是说这个献祭使神得到"安息"——祂的公义得到满足，祂的怒气得到平息，关系得到恢复。

整个过程强调了两个原则：

第一，"没有残疾的"。牲畜必须是完美的——没有瘸腿、没有瞎眼、没有皮肤病。你不能把自己不要的次品献给神——你献的必须是最好的。玛拉基书1:8后来斥责以色列人"你们将瞎眼的献为祭物，这不为恶么？将瘸腿的、有病的献上，这不为恶么？"神要求的是最好的——因为祂给我们的也是最好的。

第二，"在耶和华面前"——这个短语在短短十七节中反复出现。一切都发生在神面前——宰杀、洒血、焚烧，全在神的注视下。献祭不是一个私人的、隐秘的行为——它在神面前公开进行。罪也是如此——我们以为罪是隐秘的，但它在神面前从来都是公开的。

弟兄姐妹，利未记第一章看起来只是在讲宰杀动物的技术细节——但每一个细节都在大声宣告：罪是严重的，代价是昂贵的，赎罪需要无辜者的死亡，而神在这一切中既是公义的审判者也是恩慈的提供者。祂要求祭物——但祂也提供了祭物。以色列人的牛羊从何而来？是神赐给他们的（从埃及人那里得到的财物，在旷野中牧养的牲畜）。神先给予，然后从祂给予的当中要求回报——这是恩典的循环。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Exodus ended with the LORD's glory filling the tabernacle—Leviticus begins with the LORD calling to me from the tabernacle. Exodus's final verse said I could not enter the tabernacle (because the glory was too full); Leviticus's first verse says God spoke to me from within the tabernacle. Unable to enter—but God's voice came from inside. Brothers and sisters, this is Leviticus's starting point: God already dwells among Israel; now the question is—how can sinners approach this holy God?

Leviticus's answer: through sacrifice. Chapter 1 introduces the most fundamental offering—the burnt offering (olah, meaning "ascending," referring to everything burned into smoke rising upward).

> "When any one of you brings an offering to the LORD, you shall bring your offering of livestock from the herd or from the flock."

Brothers and sisters, note "any one of you"—the burnt offering was voluntary (the sin offering of chapter 4 was mandatory). This was what a person offered from a heartfelt desire to draw near to God, expressing dedication and gratitude.

The burnt offering had three tiers—a bull (most expensive), a sheep or goat (moderate), or a bird—turtledove or pigeon (least expensive). Brothers and sisters, these three tiers did not mean God favored the wealthy—quite the opposite, this was God's compassion: no matter how poor you were, you could still offer a burnt offering. Even if you could not afford cattle or sheep, two pigeons would suffice. Everyone—from richest to poorest—qualified to approach God.

Let me walk through the entire burnt offering process—using a bull as example:

First: "He shall lay his hand on the head of the burnt offering, and it shall be accepted for him to make atonement for him."

Laying on of hands—semikah. This was not a light touch but pressing one's full weight on the animal's head. The significance: my sin is transferred onto this animal; it dies in my place. This was the core act of substitution—I should die, but this innocent bull dies for me. Brothers and sisters, every laying of hands was a silent declaration: "I am a sinner; it is punished in my place."

Second: "He shall kill the bull before the LORD."

"He shall kill"—not the priest but the offerer himself. Brothers and sisters, this meant every Israelite had to personally slaughter the animal substituting for them. You could not stand at a distance, letting someone else complete this bloody work. You had to personally take the knife, personally slaughter, personally watch the blood flow. This was to make you deeply feel: sin's cost is death, and that death originally belonged to you.

Third: "Aaron's sons the priests shall bring the blood and throw the blood against the sides of the altar that is at the entrance of the tent of meeting."

Priests handled the blood—throwing it against the altar's sides. Blood was life (Leviticus 17:11: "The life of the flesh is in the blood"); sprinkling blood meant life poured out—substitute life replacing the sinner's life.

Fourth: skin the animal, cut it into pieces, wash the entrails and legs, then burn everything on the altar.

> "Burn all of it on the altar"—the burnt offering's distinctive feature. Other offerings (grain, peace) allowed the priest and offerer to share portions—but the burnt offering was entirely consumed. Nothing was retained for humans. Everything became smoke ascending—"a food offering, a pleasing aroma to the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, "pleasing aroma"—re'ach nichoach, literally "a scent of rest" or "a satisfying scent." Not that God enjoyed the smell of roasting meat—but that this sacrifice gave God "rest"—His justice was satisfied, His wrath was appeased, the relationship was restored.

The entire process emphasized two principles:

First, "without blemish." The animal had to be perfect—no lameness, no blindness, no skin disease. You could not offer God the rejects you didn't want—you offered your best. Malachi 1:8 later rebuked Israel: "When you offer blind animals in sacrifice, is that not evil? And when you offer those that are lame or sick, is that not evil?" God requires the best—because what He gives us is the best.

Second, "before the LORD"—this phrase recurs repeatedly in seventeen short verses. Everything happened before God—slaughter, blood, burning, all under God's gaze. Sacrifice was not a private, secret act—it was publicly performed before God. Sin is the same—we think sin is hidden, but before God it was never concealed.

Brothers and sisters, Leviticus chapter 1 may appear to discuss only technical details of slaughtering animals—but every detail loudly proclaims: sin is serious, the cost is steep, atonement requires an innocent one's death, and God throughout is both the righteous Judge and the gracious Provider. He requires the offering—but He also provides it. Where did Israel's cattle and sheep come from? God gave them (possessions from the Egyptians, livestock tended in the wilderness). God first gives, then asks back from what He gave—this is the cycle of grace.

## 历史背景（中文）

"耶和华从会幕中呼叫摩西"——与出埃及记40章的无缝衔接。在希伯来圣经中，利未记被称为"Vayikra"（"他呼叫"），取自第一个词。燔祭（olah）是古代近东最普遍的祭祀形式——在乌加里特、美索不达米亚和埃及都有完全焚烧牲畜的祭祀记录。但以色列的燔祭有两个独特之处：(1)按手——罪的象征性转移在其他文化中极为罕见；(2)只献给一位神——排他性的一神论崇拜。"坛的北边"宰杀（v.11）——北方在以色列传统中与审判有关系（耶利米书1:14"必有灾祸从北方发出"）。斑鸠和雏鸽作为穷人的祭物——路加福音2:24记载约瑟和马利亚在圣殿奉献"一对斑鸠或两只雏鸽"——耶稣的家庭经济上属于社会的低层。

## Historical Context (English)

"The LORD called Moses from the tent of meeting"—seamlessly connecting with Exodus 40. In the Hebrew Bible, Leviticus is called "Vayikra" ("He called"), from its first word. The burnt offering (olah) was the most widespread sacrificial form in the ancient Near East—records of completely burning animals appear in Ugarit, Mesopotamia, and Egypt. But Israel's burnt offering had two unique features: (1) laying on of hands—symbolic sin transfer was extremely rare in other cultures; (2) offered to only one God—exclusive monotheistic worship. Slaughter "on the north side of the altar" (v.11)—the north in Israelite tradition was associated with judgment (Jeremiah 1:14: "Out of the north disaster shall be let loose"). Turtledoves and pigeons as the poor's offering—Luke 2:24 records Joseph and Mary offering "a pair of turtledoves, or two young pigeons" at the temple—Jesus' family was economically in society's lower tier.

## 经文结构（中文）

**从会幕中的呼召（1-2节）**——耶和华从会幕中呼叫摩西、你们中间若有人献供物。

**公牛的燔祭（3-9节）**——没有残疾的公牛、按手在头上赎罪、在耶和华面前宰杀、血洒坛周围、剥皮切块洗脏腑、全烧在坛上、馨香的火祭。

**绵羊或山羊的燔祭（10-13节）**——没有残疾的公羊、宰于坛北边、血洒坛周围、切块摆在火柴上、脏腑腿用水洗、全然焚烧、馨香的火祭。

**鸟的燔祭（14-17节）**——斑鸠或雏鸽（穷人的祭物）、揪下头、血流坛旁、除嗉子脏物、撕开不撕断、焚烧、馨香的火祭。

## Structure (English)

**The Call from the Tabernacle (vv. 1-2)**—The LORD calling Moses from the tent of meeting, when anyone brings an offering.

**Bull Burnt Offering (vv. 3-9)**—An unblemished bull, laying hand on its head for atonement, killed before the LORD, blood thrown against altar sides, skinned cut washed entrails, burned entirely on altar, a pleasing aroma.

**Sheep or Goat Burnt Offering (vv. 10-13)**—An unblemished male, killed on altar's north side, blood against altar sides, cut in pieces laid on fire, entrails and legs washed, burned entirely, a pleasing aroma.

**Bird Burnt Offering (vv. 14-17)**—Turtledove or pigeon (the poor's offering), head wrung off, blood drained beside altar, crop and contents removed, torn open but not severed, burned, a pleasing aroma.

## 神学要点（中文）

**按手——替代赎罪的核心**
按手在燔祭牲头上——改革宗传统将此理解为刑罚替代（penal substitution）教义的旧约仪式表达。韦敏斯德信条第十一章论称义教导："不是将义注入他们里面……乃是把基督的顺服和所成就的义归算给他们。"罪被转移（归算）到替代者身上——正如以色列人按手将罪转移到牲畜身上。

**三个等级——恩典的可及性**
公牛、羊、鸟——三个经济等级都可以献祭。改革宗传统强调"恩典的可及性"：福音是给所有人的，不分贫富。韦敏斯德小要理问答第31问论有效恩召："有效的恩召是神的灵所完成的工作，使我们觉悟自己的罪恶和苦境，又光照我们的心使我们认识基督"——神的呼召不是根据经济或社会地位来拣选。两只鸽子的献祭和一头公牛的献祭在神面前有同样的"馨香"——因为神看的是心，不是钱包。

**全烧——完全的奉献**
燔祭全部烧掉，什么都不留给人——代表完全的献身。罗马书12:1"将身体献上，当作活祭"——基督徒的生命应当像燔祭一样完全献上。改革宗传统强调"生活的每一个方面都在神的主权之下"——不是只把周日给神、其余六天自己支配，而是全部——包括工作、家庭、思想——都献给神。

## Theology (English)

**Laying on of Hands—The Core of Substitutionary Atonement**
Laying hands on the burnt offering's head—the Reformed tradition understands this as the Old Testament ritual expression of penal substitution. Westminster Confession chapter 11 on justification: Not by infusing righteousness into them, but by...imputing the obedience and satisfaction of Christ unto them. Sin was transferred (imputed) to the substitute—just as Israelites transferred sin to the animal by laying on hands.

**Three Tiers—The Accessibility of Grace**
Bull, sheep, bird—all three economic levels could sacrifice. The Reformed tradition emphasizes "the accessibility of grace": the gospel is for everyone regardless of wealth. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q31 on effectual calling: "God calls all His elect"—not selecting based on economic or social status. Two pigeons and one bull offered the same "pleasing aroma" before God—because God sees the heart, not the wallet.

**Burned Entirely—Complete Dedication**
The burnt offering was entirely consumed; nothing was retained for humans—representing complete dedication. Romans 12:1: "Present your bodies as a living sacrifice." The Christian life should be completely offered like a burnt offering. The Reformed tradition emphasizes "every area of life is under God's sovereignty"—not giving God only Sunday while keeping six days for oneself, but everything—work, family, thoughts—offered to God.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第一章的燔祭是对基督十字架最详细的旧约预表之一。

"没有残疾的公牛"——基督是"无瑕疵无玷污的羔羊"（彼前1:19）。献祭的牲畜必须完美——基督的完美不只是外在的（没有身体残疾），更是内在的（完全无罪）。希伯来书4:15："他也曾凡事受过试探，与我们一样，只是他没有犯罪。"

"按手在燔祭牲的头上"——以赛亚书53:6："耶和华使我们众人的罪孽都归在他身上。"基督在十字架上，全人类的罪被"按手"转移到祂身上。哥林多后书5:21"神使那无罪的，替我们成为罪。"

"他要在耶和华面前宰公牛"——献祭者亲手宰杀。使徒行传2:23彼得对犹太人说："你们就藉着无法之人的手，把他钉在十字架上，杀了。"是人类亲手杀了基督——每一个罪人都是共犯。

"全烧在坛上"——基督在十字架上完全献出自己，没有保留。腓立比书2:8"存心顺服，以至于死，且死在十字架上。"不是半死——而是完全的、彻底的、毫无保留的死亡。

"馨香的火祭"——以弗所书5:2"基督爱我们，为我们舍了自己，当作馨香的供物和祭物，献与神。"基督的死在父面前是"馨香"的——因为它完全满足了公义的要求，使父的怒气得到安息。

三个等级（公牛、羊、鸟）——基督的救赎是给所有人的。不论你的社会地位、经济状况、文化背景——"凡靠着他进到神面前的人，他都能拯救到底"（来7:25）。两只鸽子的穷人和一头公牛的富人，在基督面前得到同样完全的救赎。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 1's burnt offering is among the Old Testament's most detailed prefigurations of Christ's cross.

"A male without blemish"—Christ is "But with the precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish and without spot:" (1 Peter 1:19). The sacrificial animal had to be perfect—Christ's perfection was not merely external (no physical defect) but internal (completely sinless). Hebrews 4:15: "One who in every respect has been tempted as we are, yet without sin."

"He shall lay his hand on the head of the burnt offering"—Isaiah 53:6: "The LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all." On the cross, all humanity's sin was "laid" (transferred by hand) onto Christ. 2 Corinthians 5:21: "For our sake he made him to be sin who knew no sin."

"He shall kill the bull before the LORD"—the offerer personally slaughtered. Acts 2:23: Peter to the Jews: "You crucified and killed [him] by the hands of lawless men." Humanity personally killed Christ—every sinner is an accomplice.

"Burn all of it on the altar"—Christ on the cross gave Himself completely, holding nothing back. Philippians 2:8: "He humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross." Not partial death—but complete, thorough, unreserved death.

"A pleasing aroma"—Ephesians 5:2: "Christ loved us and gave himself up for us, a fragrant offering and sacrifice to God." Christ's death was a "pleasing aroma" before the Father—because it completely satisfied justice's demands, bringing the Father's wrath to rest.

Three tiers (bull, sheep, bird)—Christ's redemption is for everyone. Regardless of social status, economic condition, or cultural background—"Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them." (Hebrews 7:25). The poor person with two pigeons and the wealthy person with a bull receive equally complete redemption before Christ.
