## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第五章继续赎罪祭的主题，并引入了赎愆祭（asham，意思是"补偿"或"亏欠"）。如果说第四章的赎罪祭处理的是"你犯了罪——必须赎罪"，第五章增加了一个维度："你犯了罪——不仅要赎罪，还要赔偿损失。"

第五章前半部分（1-13节）列举了四种需要赎罪祭的具体情况：

第一种："若有人听见发誓的声音，他本是见证，却不把所看见的、所知道的说出来，这就是罪。"弟兄姐妹，沉默也可以是罪。你知道真相却不说出来——你的沉默使正义无法实现。箴言24:11-12"人被拉到死地，你要解救；人将被杀，你须拦阻。你若说：这事我未曾知道，那衡量人心的岂不明白么？"知道真相而沉默，等于参与了不公。

第二种：触摸了不洁之物而不自知——"他却不知道，因此成了不洁，就有了罪。"弟兄姐妹，这再次说明罪的客观性——不知道不等于无罪。你不知道那只死虫是不洁的——但你碰了它，你就不洁了。无知不是免罪的理由。

第三种：触摸了别人的污秽——社会生活中不可避免的人际接触也可能导致礼仪上的不洁。

第四种：嘴里冒失发誓——"无论人在甚么事上冒失发誓，他却不知道，一知道了就要在这其中的一件上有了罪。"弟兄姐妹，嘴唇的罪是最容易犯的——不经思考就脱口而出的誓言、承诺、夸口。雅各书3:2"若有人在话语上没有过失，他就是完全人。"但无人是完全人——所以每个人都会在话语上犯罪。

然后是一个令人感动的恩典安排——赎罪祭的阶梯式降级：

如果你买得起一只羊——献一只羊。
如果买不起羊——"就要因所犯的罪，把两只斑鸠或是两只雏鸽带到耶和华面前"。
如果连鸽子也买不起——"就要因所犯的罪带供物来，就是细面伊法十分之一为赎罪祭。"

弟兄姐妹，细面——没有油、没有乳香——最简陋的奉献。但神接受它作为赎罪祭。最穷的穷人也可以赎罪——因为神不愿任何人因为贫穷而无法与祂和好。"不可加上油，也不可加上乳香，因为是赎罪祭"——赎罪祭不是庆祝的时刻（那是平安祭的油和乳香），赎罪祭是严肃地面对罪。

然后从14节开始，引入了赎愆祭（asham）——赎罪祭与赎愆祭的区别是什么？

赎罪祭处理的是罪（sin）——侵犯了神的圣洁。
赎愆祭处理的是亏欠（guilt/trespass）——不仅侵犯了神的圣洁，还造成了可计算的损失。

> "人若在耶和华的圣物上误犯了罪，有了过犯，就要照你所估的，按圣所的舍客勒拿银子，将赎愆祭牲——就是羊群中一只没有残疾的公绵羊——牵到耶和华面前为赎愆祭。"

弟兄姐妹，"在圣物上犯罪"——例如你误用了本应归给神的十一奉献，或者吃了本应属于祭司的祭物。这不只是道德上的罪——它造成了实际的经济损失（圣所和祭司失去了本属于他们的收入）。

赎愆祭的独特要求是："偿还，另外加五分之一。"

弟兄姐妹，加五分之一——即百分之一百二十。你不仅要归还你所亏欠的全部金额，还要多加五分之一作为补偿。赎罪不只是"归零"——它要求"正向的补偿"。你偷了一百块钱，不能只还一百——要还一百二十。这是正义的要求：受害者不应该因为犯罪者的忏悔而仅仅恢复原状——他们应该得到比原来更多的补偿。

弟兄姐妹，利未记第五章的赎愆祭教导了一个关键原则：真正的悔改不仅包括向神认罪——还包括向人赔偿。你不能只说"神已经赦免我了"然后就走——你还必须去赔偿你对别人造成的损失。路加福音19:8撒该的悔改就是赎愆祭的活化："主啊，我把所有的一半给穷人；我若讹诈了谁，就还他四倍。"

最后一节总结了赎愆祭的本质："这是赎愆祭，因他在耶和华面前实在有了罪。"

"实在有了罪"——不是可能有罪，不是也许有罪，而是"实在"有罪。即使你不知道，即使你是误犯的——在神面前，罪是真实的、客观的、需要处理的。但好消息是：神提供了处理的方式。从一只公绵羊到两只鸽子到一把面粉——没有人会因为太穷而无法赎罪。神的恩典覆盖了每一个经济阶层、每一种罪、每一个人。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 5 continues the sin offering theme and introduces the guilt offering (asham, meaning "compensation" or "indebtedness"). If chapter 4's sin offering addressed "you sinned—you must atone," chapter 5 adds a dimension: "you sinned—not only atone but also compensate the loss."

The first half of chapter 5 (vv. 1-13) lists four specific situations requiring a sin offering:

First: "If anyone sins in that he hears a public adjuration to testify, and though he is a witness...yet he does not speak, he shall bear his iniquity." Brothers and sisters, silence can be sin. You know the truth but don't speak it—your silence prevents justice. Proverbs 24:11-12: "Rescue those who are being taken away to death...If you say, 'Behold, we did not know this,' does not he who weighs the heart perceive it?" Knowing truth and staying silent equals participating in injustice.

Second: touching an unclean thing unknowingly—"though it is hidden from him and he has become unclean, and he realizes his guilt." Brothers and sisters, this again demonstrates sin's objectivity—not knowing does not mean not guilty. You didn't know that dead insect was unclean—but you touched it, and you became unclean. Ignorance is not grounds for acquittal.

Third: touching human uncleanness—inevitable interpersonal contact in social life could cause ritual impurity.

Fourth: rash oaths—"if anyone utters with his lips a rash oath to do evil or to do good, any sort of rash oath that people swear...when he realizes his guilt." Brothers and sisters, sins of the lips are the easiest to commit—thoughtless oaths, promises, boasts spoken without reflection. James 3:2: "If anyone does not stumble in what he says, he is a perfect man." But none are perfect—so everyone sins in speech.

Then a moving arrangement of grace—the sin offering's sliding scale:

If you could afford a lamb—offer a lamb.
If you couldn't afford a lamb—"he shall bring as his compensation two turtledoves or two pigeons to the LORD."
If you couldn't even afford pigeons—"he shall bring as his offering a tenth of an ephah of fine flour for a sin offering."

Brothers and sisters, fine flour—no oil, no frankincense—the most humble offering. But God accepted it as a sin offering. The poorest of the poor could atone—because God would not let anyone be unable to reconcile with Him due to poverty. "He shall put no oil on it and shall put no frankincense on it, for it is a sin offering"—the sin offering was not a celebration (oil and frankincense belonged to peace offerings); the sin offering was solemnly facing sin.

Then from verse 14, the guilt offering (asham) was introduced—what was the difference between sin offering and guilt offering?

The sin offering addressed sin—violating God's holiness.
The guilt offering addressed guilt/trespass—not only violating God's holiness but causing calculable loss.

> "If anyone commits a breach of faith and sins unintentionally in any of the holy things of the LORD, he shall bring to the LORD as his compensation...a ram without blemish out of the flock...for a guilt offering."

Brothers and sisters, "sinning regarding holy things"—for example, accidentally using tithe money meant for God, or eating sacrificial food belonging to priests. This was not only moral sin—it caused actual economic loss (the sanctuary and priests lost income that belonged to them).

The guilt offering's unique requirement: "He shall also make restitution...and shall add a fifth to it."

Brothers and sisters, adding a fifth—that is 120 percent. You must not only return the full amount owed but add a fifth as compensation. Atonement was not merely "returning to zero"—it required "positive restitution." If you stole a hundred dollars, you could not return just a hundred—you returned one hundred twenty. This was justice's demand: victims should not merely be restored to their original state because the offender repented—they should receive more than what was originally theirs.

Brothers and sisters, Leviticus 5's guilt offering teaches a key principle: true repentance includes not only confessing to God—but also compensating those harmed. You cannot just say "God has forgiven me" and walk away—you must also go and repay the damage you caused to others. Luke 19:8, Zacchaeus's repentance was the guilt offering come alive: "Lord, the half of my goods I give to the poor. And if I have defrauded anyone of anything, I restore it fourfold."

The final verse summarizes the guilt offering's essence: "It is a guilt offering; he has indeed incurred guilt before the LORD."

"He has indeed incurred guilt"—not possibly guilty, not maybe guilty, but "indeed" guilty. Even if you didn't know, even if it was unintentional—before God, sin was real, objective, requiring resolution. But the good news: God provided the means of resolution. From a ram to two pigeons to a handful of flour—no one was too poor to atone. God's grace covered every economic class, every type of sin, every person.

## 历史背景（中文）

赎愆祭（asham）在以色列历史上有几次重要出现：撒母耳记上6:3-4非利士人归还约柜时送了"赎愆祭"（金痔疮和金老鼠）；以赛亚书53:10"耶和华却定意将他压伤，使他受痛苦。耶和华以他为赎愆祭"——弥赛亚自己成为了asham。"加五分之一"（百分之二十的罚金）在古代近东法律中有平行——汉谟拉比法典规定某些财产犯罪需偿还数倍。但以色列的赎愆祭独特在于它同时要求向神献祭（公绵羊）和向人赔偿（本金加五分之一）——罪同时得罪了神和人，两方面都需要和解。"两只斑鸠或两只雏鸽"——路加福音2:24记载约瑟和马利亚在圣殿献的正是穷人的祭物"一对斑鸠，或两只雏鸽"——耶稣的家庭处于社会经济阶梯的最底层。

## Historical Context (English)

The guilt offering (asham) appeared at several important moments in Israel's history: 1 Samuel 6:3-4, the Philistines returning the ark sent "guilt offerings" (golden tumors and golden mice); Isaiah 53:10: "It was the will of the LORD to crush him...the LORD makes his life an offering for guilt (asham)"—the Messiah Himself became the asham. "Adding a fifth" (a 20% penalty) had parallels in ancient Near Eastern law—the Code of Hammurabi prescribed multiple-fold restitution for certain property crimes. But Israel's guilt offering was unique in requiring both sacrifice to God (a ram) and restitution to the harmed party (principal plus one-fifth)—sin offended both God and humans, requiring reconciliation on both fronts. "Two turtledoves or two young pigeons"—Luke 2:24 records Joseph and Mary offering exactly the poor person's sacrifice at the temple—Jesus' family was at the socioeconomic ladder's lowest rung.

## 经文结构（中文）

**四种特定罪行（1-4节）**——知情不举（沉默之罪）、触不洁之物不自知、触人的污秽、嘴里冒失发誓。

**赎罪祭的阶梯降级（5-13节）**——认罪→献母羊/羊羔/山羊、力量不够献两只鸽子（一赎罪一燔祭）、连鸽子也献不起就用细面（无油无乳香）、"他必蒙赦免"。

**赎愆祭——侵犯圣物（14-16节）**——在圣物上误犯、按估价拿银子、公绵羊为赎愆祭、偿还另加五分之一给祭司、"他必蒙赦免"。

**赎愆祭——不知之罪（17-19节）**——行了不可行之事虽不知道仍有罪、公绵羊为赎愆祭、"在耶和华面前实在有了罪"。

## Structure (English)

**Four Specific Sins (vv. 1-4)**—Knowing but not testifying (sin of silence), touching unclean things unknowingly, touching human uncleanness, rash oaths.

**Sin Offering Sliding Scale (vv. 5-13)**—Confess → offer female sheep/lamb/goat, if unable afford two pigeons (one sin one burnt offering), if even pigeons unaffordable use fine flour (no oil no frankincense), "he shall be forgiven."

**Guilt Offering—Trespass Against Holy Things (vv. 14-16)**—Unintentional sin regarding holy things, assessed value in silver, unblemished ram as guilt offering, restitution plus one-fifth to the priest, "he shall be forgiven."

**Guilt Offering—Unknown Sin (vv. 17-19)**—Doing what is forbidden though unknowingly still guilty, unblemished ram as guilt offering, "he has indeed incurred guilt before the LORD."

## 神学要点（中文）

**沉默也是罪——不作为的罪**
知道真相而不说出来就是罪——改革宗传统教导"不作为的罪"（sins of omission）与"作为的罪"（sins of commission）同样严重。韦敏斯德小要理问答第14问定义罪为"凡不遵守或违背神的律法的，都是罪"——"不遵从"就是不做应该做的事。雅各书4:17"人若知道行善，却不去行，这就是他的罪了。"沉默的旁观者和积极的作恶者在神面前同样有罪。

**赔偿加五分之一——正义的恢复性**
赎愆祭要求不仅归还所亏欠的，还要加五分之一——改革宗传统强调正义不仅是惩罚性的（punitive），也是恢复性的（restorative）。韦敏斯德大要理问答第141问在解释第八诫时说当将那不公义地从任何人所夺取扣留的财物归还——归还是基本的，补偿是额外的恩典要求。

**穷人的面粉赎罪祭——恩典的可及性**
从公绵羊到鸽子到面粉——改革宗传统再次强调福音的普世可及性。韦敏斯德信条论有效呼召："这有效的恩召惟独出于神白白的恩典。"白白的恩典意味着没有经济门槛——最穷的人也可以来到神面前。这不是"廉价的恩典"（没有代价），而是"可及的恩典"（代价由神承担）。

## Theology (English)

**Silence Is Also Sin—Sins of Omission**
Knowing truth yet not speaking it is sin—the Reformed tradition teaches "sins of omission" are equally grave as "sins of commission." Westminster Shorter Catechism Q14 defines sin as "any want of conformity unto, or transgression of, the law of God"—"want of conformity" is not doing what should be done. James 4:17: "So whoever knows the right thing to do and fails to do it, for him it is sin." The silent bystander and the active evildoer are equally guilty before God.

**Restitution Plus One-Fifth—Justice's Restorative Nature**
The guilt offering required returning what was owed plus one-fifth—the Reformed tradition emphasizes justice is not only punitive but restorative. Westminster Larger Catechism Q141 explaining the eighth commandment says restoring of goods unlawfully detained from the right owners—restoration is basic; compensation is an additional grace requirement.

**The Poor Person's Flour Sin Offering—Grace's Accessibility**
From a ram to pigeons to flour—the Reformed tradition again emphasizes the gospel's universal accessibility. Westminster Confession on effectual calling: "This effectual call is of God's free and special grace alone." Free grace means no economic barrier—the poorest person can come before God. This is not "cheap grace" (without cost) but "accessible grace" (the cost is borne by God).

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第五章最重要的基督论在于赎愆祭——因为以赛亚书53:10直接将弥赛亚称为asham（赎愆祭）。

"耶和华却定意将他压伤，使他受痛苦。耶和华以他为赎愆祭。"——基督不只是赎罪祭（chattat，处理罪的惩罚），也是赎愆祭（asham，处理罪的亏欠和赔偿）。基督在十字架上不只是为我们的罪受了刑罚——祂还"偿还"了我们对神的一切亏欠。弟兄姐妹，我们欠神的不只是"违反规则的罚款"——我们欠祂完全的顺服、完全的爱、完全的荣耀。基督用祂完美的一生和完美的死亡，偿还了这一切——而且"加了五分之一"：祂给了父超过我们所亏欠的。

阶梯式降级（羊→鸽子→面粉）——基督的救赎是给所有人的，无论多穷。腓立比书2:7"反倒虚己，取了奴仆的形象"——基督虚己到最低，使最低的人也能够着祂。路加福音2:24约瑟和马利亚在圣殿献了穷人的祭物（两只鸽子）——神的儿子出生在需要献穷人祭物的家庭中。基督从社会底层来——祂的救恩触及每一层。

"他必蒙赦免"——在第五章中多次出现。罗马书8:33-34"谁能控告神所拣选的人呢？有神称他们为义了。谁能定他们的罪呢？有基督耶稣已经死了。"在基督里，"他必蒙赦免"不再需要每次献祭后确认——它是一次性的、永远的、不可撤回的。

沉默之罪——基督替我们一切的罪赎罪，包括我们沉默的罪、不作为的罪、嘴唇冒失的罪。以赛亚书53:7"他被欺压，在受苦的时候却不开口"——基督选择了另一种"沉默"：祂在审判面前不为自己辩护，却为我们的沉默之罪赎了罪。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 5's most important Christology lies in the guilt offering—because Isaiah 53:10 directly calls the Messiah an asham (guilt offering).

"It was the will of the LORD to crush him; he has put him to grief; when his soul makes an offering for guilt (asham)."—Christ was not only a sin offering (chattat, addressing sin's penalty) but also a guilt offering (asham, addressing sin's indebtedness and restitution). On the cross, Christ did not only bear punishment for our sin—He also "repaid" all our debt to God. Brothers and sisters, we owed God not merely "fines for rule-breaking"—we owed Him complete obedience, complete love, complete glory. Christ through His perfect life and perfect death repaid all of this—and "added a fifth": He gave the Father more than what we owed.

The sliding scale (sheep → pigeons → flour)—Christ's redemption is for everyone regardless of poverty. Philippians 2:7: "He emptied himself, by taking the form of a servant"—Christ emptied Himself to the lowest, making Himself reachable by the lowest. Luke 2:24, Joseph and Mary offered the poor person's sacrifice (two pigeons) at the temple—God's Son was born into a family needing to offer the poor person's sacrifice. Christ came from society's bottom—His salvation reaches every level.

"He shall be forgiven"—appearing multiple times in chapter 5. Romans 8:33-34: "Who shall bring any charge against God's elect? It is God who justifies. Who is to condemn? Christ Jesus is the one who died." In Christ, "he shall be forgiven" no longer needs confirmation after each offering—it is once-for-all, eternal, irrevocable.

The sin of silence—Christ atoned for all our sins, including our silence, our omissions, our rash words. Isaiah 53:7: "He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth"—Christ chose another kind of "silence": He did not defend Himself before judgment, yet atoned for our sins of silence.
