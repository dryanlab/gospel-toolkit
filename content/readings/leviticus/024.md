## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十四章是利未记中最特殊的一章——它由三个看似不相关的内容组成：灯台和陈设饼的条例（1-9节）、一个亵渎神名的人被石头打死的叙事（10-16，23节）、以及"以眼还眼以牙还牙"的同态复仇法（17-22节）。这三个部分被放在同一章中——表面上毫无关联，实际上有一个深层的统一主题：神的名和神的秩序必须被尊重。

第一部分：灯台和陈设饼（1-9节）。

"要吩咐以色列人，把那为点灯捣成的清橄榄油拿来给你，使灯常常点着。"弟兄姐妹，这盏灯不是任何灯——它是至圣所幔子外的金灯台，从晚上到早晨在耶和华面前燃烧。"常常点着"——永不熄灭。弟兄姐妹，在圣所的黑暗中，这盏灯是唯一的光源。它象征着神的同在永不离开——即使在黑夜中，灯仍然亮着。

"你要取细面，烤成十二个饼"——十二个饼代表以色列十二个支派。每安息日更换一次，换下的饼归祭司吃。弟兄姐妹，十二个饼"常摆在耶和华面前"——以色列的十二个支派时刻在神的面前。每一个支派、每一个家庭、每一个人——都在神的记念中。你不会被遗忘。

"又要把净乳香放在每行饼上，作为纪念"——乳香代表祷告的馨香（启5:8"金炉盛满了香，就是众圣徒的祈祷"）。饼上的乳香意味着以色列的存在本身就是在神面前的一个持续的祷告。

第二部分：亵渎神名的人被打死（10-16，23节）——利未记中仅有的四个叙事段落之一。

> "有一个以色列妇人的儿子，他父亲是埃及人。"

注意这个人的身份——他是混血的，母亲是以色列人（但支派），父亲是埃及人。他在以色列营中生活，但他的身份是复杂的。在一场争斗中——"这以色列妇人的儿子亵渎了圣名，并且咒诅。"

弟兄姐妹，"亵渎圣名"——在希伯来文中，"圣名"（ha-Shem）就是指耶和华的名。这个人在争斗的愤怒中——也许是出于对自己身份的挫折感——咒骂了神的名字。这不是无意中的失言——这是对神主权的直接攻击。

社区不知道怎么处理——因为律法还没有明确规定这种情况的刑罚。所以"他们把那人收在监里，要得耶和华所指示的话。"弟兄姐妹，这是一个重要的法律原则——当现有律法没有涵盖某个情况时，不能随意判决，必须等候神的指示。

神的判决极其严厉——"把那咒诅圣名的人带到营外。叫听见的人都放手在他头上；全会众就要用石头打死他。"弟兄姐妹，"听见的人放手在他头上"——见证人把手放在犯罪者头上，象征性地把"他的罪归在他自己头上"（类似赎罪日把罪归在山羊头上的手势）。然后全会众执行死刑。

"不管是寄居的是本地人"——同一标准。弟兄姐妹，外人在以色列享有保护也承担义务——这是利未记19:34"爱外人如己"的另一面。外人被平等对待——不只是被平等地保护，也是被平等地约束。

第三部分：同态复仇法（lex talionis）（17-22节）。

> "以伤还伤，以眼还眼，以牙还牙。他怎样叫人的身体有残疾，也要照样向他行。"

弟兄姐妹，这条律法经常被误解为"允许报复"——其实恰恰相反，它是限制报复的。在古代近东，仇杀往往升级——你伤了我的眼睛，我就杀你全家。"以眼还眼"的规定说：刑罚不可超过伤害——你只能还一只眼睛，不能还一条命。这是比例原则（proportionality）——公义不是无限报复，而是对等的赔偿。

弟兄姐妹，三个看似不相关的内容其实有一个统一主题：神的名和神的秩序是神圣的。灯台永不熄灭——神的同在不可被忽视。亵渎神名者被处死——神的名不可被亵渎。以眼还眼——神的公义不可被扭曲（不可过度报复也不可不追究）。

"同归一例。我是耶和华你们的神"——无论本地人还是外人、无论灯台的油还是公义的审判——同一位神、同一套标准、同一个圣洁的秩序。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 24 is Leviticus's most unusual chapter—composed of three seemingly unrelated elements: lampstand and showbread regulations (vv. 1-9), a narrative of a man stoned for blaspheming God's name (vv. 10-16, 23), and the "eye for eye, tooth for tooth" lex talionis (vv. 17-22). These three parts placed in one chapter—superficially disconnected, actually sharing a deep unifying theme: God's name and God's order must be honored.

Part one: lampstand and showbread (vv. 1-9).

"Command the people of Israel to bring you pure oil from beaten olives for the lamp, that a light may be kept burning regularly." Brothers and sisters, this was no ordinary lamp—it was the golden lampstand outside the Most Holy Place's veil, burning from evening to morning before the LORD. "Kept burning regularly"—never extinguished. Brothers and sisters, in the sanctuary's darkness, this lamp was the sole light source. It symbolized God's presence never departing—even in night, the lamp still burned.

"You shall take fine flour and bake twelve loaves"—twelve loaves representing Israel's twelve tribes. Changed each Sabbath, the removed loaves given to priests. Brothers and sisters, twelve loaves "regularly before the LORD"—Israel's twelve tribes constantly before God. Every tribe, every family, every person—held in God's remembrance. You will not be forgotten.

"And you shall put pure frankincense on each row, as a memorial portion"—frankincense represented prayer's fragrance (Rev 5:8: "golden bowls full of incense, which are the prayers of the saints"). Frankincense on the bread meant Israel's very existence was a continual prayer before God.

Part two: the blasphemer stoned (vv. 10-16, 23)—one of Leviticus's only four narrative passages.

> "Now an Israelite woman's son, whose father was an Egyptian." Brothers and sisters, note this person's identity—mixed heritage, mother Israelite (tribe of Dan), father Egyptian. He lived in Israel's camp but his identity was complex. During a fight—"the Israelite woman's son blasphemed the Name, and cursed."

Brothers and sisters, "blasphemed the Name"—in Hebrew, "the Name" (ha-Shem) referred to the LORD's name. This man in fight-fueled anger—perhaps from frustration about his own identity—cursed God's name. This was not accidental speech—it was direct assault on God's sovereignty.

The community did not know how to proceed—existing law did not explicitly prescribe this situation's penalty. So "they put him in custody, till the will of the LORD should be declared to them." Brothers and sisters, this was an important legal principle—when existing law did not cover a situation, arbitrary judgment was forbidden; they must await God's instruction.

God's verdict was extremely severe—"Bring out of the camp the one who cursed, and let all who heard him lay their hands on his head, and let all the congregation stone him." Brothers and sisters, "lay hands on his head"—witnesses placed hands on the offender's head, symbolically putting "his guilt on his own head" (similar to the Day of Atonement gesture placing sin on the goat). Then the entire congregation executed judgment.

"You shall have the same rule for the sojourner and for the native"—one standard. Brothers and sisters, foreigners in Israel enjoyed protection but bore obligations—the other side of Leviticus 19:34's "love the stranger as yourself." Foreigners were treated equally—not only equally protected but equally bound.

Part three: lex talionis (vv. 17-22).

> "Fracture for fracture, eye for eye, tooth for tooth; whatever injury he has given a person shall be given to him."

Brothers and sisters, this law is often misunderstood as "permitting revenge"—actually the opposite, it limited revenge. In the ancient Near East, blood feuds often escalated—you injured my eye, I kill your whole family. "Eye for eye" meant: punishment must not exceed injury—you may only return one eye, not a life. This was the proportionality principle—justice was not unlimited retaliation but proportionate restitution.

Brothers and sisters, three seemingly unrelated contents actually shared a unifying theme: God's name and God's order are sacred. The lampstand never extinguished—God's presence must not be neglected. The blasphemer executed—God's name must not be profaned. Eye for eye—God's justice must not be distorted (neither excessive retaliation nor failure to prosecute).

"You shall have the same rule...I am the LORD your God"—whether native or foreigner, whether lampstand oil or justice's judgment—one God, one standard, one holy order.

## 历史背景（中文）

金灯台（menorah）后来成为犹太教最重要的象征——罗马提图斯凯旋门的浮雕上刻着罗马士兵扛走圣殿金灯台的场景。以色列国徽至今使用七枝灯台的图案。陈设饼（lechem ha-panim，字面"面前的饼"或"面容之饼"）在撒母耳记上21:1-6中出现——大卫逃亡时从祭司亚希米勒那里得到了陈设饼充饥，耶稣在马太福音12:3-4引用这个故事来回应法利赛人对安息日的指控。"以眼还眼"（lex talionis）在《汉谟拉比法典》§196-201中也有同样的规定——但汉谟拉比的lex talionis有阶级差异（对贵族是以眼还眼，对平民是赔钱），利未记的规定不分阶级——"不管是寄居的是本地人，同归一例。"拉比传统（巴比伦塔木德Bava Kamma 83b-84a）后来将"以眼还眼"解释为金钱赔偿——不是真的挖出对方的眼睛，而是赔偿等值的金钱。

## Historical Context (English)

The golden menorah later became Judaism's most important symbol—Rome's Arch of Titus relief depicts Roman soldiers carrying away the temple menorah. Israel's national emblem still uses the seven-branched lampstand. Showbread (lechem ha-panim, literally "bread of the presence" or "face bread") appeared in 1 Samuel 21:1-6—David during flight received showbread from priest Ahimelech; Jesus cited this story in Matthew 12:3-4 responding to Pharisees' Sabbath accusations. "Eye for eye" (lex talionis) appeared in Hammurabi's Code §196-201 with identical formulation—but Hammurabi's lex talionis had class distinctions (eye for eye for nobles, monetary compensation for commoners); Leviticus's version was classless—"You shall have the same rule for the sojourner and for the native." Rabbinic tradition (Babylonian Talmud Bava Kamma 83b-84a) later interpreted "eye for eye" as monetary compensation—not literally removing the offender's eye but paying equivalent monetary damages.

## 经文结构（中文）

**灯台的油（1-4节）**——清橄榄油、使灯常常点着、亚伦从晚到早经理、精金灯台。

**陈设饼（5-9节）**——十二个饼两行排列、净乳香作纪念、每安息日更换、祭司在圣处吃、至圣之物。

**亵渎神名事件（10-16，23节）**——以色列妇人之子（父埃及人）争斗中亵渎圣名→收监等候神指示→放手在头上全会众石头打死→咒诅神必治死、本地人外人同例。

**同态复仇法（17-22节）**——打死人治死、打死牲畜赔牲畜、以伤还伤以眼还眼以牙还牙、本地人外人同归一例。

## Structure (English)

**Lampstand Oil (vv. 1-4)**—Pure beaten olive oil, lamp kept burning regularly, Aaron tends evening to morning, pure gold lampstand.

**Showbread (vv. 5-9)**—Twelve loaves in two rows, pure frankincense as memorial, replaced each Sabbath, priests eat in holy place, most holy.

**Blasphemy Incident (vv. 10-16, 23)**—Israelite woman's son (Egyptian father) blasphemed Name during fight → held in custody awaiting divine instruction → hands laid on head, entire congregation stones → cursing God means death, same rule native and sojourner.

**Lex Talionis (vv. 17-22)**—Killing person means death, killing animal means restitution, fracture for fracture eye for eye tooth for tooth, same rule for all.

## 神学要点（中文）

**常燃的灯——神同在的确据**
灯永不熄灭——改革宗传统把这理解为神恩典之约的不变性。韦敏斯德信条第7章论恩典之约的永恒性。约翰福音8:12"我是世界的光。跟从我的，就不在黑暗里走。"金灯台的光是基督之光的影子——在基督里，光永远不会熄灭。

**十二个饼——神永远纪念祂的百姓**
十二个饼代表十二支派常在神面前——改革宗传统教导神对选民的保守。韦敏斯德信条第17章论圣徒的坚忍：神已经接纳在祂爱子里的人……既不会完全也不会最终从恩典的状态中堕落。你的名字在神面前——永不被移除。

**以眼还眼——公义的比例原则**
同态复仇法限制报复的过度——改革宗传统强调公义的合理性和秩序性。韦敏斯德大要理问答第135-136问论第六诫的要求与禁止——禁止"一切不正当地夺取……生命"。公义不是无限报复——是对等的、有节制的回应。

## Theology (English)

**The Ever-Burning Lamp—God's Presence Assured**
The lamp never extinguished—the Reformed tradition understands this as the covenant of grace's immutability. Westminster Confession chapter 7 on the covenant's eternality. John 8:12: "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness." The golden lampstand's light was Christ's light's shadow—in Christ, light never goes out.

**Twelve Loaves—God Always Remembers His People**
Twelve loaves representing twelve tribes constantly before God—the Reformed tradition teaches God's preservation of the elect. Westminster Confession chapter 17 on saints' perseverance: "Those whom God has accepted in His Beloved...can neither totally nor finally fall away from the state of grace." Your name before God—never removed.

**Eye for Eye—Justice's Proportionality Principle**
Lex talionis limited excessive revenge—the Reformed tradition emphasizes justice's reasonableness and orderliness. Westminster Larger Catechism Q135-136 on the sixth commandment's requirements and prohibitions—forbidding all unjust taking away of...life. Justice is not unlimited retaliation—but proportionate, restrained response.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第二十四章的三个主题都在基督里找到了成全。

金灯台——基督是世界的光。约翰福音8:12"我是世界的光。跟从我的，就不在黑暗里走，必要得着生命的光。"金灯台在圣所中永不熄灭——基督的光在世界中永不熄灭。启示录1:12-13约翰在异象中看见"七个金灯台中间有一位好像人子"——基督站在灯台中间。

陈设饼——基督是生命的粮。约翰福音6:35"耶稣说：我就是生命的粮。到我这里来的，必定不饿。"十二个饼在神面前——基督的身体为我们擘开。马太福音26:26"耶稣拿起饼来，祝福，就擘开，递给门徒，说：你们拿着吃，这是我的身体。"圣餐的饼就是利未记陈设饼的新约成全。

亵渎神名被处死——基督被控"亵渎"。马可福音14:61-64大祭司问耶稣"你是那当称颂者的儿子基督不是？"耶稣说"我是。"大祭司撕开衣服说"你们已经听见他这僭妄的话了。"弟兄姐妹，耶稣因为说了真话——"我是"——被定了"亵渎罪"。利未记24章的亵渎者确实亵渎了神——基督却是神自己被人定为"亵渎者"。宇宙中最大的冤案——神的名被人亵渎，神自己却被人当作亵渎者处死。

"以眼还眼"——基督在登山宝训中说"你们听见有话说：以眼还眼，以牙还牙。只是我告诉你们，不要与恶人作对"（太5:38-39）。弟兄姐妹，基督不是废除了以眼还眼——而是超越了它。以眼还眼是公义——基督教导的是恩典。公义说"你应得的刑罚"——恩典说"我替你承受"。在十字架上，基督没有"以眼还眼"——祂以自己的生命替代了我们的死刑。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

All three of Leviticus 24's themes found fulfillment in Christ.

The golden lampstand—Christ is the light of the world. John 8:12: "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness, but will have the light of life." The lampstand in the sanctuary never went out—Christ's light in the world never goes out. Revelation 1:12-13: John in vision saw "seven golden lampstands, and in the midst...one like a son of man"—Christ standing among the lampstands.

Showbread—Christ is the bread of life. John 6:35: "Jesus said to them, 'I am the bread of life; whoever comes to me shall not hunger.'" Twelve loaves before God—Christ's body broken for us. Matthew 26:26: "Jesus took bread, and after blessing it broke it and gave it to the disciples, and said, 'Take, eat; this is my body.'" The Lord's Supper bread is Leviticus's showbread's New Testament fulfillment.

Blasphemer executed—Christ was accused of "blasphemy." Mark 14:61-64: the high priest asked Jesus "Are you the Christ, the Son of the Blessed?" Jesus said "I am." The high priest tore his garments: "You have heard his blasphemy." Brothers and sisters, Jesus was convicted for telling the truth—"I am"—charged with "blasphemy." Leviticus 24's blasphemer truly blasphemed God—Christ was God Himself condemned as a "blasphemer." The universe's greatest injustice—God's name blasphemed by humans, and God Himself executed by humans as a blasphemer.

"Eye for eye"—Christ in the Sermon on the Mount said "Ye have heard that it hath been said, An eye for an eye, and a tooth for a tooth: But I say unto you, That ye resist not evil: but whosoever shall smite thee on thy right cheek, turn to him the other also." (Matt 5:38-39). Brothers and sisters, Christ did not abolish eye for eye—He transcended it. Eye for eye was justice—Christ taught grace. Justice says "the punishment you deserve"—grace says "I bear it for you." On the cross, Christ did not retaliate "eye for eye"—He substituted His own life for our death sentence.
