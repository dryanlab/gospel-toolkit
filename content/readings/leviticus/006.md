## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第六章分为两大部分：前半部分（1-7节）继续第五章的赎愆祭，处理人与人之间的经济犯罪；后半部分（8-30节）是"祭司手册"——给亚伦和他子孙的详细献祭操作规程。

先看前半部分——这是赎愆祭中最具体的应用场景：

> "若有人犯罪，干犯耶和华，在邻舍交付他的物上，或是在交易上行了诡诈，或是抢夺人的财物，或是欺压邻舍，或是在捡了遗失的物上行了诡诈，说谎起誓——"

弟兄姐妹，注意经文的措辞——"若有人犯罪，干犯耶和华"。这些罪表面上是对邻舍犯的——诡诈、抢夺、欺压、侵占遗失物、假誓——但经文说这些都是"干犯耶和华"。对人犯罪就是对神犯罪。大卫在犯了奸淫和谋杀之后，在诗篇51:4说："我向你犯罪，惟独得罪了你。"所有对人的罪最终都是对神的罪——因为人是按神的形象造的，伤害人就是亵渎神的形象。

赎愆祭的赔偿要求非常具体：

> "就要如数归还，另外加上五分之一，在查出他有罪的日子要交还本主。"

弟兄姐妹，"如数归还，另外加五分之一"——不是笼统的"赔偿损失"，而是精确到数字的。你偷了多少，就还多少，再加百分之二十。"在查出他有罪的日子"——不是等你方便的时候，不是等你有钱的时候，而是一旦罪被确认就要立刻赔偿。悔改不是空口说"对不起"——悔改有具体的行动和时间要求。

然后是赎愆祭的献祭："把赎愆祭牲——就是羊群中一只没有残疾的公绵羊——牵到耶和华面前。"弟兄姐妹，注意次序：先赔偿人（归还加五分之一），然后才向神献祭（公绵羊）。你不能跳过对人的赔偿直接向神献祭——那不是真悔改。马太福音5:23-24主耶稣说："你在祭坛上献礼物的时候，若想起弟兄向你怀怨，就把礼物留在坛前，先去同弟兄和好，然后来献礼物。"先与人和好，再来敬拜。

后半部分（8-30节）转入祭司的操作规程——这是给亚伦和他子孙的"工作手册"。

燔祭的条例（9-13节）："燔祭要放在坛的柴上，从晚上到天亮，坛上的火要常常烧着。"然后重复强调："在坛上必有常常烧着的火，不可熄灭。"

弟兄姐妹，"不可熄灭"——坛上的火是永不熄灭的。白天有百姓的献祭不断地烧，夜间没有人来献祭，火也要烧着。这火从来没有一刻停息——从会幕建立到后来的圣殿，这火理论上从未中断。祭司每天早晨的第一件事是加柴，确保火不灭。弟兄姐妹，这是一个何等生动的图画：神与以色列之间的关系不是间歇性的——不是周日来、周一走——而是持续不断的、永不熄灭的。

祭司收灰的细节也很有意思："穿上细麻布衣服……把坛上所烧的燔祭灰收起来，倒在坛的旁边；随后要脱去这衣服，穿上别的衣服，把灰拿到营外洁净之处。"收灰时穿圣服（因为在圣所范围内），拿到营外时换便服。每一个细节都在区分"圣"与"俗"——圣服只在圣所范围内穿，出了圣所就要换。

素祭的条例（14-18节）："所剩下的，亚伦和他子孙要吃，必在圣处不带酵而吃，要在会幕的院子里吃。"素祭剩下的归祭司——但必须在圣处吃、不带酵吃。你不能把圣物带回家当普通饭菜——它是"至圣的"。

然后是祭司自己的素祭（19-23节）："当亚伦受膏的日子，他和他子孙所要献给耶和华的供物……早晨一半，晚上一半。"普通人的素祭，祭司可以吃剩余的部分——但祭司自己的素祭呢？"都要烧了，却不可吃。"弟兄姐妹，祭司不能吃自己献的祭——因为你不能做自己献祭的受益人。你为别人服务时可以从中得到供应（别人的素祭养活你），但你为自己献祭时不能从中获利。这防止了"自肥"——祭司制度中的利益冲突被这条规定斩断了。

最后是赎罪祭的条例（24-30节）："为赎罪献这祭的祭司要吃，要在圣处……吃。"赎罪祭的肉归献祭的祭司——但有一个重要的例外："凡赎罪祭，若将血带进会幕在圣所赎罪，那肉都不可吃，必用火焚烧。"

弟兄姐妹，记得第四章——大祭司和全会众的赎罪祭，血被带进圣所。这些赎罪祭的肉不可吃——全部烧掉。为什么？因为血进了圣所意味着罪太严重了——这个赎罪祭承担了极大的罪恶，它太"沉重"了，不适合人来消化。罪的严重程度决定了祭物的处理方式。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 6 divides into two major parts: the first half (vv. 1-7) continues chapter 5's guilt offering, addressing economic crimes between people; the second half (vv. 8-30) is the "priestly manual"—detailed sacrificial operating procedures for Aaron and his sons.

First the opening section—the guilt offering's most specific application:

> "If anyone sins and commits a breach of faith against the LORD by deceiving his neighbor in a matter of deposit or security, or through robbery, or if he has oppressed his neighbor or has found something lost and lied about it, swearing falsely—"

Brothers and sisters, note the wording—"sins and commits a breach of faith against the LORD." These sins were ostensibly against neighbors—deception, robbery, oppression, keeping lost property, false oaths—yet Scripture says these all "breach faith against the LORD." Sinning against people is sinning against God. After committing adultery and murder, David said in Psalm 51:4: "Against you, you only, have I sinned." All sin against humans is ultimately sin against God—because humans are made in God's image; harming people desecrates God's image.

The guilt offering's restitution requirements were very specific:

> "He shall restore it in full and shall add a fifth to it, and give it to him to whom it belongs on the day he realizes his guilt."

Brothers and sisters, "restore in full, add a fifth"—not vague "compensation" but precise to the number. Whatever you stole, return that amount plus twenty percent. "On the day he realizes his guilt"—not when convenient, not when you have money, but immediately upon guilt being confirmed. Repentance is not empty words of "sorry"—repentance has specific actions and timing requirements.

Then the guilt sacrifice: "a ram without blemish...as a guilt offering to the LORD." Brothers and sisters, note the order: first compensate the person (return plus one-fifth), then sacrifice to God (the ram). You cannot skip human restitution and go straight to sacrificing to God—that is not true repentance. In Matthew 5:23-24, the Lord Jesus said: "If you are offering your gift at the altar and there remember that your brother has something against you, leave your gift there before the altar and go. First be reconciled to your brother, and then come and offer your gift." First reconcile with people, then come to worship.

The second half (vv. 8-30) transitions to priestly operating procedures—Aaron and his sons' "work manual."

The burnt offering regulations (vv. 9-13): "The burnt offering shall be on the hearth on the altar all night until the morning, and the fire of the altar shall be kept burning on it." Then repeatedly emphasized: "The fire on the altar shall be kept burning on it; it shall not go out."

Brothers and sisters, "shall not go out"—the altar fire was never to be extinguished. During the day, the people's sacrifices burned continuously; at night when no one came to sacrifice, the fire still burned. This fire never ceased for a moment—from the tabernacle's establishment through to the later temple, this fire theoretically never stopped. The priest's first task each morning was adding wood to ensure the fire stayed lit. Brothers and sisters, what a vivid picture: God's relationship with Israel was not intermittent—not Sunday-come-Monday-go—but continuous, never extinguished.

The ash-removal details are also fascinating: "Put on his linen garment...remove the ashes...then take off his garments and put on other garments and carry the ashes outside the camp to a clean place." Holy garments for ash removal within the sanctuary, ordinary clothes for carrying ashes outside. Every detail distinguished "holy" from "common"—holy garments only within the sanctuary; change clothes when leaving.

The grain offering regulations (vv. 14-18): "What is left of it Aaron and his sons shall eat. It shall be eaten unleavened in a holy place. In the court of the tent of meeting they shall eat it." Grain offering remainders went to priests—but must be eaten in a holy place, without leaven. You could not take holy food home as ordinary meals—it was "most holy."

Then the priests' own grain offering (vv. 19-23): "On the day when he is anointed, the offering that Aaron and his sons shall offer...half of it in the morning and half in the evening." Ordinary people's grain offerings, priests could eat the remainder—but the priests' own grain offering? "It shall be wholly burned. Every grain offering of a priest shall be wholly burned. It shall not be eaten." Brothers and sisters, priests could not eat their own offerings—because you cannot be the beneficiary of your own sacrifice. When serving others you may receive provision from their offerings (others' grain offerings sustained you), but when sacrificing for yourself you cannot profit from it. This prevented "self-enrichment"—conflicts of interest in the priestly system were severed by this regulation.

Finally the sin offering regulations (vv. 24-30): "The priest who offers it for sin shall eat it. In a holy place it shall be eaten." Sin offering meat went to the officiating priest—but with one important exception: "But no sin offering shall be eaten from which any blood is brought into the tent of meeting to make atonement in the Holy Place. It shall be burned up with fire."

Brothers and sisters, remember chapter 4—the high priest's and whole congregation's sin offerings had blood brought into the Holy Place. These sin offerings' meat could not be eaten—entirely burned. Why? Because blood entering the Holy Place meant the sin was too grave—this sin offering bore enormous guilt, too "heavy" for humans to consume. Sin's severity determined how the offering was handled.

## 历史背景（中文）

利未记6:1-7的经济犯罪赎愆祭在古代近东法律中有广泛平行。汉谟拉比法典（约公元前1754年）对盗窃的赔偿比例远高于以色列律法——偷牛可能需要赔三十倍。相比之下，以色列律法的"本金加五分之一"是相当温和的。"坛上的火不可熄灭"——在古代的波斯祆教（琐罗亚斯德教）中，圣火也是永不熄灭的，至今伊朗的火神庙仍保持着据称已烧了一千五百多年的圣火。但以色列的永火与拜火无关——它象征的是与神之间持续不断的关系。祭司换衣服的规定反映了古代近东对圣洁空间边界的严格管理——埃及祭司也有类似的洁净和换衣仪式。"瓦器要打碎"（v.28）——多孔的陶器会吸收祭物的脂肪和汁液，无法完全清洗，因此必须打碎；铜器是无孔的，可以擦磨洗净重复使用。

## Historical Context (English)

Leviticus 6:1-7's economic crime guilt offering had widespread parallels in ancient Near Eastern law. The Code of Hammurabi (c. 1754 BC) prescribed restitution ratios for theft far exceeding Israelite law—stealing an ox could require thirty-fold compensation. By comparison, Israel's "principal plus one-fifth" was relatively moderate. "The fire on the altar shall not go out"—in ancient Persian Zoroastrianism, sacred fires were also never extinguished; Iranian fire temples today still maintain fires said to have burned for over fifteen hundred years. But Israel's perpetual fire was unrelated to fire worship—it symbolized an unbroken relationship with God. The priestly garment-changing regulations reflected strict management of sacred space boundaries in the ancient Near East—Egyptian priests also had similar purification and garment-changing rituals. "The earthenware vessel shall be broken" (v.28)—porous pottery absorbed sacrificial fat and juices, could not be fully cleaned, and thus had to be destroyed; bronze vessels were non-porous, could be scoured and rinsed for reuse.

## 经文结构（中文）

**赎愆祭——经济犯罪（1-7节）**——诡诈交易、抢夺财物、欺压邻舍、侵占遗失物、假誓；如数归还加五分之一、公绵羊为赎愆祭、"都必蒙赦免"。

**燔祭的条例（8-13节）**——从晚到早坛上火常烧、祭司穿细麻衣收灰、换衣服把灰拿到营外、每日早晨加柴、"火不可熄灭"。

**素祭的条例（14-18节）**——祭司取一把烧坛、剩余在圣处无酵吃、至圣的、男丁永得的分。

**祭司的素祭（19-23节）**——受膏之日起早晚各献一半、铁鏊调油烤成、"都要烧了却不可吃"。

**赎罪祭的条例（24-30节）**——在宰燔祭处宰、至圣的、献祭祭司在圣处吃、血弹衣服须在圣处洗、瓦器打碎铜器擦净、血带入圣所的赎罪祭不可吃须烧。

## Structure (English)

**Guilt Offering—Economic Crimes (vv. 1-7)**—Deceptive dealings, robbery, oppression, keeping lost property, false oaths; full restitution plus one-fifth, unblemished ram as guilt offering, "he shall be forgiven."

**Burnt Offering Regulations (vv. 8-13)**—Fire burning on altar from evening to morning, priest in linen garments removes ashes, changes clothes to carry ashes outside camp, adds wood every morning, "fire shall not go out."

**Grain Offering Regulations (vv. 14-18)**—Priest takes a handful to burn on altar, remainder eaten unleavened in holy place, most holy, male descendants' perpetual portion.

**Priests' Grain Offering (vv. 19-23)**—From anointing day onward half morning half evening, prepared on griddle with oil, "wholly burned, shall not be eaten."

**Sin Offering Regulations (vv. 24-30)**—Slaughtered where burnt offering is slaughtered, most holy, officiating priest eats in holy place, blood-splashed garments washed in holy place, earthenware broken bronze scoured, sin offering whose blood enters Holy Place not eaten but burned.

## 神学要点（中文）

**对人犯罪就是干犯耶和华——罪的双重维度**
"干犯耶和华"——改革宗传统教导每一条罪都有双重维度：对人的横向亏欠和对神的纵向冒犯。韦敏斯德小要理问答第14问"罪是什么？"答"不遵从或违背神的律法"——所有的罪最终都是对神律法的违背。雅各书2:10"因为凡遵守全律法的，只在一条上跌倒，他就是犯了众条。"

**先赔偿人再献祭给神——悔改的正确次序**
先归还加五分之一，然后才献公绵羊——改革宗传统强调真悔改必须有"与悔改相称的果子"（太3:8）。韦敏斯德信条第十五章论悔改教导：悔改者看到也感受到自己的罪是何等污秽可憎，就为自己的罪忧伤，且恨恶自己的罪，以致转离一切罪恶，归向神，定意竭力按照神一切诫命与神同行。空口忏悔而不赔偿损失不是真悔改。

**不可熄灭的火——持续不断的敬拜**
坛火永不熄灭——改革宗传统强调不断的祷告和持续的敬拜。帖撒罗尼迦前书5:17"不住地祷告"——信徒的灵命应像坛上的火一样永不熄灭。韦敏斯德大要理问答第183问论祈祷说当"不住地祈求"。

## Theology (English)

**Sinning Against People Is Breaching Faith with the LORD—Sin's Dual Dimension**
"Breach of faith against the LORD"—the Reformed tradition teaches every sin has dual dimensions: horizontal debt to people and vertical offense against God. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q14: "What is sin?" "Sin is any want of conformity unto, or transgression of, the law of God"—all sin ultimately transgresses God's law. James 2:10: "For whoever keeps the whole law but fails in one point has become guilty of all of it."

**First Compensate People, Then Sacrifice to God—Repentance's Correct Order**
First return plus one-fifth, then offer the ram—the Reformed tradition emphasizes true repentance must bear "Bring forth therefore fruits meet for repentance:" (Matt 3:8). Westminster Confession chapter 15 on repentance says it includes "grief for, and hatred of his sins...with full purpose of, and endeavor after, new obedience." Verbal confession without restitution is not true repentance.

**The Unquenchable Fire—Continuous Worship**
The altar fire never extinguished—the Reformed tradition emphasizes unceasing prayer and continuous worship. 1 Thessalonians 5:17: "Pray without ceasing"—the believer's spiritual life should be like the altar fire, never going out. Westminster Larger Catechism Q183 on prayer: to "pray without ceasing."

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第六章的基督预表主要有三个层面。

第一，"先赔偿人再献祭给神"——基督在十字架上同时解决了纵向和横向的问题。歌罗西书1:20"藉着他在十字架上所流的血成就了和平"——这和平既是人与神之间的（纵向），也为人与人之间的和好提供了基础（横向）。以弗所书2:14-16"因他使我们和睦，将两下合而为一，拆毁了中间隔断的墙……便藉这十字架使两下归为一体，与神和好了。"

第二，"坛上的火不可熄灭"——基督的代求永不止息。希伯来书7:25"他是长远活着，替他们祈求。"基督在天上永远为信徒代求——这是永不熄灭的"圣火"。罗马书8:34"谁能定他们的罪呢？有基督耶稣已经死了，而且从死里复活，现今在神的右边，也替我们祈求。"从十字架到永恒，基督的代求之火永不熄灭。

第三，"祭司不可吃自己的祭"——基督作为大祭司，不是为自己的利益献祭。希伯来书7:27"他不像那些大祭司，每日必须先为自己的罪，后为百姓的罪献祭；因为他只一次将自己献上，就把这事成全了。"基督不需要为自己献祭——祂是无罪的大祭司。祂的全部献祭都是为了他人——完全利他的、无私的牺牲。

"如数归还加五分之一"——基督的救赎不仅恢复了我们失去的，还赐给我们比原来更多的。亚当失去了伊甸园——基督赐给我们新天新地。亚当失去了与神面对面的交通——基督赐给我们圣灵的内住。罗马书5:20"罪在哪里显多，恩典就更显多了。"基督的赎罪不是"归零"——而是"加五分之一"，恩典超过了亏欠。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 6's Christological typology operates on three main levels.

First, "compensate people first, then sacrifice to God"—Christ on the cross simultaneously resolved vertical and horizontal dimensions. Colossians 1:20: "Making peace by the blood of his cross"—this peace was between God and humans (vertical) and also provided the foundation for human reconciliation (horizontal). Ephesians 2:14-16: "For he himself is our peace, who has made us both one and has broken down...the dividing wall of hostility...and might reconcile us both to God in one body through the cross."

Second, "the altar fire shall not go out"—Christ's intercession never ceases. Hebrews 7:25: "He always lives to make intercession for them." Christ in heaven forever intercedes for believers—this is the never-extinguished "holy fire." Romans 8:34: "Who is to condemn? Christ Jesus is the one who died—more than that, who was raised—who is at the right hand of God, who indeed is interceding for us." From the cross to eternity, Christ's intercession fire never goes out.

Third, "priests shall not eat their own offering"—Christ as High Priest did not sacrifice for His own benefit. Hebrews 7:27: "He has no need, like those high priests, to offer sacrifices daily, first for his own sins and then for those of the people, since he did this once for all when he offered up himself." Christ needed no sacrifice for Himself—He was the sinless High Priest. His entire sacrifice was for others—completely altruistic, selfless sacrifice.

"Full restitution plus one-fifth"—Christ's redemption not only restored what we lost but gave us more than the original. Adam lost Eden—Christ gives us a new heaven and new earth. Adam lost face-to-face fellowship with God—Christ gives us the Spirit's indwelling. Romans 5:20: "Where sin increased, grace abounded all the more." Christ's atonement was not "returning to zero" but "adding a fifth"—grace exceeding the debt.
