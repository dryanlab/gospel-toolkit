## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十章是第十八章的"刑罚版"——第十八章列出了被禁止的行为，第二十章规定了违反者的惩罚。如果说第十八章是"你不可以做什么"，第二十章就是"如果你做了，后果是什么"。这一章充满了"治死"的判决——对我们现代人来说可能触目惊心——但它启示的是一个深刻的真理：罪是致命的。

第二十章按照刑罚的严重程度排列：

最严重的——死刑（治死/用石头打死）：

献子给摩洛（2-5节）——"总要治死他；本地人要用石头把他打死。"弟兄姐妹，这是最先被判死刑的罪——把自己的孩子烧在火中献给异教偶像。注意第4-5节：如果社区"佯为不见"——知道有人献子摩洛却装作没看见——"我就要向这人和他的家变脸"。弟兄姐妹，容忍罪恶就是参与罪恶。沉默不是无辜——沉默是同谋。

交鬼行巫术（6，27节）——"总要治死他们。"灵界的交通绕过了耶和华——它是对神主权的直接叛逆。

咒骂父母（9节）——"凡咒骂父母的，总要治死他。"弟兄姐妹，"咒骂"不是一般的顶嘴——而是对父母权威的根本否定和公开羞辱。在古代以色列的盟约社会中，父母权威是社会秩序的根基——攻击这个根基就是攻击整个社会结构。

通奸和各种性罪（10-21节）——奸夫淫妇都必治死（10节）；与继母行淫（11节）；与儿媳行淫（12节）；同性行为（13节）；同时娶母女（14节）；兽交（15-16节）——这些都被判死刑。

弟兄姐妹，为什么这些罪要被判死刑？第二十章给出了原因——不是因为古代以色列人特别残忍，而是因为这些罪特别地破坏了神所创造的秩序。每一条死刑判决都对应着一个被摧毁的创造秩序——家庭秩序（乱伦）、生命秩序（献子摩洛）、属灵秩序（交鬼行巫）、社会秩序（咒骂父母）、性别秩序（兽交同性行为）。

较轻的——"被剪除"（从民中被切断）：

与月经期妇女行房（18节）——"二人必从民中剪除。"

与姨母或舅母行淫（19-20节）——"要担当自己的罪"。

与伯叔母行淫（20节）——"要担当自己的罪"。

与兄弟之妻行淫（21节）——"他们必无子女。"

弟兄姐妹，"无子女"在古代以色列是最严重的诅咒之一——你的名字从以色列中消失、你的产业无人继承、你在盟约社区中没有后代。

第二十章的结尾（22-26节）回到了利未记的核心主题——分别为圣：

> "你们要归我为圣，因为我耶和华是圣的，并叫你们与万民有分别，使你们作我的民。"

弟兄姐妹，"分别"（hivdalti）——这个词出现在第二十章最后的总结中。神从万国中分别出以色列——正如祂把洁净的禽兽和不洁净的分别开。分别——从混沌到秩序、从死亡到生命、从不洁到圣洁——这是神在创造中就开始做的事（创世记1章分别光暗、水旱地、天空海洋），现在继续在祂的百姓中做。

弟兄姐妹，第二十章的死刑判决对我们新约的信徒意味着什么？我们不再活在旧约的民事律法之下——基督在十字架上承受了律法的一切咒诅（加3:13）。但死刑的严重性提醒我们：罪不是小事。罪的工价仍然是死（罗6:23）——只是基督替我们死了。旧约用石头打死犯罪者——新约用十字架上基督的血赦免罪人。惩罚没有被取消——惩罚被转移了：从罪人转移到了基督身上。

"我是叫你们成圣的耶和华"（8节）——弟兄姐妹，成圣的主动权不在我们手中——是耶和华"叫"我们成圣。我们不是靠自己的努力变得圣洁——是神的恩典使我们圣洁。韦敏斯德小要理问答第35问说成圣是"我们从神白白领受恩典的工作"。刑罚的严厉显明了罪的可怕——恩典的浩大显明了神爱的深度。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 20 is chapter 18's "penalty version"—chapter 18 listed prohibited behaviors; chapter 20 prescribed violators' punishments. If chapter 18 was "what you must not do," chapter 20 was "if you do it, here are the consequences." This chapter is filled with "put to death" verdicts—shocking to modern sensibilities—but it reveals a profound truth: sin is lethal.

Chapter 20 was arranged by penalty severity:

Most severe—death penalty (put to death/stoned):

Child sacrifice to Molech (vv. 2-5)—"He shall surely be put to death. The people of the land shall stone him with stones." Brothers and sisters, this was the first capital offense—burning one's own children to a pagan idol. Note verses 4-5: if the community "hides its eyes"—knowing someone sacrificed children to Molech but pretending not to see—"I myself will set my face against that man and against his clan." Brothers and sisters, tolerating evil was participating in evil. Silence was not innocence—silence was complicity.

Necromancy and sorcery (vv. 6, 27)—"They shall surely be put to death." Spiritual communication bypassing the LORD—direct rebellion against God's sovereignty.

Cursing parents (v. 9)—"Everyone who curses his father or his mother shall surely be put to death." Brothers and sisters, "cursing" was not ordinary backtalk—but fundamental denial and public shaming of parental authority. In ancient Israel's covenant society, parental authority was social order's foundation—attacking this foundation meant attacking the entire social structure.

Adultery and sexual sins (vv. 10-21)—adulterer and adulteress both put to death (v. 10); with stepmother (v. 11); with daughter-in-law (v. 12); same-sex acts (v. 13); marrying mother and daughter (v. 14); bestiality (vv. 15-16)—all capital offenses.

Brothers and sisters, why were these sins punishable by death? Chapter 20 gave the reason—not because ancient Israelites were particularly cruel, but because these sins particularly destroyed God's created order. Each death sentence corresponded to a destroyed order—family order (incest), life order (Molech sacrifice), spiritual order (necromancy), social order (cursing parents), sexual order (bestiality, same-sex acts).

Lesser—"cut off" (excommunicated from the people):

Intercourse during menstruation (v. 18)—"Both of them shall be cut off from among their people."

Relations with aunt (vv. 19-20)—"They shall bear their iniquity."

With uncle's wife (v. 20)—"They shall bear their sin."

With brother's wife (v. 21)—"They shall be childless."

Brothers and sisters, "childless" in ancient Israel was among the most severe curses—your name disappeared from Israel, your inheritance had no heir, you had no posterity in the covenant community.

Chapter 20's conclusion (vv. 22-26) returned to Leviticus's core theme—consecration:

> "You shall be holy to me, for I the LORD am holy and have separated you from the peoples, that you should be mine."

Brothers and sisters, "separated" (hivdalti)—this word appears in chapter 20's final summary. God separated Israel from the nations—just as He separated clean animals from unclean. Separation—from chaos to order, death to life, unclean to holy—this was what God began doing in creation (Genesis 1 separating light from dark, waters from land, sky from sea), now continuing among His people.

Brothers and sisters, what do chapter 20's death penalties mean for New Testament believers? We no longer live under Old Testament civil law—Christ bore all the law's curses on the cross (Gal 3:13). But the death penalty's severity reminds us: sin is no small matter. Sin's wages remain death (Rom 6:23)—only Christ died for us. The Old Testament stoned offenders—the New Testament forgives sinners through Christ's cross-shed blood. Punishment was not cancelled—punishment was transferred: from sinners to Christ.

"I am the LORD who sanctifies you" (v. 8)—Brothers and sisters, sanctification's initiative was not in our hands—the LORD "sanctifies" us. We don't become holy through our own effort—God's grace makes us holy. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q35 says sanctification is "the work of God's free grace." Penalty's severity revealed sin's horror—grace's magnitude revealed God's love's depth.

## 历史背景（中文）

古代近东的法律（如《汉谟拉比法典》、亚述中期法典、赫人法典）也对通奸、乱伦和兽交规定了严厉的刑罚——包括死刑。利未记20章的独特之处不在于刑罚的严厉（这在古代近东是普遍的），而在于刑罚的神学根基——"我是耶和华""我是圣的"。其他法典的刑罚基于社会秩序和家族荣誉——利未记的刑罚基于神的圣洁。"用石头打死"是以色列最常见的死刑方式——整个社区参与执行（申17:7"见证人要先下手，然后众民也下手"），意味着这不是私人报复而是社区共同的审判行为。"从民中剪除"（karet）在拉比传统中有争议——一些人认为是处死，另一些人认为是驱逐出社区，还有人认为是"神亲自审判"——即提前死亡或没有来世的份。

## Historical Context (English)

Ancient Near Eastern legal codes (Hammurabi, Middle Assyrian Laws, Hittite Laws) also prescribed severe penalties for adultery, incest, and bestiality—including death. Leviticus 20's uniqueness lay not in penalty severity (common in the ancient Near East) but in its theological foundation—"I am the LORD," "I am holy." Other codes based penalties on social order and family honor—Leviticus based penalties on God's holiness. "Stoning" was Israel's most common execution—the entire community participated (Deut 17:7: "The hands of the witnesses shall be first against him, and afterward the hands of all the people"), meaning this was not private revenge but communal judicial action. "Cut off" (karet) is debated in rabbinic tradition—some understood it as execution, others as community expulsion, still others as "divine judgment"—premature death or forfeiture of afterlife.

## Structure (English)

**Child Sacrifice to Molech—Death (vv. 2-5)**—Stoned; those who turn blind eye also cut off by God.

**Necromancy/Sorcery—Cut Off (v. 6)**—God sets face against, cut off.

**Core Declaration (vv. 7-8)**—Consecrate yourselves be holy, I am the LORD your God, I am the LORD who sanctifies you.

**Cursing Parents—Death (v. 9)**—Blood guilt upon them.

**Sexual Sin Penalties (vv. 10-21)**—Adultery (death), stepmother (death), daughter-in-law (death), same-sex (death), mother-daughter (burning), bestiality (death); aunt (bear iniquity), uncle's wife (bear sin, childless), brother's wife (childless).

**Consecration Summary (vv. 22-26)**—Keep statutes lest land vomit out, don't follow nations' customs, distinguish clean/unclean, be holy to Me for I am holy, separated from peoples.

**Necromancy/Sorcery—Death (v. 27)**—Stoned; inclusio with v. 6.

## 神学要点（中文）

**罪的工价是死——刑罚的神学意义**
利未记20章的死刑不是野蛮的报复——它是"罪的工价乃是死"（罗6:23）的旧约图画。改革宗传统把刑罚理解为神公义的彰显。韦敏斯德信条第6章论罪的后果："罪的工价乃是死。"每一个死刑判决都在宣告一个真理：在圣洁的神面前，罪是致命的。

**"佯为不见"——容忍罪恶的罪**
社区若对献子摩洛佯为不见则被神追讨——改革宗传统强调教会对罪的纪律责任。韦敏斯德信条第30章论教会惩戒的必要性。沉默面对罪恶不是"宽容"——是"同谋"。以西结书3:18"你不警戒他……他的血我要向你追讨。"

**"我是叫你们成圣的耶和华"——成圣是神的工作**
改革宗传统坚持成圣虽然需要人的合作（"你们要自洁成圣"v.7），但其根本动力来自神（"我是叫你们成圣的耶和华"v.8）。韦敏斯德信条第13章论成圣："在这场争战中，那残存的败坏虽然可能暂时佔有优势，但是基督成圣之灵不断加力量给人，那蒙神重生的新人靠这力量终必得胜。"

## Theology (English)

**Sin's Wages Is Death—Penalty's Theological Meaning**
Leviticus 20's death penalties were not barbaric revenge—they were the Old Testament picture of "For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord." (Rom 6:23). The Reformed tradition understands penalty as God's justice manifested. Westminster Confession chapter 6 on sin's consequences: The wages of sin is death. Every death sentence declared a truth: before the holy God, sin is lethal.

**"Hiding Eyes"—The Sin of Tolerating Evil**
The community's failure to act against Molech worship incurred God's judgment—the Reformed tradition emphasizes the church's disciplinary responsibility. Westminster Confession chapter 30 on church censures' necessity. Silence before evil is not "tolerance"—it is "complicity." Ezekiel 3:18: "If you do not warn him...his blood I will require at your hand."

**"I Am the LORD Who Sanctifies You"—Sanctification Is God's Work**
The Reformed tradition maintains that sanctification, while requiring human cooperation ("consecrate yourselves" v.7), derives its fundamental power from God ("I am the LORD who sanctifies you" v.8). Westminster Confession chapter 13 on sanctification: "Although in this life sanctification is everywhere imperfect...yet through the continual supply of strength from the sanctifying Spirit of Christ...the saints grow in grace."

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第二十章的死刑律法在基督的十字架上得到了终极的成全。

每一个死刑判决都指向十字架——因为基督在十字架上承受了所有这些罪的刑罚。加拉太书3:13"基督既为我们受了咒诅，就赎出我们脱离律法的咒诅；因为经上记着：凡挂在木头上都是被咒诅的。"弟兄姐妹，利未记20章说犯这些罪的人"必被治死"——基督代替我们被"治死"了。石头没有落在我们身上——因为它们落在了基督身上。

"我是叫你们成圣的耶和华"——基督就是我们成圣的源头。哥林多前书1:30"神又使他成为我们的智慧、公义、圣洁、救赎。"我们的圣洁不是自己修炼出来的——是基督赐给我们的。

"与万民有分别"——基督的教会在世界中被"分别"出来。约翰福音17:16"他们不属世界，正如我不属世界一样。"基督为教会祈求的不是离开世界——而是在世界中保持圣洁的分别。

约翰福音8:3-11行淫时被拿的妇人——法利赛人引用利未记20:10"奸夫淫妇都必治死"要用石头打死她。耶稣说"你们中间谁是没有罪的，谁就可以先拿石头打她。"弟兄姐妹，耶稣没有废除利未记20章的律法——祂指出了一个事实：按照这条律法，每个人都该被石头打死。没有人是无罪的。当所有人都放下石头离开后，耶稣对妇人说"我也不定你的罪。去吧，从此不要再犯罪了。"弟兄姐妹，耶稣有权定她的罪（因为只有祂是无罪的）——但祂选择了赦免。不是因为罪不严重——而是因为祂即将在十字架上为她的罪付出代价。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 20's capital punishment laws found their ultimate fulfillment at Christ's cross.

Every death sentence pointed to the cross—because Christ on the cross bore all these sins' penalties. Galatians 3:13: "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us—for it is written, 'Cursed is everyone who is hanged on a tree.'" Brothers and sisters, Leviticus 20 said those committing these sins "shall surely be put to death"—Christ was "put to death" in our place. Stones did not fall on us—because they fell on Christ.

"I am the LORD who sanctifies you"—Christ is our sanctification's source. 1 Corinthians 1:30: "God made him our wisdom and our righteousness and sanctification and redemption." Our holiness is not self-cultivated—it is Christ's gift to us.

"Separated from the peoples"—Christ's church is "separated" in the world. John 17:16: "They are not of the world, just as I am not of the world." Christ's prayer for the church was not departure from the world—but maintaining holy distinction within it.

John 8:3-11, the woman caught in adultery—Pharisees citing Leviticus 20:10 "adulterer and adulteress shall be put to death" demanded she be stoned. Jesus said: "Let him who is without sin among you be the first to throw a stone at her." Brothers and sisters, Jesus did not abolish Leviticus 20's law—He pointed out a fact: by this law, everyone deserved stoning. No one was sinless. After all dropped their stones and left, Jesus told the woman: "Neither do I condemn you; go, and from now on sin no more." Brothers and sisters, Jesus had the right to condemn her (He alone was sinless)—but He chose mercy. Not because sin was trivial—but because He was about to pay for her sin on the cross.
