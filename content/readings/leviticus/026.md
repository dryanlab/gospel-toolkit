## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十六章是利未记的高潮——也是整卷书最令人战栗、同时最令人感动的一章。这是神与以色列立约的最终声明：遵行则蒙福，悖逆则受咒诅。弟兄姐妹，这一章不是威胁——这是一位父亲在儿子出发前的严肃嘱咐，是恩典之约的双面：祝福的应许和咒诅的警告，以及最终深不可测的怜悯。

第一部分：遵行的祝福（3-13节）。

"你们若遵行我的律例，谨守我的诫命……"然后是一串美好的应许。

弟兄姐妹，注意神应许的不是天堂——而是此地此时的祝福：时雨降下、土产丰盛、打粮打到摘葡萄的时候（意思是丰收丰盛到连续不断）、吃得饱足、安然居住、平安无战事、仇敌败走。

但所有物质祝福中最伟大的是第11-12节：

> "我要在你们中间立我的帐幕；我的心也不厌恶你们。我要在你们中间行走；我要作你们的神，你们要作我的子民。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整个旧约中对神人关系最完整的宣告——"我在你们中间行走，我作你们的神，你们作我的子民。"这不是关于粮食和安全——这是关于同在。神在你们中间行走——不是高高在上的审判者，而是与你们同行的同伴。这是伊甸园的恢复——"那时，他们听见耶和华神在园中行走的声音"（创3:8）。神应许恢复祂原来的心意：与人同在。

第二部分：悖逆的咒诅——以四轮"七倍惩罚"递进（14-39节）。

> "你们若不听从我，不遵行我的诫命……我待你们就要这样。"

弟兄姐妹，咒诅不是一下子来的——它是渐进的、递进的。每一轮以色列不悔改，惩罚就"加七倍"。神给了四次警告的机会：

第一轮——痨病热病、撒种无收、败于仇敌；
第二轮——天如铁地如铜、旱灾、野兽吃儿女牲畜；
第三轮——刀剑追杀、瘟疫、粮食断绝、被交在仇敌手中；
第四轮——最可怕的——"你们要吃儿子的肉，也要吃女儿的肉。"

弟兄姐妹，这个可怕的预言后来应验了——列王纪下6:28-29饥荒围城时，两个妇人轮流吃对方的孩子——弟兄姐妹，这不是隐喻，这是历史。利未记26章的咒诅一字不差地在以色列历史中应验了。

最后——散在列邦：

> "我要把你们散在列邦中……你们的地要成为荒场；你们的城邑要变为荒凉。"

弟兄姐妹，这就是公元前722年北国被亚述掳走，公元前586年南国被巴比伦掳走的预言。但注意34-35节——当以色列被掳到列邦时，那片土地"要享受众安息"——因为以色列没有守安息年，土地就用以色列被掳的年数来补足欠下的安息年！弟兄姐妹，历史学家注意到以色列被巴比伦掳走七十年——正是从所罗门时代到被掳期间欠下的安息年数（历代志下36:21"要充满地所当享的安息，安息七十年"）。连这个数字都是精确应验的。

第三部分——最令人感动的部分（40-45节）：在一切咒诅之后，神的最终话语。

> "虽是这样，他们在仇敌之地，我却不厌弃他们，也不厌恶他们，将他们尽行灭绝，也不背弃我与他们所立的约，因为我是耶和华他们的神。"

弟兄姐妹，"虽是这样"——在所有的叛逆、所有的惩罚、所有的咒诅之后——"我却不厌弃他们"。神可以惩罚——但祂不放弃。神可以管教——但祂不离弃。

"我却要为他们的缘故记念我与他们先祖所立的约。"——弟兄姐妹，神记念约。不是因为以色列守约——他们完全没有守约——而是因为神自己的信实。约不是在以色列守约的条件下才有效——约在神的信实中有效。

弟兄姐妹，利未记第二十六章给了我们一个惊人的神学宣告：神的恩典比祂的审判更深。审判是真实的、是严厉的、是公义的——但恩典是最后的话语。以色列被掳了七十年——然后神记念了约，带他们回来（以斯拉记）。人的悖逆有尽头——神的怜悯没有尽头。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 26 is Leviticus's climax—and the entire book's most terrifying yet most moving chapter. This was God's final covenant statement with Israel: obedience brings blessing, rebellion brings curse. Brothers and sisters, this was not a threat—it was a father's solemn charge to a departing son, the covenant of grace's two faces: blessing's promise and curse's warning, and ultimately unfathomable mercy.

Part one: blessings for obedience (vv. 3-13).

"If you walk in my statutes and observe my commandments and do them..."—then a series of beautiful promises.

Brothers and sisters, note God's promises were not about heaven—but about here-and-now blessings: timely rain, abundant produce, threshing lasting until vintage (meaning harvest so abundant it was continuous), eating to satisfaction, living securely, peace without war, enemies routed.

But among all material blessings, verses 11-12 were greatest:

> "I will make my dwelling among you, and my soul shall not abhor you. And I will walk among you and will be your God, and you shall be my people."

Brothers and sisters, this was the Old Testament's most complete declaration of divine-human relationship—"I walk among you, I am your God, you are my people." This was not about grain and security—this was about presence. God walking among you—not a lofty judge above, but a companion walking alongside. This was Eden's restoration—"And they heard the voice of the LORD God walking in the garden in the cool of the day: and Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the LORD God amongst the trees of the garden." (Gen 3:8). God promised to restore His original intent: dwelling with humanity.

Part two: curses for rebellion—escalating in four rounds of "sevenfold punishment" (vv. 14-39).

> "But if you will not listen to me and will not do all these commandments...then I will do this to you."

Brothers and sisters, the curses did not come all at once—they were progressive and escalating. Each round Israel failed to repent, punishment was "multiplied sevenfold." God gave four opportunities for warning:

First round—wasting disease, fever, failed harvests, defeat by enemies;
Second round—sky like iron earth like bronze, drought, wild animals devouring children and livestock;
Third round—sword pursuit, pestilence, food cut off, delivered into enemies' hands;
Fourth round—most terrible—"You shall eat the flesh of your sons, and you shall eat the flesh of your daughters."

Brothers and sisters, this dreadful prophecy was later fulfilled—2 Kings 6:28-29, during siege famine, two women took turns eating each other's children—brothers and sisters, this was not metaphor, this was history. Leviticus 26's curses were fulfilled word-for-word in Israelite history.

Finally—scattered among nations:

> "I will scatter you among the nations...Your land shall be a desolation, and your cities shall be a waste."

Brothers and sisters, this was the prophecy of the Northern Kingdom's Assyrian exile in 722 BC and the Southern Kingdom's Babylonian exile in 586 BC. But notice verses 34-35—when Israel was exiled to the nations, the land would "enjoy its Sabbaths"—because Israel had not kept Sabbath years, the land would use the years of exile to make up owed Sabbaths! Brothers and sisters, historians note Israel's seventy-year Babylonian exile—precisely the number of owed Sabbath years from Solomon's era to exile (2 Chronicles 36:21: "to fulfill the word of the LORD by the mouth of Jeremiah, until the land had enjoyed its Sabbaths. All the days that it lay desolate it kept Sabbath, to fulfill seventy years"). Even this number was precisely fulfilled.

Part three—the most moving section (vv. 40-45): after all curses, God's final word.

> "Yet for all that, when they are in the land of their enemies, I will not spurn them, neither will I abhor them so as to destroy them utterly and break my covenant with them, for I am the LORD their God."

Brothers and sisters, "yet for all that"—after all the rebellion, all the punishment, all the curses—"I will not spurn them." God could punish—but He would not give up. God could discipline—but He would not abandon.

"But I will for their sake remember the covenant with their forefathers"—Brothers and sisters, God remembered the covenant. Not because Israel kept it—they utterly failed—but because of God's own faithfulness. The covenant was not conditional on Israel's obedience—it was effective in God's faithfulness.

Brothers and sisters, Leviticus 26 gave us an astonishing theological declaration: God's grace goes deeper than His judgment. Judgment is real, severe, and just—but grace has the final word. Israel was exiled seventy years—then God remembered the covenant and brought them back (Ezra). Human rebellion has an end—God's mercy has no end.

## 历史背景（中文）

利未记26章的咒诅清单与申命记28章（更详细的版本）构成了旧约的核心盟约文件——与古代近东条约传统（尤其是赫人宗主条约）高度吻合：序言→历史回顾→条款→祝福→咒诅→保存条约的指示。赫人条约中的咒诅清单（饥荒、瘟疫、战败、散居）与利未记26章惊人相似。"吃儿女的肉"在申命记28:53-57中也出现，并在历史书中被记录为现实事件（王下6:28-29；哀4:10）。被掳七十年的精确对应（历代志下36:21）提供了历史考古学上的旁证——巴比伦泥版记录了尼布甲尼撒打败埃及军队（605 BC）开始，到居鲁士归还犹太人（538 BC），恰好约70年。"我在你们中间行走"（12节）是恩典之约最美丽的宣告之一——后来在新约以西结书37:27、哥林多后书6:16、启示录21:3多次回响。

## Historical Context (English)

Leviticus 26's curse list and Deuteronomy 28 (more detailed version) together constituted the Old Testament's core covenant documents—closely matching ancient Near Eastern treaty traditions (especially Hittite suzerainty treaties): preamble → historical prologue → stipulations → blessings → curses → treaty storage instructions. Hittite treaty curse lists (famine, plague, military defeat, exile) strikingly parallel Leviticus 26. "Eating children's flesh" also appeared in Deuteronomy 28:53-57 and was recorded as historical reality (2 Kgs 6:28-29; Lam 4:10). The seventy-year exile's precise correspondence (2 Chr 36:21) provided historical-archaeological corroboration—Babylonian clay tablets record from Nebuchadnezzar's defeat of the Egyptian army (605 BC) to Cyrus's return of the Jews (538 BC) was approximately 70 years. "I will walk among you" (v. 12) was the covenant of grace's most beautiful declaration—later echoed in Ezekiel 37:27, 2 Corinthians 6:16, and Revelation 21:3.

## 经文结构（中文）

**禁偶像守安息日（1-2节）**——利未记全书的两大核心禁令作为序言。

**遵行的祝福（3-13节）**——时雨丰收、吃饱安居、平安无战、仇敌败退、**我在你们中间行走我作你们的神**。

**悖逆的咒诅——四轮递进（14-39节）**：
- 第一轮：痨病热病、撒种无收、败于仇敌（16-17节）
- 第二轮：天铜地铁旱灾、野兽吞噬（18-20节）
- 第三轮：刀剑瘟疫粮绝（21-26节）
- 第四轮：吃儿女的肉、城邑荒凉、圣所废场、被掳列邦（27-39节）
- 土地享受安息年（34-35节）

**悔改与恢复（40-45节）**——若承认罪孽未受割礼的心谦卑、神记念与列祖所立的约、**不厌弃不尽行灭绝、因我是耶和华他们的神**。

## Structure (English)

**No Idols, Keep Sabbath (vv. 1-2)**—Leviticus's two core prohibitions as prologue.

**Blessings for Obedience (vv. 3-13)**—Timely rain abundant harvest, eat and dwell safely, peace no war, enemies routed, **I will walk among you I will be your God**.

**Curses for Rebellion—Four Escalating Rounds (vv. 14-39)**:
- Round 1: Wasting disease fever, failed harvests, defeat (vv. 16-17)
- Round 2: Bronze sky iron earth drought, wild animals devouring (vv. 18-20)
- Round 3: Sword pestilence food cut off (vv. 21-26)
- Round 4: Eating children's flesh, desolate cities, ruined sanctuary, exile (vv. 27-39)
- Land enjoys its Sabbaths (vv. 34-35)

**Repentance and Restoration (vv. 40-45)**—If they confess iniquity, uncircumcised heart humbled, God remembers covenant with forefathers, **will not spurn or destroy utterly, for I am the LORD their God**.

## 神学要点（中文）

**盟约的双面——祝福与咒诅**
改革宗传统把创世记1章到启示录22章理解为一部"盟约史"。韦敏斯德信条第7章论盟约论。利未记26章展现了所有盟约的结构：顺服→祝福，悖逆→咒诅，悔改→恢复。但改革宗传统强调这不是简单的"因果报应"——它是关系性的：神是父，以色列是儿子，管教是为了恢复关系而非永远惩罚。

**"虽是这样"——无条件恩典的最终性**
韦敏斯德信条第十七章论圣徒的坚忍教导：凡蒙神接纳的人必定在恩典中坚信到底，在永恒里得救。利未记26:44"虽是这样"是圣徒坚忍的旧约根基——不是因为以色列的坚忍，而是因为神的坚忍。

**七倍惩罚的渐进性——神的耐心**
四轮咒诅渐进递增——改革宗传统理解神的管教是有目的的：目的是悔改，不是毁灭。彼得后书3:9"主不是耽延他的应许，乃是宽容你们，不愿有一人沉沦，乃愿人人都悔改。"每一轮咒诅都是一次呼召：转回吧！

## Theology (English)

**Covenant's Two Faces—Blessing and Curse**
The Reformed tradition understands Genesis 1 through Revelation 22 as one "covenant history." Westminster Confession chapter 7 on covenant theology. Leviticus 26 displayed all covenants' structure: obedience → blessing, rebellion → curse, repentance → restoration. But the Reformed tradition emphasizes this was not simple "cause and effect"—it was relational: God is Father, Israel is son, discipline aimed at relationship restoration not permanent punishment.

**"Yet for All That"—Grace's Unconditionality**
Westminster Confession chapter 17 on saints' perseverance: They can neither totally nor finally fall away...the Spirit of God who has begun the good work will perfect it. Leviticus 26:44's "yet for all that" is saints' perseverance's Old Testament foundation—not because of Israel's perseverance, but God's perseverance.

**Sevenfold Punishment's Progressiveness—God's Patience**
Four rounds of escalating curses—the Reformed tradition understands divine discipline as purposeful: its goal is repentance, not destruction. 2 Peter 3:9: "The Lord is not slow to fulfill his promise...not wishing that any should perish, but that all should reach repentance." Every curse round was a call: Turn back!

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第二十六章的咒诅和怜悯都指向基督。

"我要在你们中间行走"（12节）——这个应许在基督里完全成就。约翰福音1:14"道成了肉身，住在我们中间"——"住"字原文就是"支搭帐幕"，与利未记26:11"在你们中间立我的帐幕"完全呼应。神在西奈山应许与以色列同住——这个应许在伯利恒马槽中成真，在那里，道成肉身，神住在人中间。

"我要作你们的神，你们要作我的子民"——这是整本圣经的核心主题。启示录21:3"神的帐幕在人间。他要与人同住，他们要作他的子民。神要亲自与他们同在，作他们的神。"利未记的应许在新天新地中最终成全。

咒诅——基督承担了所有的咒诅。加拉太书3:13"基督既为我们受了咒诅，就赎出我们脱离律法的咒诅；因为经上记着：凡挂在木头上都是被咒诅的。"利未记26章所有可怕的咒诅——饥荒、刀剑、瘟疫、被弃——基督在十字架上全部承受了。"我的神，我的神，为什么离弃我？"（太27:46）——这是被神弃绝的极度黑暗，是利未记咒诅的顶峰，也是基督替我们承担的终极代价。

"虽是这样……我却不厌弃他们"——罗马书8:38-39"因为我深信，无论是死，是生……都不能叫我们与神的爱隔绝；这爱是在我们的主基督耶稣里的。"利未记26:44"我不厌弃他们，也不背弃我的约"——这种不放弃的爱在基督里得到了最终、最完整的表达。神不只是"不放弃"——祂差遣了自己的儿子来寻找和拯救失丧的人（路19:10）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 26's curses and mercy both pointed to Christ.

"I will walk among you" (v. 12)—this promise was completely fulfilled in Christ. John 1:14: "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us"—"dwelt" literally means "tabernacled," directly echoing Leviticus 26:11's "I will make my dwelling among you." God promised at Sinai to dwell with Israel—this promise came true at Bethlehem's manger, where the Word became flesh and God dwelt among humanity.

"I will be your God, and you shall be my people"—this was the entire Bible's central theme. Revelation 21:3: "The dwelling place of God is with man. He will dwell with them, and they will be his people, and God himself will be with them as their God." Leviticus's promise was finally fulfilled in the new heaven and earth.

Curses—Christ bore all the curses. Galatians 3:13: "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us—for it is written, 'Cursed is everyone who is hanged on a tree.'" All of Leviticus 26's terrible curses—famine, sword, pestilence, abandonment—Christ bore them all on the cross. "And about the ninth hour Jesus cried with a loud voice, saying, Eli, Eli, lama sabachthani? that is to say, My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" (Matt 27:46)—the extreme darkness of divine abandonment, Leviticus's curses' apex, and the ultimate price Christ paid for us.

"Yet for all that...I will not spurn them"—Romans 8:38-39: "For I am sure that neither death nor life...will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord." Leviticus 26:44's "I will not spurn them nor break my covenant"—this relentless love found its ultimate, most complete expression in Christ. God did not merely "not give up"—He sent His own Son to seek and save the lost (Luke 19:10).
