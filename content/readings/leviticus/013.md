## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十三章是利未记中最长的章节之一——59节经文，全部关于一个主题：皮肤病的诊断。希伯来文叫tsara'at（和合本译为"大麻风"），但它不完全等同于现代医学的麻风病（Hansen's disease）。Tsara'at是一个更广泛的概念，包括各种严重的皮肤状况——可能涉及皮肤病、霉菌感染甚至衣物上的霉斑。

第十三章看起来像一份古代的医学手册——描述了各种皮肤症状的诊断标准：白斑、红斑、疖子、烧伤后的感染、头皮和胡须上的癣——每一种都有详细的察看程序。但弟兄姐妹，这不是医学——这是神学。祭司不是医生，他们不治病；祭司是判断洁净与不洁净的裁判。他们的工作不是开药方——而是宣告"洁净"或"不洁净"。

诊断程序的核心标准有三个：(1)灾病处的毛是否变白；(2)灾病的现象是否"深于皮"（深入到皮肤以下）；(3)灾病是否在扩散。如果三个条件都满足——定为不洁净。如果不确定——关锁七天后再察看。如果情况止住了、没有扩散——定为洁净。

弟兄姐妹，为什么这么谨慎？因为"不洁净"的判定意味着那个人必须被隔离在营外——与家人分离、与社区断绝、与圣所隔绝。这不是小事。所以祭司必须极其谨慎——宁可多等七天，也不能轻率地把一个人逐出社区。

然后是第十三章中最令人心碎的段落（45-46节）：

> "身上有长大麻风灾病的，他的衣服要撕裂，也要蓬头散发，蒙着上唇，喊叫说：不洁净了！不洁净了！灾病在他身上的日子，他便是不洁净；他既是不洁净，就要独居营外。"

弟兄姐妹，撕裂衣服、蓬头散发——这是以色列人哀悼死人的方式。麻风病人在被逐出营地的那一天，要像哀悼自己的死亡一样哀悼。他还活着——但在社会意义上，他已经死了。他不再属于以色列的会众——不再能参加崇拜、不再能与家人同住、不再能触摸任何人。

> "蒙着上唇，喊叫说：不洁净了！不洁净了！"

每当有人走近，麻风病人必须大声喊叫来警告他们。弟兄姐妹，想象一下这种羞辱——你不仅被隔离，你还必须亲口向每一个路人宣告你自己的不洁净。你的身份被简化为一个词："不洁净。"

"独居营外"——营外是不洁净之物被处理的地方（赎罪祭的牛在营外烧，灰被倒在营外）。麻风病人被归入了"不洁净之物"的范畴——从人的尊严降为废物的地位。

弟兄姐妹，tsara'at在圣经中不只是一种疾病——它是罪的可见化身。罪使人与神隔绝、与社区隔绝、与家人隔绝——正如麻风使人被逐出营外。罪从内部腐蚀人——正如麻风从皮肤深处向外扩散。罪使人失去尊严——正如麻风使人必须喊叫"不洁净"。

第十三章的后半部分（47-59节）扩展到衣物上的tsara'at——发绿或发红的霉斑。衣服也需要被祭司察看——如果霉斑在扩散，衣服必须被焚烧。弟兄姐妹，不洁净不只影响人的身体——它也"传染"到人所穿戴的物品上。罪的影响不局限于个人——它扩散到你的环境、你的关系、你的文化。

第十三章虽然是一份冗长的诊断手册，但它传递了一个核心信息：不洁净（罪）是严重的——它会扩散、会隔绝、会摧毁。它必须被识别（祭司的诊断）、被隔离（逐出营外）、最终被洁净（第十四章的洁净礼）。神不忽视不洁净——祂面对它、处理它、最终克服它。

弟兄姐妹，第十三章以一种出人意料的方式显明了神的关怀——祂不只关心圣所里发生的事，也关心你皮肤上的一块斑点。没有什么是太小以至于神不在意的，也没有什么是太"私密"以至于祂不关注的。神的圣洁覆盖了生命的每一个层面。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 13 is one of Leviticus's longest—59 verses, entirely devoted to one subject: diagnosing skin diseases. The Hebrew term is tsara'at (often translated "leprosy"), but it does not exactly correspond to modern Hansen's disease. Tsara'at was a broader concept encompassing various severe skin conditions—possibly involving dermatological diseases, fungal infections, and even mold on clothing.

Chapter 13 reads like an ancient medical manual—describing diagnostic criteria for various skin symptoms: white spots, red spots, boils, post-burn infections, scalp and beard conditions—each with detailed examination procedures. But brothers and sisters, this was not medicine—this was theology. Priests were not doctors; they did not treat diseases. Priests were judges of clean and unclean. Their work was not prescribing remedies—but declaring "clean" or "unclean."

Three core diagnostic criteria: (1) whether hair in the affected area had turned white; (2) whether the condition appeared "deeper than the skin"; (3) whether the condition was spreading. If all three were met—declared unclean. If uncertain—quarantined seven days then reexamined. If the condition had stopped and not spread—declared clean.

Brothers and sisters, why such caution? Because an "unclean" verdict meant the person must be isolated outside the camp—separated from family, cut off from community, barred from the sanctuary. This was no small matter. So priests had to be extremely careful—better to wait seven more days than rashly expel someone from the community.

Then chapter 13's most heartbreaking passage (vv. 45-46):

> "The leprous person who has the disease shall wear torn clothes and let the hair of his head hang loose, and he shall cover his upper lip and cry out, 'Unclean, unclean.' He shall remain unclean as long as he has the disease. He is unclean. He shall live alone. His dwelling shall be outside the camp."

Brothers and sisters, torn clothes, disheveled hair—this was how Israelites mourned the dead. The person with tsara'at, on the day of expulsion from camp, mourned as if mourning their own death. They were still alive—but socially, they were dead. They no longer belonged to Israel's congregation—could no longer worship, no longer live with family, no longer touch anyone.

> "Cover his upper lip and cry out, 'Unclean, unclean!'"—whenever anyone approached, the afflicted person had to shout warnings. Brothers and sisters, imagine this humiliation—not only isolated, but you must personally announce your uncleanness to every passerby. Your identity reduced to one word: "Unclean."

"He shall live alone. His dwelling shall be outside the camp"—outside the camp was where unclean things were disposed of (sin offering bulls burned outside, ashes dumped outside). The afflicted person was categorized with "unclean things"—reduced from human dignity to refuse status.

Brothers and sisters, tsara'at in Scripture was not merely a disease—it was sin made visible. Sin separates from God, from community, from family—just as tsara'at expelled people from camp. Sin corrodes from within—just as tsara'at spread from deep in the skin outward. Sin strips dignity—just as tsara'at required shouting "Unclean."

The second half (vv. 47-59) extended to tsara'at on clothing—greenish or reddish mold. Garments also needed priestly examination—if mold was spreading, the garment must be burned. Brothers and sisters, uncleanness did not only affect the human body—it "infected" possessions. Sin's impact is not limited to the individual—it spreads to your environment, your relationships, your culture.

Though chapter 13 is a lengthy diagnostic manual, it conveys one core message: uncleanness (sin) is serious—it spreads, it isolates, it destroys. It must be identified (priestly diagnosis), isolated (expulsion from camp), and ultimately cleansed (chapter 14's purification rite). God does not ignore uncleanness—He confronts it, addresses it, and ultimately overcomes it.

## 历史背景（中文）

希伯来文tsara'at不等同于现代医学的麻风病（Hansen's disease，由麻风杆菌Mycobacterium leprae引起）。许多学者认为tsara'at可能包括银屑病（牛皮癣）、白癜风、湿疹、真菌感染等多种皮肤状况。衣物上的tsara'at更可能是霉菌感染。古代近东的赫人和美索不达米亚文化中也有隔离皮肤病患者的记录，但没有以色列这样详细的诊断程序。"关锁七天"的隔离观察期在现代流行病学中被认为是非常先进的——它体现了"观察期"的概念，避免了对不确定病例的轻率判断。"独居营外"——民数记12:10-15米利暗因毁谤摩西而被神击打大麻风，被关在营外七天。列王纪下5章叙利亚将军乃缦的麻风病被以利沙治愈——这些旧约故事都显示tsara'at有深刻的神学（而非纯医学）意义。

## Historical Context (English)

Hebrew tsara'at does not equate to modern Hansen's disease (caused by Mycobacterium leprae). Many scholars believe tsara'at may have included psoriasis, vitiligo, eczema, fungal infections, and other skin conditions. Tsara'at on clothing was more likely mold. Ancient Near Eastern Hittite and Mesopotamian cultures also had records of isolating skin disease patients, but none with Israel's detailed diagnostic procedures. The "seven-day quarantine" observation period is considered remarkably advanced by modern epidemiological standards—embodying the "observation period" concept, avoiding hasty judgment on uncertain cases. "Living alone outside the camp"—Numbers 12:10-15, Miriam was struck with tsara'at for slandering Moses and confined outside camp for seven days. 2 Kings 5, the Syrian general Naaman's tsara'at was healed by Elisha—these Old Testament stories demonstrate tsara'at's deep theological (not purely medical) significance.

## 经文结构（中文）

**皮肤病初步诊断（1-8节）**——疖子癣火斑→祭司察看→毛变白深于皮→不洁净；不确定→关锁七天再察看。

**各种皮肤症状详细条例（9-44节）**——旧的大麻风（白肿活肉）、全身发白反而洁净（v.13）、疖子后的感染、烧伤后的感染、头皮和胡须的癣、无害的白斑、头顶和额秃。

**麻风病人的社会隔离（45-46节）**——撕衣蓬头蒙上唇、喊"不洁净不洁净"、独居营外。

**衣物上的灾病（47-59节）**——羊毛麻布皮子上发绿发红的霉斑→祭司察看→关锁七天→扩散则焚烧→不扩散则洗涤再察看。

## Structure (English)

**Initial Skin Diagnosis (vv. 1-8)**—Swelling scab spot → priest examines → hair white deeper than skin → unclean; uncertain → quarantine seven days reexamine.

**Detailed Conditions (vv. 9-44)**—Chronic tsara'at (white swelling raw flesh), completely white paradoxically clean (v.13), post-boil infection, post-burn infection, scalp and beard conditions, harmless white spots, bald head/forehead.

**Social Isolation of Afflicted (vv. 45-46)**—Torn clothes disheveled hair covered lip, crying "Unclean unclean," dwelling alone outside camp.

**Garment Disease (vv. 47-59)**—Greenish reddish mold on wool linen leather → priest examines → quarantine seven days → spreading then burn → not spreading then wash and reexamine.

## 神学要点（中文）

**罪的可见化——麻风作为罪的预表**
麻风在圣经中是罪的最生动的旧约象征——改革宗传统一贯如此理解。罪像麻风一样从内部腐蚀、向外扩散、导致隔绝。韦敏斯德信条第六章论罪的后果教导："他们因为犯了这罪，就使他们从起初公义并与神相交的关系中堕落了，于是死在罪中。"罪的第一个后果是与神隔绝——正如麻风的第一个后果是与圣所隔绝。

**祭司的诊断角色——教会纪律的旧约根基**
祭司不治病但宣告洁净与否——改革宗传统中教会长老的纪律职能在此有旧约根基。韦敏斯德信条第30章论教会惩戒：为了挽回犯罪的弟兄……保守教会的纯洁。教会的纪律不是惩罚——是诊断和恢复。正如祭司的目标不是永远隔离麻风病人，而是等他洁净后恢复他归入会众。

**关锁七天——恩典的耐心**
不确定的病例不是立刻定为不洁净——而是等待七天再察看。改革宗传统强调神的耐心——彼得后书3:9"主不是耽延祂的应许，乃是宽容你们。"神不轻率地审判——祂给空间、给时间、给观察期。

## Theology (English)

**Sin Made Visible—Leprosy as Sin's Type**
Leprosy in Scripture is the most vivid Old Testament symbol of sin—the Reformed tradition has consistently understood it so. Sin like leprosy corrodes from within, spreads outward, leads to isolation. Westminster Confession chapter 6 on sin's consequences: They fell from their original righteousness and communion with God. Sin's first consequence is separation from God—just as leprosy's first consequence is separation from the sanctuary.

**The Priest's Diagnostic Role—Church Discipline's OT Foundation**
Priests did not cure but declared clean or unclean—the Reformed tradition's elder discipline function finds OT foundation here. Westminster Confession chapter 30 on church censures: For the reclaiming of the offending brother...preserving the purity of the church. Church discipline is not punishment—it is diagnosis and restoration. Just as the priest's goal was not permanent isolation but restoring the cleansed person to the congregation.

**Seven-Day Quarantine—Grace's Patience**
Uncertain cases were not immediately declared unclean—but observed seven days. The Reformed tradition emphasizes God's patience—2 Peter 3:9: "The Lord is not slow to fulfill his promise...but is patient toward you." God does not judge hastily—He gives space, time, and observation periods.

## 基督的影子（中文）

利未记第十三章的麻风病条例是基督医治事工最重要的旧约背景。

基督做了祭司不能做的事——祂不只诊断，祂治愈。马太福音8:2-3"有一个长大麻风的来拜他，说：主若肯，必能叫我洁净了。耶稣伸手摸他，说：我肯，你洁净了吧！他的大麻风立刻就洁净了。"

弟兄姐妹，耶稣"伸手摸他"——在利未记的体系中，触摸麻风病人会使你不洁净。但基督触摸麻风病人——不是祂被传染了不洁净，而是祂的洁净传染给了病人。不洁净遇到基督就变成了洁净。这是福音的核心逻辑——不是罪污染了基督，而是基督的圣洁胜过了罪。

"独居营外"——希伯来书13:12-13"所以，耶稣要用自己的血叫百姓成圣，也就在城门外受苦。这样，我们也当出到营外，就了他去，忍受他所受的凌辱。"基督——那无罪的——被带到"营外"（城门外的各各他），站在了麻风病人（罪人）的位置上。祂不是因为自己的不洁净被逐出——而是甘愿承担我们的不洁净。

"喊叫说：不洁净了！不洁净了！"——以赛亚书53:3"他被藐视，被人厌弃；多受痛苦，常经忧患。"基督在十字架上承受了麻风病人所有的羞辱——被隔离、被厌弃、被定罪。哥林多后书5:21"神使那无罪的，替我们成为罪。"基督成为了终极的"不洁净者"——好叫我们成为洁净。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Leviticus 13's leprosy regulations provide the most important Old Testament background for Christ's healing ministry.

Christ did what priests could not—He not only diagnosed; He healed. Matthew 8:2-3: "A leper came to him and knelt before him, saying, 'Lord, if you will, you can make me clean.' And Jesus stretched out his hand and touched him, saying, 'I will; be clean.' And immediately his leprosy was cleansed."

Brothers and sisters, Jesus "stretched out his hand and touched him"—in Leviticus's system, touching a leper made you unclean. But Christ touched the leper—not that He became infected with uncleanness, but His cleanness "infected" the sick person. Uncleanness meeting Christ became cleanness. This is the gospel's core logic—not that sin contaminated Christ, but that Christ's holiness overcame sin.

"He shall live alone outside the camp"—Hebrews 13:12-13: "So Jesus also suffered outside the gate in order to sanctify the people through his own blood. Therefore let us go to him outside the camp and bear the reproach he endured." Christ—the sinless one—was taken "outside the camp" (Golgotha outside the city gate), standing in the leper's (sinner's) place. He was not expelled for His own uncleanness—but willingly bore ours.

"Crying out, 'Unclean, unclean!'"—Isaiah 53:3: "He was despised and rejected by men, a man of sorrows and acquainted with grief." Christ on the cross bore every humiliation the leper endured—isolation, rejection, condemnation. 2 Corinthians 5:21: "For our sake he made him to be sin who knew no sin." Christ became the ultimate "unclean one"—so that we might become clean.
