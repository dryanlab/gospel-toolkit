## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，路加福音第十七章从主耶稣对门徒的教导开始，涵盖了信徒生活中最根本的几件事：绊倒人的罪、饶恕、信心，以及神国的来临。这一章虽然短，却层层递进，从日常的人际关系直到末世的大审判。

**绊倒人的罪（1-4节）**

耶稣首先说："绊倒人的事是免不了的；但那绊倒人的有祸了！就是把磨石拴在这人的颈项上，丢在海里，还强如他把这小子里的一个绊倒了。"

主不是说绊倒人无关紧要，恰恰相反，他说这比被磨石沉海还可怕。"小子"在原文里指信心幼小的弟兄姐妹。我们每个人都可能无意中成为别人跌倒的原因：我们的论断、冷漠、坏榜样，都可能绊倒软弱的弟兄。主对此极其严肃。

紧接着，主说："若是你的弟兄得罪你，就劝戒他；他若懊悔，就饶恕他。倘若他一天七次得罪你，又七次回转说'我懊悔了'，你总要饶恕他。"（3-4节）

一天七次！这不是在考验我们的数学，而是在考验我们的心。饶恕不是一种感觉，而是一个决定，一个效法基督的决定。我们自己岂不是天天得罪神，天天蒙赦免吗？

**芥菜种的信心（5-6节）**

门徒听完这些话，意识到自己做不到，就求主："求主加增我们的信心！"这是诚实的祷告。但主的回答出人意料："你们若有信心像一粒芥菜种，就是对这棵桑树说'你要拔起根来，栽在海里'，它也必听从你们。"

主不是说我们需要更大的信心，而是说我们需要真实的信心。一粒芥菜种是最小的种子，但它是活的。信心不在大小，在于对象，信心的能力不在我们里面，在于我们所信靠的神。一个孩子小小的手指能按下开关，点亮整栋大楼的灯，力量不在手指，在于电力。

**无用仆人的比喻（7-10节）**

接着主讲了一个比喻：仆人从田里回来，主人会不会对他说"你坐下来吃饭"？不会。主人会说"你先伺候我吃饭，等我吃完了，你才可以吃。"仆人做了一切所吩咐的，主人会特别感谢他吗？"不会。"

所以耶稣说："这样，你们做完了一切所吩咐的，只当说：'我们是无用的仆人，所做的本是我们应分做的。'"

这段话让人不舒服，我们做了那么多，连一句"谢谢"都没有？但主要教的不是神不感恩，而是我们不要以为自己做了多了不起的事。一切的善行都是神的恩典使我们能做的。谦卑不是贬低自己，是正确认识自己与神的关系。

**十个麻风病人（11-19节）**

耶稣往耶路撒冷去，经过撒玛利亚和加利利的边界。有十个长大麻风的迎面而来，远远地站着，高声说："耶稣，夫子，可怜我们吧！"

耶稣没有当场医治他们，而是说："你们去把身体给祭司察看。"他们去的时候就洁净了，信心在顺服中得到验证。

但结局让人心酸：十个人都得了医治，只有一个回来感恩。而这一个，竟然是撒玛利亚人，犹太人最看不起的外邦人。耶稣说："洁净了的不是十个人吗？那九个在哪里呢？除了这外族人，再没有别人回来归荣耀与神吗？"

九个犹太人得了医治就走了，不回来感恩。一个外族人回来俯伏在耶稣脚前。这是一幅令人扎心的画面，蒙恩的人不一定感恩，被看不起的人反而更知道珍惜。我们是哪一种人？

**神的国几时来到（20-37节）**

法利赛人问耶稣："神的国几时来到？"他们期待的是一个政治性的弥赛亚王国，一支军队，一场革命。但耶稣说："神的国来到不是眼所能见的。人也不得说'看哪，在这里！看哪，在那里！'因为神的国就在你们心里。"（20-21节）

"神的国就在你们心里"，更准确地说，"在你们中间"。耶稣本人就是神国的化身，他站在法利赛人面前，而他们却看不见。他们在等一个王国，王就在他们面前。

然后耶稣转向门徒，讲到人子再来的日子。他说那日子来到之前，"人子必须先受许多苦，又被这世代弃绝"（25节）。十字架在荣耀之前。

主用两个旧约的例子来描述那日子的突然性：

> "挪亚的日子怎样，人子的日子也要怎样。那时候的人又吃又喝，又娶又嫁，到挪亚进方舟的那日，洪水就来，把他们全都灭了。"（26-27节）

> "又好像罗得的日子；人又吃又喝，又买又卖，又耕种又盖造。到罗得出所多玛的那日，就有火与硫磺从天上降下来，把他们全都灭了。"（28-29节）

注意，吃喝嫁娶本身不是罪。问题在于人们完全沉浸在日常生活中，对神的警告毫无知觉。审判来到的时候，他们毫无准备。

"人子显现的日子也要这样。"（30节）

耶稣接着警告："当那日，人在房上，器具在屋里，不要下来拿；人在田里，也不要回家。你们要回想罗得的妻子。"（31-32节）

罗得的妻子，她的身体出了所多玛，心却还留在那里。她回头一望，就变成了盐柱。这是对所有信徒最严厉的警告之一：不要留恋这个将要过去的世界。

最后，主说了一句震撼的话："凡想要保全生命的，必丧掉生命；凡丧掉生命的，必救活生命。"（33节）这个原则贯穿整本福音书，你越紧紧抓住属世的安全感，越会失去真正的生命；你越为基督放手，越会得着永恒。

"我对你们说，当那一夜，两个人在一个床上，要取去一个，撇下一个。两个女人一同推磨，要取去一个，撇下一个。"（34-35节）

审判的分别不看外在条件：同一张床上、同一个磨盘旁，而是看内在的信心。与你最亲近的人也不能代替你站在神面前。每个人必须自己与神交账。

门徒问："主啊，在哪里有这事呢？"耶稣回答说："尸首在哪里，鹰也必聚在哪里。"（37节）这是一句犹太谚语，哪里有腐败，审判就必然临到。这不是预测地点，而是宣告原则：神的审判像秃鹰一样精准，绝不会弄错目标。

弟兄姐妹，这一章从饶恕讲到信心，从感恩讲到神国，最后归到一个核心问题：当人子再来的时候，你预备好了吗？不是将来某天预备，是今天。你现在活着的方式，就是你的答案。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, Luke chapter 17 begins with the Lord Jesus teaching His disciples, covering the most fundamental matters of the believer's life: the sin of causing others to stumble, forgiveness, faith, and the coming of God's kingdom. Though short, this chapter progresses layer by layer, from daily relationships to the final judgment.

**The Sin of Causing Stumbling (vv. 1-4)**

Jesus first says: "It is impossible but that offences will come: but woe unto him, through whom they come! It were better for him that a millstone were hanged about his neck, and he cast into the sea, than that he should offend one of these little ones."

The Lord is not saying that causing stumbling is insignificant, quite the opposite. He says it is worse than being drowned with a millstone. "Little ones" in the original text refers to brothers and sisters who are young in faith. Each of us may unknowingly become a cause of someone else's fall, our judgment, indifference, or bad example can all trip up a weaker brother. The Lord is extremely serious about this.

Immediately after, the Lord says: "If thy brother trespass against thee, rebuke him; and if he repent, forgive him. And if he trespass against thee seven times in a day, and seven times in a day turn again to thee, saying, I repent; thou shalt forgive him." (vv. 3-4)

Seven times a day! This is not testing our arithmetic but our hearts. Forgiveness is not a feeling but a decision, a decision to imitate Christ. Do we not ourselves offend God daily and receive daily pardon?

**Faith Like a Mustard Seed (vv. 5-6)**

After hearing all this, the disciples realized they could not do it on their own and asked: "Lord, increase our faith!" This is an honest prayer. But the Lord's answer was unexpected: "If ye had faith as a grain of mustard seed, ye might say unto this sycamine tree, Be thou plucked up by the root, and be thou planted in the sea; and it should obey you."

The Lord is not saying we need greater faith, but that we need genuine faith. A mustard seed is the smallest of seeds, but it is alive. Faith's power lies not in its size but in its object, the power of faith is not within us but in the God we trust. A child's tiny finger can flip a switch and light up an entire building, the power is not in the finger but in the electricity.

**The Parable of the Unprofitable Servant (vv. 7-10)**

Next the Lord told a parable: when a servant comes in from the field, will the master say "Come and sit down to eat"? No. The master will say "Prepare my supper, gird yourself, and serve me until I have eaten; afterward you may eat." After the servant has done everything commanded, will the master especially thank him? "I think not."

So Jesus says: "So likewise ye, when ye shall have done all those things which are commanded you, say, We are unprofitable servants: we have done that which was our duty to do."

This passage is uncomfortable, we do so much and don't even get a "thank you"? But the Lord is not teaching that God is ungrateful. Rather, He is teaching us not to think we have done anything extraordinary. All our good works are enabled by God's grace. Humility is not self-deprecation; it is rightly understanding our relationship with God.

**Ten Lepers Cleansed (vv. 11-19)**

As Jesus journeyed toward Jerusalem, passing between Samaria and Galilee, ten lepers met Him. Standing afar off, they cried: "Jesus, Master, have mercy on us!"

Jesus did not heal them on the spot but said: "Go shew yourselves unto the priests." And as they went, they were cleansed, faith was verified through obedience.

But the ending is heartbreaking: all ten were healed, yet only one returned to give thanks. And that one was a Samaritan, the very people the Jews despised most. Jesus said: "Were there not ten cleansed? but where are the nine? There are not found that returned to give glory to God, save this stranger."

Nine Jewish men received healing and walked away without gratitude. One foreigner came back and fell at Jesus' feet. This is a piercing picture, the blessed are not always grateful, and the despised sometimes treasure grace more. Which kind are we?

**The Coming of the Kingdom (vv. 20-37)**

The Pharisees asked Jesus: "When will the kingdom of God come?" They expected a political Messianic kingdom, an army, a revolution. But Jesus answered: "The kingdom of God cometh not with observation: Neither shall they say, Lo here! or, lo there! for, behold, the kingdom of God is within you." (vv. 20-21)

"The kingdom of God is within you", more precisely, "in your midst." Jesus Himself is the embodiment of the kingdom, standing right before the Pharisees, yet they could not see it. They were waiting for a kingdom; the King was standing before them.

Then Jesus turned to the disciples and spoke of the day of the Son of Man's return. He said that before that day, "the Son of man must suffer many things, and be rejected of this generation" (v. 25). The cross comes before glory.

The Lord used two Old Testament examples to describe the suddenness of that day:

"And as it was in the days of Noe, so shall it be also in the days of the Son of man. They did eat, they drank, they married wives, they were given in marriage, until the day that Noe entered into the ark, and the flood came, and destroyed them all." (vv. 26-27)

"Likewise also as it was in the days of Lot; they did eat, they drank, they bought, they sold, they planted, they builded; But the same day that Lot went out of Sodom it rained fire and brimstone from heaven, and destroyed them all." (vv. 28-29)

Note that eating, drinking, and marrying are not sinful in themselves. The problem is that people were completely absorbed in daily life, oblivious to God's warnings. When judgment came, they were utterly unprepared.

"Even thus shall it be in the day when the Son of man is revealed." (v. 30)

Jesus then warned: "In that day, he which shall be upon the housetop, and his stuff in the house, let him not come down to take it away: and he that is in the field, let him likewise not return back. Remember Lot's wife." (vv. 31-32)

Lot's wife, her body left Sodom, but her heart remained there. She looked back and became a pillar of salt. This is one of the sternest warnings to all believers: do not cling to this passing world.

Finally, the Lord spoke a staggering word: "Whosoever shall seek to save his life shall lose it; and whosoever shall lose his life shall preserve it." (v. 33) This principle runs through the entire Gospel, the more tightly you grip worldly security, the more you lose true life; the more you release for Christ's sake, the more you gain eternity.

"I tell you, in that night there shall be two men in one bed; the one shall be taken, and the other shall be left. Two women shall be grinding together; the one shall be taken, and the other left." (vv. 34-35)

The separation of judgment does not depend on external circumstances, the same bed, the same millstone, but on inward faith. Even those closest to you cannot stand before God in your place. Everyone must give their own account.

The disciples asked: "Where, Lord?" Jesus answered: "Wheresoever the body is, thither will the eagles be gathered together." (v. 37) This is a Jewish proverb, where there is corruption, judgment will surely come. This is not predicting a location but declaring a principle: God's judgment is as precise as vultures finding a carcass; it never misses its mark.

Brothers and sisters, this chapter moves from forgiveness to faith, from gratitude to the kingdom, and finally arrives at one core question: when the Son of Man returns, will you be ready? Not someday in the future, today. The way you are living right now is your answer.
