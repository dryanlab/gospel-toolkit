## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十七章有两个截然不同的段落，却共同讲述了同一个主题：传承。西罗非哈的女儿们为父亲的产业传承争取权益——神宣布我要死了、约书亚来接班。前者是地上产业的传承，后者是领袖职分的传承。

**西罗非哈的五个女儿（1-11节）**

弟兄姐妹，这是整部摩西五经中最出人意料的法律案例之一。五个女儿——玛拉、挪阿、曷拉、密迦、得撒——她们的父亲西罗非哈死在旷野，没有留下儿子。按照当时的社会习俗，产业只传给儿子——没有儿子就意味着父亲的名字和产业在以色列中消失。

但这五个女儿做了一件勇敢的事——她们"站在会幕门口，在摩西和祭司以利亚撒，并众首领与全会众面前"，为父亲的产业申诉。她们没有私下牢骚——她们公开站在以色列最高的权威面前，清晰地说明了她们的理由："我们的父亲不与可拉同党——他在自己的罪中死的，不是因为叛逆。为什么因为没有儿子就把他的名从族中除掉呢？"

弟兄姐妹，"不与可拉同党"——她们特别强调父亲不是叛逆者，他的死是"自己罪中"（普通人的死亡），不是神的特别审判。因此她们的请求是合理的：父亲虽然死了，但不应该因为没有儿子就失去产业权。

摩西不知道该怎么判决——"于是摩西将他们的案件呈到耶和华面前。"

耶和华说："西罗非哈的女儿说得有理。"弟兄姐妹——"说得有理"（ken）——字面意思是"她们是对的"。神不只是容忍她们的请求——神主动说她们是对的，并因此更改了以色列的继承法：

> "人若死了没有儿子，就要把他的产业归给他的女儿。他若没有女儿，就要把他的产业给他的弟兄。他若没有弟兄，就要把他的产业给他父亲的弟兄。"

弟兄姐妹，这是一个关于神公义的重要画面：神的律法不是僵化的、拒绝被质疑的。当有真实的不公正出现，当有人以正当的方式提出正当的请求——神愿意听、愿意回应、愿意更新祂的命令使之更加公平。五个女儿的勇敢改变了以色列的法律历史。

**神宣告摩西的死期（12-14节）**

然后——就在这个关于传承的案例之后——神转过头来对我说："你上这亚巴琳山，观看我所赐给以色列人的地。看了以后，你也必归到你列祖那里，像你哥哥亚伦一样。"

弟兄姐妹，神直接说了——"你要死了"。原因也重申了："因为你们在寻的旷野……违背了我的命，没有在涌水之地、会众眼前尊我为圣。"米利巴的那一击——三十八年后，仍是我无法进迦南的原因。

弟兄姐妹，我不辩解、不求情减轻审判——我做的第一件事是为以色列的未来祈祷：

**摩西为接班人祈祷（15-17节）**

> "摩西对耶和华说：愿耶和华万人之灵的神，立一个人治理会众，可以在他们面前出入，也可以引导他们，免得耶和华的会众如同没有牧人的羊群一般。"

弟兄姐妹，我没有求神改变祂的决定——我没有说"求你饶恕我，让我进迦南"（我在申命记3:23-26确实求过，被神拒绝了）。这里我做的是：立刻为百姓的未来祷告，求神预备下一任领袖。

"万人之灵的神"——这是一个美丽的神名。神管辖所有人的灵——祂知道谁适合，祂能预备谁。

"如同没有牧人的羊群一般"——弟兄姐妹，这句话深入我心。旷野四十年，我深知这群人需要多少的牧养、多少的引领、多少的保护。如果他们没有领袖就分散了——那不只是悲剧，那是遭遇敌人的死亡。

**约书亚的立定（18-23节）**

神指名了约书亚——"嫩的儿子约书亚是心中有圣灵的"。弟兄姐妹，神拣选接班人的标准不是军事才能、不是口才、不是政治手腕——而是"心中有圣灵"。约书亚有神的灵在他心中——这就是神对领袖的首要要求。

我按神所吩咐的："将你的尊荣给他几分"——是"几分"，不是全部。摩西是独一的，没有人能完全接续摩西。但在那几分尊荣的传递中，约书亚得以成为以色列的领袖。

"他要站在祭司以利亚撒面前；以利亚撒要凭乌陵的判断，在耶和华面前为他求问。"弟兄姐妹，约书亚不像摩西——摩西与神面对面说话，不需要乌陵。约书亚需要通过乌陵来求问神——这是职分降了一等。但这没有关系——每一代领袖按神所赐的恩典和渠道来领受神的旨意。

我按手在约书亚头上——在以利亚撒和全会众面前嘱咐他。弟兄姐妹，这是我一生最重要的时刻之一——不是过红海，不是接收律法——而是把手放在一个年轻人的头上，把使命传给他。这就是领袖职分的本质：不是牢牢握住权力，而是在对的时候把它传下去。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 27 has two very different sections yet share one theme: inheritance. The daughters of Zelophehad fight for their father's inheritance rights—then God announced I would die and Joshua would succeed me. The former was earthly inheritance transmission; the latter was leadership office succession.

**Zelophehad's Five Daughters (vv. 1-11)**

Brothers and sisters, this was one of the entire Pentateuch's most unexpected legal cases. Five daughters—Mahlah, Noah, Hoglah, Milcah, and Tirzah—their father Zelophehad had died in the wilderness without sons. By contemporary social custom, inheritance passed only to sons—no sons meant the father's name and inheritance disappearing from Israel.

But these five daughters did something courageous—they "stood before Moses and before Eleazar the priest and before the chiefs and all the congregation, at the entrance of the tent of meeting," appealing for their father's inheritance. They did not complain privately—they stood publicly before Israel's highest authorities, clearly stating their case: "Our father died in the wilderness. He was not among the company of those who gathered against the LORD in the company of Korah—he died for his own sin, and had no sons. Why should the name of our father be taken away from his clan because he had no son?"

Brothers and sisters, "not in the company of Korah"—they specifically emphasized their father was no rebel; his death was "his own sin" (ordinary human death), not divine special judgment. Therefore their request was reasonable: though the father died, his inheritance should not be lost simply because he had no sons.

Moses did not know how to rule—"Moses brought their case before the LORD."

The LORD said: "The daughters of Zelophehad are right." Brothers and sisters—"are right" (ken)—literally "they are correct." God did not merely tolerate their request—He actively affirmed they were right, and thereby revised Israel's inheritance law:

> "If a man dies and has no son, then you shall transfer his inheritance to his daughter. And if he has no daughter, then you shall give his inheritance to his brothers. And if he has no brothers, then you shall give his inheritance to his father's brothers."

Brothers and sisters, this is an important picture of God's justice: God's law is not rigid, refusing to be questioned. When genuine injustice exists, when someone brings a legitimate request in a legitimate way—God is willing to hear, respond, and update His commands to make them more just. Five daughters' courage changed Israelite legal history.

**God Announces Moses' Death (vv. 12-14)**

Then—immediately after this inheritance case—God turned to me: "Go up into this mountain of Abarim and see the land that I have given to the people of Israel. When you have seen it, you also shall be gathered to your people, as your brother Aaron was."

Brothers and sisters, God said it plainly—"you will die." The reason restated: "Because you rebelled against my word in the wilderness of Zin...you did not uphold me as holy before their eyes at the waters of Meribah." That one strike—thirty-eight years later, still the reason I cannot enter Canaan.

Brothers and sisters, I did not argue or appeal to reduce the judgment—my first act was praying for Israel's future:

**Moses Prays for a Successor (vv. 15-17)**

> "Moses spoke to the LORD, saying, 'Let the LORD, the God of the spirits of all flesh, appoint a man over the congregation who shall go out before them and come in before them, who shall lead them out and bring them in, that the congregation of the LORD may not be as sheep that have no shepherd.'"

Brothers and sisters, I did not ask God to reverse His decision—I did not say "please forgive me, let me enter Canaan" (I did ask this in Deuteronomy 3:23-26, and God refused). What I did here was: immediately pray for the people's future, asking God to prepare the next leader.

"God of the spirits of all flesh"—a beautiful name for God. God governs all human spirits—He knows who is suitable, He can prepare anyone.

"As sheep that have no shepherd"—brothers and sisters, this phrase penetrated my heart. Forty wilderness years, I deeply understood how much this people needed shepherding, guiding, protecting. If they scattered without a leader—that was not merely tragedy, it was deadly when meeting enemies.

**Joshua's Appointment (vv. 18-23)**

God named Joshua—"Joshua the son of Nun, a man in whom is the Spirit, lay your hand on him." Brothers and sisters, God's criterion for choosing a successor was not military talent, eloquence, or political skill—but "in whom is the Spirit." Joshua had God's Spirit in him—this was God's primary requirement for leaders.

As God commanded: "You shall invest him with some of your authority"—"some," not all. Moses was unique; no one could fully succeed Moses. But in that partial transfer of authority, Joshua became Israel's leader.

"He shall stand before Eleazar the priest, who shall inquire for him by the judgment of the Urim before the LORD." Brothers and sisters, Joshua was not like Moses—Moses spoke with God face to face, needing no Urim. Joshua needed the Urim to inquire of God—a step down in direct access. But this was acceptable—each generation's leader receives God's will according to the grace and channels God provides.

I laid hands on Joshua's head—charged him before Eleazar and all the congregation. Brothers and sisters, this was one of the most important moments of my life—not crossing the Red Sea, not receiving the law—but placing my hands on a young man's head, passing the mission to him. This is leadership's essence: not gripping power tightly, but passing it on at the right moment.

## 历史背景（中文）

西罗非哈女儿的案例（Zelophehad's daughters）在以色列法律史上极为重要。她们的请求产生的继承原则（女儿在无男性继承人时可继承）在约书亚记17:3-6得到了落实——她们确实获得了迦南的产业。塔木德对这个案例有大量讨论，认为这五个女儿比摩西还有更好的判断力（因为摩西需要去求问神）。历史上各文化对这个故事的解读反映了不同时代对女性继承权的态度。乌陵（Urim）和土明（Thummim）是两个神圣器物，被放在大祭司的决断胸牌里，用于在某些情况下寻求神的旨意——通常被理解为某种抽签或指示设备。具体运作方式已经失传，但它们的功能是传达是/否或选择答案的神圣指引。历史记载乌陵和土明在第二圣殿时期已经不再使用（拉2:63），以色列人寄望于一位合格的祭司来恢复使用。

## Historical Context (English)

The case of Zelophehad's daughters was extremely important in Israelite legal history. Their request's resulting inheritance principle (daughters can inherit when no male heirs exist) was implemented in Joshua 17:3-6—they did receive Canaanite inheritance. The Talmud extensively discusses this case, arguing these five daughters had better judgment than Moses (since Moses needed to consult God). Throughout history, various cultures' interpretation of this story reflects different eras' attitudes toward women's inheritance rights. The Urim and Thummim were two sacred objects placed in the high priest's decision breastpiece, used to seek God's will in certain situations—typically understood as some kind of lot or indicator device. Their exact mechanism has been lost, but their function was conveying divine guidance for yes/no or selection answers. Historical records indicate Urim and Thummim ceased being used during the Second Temple period (Ezra 2:63), with Israel hoping a qualified priest would restore their use.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神倾听女儿们——神的律法与公义**
五个女儿的请求改变了律法——改革宗传统教导神的律法的公义性。韦敏斯德信条第19章论神的律法。神的律法不是随意的文化约定——它以神的公义为基础。当律法执行导致不公义的结果，神愿意澄清和更新——不是因为律法有错，而是因为应用需要更充分的理解。"她们说得有理"——神肯定了公义的主张，即使它挑战了既有的习俗。

**领袖的传承——牧养优先于权力**
摩西听到死亡宣告后，第一个祷告是为百姓求牧者——改革宗传统教导牧养的优先性。韦敏斯德信条第30-31章论教会治理。好的领袖不思念自己的利益——他的第一个问题是"谁来牧养他们？"领袖的本质不是权力的拥有，而是使命的完成和传承。

**"心中有圣灵的人"——领袖的资格**
神选约书亚因他"心中有圣灵"——改革宗传统教导领袖职分以属灵恩赐为基础。韦敏斯德信条第30章论教会的长老和执事。不是因为口才、经历或血统——而是圣灵的内住。使徒行传6:3"当从你们中间选七个有好名声、被圣灵充满、智慧充足的人，我们就派他们管理这事。"

## Theology (English)

**God Heeds the Daughters—God's Law and Justice**
Five daughters' request changed the law—the Reformed tradition teaches God's law's justice. Westminster Confession chapter 19 on God's law. God's law is not arbitrary cultural convention—it is grounded in God's justice. When law's application produces unjust results, God is willing to clarify and update—not because the law was wrong, but because application needed fuller understanding. "They are right"—God affirmed a just claim even when it challenged existing custom.

**Leadership Succession—Shepherding Before Power**
Moses heard the death announcement and his first prayer was seeking a shepherd for the people—the Reformed tradition teaches shepherding's priority. Westminster Confession chapters 30-31 on church governance. A good leader does not think of his own interests—his first question is "who will shepherd them?" Leadership's essence is not power's possession but mission's completion and transmission.

**"A Man in Whom Is the Spirit"—Leadership Qualifications**
God chose Joshua because he had "the Spirit in him"—the Reformed tradition teaches leadership office is grounded in spiritual gifting. Westminster Confession chapter 30 on church elders and deacons. Not eloquence, experience, or lineage—but the Spirit's indwelling. Acts 6:3: "Pick out from among you seven men of good repute, full of the Spirit and of wisdom, whom we will appoint to this duty."

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第二十七章有两个强烈的基督预表。

**西罗非哈的女儿——恩典中被纳入产业的人**
五个女儿没有继承权——但因她们的请求，神给了她们产业。弟兄姐妹，我们在神面前本来没有继承权——我们不是神的儿子，我们是罪人，理应被排除在外。但基督是神的儿子——罗马书8:17"我们若是儿女，便是后嗣，就是神的后嗣，和基督同作后嗣。"在基督里，我们被纳入了神的家——成为神的后嗣。就像五个女儿因请求而得产业——我们因基督而得神的产业。加拉太书3:29"你们若属基督，就是亚伯拉罕的后裔，是照着应许承受产业的了。"

**摩西的祷告——"免得如没有牧人的羊群"**
马太福音9:36"他看见许多的人，就怜悯他们；因为他们困苦流离，如同羊没有牧人一般。"耶稣看到群众——用了和摩西祷告完全相同的画面。但摩西只是求神立一个牧人——耶稣说"我是好牧人"（约10:11）。摩西代求要一个牧人——基督自己来了作那牧人。约翰福音10:14-15"我是好牧人；我认识我的羊，我的羊也认识我……我为羊舍命。"

**约书亚继承摩西——耶稣（Yeshua）带人进入神的安息**
约书亚（Yeshua）名字与耶稣（Yeshua）完全相同——希伯来书4:8"若是约书亚曾叫他们享了安息，后来神就不再提别的日子了。"摩西（律法）无法带人进入应许之地——约书亚/耶稣才能。约书亚带以色列人越过约旦河、进入迦南——耶稣基督带信徒越过死亡、进入神的国度。摩西"把手按在约书亚头上"——这是圣职传承的印证：律法把使命传给了福音。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 27 contains two powerful Christ prefigurations.

**Zelophehad's Daughters—Those Brought Into Inheritance Through Grace**
Five daughters had no inheritance rights—but through their request, God gave them inheritance. Brothers and sisters, before God we originally had no inheritance rights—we are not God's sons, we are sinners, rightly excluded. But Christ is God's Son—Romans 8:17: "And if children, then heirs—heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ." In Christ we are brought into God's family—becoming God's heirs. As the five daughters received inheritance through asking—we receive God's inheritance through Christ. Galatians 3:29: "And if you are Christ's, then you are Abraham's offspring, heirs according to promise."

**Moses' Prayer—"Lest They Be as Sheep Without a Shepherd"**
Matthew 9:36: "When he saw the crowds, he had compassion for them, because they were harassed and helpless, like sheep without a shepherd." Jesus looking at the crowds used the exact same image as Moses' prayer. But Moses only asked God to appoint a shepherd—Jesus said "I am the good shepherd: the good shepherd giveth his life for the sheep." (John 10:11). Moses interceded for a shepherd—Christ Himself came as that shepherd. John 10:14-15: "I am the good shepherd. I know my own and my own know me...I lay down my life for the sheep."

**Joshua Succeeding Moses—Jesus (Yeshua) Leading People Into God's Rest**
Joshua (Yeshua) shares the exact same name as Jesus (Yeshua)—Hebrews 4:8: "For if Joshua had given them rest, God would not have spoken of another day later on." Moses (the law) could not lead people into the promised land—Joshua/Jesus could. Joshua brought Israel across the Jordan into Canaan—Jesus Christ brings believers through death into God's kingdom. Moses "laid his hands on Joshua"—the imprint of sacred succession: the law passed the mission to the gospel.
