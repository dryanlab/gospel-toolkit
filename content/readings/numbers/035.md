## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第三十五章建立了以色列法律制度中最具人道主义精神的制度之一——逃城。同时也规定了利未人的四十八座城邑。

**利未人的四十八座城（1-8节）**

利未人没有自己的领地——但他们需要住处。所以十二个支派要从自己的产业中拿出城邑和周围的郊野给利未人居住。一共四十八座城——分布在以色列全地。

弟兄姐妹，利未人散布在全以色列——不是被边缘化，而是被有意地分散。他们是以色列的教师、祭司辅助者、律法的传递者——他们需要在每一个社区都有存在。四十八座城遍布各支派的领地——确保以色列没有任何角落缺乏神的律法教导。

**六座逃城（9-15节）**

> "就要分出几座城，为你们作逃城，使误杀人的可以逃到那里。"

弟兄姐妹，四十八座利未城中有六座被指定为"逃城"——约旦河东三座，约旦河西三座。逃城是为谁设的？为"误杀人的"——不是为故意杀人者，而是为无意中导致他人死亡的人。

在以色列的法律框架中，杀人后死者的家属有"报血仇"的权利——"报血仇的人"（go'el hadam）可以合法地杀死杀人者。但如果杀人是无意的呢？误杀者需要一个安全的地方——逃城就是这个避难所。

**故杀与误杀的区分（16-28节）**

第三十五章用极大的篇幅区分故杀和误杀：

故杀的证据：用铁器打死（预谋的武器）、用石头打死（有意的暴力）、用木器打死、因怨恨推倒人、埋伏投掷物致死。这些都说明有预谋或仇恨——凶手必被治死。

误杀的证据：没有仇恨、没有预谋、没有埋伏——偶然的、意外的致人死亡。这样的人可以逃到逃城。

弟兄姐妹，这个区分是古代法律中非常先进的——它区分了主观意图和客观行为。同样的结果（人死了）——不同的动机导致不同的判决。故杀=死刑；误杀=逃城避难。

**逃城的规则——直到大祭司死（25-28节）**

这是逃城制度中最耐人寻味的条款：

> "会众要救这误杀人的脱离报血仇人的手，也要使他归入逃城。他要住在其中，直等到受圣膏的大祭司死了。"

弟兄姐妹，误杀者必须留在逃城里——"直等到大祭司死了"。如果他在大祭司死之前走出逃城的境外——报血仇的人有权杀他，"没有流血之罪"。但大祭司一死——误杀者自由了，可以回到自己的产业。

弟兄姐妹，为什么是大祭司的死？不是说大祭司为误杀者赎罪——而是大祭司的死标志着一个时代的结束和新时代的开始。大祭司去世，一个新的大祭司受膏——旧的账目在这个新开始中被一笔勾销。

**血的神学（30-34节）**

"故杀人、犯死罪的，你们不可收赎价代替他的命。"弟兄姐妹——故意杀人不可用金钱赎罪。生命的价值不能用金钱衡量。

"血是污秽地的；若有在地上流人血的，非流那杀人者的血，那地就不得洁净。"弟兄姐妹——无辜的血会"污秽"土地。不是比喻——在神看来，人的血渗入土地——从该隐杀亚伯开始，"你兄弟的血有声音从地里向我哀告"（创4:10）——无辜的血在神面前有声音。

最后一句："你们不可玷污所住之地，就是我住在其中之地，因为我耶和华住在以色列人中间。"弟兄姐妹——"因为我住在其中"——以色列的土地是神居住的地方。血腥的罪不只是人与人之间的事——它污染了神居住的地方。这就是为什么公义如此重要——不是因为社会需要——而是因为神与你同住。

弟兄姐妹，逃城制度的美丽在于它同时维护了两个看似矛盾的原则——公义（杀人者必须承担后果）和怜悯（误杀者有庇护的权利）。公义不会因为怜悯被废弃——误杀者仍然被限制在逃城中，不能自由回家。怜悯也不会因为公义被忽视——误杀者不必为一个意外而付出生命的代价。弟兄姐妹，这是神的性格——公义与怜悯在祂里面完美统一。诗篇85:10"慈爱和诚实彼此相遇；公义和平安彼此相亲。"逃城就是公义和平安"彼此相亲"的具体制度。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 35 established one of the most humanitarian institutions in Israelite law—the cities of refuge. It also designated the Levites' forty-eight cities.

**The Levites' Forty-Eight Cities (vv. 1-8)**

Levites had no territorial inheritance—but needed dwelling places. So the twelve tribes were to give cities and surrounding pasturelands from their own inheritance to the Levites. Forty-eight cities total—distributed throughout all Israel.

Brothers and sisters, Levites were scattered across all Israel—not marginalized but intentionally dispersed. They were Israel's teachers, priestly assistants, and law transmitters—they needed presence in every community. Forty-eight cities spanning all tribal territories ensured no corner of Israel lacked God's law teaching.

**Six Cities of Refuge (vv. 9-15)**

> "You shall select cities to be cities of refuge for you, that the manslayer who kills any person without intent may flee there."

Brothers and sisters, among the forty-eight Levitical cities, six were designated "cities of refuge"—three east of the Jordan, three west. For whom? For "the manslayer"—not intentional killers, but those who accidentally caused another's death.

In Israel's legal framework, after a killing, the victim's family had "blood avenger" rights—the go'el hadam could legally kill the killer. But what if the killing was unintentional? The manslayer needed a safe haven—the city of refuge was that sanctuary.

**Distinguishing Murder from Manslaughter (vv. 16-28)**

Chapter 35 devoted extensive space to distinguishing murder from manslaughter:

Evidence of murder: killing with iron (premeditated weapon), killing with stone (intentional violence), killing with wood, pushing someone in hatred, lying in wait and throwing objects causing death. These all indicated premeditation or hatred—the murderer must be put to death.

Evidence of manslaughter: no enmity, no premeditation, no ambush—accidental, unintentional causing of death. Such a person could flee to the city of refuge.

Brothers and sisters, this distinction was extremely advanced in ancient law—it differentiated subjective intent from objective action. Same result (death)—different motives led to different verdicts. Murder = death penalty; manslaughter = refuge asylum.

**Refuge Rules—Until the High Priest Dies (vv. 25-28)**

This was the refuge system's most intriguing provision:

> "The congregation shall restore him to his city of refuge...and he shall live in it until the death of the high priest who was anointed with the holy oil."

Brothers and sisters, the manslayer must remain in the city of refuge—"until the high priest dies." If he left the city's boundaries before the high priest's death—the blood avenger could legally kill him, "without bloodguilt." But when the high priest died—the manslayer was free, able to return to his inheritance.

Brothers and sisters, why the high priest's death? Not that the high priest atoned for the manslayer—but the high priest's death marked one era's end and a new era's beginning. The old accounts were settled in this new beginning.

**The Theology of Blood (vv. 30-34)**

"You shall accept no ransom for the life of a murderer." Brothers and sisters—intentional murder could not be ransomed with money. Life's value cannot be measured in gold.

"Blood pollutes the land, and no atonement can be made for the land for the blood that is shed in it, except by the blood of the one who shed it." Brothers and sisters—innocent blood "pollutes" the land. Not metaphorically—in God's eyes, human blood soaking into the land—from Cain killing Abel, "And he said, What hast thou done? the voice of thy brother’s blood crieth unto me from the ground." (Gen 4:10)—innocent blood has a voice before God.

The final verse: "You shall not defile the land in which you live, in the midst of which I dwell, for I the LORD dwell in the midst of the people of Israel." Brothers and sisters—"for I dwell in the midst"—Israel's land was where God dwelt. Bloody sin was not merely interpersonal—it contaminated God's dwelling place. This is why justice mattered so greatly—not because society needed it—but because God dwells with you.

## 历史背景（中文）

逃城（'ir miqlat）制度在古代近东有一些平行——古希腊有"神圣庇护所"（hieron asylon），逃到神庙中的人可以免受追杀。但以色列的逃城制度是唯一一个建立在精确的法律区分（故杀vs误杀）之上的。约书亚记20章记录了逃城的实际设立——六座城分别是：基低斯（拿弗他利，北方）、示剑（以法莲，中部）、基列亚巴/希伯伦（犹大，南方）——在约旦河西；比悉（流便）、基列的拉末（迦得）、哥兰（玛拿西）——在约旦河东。"报血仇"（go'el hadam）制度在游牧和半游牧社会中很常见——家族成员有义务为被杀的亲属报仇。逃城制度的目的不是废除报血仇——而是在其中引入法律程序和误杀保护。

## Historical Context (English)

The city of refuge ('ir miqlat) system had some ancient Near Eastern parallels—ancient Greece had "sacred asylums" (hieron asylon) where those fleeing to temples received protection from pursuit. But Israel's refuge system was uniquely built on precise legal distinction (murder vs. manslaughter). Joshua 20 records the actual establishment of the six cities: Kedesh (Naphtali, north), Shechem (Ephraim, central), Kiriath-arba/Hebron (Judah, south)—west of the Jordan; Bezer (Reuben), Ramoth in Gilead (Gad), Golan (Manasseh)—east of the Jordan. The "blood avenger" (go'el hadam) system was common in nomadic and semi-nomadic societies—family members had obligations to avenge slain relatives. The city of refuge's purpose was not abolishing blood vengeance—but introducing legal process and manslaughter protection within it.

## 经文结构（中文）

**利未人的四十八座城（1-8节）**——各支派从产业中给利未人城邑+郊野→共48城。

**逃城的设立（9-15节）**——48城中6座为逃城→河东3座河西3座→为误杀人者提供庇护→以色列人和外人均可使用。

**故杀与误杀的区分（16-24节）**——铁器/石头/木器/仇恨推倒/埋伏=故杀→死刑；无仇无预谋=误杀→逃城。

**大祭司之死与释放（25-28节）**——误杀者住逃城直到大祭司死→出城被杀无罪→大祭司死后可回。

**血的神圣性（29-34节）**——不可凭一个见证定罪→故杀不可赎→血污秽地→非流杀人者血地不得洁净→"我耶和华住在以色列中间"。

## Structure (English)

**Levites' Forty-Eight Cities (vv. 1-8)**—Each tribe gives Levites cities + pasturelands from their inheritance → 48 cities total.

**Establishing Cities of Refuge (vv. 9-15)**—6 of 48 cities as refuge → 3 east, 3 west of Jordan → shelter for manslayers → available to Israelites and foreigners alike.

**Distinguishing Murder from Manslaughter (vv. 16-24)**—Iron/stone/wood/hatred/ambush = murder → death penalty; no enmity/no premeditation = manslaughter → city of refuge.

**High Priest's Death and Release (vv. 25-28)**—Manslayer stays in refuge until high priest dies → leaving early, killed without guilt → after high priest's death, may return.

**Blood's Sanctity (vv. 29-34)**—Cannot convict on one witness → murder unredeemable → blood pollutes land → only killer's blood cleanses → "I the LORD dwell among Israel."

## 神学要点（中文）

**逃城——恩典的避难所**
误杀者有一个逃避之处——改革宗传统教导恩典为罪人提供避难所。韦敏斯德信条第11章论称义。逃城不是给完美的人——而是给犯了错但不是故意的人。基督徒的避难所不是因为我们无罪——而是因为基督是我们的逃城。

**大祭司的死带来释放——恩典的时间性**
大祭司之死=误杀者被释放——改革宗传统教导基督的死带来永远的释放。韦敏斯德信条第8章论基督为中保。旧约的释放是有条件的（大祭司死才能回家）——新约的释放是无条件的（基督一次死了就永远释放了我们）。

**血的神圣——生命的不可替代性**
故杀不可用赎价替代——改革宗传统教导人类生命的神圣不可侵犯。韦敏斯德大要理问答第135-136问论第六诫。创世记9:6"凡流人血的，他的血也必被人所流，因为神造人是照自己的形像造的。"

## Theology (English)

**Cities of Refuge—Grace's Sanctuary**
The manslayer had a place to flee—the Reformed tradition teaches grace provides sinners a refuge. Westminster Confession chapter 11 on justification. The city of refuge was not for the perfect—but for those who erred without intent. Christians' refuge is not because we are sinless—but because Christ is our city of refuge.

**High Priest's Death Brings Release—Grace's Temporality**
High priest's death = manslayer freed—the Reformed tradition teaches Christ's death brings eternal release. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ as Mediator. Old Testament release was conditional (could only return home when the high priest died)—New Testament release is unconditional (Christ died once and freed us forever).

**Blood's Sanctity—Life's Irreplaceability**
Murder cannot be ransomed—the Reformed tradition teaches human life's sacred inviolability. Westminster Larger Catechism Q135-136 on the sixth commandment. Genesis 9:6: "Whoever sheds the blood of man, by man shall his blood be shed, for God made man in his own image."

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第三十五章有旧约最清晰的基督预表之一。

**逃城——基督是我们的避难所**
希伯来书6:18"我们这逃往避难所的人可以大得勉励，抓住摆在我们前头的指望。"弟兄姐妹，逃城的人"逃往"那里——我们"逃往"基督。逃城不是天然的选择——是你被追赶时的唯一选择。信靠基督也不是轻松的决定——是你意识到自己在审判之下的唯一出路。

**大祭司之死——基督的死释放了我们**
误杀者被释放的条件是大祭司的死——基督是我们的大祭司（来4:14），祂的死释放了我们。但有一个关键区别：旧约的大祭司死了，然后有新的大祭司继任——基督死了之后复活了，不再有继任者，因为祂永远活着（来7:24-25"这位既是永远常存的，他祭司的职任就长久不更换。凡靠着他进到神面前的人，他都能拯救到底"）。误杀者的释放是暂时的（每一代大祭司死后释放一批）——我们在基督里的释放是永恒的一次性事件。

**"血有声音从地里向我哀告"——基督的血说出更美的话**
创世记4:10亚伯的血"从地里向我哀告"——希伯来书12:24"并新约的中保耶稣，以及所洒的血——这血比亚伯的血所说的更美。"弟兄姐妹，亚伯的血呼求审判——基督的血呼求赦免。无辜的血在神面前有声音——但基督的血的声音压过了一切无辜之血的呼求。祂的血不是说"报仇"——而是说"赦免他们，因为他们所做的他们不知道"（路23:34）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 35 contains one of the Old Testament's clearest Christ prefigurations.

**Cities of Refuge—Christ Is Our Refuge**
Hebrews 6:18: "We who have fled for refuge might have strong encouragement to hold fast to the hope set before us." Brothers and sisters, refugees "fled" to the city—we "flee" to Christ. The refuge city was not a natural choice—it was your only option when being pursued. Trusting Christ is not a casual decision—it is your only way out when you realize you are under judgment.

**High Priest's Death—Christ's Death Released Us**
The manslayer's release condition was the high priest's death—Christ is our High Priest (Heb 4:14), and His death released us. But a key difference: the Old Testament high priest died, then a new one succeeded—Christ died then rose, with no successor, because He lives forever (Heb 7:24-25: "He holds his priesthood permanently, because he continues forever. Consequently, he is able to save to the uttermost those who draw near to God through him"). The manslayer's release was periodic (each high priest's death released a batch)—our release in Christ is a one-time eternal event.

**"Blood Crying From the Ground"—Christ's Blood Speaks Better**
Genesis 4:10: Abel's blood "crying to me from the ground"—Hebrews 12:24: "And to Jesus, the mediator of a new covenant, and to the sprinkled blood that speaks a better word than the blood of Abel." Brothers and sisters, Abel's blood cried for judgment—Christ's blood cries for forgiveness. Innocent blood has a voice before God—but Christ's blood's voice overrides all innocent blood's cry. His blood does not say "avenge"—but "Then said Jesus, Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do. And they parted his raiment, and cast lots." (Luke 23:34).
