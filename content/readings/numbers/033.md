## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第三十三章是以色列旷野四十年的完整行程记录——四十二个站口，从埃及的兰塞到摩押平原。然后是进迦南的最终命令。

**行程记录（1-49节）**

> "以色列人按着军队，在摩西、亚伦的手下出埃及地所行的路程记在下面。摩西遵着耶和华的吩咐记载他们所行的路程。"

弟兄姐妹，"摩西遵着耶和华的吩咐记载"——这不是随手写的旅行日记，这是奉神的命令做的正式记录。神要以色列人记住每一个停留过的地方。

四十二个站口——从兰塞出发，经过疏割、以倘、比哈希录（过红海的地方）、旷野中的各个营地——直到摩押平原。弟兄姐妹，大多数站名我们除了这份清单之外一无所知——没有故事、没有事件、只有名字。拉玛、拿哈、利提玛……这些名字对以色列人来说，是每一段旷野经历的坐标。

但有一些站名是我们知道的：玛拉（苦水变甜）、以琳（十二股水泉七十棵棕树）、西奈的旷野（领受律法）、基博罗哈他瓦（贪肉的坟墓）、何珥山（亚伦死在那里）。每一个名字背后都有一段故事——恩典的、审判的、眼泪的。

弟兄姐妹，为什么神要求记录这份清单？因为旅途的每一步都是有意义的——不只是终点（迦南）有意义。那些无名的营地、那些平淡的日子、那些没有大事件发生的站口——也是旷野旅途的一部分。弟兄姐妹，你生命中有多少"无名的站口"？没有戏剧性的事件——只是日复一日、普通的忠诚。神记住了每一个站口。

这份清单也告诉我们：旷野不是绕圈子——尽管漫长，但路程是有方向的。从兰塞到摩押平原——不是回到原点，而是到达了约旦河边。弟兄姐妹，当你觉得你的人生在旷野中漂泊的时候——也许你比你以为的更接近应许之地。

第38-39节记录了亚伦的死——"以色列人出了埃及地后四十年，五月初一日，祭司亚伦遵着耶和华的吩咐上何珥山，就死在那里。亚伦死在何珥山的时候年一百二十三岁。"弟兄姐妹，旅程记录中嵌入了亚伦的死——旅途的一部分就是同行者的离去。

**进迦南的命令和警告（50-56节）**

到了摩押平原，神给了最后的命令和警告：

> "你们过约旦河进迦南地的时候，就要从你们面前赶出那里所有的居民，毁灭他们一切錾成的石像和他们一切铸成的偶像，又拆毁他们一切的丘坛。"

弟兄姐妹，三件事要做：赶出居民、毁灭偶像、拆毁丘坛。这不是简单的军事占领——是彻底的属灵洁净。迦南地的问题不只是人口——而是偶像。如果你占领了土地却保留了偶像——土地最终会反过来占领你。

"倘若你们不赶出那地的居民，所容留的居民就必作你们眼中的刺，肋下的荆棘，也必在你们所住的地上扰害你们。"弟兄姐妹——"眼中的刺，肋下的荆棘"——不会致命，但会持续刺痛。你不处理的罪不会消失——它会成为你一辈子的刺和荆棘。

最严肃的警告是最后一句："而且我素常有意怎样待他们，也必照样待你们。"弟兄姐妹——如果以色列人效仿了迦南人的偶像崇拜——神会用审判迦南人的同样方式来审判以色列人。以色列不是因为自己的义被拣选——他们与迦南人在本质上没有区别。如果他们犯了迦南人同样的罪——他们会遭受迦南人同样的命运。

弟兄姐妹，后来的历史证明了这个警告的真实性——以色列人确实没有完全赶出迦南居民（士师记1-2章），确实被偶像引诱（士师记时期反复拜巴力），最终——被掳到巴比伦（王下25章）。神的警告不是空话——是历史的预言。

弟兄姐妹，第三十三章教导我们两件事：第一，记住你走过的路——每一个站口都是恩典的见证；第二，彻底处理你生命中的偶像——不要留下"眼中的刺"。回头看是为了感恩和见证——向前看是为了洁净和得胜。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 33 was the complete itinerary of Israel's forty wilderness years—forty-two stations, from Rameses in Egypt to the plains of Moab. Then came the final command to enter Canaan.

**The Travel Record (vv. 1-49)**

> "These are the stages of the people of Israel, when they went out of the land of Egypt by their companies under the leadership of Moses and Aaron. Moses wrote down their starting places, stage by stage, by command of the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, "Moses wrote down by command of the LORD"—this was not a casual travel diary, but a formal record made by God's command. God wanted Israel to remember every place they had stopped.

Forty-two stations—departing from Rameses, through Succoth, Etham, Pi-hahiroth (Red Sea crossing), through various wilderness camps—to the plains of Moab. Brothers and sisters, most station names we know nothing about beyond this list—no stories, no events, just names. Rithmah, Rimmon-perez, Libnah...for Israel, these names were coordinates of every wilderness experience.

But some names we know: Marah (bitter water made sweet), Elim (twelve springs, seventy palm trees), Wilderness of Sinai (receiving the law), Kibroth-hattaavah (graves of craving), Mount Hor (Aaron died there). Each name had a story behind it—of grace, judgment, tears.

Brothers and sisters, why did God require recording this list? Because every step of the journey mattered—not just the destination (Canaan). Those unnamed camps, those ordinary days, those uneventful stations—were also part of the wilderness journey. Brothers and sisters, how many "unnamed stations" does your life have? No dramatic events—just day after day of ordinary faithfulness. God remembers every station.

This list also tells us: the wilderness was not going in circles—though lengthy, the journey had direction. From Rameses to the plains of Moab—not back to the starting point, but arriving at the Jordan's edge. Brothers and sisters, when you feel your life is drifting in the wilderness—perhaps you are closer to the promised land than you think.

Verses 38-39 recorded Aaron's death—"Aaron the priest went up Mount Hor at the command of the LORD and died there, in the fortieth year after the people of Israel had come out of the land of Egypt...Aaron was 123 years old when he died on Mount Hor." Brothers and sisters, embedded in the journey record was Aaron's death—part of any journey is companions departing.

**The Canaan Entry Command and Warning (vv. 50-56)**

At the plains of Moab, God gave the final command and warning:

> "When you pass over the Jordan into the land of Canaan, then you shall drive out all the inhabitants of the land from before you and destroy all their figured stones and destroy all their metal images and demolish all their high places."

Brothers and sisters, three things to do: drive out inhabitants, destroy idols, demolish high places. This was not simple military occupation—it was thorough spiritual cleansing. Canaan's problem was not merely population—but idols. If you occupied the land but kept the idols—the land would eventually occupy you.

"But if you do not drive out the inhabitants of the land from before you, then those of them whom you let remain shall be as barbs in your eyes and thorns in your sides, and they shall trouble you in the land where you dwell." Brothers and sisters—"barbs in your eyes and thorns in your sides"—not fatal, but persistently painful. Sin you don't deal with doesn't disappear—it becomes lifelong barbs and thorns.

The most solemn warning was the last sentence: "And I will do to you as I thought to do to them." Brothers and sisters—if Israel imitated Canaanite idolatry—God would judge Israel the same way He judged Canaan. Israel was not chosen because of their own righteousness—they were no different from Canaan in essence. If they committed Canaan's sins—they would suffer Canaan's fate.

Brothers and sisters, later history proved this warning true—Israel indeed did not fully drive out Canaan's inhabitants (Judges 1-2), indeed was enticed by idols (repeatedly worshipping Baal during the Judges period), and ultimately—was exiled to Babylon (2 Kings 25). God's warning was not empty words—it was prophetic history.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 33 teaches two things: first, remember the road you've walked—every station is a witness of grace; second, thoroughly deal with idols in your life—leave no "barbs in your eyes." Looking back is for gratitude and testimony—looking forward is for cleansing and victory.

## 历史背景（中文）

民数记33章的四十二个站口是以色列旷野行程最完整的记录——但考古学无法确认大部分地名。从兰塞（Pi-Ramesses，今尼罗河三角洲东部）出发是比较确定的——这是埃及第十九王朝的首都。红海渡口的确切位置仍有争议（苏伊士湾北端、苦湖区域或其他）。西奈山的位置也有多种说法（传统的Jebel Musa、Jebel Serbal或沙特阿拉伯的Jebel al-Lawz等）。"四十二"这个数字在犹太传统中有特殊意义——马太福音1:17的耶稣家谱中也有42代（从亚伯拉罕到基督分三组各14代），一些教父（如耶柔米）将旷野四十二站口与基督家谱42代联系起来。迦南偶像的"錾成的石像"（maskit）可能指雕刻的石碑或画像石，"铸成的偶像"（massekah）指金属铸造的神像，"丘坛"（bamot）是露天的崇拜高处。

## Historical Context (English)

Numbers 33's forty-two stations constitute the most complete record of Israel's wilderness itinerary—but archaeology cannot confirm most place names. Departure from Rameses (Pi-Ramesses, today's eastern Nile Delta) is relatively certain—it was the Nineteenth Dynasty capital. The Red Sea crossing's exact location remains debated (northern Gulf of Suez, Bitter Lakes area, or other). Mount Sinai's location also has multiple theories (traditional Jebel Musa, Jebel Serbal, or Saudi Arabia's Jebel al-Lawz, etc.). "Forty-two" held special significance in Jewish tradition—Matthew 1:17's genealogy of Jesus also had 42 generations (Abraham to Christ in three groups of 14), and some church fathers (like Jerome) connected the wilderness's forty-two stations with Christ's genealogy of 42 generations. Canaan's "figured stones" (maskit) likely referred to carved stelae or painted stones, "metal images" (massekah) to cast metal idols, "high places" (bamot) to open-air worship elevations.

## 经文结构（中文）

**旅程记录序言（1-2节）**——摩西遵耶和华吩咐记录路程。

**四十二站口（3-49节）**——兰塞→疏割→以倘→比哈希录(过红海)→……→西奈旷野→……→基博罗哈他瓦→……→何珥山(亚伦死)→……→摩押平原。

**进迦南命令（50-53节）**——赶出居民→毁灭石像偶像→拆毁丘坛→夺地住其中。

**分地原则（54节）**——按家室拈阄、人多多分人少少分。

**不赶出居民的后果（55-56节）**——留下的居民成眼中刺肋下荆棘→"我怎样待他们也必照样待你们"。

## Structure (English)

**Journey Record Preface (vv. 1-2)**—Moses records journey stages by the LORD's command.

**Forty-two Stations (vv. 3-49)**—Rameses → Succoth → Etham → Pi-hahiroth (Red Sea crossing) → ... → Wilderness of Sinai → ... → Kibroth-hattaavah → ... → Mount Hor (Aaron dies) → ... → Plains of Moab.

**Canaan Entry Command (vv. 50-53)**—Drive out inhabitants → destroy stone images/idols → demolish high places → take and dwell.

**Land Distribution Principle (v. 54)**—Lots by families; larger gets more, smaller gets less.

**Consequences of Not Driving Out Inhabitants (vv. 55-56)**—Remaining become barbs in eyes, thorns in sides → "I will do to you as I thought to do to them."

## 神学要点（中文）

**记住你走过的路——记忆的神学**
神命令摩西记录旅程——改革宗传统教导记忆和见证的重要性。韦敏斯德信条第1章论圣经的记录和保存。以色列的旅程不是消失在沙漠中的足迹——它被正式记录、代代传承。申命记4:9"你只要谨慎……免得你忘记你亲眼所看见的事。"记忆是信仰的操练——忘记神的作为是堕落的开始。

**彻底清除偶像——不留余地**
"毁灭一切石像、偶像、丘坛"——改革宗传统教导对偶像的彻底排斥。韦敏斯德大要理问答第105问论第二诫。不是容忍偶像然后希望它们不影响你——而是彻底摧毁它们。生命中的偶像（不只是石头的雕像——可以是金钱、地位、情欲、任何取代神位置的东西）必须被完全拆除。

**"我怎样待他们也照样待你们"——选民不享有特权**
以色列不因选民身份获得道德豁免——改革宗传统教导神的公正不偏待人。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理。罗马书2:11"因为神不偏待人。"选民身份是更大的责任不是更多的自由。以色列犯了迦南人的罪就遭受迦南人的命运——教会犯了世界的罪就失去对世界的见证。

## Theology (English)

**Remember the Road You Walked—The Theology of Memory**
God commanded Moses to record the journey—the Reformed tradition teaches memory and witness's importance. Westminster Confession chapter 1 on Scripture's recording and preservation. Israel's journey was not footprints disappearing in desert sand—it was formally recorded, transmitted across generations. Deuteronomy 4:9: "Only take care...lest you forget the things your eyes have seen." Memory is a faith discipline—forgetting God's works is the beginning of falling away.

**Thoroughly Remove Idols—Leave No Remnant**
"Destroy all images, idols, high places"—the Reformed tradition teaches thorough rejection of idols. Westminster Larger Catechism Q105 on the second commandment. Not tolerating idols while hoping they won't influence you—but thoroughly destroying them. Life's idols (not just stone statues—money, status, lust, anything displacing God) must be completely dismantled.

**"I Will Do to You as I Thought to Do to Them"—The Elect Have No Privilege**
Israel's elect status did not grant moral immunity—the Reformed tradition teaches God's impartial justice. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence. Romans 2:11: For God shows no partiality. Election is greater responsibility, not greater freedom. Israel committing Canaan's sins suffered Canaan's fate—the church committing the world's sins loses its witness to the world.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第三十三章指向基督在两个方面。

**四十二站口——基督与教会的旷野旅途**
以色列四十二站口——马太福音1:17基督家谱也是42代（14+14+14）。从亚伯拉罕到基督——是救赎历史的"行程记录"。每一代都是一个"站口"——有些平凡，有些充满悲剧（被掳）。但每一个站口都不是白走的——它们一步一步把历史引向了基督的降生。弟兄姐妹，你生命中平凡无名的日子——也是通向基督的站口。

**"赶出居民毁灭偶像"——基督洁净圣殿**
神命令以色列赶出居民、毁灭偶像——约翰福音2:15-16耶稣进圣殿"就拿绳子做成鞭子，把牛羊都赶出殿去，倒出兑换银钱之人的银钱，推翻他们的桌子。"基督洁净圣殿——就是"赶出"和"毁灭"在新约的成全。基督不只是温柔的牧人——祂也是圣殿的洁净者。祂不容忍偶像在神的殿中——也不容忍偶像在信徒的心中。哥林多前书3:16"岂不知你们是神的殿，神的灵住在你们里头吗？"你的心就是神的殿——基督要洁净它，赶出一切不属于神的东西。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 33 points to Christ in two aspects.

**Forty-two Stations—Christ and the Church's Wilderness Journey**
Israel's forty-two stations—Matthew 1:17's genealogy of Christ also has 42 generations (14+14+14). From Abraham to Christ—was redemption history's "journey record." Each generation was a "station"—some ordinary, some filled with tragedy (exile). But no station was walked in vain—step by step they led history to Christ's birth. Brothers and sisters, your life's ordinary, unnamed days—are also stations leading toward Christ.

**"Drive Out Inhabitants, Destroy Idols"—Christ Cleanses the Temple**
God commanded Israel to drive out inhabitants and destroy idols—John 2:15-16: Jesus entered the temple and "making a whip of cords, he drove them all out of the temple...and he poured out the coins of the money-changers and overturned their tables." Christ cleansing the temple—was "driving out" and "destroying" fulfilled in the New Testament. Christ is not only a gentle shepherd—He is also the temple's cleanser. He tolerates no idols in God's temple—nor in believers' hearts. 1 Corinthians 3:16: "Do you not know that you are God's temple and that God's Spirit dwells in you?" Your heart is God's temple—Christ will cleanse it, driving out everything that does not belong to God.
