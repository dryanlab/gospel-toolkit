## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十六章是民数记的第二次人口普查——与第一章遥遥相对。第一章在西奈山脚下数点了第一代人——六十万三千五百五十名二十岁以上能打仗的男丁。现在，三十八年过去了，瘟疫之后，在摩押平原约旦河边——神说：再数一次。

**为什么要第二次数点？**

弟兄姐妹，第二次数点有三个目的：

第一——确认审判的完成。第十四章神宣布那一代人要死在旷野——"被数的人中，没有一个是摩西和祭司亚伦从前在西奈的旷野所数的以色列人……除了耶孚尼的儿子迦勒和嫩的儿子约书亚以外，连一个人也没有存留。"弟兄姐妹，六十万三千五百五十人——除了迦勒和约书亚——在三十八年间全部死了。神说他们要死在旷野——他们就死在了旷野。这是神话语的严肃性：祂说的每一句话都会成就。

第二——为分地做准备。"你要按着人名的数目将地分给这些人为业。人多的，你要把产业多分给他们；人少的，你要把产业少分给他们。"第二次数点不再是为了编排军队——而是为了分配迦南的土地。战争已经近在眼前——但神已经在计划分地了。在进入之前就规划好了每个支派的产业。

第三——这是新一代的军事登记。这些人——不是在埃及出生、在红海边长大的那一代——而是在旷野出生、在旷野长大的孩子们——现在他们二十岁了，可以打仗了。他们没有埃及奴隶的记忆——他们只知道旷野、吗哪、云柱和火柱。他们是更纯粹的旷野之子。

**两次数点的对比**

第一次（民数记1章，西奈）：603,550人
第二次（民数记26章，摩押）：601,730人

弟兄姐妹——差距只有1,820人！三十八年的旷野漂泊、无数的瘟疫和审判——总人数几乎没有变化。这意味着什么？意味着在六十万人死去的同时——又有六十万人在旷野中出生和成长。一代人倒下了——但下一代人站起来了。死亡没有减少以色列——新的生命填补了每一个空缺。

但各支派的变化很大：

西缅支派从59,300锐减到22,200——减少了63%！弟兄姐妹，心利——在什亭公然行淫的那个人——是"西缅一个宗族的首领"。西缅支派在毘珥的瘟疫中损失最为惨重。罪不只影响个人——它影响整个社群。

玛拿西支派从32,200增加到52,700——增长了63.7%。有些支派在旷野中增长了——神的祝福并不均匀分布在每个支派。

> **"然而可拉的众子没有死亡"（11节）**

弟兄姐妹，第二十六章在支派记录的中间插入了一句简短而惊人的话——"然而可拉的众子没有死亡。"第十六章可拉叛乱，地开口吞了可拉和他一党——但可拉的儿子们没有跟随父亲的叛逆。他们存活了下来。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇42、44-49、84-85、87-88——这些被称为"可拉后裔的诗"——是以色列诗歌文学中最美的一些篇章。"如鹿切慕溪水，我的心切慕你"（诗42:1）——这是可拉后裔写的。叛逆者的后代写出了最渴慕神的诗歌——恩典大过审判。

**利未人的单独数点（57-62节）**

利未人不在战斗人数中——他们从一个月以上的男丁被数，共23,000人。他们"没有数在以色列人中；因为在以色列人中，没有分给他们产业"——他们的产业是耶和华自己（第十八章"我就是你的分"）。

**结语：除了迦勒和约书亚（63-65节）**

> "被数的人中，没有一个是摩西和祭司亚伦从前在西奈的旷野所数的以色列人……除了耶孚尼的儿子迦勒和嫩的儿子约书亚以外，连一个人也没有存留。"

弟兄姐妹，六十万中只剩两个人。两个——在十二个探子中唯一有信心的两个。他们看到了同样的巨人、同样的城墙——但他们说"我们上去吧"。三十八年后——他们的同辈全部死了，但他们还活着。信心使他们存活了一整代人——不信使六十万人死在了旷野。

弟兄姐妹，第二十六章是民数记的轴心——从旧的一代转到新的一代。审判已经执行完毕——六十万人死了。应许即将应验——新的六十万人站在约旦河边，等待进入迦南。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 26 is Numbers' second census—bookending chapter 1. Chapter 1 at Mount Sinai numbered the first generation—603,550 men twenty and older fit for war. Now, thirty-eight years later, after the plague, on the plains of Moab by the Jordan—God said: count again.

**Why a Second Census?**

Brothers and sisters, the second count served three purposes:

First—confirming judgment's completion. Chapter 14 decreed that generation would die in the wilderness—"Among those who were listed there was not one of those listed by Moses and Aaron the priest, who had listed the people of Israel in the wilderness of Sinai...not one of them was left, except Caleb the son of Jephunneh and Joshua the son of Nun." Brothers and sisters, 603,550 people—except Caleb and Joshua—all died within thirty-eight years. God said they would die in the wilderness—and they did. This is God's word's seriousness: every word He speaks comes to pass.

Second—preparing for land distribution. "To these the land shall be divided for inheritance according to the number of names. To a large tribe you shall give a large inheritance, and to a small tribe a small inheritance." The second census was no longer for army organization—but for allocating Canaan's land. War was imminent—yet God was already planning distribution. Before entering, each tribe's inheritance was already planned.

Third—this was the new generation's military registration. These people—not born in Egypt or raised at the Red Sea—but born and raised in the wilderness. Now they were twenty, fit for war. They had no memories of Egyptian slavery—only wilderness, manna, the pillar of cloud and fire. They were purer children of the wilderness.

**Comparing the Two Censuses**

First (Numbers 1, Sinai): 603,550
Second (Numbers 26, Moab): 601,730

Brothers and sisters—a difference of only 1,820! Thirty-eight years of wilderness wandering, countless plagues and judgments—the total barely changed. What does this mean? While 600,000 died—another 600,000 were born and raised in the wilderness. One generation fell—the next rose. Death did not diminish Israel—new life filled every vacancy.

But tribal variations were dramatic:

Simeon plummeted from 59,300 to 22,200—a 63% decrease! Brothers and sisters, Zimri—who openly sinned at Shittim—was "a chief in Simeon." Simeon suffered most from Peor's plague. Sin affects not just individuals—but entire communities.

Manasseh grew from 32,200 to 52,700—a 63.7% increase. Some tribes grew in the wilderness—God's blessings were not evenly distributed.

> **"The Sons of Korah Did Not Die" (v. 11)**

Brothers and sisters, chapter 26 inserted a brief, stunning note amid tribal records—"the sons of Korah did not die." Chapter 16: Korah rebelled, the earth swallowed him—but Korah's sons did not follow their father's rebellion. They survived.

Brothers and sisters, Psalms 42, 44-49, 84-85, 87-88—called "Psalms of the Sons of Korah"—are among Israel's most beautiful poetic literature. "As the hart panteth after the water brooks, so panteth my soul after thee, O God." (Ps 42:1)—written by Korah's descendants. Rebels' offspring wrote the most God-longing poetry—grace exceeds judgment.

**Levites Counted Separately (vv. 57-62)**

Levites were not in the fighting count—males one month and older numbered 23,000. They "were not listed among the people of Israel, because there was no inheritance given to them"—their inheritance was the LORD Himself (chapter 18: "I am your portion").

**Conclusion: Only Caleb and Joshua (vv. 63-65)**

> "Among those listed there was not one of those listed by Moses and Aaron the priest...not one of them was left, except Caleb and Joshua."

Brothers and sisters, from 600,000 only two remained. Two—the only faithful ones among twelve spies. They saw the same giants, the same walls—but said "let us go up." Thirty-eight years later—their peers all dead, but they lived. Faith sustained them an entire generation—unbelief killed 600,000 in the wilderness.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 26 is Numbers' pivot—from the old generation to the new. Judgment was complete—600,000 dead. The promise was about to be fulfilled—new 600,000 standing by the Jordan, awaiting entry into Canaan.

## 历史背景（中文）

古代近东的人口普查在行政和军事上都很常见——埃及、美索不达米亚和赫梯帝国都有详细的人口登记记录。以色列的普查独特之处在于它是按支派和宗族组织的——这反映了以色列社会以血缘-部落关系为基础的结构。两次普查的总数惊人地接近（603,550 vs 601,730），从人口学角度看是合理的——高出生率和高死亡率（旷野条件下）可以导致总量的基本稳定。西缅支派的剧烈缩减在后来的以色列历史中得到了印证——在约书亚分地时，西缅没有获得独立的领地，而是被分配在犹大境内的城邑（书19:1-9）。后来西缅几乎完全被犹大吸收，失去了独立的部落身份。

## Historical Context (English)

Ancient Near Eastern censuses were common for both administrative and military purposes—Egypt, Mesopotamia, and the Hittite empire all had detailed population registration records. Israel's census was unique in its tribal-clan organization—reflecting Israel's kinship-based social structure. The two censuses' remarkably close totals (603,550 vs. 601,730) are demographically plausible—high birth rates and high death rates (under wilderness conditions) could produce basic population stability. Simeon's dramatic decline was confirmed in later Israelite history—in Joshua's land distribution, Simeon received no independent territory but was assigned cities within Judah's borders (Josh 19:1-9). Simeon was later almost completely absorbed by Judah, losing independent tribal identity.

## 经文结构（中文）

**数点命令（1-4节）**——瘟疫之后→摩西和以利亚撒→按宗族数二十岁以上男丁。

**十二支派名册（5-51节）**——流便→西缅→迦得→犹大→以萨迦→西布伦→玛拿西→以法莲→便雅悯→但→亚设→拿弗他利→总数601,730。

**分地原则（52-56节）**——按人数多少分地、拈阄决定位置。

**利未人的单独数点（57-62节）**——革顺/哥辖/米拉利三族→利未男丁23,000人→不在以色列人中分产业。

**结语：旧一代全部死去（63-65节）**——没有一个西奈普查的人存留→除了迦勒和约书亚。

## Structure (English)

**Census Command (vv. 1-4)**—After the plague → Moses and Eleazar → count males twenty and older by clans.

**Twelve Tribes Registry (vv. 5-51)**—Reuben → Simeon → Gad → Judah → Issachar → Zebulun → Manasseh → Ephraim → Benjamin → Dan → Asher → Naphtali → Total 601,730.

**Land Distribution Principles (vv. 52-56)**—Distribute by population size; lots determine location.

**Levites Counted Separately (vv. 57-62)**—Gershon/Kohath/Merari three clans → Levite males 23,000 → no inheritance among Israel.

**Conclusion: Old Generation All Dead (vv. 63-65)**—Not one person from Sinai census remains → except Caleb and Joshua.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神话语的绝对可靠——审判宣告的完全应验**
六十万人中除迦勒约书亚全部死在旷野——改革宗传统教导神话语的绝对确定性。韦敏斯德信条第1章论圣经。民数记14:29"你们的尸首必倒在这旷野。"三十八年后——一个不差。"天地要废去，我的话却不能废去"（太24:35）。

**信心的保守力——迦勒和约书亚**
六十万人不信而死，两个人因信存留——改革宗传统教导信心的保守（perseverance of the saints）。韦敏斯德信条第17章论圣徒的坚忍。迦勒和约书亚不是靠自己的力量活了三十八年——是神保守了他们。信心不是人的成就——是神保守恩典的果子。

**新一代——恩典的延续**
旧一代死了、新一代兴起——改革宗传统教导盟约的代际传承。韦敏斯德信条第25章论教会。神的应许不因一代人的失败而终止——它传给下一代。教会不属于任何一代人——它跨越世代。

## Theology (English)

**God's Word Absolutely Reliable—Judgment Declaration Completely Fulfilled**
600,000 except Caleb and Joshua all died in the wilderness—the Reformed tradition teaches God's word's absolute certainty. Westminster Confession chapter 1 on Scripture. Numbers 14:29: Your dead bodies shall fall in this wilderness. Thirty-eight years later—not one missing. "Heaven and earth shall pass away, but my words shall not pass away." (Matt 24:35).

**Faith's Preserving Power—Caleb and Joshua**
600,000 died in unbelief, two preserved by faith—the Reformed tradition teaches perseverance of the saints. Westminster Confession chapter 17 on saints' perseverance. Caleb and Joshua did not survive thirty-eight years by their own strength—God preserved them. Faith is not human achievement—but the fruit of God's preserving grace.

**New Generation—Grace's Continuation**
Old generation died, new generation rose—the Reformed tradition teaches covenant's generational transmission. Westminster Confession chapter 25 on the church. God's promise does not end with one generation's failure—it passes to the next. The church belongs to no single generation—it spans generations.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第二十六章有三个指向基督的画面。

**六十万死了、六十万兴起——死亡与复活**
一代人完全死去——但新一代从中兴起，人数几乎相同。弟兄姐妹，这是死亡与复活的集体画面。哥林多前书15:42-44"所种的是必朽坏的，复活的是不朽坏的……所种的是血气的身体，复活的是灵性的身体。"旧的一代（血气的）死去——新的一代（灵性的）兴起。旧约的以色列倒在旷野——新约的教会从基督的复活中兴起。

**迦勒和约书亚——信心的余民**
六十万中只有两人存留——罗马书11:5"如今也是这样，照着拣选的恩典，还有所留的余数。"基督的教会不是多数人——而是信心的余民。不是六十万——而是两个。但这两个就够了——因为神的应许通过他们延续。

**"然而可拉的众子没有死亡"——恩典超越审判**
可拉被地吞了——但他的后代存活并写出了最美的赞美诗。罗马书5:20"罪在哪里显多，恩典就更显多了。"弟兄姐妹，在基督里——你父辈的失败不决定你的命运。可拉的后裔成为了圣殿诗班——你在基督里可以成为新造的人，不被过去定义。哥林多后书5:17"若有人在基督里，他就是新造的人，旧事已过，都变成新的了。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 26 contains three images pointing to Christ.

**600,000 Died, 600,000 Rose—Death and Resurrection**
An entire generation died—but a new one rose from among them, nearly identical in number. Brothers and sisters, this is a collective death-and-resurrection picture. 1 Corinthians 15:42-44: "What is sown is perishable; what is raised is imperishable...It is sown a natural body; it is raised a spiritual body." The old generation (natural) died—the new generation (spiritual) rose. Old Testament Israel fell in the wilderness—the New Testament church rose from Christ's resurrection.

**Caleb and Joshua—The Faithful Remnant**
From 600,000 only two remained—Romans 11:5: "So too at the present time there is a remnant, chosen by grace." Christ's church is not the majority—but the faithful remnant. Not 600,000—but two. Yet these two sufficed—because God's promise continued through them.

**"The Sons of Korah Did Not Die"—Grace Surpasses Judgment**
Korah was swallowed by the earth—but his descendants survived and wrote the most beautiful psalms. Romans 5:20: "Where sin increased, grace abounded all the more." Brothers and sisters, in Christ—your forebears' failure does not determine your destiny. Korah's descendants became temple choir—you in Christ can become a new creation, undefined by the past. 2 Corinthians 5:17: "If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come."
