## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第三十二章讲述了一个差点酿成灾难的请求——流便和迦得两个支派看到约旦河东的好牧场，请求留在这边不过河。我听到这个请求的时候——心都凉了。

**流便和迦得的请求（1-5节）**

> "流便子孙和迦得子孙的牲畜极其众多；他们看见雅谢地和基列地是可牧放牲畜之地。"

弟兄姐妹，他们看见了肥沃的牧场——约旦河东，以色列刚刚征服了西宏和噩的领地。广袤的草原、充足的水源——他们的大量牲畜在这里可以兴旺。于是他们提出了请求："求你把这地给我们为业，不要领我们过约旦河。"

弟兄姐妹——"不要领我们过约旦河"——这句话让我瞬间想到了三十八年前的加低斯巴尼亚。

**摩西的愤怒（6-15节）**

> "难道你们的弟兄去打仗，你们竟坐在这里吗？你们为何使以色列人灰心丧胆、不过去进入耶和华所赐给他们的那地呢？"

弟兄姐妹，我直接把他们与他们的先祖对比——三十八年前十个探子使以色列人灰心、不敢进迦南——导致整整一代人死在旷野。现在两个支派也不想过河——如果其他支派效仿呢？如果以色列人看到这两个支派留在安全的河东而失去进攻迦南的勇气呢？

"谁知，你们起来接续先祖，增添罪人的数目，使耶和华向以色列大发烈怒。"弟兄姐妹——我的话很重——"增添罪人的数目"！我把他们的请求等同于不信和叛逆。

"你们若退后不跟从他，他还要把以色列人撇在旷野，便是你们使这众民灭亡。"弟兄姐妹——旷野再漂流四十年？两个支派的自私可能导致全以色列的毁灭。

**妥协的方案（16-27节）**

流便和迦得听了之后赶紧提出了一个修改方案——这个方案改变了一切：

"我们自己要带兵器行在以色列人的前头，好把他们领到他们的地方。"弟兄姐妹，他们不只是愿意过河帮忙——他们主动要求"走在前头"！先锋部队——最危险的位置。

"我们不回家，直等到以色列人各承受自己的产业。"弟兄姐妹——他们把家人安置在河东，自己全部过河参战，直到迦南全部征服才回来。这意味着他们可能几年不能回家——在前线打完所有的仗。

我同意了。但加了一句严厉的警告："倘若你们不这样行，就得罪耶和华，要知道你们的罪必追上你们。"弟兄姐妹——"你们的罪必追上你们"——罪是追赶人的。你以为你可以逃跑——但罪会追上来。

**约旦河东的分地（33-42节）**

迦得建造了底本、亚大录等城；流便建造了希实本、以利亚利等城。玛拿西的半支派（玛吉的后裔）也占领了基列。

弟兄姐妹，从此以色列成了"约旦河两岸"的民族——十二个支派中有两个半在河东，九个半在河西。这个地理分裂在后来的以色列历史中反复造成问题——约书亚记22章河东支派筑坛差点引发内战，士师记时期河东支派常常被忽略。

弟兄姐妹，第三十二章的核心教训是：个人或群体的利益不能以牺牲集体的使命为代价。流便和迦得想要好牧场——这没有错。但如果他们的选择导致其他支派灰心丧胆——那就成了罪。最终的解决方案是两全其美的：得到了想要的土地，但先完成了对弟兄的责任。

弟兄姐妹，你可以选择你自己的道路——但不能在完成对群体的责任之前。自由必须在责任的框架内行使。你的弟兄还在打仗——你不能坐在这里。

弟兄姐妹，这个故事还提醒我们——好东西可以成为试探。约旦河东的草场是好的——没有人说它不好。但当好东西让你停下脚步、不再追求神的应许之地的时候——好东西就成了阻碍。以色列人被埃及的"肉锅"引诱不想走旷野（出16:3）——现在流便和迦得被河东的牧场引诱不想过河。好的环境和丰富的资源可以成为灵性的安慰剂——让你以为"这里就够了"，不再追求更深、更完整的应许。弟兄姐妹，迦南是神指定的应许之地，不是河东。不要在河东停下来。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 32 told of a request that nearly became catastrophic—the tribes of Reuben and Gad, seeing the good pastureland east of the Jordan, asked to stay on this side and not cross over. When I heard this request—my heart went cold.

**Reuben and Gad's Request (vv. 1-5)**

> "Now the people of Reuben and the people of Gad had a very great number of livestock. And they saw the land of Jazer and the land of Gilead, and behold, the place was a place for livestock."

Brothers and sisters, they saw fertile pastureland—east of the Jordan, territory just conquered from Sihon and Og. Vast grasslands, abundant water—their numerous livestock could thrive here. So they requested: "Let this land be given to your servants for a possession. Do not take us across the Jordan."

Brothers and sisters—"do not take us across the Jordan"—this instantly recalled Kadesh-barnea thirty-eight years ago.

**Moses' Anger (vv. 6-15)**

> "Shall your brothers go to the war while you sit here? Why will you discourage the heart of the people of Israel from going over into the land that the LORD has given them?"

Brothers and sisters, I directly compared them to their ancestors—thirty-eight years ago ten spies discouraged Israel from entering Canaan—causing an entire generation to die in the wilderness. Now two tribes also did not want to cross—what if other tribes followed? What if Israel, seeing these two tribes safely staying east of the Jordan, lost courage to attack Canaan?

"And behold, you have risen in your fathers' place, a brood of sinful men, to increase still more the fierce anger of the LORD against Israel!" Brothers and sisters—my words were heavy—"a brood of sinful men"! I equated their request with unbelief and rebellion.

"If you turn away from following him, he will again abandon them in the wilderness, and you will destroy all this people." Brothers and sisters—another forty years of wilderness wandering? Two tribes' selfishness could destroy all Israel.

**The Compromise Solution (vv. 16-27)**

Reuben and Gad immediately offered a modified plan—this changed everything:

"We will take up arms, ready to go before the people of Israel, until we have brought them to their place." Brothers and sisters, they didn't merely agree to help across the river—they volunteered to "go before"! The vanguard—the most dangerous position.

"We will not return to our homes until each of the people of Israel has gained his inheritance." Brothers and sisters—they would settle families east, then all warriors would cross and fight until all Canaan was conquered. This meant potentially years away from home—fighting every battle on the front line.

I agreed. But added a stern warning: "But if you will not do so, behold, you have sinned against the LORD, and be sure your sin will find you out." Brothers and sisters—"your sin will find you out"—sin pursues people. You think you can escape—but sin catches up.

**East Jordan Land Allocation (vv. 33-42)**

Gad built Dibon, Ataroth and other cities; Reuben built Heshbon, Elealeh and others. Half the tribe of Manasseh (Makir's descendants) also occupied Gilead.

Brothers and sisters, from then on Israel became a "both banks of the Jordan" nation—two and a half of twelve tribes on the east bank, nine and a half on the west. This geographic division repeatedly caused problems in later Israelite history—Joshua 22's east-bank altar nearly sparked civil war; during the Judges period east-bank tribes were often neglected.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 32's core lesson: individual or group interests cannot come at the cost of the collective mission. Reuben and Gad wanting good pasture—nothing wrong with that. But if their choice caused other tribes to lose heart—that became sin. The final solution achieved both: they received desired land, but first fulfilled responsibility to their brothers.

Brothers and sisters, you can choose your own path—but not before completing your responsibility to the community. Freedom must be exercised within responsibility's framework. Your brothers are still fighting—you cannot sit here.

## 历史背景（中文）

约旦河东两个半支派（流便、迦得、玛拿西半支派）的领地在后来的以色列历史中命运坎坷——它们是最先被外族征服的以色列领地。亚述帝国在公元前733年将约旦河东的以色列人掳去（王下15:29），比西岸被掳早了十多年。这个地理上的脆弱性——没有约旦河的天然屏障——使得河东支派始终暴露在东方敌人面前。约书亚记22章记载的"大坛"事件——河东支派渡河返回后在约旦河边筑了一座大坛，河西支派以为他们要分裂敬拜，差点发动内战。后来解释这只是一座"见证坛"才化解了冲突。流便支派在后来的以色列历史中逐渐消失——士师记5:15-16底波拉之歌批评"流便人为什么坐在溪水旁？"可见流便早期就有"不参与集体行动"的倾向。

## Historical Context (English)

The Transjordan two-and-a-half tribes' (Reuben, Gad, half-Manasseh) territory had a troubled history in later Israel—they were the first Israelite territories conquered by foreign powers. Assyria deported Transjordan Israelites in 733 BC (2 Kgs 15:29), more than a decade before the west bank's exile. This geographic vulnerability—lacking the Jordan River's natural barrier—left the eastern tribes perpetually exposed to enemies from the east. Joshua 22 records the "great altar" incident—when east-bank tribes returned and built a large altar by the Jordan, west-bank tribes suspected worship schism and nearly launched civil war. Only the explanation that it was a "witness altar" resolved the conflict. The tribe of Reuben gradually disappeared in later Israelite history—Judges 5:15-16 (Deborah's Song) criticized "Why did you sit among the sheepfolds?" showing Reuben's early tendency toward non-participation in collective action.

## 神学要点（中文）

**个人选择与集体责任——弟兄不可被遗弃**
流便迦得的选择差点导致集体灾难——改革宗传统教导教会是一个不可分割的身体。韦敏斯德信条第26章论圣徒相通。"你们的弟兄去打仗你们坐这里？"——在盟约群体中，你的选择不只影响你自己。罗马书14:7"我们没有一个人为自己活，也没有一个人为自己死。"

**"你们的罪必追上你们"——罪的不可逃避性**
摩西的警告——改革宗传统教导罪的后果是确定的。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理。加拉太书6:7"神是轻慢不得的；人种的是甚么，收的也是甚么。"罪不会因为你忽略它就消失——"必追上你们"。

**先完成责任再享受权益——十字架在冠冕之前**
流便和迦得先过河打仗，然后才能回河东享受产业——改革宗传统教导使命优先于个人利益。路加福音9:23"若有人要跟从我，就当舍己，天天背起他的十字架来跟从我。"先十字架后冠冕——先责任后自由。

## Theology (English)

**Individual Choice and Collective Responsibility—Brothers Must Not Be Abandoned**
Reuben and Gad's choice nearly caused collective disaster—the Reformed tradition teaches the church is one indivisible body. Westminster Confession chapter 26 on communion of saints.: Shall your brothers go to war while you sit here?—in a covenant community, your choices don't only affect yourself. Romans 14:7: "None of us lives to himself, and none of us dies to himself."

**"Your Sin Will Find You Out"—Sin's Inescapability**
Moses' warning—the Reformed tradition teaches sin's consequences are certain. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence. Galatians 6:7: God is not mocked, for whatever one sows, that will he also reap. Sin does not disappear because you ignore it—"it will find you out."

**Complete Responsibility Before Enjoying Rights—Cross Before Crown**
Reuben and Gad fought first across the Jordan, then could return east to enjoy their inheritance—the Reformed tradition teaches mission before personal gain. Luke 9:23: "If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me." First cross, then crown—first responsibility, then freedom.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第三十二章预表基督在两个方面。

**走在前头——基督是先锋**
流便和迦得承诺"走在以色列人前头"——希伯来书12:2"仰望为我们信心创始成终的耶稣。他因那摆在前面的喜乐，就轻看羞辱，忍受了十字架的苦难。"基督走在前头——祂不是在后面推我们向前，祂是在前面引我们通过死亡、进入生命。约翰福音10:4"他走在前头，羊也跟着他。"

弟兄姐妹，流便和迦得的承诺是暂时的——打完仗就回家。基督走在前头是永远的——祂永远在我们前面开路。

**"你们的罪必追上你们"——在基督里罪不再追赶**
对不信的人，罪必追上——"你们的罪必追上你们"。但对在基督里的人——罗马书8:1"如今，那些在基督耶稣里的就不定罪了。"罪的追赶在十字架上被终结了——不是罪不存在了，而是基督代替我们被罪追上。以赛亚书53:5"哪知他为我们的过犯受害，为我们的罪孽压伤。"我们的罪"追上"了基督——但没有追上我们。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 32 prefigures Christ in two aspects.

**Going Before—Christ Is the Vanguard**
Reuben and Gad promised to "go before Israel"—Hebrews 12:2: "Looking to Jesus, the founder and perfecter of our faith, who for the joy that was set before him endured the cross, despising the shame." Christ goes before—He does not push us forward from behind; He leads us through death into life. John 10:4: "He goes before them, and the sheep follow him."

Brothers and sisters, Reuben and Gad's commitment was temporary—return home after fighting. Christ's going before is eternal—He forever opens the way before us.

**"Your Sin Will Find You Out"—In Christ Sin No Longer Pursues**
For the unbelieving, sin will find out—"your sin will find you out." But for those in Christ—Romans 8:1: "There is therefore now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus." Sin's pursuit ended at the cross—not that sin ceased existing, but Christ was "found out" by sin in our place. Isaiah 53:5: "He was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities." Our sin "found" Christ—but did not find us.
