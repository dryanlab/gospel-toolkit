## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十四章是审判——第十五章是应许。就在宣布那一代人要死在旷野之后，神立刻开口说："你们到了我所赐给你们居住的地……"以色列人刚刚被判死在旷野，神却已经在谈进入迦南后的礼仪——因为下一代人会进去。这是旷野中的盼望章。

**进迦南后的献祭补充（1-16节）**

> "你们到了我所赐给你们居住的地，若愿意献祭……"

弟兄姐妹，这些条例关于进迦南后如何调整献祭比例——燔祭、平安祭要配相应的素祭（调油细面）和奠祭（酒）。比例按牲口大小：羊羔（细面十分之一，油四分之一欣）→公绵羊（细面十分之二，油三分之一欣）→公牛（细面十分之三，油二分之一欣）。

重要的是这段话的背景——它在审判宣告之后立刻说"你们到了那地"。神没有说"也许下一代人会到那地"——祂肯定地说"你们到了"。神的应许不因这一代人的失败而取消——它只是推迟，由下一代来承接。

第13-16节特别强调外邦人（寄居者）与以色列人的平等——"会众有一个条例，就是你们和寄居的外人同归一例，作为你们世世代代永远的定例。"弟兄姐妹，旷野中有非以色列人同行（民9:14），他们进献祭时与以色列人完全相同的条例。恩典不是种族特权——而是对信靠同一位神的人开放的。

**初熟的举祭（17-21节）**

> "你们到了我所领你们进去的那地，吃那地的粮食，就要把举祭献给耶和华。你们要用初熟的麦子磨面，做饼当举祭奉献。"

弟兄姐妹，这是"初熟之物"原则——进入迦南、第一次收割、第一条面包，先献给神。不是在收获结束后的剩余——而是最先的那一份。神是第一、不是最后。承认神是一切供应的来源。

**误犯与故犯（22-31节）**

这一段做了一个重要的区分——误犯（无意间犯罪）与故犯（擅敢行事）。

误犯：全会众误犯→献公牛作燔祭+公山羊作赎罪祭，祭司为他们赎罪，他们就得蒙赦免。个人误犯→献一岁母山羊，祭司为他赎罪，他就蒙赦免。外邦寄居者同一条例。

故犯："但那擅敢行事的……他亵渎了耶和华，必从民中剪除。因他藐视耶和华的言语，违背耶和华的命令，那人总要剪除；他的罪孽要归到他身上。"

弟兄姐妹，"擅敢行事"（bᵉyad ramah，字面意思"高举的手"）指有意、公然的藐视——不是软弱和失败，而是刻意的反叛和蔑视。这样的罪没有赎罪祭——因为它拒绝了赎罪的框架本身。这不是说有些罪不可赦免——而是说真正的悔改必须伴随真正承认"我错了"。刻意拒绝认错的人，没有办法被赦免——不是因为神不愿意赦免，而是因为他拒绝了赦免的途径。

**安息日捡柴者被处死（32-36节）**

> "以色列人在旷野的时候，遇见一个人在安息日捡柴。"

弟兄姐妹，这个案例应用了上文的原则——这是"擅敢行事"的具体例子。安息日的诫命是明确的——不可劳作（出20:10）。这个人不是不知道——他是在安息日公然捡柴，有人亲眼看见。

他被带到摩西和亚伦面前，因为"还没有指明当怎样办他"——律法说不可在安息日工作，但没有具体规定违反的惩罚。耶和华的答案是：处死，全会众在营外用石头打死他。

弟兄姐妹，这听起来似乎过于严厉。但理解的关键在于：旷野中的以色列是一个刚刚从奴役中出来、正在被神塑造的社区。公然践踏最基本的诫命——安息日是十诫之一——如果不严肃处理，会导致整个律法体系的崩溃。这是关于整个群体维护圣洁的严肃时刻，不是个人的随意小事。

**衣边的繸子（37-41节）**

第十五章以一个温柔的安排结束——"你吩咐以色列人，叫他们世世代代在衣服边上做䍁子，又在底边的䍁子上钉一根蓝细带子。你们佩带这䍁子，好叫你们看见就记念遵行耶和华一切的命令，不随从自己的心意、眼目行邪淫。"

弟兄姐妹，繸子（tzitzit）是神给以色列人记忆神的命令的外在提醒。每次穿衣，每次走路，低头看见繸子——"记念遵行耶和华的命令"。神知道人的本性——我们容易忘记、容易偏离。所以祂给了一个具体的、日常的提醒：看见繸子，就想起我的命令。

"我是耶和华你们的神，曾把你们从埃及地领出来，要作你们的神。我是耶和华你们的神。"弟兄姐妹，第十五章以这句话结束——这是整个律法的基础：不是因为你很好所以守律法，而是因为"我是耶和华你们的神"，因为祂已经拯救了你，所以你活在盟约的框架里。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 14 was judgment—chapter 15 was promise. Immediately after declaring that generation would die in the wilderness, God opened with: "When you come into the land you are to inhabit, which I am giving you..." Israel had just been sentenced to death in the wilderness, yet God was already discussing Canaan's rituals—because the next generation would enter. This was the wilderness chapter of hope.

**Supplementary Sacrificial Ordinances for Canaan (vv. 1-16)**

> "When you come into the land you are to inhabit, which I am giving you, and you offer to the LORD offerings by fire..."

Brothers and sisters, these ordinances addressed adjusting offering proportions after entering Canaan—burnt offerings and peace offerings required accompanying grain offerings (fine flour mixed with oil) and drink offerings (wine). Proportions by animal size: lamb (tenth of flour, quarter-hin oil) → ram (two-tenths flour, third-hin oil) → bull (three-tenths flour, half-hin oil).

Importantly, this passage's context—coming immediately after the judgment declaration—said "when you come to the land." God did not say "perhaps the next generation will reach the land"—He said it definitively. God's promise was not cancelled by this generation's failure—merely postponed, to be received by the next generation.

Verses 13-16 especially emphasized Gentile equality—"For the assembly, there shall be one statute for you and for the stranger who sojourns with you, a statute forever throughout your generations." Brothers and sisters, non-Israelites traveled with Israel in the wilderness (Num 9:14); for offerings they followed identical ordinances. Grace was not racial privilege—but open to all who trusted the same God.

**First Fruits Wave Offering (vv. 17-21)**

> "When you come into the land to which I bring you and when you eat of the bread of the land, you shall present a contribution to the LORD. Of the first of your dough you shall present a loaf as a contribution."

Brothers and sisters, this was the "firstfruits" principle—entering Canaan, first harvest, first loaf of bread, offered to God first. Not leftovers after harvest—but the first portion. God is first, not last. Acknowledging God as the source of all provision.

**Unintentional vs. Intentional Sin (vv. 22-31)**

This passage made an important distinction—unintentional sin vs. presumptuous sin.

Unintentional: whole congregation errs → offer bull as burnt offering + male goat as sin offering, priest atones for them, they receive forgiveness. Individual errs → offer yearling female goat, priest atones, person forgiven. Same ordinance for sojourning foreigners.

Presumptuous: "But the person who does anything with a high hand...reviles the LORD, and that person shall be cut off...because he has despised the word of the LORD and broken his commandment, that person shall be utterly cut off; his iniquity shall be on him."

Brothers and sisters, "high-handed" (bᵉyad ramah, literally "raised hand") referred to intentional, open contempt—not weakness and failure, but deliberate rebellion and disdain. Such sin had no sin offering—because it rejected the atonement framework itself. This did not mean some sins are unforgivable—but that genuine repentance must accompany genuine acknowledgment of "I was wrong." One who deliberately refuses to acknowledge sin has no way to receive forgiveness—not because God is unwilling, but because they refused forgiveness's pathway.

**Sabbath Wood-Gatherer Executed (vv. 32-36)**

> "While the people of Israel were in the wilderness, they found a man gathering sticks on the Sabbath day."

Brothers and sisters, this case applied the above principle—a concrete example of "high-handed" sin. The Sabbath commandment was explicit—no work (Exod 20:10). This man was not ignorant—he openly gathered sticks on the Sabbath, witnessed by others.

He was brought before Moses and Aaron, "because it had not been made clear what should be done to him"—the law said no work on Sabbath but specified no concrete punishment for violation. The LORD's answer: death, entire congregation stones him outside the camp.

Brothers and sisters, this sounds extremely harsh. But the key is understanding: Israel in the wilderness was a community just emerging from slavery, being shaped by God. Openly trampling the most basic commandment—the Sabbath was one of the Ten—left unaddressed would cause the entire legal system's collapse. This was a solemn moment for the entire community maintaining holiness, not a casual individual matter.

**Tassels on Garments (vv. 37-41)**

Chapter 15 closed with a tender provision—"Speak to the people of Israel, and tell them to make tassels on the corners of their garments throughout their generations...And it shall be a tassel for you to look at and remember all the commandments of the LORD, to do them, not to follow after your own heart and your own eyes."

Brothers and sisters, the tassels (tzitzit) were God's external reminder for Israel to remember His commands. Every time you dressed, every time you walked, looking down and seeing tassels—"remember to do all the LORD's commandments." God knew human nature—we easily forget, easily drift. So He provided a concrete, daily reminder: see the tassels, remember my commands.

"I am the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt to be your God: I am the LORD your God." Brothers and sisters, chapter 15 ended with this sentence—this was the entire law's foundation: not "obey the law because you're good," but "because I am the LORD your God," because He already redeemed you, therefore you live within the covenant framework.

## 历史背景（中文）

繸子（tzitzit）至今仍是犹太男性宗教服装的重要部分——塔利特（祈祷披巾）和内穿的tzitzit背心都有四角的繸子，每个角上缠绕着特定数目的线圈。蓝色带子（tekhelet）的制作工艺在中世纪失传，直到现代科学家发现了可能提取该蓝色的海螺（murex trunculus）——今天有些正统犹太人在繸子上恢复了蓝线的使用。新约马太福音9:20记载血漏妇人摸了耶稣的"衣裳繸子"——就是这里规定的tzitzit。马太福音23:5记载耶稣批评法利赛人"放宽他们的经文，加大他们的繸子"——说明繸子在第一世纪已经变成了炫耀虔诚的标志。"初熟的面饼"（Challah）献祭后来成为安息日的哈拉面包（Challah bread）传统的来源——犹太家庭至今在安息日桌上摆上两个哈拉面包，纪念旷野的双份吗哪。

## Historical Context (English)

Tassels (tzitzit) remain an important part of Jewish male religious dress—both the tallit (prayer shawl) and the inner tzitzit garment have four-cornered tassels with specific numbers of winding. The blue thread (tekhelet) manufacturing was lost in the Middle Ages, until modern scientists discovered the possible source snail (murex trunculus)—today some Orthodox Jews have resumed using blue threads in their tassels. Matthew 9:20 in the New Testament records the bleeding woman touching "the fringe of his garment"—the tzitzit prescribed here. Matthew 23:5 records Jesus criticizing Pharisees for "making their tassels long"—showing tassels had become piety-display symbols by the first century. The "first dough" offering (Challah) later became the source of the Sabbath challah bread tradition—Jewish families to this day place two challah loaves on the Sabbath table, commemorating the wilderness's double manna.

## Structure (English)

**Canaan Sacrificial Proportions (vv. 1-16)**—Burn offering/peace offering + grain/drink offering ratios, sojourning foreigners same ordinance.

**First-Fruits Wave Offering (vv. 17-21)**—Upon entering land, first grain harvest requires wave offering first.

**Forgiveness for Unintentional Sin (vv. 22-29)**—Congregation errs → bull + male goat; individual errs → female goat; foreigners same; priest atones, forgiven.

**Intentional Sin Cut Off (vv. 30-31)**—High-handed person despises LORD → must be cut off, bears own iniquity.

**Sabbath Wood-Gatherer Executed (vv. 32-36)**—Concrete case: Sabbath wood-gathering in wilderness → stoned by entire congregation.

**Tassels on Garment Edges (vv. 37-41)**—Four-corner tassels with blue thread → see them remember commandments → not following heart and eyes → I am the LORD your God.

## 神学要点（中文）

**误犯可赦，故犯剪除——罪的严重性区分**
神区分无意犯罪和刻意犯罪——改革宗传统教导罪的不同层次。韦敏斯德大要理问答第151问论更严重的罪：就其本身而言，更加严重的有……明知故犯……违背……良心。故犯是拒绝了悔改本身——不是无力改变，而是不愿改变。希伯来书10:26"我们得知真道之后，若故意犯罪，赎罪的祭就再没有了。"这不是失去救恩——而是拒绝了赦免的路。

**繸子——具身化的记忆系统**
神给以色列可见的提醒——改革宗传统教导圣礼和外在的标记的价值。韦敏斯德信条第27章"圣礼是恩典之约的圣洁标记和印证……坚固和增加我们对祂的信心。"神知道人的健忘——繸子是"不要忘记神的命令"的具体化。圣洗礼和主餐是新约的"繸子"——每次领受，提醒我们与基督的盟约。

**"我是耶和华你们的神"——恩典在律法之先**
律法的基础是"我把你们从埃及领出来"——改革宗传统教导十诫是恩典框架下的盟约律法，不是赚取救恩的途径。韦敏斯德信条第19章论神的律法。以色列守律法不是为了被爱——而是因为已经被爱、已经被救赎，在感恩中活出盟约关系。

## Theology (English)

**Unintentional Sin Forgivable, Intentional Sin Cut Off—Distinguishing Sin's Severity**
God distinguishes unintentional and deliberate sin—the Reformed tradition teaches sin's different levels. Westminster Larger Catechism Q151 on more grievous sins: "more heinous in themselves...sins...against better knowledge...against conscience." Presumptuous sin rejected repentance itself—not inability to change but unwillingness. Hebrews 10:26: "If we go on sinning deliberately after receiving the knowledge of the truth, there no longer remains a sacrifice for sins." This is not losing salvation—but refusing forgiveness's pathway.

**Tassels—An Embodied Memory System**
God provided Israel visible reminders—the Reformed tradition teaches the value of sacraments and external signs. Westminster Confession chapter 27: Sacraments are holy signs and seals of the covenant of grace...to strengthen and increase their faith in Him. God knew human forgetfulness—tassels embodied "do not forget God's commands." Baptism and the Lord's Supper are the New Testament's "tassels"—each reception reminding us of the covenant with Christ.

**"I Am the LORD Your God"—Grace Precedes Law**
The law's foundation was "I brought you out of Egypt"—the Reformed tradition teaches the Ten Commandments are covenant law within a grace framework, not a means to earn salvation. Westminster Confession chapter 19 on the law of God. Israel kept the law not to be loved—but because already loved, already redeemed, living out covenant relationship in gratitude.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第十五章有三个指向基督的画面。

**第一，"你们到了那地"——在审判中的盼望**
第十四章宣判那代人死在旷野——第十五章紧接着说进迦南后怎么做。这预表了基督：即使在罪的审判中，神已经在为下一代（新一代的信徒，旧约的"余民"）预备道路。以弗所书2:6-7"叫我们在基督耶稣里，一同坐在天上，要将他极丰富的恩典……显明出来。"审判的话语不是最后的话语——恩典的应许才是。

**第二，繸子上的蓝线——基督是我们的记念**
马太福音9:20血漏妇人摸了耶稣衣服的"繸子"就得了医治。弟兄姐妹，繸子原是"记念神的命令"——但当耶稣来了，摸祂的繸子直接带来了医治和救恩。律法的繸子说"记住，遵行"——基督说"到我这里来，你们就得安息"（太11:28）。我们不再靠繸子来记住律法——我们靠圣灵来记住基督。约翰福音14:26"圣灵……要将我所说的一切话都提醒你们。"

**第三，"擅敢行事"的无路可逃——唯独基督有赎罪祭**
故犯者没有赎罪祭——但基督是所有罪的赎罪祭，包括故犯。希伯来书9:14"何况基督藉着永远的灵，将自己无瑕无疵献给神，他的血岂不更能洗净你们的心，除去你们的死行，使你们事奉那永生神么？"弟兄姐妹，故犯的人回到神面前、真诚认罪——基督的血足够赦免一切。不是说故犯可以随意犯——而是说即使最严重的罪，只要真正悔改，基督的血都够用。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 15 contains three images pointing to Christ.

**First, "When You Come to the Land"—Hope Within Judgment**
Chapter 14 sentenced that generation to die in the wilderness—chapter 15 immediately addressed what to do after entering Canaan. This prefigured Christ: even within sin's judgment, God was already preparing a path for the next generation (the new generation of believers, the Old Testament "remnant"). Ephesians 2:6-7: "Raised us up with him and seated us with him in the heavenly places in Christ Jesus, so that in the coming ages he might show the immeasurable riches of his grace." The words of judgment were not the final word—the promise of grace was.

**Second, Blue Thread Tassels—Christ Is Our Remembrance**
Matthew 9:20: the bleeding woman touched Jesus' "fringe" (tassels) and was healed. Brothers and sisters, tassels originally said "remember God's commands"—but when Jesus came, touching His tassels directly brought healing and salvation. The law's tassels said "remember, obey"—Christ said "Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest." (Matt 11:28). We no longer rely on tassels to remember the law—we rely on the Spirit to remember Christ. John 14:26: "The Holy Spirit...will bring to your remembrance all that I have said to you."

**Third, "High-Handed" Sin Has No Escape—Only Christ Has the Atonement**
The presumptuous sinner had no sin offering—but Christ is the atonement for all sin, including deliberate sin. Hebrews 9:14: "How much more will the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without blemish to God, purify our conscience from dead works to serve the living God?" Brothers and sisters, the deliberately sinning person returning to God, genuinely confessing—Christ's blood is sufficient to forgive everything. Not that deliberate sin can be casually committed—but even the most serious sin, if truly repented, Christ's blood is sufficient.
