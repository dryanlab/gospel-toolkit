## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十一章是民数记的转折点——从顺服变成了抱怨。出发才三天（10:33），以色列就开始发怨言了。第一到第十章的秩序和美好，在第十一章碎了。

**他备拉的火（1-3节）**

> "众百姓发怨言，他们的恶语达到耶和华的耳中。耶和华听见了就怒气发作，使火在他们中间焚烧，直烧到营的边界。"

弟兄姐妹，经文没有说他们抱怨什么——只说"发怨言"。可能是路途辛苦、天气炎热、水源不够——但具体原因不重要。重要的是：他们刚刚出发三天就抱怨了。三天前他们还在西奈山下守逾越节、听银号吹响、看云柱引路——现在就在抱怨。

神的回应是火——"耶和华的火烧在他们中间"。不是比喻的火——是真实的火，烧在营的边界。百姓恐惧，向摩西哀求，摩西代祷，火才熄了。那地方因此叫"他备拉"——燃烧的地方。

**贪欲的哭号（4-9节）**

然后是更严重的抱怨——"闲杂人大起贪欲的心；以色列人又哭号说：谁给我们肉吃呢？"

弟兄姐妹，"闲杂人"是跟着以色列出埃及的非以色列人群体（出12:38），他们首先带头抱怨——然后以色列人跟着一起哭号。传染性的不满——少数人的贪欲迅速蔓延到整个群体。

他们抱怨什么？"我们记得在埃及的时候不花钱就吃鱼，也记得有黄瓜、西瓜、韭菜、葱、蒜。现在我们的心血枯竭了，除这吗哪以外，在我们眼前并没有别的东西。"

弟兄姐妹，他们记得埃及的好处——鱼、黄瓜、西瓜——却忘了埃及的苦处——鞭子、砖窑、杀男婴。罪的记忆总是选择性的：它美化过去的罪中之乐，忽略过去的罪中之苦。他们嫌弃吗哪——那是神每天早上从天上降下的食物！他们不是没有吃的——他们是不满意神给的食物。

**摩西的崩溃（10-15节）**

弟兄姐妹，然后是摩西——以色列最伟大的领袖——的崩溃。

> "你为何苦待仆人？我为何不在你眼前蒙恩，竟把这管理百姓的重任加在我身上呢？这百姓岂是我怀的胎，岂是我生下来的呢？"

摩西对神吼叫了——不是对百姓，是对神。"管理这百姓的责任太重了，我独自担当不起。你这样待我……求你立时将我杀了！"弟兄姐妹，摩西宁可死也不愿再领导这群抱怨的人。这不是假戏——这是真正的绝望。先知也是人，领袖也有承受的极限。

**七十长老分担（16-17, 24-30节）**

神的回应不是责备摩西——而是给他帮手。"你从以色列的长老中招聚七十个人……我要把降于你身上的灵分赐他们，他们就和你同当这管百姓的重任。"

弟兄姐妹，神没有说"你太软弱了，振作起来"——祂说"你不用一个人扛"。神把摩西身上的灵分赐七十个长老——他们一起承担领导的重任。

然后发生了一个有趣的插曲——伊利达和米达没有去帐幕，却在营里也受感说话。约书亚着急了："请我主摩西禁止他们！"但摩西说了一句伟大的话——"你为我的缘故嫉妒人吗？惟愿耶和华的百姓都受感说话！愿耶和华把他的灵降在他们身上！"

弟兄姐妹，摩西不嫉妒——他巴不得所有人都有圣灵。这是真正伟大的领袖——不是垄断权力而是渴望分享。这句话后来在五旬节那天应验了——圣灵降在所有信徒身上（徒2章）。

**鹌鹑与瘟疫（18-20, 31-35节）**

神给了他们想要的肉——但方式令人恐惧。"有风从耶和华那里刮起，把鹌鹑由海面刮来……离地面约有二肘。"

弟兄姐妹，两肘大约一米——鹌鹑厚达一米铺满营的四周，约一天路程的范围！百姓疯狂地收集——"至少的也取了十贺梅珥"（约2200升！）。然后——"肉在他们牙齿之间尚未嚼烂，耶和华的怒气就向他们发作，用最重的灾殃击杀了他们。"

弟兄姐妹，他们得到了想要的——然后死了。诗篇106:15"他将他们所求的赐给他们，却使他们的心灵软弱。"有时候，神给你想要的东西——恰恰是最严厉的审判。

那地方叫"基博罗哈他瓦"——贪欲之人的坟墓。他们的坟墓就是他们的贪欲。弟兄姐妹，不是肉杀了他们——是贪欲杀了他们。他们不是没有食物——他们是不满足于神所赐的。不满足不只是态度问题——它是对神供应的否认，是对出埃及的叛逆。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 11 is Numbers' turning point—from obedience to complaining. Merely three days after departure (10:33), Israel began grumbling. Chapters 1-10's order and beauty shattered in chapter 11.

**Fire at Taberah (vv. 1-3)**

> "And the people complained in the hearing of the LORD about their misfortunes, and when the LORD heard it, his anger was kindled, and the fire of the LORD burned among them and consumed some outlying parts of the camp."

Brothers and sisters, the text did not specify what they complained about—just "complained." Perhaps the journey's hardship, the heat, insufficient water—but the specific cause was immaterial. What mattered: they complained three days after departing. Three days earlier they were at Sinai keeping Passover, hearing silver trumpets, watching the cloud pillar—now complaining.

God's response was fire—"the fire of the LORD burned among them." Not metaphorical—real fire, burning at the camp's edge. The people were terrified, cried to Moses, Moses interceded, and the fire subsided. That place was named "Taberah"—the place of burning.

**The Craving's Cry (vv. 4-9)**

Then came more serious complaint—"the rabble among them had a strong craving. And the people of Israel also wept again and said, 'Oh that we had meat to eat!'"

Brothers and sisters, the "rabble" were non-Israelites who left Egypt with Israel (Exod 12:38), they led the complaint—then Israelites joined the weeping. Contagious discontent—a few people's craving rapidly spreading to the entire community.

What did they complain about? "We remember the fish we ate in Egypt that cost nothing, the cucumbers, the melons, the leeks, the onions, and the garlic. But now our strength is dried up, and there is nothing at all but this manna to look at."

Brothers and sisters, they remembered Egypt's benefits—fish, cucumbers, melons—but forgot Egypt's misery—whips, brick kilns, infant killing. Sin's memory is always selective: it glorifies past sinful pleasures while ignoring past sinful suffering. They despised manna—food God sent from heaven every morning! They were not without food—they were unsatisfied with God's provision.

**Moses' Breakdown (vv. 10-15)**

Brothers and sisters, then came Moses—Israel's greatest leader—breaking down.

> "Why have you dealt ill with your servant? And why have I not found favor in your sight, that you lay the burden of all this people on me? Did I conceive all this people? Did I give them birth?"

Moses shouted at God—not the people, at God. "I am not able to carry all this people alone; the burden is too heavy for me. If you will treat me like this, kill me at once!" Brothers and sisters, Moses would rather die than continue leading these complaining people. This was no performance—this was genuine despair. Prophets are human too; leaders have limits.

**Seventy Elders Share the Burden (vv. 16-17, 24-30)**

God's response was not rebuking Moses—but giving him help. "Gather for me seventy men of the elders of Israel...I will take some of the Spirit that is on you and put it on them, and they shall bear the burden of the people with you."

Brothers and sisters, God did not say "you're too weak, toughen up"—He said "you don't have to bear it alone." God distributed Moses' Spirit to seventy elders—they would share leadership's burden together.

Then an interesting episode—Eldad and Medad had not gone to the tabernacle, yet prophesied in camp. Joshua was alarmed: "My lord Moses, stop them!" But Moses spoke a magnificent word—"Are you jealous for my sake? Would that all the LORD's people were prophets, that the LORD would put his Spirit on them!"

Brothers and sisters, Moses was not jealous—he wished everyone had the Spirit. This was truly great leadership—not monopolizing power but longing to share it. This word was fulfilled at Pentecost—the Spirit fell on all believers (Acts 2).

**Quail and Plague (vv. 18-20, 31-35)**

God gave them the meat they wanted—but terrifyingly. "A wind from the LORD...brought quail from the sea...about two cubits above the ground."

Brothers and sisters, two cubits was about one meter—quail piled a meter deep surrounding the camp, a day's journey in every direction! The people collected frantically—"whoever gathered least had ten homers" (about 2,200 liters!). Then—"While the meat was yet between their teeth, before it was consumed, the anger of the LORD was kindled against the people, and the LORD struck down the people with a very great plague."

Brothers and sisters, they got what they wanted—then died. Psalm 106:15: "He gave them what they asked, but sent a wasting disease among them." Sometimes God giving you what you want is precisely the most severe judgment.

The place was named "Kibroth-hattaavah"—graves of craving. Their graves were their craving. Brothers and sisters, meat did not kill them—craving killed them. They were not without food—they were unsatisfied with what God gave. Dissatisfaction was not just an attitude problem—it was denial of God's provision, rebellion against the exodus.

## 历史背景（中文）

鹌鹑迁徙是地中海地区已知的自然现象——每年春秋两季，大批欧洲鹌鹑从非洲飞越地中海到欧洲（或反向），在西奈半岛沿海地带大量降落——因为长途飞行后体力耗尽。"离地面约有二肘"可能指鹌鹑的飞行高度而不是堆积厚度——疲惫的鹌鹑低飞，容易被人徒手捕捉。十贺梅珥约2200升——按一只鹌鹑约200克计算，这是惊人的数量。"灾殃"的性质不确定——有学者认为是食物中毒（大量鸟类可能携带病原体），但经文归因于"耶和华的怒气"。七十长老制度成为以色列后来公会（Sanhedrin，71人，含大祭司）的先例。摩西"求你杀了我"的祷告与以利亚在列王纪上19:4的祷告相似——"耶和华啊，罢了！求你取我的性命"——两位最伟大的先知都经历过极深的绝望。

## Historical Context (English)

Quail migration is a known natural phenomenon in the Mediterranean—every spring and autumn, European quail fly between Africa and Europe across the Mediterranean, landing in great numbers along the Sinai Peninsula's coast—exhausted from long flights. "About two cubits above the ground" may refer to flight height rather than pile depth—exhausted quail flew low, easily caught by hand. Ten homers equaling about 2,200 liters—at roughly 200 grams per quail, this was a staggering quantity. The "plague's" nature is uncertain—some scholars suggest food poisoning (large bird numbers may carry pathogens), but the text attributes it to "the LORD's anger." The seventy-elder system became the precedent for Israel's later Sanhedrin (71 members including the high priest). Moses' "kill me" prayer paralleled Elijah's in 1 Kings 19:4—"It is enough; now, O LORD, take away my life"—both of the greatest prophets experienced profound despair.

## Structure (English)

**Fire at Taberah (vv. 1-3)**—People complain → God's fire burns camp's edge → Moses intercedes → fire subsides.

**Craving for Meat (vv. 4-9)**—Rabble leads craving → Israel weeps for meat → remembers Egypt's fish → despises manna.

**Moses' Breakdown (vv. 10-15)**—Overwhelmed, cries to God → "Did I conceive this people?" → "Kill me at once."

**Seventy Elders Receive Spirit (vv. 16-17, 24-30)**—God distributes Moses' Spirit to 70 elders → Eldad and Medad prophesy in camp → Joshua wants to stop them → Moses: "Would that all were prophets!"

**Quail and Plague (vv. 18-23, 31-35)**—God sends quail two cubits high → people collect greedily → plague strikes before meat chewed → Kibroth-hattaavah = graves of craving.

## 神学要点（中文）

**不满足是对神供应的叛逆**
嫌弃吗哪要肉——改革宗传统教导知足是信心的果实。韦敏斯德大要理问答第147-148问论第十诫禁止贪婪。提摩太前书6:6"敬虔加上知足的心便是大利了。"以色列不是没有食物——他们是不满意神给的。不满足不只是不礼貌——它是不信的表现。

**领袖的软弱——恩典中的真实**
摩西的崩溃——改革宗传统承认信徒和领袖都会软弱。韦敏斯德信条第18章论确据可以被"多种方式动摇"。最伟大的先知也会绝望——但神不丢弃他们。神没有责备摩西——而是给他帮助。

**分灵——圣灵的丰富**
神把摩西的灵分给七十人——改革宗传统教导圣灵的丰富和主权。圣灵不是有限的资源——分给七十人不意味着摩西得的少了。约珥书2:28"我要将我的灵浇灌凡有血气的。"摩西说"惟愿都受感说话"——这是旧约对五旬节最明确的期盼。

## Theology (English)

**Dissatisfaction Is Rebellion Against God's Provision**
Despising manna wanting meat—the Reformed tradition teaches contentment as faith's fruit. Westminster Larger Catechism Q147-148 on the tenth commandment forbidding covetousness. 1 Timothy 6:6: "Godliness with contentment is great gain." Israel was not without food—they were unsatisfied with God's provision. Dissatisfaction is not merely rude—it is an expression of unbelief.

**Leader's Weakness—Authenticity in Grace**
Moses' breakdown—the Reformed tradition acknowledges believers and leaders can be weak. Westminster Confession chapter 18 on assurance being: shaken in various ways. The greatest prophet also despaired—but God did not abandon him. God did not rebuke Moses—He gave him help.

**Shared Spirit—The Spirit's Abundance**
God distributed Moses' Spirit to seventy—the Reformed tradition teaches the Spirit's abundance and sovereignty. The Spirit is not a limited resource—distributing to seventy did not diminish Moses'. Joel 2:28: "I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh." Moses said "Would that all were prophets"—the Old Testament's clearest anticipation of Pentecost.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第十一章在基督里有多层回响。

**吗哪与生命的粮**
约翰福音6:31-35以色列人对耶稣说"我们的祖宗在旷野吃过吗哪"——耶稣回答"我就是生命的粮。"弟兄姐妹，旷野中以色列厌弃了吗哪——同样，犹太人在约翰福音6章也拒绝了基督。拒绝神的供应——无论是吗哪还是基督——结果都是死亡。"到我这里来的，必定不饿；信我的，永远不渴。"

**摩西的崩溃——基督的代祷**
摩西说"管理这百姓的责任太重了"——基督却甘愿背负全人类的罪。以赛亚书53:4"他诚然担当我们的忧患，背负我们的痛苦。"摩西几乎被压垮——基督确实被压碎了，但祂没有说"杀了我"——祂说"成了"（约19:30）。

**分灵给七十人——五旬节**
摩西说"惟愿耶和华的百姓都受感说话！愿耶和华把他的灵降在他们身上！"——使徒行传2:17-18引用约珥书说"在末后的日子，我要将我的灵浇灌凡有血气的。你们的儿女要说预言。"弟兄姐妹，摩西的愿望在五旬节应验了——圣灵不再只降在七十个长老身上，而是降在所有信基督的人身上。

**贪欲的坟墓——罗马书8:6的警告**
"体贴肉体的，就是死。"基博罗哈他瓦——贪欲之人的坟墓——是罗马书8:6最生动的旧约图解。以色列人体贴肉体的渴望（要肉吃）——结果是死亡。"体贴圣灵的，乃是生命平安。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 11 resonates with Christ on multiple levels.

**Manna and the Bread of Life**
John 6:31-35: Israel told Jesus "Our fathers ate manna in the wilderness"—Jesus answered, "I am the bread of life." Brothers and sisters, wilderness Israel despised manna—likewise, Jews in John 6 rejected Christ. Rejecting God's provision—whether manna or Christ—results in death. "Whoever comes to me shall not hunger, and whoever believes in me shall never thirst."

**Moses' Breakdown—Christ's Intercession**
Moses said "the burden is too heavy for me"—Christ willingly bore all humanity's sin. Isaiah 53:4: "Surely he has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows." Moses nearly broke—Christ was indeed crushed, yet He did not say "kill me"—He said "When Jesus therefore had received the vinegar, he said, It is finished: and he bowed his head, and gave up the ghost." (John 19:30).

**Spirit Shared with Seventy—Pentecost**
Moses said "Would that all the LORD's people were prophets, that the LORD would put his Spirit on them!"—Acts 2:17-18 quoted Joel: "In the last days I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh. Your sons and daughters shall prophesy." Brothers and sisters, Moses' wish was fulfilled at Pentecost—the Spirit no longer fell on just seventy elders, but on all who believe in Christ.

**Graves of Craving—Romans 8:6's Warning**
"To set the mind on the flesh is death." Kibroth-hattaavah—graves of craving—is Romans 8:6's most vivid Old Testament illustration. Israel indulged flesh's desire (wanting meat)—the result was death. "To set the mind on the Spirit is life and peace."
