## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第九章讲两件意义深远的事：第二次逾越节和云柱火柱的引导。这两件事的主题是一个——神的信实：祂信守过去的拯救（逾越节），也在未来的路上引导（云柱火柱）。

**在旷野守逾越节（1-14节）**

> "以色列人出埃及地以后，第二年正月……守逾越节。"

弟兄姐妹，这是出埃及后的第二个逾越节——距离那一夜、那个改变历史的夜晚，刚好一年。以色列人在西奈旷野，帐幕已经立好，利未人已经就位——现在他们在旷野中重新守逾越节。

为什么在旷野中还要守逾越节？因为逾越节不只是纪念过去——它是盟约的更新。每一次守逾越节，以色列都在对神说："我们记得那一夜你的拯救。"神也在对以色列说："我记得我的约——你们是我从埃及领出来的百姓。"

然后出现了一个实际的问题——"有几个人因死尸而不洁净，不能在那日守逾越节。"弟兄姐妹，这几个人不是故意不守节——他们可能是刚刚为亲人办了丧事，因此在礼仪上不洁净。他们来找摩西说："我们虽因死尸而不洁净，为何被阻止？"这不是叛逆——这是渴望。他们想要守节，不想被排除在外。

摩西没有自己作主——他说"你们暂且等候，我可以去听耶和华指着你们是怎样吩咐的。"弟兄姐妹，这是摩西最令人尊敬的品质之一——面对没有先例的问题时，他不凭自己的智慧判断，而是去问神。

神的答案出人意料地恩慈——不是"不洁净就不能守"，而是"二月十四日补守"。因不洁净或在远方行路而无法在正月守节的人，可以在一个月后补守——一切规矩与正月相同。弟兄姐妹，神的律法不是死板的——在公义的框架中有恩典的灵活。不洁净不是永久的排斥——是暂时的，有恢复的路。

但注意第13节——"那洁净而不行路的人若推辞不守逾越节，那人要从民中剪除。"弟兄姐妹，如果你没有正当理由却不守节——那是故意的轻慢，要被"从民中剪除"。不洁净的人有补守的机会——但故意拒绝的人没有借口。恩典延伸给渴望的人——审判临到轻慢的人。

第14节更令人惊讶——"若有外人寄居在你们中间，愿意向耶和华守逾越节……不管是寄居的是本地人，同归一例。"弟兄姐妹，外邦人可以守逾越节！这是旧约中最明确的"恩典对外邦人开放"的经文之一。以色列的节期不是种族排他的——任何愿意归向耶和华的人都可以参与。

**云柱火柱的引导（15-23节）**

第九章下半段转到云柱——"立起帐幕的那日，有云彩遮盖帐幕……从晚上到早晨，云彩在其上，形状如火。"

弟兄姐妹，白天是云柱、晚上是火柱——从出埃及记13:21开始，神就用这种可见的方式引导以色列。现在云柱不只是在行军路上——它停在帐幕上方，与帐幕同在。

"云彩几时从帐幕收上去，以色列人就几时起行；云彩在那里停住，以色列人就在那里安营。"弟兄姐妹，以色列的行止完全取决于云柱——不是将军的军事判断、不是地形的分析、不是投票的决定。是神说走就走、神说停就停。

第21-22节描述了这种等候的不确定性——"有时从晚上到早晨……有时昼夜……云彩停留在帐幕上，无论是两天、是一月、是一年。"弟兄姐妹，你不知道什么时候走、不知道要停多久。可能刚搭好帐棚，第二天早上云就走了——你得赶紧拆。可能在同一个地方住了一年——你以为永远不走了，突然云起来了。

弟兄姐妹，这就是信心的生活——你不掌控进度，神掌控。你不决定何时行动、何时等待——神决定。你的唯一责任是：观察云柱，云走你走，云停你停。

"他们遵耶和华的吩咐安营，也遵耶和华的吩咐起行。"弟兄姐妹，第九章以顺服结束——以色列在这里还是顺服的，跟着云走跟着云停。但旷野的路还长——从第十一章开始，他们就开始抱怨："我们不想跟着走了。"

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 9 addresses two profoundly significant events: the second Passover and the cloud-and-fire guidance. Both share one theme—God's faithfulness: He honored past deliverance (Passover) and guided the future journey (cloud and fire pillar).

**Passover in the Wilderness (vv. 1-14)**

> "In the second year after they had come out of the land of Egypt, in the first month...they kept the Passover."

Brothers and sisters, this was the second Passover after the exodus—exactly one year since that night, that history-changing night. Israel was in the Sinai wilderness, tabernacle erected, Levites in position—now they observed Passover again in the wilderness.

Why keep Passover in the wilderness? Because Passover was not merely past remembrance—it was covenant renewal. Each Passover, Israel said to God: "We remember that night of your deliverance." God also said to Israel: "I remember my covenant—you are my people whom I brought from Egypt."

Then a practical problem arose—"certain men who were unclean through touching a dead body could not keep the Passover on that day." Brothers and sisters, these men did not deliberately refuse—they may have just attended a family member's funeral, rendering them ritually unclean. They came to Moses: "Why are we kept from bringing the LORD's offering?" This was not rebellion—this was longing. They wanted to observe the feast, unwilling to be excluded.

Moses did not decide himself—"Wait, that I may hear what the LORD will command concerning you." Brothers and sisters, this was one of Moses' most admirable qualities—facing unprecedented questions, he did not rely on his own wisdom but asked God.

God's answer was unexpectedly gracious—not "unclean means excluded," but "observe it on the fourteenth day of the second month." Those unable to keep the feast in the first month due to uncleanness or travel could keep it one month later—all rules identical. Brothers and sisters, God's law was not rigid—within justice's framework there was grace's flexibility. Uncleanness was not permanent exclusion—it was temporary, with a path of restoration.

But note verse 13—"if anyone who is clean and is not on a journey fails to keep the Passover, that person shall be cut off." Brothers and sisters, no legitimate reason yet refusing the feast—deliberate contempt, resulting in being "cut off." The unclean had a second chance—but the deliberately refusing had no excuse. Grace extended to the longing—judgment fell on the contemptuous.

Verse 14 was even more remarkable—"if a stranger who sojourns among you would keep the Passover to the LORD...there shall be one statute for the sojourner and for the native." Brothers and sisters, foreigners could keep Passover! This was one of the Old Testament's clearest declarations of "grace open to Gentiles." Israel's feast was not ethnically exclusive—anyone willing to turn to the LORD could participate.

**Cloud and Fire Pillar Guidance (vv. 15-23)**

Chapter 9's second half turned to the cloud—"On the day that the tabernacle was set up, the cloud covered the tabernacle...from evening to morning it was over the tabernacle, having the appearance of fire."

Brothers and sisters, cloud by day, fire by night—since Exodus 13:21, God visibly guided Israel this way. Now the cloud was not merely over the marching route—it rested above the tabernacle, present with the tabernacle.

"Whenever the cloud lifted from over the tent, the people of Israel would set out, and in the place where the cloud settled down, there the people of Israel would camp." Brothers and sisters, Israel's movements depended entirely on the cloud—not generals' military judgment, not terrain analysis, not democratic vote. When God said go, they went; when God said stop, they stopped.

Verses 21-22 described this waiting's uncertainty—"sometimes from evening to morning...sometimes day and night...whether it was two days, or a month, or a longer time." Brothers and sisters, you did not know when to march, did not know how long to stay. Perhaps you had just pitched your tent and the next morning the cloud moved—you had to pack immediately. Perhaps you camped in one place for a year—thinking you would stay forever, and suddenly the cloud rose.

Brothers and sisters, this was the life of faith—you did not control the schedule; God did. You did not decide when to act, when to wait—God decided. Your only responsibility was: watch the cloud. Cloud moves, you move. Cloud stops, you stop.

"At the command of the LORD they camped, and at the command of the LORD they set out." Brothers and sisters, chapter 9 ended with obedience—Israel was still obedient here, following the cloud. But the wilderness road stretched long—from chapter 11, they began complaining: "We don't want to follow anymore."

## 历史背景（中文）

"第二次逾越节"（Pesach Sheni）至今仍是犹太传统中的一个次要节日（以珥月14日，即犹太历二月14日），被视为"第二次机会"的象征——错过了第一次，还可以补。这个传统直接来自民数记9:9-13。犹太释经传统认为那几个因死尸不洁净的人可能是抬约瑟棺木的人（出13:19），因此因圣洁的义务而不洁净——更值得给补守的恩典。云柱火柱的神学意义在以西结书中继续发展——以西结书10:18-19记载耶和华的荣光（以云和火的形式）从圣殿东门离开——这被视为神离弃耶路撒冷的标志。后来以西结书43:1-5记载荣光从东门返回——末世的恢复。

## Historical Context (English)

The "Second Passover" (Pesach Sheni) remains a minor holiday in Jewish tradition (Iyyar 14, second month 14th), seen as a symbol of "second chances"—miss the first, still observe later. This tradition comes directly from Numbers 9:9-13. Jewish exegetical tradition suggests the unclean men may have been those carrying Joseph's coffin (Exod 13:19), thus unclean due to a holy obligation—all the more deserving of the make-up opportunity's grace. The cloud-and-fire pillar's theological significance continued developing in Ezekiel—Ezekiel 10:18-19 records the LORD's glory (in cloud and fire form) departing the temple's east gate—seen as God forsaking Jerusalem. Later Ezekiel 43:1-5 records glory returning through the east gate—eschatological restoration.

## 经文结构（中文）

**旷野中守逾越节（1-5节）**——第二年正月十四日黄昏、按律例典章守节。

**不洁净者的困境与补守（6-12节）**——因死尸不洁→求问摩西→神赐恩：二月十四日补守、一切规矩相同。

**警告与恩典（13-14节）**——洁净而拒守者被剪除；外邦寄居者可同守。

**云柱火柱的引导（15-23节）**——白天云柱夜间火柱在帐幕上→云起则行云停则营→无论两天一月一年→凭神的吩咐起行安营。

## Structure (English)

**Passover in the Wilderness (vv. 1-5)**—Second year first month fourteenth at twilight, observed according to statutes.

**The Unclean's Dilemma and Make-Up Observance (vv. 6-12)**—Unclean from corpse → inquire of Moses → God's grace: observe second month fourteenth, all rules identical.

**Warning and Grace (vv. 13-14)**—Clean person who refuses is cut off; foreign sojourners may observe.

**Cloud and Fire Pillar Guidance (vv. 15-23)**—Cloud by day fire by night over tabernacle → cloud lifts they march, cloud settles they camp → whether two days, a month, or a year → march and camp at God's command.

## 神学要点（中文）

**逾越节——盟约的更新与记忆**
旷野中守逾越节——改革宗传统教导圣礼是盟约的标记和印证。韦敏斯德信条第27章论圣礼：圣礼是恩典之约的圣洁标记和印证。逾越节在旷野中重新守——正如主餐在教会中反复领受——不是一次性的，而是反复更新的盟约确认。

**第二次机会——恩典中的灵活**
不洁净者可以补守——改革宗传统教导神的恩典考虑人的处境。韦敏斯德信条第5章论天意：神……按照其无谬的预知和自由不变的旨意，安排一切。但神的安排中有恩典的空间——不洁净不是终极排斥。

**云柱——神的引导与人的等候**
云行则行云停则停——改革宗传统强调顺服神的带领。韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问"以祂为乐"。以色列不掌控进度——神掌控。诗篇27:14"要等候耶和华！当壮胆，坚固你的心！我再说，要等候耶和华！"等候不是被动——是积极的信靠。

## Theology (English)

**Passover—Covenant Renewal and Remembrance**
Passover in the wilderness—the Reformed tradition teaches sacraments are signs and seals of the covenant. Westminster Confession chapter 27 on sacraments: Sacraments are holy signs and seals of the covenant of grace. Passover re-observed in the wilderness—as the Lord's Supper repeatedly received in the church—not one-time but recurring covenant confirmation.

**Second Chance—Flexibility Within Grace**
The unclean could observe later—the Reformed tradition teaches God's grace considers human circumstances. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence: God...according to His infallible foreknowledge and free and immutable counsel, orders all things. But within God's ordering there is room for grace—uncleanness is not ultimate exclusion.

**The Cloud Pillar—God's Guidance and Human Waiting**
Cloud moves they move, cloud stops they stop—the Reformed tradition emphasizes obedience to God's leading. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "to enjoy him forever." Israel did not control the schedule—God did. Psalm 27:14: "Wait for the LORD; be strong, and let your heart take courage; wait for the LORD!" Waiting is not passive—it is active trust.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第九章的两个画面在基督里有荣耀的成全。

**逾越节的羔羊——基督我们的逾越节**
哥林多前书5:7"因为我们逾越节的羔羊基督已经被杀献祭了。"以色列在旷野守逾越节纪念出埃及的拯救——教会守主餐纪念十字架的拯救。"一根骨头也不可折断"（12节）——约翰福音19:36"他的骨头一根也不可折断。"旧约的逾越节条例在基督的十字架上逐字应验。

第二次逾越节——恩典给"不洁净"的人第二次机会。弟兄姐妹，彼得三次不认主，却得到了第三次（不只是第二次！）的恢复机会（约21:15-17）。拿答亚比户献凡火立刻被杀——但神给不洁净的人一个月后的补守。基督的恩典比这更大——不是一个月后的补守，而是永远的赦免。

外邦人可守逾越节——以弗所书2:13"你们从前远离神的人，如今却在基督耶稣里，靠着他的血，已经得亲近了。"旧约已经预示：外邦人可以加入以色列的盟约。基督将这扇门完全打开——"同归一例"变成了"不分犹太人、希利尼人"（加3:28）。

**云柱——基督是道路**
约翰福音14:6"我就是道路、真理、生命。"以色列跟着云柱走——信徒跟着基督走。云柱白天遮荫、夜间照明——基督在一切处境中引导我们。"云彩停留……无论是两天、是一月、是一年"——基督的引导有时要我们等待、有时要我们行动——我们不掌控节奏，祂掌控。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 9's two images find glorious fulfillment in Christ.

**The Passover Lamb—Christ Our Passover**
1 Corinthians 5:7: "For Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed." Israel kept Passover in the wilderness remembering the exodus—the church keeps the Lord's Supper remembering the cross's deliverance. "Not a bone shall be broken" (v. 12)—John 19:36: "Not one of his bones will be broken." The Old Testament Passover ordinance was literally fulfilled at Christ's cross.

The second Passover—grace giving the "unclean" a second chance. Brothers and sisters, Peter denied the Lord three times, yet received a third (not just second!) restoration chance (John 21:15-17). Nadab and Abihu offered unauthorized fire and died instantly—but God gave the unclean a make-up observance one month later. Christ's grace is even greater—not a make-up one month later, but eternal forgiveness.

Foreigners could keep Passover—Ephesians 2:13: "But now in Christ Jesus you who once were far off have been brought near by the blood of Christ." The Old Testament already foreshadowed: Gentiles could join Israel's covenant. Christ threw that door wide open—"one statute for all" became "There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither bond nor free, there is neither male nor female: for ye are all one in Christ Jesus." (Gal 3:28).

**The Cloud Pillar—Christ Is the Way**
John 14:6: "I am the way, and the truth, and the life." Israel followed the cloud—believers follow Christ. Cloud provided shade by day, light by night—Christ guides us in all circumstances. "The cloud remained...whether two days, a month, or longer"—Christ's guidance sometimes calls us to wait, sometimes to act—we do not control the pace; He does.
