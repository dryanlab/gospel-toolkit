## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第六章是民数记最美的一章之一——它以拿细耳人的条例开始，以那句流传千古的亚伦祝福结束："愿耶和华赐福给你，保护你……"

**拿细耳人的条例（1-21节）**

> "无论男女许了特别的愿，就是拿细耳人的愿，要离俗归耶和华。"

"拿细耳"（Nazirite）来自希伯来文"nazar"，意思是"分别出来、圣别的人"。这不是神强迫的——而是人自愿许的特别的愿。男女都可以，不限支派。拿细耳人的愿期可长可短——但在愿期内要遵守三条严格的禁令：

第一：不可喝酒也不可接触任何葡萄制品——"从核至皮所做的物都不可吃"。弟兄姐妹，为什么禁葡萄？葡萄酒在以色列是喜乐的象征（诗篇104:15），拒绝葡萄是在说："我在一段时间内放弃正当的喜乐，来专注于神。"这不是说葡萄酒是罪——而是自愿的禁欲，为了更高的专注。

第二：不可剃头——"要由发绺长长了"。弟兄姐妹，在古代以色列，头发是力量和尊严的象征（参基甸的长发、参孙的力量在头发中）。不剃头意味着："这段时间我的外表是拿细耳誓愿的见证——我公开地、可见地献上自己。"头发是他归神状态的外在记号。

第三：不可挨近死尸——"他的父母或是弟兄姊妹死了的时候，他不可因他们使自己不洁净，因为那离俗归神的凭据是在他头上。"弟兄姐妹，这条最严苛——就算父母去世，也不能去参加葬礼。这是最难的禁令，因为它要求拿细耳人把对神的委身置于家庭孝道之上。不是说孝道不重要——而是在愿期内，神的呼召优先于一切人间的义务。

如果拿细耳人意外接触了死尸怎么办？愿期作废，从头开始——而且要献赎罪祭、燔祭、赎愆祭。不是小事——玷污了圣别，需要代价。

满了愿期怎么办？到会幕门口，献燔祭（公羊羔）、赎罪祭（母羊羔）、平安祭（公羊），再加素祭、摇祭，最后——剃去头发，烧在平安祭的火上。那长长的头发——愿期的见证——最终被献给神。仪式结束，拿细耳人重新喝酒，回归正常生活。

弟兄姐妹，拿细耳制度是以色列最早的"圣别生活"典范——任何平民都可以自愿委身于更高的圣洁标准，在有限的时间内活得像祭司一样。它传达了一个深刻的信念：普通人也可以特别地亲近神。

**亚伦的祝福（22-27节）**

然后第六章以一段无比美丽的文字结束——神命令亚伦和祭司们这样祝福以色列：

> "愿耶和华赐福给你，保护你。愿耶和华使他的脸光照你，赐恩给你。愿耶和华向你仰脸，赐你平安。"

弟兄姐妹，这三句话是旧约中最完整的三重祝福——"赐福/保护"，"脸光照/赐恩"，"仰脸/赐平安"。每一句都有两个动词，三句话共六个动词，形成一个完美的平行结构。

"脸光照"——在希伯来文化中，一个人向你展现脸面意味着接纳和喜悦；转过脸去意味着拒绝和愤怒。"愿耶和华使他的脸光照你"——愿神对你喜悦，像父亲看着爱子那样看你。

"仰脸"——比"脸光照"更进一步——不只是转脸看你，而是"仰脸"，带着专注和深情的注目。"赐你平安"——希伯来文shalom——不只是没有战争，而是全面的好、完整、繁荣、与神与人的和谐。

弟兄姐妹，这个祝福不是装饰性的——"他们要如此奉我的名为以色列人祝福；我也要赐福给他们。"神说：当祭司念出这些话，神自己在祝福以色列。祭司的口是神赐福的管道。

弟兄姐妹，这三句祝福总共有十五个希伯来词——3、5、7——每一句都比上一句增加两个词，像波浪一样层层递进。这不是随意的文学结构——这是精心设计的美丽。一位宣讲这段祝福的祭司，是在将神的赐福倾倒在百姓身上。从古至今，犹太祭司每安息日都还在念这段祝福——"愿耶和华赐福给你，保护你……"这声音贯穿了三千年，在耶路撒冷、在旷野、在今天的每一个会堂。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 6 is one of Numbers' most beautiful chapters—opening with the Nazirite ordinance and closing with that timeless Aaronic blessing: "The LORD bless you and keep you..."

**The Nazirite Ordinance (vv. 1-21)**

> "When either a man or a woman makes a special vow, the vow of a Nazirite, to separate himself to the LORD..."

"Nazirite" comes from the Hebrew "nazar," meaning "a separated, consecrated person." This was not God-compelled—but a special vow voluntarily made by people. Both men and women could take it, from any tribe. Nazirite vow periods could be long or short—but during the vow period, three strict prohibitions applied:

First: no wine and no contact with any grape products—"from seeds to skins, he shall eat nothing." Brothers and sisters, why prohibit grapes? Wine in Israel symbolized joy (Ps 104:15); refusing grapes was saying, "For a period I voluntarily forego legitimate joy to focus on God." This did not mean wine was sinful—but voluntary abstinence for higher focus.

Second: no haircut—"he shall let the locks of hair of his head grow long." Brothers and sisters, in ancient Israel, hair symbolized strength and dignity (see Gideon's long hair, Samson's strength in his hair). Not cutting hair meant: "During this period my appearance testifies to my Nazirite vow—I publicly, visibly consecrate myself." Hair was the external mark of his consecrated state.

Third: no contact with corpses—"even if his father or mother, his brother or sister dies, he must not make himself unclean...because his separation to God is on his head." Brothers and sisters, this was harshest—even if parents died, he could not attend the funeral. This was the most difficult prohibition, requiring the Nazirite to place commitment to God above family filial piety. Not that filial piety was unimportant—but during the vow period, God's calling took precedence over all human obligations.

If a Nazirite accidentally contacted a corpse? The vow period was void, starting over—with sin offering, burnt offering, and guilt offering required. Not minor—defiling consecration required a price.

When the vow period was fulfilled? Come to the tabernacle entrance, offer burnt offering (male lamb), sin offering (female lamb), peace offering (ram), plus grain offering and wave offering, finally—shave the head, burn the hair in the peace offering fire. That long hair—testimony of the vow period—ultimately offered to God. Ceremony complete, the Nazirite resumed drinking wine, returning to normal life.

Brothers and sisters, the Nazirite system was Israel's earliest model of "consecrated living"—any common person could voluntarily commit to a higher holiness standard, living for a limited time like a priest. It conveyed a profound conviction: ordinary people could also draw especially close to God.

**Aaron's Blessing (vv. 22-27)**

Then chapter 6 closed with words of incomparable beauty—God commanding Aaron and priests to bless Israel:

> "The LORD bless you and keep you; the LORD make his face shine on you and be gracious to you; the LORD turn his face toward you and give you peace."

Brothers and sisters, these three sentences are the Old Testament's most complete threefold blessing—"bless/keep," "face shine/be gracious," "turn toward/give peace." Each sentence has two verbs, three sentences sharing six verbs, forming a perfect parallel structure.

"Face shine"—in Hebrew culture, someone showing you their face meant acceptance and delight; turning away meant rejection and anger. "The LORD make his face shine on you"—may God be pleased with you, looking at you as a father looks at his beloved child.

"Turn his face toward you"—going beyond "face shine"—not just turning to look at you, but looking at you with focused, deep affection. "Give you peace"—Hebrew shalom—not merely absence of war, but comprehensive good, wholeness, prosperity, harmony with God and humanity.

Brothers and sisters, this blessing was not decorative—"So shall they put my name upon the people of Israel, and I will bless them." God said: when the priest spoke these words, God Himself was blessing Israel. The priest's mouth was the channel of God's blessing.

## 历史背景（中文）

拿细耳人在以色列历史中最著名的例子是：参孙（士13:5，从出生就是拿细耳人）、撒母耳（撒上1:11，哈拿的许愿）、施洗约翰（路1:15，"清酒浓酒都不喝"）。注意参孙是强制性的拿细耳人（神在他出生前就指定），而民6章的制度是自愿的。新约时代保罗也遵守过拿细耳人的誓愿（徒18:18，21:23-26）。亚伦的祝福（民6:24-26）是现存最古老的完整圣经文本之一——1979年在耶路撒冷基辛诺姆（Ketef Hinnom）出土的两块银卷轴（约公元前7世纪）上，发现了这段祝福的铭文——比死海古卷还要早400年。这是已知最古老的圣经文本的实物证明。

## Historical Context (English)

Israel's most famous Nazirites: Samson (Judg 13:5, a Nazirite from birth), Samuel (1 Sam 1:11, Hannah's vow), John the Baptist (Luke 1:15, "he will drink no wine nor strong drink"). Note Samson was a compulsory Nazirite (God designated before birth), while Numbers 6's system was voluntary. In the New Testament era, Paul also observed a Nazirite vow (Acts 18:18, 21:23-26). The Aaronic blessing (Num 6:24-26) is one of the oldest complete biblical texts in existence—two silver scrolls discovered in 1979 at Ketef Hinnom in Jerusalem (c. 7th century BC) bore the inscription of this blessing—predating the Dead Sea Scrolls by 400 years. This is the oldest known physical evidence of a biblical text.

## 经文结构（中文）

**拿细耳人许愿的条例（1-8节）**——离俗归耶和华、三项禁令（酒/头发/死尸）、头发是圣别凭据。

**玷污后的处理（9-12节）**——意外接触死尸→七日洁净→第八日献祭→愿期重新开始。

**满愿的仪式（13-21节）**——献燔祭赎罪祭平安祭+素祭→剃发烧在祭火上→祭司摇祭→拿细耳人可以喝酒了。

**亚伦的祝福（22-27节）**——三重祝福：赐福保护、脸光照赐恩、仰脸赐平安；奉神的名祝福神自己也赐福。

## Structure (English)

**Nazirite Vow Ordinance (vv. 1-8)**—Separate to the LORD, three prohibitions (wine/hair/corpses), hair as consecration token.

**Handling Defilement (vv. 9-12)**—Accidental corpse contact → seven-day purification → eighth day offering → vow period restarts.

**Fulfillment Ceremony (vv. 13-21)**—Offer burnt/sin/peace offerings + grain offering → shave head and burn in sacred fire → priest's wave offering → Nazirite may drink wine.

**Aaron's Blessing (vv. 22-27)**—Threefold blessing: bless and keep, face shine and be gracious, turn toward and give peace; blessed in God's name and God Himself blesses.

## 神学要点（中文）

**自愿委身——超越律法的顺服**
拿细耳人的许愿是自愿的——改革宗传统教导信徒的顺服应超越最低要求。韦敏斯德信条第16章论善行：善行就是神在圣经中所吩咐的，而不是无圣经根据的人的热心或善意的发明。但拿细耳人的许愿显示：在律法的架构内，有超越最低义务的自愿委身空间。

**神的脸面——约的亲密关系**
"脸光照""仰脸"——改革宗传统看重与神的亲密关系。韦敏斯德信条第18章论得救恩的确据：真实的信徒……因着圣灵，可以从信心和顺服的果效中，得到确据。神"向你仰脸"是最高的恩典——不只是神存在，而是神注目于你、关顾你、与你有位格的相交。

**平安（Shalom）——全面的救赎**
"赐你平安"不只是免于战争——改革宗传统教导救赎是全面的。韦敏斯德小要理问答第36问"称义、得儿子的名分与成圣，在今生所带给我们的福乐，就是确知神的慈爱、得良心的平安、在圣灵中的喜乐"。Shalom是完整的好——身体、灵魂、关系、将来全都在神的保护和祝福之下。

## Theology (English)

**Voluntary Commitment—Obedience Beyond the Law**
Nazirite vowing was voluntary—the Reformed tradition teaches believers' obedience should exceed minimum requirements. Westminster Confession chapter 16 on good works: "Good works are only such as God has commanded in His holy Word, and not such as, without the warrant thereof, are devised by men." Yet the Nazirite vow shows: within law's framework, there is room for voluntary commitment beyond minimum obligations.

**God's Face—Covenant Intimacy**
"Face shine," "turn toward"—the Reformed tradition values intimate relationship with God. Westminster Confession chapter 18 on assurance of grace: True believers...may in this life be certainly assured...through the testimony of the Spirit of adoption. God "turning His face toward you" is supreme grace—not merely God existing, but God gazing upon you, caring for you, having personal fellowship with you.

**Peace (Shalom)—Comprehensive Redemption**
"Give you peace" means more than absence of war—the Reformed tradition teaches redemption is comprehensive. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q35: "The benefits of justification are pardon of sin and acceptance as righteous in God's sight." Shalom is total good—body, soul, relationships, future all under God's protection and blessing.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第六章的两个主题在基督里都有深刻的成全。

**拿细耳人——基督是终极的"分别为圣者"**
基督的整个一生都是拿细耳式的委身——完全分别归给神，在一切事上寻求神的荣耀而非自己的喜乐。约翰福音4:34"我的食物就是遵行差我来者的旨意。"基督不喝旷野里撒但给的葡萄酒（太4:3）；在十字架上拒绝喝那使人麻醉的苦胆调的酒（太27:34）——祂要清醒地承受全部的苦难，不用任何减痛剂。祂是彻底的拿细耳人——不为自己，而为我们的救赎。

**亚伦的祝福——基督是大祭司的祝福**
希伯来书7:25-26"他长远活着，替他们祈求，就能拯救那些靠着他进到神面前的人……他圣洁、无邪恶、无玷污、远离罪人，高过诸天。"亚伦的祝福是通过人手传递的——有限的、暂时的祭司。基督是永恒的大祭司——祂的祝福不会过期，不会因自身的死亡而中断。

"愿耶和华使他的脸光照你"——弟兄姐妹，在基督里，神的脸已经向你转过来了。约翰福音1:14"充充满满地有恩典有真理"，约翰福音14:9"人看见了我，就是看见了父。"在基督里，神的脸不再隐藏——神的荣光在耶稣基督的面上显明。哥林多后书4:6"那吩咐光从黑暗里照出来的神，已经照在我们心里，叫我们得知神荣耀的光，显在耶稣基督的面上。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 6's two themes find profound fulfillment in Christ.

**The Nazirite—Christ Is the Ultimate "Consecrated One"**
Christ's entire life was Nazirite-like commitment—completely separated to God, in all things seeking God's glory rather than His own comfort. John 4:34: "My food is to do the will of him who sent me." Christ refused Satan's wine in the wilderness (Matt 4:3); at the cross refused the pain-numbing wine mixed with gall (Matt 27:34)—He would bear suffering fully conscious, using no painkiller. He was the ultimate Nazirite—not for Himself, but for our redemption.

**Aaron's Blessing—Christ Is the High Priest's Blessing**
Hebrews 7:25-26: "He is able to save to the uttermost those who draw near to God through him, since he always lives to make intercession for them...He is holy, innocent, unstained, separated from sinners, and exalted above the heavens." Aaron's blessing was transmitted through human hands—limited, temporary priests. Christ is the eternal High Priest—His blessing does not expire, not interrupted by His own death.

"The LORD make his face shine on you"—brothers and sisters, in Christ, God's face has already turned toward you. John 1:14: "full of grace and truth"; John 14:9: "Whoever has seen me has seen the Father." In Christ, God's face is no longer hidden—God's glory is revealed in Jesus Christ's face. 2 Corinthians 4:6: "For God, who said, 'Let light shine out of darkness,' has shone in our hearts to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ."
