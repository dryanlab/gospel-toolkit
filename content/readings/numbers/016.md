## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十六章是民数记最戏剧性的一章——可拉叛乱。地裂开了，火从天降，瘟疫爆发——一章之内死了14,700人。这不是普通的政治叛乱——这是对神所立的权柄秩序的全面挑战。

**叛乱的联盟（1-3节）**

可拉是利未人，哥辖家族——负责搬运至圣之物的家族。大坍和亚比兰是流便支派的——流便是长子，却失去了领导权。还有250个"有名望选入会中的人"。这是一个精心组织的联盟——利未人要祭司权、流便人要政治权、250个首领要更大的权力。

他们的控诉听起来很民主："你们擅自专权！全会众个个既是圣洁，耶和华也在他们中间，你们为什么自高，超过耶和华的会众呢？"弟兄姐妹，这话表面上是"人人平等"——但实质是"我们也想当领袖"。他们没有问"神的心意是什么"——而是问"凭什么你们在上面"。

**摩西的回应（4-11节）**

摩西听见就俯伏在地——不是恐惧，而是在神面前寻求智慧。然后他对可拉说："到了早晨，耶和华必指示谁是属他的。"弟兄姐妹，摩西没有说"我来证明我是对的"——他说"让神来决定"。

然后摩西指出可拉的核心问题："以色列的神从以色列会中将你们分别出来，使你们亲近他，办耶和华帐幕的事……这岂为小事？你们还要求祭司的职任吗？"弟兄姐妹，可拉已经是利未人——他已经比其他以色列人更靠近神了。他搬运至圣之物——那是极大的荣耀和责任。但他不满足——他还要祭司的职分。

**大坍亚比兰的拒绝（12-15节）**

大坍和亚比兰更加傲慢——他们说摩西"将我们从流奶与蜜之地领上来，要在旷野杀我们"。弟兄姐妹，他们把埃及称为"流奶与蜜之地"！颠倒黑白到了极点——流奶与蜜的是迦南，不是埃及。埃及是奴役之地。但叛逆者的记忆总是扭曲的——他们美化过去的囚牢，丑化今天的旷野。

**地开口（28-35节）**

摩西宣布了一个前所未有的测试："倘若耶和华创作一件新事，使地开口，把他们和一切属他们的都吞下去……你们就明白这些人是藐视耶和华了。"

弟兄姐妹，"创作一件新事"——这不是寻常的审判。这是创世以来从未发生过的——地面裂开，吞没活人。

> "摩西刚说完了这一切话，他们脚下的地就开了口，把他们和他们的家眷，并一切属可拉的人丁、财物，都吞下去。这样，他们和一切属他们的，都活活地坠落阴间；地口在他们上头照旧合闭，他们就从会中灭亡。"

同时——"又有火从耶和华那里出来，烧灭了那献香的二百五十个人。"

弟兄姐妹，地裂开、火从天降——两种超自然的审判同时发生。可拉、大坍、亚比兰和他们的家眷被地吞了；250个献香者被火烧灭了。

**百姓的反应和瘟疫（41-50节）**

弟兄姐妹，你以为百姓看见了这一切会惧怕悔改吗？恰恰相反——"第二天，以色列全会众都向摩西、亚伦发怨言说：你们杀了耶和华的百姓了。"

这是令人震惊的——他们亲眼看见了地裂开、火从天降，却说这是摩西和亚伦干的？他们把神的审判归咎于人的权力斗争？弟兄姐妹，不信到了极点就是把神的作为解释为人的阴谋。

神再次要灭绝他们——瘟疫已经发作。然后发生了第十六章最动人的一幕：

> "摩西对亚伦说：拿你的香炉，把坛上的火盛在其中，又加上香，快快带到会众那里，为他们赎罪；因为有忿怒从耶和华那里出来，瘟疫已经发作了。亚伦照着摩西所说的拿来，跑到会中……他就加上香，为百姓赎罪。他站在活人死人中间，瘟疫就止住了。"

弟兄姐妹，"他站在活人死人中间"——这是整本圣经最感人的画面之一。亚伦——大祭司——跑进瘟疫中，站在死亡和生命的交界处，用香的赎罪挡住了神的忿怒。他不是远远地观看——他跑进了死亡的中间。

除了可拉事件中已死的之外，瘟疫又杀了14,700人。如果不是亚伦跑进去——死的人会更多。

弟兄姐妹，可拉叛乱教导我们一个沉重的功课：对神所安排的秩序的挑战，不是民主辩论——它是对神主权的叛逆。可拉不是在对抗摩西——他是在对抗安排摩西的神。而亚伦站在活人死人中间——不是维护自己的权力，而是用自己的身体挡住了神的忿怒，拯救了他不配拯救的百姓。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 16 is Numbers' most dramatic chapter—Korah's rebellion. The earth opened, fire fell from heaven, plague erupted—14,700 died in one chapter. This was no ordinary political revolt—it was a comprehensive challenge to God's established authority structure.

**The Rebel Alliance (vv. 1-3)**

Korah was a Levite, Kohathite clan—the clan responsible for transporting the most holy objects. Dathan and Abiram were from Reuben's tribe—Reuben was firstborn but had lost leadership. Plus 250 "chiefs of the congregation, chosen from the assembly, well-known men." This was a carefully organized coalition—Levites wanting priestly rights, Reubenites wanting political power, 250 chiefs wanting greater authority.

Their charge sounded democratic: "You have gone too far! For all in the congregation are holy, every one of them, and the LORD is among them. Why then do you exalt yourselves above the assembly of the LORD?" Brothers and sisters, this sounded like "everyone is equal"—but actually meant "we want to lead too." They did not ask "what is God's will"—but "why are you on top."

**Moses' Response (vv. 4-11)**

Moses fell on his face—not in fear, but seeking wisdom before God. Then to Korah: "In the morning the LORD will show who is his." Brothers and sisters, Moses did not say "I'll prove I'm right"—he said "let God decide."

Then Moses identified Korah's core issue: "The God of Israel has separated you from the congregation of Israel, to bring you near to himself, to do service in the tabernacle...is it too small a thing for you? And would you seek the priesthood also?" Brothers and sisters, Korah was already a Levite—already nearer to God than other Israelites. He carried the most holy objects—tremendous honor and responsibility. But he was unsatisfied—he wanted the priesthood too.

**Dathan and Abiram's Defiance (vv. 12-15)**

Dathan and Abiram were even more arrogant—calling Egypt "a land flowing with milk and honey"! Brothers and sisters, they called Egypt the land of milk and honey! Complete inversion of reality—it was Canaan that flowed with milk and honey, not Egypt. Egypt was the land of slavery. But rebels' memories are always distorted—glorifying past prisons, vilifying today's wilderness.

**The Earth Opens (vv. 28-35)**

Moses announced an unprecedented test: "If the LORD creates something new, and the ground opens its mouth and swallows them up...then you shall know that these men have despised the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, "creates something new"—this was no ordinary judgment. Never before since creation had this happened—the ground splitting open, swallowing the living.

> "As soon as he finished speaking all these words, the ground under them split apart. And the earth opened its mouth and swallowed them up, with their households and all the people who belonged to Korah and all their goods. So they and all that belonged to them went down alive into Sheol, and the earth closed over them."

Simultaneously—"And fire came out from the LORD and consumed the 250 men offering the incense."

Brothers and sisters, earth splitting, fire from heaven—two supernatural judgments occurring simultaneously. Korah, Dathan, Abiram and their households swallowed by earth; 250 incense-offerers consumed by fire.

**The People's Reaction and Plague (vv. 41-50)**

Brothers and sisters, you would think seeing all this would produce repentant fear? The opposite—"The next day all the congregation of the people of Israel grumbled against Moses and Aaron, saying, 'You have killed the people of the LORD.'"

This was staggering—they witnessed the earth open and fire descend, yet blamed Moses and Aaron? They attributed God's judgment to human power struggle? Brothers and sisters, unbelief at its extreme interprets God's acts as human conspiracy.

God again moved to destroy them—plague had begun. Then chapter 16's most moving scene:

"Moses said to Aaron, 'Take your censer, and put fire on it from before the altar and lay incense on it and carry it quickly to the congregation and make atonement for them, for wrath has gone out from the LORD; the plague has begun.' So Aaron...ran into the midst of the assembly...he put on the incense and made atonement for the people. And he stood between the dead and the living, and the plague was stopped."

Brothers and sisters, "he stood between the dead and the living"—one of Scripture's most moving images. Aaron—the high priest—ran into the plague, standing at the boundary between death and life, using incense's atonement to block God's wrath. He did not watch from afar—he ran into death's midst.

Beyond those already dead from Korah's incident, the plague killed another 14,700. Had Aaron not run in—far more would have died.

Brothers and sisters, Korah's rebellion teaches a weighty lesson: challenging God's arranged order is not democratic debate—it is rebellion against God's sovereignty. Korah was not opposing Moses—he was opposing the God who appointed Moses. And Aaron standing between the dead and living—was not defending his own power, but using his own body to block God's wrath, saving people who did not deserve saving.

## 历史背景（中文）

可拉叛乱是以色列旷野经历中最大规模的内部叛乱——涉及利未人、流便支派和250位首领的联合。犹太传统（米德拉什和塔木德）对可拉有大量的讨论——Pirkei Avot 5:17将可拉的争论定义为"不是为天国的争论"（即出于私利），与希列-沙玛的学术辩论（为天国的争论）对比。犹大书1:11新约引用可拉作为叛逆的典型——"又走了可拉的叛逆路，而灭亡了。""地开口吞人"的超自然事件在古代近东文献中没有明确的平行——这被视为独一无二的神圣审判。哥辖家族的可拉的后裔并没有全部灭亡——诗篇中有11篇（42, 44-49, 84-85, 87-88）的标题是"可拉后裔的诗"——说明可拉的一些后代存活下来，后来还成为圣殿诗班的重要成员。

## Historical Context (English)

Korah's rebellion was the largest internal revolt in Israel's wilderness experience—involving Levites, Reuben's tribe, and 250 chiefs in coalition. Jewish tradition (Midrash and Talmud) extensively discusses Korah—Pirkei Avot 5:17 defines Korah's dispute as "not for the sake of Heaven" (motivated by self-interest), contrasting with Hillel-Shammai's academic debates ("for the sake of Heaven"). Jude 1:11 in the New Testament cites Korah as the archetype of rebellion—"perished in Korah's rebellion." The "earth opening to swallow people" supernatural event has no clear parallel in ancient Near Eastern literature—viewed as uniquely divine judgment. Korah's Kohathite descendants did not all perish—11 Psalms (42, 44-49, 84-85, 87-88) are titled "Of the Sons of Korah"—indicating some of Korah's descendants survived, later becoming important temple choir members.

## 经文结构（中文）

**叛乱联盟的形成（1-3节）**——可拉(利未人)+大坍亚比兰(流便)+250首领→"全会众个个圣洁"→质疑摩西亚伦权柄。

**摩西的回应与试验（4-17节）**——俯伏在地→"早晨耶和华指示"→指出可拉不知足→大坍亚比兰拒绝来→摩西发怒→明日献香试验。

**地裂开+火降下（18-35节）**——可拉一党献香→摩西亚伦代祷"一人犯罪？"→百姓离开→地开口吞可拉大坍→火烧250人。

**第二天：更大的叛逆与瘟疫（36-50节）**——百姓反说"你们杀了人"→神要灭绝→亚伦拿香炉跑入会中→站在活人死人中间→瘟疫止住→死14,700人。

## Structure (English)

**Rebel Coalition Forms (vv. 1-3)**—Korah(Levite)+Dathan/Abiram(Reuben)+250 chiefs → "all congregation holy" → challenge Moses/Aaron's authority.

**Moses' Response and Test (vv. 4-17)**—Falls prostrate → "morning the LORD will show" → exposes Korah's discontent → Dathan/Abiram refuse to come → Moses angry → incense test tomorrow.

**Earth Opens + Fire Falls (vv. 18-35)**—Korah's faction offers incense → Moses/Aaron intercede "one man sins?" → people withdraw → earth swallows Korah/Dathan → fire consumes 250.

**Next Day: Greater Rebellion and Plague (vv. 36-50)**—People blame "you killed them" → God moves to destroy → Aaron takes censer runs into assembly → stands between dead and living → plague stops → 14,700 dead.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神所安排的权柄不可挑战——职分的神圣性**
可拉挑战摩西和亚伦的权柄——改革宗传统教导职分（office）的神圣性。韦敏斯德信条第23章论国家官员、第30-31章论教会治理。罗马书13:1-2"没有权柄不是出于神的……所以抗拒掌权的就是抗拒神的命。"可拉的问题不是他说的话是否有道理——而是他挑战的是神所设立的权柄秩序。

**不知足的毒根**
可拉已是利未人却要祭司职——改革宗传统教导知足和对神安排的接受。韦敏斯德大要理问答第148问论第十诫。以色列的问题从第十一章的不满意吗哪到第十六章的"不满意我的职分"——是同一个根：不知足。神给你的位置，在你看来"岂为小事"吗？

**大祭司站在活人死人中间——代赎的核心画面**
亚伦跑进瘟疫中站在活人死人之间——这是旧约最清晰的"中保"画面之一。韦敏斯德信条第8章论基督为中保。大祭司不是远远地祈求——他用自己的身体挡在忿怒面前。这是代赎的本质：站在审判和恩典的交界处。

## Theology (English)

**God's Appointed Authority Must Not Be Challenged—The Sanctity of Office**
Korah challenged Moses and Aaron's authority—the Reformed tradition teaches the sanctity of office. Westminster Confession chapters 23 on civil magistrates, 30-31 on church government. Romans 13:1-2: "There is no authority except from God...whoever resists the authorities resists what God has appointed." Korah's problem was not whether his words made sense—but that he challenged God's established authority structure.

**The Poisonous Root of Discontent**
Korah was already a Levite yet wanted the priesthood—the Reformed tradition teaches contentment and acceptance of God's arrangement. Westminster Larger Catechism Q148 on the tenth commandment. Israel's problem from chapter 11's dissatisfied with manna to chapter 16's "dissatisfied with my position"—the same root: discontent. Is the position God gave you "too small a thing"?

**The High Priest Standing Between Dead and Living—Atonement's Core Image**
Aaron ran into the plague standing between dead and living—one of the Old Testament's clearest "mediator" images. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ the Mediator. The high priest did not pray from afar—he used his own body to block wrath. This is atonement's essence: standing at the boundary between judgment and grace.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第十六章的亚伦是基督最直接的旧约画面之一。

**亚伦站在活人死人中间——基督站在我们和神的忿怒之间**
希伯来书7:25"凡靠着他进到神面前的人，他都能拯救到底；因为他是长远活着，替他们祈求。"亚伦跑进瘟疫中——基督从天降到罪的世界里。亚伦站在死亡和生命的交界处——基督站在我们和神的忿怒之间。亚伦用香挡住了瘟疫——基督用自己的血挡住了永死。

但亚伦的救赎是有限的——他挡住了瘟疫，但14,700人已经死了。基督的救赎是完全的——"凡靠着他进到神面前的人，他都能拯救到底。"亚伦自己最终也要死——基督"是长远活着"的。

**地开口吞灭叛逆者——基督战胜死亡**
叛逆者被地吞了——基督自己进入了死亡的深渊，然后胜过了它。使徒行传2:24"神却将死的痛苦解释了，叫他复活，因为他原不能被死拘禁。"可拉下到阴间是永远的审判——基督下到阴间是暂时的得胜之旅。

**可拉的后裔写了赞美诗——恩典超越审判**
弟兄姐妹，可拉被地吞了——但他的后裔没有全部灭亡。诗篇42篇"如鹿切慕溪水，我的心切慕你"——这是可拉后裔写的。诗篇84篇"在你的院宇住一日，胜似在别处住千日"——也是可拉后裔写的。弟兄姐妹，可拉叛逆被审判——但他的后代中生出了圣殿中最美的诗歌。罗马书5:20"罪在哪里显多，恩典就更显多了。"神的恩典大过可拉的叛逆——祂从叛逆者的后裔中生出了赞美。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 16's Aaron is one of the most direct Old Testament images of Christ.

**Aaron Standing Between Dead and Living—Christ Standing Between Us and God's Wrath**
Hebrews 7:25: "He is able to save to the uttermost those who draw near to God through him, since he always lives to make intercession for them." Aaron ran into the plague—Christ descended from heaven into the sinful world. Aaron stood at death and life's boundary—Christ stands between us and God's wrath. Aaron used incense to stop the plague—Christ used His own blood to stop eternal death.

But Aaron's redemption was limited—he stopped the plague, but 14,700 had already died. Christ's redemption is complete—"He is able to save to the uttermost." Aaron himself would eventually die—Christ "always lives."

**Earth Swallowing Rebels—Christ Conquering Death**
Rebels were swallowed by the earth—Christ Himself entered death's abyss, then conquered it. Acts 2:24: "God raised him up, loosing the pangs of death, because it was not possible for him to be held by it." Korah descended to Sheol as eternal judgment—Christ descended to Sheol as a temporary victorious journey.

**Korah's Descendants Wrote Praise—Grace Surpasses Judgment**
Brothers and sisters, Korah was swallowed by the earth—but his descendants did not all perish. Psalm 42: "As a deer pants for flowing streams, so pants my soul for you"—written by sons of Korah. Psalm 84: "A day in your courts is better than a thousand elsewhere"—also by sons of Korah. Brothers and sisters, Korah's rebellion was judged—but from his descendants came the temple's most beautiful songs. Romans 5:20: "Where sin increased, grace abounded all the more." God's grace exceeded Korah's rebellion—from rebels' descendants He brought forth praise.
