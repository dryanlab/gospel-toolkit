## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十三章是巴兰故事的高潮之一——巴勒两次安排巴兰咒诅以色列，两次得到的却是祝福。这一章包含巴兰的第一和第二篇神谕——两段极其优美的诗歌。

**第一次：七座坛与第一篇神谕（1-12节）**

巴勒照巴兰的要求筑了七座坛，每座坛上献一只公牛、一只公羊——一共七只公牛、七只公羊。弟兄姐妹，这是古代近东咒诅仪式的标准程序——通过昂贵的献祭来讨好神灵，然后在这个"属灵平台"上发出咒诅。巴勒花了重金——十四头大牲口——来为咒诅铺路。

巴兰上了"净光的高处"去求见耶和华——"或者耶和华来迎见我。"然后他回来，开口说的却是：

> "巴勒引我出亚兰，摩押王引我出东山，说：来啊，为我咒诅雅各；来啊，怒骂以色列。神没有咒诅的，我焉能咒诅？耶和华没有怒骂的，我焉能怒骂？"

弟兄姐妹，巴兰的第一句话就翻转了巴勒的期望——"神没有咒诅的，我焉能咒诅？"这不是巴兰的选择——是他无力改变的事实。他想咒诅——但他做不到，因为神已经祝福了这个民族。

然后巴兰描述了他所看见的以色列："我从高峰看他，从小山望他；这是独居的民，不列在万民中。谁能数点雅各的尘土？谁能计算以色列的四分之一？"

弟兄姐妹，"独居的民，不列在万民中"——以色列在列国中是独特的，被分别出来的。"谁能数点雅各的尘土"——这直接呼应了创世记13:16神对亚伯拉罕的应许："我也要使你的后裔如同地上的尘沙那样多。"巴兰——一个外邦先知——在无意中确认了亚伯拉罕之约的应验。

然后巴兰说了一句令人惊讶的话："我愿如义人之死而死；我愿如义人之终而终。"弟兄姐妹，巴兰渴望以色列义人的结局——但他不愿意活以色列义人的生活。他想要结局的祝福，不想付出过程的代价。这是许多人的写照——想要天堂的安慰，不想走天路的窄门。

巴勒崩溃了——"你向我做的是什么事呢？我领你来咒诅我的仇敌，不料，你竟为他们祝福！"

**第二次：换地点与第二篇神谕（13-30节）**

巴勒的策略——换个地方试试。他领巴兰到琐腓田、毘斯迦山顶，在那里"只能看见他们边界上的人"——也许看少一点会更容易咒诅？这是迷信的逻辑：也许换一个角度，咒诅就会生效。

同样的仪式——七座坛，七只公牛，七只公羊。又是十四头牲口。巴勒为了咒诅以色列，已经献了二十八头大牲口了——代价极其昂贵，但全部落空。

巴兰的第二篇神谕比第一篇更加有力：

> "神非人，必不致说谎，也非人子，必不致后悔。他说话岂不照着行呢？他发言岂不要成就呢？"

弟兄姐妹，这是圣经中关于神的信实性最经典的宣告之一——出自一个外邦先知之口！神不像人——人会说谎、会后悔、会改变主意。但神说了就一定做到——"他说话岂不照着行呢？"

"我奉命祝福；神也曾赐福，此事我不能翻转。"弟兄姐妹，巴兰已经完全明白了——这个祝福他无法翻转。不是因为他不想翻转——而是因为他不能。

"他未见雅各中有罪孽，也未见以色列中有奸恶。"弟兄姐妹，以色列人难道真的没有罪吗？当然不是——他们在旷野反复犯罪。但从盟约的角度看——神看以色列不是按他们的罪来看，而是按祂的盟约来看。在基督里——神看信徒不是按他们的失败来看，而是按基督的义来看。这是称义的核心。

"耶和华他的神和他同在；有欢呼王的声音在他们中间。"弟兄姐妹——"有欢呼王的声音"——以色列营中有君王的欢呼声！这是对弥赛亚的预言——以色列中有一位王。

"断没有法术可以害雅各，也没有占卜可以害以色列。"弟兄姐妹，这直接否定了巴勒的整个策略——他用巴兰的法术和占卜来对付以色列，但"断没有"！没有任何超自然的邪恶力量可以推翻神的祝福。

"这民起来，仿佛母狮，挺身，好像公狮，未曾吃野食，未曾喝被伤者之血，决不躺卧。"以色列不是温顺的羊——在神的祝福下，他们是凶猛的狮子。

巴勒再次绝望——"你一点不要咒诅他们，也不要为他们祝福。"弟兄姐妹，巴勒的策略从"请咒诅他们"缩水成了"至少不要祝福他们"——连维持中立都做不到，因为每次巴兰开口，就是祝福。

但巴勒仍不死心——"来吧，我领你往别处去，或者神喜欢你在那里为我咒诅他们。"第三次换地点——到毘珥山顶，又是七座坛、七只公牛、七只公羊。弟兄姐妹，已经四十二头牲口了！巴勒的执念令人惊叹——他宁可一次又一次地付出巨大代价，也不愿意接受一个简单的事实：以色列是蒙福的，这不可改变。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 23 was one of the Balaam story's climaxes—Balak twice arranged for Balaam to curse Israel, and twice received blessings instead. This chapter contains Balaam's first and second oracles—two passages of extraordinary poetry.

**First Attempt: Seven Altars and the First Oracle (vv. 1-12)**

Balak built seven altars as Balaam requested, offering one bull and one ram on each—seven bulls and seven rams total. Brothers and sisters, this was the standard ancient Near Eastern cursing ritual—expensive sacrifices to win divine favor, then launching curses from this "spiritual platform." Balak spent lavishly—fourteen large animals—paving the way for curses.

Balaam ascended "a bare height" to meet the LORD—"perhaps the LORD will come to meet me." Then he returned and spoke:

> "From Aram Balak has brought me, the king of Moab from the eastern mountains: 'Come, curse Jacob for me, and come, denounce Israel!' How can I curse whom God has not cursed? How can I denounce whom the LORD has not denounced?"

Brothers and sisters, Balaam's very first words overturned Balak's expectation—"How can I curse whom God has not cursed?" This was not Balaam's choice—it was a fact he was powerless to change. He might have wanted to curse—but he could not, because God had blessed this nation.

Then Balaam described what he saw of Israel: "For from the top of the crags I see him, from the hills I behold him; behold, a people dwelling alone, and not counting itself among the nations! Who can count the dust of Jacob or number the fourth part of Israel?"

Brothers and sisters, "a people dwelling alone, not counting itself among the nations"—Israel was unique among nations, set apart. "Who can count the dust of Jacob"—directly echoing Genesis 13:16, God's promise to Abraham: "I will make your offspring as the dust of the earth." Balaam—a Gentile prophet—inadvertently confirmed the Abrahamic covenant's fulfillment.

Then Balaam said something striking: "Let me die the death of the upright, and let my end be like his!" Brothers and sisters, Balaam desired Israel's righteous people's end—but was unwilling to live the righteous person's life. He wanted the blessing of the destination without the cost of the journey. This describes many—wanting heaven's comfort without the narrow gate.

Balak collapsed—"What have you done to me? I took you to curse my enemies, and behold, you have done nothing but bless them!"

**Second Attempt: Changed Location and the Second Oracle (vv. 13-30)**

Balak's strategy—try a different spot. He brought Balaam to the field of Zophim, atop Pisgah, where "you can see only a fraction of them"—perhaps seeing fewer would make cursing easier? Superstitious logic: perhaps a different angle would make the curse work.

Same ritual—seven altars, seven bulls, seven rams. Another fourteen animals. Balak had now sacrificed twenty-eight large animals to curse Israel—enormously expensive, all in vain.

Balaam's second oracle was even more powerful than the first:

> "God is not man, that he should lie, or a son of man, that he should change his mind. Has he said, and will he not do it? Or has he spoken, and will he not fulfill it?"

Brothers and sisters, this was one of Scripture's most classic declarations of God's faithfulness—from a Gentile prophet's mouth! God is not like humans—humans lie, regret, change their minds. But God's word is certain—"Has he said, and will he not do it?"

"I have received a command to bless: he has blessed, and I cannot revoke it." Brothers and sisters, Balaam understood completely—this blessing he could not revoke. Not because he chose not to—but because he could not.

"He has not beheld misfortune in Jacob, nor has he seen trouble in Israel." Brothers and sisters, was Israel truly sinless? Of course not—they sinned repeatedly in the wilderness. But from the covenant perspective—God viewed Israel not according to their sins but according to His covenant. In Christ—God views believers not according to their failures but according to Christ's righteousness. This is justification's core.

"The LORD their God is with them, and the shout of a king is among them." Brothers and sisters—"the shout of a king"—a king's acclamation sounds among Israel's camp! This was a messianic prophecy—a king was in Israel.

"There is no enchantment against Jacob, no divination against Israel." Brothers and sisters, this directly negated Balak's entire strategy—he was using Balaam's enchantment and divination against Israel, but "there is none!" No supernatural evil force could overturn God's blessing.

"Behold, a people! As a lioness it rises up and as a lion it lifts itself; it does not lie down until it has devoured the prey and drunk the blood of the slain." Israel was no tame sheep—under God's blessing, they were fierce lions.

Balak again despaired—"Do not curse them at all, and do not bless them at all." Brothers and sisters, Balak's strategy shrank from "please curse them" to "at least don't bless them"—he couldn't even maintain neutrality, because every time Balaam opened his mouth, blessings flowed.

But Balak still would not give up—"Come now, I will take you to another place. Perhaps it will please God that you may curse them for me from there." A third location change—to Mount Peor's summit, again seven altars, seven bulls, seven rams. Brothers and sisters, forty-two animals already! Balak's obsession was astonishing—he would pay enormous costs repeatedly rather than accept a simple fact: Israel was blessed, and this could not be changed.

## 历史背景（中文）

古代近东的咒诅仪式通常包含几个要素：(1)在高处献祭（被认为离神界更近），(2)特定数量的牲畜（七是最常见的神圣数字），(3)先知或祭司进入出神状态接受神谕。巴兰的程序完全符合这一模式。"七座坛"的结构在马里文献（约公元前18世纪）中有相似记载。毘斯迦山和毘珥山都在约旦河东岸——从这些高地可以俯瞰以色列营地在摩押平原上的壮观景象。巴兰的诗歌在希伯来文学中被归为"mashal"（箴言/神谕），其文学结构（平行体、隐喻、对比）代表了古代近东诗歌的最高水平。"神非人必不致说谎"（23:19）后来成为以色列神学的核心宣告之一——撒母耳记上15:29"以色列的大能者……必不致说谎，也不致后悔"直接呼应这句。

## Historical Context (English)

Ancient Near Eastern cursing rituals typically included several elements: (1) sacrificing at high places (believed closer to the divine realm), (2) specific numbers of livestock (seven being the most common sacred number), (3) prophets or priests entering ecstatic states to receive oracles. Balaam's procedure matched this pattern perfectly. The "seven altars" structure has parallels in the Mari documents (c. 18th century BC). Pisgah and Peor were both east of the Jordan—from these heights one could overlook the spectacular sight of Israel's camp spread across the plains of Moab. Balaam's poems are classified in Hebrew literature as "mashal" (proverbs/oracles); their literary structure (parallelism, metaphor, contrast) represents ancient Near Eastern poetry's highest level. "God is not man, that he should lie" (23:19) later became one of Israelite theology's core declarations—1 Samuel 15:29 "the Glory of Israel will not lie or have regret" directly echoes it.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"神非人必不致说谎"——神的不变性**
巴兰的宣告——改革宗传统教导神的不变性（immutability）。韦敏斯德信条第2章"神自有永有，他的存有和属性都是无限的、永恒的、不变的。"神不说谎——因为说谎与祂的本性矛盾。祂不后悔——不是因为祂不会改变策略，而是因为祂从永恒中已经定好了旨意。这给信徒极大的安慰——神的应许不会因为时间、环境或人的失败而改变。

**称义的画面——"他未见雅各中有罪孽"**
以色列有罪——但神"未见"他们中有罪孽——改革宗传统教导称义。韦敏斯德信条第11章"称义是神白白恩典的作为。"称义不是说信徒没有罪——而是说神因为基督的义不再按罪来定他们。"他未见雅各中有罪孽"不是因为雅各无罪——而是因为神选择用盟约的眼睛来看。

**法术和占卜无效——神的主权保护**
"断没有法术可以害雅各"——改革宗传统教导神主权超越一切灵界势力。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理。罗马书8:38-39"都不能叫我们与神的爱隔绝。"没有任何属灵势力可以推翻神对选民的保护。

## Theology (English)

**"God Is Not Man That He Should Lie"—God's Immutability**
Balaam's declaration—the Reformed tradition teaches God's immutability. Westminster Confession chapter 2: God is infinite in being and perfection...immutable. God does not lie—because lying contradicts His nature. He does not regret—not because He cannot adjust strategy, but because from eternity He has already established His purpose. This gives believers great comfort—God's promises do not change due to time, circumstances, or human failure.

**A Justification Picture—"He Has Not Beheld Misfortune in Jacob"**
Israel was sinful—yet God "has not beheld" iniquity in them—the Reformed tradition teaches justification. Westminster Confession chapter 11: "Justification is an act of God's free grace." Justification does not mean believers have no sin—but that God, because of Christ's righteousness, no longer condemns them for sin. "He has not beheld misfortune in Jacob" is not because Jacob was sinless—but because God chose to look with covenant eyes.

**Enchantment and Divination Ineffective—God's Sovereign Protection**
"There is no enchantment against Jacob"—the Reformed tradition teaches God's sovereignty surpasses all spiritual forces. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence. Romans 8:38-39: "Nothing will be able to separate us from the love of God." No spiritual power can overturn God's protection of His elect.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第二十三章有三个指向基督的画面。

**"神非人必不致说谎"——基督是神的信实的体现**
希伯来书6:17-18"照样，神愿意为那承受应许的人格外显明他的旨意是不更改的，就起了誓。借这两件不更改的事，神决不能说谎。"基督是神所有应许的"阿们"——哥林多后书1:20"神的应许，不论有多少，在基督都是是的。"巴兰宣告了神的信实——基督成全了神的信实。

**"他未见雅各中有罪孽"——基督里的称义**
弟兄姐妹，以色列有罪——但神不按罪来看他们。这正是基督里称义的预表——罗马书4:7-8"得赦免其过、遮盖其罪的，这人是有福的！主不算为有罪的，这人是有福的！"基督的义遮盖了信徒的罪——不是罪不存在，而是神不再"算为有罪"。巴兰从高处看以色列——看见的是一个蒙福的、被保护的、没有罪孽的民族。这是父神从永恒中看基督里的教会的视角。

**"有欢呼王的声音在他们中间"——基督是王**
以色列中"有欢呼王的声音"——这指向弥赛亚君王。约翰福音12:13"和散那！奉主名来的以色列王是应当称颂的！"基督进入耶路撒冷——百姓欢呼"以色列的王"。巴兰在以色列营外预言了一个王的存在——七百年后大卫登基，一千四百年后基督来到。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 23 contains three images pointing to Christ.

**"God Is Not Man That He Should Lie"—Christ Embodies God's Faithfulness**
Hebrews 6:17-18: "So when God desired to show more convincingly to the heirs of the promise the unchangeable character of his purpose, he guaranteed it with an oath, so that by two unchangeable things, in which it is impossible for God to lie." Christ is the "Amen" of all God's promises—2 Corinthians 1:20: "For all the promises of God find their Yes in him." Balaam declared God's faithfulness—Christ fulfilled God's faithfulness.

**"He Has Not Beheld Misfortune in Jacob"—Justification in Christ**
Brothers and sisters, Israel was sinful—but God did not view them according to sin. This is exactly the prefiguring of justification in Christ—Romans 4:7-8: "Blessed are those whose lawless deeds are forgiven, and whose sins are covered; blessed is the man against whom the Lord will not count his sin." Christ's righteousness covers believers' sin—not that sin doesn't exist, but God no longer "counts it." Balaam from the heights looked at Israel—seeing a blessed, protected, iniquity-free nation. This is the Father's eternal perspective looking at the church in Christ.

**"The Shout of a King Among Them"—Christ Is King**
Among Israel "the shout of a king"—pointing to the messianic King. John 12:13: "Hosanna! Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord, even the King of Israel!" Christ entering Jerusalem—people shouting "the King of Israel." Balaam prophesied a king's presence from outside Israel's camp—seven hundred years later David enthroned, fourteen hundred years later Christ came.
