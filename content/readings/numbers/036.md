## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第三十六章——民数记的最后一章——以西罗非哈的女儿们结束，正如第二十七章以她们开始。整本民数记的最后一笔，画在了五个女儿的婚姻上。

**问题的提出（1-4节）**

弟兄姐妹，第二十七章神判定西罗非哈的女儿们可以继承父亲的产业——这个判决改变了以色列的继承法。但现在玛拿西支派的族长们发现了一个新问题：如果这些女儿嫁给其他支派的男人呢？

> "他们若嫁以色列别支派的人，就必将我们祖宗所遗留的产业加在他们丈夫支派的产业中。这样，我们拈阄所得的产业就要减少了。"

弟兄姐妹，在以色列的家庭制度中，妻子的财产归入丈夫的家族。如果西罗非哈的女儿们嫁到犹大支派——她们在玛拿西的土地就会变成犹大的土地。长此以往——支派之间的产业界限会模糊化，原本按拈阄精心分配的土地会通过婚姻重新洗牌。

更严重的是禧年的问题——每五十年的禧年，被卖的土地要回归原主。但通过婚姻转移的土地不受禧年条款的约束——它会永久性地从一个支派转到另一个支派。

**神的解决方案——同族通婚（5-9节）**

> "摩西照耶和华的话吩咐以色列人说：约瑟支派的人说得有理。"

弟兄姐妹，神再次肯定了合理的请求——"说得有理"。然后给出了解决方案："他们可以随意嫁人，只是要嫁同宗支派的人。"

弟兄姐妹，"随意嫁人"——她们有自由选择；"只是同宗支派"——自由在一个框架内。这不是取消她们的继承权——而是加了一个条件：继承了产业的女子要在本支派内结婚。这保护了两个原则：女儿的继承权不被撤回，支派的产业边界不被打破。

"这样，以色列人的产业就不从这支派归到那支派，因为以色列人要各守各祖宗支派的产业。"弟兄姐妹——"各守各祖宗支派的产业"——这是一个关于稳定性的原则。产业不应该在世代之间随意流动——每个支派的根基应该是牢固的。

**西罗非哈的女儿们的顺服（10-12节）**

> "耶和华怎样吩咐摩西，西罗非哈的众女儿就怎样行。西罗非哈的女儿玛拉、得撒、曷拉、密迦、挪阿都嫁了他们伯叔的儿子。他们嫁入约瑟儿子、玛拿西子孙的族中；他们的产业仍留在同宗支派中。"

弟兄姐妹，五个女儿完全顺服了神的命令——她们嫁给了自己伯叔的儿子（堂兄弟），留在了玛拿西支派内。她们的产业"仍留在同宗支派中"——问题完美解决。

弟兄姐妹，这五个女儿的故事贯穿了民数记的最后十章——从第二十七章的勇敢请愿到第三十六章的顺服嫁娶。她们不只是被动地接受命运——她们积极地参与了以色列法律的塑造。但当神给出了新的条件时——她们也完全接受了。勇气和顺服不是矛盾的——真正的勇气知道什么时候该争取，什么时候该接受。

**民数记的结语（13节）**

> "这是耶和华在摩押平原、约旦河边、耶利哥对面藉着摩西所吩咐以色列人的命令典章。"

弟兄姐妹，民数记到此结束了。它始于西奈山脚下的人口普查——六十万男丁准备出发。它终于摩押平原约旦河边——新的六十万人准备过河。中间是三十八年的旷野漂流、无数次的失败和审判、亚伦和米利暗的死、巴兰的预言、两次人口普查、详尽的法律条例。

弟兄姐妹，民数记的故事还没有真正结束——它在等待约书亚记来完成。以色列人站在约旦河边——但还没有过河。应许之地在对面——肉眼可见，但脚还没有踏上去。这是整本民数记的张力：应许是确定的——但实现需要等待、需要信心、需要一步一步地走。

弟兄姐妹，你也许也站在你人生的约旦河边——应许在对面，但你还没有过河。民数记告诉你：旧的一代失败了，但新的一代会过去。旷野不是终点——迦南在前方。过河的那一天会来的。

这是耶和华藉着摩西所吩咐的。阿们。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 36—Numbers' final chapter—ends with Zelophehad's daughters, just as chapter 27 began with them. The entire book of Numbers' last brushstroke was painted on five daughters' marriages.

**The Problem Raised (vv. 1-4)**

Brothers and sisters, in chapter 27 God ruled that Zelophehad's daughters could inherit their father's estate—this ruling changed Israelite inheritance law. But now Manasseh's tribal leaders discovered a new problem: what if these daughters married men from other tribes?

> "If they are married to any of the sons of the other tribes of the people of Israel, then their inheritance will be taken from the inheritance of our fathers and added to the inheritance of the tribe into which they marry."

Brothers and sisters, in Israel's family system, a wife's property transferred to her husband's clan. If Zelophehad's daughters married into Judah—their Manasseh land would become Judah's land. Over time—tribal boundaries would blur, and land carefully distributed by lots would be reshuffled through marriage.

More seriously, the Jubilee problem—every fifty years, sold land reverted to original owners. But land transferred through marriage was not covered by Jubilee provisions—it would permanently shift from one tribe to another.

**God's Solution—Within-Tribe Marriage (vv. 5-9)**

> "Moses commanded the people of Israel according to the word of the LORD, saying, 'The tribe of the people of Joseph is right.'"

Brothers and sisters, God again affirmed a reasonable request—"is right." Then the solution: "Let them marry whom they think best, only they shall marry within the clan of the tribe of their father."

Brothers and sisters, "marry whom they think best"—they had freedom of choice; "only within the clan"—freedom within a framework. This did not revoke their inheritance right—but added a condition: daughters who inherited land must marry within their tribe. This protected two principles: daughters' inheritance rights remained intact, tribal estate boundaries remained unbroken.

"So no inheritance shall be transferred from one tribe to another, for each of the tribes of the people of Israel shall hold on to its own inheritance." Brothers and sisters—"hold on to its own inheritance"—a stability principle. Estates should not randomly flow between generations—each tribe's foundation should be firm.

**Zelophehad's Daughters' Obedience (vv. 10-12)**

> "The daughters of Zelophehad did as the LORD commanded Moses. Mahlah, Tirzah, Hoglah, Milcah, and Noah, the daughters of Zelophehad, married sons of their father's brothers. They were married into the clans of the people of Manasseh the son of Joseph, and their inheritance remained in the tribe of their father's clan."

Brothers and sisters, the five daughters fully obeyed God's command—they married their paternal cousins, remaining within Manasseh. Their inheritance "remained in the tribe"—problem perfectly resolved.

Brothers and sisters, these five daughters' story spanned Numbers' final ten chapters—from chapter 27's courageous petition to chapter 36's obedient marriage. They did not passively accept fate—they actively participated in shaping Israelite law. But when God gave new conditions—they fully accepted them. Courage and obedience are not contradictory—true courage knows when to advocate and when to accept.

**Numbers' Closing Statement (v. 13)**

> "These are the commandments and the rules that the LORD commanded through Moses to the people of Israel in the plains of Moab by the Jordan at Jericho."

Brothers and sisters, Numbers ends here. It began with a census at the foot of Sinai—600,000 men preparing to depart. It ends on the plains of Moab by the Jordan—a new 600,000 preparing to cross. Between: thirty-eight years of wilderness wandering, countless failures and judgments, Aaron and Miriam's deaths, Balaam's prophecies, two censuses, exhaustive legal ordinances.

Brothers and sisters, Numbers' story has not truly ended—it awaits Joshua to complete. Israel stands at the Jordan's edge—but has not yet crossed. The promised land is across—visible, but feet have not yet touched it. This is Numbers' tension: the promise is certain—but fulfillment requires waiting, faith, and step-by-step walking.

Brothers and sisters, perhaps you also stand at your life's Jordan—the promise is across, but you haven't crossed yet. Numbers tells you: the old generation failed, but the new one will cross over. The wilderness is not the destination—Canaan lies ahead. The day of crossing will come.

These are the commandments the LORD gave through Moses. Amen.

## 历史背景（中文）

民数记以"这是……在摩押平原约旦河边耶利哥对面藉着摩西所吩咐的命令典章"结尾——与利未记的结尾（"这是耶和华在西奈山……所吩咐摩西的"利26:46）形成对比。利未记在西奈山——民数记在摩押平原。以色列人从西奈走到了约旦河边——地理的移动反映了叙事的推进。西罗非哈的女儿们的故事在犹太传统中被高度重视——塔木德（Bava Batra 119b）赞美她们为"智慧的、虔诚的、有德行的"女子，认为她们比摩西更有洞察力（因为她们先看到了产业法的不足）。她们"嫁了伯叔的儿子"——堂兄弟婚姻在古代以色列是完全合法的（不在利18章的禁婚范围内）。民数记从数点（census/numbers）开始，以婚姻法结束——从军事编制到家庭律法——涵盖了以色列社会生活的全部维度。

## Historical Context (English)

Numbers ends with "These are the commandments...in the plains of Moab by the Jordan at Jericho through Moses"—contrasting with Leviticus' ending ("These are the commandments...on Mount Sinai" Lev 26:46). Leviticus at Sinai—Numbers at the plains of Moab. Israel had traveled from Sinai to the Jordan's edge—geographic movement reflecting narrative progression. Zelophehad's daughters' story was highly valued in Jewish tradition—the Talmud (Bava Batra 119b) praised them as "wise, pious, and virtuous" women, believing they had greater insight than Moses (since they first identified the inheritance law's gap). Their marrying "sons of their father's brothers"—cousin marriage was fully legal in ancient Israel (not within Leviticus 18's prohibited relationships). Numbers began with a census, ended with marriage law—from military organization to family legislation—encompassing all dimensions of Israelite social life.

## Structure (English)

**Manasseh Chiefs' Problem (vv. 1-4)**—Daughters inheriting land then marrying into other tribes → estate transfers to other tribes → Jubilee cannot recover.

**God's Solution (vv. 5-9)**—"The tribe of Joseph is right" → "marry whom they wish, only within their father's tribe" → inheritance does not transfer between tribes.

**Five Daughters' Obedience (vv. 10-12)**—Married paternal cousins → married within Manasseh → inheritance remained in same clan.

**Numbers' Closing (v. 13)**—"These are the commandments...the LORD commanded through Moses...in the plains of Moab by the Jordan at Jericho."

## 神学要点（中文）

**框架内的自由——自由与秩序的统一**
"随意嫁人只要同宗"——改革宗传统教导自由在秩序的框架内运作。韦敏斯德信条第20章论基督徒自由。加拉太书5:13"你们蒙召是要得自由，只是不可将你们的自由当作放纵情欲的机会，总要用爱心互相服事。"真正的自由不是无限制——而是在神的秩序中有意义的选择。

**产业的稳定性——盟约的持久性**
支派产业不可被转移——改革宗传统教导神盟约的持久性和确定性。韦敏斯德信条第7章论恩典之约。神分配的不可被人随意重新分配——"各守各祖宗支派的产业"。在基督里——"神的恩赐和选召是没有后悔的"（罗11:29）。

**勇气与顺服的统一——真正的信心**
五个女儿在第27章勇敢请愿、在第36章顺服嫁娶——改革宗传统教导信心既有勇气也有顺服。韦敏斯德信条第16章论善功。真正的信心不只是争取权益——也包括在得到之后负责任地管理。她们争取了产业——然后负责任地保护了它。

## Theology (English)

**Freedom Within Framework—Unity of Freedom and Order**
"Marry whom they wish, only within their tribe"—the Reformed tradition teaches freedom operates within order's framework. Westminster Confession chapter 20 on Christian liberty. Galatians 5:13: "You were called to freedom, brothers. Only do not use your freedom as an opportunity for the flesh, but through love serve one another." True freedom is not unrestricted—but meaningful choice within God's order.

**Estate Stability—Covenant's Permanence**
Tribal inheritance cannot be transferred—the Reformed tradition teaches God's covenant's permanence and certainty. Westminster Confession chapter 7 on the covenant of grace. What God distributed cannot be casually redistributed—"each shall hold on to its own inheritance." In Christ—"For the gifts and calling of God are without repentance." (Rom 11:29).

**Unity of Courage and Obedience—True Faith**
The five daughters courageously petitioned in chapter 27, obediently married in chapter 36—the Reformed tradition teaches faith contains both courage and obedience. Westminster Confession chapter 16 on good works. True faith is not only fighting for rights—but also responsibly managing what has been obtained. They fought for inheritance—then responsibly protected it.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第三十六章——民数记的终章——指向基督在两个方面。

**产业留在支派中——在基督里的产业不可失去**
西罗非哈的女儿们嫁给了同族的人，确保产业不会离开——约翰福音10:28-29"我又赐给他们永生；他们永不灭亡，谁也不能从我手里把他们夺去。我父把他们赐给我，他比万有都大，谁也不能从我父手里把他们夺去。"弟兄姐妹，在基督里的产业是不可转移的——没有任何力量可以把你从基督手中夺走。产业"仍留在同宗支派中"——你在基督里，永远在基督里。

**民数记从旷野开始、在约旦河边结束——等候基督带我们过河**
以色列站在约旦河边但还没有过河——约书亚（Yeshua/耶稣）将在下一卷书带他们过去。弟兄姐妹，我们的人生也常常站在"约旦河边"——应许在对面，但还没到。

希伯来书11:13"这些人都是存着信心死的，并没有得着所应许的；却从远处望见，且欢喜迎接。"民数记的结尾是一个"望见但未到达"的状态——就像旧约整体在等待基督一样。但正如约书亚最终带以色列过了约旦河——耶稣基督最终带我们进入神的国度。

弟兄姐妹，民数记结束了——但故事还没有结束。约旦河在等着我们过去。阿们。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 36—Numbers' final chapter—points to Christ in two aspects.

**Inheritance Remaining in the Tribe—Inheritance in Christ Cannot Be Lost**
Zelophehad's daughters married within their tribe, ensuring inheritance would not leave—John 10:28-29: "I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand. My Father, who has given them to me, is greater than all, and no one is able to snatch them out of the Father's hand." Brothers and sisters, inheritance in Christ is non-transferable—no force can seize you from Christ's hand. The inheritance "remained in the tribe"—you are in Christ, forever in Christ.

**Numbers Begins in the Wilderness, Ends at the Jordan—Waiting for Christ to Lead Us Across**
Israel stood at the Jordan but had not yet crossed—Joshua (Yeshua/Jesus) would lead them across in the next book. Brothers and sisters, our lives also often stand at "the Jordan's edge"—the promise is across, but we haven't arrived.

Hebrews 11:13: "These all died in faith, not having received the things promised, but having seen them and greeted them from afar." Numbers' ending is a "seen but not yet reached" state—just as the entire Old Testament awaited Christ. But as Joshua ultimately led Israel across the Jordan—Jesus Christ ultimately leads us into God's kingdom.

Brothers and sisters, Numbers has ended—but the story has not. The Jordan awaits our crossing. Amen.
