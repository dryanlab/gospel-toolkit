## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十一章是一个转折点——旷野故事开始从"失败与审判"转向"胜利与征服"。这一章有三个主要段落：铜蛇、水井之歌、两场对外战争的胜利。

**亚拉得王的失败与以色列的第一次胜利（1-3节）**

迦南人亚拉得王主动攻击以色列，掳了几个人。以色列人向神发愿："你若将这民交付我手，我就把他们的城邑尽行毁灭。"

神应允了——他们把迦南人和他们的城邑"尽行毁灭"（herem，圣战毁灭）。那地方叫做"何珥玛"（毁灭之意）。

弟兄姐妹，这是很重要的历史节点——第一次，以色列人向迦南进攻并胜利了。四十年前他们在迦南探子的报告后退缩不敢进攻——现在，新的一代开始前进了。

**铜蛇——信心的一望（4-9节）**

但胜利之后，百姓又开始抱怨。绕行以东的长路令他们烦躁——"你们为什么把我们从埃及领出来、使我们死在旷野呢？这里没有粮，没有水，我们的心厌恶这淡薄的食物。"

弟兄姐妹，"厌恶这淡薄的食物"——他们厌恶的是吗哪！神每天供应的天粮！这是以色列最高级别的忘恩负义——将神的礼物叫做"淡薄"（nequellah，字面意思"轻蔑的、不值一提的"）。

于是神使火蛇进入营中，咬死了许多人。

百姓悔改求告摩西——"我们怨讟耶和华和你，有罪了。"这次他们明确认罪："我们有罪了"——不是辩解，不是推卸责任。

神对我说："你制造一条火蛇，挂在杆子上；凡被咬的，一望这蛇，就必得活。"

弟兄姐妹，我照做了——铜蛇挂在杆子上，被蛇咬的人"一望"就活了。

这是整个旧约中最奇特的救治方式之一——不是药物，不是手术，甚至不是祷告后的超自然治愈——而是"望"。望一眼那挂在杆子上的铜蛇，就活了。信心之望，不是努力，不是成就——就是"看"。

"凡被咬的，一望就活"——没有例外。无论你被咬得多深，无论毒素已经扩散多远——只要你望一眼，就活了。但如果你不望呢？如果你因为骄傲、因为无知、因为太痛苦而无法抬头呢？毒素继续，直到死亡。

**水井之歌（10-20节）**

> "当时，以色列人唱歌说：井阿，涌上水来！你们要向这井歌唱。这井是首领和民中的尊贵人用圭用杖所挖所掘的。"

弟兄姐妹，在米利巴他们为没有水抱怨——现在他们为水唱歌！旷野旅途中居然有了歌声。同样是水，同样是旷野——但这次他们选择了感恩而不是抱怨。这是属灵成熟的标志：看见同样的处境，选择用不同的心态回应。

**西宏和噩的战败（21-35节）**

以色列人再次礼貌地请求亚摩利王西宏借道。西宏拒绝、出兵攻打。以色列人反击，大败西宏，占领了他的全部土地——从亚嫩河到雅博河。然后巴珊王噩也出兵。神对我说："不要怕！因我已将他和他的众民，并他的地，都交在你手中。"以色列人杀了噩和他所有的人，占领了巴珊。

弟兄姐妹，第二十一章结束时，以色列人已经征服了约旦河东的大片土地——亚摩利人的领地和巴珊。他们站在了迦南的门口。

这一章的弧线是：从抱怨（厌恶吗哪）→被咬（审判）→认罪（悔改）→一望铜蛇（信心）→得救（恩典）→感恩（水井之歌）→胜利（西宏和噩）。弟兄姐妹，这是救赎的完整模式：审判→悔改→信心→恩典→感恩→丰盛。每一次你从抱怨转向感恩，都是一次小小的出埃及。

弟兄姐妹，铜蛇事件还有一个重要的细节——神没有把蛇从营中移走。被咬的可能性仍然存在——但解药已经预备了。神没有消除旷野的艰难——祂在艰难中预备了救治。这是信仰生活的真实写照：苦难不会消失，但被举起的基督永远在那里，等待你抬头望祂。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 21 was a turning point—the wilderness story began shifting from "failure and judgment" toward "victory and conquest." This chapter has three main sections: the bronze serpent, the song of the well, and two victorious external battles.

**King Arad's Defeat and Israel's First Victory (vv. 1-3)**

The Canaanite king Arad proactively attacked Israel, capturing some people. Israel vowed to God: "If you will indeed give this people into my hand, then I will devote their cities to destruction."

God granted it—they "devoted to destruction" (herem, sacred war destruction) the Canaanites and their cities. The place was named "Hormah" (meaning destruction).

Brothers and sisters, this was an important historical moment—for the first time, Israel attacked Canaan and won. Forty years earlier they had retreated in fear after the spies' report—now the new generation began moving forward.

**The Bronze Serpent—One Look of Faith (vv. 4-9)**

But after victory, the people complained again. The long detour around Edom frustrated them—"Why have you brought us up out of Egypt to die in the wilderness? For there is no food and no water, and we loathe this worthless food."

Brothers and sisters, "we loathe this worthless food"—they despised manna! God's daily heavenly provision! This was Israel's highest ingratitude—calling God's gift "worthless" (nequellah, literally "contemptible, not worth mentioning").

So God sent fiery serpents among the camp, biting many to death.

The people repented and appealed to Moses—"We have sinned, for we have spoken against the LORD and against you." This time they confessed clearly: "We have sinned"—no explanation, no blame-shifting.

God said to me: "Make a fiery serpent and set it on a pole, and everyone who is bitten, when he sees it, shall live."

Brothers and sisters, I did so—the bronze serpent was set on a pole; those bitten "looked" and lived.

This was one of the Old Testament's most unusual healings—not medicine, not surgery, not even supernatural healing after prayer—but "looking." One look at the serpent hung on the pole, and you lived. Faith's gaze, not effort, not achievement—just "seeing."

"Everyone who is bitten, when he sees it, shall live"—no exceptions. However deep the bite, however far the venom had spread—just one look and you lived. But what if you didn't look? If pride, ignorance, or too much pain prevented lifting your eyes? The venom continued until death.

**The Song of the Well (vv. 10-20)**

> "Then Israel sang this song: 'Spring up, O well!—Sing to it!—the well that the princes made, that the nobles of the people dug, with the scepter and with their staffs.'"

Brothers and sisters, at Meribah they complained about having no water—now they sang about water! In the wilderness journey came song. The same situation, the same wilderness—but this time they chose gratitude over complaint. A mark of spiritual maturity: seeing identical circumstances, choosing a different response.

**Sihon and Og's Defeat (vv. 21-35)**

Israel again politely requested passage through Sihon the Amorite king's territory. Sihon refused and attacked. Israel counter-attacked, decisively defeating Sihon and occupying all his territory—from the Arnon to the Jabbok. Then Og king of Bashan also attacked. God said to me: "Do not fear him, for I have given him into your hand, and all his people, and his land." Israel killed Og and all his people, occupying Bashan.

Brothers and sisters, by chapter 21's end, Israel had conquered large territory east of the Jordan—the Amorites' land and Bashan. They stood at Canaan's doorstep.

This chapter's arc: complaint (despising manna) → bitten (judgment) → confession (repentance) → one look at the bronze serpent (faith) → saved (grace) → gratitude (song of the well) → victory (Sihon and Og). Brothers and sisters, this is redemption's complete pattern: judgment → repentance → faith → grace → gratitude → abundance. Every time you turn from complaint to gratitude, it is a small exodus.

## 历史背景（中文）

铜蛇（Nehushtan）后来成为以色列崇拜的对象——列王纪下18:4记载希西家王改革时"砸碎了摩西所造的铜蛇，因为到那时以色列人仍向铜蛇烧香，称那铜蛇叫尼曷斯坦"。这段历史说明：神的工具可以变成偶像——无论多么有意义的纪念物，都可能被误用。西宏王（Sihon）和巴珊王噩（Og）的征服是以色列军事史的重要里程碑，这段历史在申命记2-3章、约书亚记等书中多次回顾。巴珊王噩据说是"利乏音人所剩下的"——一个巨人族的后裔（申3:11记载他的铁床超过4米长）。约旦河东的亚摩利人领地征服后，成为流便、迦得和玛拿西半支派的分地（民32章）。"水井之歌"是民数记中少见的诗歌片段，与出埃及记15章的红海颂歌和申命记32章的摩西之歌一起，是旷野诗歌文学的代表。

## Historical Context (English)

The bronze serpent (Nehushtan) later became an object of Israelite worship—2 Kings 18:4 records King Hezekiah's reform included smashing "the bronze serpent that Moses had made, for until those days the people of Israel had made offerings to it (it was called Nehushtan)." This history illustrates: God's tools can become idols—however meaningful, any memorial can be misused. Sihon's and Og's conquests were important milestones in Israelite military history, recalled multiple times in Deuteronomy 2-3, Joshua, and elsewhere. King Og was reportedly "one of the remnant of the Rephaim"—a giant clan descendant (Deut 3:11 records his iron bed exceeded 4 meters in length). After conquest, the Transjordan Amorite territory became the allotment for Reuben, Gad, and half of Manasseh (Numbers 32). The "Song of the Well" is one of Numbers' rare poetic passages, alongside Exodus 15's Red Sea song and Deuteronomy 32's Song of Moses as representatives of wilderness poetic literature.

## Structure (English)

**King Arad Defeated (vv. 1-3)**—Canaanites attack Israel → Israel vows → God grants → total destruction → place named "Hormah" (destruction).

**Fiery Serpents and Bronze Serpent (vv. 4-9)**—Detouring Edom → people complain, despise manna → fiery serpents kill many → people confess sin → God commands bronze serpent on pole → bitten "look" and live.

**Journey Log and Song of the Well (vv. 10-20)**—Multiple camp sites → "Spring up, O well" → Israel sings in gratitude.

**King Sihon's Defeat (vv. 21-32)**—Passage request refused → Israel counter-attacks decisively → occupies all Amorite land.

**King Og of Bashan's Defeat (vv. 33-35)**—Og attacks → "Do not fear!" → Israel victorious, occupies Bashan.

## 神学要点（中文）

**一望得救——信心的本质**
铜蛇一望得救——改革宗传统教导信心是接受神所设立的救治方式。韦敏斯德小要理问答第86问"「信服耶稣基督」是什么意思？"答："信服耶稣基督是神所赐的得救恩典；使我们借此可以照着福音的信息与劝勉而接受基督，并惟独靠祂得救。"一望不是伟大的行动——而是转过脸来接受神提供的救治。真信心不复杂——它是转向、接受、依靠。

**感恩转化处境——水井之歌**
同样的旷野，从抱怨到歌唱——改革宗传统教导感恩是盟约生活的核心德行。韦敏斯德大要理问答第105问论第四诫，以感恩为敬拜的核心动机。腓立比书4:11"我学会了，无论在什么状态可以知足。"知足不是天生的——是"学会的"。从铜蛇一望到水井之歌，是信心后的感恩转化。

**圣战的神学——耶和华战士**
以色列击败西宏和噩——改革宗传统教导神在历史中的主权。韦敏斯德信条第3章论神的永恒旨意。以色列的胜利不是军事优越性——"不要怕！因我已将他交在你手中。"神命定的胜利无人能拦阻。

## Theology (English)

**One Look Saves—Faith's Essence**
Bronze serpent, one look, saved—the Reformed tradition teaches faith accepts God's appointed remedy. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q86: "Faith in Jesus Christ is a saving grace, whereby we receive and rest upon him alone for salvation." One look was not a great act—but turning to accept God's provided cure. True faith is not complex—it is turning, receiving, trusting.

**Gratitude Transforms Circumstances—Song of the Well**
Same wilderness, from complaining to singing—the Reformed tradition teaches gratitude as the covenant life's core virtue. Westminster Larger Catechism Q105 on the fourth commandment, with gratitude as worship's core motivation. Philippians 4:11: "I have learned, in whatever situation I am, to be content." Contentment is not innate—it is "learned." From the bronze serpent's one look to the song of the well is faith's subsequent gratitude transformation.

**Holy War Theology—The LORD as Warrior**
Israel defeating Sihon and Og—the Reformed tradition teaches God's sovereignty in history. Westminster Confession chapter 3 on God's eternal decree. Israel's victory was not military superiority—"Do not fear him, for I have given him into your hand." God's appointed victory no one can obstruct.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第二十一章有新约中最明确的旧约应用之一。

**铜蛇——基督被举起**
约翰福音3:14-15"摩西在旷野怎样举蛇，人子也必照样被举起来，叫一切信他的都得永生。"耶稣自己把铜蛇与祂被钉十字架直接等同——这是圣经中最清晰的预表成全。

铜蛇的材料是铜（象征审判）——基督"成为罪"（林后5:21），代替我们承受审判。铜蛇被举在杆上——基督被举在十字架上。被蛇咬的人"一望"就活——一切相信基督被举起的人"都得永生"。不需要任何其他条件——不需要律法的完全遵守，不需要一系列的善功——只需要"信"，只需要"看"。

约翰福音3:16紧接在3:14-15之后——"神爱世人，甚至将他的独生子赐给他们，叫一切信他的，不至灭亡，反得永生。"铜蛇的语境直接引出了基督教最著名的经文。弟兄姐妹，你是被蛇咬的人——罪的毒素在你里面。解药已经被挂起来了。你愿意"一望"吗？

**水井之歌——圣灵的活水**
以色列人为水井唱歌——约翰福音4:14"我所赐的水要在他里头成为泉源，直涌到永生。"以色列在旷野的水井是有限的——基督所赐的水是永远不渴。

**西宏和噩的征服——基督征服仇敌**
以色列征服亚摩利王西宏和巴珊王噩——歌罗西书2:15"既将一切执政的、掌权的掳来，明显给众人看，就仗着十字架夸胜。"基督通过十字架征服了罪、死亡和魔鬼——不是通过回避，而是通过直接进入战场、正面击败仇敌。以色列进入了约旦河东的土地——基督的子民将进入永恒的应许之地。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 21 contains one of the New Testament's most explicit Old Testament applications.

**The Bronze Serpent—Christ Lifted Up**
John 3:14-15: "As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up, that whoever believes in him may have eternal life." Jesus Himself directly equated the bronze serpent with His crucifixion—the clearest type-fulfillment in Scripture.

The serpent's material was bronze (representing judgment)—Christ "For he hath made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in him." (2 Cor 5:21), bearing judgment on our behalf. The bronze serpent was lifted on a pole—Christ was lifted on the cross. Those bitten "looked" and lived—everyone believing in Christ being lifted up "may have eternal life." No other conditions required—not complete law-keeping, not a series of good deeds—only "believe," only "look."

John 3:16 follows immediately after 3:14-15—"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life." The bronze serpent's context directly yields Christianity's most famous verse. Brothers and sisters, you are the bitten person—sin's venom is within you. The antidote has been hung up. Will you "look"?

**Song of the Well—The Spirit's Living Water**
Israel sang for the well—John 4:14: "The water that I will give him will become in him a spring of water welling up to eternal life." Israel's wilderness well was limited—the water Christ gives never runs dry.

**Conquering Sihon and Og—Christ Conquering Enemies**
Israel conquered Sihon and Og—Colossians 2:15: "He disarmed the rulers and authorities and put them to open shame, by triumphing over them in him." Christ through the cross conquered sin, death, and the devil—not by avoidance but by entering the battlefield directly, defeating enemies head-on. Israel entered Transjordania—Christ's people will enter the eternal promised land.
