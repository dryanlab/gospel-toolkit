## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十九章讲的是红母牛条例——以色列律法中最神秘、最令人费解的仪式之一。犹太传统说，连所罗门——世上最智慧的王——也无法完全理解红母牛的含义。但让我尽力为你们解开这个奥秘。

**红母牛的条例（1-10节）**

> "你要吩咐以色列人，把一只没有残疾、未曾负轭、纯红的母牛牵到你这里来。"

弟兄姐妹，这只母牛的要求极其严格：(1)纯红——传统说不能有超过两根非红色的毛；(2)没有残疾——完美无缺；(3)未曾负轭——从未被用于劳动。这几乎是不可能找到的——犹太传统记载，从摩西到第二圣殿被毁（公元70年），以色列历史上总共只制备了九只红母牛。

这只母牛不是在帐幕里宰杀的——是在营外。祭司以利亚撒（不是亚伦，注意）用指头蘸血向会幕前面弹七次——然后整只牛连皮带肉带血带粪全部焚烧，同时把香柏木、牛膝草和朱红色线丢进火中。

弟兄姐妹，"全部焚烧"——这不是普通的燔祭（燔祭不烧皮和粪）。红母牛是彻底的、完全的焚烧——什么都不留。香柏木（最高的树）、牛膝草（最矮的草）和朱红色线（红色/血的颜色）一同投入——从最高到最低，从植物到颜料，全部归于灰烬。

烧完之后——灰被收集起来，存在营外洁净的地方。这灰与"活水"（流动的泉水，不是死水）混合，制成"除污秽的水"（mei niddah）。

**除污秽水的用途（11-22节）**

这水的唯一用途——洁净因接触死尸而不洁净的人。

> "摸了人死尸的，就必七天不洁净。那人到第三天要用这除污秽的水洁净自己，第七天就洁净了。"

弟兄姐妹，死亡是最高级别的不洁——不是道德上的不洁，而是礼仪上的不洁。在旷野中，以色列人不断有人死去——特别是神宣布那一代人要死在旷野之后（第十四章），死亡变得日常化。每一次有人死了，接触过死尸的人（家人、抬棺者）就会变得不洁净。不洁净的人不能进入圣所、不能参加节期、不能靠近会幕——他们在礼仪上与神隔绝。

除污秽的水提供了恢复的途径——第三天和第七天由一个洁净的人用牛膝草蘸水洒在不洁净的人身上，第七天就洁净了。

**最大的悖论**

弟兄姐妹，红母牛条例最令人困惑的地方——是它的悖论性：

洁净不洁净之人的灰——使制备它的所有人变得不洁净。宰牛的人不洁净、烧牛的人不洁净、收灰的人不洁净、洒水的人也不洁净（直到晚上）。使别人洁净的东西——让自己变得不洁净。

弟兄姐妹，这个悖论是犹太拉比千年来冥思苦想的——为什么洁净别人的东西会玷污自己？米德拉什说连摩西都不明白。但我相信，这个悖论在基督里得到了最深刻的解答——基督是那只"没有残疾、未曾负轭、纯红的母牛"——祂洁净了所有人，代价是祂自己"成为罪"（林后5:21）。使我们洁净的那一位——自己承担了我们的不洁。

弟兄姐妹，如果你不洁净——接触过死亡的阴影、罪的后果、世界的污染——红母牛的灰和活水为你预备了洁净。你不需要永远停留在不洁中——有恢复的路。"若第三天不洁净自己，第七天就不洁净了"——弟兄姐妹，拖延洁净只会让不洁持续更久。当恢复的途径已经为你预备好了——赶快去用它。

"凡摸了人死尸、不洁净自己的，就玷污了耶和华的帐幕，这人必从以色列中剪除。"弟兄姐妹，拒绝洁净——不是无辜的忽略，而是对神洁净途径的藐视。你不洁净不是你的错——但有了洁净的途径却不使用，那就是你的选择，你的叛逆。

弟兄姐妹，红母牛条例在四十年旷野漂泊中至关重要——因为每天都有人在死去。第十四章宣布那一代人要死在旷野——六十万成年男性加上他们的妻子，在四十年内陆续死亡。每一天、每一个营地、每一个家庭都在经历死亡。接触死尸变得不可避免——你的父亲死了、你的兄弟死了、你的邻居死了。如果没有红母牛的灰——整个以色列将很快变成一个全体不洁净、无人能敬拜神的社区。红母牛条例是旷野中维持敬拜的生命线。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 19 addresses the red heifer ordinance—one of Israeli law's most mysterious and perplexing rituals. Jewish tradition says even Solomon—the world's wisest king—could not fully understand the red heifer's meaning. But let me try to unlock this mystery for you.

**The Red Heifer Ordinance (vv. 1-10)**

> "Tell the people of Israel to bring you a red heifer without defect, in which there is no blemish, and on which a yoke has never come."

Brothers and sisters, this heifer's requirements were extremely strict: (1) completely red—tradition says no more than two non-red hairs; (2) without defect—perfectly flawless; (3) never yoked—never used for labor. Finding such an animal was nearly impossible—Jewish tradition records that from Moses to the Second Temple's destruction (70 AD), only nine red heifers were ever prepared in all Israelite history.

This heifer was not slaughtered in the tabernacle—but outside the camp. The priest Eleazar (not Aaron, note) sprinkled its blood seven times toward the tabernacle entrance—then the entire cow, skin, flesh, blood, dung, was completely burned, with cedar wood, hyssop, and scarlet yarn thrown into the fire.

Brothers and sisters, "completely burned"—this was not an ordinary burnt offering (which did not burn skin and dung). The red heifer was a thorough, complete burning—nothing left. Cedar wood (the tallest tree), hyssop (the shortest plant), and scarlet yarn (the color of red/blood) together invested—from highest to lowest, from plant to pigment, all reduced to ashes.

After burning—ashes were collected, stored in a clean place outside camp. These ashes mixed with "living water" (flowing spring water, not stagnant) produced "water for impurity" (mei niddah).

**The Purification Water's Use (vv. 11-22)**

This water had one purpose—purifying those made unclean by contact with a corpse.

> "Whoever touches the dead body of any person shall be unclean seven days. He shall cleanse himself with the water on the third day and on the seventh day, and so be clean."

Brothers and sisters, death was the highest level of uncleanness—not moral but ritual. In the wilderness, Israelites were constantly dying—especially after God declared that generation would die in the wilderness (chapter 14), death became routine. Every time someone died, those who contacted the corpse (family, pallbearers) became unclean. The unclean could not enter the sanctuary, could not participate in festivals, could not approach the tabernacle—they were ritually separated from God.

The purification water provided a restoration path—on the third and seventh days, a clean person using hyssop sprinkled the water on the unclean person; by the seventh day they were clean.

**The Greatest Paradox**

Brothers and sisters, the red heifer ordinance's most puzzling aspect—its paradoxical nature:

The ashes that purified the unclean—made everyone who prepared them unclean. The one who slaughtered was unclean, the burner unclean, the ash gatherer unclean, the sprinkler also unclean (until evening). What made others clean—made oneself unclean.

Brothers and sisters, this paradox has occupied Jewish rabbis for millennia—why does what purifies others defile oneself? The Midrash says even Moses did not understand. But I believe this paradox finds its deepest answer in Christ—Christ was that "red heifer without defect, never yoked"—He purified everyone, at the cost of Himself "For he hath made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in him." (2 Cor 5:21). The One who made us clean—bore our uncleanness.

Brothers and sisters, if you are unclean—having contacted death's shadow, sin's consequences, the world's contamination—the red heifer's ashes and living water have prepared your purification. You need not remain unclean forever—there is a restoration path. "If he does not cleanse himself on the third day, on the seventh day he will not become clean"—brothers and sisters, delaying purification only prolongs uncleanness. When the restoration path has been prepared for you—go use it quickly.

"Whoever touches a dead person and does not cleanse himself defiles the tabernacle of the LORD, and that person shall be cut off." Brothers and sisters, refusing purification is not innocent neglect but contempt for God's purification pathway. Being unclean is not your fault—but having the purification path yet refusing to use it is your choice, your rebellion.

## 历史背景（中文）

红母牛条例（Parah Adumah）在犹太传统中被视为"律法中最大的奥秘"——米示拿专门有一篇"Parah"（母牛篇）讨论此条例。塔木德记载犹太历史上只制备了九只红母牛（第一只由以利亚撒在摩西时代制备，最后一只在第二圣殿时期）。至今以色列有些宗教团体仍在寻找符合条件的红母牛——因为他们相信第三圣殿的重建需要第十只红母牛来恢复洁净礼。"牛膝草"（ezov）在以色列是常见的草本植物——出埃及记12:22以色列人用牛膝草蘸血涂在门框上（逾越节），利未记14:4-6用于痲疯洁净礼。牛膝草从低处生长——象征谦卑和洁净。"活水"（mayim chayyim）在犹太洁净律法中有特殊地位——只有流动的泉水或雨水才算"活水"，死水（池塘、水缸里的水）不够。

## Historical Context (English)

The red heifer ordinance (Parah Adumah) was considered in Jewish tradition "the law's greatest mystery"—the Mishnah has a dedicated tractate "Parah" discussing it. The Talmud records only nine red heifers prepared in all Jewish history (the first by Eleazar in Moses' era, the last during the Second Temple period). Today some Israeli religious groups still search for qualifying red heifers—believing the Third Temple's rebuilding requires a tenth red heifer to restore the purification rite. "Hyssop" (ezov) was a common herb in Israel—Exodus 12:22 Israelites used hyssop to apply blood to doorframes (Passover); Leviticus 14:4-6 used in leprosy purification. Hyssop grows low—symbolizing humility and cleansing. "Living water" (mayim chayyim) held special status in Jewish purity law—only flowing spring water or rainwater qualified as "living water"; stagnant water (ponds, cisterns) was insufficient.

## 经文结构（中文）

**红母牛的条件和焚烧（1-6节）**——纯红无残疾未负轭→营外宰杀→以利亚撒弹血七次→全牛焚烧+香柏木牛膝草朱红线。

**制备者的不洁（7-10节）**——祭司不洁到晚上、烧牛者不洁、收灰者不洁→灰存营外→"除罪的"→永远定例。

**接触死尸者的洁净（11-19节）**——摸死尸七天不洁→第三天第七天洒除污秽水→洁净人用牛膝草蘸水洒→不洁净自己者被剪除。

**不洁的传染性（20-22节）**——拒绝洁净者玷污帐幕→不洁人摸的一切物不洁净→摸这物的人也不洁到晚上。

## Structure (English)

**Red Heifer Requirements and Burning (vv. 1-6)**—Completely red, without defect, never yoked → slaughtered outside camp → Eleazar sprinkles blood seven times → entire cow burned + cedar wood hyssop scarlet yarn.

**Preparers' Uncleanness (vv. 7-10)**—Priest unclean until evening, burner unclean, ash gatherer unclean → ashes stored outside camp → "for purification" → perpetual statute.

**Purification for Corpse Contact (vv. 11-19)**—Touch corpse: seven days unclean → third and seventh day sprinkle purification water → clean person uses hyssop to sprinkle → refusing purification means being cut off.

**Uncleanness's Contagion (vv. 20-22)**—Refusing purification defiles the tabernacle → everything the unclean touches becomes unclean → whoever touches those things unclean until evening.

## 神学要点（中文）

**最大的悖论——洁净他人却玷污自己**
红母牛使不洁者洁净、使洁净者不洁——改革宗传统看到这里有替代性赎罪的深刻模式。韦敏斯德信条第8章论基督为中保。哥林多后书5:21"神使那无罪的，替我们成为罪，好叫我们在他里面成为神的义。"洁净的代价是自己变得不洁——基督洁净我们的代价是祂自己"成为罪"。

**死亡的不洁——罪的后果**
接触死尸是最高级不洁——改革宗传统教导死亡是罪的后果。韦敏斯德小要理问答第19问"全人类由于堕落，丧失了与神之间的相交，反落在神的忿怒和咒诅之下，要受今生的一切愁苦悲惨，就是要死亡，并且永远在地狱受苦。"死亡不是自然的——它是罪进入世界的结果（创2:17"你必定死"，罗5:12"死就临到众人"）。接触死亡让你"不洁净"——因为死亡本身就是罪的诅咒的可见表征。

**拒绝洁净比不洁净更严重**
不洁净可被洁净，拒绝洁净被剪除——改革宗传统教导拒绝恩典比犯罪更严重。希伯来书10:29"何况人践踏神的儿子……就是亵慢施恩的圣灵，你们想，他要受的刑罚该怎样加重呢？"

## Theology (English)

**The Greatest Paradox—Purifying Others Yet Defiling Oneself**
The red heifer made the unclean clean and the clean unclean—the Reformed tradition sees here a deep pattern of substitutionary atonement. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ as Mediator. 2 Corinthians 5:21: "For our sake he made him to be sin who knew no sin, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God." Purification's cost was becoming unclean oneself—Christ's cost of purifying us was Himself "becoming sin."

**Death's Uncleanness—Sin's Consequence**
Corpse contact was the highest uncleanness—the Reformed tradition teaches death is sin's consequence. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q19: "The punishment of sin is all miseries in this life, death itself, and the pains of hell forever." Death is not natural—it resulted from sin entering the world (Gen 2:17 "you shall surely die," Rom 5:12 "death spread to all men"). Contacting death made you "unclean"—because death itself was sin's curse made visible.

**Refusing Purification Worse Than Uncleanness**
Uncleanness could be purified; refusing purification meant being cut off—the Reformed tradition teaches rejecting grace is more serious than sinning. Hebrews 10:29: "How much worse punishment do you think will be deserved by the one who has trampled underfoot the Son of God...and has outraged the Spirit of grace?"

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第十九章的红母牛是基督最深刻的旧约预表之一。

**红母牛——基督**
希伯来书9:13-14"若山羊和公牛的血，并母牛犊的灰，洒在不洁的人身上，尚且叫人成圣，身体洁净，何况基督藉着永远的灵，将自己无瑕无疵献给神，他的血岂不更能洗净你们的心，除去你们的死行，使你们事奉那永生神吗？"

弟兄姐妹，希伯来书直接把红母牛的灰与基督的血对比。红母牛的灰只能洁净外在的礼仪不洁——基督的血洁净内心的"死行"。红母牛需要一次又一次地制备——基督"一次献祭，便叫那得以成圣的人永远完全"（来10:14）。

**营外焚烧——基督在城外受死**
红母牛在营外被宰杀和焚烧——希伯来书13:11-12"所以耶稣要用自己的血叫百姓成圣，也在城门外受苦。"营外是不洁净的地方——基督去了不洁之地，为我们受苦。

**活水——圣灵**
红母牛的灰混合"活水"——约翰福音7:38-39"信我的人……从他腹中要流出活水的江河来。耶稣这话是指着信他之人要受圣灵说的。"基督的赎罪（灰=十字架的功效）加上圣灵（活水）——这两者结合，才完成完整的洁净。十字架没有圣灵是死的历史知识——圣灵没有十字架是没有根基的情感——两者结合才是完整的救赎。

**悖论的解答——无罪者成为罪**
为什么洁净别人的会玷污自己？因为洁净的代价是承担不洁。基督"替我们成为罪"——祂洁净了我们，代价是祂自己承担了罪的一切后果。这就是红母牛悖论千年之谜的答案：替代性赎罪。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 19's red heifer is one of the most profound Old Testament prefigurations of Christ.

**The Red Heifer—Christ**
Hebrews 9:13-14: "For if the blood of goats and bulls, and the sprinkling of defiled persons with the ashes of a heifer, sanctify for the purification of the flesh, how much more will the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without blemish to God, purify our conscience from dead works to serve the living God?"

Brothers and sisters, Hebrews directly compared red heifer ashes with Christ's blood. The ashes purified only external ritual uncleanness—Christ's blood purifies the heart's "dead works." The red heifer needed repeated preparation—Christ "For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified." (Heb 10:14).

**Burned Outside the Camp—Christ Died Outside the City**
The red heifer was slaughtered and burned outside the camp—Hebrews 13:11-12: "So Jesus also suffered outside the gate in order to sanctify the people through his own blood." Outside the camp was the unclean place—Christ went to the unclean place, suffering for us.

**Living Water—The Holy Spirit**
Red heifer ashes mixed with "living water"—John 7:38-39: "Whoever believes in me...out of his heart will flow rivers of living water. Now this he said about the Spirit." Christ's atonement (ashes = the cross's efficacy) plus the Spirit (living water)—these two together complete full purification. The cross without the Spirit is dead historical knowledge—the Spirit without the cross is rootless emotion—together they are complete redemption.

**The Paradox Answered—The Sinless Becoming Sin**
Why did purifying others defile oneself? Because purification's cost was bearing uncleanness. Christ "became sin for us"—He purified us at the cost of bearing sin's every consequence. This is the answer to the red heifer paradox's thousand-year mystery: substitutionary atonement.
