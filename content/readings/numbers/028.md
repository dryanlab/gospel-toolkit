## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十八章（连同第二十九章）是以色列年历节期祭祀的完整日程表——从每天的常祭，到每周安息日，到每月月朔，再到逾越节和五旬节。表面上看这是一章枯燥的礼仪条例，但弟兄姐妹，如果你明白了它的内在含义，你会发现这是关于"不断与神相遇"的一首诗。

**常祭：每天两次（1-8节）**

"你要吩咐以色列人说：献给我的供物……要按日期献给我。"然后是每天的规定：早晨一只公羊羔、黄昏一只公羊羔，配上素祭（调油细面）和奠祭（醇酒）。

弟兄姐妹，这是"tamid"——"常献的燔祭"，意思是"持续的、不间断的"。无论发生什么——以色列在旷野流浪也好，正在打仗也好，遭遇瘟疫也好——每天早晨和黄昏的祭不能停。

为什么是早晨和黄昏？弟兄姐妹，每一天的开始和结束都以献祭来标志——把每一天的头和尾都交给神。早晨的祭说"今天这一天从神开始"，黄昏的祭说"这一天在神面前结束"。整个人生不就是由这样的早晨和黄昏拼成的吗？神要以色列人——每一天——都活在祂的面前。

詩篇141:2"愿我的祷告如香陈在你面前，我举手祈求如献晚祭。"祷告如早晚的香——新约信徒的"常祭"是不间断的祈祷和敬拜。帖撒罗尼迦前书5:17"不住地祷告。"

**安息日祭：双倍（9-10节）**

安息日另加两只羊羔——加上常祭就是四只，比平日双倍。安息日是更多的祭，不是更少的祭——安息日的祭告诉我们：安息日是敬拜的高峰，不是宗教的空缺。安息日不是"不工作的一天"——是"特别与神相遇的一天"。

**月朔祭：新月（11-15节）**

每个月的第一天——两只公牛犊、一只公绵羊、七只羊羔、一只公山羊（赎罪祭）。月朔祭提醒以色列人：每一个新月的到来都是时间的恩赐——用献祭来迎接新的时间段。弟兄姐妹，你的每个月有没有一个向神交出的时刻？每一天、每一周、每一个月——节奏性的奉献将时间的每个单位都标记为圣洁的。

**逾越节和无酵节（16-25节）**

正月十四日是逾越节——正月十五日开始七天无酵节，第一天和第七天有圣会，中间每天献额外的祭（两只公牛、一只公羊、七只羊羔、一只山羊）。

弟兄姐妹，逾越节是以色列历法的核心——出埃及的纪念。每一年，当你杀逾越节的羊羔、吃无酵饼和苦菜的时候，你重新经历了那个出埃及之夜。历史不是遥远的过去——它是每年一次被重新活出来的现实。弟兄姐妹，神的救恩不是只发生一次的事件——每一代人都要把它重新接受为自己的故事。

无酵饼七天——出埃及的仓促（没有时间等面发酵）。七天不碰酵——在内在里清除一切发酵物（骄傲、罪、旧生命的影响）。

**七七节（初熟节/五旬节，26-31节）**

"七七节庄稼初熟，你们献新素祭给耶和华的日子，当有圣会。"七七节——逾越节后数五十天（Pentecost）。这是初熟之物献给神的时刻——第一批收割的果实属于神。

弟兄姐妹，为什么这些节期的祭祀如此详细、如此繁多？在进迦南之前，神要以色列人把整个年历的节奏都建立在献祭和敬拜上——每天、每周、每月、每个大节期都有专门的祭。以色列的时间不是中性的——它是圣洁的、被神标记的时间。

弟兄姐妹，新约信徒活在不同的节奏里——我们没有每天的羊羔，但我们有每天的祷告；我们没有月朔祭，但我们有主日的敬拜；我们没有逾越节，但我们有主的晚餐——"你们每逢吃这饼，喝这杯，是表明主的死，直等到他来"（林前11:26）。

旧约的祭祀日历是新约灵修节奏的蓝图——不是负担，而是框架。神在说："让每一个时间单位都成为与我相遇的机会。"

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 28 (along with chapter 29) was Israel's annual festival sacrifice schedule—from daily regular offerings, to weekly Sabbath, to monthly New Moon, to Passover and Pentecost. On the surface this seems a dry chapter of ritual regulations, but brothers and sisters, if you understand its inner meaning, you'll find it is a poem about "continuous encounter with God."

**Regular Offering: Twice Daily (vv. 1-8)**

"Command the people of Israel and say to them, 'My offering, my food for my food offerings...you shall be careful to offer to me at its appointed time.'" Then the daily regulation: one lamb in the morning, one lamb at twilight, with grain offering (fine flour mixed with oil) and drink offering (wine).

Brothers and sisters, this was "tamid"—"the regular burnt offering," meaning "continuous, uninterrupted." No matter what—Israel wandering in the wilderness, fighting battles, experiencing plagues—the morning and evening offerings could not stop.

Why morning and evening? Brothers and sisters, each day's beginning and end were marked by sacrifice—giving each day's head and tail to God. The morning offering said "today begins with God"; the evening offering said "this day ends before God." Isn't all of human life assembled from such mornings and evenings? God wanted Israel—every day—to live in His presence.

Psalm 141:2: "Let my prayer be counted as incense before you, and the lifting up of my hands as the evening sacrifice!" Prayer like morning and evening incense—New Testament believers' "tamid" is unceasing prayer and worship. 1 Thessalonians 5:17: "Pray without ceasing."

**Sabbath Offering: Double (vv. 9-10)**

The Sabbath required two additional lambs—plus the regular offering that's four, double the weekday. The Sabbath had more offerings, not fewer—the Sabbath sacrifice tells us: the Sabbath was worship's peak, not religion's absence. The Sabbath was not "a day without work"—but "a day of special encounter with God."

**New Moon Offering (vv. 11-15)**

Each month's first day—two young bulls, one ram, seven lambs, one male goat (sin offering). The New Moon offering reminded Israel: each new month's arrival was a gift of time—receive new time periods with sacrifice. Brothers and sisters, does each of your months have a moment surrendered to God? Every day, every week, every month—rhythmic offerings mark each time unit as holy.

**Passover and Unleavened Bread (vv. 16-25)**

The fourteenth day of the first month was Passover—beginning the fifteenth, seven days of unleavened bread, with holy convocations on the first and seventh days, additional daily offerings throughout (two bulls, one ram, seven lambs, one goat).

Brothers and sisters, Passover was Israel's calendar's core—the Exodus memorial. Each year, when you slaughtered the Passover lamb, ate unleavened bread and bitter herbs, you re-experienced that Exodus night. History was not remote past—it was reality re-enacted annually. Brothers and sisters, God's salvation was not a once-only event—each generation must receive it anew as their own story.

Unleavened bread for seven days—the Exodus's haste (no time to wait for dough to rise). Seven days without leaven—internally removing all leavening (pride, sin, old life's influences).

**Festival of Weeks—Firstfruits/Pentecost (vv. 26-31)**

"On the day of the firstfruits, when you offer a grain offering of new grain to the LORD at your Festival of Weeks, you shall have a holy convocation." The Festival of Weeks—fifty days after Passover (Pentecost). This was the firstfruits' moment offered to God—the first harvested produce belonged to God.

Brothers and sisters, why were these festival offerings so detailed and numerous? Before entering Canaan, God wanted Israel to build the entire year's rhythm on sacrifice and worship—every day, every week, every month, every major festival had dedicated offerings. Israel's time was not neutral—it was holy, God-marked time.

Brothers and sisters, New Testament believers live in a different rhythm—we have no daily lamb, but we have daily prayer; no New Moon sacrifice, but Sunday worship; no Passover, but the Lord's Supper—"For as often as ye eat this bread, and drink this cup, ye do shew the Lord’s death till he come." (1 Cor 11:26).

The Old Testament sacrificial calendar was the blueprint for New Testament devotional rhythm—not a burden, but a framework. God was saying: "Let every time unit become an opportunity to meet with me."

## 历史背景（中文）

以色列的节期祭祀日历在古代近东是独一无二的——不是服务于农业或政治目的的宗教节庆，而是以历史性的神圣事件（出埃及、西奈山）为基础的纪念节期。常献的燔祭（tamid）对于圣殿的运作至关重要——每天早晚各一次的献祭在以色列历史中极少中断（马加比起义期间曾被中断，以及第二圣殿被毁时）。"tamid"这个概念后来发展成犹太每日祈祷（Shacharit=早祷，Mincha=午/下午祷，Maariv=晚祷）——以早晚祭为蓝本。五旬节（Pentecost）这个希腊名字意为"第五十天"——在新约中成为圣灵降临的日子（使2:1-4）。许多解经家看到逾越节（基督受死）→初熟节（基督复活）→五旬节（圣灵降临）的神学平行。

## Historical Context (English)

Israel's festival sacrifice calendar was unique in the ancient Near East—not religious celebrations serving agricultural or political purposes, but memorial festivals based on historic divine events (Exodus, Sinai). The regular burnt offering (tamid) was critical to temple operations—daily morning and evening sacrifices were rarely interrupted in Israelite history (interrupted during the Maccabean revolt and Second Temple's destruction). The "tamid" concept later developed into Jewish daily prayer (Shacharit=morning prayer, Mincha=afternoon prayer, Maariv=evening prayer)—modeled on morning and evening sacrifices. "Pentecost" (Greek for "fiftieth day") became in the New Testament the day of the Spirit's descent (Acts 2:1-4). Many commentators see the theological parallel: Passover (Christ's death) → Firstfruits (Christ's resurrection) → Pentecost (Holy Spirit's descent).

## 经文结构（中文）

**常祭（1-8节）**——每日早晚各一只羊羔+素祭+奠祭=tamid（持续不断）。

**安息日祭（9-10节）**——每安息日另加两只羊羔（双倍）。

**月朔祭（11-15节）**——每月朔：两公牛+一公羊+七羊羔+一山羊（赎罪）。

**逾越节和无酵节（16-25节）**——正月十四日逾越节；十五日起七天无酵节；第一天第七天圣会；每天额外两公牛+一公羊+七羊羔+一山羊。

**七七节/五旬节（26-31节）**——初熟之物新素祭；圣会；两公牛+一公羊+七羊羔+一山羊。

## Structure (English)

**Regular Offering (vv. 1-8)**—Daily morning and evening one lamb each + grain offering + drink offering = tamid (continuous, uninterrupted).

**Sabbath Offering (vv. 9-10)**—Each Sabbath: additional two lambs (double the weekday).

**New Moon Offering (vv. 11-15)**—Each New Moon: two young bulls + one ram + seven lambs + one goat (sin).

**Passover and Unleavened Bread (vv. 16-25)**—Month 1, day 14: Passover; day 15 through 7 days unleavened; first and seventh days: holy convocations; daily: two bulls + one ram + seven lambs + one goat.

**Festival of Weeks/Pentecost (vv. 26-31)**—Firstfruits new grain offering; holy convocation; two bulls + one ram + seven lambs + one goat.

## 神学要点（中文）

**时间的神圣化——节奏性敬拜**
整年的每个时间单位都有专属的献祭——改革宗传统教导时间属于神、应被圣化。韦敏斯德信条第21章论宗教崇拜，第22章论安息日。时间不是中性的材料——每个"今天"都是神赐予的礼物，应以感恩和献祭回应。韦敏斯德小要理问答第59问论安息日："从世界的起头直到基督复活之前，神是规定七日的第七日为安息日；基督复活之后，神就规定七日的头一日为基督徒的安息日，直到世界的末了。"

**tamid——不间断的献祭与不住地祷告**
常祭从不停止——改革宗传统教导不间断敬拜的精神。帖撒罗尼迦前书5:17"不住地祷告。"早晚祭的节奏是信仰生活的时间框架——每天都在祂面前开始，在祂面前结束。没有"不需要祈祷"的日子——tamid就是"总是、不间断地"。

**逾越节——救赎历史的年度重演**
以色列人每年过逾越节——改革宗传统教导圣礼是"可见的道"。韦敏斯德信条第27章论圣礼。重演不是戏剧表演——而是信仰团体对救恩历史的参与。每次擘饼、每次举杯——信徒参与了基督的死与复活。

## Theology (English)

**The Sanctification of Time—Rhythmic Worship**
Every time unit of the whole year had dedicated sacrifices—the Reformed tradition teaches time belongs to God and should be sanctified. Westminster Confession chapters 21-22 on religious worship and the Sabbath. Time is not neutral material—each "today" is God's given gift, to be answered with gratitude and sacrifice. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q60 on remembering the Sabbath: "Six days thou shalt labor and do all thy work; but the seventh day is the Sabbath of the Lord thy God."

**Tamid—Unceasing Sacrifice and Unceasing Prayer**
The regular offering never stopped—the Reformed tradition teaches the spirit of continuous worship. 1 Thessalonians 5:17: "Pray without ceasing." Morning and evening sacrifices' rhythm was faith life's time framework—every day beginning and ending in His presence. There is no day "without needing to pray"—tamid means "always, without interruption."

**Passover—Annual Re-enactment of Redemption History**
Israel observed Passover annually—the Reformed tradition teaches sacraments are "visible words." Westminster Confession chapter 27 on sacraments. Re-enactment is not theatrical performance—but the faith community's participation in salvation history. Each breaking of bread, each cup raised—believers participate in Christ's death and resurrection.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第二十八章的节期在新约中有完美的成全。

**常祭的早晚羊羔——基督是永远的祭**
每天早晨一只羊羔、黄昏一只——希伯来书10:1"律法既是将来美事的影儿，不是本物的真像，总不能藉着每年常献一样的祭物，叫那近前来的人得以完全。"常祭每天重复——提醒了自身的不足：需要不断重复才能维持。基督"一次献祭，便叫那得以成圣的人永远完全"（来10:14）。tamid（常祭）指向了那个不再需要重复的、一次成就的祭——基督自己。

**逾越节——基督是我们的逾越节**
哥林多前书5:7"我们逾越节的羔羊基督已经被杀献祭了。"每年一次的逾越节羔羊——指向了一次成就的那只羔羊：启示录5:12"曾被杀的羔羊是配得权柄、丰富、智慧、能力、尊贵、荣耀、颂赞的。"逾越节的血涂在门楣上保护以色列——基督的血涂在信徒心上保护他们脱离神的审判。

**五旬节——圣灵的降临**
五旬节是初熟之物节——使徒行传2:1-4"五旬节到了，门徒都聚集在一处，忽然从天上有响声下来……圣灵充满了他们。"五旬节的"初熟之物"是圣灵——圣灵是新创造的"初熟之物"（罗8:23"我们这有圣灵初熟之果子的，也是自己心里叹息，等候得着儿子的名分，乃是我们的身体得赎"）。节期的完全成就是逾越节（十字架）→初熟节（复活）→五旬节（圣灵降临）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 28's festivals find perfect fulfillment in the New Testament.

**Morning and Evening Tamid Lambs—Christ Is the Eternal Sacrifice**
One lamb each morning and evening—Hebrews 10:1: "For since the law has but a shadow of the good things to come instead of the true form of these realities, it can never, by the same sacrifices that are continually offered every year, make perfect those who draw near." The regular offering repeated daily—reminding of its own insufficiency: needing constant repetition to maintain. Christ "For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified." (Heb 10:14). Tamid (regular offering) pointed to that once-and-for-all sacrifice no longer needing repetition—Christ Himself.

**Passover—Christ Is Our Passover**
1 Corinthians 5:7: "For Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed." The annual Passover lamb—pointing to the once-for-all lamb: Revelation 5:12: "Worthy is the Lamb who was slain, to receive power and wealth and wisdom and might and honor and glory and blessing!" The Passover blood on doorframes protected Israel—Christ's blood on believers' hearts protects them from God's judgment.

**Pentecost—The Holy Spirit's Descent**
Pentecost was the firstfruits festival—Acts 2:1-4: "When the day of Pentecost arrived, they were all together in one place...they were all filled with the Holy Spirit." Pentecost's "firstfruits" was the Holy Spirit—the Spirit is the "firstfruits" of new creation (Rom 8:23: "We ourselves, who have the firstfruits of the Spirit, groan inwardly as we wait eagerly for adoption as sons"). The festivals' complete fulfillment: Passover (cross) → Firstfruits (resurrection) → Pentecost (Holy Spirit's descent).
