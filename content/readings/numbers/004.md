## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第四章是关于利未三族搬运会幕的详细规程——当以色列营起行时，谁负责拆什么、包什么、搬什么。这一章看起来是行政细节——但它隐藏着生死攸关的神学原则。

第四章的数点标准与第三章不同——第三章数"一个月以上的男子"（确认身份），第四章数"三十岁直到五十岁"的人（确认工作资格）。弟兄姐妹，服事神的工作有年龄限制——三十岁开始（足够成熟），五十岁结束（体力仍够）。这二十年是利未人的正式服役期。

首先是哥辖家族（1-20节）——他们负责搬运至圣之物：约柜、陈设饼桌子、金灯台、金香坛、燔祭坛。但这里有一条极其严格的规定：

> "将要起营的时候，亚伦和他儿子要进去摘下遮掩柜的幔子，用以蒙盖法柜。"

弟兄姐妹，哥辖人不能直接看到或触碰圣物——必须先由亚伦和祭司们进去，用特定的布料和海狗皮把每一件圣物包好、遮盖好、穿上杠——然后哥辖人才能进来搬运。他们搬的是一个一个包好的"包裹"——里面装的是什么，他们不能亲眼看到。

> "只是不可摸圣物，免得他们死亡。"
> "只是他们连片时不可进去观看圣所，免得他们死亡。"

弟兄姐妹，这里两次出现"免得他们死亡"——看一眼就会死，摸一下就会死。为什么？因为这些圣物——特别是约柜——代表了神在地上的宝座。约柜上方是施恩座，是神的荣光显现的地方。凡人直接面对神的荣光——必死无疑。出埃及记33:20神对摩西说"你不能看见我的面，因为人见我的面不能存活"。

哥辖人搬运圣物是用杠扛在肩上——不是用车拉。弟兄姐妹，后来大卫用车运约柜（撒下6:3），结果乌撒伸手扶柜被击杀——就是因为违反了民数记4章的规定。约柜必须用杠抬，不能用车拉。

每件圣物的包裹颜色不同：
约柜——内层蓝色毯子，外层海狗皮（到达目的地后，蓝色朝外）
陈设饼桌子——蓝色毯子+朱红色毯子+海狗皮
金灯台——蓝色毯子+海狗皮
金香坛——蓝色毯子+海狗皮
燔祭坛——紫色毯子+海狗皮

弟兄姐妹，蓝色（tekhelet）在以色列是神圣的颜色——代表天空和神的临在。约柜是唯一外层覆盖蓝色的——当以色列行军时，人们从远处只能看到一个蓝色的包裹在利未人肩上——那就是神的同在在行走。

然后是革顺家族（21-28节）——负责搬运会幕的布幕、罩棚、门帘、院子的帷子和绳子。他们可以用车运（民7:7给了两辆车），因为这些不是至圣之物——是布料和绳索。

米拉利家族（29-33节）——负责搬运帐幕的板、闩、柱子和带卯的座。最重的工作——木头和金属框架。他们得到四辆车（民7:8）——最多的运输资源。

弟兄姐妹，三族的工作加在一起——总计8,580人在服役期内。他们的分工精确到每一块板、每一根杠、每一张幔子由谁负责。"各人所当办的，所当抬的"——没有含糊的空间。

弟兄姐妹，第四章教导我们：服事神是有秩序的——不是想怎么做就怎么做。每个人有自己的职分，不可越界——哥辖人不能做革顺人的工作，革顺人不能碰哥辖人的圣物。这不是刻板——这是对神圣洁的尊重。当人们随意对待圣洁的事物时——死亡就是结果。

"这样待他们，好使他们活着，不至死亡"——弟兄姐妹，这些繁琐的规定不是为了为难利未人——而是为了保护他们的生命。圣洁是致命的——如果没有正确的程序和保护，接触圣物就会丧命。利未记和民数记一再教导我们：神的圣洁不是抽象的概念——它有实际的、致命的力量。但正因如此，神预备了保护的方式——包裹、遮盖、杠穿上——每一步都是恩典的安排。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 4 details the Levite clans' tabernacle transport procedures—when Israel broke camp, who disassembled what, wrapped what, carried what. This chapter appears to be administrative detail—but it conceals life-and-death theological principles.

Chapter 4's census criteria differed from chapter 3—chapter 3 counted "males from one month" (confirming identity); chapter 4 counted "from thirty years old up to fifty" (confirming work eligibility). Brothers and sisters, serving God had age limits—beginning at thirty (sufficiently mature), ending at fifty (still physically able). These twenty years were the Levites' active service period.

First, the Kohathites (vv. 1-20)—responsible for transporting the most holy objects: ark, showbread table, golden lampstand, golden incense altar, burnt offering altar. But here was an extremely strict regulation:

> "When the camp is to set out, Aaron and his sons shall go in and take down the veil of the screen and cover the ark of the testimony with it."

Brothers and sisters, Kohathites could not directly see or touch the holy objects—first Aaron and the priests had to enter, wrap each sacred item in specific fabrics and sea-cow skins, insert carrying poles—only then could Kohathites come in to carry. They carried individually wrapped "packages"—what was inside they could not see with their own eyes.

> "They must not touch the holy things, lest they die."
> "They must not go in to look on the holy things even for a moment, lest they die."

Brothers and sisters, "lest they die" appeared twice—one look meant death, one touch meant death. Why? Because these holy objects—especially the ark—represented God's earthly throne. Above the ark was the mercy seat where God's glory appeared. Mortals directly facing God's glory—certain death. Exodus 33:20: God told Moses, "You cannot see my face, for man shall not see me and live."

Kohathites carried holy objects on shoulder poles—not by cart. Brothers and sisters, David later used a cart for the ark (2 Sam 6:3), and Uzzah reached out to steady it and was struck dead—precisely for violating Numbers 4's regulations. The ark must be carried by poles, never carted.

Each holy object was wrapped in different colors:
Ark—inner blue cloth, outer sea-cow skin (at destination, blue facing out)
Showbread table—blue cloth + scarlet cloth + sea-cow skin
Golden lampstand—blue cloth + sea-cow skin
Golden incense altar—blue cloth + sea-cow skin
Burnt offering altar—purple cloth + sea-cow skin

Brothers and sisters, blue (tekhelet) was Israel's sacred color—representing sky and God's presence. The ark was the only item with blue as outermost covering—during march, people from afar could see only a blue-wrapped bundle on Levites' shoulders—that was God's presence in motion.

Then the Gershonites (vv. 21-28)—carrying tabernacle curtains, coverings, hangings, courtyard curtains and cords. They could use carts (Num 7:7 gave them two), since these were not most-holy objects—just fabrics and cords.

The Merarites (vv. 29-33)—carrying tabernacle frames, bars, pillars, and bases. The heaviest work—wood and metal framework. They received four carts (Num 7:8)—the most transport resources.

Brothers and sisters, all three clans' work totaled 8,580 men in active service. Their assignments were precise to every board, every pole, every curtain and who was responsible. "Each one with his task of serving or carrying"—no ambiguity.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 4 teaches: serving God has order—not doing whatever you please. Each person had their role, no boundary-crossing—Kohathites could not do Gershonites' work, Gershonites could not touch Kohathites' holy objects. This was not rigidity—it was respect for God's holiness. When people treated holy things casually—death resulted.

"This is how you shall deal with them, that they may live and not die"—Brothers and sisters, these elaborate regulations were not to burden Levites—but to protect their lives. Holiness is lethal—without proper procedures and protections, contact with holy things meant death. Leviticus and Numbers repeatedly teach: God's holiness is not abstract concept—it has real, lethal power.

## 历史背景（中文）

"三十岁到五十岁"的服役期限在后来有变化——历代志上23:24大卫把起始年龄降低到二十岁。"海狗皮"（tachash，和合本译法）的确切含义有争议——可能是海牛皮、海豹皮或某种珍贵的皮革。七十士译本译为"蓝色的"（hyacinthine），暗示它可能是一种蓝色的皮革。蓝色染料（tekhelet）在古代极其昂贵——提取自一种地中海海螺（murex trunculus）——每件蓝色织物都价值不菲。会幕搬运的实际困难不可低估——铜祭坛重约数百磅，帐幕的48块包金木板每块约75公斤（按照出26章的尺寸估算）。米拉利家族需要四辆牛车来运这些重物。以色列在旷野的四十年中搬了四十多次营（民33章列出了42站），每次都需要完整地拆卸和重建会幕。

## Historical Context (English)

The "thirty to fifty" service age later changed—1 Chronicles 23:24 recorded David lowering the starting age to twenty. "Sea-cow skin" (tachash, a debated translation) may have been dugong, seal, or another precious leather. The Septuagint translated it as "hyacinthine" (blue-colored), suggesting it may have been blue-dyed leather. Blue dye (tekhelet) was extraordinarily expensive in antiquity—extracted from a Mediterranean sea snail (murex trunculus)—each blue textile was highly valuable. Tabernacle transport's practical difficulty cannot be underestimated—the bronze altar weighed hundreds of pounds; the tabernacle's 48 gold-overlaid boards weighed approximately 75 kg each (estimated from Exodus 26's dimensions). Merarites needed four ox-carts for these heavy items. During Israel's forty wilderness years, they broke camp over forty times (Numbers 33 lists 42 stations), each time requiring complete tabernacle disassembly and reconstruction.

## 经文结构（中文）

**哥辖家族的职务（1-20节）**——数三十到五十岁、搬运至圣物（约柜桌子灯台坛）、祭司先包裹遮盖→哥辖人才搬、不可看不可摸免得死。

**革顺家族的职务（21-28节）**——搬帐幕罩棚门帘帷子绳子、在以他玛指挥下。

**米拉利家族的职务（29-33节）**——搬板闩柱子带卯的座、器具逐一指派。

**数点结果（34-49节）**——哥辖2,750+革顺2,630+米拉利3,200=总计8,580人。

## Structure (English)

**Kohathites' Duties (vv. 1-20)**—Count ages thirty to fifty, transport most holy objects (ark table lampstand altars), priests wrap and cover first → then Kohathites carry, must not look or touch lest they die.

**Gershonites' Duties (vv. 21-28)**—Carry tent coverings hangings curtains cords, under Ithamar's direction.

**Merarites' Duties (vv. 29-33)**—Carry frames bars pillars bases, equipment assigned item by item.

**Census Results (vv. 34-49)**—Kohath 2,750 + Gershon 2,630 + Merari 3,200 = total 8,580.

## 神学要点（中文）

**圣洁是致命的——需要中保**
"免得死亡"的反复警告——改革宗传统教导罪人不能直接面对神的圣洁。韦敏斯德信条第8章论基督为中保的必要性。利未人需要祭司先包好圣物才能搬运——我们需要基督作中保才能到神面前。没有中保的接触是致命的。

**各按其职——恩赐的多样性**
三个家族不同的工作——改革宗传统教导属灵恩赐的多样性。韦敏斯德大要理问答第159问论神话语的传讲。哥林多前书12:4-6"恩赐原有分别，圣灵却是一位。职事也有分别，主却是一位。"不是每个利未人都做一样的事——但每个人的工作都同样重要。

**蓝色的约柜——神的同在可见又不可见**
约柜被包裹但蓝色可见——改革宗传统教导神同时是隐藏的又是显明的（Deus absconditus et revelatus）。以赛亚书45:15"救主以色列的神啊，你实在是自隐的神。"神的荣光被遮盖（人不能直接看），但蓝色的标记可见（你知道祂在那里）。

## Theology (English)

**Holiness Is Lethal—A Mediator Is Needed**
Repeated "lest they die" warnings—the Reformed tradition teaches sinners cannot directly face God's holiness. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on the Mediator's necessity. Levites needed priests to wrap holy objects before transporting—we need Christ as Mediator to approach God. Unmediated contact is fatal.

**Each in Their Role—Diversity of Gifts**
Three clans with different work—the Reformed tradition teaches spiritual gifts' diversity. Westminster Larger Catechism Q159 on preaching the Word. 1 Corinthians 12:4-6: "There are varieties of gifts, but the same Spirit; varieties of service, but the same Lord." Not every Levite did the same thing—but each person's work was equally important.

**The Blue-Covered Ark—God's Presence Both Visible and Hidden**
The ark wrapped yet blue visible—the Reformed tradition teaches God is simultaneously hidden and revealed (Deus absconditus et revelatus). Isaiah 45:15: "Truly, you are a God who hides himself, O God of Israel, the Savior." God's glory was veiled (humans cannot look directly), yet the blue marker was visible (you know He is there).

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第四章的圣物搬运规程在基督里有深远的对应。

蓝色包裹的约柜——基督的神性被人性"包裹"。约翰福音1:14"道成了肉身，住在我们中间，充充满满地有恩典有真理。我们也见过他的荣光。"弟兄姐妹，基督的荣光在肉身中被"遮盖"了——但门徒仍然"见过他的荣光"。正如蓝色透过包裹仍然可见——基督的神性透过人性仍然显明。

"不可看不可摸免得死亡"——但基督说"你们到我这里来"。马太福音11:28"凡劳苦担重担的人可以到我这里来，我就使你们得安息。"旧约的圣物不可触碰——新约的基督邀请人触碰。路加福音8:44-46血漏妇人"摸了耶稣的衣裳繸子……耶稣说：有人摸我。"旧约摸圣物会死——新约摸基督得医治。改变的不是圣洁的标准——而是基督成了中保，使罪人可以安全地靠近神。

利未人用肩膀扛圣物——基督用肩膀背十字架。以赛亚书9:6"政权必担在他的肩头上。"路加福音23:26"西门……背着十字架跟随耶稣。"利未人把神的同在扛在肩上——基督把世人的罪扛在肩上。利未人的"扛"指向圣所——基督的"背"指向各各他。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 4's holy object transport regulations found profound counterparts in Christ.

The blue-wrapped ark—Christ's divinity "wrapped" in humanity. John 1:14: "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we have seen his glory, glory as of the only Son from the Father, full of grace and truth." Brothers and sisters, Christ's glory was "veiled" in flesh—yet disciples still "saw his glory." Just as blue showed through the wrapping—Christ's divinity showed through His humanity.

"Must not look or touch lest they die"—but Christ said "Come to me." Matthew 11:28: "Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest." Old Testament holy objects were untouchable—New Testament Christ invited touch. Luke 8:44-46, the bleeding woman "touched the fringe of his garment...Jesus said, 'Someone touched me.'" Old Testament: touching holy things brought death. New Testament: touching Christ brought healing. What changed was not holiness's standard—but Christ became Mediator, enabling sinners to safely approach God.

Levites carried holy objects on shoulders—Christ carried the cross on His shoulders. Isaiah 9:6: "The government shall be upon his shoulder." Luke 23:26: "Simon...carrying the cross behind Jesus." Levites bore God's presence on their shoulders—Christ bore the world's sin on His shoulders. Levites' "bearing" pointed to the sanctuary—Christ's "bearing" pointed to Golgotha.
