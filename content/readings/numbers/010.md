## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十章是以色列出发的时刻——从西奈山起行。前九章都是准备：数点人口、安排营地、组织利未人、守逾越节、观察云柱——现在，终于要出发了。

**银号（1-10节）**

> "你要用银子做两枝号，都要锤出来的。"

弟兄姐妹，号角在以色列的生活中有三个用途：(1)招聚会众——吹号全会众聚到会幕门口；(2)命令起行——吹出大声（teruah，猛烈的吹奏），营就按顺序出发；(3)战争和节期——在战场上和节日中吹号，"在你们的神面前作为纪念"。

注意：只有祭司才能吹号——"亚伦子孙作祭司的要吹这号。"弟兄姐妹，号令不是谁都能发的——只有被神授权的人才有权号令以色列。吹号是属灵领导权的象征。

两枝号，两种吹法：平吹（招聚）和猛吹（起行/争战）。吹一枝——首领聚集；吹两枝——全会众聚集。简单的信号系统，但在两百万人的营地中至关重要。

最美的应许在第9节——"在自己的地，与欺压你们的敌人打仗，就要用号吹出大声，便在耶和华你们的神面前得蒙纪念。"弟兄姐妹，"得蒙纪念"不是说神会忘记你——而是号声提醒以色列：你不是独自在战斗，有一位在天上的神听见你的号角。

**出发！（11-28节）**

> "第二年二月二十日，云彩从法柜的帐幕收上去。"

弟兄姐妹，这是以色列在西奈山停留了将近一年之后的出发——他们在西奈接受了律法（出19-24章）、建造了帐幕（出25-40章）、颁布了祭祀和洁净条例（利未记全书）、数点了人口和组织了利未人（民1-9章）。现在一切就绪——云柱起来了，出发！

> "以色列人就按站往前行，离开西奈的旷野，云彩停住在巴兰的旷野。"

行军顺序严格按照第二章的安排：犹大营先行（打头阵）→革顺和米拉利的利未人带着帐幕框架→流便营→哥辖的利未人扛着圣物→以法莲营→但营殿后。弟兄姐妹，帐幕的拆运安排得很巧妙——先走的革顺和米拉利带着帐幕框架和罩棚，后走的哥辖带着圣物。等哥辖人带着约柜和桌子到达新营地时，帐幕的框架已经搭好了——圣物可以直接放进去。

**何巴的邀请（29-32节）**

然后是一段出人意料的插曲——摩西邀请岳父的亲戚何巴同行。

> "我们要行路，往耶和华所应许之地去……现在求你和我们同去，我们必厚待你。"

弟兄姐妹，何巴是米甸人——不是以色列人。摩西为什么邀请他？"因为你知道我们要在旷野安营，你可以当作我们的眼目。"何巴是旷野的原住民——他熟悉地形、水源、路径。以色列有云柱引导没错——但神也使用人的智慧和经验作为实际的帮助。

何巴一开始拒绝——"我不去，我要回本地本族那里去。"但摩西坚持邀请他。后来的经文暗示何巴最终同行了——士师记1:16和4:11记载他的后代（基尼人）住在以色列中。弟兄姐妹，摩西对何巴的邀请是旧约中"呼召外邦人进入应许之福"的又一个实例——与第九章外邦人可守逾越节遥相呼应。

**约柜的祷告（33-36节）**

第十章以两句著名的祷告结束——当约柜出发和停下时摩西的宣告：

"耶和华啊，求你兴起！愿你的仇敌四散！愿恨你的人从你面前逃跑！"——出发时的祷告。
"耶和华啊，求你回到以色列的千万人中！"——停下时的祷告。

弟兄姐妹，出发时呼求神去打仗——停下时呼求神回来同住。神既是战士（出征时在前头）又是同住者（安营时在中间）。以色列不是在一个空旷的旷野中自己走路——神在前头带路，神在中间同行，神在后面保护。

弟兄姐妹，第十章是激动人心的——以色列终于出发了！一年的准备完成了，两百多万人浩浩荡荡向应许之地进发。银号吹响，云柱前行，约柜引路，摩西祷告。这是信心的最高点之一——一切都按照神的计划进行。但弟兄姐妹——下一章就是抱怨。出发的热情总是容易的，路上的坚持才是真正的考验。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 10 is the moment Israel sets out—departing from Mount Sinai. The first nine chapters were all preparation: census, camp arrangement, Levite organization, Passover, cloud observation—now, at last, departure.

**The Silver Trumpets (vv. 1-10)**

> "Make two silver trumpets. Of hammered work you shall make them."

Brothers and sisters, trumpets in Israel's life served three purposes: (1) assembling the congregation—when blown, the whole community gathered at the tabernacle entrance; (2) commanding departure—with alarm blasts (teruah, intense blowing), camps departed in order; (3) battle and festivals—blown in warfare and celebrations, "a reminder before your God."

Note: only priests could blow the trumpets—"The sons of Aaron, the priests, shall blow the trumpets." Brothers and sisters, not anyone could issue commands—only God-authorized persons could order Israel. Trumpet-blowing symbolized spiritual leadership authority.

Two trumpets, two blowing styles: steady blow (assembly) and alarm blast (departure/battle). One trumpet—leaders gather; two—entire congregation gathers. A simple signal system, but vital in a camp of two million.

The most beautiful promise was verse 9—"When you go to war...you shall sound an alarm with the trumpets, that you may be remembered before the LORD your God." Brothers and sisters, "be remembered" did not mean God would forget—the trumpet sound reminded Israel: you do not fight alone; a God in heaven hears your trumpet.

**Departure! (vv. 11-28)**

> "In the second year, in the second month, on the twentieth day of the month, the cloud lifted from over the tabernacle of the testimony."

Brothers and sisters, this was Israel's departure after nearly a year at Sinai—receiving the law (Exod 19-24), building the tabernacle (Exod 25-40), receiving sacrificial and purity ordinances (all Leviticus), census and Levite organization (Num 1-9). Now everything was ready—the cloud rose, departure!

> "And the people of Israel set out...from the wilderness of Sinai. And the cloud settled down in the wilderness of Paran."

March order strictly followed chapter 2's arrangement: Judah's camp first (vanguard) → Gershonite and Merarite Levites with tabernacle framework → Reuben's camp → Kohathite Levites bearing holy objects → Ephraim's camp → Dan's camp rear guard. Brothers and sisters, the tabernacle transport was cleverly arranged—Gershonites and Merarites leading with framework and coverings, Kohathites following with sacred objects. By the time Kohathites arrived at the new campsite with ark and table, the framework was already erected—sacred objects could be placed directly inside.

**Hobab's Invitation (vv. 29-32)**

Then an unexpected episode—Moses invited his father-in-law's relative Hobab to join them.

> "We are setting out for the place of which the LORD said, 'I will give it to you.' Come with us, and we will do good to you."

Brothers and sisters, Hobab was a Midianite—not an Israelite. Why did Moses invite him? "For you know where we should camp in the wilderness, and you will serve as eyes for us." Hobab was a wilderness native—familiar with terrain, water sources, paths. Israel had the cloud pillar's guidance—but God also used human wisdom and experience as practical help.

Hobab initially refused—"I will not go. I will depart to my own land." But Moses persisted. Later texts suggest Hobab ultimately joined—Judges 1:16 and 4:11 record his descendants (Kenites) living among Israel. Brothers and sisters, Moses' invitation to Hobab was another Old Testament instance of "calling Gentiles into the promise's blessing"—echoing chapter 9's Gentile participation in Passover.

**The Ark's Prayer (vv. 33-36)**

Chapter 10 closed with two famous prayers—Moses' declarations when the ark set out and stopped:

"Arise, O LORD, and let your enemies be scattered, and let those who hate you flee before you!"—the departure prayer.
"Return, O LORD, to the ten thousands of Israel!"—the stopping prayer.

Brothers and sisters, when departing, calling God to fight; when stopping, calling God to dwell. God was both warrior (leading in advance) and companion (dwelling in their midst). Israel was not walking alone through an empty wilderness—God led ahead, walked alongside, and protected from behind.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 10 was exhilarating—Israel finally set out! A year of preparation complete, over two million marching magnificently toward the Promised Land. Silver trumpets sounding, cloud pillar advancing, ark leading, Moses praying. This was one of faith's highest points—everything proceeding according to God's plan. But brothers and sisters—the next chapter was complaints. Departure's enthusiasm is always easy; the journey's perseverance is the real test.

## 历史背景（中文）

"银号"（chatsotsrah）与"羊角号"（shofar）不同——银号是金属制的直管号角，类似于埃及墓葬壁画中的军号；shofar是弯曲的公羊角。银号用于日常的军事和仪式场合；shofar用于更庄严的宗教场合（如赎罪日和禧年的宣告）。提多凯旋门（公元82年）上的浮雕显示了罗马军队从耶路撒冷圣殿劫掠的器物——其中有一对银号（chatsotsrah），与民数记10章的描述一致。巴兰旷野（v.12）是西奈半岛东北部的大片荒原——以色列从西奈山向东北方向行进，经巴兰旷野前往迦南地南界。摩西的祷告"耶和华啊求你兴起"（v.35）后来被大卫在诗篇68:1引用——成为以色列出征时最经典的战争祷告。

## Historical Context (English)

"Silver trumpets" (chatsotsrah) differed from the "ram's horn" (shofar)—silver trumpets were straight metal tubes, similar to military horns depicted in Egyptian tomb paintings; the shofar was a curved ram's horn. Silver trumpets were for daily military and ceremonial occasions; shofar for more solemn religious occasions (such as Day of Atonement and Jubilee proclamations). The Arch of Titus (82 AD) relief shows Roman soldiers carrying plunder from Jerusalem's temple—including a pair of silver trumpets (chatsotsrah), consistent with Numbers 10's description. The wilderness of Paran (v. 12) was the vast wasteland in northeastern Sinai Peninsula—Israel marched northeast from Sinai, through Paran toward Canaan's southern border. Moses' prayer "Arise, O LORD" (v. 35) was later quoted by David in Psalm 68:1—becoming Israel's most classic battle prayer at departure.

## 经文结构（中文）

**银号的制作与用法（1-10节）**——两枝银号锤出、招聚会众/命令起行/争战节期三种功用、只有祭司吹号。

**从西奈出发（11-28节）**——二月二十日云起→犹大营先行→革顺米拉利带帐幕→流便营→哥辖带圣物→以法莲营→但营殿后。

**何巴的邀请（29-32节）**——摩西邀米甸人何巴同行当"眼目"、何巴初拒→摩西再邀。

**约柜的祷告（33-36节）**——出发祷告"求你兴起仇敌四散"→停下祷告"求你回到千万人中"。

## Structure (English)

**Silver Trumpets: Crafting and Usage (vv. 1-10)**—Two hammered silver trumpets, three functions: assembly/march/battle-festival, only priests blow.

**Departure from Sinai (vv. 11-28)**—Second month twentieth cloud lifts → Judah's camp first → Gershon/Merari with tabernacle → Reuben's camp → Kohath with holy objects → Ephraim's camp → Dan's camp rear guard.

**Hobab's Invitation (vv. 29-32)**—Moses invites Midianite Hobab as wilderness "eyes," Hobab initially refuses → Moses persists.

**The Ark's Prayer (vv. 33-36)**—Departure prayer: "Arise, let enemies scatter" → stopping prayer: "Return to the ten thousands."

## 神学要点（中文）

**号声——呼求神的记忆**
"在你们的神面前得蒙纪念"——改革宗传统教导祷告是蒙悦纳的。韦敏斯德小要理问答第98问论祷告："祷告是奉基督的名，向神祈求心中所愿而又合乎神旨意的事。"号声是以色列在战争和节期中向神发出的呼求——神听见号声并"纪念"祂的百姓。不是神会忘记——而是号声是盟约关系中的正式呼吁。

**云柱引导与人的智慧并用**
摩西请何巴当"眼目"——改革宗传统不反对使用次级原因（第二因）。韦敏斯德信条第5章论天意。神用云柱引导大方向——但也使用熟悉旷野的人来帮助实际行路。信靠神不等于否定人的智慧——而是在信靠神的框架内使用一切合法的手段。

**约柜的祷告——神既是战士又是同住者**
出发和安营时的双重祷告——改革宗传统教导神的全面属性。韦敏斯德小要理问答第4问："神是个灵，祂的本体、智慧、权能、圣洁、公义、恩慈、真实，都是无限无量、无始无终，永无改变的。"神不只在战争中保护你，也在平安中与你同住。祂不只是远方的将军——祂是"以色列千万人中"的同住者。以马内利——神与我们同在。

## Theology (English)

**Trumpet Sound—Calling Upon God's Remembrance**
"Be remembered before the LORD your God"—the Reformed tradition teaches prayer is received. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q98 on prayer. The trumpet was Israel's cry to God in battle and festival—God heard the trumpet and "remembered" His people. Not that God forgets—but the trumpet was a formal appeal within the covenant relationship.

**Cloud Pillar Guidance Alongside Human Wisdom**
Moses asked Hobab to serve as "eyes"—the Reformed tradition does not oppose secondary causes. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence. God guided the general direction with the cloud—but also used people familiar with the wilderness for practical travel help. Trusting God does not negate human wisdom—it uses all lawful means within the framework of trusting God.

**The Ark's Prayer—God as Both Warrior and Companion**
Dual prayers at departure and camping—the Reformed tradition teaches God's comprehensive attributes. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q4. God not only protects in battle but dwells with you in peace. He is not just a distant general—He is the One dwelling among "the ten thousands of Israel." Emmanuel—God with us.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第十章的三个画面在基督里有完美的对应。

**银号——福音的号角**
哥林多前书15:52"号筒末次吹响的时候，死人要复活成为不朽坏的。"以色列的银号招聚百姓——福音的号角招聚万民归基督。帖撒罗尼迦前书4:16"因为主必亲自从天降临，有呼叫的声音和天使长的声音，又有神的号吹响。"银号命令以色列起行——末日的号角命令死人复活。

**约柜在前行——基督为先锋**
"耶和华的约柜在前头行了三天的路程，为他们寻找安歇的地方。"弟兄姐妹，约柜在前面开路——基督在我们前面开路。希伯来书6:20"作先锋的耶稣，既照着麦基洗德的等次成了永远的大祭司，就为我们进入幔内。"约柜为以色列寻找安歇之处——基督为我们预备天上的居所。约翰福音14:2-3"我去原是为你们预备地方去……我必再来接你们到我那里去。"

**"求你兴起！"——基督的得胜**
摩西的战争祷告"求你兴起，愿你的仇敌四散"——歌罗西书2:15"既将一切执政的、掌权的掳来，明显给众人看，就仗着十字架夸胜。"基督在十字架上"兴起"——一切属灵的仇敌四散。"求你回到以色列的千万人中"——马太福音28:20"我就常与你们同在，直到世界的末了。"出征时祂争战——安营时祂同住。基督从不离开祂的百姓。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 10's three images find perfect counterparts in Christ.

**Silver Trumpets—The Gospel Trumpet**
1 Corinthians 15:52: "At the last trumpet...the dead will be raised imperishable." Israel's silver trumpets assembled the people—the gospel trumpet summons all nations to Christ. 1 Thessalonians 4:16: "For the Lord himself will descend from heaven with a cry of command, with the voice of an archangel, and with the sound of the trumpet of God." Silver trumpets commanded Israel to march—the final trumpet commands the dead to rise.

**The Ark Going Before—Christ as Forerunner**
"The ark of the covenant of the LORD went before them three days' journey, to seek out a resting place for them." Brothers and sisters, the ark went ahead opening the way—Christ goes ahead opening our way. Hebrews 6:20: "Jesus has gone as a forerunner on our behalf, having become a high priest forever after the order of Melchizedek." The ark sought Israel's resting place—Christ prepares our heavenly dwelling. John 14:2-3: "I go to prepare a place for you...I will come again and will take you to myself."

**"Arise!"—Christ's Victory**
Moses' battle prayer "Arise, O LORD, and let your enemies be scattered"—Colossians 2:15: "He disarmed the rulers and authorities and put them to open shame, by triumphing over them in the cross." Christ "arose" at the cross—all spiritual enemies scattered. "Return, O LORD, to the ten thousands"—Matthew 28:20: "I am with you always, to the end of the age." When marching He fights—when camping He dwells. Christ never leaves His people.
