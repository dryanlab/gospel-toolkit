## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第八章讲两件事：点灯台（1-4节）和利未人的洁净与就职（5-26节）。两件事看起来不相关——灯台是器具，利未人是人——但它们在神学上紧密相连：灯台照亮圣所，利未人在圣所中服事——光和人都要被洁净、被分别为圣。

**金灯台的点燃（1-4节）**

> "你告诉亚伦说：点灯的时候，七盏灯都要向灯台前面发光。"

弟兄姐妹，灯台放在圣所里——圣所没有窗户，没有自然光。如果灯台熄了，圣所就是完全的黑暗。亚伦每天早晚要点灯——保持圣所永远有光。

为什么七盏灯都要"向灯台前面发光"？灯台在圣所的南侧，光要照向北侧的陈设饼桌子——让祭司能看到桌上的饼。弟兄姐妹，光和饼在圣所里相对而立——光照亮饼，饼在光中被看见。后来基督说"我是世界的光"（约8:12）又说"我是生命的粮"（约6:35）——光和粮在基督里合一了。

"这灯台的作法是用金子锤出来的，连座带花都是锤出来的。"弟兄姐妹，灯台是一整块金子锤打成的——不是铸造的、不是焊接的，而是一块金子被锤打、弯曲、塑形而成。每一下锤打都是设计的一部分——美丽来自于击打。

**利未人的洁净与就职（5-26节）**

然后是利未人的就职典礼——这比祭司的就职简单一些（利8章），但仍然庄严。

洁净的程序："用除罪水弹在他们身上，又叫他们用剃头刀刮全身，洗衣服，洁净自己。"弟兄姐妹，三个步骤——除罪水弹洒（象征洁净）、剃全身（彻底去除旧的）、洗衣服（外在的洁净配合内在的洁净）。利未人要从头到脚被洁净——不留任何不洁之处。

然后是极其特别的仪式——"以色列人要按手在他们头上"。弟兄姐妹，不是祭司按手——是以色列全会众按手在利未人头上！这意味着什么？这是一个代表性的转移——以色列人把自己的身份和义务（长子归神的义务）转移到利未人身上。

"亚伦也将他们奉到耶和华面前，为以色列人当作摇祭。"弟兄姐妹，"摇祭"是祭司把供物在神面前摇动——象征奉献给神。利未人被当作"活的摇祭"——像牲口一样被奉献给神，但他们是活的人。弟兄姐妹，保罗在罗马书12:1说的"将身体献上当作活祭"——这个概念的旧约根源就在民数记8章。利未人是旧约的"活祭"。

然后利未人自己也要献祭——"利未人要按手在那两只牛的头上；你要将一只作赎罪祭，一只作燔祭。"弟兄姐妹，利未人虽然是替代长子的特别群体，但他们自己也是罪人——他们也需要赎罪祭和燔祭。替代者本身也需要被赎——因为人类的替代总是不完美的。只有基督——那位无罪的替代者——不需要为自己赎罪。

神再次解释利未人的身份："因为他们是从以色列人中全然给我的，我拣选他们归我，是代替以色列人中一切头生的。"弟兄姐妹，这是第三章替代原则的重述——利未人代替长子、归给神、给亚伦使用。

最后是服役年龄——"从二十五岁以外，他们要前来任职……到了五十岁要停工退任。"弟兄姐妹，注意：第四章说三十岁开始搬运圣物，这里说二十五岁开始服事。犹太传统解释说：二十五到三十岁是学徒期——在这五年里，利未人学习如何服事但不独立承担重物；三十岁才开始搬运最重的圣物。到五十岁退任——"不再办事"，但可以"和弟兄一同伺候，谨守所吩咐的"——退休的利未人不离开，他们成为年轻利未人的导师和顾问。

弟兄姐妹，第八章教导我们：服事神的人需要被洁净——不是你觉得自己干净就行，而是要经历一个仪式性的、公开的、由神规定的洁净过程。利未人不能跳过洁净直接开始工作——洁净是服事的前提。今天我们没有除罪水和剃头刀——但洗礼是新约的洁净仪式，也是委身服事的公开宣告。

弟兄姐妹，金灯台从一块金子锤打而成——利未人从以色列的普通人中被分别出来。灯台在圣所里发光——利未人在帐幕中服事。两者都要经过"锤打"和"洁净"才能发挥功用。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 8 addresses two matters: lighting the lampstand (vv. 1-4) and the Levites' purification and commissioning (vv. 5-26). These appear unrelated—a lampstand is furniture, Levites are people—but they are theologically connected: the lampstand illuminated the sanctuary, Levites served in it—both light and persons must be purified and consecrated.

**Lighting the Golden Lampstand (vv. 1-4)**

> "Speak to Aaron and say to him, When you set up the lamps, the seven lamps shall give light in front of the lampstand."

Brothers and sisters, the lampstand stood in the Holy Place—which had no windows, no natural light. If the lampstand went out, the sanctuary was absolute darkness. Aaron lit it morning and evening—keeping the sanctuary always illuminated.

Why must all seven lamps "give light in front of the lampstand"? The lampstand stood on the sanctuary's south side, its light directed northward toward the table of showbread—so priests could see the bread. Brothers and sisters, light and bread faced each other in the Holy Place—light illuminated bread, bread was visible in the light. Later Christ said "Then spake Jesus again unto them, saying, I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the light of life." (John 8:12) and "And Jesus said unto them, I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger; and he that believeth on me shall never thirst." (John 6:35)—light and bread became one in Christ.

"The lampstand was made of hammered gold, from its base to its flowers, it was hammered work." Brothers and sisters, the lampstand was hammered from one piece of gold—not cast, not welded, but one block of gold beaten, bent, shaped into form. Every hammer blow was part of the design—beauty came from being struck.

**Levite Purification and Commissioning (vv. 5-26)**

Then the Levites' commissioning ceremony—simpler than the priests' ordination (Lev 8), but still solemn.

Purification procedure: "Sprinkle the water of purification upon them, and let them go with a razor over all their body, and wash their clothes and cleanse themselves." Brothers and sisters, three steps—sprinkling purification water (symbolizing cleansing), shaving the entire body (thoroughly removing the old), washing clothes (outward cleanliness matching inward). Levites were cleansed head to toe—no unclean spot remaining.

Then the remarkable ceremony—"the people of Israel shall lay their hands on the Levites." Brothers and sisters, not priests laying hands—the entire Israelite congregation laying hands on Levites! What did this mean? A representative transfer—Israelites transferred their identity and obligation (the firstborn-belongs-to-God obligation) onto the Levites.

"Aaron shall offer the Levites before the LORD as a wave offering from the people of Israel." Brothers and sisters, the "wave offering" was when priests waved the offering before God—symbolizing dedication to God. Levites were presented as "living wave offerings"—dedicated to God like animals, but as living persons. Brothers and sisters, Paul's Romans 12:1 call to "present your bodies as a living sacrifice"—this concept's Old Testament root was Numbers 8. Levites were the Old Testament's "living sacrifices."

Then Levites themselves had to offer sacrifices—"the Levites shall lay their hands on the heads of the bulls, and you shall offer the one for a sin offering and the other for a burnt offering." Brothers and sisters, though Levites were the special group substituting for firstborn, they themselves were sinners—they too needed sin and burnt offerings. The substitutes themselves needed redemption—because human substitution is always imperfect. Only Christ—the sinless Substitute—needed no atonement for Himself.

God again explained the Levites' identity: "For they are wholly given to me from among the people of Israel. Instead of all who open the womb, the firstborn of all the people of Israel, I have taken them for myself." Brothers and sisters, this restated chapter 3's substitution principle—Levites replacing firstborn, belonging to God, given to Aaron.

Finally the service age—"from twenty-five years old and upward they shall come to do duty...at the age of fifty years they shall withdraw." Brothers and sisters, note: chapter 4 said thirty for carrying holy objects; here twenty-five for service. Jewish tradition explains: twenty-five to thirty was apprenticeship—five years learning to serve without independently bearing heavy objects; full carrying began at thirty. At fifty, retirement—"no longer serve," but "minister with their brothers...guarding"—retired Levites did not leave; they became mentors and advisors to younger Levites.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 8 teaches: those who serve God need purification—not self-assessed cleanliness, but a ritual, public, God-prescribed purification process. Levites could not skip purification and begin working—purification was service's prerequisite. Today we have no purification water or razors—but baptism is the New Testament's purification ceremony and public declaration of committed service.

Brothers and sisters, the golden lampstand hammered from one piece of gold—Levites separated from Israel's ordinary people. The lampstand shining in the sanctuary—Levites serving in the tabernacle. Both required "hammering" and "purification" before functioning.

## 历史背景（中文）

"二十五岁"与"三十岁"的差异（民8:24 vs 4:3）有几种解释：(1)塔木德认为25-30岁是培训期（Hullin 24a）；(2)大卫后来把起始年龄降到二十岁（代上23:24-27），因为不再需要搬运帐幕；(3)另一种解释认为4章的"三十岁"只适用于搬运最重的物件，其他较轻的工作从25岁开始。"除罪水"（mei niddah，直译"分别之水"）是用红母牛灰混合的水（民19章会详细说明）——这是以色列最神秘的洁净仪式之一。"摇祭"（tenufah）通常是将供物在祭司手中前后摇动——但对利未人显然不能真的"摇"人，犹太传统认为这是象征性的动作——祭司将利未人"呈现"在神面前。

## Historical Context (English)

The "twenty-five" versus "thirty" discrepancy (Num 8:24 vs 4:3) has several explanations: (1) the Talmud considers 25-30 a training period (Hullin 24a); (2) David later lowered the starting age to twenty (1 Chr 23:24-27), since tabernacle transport was no longer needed; (3) another explanation holds chapter 4's "thirty" applied only to carrying the heaviest objects, with lighter duties starting at 25. "Water of purification" (mei niddah, literally "water of separation") was water mixed with red heifer ashes (Numbers 19 will elaborate)—one of Israel's most mysterious purification rites. "Wave offering" (tenufah) normally involved waving the offering back and forth in the priest's hands—but Levites obviously could not be physically "waved"; Jewish tradition considers this a symbolic action—the priest "presenting" Levites before God.

## 经文结构（中文）

**金灯台的点燃（1-4节）**——七盏灯向灯台前面发光、灯台用金子锤出连座带花。

**利未人洁净的程序（5-7节）**——除罪水弹洒→剃全身→洗衣服。

**就职仪式（8-19节）**——取牛献素祭→全会众按手在利未人头上→亚伦当摇祭奉上→利未人按手在牛头上→一只赎罪祭一只燔祭→利未人代替长子归神。

**执行与回顾（20-22节）**——全会众和利未人照样行。

**服役年龄（23-26节）**——二十五岁任职、五十岁退任（仍可伺候谨守）。

## Structure (English)

**Lighting the Golden Lampstand (vv. 1-4)**—Seven lamps giving light toward the front, lampstand hammered from gold including base and flowers.

**Levite Purification Procedure (vv. 5-7)**—Sprinkle purification water → shave entire body → wash clothes.

**Commissioning Ceremony (vv. 8-19)**—Take bulls with grain offering → whole congregation lays hands on Levites → Aaron presents as wave offering → Levites lay hands on bulls → one sin offering one burnt offering → Levites substitute for firstborn belonging to God.

**Execution and Review (vv. 20-22)**—Congregation and Levites did accordingly.

**Service Age (vv. 23-26)**—Begin duty at twenty-five, retire at fifty (may still minister and guard).

## 神学要点（中文）

**洁净先于服事——称义先于成圣**
利未人必须先洁净再服事——改革宗传统教导称义是成圣的基础。韦敏斯德信条第13章论成圣：在有效恩召中被重生的人……藉着基督的受死与复活之功德……他们身上的罪体被消灭。洁净不是人自己做的——除罪水、赎罪祭——都是神提供的洁净手段。

**活祭——全人的奉献**
利未人当作摇祭被奉上——改革宗传统教导信徒全人的奉献。罗马书12:1"将身体献上，当作活祭，是圣洁的，是神所喜悦的。"利未人不只是把某样东西献给神——他们自己就是献物。韦敏斯德信条第16章论善行。

**替代者也需赎罪——人类中保的不完全**
利未人替代长子却自己也要献赎罪祭——改革宗传统教导唯独基督是完美中保。韦敏斯德信条第8章论基督为中保：唯有主耶稣基督才是教会的元首……祂被父差遣，取了人性。人类的替代者不够——需要一位完美的、无罪的替代者。

## Theology (English)

**Purification Before Service—Justification Before Sanctification**
Levites must be purified before serving—the Reformed tradition teaches justification is sanctification's foundation. Westminster Confession chapter 13 on sanctification: "They who are effectually called and regenerated...through the virtue of Christ's death and resurrection...the body of sin is destroyed." Purification was not self-achieved—purification water, sin offerings—all were God-provided means.

**Living Sacrifice—Whole-Person Offering**
Levites presented as wave offerings—the Reformed tradition teaches believers' whole-person offering. Romans 12:1: "Present your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and acceptable to God." Levites did not merely give something to God—they themselves were the offering. Westminster Confession chapter 16 on good works.

**Substitutes Also Need Atonement—Human Mediation's Imperfection**
Levites substituted for firstborn yet needed their own sin offerings—the Reformed tradition teaches only Christ is the perfect mediator. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ as Mediator: "It pleased God...to choose and ordain the Lord Jesus, His only begotten Son...the only Mediator." Human substitutes are insufficient—a perfect, sinless Substitute is needed.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第八章的两个画面在基督里有直接的成全。

**金灯台——基督是世界的光**
约翰福音8:12"我是世界的光。跟从我的，就不在黑暗里走，必要得着生命的光。"灯台在圣所发光——基督在世界发光。灯台从一块金子锤打而成——基督在十字架上被"锤打"："他为我们的过犯受害"（赛53:5）。灯台的美丽来自锤打——基督荣耀的救赎来自受苦。

启示录1:12-13约翰看见"七个金灯台……灯台中间有一位好像人子。"弟兄姐妹，旧约的灯台在圣所里发光——启示录的灯台就是教会，人子在教会中间行走。基督是灯台的光源——教会是灯台。

**利未人的洁净——基督洁净教会**
以弗所书5:25-26"基督爱教会，为教会舍己。要用水藉着道把教会洗净，成为圣洁。"利未人被除罪水弹洒、剃全身、洗衣服——基督用水和道洁净教会。利未人的洁净是外在的仪式——基督的洁净是内在的更新。

利未人被当作"活的摇祭"——基督将信徒从死人中活过来、活着献给神。罗马书6:13"要像从死里复活的人，将自己献给神。"利未人的活祭是暂时的（到五十岁退任）——信徒在基督里的活祭是一生的、永恒的。

"以色列人按手在利未人头上"——按手意味着身份的转移。弟兄姐妹，我们的罪按在了基督头上："耶和华使我们众人的罪孽都归在他身上"（赛53:6）。以色列人按手在利未人身上——是有限的转移。罪人的手按在基督身上——是终极的、完全的转移。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 8's two images find direct fulfillment in Christ.

**The Golden Lampstand—Christ Is the Light of the World**
John 8:12: "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness, but will have the light of life." The lampstand shone in the sanctuary—Christ shines in the world. The lampstand was hammered from one block of gold—Christ was "hammered" on the cross: "But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed." (Isa 53:5). The lampstand's beauty came from hammering—Christ's glorious redemption came from suffering.

Revelation 1:12-13: John saw "seven golden lampstands, and in the midst of the lampstands one like a son of man." Brothers and sisters, the Old Testament lampstand illuminated the sanctuary—Revelation's lampstands are the churches, and the Son of Man walks among them. Christ is the lampstand's light source—the church is the lampstand.

**Levite Purification—Christ Purifies the Church**
Ephesians 5:25-26: "Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her, that he might sanctify her, having cleansed her by the washing of water with the word." Levites were sprinkled with purification water, shaved, clothes washed—Christ cleanses the church with water and the word. Levite purification was outward ritual—Christ's cleansing is inward renewal.

Levites presented as "living wave offerings"—Christ raises believers from death to living dedication. Romans 6:13: "Present yourselves to God as those who have been brought from death to life." Levites' living sacrifice was temporary (retiring at fifty)—believers' living sacrifice in Christ is lifelong, eternal.

"The people of Israel laid their hands on the Levites"—laying on of hands meant identity transfer. Brothers and sisters, our sin was laid upon Christ: "All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all." (Isa 53:6). Israel's hands on Levites—limited transfer. Sinners' guilt on Christ—ultimate, complete transfer.
