## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第五章讲两件事：营地洁净（1-10节）和疑妻行淫的苦水条例（11-31节）。这一章让现代读者感到困惑——特别是疑妻喝苦水的仪式，看起来奇怪甚至不公平。让我们先从神学角度理解它的背景，再思考它向我们传达的深刻信息。

**营地洁净（1-10节）**

> "你吩咐以色列人，使一切长大痲疯的，患漏症的，并因死尸不洁净的，都出营外去。无论男女都要使他们出到营外，免得污秽他们的营；这营是我所住的。"

弟兄姐妹，"这营是我所住的"——这是理解整个第五章的钥匙。神住在以色列的营地中间——因此营地必须洁净。不是出于卫生考虑（虽然这也有益处），而是出于神学要求：神的圣洁不能与不洁同住。

痲疯、漏症、接触死尸——这三种情况在利未记里已经详细讨论过（利13-15章）。在这里，重点是：不洁净的人必须出到营外，等待洁净。他们没有被抛弃——有恢复的路，但必须先出营，不能留在神的同在中。

接下来是认罪赔偿的条例（5-10节）——"无论男女，若犯了人所常犯的罪，以至干犯耶和华，那人就有了罪。他要承认所犯的罪，将所亏负人的，如数赔还，另外加上五分之一。"

弟兄姐妹，干犯人就是干犯神——不可以只对神认罪而不对受害人赔偿。罪的赔还是具体的：本金加上五分之一。如果受害人已经去世也没有亲属，赔还归给祭司——没有"找不到人赔还"而逃脱的余地。这是公义的律法——不只是礼仪，而是实际的社会正义。

**疑妻喝苦水的条例（11-31节）**

这个条例在整本圣经里几乎是独一无二的——它涉及一个丈夫怀疑妻子不贞但没有证据的情况。让我们先描述程序：

丈夫带妻子到祭司面前，带来大麦面（不加油不加乳香——这是"疑恨的素祭"）。祭司用圣水盛在瓦器里，从帐幕地上取点尘土放入水中。妇人蓬头散发站在耶和华面前，手持素祭。祭司叫她起誓，宣读祝福与咒诅的条件，然后把咒诅的话写在书上，抹在苦水里，叫妇人喝下。

结果只有两种：若妇人真的不洁净，"肚腹发胀，大腿消瘦"——若妇人清洁无辜，就"免受这灾，且要怀孕"。

弟兄姐妹，这个条例对现代读者最不公平的地方在于：它只适用于被怀疑的妻子，不适用于被怀疑的丈夫。第31节"男人就为无罪，妇人必担当自己的罪孽"——这看起来是双重标准。

让我们诚实地面对这个张力。这条例是在古代父权社会背景下颁布的——它的一些具体形式反映了那个时代的社会结构。但它也有保护妇女的维度：在没有证据的情况下，丈夫不能随意休妻或惩罚妻子——必须把她带到神面前、由神来判断。神是最终的裁判者，不是愤怒的丈夫。一个被怀疑但清白的妻子，神会证明她的清白，"且要怀孕"——是祝福，不是惩罚。

而真正行了淫的妇人，咒诅会显明——这既是对行淫者的审判，也是对神义的维护：罪不能被遮掩，神知道一切隐秘的事。

弟兄姐妹，这条例最深刻的信息是：神是嫉妒的神（出20:5"我耶和华你的神是忌邪的神"），祂对盟约的忠诚极其认真。婚姻是盟约——破坏盟约的忠贞是严重的罪。在没有人类证人、没有司法系统的旷野环境中，神提供了一种超自然的司法程序——把裁判权交在祂自己手中。

最终，咒诅还是祝福——不取决于人类的判断，而取决于神的全知。这个条例的核心不是歧视——而是"万事都赤露敞开，在那与我们有关系的主眼前无不如此"（来4:13）。

弟兄姐妹，第五章的三个条例有一个共同的主题：营地是神的居所，任何污秽——身体的不洁、财务的亏负、婚约的破坏——都不能存在于这个空间。这不是苛刻的规定，而是在告诉我们：神的同在是真实的、圣洁的、需要被认真对待的。我们的日常生活——身体、金钱、婚姻——都在神的眼目之下，都在祂的治理之中。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 5 addresses two matters: camp purity (vv. 1-10) and the ordeal of the woman suspected of adultery (vv. 11-31). This chapter puzzles modern readers—especially the bitter water ritual, which appears strange and even unfair. Let us first understand it theologically, then reflect on its profound message.

**Camp Purity (vv. 1-10)**

> "Command the people of Israel that they put out of the camp everyone who is leprous or has a discharge and everyone who is unclean through contact with the dead...that they may not defile their camp, in the midst of which I dwell."

Brothers and sisters, "in the midst of which I dwell"—this is the key to all of chapter 5. God dwelt in Israel's camp—therefore the camp must be clean. Not for hygienic reasons (though that had benefits too), but for theological necessity: God's holiness cannot cohabit with uncleanness.

Leprosy, discharge, contact with corpses—these three conditions were detailed in Leviticus (Lev 13-15). Here the emphasis: the unclean must go outside the camp, awaiting purification. They were not abandoned—there was a path of restoration, but they must first exit the camp, unable to remain in God's presence.

Then the restitution ordinance (vv. 5-10)—"When a man or woman commits any of the sins that people commit by breaking faith with the LORD...they shall confess their sin...and make full restitution for his wrong, adding a fifth to it."

Brothers and sisters, sinning against people is sinning against God—one cannot merely confess to God while refusing restitution to the victim. Sin's restitution was concrete: principal plus one-fifth. If the victim died with no relatives, restitution went to the priest—no escape through "can't find anyone to repay." This was just law—not merely ritual but actual social justice.

**The Bitter Water Ordeal (vv. 11-31)**

This ordinance is nearly unique in all Scripture—involving a husband suspecting his wife of infidelity but having no evidence. Let me first describe the procedure:

The husband brought his wife before the priest, bringing barley flour (no oil, no frankincense—this was the "grain offering of jealousy"). The priest used holy water in an earthen vessel, adding dust from the tabernacle floor. The woman stood disheveled before the LORD holding the grain offering. The priest made her swear, read the blessing and cursing conditions, then wrote the cursing words on a scroll, washed them into the bitter water, and made the woman drink.

Only two outcomes: if the woman was truly defiled, "her womb shall swell, and her thigh fall away"; if the woman was clean and innocent, she would "be free and conceive children."

Brothers and sisters, what disturbs modern readers most is that this ordinance applied only to the suspected wife, not the suspected husband. Verse 31: "The man shall be free from iniquity, but the woman shall bear her iniquity"—this appears to be a double standard.

Let us honestly face this tension. This ordinance was given in an ancient patriarchal society—some specific forms reflect that era's social structure. But it also had a protective dimension for women: without evidence, a husband could not arbitrarily divorce or punish his wife—he must bring her before God for God to judge. God was the ultimate arbiter, not an enraged husband. A suspected but innocent wife would have God vindicate her, "and she shall conceive children"—blessing, not punishment.

And the truly unfaithful woman—the curse would reveal it—this was both judgment on the adulteress and maintenance of divine justice: sin cannot be concealed; God knows all hidden things.

Brothers and sisters, this ordinance's deepest message is: God is a jealous God (Exod 20:5: "I the LORD your God am a jealous God"), who takes covenant faithfulness with utmost seriousness. Marriage is covenant—breaking covenant faithfulness is grave sin. In the wilderness environment without human witnesses or judicial system, God provided a supernatural judicial procedure—placing judgment in His own hands.

Ultimately, whether curse or blessing came depended not on human judgment but on God's omniscience. This ordinance's core was not discrimination—but "Neither is there any creature that is not manifest in his sight: but all things are naked and opened unto the eyes of him with whom we have to do." (Heb 4:13).

## 历史背景（中文）

疑妻喝苦水的条例（sotah，希伯来文"偏离之人"）在古代近东有类似的对应——巴比伦的汉谟拉比法典（约公元前1754年）第132条也有类似规定：被指控通奸的妇人必须跳入河中，若沉下就有罪，若浮起就无罪（河神审判）。与巴比伦法典相比，民数记的条例实际上更人道——不是生死博弈，而是把结果交在神手中、由神的超自然介入来裁判。犹太米示拿（Sotah篇）详细记载了此条例的实施细节，并说拉比约哈南·本·撒迦伊（公元70年后）废除了此条例——因为通奸已经过于普遍，条例失去了意义（也可能因为没有圣殿就无法执行）。

## Historical Context (English)

The bitter water ordeal (sotah, Hebrew for "one who has gone astray") had ancient Near Eastern parallels—Hammurabi's Code (c. 1754 BC) paragraph 132 had similar provisions: a woman accused of adultery had to jump into the river; if she sank she was guilty, if she floated she was innocent (river god judgment). Compared to Babylonian law, Numbers' ordinance was actually more humane—not a life-or-death gamble, but placing the outcome in God's hands for His supernatural intervention to adjudicate. The Jewish Mishnah (tractate Sotah) detailed the ordinance's implementation, noting that Rabbi Johanan ben Zakkai (after 70 AD) abolished the ordinance—because adultery had become too widespread for it to be meaningful (also possibly because without the Temple it could not be executed).

## 经文结构（中文）

**营地洁净令（1-4节）**——痲疯/漏症/触死尸者出营外，因为神住在营中。

**认罪赔偿条例（5-10节）**——犯罪须认罪赔还本金+五分之一，无亲属受还则归祭司。

**疑妻行淫的程序（11-22节）**——丈夫带妻到祭司前→大麦面素祭→圣水加尘土→妇人蓬发起誓→喝苦水→咒诅或祝福。

**苦水的结果（23-31节）**——有罪：肚腹发胀大腿消瘦；清洁：免受灾且怀孕。结语：神是裁判者。

## Structure (English)

**Camp Purification Order (vv. 1-4)**—Leprous/discharge/corpse-touched persons go outside camp, because God dwells within.

**Confession and Restitution Ordinance (vv. 5-10)**—Sin requires confession and restitution of principal plus one-fifth; if no relatives, goes to priest.

**Suspected Adultery Procedure (vv. 11-22)**—Husband brings wife to priest → barley grain offering → holy water with dust → woman disheveled and sworn → drinks bitter water → curse or blessing.

**Bitter Water Outcomes (vv. 23-31)**—Guilty: abdomen swells, thigh wastes away; Clean: free from disaster and conceives. Conclusion: God is the arbiter.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神住在营中——圣洁是地点性的要求**
"这营是我所住的"——改革宗传统教导神的同在要求圣洁的环境。韦敏斯德信条第2章论神的本质：祂……至圣，……祂的圣洁是极其纯洁的……神住在人中间是恩典，但同时要求人的圣洁。今天，圣灵住在信徒心中——"岂不知你们是神的殿，神的灵住在你们里头吗？"（林前3:16）

**婚约与神的忌邪**
疑妻条例背后是神对盟约忠贞的要求——改革宗传统教导神是忌邪的神，祂对盟约关系极其认真。韦敏斯德小要理问答第4问"神是个灵，祂的本体、智慧、权能、圣洁、公义、恩慈、真实，都是无限无量、无始无终，永无改变的。"神对婚姻盟约的认真，是祂对以色列盟约认真的缩影。

**公义需要证明——神的全知作保障**
没有人类证人时，神介入作裁判——改革宗传统教导神的全知。韦敏斯德信条第2章"过去、现在、将来的一切事情，祂都全知，……没有任何事情是偶然或不确定的。"无辜者不用惧怕苦水——神知道真相。

## Theology (English)

**God Dwells in the Camp—Holiness Is a Locational Requirement**
"In the midst of which I dwell"—the Reformed tradition teaches God's presence requires a holy environment. Westminster Confession chapter 2 on God's nature: He is...most holy...His holiness is most pure. God dwelling among people is grace, simultaneously requiring human holiness. Today the Spirit dwells in believers—"Know ye not that ye are the temple of God, and that the Spirit of God dwelleth in you?" (1 Cor 3:16)

**Marriage Covenant and God's Jealousy**
The suspected adultery ordinance rested on God's demand for covenant faithfulness—the Reformed tradition teaches God is a jealous God who takes covenant relationships with utmost seriousness. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q4: "God is a Spirit, infinite, eternal, and unchangeable in His being, wisdom, power, holiness, justice, goodness, and truth." God's seriousness about the marriage covenant is a microcosm of His seriousness about the covenant with Israel.

**Justice Requires Evidence—God's Omniscience as Guarantee**
Without human witnesses, God intervened as arbiter—the Reformed tradition teaches God's omniscience. Westminster Confession chapter 2: His knowledge is most holy, most free, most absolute...nothing being to Him contingent or uncertain. The innocent need not fear the bitter water—God knows the truth.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第五章在基督里有两个层面的回响。

洁净出营——基督"在营外受苦"。希伯来书13:11-13"原来牲畜的血，被大祭司带入圣所作赎罪祭，牲畜的身子都烧在营外。所以耶稣，要用自己的血叫百姓成圣，也在城门外受苦。这样，我们也当出到营外就了他去，忍受他所受的凌辱。"不洁净者出营——基督也在城外受死。祂认同了我们的不洁净，在营外——在"不洁净"的地方——为我们受死。

苦水条例——基督喝尽了咒诅的苦杯。加拉太书3:13"基督既为我们受了咒诅，就赎出我们脱离律法的咒诅。"被怀疑的妇人喝下苦水，让神来裁判——基督在客西马尼园说"我父啊，这杯若可行，求你叫这杯离开我"（太26:39），然后顺服地喝下了罪的苦水——为了有罪的人。

清白的妇人喝苦水后"且要怀孕"——是新生命的祝福。弟兄姐妹，基督喝下苦杯之后，带来的是新生命——约翰福音12:24"一粒麦子……死了，就结出许多子粒来。"基督的受死是苦水——基督的复活是怀孕的应许。祂死了——但从死亡中带出了无数的生命。

认罪赔偿——"如数赔还，另外加上五分之一"。弟兄姐妹，我们亏负了神——我们的罪无法自行偿还。基督替我们还清了全部的债——不只是本金，连利息也还了。罗马书8:1"如今，那些在基督耶稣里的就不定罪了。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 5 resonates with Christ on two levels.

The unclean going outside the camp—Christ "suffered outside the gate." Hebrews 13:11-13: "For the bodies of those animals whose blood is brought into the holy places by the high priest as a sacrifice for sin are burned outside the camp. So Jesus also suffered outside the gate in order to sanctify the people through his own blood. Therefore let us go to him outside the camp and bear the reproach he endured." The unclean went outside—Christ also died outside the city. He identified with our uncleanness, dying outside the camp—in the "unclean" place—for us.

The bitter water ordeal—Christ drank the cursed bitter cup to its dregs. Galatians 3:13: "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us." The suspected woman drank the bitter water, letting God judge—Christ in Gethsemane said "And he went a little farther, and fell on his face, and prayed, saying, O my Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me: nevertheless not as I will, but as thou wilt." (Matt 26:39), then obediently drank the cup of sin—for guilty people.

The innocent woman after drinking the bitter water "shall conceive children"—blessing of new life. Brothers and sisters, after Christ drank the bitter cup, He brought new life—John 12:24: "Unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies...it bears much fruit." Christ's suffering was the bitter water—Christ's resurrection was the promise of conception. He died—yet from death brought forth countless lives.

Confession and restitution—"make full restitution...adding a fifth." Brothers and sisters, we have wronged God—our sin cannot self-repay. Christ paid our entire debt—not just the principal but the interest too. Romans 8:1: "There is therefore now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus."
