## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十八章回答了第十七章结尾的恐惧之问："凡挨近耶和华帐幕的是必死的。我们都要死亡吗？"答案是：不——因为有祭司和利未人作为中间人，保护你们。

这一章完全是对亚伦说的——不是对摩西，不是对以色列全会众——而是直接对大祭司。这是祭司和利未人的"职务说明书"和"薪酬制度"。

**祭司与利未人的职责（1-7节）**

> "你和你的儿子，并你本族的人，要一同担当干犯圣所的罪孽。"

弟兄姐妹，"担当罪孽"——这是极重的话。如果有人不当地接近圣所，祭司要为此负责。不是说祭司替人犯罪——而是祭司体系的功能就是在百姓和神的圣洁之间作缓冲。如果缓冲失败了——错在祭司。

利未人与祭司的关系是明确的：利未人"与你联合，服事你"——他们是祭司的助手。但有一条不可逾越的界线——"不可挨近圣所的器具和坛，免得他们和你们都死亡。"利未人可以在外围工作，但不能触碰圣所的核心。可拉的悲剧就是因为利未人想越过这条线。

"凡挨近的外人必被治死"——这条禁令回答了百姓的恐惧：不是所有人都会死——但没有正当身份靠近圣所的人会死。祭司和利未人存在的意义，就是确保普通人不需要冒险靠近。

**祭司的收入——"我就是你的分"（8-20节）**

然后是祭司家族的经济供应。祭司不种地、不经商——他们全职侍奉圣所。那他们吃什么？

以色列人献给神的供物中，有一部分归给祭司：至圣之物（素祭、赎罪祭、赎愆祭中不经火的部分）——只有祭司家的男丁可以吃，要在圣所范围内吃；举祭和摇祭——祭司全家（包括女儿，只要洁净）可以吃；油、新酒、五谷的初熟之物和一切"永献之物"——都归祭司。

弟兄姐妹，注意这里的原则：以色列人不是直接给祭司报酬——他们是献给神的，神再把一部分分配给祭司。祭司的收入不是来自百姓的雇佣——而是来自神的恩赐。

然后是最深刻的一句——"耶和华对亚伦说：你在以色列人的境内不可有产业，在他们中间也不可有分。我就是你的分，是你的产业。"

弟兄姐妹，"我就是你的分"——其他十一个支派各有自己的土地——犹大有犹大的地、便雅悯有便雅悯的地。但利未人没有——他们的"产业"是神自己。这不是匮乏——这是最大的丰富。诗篇16:5"耶和华是我的产业，是我杯中的分；我所得的，你为我持守。"诗篇73:25"除你以外，在天上我有谁呢？除你以外，在地上我也没有所爱慕的。"

**利未人的收入——十分之一（21-24节）**

利未人也没有地——他们的收入是以色列人所出产的十分之一。"凡以色列中出产的十分之一，我已赐给利未的子孙为业。"弟兄姐妹，十一个支派的总产出中，十分之一给利未人——这是利未人作为会幕服役者的"工资"。

**利未人的十分之一的十分之一（25-32节）**

然后是一个巧妙的经济结构——利未人从以色列人那里收到十分之一，但利未人也要从自己的收入中拿出十分之一给祭司！"你们从以色列人中所取的十分之一，要再从那十分之一中取十分之一作为举祭献给耶和华。"

弟兄姐妹，连利未人也要奉献——他们不是奉献的终点，而是奉献链条中的一环。每个以色列人奉献收入的十分之一给利未人→利未人奉献收入的十分之一给祭司。整个系统确保了圣所的运作、祭司的供应、利未人的生计。

弟兄姐妹，第十八章的核心信息是两个看似矛盾的真理的统一：第一，接近神是极其危险的——错误的人靠近圣所会死亡；第二，神设立了一个制度来保护你——祭司和利未人存在的意义就是让你可以安全地与神同住。以色列营地的核心是会幕——会幕周围是利未人——利未人周围是十二支派。这三层结构确保了神的圣洁不会杀死祂的百姓。

弟兄姐妹，这个制度的最终目的不是让人远离神——而是让人可以安全地靠近神。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 18 answers chapter 17's fearful closing question: "Everyone who comes near the LORD's tabernacle shall die. Are we all to perish?" The answer: no—because priests and Levites serve as intermediaries, protecting you.

This chapter was spoken entirely to Aaron—not to Moses, not to all Israel—but directly to the high priest. This was the priests' and Levites' "job description" and "compensation system."

**Priestly and Levitical Responsibilities (vv. 1-7)**

> "You and your sons and your father's house with you shall bear iniquity connected with the sanctuary."

Brothers and sisters, "bear iniquity"—these were weighty words. If someone improperly approached the sanctuary, priests bore responsibility. Not that priests sinned vicariously—but the priestly system's function was buffering between the people and God's holiness. If the buffer failed—the fault was the priests'.

Levites' relationship to priests was clear: Levites "join you and minister to you"—they were priests' assistants. But an uncrossable boundary existed—"they must not come near to the vessels of the sanctuary or to the altar lest both they and you die." Levites could work at the perimeter but could not touch the sanctuary's core. Korah's tragedy resulted from Levites trying to cross that line.

"Any outsider who comes near shall be put to death"—this prohibition answered the people's fear: not everyone would die—but those without proper standing approaching the sanctuary would. Priests and Levites existed to ensure ordinary people need not risk approaching.

**Priestly Income—"I Am Your Portion" (vv. 8-20)**

Then the priestly family's economic provision. Priests did not farm or trade—they served the sanctuary full-time. What would they eat?

From Israel's offerings to God, portions went to priests: most holy things (grain, sin, guilt offerings not consumed by fire)—only priestly males could eat, within the sanctuary precincts; wave and heave offerings—the entire priestly family (including daughters, if clean) could eat; oil, wine, grain firstfruits and all "devoted things"—all went to priests.

Brothers and sisters, note the principle: Israelites did not directly pay priests—they offered to God, and God allocated portions to priests. Priestly income came not from the people's employment but from God's gift.

Then the most profound statement—"The LORD said to Aaron, 'You shall have no inheritance in their land, neither shall you have any portion among them. I am your portion and your inheritance among the people of Israel.'"

Brothers and sisters, "I am your portion"—the other eleven tribes each had their own land—Judah had Judah's territory, Benjamin had Benjamin's. But Levites had none—their "inheritance" was God Himself. This was not deprivation—it was the greatest abundance. Psalm 16:5: "The LORD is my chosen portion and my cup; you hold my lot." Psalm 73:25: "Whom have I in heaven but you? And there is nothing on earth that I desire besides you."

**Levitical Income—Tithes (vv. 21-24)**

Levites also had no land—their income was a tenth of Israel's produce. "To the Levites I have given every tithe in Israel for an inheritance." Brothers and sisters, from the total produce of eleven tribes, a tenth went to Levites—their "salary" for tabernacle service.

**The Tithe of the Tithe (vv. 25-32)**

Then an elegant economic structure—Levites received tithes from Israel, but Levites also gave a tenth of their income to priests! "When you take from the people of Israel the tithe...you shall present a contribution from it to the LORD, a tithe of the tithe."

Brothers and sisters, even Levites gave offerings—they were not the endpoint of giving but a link in the giving chain. Every Israelite gave a tenth to Levites → Levites gave a tenth to priests. The entire system ensured sanctuary operations, priestly provision, and Levitical livelihood.

Brothers and sisters, chapter 18's core message unifies two seemingly contradictory truths: first, approaching God is extremely dangerous—the wrong person approaching the sanctuary would die; second, God established a system to protect you—priests and Levites existed so you could safely dwell with God. Israel's camp center was the tabernacle—surrounded by Levites—surrounded by twelve tribes. This three-layer structure ensured God's holiness would not kill His people.

Brothers and sisters, this system's ultimate purpose was not keeping people away from God—but enabling people to safely approach God.

## 历史背景（中文）

十分之一制度（Ma'aser）在古代近东有广泛的先例——亚述和巴比伦的神庙都接受类似的贡赋。但以色列的独特之处在于：十分之一不归国王或政府——而是归利未人和祭司（即宗教-教育系统）。利未人后来被分配了48座城市散布在各支派的领地中（民35:1-8），其中6座是逃城——利未人虽然没有连续的领地，但他们在全以色列都有存在。这使他们成为了以色列的教师阶层——他们教导律法、主持仪式、解决纠纷。"我就是你的分"——这句话后来成为以色列灵性传统中极其重要的概念。利未人虽然"最穷"（没有土地），却"最富"（有神作为产业）。犹太传统说："以神为分的人，全世界都是他的。"

## Historical Context (English)

The tithing system (Ma'aser) had widespread ancient Near Eastern precedent—Assyrian and Babylonian temples received similar contributions. But Israel's uniqueness: tithes did not go to kings or government—but to Levites and priests (the religious-educational system). Levites were later allocated 48 cities scattered across all tribal territories (Num 35:1-8), including 6 cities of refuge—though Levites had no contiguous territory, they had presence throughout all Israel. This made them Israel's teaching class—teaching law, conducting ceremonies, settling disputes. "I am your portion"—this statement later became an extremely important concept in Israelite spiritual tradition. Levites were the "poorest" (no land) yet "richest" (having God as inheritance). Jewish tradition says: "Whoever has God as their portion has the whole world."

## 经文结构（中文）

**祭司和利未人的职责（1-7节）**——祭司担当圣所罪孽、利未人联合服事但不可碰圣物、"凡挨近的外人必被治死"→祭司职任是"赏赐"。

**祭司的收入（8-19节）**——至圣之物归祭司男丁、举祭摇祭归全家、初熟之物和永献之物归祭司。

**"我就是你的分"（20节）**——祭司利未人无地产、神自己是他们的产业。

**利未人的十分之一（21-24节）**——以色列人出产的十分之一归利未人为业。

**利未人的奉献（25-32节）**——利未人从十分之一中再取十分之一给祭司→"至好的"举起献给耶和华。

## Structure (English)

**Priestly and Levitical Duties (vv. 1-7)**—Priests bear sanctuary iniquity, Levites serve jointly but cannot touch holy things, "outsider approaching dies" → priesthood is a "gift."

**Priestly Income (vv. 8-19)**—Most holy things to priestly males, wave/heave offerings to whole family, firstfruits and devoted things to priests.

**"I Am Your Portion" (v. 20)**—Priests/Levites have no land inheritance, God Himself is their inheritance.

**Levitical Tithes (vv. 21-24)**—Tenth of Israel's produce to Levites as inheritance.

**Levitical Offerings (vv. 25-32)**—Levites give a tenth of their tenth to priests → "best" lifted as offering to the LORD.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"我就是你的分"——以神为至宝**
利未人没有土地却有神——改革宗传统教导以神为至宝。韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问"（一）荣耀神；（二）以祂为乐，直到永远。"以祂为"乐"——不是以土地、财富、产业为乐。诗篇73:25-26"除你以外……在地上我也没有所爱慕的……神是我心里的力量，又是我的福分，直到永远。"

**十分之一——一切属于神**
十分之一制度——改革宗传统教导一切属于神。韦敏斯德大要理问答第141问论第八诫。以色列人不是在"给"神十分之一——他们是在"归还"属于神的部分。一切产出都是神的——人只是管家。

**保护的制度——恩典的架构**
祭司和利未人保护百姓免于因靠近圣所而死——改革宗传统教导神的恩典先于人的行动。韦敏斯德信条第8章论基督为中保。神不是设了一个致命的陷阱——祂设了一个保护系统。祂的圣洁是真实的（靠近会死），但祂的爱也是真实的（祂提供了安全靠近的途径）。

## Theology (English)

**"I Am Your Portion"—God as Supreme Treasure**
Levites had no land yet had God—the Reformed tradition teaches God as supreme treasure. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "Man's chief end is to glorify God, and to enjoy him forever." Enjoying God—not land, wealth, or estates. Psalm 73:25-26: "Besides you, I desire nothing on earth...God is the strength of my heart and my portion forever."

**Tithes—All Belongs to God**
The tithing system—the Reformed tradition teaches everything belongs to God. Westminster Larger Catechism Q141 on the eighth commandment. Israelites were not "giving" God a tenth—they were "returning" God's portion. All produce was God's—humans were merely stewards.

**Protective System—Grace's Framework**
Priests and Levites protected people from death by approaching the sanctuary—the Reformed tradition teaches God's grace precedes human action. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ as Mediator. God did not set a deadly trap—He established a protection system. His holiness was real (approaching meant death), but His love was also real (He provided a safe approach pathway).

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第十八章的核心概念——祭司作为中保保护百姓——在基督里有完美的成全。

**"我就是你的分"——基督是我们的至宝**
腓立比书3:8"我将万事当作有损的，因我以认识我主基督耶稣为至宝。"保罗放弃了一切——法利赛人的地位、罗马公民权、学术成就——以基督为至宝。这与利未人放弃土地产业、以神为产业完全平行。弟兄姐妹，你能说"除你以外，在地上我也没有所爱慕的"吗？

**祭司保护百姓——基督保护教会**
祭司存在的意义是让百姓可以安全地与神同住——基督存在的意义是让罪人可以安全地到神面前。希伯来书4:16"所以，我们只管坦然无惧地来到施恩的宝座前，为要得怜恤，蒙恩惠，作随时的帮助。"旧约的百姓需要通过祭司才能安全靠近——新约的信徒因着基督可以"坦然无惧地来到"。不再需要利未人作缓冲——因为基督是永远的大祭司。

**十分之一的奉献链——教会的供应**
利未人靠十分之一生活、全职侍奉——保罗说"主也是这样命定，叫传福音的靠着福音养生"（林前9:14）。教会的牧师和传道人如同新约的利未人——他们全职侍奉，教会供应他们的生活。这不是恩惠——而是神的命定。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 18's core concept—priests as mediators protecting people—finds perfect fulfillment in Christ.

**"I Am Your Portion"—Christ Is Our Supreme Treasure**
Philippians 3:8: "I count everything as loss because of the surpassing worth of knowing Christ Jesus my Lord." Paul gave up everything—Pharisaic status, Roman citizenship, academic achievement—counting Christ supreme. This perfectly parallels Levites relinquishing land inheritance, having God as inheritance. Brothers and sisters, can you say "there is nothing on earth I desire besides you"?

**Priests Protecting People—Christ Protecting the Church**
Priests' purpose was enabling people to safely dwell with God—Christ's purpose is enabling sinners to safely approach God. Hebrews 4:16: "Let us then with confidence draw near to the throne of grace, that we may receive mercy and find grace to help in time of need." Old Testament people needed priests to safely approach—New Testament believers through Christ may "draw near with confidence." No longer needing Levites as buffer—because Christ is the eternal High Priest.

**The Tithing Chain—Church Support**
Levites lived by tithes, serving full-time—Paul said "Even so hath the Lord ordained that they which preach the gospel should live of the gospel." (1 Cor 9:14). Church pastors and ministers are like New Testament Levites—serving full-time, supported by the church. This is not charity—but God's ordinance.
