## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第十三章是民数记——也是整个以色列历史——的一个决定性转折点。十二个探子去窥探迦南地四十天，回来时带来了两种截然不同的报告。这一章的选择，影响了此后四十年。

**差派探子（1-20节）**

> "你打发人去窥探我所赐给以色列人的迦南地，他们每支派中要打发一个人，都要作首领的。"

弟兄姐妹，神说"我所赐给以色列人的迦南地"——在神的旨意里，迦南地已经是以色列的了。差派探子不是为了决定是否要去——而是为了了解如何去。但探子的报告变成了"是否要去"的辩论。

十二个探子都是支派首领——不是普通人，而是以色列最优秀的代表。他们出发前的任务是实际的侦察："看那地如何，其中所住的民是强是弱，是多是少……所住之地是好是歹……你们要放开胆量，把那地的果子带些来。"

四十天后他们回来了——带着以实各谷的葡萄，一挂葡萄大到要两个人用杠抬（23节）。这不是贫瘠之地——这是真正"流奶与蜜"之地的实物证明。

**两种报告（27-33节）**

然后，十二个探子给出了两种报告。

先是一致的事实报告："我们到了你所打发我们去的那地，果然是流奶与蜜之地；这就是那地的果子。"然后立刻转折——"然而住那地的民强壮，城邑也坚固宽大，并且我们在那里看见了亚衲族的人。"弟兄姐妹，他们说的都是事实——但十个人用事实来支持放弃，两个人用同样的事实来支持进攻。

迦勒——犹大支派的探子——站出来说："我们立刻上去得那地罢！我们足能得胜。"这是信心的声音。

但其余十个探子（包括以法莲支派的何细亚，此时名字还未改为约书亚——神在第17节已经将他改名了）说："我们不能上去攻击那民，因为他们比我们强壮。"然后是最具破坏性的一句："我们在那里看见亚衲族人……据我们看，自己就如蚱蜢一样；据他们看，我们也是如此。"

弟兄姐妹，"蚱蜢"——这是十个探子如何看待自己的。他们的问题不是迦南人太强大——而是他们忘记了谁和他们同行。他们看见了城墙、看见了伟人、看见了亚衲族——他们没有看见那在西奈山与他们立约的神。

迦勒和约书亚的信心建立在什么基础上？不是"我们很强壮"——而是"神应许了，所以我们能得胜"。信心不是无视现实——迦勒也看见了高墙和伟人。但他的分析框架不是"我们 vs 他们"，而是"神 vs 他们"。

弟兄姐妹，两种报告的差别不是事实的差别——而是信心的差别。同样的城墙、同样的伟人、同样的葡萄——十个人看见了不可能，两个人看见了神的应许。

申命记1:32-33记载了神后来对以色列人说的话："他们这事上你们仍不信服耶和华你们的神，他在路上在你们前面行走，为你们找安营的地方——夜间在火柱里，日间在云柱里。"

弟兄姐妹，他们见过神每天的带领，却在最关键的时刻选择了不信。不信不是因为缺乏证据——他们有四十年的证据。不信是因为人心的倾向——眼见为实，神的应许更难。

弟兄姐妹，让我们重新看那一挂葡萄——两个人用杠抬着。这挂葡萄是迦南美地真实性的见证——神没有骗以色列，那地确实"流奶与蜜"。十个探子手里捧着这挂葡萄，嘴里却说"我们不能上去"。

他们一面承认神的应许是真实的（"果然是流奶与蜜之地"），一面拒绝相信神有能力将它赐给他们。弟兄姐妹，这正是我们许多人的光景——我们承认神的应许是好的，却不相信它适用于我们。我们说"神对别人很好"，却不说"神也会这样对我"。信心的缺口就在这里：从"神说了"到"这包括我"——这一步，需要真实的信心，需要过去经历的支撑，需要圣灵的帮助。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 13 was a decisive turning point in Numbers—and all Israelite history. Twelve spies went to scout Canaan for forty days; upon return they brought two vastly different reports. This chapter's choice affected the next forty years.

**Sending the Spies (vv. 1-20)**

> "Send men to spy out the land of Canaan, which I am giving to the people of Israel. From each tribe of their fathers you shall send a man, every one a chief among them."

Brothers and sisters, God said "which I am giving to Israel"—in God's will, Canaan was already Israel's. Sending spies was not to decide whether to go—but to understand how to go. But the spies' report became a debate about whether to go.

Twelve spies were all tribal leaders—not ordinary men, but Israel's finest representatives. Their mission before departure was practical reconnaissance: "See what the land is, and whether the people who dwell in it are strong or weak...and whether the land is rich or poor...And bring back some of the fruit of the land."

Forty days later they returned—carrying grapes from Eshcol Valley, a cluster so large two men carried it on a pole (v. 23). This was no barren land—this was tangible proof of a truly "milk and honey" land.

**Two Reports (vv. 27-33)**

Then twelve spies gave two reports.

First, unanimous factual reporting: "We came to the land to which you sent us. It flows with milk and honey, and this is its fruit." Immediately a pivot—"However, the people who dwell in the land are strong, and the cities are fortified and very large." Brothers and sisters, they all spoke truth—but ten used facts to support giving up, two used the same facts to support attacking.

Caleb—Judah's spy—stood up and said: "Let us go up at once and occupy it, for we are well able to overcome it." This was faith's voice.

But the other ten spies said: "We are not able to go up against the people, for they are stronger than we are." Then the most destructive statement: "And there we saw the Nephilim...and we seemed to ourselves like grasshoppers, and so we seemed to them."

Brothers and sisters, "grasshoppers"—this was how ten spies saw themselves. Their problem was not that Canaanites were too strong—it was that they forgot who traveled with them. They saw walls, saw giants, saw the Anakim—they did not see the God who had covenanted with them at Sinai.

On what foundation did Caleb and Joshua's faith rest? Not "we are strong"—but "God promised, therefore we can overcome." Faith does not ignore reality—Caleb also saw the high walls and giants. But his analytical framework was not "us vs them" but "God vs them."

Brothers and sisters, the two reports' difference was not factual difference—it was a faith difference. Same walls, same giants, same grapes—ten saw impossibility, two saw God's promise.

Deuteronomy 1:32-33 records God's later words to Israel: "In spite of this word you did not believe the LORD your God, who went before you in the way to seek you out a place to pitch your tents, in fire by night and in the cloud by day."

Brothers and sisters, they had witnessed God's daily leading yet chose unbelief at the critical moment. Unbelief was not from lack of evidence—they had forty years of evidence. Unbelief was human nature's inclination—seeing is believing, but God's promises require more.

## 历史背景（中文）

"亚衲族"（Anakim）在古代传说中是身材巨大的部族——可能是体型较大的迦南部落被夸大描述。申命记2:10-11提到他们"以前住那里，是身量高大的民，与亚衲人一样"，和以色列人一样害怕他们。但约书亚在迦南征服战争中最终消灭了亚衲族（书11:21-22）——证明他们并非不可战胜。"以实各谷"（Valley of Eshcol，葡萄谷）被一些学者认为在希伯仑附近，今天该地区仍以葡萄种植闻名（其中包括希伯仑葡萄酒）。探子窥探迦南的路线——从寻的旷野到利合（哈马口附近）——是约200公里的跋涉，四十天返回（往返），平均每天约10公里。申命记1:22-23记载差派探子实际上是百姓先请求的，然后神允准。这增加了以色列悲剧的层次：他们自己要求差探子，探子回来后他们又不相信探子的好消息（迦勒的报告），反而相信坏消息。

## Historical Context (English)

The "Anakim" in ancient tradition were a tribe of great stature—possibly larger-than-average Canaanite clans exaggerated in description. Deuteronomy 2:10-11 mentions others "who were formerly there, a people great and many, and tall as the Anakim," fearing them just as Israel did. But Joshua ultimately destroyed the Anakim in Canaan's conquest (Josh 11:21-22)—proving they were not invincible. The Valley of Eshcol (Grape Valley) is located by some scholars near Hebron, a region still famous for viticulture today (including Hebron wine). The spies' Canaan reconnaissance route—from Zin wilderness to Rehob (near Lebo-hamath)—was about 200 km, forty days roundtrip, averaging about 10 km per day. Deuteronomy 1:22-23 records that sending spies was actually the people's request first, then God permitted it. This adds another layer to Israel's tragedy: they themselves asked to send spies, yet after the spies returned, they rejected the good report (Caleb's) and believed the bad.

## 经文结构（中文）

**差派十二探子（1-16节）**——每支派一名首领、约书亚已改名（v.16）。

**侦察任务（17-20节）**——看民强弱多少、地好歹、城营还是坚城、土肥瘠、有无树木、带果子回来。

**窥探四十天（21-25节）**——从寻的旷野到哈马口、见亚衲族、砍以实各谷葡萄（两人抬）。

**两种报告（26-33节）**——事实一致：流奶与蜜+坚城强民；分歧：迦勒"立刻上去"vs十人"我们不能"；最终：自比蚱蜢的失败主义。

## Structure (English)

**Sending Twelve Spies (vv. 1-16)**—One leader per tribe, Joshua already renamed (v. 16).

**Reconnaissance Mission (vv. 17-20)**—Assess people's strength, land quality, fortified vs. open cities, soil fertility, trees, bring back fruit.

**Forty-Day Scouting (vv. 21-25)**—From Zin wilderness to Lebo-hamath, encounter Anakim, cut Eshcol Valley grapes (two men carry).

**Two Reports (vv. 26-33)**—Facts agreed: milk and honey + fortified cities strong people; division: Caleb "let us go up" vs. ten "we cannot"; final: defeatist self-comparison to grasshoppers.

## 神学要点（中文）

**信心的框架——神 vs 仇敌，而非我们 vs 仇敌**
迦勒信心与十人不信的对比——改革宗传统教导信心以神的应许为基础。韦敏斯德小要理问答第86问"信服耶稣基督是神所赐的得救恩典；使我们借此可以照着福音的信息与劝勉而接受基督，并惟独靠祂得救。"迦勒的框架是"神应许了"——十人的框架是"我们不够强"。同样的事实，不同的信心，不同的结论。

**不信的根源——心不是眼**
探子"眼见为实"却不信神的话——改革宗传统教导信心超越感官经验。韦敏斯德信条第14章论救恩的信心：这信心使基督徒相信神话语中所启示为真的一切事情，并顺服其中的各种命令。不信不是因为缺乏证据——以色列有出埃及、云柱、吗哪等无数证据。不信是心的选择：宁愿相信眼睛看见的困难，不愿相信神的应许。

**应许已给——行动是顺服**
神说"我所赐给"——不是"我将要考虑赐给"。改革宗传统教导神的主权和人的责任并行。韦敏斯德信条第3章论神的永恒旨意。应许是确定的——但以色列需要用行动来领受这应许。不行动就是不信。

## Theology (English)

**Faith's Framework—God vs. Enemies, Not Us vs. Enemies**
Contrast of Caleb's faith vs. ten's unbelief—the Reformed tradition teaches faith is based on God's promises. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q86: "Faith in Jesus Christ is a saving grace, whereby we...receive and rest upon him." Caleb's framework: "God promised"—ten's framework: "we're not strong enough." Same facts, different faith, different conclusions.

**Unbelief's Root—Heart Not Eyes**
Spies' "seeing is believing" yet rejecting God's word—the Reformed tradition teaches faith transcends sensory experience. Westminster Confession chapter 14 on saving faith: By this faith, a Christian believeth to be true whatsoever is revealed in the Word...and yieldeth obedience to the commands thereof. Unbelief was not from lack of evidence—Israel had the exodus, cloud pillar, manna, countless evidence. Unbelief was heart's choice: preferring to believe visible difficulties over God's promises.

**Promise Already Given—Action Is Obedience**
God said "which I am giving"—not "which I am considering giving." Reformed tradition teaches God's sovereignty and human responsibility in parallel. Westminster Confession chapter 3 on God's eternal decrees. The promise was certain—but Israel needed to receive it through action. Inaction was unbelief.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第十三章的"两种报告"在新约有深刻的对应。

迦勒和约书亚的少数信心——基督的道路总是窄门。马太福音7:13-14"通往灭亡，那门是宽的，路是大的，进去的人也多；引到永生，那门是窄的，路是小的，找着的人也少。"在十二人中，只有两人有信心——少数人走窄门。

"自比蚱蜢"——骄傲的悖论。十个探子说"据我们看，自己就如蚱蜢一样"——这听起来像谦卑，实际上是不信。他们没有把自己比作"神手里的工具"——而是比作"蚱蜢"。真正的谦卑不是自我贬低——而是把一切倚靠转移到神身上。保罗说"我靠着那加给我力量的，凡事都能做"（腓4:13）——不是"我很强"，也不是"我如蚱蜢"，而是"靠主能做"。

"迦南地已经是神赐给的"——在基督里，我们已经有了一切属灵的福分。以弗所书1:3"他在基督里曾赐给我们天上各样属灵的福气。"不是"将来可能会有"——而是"已经赐给"。我们进入这些福分，如同以色列进入迦南——不是靠自己的力量，而是靠神的应许和圣灵的引导。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 13's "two reports" has profound New Testament counterparts.

Caleb and Joshua's minority faith—Christ's path is always the narrow gate. Matthew 7:13-14: "For the gate is wide and the way is easy that leads to destruction...for the gate is narrow and the way is hard that leads to life, and those who find it are few." Among twelve, only two had faith—the minority taking the narrow way.

"Like grasshoppers"—pride's paradox. Ten spies said "we seemed to ourselves like grasshoppers"—this sounds like humility but was actually unbelief. They did not compare themselves to "tools in God's hands" but to "grasshoppers." True humility is not self-deprecation—it is transferring all reliance to God. Paul said "I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me." (Phil 4:13)—not "I am strong," not "I am a grasshopper," but "through the Lord I can."

"Canaan already given by God"—in Christ, we already have all spiritual blessings. Ephesians 1:3: "He has blessed us in Christ with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places." Not "may possibly have in the future"—but "already blessed." We enter these blessings as Israel entered Canaan—not by our own strength, but by God's promise and the Spirit's guidance.
