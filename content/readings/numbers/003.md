## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第三章讲的是利未支派的组织和一个极其深刻的神学原则——替代。利未人替代了以色列所有的长子。

第三章以一个痛苦的回忆开头——"拿答、亚比户在西奈的旷野向耶和华献凡火的时候就死在耶和华面前了。他们也没有儿子。"弟兄姐妹，亚伦的四个儿子中两个已经死了（利未记第10章），而且"没有儿子"——他们的支脉断绝了。只剩下以利亚撒和以他玛继续祭司的职分。这个回忆放在利未人组织架构的开头——提醒利未人：圣职不是特权，而是要你的命的责任。不按照规矩来，你就会死。

然后是利未人的角色定义——"你要将利未人给亚伦和他的儿子"。弟兄姐妹，利未人不是祭司——他们是祭司的助手。祭司是亚伦的后裔（以利亚撒和以他玛的家族），利未人是整个利未支派的其他成员。祭司献祭——利未人搬运、看守、清洁、维护会幕。

利未人有三个家族，按照利未的三个儿子分：革顺、哥辖、米拉利。

革顺家族——在会幕的西边安营。负责帐幕的罩棚、门帘和幔子——就是会幕外层的布料和绳索。人数7,500。

哥辖家族——在会幕的南边安营。负责约柜、桌子、灯台、两座坛和圣所的器皿——这是最神圣的器具。人数8,600。

米拉利家族——在会幕的北边安营。负责帐幕的板、闩、柱子和带卯的座——就是会幕的骨架和框架结构。人数6,200。

弟兄姐妹，注意哥辖家族负责最神圣的器具（包括约柜）——他们的位置也最靠近帐幕入口。但这个特权也带着最大的危险——撒母耳记下6章乌撒伸手扶约柜被神击杀——碰触圣物是致命的。

然后是第三章最核心的神学宣告——替代：

> "我从以色列人中拣选了利未人，代替以色列人一切头生的；利未人要归我。因为凡头生的是我的；我在埃及地击杀一切头生的那日就把以色列中一切头生的，连人带牲畜都分别为圣归我。"

弟兄姐妹，这段话把我们带回了出埃及记的逾越节之夜。那一夜，神击杀了埃及所有的长子——但以色列的长子因为羔羊的血而被"逾越"（保全了）。从那一刻起，以色列所有的长子都属于神——因为神用羔羊的血赎了他们的命。

但现在，神做了一个替换——用整个利未支派来"代替"以色列所有的长子。利未人有22,000人——以色列长子有22,273人。多出的273人怎么办？每人交五舍客勒的赎银——总计1,365舍客勒。

弟兄姐妹，这个替代制度传达了一个极其深刻的原则：在神面前，一个人可以代替另一个人。你的长子本该归神——但利未人替他去了。替代——substitution——这是利未记和民数记一再出现的核心概念。祭牲代替罪人被杀（利1:4），赎罪日的山羊代替以色列承担罪（利16:21-22），现在利未人代替长子归给神。

弟兄姐妹，整个旧约的替代制度都是影子——它们指向那位终极的替代者。以赛亚书53:5"他为我们的过犯受害，为我们的罪孽压伤。"基督是终极的替代——不是利未人代替长子，而是神的儿子代替全人类。

利未人的数点标准与其他支派不同——"从一个月以外的男子"，而不是二十岁以上。弟兄姐妹，利未人从婴儿期就被算作属神的——他们的身份不是长大后自己选择的，而是从出生就被神认定的。这与改革宗传统的婴儿洗礼观呼应——盟约的孩子从出生就属于神的百姓。

弟兄姐妹，第三章教导我们：替代不是廉价的——它是有代价的。利未人放弃了分地的权利（他们没有自己的领土），放弃了打仗的权利（他们不参加军队），一生侍奉帐幕。他们的"自由"被交换成了"服事"。但这个代价换来了什么？换来了以色列所有长子的自由——换来了整个民族可以在神面前存活。

弟兄姐妹，基督的替代也是有代价的——而且代价无限大。腓立比书2:6-8"他本有神的形象……反倒虚己，取了奴仆的形象……存心顺服，以至于死，且死在十字架上。"

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 3 addresses the Levite tribe's organization and an extraordinarily profound theological principle—substitution. The Levites substituted for all of Israel's firstborn.

Chapter 3 opened with a painful memory—"Nadab and Abihu died before the LORD when they offered unauthorized fire before the LORD in the wilderness of Sinai, and they had no children." Brothers and sisters, two of Aaron's four sons were already dead (Leviticus 10), and "had no children"—their line was cut off. Only Eleazar and Ithamar continued the priestly office. This memory placed at the head of the Levite organizational structure reminded Levites: sacred office was not privilege but life-threatening responsibility. Failure to follow protocol meant death.

Then the Levites' role defined—"Bring the tribe of Levi near, and set them before Aaron the priest, that they may minister to him." Brothers and sisters, Levites were not priests—they were priests' assistants. Priests were Aaron's descendants (Eleazar and Ithamar's families); Levites were the rest of the tribe. Priests sacrificed—Levites transported, guarded, cleaned, and maintained the tabernacle.

Levites had three clans, divided by Levi's three sons: Gershon, Kohath, Merari.

Gershonites—camped west of the tabernacle. Responsible for tent coverings, curtains, and hangings—the tabernacle's outer fabrics and cords. Number: 7,500.

Kohathites—camped south of the tabernacle. Responsible for the ark, table, lampstand, two altars, and sanctuary vessels—the most sacred furnishings. Number: 8,600.

Merarites—camped north of the tabernacle. Responsible for frames, bars, pillars, and bases—the tabernacle's structural skeleton. Number: 6,200.

Brothers and sisters, note the Kohathites handled the most sacred objects (including the ark)—positioned nearest the tabernacle entrance. But this privilege carried the greatest danger—2 Samuel 6, Uzzah reached out to steady the ark and God struck him dead—touching holy objects was lethal.

Then chapter 3's core theological declaration—substitution:

> "Behold, I have taken the Levites from among the people of Israel instead of every firstborn who opens the womb among the people of Israel. The Levites shall be mine, for all the firstborn are mine. On the day that I struck down all the firstborn in the land of Egypt, I consecrated for my own all the firstborn in Israel."

Brothers and sisters, this passage brought us back to Exodus's Passover night. That night God struck all Egypt's firstborn—but Israel's firstborn were "passed over" (preserved) because of the lamb's blood. From that moment, all Israel's firstborn belonged to God—because God redeemed their lives with the lamb's blood.

Now God made a substitution—the entire tribe of Levi "instead of" all Israel's firstborn. Levites numbered 22,000—Israel's firstborn numbered 22,273. The excess 273? Each paid five shekels redemption silver—totaling 1,365 shekels.

Brothers and sisters, this substitution system conveyed an extraordinarily profound principle: before God, one person could stand in for another. Your firstborn should have belonged to God—but a Levite went in his place. Substitution—this was Leviticus and Numbers' recurring core concept. Sacrificial animals substituted for sinners in death (Lev 1:4), Day of Atonement goats bore Israel's sin as substitutes (Lev 16:21-22), now Levites substituted for firstborn belonging to God.

Brothers and sisters, the entire Old Testament substitution system was shadow—pointing to the ultimate Substitute. Isaiah 53:5: "He was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities." Christ is the ultimate Substitute—not Levites for firstborn, but God's Son for all humanity.

Levite census criteria differed from other tribes—"from a month old and upward," not twenty and above. Brothers and sisters, Levites were counted as God's from infancy—their identity was not self-chosen at maturity but God-designated from birth. This echoes the Reformed tradition's infant baptism view—covenant children belong to God's people from birth.

## 历史背景（中文）

长子归神的传统在古代近东有广泛的宗教背景——许多文化都有向神明献上长子或初产物的做法。以色列的独特之处在于：神不要求以色列人真的把长子杀了献祭（这是摩洛崇拜的做法，利18:21明确禁止），而是用利未人来替代。这是一种"人道化"的长子奉献——用替代取代了实际的牺牲。"五舍客勒"的赎银数目后来成为犹太教"赎长子"仪式（Pidyon HaBen）的标准金额——至今犹太家庭在男婴出生后第31天举行此仪式，用五个银币赎回长子。利未人的三个家族在后来的以色列历史中有不同的命运——哥辖家族出了摩西和亚伦（以及后来的撒母耳），革顺和米拉利家族在大卫时期被组织为圣殿诗班和门卫。

## Historical Context (English)

Firstborn dedication had widespread ancient Near Eastern religious background—many cultures practiced offering firstborn or first-fruits to deities. Israel's uniqueness: God did not require actually sacrificing the firstborn (that was Molech worship, explicitly forbidden in Lev 18:21), but substituted Levites instead. This was a "humanized" firstborn dedication—substitution replacing actual sacrifice. The "five shekels" redemption amount later became the standard for the Jewish Pidyon HaBen (Redemption of the Firstborn) ceremony—to this day Jewish families perform this ceremony on the 31st day after a male infant's birth, using five silver coins to redeem the firstborn. The three Levite clans had different destinies in later Israelite history—Kohathites produced Moses and Aaron (and later Samuel); Gershonites and Merarites were organized as temple singers and gatekeepers in David's era.

## 经文结构（中文）

**亚伦的后代与拿答亚比户之死（1-4节）**——四子中二死无后、以利亚撒以他玛继续供职。

**利未人的角色（5-10节）**——服事亚伦和祭司、办理帐幕的事、归给亚伦。

**替代的原则（11-13节）**——利未人代替以色列一切头生的、因为头生的在逾越节归了神。

**利未三族的数点和职责（14-39节）**——革顺(7,500)西边管罩棚幔子、哥辖(8,600)南边管约柜桌子灯台坛、米拉利(6,200)北边管板闩柱座、总数22,000。

**长子赎银（40-51节）**——以色列长子22,273比利未人多273、每人五舍客勒赎回、共1,365舍客勒交给祭司。

## Structure (English)

**Aaron's Descendants and Nadab/Abihu's Death (vv. 1-4)**—Two of four sons died childless, Eleazar and Ithamar continued.

**Levites' Role (vv. 5-10)**—Serve Aaron and priests, manage tabernacle affairs, given to Aaron.

**Substitution Principle (vv. 11-13)**—Levites instead of all Israel's firstborn, because firstborn became God's at Passover.

**Three Levite Clans' Census and Duties (vv. 14-39)**—Gershon(7,500) west manages coverings curtains, Kohath(8,600) south manages ark table lampstand altars, Merari(6,200) north manages frames bars pillars bases, total 22,000.

**Firstborn Redemption Silver (vv. 40-51)**—Israel's firstborn 22,273 exceed Levites by 273, five shekels each redeemed, 1,365 shekels total to priests.

## 神学要点（中文）

**替代——赎罪的核心机制**
利未人代替长子——改革宗传统把替代性赎罪视为福音的核心。韦敏斯德信条第8章论基督为中保：主耶稣……藉着祂一次的牺牲……充分地满足了父的公义。旧约的替代都是暂时的——利未人替长子、祭牲替罪人——基督的替代是终极的、永远的。

**长子归神——一切都属于神**
逾越节之夜后长子归神——改革宗传统教导万物属于神。韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问"（一）荣耀神；（二）以祂为乐，直到永远。"你的长子——你最珍贵的——属于神。你的一切都是神的。利未人的替代不改变这个原则——它只是改变了执行方式。

**从出生就属神——盟约的身份**
利未人从一个月起就被数点——改革宗传统教导盟约的孩子从出生就属于神。韦敏斯德信条第28章论洗礼：不仅信主本人当受洗，其婴孩也当受洗。利未人的身份不是成年后的选择——是从出生就被神确立的。

## Theology (English)

**Substitution—Atonement's Core Mechanism**
Levites substituted for firstborn—the Reformed tradition views substitutionary atonement as the gospel's core. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ as Mediator: "The Lord Jesus...by His one sacrifice...fully satisfied the justice of His Father." Old Testament substitutions were temporary—Levites for firstborn, animals for sinners—Christ's substitution is ultimate and eternal.

**Firstborn Belong to God—Everything Is God's**
After Passover night firstborn belonged to God—the Reformed tradition teaches all things belong to God. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "Man's chief end is to glorify God." Your firstborn—your most precious—belongs to God. Everything you have is God's. Levitical substitution did not change this principle—only changed the execution method.

**Belonging to God from Birth—Covenant Identity**
Levites counted from one month—the Reformed tradition teaches covenant children belong to God from birth. Westminster Confession chapter 28 on baptism: Not only those that do actually profess faith...but also the infants of believers are to be baptized. Levite identity was not an adult choice—it was established by God from birth.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第三章的替代制度是基督替代性赎罪的最直接的旧约预表之一。

利未人代替长子——基督代替所有罪人。彼得前书3:18"因基督也曾一次为罪受苦，就是义的代替不义的，为要引我们到神面前。""义的代替不义的"——这就是民数记3章替代原则的终极成全。利未人替代长子是一群人替代一群人——基督的替代是一人替代所有人。罗马书5:19"因一人的悖逆，众人成为罪人；照样，因一人的顺从，众人也成为义了。"

五舍客勒的赎银——基督的赎价是无价的。多出的273个长子需要用银子赎回——人的替代总有不够的地方。但基督的救赎是完全的——没有人"多出来"需要另外赎回。希伯来书10:14"因为他一次献祭，便叫那得以成圣的人永远完全。"

长子归神因为逾越节的救赎——信徒归基督因为十字架的救赎。哥林多前书6:19-20"你们不是自己的人，因为你们是重价买来的。所以，要在你们的身子上荣耀神。"弟兄姐妹，逾越节的羔羊救了以色列的长子——十字架的羔羊救了全世界的罪人。你被"买"了——你属于那位用血赎你的神。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 3's substitution system is one of the most direct Old Testament prefigurations of Christ's substitutionary atonement.

Levites substituted for firstborn—Christ substituted for all sinners. 1 Peter 3:18: "For Christ also suffered once for sins, the righteous for the unrighteous, that he might bring us to God." "The righteous for the unrighteous"—this was Numbers 3's substitution principle's ultimate fulfillment. Levites substituting for firstborn was one group for another—Christ's substitution was one Person for all. Romans 5:19: "For as by the one man's disobedience the many were made sinners, so by the one man's obedience the many will be made righteous."

Five shekels redemption silver—Christ's redemption price was priceless. The excess 273 firstborn needed monetary redemption—human substitution always fell short. But Christ's redemption was complete—no one "left over" needing separate ransom. Hebrews 10:14: "For by a single offering he has perfected for all time those who are being sanctified."

Firstborn belonged to God because of Passover's redemption—believers belong to Christ because of the cross's redemption. 1 Corinthians 6:19-20: "You are not your own, for you were bought with a price. So glorify God in your body." Brothers and sisters, the Passover lamb saved Israel's firstborn—the cross's Lamb saved the world's sinners. You were "bought"—you belong to the God who redeemed you with blood.
