## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十二章开始了民数记最奇特的故事——巴兰和他的驴。以色列人在摩押平原安营，离约旦河、离迦南只有一步之遥——但故事视角突然转移，我们来到了摩押王巴勒的宫廷，然后来到了先知巴兰的家门口。

**巴勒的恐惧（1-6节）**

"摩押人因以色列民甚多，就大大惧怕，心内忧急。"弟兄姐妹，巴勒看见了以色列人如何击败了亚摩利王西宏和巴珊王噩——现在这个庞大的队伍安营在他的边界旁。恐惧使他想到了一个计谋：召来先知巴兰，用咒诅代替战争。

"求你来为我咒诅他们，或者我能得胜，攻打他们，赶出此地。因为我知道，你为谁祝福，谁就得福；你咒诅谁，谁就受咒诅。"弟兄姐妹，巴兰的名声显然已经传遍了古代近东——他是一个被认为有超自然力量的先知。巴勒相信，在战争之前通过咒诅破坏敌人的灵性保护，是有效的。这是古代近东的"神圣战争"概念——先在属灵层面击败对手，然后军事上再追击。

**巴兰的第一次拒绝（7-14节）**

使臣带着"卦金"（占卜的酬劳）到了巴兰那里。巴兰说："你们今夜在这里住宿，我必照耶和华所晓谕我的回报你们。"

弟兄姐妹，注意——巴兰说"耶和华"（YHWH）——以色列神的名字！他不是一个迦南的多神论者，他与以色列的神有某种交通。神对他说："你不可同他们去，也不可咒诅那民，因为那民是蒙福的。"

巴兰回报了使臣："耶和华不容我和你们同去。"第一次是拒绝。巴勒的使臣回去了。

**更大的诱惑（15-21节）**

巴勒不死心——派了地位更高的使臣，带来更大的承诺："我必大大尊荣你，你向我说甚么，我都依从，只求你来咒诅这民。"

巴兰说了一句听起来很虔诚的话："巴勒就是将他满屋的金银给我，我行大事小事也不得越过耶和华我神的命。"然后——他又去问神了！弟兄姐妹，当答案已经说清楚了（"不可去"），为什么还要再问一次？因为他在寻找神改口的可能性。

神给了一个条件式的许可："这些人若来召你，你就起来同他们去，你只要遵行我对你所说的话。"弟兄姐妹，有解经家认为这里神是在容许巴兰去但已经不满意他的心态——神的话是"你只要遵行我对你说的话"——已经预示了将要发生的事。

**驴和使者——神在路上的拦截（22-41节）**

"神因他去就发了怒"——神说了可以去，但神又发怒了？弟兄姐妹，这里反映了一个重要的神学真理：神有时容许人做祂并不喜悦的事，但祂的容许不等于祂的祝福。巴兰可以去，但他去的动机（贪财、追求尊荣）是神所不喜悦的。

耶和华的使者拿着刀站在路上拦截他。驴三次看见使者——三次转道：第一次走进田间，第二次靠墙挤压巴兰的脚，第三次直接卧倒在地下。

每一次，巴兰都打驴——他的眼睛看不见使者，他只看见驴"不正常"的行为。

然后神叫驴开口说话——"我向你行了什么，你竟打我这三次呢？"弟兄姐妹，一头驴说话了！巴兰居然没有被吓到——他和驴争辩！"因为你戏弄我，我恨不能手中有刀，把你杀了。"

然后神开了巴兰的眼——他看见了使者，俯伏在地。使者对他说："你为何这三次打你的驴呢？我出来敌挡你，因你所行的，在我面前偏僻。"

弟兄姐妹，这个故事的奇异之处——在于一个被认为能"看见"神界的先知，被自己的驴所看见的神界拦住了。驴的眼睛比先知更灵敏。有时候，神用最卑微的方式拦住人——通过一头驴，通过一个孩子，通过一句朴素的话。

使者最终说："你同这些人去罢！你只要说我对你说的话。"弟兄姐妹，巴兰可以去——但他的嘴巴不属于他自己。他要去说神的话，无论那是什么。巴勒以为他召来了一个可以按命令咒诅的工具——他得到的是一个被神管控的嘴巴。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 22 began Numbers' most unusual story—Balaam and his donkey. Israel camped on the plains of Moab, one step from the Jordan, from Canaan—but the narrative perspective suddenly shifted to Moabite king Balak's court, then to prophet Balaam's doorstep.

**Balak's Fear (vv. 1-6)**

"Moab was in great dread of the people, because they were many. Moab was overcome with fear." Brothers and sisters, Balak had seen how Israel defeated Sihon king of the Amorites and Og king of Bashan—now this enormous multitude was camped on his border. Fear led him to a stratagem: summon prophet Balaam, substitute curses for war.

"Come now, curse this people for me, since they are too mighty for me. Perhaps I shall be able to defeat them and drive them from the land, for I know that he whom you bless is blessed, and he whom you curse is cursed." Brothers and sisters, Balaam's reputation had clearly spread throughout the ancient Near East—he was a prophet believed to have supernatural power. Balak believed that through cursing before battle, breaking the enemy's spiritual protection, was effective. This was the ancient Near East's "sacred warfare" concept—first defeat the opponent spiritually, then pursue militarily.

**Balaam's First Refusal (vv. 7-14)**

Envoys arrived with "fees for divination." Balaam said: "Lodge here tonight, and I will bring back word to you, as the LORD speaks to me."

Brothers and sisters, note—Balaam said "the LORD" (YHWH)—Israel's God's name! He was not a Canaanite polytheist; he had some communication with Israel's God. God told him: "You shall not go with them. You shall not curse the people, for they are blessed."

Balaam reported to the envoys: "The LORD refuses to let me go with you." First refusal. Balak's envoys left.

**Greater Temptation (vv. 15-21)**

Balak would not give up—he sent higher-ranking envoys with greater promises: "I will surely do you great honor, and whatever you say to me I will do. Come, curse this people for me."

Balaam said something sounding pious: "Though Balak were to give me his house full of silver and gold, I could not go beyond the command of the LORD my God." Then—he went to ask God again! Brothers and sisters, when the answer was already clear ("do not go"), why ask again? Because he was seeking the possibility of God changing His answer.

God gave a conditional permission: "If the men have come to call you, rise, go with them; but only do what I tell you." Brothers and sisters, some interpreters believe here God was permitting Balaam while already displeased with his attitude—God's words "only do what I tell you" already foreshadowed what would happen.

**The Donkey and the Angel—God's Roadblock (vv. 22-41)**

"God's anger was kindled because he went"—God said he could go, yet God was angry? Brothers and sisters, this reflects an important theological truth: God sometimes permits people to do what He does not delight in, but His permission does not equal His blessing. Balaam could go, but his motivation (greed, pursuit of honor) was what God disapproved.

The angel of the LORD stood in the road with a drawn sword. The donkey saw the angel three times—three times turned aside: first into the field, then pressed against the wall crushing Balaam's foot, then lay down in the road.

Each time, Balaam struck the donkey—his eyes could not see the angel; he only saw the donkey's "abnormal" behavior.

Then God opened the donkey's mouth—"What have I done to you, that you have struck me these three times?" Brothers and sisters, a donkey spoke! And Balaam was apparently not startled—he argued with the donkey! "Because you have made a fool of me. I wish I had a sword in my hand, for then I would kill you."

Then God opened Balaam's eyes—he saw the angel, fell on his face. The angel said: "Why have you struck your donkey these three times? Behold, I have come out to oppose you because your way is perverse before me."

Brothers and sisters, this story's strangeness—a prophet believed able to "see" the divine realm, was stopped by his donkey who could see what the prophet couldn't. The donkey's eyes were keener than the prophet's. Sometimes God blocks people in the most humble ways—through a donkey, through a child, through a simple word.

The angel finally said: "Go with the men, but speak only the word that I tell you." Brothers and sisters, Balaam could go—but his mouth did not belong to him. He would speak God's words, whatever they were. Balak thought he had summoned a curse-on-command tool—he got a God-controlled mouth.

## 历史背景（中文）

巴兰（Balaam ben Beor）是旧约中最神秘的人物之一——他不是以色列人，却能与以色列的神（耶和华/YHWH）直接交通。他可能是古代近东的职业占卜师/先知，来自幼发拉底河附近的毘夺（今叙利亚北部）。1967年，考古学家在约旦的Deir Alla遗址发现了一段公元前约800年的阿拉米文文本，记载了"比珥之子巴兰"（Balaam son of Beor）的预言——与圣经中的人物名字完全相符。这是圣经人物在圣经外文献中最早的独立考古印证之一。彼得后书2:15、犹大书11和启示录2:14都提到巴兰作为"为利害道"的假先知的代表——他的名字成了为金钱出卖神圣使命的象征。驴开口说话的故事——彼得后书2:16"他却被无语的驴驾驭，驴以人的声音说话，止住了先知的狂妄。"古代近东文献中有动物说话的神话，但圣经中动物开口说人话只有两次：创世记3章的蛇和这里的驴。

## Historical Context (English)

Balaam (son of Beor) was one of the Old Testament's most mysterious figures—not an Israelite, yet directly communicating with Israel's God (YHWH). He was likely an ancient Near Eastern professional diviner/prophet from Pethor near the Euphrates (today's northern Syria). In 1967, archaeologists at Jordan's Deir Alla site discovered an Aramaic text dated to approximately 800 BC recording "Balaam son of Beor's" prophecy—perfectly matching the biblical figure's name. This is one of the earliest independent archaeological corroborations of a biblical figure in non-biblical literature. 2 Peter 2:15, Jude 11, and Revelation 2:14 all mention Balaam as the archetypal false prophet "who loved gain from wrongdoing"—his name became the symbol of selling sacred mission for money. The donkey speaking—2 Peter 2:16: "he was rebuked for his own transgression; a speechless donkey spoke with human voice and restrained the prophet's madness." Ancient Near Eastern literature has talking animals in mythology, but the Bible records animals speaking in human voice only twice: Genesis 3's serpent and this donkey.

## Structure (English)

**Moabite King Balak's Fear and Strategy (vv. 1-6)**—Israel camps on Moab's plains → Balak fears → sends envoys to summon curse-prophet Balaam.

**Balaam's First Refusal (vv. 7-14)**—Envoys arrive with divination fees → Balaam consults God → God says "do not go, the people are blessed" → envoys return.

**Greater Envoys and Conditional Permission (vv. 15-21)**—Higher-ranking envoys with greater honor promises → Balaam asks again → God says "only do what I say" → Balaam departs.

**Donkey's Three Roadblocks and Angel's Appearance (vv. 22-35)**—God angered by his going → angel with drawn sword blocks path → donkey turns aside three times → Balaam strikes donkey three times → donkey speaks → Balaam's eyes opened → angel rebukes → angel sends him but only to speak God's words.

**Balak Welcomes and First Meeting (vv. 36-41)**—Balak comes to meet → Balaam says "I can only speak what God conveys to me."

## 神学要点（中文）

**神控制仇敌的咒诅——神的保护**
巴勒用咒诅对付以色列——改革宗传统教导神的护理保护祂的百姓。韦敏斯德信条第5章论神的护理。罗马书8:31"神若帮助我们，谁能敌挡我们呢？"巴兰无法咒诅蒙神祝福的民——这是护理神学的实践：所有想伤害神选民的力量，最终都在神的主权之下被翻转。

**条件性允许与神的不悦——隐藏的旨意与启示的旨意**
神允许巴兰去，却因他去而发怒——改革宗传统区分神的隐藏旨意（secret will）和启示旨意（revealed will）。韦敏斯德信条第3章。神有时允许人做祂并不喜悦的事，同时执行祂更大的计划。巴兰的贪心是真实的，神的愤怒也是真实的——但神仍然用这一切成就祂的旨意。

**眼睛被打开——属灵的眼睛**
驴的眼睛看见使者，巴兰的眼睛看不见——改革宗传统教导属灵辨别力是神的恩赐。列王纪下6:17以利沙祷告"愿耶和华开这仆人的眼目，使他能看见"。属灵的眼睛不是自然的能力——是神的恩赐。巴兰有先知的名声，却比驴更瞎——属灵地位不等于属灵眼睛。

## Theology (English)

**God Controls Enemies' Curses—Divine Protection**
Balak used curses against Israel—the Reformed tradition teaches God's providence protecting His people. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence. Romans 8:31: If God is for us, who can be against us? Balaam could not curse people blessed by God—this is providence theology in practice: all forces seeking to harm God's elect are ultimately reversed under God's sovereignty.

**Conditional Permission and God's Displeasure—Secret vs. Revealed Will**
God permitted Balaam to go yet was angered by his going—the Reformed tradition distinguishes God's secret will and revealed will. Westminster Confession chapter 3. God sometimes permits people to do what He does not delight in, while executing His greater plan. Balaam's greed was real, God's anger was real—but God still used all of it to accomplish His purpose.

**Eyes Opened—Spiritual Eyes**
The donkey's eyes saw the angel, Balaam's could not—the Reformed tradition teaches spiritual discernment is God's gift. 2 Kings 6:17: Elisha prayed "O LORD, please open his eyes that he may see." Spiritual eyes are not natural capacity—they are God's gift. Balaam had a prophet's reputation yet was blinder than his donkey—spiritual status does not equal spiritual sight.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第二十二章预表了基督在两个方面。

**神管控巴兰的嘴——圣灵管控先知**
巴兰无法按自己的意思说话——他只能说神传给他的话。约翰福音16:13"真理的圣灵来了，他要引导你们明白一切的真理；他不是凭自己说的，乃是把他所听见的都说出来，并要把将来的事告诉你们。"圣灵不随人意说话——祂传讲神的话语。旧约先知（包括巴兰这样的外邦先知）的"被控制"，预表了新约圣灵的工作——圣灵管控信徒的口，使他们说神所喜悦的话。

**路上的天使——基督是道路上的守卫**
天使拦截巴兰——约翰福音14:6"我就是道路、真理、生命。"基督是道路上的守卫——祂知道哪条路是偏僻的，哪条路通向生命。巴兰的路"在神面前偏僻"——神出来拦截。弟兄姐妹，有时候神不顺我们的意愿，不是因为祂不爱我们——而是因为我们的路"在祂面前偏僻"。天使拦住了巴兰才救了他——神的拦截常常是恩典的形式。

**被神管控的咒诅变为祝福——十字架的逆转**
巴勒召巴兰来咒诅以色列——结果巴兰只能为以色列祝福（第二十三、二十四章将展示这个逆转）。这预表了十字架：敌对势力意图诅咒基督、消灭福音——但十字架本身变成了最大的祝福。腓立比书2:8-9"他自己卑微，存心顺服，以至于死，且死在十字架上。所以神将他升为至高。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 22 prefigures Christ in two aspects.

**God Controls Balaam's Mouth—The Spirit Controls Prophets**
Balaam could not speak as he wished—only what God conveyed to him. John 16:13: "When the Spirit of truth comes, he will guide you into all the truth, for he will not speak on his own authority, but whatever he hears he will speak, and he will declare to you the things that are to come." The Spirit does not speak at human discretion—He transmits God's words. Old Testament prophets' (including Balaam the Gentile prophet's) being "controlled" prefigures the New Testament Spirit's work—the Spirit controls believers' mouths, enabling them to speak what pleases God.

**The Angel on the Road—Christ Guards the Way**
The angel intercepted Balaam—John 14:6: "I am the way, and the truth, and the life." Christ is the road's guardian—He knows which path is perverse, which leads to life. Balaam's path "was perverse before God"—God came out to oppose it. Brothers and sisters, sometimes God doesn't comply with our wishes—not because He doesn't love us, but because our path "is perverse before Him." The angel stopping Balaam actually saved him—God's opposition is often grace's form.

**God-Controlled Curses Becoming Blessings—The Cross's Reversal**
Balak summoned Balaam to curse Israel—but Balaam could only bless Israel (chapters 23 and 24 will display this reversal). This prefigures the cross: hostile forces intended to curse Christ and eliminate the gospel—but the cross itself became the greatest blessing. Philippians 2:8-9: "He humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross. Therefore God has highly exalted him."
