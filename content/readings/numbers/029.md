## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十九章是以色列节期日历的高潮——第七个月的三个大节：七月初一的吹角节、七月初十的赎罪日、七月十五开始的住棚节（七天加第八天的大会）。如果说第二十八章是年历的平日和春节，第二十九章就是年历中最神圣、最隆重的秋收节期群。

**吹角节（七月初一，1-6节）**

> "七月初一日，你们当有圣会；什么劳碌的工都不可做，是你们当守为吹角的日子。"

弟兄姐妹，吹角节（利23:24称"纪念吹角"，后来发展为犹太新年Rosh Hashanah）是第七个月的开始。第七个月在以色列历法中是最神圣的一个月——安息年的循环终点、赎罪日、住棚节，都在这个月。吹角节用号角的声音宣告这个神圣月份的开始。

号角（shofar）的声音——牛角号的嘹亮声音——在旷野中代表了多种意义：神在西奈山显现时号角吹响（出19:16）；安息年和禧年开始时号角吹响（利25:9）；争战的集结号；年末审判的警告。号角一吹——以色列人知道："重要的事情要发生了。"

弟兄姐妹，吹角节的神学含义是"唤醒和预备"。在赎罪日前十天（这十天犹太传统称"敬畏日"）——号角开始提醒人们：审判临近，要悔改预备。吹角节与赎罪日的关系：预警+准备→然后罪得赦免。

**赎罪日（七月初十，7-11节）**

> "七月初十日，你们当有圣会；要刻苦己心，什么工都不可做。"

弟兄姐妹，赎罪日（Yom Kippur）是以色列宗教年历中最神圣的一天——利未记16章已经详细规定了大祭司在这天的仪式（两只山羊：一只献祭、一只作替罪羊驱逐到旷野）。第二十九章只补充了在常祭之外额外的燔祭规定——公牛犊一只、公羊一只、七只羊羔、一只公山羊。

"刻苦己心"——禁食、自我克制、悔罪。这一天是全以色列集体的认罪和赎罪——大祭司进入至圣所，一年一次，为全体以色列民赎罪。

赎罪日是旧约最核心的神学时刻——整个以色列的罪被大祭司象征性地"搬运"到旷野的替罪羊身上，带走。弟兄姐妹，这一天不是自我的成就——是对自己的彻底否定和对神赦罪的彻底依赖。

**住棚节（七月十五至二十二，12-38节）**

> "七月十五日，你们当有圣会；什么劳碌的工都不可做，要向耶和华守节七日。"

弟兄姐妹，住棚节是以色列三大朝圣节期之一（逾越节、五旬节、住棚节）。这七天，以色列人住在用树枝搭成的棚子里——纪念旷野漂流时住帐棚的生活（利23:43"因为我将以色列人从埃及地领出来的时候，曾使他们住在棚里"）。

住棚节最惊人的特点是它的牛的数量——从第一天的十三只公牛开始，每天递减一只：十三、十二、十一……直到第七天七只。七天合计七十只公牛（加上公羊14只、羊羔98只）。弟兄姐妹，七十只公牛——这是以色列节期中最大规模的献祭。

为什么是七十只？犹太传统说：七十代表七十个外邦国家——以色列在住棚节代表全人类向神献祭。住棚节不只是以色列的节期——它有全球性的眼光：以色列是神祭司国度，代表万国献祭。撒迦利亚书14:16-17预言末世"列国中剩下的人，都要年年上来，跪拜大君王万军之耶和华，并守住棚节"。

**第八天：大会（35-38节）**

七天之后——"第八天，你们当有严肃会；什么劳碌的工都不可做。"第八天（Shemini Atzeret）是住棚节结束后额外的一天圣会——只献公牛一只、公羊一只、七只羊羔（比任何一天都少）。

弟兄姐妹，从十三只公牛递减到一只——然后是第八天。第七天是完结，第八天是新的开始。七是完全，八是新创造。弟兄姐妹，八是复活的数字——耶稣在七日后的第一日（即第八天）复活。住棚节的第八天——在万国朝圣之后——有一个属于神自己的特别安静的最后一天：只有一只公牛，不是大宴会，而是神与人最亲密的相聚。

**结语（39-40节）**

"这些祭要在你们的节期献给耶和华，都在所许的愿并甘心所献的以外。"弟兄姐妹，这些节期献祭是"规定的最低要求"——许的愿和甘心的奉献是另外的。神不是要以色列人满足于最低限度——敬拜没有上限。

弟兄姐妹，整个节期日历告诉我们：信仰不是私人的灵修，而是有节奏的、有结构的、有群体的生命。从每天的tamid，到每周的安息日，到每月的月朔，到每年的春节（逾越节/五旬节）和秋节（吹角节/赎罪日/住棚节）——生命的每个维度都在神面前被标记和圣化。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 29 was the climax of Israel's festival calendar—the seventh month's three great festivals: the first day's Feast of Trumpets, the tenth day's Day of Atonement, and the fifteenth day's Feast of Tabernacles (seven days plus an eighth day's solemn assembly). If chapter 28 was the calendar's ordinary days and spring festivals, chapter 29 was the calendar's most holy and solemn autumn festival cluster.

**Feast of Trumpets—First Day of Seventh Month (vv. 1-6)**

> "On the first day of the seventh month you shall have a holy convocation. You shall not do any ordinary work. It is a day for you to blow the trumpets."

Brothers and sisters, the Feast of Trumpets (Leviticus 23:24 called it "a memorial of blowing of trumpets," later developing into the Jewish New Year Rosh Hashanah) opened the seventh month. The seventh month was Israel's holiest month—the sabbatical cycle's endpoint, the Day of Atonement, the Feast of Tabernacles all fell within it. The Feast of Trumpets proclaimed this holy month's beginning with trumpet sounds.

The trumpet's sound (shofar)—the clear sound of a ram's horn—represented multiple meanings in the wilderness: trumpets sounded when God appeared at Sinai (Exod 19:16); trumpets sounded to begin sabbatical and jubilee years (Lev 25:9); war's assembly call; end-year judgment's warning. When trumpets sounded—Israel knew: "Something important is about to happen."

Brothers and sisters, the Feast of Trumpets' theological meaning was "awakening and preparation." In the ten days before the Day of Atonement (called "Days of Awe" in Jewish tradition)—trumpets began reminding people: judgment approaches, repent and prepare. The relationship between Feast of Trumpets and Day of Atonement: warning + preparation → then sins forgiven.

**Day of Atonement—Seventh Month Tenth Day (vv. 7-11)**

> "On the tenth day of this seventh month you shall have a holy convocation and afflict yourselves. You shall not do any work."

Brothers and sisters, the Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur) was the holiest day in Israel's religious calendar—Leviticus 16 already detailed the high priest's rituals (two goats: one sacrificed, one as scapegoat driven into the wilderness). Chapter 29 only added the additional burnt offering regulations beyond the regular offering—one young bull, one ram, seven lambs, one male goat.

"Afflict yourselves"—fasting, self-restraint, confession. This was all Israel's collective confession and atonement—the high priest entered the Most Holy Place, once a year, to atone for all Israel.

The Day of Atonement was the Old Testament's most central theological moment—all Israel's sins were symbolically "transferred" by the high priest to the wilderness scapegoat, carried away. Brothers and sisters, this day was not personal achievement—but complete self-denial and complete dependence on God's forgiveness.

**Feast of Tabernacles—Seventh Month Fifteenth Through Twenty-Second (vv. 12-38)**

> "On the fifteenth day of the seventh month you shall have a holy convocation. You shall not do any ordinary work, and you shall keep a feast to the LORD seven days."

Brothers and sisters, the Feast of Tabernacles was one of Israel's three pilgrimage festivals (Passover, Pentecost, Tabernacles). For these seven days, Israelites lived in booths made of branches—commemorating wilderness life in tents (Lev 23:43: "I made the people of Israel dwell in booths when I brought them out of the land of Egypt").

Tabernacles' most striking feature was its bulls—starting with thirteen on day one, decreasing by one daily: thirteen, twelve, eleven...until seven on day seven. Seven days totaled seventy bulls (plus fourteen rams, ninety-eight lambs). Brothers and sisters, seventy bulls—the largest sacrifice of any Israelite festival.

Why seventy? Jewish tradition says: seventy represented the seventy Gentile nations—Israel at Tabernacles offered sacrifices on behalf of all humanity. Tabernacles was not only Israel's festival—it had global vision: Israel as a priestly kingdom, offering on behalf of all nations. Zechariah 14:16-17 prophesied in the last days "everyone who survives of all the nations that have come against Jerusalem shall go up year after year to worship the King, the LORD of hosts, and to keep the Feast of Booths."

**Eighth Day: Solemn Assembly (vv. 35-38)**

After the seven days—"On the eighth day you shall have a solemn assembly; you shall not do any ordinary work." The eighth day (Shemini Atzeret) was an additional holy convocation after Tabernacles—only one bull, one ram, seven lambs (fewer than any other day).

Brothers and sisters, from thirteen bulls decreasing to one—then the eighth day. Day seven was completion, day eight a new beginning. Seven is completeness, eight is new creation. Brothers and sisters, eight is resurrection's number—Jesus rose on the first day after seven days (i.e., the eighth day). Tabernacles' eighth day—after all nations' pilgrimage—had one final, specially quiet day belonging to God Himself: only one bull, not a grand feast, but the most intimate gathering between God and people.

**Closing Statement (vv. 39-40)**

"These you shall offer to the LORD at your appointed feasts, in addition to your vow offerings and your freewill offerings." Brothers and sisters, these festival offerings were "the minimum required"—vows and freewill offerings were additional. God did not want Israel satisfied with minimum requirements—worship has no ceiling.

Brothers and sisters, the entire festival calendar tells us: faith is not private devotion but rhythmically structured communal life. From the daily tamid, to weekly Sabbath, to monthly New Moon, to annual spring festivals (Passover/Pentecost) and autumn festivals (Trumpets/Atonement/Tabernacles)—every dimension of life is marked and sanctified before God.

## 历史背景（中文）

住棚节七天七十只公牛的总量——成为犹太传统中"以色列代表七十国献祭"的重要依据。塔木德（Sukkah 55b）明确说："七十头公牛对应七十个国家。"这个解释在教父文献中也被引用（耶柔米等人提到以色列在住棚节为外邦人祈祷）。赎罪日（Yom Kippur）在现代犹太人中仍是最重要的宗教节期——即使不怎么守宗教的犹太人也通常在赎罪日禁食并进会堂。1973年的赎罪日战争（Yom Kippur War）之所以得名，是因为埃及和叙利亚选择在以色列人最为集中禁食祈祷的这一天发动突袭。住棚节（Sukkot）的棚子至今仍被以色列人（和全球守节的基督徒）搭建——以色列全国在十月可以看到家家户户院子里的棚子。约翰福音7:37-38发生在住棚节期间——"节期的末日，就是最大之日，耶稣站着高声说：人若渴了，可以到我这里来喝。"

## Historical Context (English)

The seventy bulls total across Tabernacles' seven days became the important basis for the Jewish tradition of "Israel offering sacrifices representing seventy nations." The Talmud (Sukkah 55b) explicitly states: "Seventy bulls correspond to seventy nations." This interpretation was also cited in patristic literature (Jerome and others mentioned Israel praying for Gentiles during Tabernacles). The Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur) remains the most important religious holiday for modern Jews—even less observant Jews typically fast and attend synagogue on Yom Kippur. The 1973 Yom Kippur War was named because Egypt and Syria chose to launch their surprise attack on the day Israelis were most concentrated in fasting and prayer. The Feast of Tabernacles' booths are still built today by Israelites (and globally by observant Christians)—during October one can see booths in courtyards throughout Israel. John 7:37-38 took place during Tabernacles—"On the last day of the feast, the great day, Jesus stood up and cried out, 'If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink.'"

## Structure (English)

**Feast of Trumpets (Month 7, Day 1, vv. 1-6)**—Holy convocation, blow trumpets, no work → one bull + one ram + seven lambs + one goat (sin).

**Day of Atonement (Month 7, Day 10, vv. 7-11)**—Holy convocation, afflict yourselves, no work → one bull + one ram + seven lambs + one goat.

**Feast of Tabernacles Seven Days (Month 7, Days 15-21, vv. 12-34)**—Day 1: 13 bulls, decreasing by one each day: 13→12→11→10→9→8→7; daily + 2 rams + 14 lambs + 1 goat (sin). Seven days' 70 bulls represent 70 nations.

**Eighth Day Solemn Assembly (vv. 35-38)**—1 bull + 1 ram + 7 lambs + 1 goat.

**Closing (vv. 39-40)**—Festival offerings beyond vows and freewill; Moses tells Israel.

## 神学要点（中文）

**第七个月——圣洁时间的高峰**
三个节期集中在第七个月——改革宗传统教导神对时间的主权。韦敏斯德信条第21章。安息年的七年循环、安息日的七天循环、第七个月的神圣集中——七是神完全的标记。弟兄姐妹，我们不是活在中性时间里——我们活在神主宰的时间里。每个第七天、每个圣化的时刻——都是宇宙节奏的一部分。

**住棚节代表七十国——普世性的盼望**
七十只公牛代表七十国——改革宗传统教导神的救赎有普世性。韦敏斯德信条第35章论末世。以色列是祭司国度不是排他性的民族——"万国的光"。撒迦利亚书14:16末世住棚节的普世性预言指向了最终万国朝圣的实现。

**赎罪日的集体悔罪——全会众的认罪**
赎罪日是以色列集体刻苦己心——改革宗传统教导公共认罪的重要性。尼希米记9章、但以理书9章都有集体认罪的范例。教会的公共崇拜中包含集体的认罪——不只是个人默想，而是全体在神面前承认罪和软弱。

## Theology (English)

**The Seventh Month—Peak of Sacred Time**
Three festivals concentrated in the seventh month—the Reformed tradition teaches God's sovereignty over time. Westminster Confession chapter 21. The seven-year sabbatical cycle, the seven-day Sabbath cycle, the sacred concentration of the seventh month—seven was God's completeness marker. Brothers and sisters, we do not live in neutral time—we live in God-governed time. Every seventh day, every sanctified moment—was part of the cosmic rhythm.

**Tabernacles Representing Seventy Nations—Universal Hope**
Seventy bulls representing seventy nations—the Reformed tradition teaches God's redemption's universality. Westminster Confession chapter 35 on eschatology. Israel was a priestly kingdom, not an exclusive ethnicity—"a light to the nations." Zechariah 14:16's eschatological prophecy of universal Tabernacles observance pointed to the ultimate fulfillment of all nations' pilgrimage.

**Day of Atonement's Collective Confession—All Congregation's Recognition of Sin**
The Day of Atonement was Israel's collective self-affliction—the Reformed tradition teaches public confession's importance. Nehemiah 9 and Daniel 9 both provide examples of collective confession. Public corporate worship includes collective confession—not only personal meditation, but the entire body acknowledging sin and weakness before God.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第二十九章的三个节期都有基督预表。

**吹角节——基督再来的号角**
哥林多前书15:52"号筒末次吹响的时候，死人要复活，成为不朽坏的，我们也要改变。"帖撒罗尼迦前书4:16"主必亲自从天降临，有呼叫的声音和天使长的声音，又有神的号吹响……死在基督里的人必先复活。"吹角节的号角宣告审判临近——这直接预表了末世基督再来前的号角。每一年吹角节的号角是一次预演：警醒！审判临近！为那最终的审判日预备！

**赎罪日——基督一次成就的赎罪**
希伯来书9:11-12"基督已经来到，作了将来美事的大祭司，经过那更大更全备的帐幕，不是人手所造的……他乃是凭自己的血，只一次进入圣所，成了永远赎罪的事。"大祭司一年一次进入至圣所——基督一次进入天上真圣所。大祭司带着公牛和山羊的血——基督带着自己的血。大祭司的赎罪每年重复——基督的赎罪"只一次……永远赎罪"。赎罪日的"刻苦己心"——对基督徒是每天认罪、每天领受基督赦罪的恩典。

**住棚节——末世万国与神同住**
约翰福音7:37-38住棚节末日耶稣说"人若渴了，可以到我这里来喝"——活水的应许在住棚节颁布。启示录21:3"神的帐幕在人间。他要与人同住，他们要作他的子民。"旷野的棚子是临时的居所——预表神最终要与人永远同住。住棚节说"我们曾住在棚里"——末世的实现是神的"帐幕"（shekinah）永远地与人同住。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 29's three festivals all contain Christ prefigurations.

**Feast of Trumpets—Christ's Return's Trumpet**
1 Corinthians 15:52: "At the last trumpet. For the trumpet will sound, and the dead will be raised imperishable, and we shall be changed." 1 Thessalonians 4:16: "The Lord himself will descend from heaven with a cry of command, with the voice of an archangel, and with the sound of the trumpet of God. And the dead in Christ will rise first." The Feast of Trumpets' trumpet announced approaching judgment—directly prefiguring the trumpet before Christ's return at the last day. Every annual Feast of Trumpets was a rehearsal: be alert! Judgment approaches! Prepare for that final judgment day!

**Day of Atonement—Christ's Once-for-All Atonement**
Hebrews 9:11-12: "But when Christ appeared as a high priest of the good things that have come...he entered once for all into the holy places, not by means of the blood of goats and calves but by means of his own blood, thus securing an eternal redemption." The high priest entered the Most Holy Place once a year—Christ entered heaven's true Holy Place once. The high priest brought bull and goat blood—Christ brought His own blood. The high priest's atonement repeated annually—Christ's atonement "once...securing an eternal redemption." The Day of Atonement's "afflicting yourselves"—for Christians is daily confession, daily receiving Christ's forgiveness.

**Feast of Tabernacles—End-Times All Nations Dwelling With God**
John 7:37-38, during Tabernacles' last day Jesus said "If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink"—the living water promise proclaimed at Tabernacles. Revelation 21:3: "The dwelling place of God is with man. He will dwell with them, and they will be his people." The wilderness booths were temporary dwellings—prefiguring God ultimately dwelling with people forever. Tabernacles said "we once lived in booths"—the end-times fulfillment is God's "tabernacle" (shekinah) permanently dwelling with humanity.
