## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十章是民数记最沉重的一章——对我来说。这一章记载了三件大事：米利暗的死、我在米利巴的失败、亚伦的死。三件事——一个旧时代的终结，一个新时代的开始。

**米利暗的死（1节）**

> "正月间，以色列全会众到了寻的旷野，就住在加低斯。米利暗死在那里，就葬在那里。"

弟兄姐妹，米利暗——我的姐姐。是她在我婴儿时看着我漂在尼罗河上的篓子；是她聪明地建议法老的女儿找一个希伯来奶妈；是她在红海边击鼓起舞、领众女子唱"你们要歌颂耶和华，因他大大得胜"（出15:21）。

圣经只用一句话记载了她的死——"米利暗死在那里，就葬在那里。"没有哀悼的描述，没有眼泪的记录。下一句立刻就是"会众没有水喝，就聚集攻击摩西、亚伦"。弟兄姐妹，以色列的百姓甚至没有为她哀悼——就立刻又开始抱怨了。这是旷野的残酷现实。

**米利巴的水——我最大的失败（2-13节）**

米利暗刚死，百姓就来争闹要水喝——"我们的弟兄曾死在耶和华面前，我们恨不得与他们同死！"弟兄姐妹，四十年的旷野漂流，同样的抱怨——水、食物、归埃及的渴望。

神对我说："你拿着杖去……在他们眼前吩咐磐石发出水来。"

弟兄姐妹，注意神的命令——是"吩咐"磐石，不是"击打"磐石。三十八年前，在出埃及记17章，神让我击打何烈山的磐石，水就出来了。这一次不同——神说用言语吩咐，不是杖击打。

但是我失败了。

> "摩西说：你们这些背叛的人听我说：我为你们使水从这磐石中流出来吗？摩西举手，用杖击打磐石两下。"

弟兄姐妹，我做错了几件事：我叫他们"背叛的人"——神没有叫我这样说；我说"我为你们使水流出来"——把荣耀归给自己；我击打了磐石两下——神说的是"吩咐"，不是"击打"；而且是打了两下——第一下没有水、愤怒地又打了第二下。

水仍然出来了——神的恩典超过了我的失败。但神对我和亚伦说："因为你们不信我，不在以色列人眼前尊我为圣，所以你们必不得领这会众进我所赐给他们的地去。"

弟兄姐妹，这是我听过最重的话。我带领以色列人四十年——从埃及到旷野、从旷野到约旦河边——但我进不去迦南。不是因为我没有尽力，不是因为我不忠诚——而是因为在米利巴这一刻，我"不信"、没有"尊神为圣"。

弟兄姐妹，领袖被要求承担更重的责任。雅各书3:1"我的弟兄们，不要多人作先生，因为晓得我们要受更重的判断。"我服事了神四十年——但在百姓面前的这一刻，我用愤怒代替了信靠，用自己的行动代替了神的指示。结果是严厉的。

**以东拒绝借道（14-21节）**

以色列人需要绕道进迦南——最近的路是穿过以东的领土。以东人是以扫的后裔——以扫是雅各（以色列）的孪生兄弟。我以兄弟的名义请求："求你容我们从你的地经过……我们不走田间和葡萄园，只走大道。"

但以东说：不。带兵出来威胁。弟兄姐妹，以东不肯——以色列只好绕行。这绕行让旅程更长更艰苦。兄弟拒绝了兄弟。

**亚伦的死（22-29节）**

然后是这一章最令我心碎的部分。神对我说："亚伦要归到他列祖那里……你带亚伦和他的儿子以利亚撒上何珥山，把亚伦的圣衣脱下来，给他的儿子以利亚撒穿上；亚伦必死在那里。"

弟兄姐妹，"摩西就照耶和华所吩咐的行。三人当着会众的眼前上了何珥山。摩西把亚伦的圣衣脱下来，给他的儿子以利亚撒穿上，亚伦就死在山顶那里。于是摩西和以利亚撒下了山。"

弟兄姐妹，我亲手脱下了我哥哥的圣衣——大祭司的袍子、胸牌、头巾——一件一件，脱下来穿在以利亚撒身上。然后亚伦就死了，在山顶上。我和以利亚撒下了山，亚伦留在山顶。

这一章开始于米利暗的死，结束于亚伦的死。我的姐姐和哥哥——旷野四十年的同工——在这一章里都离开了。只剩下我。但我也进不去迦南——我的命运将在申命记最后一章里来到。

弟兄姐妹，神的忠仆——无论多么伟大——都会终结自己的使命，让下一代承接。没有人是不可或缺的——除了神自己。米利暗、亚伦、摩西——都只是旷野的路过者。迦南的使命属于约书亚。每一代都忠实地完成自己的一段，然后交棒——这是神的方式。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 20 was Numbers' heaviest chapter—for me. It records three great events: Miriam's death, my failure at Meribah, Aaron's death. Three events—one old era's end, one new era's beginning.

**Miriam's Death (v. 1)**

> "And the people of Israel, the whole congregation, came into the wilderness of Zin in the first month, and the people stayed in Kadesh. And Miriam died there and was buried there."

Brothers and sisters, Miriam—my sister. She watched over me as an infant floating on the Nile in the basket; she cleverly suggested to Pharaoh's daughter finding a Hebrew nursemaid; she led women in tambourine and dance at the Red Sea: "And Miriam answered them, Sing ye to the LORD, for he hath triumphed gloriously; the horse and his rider hath he thrown into the sea." (Exod 15:21).

Scripture records her death in one sentence—"Miriam died there and was buried there." No description of mourning, no record of tears. The very next sentence: "There was no water for the congregation. And they assembled against Moses and against Aaron." Brothers and sisters, Israel's people didn't even mourn her—they immediately resumed complaining. This was the wilderness's cruel reality.

**Meribah's Water—My Greatest Failure (vv. 2-13)**

Miriam had just died; the people came demanding water—"Would that we had perished when our brothers perished before the LORD!" Brothers and sisters, forty years of wilderness wandering, the same complaints—water, food, longing to return to Egypt.

God said to me: "Take the staff...tell the rock before their eyes to yield its water."

Brothers and sisters, note God's command—"tell" the rock, not "strike" the rock. Thirty-eight years earlier, in Exodus 17, God told me to strike the rock at Horeb, and water came out. This time was different—God said use words to command, not the staff to strike.

But I failed.

> "Moses said to them, 'Hear now, you rebels: shall we bring water for you out of this rock?' And Moses lifted up his hand and struck the rock with his staff twice."

Brothers and sisters, I did several things wrong: I called them "rebels"—God had not told me to say that; I said "shall we bring water"—taking credit myself; I struck the rock twice—God said "tell," not "strike"; and I struck twice—no water the first time, then striking again in anger.

Water still came out—God's grace exceeded my failure. But God said to me and Aaron: "Because you did not believe in me, to uphold me as holy in the eyes of the people of Israel, therefore you shall not bring this assembly into the land that I have given them."

Brothers and sisters, these were the heaviest words I ever heard. I led Israel forty years—from Egypt through the wilderness to the Jordan's edge—but I could not enter Canaan. Not because I hadn't tried, not because I lacked faithfulness—but because at this Meribah moment, I "did not believe," failed to "uphold God as holy."

Brothers and sisters, leaders bear heavier responsibility. James 3:1: "Not many of you should become teachers, my brothers, for you know that we who teach will be judged with greater strictness." I had served God forty years—but in this moment before the people, I substituted anger for trust, my own action for God's instruction. The consequence was severe.

**Edom Refuses Passage (vv. 14-21)**

Israel needed a route to Canaan—the shortest path went through Edom's territory. The Edomites descended from Esau—Jacob (Israel)'s twin brother. I appealed on brotherly terms: "Please let us pass through your land...we will not pass through field or vineyard, only along the King's Highway."

But Edom said: no. Came out with military force. Brothers and sisters, Edom refused—Israel had to detour. This detour made the journey longer and harder. Brother refused brother.

**Aaron's Death (vv. 22-29)**

Then the chapter's most heartbreaking section. God said to me: "Aaron shall be gathered to his people...Take Aaron and Eleazar his son and bring them up to Mount Hor. And strip Aaron of his garments and put them on Eleazar his son. And Aaron shall be gathered to his people and shall die there."

Brothers and sisters, "Moses did as the LORD commanded. They went up Mount Hor in the sight of all the congregation. And Moses stripped Aaron of his garments and put them on Eleazar his son. And Aaron died there on the top of the mountain. Then Moses and Eleazar came down from the mountain."

Brothers and sisters, I personally removed my brother's sacred garments—the high priestly robe, breastpiece, turban—piece by piece, transferring them to Eleazar. Then Aaron died on the mountaintop. I and Eleazar came down; Aaron remained on the summit.

This chapter began with Miriam's death and ended with Aaron's. My sister and brother—forty years' wilderness companions—both departed in this chapter. Only I remained. But I too could not enter Canaan—my fate would come in Deuteronomy's final chapter.

Brothers and sisters, God's faithful servants—however great—all complete their missions and pass the baton to the next generation. No one is indispensable—except God Himself. Miriam, Aaron, Moses—all were wilderness sojourners. Canaan's mission belonged to Joshua. Each generation faithfully completes its segment, then hands off—this is God's way.

## 历史背景（中文）

加低斯（Kadesh-barnea）是以色列旷野漂流的主要营地之一——民数记13章从这里派出探子，民数记20章这里记载米利暗的死亡。考古学认为加低斯在今天西奈半岛北部（Ain el-Qudeirat一带）。何珥山的位置争议较大——一种传统认为是今天约旦的Jebel Harun（亚伦山），山顶至今有一座纪念亚伦的清真寺；另一说认为是加低斯附近的山。"亚伦哀哭三十天"——以色列人为亚伦哀哭30天（民20:29），与摩西的哀哭期相同（申34:8），都是30天；只有摩西的老师梅瑟（Metatron？）哀哭时间不详。相比之下，大卫的儿子押沙龙死时大卫只哀哭了很短时间。米利巴（Meribah）字面意思"争闹"——这个地名记录了以色列在旷野两次向神争闹的地方（出17:7和民20:13），提醒后来的以色列人不要效仿（诗95:8"不可硬着心，像在米利巴，像在旷野的玛撒"）。

## Historical Context (English)

Kadesh-barnea was one of Israel's main wilderness camp sites—Numbers 13 sent spies from here, Numbers 20 records Miriam's death here. Archaeologists identify Kadesh in today's northern Sinai Peninsula (around Ain el-Qudeirat). Mount Hor's location is disputed—one tradition identifies it as today's Jebel Harun (Aaron's Mountain) in Jordan, whose summit still has a mosque commemorating Aaron; another view places it near Kadesh. "Mourning for Aaron thirty days"—Israel mourned Aaron for 30 days (Num 20:29), matching Moses' mourning period (Deut 34:8), both 30 days. Meribah (literally "quarreling/strife")—this place name recorded Israel's two wilderness disputes with God (Exod 17:7 and Num 20:13), warning later Israelites against imitation (Ps 95:8: "Do not harden your hearts, as at Meribah, as on the day at Massah in the wilderness").

## 经文结构（中文）

**米利暗之死（1节）**——到加低斯，米利暗死在那里葬在那里，一句话带过。

**米利巴水事件（2-13节）**——百姓无水→聚集攻击→摩西亚伦俯伏→神说"吩咐磐石"→摩西怒打磐石两下→水出来→神宣判"你们不得进迦南"→此地名叫米利巴（争闹）。

**以东拒绝借道（14-21节）**——摩西以兄弟名义请求借道→以东拒绝→带兵威胁→以色列绕行离开。

**亚伦在何珥山死亡（22-29节）**——神宣告亚伦将死→三人上何珥山→摩西脱亚伦圣衣给以利亚撒穿→亚伦死在山顶→以色列全家哀哭三十天。

## Structure (English)

**Miriam's Death (v. 1)**—Arrive at Kadesh, Miriam dies and is buried there, one sentence.

**Meribah Water Incident (vv. 2-13)**—People have no water → assemble against leaders → Moses/Aaron prostrate → God says "tell the rock" → Moses angrily strikes rock twice → water flows → God pronounces "you cannot enter Canaan" → place named Meribah (strife).

**Edom Refuses Passage (vv. 14-21)**—Moses appeals in brotherly name for passage → Edom refuses → threatens with military force → Israel detours and leaves.

**Aaron Dies at Mount Hor (vv. 22-29)**—God announces Aaron will die → three ascend Mount Hor → Moses removes Aaron's garments and places on Eleazar → Aaron dies on mountaintop → all Israel mourns thirty days.

## 神学要点（中文）

**领袖的更重责任——在百姓面前尊神为圣**
摩西击打磐石被审判——改革宗传统教导领袖在神面前承担更重的责任。雅各书3:1"不要多人作先生，因为晓得我们要受更重的判断。"神对摩西的要求比对普通百姓更高——摩西代表神在百姓面前——他的失败是对神圣洁的公然亵渎。韦敏斯德信条第16章论善功：领袖的善功不只是行义，还包括用正确的方式行义（以正确的动机、按神的命令、为了神的荣耀）。

**愤怒的罪——以情绪代替服从**
摩西的问题是愤怒——他恼恨百姓、用愤怒行事。诗篇106:32-33"他们在米利巴水使耶和华发怒……摩西因他们的缘故心里急躁，以致口出急躁的话。"以弗所书4:26"生气却不要犯罪"——摩西的生气变成了违背神命令的罪。

**圣职的传承——职分大于个人**
亚伦的圣衣转给以利亚撒——改革宗传统教导教会职分的连续性。韦敏斯德信条第29-31章论教会职分的设立和传承。职分不属于某个个人——它属于神，暂时由人来担任，然后传承给下一任。教会不依靠任何个人的伟大——而依靠神不变的旨意和圣职的传承。

## Theology (English)

**Leaders' Greater Responsibility—Upholding God's Holiness Before the People**
Moses' striking the rock was judged—the Reformed tradition teaches leaders bear greater accountability before God. James 3:1: "Not many of you should become teachers, my brothers, for you know that we who teach will be judged with greater strictness." God's standard for Moses exceeded ordinary people's—Moses represented God before the people—his failure was open desecration of God's holiness. Westminster Confession chapter 16 on good works: leaders' good works include not only doing right but doing it rightly (with right motive, per God's command, for God's glory).

**The Sin of Anger—Substituting Emotion for Obedience**
Moses' problem was anger—he was furious at the people, acting in rage. Psalm 106:32-33: "They angered him at the waters of Meribah...for they made his spirit bitter, and he spoke rashly with his lips." Ephesians 4:26: "Be angry and do not sin"—Moses' anger became sin that violated God's command.

**Sacred Office's Succession—Office Greater Than the Individual**
Aaron's garments transferred to Eleazar—the Reformed tradition teaches the continuity of church office. Westminster Confession chapters 29-31 on church office's establishment and succession. Office does not belong to any individual—it belongs to God, temporarily held by a person, then transmitted to the next. The church does not depend on any individual's greatness—but on God's unchanging will and the succession of sacred office.

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第二十章有三个指向基督的画面。

**磐石被击打流出水——基督一次受苦**
出埃及记17章：磐石被击打，水流出——基督在十字架上受死。民数记20章：神说"吩咐磐石"，不是再次击打——因为磐石只需要被击打一次。哥林多前书10:4"都喝了一样的灵水——所喝的是出于随着他们的灵磐石，那磐石就是基督。"基督只死了一次——希伯来书9:28"基督既然一次被献，担当了多人的罪。"摩西第二次击打磐石的错误——预示了"基督需要再次受苦"这种错误神学的危险。十字架一次，永远有效。

**亚伦的圣衣转给以利亚撒——基督是终极大祭司**
亚伦死了，职分传给以利亚撒——但以利亚撒最终也会死，职分还要继续传。这个循环只有一个终点：希伯来书7:23-24"那些成为祭司的，数目本来多，是因为有死阻隔，不能长久。这位既是永远常存的，他祭司的职任就长久不更换。"基督是不需要传承的大祭司——因为祂永远活着。

**摩西不能进迦南——律法不能带人进神的安息**
摩西是律法的给予者——但律法的给予者不能带人进应许之地。约书亚（Yeshua，与耶稣同名）把以色列带进迦南——正如耶稣基督把信徒带进神的安息。希伯来书4:8"若是约书亚曾叫他们享了安息，后来神就不再提别的日子了。"律法在外面等候——恩典带人进入。摩西看见了迦南却不能进去——律法揭示了应许却不能实现应许。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 20 contains three images pointing to Christ.

**The Rock Struck, Water Flowing—Christ Suffered Once**
Exodus 17: rock struck, water flows—Christ dying on the cross. Numbers 20: God says "tell the rock," not strike it again—because the rock only needed striking once. 1 Corinthians 10:4: "All drank the same spiritual drink. For they drank from the spiritual Rock that followed them, and the Rock was Christ." Christ died only once—Hebrews 9:28: "Christ, having been offered once to bear the sins of many." Moses' error of striking the rock a second time—foreshadowed the danger of the erroneous theology that "Christ needs to suffer again." The cross: once, eternally effective.

**Aaron's Garments Transferred to Eleazar—Christ Is Ultimate High Priest**
Aaron died, office transferred to Eleazar—but Eleazar would also eventually die, the office continuing. This cycle has one endpoint: Hebrews 7:23-24: "The former priests were many in number, because they were prevented by death from continuing in office, but he holds his priesthood permanently, because he continues forever." Christ is a High Priest requiring no succession—because He lives forever.

**Moses Cannot Enter Canaan—The Law Cannot Lead People into God's Rest**
Moses was the law's giver—but the law's giver could not lead people into the promised land. Joshua (Yeshua, same name as Jesus) brought Israel into Canaan—just as Jesus Christ brings believers into God's rest. Hebrews 4:8: "For if Joshua had given them rest, God would not have spoken of another day later on." The law waited outside—grace brings people in. Moses saw Canaan but could not enter—the law reveals the promise but cannot fulfill it.
