## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。第二十四章是巴兰预言的最高潮——他的第三和第四篇神谕。在这里，一个外邦先知说出了旧约中最清晰的弥赛亚预言之一："有星要出于雅各，有杖要兴于以色列。"

**第三篇神谕——以色列营地的荣美（1-9节）**

这一次，巴兰不再去"求法术"了——他转身面向旷野，举目看以色列人按支派居住的营地。"神的灵就临到他身上。"

弟兄姐妹，注意区别：前两次巴兰"去求见耶和华"——是他主动寻求神谕。这一次"神的灵临到他身上"——是神主动启示。巴兰不再扮演职业先知的角色——他被神的灵完全掌控了。

他的第三篇诗歌以自我介绍开始："比珥的儿子巴兰说，眼目睁开的人说，得听神的言语，得见全能者的异象，眼目睁开而仆倒的人说。"弟兄姐妹，巴兰此刻经历了真正的属灵异象——他不是在表演先知的技艺，他确实看见了。

> "雅各啊，你的帐棚何等华美！以色列啊，你的帐幕何其华丽！如接连的山谷，如河旁的园子，如耶和华所栽的沉香树，如水边的香柏木。"

弟兄姐妹，从摩押的高处俯瞰，以色列的营地——十二支派整齐排列，会幕在中央，利未人环绕其外——在巴兰眼中如同一片壮丽的花园。

然后预言升级了："水要从他的桶里流出；种子要撒在多水之处。他的王必超过亚甲；他的国必要振兴。"弟兄姐妹——"他的王必超过亚甲"——亚甲（Agag）是亚玛力王的名号。以色列的王将比地上最大的君王更伟大。"他的国必要振兴"——以色列不只是一个部落——它将成为一个国度。

"凡给你祝福的，愿他蒙福；凡咒诅你的，愿他受咒诅。"弟兄姐妹，这直接呼应了创世记12:3神对亚伯拉罕的应许！"为你祝福的，我必赐福与他；那咒诅你的，我必咒诅他。"巴兰——试图咒诅以色列的人——他所引用的话正是他自己命运的宣判：你咒诅以色列，你自己就要受咒诅。

**巴勒的愤怒（10-14节）**

> "巴勒向巴兰生气，就拍起手来"——弟兄姐妹，在古代近东拍手是愤怒或蔑视的表示。巴勒三次召来巴兰，三次得到祝福而不是咒诅。四十二只大牲口白白献了——代价巨大，结果是零。"如今你快回本地去吧！"

巴兰回答："耶和华说什么，我就要说什么。"然后——巴兰准备离开之前，做了一件出乎巴勒意料的事——他主动给出了第四篇预言："你来，我告诉你这民日后要怎样待你的民。"

**第四篇神谕——弥赛亚预言（15-19节）**

> "我看他却不在现时；我望他却不在近日。有星要出于雅各，有杖要兴于以色列，必打破摩押的四角，毁坏扰乱之子。"

弟兄姐妹，"有星要出于雅各，有杖要兴于以色列"——这是旧约最重要的弥赛亚预言之一！

"星"——在古代近东，星是王权的象征。一颗新星的升起代表一位新王的诞生。马太福音2:1-2"当希律王的时候，耶稣生在犹太的伯利恒。有几个博士从东方来到耶路撒冷，说：那生下来作犹太人之王的在哪里？我们在东方看见他的星。"弟兄姐妹——东方博士看见的星，就是巴兰预言的那颗星！巴兰来自东方（幼发拉底河畔的毘夺）——东方博士可能就是巴兰预言传统的后裔！

"杖"——王权的权杖。"有杖要兴于以色列"——一个掌权者要从以色列兴起。

"我看他却不在现时；我望他却不在近日"——这不是近期的事——巴兰看到了远处的未来。他看见了一个时间上遥远的、但确定会来的王。

"必打破摩押的四角，毁坏扰乱之子"——这一句直接对着巴勒说——你的国家将被这位未来的王击败。弟兄姐妹，巴勒花了大代价召来巴兰咒诅以色列——结果不仅没有咒诅，反而得到了一个预言：你的国将被以色列的王摧毁。

**末世预言（20-25节）**

巴兰的视野进一步扩展——他看到了亚玛力（"原为诸国之首，但他终必沉沦"）、基尼人（"必至衰微"）、亚述（"有人从基提来苦害亚述"）。这些简短的预言描绘了以色列周围列国的命运——一个接一个最终都将沉沦。

然后——"于是巴兰起来，回他本地去；巴勒也回去了。"弟兄姐妹，故事看似结束了。但巴兰的故事还有一个黑暗的尾声——第三十一章将揭示巴兰用另一种方式伤害了以色列。他无法咒诅他们——他就教唆摩押女子引诱以色列人犯罪。

弟兄姐妹，巴兰是旧约中最矛盾的人物之一——他说出了最伟大的祝福和预言，却最终因贪财走向了毁灭。他有先知的嘴——但没有先知的心。他"愿如义人之死而死"——但他活得像一个追逐金银的占卜师。启示录2:14"巴兰的教训……引诱以色列人犯罪。"弟兄姐妹，嘴巴上的正确不等于生命的正确——认识真理并不等于活在真理里面。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Chapter 24 was the climax of Balaam's prophecies—his third and fourth oracles. Here, a Gentile prophet uttered one of the Old Testament's clearest messianic prophecies: "A star shall come out of Jacob, and a scepter shall rise out of Israel."

**Third Oracle—The Beauty of Israel's Camp (vv. 1-9)**

This time Balaam no longer went "to seek omens"—he turned toward the wilderness and lifted his eyes to view Israel camped by tribes. "The Spirit of God came upon him."

Brothers and sisters, note the distinction: the previous two times Balaam "went to meet the LORD"—he actively sought the oracle. This time "the Spirit of God came upon him"—God initiated the revelation. Balaam no longer played the professional prophet's role—he was completely controlled by God's Spirit.

His third poem began with self-introduction: "The oracle of Balaam the son of Beor, the oracle of the man whose eye is opened, the oracle of him who hears the words of God, who sees the vision of the Almighty, falling down with his eyes uncovered."

> "How lovely are your tents, O Jacob, your encampments, O Israel! Like palm groves that stretch afar, like gardens beside a river, like aloes that the LORD has planted, like cedar trees beside the waters."

Brothers and sisters, from Moab's heights looking down, Israel's camp—twelve tribes arranged in order, tabernacle at center, Levites surrounding it—appeared to Balaam as a magnificent garden.

Then the prophecy escalated: "Water shall flow from his buckets, and his seed shall be in many waters; his king shall be higher than Agag, and his kingdom shall be exalted." Brothers and sisters—"his king shall be higher than Agag"—Agag was the Amalekite royal title. Israel's king would surpass earth's greatest monarchs.

"Blessed are those who bless you, and cursed are those who curse you." Brothers and sisters, this directly echoed Genesis 12:3's Abrahamic promise! Balaam—the one attempting to curse Israel—quoted the very words pronouncing his own fate: curse Israel, and you yourself shall be cursed.

**Balak's Fury (vv. 10-14)**

"Balak's anger was kindled against Balaam, and he struck his hands together." Brothers and sisters, in the ancient Near East, hand-striking expressed anger or contempt. Three times Balak summoned Balaam, three times receiving blessings instead of curses. Forty-two animals sacrificed in vain.

Balaam replied: "What the LORD speaks, that will I speak." Then before departing, he voluntarily gave a fourth prophecy: "Come, I will let you know what this people will do to your people in the latter days."

**Fourth Oracle—The Messianic Prophecy (vv. 15-19)**

> "I see him, but not now; I behold him, but not near: a star shall come out of Jacob, and a scepter shall rise out of Israel; it shall crush the forehead of Moab and break down all the sons of Sheth."

Brothers and sisters, "a star shall come out of Jacob, and a scepter shall rise out of Israel"—one of the Old Testament's most important messianic prophecies!

"Star"—in the ancient Near East, stars symbolized royalty. A new star's rising represented a new king's birth. Matthew 2:1-2: "After Jesus was born in Bethlehem...wise men from the east came to Jerusalem, saying, 'Where is he who has been born king of the Jews? For we saw his star when it rose.'" Brothers and sisters—the star the Magi saw was the star Balaam prophesied! Balaam came from the East (Pethor near the Euphrates)—the Magi may have been heirs of Balaam's prophetic tradition!

"Scepter"—the royal staff. "A scepter shall rise out of Israel"—a ruler would arise from Israel.

"I see him, but not now; I behold him, but not near"—this was not imminent—Balaam saw the distant future. He saw a temporally remote but certain coming king.

**Eschatological Prophecies (vv. 20-25)**

Balaam's vision expanded further—Amalek ("the first among the nations, but its end is destruction"), the Kenites ("shall be wasted"), Asshur ("ships from Kittim shall come and afflict Asshur"). Brief prophecies depicting the surrounding nations' fate—one after another ultimately perishing.

Then—"Balaam rose and went back to his place. And Balak also went his way." Brothers and sisters, the story seemed finished. But Balaam's story had a dark epilogue—chapter 31 would reveal Balaam used another method to harm Israel. Unable to curse them—he taught Moabite women to seduce Israelites into sin.

Brothers and sisters, Balaam was one of the Old Testament's most contradictory figures—he spoke the greatest blessings and prophecies yet ultimately perished through greed. He had a prophet's mouth—but not a prophet's heart. He desired "the death of the upright"—but lived like a gold-chasing diviner. Revelation 2:14: "The teaching of Balaam...who taught Israel to sin." Brothers and sisters, verbal correctness does not equal life correctness—knowing truth is not the same as living in truth.

## 历史背景（中文）

"有星要出于雅各"——这句话在犹太历史中产生了深远的影响。公元132年的犹太起义领袖西门·巴科赫巴（Simon Bar Kokhba）的名字"巴科赫巴"就是"星之子"——当时最伟大的拉比阿基瓦宣称他就是民数记24:17预言的弥赛亚。起义失败后（公元135年），阿基瓦被罗马人处死，巴科赫巴被改称"巴科齐巴"（"谎言之子"）。基督教传统从一开始就将这颗星与基督的降生联系——马太福音2章"东方博士"的记载被视为巴兰预言的直接应验。"基提"（Kittim）在不同时代被解释为塞浦路斯、希腊或罗马——死海古卷中"基提"指代罗马帝国。因此24:24"有人从基提来苦害亚述"可能预指罗马帝国对近东的征服。

## Historical Context (English)

"A star shall come out of Jacob"—this verse had profound influence in Jewish history. The leader of the Jewish revolt in 132 AD, Simon Bar Kokhba, whose name "Bar Kokhba" means "Son of the Star"—the greatest rabbi of that era, Akiva, declared him the Messiah prophesied in Numbers 24:17. After the revolt's failure (135 AD), Akiva was executed by Rome, and Bar Kokhba was renamed "Bar Koziba" ("Son of the Lie"). Christian tradition from its beginning connected this star with Christ's birth—Matthew 2's "wise men from the East" was viewed as direct fulfillment of Balaam's prophecy. "Kittim" was interpreted in different eras as Cyprus, Greece, or Rome—the Dead Sea Scrolls use "Kittim" for the Roman Empire. Thus 24:24 "ships shall come from Kittim and afflict Asshur" may predict Rome's conquest of the Near East.

## Structure (English)

**Third Oracle (vv. 1-9)**—Balaam faces wilderness → Spirit of God comes → "How lovely your tents" → "His king higher than Agag" → "Blessed are those who bless you, cursed those who curse you" (= Gen 12:3).

**Balak's Fury, Balaam's Response (vv. 10-14)**—Balak strikes hands: go home! → Balaam: what LORD says I speak → Balaam voluntarily prophesies "latter days."

**Fourth Oracle: Messianic Prophecy (vv. 15-19)**—"I see him, but not now" → "A star from Jacob, a scepter from Israel" → "shall crush Moab" → "one from Jacob shall exercise dominion."

**Brief Prophecies of Nations' Fates (vv. 20-25)**—Amalek ultimately perishes → Kenites wasted → Asshur afflicted by Kittim → both parties return home.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"有星出于雅各"——弥赛亚预言与神的计划**
巴兰的第四篇神谕——改革宗传统教导弥赛亚预言贯穿旧约。韦敏斯德信条第8章论基督为中保。巴兰看到了"不在现时"的未来——改革宗传统教导神的计划跨越时间。先知看到的不是他们自己的时代——而是神永恒的旨意在时间中的展开。

**"凡祝福你的蒙福、咒诅你的受咒诅"——亚伯拉罕之约的延续**
巴兰引用了创世记12:3——改革宗传统教导一个连贯的盟约历史。韦敏斯德信条第7章论恩典之约。从亚伯拉罕到巴兰到基督——不是不同的计划，而是同一个盟约在不同历史阶段的展开。

**正确的话语与错误的生命——巴兰的悲剧**
巴兰说了正确的话却过了错误的人生——改革宗传统严肃地区分有效恩召和普遍恩典。韦敏斯德信条第10章论有效恩召。巴兰蒙了普遍恩典（能说预言）却没有蒙有效恩召（内心没有更新）。彼得后书2:15"他们离弃正路，就走差了，随从比珥之子巴兰的路。"

## Theology (English)

**"A Star From Jacob"—Messianic Prophecy and God's Plan**
Balaam's fourth oracle—the Reformed tradition teaches messianic prophecy runs through the Old Testament. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ as Mediator. Balaam saw a future "not now"—the Reformed tradition teaches God's plan spans time. Prophets saw not their own era—but God's eternal purpose unfolding in time.

**"Blessed Those Who Bless, Cursed Those Who Curse"—Abrahamic Covenant's Continuation**
Balaam cited Genesis 12:3—the Reformed tradition teaches one coherent covenant history. Westminster Confession chapter 7 on the covenant of grace. From Abraham to Balaam to Christ—not different plans but the same covenant in different historical stages.

**Right Words, Wrong Life—Balaam's Tragedy**
Balaam spoke rightly yet lived wrongly—the Reformed tradition seriously distinguishes effectual calling from common grace. Westminster Confession chapter 10 on effectual calling. Balaam received common grace (ability to prophesy) without effectual calling (no heart renewal). 2 Peter 2:15: "Forsaking the right way, they have gone astray. They have followed the way of Balaam, the son of Beor."

## 基督的影子（中文）

民数记第二十四章有旧约最直接的弥赛亚预言之一。

**"有星要出于雅各"——伯利恒之星**
马太福音2:1-2"有几个博士从东方来到耶路撒冷，说：那生下来作犹太人之王的在哪里？我们在东方看见他的星。"弟兄姐妹，巴兰来自东方——东方博士可能继承了巴兰关于以色列的星的预言传统。当那颗星真的出现——他们认出了它。启示录22:16"我是大卫的根，又是他的后裔。我是明亮的晨星。"基督自称"明亮的晨星"——祂就是那出于雅各的星。

**"有杖要兴于以色列"——基督的王权**
创世记49:10"圭必不离犹大，杖必不离他两脚之间，直等细罗（就是赐平安者）来到。"从雅各的预言到巴兰的预言——王权的杖一步步清晰：来自以色列→来自犹大→细罗（赐平安者）=基督。以赛亚书11:1"从耶西的本（原文作墩）生出一条"——大卫的后裔、犹大支派、以色列的王——就是基督。

**"我看他不在现时"——基督来临的确定性与时间性**
巴兰"看见了"但"不在现时"——确定但尚未来。这是旧约先知对弥赛亚的共同经验：确定祂会来，但不清楚具体什么时候。彼得前书1:10-11"众先知早已详细的寻求考察……要晓得圣灵预先证明基督受苦、后来得荣耀，是指着什么时候。"弟兄姐妹，巴兰在公元前1400年左右看见了基督——他确定地看见了，但隔着一千四百年的时间。对我们——基督已经来了、已经死了、已经复活了——我们等候的是祂第二次来。"我看他不在现时"——也是我们对基督再来的信心：确定，但尚未。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Numbers 24 contains one of the Old Testament's most direct messianic prophecies.

**"A Star Shall Come Out of Jacob"—The Star of Bethlehem**
Matthew 2:1-2: "Wise men from the east came to Jerusalem, saying, 'Where is he who has been born king of the Jews? For we saw his star when it rose.'" Brothers and sisters, Balaam came from the East—the Magi may have inherited Balaam's prophetic tradition about Israel's star. When that star actually appeared—they recognized it. Revelation 22:16: "I am the root and the descendant of David, the bright morning star." Christ called Himself "the bright morning star"—He is the star from Jacob.

**"A Scepter Shall Rise Out of Israel"—Christ's Kingship**
Genesis 49:10: "The scepter shall not depart from Judah...until tribute comes to him." From Jacob's prophecy to Balaam's—the royal scepter progressively clarified: from Israel → from Judah → Shiloh (peace-bringer) = Christ. Isaiah 11:1: "A shoot from the stump of Jesse"—David's descendant, Judah's tribe, Israel's king—is Christ.

**"I See Him, But Not Now"—Christ's Coming: Certain Yet Future**
Balaam "saw" but "not now"—certain yet unfulfilled. This was Old Testament prophets' shared experience of the Messiah: certain He would come, unclear exactly when. 1 Peter 1:10-11: "The prophets who prophesied about the grace...searched and inquired carefully, inquiring what person or time the Spirit of Christ in them was indicating." Brothers and sisters, Balaam around 1400 BC saw Christ—he saw certainly, but across fourteen hundred years. For us—Christ has come, died, risen—we await His second coming. "I see him, but not now"—is also our faith in Christ's return: certain, but not yet.
