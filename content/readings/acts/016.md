## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是路加。使徒行传十六章是第二次宣教旅行的开始，也是福音从亚洲迈向欧洲的历史性时刻。这一章，我亲历了提摩太的加入、神奇异的异象、腓立比城第一批信徒的归主，以及保罗和西拉在狱中赞美的神圣时刻。

一、提摩太的加入（16:1-5）

保罗来到特庇和路司得，在那里有一个门徒，名叫提摩太，是信主之犹太妇人的儿子，他父亲是希腊人；路司得和以哥念的弟兄都称赞他。保罗要带他同去，就为他行了割礼（徒16:1-3）。

为何保罗要让提摩太受割礼？因为提摩太是犹太母亲所生，在犹太人眼中本应受割礼，这是为了不在犹太人中传道时产生不必要的拦阻——这与大会宣告的外邦人不需要受割礼并不矛盾，这是为了传道的实际需要而做的让步。这展示了保罗"向犹太人，我就作犹太人"（林前9:20）的传道智慧。

二、马其顿的呼声（16:6-10）

圣灵禁止他们在亚西亚讲道，他们来到每西亚，又想要入庇推尼去，耶稣的灵却不许。他们就越过每西亚，下到特罗亚。在那里，夜间有异象现给保罗，有一个马其顿人站着求他说："请你过来，帮助我们！"保罗见了这异象，我们随即想要往马其顿去，以为神召我们传福音给那里的人听。（徒16:6-10）

请注意"我们"——这是《使徒行传》中第一次出现第一人称复数，表明我路加从这里加入了使徒团队。这是福音历史上的伟大时刻：**福音从亚洲踏入欧洲。** 历史的转折往往在一个异象和一个顺从中发生。

三、吕底亚的归主（16:11-15）

我们在腓立比待了几天。安息日，我们出城门，到了河边，以为那里有祷告的地方，就坐下对那聚集的妇女讲道。有一个卖紫色布匹的妇人，名叫吕底亚，是推雅推喇城的人，素来敬拜神，他听见了，主就开导他的心，叫他留心听保罗所讲的话（徒16:14）。

注意这一句话的神学深度："主就开导他的心"——不是吕底亚自己打开了心门，而是**主主动地开导他的心**。这是改革宗有效呼召教义的最清晰例证之一：人的信心是神主权工作的结果，而非原因。吕底亚受了洗，连同他一家，就请我们到他家里住。

四、占卜的使女（16:16-24）

有一个使女，被占卜的鬼附着，她用占卜，为主人们赚了许多钱财。她跟着保罗和我们，喊着说："这些人是至高神的仆人，对你们传说救人的道。"她一连多日这样喊叫，保罗心里厌烦，转身对那鬼说："我奉耶稣基督的名，吩咐你从他身上出去！"那鬼当时就出去了（徒16:16-18）。

这件事引来了麻烦。使女的主人们见得钱的希望断了，就拿住保罗和西拉，拉他们进市中心，告在官长面前。官长下令剥去他们的衣裳，用棍打了他们，然后把他们交给禁卒，吩咐他严密看守。

五、狱中的赞美（16:25-34）

约在半夜，保罗和西拉祷告，唱诗赞美神，众囚犯也侧耳而听。忽然地大震动，甚至监狱的根基都摇动了，监门立刻全开，众囚犯的锁链也都松开了（徒16:25-26）。

弟兄姐妹，在半夜、在牢狱、在刚被打过之后——他们不是在哀叹，而是在**唱诗赞美神**。这是何等的信心！这不是假装苦难不存在，而是在苦难中仍然相信神的主权和良善。赞美先于释放，信心先于神迹。

禁卒醒了，看见监门全开，以为囚犯逃走，就拔出刀来要自杀。保罗大声喊着说："不要伤害自己！我们都在这里呢。"禁卒颤抖着俯伏在保罗西拉脚前，问说："二位先生，我当怎样行才可以得救？"他们说："当信主耶稣，你和你一家都必得救"（徒16:30-31）。

禁卒当夜就受洗，他和他全家都欢喜，因为信了神。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Luke. Acts 16 opens the second missionary journey—a historic moment when the gospel stepped from Asia into Europe. In this chapter, I personally witnessed Timothy joining the team, a remarkable vision, the conversion of the first believers in Philippi, and the sacred moment of Paul and Silas worshiping in prison.

I. Timothy Joins (16:1-5)

Paul came to Derbe and Lystra, where there was a disciple named Timothy, the son of a Jewish woman who was a believer, but his father was a Greek. The brothers at Lystra and Iconium spoke well of him. Paul wanted Timothy to accompany him, and he circumcised him (Acts 16:1-3).

Why did Paul have Timothy circumcised? Because Timothy was born of a Jewish mother, he was considered Jewish, and circumcision would remove an unnecessary barrier to Jewish ministry—this did not contradict the council's ruling that Gentiles need not be circumcised; it was a practical accommodation for missionary effectiveness. This displays Paul's wisdom: "And unto the Jews I became as a Jew, that I might gain the Jews; to them that are under the law, as under the law, that I might gain them that are under the law;" (1 Cor 9:20).

II. The Macedonian Call (16:6-10)

The Holy Spirit forbade them to speak the word in Asia, and they came to Mysia, attempted to enter Bithynia—but the Spirit of Jesus did not allow them. So they passed by Mysia and went down to Troas. A vision appeared to Paul in the night: a man of Macedonia was standing there, urging him, "Come over to Macedonia and help us!" When Paul had seen the vision, **we** immediately sought to go on into Macedonia, concluding that God had called us to preach the gospel there (Acts 16:6-10).

Notice "we"—this is the first occurrence of the first-person plural in Acts, indicating that I, Luke, joined the apostolic team here. This is a great moment in gospel history: **the gospel stepped from Asia into Europe.** History's turning points often occur at a single vision and one act of obedience.

III. Lydia's Conversion (16:11-15)

We stayed in Philippi for several days. On the Sabbath day we went outside the gate to the riverside, where we supposed there was a place of prayer, and sat down and spoke to the women who had gathered. One who heard us was a woman named Lydia, from the city of Thyatira, a seller of purple goods, who was a worshiper of God. **The Lord opened her heart** to pay attention to what was said by Paul (Acts 16:14).

Notice the theological depth of this phrase: "The Lord opened her heart"—it was not Lydia who opened her own heart, but **the Lord who actively opened it**. This is one of the clearest examples of the Reformed doctrine of effectual calling: human faith is the result of God's sovereign work, not its cause. Lydia was baptized, with her household, and urged us to stay at her home.

IV. The Slave Girl with a Spirit (16:16-24)

A slave girl with a spirit of divination followed Paul and us, crying out, "These men are servants of the Most High God, who proclaim to you the way of salvation." She kept doing this for many days. Paul, greatly annoyed, turned and said to the spirit, "I command you in the name of Jesus Christ to come out of her." And it came out that very hour (Acts 16:16-18).

This brought trouble. Her owners, seeing their hope of gain gone, seized Paul and Silas, dragged them into the marketplace, charged them before the magistrates. The magistrates ordered them to be beaten with rods and thrown into prison, commanding the jailer to keep them securely.

V. Praise in Prison (16:25-34)

About midnight Paul and Silas were praying and singing hymns to God, and the prisoners were listening to them. Suddenly there was a great earthquake, so that the foundations of the prison were shaken. And immediately all the doors were opened, and everyone's bonds were unfastened (Acts 16:25-26).

Brothers and sisters, at midnight, in a prison, immediately after being beaten—they were not lamenting but **singing hymns to God**. What faith! Not pretending suffering doesn't exist, but believing in God's sovereignty and goodness through suffering. Praise precedes deliverance; faith precedes miracle.

The jailer awoke and, seeing the prison doors open, drew his sword to kill himself. Paul cried with a loud voice, "Do not harm yourself, for we are all here!" The jailer fell down trembling before Paul and Silas and asked, "Sirs, what must I do to be saved?" They said, "And brought them out, and said, Sirs, what must I do to be saved? And they said, Believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, and thou shalt be saved, and thy house." (Acts 16:30-31).

That very night the jailer was baptized, with his entire household, and he rejoiced along with his entire household that he had believed in God.

## 历史背景（中文）

腓立比是马其顿的首府之一，罗马殖民城市，以第一次腓立比战役（公元前42年）闻名——屋大维和安东尼在此击败布鲁图斯。作为殖民城市，腓立比居民以罗马公民自豪（参16:21"我们是罗马人"），享有罗马法律的保护。吕底亚来自推雅推喇，该城以紫色染料工业著称（考古出土大量染缸遗址）。紫色布匹是罗马帝国最昂贵的商品之一，专供贵族和皇室使用，说明吕底亚的社会经济地位较高。腓立比可能没有足够十人的犹太男性来建立会堂（要求10名成年男性），所以只有在河边的非正式祷告聚会。

## Historical Context (English)

Philippi was one of Macedonia's leading cities, a Roman colony famous for the Battle of Philippi (42 BC) where Octavian and Antony defeated Brutus. As a colony, Philippians took pride in Roman citizenship (cf. 16:21 "We are Romans") and enjoyed Roman legal protections. Lydia came from Thyatira, renowned for its purple dye industry (archaeologists have unearthed numerous dye vats). Purple goods were among the most expensive commodities in the Roman Empire, reserved for aristocracy and royalty, suggesting Lydia held substantial social and economic status. Philippi apparently lacked the ten Jewish men required to establish a synagogue, hence only the informal prayer gathering by the river.

## 经文结构（中文）

全章以**三次相遇**为骨架：**（一）提摩太**（1-5节）——加入团队，割礼的智慧；**（二）异象与欧洲**（6-12节）——圣灵的禁止与引导，从亚洲到欧洲；**（三）三个腓立比人**（13-40节）：吕底亚（商人、上流社会）、占卜使女（受苦的底层）、禁卒（罗马军事官员）——三个完全不同社会背景的人都归主了，展示福音跨越阶级的大能。章节以法律冲突与保罗主张罗马公民权利结束，为整个旅程奠定政治背景。

## Structure (English)

The chapter is framed around **three encounters**: **(1) Timothy** (vv. 1-5)—joining the team, the wisdom of circumcision; **(2) Vision and Europe** (vv. 6-12)—Spirit's prohibition and leading, Asia to Europe; **(3) Three Philippians** (vv. 13-40): Lydia (merchant, upper class), the slave girl (suffering underclass), the jailer (Roman military official)—three people from completely different social backgrounds all come to faith, displaying the gospel's power to cross class boundaries. The chapter ends with legal conflict and Paul asserting Roman citizenship rights, establishing the political backdrop for the whole journey.

## 神学要点（中文）

本章的核心神学是**圣灵对宣教路径的主权**和**神对个别灵魂的拣选**。圣灵"禁止""不许"——这不是偶然，而是神对宣教的主权引导。改革宗传教学（missiology）强调：宣教不是人的战略，而是圣灵主导、人顺从的工作。吕底亚事件中"主开导他的心"是有效呼召的典型例证——神的工作先于人的回应。禁卒的"我当怎样行才可以得救"与"当信主耶稣"的回答，是新约中最简洁的福音摘要。这里没有提到悔改仪式、行为改变或任何附加条件——信心就是一切，信心本身是神所赐的礼物（弗2:8-9）。

## Theology (English)

The central theology is **the Spirit's sovereignty over missionary routes** and **God's election of individual souls**. The Spirit "forbade" and "did not allow"—not by chance, but by God's sovereign guidance of mission. Reformed missiology emphasizes: mission is not human strategy but Spirit-led work with human obedience. The Lydia episode—"the Lord opened her heart"—is the classic instance of effectual calling; God's work precedes human response. The jailer's "What must I do to be saved?" and the answer "Believe in the Lord Jesus" is the most concise gospel summary in the New Testament. No repentance rituals, behavioral changes, or additional conditions are mentioned—faith is everything, and faith itself is God's gift (Eph 2:8-9).

## 基督的影子（中文）

保罗和西拉在狱中赞美的场景，在新约救恩历史中有深刻的基督论意涵。约拿从大鱼腹中祷告（拿2章），约瑟从坑里被拉出，大卫从追杀中被拯救——旧约充满了神在最黑暗处拯救神仆的模式。基督自己在十字架最黑暗的时刻后复活，是这个模式的终极实现。保罗在"午夜"唱诗，照应了主复活在"天未亮时"的黎明前黑暗；地震打开牢门，照应了天使卷石头使坟墓敞开。此外，禁卒的归主展示了福音的普世性——一个执行罗马帝国刑罚的军官，在看见基督大能的那一刻，跪在使徒面前问如何得救。权柄跪在恩典面前。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

The scene of Paul and Silas worshiping in prison carries profound Christological meaning in NT salvation history. Jonah praying in the fish's belly (Jonah 2), Joseph pulled from the pit, David delivered from pursuit—the Old Testament is full of God delivering His servants at their darkest moments. Christ Himself rising after the cross's darkest hour is the ultimate fulfillment of this pattern. Paul singing "at midnight" echoes the pre-dawn darkness before the Lord's resurrection "while it was still dark"; the earthquake opening prison doors echoes the angel rolling the stone from the tomb. Furthermore, the jailer's conversion displays the universality of the gospel—a Roman imperial punishment officer, upon witnessing Christ's power, kneels before the apostles asking how to be saved. Authority kneels before grace.
