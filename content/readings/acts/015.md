## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是路加。使徒行传十五章记载的耶路撒冷大会，是初代教会历史上最重要的一次会议。这次会议的决定奠定了基督教作为全人类救恩的基础，防止了教会滑入犹太律法主义，守住了"唯独恩典、唯独信心"的福音核心。

一、争论的起因（15:1-5）

有几个人从犹太下来，教导弟兄说："你们若不按摩西的规条受割礼，不能得救"（徒15:1）。这是严重的神学危机！如果割礼是得救的必要条件，那么恩典就不是恩典，因信称义就成了空话。

保罗和巴拿巴与他们大大地争论辩驳。教会决定让保罗和巴拿巴并会中几个人，上耶路撒冷去，与使徒和长老商议这问题。他们经过腓尼基、撒马利亚，把外邦人归主的事述说出来，叫众弟兄都甚欢喜。到了耶路撒冷，蒙教会、使徒，和长老接待。但有几个信了的法利赛人站起来说："外邦人必须受割礼，守摩西的律法"（徒15:5）。

二、使徒和长老的议论（15:6-18）

使徒和长老聚会商议这事。争论许久之后，彼得站起来说话。他回顾了哥尼流事件——神拣选他去向外邦人传道，神将圣灵赐给他们，正如赐给我们一样，"神知道人心，藉着赐圣灵给他们，证明了这事，并不把他们和我们区别，正是因为信心，洁净了他们的心"（徒15:8-9）。

然后彼得问了一个击中要害的问题："我们的祖宗和我们尚且担当不起的轭，为甚么现在要惹神发怒，把这轭放在门徒的颈项上呢？我们得救乃是因主耶稣的恩，和他们一样，这是我们所信的"（徒15:10-11）。

这是彼得神学上最成熟的宣告：律法是人担当不起的轭。连以色列人自己都无法遵守律法，怎能把律法强加给外邦人？得救的根基唯一的：是主耶稣的恩典。

众人就默然不语，听巴拿巴和保罗述说神藉他们在外邦人中所行的神迹奇事。之后雅各起来发言，引用阿摩司书9:11-12的预言："此后我要回来，重新修造大卫倒塌的帐幕……叫余剩的人，就是凡称为我名下的外邦人，都寻求主"（徒15:16-17）。这预言早已预告了外邦人的归主，不是律法的要求，而是神救赎计划的实现。

三、大会的决定（15:19-35）

雅各提出了决议：不要使从外邦归主的人难堪，只要吩咐他们戒绝偶像的污秽和奸淫，并勒死的牲畜和血。这四条都是实际的道德要求，目的是促进犹太信徒和外邦信徒之间的团契，而不是得救的条件。

大会写了信，差遣巴撒巴的犹大和西拉同保罗巴拿巴一起下安提阿，当众宣读。信中写道："圣灵和我们定意……"（徒15:28）——这是使徒会议授权的表达，教会在圣灵的引导下做出决定。

众人读了，因信中劝慰的话就欢喜了。弟兄姐妹，这次大会的结论是福音的大宪章：外邦人不需要成为犹太人才能得救，恩典是敞开给所有人的，无论民族、无论背景、无论文化。这是保罗日后在加拉太书和罗马书中详细阐述的真理的历史确证。

四、保罗和巴拿巴的分开（15:36-41）

过了些日，保罗对巴拿巴说："我们回到各城，去望望弟兄怎么样"（徒15:36）。就在这里，发生了一件令人遗憾的事——为了要带约翰马可同去的争论，保罗和巴拿巴分开了。这个争论很激烈，以至于他们彼此分开。

巴拿巴带着马可往居比路去，保罗挑了西拉，蒙弟兄们将主的恩惠交托他，出发往叙利亚和基利家去，坚固那里的教会（徒15:40-41）。

弟兄姐妹，连使徒们也有争论，连大能的保罗也有软弱——但神的旨意借着人的软弱成就。结果是一个团队变成两个团队，福音的工作反而扩展了。马可后来也被保罗重新接纳（提后4:11），说明神的恩典在关系的破裂和修复中同样工作。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Luke. Acts 15 records the Jerusalem Council, the most important meeting in early church history. Its decision laid the foundation of Christianity as salvation for all humanity, preventing the church from sliding into Jewish legalism and preserving the gospel core of grace alone, faith alone.

I. The Origin of the Controversy (15:1-5)

Some men came down from Judea and were teaching the brothers, "And certain men which came down from Judaea taught the brethren, and said, Except ye be circumcised after the manner of Moses, ye cannot be saved." (Acts 15:1). This was a severe theological crisis! If circumcision is required for salvation, then grace is not grace, and justification by faith becomes empty.

Paul and Barnabas had no small dissension and debate with them. The church appointed Paul and Barnabas and some others to go up to Jerusalem to discuss this question with the apostles and elders. Passing through Phoenicia and Samaria, they described the conversion of the Gentiles, bringing great joy to all the brothers. In Jerusalem they were welcomed by the church, apostles, and elders. But some believers who were Pharisees rose up and said, "But there rose up certain of the sect of the Pharisees which believed, saying, That it was needful to circumcise them, and to command them to keep the law of Moses." (Acts 15:5).

II. The Apostles' and Elders' Deliberation (15:6-18)

The apostles and elders gathered to consider this matter. After much debate, Peter rose and said. He recalled the Cornelius incident—God chose him to preach to the Gentiles; God gave them the Holy Spirit just as He had given it to them, "And put no difference between us and them, purifying their hearts by faith." (Acts 15:9).

Then Peter asked the piercing question: "Now therefore why tempt ye God, to put a yoke upon the neck of the disciples, which neither our fathers nor we were able to bear? But we believe that through the grace of the LORD Jesus Christ we shall be saved, even as they." (Acts 15:10-11).

This was Peter's most theologically mature declaration: the law is a yoke no one can bear. If Israel herself could not keep the law, how could they impose it on Gentiles? There is only one foundation for salvation: the grace of the Lord Jesus.

The assembly fell silent and listened to Barnabas and Paul tell of all the signs and wonders God had done through them among the Gentiles. Then James rose and spoke, quoting Amos 9:11-12: "After this I will return, and will build again the tabernacle of David, which is fallen down; and I will build again the ruins thereof, and I will set it up: That the residue of men might seek after the Lord, and all the Gentiles, upon whom my name is called, saith the Lord, who doeth all these things." (Acts 15:16-17). This prophecy had long anticipated the turning of Gentiles—not through law, but through God's redemptive plan.

III. The Council's Decision (15:19-35)

James proposed the resolution: not to trouble those turning to God from the Gentiles, but to write to them to abstain from things polluted by idols, sexual immorality, things strangled, and blood. These four requirements were practical moral guidelines to promote fellowship between Jewish and Gentile believers, not conditions for salvation.

The council wrote a letter and sent Judas Barsabbas and Silas along with Paul and Barnabas to Antioch to read it aloud. The letter stated, "For it seemed good to the Holy Ghost, and to us, to lay upon you no greater burden than these necessary things;" (Acts 15:28)—the language of apostolic authorization; the church under the Spirit's guidance making its decision.

When the people read it, they rejoiced at its encouragement. Brothers and sisters, this council's conclusion is the Magna Carta of the gospel: Gentiles need not become Jews to be saved; grace is open to all, regardless of ethnicity, background, or culture. This is the historical confirmation of the truth Paul later elaborated in Galatians and Romans.

IV. Paul and Barnabas Separate (15:36-41)

After some days, Paul said to Barnabas, "And some days after Paul said unto Barnabas, Let us go again and visit our brethren in every city where we have preached the word of the LORD, and see how they do." (Acts 15:36). Here, a regrettable event occurred—a sharp disagreement about taking John Mark led to Paul and Barnabas separating.

Barnabas took Mark and sailed for Cyprus; Paul chose Silas, was commended by the brothers to the grace of the Lord, and departed for Syria and Cilicia, strengthening the churches (Acts 15:40-41).

Brothers and sisters, even apostles have disputes, even the mighty Paul has weaknesses—but God's purposes are accomplished through human frailty. The result was one team becoming two; the gospel work actually expanded. Mark was later restored to Paul's fellowship (2 Tim 4:11), showing that God's grace works through the breaking and restoration of relationships alike.

## 历史背景（中文）

耶路撒冷大会约发生在公元49年，是基督教历史上第一次"大公会议"的雏形。当时保罗已完成第一次宣教旅行，安提阿教会作为外邦宣教的大本营已颇具规模。"割礼派"（περιτομή，Circumcision Party）是早期教会内的一个强势群体，他们认为外邦信徒必须遵守犹太律法。这次争论的实质是：基督教是犹太教的一个派别，还是面向全人类的新的创造？大会的决定确立了后者。雅各引用的阿摩司书9:11-12，在七十士译本中与希伯来文有出入，雅各使用的是七十士译本版本，这在外邦听众面前更有说服力。

## Historical Context (English)

The Jerusalem Council occurred around 49 AD, a prototype of Christianity's first "ecumenical council." Paul had completed his first missionary journey, and the Antioch church as the Gentile mission headquarters was already substantial. The "Circumcision Party" (περιτομή) was a strong faction in the early church insisting Gentile believers must follow Jewish law. The core question: was Christianity a Jewish sect, or a new creation for all humanity? The council's decision established the latter. The Amos 9:11-12 passage James quoted differs in the Septuagint (Greek OT) from the Hebrew; James used the Septuagint version, which was more persuasive before a mixed Jewish-Gentile audience.

## 经文结构（中文）

本章结构为**问题→辩论→决议→传达→后记**。**起因**（1-5节）——割礼派的主张引发危机；**议论**（6-18节）——三位发言人：彼得（从哥尼流事件论恩典）、巴拿巴与保罗（神迹为证）、雅各（从预言论神的计划）；**决议**（19-29节）——四条实际要求，圣灵印证；**传达**（30-35节）——众人欢喜；**后记**（36-41节）——保罗与巴拿巴分开，第二次宣教旅行准备。三位发言人各有侧重：彼得以个人经历论神学原则，巴拿巴保罗以神迹为历史证据，雅各以旧约为预言根据——三重论证共同确立了结论。

## Structure (English)

The chapter is structured as **problem → debate → decision → communication → postscript**. **Origin** (vv. 1-5)—the Circumcision Party's claims create crisis; **Deliberation** (vv. 6-18)—three speakers: Peter (grace from Cornelius), Barnabas and Paul (miracles as evidence), James (prophecy as God's plan); **Decision** (vv. 19-29)—four practical requirements, confirmed by the Spirit; **Communication** (vv. 30-35)—people rejoice; **Postscript** (vv. 36-41)—Paul and Barnabas separate, preparing for the second journey. The three speakers each contribute differently: Peter argues theological principle from personal experience, Barnabas and Paul provide historical evidence through miracles, James provides prophetic foundation from Scripture—a triple argument jointly establishing the conclusion.

## 神学要点（中文）

本章的核心神学是**恩典的充足性与律法的限制**。彼得的宣告"我们得救乃是因主耶稣的恩"（15:11）是新约中最明确的"唯独恩典"陈述之一。改革宗神学将此概括为"不加任何条件的恩典"——得救不需要任何附加条件，不需要割礼，不需要守律法，只需要相信主耶稣。这次大会的决定从历史上预防了"半律法主义"——一种认为恩典需要人的顺服来配合才完整的错误观点。此外，"圣灵和我们定意"（15:28）揭示了**圣灵对教会治理的主权**——教会的决定不只是人的共识，而是在圣灵引导下的决定。这是改革宗教会论"教会公议"的神学根据。

## Theology (English)

The central theology is **the sufficiency of grace and the limits of law**. Peter's declaration, "we believe that we will be saved through the grace of the Lord Jesus, just as they will" (15:11), is one of the clearest "grace alone" statements in the New Testament. Reformed theology summarizes this as unconditional grace—salvation requires no additional conditions, no circumcision, no law-keeping, only faith in the Lord Jesus. This council's decision historically prevented "semi-legalism"—the error that grace needs human obedience to be complete. Furthermore, "it has seemed good to the Holy Spirit and to us" (15:28) reveals the **Spirit's sovereignty over church governance**—the church's decision is not merely human consensus but guidance by the Spirit. This is the theological basis for the Reformed doctrine of church councils.

## 基督的影子（中文）

耶路撒冷大会的核心论证依赖旧约预言——阿摩司书9:11-12预言列国归主，是神早在旧约时代就已宣告的旨意。这揭示了救恩历史的整体性：**从亚伯拉罕的应许（创12:3"万族都必因你得福"）→旧约的预言（摩9:11-12）→耶稣的成全→外邦人的归主**，是一条贯穿圣经的救恩线索。大会的决定就是承认：这条线索在耶稣基督里完全实现了。外邦人归主不是计划外的事，而是神从创世以前就预定的旨意在历史中的展开。律法是影子，基督是实体；割礼是外在记号，圣灵的内住是最终现实。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

The Jerusalem Council's central argument relies on Old Testament prophecy—Amos 9:11-12's prediction of nations turning to the Lord was a purpose God declared in the OT era. This reveals the unity of salvation history: **from Abraham's promise (Gen 12:3, "in you all families of the earth shall be blessed") → OT prophecy (Amos 9:11-12) → fulfillment in Jesus → Gentiles turning**—a single thread of redemption running through Scripture. The council's decision acknowledged that this thread was fully realized in Jesus Christ. Gentile conversion was not an afterthought but God's predetermined purpose from before creation unfolding in history. The law was shadow; Christ is substance. Circumcision was external sign; the Spirit's indwelling is the ultimate reality.
