## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是路加。使徒行传十四章记载了保罗和巴拿巴在以哥念、路司得、特庇三个城市的宣教事工。这是第一次宣教旅行的后半程，也是我见证神迹与逼迫并行、教会在苦难中生长的鲜活记录。

一、以哥念的宣教（14:1-7）

离开彼西底的安提阿后，我们来到以哥念。保罗和巴拿巴照样进了犹太会堂讲道，"说得犹太人和希腊人信的很多"（徒14:1）。然而不信的犹太人挑拨外邦人，使他们对弟兄们心生恶意。

尽管如此，主在两人手里赐下神迹奇事，印证所传福音的真实。他们在那里住了许多日子，直到城里的人分裂成两派——有人站在犹太人一边，有人站在使徒一边。当外邦人和犹太人的官长商议要凌辱并打死他们时，保罗和巴拿巴知道了，就逃往吕高尼的路司得和特庇，以及周围地方，在那里继续传福音。

弟兄姐妹，这是智慧的撤退，不是怯懦的逃跑。主自己也曾说："这城里的人若逼迫你们，就逃到那城里去"（太10:23）。使徒的策略是：在一处被逼迫，就去另一处传道。逼迫本身成了福音扩张的动力。

二、路司得的神迹与悲剧（14:8-20）

路司得有一个人，两脚无力，生来是瘸腿的，从未走过路。他坐着听保罗讲道。保罗定睛看他，见他有信心可以得痊愈，就大声说："你起来，两脚站直！"那人就跳起来，而且行走（徒14:10）。

群众看见保罗所行的事，就用吕高尼的话大声说："有神藉着人形降临到我们中间了！"他们称巴拿巴为宙斯，称保罗为希耳米，因为他在他们中间为说话的首领。城外宙斯庙的祭司牵着牛，拿着花圈，来到众人那里，要和他们一同献祭。

保罗和巴拿巴听见，就撕裂衣服，跳进众人中间，呼喊说："诸君，为甚么做这事呢？我们也是人，性情和你们一样，我们传福音给你们，是叫你们离弃这些虚妄，归向那创造天地和海并其中万物的永生神"（徒14:14-15）。

这是使徒书信中少见的、面向纯外邦人的布道。保罗没有引用旧约经文，而是从自然神学出发——创造、护理、神留下自己的见证。他说神在过去容忍万国各行其道，却没有撇下自己，用行善的事为自己作证，就是降雨给你们、赐五谷的时候，叫你们心满意足。

然而即便这样，他们几乎拦阻不住众人向他们献祭。不久，犹太人从安提阿和以哥念赶来，劝说众人离保罗而去。他们用石头打保罗，以为他是死了，就把他拖到城外。门徒围绕他的时候，他起来，走进城去，第二天同巴拿巴往特庇去了（徒14:19-20）。

刚才还被当神一样崇拜，转眼就被石头打死——人心的善变，群情的危险，在这一章展示得淋漓尽致。但保罗起来了！从石头堆里起来，继续传道。这份坚忍，是圣灵所赐的恩典。

三、坚固门徒（14:21-28）

在特庇传福音，得了许多门徒之后，他们折回已经建立的教会。保罗和巴拿巴在各城坚固门徒，劝勉他们要恒守信仰，又说"我们进入神的国，必须经历许多艰难"（徒14:22）。

他们在各教会选立了长老，祷告禁食之后，将这些长老交托所信的主。保罗的宣教模式是：传福音建立教会→差立长老→再出发。这是改革宗教会论的根基——有序的教会治理不是束缚，而是神对子民的保护。

最终他们回到安提阿，召集教会众人，述说神藉着他们所行的一切事，并说神怎样开了信道的门，给外邦人进入。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Luke. Acts 14 records Paul and Barnabas' missionary work in Iconium, Lystra, and Derbe. This is the latter half of the first missionary journey—a living record of miracles and persecution running in parallel, the church growing through suffering.

I. Ministry in Iconium (14:1-7)

After leaving Pisidian Antioch, we came to Iconium. Paul and Barnabas went as usual into the Jewish synagogue and spoke in such a way "And it came to pass in Iconium, that they went both together into the synagogue of the Jews, and so spake, that a great multitude both of the Jews and also of the Greeks believed." (Acts 14:1). However, the unbelieving Jews stirred up the Gentiles and poisoned their minds against the brothers.

Even so, the Lord granted them to perform signs and wonders through their hands, confirming the truth of the gospel. They remained there many days, until the city was divided—some sided with the Jews, others with the apostles. When a plot was formed by both Gentiles and Jews with their rulers to mistreat and stone them, they learned of it and fled to Lystra and Derbe in Lycaonia and the surrounding region, continuing to preach the gospel.

Brothers and sisters, this was a wise withdrawal, not cowardly retreat. The Lord himself said, "But when they persecute you in this city, flee ye into another: for verily I say unto you, Ye shall not have gone over the cities of Israel, till the Son of man be come." (Matt 10:23). The apostles' strategy: when persecuted in one place, proclaim in another. Persecution itself became the engine of gospel expansion.

II. Miracle and Tragedy in Lystra (14:8-20)

At Lystra there was a man crippled in his feet, lame from birth, who had never walked. He sat there listening to Paul speak. Paul, looking intently at him and seeing he had faith to be healed, said in a loud voice, "Stand upright on your feet." And he sprang up and began walking (Acts 14:10).

When the crowds saw what Paul had done, they lifted up their voices in the Lycaonian language, saying, "The gods have come down to us in the likeness of men!" They called Barnabas Zeus, and Paul, Hermes, because he was the chief speaker. The priest of Zeus whose temple was at the entrance to the city brought oxen and garlands to the gates to offer sacrifice with the crowds.

Paul and Barnabas tore their garments and rushed out into the crowd, crying out, "Which when the apostles, Barnabas and Paul, heard of, they rent their clothes, and ran in among the people, crying out, And saying, Sirs, why do ye these things? We also are men of like passions with you, and preach unto you that ye should turn from these vanities unto the living God, which made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and all things that are therein:" (Acts 14:14-15).

This is one of the rare pure-Gentile evangelistic speeches in Acts. Paul did not quote the Old Testament but started from natural theology—creation, providence, God's witness through goodness. He said God in past generations allowed all nations to walk in their own ways, yet He did not leave Himself without witness by doing good, giving rains and fruitful seasons, satisfying your hearts with food and gladness.

Even so, they could hardly restrain the crowds from offering sacrifice to them. Soon, Jews from Antioch and Iconium arrived, persuading the crowds to turn against Paul. They stoned Paul and dragged him out of the city, supposing him to be dead. But when the disciples gathered around him, he rose up and entered the city, and on the next day went on with Barnabas to Derbe (Acts 14:19-20).

Just worshipped as a god, then stoned as dead—the fickleness of crowds, the danger of mass emotion, all displayed vividly in this chapter. But Paul arose! From the stones he rose and continued preaching. This endurance was grace granted by the Holy Spirit.

III. Strengthening the Disciples (14:21-28)

After preaching the gospel in Derbe and making many disciples, they retraced their steps to the churches they had established. Paul and Barnabas strengthened the souls of the disciples, encouraging them to continue in the faith, saying "Confirming the souls of the disciples, and exhorting them to continue in the faith, and that we must through much tribulation enter into the kingdom of God." (Acts 14:22).

They appointed elders in every church; with prayer and fasting they committed them to the Lord in whom they had believed. Paul's missionary pattern: preach the gospel, establish churches, appoint elders, move on. This is the foundation of Reformed ecclesiology—ordered church governance is not a constraint but God's protection of His people.

Finally they returned to Antioch, gathered the church, and declared all that God had done with them, and how He had opened a door of faith for the Gentiles.

## 历史背景（中文）

路司得（Lystra）是罗马在吕高尼地区设立的殖民城市，居民以农耕为主，文化程度较低，保留着古老的多神信仰。当地流传宙斯和希耳米曾以人形拜访此地的传说（参奥维德《变形记》），这解释了为何当地人会将保罗和巴拿巴当作神。考古学家在距现代土耳其城市哈通萨拉伊不远处发现了路司得的遗址，出土的铭文证实了宙斯崇拜在当地的盛行。"用石头打保罗"是律法规定的处决方式，死刑后将尸体拖到城外也符合当时习俗。提摩太就是路司得人（徒16:1），可能是在第一次宣教旅行时信主的。

## Historical Context (English)

Lystra was a Roman colony established in the Lycaonian region, populated mainly by farmers, with lower literacy and ancient polytheistic beliefs preserved. Local legends told of Zeus and Hermes visiting the area in human form (cf. Ovid's Metamorphoses), explaining why the locals took Paul and Barnabas for gods. Archaeologists have identified the site of Lystra near modern Hatunsaray in Turkey; inscriptions found there confirm the prevalence of Zeus worship. Stoning was the legally prescribed execution method, and dragging the body outside the city after death was also customary practice. Timothy was from Lystra (Acts 16:1), likely converted during this first missionary journey.

## 经文结构（中文）

第十四章结构紧凑，以**城市为单元**推进：**以哥念**（1-7节）——分裂与逃跑；**路司得**（8-20节）——神迹、被奉为神、石头殴打，形成极强对比；**特庇**（21节）——简短但成果丰盛；**回程**（21-28节）——坚固教会、立长老、述说神的作为。第二段的戏剧性最强：从被崇拜到被打死，仅一段之隔，展示了外邦人信仰的脆弱与群情的危险。全章以使徒回到安提阿述职结束，形成第一次宣教旅行的完整闭环。

## Structure (English)

Chapter 14 moves efficiently from **city to city**: **Iconium** (vv. 1-7)—division and flight; **Lystra** (vv. 8-20)—miracle, deification, stoning (a dramatic contrast); **Derbe** (v. 21)—brief but fruitful; **return journey** (vv. 21-28)—strengthening churches, appointing elders, reporting God's works. The Lystra section is most dramatic: from worship to near-death stoning in a single paragraph, exposing the fragility of pagan faith and the danger of mob emotion. The chapter closes with the apostles returning to Antioch to report, completing the full arc of the first missionary journey.

## 神学要点（中文）

本章的核心神学主题之一是**普遍恩典的神学**（徒14:16-17）。保罗对外邦人说：神在过去容忍万国各行其道，但"你们应当仰望那创造天地……的活神"。这是新约中最明确的普遍启示神学之一——神藉着创造和历史，向所有人见证自己的存在和良善。改革宗神学将此理解为"普遍恩典"——神对所有受造物都有善意的看顾，雨水、粮食、季节是神的恩赐，即便对不认识他的人也是如此。其次，"我们进入神的国，必须经历许多艰难"（14:22）——这是韦敏斯德信条所教导的"圣徒在苦难中的坚忍"的圣经根据。

## Theology (English)

A central theological theme is **common grace** (Acts 14:16-17). Paul told the Gentiles: in past generations God allowed all nations to walk in their own ways, yet He did not leave Himself without witness, providing rains and seasons and food. This is one of the clearest New Testament statements of natural theology—God bears witness to all people through creation and history. Reformed theology understands this as "common grace"—God's benevolent care for all creatures; rain, grain, and seasons are God's gifts even to those who do not know Him. Second, "through many tribulations we must enter the kingdom of God" (14:22)—this is the biblical basis for the Westminster Confession's teaching on the: perseverance of the saints in suffering.

## 基督的影子（中文）

路司得瘸腿人得医治呼应了耶稣在约翰福音5章医治毕士大池旁瘸腿人的神迹——都是生来残疾、一句话得痊愈、立刻起来行走。这个模式来自以赛亚书35:6的预言："那时，瘸子必跳跃如鹿。"神迹证明神国已经临近，基督的救赎大能借着使徒继续工作。保罗从石头堆中起来也有复活的意象——门徒围绕他的时候他起来了，继续传道，这预表基督徒的受苦与复活的盼望：即便经历如死的苦难，也要像主一样起来，继续走完使命。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

The healing of the lame man in Lystra echoes Jesus' healing of the paralytic at Bethesda in John 5—both lame from birth, both healed by a single word, both immediately rising and walking. This pattern fulfills Isaiah 35:6's prophecy: "Then shall the lame man leap like a deer." The miracle testifies that God's kingdom has drawn near; Christ's redemptive power continues working through the apostles. Paul's rising from the stones also carries resurrection imagery—when the disciples gathered around him he arose and continued preaching, foreshadowing the Christian's suffering and resurrection hope: even through death-like suffering, rise as the Lord did and complete the mission.
