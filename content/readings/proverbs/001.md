## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门，大卫的儿子，以色列的王。箴言书是我在人生的某个阶段写下的教训，献给我的儿子，也献给每一个正在人生路上面临诱惑和选择的年轻人。

**写作的目的（第1-6节）**

> "以色列王大卫儿子所罗门的箴言：要使人晓得智慧和训诲，分辨通达的言语，使人处事领受智慧、仁义、公平、正直的训诲，使愚蒙人灵明，使少年人有知识和谋略。"

"使愚蒙人灵明"。注意这个词：箴言不是写给智慧人的，而是写给"愚蒙人"的。我们都是愚蒙的。在神面前、在时间面前、在永恒面前，我们都需要被"灵明"。接受教导的人才能变得智慧；拒绝教导的人永远是愚蒙的。

**智慧的开端（第7节）**

> "敬畏耶和华是知识的开端；愚妄人藐视智慧和训诲。"

整本箴言书的核心在这里："敬畏耶和华是知识的开端"。不是"学习是知识的开端"，不是"努力是知识的开端"，而是"敬畏耶和华"。这意味着什么？

"敬畏"不是恐惧，而是敬重、顺服、以神为绝对的权威。当你把神放在知识的中心，你的整个世界观就改变了。敬畏耶和华才是知识的"开端"。如果没有这个根基，你所有的知识都是建在沙子上的。

"愚妄人藐视智慧和训诲"。愚妄人的特征不是智商低，而是"藐视"。他们知道有智慧和训诲，但他们不在乎。骄傲使人愚蒙，不是智商使人愚蒙。你有没有"藐视"过任何人的教训？觉得自己不需要被教？那就是愚妄的起点。

**父亲的劝勉（第8-19节）**

> "我儿，要听你父亲的训诲，不可离弃你母亲的法则；因为这要作你头上的华冠，你项上的金链。"

所罗门在第7节讲完"敬畏耶和华"的大原则之后，马上转到一件最具体的事：听从父母。这不是巧合。敬畏神不是一个抽象的概念，它首先落实在家庭里。你怎样对待父母的教训，就反映你怎样对待神的权柄。经文用"华冠"和"金链"来形容顺服。顺服不是枷锁，而是装饰。听从教训的人是美的、是尊贵的。

> "我儿，恶人若引诱你，你不可随从。他们若说：'你与我们同去，我们要埋伏流人之血，要蹲伏害无辜之人。我们好像阴间，把他们活活吞下；他们如同下坑的人，被我们囫囵吞了。我们必得各样宝物，将所抢的装满房屋。你与我们大家同分，我们共用一个囊袋。'"

注意，恶人的引诱永远有同一个套路："与我们同去"，先拉你进圈子；"必得各样宝物"，再用利益诱惑你。罪的引诱从来不会告诉你代价，只告诉你好处。"共用一个囊袋"，看起来像团结，其实是捆绑。当有人说"跟我们一起，保证赚"的时候，你要格外小心。

> "我儿，不要与他们同行一道，禁止你脚走他们的路。因为他们的脚奔跑行恶，他们急速流人的血。好像飞鸟，网罗设在眼前仍不躲避。这些人埋伏，是为自流己血；蹲伏，是为自害己命。凡贪恋财利的，所行之路都是如此；这贪恋之心乃夺去得财者之命。"

这里有一个惊人的反转：恶人以为在害别人，其实是在"自流己血"。罪最大的欺骗就是让你以为你在占便宜，其实你在毁灭自己。"飞鸟看见网罗仍不躲避"，我们常常也是这样：明明看到危险，还是往里冲。贪婪蒙蔽了我们的眼睛。"贪恋之心乃夺去得财者之命"，贪财不只是道德问题，而是生死问题。提摩太前书6:10说"贪财是万恶之根"，所罗门在这里已经说得很清楚了。

**智慧的呼声（第20-23节）**

> "智慧在街市上呼喊，在宽阔处发声，在热闹街头呼喊，在城门口，在城中发出言语说：你们愚昧人喜爱愚昧，亵慢人喜欢亵慢，愚蒙人恨恶知识，要到几时呢？你们当因我的责备回转；我要将我的灵浇灌你们，将我的话指示你们。"

"智慧在街市上呼喊"。不是在象牙塔里、不是在学术期刊里，而是在"街市上"。智慧是公开的、是对所有人可得的。神的智慧不是只给精英的，任何人都可以来寻求。

"我要将我的灵浇灌你们"。这是圣灵的预表！箴言1章的智慧在说："我要将我的灵浇灌你们"。约翰福音14:26说"但保惠师，就是父因我的名所要差来的圣灵，祂要教导你们一切的事。"真正的智慧不是靠学习来的，而是靠圣灵的光照来的。

**拒绝的后果（第24-31节）**

> "我呼唤，你们不肯听；我伸手，无人理会；反轻弃我一切的劝戒，不肯受我的责备。你们遭难的时候，我就发笑；惊恐临到你们的时候，我便嗤笑……那时，他们必呼求我，我却不答应，必切切寻找我，却寻不见。"

这是整首诗最可怕的部分：拒绝智慧的后果是，到你需要智慧的时候，你找不到了。智慧不是永远等你的。今天你拒绝呼唤的智慧，明天苦难来临的时候你再去寻求，"却寻不见"。

弟兄姐妹，"趁可寻找的时候寻找"。有一个窗口期。不要等到苦难来了才想到要寻求神的智慧。

**智慧与安全（第33节）**

> "惟有听从我的，必安然居住，得享安静，不怕灾祸。"

整章以这个承诺结束：听从智慧的人"安然居住"。不是没有问题，而是"不怕灾祸"。智慧不是消除苦难的咒语，而是在苦难中稳固的根基。敬畏耶和华的人，在风暴中仍然"安然居住"。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon, son of David, King of Israel. Proverbs contains the instruction I wrote during a certain season of life, dedicated to my son and to every young person walking life's road, facing temptation and choice.

**The Purpose of Writing (vv. 1-6)**

> "The proverbs of Solomon, son of David, king of Israel: To know wisdom and instruction, to understand words of insight, to receive instruction in wise dealing, in righteousness, justice, and equity; to give prudence to the simple, knowledge and discretion to the youth."

"To give prudence to the simple." Proverbs was not written for the wise but for the "simple." We are all simple. Before God, before time, before eternity, we all need to be enlightened. Those who accept teaching become wise; those who refuse teaching remain simple forever.

**The Beginning of Wisdom (v. 7)**

> "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge; fools despise wisdom and instruction."

Proverbs' core is here: "the fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge." Not "learning is the beginning," not "hard work is the beginning," but "fearing the LORD." When you place God at knowledge's center, your entire worldview changes.

"Fools despise wisdom and instruction." The fool's characteristic is not low IQ but "despising." They know wisdom and instruction exist, but don't care. Pride makes people foolish, not intellect. Have you ever "despised" someone's instruction? Felt you didn't need teaching? That is folly's starting point.

**A Father's Exhortation (vv. 8-19)**

> "Hear, my son, your father's instruction, and forsake not your mother's teaching, for they are a graceful garland for your head and pendants for your neck."

Right after the great principle of "fear the LORD" in verse 7, Solomon turns to the most concrete thing: listen to your parents. This is no coincidence. Fearing God is not an abstract concept; it first takes root in the family. How you treat your parents' instruction reflects how you treat God's authority. "A graceful garland" and "pendants": obedience is not a chain but an adornment. Those who heed instruction are beautiful and honored.

> "My son, if sinners entice you, do not consent. If they say, 'Come with us, let us lie in wait for blood...we shall find all precious goods, we shall fill our houses with plunder; throw in your lot among us; we will all have one purse.'"

Notice: the enticement of the wicked always follows the same script. "Come with us," first they pull you into the circle; "we shall find precious goods," then they lure you with profit. Sin's invitation never tells you the cost, only the benefit. "One purse" looks like solidarity but is really bondage. When someone says "join us, guaranteed profit," be on guard.

> "My son, do not walk in the way with them; hold back your foot from their paths, for their feet run to evil, and they make haste to shed blood. For in vain is a net spread in the sight of any bird, but these men lie in wait for their own blood; they set an ambush for their own lives. Such are the ways of everyone who is greedy for unjust gain; it takes away the life of its possessors."

Here is the stunning reversal: the wicked think they are harming others, but they are shedding "their own blood." Sin's greatest deception is making you think you are gaining, when you are destroying yourself. "A net spread in the sight of any bird," yet we are often the same: we see the danger clearly and still rush in. Greed blinds our eyes. "It takes away the life of its possessors." Greed is not merely a moral issue, but a matter of life and death. 1 Timothy 6:10 says "the love of money is the root of all kinds of evil." Solomon already makes this crystal clear here.

**Wisdom's Call (vv. 20-23)**

> "Wisdom cries aloud in the street, in the markets she raises her voice...saying, 'How long, O simple ones, will you love being simple?...Behold, I will pour out my spirit to you; I will make my words known to you.'"

"Wisdom cries aloud in the street," not in an ivory tower, but "in the street." Wisdom is public, available to all. "I will pour out my spirit to you." The Holy Spirit's foreshadowing! John 14:26: "the Helper, the Holy Spirit...will teach you all things." True wisdom comes not through study, but through the Spirit's illumination.

**The Consequences of Rejection (vv. 24-31)**

> "Because I have called and you refused to listen...I will also laugh at your calamity; I will mock when terror strikes you...Then they will call upon me, but I will not answer; they will seek me diligently but will not find me."

This is the most terrifying consequence of rejection: when you finally need wisdom, you cannot find it. Today's rejected call, tomorrow's unanswered cry. When calamity strikes and you seek wisdom, "will not find it."

**Wisdom's Safety (v. 33)**

> "But whoever listens to me will dwell secure and will be at ease, without dread of disaster."

The chapter ends with this promise: those who listen to wisdom "dwell secure." Not without problems, but "without dread of disaster." Those who fear the LORD, in storms still "dwell secure."

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言书第一章是整本书的序言和纲领。"所罗门的箴言"这个标题覆盖整本书，尽管书中实际包含多位作者的内容（见箴22:17"智慧人的言语"、箴30"雅基的儿子亚古珥的言语"、箴31"利慕伊勒王的言语"）。"敬畏耶和华是知识的开端"（第7节）被称为箴言的"主题句"，这句话在箴言书中以不同形式出现多次（参箴9:10"敬畏耶和华是智慧的开端"）。第20-33节的"智慧"（chokhmah）在学术界有争议。有人认为这是拟人化的智慧（personification），有人认为这是对神圣智慧（divine wisdom）的预表，甚至是对基督前身的预表。箴言书在犹太传统中属于"圣文集"（Ketuvim），与诗篇、约伯记等被归为以色列的智慧文学。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs chapter 1 is the entire book's preface and outline. "Proverbs of Solomon" covers the whole book, though it actually contains multiple authors (see Prov 22:17 "the words of the wise," Prov 30 "the words of Agur son of Jakeh," Prov 31 "the words of King Lemuel"). Verse 7 "the fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" is called Proverbs' "theme verse," appearing in various forms multiple times (cf. Prov 9:10 "the fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom"). Proverbs 1:20-33's "Wisdom" (chokhmah) is academically debated—some see personification, others a foreshadowing of divine wisdom, even a Christ-type. Proverbs belongs to the "Writings" (Ketuvim) in Jewish tradition, alongside Psalms and Job as Israel's wisdom literature.

## 经文结构（中文）

**序言：写作目的（1-7节）**：所罗门箴言→使人有智慧训诲→使愚蒙人灵明→"敬畏耶和华是知识的开端"。
**父亲的劝勉：听从我的教训（8-19节）**：听从父母教训→不要从恶人→恶人伏击流人血→贪财之人自丧己命。
**智慧的呼唤（20-33节）**：街市上呼喊→责备愚妄→要将我灵浇灌→拒绝者必受审判→到时寻找不见→听从者安然居住。

## Structure (English)

**Prologue: Purpose of Writing (vv. 1-7)**: Solomon's proverbs → to know wisdom → give prudence to simple → "fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge."
**Father's Exhortation: Hear My Teaching (vv. 8-19)**: Hear father and mother → don't follow sinners → sinners lie in wait → greedy destroy themselves.
**Wisdom's Call (vv. 20-33)**: Crying aloud in streets → rebuking the simple → "I will pour out my spirit" → rejecters face judgment → seek and not find → listeners dwell secure.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"敬畏耶和华是知识的开端"：知识的神学根基**
改革宗传统教导神是一切知识的根基。加尔文在《基督教要义》开篇说："几乎所有的智慧……包括两部分：对神的认识和对自己的认识。"敬畏耶和华不是知识的"一部分"，而是"开端"。离开神，知识会被滥用，成为骄傲的工具而不是谦卑的学习。

**"我要将我的灵浇灌你们"：圣灵是智慧的源头**
改革宗传统教导圣灵光照圣经和赐予智慧。韦敏斯德信条第一章教导：我们关于圣经完全权威的信仰来自于圣灵在我们心中的见证。真正的智慧不是人类理性的产品，而是圣灵赐予的礼物（林前2:10-12）。

**"愚妄人藐视智慧"：骄傲是愚蒙的根源**
改革宗传统教导骄傲是原罪的核心。韦敏斯德信条第6章论原罪和堕落。始祖的罪是渴望"如神"，是骄傲。"愚妄人藐视"，藐视是骄傲的表现。谦卑是智慧的前提，骄傲使人愚蒙。

## Theology (English)

**"Fear of the LORD Is the Beginning of Knowledge": Knowledge's Theological Foundation**
The Reformed tradition teaches God is all knowledge's foundation. Calvin opens the Institutes: "Nearly all the wisdom we possess...consists of two parts: the knowledge of God and of ourselves." Fearing the LORD is not "part" of knowledge, but its "beginning." Without God, knowledge is misused, becoming a tool of pride rather than humble learning.

**"I Will Pour Out My Spirit": The Spirit Is Wisdom's Source**
The Reformed tradition teaches the Spirit illumines Scripture and grants wisdom. Westminster Confession chapter 1. True wisdom is not human rationality's product—but the Spirit's gift (1 Cor 2:10-12).

**"Fools Despise Wisdom": Pride Is Folly's Root**
The Reformed tradition teaches pride is original sin's core. Westminster Confession chapter 6 on original sin. Adam and Eve's sin was desiring to "be like God"—pride. "Fools despise"—despising is pride's expression. Humility is wisdom's prerequisite—pride makes people foolish.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督是智慧本身**。哥林多前书1:24"但我们所传的……是神的能力，神的智慧。"1:30"但你们得在基督耶稣里，是本乎神，神又使他成为我们的智慧。"箴言1章的"智慧"在基督身上完全应验。基督不只是"传授"智慧，祂就是智慧本身。

**"我要将我的灵浇灌你们"：基督赐圣灵**。约翰福音7:38-39"信我的人……从他腹中要流出活水的江河来。耶稣这话是指着信他之人要受圣灵说的。"箴言1:23"我要将我的灵浇灌你们"。这个应许在五旬节由基督差派圣灵而完全成就（徒2:33）。

**"不怕灾祸"：在基督里的安全**。罗马书8:35-39"谁能使我们与基督的爱隔绝呢？难道是患难吗……凡这一切，靠着爱我们的主，已经得胜有余了。"箴言1:33"听从我的必安然居住不怕灾祸"，在基督里最终应验。不是没有灾祸，而是在基督的爱中"不怕"。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Is Wisdom Itself**. 1 Corinthians 1:24 "Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God"; 1:30 "he is the source of your life in Christ Jesus, whom God made our wisdom." Proverbs 1's "Wisdom" is fully realized in Christ. Christ doesn't merely "teach" wisdom; He is wisdom itself.

**"I Will Pour Out My Spirit": Christ Sends the Spirit**. John 7:38-39 "Whoever believes in me, as the Scripture has said, 'Out of his heart will flow rivers of living water.' Now this he said about the Spirit." Proverbs 1:23 "I will pour out my spirit." This promise was completely fulfilled at Pentecost when Christ sent the Spirit (Acts 2:33).

**"Without Dread of Disaster": Safety in Christ**. Romans 8:35-39: "Who shall separate us from the love of Christ? Shall tribulation...? No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us." Proverbs 1:33 "whoever listens will dwell secure without dread of disaster," ultimately fulfilled in Christ. Not without disaster, but "without dread" in Christ's love.
