## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第十五章以一句极为实用的智慧开场——关于我们的口，关于我们的回应，关于在争吵来临时如何选择。

**柔和的回答（第1节）**

> "回答柔和，使怒消退；言语暴戾，触动怒气。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整本箴言中最实用的一句格言之一。争吵要来的时候——你的回应决定了火势。"柔和的回答"是一盆水——能灭火。"暴戾的言语"是一桶油——会助燃。弟兄姐妹，下次有人对你发脾气的时候——你的第一反应是什么？以怒还怒？还是柔和地回答？弟兄姐妹，柔和不是软弱——柔和需要更大的力量，因为你要克制自己天然的反应。

智慧人知道：赢得争论和赢得人心是两回事。你可以言语暴戾地"赢得"一场争论——但你永远地失去了那个人。还是柔和地回答——也许你没有"赢得"论点——但你赢得了关系。弟兄姐妹，你更在乎赢得论点还是赢得人心？

**耶和华在各处（第3节）**

> "耶和华的眼目无处不在；恶人善人他都鉴察。"

弟兄姐妹，这节经文改变了你所有的行为动机。如果你只在有人看见的时候才行善——那不是善，那是表演。弟兄姐妹，"无处不在的眼目"意味着你在黑暗中做的事、你在关上门之后做的事、你在独处时做的事——神都看见了。这不是要叫你恐惧——而是要叫你诚实。弟兄姐妹，你在没有人看见的时候——是什么样的人？那才是真实的你。

**愁苦的心与喜乐的心（第13-15节）**

> "心中喜乐，面带笑容；心里忧愁，灵被损伤。有聪明的心，寻求知识；愚昧人的口以吃喝为乐。困苦人的日子都是愁苦；心中欢乐的，常享丰筵。"

弟兄姐妹，"心中欢乐的常享丰筵"——弟兄姐妹，不是因为他的饭菜特别丰盛——而是因为他的心让他把每一顿平凡的饭都变成丰筵。两个人面对同样的处境——一个看到的是愁苦，一个看到的是丰筵——区别不在处境，而在心态。

弟兄姐妹，你的内心影响你看外部世界的方式。一个心中有喜乐的人——即使在苦难中也能"享丰筵"。一个心中充满苦毒的人——即使处境很好也"日日是愁苦"。弟兄姐妹，你的心里种的是什么？愁苦，还是喜乐？

**谦卑与敬畏耶和华（第33节）**

> "敬畏耶和华是智慧的训诲；尊荣以前，必有谦卑。"

弟兄姐妹，"尊荣以前必有谦卑"——这是箴言中反复出现的原则。弟兄姐妹，你想得到尊荣吗？那就先学谦卑。这是颠覆人自然本能的道理——人的本能是：想高就要自我推销、想有地位就要显示实力。但神的原则是相反的——先谦卑，然后神来高举你。

弟兄姐妹，把这个原则用在你的生活里——在家庭里：先谦卑服事，再赢得尊重。在职场里：先低头学习，再被委以重任。在教会里：先甘心服事，再被神高举。弟兄姐妹，有谁试图走捷径——绕过谦卑直接要尊荣——结果是什么？骄傲的倒塌，不是预期的尊荣。

**神离恶人远，垂听义人的祷告（第29节）**

> "耶和华离恶人远；却听义人的祷告。"

弟兄姐妹，"离恶人远"——弟兄姐妹，这句话应该让我们警醒。如果你一直在做神憎恶的事——你的祷告为什么会被听见？以赛亚书1:15"你们举手祷告，我必遮眼不看；就是你们多多祈祷，我也不听，因为你们的手都是满了血腥。"弟兄姐妹，不是神的耳朵不好使——而是你的生命把神推远了。弟兄姐妹，你希望你的祷告被神听见吗？那就先让你的生命转向祂。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 15 opens with an extremely practical wisdom—about our mouths, our responses, how to choose when argument arrives.

**A Soft Answer (v. 1)**

> "A soft answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger."

Brothers and sisters, one of Proverbs' most practical sayings. When argument approaches—your response determines the fire's intensity. "A soft answer" is a bucket of water—extinguishing fire. "A harsh word" is a bucket of oil—feeding flames. Next time someone erupts at you—what's your first reaction? Returning anger for anger? Or answering softly? Soft is not weakness—it requires greater strength, overriding your natural reaction.

The wise know: winning an argument and winning a person's heart are two different things. You can "win" an argument with harsh words—and permanently lose that person. Or answer softly—perhaps not "winning" the point—but winning the relationship.

**The LORD Is Everywhere (v. 3)**

> "The eyes of the LORD are in every place, keeping watch on the evil and the good."

Brothers and sisters, this verse changes all your behavioral motivation. If you only do good when others watch—that's not goodness, that's performance. "Eyes in every place" means what you do in the dark, behind closed doors, in private—God sees. Not meant to terrify—but to make you honest. Who are you when no one is watching? That is the real you.

**A Sorrowful Heart and a Joyful Heart (vv. 13-15)**

> "A glad heart makes a cheerful face, but by sorrow of heart the spirit is crushed...All the days of the afflicted are evil, but the cheerful of heart has a continual feast."

Brothers and sisters, "the cheerful of heart has a continual feast"—not because their food is especially lavish—but because their heart transforms every ordinary meal into a feast. Two people face the same situation—one sees suffering, one sees feasting—the difference isn't the situation but the heart.

**Humility and Fear of the LORD (v. 33)**

> "The fear of the LORD is instruction in wisdom, and humility comes before honor."

Brothers and sisters, "humility comes before honor"—Proverbs' recurring principle. Want honor? First learn humility. This contradicts natural instinct—instinct says: want to rise, self-promote; want status, display strength. But God's principle is opposite—first humble yourself, then God exalts you. Use this in your life—in family: first serve humbly, then earn respect. In work: first lower your head to learn, then be entrusted with responsibility.

**The LORD Hears the Righteous (v. 29)**

> "The LORD is far from the wicked, but he hears the prayer of the righteous."

Brothers and sisters, "far from the wicked"—if you're constantly doing what God hates—why would your prayers be heard? Isaiah 1:15: "When you spread out your hands, I will hide my eyes from you; even though you make many prayers, I will not listen." Not that God's ears are failing—but your life has pushed God away. Want your prayers heard? First turn your life toward Him.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言15:1"回答柔和使怒消退"在古代近东的智慧文学（如埃及的《阿美内莫帕教训》）中有类似的表达，显示了这是跨文化的人类智慧经验。箴言15:3"耶和华的眼目无处不在"与神的全知神学密切相关，也反映了智慧传统中"神在场"（divine presence）的核心信念。第29节"耶和华离恶人远却听义人的祷告"被彼得前书3:12直接引用："因为主的眼看顾义人，主的耳听他们的祈祷；惟有行恶的人，主向他们变脸。"显示了这个原则在新约伦理教导中的持续重要性。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 15:1 "a soft answer turns away wrath" has analogues in ancient Near Eastern wisdom literature (e.g., Egyptian "Instruction of Amenemope"), showing this is cross-cultural human wisdom experience. Verse 3 "the eyes of the LORD are in every place" relates closely to omniscience theology, reflecting the wisdom tradition's core belief in "divine presence." Verse 29 "the LORD hears the prayer of the righteous" is directly quoted in 1 Peter 3:12, demonstrating this principle's continuing importance in NT ethical teaching.

## 经文结构（中文）

**柔和的回答（1-2节）**——柔和使怒消退→暴戾触动怒气→智慧人的口散布知识。
**耶和华无处不在（3-11节）**——眼目无处不在→柔和的舌头是生命树→愚妄人的口吐出愚妄。
**喜乐的心（12-20节）**——亵慢人不爱受责→喜乐的心使面带笑容→心中欢乐的常享丰筵。
**听劝言与骄傲（21-33节）**——无知人以愚妄为乐→人心筹划→耶和华的旨意成就→尊荣以前必有谦卑。

## Structure (English)

**Soft Answer (vv. 1-2)**—Soft turns away wrath → harsh stirs anger → wise mouth spreads knowledge.
**The LORD Is Everywhere (vv. 3-11)**—Eyes in every place → gentle tongue is tree of life → fool's mouth pours out folly.
**The Joyful Heart (vv. 12-20)**—Scoffer doesn't love reproof → glad heart makes cheerful face → cheerful of heart has continual feast.
**Counsel and Pride (vv. 21-33)**—Fool takes pleasure in folly → people plan → LORD's purpose prevails → humility comes before honor.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"柔和回答"——圣灵所结的果子**
改革宗传统教导成圣产生圣灵果子。加拉太书5:22-23"圣灵所结的果子就是仁爱、喜乐、和平、忍耐、恩慈、良善、信实、温柔、节制。""柔和的回答"对应"温柔"（gentleness/prautes）——是圣灵在信徒心中产生的，不是靠意志力强压出来的。

**"神的眼目无处不在"——上帝的无处不在**
改革宗传统教导神的无处不在（omnipresence）。韦敏斯德信条第2章。"无处不在的眼目"不是恐惧的源头——而是信心的基础：没有一处你能走到神不在的地方，也没有一处你能在神的眼目之外受害。

**"谦卑在尊荣之前"——神的颠覆性经济**
改革宗传统教导神的国度是颠覆性的。路加福音1:52"他叫有权柄的失位，叫卑贱的升高。"谦卑在尊荣之前——这是神的经济，与人的经济完全相反。

## Theology (English)

**"Soft Answer"—Fruit of the Spirit**
The Reformed tradition teaches sanctification produces the Spirit's fruit. Galatians 5:22-23: "the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control." "A soft answer" corresponds to "gentleness"—produced by the Spirit in believers, not forced by willpower.

**"The LORD's Eyes Everywhere"—God's Omnipresence**
The Reformed tradition teaches omnipresence. Westminster Confession chapter 2.: Eyes everywhere is not fear's source—but faith's foundation: no place you can go where God is absent; no place where you can be harmed outside God's sight.

**"Humility Before Honor"—God's Subversive Economy**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's kingdom is subversive. Luke 1:52: "he has brought down the mighty from their thrones and exalted those of humble estate." Humility before honor—God's economy, completely opposite to humanity's.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"柔和的回答"——基督面对指控的沉默**——以赛亚书53:7"他被欺压，在受苦的时候却不开口；他像羊羔被牵到宰杀之地……他也不开口。"彼得前书2:23"他被骂不还口；受害不说威吓的话。"基督是"柔和回答"的最高典范——面对最不公的指控，祂的沉默就是最完美的柔和。

**"谦卑在尊荣之前"——基督的道路**——腓立比书2:8-9"就自己卑微，存心顺服以至于死，且死在十字架上。所以神将他升为至高，又赐给他那超乎万名之上的名。"基督是"谦卑在尊荣之前"的终极实现——最深的谦卑（十字架之死）→最高的尊荣（神将祂升为至高）。

**"耶和华听义人的祷告"——基督的代祷**——希伯来书7:25"他是长远活着，替他们祈求。"约翰福音11:41-42"父啊……我知道你常听我。"基督是唯一完全义的人——祂的代祷是完全被听见的——而我们的祷告藉着祂被神所听。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Soft Answer"—Christ's Silence Before Accusers**—Isaiah 53:7: "He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth; like a lamb that is led to the slaughter...he opened not his mouth." 1 Peter 2:23: "When he was reviled, he did not revile in return." Christ is the supreme example of "soft answer"—facing the most unjust accusations, His silence was perfect gentleness.

**"Humility Before Honor"—Christ's Path**—Philippians 2:8-9: "he humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross. Therefore God has highly exalted him." Christ is "humility before honor's" ultimate realization—deepest humility (cross death) → highest honor (God exalted Him).

**"The LORD Hears the Righteous"—Christ's Intercession**—Hebrews 7:25: "he always lives to make intercession for them." John 11:41-42: "Father...I knew that you always hear me." Christ is the only perfectly righteous person—His intercession is perfectly heard—and our prayers are heard by God through Him.
