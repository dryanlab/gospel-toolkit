## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第二十章以几个关于道德、人性和神的主权的深刻洞见为核心，特别是关于自我欺骗的危险和神对人生命的主权引导。

**酒的警告（第1节）**

> "酒是讥笑人的；浓酒是叫人喧嚷的；凡因酒错误的，就无智慧。"

弟兄姐妹，"酒是讥笑人的"——把酒拟人化——酒在"讥笑"那些被它控制的人。弟兄姐妹，任何让你失去自控的东西——在讥笑你。你以为你在享受它——实际上它在控制你、讥笑你的软弱。"凡因酒错误的就无智慧"——弟兄姐妹，判断一个人是否有智慧——看他能不能抵制让他失控的东西。

**谁能说"我洁净了我的心"（第9节）**

> "谁能说我洁净了我的心，我脱净了我的罪呢？"

弟兄姐妹，这是修辞性的问题——答案是：没有人。弟兄姐妹，有些人会说："我不需要什么救主，我自己过得挺好的。"箴言问：你能说"我洁净了我的心"吗？如果你诚实回答——答案是不能。弟兄姐妹，自以为义是人最常见的自欺。每一个人都有不洁净的心——不是道德表现不好的人，是每一个人。

**神造的耳目（第12节）**

> "能听的耳，能看的眼，都是耶和华所造的。"

弟兄姐妹，这句话非常简洁——但它的含义是深刻的。你的耳朵是神造的——你的眼睛是神造的。弟兄姐妹，你的感官不是偶然进化的产物——而是神精心设计的。这意味着：你用眼睛看什么、用耳朵听什么——要向神负责。弟兄姐妹，你在用神造的眼睛看什么？你在用神造的耳朵听什么？

**人的脚步由耶和华引定（第24节）**

> "人的脚步为耶和华所定；人岂能明白自己的路呢？"

弟兄姐妹，这是整章最重要的一节——也是箴言16:9的另一个表达。"人的脚步为耶和华所定"——你的每一步都在神的引导下。"人岂能明白自己的路呢？"——弟兄姐妹，你能完全明白自己正在走的路吗？你以为你在走某一条路——但神从永恒的视角看——祂看到的是完全不同的图画。

弟兄姐妹，这句话是谦卑的邀请——不是宿命论。不是说"随便吧，反正神控制一切"——而是说："我不能完全明白我的路——所以我要信靠那位引定我脚步的神。"弟兄姐妹，走路不需要看到全程——只需要信靠那位看到全程的神。

**诚实的重要（第23节）**

> "两样的砝码为耶和华所憎恶；诡诈的天平也为不善。"

弟兄姐妹，这个主题在箴言中反复出现。为什么？因为欺诈是如此普遍——在所罗门的时代是这样，在我们的时代也是这样。弟兄姐妹，在你的生意中、在你的关系中、在你的言语中——你诚实吗？"两样的砝码"——一个用来买、一个用来卖——双重标准——这是神所憎恶的。

**听从父母（第20节）**

> "咒骂父母的，他的灯必灭，变为漆黑的黑暗。"

弟兄姐妹，"咒骂父母"在以色列律法中是极严重的罪（出21:17）。"他的灯必灭变为漆黑的黑暗"——弟兄姐妹，不孝顺父母带来的后果是属灵的黑暗。弟兄姐妹，你现在与父母的关系如何？你有没有用言语伤害过他们？有没有机会去修复？弟兄姐妹，趁父母还在，修复关系、表达感谢——不要等到"灯灭"了才后悔。

弟兄姐妹，箴言第二十章教导我们：没有人能说自己的心是洁净的——我们都需要神。我们的脚步由神引定——我们需要谦卑地信靠祂。我们的感官是神的礼物——要用于祂的荣耀。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 20 centers on profound insights about morality, human nature, and divine sovereignty—especially the danger of self-deception and God's sovereign guidance over human life.

**Wine's Warning (v. 1)**

> "Wine is a mocker, strong drink a brawler, and whoever is led astray by it is not wise."

Brothers and sisters, "wine is a mocker"—wine personified—mocking those controlled by it. Whatever makes you lose self-control is mocking you. You think you're enjoying it—actually it's controlling you, mocking your weakness. "Whoever is led astray is not wise"—a person's wisdom is partly measured by their ability to resist what makes them lose control.

> **"Who Can Say I Have Made My Heart Pure?" (v. 9)**

> "Who can say, 'I have made my heart pure; I am clean from my sin'?"

Brothers and sisters, a rhetorical question—answer: no one. Some say: "I don't need any savior; I'm doing fine on my own." Proverbs asks: can you say "I have made my heart pure"? If you answer honestly—no. Self-righteousness is humanity's most common self-deception. Everyone has an impure heart—not just morally failing people, but every person.

**God Made the Ear and Eye (v. 12)**

> "The hearing ear and the seeing eye, the LORD has made them both."

Brothers and sisters, very concise—but profoundly meaningful. Your ears are God-made; your eyes are God-made. Your senses are not accident-evolution products—but God's careful design. This means: what you use your eyes to see, what you use your ears to hear—you're accountable to God.

**A Man's Steps Are from the LORD (v. 24)**

> "A man's steps are from the LORD; how then can man understand his way?"

Brothers and sisters, the chapter's most important verse—another expression of Proverbs 16:9. "Steps from the LORD"—every step is under God's guidance. "How can man understand his way?"—can you fully understand the path you're walking? You think you're on one path—God from an eternal perspective sees a completely different picture.

This is an invitation to humility—not fatalism. Not "whatever, God controls everything anyway"—but "I can't fully understand my path—so I will trust the God who has established my steps." Walking doesn't require seeing the whole journey—only trusting the One who sees the whole journey.

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 20 teaches: no one can say their heart is pure—we all need God. Our steps are established by God—we need to humbly trust Him. Our senses are God's gifts—to be used for His glory.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言第二十章是所罗门格言集（10-22:16）的中间部分。第9节"谁能说我洁净了我的心"在历史上被广泛引用来论证人的罪性——早期教父、宗教改革家都用这节来反驳"人可以靠自己达到无罪"的펠拉纠主义（Pelagianism）主张。第24节"人的脚步为耶和华所定"与诗篇37:23"义人的脚步被耶和华立定"呼应——两处都强调神对人生路径的主权性引导。第1节关于酒的警告在旧约中反复出现（参箴23:29-35对酗酒的详细描述），与以色列的酒文化背景有关——以色列确实有葡萄酒文化，但过度饮酒被视为愚蒙。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 20 is in the middle section of the Solomon collection (10-22:16). Verse 9 "who can say I have made my heart pure" has been widely cited throughout history to argue for human sinfulness—early church fathers and Reformers used this verse to refute Pelagianism's claim that humans can achieve sinlessness on their own. Verse 24 "a man's steps are from the LORD" echoes Psalm 37:23 "the steps of a man are established by the LORD"—both emphasizing God's sovereign guidance of human life's path. Verse 1 on wine's warning appears repeatedly in the OT (cf. Prov 23:29-35's detailed description of drunkenness), related to Israel's wine culture—Israel did have a wine culture, but excessive drinking was seen as foolish.

## 经文结构（中文）

**酒与争竞（1-3节）**——酒是讥笑人的→避开争竞是人的尊荣。
**懒惰与勤劳（4-6节）**——懒惰人冬天不耕种→忠信人谁能遇见。
**人心的不洁（7-14节）**——谁能说我心洁净→神造的耳目→买卖都要称义。
**计谋与引导（18-25节）**——谋略靠计划成立→人的脚步为耶和华所定→轻率发誓是网罗。
**报仇与等候（22节）**——不要说我报复这人→要等候耶和华。

## Structure (English)

**Wine and Quarreling (vv. 1-3)**—Wine is a mocker → avoiding strife is man's honor.
**Laziness and Diligence (vv. 4-6)**—Sluggard won't plow in autumn → a faithful man who can find.
**Heart's Impurity (vv. 7-14)**—Who can say my heart is pure → God made ear and eye → buying and selling both righteous.
**Plans and Guidance (vv. 18-25)**—Plans established by counsel → man's steps from LORD → rash vow is a snare.
**Vengeance and Waiting (v. 22)**—Don't say I'll repay → wait for the LORD.

## 神学要点（中文）

**人全然败坏——"谁能说我洁净了我的心"**
改革宗传统教导全然败坏（total depravity）。韦敏斯德信条第6章。"谁能说我洁净了我的心"——修辞性的否定——没有人。罗马书3:10"没有义人，连一个也没有。"全然败坏不是说每个人都尽可能地坏——而是说罪的败坏影响了人的每一个部分，包括心灵。

**"人的脚步为耶和华所定"——神的护理**
改革宗传统教导神对一切事的护理。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理：神……护理他所造的万物，以及万物的一切行动。人的每一步都在神的护理之下——这不消除人的责任，但确立了神的主权。

**"神造的耳目"——受造物对创造者的责任**
改革宗传统教导人要向创造者负责。韦敏斯德信条第4章论创造。耳朵和眼睛是神的礼物——如何使用是管家的责任。马太福音6:22"眼睛就是身上的灯；你的眼睛若了亮，全身就光明。"

## Theology (English)

**Total Depravity—"Who Can Say I Have Made My Heart Pure"**
The Reformed tradition teaches total depravity. Westminster Confession chapter 6.: Who can say I have made my heart pure—rhetorical negation—no one. Romans 3:10: "None is righteous, no, not one." Total depravity doesn't mean everyone is as bad as possible—but that sin's corruption affects every part of humanity, including the heart.

**"Man's Steps from the LORD"—God's Providence**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's providence over all things. Westminster Confession chapter 5. Every step is under God's providence—this doesn't eliminate human responsibility but establishes divine sovereignty.

**"God Made the Ear and Eye"—Creation's Accountability to Creator**
The Reformed tradition teaches humans are accountable to their Creator. Westminster Confession chapter 4. Ears and eyes are God's gifts—how they're used is stewardship's responsibility. Matthew 6:22: "The eye is the lamp of the body."

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"谁能说我洁净了我的心"——唯有基督有洁净的心**——希伯来书4:15"他也曾凡事受过试探，与我们一样，只是他没有犯罪。"约翰福音8:46"你们中间谁能指证我有罪呢？"箴言20:9的修辞问题"谁能说我洁净了我的心"——唯一的答案是：耶稣基督。祂是唯一心灵洁净的人——也因此成为我们唯一有效的救主。

**"人的脚步为耶和华所定"——基督完全顺服的人生**——约翰福音4:34"我的食物就是遵行差我来者的旨意，做成他的工。"8:28-29"我没有凭着自己做什么……父没有离开我……因为我常做他所喜悦的事。"基督的每一步都完全在父神的引定中——祂是"脚步为耶和华所定"的完美实现。

**"不要说我报仇"——基督教导饶恕**——马太福音5:38-39"你们听见有话说：'以眼还眼，以牙还牙。'只是我告诉你们：不要与恶人作对。有人打你的右脸，连左脸也转过来由他打。"箴言20:22"要等候耶和华，他必拯救你"——基督把等候神审判的耐心提升为积极的爱仇敌（路6:27-28）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Who Can Say I Have Made My Heart Pure"—Only Christ Has a Pure Heart**—Hebrews 4:15: "yet without sin." John 8:46: "Which one of you convicts me of sin?" Proverbs 20:9's rhetorical question "who can say I have made my heart pure"—the only answer: Jesus Christ. He is the only person with a pure heart—therefore our only effective Savior.

**"Man's Steps from the LORD"—Christ's Perfectly Obedient Life**—John 4:34: "My food is to do the will of him who sent me." John 8:28-29: "I do nothing on my own authority...the Father has not left me alone...for I always do the things that are pleasing to him." Christ's every step was completely in the Father's establishment—perfect realization of "steps from the LORD."

**"Don't Say I Will Repay"—Christ Teaches Forgiveness**—Matthew 5:38-39: "You have heard...An eye for an eye...But I say to you, Do not resist the one who is evil." Proverbs 20:22 "wait for the LORD, he will save you"—Christ elevated waiting for God's justice into the active posture of loving enemies (Luke 6:27-28).
