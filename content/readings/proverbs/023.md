## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第二十三章属于"智慧人的言语"——包含了关于节制、教养儿女、和避免嫉妒的深刻教训。这一章有两段最著名的经文——关于管教儿女和关于醉酒的警告。

**席上的节制（第1-3节）**

> "你若与官长坐席，要留意在你面前的是谁。你若是贪食的，就当拿刀放在喉咙上。不可贪恋他的美食，因为是哄人的食物。"

弟兄姐妹，"拿刀放在喉咙上"——这是夸张的修辞——但意思很清楚：你的食欲会害了你。弟兄姐妹，在古代近东，与官长坐席是重要的政治场合——在那里你可能因为贪食而失去戒心、被人利用。弟兄姐妹，今天也是一样——商务宴请、社交场合——你的贪欲（无论是食物还是关系）可能让你做出后悔的决定。

**不要嫉妒罪人（第17节）**

> "你心中不要嫉妒罪人，只要终日敬畏耶和华。因为至终必有善报，你的指望也不会断绝。"

弟兄姐妹，"不要嫉妒罪人"——弟兄姐妹，你有没有看过那些不敬畏神的人活得比你好、赚得比你多、日子过得比你舒服——然后心里想"我这么敬虔有什么用？"弟兄姐妹，诗篇73篇的诗人也有过同样的挣扎——"等我进了神的圣所，我才明白他们的结局。"弟兄姐妹，罪人的"好日子"是暂时的——"至终必有善报"——你的等待不会白费。

**管教儿女（第13-14节）**

> "不可不管教孩童；你用杖打他，他必不至于死。你要用杖打他，就可以救他的灵魂免下阴间。"

弟兄姐妹，这是箴言中关于管教的经典经文。弟兄姐妹，"杖"在古代以色列不只是惩罚的工具——也是牧人引导羊的工具。弟兄姐妹，管教的目的不是发泄愤怒——而是"救他的灵魂"。

弟兄姐妹，现代社会对体罚有不同的看法——但箴言的核心教训不是关于"打不打"——而是关于"管不管"。"不可不管教孩童"——放任不管是父母的失职。无论用什么方式——纪律、后果、引导——孩子需要被管教。弟兄姐妹，没有管教的爱是溺爱——溺爱不是爱。

> **"我儿，你要听"（第22-26节）**

> "你要听从生你的父亲；你母亲老了，也不可轻看她。你当买真理；就是智慧、训诲和聪明也都不可卖。义人的父亲必大得快乐；人生智慧的儿子，必因他欢喜。你要使父母欢喜，使生你的快乐。我儿，要将你的心归我；你的眼目也要喜悦我的道路。"

弟兄姐妹，"你当买真理不可卖"——弟兄姐妹，真理是要"买"的——需要付出代价——时间、精力、甚至名誉。但一旦得到了——"不可卖"——不要为了任何利益放弃真理。弟兄姐妹，你有没有曾经为了方便而妥协了真理？为了关系而牺牲了诚实？箴言说：无论什么代价，买了真理就不可卖。

**醉酒的警告（第29-35节）**

> "谁有祸患？谁有忧愁？谁有争斗？谁有哀叹？谁无故受伤？谁眼目红赤？就是那流连饮酒到处寻找调和酒的人。酒发红，在杯中闪烁，你不可观看，虽然下咽舒畅，终久是咬你如蛇，刺你如毒蛇。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整本圣经中关于醉酒最生动、最详细的描写。六个"谁"的修辞问题——祸患、忧愁、争斗、哀叹、无故受伤、眼目红赤——全部指向一个答案：流连饮酒的人。

"虽然下咽舒畅终久是咬你如蛇"——弟兄姐妹，入口的舒畅和终久的后果——这是所有瘾性行为的本质。弟兄姐妹，任何东西如果入口舒畅但终久像蛇一样咬你——无论是酒、是毒品、是不良关系、是网络成瘾——那都是"蛇"。智慧人看到"终久"——不只看"入口"。

弟兄姐妹，箴言二十三章教导我们：节制你的欲望——不要嫉妒罪人——管教你的孩子——买了真理就不可卖——认清"入口舒畅终久如蛇"的事物。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 23 belongs to "the words of the wise"—containing profound teaching about moderation, training children, and avoiding envy.

**Restraint at the Table (vv. 1-3)**

> "When you sit down to eat with a ruler, observe carefully what is before you, and put a knife to your throat if you are given to appetite."

Brothers and sisters, "put a knife to your throat"—hyperbolic rhetoric—but the meaning is clear: your appetite will harm you. At ancient Near Eastern banquets with officials, gluttony could lower your guard, leaving you exploited.

**Don't Envy Sinners (v. 17)**

> "Let not your heart envy sinners, but continue in the fear of the LORD all the day. Surely there is a future, and your hope will not be cut off."

Brothers and sisters, have you seen godless people living better, earning more, having easier lives—and thought "what's the point of my faithfulness?" Psalm 73's psalmist had the same struggle—"until I went into God's sanctuary; then I understood their end." Sinners' "good days" are temporary—"surely there is a future"—your waiting will not be wasted.

**Training Children (vv. 13-14)**

> "Do not withhold discipline from a child; if you strike him with a rod, he will not die. If you strike him with the rod, you will save his soul from Sheol."

Brothers and sisters, "the rod" in ancient Israel was not only a punishment tool but also a shepherd's guidance tool. Discipline's purpose isn't venting anger—but "saving his soul." The core teaching isn't about "to spank or not"—but "to discipline or not." "Do not withhold discipline"—permissiveness is parental negligence. Love without discipline is indulgence—and indulgence is not love.

> **"Buy Truth and Do Not Sell It" (vv. 22-26)**

> "Buy truth, and do not sell it; buy wisdom, instruction, and understanding."

Brothers and sisters, truth must be "bought"—costing time, effort, even reputation. But once obtained—"do not sell it"—don't surrender truth for any benefit. Have you ever compromised truth for convenience? Sacrificed honesty for relationships? Proverbs says: whatever the cost, once you've bought truth, don't sell it.

**Drunkenness Warning (vv. 29-35)**

> "Who has woe? Who has sorrow? Who has strife? Who has complaining? Who has wounds without cause? Who has redness of eyes? Those who tarry long over wine...Do not look at wine when it is red...In the end it bites like a serpent and stings like an adder."

Brothers and sisters, the Bible's most vivid drunkenness description. Six "who" questions—all pointing to one answer: those lingering over wine. "Though it goes down smoothly, in the end it bites like a serpent"—the essence of all addictive behavior. Anything pleasant going in but ultimately biting like a snake—whether alcohol, drugs, harmful relationships, internet addiction—is a "serpent." The wise see "the end"—not just "the entrance."

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 23 teaches: restrain your desires—don't envy sinners—discipline your children—buy truth and never sell it—recognize things "smooth going down but biting like a serpent in the end."

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言23章属于"智慧人的言语"段落（22:17-24:22），有学者认为这段与古埃及的《阿美内莫帕教训》有多处相似。特别是23:1-3关于"与官长坐席"的教训与埃及文本中关于在上级面前用餐的建议非常相似。然而，以色列的版本增加了独特的神学维度——"敬畏耶和华"。第13-14节关于杖打管教的经文在历史上有丰富的解释传统——犹太智慧传统和清教徒教育传统都高度重视纪律性的教养。第29-35节被认为是古代文学中对醉酒最详细和最生动的描写之一——六个修辞问题加上蛇的意象构成了一幅完整的"酒精毁灭"图画。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 23 belongs to the "words of the wise" section (22:17-24:22), which some scholars believe has multiple parallels with the ancient Egyptian "Instruction of Amenemope." Especially 23:1-3's teaching about "dining with a ruler" closely resembles Egyptian texts about eating in a superior's presence. However, Israel's version adds a unique theological dimension—"fearing the LORD." Verses 13-14 on rod discipline have a rich interpretive history—both Jewish wisdom tradition and Puritan educational tradition highly valued disciplinary upbringing. Verses 29-35 are considered one of the most detailed and vivid descriptions of drunkenness in ancient literature—six rhetorical questions plus serpent imagery forming a complete "alcohol destruction" picture.

## Structure (English)

**Restraint at Table (vv. 1-8)**—Dining with ruler → knife to throat → don't crave delicacies → don't eat miser's food.
**Disciplining Children (vv. 9-16)**—Don't speak to fools → don't move ancient boundary → discipline child → save his soul.
**Don't Envy Sinners (vv. 17-18)**—Don't envy → fear LORD all day → surely there is a future.
**Buy Truth Don't Sell (vv. 19-28)**—Buy truth → make parents glad → give me your heart → prostitute like deep pit.
**Drunkenness Warning (vv. 29-35)**—Six "who" questions → tarrying over wine → smooth going down → bites like serpent.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"不要嫉妒罪人"——苦难与护理的神学**
改革宗传统教导苦难有神的旨意。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理。义人面对罪人的"好日子"时会产生嫉妒——但"至终必有善报"是护理教义的安慰：神看到终极的图画，而你只看到现在。诗篇73:17"等我进了神的圣所，我才明白他们的结局。"

**"不可不管教孩童"——管教的神学**
改革宗传统教导管教是爱的表现。韦敏斯德信条第13章论成圣。希伯来书12:6"因为主所爱的，他必管教。"管教的缺失不是爱——是忽略。智慧的管教"救他的灵魂"——方式可以随时代调整，但原则不变：每个孩子都需要被引导、被纠正、被保护。

**"买真理不可卖"——真理的无价性**
改革宗传统教导真理（神的话语）是无价的。韦敏斯德信条第1章论圣经。"买"意味着付出代价——学习、思考、祷告。"不可卖"意味着没有什么能买走你的信仰——金钱、权力、关系、舒适都不能。

## Theology (English)

**"Don't Envy Sinners"—Theology of Suffering and Providence**
The Reformed tradition teaches suffering has divine purpose. Westminster Confession chapter 5. Righteous people facing sinners' "good days" may feel envy—but "surely there is a future" is providence doctrine's comfort: God sees the ultimate picture; you only see the present. Psalm 73:17.

**"Do Not Withhold Discipline"—Theology of Discipline**
The Reformed tradition teaches discipline is love's expression. Westminster Confession chapter 13. Hebrews 12:6: the Lord disciplines the one he loves. Discipline's absence isn't love—it's neglect. Wise discipline "saves his soul"—methods may adjust with times, but the principle remains.

**"Buy Truth Don't Sell It"—Truth's Pricelessness**
The Reformed tradition teaches truth (God's Word) is priceless. Westminster Confession chapter 1. "Buy" means paying a price—study, thought, prayer. "Don't sell" means nothing can buy your faith—not money, power, relationships, or comfort.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"不要嫉妒罪人"——基督应许的终极公义**——马太福音25:46"这些人要往永刑里去；那些义人要往永生里去。"箴言23:17"至终必有善报"的"至终"在基督的再来时完全应验——那时一切的不公义将被纠正，义人的等待将得到永恒的回报。

**"你当买真理不可卖"——基督就是真理**——约翰福音14:6"我就是道路、真理、生命。""买真理"最终是"买基督"——你为了得到基督愿意付出什么？马太福音13:45-46"天国好像买卖人寻找好珠子，遇见一颗重价的珠子，就去变卖他一切所有的，买了这颗珠子。"基督就是那颗重价的珠子——买了不可卖。

**"终久是咬你如蛇"——基督胜过蛇**——创世记3:15"他要伤你的头，你要伤他的脚跟。"箴言23:32的"蛇"意象呼应了创世记的"蛇"——罪的诱惑。但基督已经踩碎了蛇的头——在十字架上胜过了罪的"毒"（西2:15）。弟兄姐妹，在基督里你不再需要被蛇咬——因为那蛇的头已经被踩碎了。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Don't Envy Sinners"—Christ's Promise of Ultimate Justice**—Matthew 25:46: "these will go away into eternal punishment, but the righteous into eternal life." Proverbs 23:17's "surely there is a future" fully comes true at Christ's return—all injustice corrected, righteous people's waiting eternally rewarded.

**"Buy Truth Don't Sell It"—Christ Is the Truth**—John 14:6: "I am the way, and the truth, and the life." "Buying truth" is ultimately "buying Christ"—what will you pay for Christ? Matthew 13:45-46: "the kingdom of heaven is like a merchant...who, on finding one pearl of great value, went and sold all that he had and bought it." Christ is that pearl of great price—bought and never sold.

**"Bites Like a Serpent"—Christ Conquered the Serpent**—Genesis 3:15: "he shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise his heel." Proverbs 23:32's "serpent" imagery echoes Genesis's "serpent"—sin's temptation. But Christ crushed the serpent's head—conquering sin's "venom" on the cross (Col 2:15). In Christ you no longer need to be bitten—for that serpent's head has been crushed.
