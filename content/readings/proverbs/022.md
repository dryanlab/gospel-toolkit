## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第二十二章以一句关于名誉的深刻格言开场，接着进入了"智慧人的言语"这一新的段落——这是箴言书中写作风格的重要转变。

**美名胜于大财（第1节）**

> "美名胜过大财；恩宠强如金银。"

弟兄姐妹，"美名胜过大财"——弟兄姐妹，在一个以财富衡量成功的时代，这句话是颠覆性的。什么是"美名"？就是你在别人眼中真实的为人——你的品格、你的诚信、你的可靠。弟兄姐妹，财富可以一夜消失——但品格是日积月累的。名声可以被毁——但需要很多年的不诚实才能毁掉一个好名声。

"恩宠强如金银"——"恩宠"（chen，在希伯来文中是恩典、美好的意思）——拥有人的恩宠、受人的喜爱——比拥有金银更宝贵。弟兄姐妹，你花多少时间在积累财富上——又花多少时间在建立品格上？

**富贫的共同起源（第2节）**

> "富户穷人在世相遇，都为耶和华所造。"

弟兄姐妹，"都为耶和华所造"——这一句话摧毁了所有基于财富的阶级制度。弟兄姐妹，在神面前，富人和穷人是平等的——因为都是"耶和华所造的"。你的财富不给你更多的人格价值，你的贫穷不减少你的人格价值。弟兄姐妹，这是圣经对人类平等最根本的神学根基——不是因为我们的能力相同，而是因为我们的创造者相同。

**及早教导（第6节）**

> "教养孩童，使他走当行的道，就是到老他也不偏离。"

弟兄姐妹，"教养孩童"——"教养"在希伯来文（chanak）原意是"开始"——在古代也指"在婴儿开始吃东西时把甜的东西放在他嘴里让他能接受食物"——是一种训练的意象。弟兄姐妹，童年的教养影响一生。"到老他也不偏离"——不是绝对的保证——而是一般性的人生规律：年幼时的塑造在人的灵魂里留下不可磨灭的印记。

弟兄姐妹，如果你是父母——今天你怎样教养你的孩子，他老的时候会是什么样的人。弟兄姐妹，家庭是教会以外最重要的门训中心。如果你在一个信仰的家庭长大——感谢神给了你这个礼物。如果你没有——你可以从你这代开始改变。

**不可挪移地界（第28节）**

> "你的先祖所立的地界，你不可挪移。"

弟兄姐妹，"地界"——古代以色列土地是按家族分配的，地界石是神圣不可侵犯的（参申19:14）。"不可挪移"——不要侵占别人的份额。弟兄姐妹，这不只是土地问题——而是一切"界限"的原则。弟兄姐妹，你有没有在侵占别人的份额——无论是土地、版权、荣誉、还是机会？

**智慧人的言语（第17-21节）**

> "你当侧耳听智慧人的言语，留心领会我的知识……我今日将这些事指教你，特特地指教你，使你知道真实的言语，也使你能将真实话回答那打发你来的人。"

弟兄姐妹，箴言第二十二章17节之后进入了"智慧人的言语"这一新的段落——有些学者认为这段话与古埃及的《阿美内莫帕教训》有相似之处。弟兄姐妹，真理是普遍的——即使在以色列以外的民族中，神也埋下了对真理的寻求。但以色列的智慧有一个独特之处：它的根基是"敬畏耶和华"——而不只是实用的人生技巧。

弟兄姐妹，箴言二十二章教导我们：美名比财富更贵重——品格的建立从童年开始——神造了所有人所以在祂面前都是平等的——尊重界限是公义的基本要求。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 22 opens with a profound saying about reputation, then moves into the "words of the wise"—an important stylistic shift in Proverbs.

**A Good Name Is Better Than Riches (v. 1)**

> "A good name is to be chosen rather than great riches, and favor is better than silver or gold."

Brothers and sisters, "a good name rather than great riches"—in an era measuring success by wealth, this is revolutionary. What is "a good name"? Who you genuinely are in others' eyes—your character, integrity, reliability. Wealth can disappear overnight—but character is accumulated daily. It takes many years of dishonesty to destroy a good name.

**Rich and Poor Share a Common Origin (v. 2)**

> "The rich and the poor meet together; the LORD is the maker of them all."

Brothers and sisters, "the LORD is the maker of them all"—one sentence demolishes all wealth-based class systems. Before God, rich and poor are equal—both "made by the LORD." Your wealth doesn't give you more personal value; your poverty doesn't diminish your personal value. Scripture's most foundational theological basis for human equality—not because our abilities are equal, but because our Creator is the same.

**Train Up a Child (v. 6)**

> "Train up a child in the way he should go; even when he is old he will not depart from it."

Brothers and sisters, "train up" (chanak in Hebrew) originally meant "to begin"—in antiquity also referred to placing sweet things in an infant's mouth when it begins eating, to train it to receive food—a training image. Childhood formation influences a lifetime. "Even when old he will not depart"—not an absolute guarantee—but a general life pattern: formation in youth leaves indelible marks on the soul.

If you're a parent—how you train your child today determines who they'll be when old. Family is the most important discipleship center outside the church. If you grew up in a faith-filled family—thank God for this gift. If not—you can begin changing from your generation.

**Do Not Move the Boundary Stone (v. 28)**

> "Do not move the ancient landmark that your fathers have set."

Brothers and sisters, boundary stones were sacred and inviolable in ancient Israel (cf. Deut 19:14). "Do not move"—don't encroach on others' portions. Not just a land issue—but the principle of all "boundaries." Are you encroaching on others' portions—whether land, copyright, honor, or opportunity?

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 22 teaches: a good name is more precious than wealth—character building begins in childhood—God made all people so all are equal before Him—respecting boundaries is justice's basic requirement.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言22:17开始的"智慧人的言语"（22:17-24:22）标志着箴言书的第三个主要段落——从格言集转向更长的教训。学术界注意到这段与古埃及的《阿美内莫帕教训》（约公元前1200-900年）有显著的相似之处，约有11-12段落有直接对应。这引发了关于借鉴和共同传统的学术讨论。保守的解释认为：以色列智慧人可能知道埃及的智慧传统，但通过以色列的"敬畏耶和华"框架对其进行了神学改造。第6节"教养孩童"是Christian education（基督教教育）运动最常引用的经文之一，也常被用于家庭敬拜（Family Worship）的讨论。

## Historical Context (English)

The "Words of the Wise" beginning at Proverbs 22:17 (22:17-24:22) marks Proverbs' third major section—shifting from sayings to longer instructions. Scholars have noted significant similarities with the ancient Egyptian "Instruction of Amenemope" (c. 1200-900 BC), with about 11-12 passages having direct parallels. This sparked scholarly discussion about borrowing and shared traditions. Conservative interpretation: Israelite wisdom figures may have known Egyptian wisdom tradition but theologically transformed it through Israel's "fear of the LORD" framework. Verse 6 "train up a child" is one of the most frequently cited biblical texts in Christian education movements and family worship discussions.

## 经文结构（中文）

**美名与财富（1-5节）**——美名胜大财→富贫都为耶和华所造→谦卑敬畏的奖赏→谨慎躲避网罗。
**教养儿女（6-16节）**——教养孩童使他走当行的道→债主统管穷人→多给的必多贫穷。
**智慧人的言语（17-29节）**——当侧耳听→不可挪移地界→手段殷勤的必站在君王面前。

## Structure (English)

**Good Name and Wealth (vv. 1-5)**—Good name better than riches → rich and poor both made by LORD → humility and fear's reward → prudence avoids snares.
**Train Children (vv. 6-16)**—Train up a child in the way → borrower is slave to lender → giving to poor lacks nothing.
**Words of the Wise (vv. 17-29)**—Incline your ear → don't move the ancient landmark → skillful hand will stand before kings.

## 神学要点（中文）

**美名——品格与荣耀神**
改革宗传统教导基督徒的品格是荣耀神的方式。韦敏斯德大要理问答第112问论第三诫——"不可妄称耶和华神的名"包括以任何方式败坏基督徒的名声（这影响到神的名）。建立"美名"不是为了个人荣耀——而是为了使神的名得荣耀。

**"富贫都为耶和华所造"——人的平等与尊严**
改革宗传统教导人的尊严来自按神形像被造。韦敏斯德信条第4章。"都为耶和华所造"建立了人类根本的平等——超越了经济、种族、阶级的一切分别。这是改革宗社会伦理的神学基础。

**"教养孩童"——家庭门训**
改革宗传统高度重视家庭敬拜和家庭门训。清教徒传统特别强调父亲作为家庭牧师的责任。韦敏斯德大要理问答第156问论读经、第158问论向孩子教导圣经的责任。

## Theology (English)

**Good Name—Character and Glorifying God**
The Reformed tradition teaches Christians' character glorifies God. WLC Q112 on the third commandment—not taking the LORD's name in vain includes dishonoring the Christian's name in any way (affecting God's name). Building "a good name" is not for personal glory—but for God's name to be honored.

**"Both Made by the LORD"—Human Equality and Dignity**
The Reformed tradition teaches human dignity comes from being created in God's image. Westminster Confession chapter 4.: Both made by the LORD establishes humanity's fundamental equality—transcending all economic, racial, class distinctions. The theological foundation of Reformed social ethics.

**"Train Up a Child"—Family Discipleship**
The Reformed tradition highly values family worship and discipleship. Puritan tradition especially emphasized fathers' responsibility as family pastors. WLC Q156 on reading Scripture; Q158 on the responsibility to teach Scripture to children.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"美名胜过大财"——基督超乎万名之上的名**——腓立比书2:9-11"神将他升为至高，又赐给他那超乎万名之上的名，叫一切在天上的、地上的，和地底下的，因耶稣的名无不屈膝，无不口称耶稣基督为主。"箴言22:1的"美名"在基督的名上达到无限的实现——祂的名超乎万名，胜过一切财富和荣耀。

**"富贫都为耶和华所造"——基督打破贫富分隔**——加拉太书3:28"并不分犹太人、希腊人，自主的、为奴的，或男或女；因为你们在基督耶稣里都成为一了。"基督的救赎跨越一切社会分别——在基督里，富贫、贵贱、种族的分别被超越。

**"教养孩童"——基督是最终的教导者**——马太福音28:19-20"所以，你们要去，使万民作我的门徒……教训他们遵守凡我所吩咐你们的。"最终的"教养孩童"命令来自基督——使万民作门徒、用祂的教导来"教养"。祂自己是最终的智慧教师（太12:42"在这里有一人比所罗门更大"）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"A Good Name Is Better Than Riches"—Christ's Name Above Every Name**—Philippians 2:9-11: "God has highly exalted him and bestowed on him the name that is above every name." Proverbs 22:1's "good name" reaches infinite fulfillment in Christ's name—His name above every name, surpassing all wealth and honor.

**"Both Made by the LORD"—Christ Breaks the Rich-Poor Divide**—Galatians 3:28: "There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free, there is no male and female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus." Christ's redemption transcends all social distinctions—in Christ, rich-poor, noble-lowly, racial distinctions are surpassed.

**"Train Up a Child"—Christ Is the Ultimate Teacher**—Matthew 28:19-20: "Go therefore and make disciples of all nations...teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you." The ultimate "train up a child" commission comes from Christ—making disciples of all nations, "training" with His teaching. He Himself is the ultimate wisdom teacher (Matt 12:42 "something greater than Solomon is here").
