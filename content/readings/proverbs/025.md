## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第二十五章开始了一个新的段落——"以下也是所罗门的箴言，是犹大王希西家的人所誊录的"——说明这些格言在所罗门去世后约250年才被编辑收集。这一章充满了生动的自然意象。

**神隐藏，君王寻求（第2-3节）**

> "将事隐秘乃神的荣耀；将事察清乃君王的荣耀。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整本箴言中最深奥的格言之一。"将事隐秘乃神的荣耀"——神不把一切都告诉你——这是祂荣耀的一部分。弟兄姐妹，你有没有抱怨过神不把事情告诉你？为什么祂不解释？为什么要受苦？为什么这件事会发生？弟兄姐妹，申命记29:29"隐秘的事是属耶和华我们神的；惟有明显的事是永远属我们和我们子孙的。"有些事是神隐藏的——你不需要知道——也不应该知道。信心的功课就是在不知道的时候仍然信靠。

"将事察清乃君王的荣耀"——君王的荣耀相反——在于把隐藏的事察清、分辨、判断。弟兄姐妹，这也提醒我们：地上的权柄有寻求真相的责任——而不是隐瞒真相。好的领导者是"察清"的人，不是"隐秘"的人。

**冬天吃雪，炎热中的使者（第13节）**

> "忠信的使者，叫差他的人心里舒畅，就如在收割时有冰雪的凉意。"

弟兄姐妹，"冰雪的凉意"——在炎热的以色列夏天，有冰雪是极为罕见的奢侈——就是这样的清凉使人"心里舒畅"。弟兄姐妹，"忠信的使者"给差他的人也带来这样的舒畅。弟兄姐妹，你是可以被信赖的人吗？别人把任务交给你——你能忠信地完成，让他们"心里舒畅"？

**当面责备胜于背后谄媚（第12节）**

> "智慧人的责备，临到听从的耳中，好像金耳环和精金的妆饰。"

弟兄姐妹，"金耳环和精金的妆饰"——责备在这里是比较是宝贝的！弟兄姐妹，通常我们把责备看作令人难受的事——但智慧人把它视为"金耳环"。为什么？因为真实的责备给你更贵重的东西——真实的认识自己。弟兄姐妹，你最近有没有收到过一个让你难受的责备——但如果你诚实地回应，可能让你成长的？

**蜜吃多了不好（第16节）**

> "你得了蜜吗？只可吃够，恐怕你吃多了，就呕吐出来。"

弟兄姐妹，连好东西也有度。蜜是甜的——但吃多了"就呕吐出来"。弟兄姐妹，这个原则适用于一切好事——休息、食物、娱乐、甚至好的关系——都需要节制。弟兄姐妹，"节制"是圣灵果子之一（加5:23）——不是因为那些东西不好，而是因为失控本身就是问题。

**用炭火堆在仇敌头上（第21-22节）**

> "你的仇敌若饿了，就给他饭吃；若渴了，就给他水喝；因为你这样行，就是把炭火堆在他头上；耶和华也必赏赐你。"

弟兄姐妹，"把炭火堆在他头上"——这是箴言中最奇特的意象之一。弟兄姐妹，"炭火"代表的是什么？有学者认为"炭火"指的是羞耻——善待仇敌让他感到自己行为的羞耻，从而改变。弟兄姐妹，保罗在罗马书12:20引用了这节经文，把它放在"不要以恶报恶"的上下文中。弟兄姐妹，以善报恶不是软弱——而是神所赐的最有力量的回应方式。当你给仇敌饭吃的时候——你不是在帮他——你是在改变他。

弟兄姐妹，箴言二十五章教导我们：神隐藏有祂的荣耀——我们不需要知道一切。忠信是最宝贵的品质之一。节制是所有好事的保护。以善报恶是最有力量的回应。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 25 begins a new section—"These also are proverbs of Solomon which the men of Hezekiah king of Judah copied"—showing these sayings were compiled and collected about 250 years after Solomon's death. This chapter is full of vivid natural imagery.

**God Conceals, Kings Search (vv. 2-3)**

> "It is the glory of God to conceal things, but the glory of kings is to search things out."

Brothers and sisters, one of Proverbs' most profound sayings. "God's glory to conceal"—God doesn't tell you everything—this is part of His glory. Have you complained about God not explaining things? Why no explanation? Why suffering? Deuteronomy 29:29: "The secret things belong to the LORD our God, but the things that are revealed belong to us." Some things God conceals—you don't need to know them. Faith's lesson is trusting when you don't know.

**Faithful Messenger Like Snow's Coolness (v. 13)**

> "Like the cold of snow in harvest time is a faithful messenger to those who send him."

Brothers and sisters, "cold of snow in harvest"—in Israel's hot summer, snow was an extremely rare luxury—its coolness brings refreshment. A faithful messenger brings the same comfort. Are you trustworthy? When others give you tasks—can you faithfully complete them, letting them be refreshed?

**Reproof Like Gold Jewelry (v. 12)**

> "Like a gold ring or an ornament of gold is a wise reprover to a listening ear."

Brothers and sisters, "gold ring and ornament"—reproof here is compared to treasure! Usually we see reproof as unpleasant—but the wise see it as "gold jewelry." Why? Because genuine reproof gives something more precious—genuine self-knowledge.

**Even Honey Has Limits (v. 16)**

> "If you have found honey, eat only enough for you, lest you have your fill of it and vomit it."

Brothers and sisters, even good things have limits. Honey is sweet—but too much and you vomit. This principle applies to everything good—rest, food, entertainment, even good relationships—all need moderation. "Self-control" is a fruit of the Spirit (Gal 5:23)—not because those things are bad, but because loss of control itself is the problem.

**Heap Burning Coals on Your Enemy's Head (vv. 21-22)**

> "If your enemy is hungry, give him bread to eat, and if he is thirsty, give him water to drink, for you will heap burning coals on his head, and the LORD will reward you."

Brothers and sisters, "heap burning coals"—one of Proverbs' strangest images. Some scholars believe "burning coals" signifies shame—treating your enemy kindly makes him feel the shame of his behavior, leading to change. Paul quotes this in Romans 12:20, placing it in the context of "do not repay evil for evil." Repaying evil with good is not weakness—it's the most powerful response God gives. When you give your enemy bread—you're not just helping him—you're changing him.

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 25 teaches: God conceals with glory—we don't need to know everything. Faithfulness is one of the most precious qualities. Moderation protects all good things. Repaying evil with good is the most powerful response.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言25:1"以下也是所罗门的箴言，是犹大王希西家的人所誊录的"——这是箴言书中唯一提到编辑者的标题。希西家王（约公元前715-686年）以其宗教改革和对文化的重视著称（参王下18-20章）。这说明所罗门的箴言在他死后约250-270年才被整理收录成现在的形式——也提醒我们箴言书是一个编辑过程的产物，而不是所罗门一次写就的。第21-22节"把炭火堆在仇敌头上"被保罗在罗马书12:20引用——是旧约箴言直接进入新约伦理教导的典型例子。"炭火"的解释在学术界有争议：1）羞耻感；2）古埃及仪式（把热炭放在头上表示忏悔）；3）软化或融化——无论哪种解释，善待仇敌的核心意义是一致的。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 25:1 "These also are proverbs of Solomon which the men of Hezekiah king of Judah copied"—the only superscription in Proverbs mentioning editors. King Hezekiah (c. 715-686 BC) was known for religious reform and cultural emphasis (2 Kings 18-20). This shows Solomon's proverbs were compiled about 250-270 years after his death—reminding us Proverbs is an editorial process product, not written by Solomon in one sitting. Verses 21-22 "heap burning coals on his head" is quoted by Paul in Romans 12:20—a classic example of an OT proverb directly entering NT ethical teaching. "Burning coals" interpretation is debated: 1) shame; 2) ancient Egyptian ritual (placing hot coals on head symbolizing repentance); 3) softening/melting—regardless of interpretation, the core meaning of treating enemies kindly is consistent.

## 经文结构（中文）

**神和君王的荣耀（1-7节）**——神隐秘是荣耀→君王察清是荣耀→不要在君王面前自高。
**争执与调解（8-15节）**——不要仓促争论→不要泄漏别人的秘密→当面责备好过背后谄媚。
**节制（16-17节）**——蜜吃够了就好→邻舍的家少去。
**各种比喻（18-26节）**——作假见证如大头钉→炭火堆仇敌头上→回答愚昧人。
**以善报恶（21-22节）**——仇敌饿了给饭→耶和华必赏赐你。

## Structure (English)

**God's and King's Glory (vv. 1-7)**—God's glory to conceal → king's glory to search → don't exalt yourself before king.
**Dispute and Mediation (vv. 8-15)**—Don't hasten to quarrel → don't betray confidences → open rebuke better than hidden love.
**Moderation (vv. 16-17)**—Eat only enough honey → visit neighbor sparingly.
**Various Comparisons (vv. 18-26)**—False witness like sharp weapon → heaping coals on enemy → answering a fool.
**Repaying Evil with Good (vv. 21-22)**—Give hungry enemy bread → LORD will reward you.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"将事隐秘乃神的荣耀"——神的无限超越性**
改革宗传统教导神的超越性（transcendence）。韦敏斯德信条第2章：神是无限、永恒、不变的……超乎人的理解。箴言25:2的神学是：神不欠你解释。祂的荣耀部分在于祂的隐秘——就如光包含了我们看不见的波段。信心的成熟是在"不知道"中安息，因为你认识那位知道一切的神。

**"把炭火堆在仇敌头上"——以善胜恶**
改革宗传统教导以爱回应敌对。韦敏斯德信条第20章论基督徒的自由。箴言25:21-22与马太福音5:44"要爱你们的仇敌"直接对应——这不是软弱或妥协，而是最有力的道德回应。以善胜恶（罗12:21）是基督徒伦理的核心。

**"节制"——圣灵的果子**
改革宗传统教导成圣产生节制的美德。韦敏斯德大要理问答第77问。"蜜吃够了就好"不是禁欲主义——而是节制。任何东西失去节制就成了偶像——即使是好东西。节制是对神所赐一切的适当的、感恩的享受。

## Theology (English)

**"God's Glory to Conceal"—God's Infinite Transcendence**
The Reformed tradition teaches divine transcendence. Westminster Confession chapter 2: There is but one only...God who is...infinite...incomprehensible. Proverbs 25:2's theology: God owes you no explanation. His glory is partly in His concealment—like light containing wavelengths we can't see. Faith's maturity is resting in "not knowing" because you know the God who knows all.

**"Heap Burning Coals"—Overcoming Evil with Good**
The Reformed tradition teaches responding to hostility with love. Westminster Confession chapter 20. Proverbs 25:21-22 directly corresponds to Matthew 5:44 "love your enemies"—not weakness or compromise, but the most powerful moral response. Overcoming evil with good (Rom 12:21) is the core of Christian ethics.

**"Moderation"—The Spirit's Fruit**
The Reformed tradition teaches sanctification produces the virtue of moderation. WLC Q77. "Enough honey" is not asceticism—but moderation. Anything without moderation becomes an idol—even good things. Moderation is the proper, grateful enjoyment of all God gives.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"将事隐秘乃神的荣耀"——基督揭示隐藏的奥秘**——歌罗西书2:2-3"知道神奥秘的基督……在祂里面藏着一切智慧知识的宝藏。"基督来到世上是为了"察清"神隐藏已久的救恩奥秘（弗3:9"这奥秘从创世以来隐藏在创造万物之神里面"）——神在基督里打开了祂最深的宝藏。

**"把炭火堆在仇敌头上"——基督的十字架以善胜恶**——罗马书5:10"我们作仇敌的时候，且藉着神儿子的死，得与神和好。"当我们还是神的仇敌的时候，基督给了我们"饭吃"——祂自己的身体和血。这是最终极的"把炭火堆在仇敌头上"——用爱完全地软化了仇敌的心。

**"冰雪的凉意"——基督是活水**——约翰福音7:37-38"人若渴了，可以到我这里来喝……从他腹中要流出活水的江河来。"箴言25:13"冰雪的凉意"在基督里变成了永不干涸的"活水"——不只是暂时的清凉，而是永恒的解渴。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"God's Glory to Conceal"—Christ Reveals Hidden Mysteries**—Colossians 2:2-3: "God's mystery, which is Christ...in whom are hidden all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge." Christ came to "search out" the mystery of salvation hidden for ages (Eph 3:9)—in Christ, God opened His deepest treasures.

**"Heap Burning Coals"—Christ's Cross Overcomes Evil with Good**—Romans 5:10: "while we were enemies we were reconciled to God by the death of his Son." When we were still God's enemies, Christ gave us "bread to eat"—His own body and blood. The ultimate "heaping coals on the enemy's head"—completely softening the enemy's heart with love.

**"Cold of Snow"—Christ Is Living Water**—John 7:37-38: "If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink...out of his heart will flow rivers of living water." Proverbs 25:13's "cold of snow" becomes in Christ the never-drying "living water"—not temporary coolness but eternal quenching.
