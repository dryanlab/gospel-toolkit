## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第二十七章充满了关于友谊、自知和日常生活智慧的格言——其中最著名的一句是"铁磨铁，磨出刃来"——关于真正的友谊如何让人变得更锋利。

**不要为明天自夸（第1节）**

> "不要为明日自夸，因为一日要生何事，你尚且不能知道。"

弟兄姐妹，"不要为明日自夸"——弟兄姐妹，你有没有说过"明天我要……"然后发现明天完全不是你计划的？雅各书4:13-14"你们有话说：今天明天我们要往某城里去，做买卖得利。其实明天如何，你们还不知道。"弟兄姐妹，计划不是罪——但以为你能控制明天就是自夸。你可以计划——但不要自夸。把明天交给神。

**让别人夸你（第2节）**

> "要别人夸奖你，不可用口自夸；等外人称赞你，不可用嘴自称。"

弟兄姐妹，自夸是最不聪明的策略之一——不是因为谦虚是美德（虽然确实是）——而是因为自夸根本不起作用。弟兄姐妹，你自己说自己好——别人会怀疑。别人说你好——那才是真实的见证。弟兄姐妹，让你的行为为你说话——让你做过的事为你辩护——不需要你自己开口。

**朋友的忠诚劝戒（第5-6节）**

> "当面的责备强如背地的爱情。朋友加的伤痕出于忠诚；仇敌连连亲嘴却是多余。"

弟兄姐妹，"朋友加的伤痕出于忠诚"——这是整本箴言中关于友谊最深刻的格言。弟兄姐妹，真正的朋友会让你不舒服——因为他会告诉你不想听但需要听的话。一个从来不责备你的朋友——不是真朋友——是讨好你的人。"仇敌连连亲嘴却是多余"——弟兄姐妹，仇敌的亲嘴（谄媚、奉承）看起来很好——但背后可能是犹大的亲嘴。

弟兄姐妹，"当面的责备强如背地的爱情"——你在背后爱一个人、但当面不敢责备他——这种爱不如当面的责备有价值。弟兄姐妹，真正爱一个人就要敢于当面告诉他真相——即使他会生气、即使他会暂时不理你。因为"朋友加的伤痕出于忠诚"——那些伤痕最终会带来医治。

**铁磨铁（第17节）**

> "铁磨铁，磨出刃来；朋友相感也是如此。"

弟兄姐妹，"铁磨铁磨出刃来"——两块铁互相摩擦——产生热量、火花——但结果是"刃"——锋利。弟兄姐妹，真正的友谊不是永远舒适的——真正的友谊包含摩擦。但那种摩擦的目的不是毁坏——而是"磨出刃来"。弟兄姐妹，如果你身边有一个朋友经常"磨"你——让你不舒服、让你思考、挑战你的假设——不要抛弃他。他可能是神赐给你的"磨铁"。

**水面照脸，人心照人（第19节）**

> "水中照脸，彼此相符；人与人，心也相对。"

弟兄姐妹，"水面照脸"——在古代没有镜子的时候——人在水面上看到自己的脸。"人与人心也相对"——你与别人的互动就像水面一样——反射出你自己的心。弟兄姐妹，如果你总是遇到不友善的人——也许问题不在他们身上。如果你总是发现别人对你有敌意——也许你的心在"反射"什么。

**看顾你的羊群（第23-27节）**

> "你要详细知道你羊群的境况，留心料理你的牛群。因为资财不能永有，冠冕岂能存到万代？干草割去，嫩草发现，山上的菜蔬也被收敛。羊羔之毛是为你做衣服；山羊是为作田地的价值。并有母山羊奶够你吃，也够你的家人吃，且够养你的婢女。"

弟兄姐妹，这段话用牧人的意象描述了勤勉管理的智慧。"详细知道你羊群的境况"——弟兄姐妹，无论你管理的是什么——你要"详细知道"它的状况。不是笼统地大概知道——而是"详细"。一个好的牧人知道每一只羊的名字和状况——一个好的管理者知道每一个项目的细节和需要。

弟兄姐妹，箴言二十七章教导我们：不要为明天自夸——让行为说话。真朋友会伤你——但那是忠诚的伤。铁磨铁磨出刃来——不要逃避摩擦。详细知道你的责任——勤勉管理。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 27 is filled with sayings about friendship, self-knowledge, and daily life wisdom—most famously "iron sharpens iron"—about how true friendship makes people sharper.

**Don't Boast About Tomorrow (v. 1)**

> "Do not boast about tomorrow, for you do not know what a day may bring."

Brothers and sisters, James 4:13-14: "Come now, you who say, 'Today or tomorrow we will go into such and such a town'...Yet you do not know what tomorrow will bring." Planning is not sin—but thinking you can control tomorrow is boasting. You can plan—but don't boast. Give tomorrow to God.

**Let Others Praise You (v. 2)**

> "Let another praise you, and not your own mouth; a stranger, and not your own lips."

Brothers and sisters, self-praise is one of the least effective strategies—not only because humility is virtue—but because self-praise simply doesn't work. When you say you're good—people doubt. When others say you're good—that's genuine witness. Let your deeds speak for you.

**Faithful Wounds of a Friend (vv. 5-6)**

> "Better is open rebuke than hidden love. Faithful are the wounds of a friend; profuse are the kisses of an enemy."

Brothers and sisters, "faithful wounds of a friend"—Proverbs' most profound saying about friendship. A true friend makes you uncomfortable—telling you what you don't want to hear but need to hear. A friend who never rebukes you isn't a true friend—they're a people-pleaser. "Profuse kisses of an enemy"—the enemy's kisses (flattery) look good—but behind them may be Judas's kiss.

**Iron Sharpens Iron (v. 17)**

> "Iron sharpens iron, and one man sharpens another."

Brothers and sisters, "iron sharpens iron"—two pieces of iron rubbing together—producing heat, sparks—but resulting in "sharpness." True friendship isn't permanently comfortable—it contains friction. But that friction's purpose isn't destruction—but "sharpening." If you have a friend who frequently "grinds" you—making you uncomfortable, making you think, challenging your assumptions—don't discard them. They may be God's gift of "sharpening iron."

**Know the Condition of Your Flocks (vv. 23-27)**

> "Know well the condition of your flocks, and give attention to your herds, for riches do not last forever."

Brothers and sisters, whatever you manage—you must "know well" its condition. Not vaguely approximately—but "in detail." A good shepherd knows every sheep's name and condition—a good manager knows every project's details and needs.

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 27 teaches: don't boast about tomorrow—let deeds speak. True friends wound you—but those are faithful wounds. Iron sharpens iron—don't flee friction. Know your responsibilities in detail—manage diligently.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言27章属于"希西家的人所誊录的所罗门箴言"（25-29章）。第6节"朋友加的伤痕出于忠诚"（Faithful are the wounds of a friend）是英语世界中最广为人知的箴言格言之一。这句话被清教徒特别看重——他们在教会纪律和"属灵友谊"（spiritual friendship）的实践中频繁引用。第17节"铁磨铁磨出刃来"（Iron sharpens iron）在现代基督教门训运动中被广泛引用——"铁磨铁"成为弟兄姐妹之间互相问责和成长的代名词。第23-27节的牧羊意象在以色列农牧社会中是最常见的生活场景，与诗篇23篇的牧人意象一脉相承。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 27 belongs to "Solomon's proverbs copied by Hezekiah's men" (chs. 25-29). Verse 6 "faithful are the wounds of a friend" is one of the most widely known Proverbs sayings in the English-speaking world—particularly valued by Puritans who frequently cited it in church discipline and "spiritual friendship" practice. Verse 17 "iron sharpens iron" is widely quoted in modern Christian discipleship movements—"iron sharpens iron" has become synonymous with mutual accountability and growth among believers. Verses 23-27's shepherding imagery was the most common life scene in Israel's agro-pastoral society, continuing the shepherd imagery of Psalm 23.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"朋友加的伤痕出于忠诚"——教会纪律的神学基础**
改革宗传统教导教会纪律是教会三大标记之一。韦敏斯德信条第30章论教会的劝惩。"朋友的伤痕"是教会劝惩的智慧原型——不是出于恶意，而是出于"忠诚"。马太福音18:15-17的教会纪律程序——先私下、再两三人、最后教会——正是"忠诚的伤"的制度化实践。

**"铁磨铁"——团体成圣**
改革宗传统教导成圣不是孤立的个人行为，而是在教会团体中发生的。韦敏斯德信条第25章论教会。"铁磨铁"是团体成圣的最佳意象——你需要别人来"磨"你——孤立的铁不会变锋利。

**"不要为明日自夸"——信靠神的主权**
改革宗传统教导人不能控制未来。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理。"不为明日自夸"是护理教义的实际应用——因为明天在神手中，不在你手中。计划是智慧的——但自夸是骄傲的。区别在于你的心态：是信靠神还是信靠自己。

## Theology (English)

**"Faithful Wounds"—Theological Basis for Church Discipline**
The Reformed tradition teaches church discipline is one of the church's three marks. Westminster Confession chapter 30 on church censures.: A friend's wounds is the wisdom prototype of church discipline—not from malice but from "faithfulness." Matthew 18:15-17's church discipline procedure is "faithful wounds" institutionalized.

**"Iron Sharpens Iron"—Corporate Sanctification**
The Reformed tradition teaches sanctification is not isolated individual action but occurs in church community. Westminster Confession chapter 25.: Iron sharpens iron is the best image for corporate sanctification—you need others to "sharpen" you—isolated iron never becomes sharp.

**"Don't Boast About Tomorrow"—Trusting God's Sovereignty**
The Reformed tradition teaches humans cannot control the future. Westminster Confession chapter 5.: Don't boast about tomorrow is providence doctrine's practical application—because tomorrow is in God's hand, not yours.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"朋友加的伤痕出于忠诚"——基督对彼得的责备**——马太福音16:23"耶稣转过来对彼得说：撒但，退我后边去吧！"基督对彼得的严厉责备是最深的爱——彼得当时不理解，但这"伤痕"拯救了他。约翰福音21:15-17基督三次问"你爱我吗"也是"忠诚的伤"——恢复了否认祂三次的彼得。

**"铁磨铁"——基督与门徒的关系**——马可福音3:14"他设立十二个人，要他们常与自己同在。"基督在三年中"磨"了门徒——挑战他们的观念、纠正他们的错误、扩展他们的视野。最终这些"铁"变成了"刃"——五旬节以后门徒成为了锋利的福音使者。

**"详细知道你羊群的境况"——基督是好牧人**——约翰福音10:14"我是好牧人；我认识我的羊，我的羊也认识我。"10:3"他按着名叫自己的羊。"箴言27:23的"详细知道羊群"在基督身上得到了最完美的体现——祂不只是管理我们，祂"按名"认识每一只羊。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Faithful Wounds"—Christ's Rebuke of Peter**—Matthew 16:23: "Get behind me, Satan!" Christ's severe rebuke of Peter was deepest love—Peter didn't understand then, but those "wounds" saved him. John 21:15-17's three-time "do you love me" was also "faithful wounding"—restoring the Peter who denied Him three times.

**"Iron Sharpens Iron"—Christ's Relationship with Disciples**—Mark 3:14: "He appointed twelve to be with him." Christ "sharpened" disciples over three years—challenging their views, correcting errors, expanding vision. These "irons" became "blades"—after Pentecost, disciples became sharp gospel messengers.

**"Know Your Flocks"—Christ Is the Good Shepherd**—John 10:14: "I am the good shepherd. I know my own and my own know me." John 10:3: "He calls his own sheep by name." Proverbs 27:23's "know your flocks" finds perfect embodiment in Christ—He doesn't just manage us; He knows each sheep "by name."
