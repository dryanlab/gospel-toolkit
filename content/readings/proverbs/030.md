## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，箴言第三十章不是所罗门写的——而是"雅基的儿子亚古珥的言语"——一位神秘的智慧人。他的话以极度的谦卑和敬畏开始，然后用一系列精妙的"数字格言"观察自然和人生。

**亚古珥的谦卑（第1-4节）**

> "雅基的儿子亚古珥的言语就是真言。这人对以铁和乌甲说：我比众人更蠢笨，也没有人的聪明。我没有学好智慧，也不认识至圣者。谁升天又降下来？谁聚风在掌中？谁包水在衣服里？谁立定地的四极？他名叫什么？他儿子名叫什么？你知道吗？"

弟兄姐妹，"我比众人更蠢笨"——亚古珥以一个惊人的自我否定开始。弟兄姐妹，他不是假谦虚——他是真的在面对神的无限时感到自己的渺小。"谁升天又降下来？谁聚风在掌中？"——这些问题与约伯记38章神对约伯的质问相似——答案都是：只有神。

"他儿子名叫什么你知道吗？"——弟兄姐妹，这是旧约中最接近暗示"神的儿子"的经文之一。弟兄姐妹，新约的回答是：祂儿子名叫耶稣。

**不多不少的祷告（第7-9节）**

> "我求你两件事，在我未死之先，不要拒绝我：求你使虚假和谎言远离我；使我也不贫穷也不富足；赐给我需用的饮食，恐怕我饱足不认你，说耶和华是谁呢？又恐怕我贫穷就偷窃，以致亵渎我神的名。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整本箴言中最实际、最有智慧的祷告之一。"不贫穷也不富足"——弟兄姐妹，这不是懒惰的中庸——而是深刻的自知之明。亚古珥知道自己的弱点——如果太富就会骄傲不认神——如果太穷就可能被逼偷窃。弟兄姐妹，大多数人的祷告是"主啊给我更多"——亚古珥的祷告是"主啊给我刚好"。

弟兄姐妹，你有没有想过——你真的需要你正在追求的那么多吗？更多的钱带来更多的担忧——更大的房子带来更大的维护——更高的地位带来更大的压力。亚古珥的智慧是：够就好了。保罗在腓立比书4:12说："我知道怎样处卑贱，也知道怎样处丰富……我靠着那加给我力量的，凡事都能做。"知足——不是不上进——而是在任何处境中找到平安。

**四样小而有智慧的（第24-28节）**

> "地上有四样小物，却甚聪明：蚂蚁是无力之类，却在夏天预备粮食；沙番是软弱之类，却在磐石中造房；蝗虫没有君王，却都成群而出；守宫用爪抓墙，却住在王宫。"

弟兄姐妹，四样小而有智慧的生物——蚂蚁（预备）、沙番（选好根基）、蝗虫（团队合作）、守宫（胆量，敢住在王宫）。弟兄姐妹，这些小小的动物教了我们四个大功课：

蚂蚁——未雨绸缪。你在"夏天"（顺境）预备"冬天"（逆境）了吗？
沙番——选好根基。你的"房子"建在什么上面？磐石还是沙子？
蝗虫——团队的力量。单个蝗虫无害——但成群的蝗虫可以吞噬一切。团结使弱者变强。
守宫——有胆量。一只小小的壁虎——敢住在王宫里。弟兄姐妹，你的环境不决定你的位置——你的勇气决定。

弟兄姐妹，箴言三十章的核心信息是谦卑和知足。亚古珥不自称有智慧——而是坦承自己的无知。他不求多也不求少——而是求刚好。他从最小的生物中看到了神的智慧。弟兄姐妹，真正的智慧往往从承认"我比众人更蠢笨"开始。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 30 was not written by Solomon—but by "Agur son of Jakeh"—a mysterious wisdom figure. His words begin with extreme humility and awe, then use a series of exquisite "numerical sayings" to observe nature and life.

**Agur's Humility (vv. 1-4)**

> "The words of Agur son of Jakeh...I am more stupid than any man, and I do not have the understanding of a man...Who has ascended to heaven and come down? Who has gathered the wind in his fists? Who has wrapped up the waters in a garment? Who has established all the ends of the earth? What is his name, and what is his son's name? Surely you know!"

Brothers and sisters, "I am more stupid than any man"—Agur begins with stunning self-negation. Not false humility—genuine smallness before God's infinity. "What is his son's name? Surely you know!"—one of the OT's closest hints at "God's Son." The NT's answer: His Son's name is Jesus.

**The Prayer of Neither Too Much Nor Too Little (vv. 7-9)**

> "Two things I ask of you; deny them not to me before I die: Remove far from me falsehood and lying; give me neither poverty nor riches; feed me with the food that is needful for me, lest I be full and deny you and say, 'Who is the LORD?' or lest I be poor and steal and profane the name of my God."

Brothers and sisters, "neither poverty nor riches"—not lazy mediocrity—but profound self-knowledge. Agur knows his weaknesses—too rich and he'd grow proud; too poor and he might steal. Most pray "Lord, give me more"—Agur prays "Lord, give me just enough."

Have you considered—do you truly need as much as you're pursuing? More money brings more worry—larger houses bring more maintenance—higher status brings more pressure. Agur's wisdom: enough is enough. Paul in Philippians 4:12: "I know how to be brought low, and I know how to abound...I can do all things through him who strengthens me." Contentment—not lacking ambition—but finding peace in any circumstance.

**Four Small but Wise Things (vv. 24-28)**

> "Four things on earth are small, but they are exceedingly wise: the ants are a people not strong, yet they provide their food in the summer; the rock badgers are a people not mighty, yet they make their homes in the cliffs; the locusts have no king, yet all of them march in rank; the lizard you can take in your hands, yet it is in kings' palaces."

Brothers and sisters, four small wise creatures—ants (preparation), rock badgers (choosing good foundations), locusts (teamwork), lizards (boldness, daring to live in palaces). Four great lessons from small animals: Prepare ahead. Choose your foundation wisely. Unite for strength. Be bold—your environment doesn't determine your position; your courage does.

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 30's core message is humility and contentment. Agur doesn't claim wisdom—but confesses his ignorance. He asks for neither too much nor too little—just enough. He sees God's wisdom in the smallest creatures. True wisdom often begins with admitting "I am more stupid than any man."

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言30章的作者"亚古珥"身份不明——"雅基的儿子"在以色列人名中没有其他出现。有学者认为亚古珥可能是非以色列人——也许是以东或北阿拉伯的智慧人——这与箴言包含非以色列作者（如31章的利慕伊勒）一致。第4节"他儿子名叫什么"在犹太传统中有不同解释——有些拉比认为指的是以色列民族，有些认为指的是摩西——而基督教传统从早期教父开始就将其解读为对"神的儿子"（基督）的预言性暗示。第7-9节"不贫穷也不富足"的祷告在基督教灵修传统中非常重要——成为"知足"和"简朴生活"教导的核心经文。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 30's author "Agur" is unidentified—"son of Jakeh" appears nowhere else in Israelite names. Some scholars suggest Agur may have been non-Israelite—perhaps an Edomite or North Arabian sage—consistent with Proverbs including non-Israelite authors (King Lemuel in ch. 31). Verse 4's "what is his son's name" has various Jewish interpretations—some rabbis say Israel, some say Moses—while Christian tradition from early church fathers reads it as a prophetic hint at "God's Son" (Christ). Verses 7-9's "neither poverty nor riches" prayer has been profoundly important in Christian spiritual tradition—becoming a core text for "contentment" and "simple living" teaching.

## Structure (English)

**Agur's Humility (vv. 1-6)**—More stupid than any → who ascended and descended → what is his son's name → every word of God proves true.
**The Prayer of Just Enough (vv. 7-9)**—Two things I ask → remove falsehood → neither poverty nor riches → food that is needful.
**Numerical Sayings Collection (vv. 10-33)**—Don't slander a servant → there are those who → the leech has two daughters → three things that make earth tremble → four things small but wise.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"我比众人更蠢笨"——谦卑作为智慧的起点**
改革宗传统教导人在神面前的全然无能。韦敏斯德信条第6章和第9章论人的败坏和意志的无能。亚古珥的"我比众人更蠢笨"不是自嘲——而是在神的智慧面前真实的自我认识。加尔文在《基督教要义》1.1中说："认识自己，就会被引导去认识神。"

**"不贫穷也不富足"——知足的神学**
改革宗传统教导知足是信心的表现。韦敏斯德大要理问答第147问论第十诫——第十诫禁止什么？不满足于自己的处境。亚古珥的祷告是对第十诫的正面回应——主动选择知足，而不是被动地忍受。提摩太前书6:6-8"然而，敬虔加上知足的心便是大利了。"

**"四样小而有智慧"——从受造物学习神的智慧**
改革宗传统教导自然启示——受造物反映创造者的智慧。韦敏斯德信条第1章论自然和特殊启示。蚂蚁、沙番、蝗虫、守宫——都是神智慧的"教科书"。罗马书1:20"自从造天地以来，神的永能和神性是明明可知的，虽是眼不能见，但藉着所造之物就可以晓得。"

## Theology (English)

**"More Stupid Than Any Man"—Humility as Wisdom's Starting Point**
The Reformed tradition teaches human total inability before God. Westminster Confession chapters 6, 9. Agur's: more stupid than any man is not self-deprecation—but genuine self-knowledge before God's wisdom. Calvin: "Knowledge of ourselves leads to knowledge of God" (Institutes 1.1).

**"Neither Poverty Nor Riches"—Theology of Contentment**
The Reformed tradition teaches contentment is faith's expression. WLC Q147 on the tenth commandment—"forbids discontent with our own estate." Agur's prayer is a positive response to the tenth commandment—actively choosing contentment. 1 Timothy 6:6-8: "godliness with contentment is great gain."

**"Four Small but Wise"—Learning God's Wisdom from Creation**
The Reformed tradition teaches natural revelation—creation reflects the Creator's wisdom. Westminster Confession chapter 1. Ants, badgers, locusts, lizards—all "textbooks" of God's wisdom. Romans 1:20: "his invisible attributes...have been clearly perceived, ever since the creation of the world, in the things that have been made."

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"他儿子名叫什么"——基督是神的儿子**——马太福音16:16"西门彼得回答说：你是基督，是永生神的儿子。"亚古珥问"他儿子名叫什么你知道吗？"——新约揭示了答案：耶稣基督，神的独生子。约翰福音3:16"神爱世人，甚至将他的独生子赐给他们。"

**"谁升天又降下来"——基督的升天降下**——约翰福音3:13"除了从天降下仍旧在天的人子，没有人升过天。"以弗所书4:9-10"既说升上，岂不是先降在地下吗？那降下的就是远升诸天之上要充满万有的。"亚古珥的修辞问题在基督身上得到了回答——基督是那位升天又降下的。

**"不贫穷也不富足"——基督虽富却成为贫穷**——哥林多后书8:9"你们知道我们主耶稣基督的恩典：他本来富足，却为你们成了贫穷，叫你们因他的贫穷可以成为富足。"基督超越了亚古珥的"不贫穷也不富足"的祷告——祂主动从最富（神的荣耀）降到最穷（十字架的羞辱）——为要使我们从穷变富。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"What Is His Son's Name"—Christ Is God's Son**—Matthew 16:16: "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God." Agur asks "what is his son's name? Surely you know!"—the NT reveals the answer: Jesus Christ, God's only Son. John 3:16: "God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son."

**"Who Ascended and Descended"—Christ's Ascent and Descent**—John 3:13: "No one has ascended into heaven except he who descended from heaven, the Son of Man." Ephesians 4:9-10: "He who descended is the one who also ascended far above all the heavens." Agur's rhetorical question is answered in Christ—He is the One who ascended and descended.

**"Neither Poverty Nor Riches"—Christ Though Rich Became Poor**—2 Corinthians 8:9: "though he was rich, yet for your sake he became poor, so that you by his poverty might become rich." Christ transcends Agur's "neither poverty nor riches" prayer—He voluntarily went from the richest (God's glory) to the poorest (the cross's shame)—to make us rich from poor.
