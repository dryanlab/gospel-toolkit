## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第十一章继续义人与恶人的对比——但特别聚焦于"正直"和"公义"在日常生活中的表现：商业交易、财富使用、人际关系、社区影响。

**诡诈与正直（第1-3节）**

> "诡诈的天平为耶和华所憎恶；公平的砝码为他所喜悦。骄傲来，羞耻也来；谦逊人却有智慧。正直人的纯正必引导自己；奸诈人的乖僻必毁灭自己。"

弟兄姐妹，"诡诈的天平"——在古代社会，买卖用天平称量银子和谷物。用假砝码是当时最常见的欺诈手段。弟兄姐妹，神关心你的商业行为——你的生意是否诚实——你的交易是否公平。"耶和华所憎恶"——弟兄姐妹，神不只关心你主日在教会做什么——祂关心你周一到周六在市场上做什么。你的天平公平吗？你的账目诚实吗？

"骄傲来羞耻也来"——弟兄姐妹，这是箴言中最简洁有力的警告之一。骄傲和羞耻是一对——骄傲先到，羞耻跟着就来。弟兄姐妹，每一个骄傲的人最终都会经历羞耻——不是"可能"而是"必然"。

**财富的虚幻与义的拯救（第4-6节）**

> "发怒的日子资财无益；惟有公义能救人脱离死亡。完全人的义必指引他的路；但恶人必因自己的恶跌倒。正直人的义必拯救自己；奸诈人必陷在自己的罪孽中。"

弟兄姐妹，"发怒的日子资财无益"——"发怒的日子"指的是神审判的日子。弟兄姐妹，那一天你的银行账户没有用、你的房产没有用、你的股票没有用——"惟有公义能救人脱离死亡"。弟兄姐妹，你在为哪一天预备？是明天还是那一天？如果你只为明天预备——你是聪明的。如果你也为"那一天"预备——你才是有智慧的。

**慷慨与吝啬（第24-26节）**

> "有施散的，却更增添；有吝惜过度的，反致穷乏。好施舍的，必得丰裕；滋润人的，必得滋润。屯粮不卖的，民必咒诅他；情愿出卖的，人必为他祝福。"

弟兄姐妹，"有施散的却更增添"——这违反了经济学的基本逻辑——给出去应该变少才对。但神的经济学不同——给出去反而"更增添"。弟兄姐妹，这不是"种子信心"的错误教导——不是说你给一块钱神还你一百块。而是说：慷慨的人活在神祝福的循环中——"滋润人的必得滋润"。你的心越大——神给你的越多——不一定是钱，而是丰盛的生命。

"屯粮不卖的民必咒诅他"——弟兄姐妹，在饥荒中囤积粮食抬高价格——古今中外都令人愤怒。但这不只是经济行为——而是道德选择。弟兄姐妹，你有能力帮助人却选择不帮——你手里有"粮"却选择"屯"——你的邻舍"必咒诅你"。而"情愿出卖的"——在别人需要的时候慷慨——"人必为他祝福"。

**义人是城市的祝福（第10-11节）**

> "义人享福，合城喜乐；恶人灭亡，人都欢呼。城因正直人的祝福便高举；却因邪恶人的口就倾覆。"

弟兄姐妹，义人不只是自己得福——他是整个城市的祝福。弟兄姐妹，一个正直的人在一个社区里——整个社区都因他"高举"。一个腐败的官员在一个城市里——整个城市都因他"倾覆"。弟兄姐妹，你在你的圈子里是"祝福"还是"倾覆"？你的存在让你周围的人更好还是更糟？

**才貌与智慧（第22节）**

> "妇女美貌而无见识，如同金环带在猪鼻上。"

弟兄姐妹，这是箴言中最生动的比喻之一——金环本是贵重的——但带在猪鼻上就完全不搭配了。外在的美丽如果没有内在的智慧——就像金子用错了地方。弟兄姐妹，这不只是对女性说的——任何外在的成就如果没有内在的品格——都是"金环带在猪鼻上"。

弟兄姐妹，箴言第十一章教导我们：正直不是抽象的美德——而是在天平上、在钱包里、在社区中活出来的。你的公义影响你自己、你的家庭、你的城市。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 11 continues the righteous-wicked contrast—but particularly focuses on how "integrity" and "righteousness" manifest in daily life: business dealings, wealth usage, relationships, community impact.

**Dishonesty and Integrity (vv. 1-3)**

> "A false balance is an abomination to the LORD, but a just weight is his delight. When pride comes, then comes disgrace, but with the humble is wisdom."

Brothers and sisters, "a false balance"—in ancient society, buying and selling used scales to weigh silver and grain. False weights were the most common fraud. God cares about your business conduct—whether your dealings are honest, your transactions fair. "An abomination to the LORD"—God doesn't only care what you do on Sunday at church—He cares what you do Monday through Saturday at the market.

"When pride comes, then comes disgrace"—one of Proverbs' most concise warnings. Pride and disgrace are paired—pride arrives first, disgrace follows inevitably.

**Wealth's Vanity and Righteousness' Salvation (vv. 4-6)**

> "Riches do not profit in the day of wrath, but righteousness delivers from death."

Brothers and sisters, "the day of wrath"—God's judgment day. That day your bank account is useless, your real estate meaningless—"only righteousness delivers from death." Are you preparing for tomorrow or for that day?

**Generosity and Stinginess (vv. 24-26)**

> "One gives freely, yet grows all the richer; another withholds what he should give, and only suffers want. Whoever brings blessing will be enriched, and one who waters will himself be watered."

Brothers and sisters, "gives freely yet grows richer"—this defies basic economics. But God's economics differ—giving away actually "grows richer." Not seed-faith error—not give one dollar get one hundred back. Rather: generous people live in God's blessing cycle—"one who waters will himself be watered."

> "People curse him who holds back grain, but a blessing is on the head of him who sells it." Having ability to help but choosing not to—your neighbor will "curse you."

**The Righteous Bless the City (vv. 10-11)**

> "When it goes well with the righteous, the city rejoices...By the blessing of the upright a city is exalted."

Brothers and sisters, the righteous don't only benefit themselves—they bless the entire city. One upright person in a community—the whole community is "exalted." One corrupt official in a city—the whole city is "overthrown." Are you a "blessing" or an "overthrow" in your circle?

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 11 teaches: integrity is not abstract virtue—it is lived out on the scales, in the wallet, in the community. Your righteousness affects yourself, your family, your city.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言第十一章延续了第十章开始的"所罗门格言集"的对偶体风格。"诡诈的天平"（第1节）直接呼应利未记19:35-36"你们施行审判不可行不义……要用公道天平、公道砝码"和申命记25:13-16"你囊中不可有一大一小两样的砝码"。在古代近东，度量衡标准由宫廷或圣殿保管——使用假砝码不仅是商业欺诈，更是对神圣秩序的蔑视。考古发现在以色列境内出土了大量标准和非标准的砝码——证明欺诈性的度量在古代确实普遍存在。"骄傲来羞耻也来"在希伯来原文中是一个极简的平行句——仅用四个希伯来词表达了一个深刻的人生规律。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 11 continues the antithetical style of the "Solomon collection" begun in chapter 10. "False balance" (v. 1) directly echoes Leviticus 19:35-36 "you shall do no wrong in judgment, in measures...you shall have just balances" and Deuteronomy 25:13-16. In the ancient Near East, measurement standards were kept by palace or temple—using false weights was not just commercial fraud but contempt for sacred order. Archaeology has unearthed numerous standard and non-standard weights in Israel—proving fraudulent measures were indeed widespread in antiquity.

## 经文结构（中文）

**诡诈与正直（1-3节）**——诡诈天平为耶和华憎恶→骄傲带来羞耻→正直引导自己。
**义的拯救（4-8节）**——资财在发怒日无益→公义救人脱死→义指引道路→恶人陷自己罪中。
**义人影响社区（9-14节）**——义人享福合城喜乐→正直人使城高举→多人商议事就成。
**慷慨与吝啬（15-26节）**——施散更增添→滋润人必得滋润→屯粮不卖被咒诅。
**寻义与行恶（27-31节）**——早晨寻求善→仗恃自己财物的必跌倒→义人在世尚且受报。

## Structure (English)

**Dishonesty and Integrity (vv. 1-3)**—False balance abomination → pride brings disgrace → integrity guides.
**Salvation Through Righteousness (vv. 4-8)**—Riches useless on wrath-day → righteousness delivers → righteousness guides → wicked fall.
**Righteous Impact on Community (vv. 9-14)**—Righteous prosper, city rejoices → upright exalt city → many counselors bring success.
**Generosity and Stinginess (vv. 15-26)**—Giving freely grows richer → watering gets watered → withholding grain brings curse.
**Seeking Good and Doing Evil (vv. 27-31)**—Seeking good finds favor → trusting riches will fall → righteous rewarded on earth.

## 神学要点（中文）

**商业伦理——信仰在市场中的实践**
改革宗传统教导信仰必须在日常生活中实践。韦敏斯德小要理问答第74问论第八诫："第八诫吩咐我们做什么？"答："第八诫吩咐我们合法地取得并增进自己和别人的财富与产业。""诡诈的天平"直接违反了第八诫——通过不诚实获取利益就是偷盗。

**"骄傲来羞耻也来"——罪的必然后果**
改革宗传统教导罪必带来后果。韦敏斯德信条第6章论人的堕落与罪。骄傲和羞耻的关系不是偶然的——而是因果性的。骄傲是人心取代神的位置——而羞耻是这种僭越的必然结果。

**"慷慨施散"——基督徒管家的责任**
改革宗传统教导基督徒是财富的管家而非拥有者。韦敏斯德大要理问答第141问论第八诫要求公平慷慨对待他人。"有施散的却更增添"的原则来自神是终极供应者——你给出去不会变穷，因为你的供应来源不是你自己，而是神。

## Theology (English)

**Business Ethics—Faith Practiced in the Marketplace**
The Reformed tradition teaches faith must be practiced in daily life. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q74 on the eighth commandment. "A false balance" directly violates the eighth commandment—gaining through dishonesty is theft.

**"Pride Comes, Then Disgrace"—Sin's Inevitable Consequence**
The Reformed tradition teaches sin inevitably brings consequences. Westminster Confession chapter 6 on the fall. Pride and disgrace's relationship is not coincidental—but causal. Pride is the human heart displacing God—disgrace is this usurpation's inevitable result.

**"Giving Freely"—The Christian Steward's Responsibility**
The Reformed tradition teaches Christians are stewards not owners of wealth. WLC Q141. "One gives freely yet grows richer" because God is the ultimate provider—you won't become poor by giving, because your supply source is not yourself but God.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"公义能救人脱离死亡"——基督的义归算给我们**——罗马书3:22"就是神的义，因信耶稣基督加给一切相信的人。"箴言11:4的"公义能救人脱离死亡"在基督里得到了终极成就——不是我们自己的义（以赛亚书64:6"我们所有的义都像污秽的衣服"），而是基督的义归算给信徒（罗马书4:5"不做工的，只信称罪人为义的神，他的信就算为义"）。

**"有施散的却更增添"——基督的倒空与充满**——腓立比书2:6-9"他本有神的形像……反倒虚己……所以神将他升为至高。"基督是"施散更增添"的最高典范——祂倒空了自己、舍弃了一切、死在十字架上——结果神将祂升为至高，赐给祂超乎万名之上的名。

**"城因正直人的祝福便高举"——基督是城市的祝福**——以弗所书1:22-23"又将万有服在他的脚下，使他为教会作万有之首。教会是他的身体。"基督通过教会祝福城市——信徒作为基督的身体活在社区中，成为那"使城高举"的正直人。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Righteousness Delivers from Death"—Christ's Righteousness Imputed to Us**—Romans 3:22: "the righteousness of God through faith in Jesus Christ for all who believe." Proverbs 11:4's "righteousness delivers from death" finds ultimate fulfillment in Christ—not our own righteousness (Isa 64:6 "all our righteous deeds are like a polluted garment") but Christ's righteousness imputed to believers (Rom 4:5).

**"Gives Freely Yet Grows Richer"—Christ's Emptying and Filling**—Philippians 2:6-9: "though he was in the form of God...emptied himself...Therefore God has highly exalted him." Christ is the supreme example of "giving freely yet growing richer"—He emptied Himself, gave up everything, died on the cross—and God exalted Him to the highest, giving Him the name above every name.

**"By the Blessing of the Upright a City Is Exalted"—Christ Blesses the City**—Ephesians 1:22-23. Christ through the church blesses cities—believers as Christ's body living in communities become those "upright" who "exalt the city."
