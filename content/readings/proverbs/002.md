## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第二章是一首"如果……就……"的诗——它描述了寻求智慧所需要的条件和所带来的果效。这首诗的核心信息是：智慧不是送上门来的，它需要像寻找宝藏一样去挖掘。

**寻求的条件（第1-4节）**

> "我儿，你若领受我的言语，存记我的命令，侧耳听智慧，专心求聪明，呼求明哲，扬声求聪明，坚心寻找，如寻找银子，如搜寻隐藏的珍宝。"

弟兄姐妹，注意这里的动词——领受、存记、侧耳听、专心求、呼求、扬声求、坚心寻找、如寻找银子。弟兄姐妹，七到八个动词！寻求智慧是一个主动的、多方位的努力。

"如寻找银子"——弟兄姐妹，你寻找工作、寻找爱人、寻找商机——用多大的热情？那种热情应该用在寻求智慧上。矿工为了银子会挖穿山体——你为了智慧愿意付出多大的代价？

**寻求的来源（第5-6节）**

> "你就明白敬畏耶和华，得以认识神。因为耶和华赐人智慧；知识和聪明都由他口而出。"

弟兄姐妹，五节经文讲了所有的条件之后——第六节揭示了真正的来源："因为耶和华赐人智慧。"弟兄姐妹，不要误解——这不是说"只要等神给就行了，不用努力"。而是说：你的努力是方向——神的赐予是内容。你把心对准智慧、主动寻求——然后神把智慧倒在你里面。两者缺一不可。

**智慧的保护（第7-11节）**

> "他为正直人存留真智慧，为行为纯正的人作盾牌，为要保守公平人的路，护庇虔敬人的道。那时，你必明白仁义、公平，和正直，以及一切善道……智慧必入你心，你心必以知识为美。谋略必护卫你；聪明必保守你。"

弟兄姐妹，智慧是保护性的——"盾牌""护庇""护卫""保守"。弟兄姐妹，没有智慧的人在人生的道路上是赤手空拳的——每一个诱惑都能击倒他。有智慧的人有盾牌——诱惑来的时候有辨别力、有保护。

**淫妇和外邦女人的危险（第16-19节）**

> "智慧必救你脱离淫妇，就是那专说谄媚话的外邦女人。她离弃幼年的配偶，忘了神的盟约……凡到她那里去的都不得转回，也得不着生命的路。"

弟兄姐妹，这一段对年轻人极其重要——智慧保护你"脱离淫妇"。"外邦女人"代表的不只是一个人——而是一切吸引你离开神的约、离开神的道的事物。弟兄姐妹，"离弃幼年的配偶，忘了神的盟约"——凡让你背离你在神面前所许的誓言的——都是那"外邦女人"的诱惑。

"凡到她那里去的都不得转回"——弟兄姐妹，这个警告是严肃的。有些诱惑一旦进去就很难出来——不是因为神不能拯救，而是因为罪的习惯一旦形成就很难打破。智慧告诉你：在门口就拒绝，不要以为自己进去了还能轻松出来。

**走正直的道（第20-22节）**

> "这样，你便可以行善人的道，守义人的路。因为正直人必在地上居住；完全人必在地上长存。至于恶人，必从地上剪除；奸诈人，必从其中拔出去。"

弟兄姐妹，最后的承诺——寻求智慧、走正直的路——"正直人必在地上居住"。不是没有挑战、不是没有困难——但在神的应许中，行正直之道的人有根基。弟兄姐妹，你今天的选择在建造你的未来。每一个"寻找智慧如寻找银子"的日子——在为你的将来打根基。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 2 is an "if...then..." poem—describing the conditions for seeking wisdom and its resulting blessings. This chapter's core message: wisdom doesn't come to your door; it requires searching like treasure.

**The Conditions for Seeking (vv. 1-4)**

> "My son, if you receive my words and treasure up my commandments...making your ear attentive to wisdom and inclining your heart to understanding...if you seek it like silver and search for it as for hidden treasures."

Brothers and sisters, count the verbs—receive, treasure, incline ear, incline heart, call out, raise voice, seek, search. Seven to eight verbs! Seeking wisdom is an active, multi-faceted effort. "Like silver"—how passionately do you seek jobs, love, opportunities? That passion should be directed toward wisdom.

**The Source of Wisdom (vv. 5-6)**

> "Then you will understand the fear of the LORD and find the knowledge of God. For the LORD gives wisdom; from his mouth come knowledge and understanding."

Brothers and sisters, after five verses of conditions—verse six reveals the true source: "the LORD gives wisdom." Don't misunderstand—not "just wait for God to give it, no effort needed." Rather: your effort is the direction—God's gift is the content. You orient your heart toward wisdom, actively seek—then God pours wisdom into you. Both are necessary.

**Wisdom's Protection (vv. 7-11)**

> "He stores up sound wisdom for the upright; he is a shield to those who walk in integrity...then you will understand righteousness and justice and equity, every good path; for wisdom will come into your heart, and knowledge will be pleasant to your soul; discretion will watch over you, understanding will guard you."

Brothers and sisters, wisdom is protective—"shield," "guard," "watch over," "guard." Without wisdom, a person faces life's road unarmed—every temptation can knock him down.

**The Warning About the Adulteress (vv. 16-19)**

> "So you will be delivered from the forbidden woman, from the adulteress with her smooth words, who forsakes the companion of her youth and forgets the covenant of her God...none who go to her come back, nor do they regain the paths of life."

Brothers and sisters, "none who go to her come back"—a solemn warning. Some temptations, once entered, are very hard to exit—not because God can't save, but because sinful habits once formed are hard to break. Wisdom says: refuse at the door; don't think you can enter and casually exit.

**Walking the Straight Path (vv. 20-22)**

> "So you will walk in the way of the good and keep to the paths of the righteous. For the upright will inhabit the land, and those with integrity will remain in it."

Brothers and sisters, the final promise—seeking wisdom, walking upright paths—"the upright will inhabit the land." Not without challenges—but in God's promise, those walking upright have foundation. Every day "seeking wisdom like silver" builds your future's foundation.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言第二章是一首希伯来诗歌中罕见的"条件诗"——整章由一个长条件句（第1-11节）和结果（第12-22节）组成，在希伯来文中形成精巧的对称结构。"如寻找银子如搜寻隐藏的珍宝"反映了古代近东的矿业知识——以色列和周边地区确实有银矿和其他矿藏，需要大量艰苦挖掘。"外邦女人"（foreign woman，zara）和"淫妇"（adulteress，nokhriya）是箴言中的反复主题——学者们讨论这是否只指真实的性诱惑，还是也象征性地指向一切偶像崇拜（因为以色列被叫做神的"妻子"，偶像崇拜被比作"淫乱"）。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 2 is a rare "conditional poem" in Hebrew poetry—the entire chapter forms one long conditional sentence (vv. 1-11) and result (vv. 12-22), creating an elegant symmetric structure in Hebrew. "Like silver, like hidden treasure" reflects ancient Near Eastern mining knowledge—Israel and surrounding regions did have silver mines requiring arduous excavation. The "forbidden woman" (zara) and "adulteress" (nokhriya) are recurring Proverbs themes—scholars debate whether these refer only to actual sexual temptation or also symbolically to all idolatry (since Israel is called God's "wife," and idolatry is compared to "adultery").

## 经文结构（中文）

**寻求的条件（1-4节）**——领受存记→侧耳专心→呼求扬声→如寻银子如搜宝藏。
**寻求的来源和结果（5-11节）**——明白敬畏耶和华→认识神→耶和华赐智慧→盾牌护庇→智慧入心→护卫保守。
**恶人和淫妇的警告（12-19节）**——救你脱离恶人→脱离淫妇→她忘神盟约→到她那里不得转回。
**正直道路的应许（20-22节）**——行善人的道→正直人必居住→恶人从地上剪除。

## Structure (English)

**Conditions for Seeking (vv. 1-4)**—Receive, treasure → incline ear, heart → call out, raise voice → like silver, like hidden treasure.
**Source and Result of Seeking (vv. 5-11)**—Understand fear of the LORD → know God → LORD gives wisdom → shield, protection → wisdom in heart → discretion guards.
**Warning About Wicked and Adulteress (vv. 12-19)**—Delivered from wicked man → from adulteress → she forgets God's covenant → none who go come back.
**Promise of Upright Path (vv. 20-22)**—Walk in way of good → upright inhabit land → wicked cut off.

## 神学要点（中文）

**恩典与努力的配合**
改革宗传统教导人的责任与神的主权并不矛盾。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理和人的责任。"如寻银子"的努力是人的责任——"耶和华赐智慧"是神的主权。两者并行——都是真实的。这是改革宗神学的"协同工作"（concurrence）原则——神在人的努力中工作。

**"脱离淫妇"——道德纯洁**
改革宗传统强调道德纯洁。韦敏斯德小要理问答第71-72问论第七诫："第七诫禁止什么？……一切不贞洁的意念、言语和行为。"智慧不是理论——它有实际的道德保护功能。

**"神的盟约"——约束道德的根基**
改革宗传统教导道德的神约基础。韦敏斯德信条第19章论神的律法。"忘了神的盟约"是道德堕落的根本原因——不是缺乏教育，而是忘记了与神的关系和约定。

## Theology (English)

**Grace and Effort Together**
The Reformed tradition teaches human responsibility and divine sovereignty are not contradictory. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence and responsibility. "Seeking like silver" is human responsibility—"the LORD gives wisdom" is divine sovereignty. Both run parallel. The Reformed "concurrence" principle—God works through human effort.

**"Delivered from the Adulteress"—Moral Purity**
The Reformed tradition emphasizes moral purity. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q71-72 on the seventh commandment. Wisdom is not theoretical—it has practical moral protective function.

**"God's Covenant"—The Foundation Binding Morality**
The Reformed tradition teaches morality's covenantal foundation. Westminster Confession chapter 19 on God's law.: Forgetting God's covenant is the fundamental cause of moral collapse—not lack of education, but forgetting relationship and commitment to God.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督是隐藏的宝藏**——马太福音13:44"天国好像宝贝藏在地里，人发现了就把它藏起来，欢欢喜喜地去变卖一切所有的，买这块地。"箴言2:4"如搜寻隐藏的珍宝"——基督就是那隐藏的宝藏，值得变卖一切去寻求和获得。

**"智慧保护你"——基督是信徒的盾牌**——以弗所书6:16"拿起信德当作盾牌，可以熄灭那恶者一切的火箭。"箴言2:7-8的"盾牌护庇"在基督里找到终极的表达——不只是智慧保护，而是基督自己作为信徒的"避难所"（来6:18）。

**"忘了神的盟约"对比"基督是新约的中保"**——希伯来书8:6"如今耶稣所得到的职任是更美的……他作更美之约的中保。"淫妇"忘了神的盟约"——而基督建立了永远的新约，不再被忘记。弟兄姐妹，在基督里你不会"忘了神的盟约"——因为基督自己把这约刻在你心上（来8:10）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Is the Hidden Treasure**—Matthew 13:44: "The kingdom of heaven is like treasure hidden in a field, which a man found and covered up. Then in his joy he goes and sells all that he has and buys that field." Proverbs 2:4 "search for it as for hidden treasures"—Christ is that hidden treasure, worth selling everything to seek and obtain.

**"Wisdom Guards You"—Christ Is the Believer's Shield**—Ephesians 6:16: "take up the shield of faith, with which you can extinguish all the flaming darts of the evil one." Proverbs 2:7-8's "shield and protection" finds ultimate expression in Christ—not just wisdom's protection but Christ Himself as the believer's "refuge" (Heb 6:18).

**"Forgetting God's Covenant" vs. "Christ Is the Mediator of the New Covenant"**—Hebrews 8:6: "But as it is, Christ has obtained a ministry that is as much more excellent than the old as the covenant he mediates is better." The adulteress "forgets God's covenant"—but Christ established an eternal new covenant never to be forgotten. In Christ you will not "forget God's covenant"—because Christ writes the covenant on your heart (Heb 8:10).
