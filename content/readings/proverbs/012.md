## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第十二章继续用对偶格言对比义人和恶人——但这一章有一节极为独特的经文——"义人顾惜他牲畜的命"——教导我们义人的怜悯甚至延伸到动物。

**喜爱管教与恨恶责备（第1节）**

> "喜爱管教的就是喜爱知识；恨恶责备的却是畜类。"

弟兄姐妹，开门见山——"恨恶责备的却是畜类"。这话很重——但很真实。弟兄姐妹，你对责备的反应暴露了你是什么人。智慧人听到责备——即使难受——也会接受并改正。但愚蒙人听到责备——立刻愤怒、反击、拒绝。弟兄姐妹，下次有人责备你的时候——先别发火——先想想：他说的有没有道理？

**勤劳耕种与追逐虚浮（第11节）**

> "耕种自己田地的，必得饱食；追逐虚浮的，却是无知。"

弟兄姐妹，"耕种自己田地的"——不是去耕种别人的田地——而是"自己的"。弟兄姐妹，你有没有总是在羡慕别人的"田地"——别人的工作、别人的才华、别人的机会——而忽略了自己手里已经有的？"追逐虚浮的"——追逐那些看起来光鲜但没有实质的事物。弟兄姐妹，脚踏实地耕种你自己的田地——比追逐一百个虚浮的梦更有智慧。

**义人顾惜牲畜的命（第10节）**

> "义人顾惜他牲畜的命；恶人的怜悯也是残忍。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整本箴言中最独特的一节——"义人顾惜他牲畜的命"。弟兄姐妹，一个人的品格可以从他怎样对待比他弱小的生命来判断。义人对动物都有怜悯——恶人即使"怜悯"也是残忍的。弟兄姐妹，你怎样对待无法回报你的生命——宠物、流浪动物、甚至昆虫——暴露了你内心真正的品质。

"恶人的怜悯也是残忍"——弟兄姐妹，有些人表面上"帮助"你——实际上是在利用你。他们的"怜悯"有条件——他们的"善良"有目的。真正的怜悯是不求回报的——就像义人对牲畜的怜悯：动物不能报答他，但他仍然顾惜它的命。

**口舌的智慧（第13-14节、17-19节）**

> "嘴唇的过失是恶人的网罗；但义人必脱离患难。人因口所结的果子必饱得美物；人手所做的，必为自己的报应。愚妄人的道在自己眼中看为正直；惟有听劝言的是有智慧的人。"

弟兄姐妹，"愚妄人的道在自己眼中看为正直"——弟兄姐妹，这就是自欺的危险。每个人在自己眼里都觉得自己是对的。弟兄姐妹，如果你总觉得自己是对的——你可能正处于最危险的状态。因为最危险的人不是知道自己错了的人——而是以为自己永远对的人。"惟有听劝言的是有智慧的人"——智慧的人知道自己可能错了——所以他听别人的劝告。

"说话浮躁的如刀刺人；但智慧人的舌头却为医人的良药。"弟兄姐妹，你的话是"刀"还是"良药"？有些人说话不过大脑——"浮躁"——结果如刀刺人。但智慧人的话经过思考——"为医人的良药"。弟兄姐妹，下次开口之前想一想：我这句话是在刺人还是在医人？

**诚实的嘴唇（第22节）**

> "说谎言的嘴为耶和华所憎恶；行事诚实的为他所喜悦。"

弟兄姐妹，这是箴言中反复出现的主题——神憎恶谎言。弟兄姐妹，小谎言也是谎言——"善意的谎言"也是谎言。诚实不是因为方便——而是因为神喜悦诚实。

弟兄姐妹，箴言十二章教我们：接受责备是智慧的起点——耕种自己的田地好过追逐虚浮——顾惜比你弱小的生命是义人的标志——你的舌头可以是刀也可以是良药——诚实永远是唯一的选择。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 12 continues contrasting the righteous and the wicked—but this chapter contains one remarkably unique verse—"whoever is righteous has regard for the life of his beast"—teaching that the righteous person's compassion extends even to animals.

**Loving Discipline and Hating Reproof (v. 1)**

> "Whoever loves discipline loves knowledge, but he who hates reproof is stupid."

Brothers and sisters, straight to the point—"he who hates reproof is stupid." Harsh—but true. Your reaction to reproof reveals what kind of person you are. The wise hear reproof—even uncomfortably—and accept and correct. But the foolish hear reproof—and immediately rage, retaliate, refuse. Next time someone reproves you—before getting angry—consider: does their point have merit?

**Tilling Your Own Ground (v. 11)**

> "Whoever works his land will have plenty of bread, but he who follows worthless pursuits lacks sense."

Brothers and sisters, "works his own land"—not someone else's field—but "his own." Do you constantly envy others' "fields"—their jobs, talents, opportunities—while neglecting what you already have? Faithfully tilling your own field is wiser than chasing a hundred empty dreams.

**The Righteous Cares for His Animal (v. 10)**

> "Whoever is righteous has regard for the life of his beast, but the mercy of the wicked is cruel."

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs' most unique verse—"has regard for the life of his beast." A person's character can be judged by how they treat those weaker than themselves. The righteous show compassion even to animals—the wicked, even their "mercy" is cruel. How you treat lives that cannot repay you—pets, strays, even insects—reveals your heart's true quality.

**The Tongue's Wisdom (vv. 13-14, 17-19)**

> "The fool's way is right in his own eyes, but a wise man listens to advice...There is one whose rash words are like sword thrusts, but the tongue of the wise brings healing."

Brothers and sisters, "the fool's way is right in his own eyes"—self-deception's danger. Everyone thinks they're right. If you always think you're right—you may be in the most dangerous state. The most dangerous person isn't the one who knows they're wrong—but the one who thinks they're always right.

"Rash words like sword thrusts, but the tongue of the wise brings healing." Is your speech a "sword" or "medicine"? Before speaking, consider: am I stabbing or healing?

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 12 teaches: accepting reproof is wisdom's starting point—tilling your own field beats chasing emptiness—caring for weaker lives is the righteous person's mark—your tongue can be sword or medicine—honesty is always the only choice.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言12:10"义人顾惜他牲畜的命"在古代近东法律和文学中是独特的——虽然其他古代文明也有动物保护的法律（如赫梯法典对虐待动物有罚款），但将善待动物直接与"义人"的品格联系起来是以色列智慧传统的独到之处。这节经文与摩西律法中对动物的保护呼应——出23:12安息日让牛驴休息、申25:4"牛在场上踹谷的时候不可笼住它的嘴"、申22:6-7母鸟和小鸟不可一起取走。犹太传统发展出"tza'ar ba'alei chayyim"（防止动物痛苦）的律法原则，直接来自这些经文。现代动物权利运动也常引用箴言12:10作为动物伦理的圣经基础。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 12:10 "whoever is righteous has regard for the life of his beast" is unique in the ancient Near East—while other civilizations had animal protection laws (Hittite law codes penalized animal abuse), directly connecting animal care to the "righteous" person's character is distinctive to Israel's wisdom tradition. This verse echoes Mosaic law's animal protections—Exodus 23:12 Sabbath rest for ox and donkey, Deuteronomy 25:4 "you shall not muzzle an ox when it is treading grain," Deuteronomy 22:6-7 not taking a mother bird with her young. Jewish tradition developed "tza'ar ba'alei chayyim" (preventing animal suffering) as a legal principle directly from these texts. Modern animal rights movements also frequently cite Proverbs 12:10 as a biblical basis for animal ethics.

## 经文结构（中文）

**管教与知识（1-3节）**——喜爱管教=喜爱知识→善人蒙恩→义人的根不动摇。
**勤劳与虚浮（4-11节）**——才德妇人是丈夫冠冕→义人顾惜牲畜→耕种田地得饱食。
**口舌的力量（12-22节）**——嘴唇过失是网罗→浮躁如刀刺人→智慧舌头为良药→谎言嘴为耶和华憎恶。
**义人的安稳（23-28节）**——通达人隐藏知识→义人道路是生命→在其途中没有死亡。

## Structure (English)

**Discipline and Knowledge (vv. 1-3)**—Loving discipline = loving knowledge → good man finds favor → righteous root unmoved.
**Diligence and Vanity (vv. 4-11)**—Excellent wife is husband's crown → righteous cares for beast → working land gets bread.
**Power of Speech (vv. 12-22)**—Lips' transgression is snare → rash words like swords → wise tongue heals → lying lips abomination to LORD.
**The Righteous' Security (vv. 23-28)**—Prudent man conceals knowledge → righteous path is life → in its pathway there is no death.

## 神学要点（中文）

**受造物的关怀——管理的使命**
改革宗传统教导人是受造界的管家。韦敏斯德小要理问答第10问论人按神形像被造："神照着自己的形像造男造女……并赋予他们管理、支配一切受造之物的权柄。""义人顾惜他牲畜的命"（箴12:10）是管家使命的具体表现——不是剥削性的统治，而是怜悯性的管理。

**接受责备——属灵成长的必经之路**
改革宗传统教导成圣是渐进的过程，需要管教。韦敏斯德信条第13章论成圣：信徒的败坏可能一时得胜。成圣不是一次性完成的——需要持续的责备、管教和改正。"喜爱管教的就是喜爱知识"——拒绝管教就是拒绝成长。

**"耕种自己田地的"——忠心管家与呼召**
改革宗传统教导每个人有特定的呼召（calling）。你的"田地"就是神交给你的领域——你的家庭、你的工作、你的恩赐。忠心耕种"自己的田地"好过追逐别人的领域。

## Theology (English)

**Care for Creation—The Stewardship Mandate**
The Reformed tradition teaches humans are creation's stewards. WSC Q10 on humans created in God's image, given dominion (Gen 1:28). "The righteous has regard for his beast" is stewardship's concrete expression—not exploitative dominion but compassionate management.

**Accepting Reproof—Spiritual Growth's Necessary Path**
The Reformed tradition teaches sanctification is gradual, requiring discipline. Westminster Confession chapter 13. Sanctification isn't one-time—requiring ongoing reproof, discipline, correction. "Whoever loves discipline loves knowledge"—refusing discipline is refusing growth.

**"Works His Own Land"—Faithful Stewardship and Calling**
The Reformed tradition teaches each person has a specific calling. Your "land" is the domain God entrusted you—family, work, gifts. Faithfully tilling "your own field" is better than chasing others' domains.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"义人顾惜他牲畜的命"——基督关怀一切受造**——马太福音10:29"两个麻雀不是卖一分银子吗？若是你们的父不许，一个也不能掉在地上。"基督宣告天父关心每一只麻雀——多么更关心人。箴言12:10的义人怜悯在基督身上得到了最完全的体现——祂怜悯一切受造物。

**"智慧人的舌头为医人的良药"——基督的话语有医治能力**——路加福音4:22"众人都称赞他，并稀奇他口中所出的恩言。"基督的话是真正的"良药"——对病人说"你的罪赦了"（路5:20），对死人说"起来"（路7:14），对风浪说"住了吧"（可4:39）。

**"喜爱管教的就是喜爱知识"——基督顺服到死**——希伯来书5:8"他虽然为儿子，还是因所受的苦难学了顺从。"基督是喜爱管教的最高典范——祂学了顺从，以至于死在十字架上。腓立比书2:8"既有人的样子，就自己卑微，存心顺服，以至于死，且死在十字架上。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"The Righteous Cares for His Beast"—Christ Cares for All Creation**—Matthew 10:29: "Are not two sparrows sold for a penny? And not one of them will fall to the ground apart from your Father." Christ declared the Father cares for every sparrow—how much more for people. Proverbs 12:10's righteous compassion finds fullest embodiment in Christ—He has compassion on all creation.

**"The Wise Tongue Brings Healing"—Christ's Words Have Healing Power**—Luke 4:22: "all spoke well of him and marveled at the gracious words." Christ's words are true "medicine"—to the sick "And when he saw their faith, he said unto him, Man, thy sins are forgiven thee." (Luke 5:20), to the dead "arise" (Luke 7:14), to the storm "And he arose, and rebuked the wind, and said unto the sea, Peace, be still. And the wind ceased, and there was a great calm." (Mark 4:39).

**"Whoever Loves Discipline Loves Knowledge"—Christ's Obedience to Death**—Hebrews 5:8: "Although he was a son, he learned obedience through what he suffered." Christ is the supreme example of loving discipline—He learned obedience, even to death on the cross (Phil 2:8).
