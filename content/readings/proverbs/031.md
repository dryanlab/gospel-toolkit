## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，箴言第三十一章是整本箴言书的最后一章——也是最著名的一章。前半段是"利慕伊勒王的言语，是他母亲教训他的"——后半段是"才德的妇人"这首字母诗，用希伯来文二十二个字母赞美一位理想的妻子和母亲。

**利慕伊勒母亲的劝诫（第1-9节）**

> "利慕伊勒王的言语，是他母亲教训他的真言。我的儿啊，我腹中生的儿啊，我许愿得的儿啊，你当怎样呢？不要将你的精力给妇女；也不要有败坏君王的行为。利慕伊勒啊，君王喝酒，君王喝酒不相宜；王子说浓酒在哪里也不相宜。"

弟兄姐妹，一位母亲对她儿子的教训——三个"我的"反映了深切的母爱。"不要将你的精力给妇女"——不是反对婚姻，而是警告随意的情欲关系。"君王喝酒不相宜"——因为醉酒会导致不公正的判断。弟兄姐妹，权力越大、责任越大——领导者的放纵不只影响自己，还影响他治理下的人。

**才德的妇人（第10-31节）**

> "才德的妇人谁能得着呢？她的价值远胜过珍珠。"

弟兄姐妹，这首诗不是在描述一个超级女人让你望尘莫及——而是在赞美一种品格的美。"价值远胜过珍珠"——弟兄姐妹，外表的美是暂时的——品格的美是永久的。第30节明确说了："艳丽是虚假的，美容是虚浮的；惟敬畏耶和华的妇女必得称赞。"弟兄姐妹，最终衡量一个人（男人或女人）价值的不是外表——而是"敬畏耶和华"。

"她丈夫心里倚靠她，必不缺少利益。她一生使丈夫有益而无损。"弟兄姐妹，"倚靠"——一个丈夫能倚靠他的妻子——这不是妻子的软弱，而是妻子的力量。弟兄姐妹，被信任——被倚靠——是最大的称赞之一。

"她寻找羊绒和麻，甘心用手做工。她好像商船从远方运粮来。未到黎明她就起来，把食物分给家中的人，把当做的工分给使女。"弟兄姐妹，这位妇人是勤劳的——甘心做工、天未亮就起来。弟兄姐妹，注意"甘心"——不是被迫的——而是"甘心"的。她的勤劳来自内在的动力，不是外在的压迫。

"她想得田地就买来；用手所得之利栽种葡萄园。"弟兄姐妹，她有商业头脑——买田地、种葡萄园。弟兄姐妹，这首诗打破了"贤妻只管家务"的刻板印象——这位妇人也是商人、投资者、企业家。才德的妇人不是被局限在厨房里——而是在家庭和社会中发挥全面的影响力。

"她张手周济困苦人，伸手帮补穷乏人。"弟兄姐妹，她不是只管自己的家——她关心社区——周济困苦人、帮补穷乏人。弟兄姐妹，真正的才德不是关起门来经营好自己的小世界——而是打开门去祝福更大的世界。

"她开口就发智慧；她舌上有仁慈的法则。她观察家务，并不吃闲饭。"弟兄姐妹，"舌上有仁慈的法则"——她说话有智慧、有仁慈——她的口是"生命的泉源"。

**最终的赞美（第28-31节）**

> "她的儿女起来称她有福；她的丈夫也称赞她说：才德的女子很多，惟独你超过一切。艳丽是虚假的，美容是虚浮的；惟敬畏耶和华的妇女必得称赞。愿她享受操作所得的；愿她的工作在城门口荣耀她。"

弟兄姐妹，"她的儿女起来称她有福"——弟兄姐妹，这是一位母亲能得到的最美好的回报——你的孩子长大后站起来说："妈妈，你是有福的。"弟兄姐妹，这比任何奖杯、任何头衔、任何金钱都更有价值。

弟兄姐妹，箴言书以这首"才德的妇人"结尾是有深意的——箴言从"敬畏耶和华是知识的开端"开始（1:7），以"惟敬畏耶和华的妇女必得称赞"结束（31:30）。整本箴言书首尾呼应——一切始于敬畏，一切终于敬畏。而"才德的妇人"是敬畏耶和华在日常生活中的最美表达——不是在圣殿里、不是在祭坛上、而是在家庭里、在市场上、在邻里间。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 31 is Proverbs' final and most famous chapter. The first half is "the words of King Lemuel...an oracle that his mother taught him"—the second half is the "woman of noble character" acrostic poem, using Hebrew's twenty-two letters to praise an ideal wife and mother.

**Lemuel's Mother's Counsel (vv. 1-9)**

> "The words of King Lemuel. An oracle that his mother taught him: What are you doing, my son?...Do not give your strength to women...It is not for kings, O Lemuel, it is not for kings to drink wine."

Brothers and sisters, a mother's instruction to her son—three "my" reflecting deep maternal love. "Don't give your strength to women"—not opposing marriage, but warning against casual sensual relationships. Greater power means greater responsibility—a leader's indulgence affects not just himself but those he governs.

**The Woman of Noble Character (vv. 10-31)**

> "An excellent wife who can find? She is far more precious than jewels."

Brothers and sisters, this poem doesn't describe a superwoman making you feel inadequate—it celebrates a kind of character beauty. "Far more precious than jewels"—external beauty is temporary; character beauty is permanent. Verse 30 states clearly: "Charm is deceptive, and beauty is fleeting; but a woman who fears the LORD is to be praised."

"The heart of her husband trusts in her"—a husband can rely on his wife—not wife's weakness but wife's strength. Being trusted—being relied upon—is one of the highest compliments.

"She seeks wool and flax, and works with willing hands. She is like the merchant ships; she brings her food from afar." Brothers and sisters, "willing hands"—not forced—but "willing." Her diligence comes from internal drive, not external pressure.

"She considers a field and buys it; with the fruit of her hands she plants a vineyard." Brothers and sisters, she has business acumen—buying fields, planting vineyards. This poem shatters the stereotype of "virtuous wife only does housework"—this woman is also merchant, investor, entrepreneur.

"She opens her hand to the poor and reaches out her hands to the needy." Not only managing her home—she cares for community—helping the poor and needy. True virtue doesn't close the door to manage one's small world—it opens the door to bless the larger world.

**Final Praise (vv. 28-31)**

> "Her children rise up and call her blessed; her husband also, and he praises her: 'Many women have done excellently, but you surpass them all.' Charm is deceptive, and beauty is fleeting, but a woman who fears the LORD is to be praised."

Brothers and sisters, "her children rise up and call her blessed"—the most beautiful reward a mother can receive—your grown children standing and saying: "Mom, you are blessed." Worth more than any trophy, title, or money.

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs ending with this "woman of noble character" is deeply intentional—Proverbs begins "the fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" (1:7) and ends "a woman who fears the LORD is to be praised" (31:30). The entire book forms an inclusio—everything begins with fear, everything ends with fear. The "woman of noble character" is the fear of the LORD's most beautiful expression in daily life—not in the temple, not at the altar, but in the home, the marketplace, the neighborhood.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言31:10-31的"才德的妇人"诗（Eshet Chayil，字面意思是"勇力的女人"）是一首字母诗（acrostic poem）——希伯来文22个字母，每个字母开头一节，共22节。"利慕伊勒王"的身份不明——犹太传统认为"利慕伊勒"是所罗门的另一个名字，他母亲拔示巴教训他。"chayil"这个词通常用来描述军事上的勇力（如"大能的勇士"），用在妇人身上是非常有力的——暗示这位妇人的品格具有战士般的力量。在犹太传统中，每个安息日晚上丈夫要对妻子唱诵这首诗——作为对妻子的赞美。这首诗也被用于犹太葬礼——在棺材旁诵读以纪念一位"才德的妇人"。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 31:10-31's "woman of noble character" poem (Eshet Chayil, literally "woman of valor/strength") is an acrostic poem—22 Hebrew letters, each beginning one verse, totaling 22 verses. "King Lemuel's" identity is unknown—Jewish tradition considers "Lemuel" another name for Solomon, taught by his mother Bathsheba. The word "chayil" usually describes military valor ("mighty warrior"); applied to a woman, it is very powerful—implying this woman's character has warrior-like strength. In Jewish tradition, every Sabbath evening husbands recite this poem to their wives—as praise. The poem is also used at Jewish funerals—recited beside the coffin to memorialize a "woman of noble character."

## 神学要点（中文）

**"惟敬畏耶和华的妇女必得称赞"——箴言的首尾呼应**
改革宗传统教导敬畏耶和华是智慧生活的全部内容。韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问："（一）荣耀神；（二）以祂为乐，直到永远。"箴言从1:7"敬畏耶和华是知识的开端"到31:30"惟敬畏耶和华的妇女必得称赞"形成了完美的首尾呼应。一切的智慧始于敬畏——一切的美德归于敬畏。

**"才德的妇人"——恩典在日常生活中的表达**
改革宗传统教导成圣是在日常生活中活出的。韦敏斯德信条第13章论成圣。这位妇人的"才德"不是在特殊场合展示的——而是在日常的做工、买卖、管理、周济中表达的。这是改革宗传统所说的"天职"（vocation）的完美典范——日常工作就是服事神。

**"chayil"——品格的力量**
希伯来文"chayil"通常指军事上的勇力。用在妇人身上表明：品格需要力量——善良需要刚强——忠心需要坚持。这不是软弱的美德——而是"勇力"的品格。改革宗传统教导信心需要坚忍（韦敏斯德信条第17章论圣徒的坚忍）。

## Theology (English)

**"A Woman Who Fears the LORD Is to Be Praised"—Proverbs' Inclusio**
The Reformed tradition teaches fearing the LORD is the entirety of wise living. WSC Q1. Proverbs forms a perfect inclusio from 1:7 "fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" to 31:30 "a woman who fears the LORD is to be praised." All wisdom begins with fear—all virtue returns to fear.

**"Woman of Noble Character"—Grace Expressed in Daily Life**
The Reformed tradition teaches sanctification is lived out daily. Westminster Confession chapter 13. This woman's: noble character is not displayed on special occasions—but expressed in daily work, commerce, management, charity. The perfect example of Reformed "vocation"—daily work is serving God.

**"Chayil"—Character's Strength**
Hebrew "chayil" usually means military valor. Applied to a woman it shows: character requires strength—goodness requires courage—faithfulness requires perseverance. Not weak virtue—but "valorous" character. The Reformed tradition teaches faith requires perseverance (Westminster Confession chapter 17).

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"才德的妇人"——教会是基督的新妇**——以弗所书5:25-27"你们作丈夫的，要爱你们的妻子，正如基督爱教会，为教会舍己……要使教会成为圣洁……毫无瑕疵。"箴言31的"才德妇人"在新约的类比中就是教会——基督的新妇——她的"才德"来自基督的爱和圣灵的洁净。

**"她丈夫心里倚靠她"——基督喜悦教会**——以弗所书5:29-30"从来没有人恨恶自己的身子，总是保养顾惜……因我们是他身上的肢体。"基督对教会（祂的新妇）的爱——就像那丈夫对才德妇人的倚靠和赞美。

**"艳丽是虚假美容是虚浮"——基督看重内心**——撒母耳记上16:7"人是看外貌，耶和华是看内心。"彼得前书3:3-4"不要以外面的辫头发、戴金饰、穿美衣为妆饰，只要以里面存着长久温柔安静的心为妆饰；这在神面前是极宝贵的。"箴言31:30的优先级——内在的敬畏超越外在的美丽——在新约中被完全确认。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Woman of Noble Character"—The Church Is Christ's Bride**—Ephesians 5:25-27: "Husbands, love your wives, as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her...that she might be holy...without blemish." Proverbs 31's "woman of noble character" in NT analogy is the church—Christ's bride—whose "noble character" comes from Christ's love and the Spirit's cleansing.

**"Her Husband Trusts Her"—Christ Delights in the Church**—Ephesians 5:29-30: "no one ever hated his own flesh, but nourishes and cherishes it...because we are members of his body." Christ's love for the church (His bride)—like that husband's trust and praise for the noble woman.

**"Charm Is Deceptive, Beauty Fleeting"—Christ Values the Heart**—1 Samuel 16:7: "man looks on the outward appearance, but the LORD looks on the heart." 1 Peter 3:3-4: "Do not let your adorning be external...but let your adorning be the hidden person of the heart." Proverbs 31:30's priority—inward fear surpassing outward beauty—is fully confirmed in the NT.
