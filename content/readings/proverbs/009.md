## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第九章是箴言前九章"智慧序言"的结尾——也是整个序言最戏剧性的一章。这里有两场宴席——智慧的筵席和愚昧的筵席——你必须选一桌坐下。

**智慧的筵席（第1-6节）**

> "智慧建造房屋，凿成七根柱子，宰杀牲畜，调和旨酒，设摆筵席，打发使女出去，自己在城中至高处呼叫，说：谁是愚蒙人，可以转到这里来！又对那无知的人说：你们来，吃我的饼，喝我调和的酒。你们愚蒙人，要舍弃愚蒙，就得存活，并要走光明的道。"

弟兄姐妹，"建造房屋凿成七根柱子"——七根柱子代表完全和稳固。智慧不是住在茅草屋里——她有一座"七根柱子"的宏伟殿堂。弟兄姐妹，智慧的根基是坚固的——不是今天站立明天倒塌的流行观点。

"宰杀牲畜调和旨酒设摆筵席"——弟兄姐妹，智慧不是给你干巴巴的教条——她摆了一桌丰盛的筵席。弟兄姐妹，追求智慧不是苦修——而是享受盛宴。诗篇34:8"你们要尝尝主恩的滋味，便知道他是美善。"智慧的道路是丰盛的、是有味道的、是令人满足的。

> "谁是愚蒙人可以转到这里来"

智慧邀请的对象是"愚蒙人"！不是聪明人——而是愚蒙人。弟兄姐妹，你承认自己是愚蒙的吗？只有承认自己愚蒙的人才能来到智慧的筵席。马太福音9:12"康健的人用不着医生，有病的人才用得着。"

**智慧的忠告（第7-12节）**

> "指斥亵慢人的，必被辱骂；责备恶人的，必被玷污。不要责备亵慢人，恐怕他恨你；要责备智慧人，他必爱你。教导智慧人，他就越发有智慧；指示义人，他就增长学问。"

弟兄姐妹，这是非常实际的人际关系智慧——"不要责备亵慢人"。为什么？因为他不会接受——他只会恨你。弟兄姐妹，这不是说"放弃恶人"——而是说"分辨时机和对象"。你的责备是宝贵的——不要浪费在不愿意听的人身上。把你的精力用在那些愿意被教导的人身上——"教导智慧人他就越发有智慧"。

"敬畏耶和华是智慧的开端；认识至圣者便是聪明。"弟兄姐妹，第10节再次回到箴言的主题——和1:7一样——形成首尾呼应。整个序言从"敬畏耶和华"开始，又以"敬畏耶和华"结束。弟兄姐妹，这不是重复——这是强调：一切智慧的根基就在这里——敬畏耶和华。

**愚昧的筵席（第13-18节）**

> "愚昧的妇人喧嚷；她是愚蒙，一无所知。她坐在自己的家门口，坐在城中高处的座位上，呼叫过路的，就是直行其道的人，说：谁是愚蒙人，可以转到这里来！又对那无知的人说：偷来的水是甜的，暗吃的饼是好的。人却不知有阴魂在她那里，她的客在阴间的深处。"

弟兄姐妹，愚昧也在同样的地方——"城中高处"——发出同样的呼唤——"谁是愚蒙人可以转到这里来"。弟兄姐妹，两桌筵席用同样的话邀请你！区别不在于邀请——而在于结局。

"偷来的水是甜的暗吃的饼是好的"——弟兄姐妹，罪的诱惑力在于"偷"和"暗"——禁忌的东西格外吸引人。弟兄姐妹，你有没有经历过这个——明知不应该做的事反而格外有吸引力？这就是愚昧的筵席——用"偷来的甜"引诱你。

但最后一节揭露了真相——"人却不知有阴魂在她那里，她的客在阴间的深处。"弟兄姐妹，那些坐在愚昧桌前的客人——已经在阴间了。他们只看到了眼前的"甜"——没有看到桌子底下的深渊。

弟兄姐妹，箴言前九章就这样结束了——两桌筵席摆在你面前。智慧的筵席：七根柱子、丰盛的食物、光明的道路、生命。愚昧的筵席：偷来的水、暗吃的饼、阴间的深处、死亡。你选哪一桌？

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 9 is the finale of Proverbs' first nine chapters—the "wisdom prologue"—and its most dramatic chapter. Two banquets are set—wisdom's feast and folly's feast—and you must choose which table to sit at.

**Wisdom's Banquet (vv. 1-6)**

"Wisdom has built her house; she has hewn her seven pillars. She has slaughtered her beasts; she has mixed her wine; she has also set her table. She has sent out her young women to call from the highest places in the town, 'Whoever is simple, let him turn in here!' To him who lacks sense she says, 'Come, eat of my bread and drink of the wine I have mixed. Leave your simple ways, and live, and walk in the way of insight.'"

Brothers and sisters, "seven pillars"—representing completeness and stability. Wisdom doesn't live in a straw hut—she has a magnificent hall with seven pillars. "Slaughtered beasts, mixed wine, set table"—wisdom doesn't offer dry doctrine—she sets a lavish feast. Pursuing wisdom is not asceticism—but enjoying a banquet. Psalm 34:8: "Oh, taste and see that the LORD is good!"

"Whoever is simple"—wisdom's invitation targets the "simple"! Not the clever—but the simple. Do you admit you're simple? Only those who admit their simplicity can come to wisdom's feast.

**Wisdom's Counsel (vv. 7-12)**

> "Do not reprove a scoffer, or he will hate you; reprove a wise man, and he will love you. Give instruction to a wise man, and he will be still wiser; teach a righteous man, and he will increase in learning."

Brothers and sisters, practical relational wisdom—"don't reprove a scoffer." Not "abandon the wicked"—but "discern timing and audience." Your reproof is precious—don't waste it on the unwilling.

> "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom, and the knowledge of the Holy One is insight." Verse 10 returns to Proverbs' theme—echoing 1:7—forming an inclusio. The entire prologue begins with "fear of the LORD" and ends with "fear of the LORD."

**Folly's Banquet (vv. 13-18)**

> "The woman Folly is loud; she is seductive and knows nothing. She sits at the door of her house; she takes a seat on the highest places of the town, calling to those who pass by...'Stolen water is sweet, and bread eaten in secret is pleasant.' But he does not know that the dead are there, that her guests are in the depths of Sheol."

Brothers and sisters, folly sits in the same place—"highest places"—issuing the same call—"whoever is simple, turn in here." Two tables with the same invitation! The difference isn't in the invitation—but in the destination.

"Stolen water is sweet"—sin's appeal lies in "stolen" and "secret"—forbidden things are especially attractive. But the final verse exposes the truth—"the dead are there, her guests are in the depths of Sheol." Those sitting at folly's table—are already in Sheol. They see the immediate "sweetness"—but not the abyss under the table.

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs' first nine chapters end like this—two banquet tables before you. Wisdom's: seven pillars, lavish food, the path of light, life. Folly's: stolen water, secret bread, depths of Sheol, death. Which table do you choose?

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言第九章是所谓的"智慧序言"（箴1-9章）的最终章。整个序言以两位女性的对比达到高潮——"智慧女士"（Lady Wisdom）和"愚昧女人"（Woman Folly）——这是古代近东文学中常见的拟人化手法。"七根柱子"在学术界有多种解释：有人认为指七天创造、有人认为指七种智慧美德、有人认为指古代房屋的实际建筑结构（大厅两排各三根柱子加一根中心柱）。犹太传统（米大示）将"七根柱子"解释为摩西五经加先知书和圣文集（Torah + Nevi'im + Ketuvim）——即整本圣经。第10节"敬畏耶和华是智慧的开端"与1:7"敬畏耶和华是知识的开端"形成完美的首尾呼应（inclusio），标志着序言的完结。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 9 is the final chapter of the "wisdom prologue" (Prov 1-9). The entire prologue climaxes with two women's contrast—"Lady Wisdom" and "Woman Folly"—a common personification in ancient Near Eastern literature. "Seven pillars" has multiple scholarly interpretations: seven days of creation, seven wisdom virtues, or actual architectural structure (three pillars on each side plus one central). Jewish tradition (Midrash) interprets "seven pillars" as Torah + Prophets + Writings—the entire Bible. Verse 10 "fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom" with 1:7 "fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" forms a perfect inclusio marking the prologue's completion.

## 神学要点（中文）

**两条道路的选择——人的责任**
改革宗传统教导神的预定和人的责任并行。韦敏斯德信条第3章论预定与第9章论意志自由。箴言9章摆出两桌筵席让人"选择"——这是真实的道德选择。弟兄姐妹，虽然得救完全出于神的恩典，但人面对智慧和愚昧时的"选择"是真实的责任。

**"敬畏耶和华是智慧的开端"——首尾呼应的核心教义**
韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问"（一）荣耀神；（二）以祂为乐，直到永远"。敬畏不是恐惧——而是以神为中心的生活态度。在智慧的序言中两次出现（1:7和9:10）——说明这是箴言神学的绝对核心。

**智慧的丰盛——恩典不是苦修**
改革宗传统教导恩典带来喜乐。韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问的"以祂为乐"——智慧的筵席是丰盛的、是美好的。信仰不是苦行——而是"尝尝主恩的滋味便知道他是美善"（诗34:8）。

## Theology (English)

**Two Paths—Human Responsibility**
The Reformed tradition teaches divine predestination and human responsibility run parallel. Westminster Confession chapters 3 and 9. Proverbs 9 sets two tables for people to "choose"—real moral choice. Though salvation is entirely by grace, the "choice" between wisdom and folly is genuine responsibility.

**"Fear of the LORD Is Wisdom's Beginning"—The Core Doctrine**
Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "Man's chief end is to glorify God, and to enjoy him forever." Fear is not terror—but a God-centered life orientation. Appearing twice in the prologue (1:7 and 9:10)—this is Proverbs' theological absolute center.

**Wisdom's Abundance—Grace Is Not Asceticism**
The Reformed tradition teaches grace brings joy. WSC Q1's "enjoy him forever"—wisdom's banquet is lavish, beautiful. Faith is not austerity—but "O taste and see that the LORD is good: blessed is the man that trusteth in him." (Ps 34:8).

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督是智慧的筵席**——马太福音22:1-14天国好像一个王为儿子摆设筵席——基督就是那摆筵席的王子。约翰福音6:35"我就是生命的粮；到我这里来的必定不饿。"智慧的"饼"和"酒"在基督里成为了圣餐——"这是我的身体为你们舍的……这杯是用我血所立的新约"（林前11:24-25）。

**"七根柱子"——基督建造教会**——马太福音16:18"我要把我的教会建造在这磐石上。"智慧"建造房屋"——基督建造教会。以弗所书2:20-22"建造在使徒和先知的根基上，有基督耶稣自己为房角石。"

**"偷来的水是甜的"——基督对罪的警告**——马太福音7:13-14"引到灭亡，那门是宽的，路是大的，进去的人也多。引到永生，那门是窄的，路是小的，找着的人也少。"愚昧的"宽门"看似甜蜜——实际通向阴间。基督的"窄门"看似困难——实际通向永生。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Is Wisdom's Banquet**—Matthew 22:1-14 the kingdom is like a king giving a wedding feast for his son—Christ is that prince. John 6:35: "I am the bread of life; whoever comes to me shall not hunger." Wisdom's "bread" and "wine" become the Lord's Supper in Christ—"And when he had given thanks, he brake it, and said, Take, eat: this is my body, which is broken for you: this do in remembrance of me. After the same manner also he took the cup, when he had supped, saying, This cup is the new testament in my blood: this do ye, as oft as ye drink it, in remembrance of me." (1 Cor 11:24-25).

**"Seven Pillars"—Christ Builds the Church**—Matthew 16:18: "I will build my church on this rock." Wisdom "built her house"—Christ builds His church. Ephesians 2:20-22: "built on the foundation of the apostles and prophets, Christ Jesus himself being the cornerstone."

**"Stolen Water Is Sweet"—Christ's Warning About Sin**—Matthew 7:13-14: "the gate is wide and the way is easy that leads to destruction...the gate is narrow and the way is hard that leads to life." Folly's "wide gate" seems sweet—but leads to Sheol. Christ's "narrow gate" seems hard—but leads to eternal life.
