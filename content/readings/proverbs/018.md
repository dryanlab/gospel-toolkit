## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。箴言第十八章包含了若干箴言中最深刻的格言，特别是那一句令无数人在风暴中得到安慰的话——"耶和华的名是坚固台，义人奔入便得安全。"

**独自疏隔的危险（第1节）**

> "与人疏隔的，都随自己的心意；他反抗一切真智慧。"

弟兄姐妹，"与人疏隔"——与团体脱离、与弟兄姐妹隔绝——是危险的。弟兄姐妹，孤立的人最容易走进误区——因为他没有其他声音来挑战他的想法。"随自己的心意"——听起来像自由，但箴言说这是"反抗一切真智慧"。弟兄姐妹，如果你正在把自己与教会、与弟兄姐妹疏隔开来——这是一个危险的信号。

**智慧话语的力量（第4节）**

> "人口中的话如同深水；智慧的泉源好像流动的河水。"

弟兄姐妹，"深水"——有些话表面看起来简单——但它的深度是无底的。你有没有听过一句话、多年后才真正明白它的意思？那就是"深水"。智慧人的话——需要你潜下去才能取到它的精华。

**耶和华的名是坚固台（第10节）**

> "耶和华的名是坚固台；义人奔入便得安全。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整本箴言中最震撼人心的格言之一。"坚固台"——弟兄姐妹，古代战争中，坚固台是最后的避难所。当城市被攻破，剩余的人奔入坚固台，敌人就很难攻入了。弟兄姐妹，"耶和华的名"就是那座坚固台。

"义人奔入便得安全"——弟兄姐妹，两个关键词："奔入"和"得安全"。不是慢慢走过去——而是"奔入"——像逃命一样奔向神。弟兄姐妹，当危险来临的时候你的第一反应是什么？是打电话给朋友？刷社交媒体？还是"奔入"耶和华的名？

"便得安全"——弟兄姐妹，不是"可能得安全"——不是"如果运气好就得安全"——而是"便得安全"。进入坚固台就是安全——没有条件、没有例外。弟兄姐妹，这个安全不是说你不会有苦难——而是说你在苦难中有不能被动摇的保护。

**心灵的伤（第14节）**

> "人有疾病，心能忍耐；心灵忧伤，谁能承当呢？"

弟兄姐妹，"心灵忧伤谁能承当"——弟兄姐妹，身体的病可以忍耐——但"心灵的忧伤"是不同级别的痛苦。弟兄姐妹，你有没有经历过那种内心的空洞——比任何身体的痛都难受？那就是"心灵忧伤"。弟兄姐妹，这是为什么我们需要那座"坚固台"——身体的医生可以治疗身体——但"耶和华的名"才是心灵忧伤的真正医治。

**先说后听（第13节）**

> "未曾听完先回答的，便是他的愚昧和羞耻。"

弟兄姐妹，"未曾听完先回答"——我们太熟悉这个了。对方话还没说完——你心里已经在想回答了。弟兄姐妹，真正的倾听是让对方把话说完——不打断、不急于回应——然后才回答。这不只是礼貌——而是智慧。你急于回答的时候——你可能错过了对方话语中最重要的部分。

**朋友的真义（第24节）**

> "滥交朋友的，自取败坏；但有一朋友比兄弟更亲密。"

弟兄姐妹，"滥交朋友的自取败坏"——弟兄姐妹，朋友不是越多越好。弟兄姐妹，那种表面热闹、实际上遇到事情就消失的"朋友"——不如没有。"但有一朋友比兄弟更亲密"——这是一句关于珍贵友谊的格言。弟兄姐妹，你有没有那种"比兄弟更亲密"的朋友？不是在社交媒体上点赞的——而是在你最黑暗的时刻与你同在的？弟兄姐妹，这种朋友是神赐的礼物——要珍惜。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. Proverbs 18 contains some of Proverbs' most profound sayings, especially that one verse comforting countless people in storms—"The name of the LORD is a strong tower; the righteous man runs into it and is safe."

**Danger of Isolation (v. 1)**

> "Whoever isolates himself seeks his own desire; he breaks out against all sound judgment."

Brothers and sisters, "isolates himself"—withdrawing from community, separating from brothers and sisters—is dangerous. The isolated person most easily goes astray—no other voices to challenge their thinking. "Seeks his own desire"—sounds like freedom, but Proverbs says this is "breaking against all sound judgment."

**The LORD's Name Is a Strong Tower (v. 10)**

> "The name of the LORD is a strong tower; the righteous man runs into it and is safe."

Brothers and sisters, "strong tower"—in ancient warfare, the strong tower was the final refuge. When a city fell, survivors ran into the strong tower; enemies found it nearly impossible to break in. "The name of the LORD" is that strong tower.

"The righteous man runs into it and is safe"—two key words: "runs into" and "is safe." Not slowly walking over—but "running"—fleeing for life toward God. When danger comes, what's your first reaction? Call a friend? Scroll social media? Or "run into" the LORD's name?

"Is safe"—not "might be safe," not "safe if lucky"—but "is safe." Entering the strong tower means safety—no conditions, no exceptions. This safety isn't absence of trouble—but unshakeable protection in trouble.

**The Wounded Spirit (v. 14)**

> "A man's spirit will endure sickness, but a crushed spirit who can bear?"

Brothers and sisters, "who can bear a crushed spirit"—physical illness can be endured—but the "crushed spirit" is a different level of pain. Have you experienced that inner emptiness—more painful than any physical pain? That is the "crushed spirit." This is why we need that "strong tower"—physical doctors treat the body—but "the name of the LORD" is the true healing for the crushed spirit.

**Listen Before Answering (v. 13)**

> "If one gives an answer before he hears, it is his folly and shame."

Brothers and sisters, "answers before hearing"—too familiar. The other person isn't finished speaking—and you're already formulating your response. True listening lets them finish—no interrupting, no rushing to respond—then answer. When you rush to answer—you may miss the most important part of what they're saying.

**True Friendship (v. 24)**

> "A man of many companions may come to ruin, but there is a friend who sticks closer than a brother."

Brothers and sisters, "many companions may come to ruin"—friends aren't better the more there are. Surface-level, disappear-when-needed "friends"—better not to have them. "A friend who sticks closer than a brother"—a saying about precious friendship. Do you have such a friend? Not social-media-likes—but present in your darkest moment? Such a friend is God's gift—treasure them.

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言18:10"耶和华的名是坚固台"——"坚固台"（migdal-oz，字面意思是"力量的塔"）在古代以色列是常见的军事防御设施，特别指城墙内部的高塔，敌军攻破城门后守军可以退守其中。这个意象在圣经中多次出现（参诗篇61:3"我要住在你的帐幕中，直到永远；我要投靠在你翅膀的荫下"）。第24节"有一朋友比兄弟更亲密"（有KJV版本译为"a friend who sticks closer than a brother"）是基督教历史上被用来描述基督与信徒关系的经典经文。约翰加尔文在注释此节时说：没有任何人类友谊能真正实现这个描述——只有基督与信徒的关系才是真正的"比兄弟更亲密"。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 18:10's "strong tower" (migdal-oz, literally "tower of strength") was a common military defense structure in ancient Israel—specifically the high tower inside city walls where defenders could retreat after enemies broke through the gates. Verse 24 "a friend who sticks closer than a brother" (KJV) is a classic text used throughout church history to describe Christ's relationship with believers. Calvin in his commentary stated: no human friendship can truly fulfill this description—only Christ's relationship with believers is truly "closer than a brother."

## 经文结构（中文）

**孤立与智慧（1-5节）**——疏隔者随自己心意→恶人口中骄傲→义人的口是生命泉源。
**恶人的辩护（6-12节）**——愚妄人嘴唇争竞→耶和华的名是坚固台→义人奔入便得安全。
**先听后说（13-17节）**——未听完先回答是愚昧→心灵忧伤谁能承当→抽签解决争端。
**口与舌（20-24节）**——人口腹中的果子必饱足→舌头有死生之权→有一朋友比兄弟亲密。

## Structure (English)

**Isolation and Wisdom (vv. 1-5)**—Isolated seeks own desire → wicked's mouth proud → righteous mouth a fountain of life.
**The Wicked's Defense (vv. 6-12)**—Fool's lips quarrel → LORD's name is strong tower → righteous run in and are safe.
**Listen Before Speaking (vv. 13-17)**—Answering before hearing is folly → crushed spirit who can bear → casting lots settles disputes.
**Mouth and Tongue (vv. 20-24)**—Fruits of mouth satisfy → tongue has power of life and death → friend closer than a brother.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"耶和华的名"——神的启示性名字**
改革宗传统教导神的名字表达了祂的属性和本质。韦敏斯德大要理问答第112问论第三诫：第三诫要求我们用圣洁和敬畏使用神的名字。"耶和华的名是坚固台"——不只是一个称呼——而是神整个属性和能力的凝聚。信靠"耶和华的名"就是信靠祂整个的存在和承诺。

**"与人疏隔"——团体的神学**
改革宗传统教导教会团体对信仰成长的必要性。韦敏斯德信条第25章论教会。"疏隔者随自己心意"是教会危机的起点。加尔文说："神是我们的父，教会就是我们的母。"没有团体的信仰是孤立的、容易偏离的。

**"舌头有死生之权"**——对应改革宗对语言神学的重视。韦敏斯德大要理问答第145问。言语是神照自己形像赋予人的能力——神用话语创造，人用话语影响——因此"死生之权"是真实的神学陈述。

## Theology (English)

**"The Name of the LORD"—God's Revelatory Name**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's name expresses His character and essence. WLC Q112 on the third commandment. "The name of the LORD is a strong tower"—not just a label—but the condensation of God's entire character and power. Trusting "the LORD's name" is trusting His entire being and promises.

**"Isolation"—Theology of Community**
The Reformed tradition teaches church community is necessary for faith growth. Westminster Confession chapter 25.: The isolated man seeks his own desire is the starting point of church crisis. Calvin: "God is our Father, the Church is our mother." Faith without community is isolated and easily drifts.

**"Tongue Has Power of Life and Death"**—Corresponds to Reformed emphasis on theology of language. WLC Q145. Speech is God's God-image gift to humanity—God created with words, humans influence with words—therefore "power of life and death" is a genuine theological statement.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"耶和华的名是坚固台"——基督是我们的坚固台**——希伯来书6:18"我们……好像逃往避难所持定摆在我们前头指望的人可以大得勉励。"约翰福音10:9"我就是门；凡从我进来的，必然得救。"箴言18:10的"奔入坚固台"在基督里找到了终极实现——基督是最后的避难所，进入祂里面的必得安全。

**"有一朋友比兄弟更亲密"——基督是最亲密的朋友**——约翰福音15:13-15"人为朋友舍命，人的爱心没有比这个更大的。你们若遵行我所吩咐的，就是我的朋友了……我乃称你们为朋友。"基督不只是"比兄弟更亲密的朋友"——祂为朋友舍命，这是爱的最高表达。

**"心灵忧伤谁能承当"——基督担当了我们的忧伤**——以赛亚书53:4"他诚然担当我们的忧患，背负我们的痛苦。"马太福音11:28"凡劳苦担重担的人可以到我这里来，我就使你们得安息。"箴言18:14问"心灵忧伤谁能承当"——新约的回答是：基督担当了。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"The LORD's Name Is a Strong Tower"—Christ Is Our Strong Tower**—Hebrews 6:18: "we who have fled for refuge might have strong encouragement." John 10:9: "I am the door. If anyone enters by me, he will be saved." Proverbs 18:10's "running into the strong tower" finds ultimate realization in Christ—Christ is the final refuge; those entering Him will be safe.

**"A Friend Closer Than a Brother"—Christ Is the Closest Friend**—John 15:13-15: "Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends...I have called you friends." Christ is not merely "a friend closer than a brother"—He laid down His life for friends, love's highest expression.

**"A Crushed Spirit Who Can Bear"—Christ Bore Our Sorrows**—Isaiah 53:4: "Surely he has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows." Matthew 11:28: "Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest." Proverbs 18:14 asks "who can bear a crushed spirit"—the NT's answer: Christ bore it.
