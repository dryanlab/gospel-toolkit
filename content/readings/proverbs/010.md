## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是所罗门。从箴言第十章开始，进入了箴言书的第二大段——这不再是连续的讲论，而是一句一句独立的格言。每一句都是一颗宝石——需要你慢慢品味。第十章的核心主题是义人和恶人的对比。

**义人之子与恶人之子（第1节）**

> "所罗门的箴言。智慧之子使父亲欢乐；愚昧之子叫母亲担忧。"

弟兄姐妹，整个第二段以这句话开头——因为这是最根本的人际关系。你的智慧或愚昧最先影响的不是你自己——而是你的父母。弟兄姐妹，你的母亲有没有因为你的选择而"担忧"？你的父亲有没有因为你的作为而"欢乐"？你以为你的人生是你自己的——但你的每一个选择都在影响爱你的人。

**勤劳与懒惰（第4-5节）**

> "手懒的，要受贫穷；手勤的，却要富足。夏天聚敛的是智慧之子；收割时沉睡的是贻羞之子。"

弟兄姐妹，"手懒的要受贫穷"——这不是绝对的财富神学——而是一般性的人生智慧。勤劳不保证你成为富翁——但懒惰几乎保证你受贫穷。弟兄姐妹，"夏天聚敛"——在该工作的时候工作，在该预备的时候预备。"收割时沉睡"——机会来了你却在睡觉。弟兄姐妹，多少人错过了人生的机会——不是因为没有机会——而是因为机会来的时候他们在"沉睡"。

**口舌的力量（第11-14节）**

> "义人的口是生命的泉源；恶人的口藏着强暴。恨，能挑启争端；爱，能遮掩一切过错。明哲人嘴里有智慧；无知人背上受刑杖。智慧人积存知识；愚妄人的口速致败坏。"

弟兄姐妹，"义人的口是生命的泉源"——你的话可以给人生命。一句安慰的话可以拯救一个绝望的人——一句鼓励的话可以改变一个人的一生。弟兄姐妹，你的嘴巴是"生命的泉源"还是"败坏的根源"？

"爱能遮掩一切过错"——弟兄姐妹，彼得前书4:8引用了这句话："最重要的是彼此切实相爱，因为爱能遮掩许多的罪。"不是说假装看不见——而是说爱使你选择饶恕而不是揭露、选择遮盖而不是散布。

**义人的根基（第25节）**

> "暴风一过，恶人归于无有；义人的根基却是永久。"

弟兄姐妹，这是第十章最有力的意象之一——暴风来了——恶人被吹走了——义人还站着。为什么？因为义人有"根基"。弟兄姐妹，耶稣在登山宝训的结尾用了同样的意象——聪明人把房子建在磐石上，雨淋、水冲、风吹——房子不倒（太7:24-25）。弟兄姐妹，你的人生建在什么上面？如果建在金钱上——金融风暴一来就倒了。如果建在名声上——一个丑闻就倒了。但如果建在义的根基上——"暴风一过"你还在那里。

**义人的盼望与恶人的结局（第27-30节）**

> "敬畏耶和华使人日子加多；但恶人的年岁必被减少。义人的盼望必得喜乐；恶人的指望必至灭没。耶和华的道是正直人的保障，却成了作孽人的败坏。义人永不挪移；恶人不得住在地上。"

弟兄姐妹，"义人的盼望必得喜乐"——弟兄姐妹，义人不是没有等待——而是等待中有"喜乐"。因为义人知道结局是好的——不管过程多么艰难。罗马书8:28"万事都互相效力，叫爱神的人得益处。"盼望之所以带来喜乐——是因为盼望的对象是可靠的。

弟兄姐妹，箴言第十章用三十二句格言画出了两种人生——义人和恶人、勤劳和懒惰、智慧的口和愚昧的口、永久的根基和被吹走的无有。弟兄姐妹，每一天你都在选择——你是在建造义人的根基，还是在堆积恶人的稻草？

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Solomon. From Proverbs 10, we enter Proverbs' second major section—no longer continuous discourse but independent, sentence-by-sentence sayings. Each is a gem—requiring slow savoring. Chapter 10's core theme is the contrast between the righteous and the wicked.

**A Wise Son and a Foolish Son (v. 1)**

> "The proverbs of Solomon. A wise son makes a glad father, but a foolish son is a sorrow to his mother."

Brothers and sisters, the entire second section opens here—because this is the most fundamental relationship. Your wisdom or folly first affects not yourself—but your parents. Every choice you make impacts those who love you.

**Diligence and Laziness (vv. 4-5)**

> "A slack hand causes poverty, but the hand of the diligent makes rich. He who gathers in summer is a prudent son, but he who sleeps in harvest is a son who brings shame."

Brothers and sisters, "a slack hand causes poverty"—not absolute prosperity theology—but general life wisdom. "Sleeps in harvest"—the opportunity came but you were sleeping. How many miss life's opportunities—not because there were none—but because they were "sleeping" when opportunity arrived.

**The Power of Speech (vv. 11-14)**

> "The mouth of the righteous is a fountain of life...Hatred stirs up strife, but love covers all offenses."

Brothers and sisters, "a fountain of life"—your words can give life. One comforting word can rescue someone in despair. Is your mouth a "fountain of life" or a "source of ruin"? "Love covers all offenses"—1 Peter 4:8 quotes this: "love covers a multitude of sins." Not pretending not to see—but love choosing forgiveness over exposure.

**The Righteous Foundation (v. 25)**

> "When the tempest passes, the wicked is no more, but the righteous is established forever."

Brothers and sisters, the chapter's most powerful image—the tempest comes—the wicked are blown away—the righteous still stand. Why? Because the righteous have "foundation." Jesus used the same image at the Sermon on the Mount's end—the wise man built on rock (Matt 7:24-25). What is your life built on?

**The Righteous Hope (vv. 27-30)**

> "The fear of the LORD prolongs life...The hope of the righteous brings joy, but the expectation of the wicked will perish."

Brothers and sisters, "the hope of the righteous brings joy"—the righteous don't avoid waiting—but have joy in waiting. Because they know the ending is good—however difficult the process. Romans 8:28: "all things work together for good, for those who love God."

Brothers and sisters, Proverbs 10 paints two lives with thirty-two sayings—righteous and wicked, diligent and lazy, wise mouth and foolish mouth, permanent foundation and blown-away nothing. Every day you choose—are you building the righteous foundation or piling up the wicked's straw?

## 历史背景（中文）

箴言第十章标志着从"智慧序言"（1-9章）到"所罗门格言集"（10-22:16）的转折。从这里开始，不再是连续的讲论，而是独立的对偶格言（antithetical parallelism）——每一节的上半句和下半句形成对比。这种"对偶体"是希伯来诗歌的基本形式之一。第十章几乎全部使用"但"（waw adversative）连接上下句——形成完美的义人/恶人对比结构。"爱能遮掩一切过错"（第12节）被彼得前书4:8直接引用，成为新约伦理的重要基础。

## Historical Context (English)

Proverbs 10 marks the transition from "wisdom prologue" (chs. 1-9) to "Solomon's Proverb Collection" (10-22:16). From here, no longer continuous discourse but independent antithetical parallelisms—each verse's two halves forming contrast. This antithetical form is one of Hebrew poetry's basic structures. Chapter 10 almost entirely uses adversative waw ("but") connecting halves—forming perfect righteous/wicked contrast structure. "Love covers all offenses" (v. 12) is directly quoted in 1 Peter 4:8, becoming an important NT ethical foundation.

## 经文结构（中文）

**核心对比贯穿全章**（32节格言）
- **父母与子女（1节）**——智慧之子vs愚昧之子
- **财富与勤劳（2-5节）**——不义之财vs勤劳之手
- **口舌的力量（6-14节）**——生命泉源vs藏着强暴；爱遮掩vs恨挑争
- **义人的安全（15-22节）**——耶和华赐福vs恶人的惧怕
- **义人的永久（23-32节）**——暴风后义人站立vs恶人归于无有

## Structure (English)

**Core Contrast Throughout** (32 sayings)
- **Parents and Children (v. 1)**—Wise son vs. foolish son
- **Wealth and Diligence (vv. 2-5)**—Ill-gotten treasures vs. diligent hand
- **Power of Speech (vv. 6-14)**—Fountain of life vs. violence; love covers vs. hatred stirs
- **The Righteous' Security (vv. 15-22)**—LORD's blessing vs. wicked's fear
- **The Righteous' Permanence (vv. 23-32)**—Righteous stand after tempest vs. wicked perish

## 神学要点（中文）

**义人与恶人的对比——"两条道路"神学**
改革宗传统教导有两条路——生命之路和灭亡之路。韦敏斯德大要理问答第29问论与神有约之人的益处和不与神有约之人的结局。箴言10的对偶格言体现了"两条道路"的神学——每一句都在划分义与恶、生与死。

**"爱能遮掩一切过错"——赦免的神学**
改革宗传统教导赦免是基督徒生命的核心。韦敏斯德小要理问答第36问论今生的福乐："确知神的慈爱、得良心的平安、在圣灵中的喜乐。""遮掩"不是掩盖罪——而是选择不追究、不散布、不报复。这与诗篇32:1"遮盖其罪的这人是有福的"呼应。

**勤劳——呼召与职业伦理**
改革宗传统特别强调"天职"（vocation）——日常工作也是服事神。韦敏斯德信条第16章论善行。"手勤的却要富足"不是prosperity gospel——而是对忠心管家的一般性应许。加尔文和清教徒的"天职"伦理正是建立在这类箴言之上。

## Theology (English)

**Righteous vs. Wicked—"Two Ways" Theology**
The Reformed tradition teaches two paths—life and destruction. Westminster Larger Catechism Q29. Proverbs 10's antithetical proverbs embody "two ways" theology—each saying dividing righteous/wicked, life/death.

**"Love Covers All Offenses"—Theology of Forgiveness**
The Reformed tradition teaches forgiveness is core to Christian life. WSC Q36 on justification's benefits. "Covering" isn't hiding sin—but choosing not to pursue, spread, or retaliate. Echoing Psalm 32:1 "blessed is the one whose sin is covered."

**Diligence—Calling and Work Ethic**
The Reformed tradition especially emphasizes "vocation"—daily work is also serving God. Westminster Confession chapter 16 on good works.: The hand of the diligent makes rich isn't prosperity gospel—but a general promise to faithful stewards. Calvin's and the Puritans' "vocation" ethic is built on such proverbs.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"暴风一过义人的根基永久"——基督是磐石**——马太福音7:24-25"所以，凡听见我这话就去行的，好比一个聪明人把房子盖在磐石上。雨淋、水冲、风吹，撞着那房子，房子总不倒塌，因为根基立在磐石上。"箴言10:25的"义人的根基永久"在基督的教导中得到了最清晰的表达——基督自己就是那磐石根基。

**"爱能遮掩一切过错"——基督的爱遮盖我们的罪**——罗马书4:7-8"得赦免其过、遮盖其罪的，这人是有福的。主不算为有罪的，这人是有福的。"箴言10:12的"遮掩"在基督的十字架上得到了终极成就——基督的血遮盖了我们一切的过犯（西1:14）。

**"义人的口是生命的泉源"——基督的话就是生命**——约翰福音6:68"西门彼得回答说：主啊，你有永生之道，我们还归从谁呢？"基督的口是最真实的"生命的泉源"——祂的每一句话都带着生命的能力。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"The Righteous Is Established Forever"—Christ Is the Rock**—Matthew 7:24-25: "Everyone then who hears these words of mine and does them will be like a wise man who built his house on the rock." Proverbs 10:25's "righteous established forever" finds clearest expression in Christ's teaching—Christ Himself is that rock foundation.

**"Love Covers All Offenses"—Christ's Love Covers Our Sin**—Romans 4:7-8: "Blessed are those whose lawless deeds are forgiven, and whose sins are covered." Proverbs 10:12's "covering" finds ultimate fulfillment on Christ's cross—Christ's blood covers all our transgressions (Col 1:14).

**"The Righteous Mouth Is a Fountain of Life"—Christ's Words Are Life**—John 6:68: "Lord, to whom shall we go? You have the words of eternal life." Christ's mouth is the truest "fountain of life"—every word He speaks carries life's power.
