## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，诗篇第一百一十九篇是整本圣经中最长的一章——176节，用希伯来文二十二个字母写成的字母诗。每八节共用一个字母开头。这不是一首简单的诗——它是一首关于一个人与神的话语的爱情故事。

**从苦难中热爱律法（第1-8节，字母阿列夫）**

> "行为完全、遵行耶和华律法的，这人是有福的！遵守他的法度、一心寻求他的，这人是有福的！"

弟兄姐妹，这首诗以"有福"开篇——就像诗篇1篇一样。真正有福的人不是拥有最多的人——而是遵行耶和华律法的人。弟兄姐妹，你对神的话语有没有真正的热爱？不是"我应该读圣经"的罪恶感——而是"我热爱这话语"的真实情感？

> **"我怎能洁净我的行为？"（第9-16节，字母贝特）**

> "少年人用什么洁净他的行为呢？是要遵行你的话。"

弟兄姐妹，这是每一个年轻人需要听到的话——"用你的话"。世界告诉年轻人——用经验来洁净自己。时间会让你成熟、挫折会让你成长。但诗篇119说：神的话语是洁净的源头。不是年纪、不是经历——而是遵行神的话。

> **"我是外乡人（第17-24节，字母基梅尔）**

> "我是你在世上的旅客；求你不要向我掩藏你的命令。我心时刻切慕你的典章。"

弟兄姐妹，"我是外乡人"——这个世界不是我的家。弟兄姐妹，你有没有这种感觉——你在这个世界里像一个外乡人？这不是病态的——而是属灵的现实。彼得前书2:11"你们是客旅，是寄居的。"外乡人最需要的是什么？地图——向导——指南。神的律法就是外乡人在这个世界旅行的地图。

**试炼中的安慰（第49-56节，字母扎因）**

> "求你记念向仆人所说的话，因为你使我有了盼望。这话在我受苦的时候安慰了我，因为你的话使我苏醒了。"

弟兄姐妹，诗人在苦难中——但神的话语"安慰了我"、"使我苏醒了"。弟兄姐妹，你有没有在极度痛苦中被一句经文点亮？那不是巧合——那是圣灵用祂的话语安慰你。神的话语不是在平静时才有用——它在苦难中才发出最亮的光。

**神的话语胜过金银（第127-128节）**

> "所以我爱你的命令胜过金子，胜过精金。我以你一切的训词为正直，所以我恨恶一切虚假的道路。"

弟兄姐妹，"胜过金子胜过精金"——这是同一个比较在诗篇19:10也出现过的。两首诗都说：神的话比金子更贵重。弟兄姐妹，你的实际行动表明你认为什么更贵重？你花多少时间在挣钱上——花多少时间在神的话上？不是要你穷——而是让你检查你的价值观。

**你的话是灯（第105节）**

> "你的话是我脚前的灯，是我路上的光。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗最著名的一节——也是整本圣经中关于神话语的最经典的表达之一。"灯"——不是探照灯——而是一盏灯。弟兄姐妹，探照灯能照到很远——但你只能靠着灯手里的那一盏走。灯只照你"脚前"——不是一百步之外。弟兄姐妹，神不给你看到很远的将来——祂给你足够走下一步的光。"路上的光"——是照你正在走的路、而不是你将来要走的路。

> **"我将你的话藏在心里"（第11节）**

> "我将你的话藏在心里，免得我得罪你。"

弟兄姐妹，"藏在心里"——不只是记在脑里——而是"藏"在心的深处。弟兄姐妹，你的手机存了多少信息？你的电脑有多大的硬盘？但你的心里"藏"了多少神的话？弟兄姐妹，信息时代我们习惯了"外部存储"——什么都放在手机里、云端——但诗篇119说：神的话要存在内部——在你的心里。因为当试探来的时候——你来不及掏手机查经文——你需要心里已经"藏"了的话语立刻出来保护你。

> **"求你开我的眼睛"（第18节）**

> "求你开我的眼睛，使我看出你律法中的奇妙。"

弟兄姐妹，"开我的眼睛"——弟兄姐妹，你读圣经的时候有没有觉得"看不出什么"？读了但没有感动、没有亮光？诗人的祷告给了你方法——"求你开我的眼睛"。弟兄姐妹，读经不只是用眼睛看——还需要圣灵开你属灵的眼睛。每次读经之前先祷告这一句："主啊，开我的眼睛，使我看出你话语中的奇妙。"这个祷告会改变你整个读经的经历。

> **"受苦之前我走迷了路"（第67节）**

> "我未受苦以先走迷了路，现在却遵守你的话。"

弟兄姐妹，"未受苦以先走迷了路"——弟兄姐妹，苦难常常是神把你拉回来的方式。你顺利的时候——你容易忘记神。你受苦的时候——你被迫回到神面前。弟兄姐妹，苦难不是惩罚——而是牧人用杖把走迷的羊拉回羊圈。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇119用176节诗，用一个人一生的呼喊，用字母表从头到尾，来表达一件事——我热爱你的话语。弟兄姐妹，这种热爱是从哪里来的？不是天生的——而是圣灵赐给的。如果你还没有这种热爱——求圣灵赐给你。如果你已经有了——就去读，去思想，去遵行。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 119 is the longest chapter in all Scripture—176 verses, an acrostic poem written with Hebrew's twenty-two letters. This is not a simple poem—it is a love story between a person and God's Word.

**Blessed Are Those Who Walk in the Law (vv. 1-8)**

> "Blessed are those whose way is blameless, who walk in the law of the LORD! Blessed are those who keep his testimonies, who seek him with their whole heart."

Brothers and sisters, this psalm opens with "blessed"—just like Psalm 1. The truly blessed are not those who have the most—but those who walk in the LORD's law. Do you have genuine love for God's Word?

> **"How Can a Young Man Keep His Way Pure?" (vv. 9-16)**

> "How can a young man keep his way pure? By guarding it according to your word."

Brothers and sisters, every young person needs to hear this—"by your word." The world says experience purifies you. But Psalm 119 says: God's Word is purification's source.

> **"I Am a Sojourner" (vv. 17-24)**

> "I am a sojourner on the earth; hide not your commandments from me!"

Brothers and sisters, "I am a sojourner"—this world is not my home. 1 Peter 2:11: "you are sojourners and exiles." What does a sojourner most need? A map, a guide. God's law is the sojourner's map for traveling this world.

**Comfort in Affliction (vv. 49-56)**

> "Remember your word to your servant, in which you have made me hope. This is my comfort in my affliction, that your promise gives me life."

Brothers and sisters, in suffering God's Word "gives me life." Have you been lit up by a verse in extreme pain? That's not coincidence—that's the Spirit comforting you with His Word. God's Word isn't only useful in calm times—it shines brightest in suffering.

**Better Than Gold (vv. 127-128)**

> "I love your commandments above gold, above fine gold."

Brothers and sisters, "better than gold, better than fine gold"—the same comparison appears in Psalm 19:10. Both psalms say: God's Word is more precious than gold. What does your actual behavior reveal about what you consider more precious?

**Your Word Is a Lamp (v. 105)**

> "Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path."

Brothers and sisters, the psalm's most famous verse—one of Scripture's most classic expressions about God's Word. "A lamp"—not a floodlight. A lamp illuminates just your "feet"—not a hundred steps ahead. God doesn't show you the distant future—He gives you enough light for the next step. The light illuminates the path you're walking—not the one you'll walk in the future.

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 119 uses 176 verses, one person's lifelong cry, the alphabet from beginning to end, to express one thing—I love your Word. This love doesn't come naturally—it is given by the Spirit. If you don't have this love yet—ask the Spirit to give it. If you already have it—then read, meditate, obey.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇119是整本圣经中最长的章节——176节。它是一首字母诗（acrostic poem）：希伯来文有22个字母，每个字母对应8节诗，共22×8=176节。每一组8节都以同一个希伯来字母开头。作者不详——标题只是"诗篇"没有名字归属。内容上，诗篇119使用了七到八个不同的词来指称神的话语：torah（律法）、edah（法度/法令）、mitzvah（命令）、hok/hukkim（律例）、mishpat（典章/判例）、emunah（诚实/信实）、imrah（言语）、dabar（话语）。这首诗的主题是对神话语的热爱——不是律法主义的遵守，而是情感上的爱慕。第105节"你的话是我脚前的灯，是我路上的光"是基督教最广为人知的圣经记忆经文之一。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 119 is the longest chapter in all Scripture—176 verses. An acrostic poem: Hebrew has 22 letters; each letter corresponds to 8 verses, totaling 22×8=176. Each group of 8 begins with the same Hebrew letter. Author unknown—no attribution in the title. The psalm uses seven to eight different Hebrew words for God's Word: torah (law), edah (testimonies), mitzvah (commandments), hok/hukkim (statutes), mishpat (rules/judgments), emunah (faithfulness), imrah (promise/word), dabar (word). The psalm's theme is love for God's Word—not legalistic compliance but emotional devotion. Verse 105 "your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path" is one of Christianity's most widely memorized Bible verses.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神话语的充足性（Sola Scriptura）**
改革宗传统教导圣经的充足性。韦敏斯德信条第1章：圣经的全部旨意……已充分明确地载于圣经之中……我们不可在任何时候、任何地方……增添圣经。诗篇119对神话语的热爱是"唯独圣经"原则的诗歌表达——神的话是充足的指引、安慰和光照。

**律法的积极功能**
改革宗传统教导律法的三种用途，特别是第三种——信徒生活的规范。韦敏斯德大要理问答第97问：律法在被更新之人的手中有什么特别的用处？诗篇119显示律法对信徒不是重担而是喜乐——"我何等爱慕你的律法"（第97节）。

**"你的话是灯"——圣经的启示性**
改革宗传统教导圣经的充足光照。韦敏斯德信条第1章：圣经……以其本身发出全备的见证，因此必须被接受。诗篇119:105的"灯"和"光"精确描述了圣经的认识论功能——不是全部的光，而是足够走下一步的光。

## Theology (English)

**Scripture's Sufficiency (Sola Scriptura)**
The Reformed tradition teaches Scripture's sufficiency. Westminster Confession chapter 1: "The whole counsel of God...is either expressly set down in Scripture, or by good and necessary consequence may be deduced from Scripture: unto which nothing at any time is to be added." Psalm 119's love for God's Word is the poetic expression of Sola Scriptura.

**The Law's Positive Function**
The Reformed tradition teaches three uses of the law, especially the third—the rule for believers' lives. Westminster Larger Catechism Q97: "What special use is there of the moral law to the regenerate?" Psalm 119 shows the law is not burden but joy for believers—"How I love your law!" (v. 97).

**"Your Word Is a Lamp"—Scripture's Illuminating Nature**
The Reformed tradition teaches Scripture's sufficient illumination. Westminster Confession chapter 1. Psalm 119:105's "lamp" and "light" precisely describe Scripture's epistemological function—not all light, but enough to take the next step.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督是道成肉身的"话语"**——约翰福音1:1-14"太初有道……道成了肉身……充充满满地有恩典有真理。"诗篇119热爱的"话语"（dabar）在约翰福音中成为了一个位格——耶稣基督。弟兄姐妹，热爱神的话最终指向热爱说话的那位——而那位已经在基督里道成肉身了。

**"你的话使我苏醒"——基督的话有生命力**——约翰福音6:63"我对你们所说的话，就是灵就是生命。"诗篇119:25"求你照你的话使我苏醒"——基督所说的话有直接的生命力：约翰福音11:43"拉撒路出来！"死人应声复活。祂的话有最终极的"苏醒"能力。

**"你的话是灯"——基督是世界的光**——约翰福音8:12"我是世界的光；跟从我的，就不在黑暗里走，必要得着生命的光。"诗篇119:105的"光"在基督里变成了位格性的光——不只是文字的启示，而是那真光本身降临在我们中间。诗篇119读到最后——它所热爱的话语走下书页，成为了拿撒勒人耶稣。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Is the Incarnate "Word"**—John 1:1-14: "In the beginning was the Word...And the Word became flesh...full of grace and truth." The "word" (dabar) Psalm 119 loves became a person in John's Gospel—Jesus Christ. Loving God's Word ultimately points to loving the One who speaks—who became flesh in Christ.

**"Your Word Gives Me Life"—Christ's Words Have Life-Power**—John 6:63: "The words that I have spoken to you are spirit and life." Psalm 119:25 "give me life according to your word"—Christ's words have direct life-power: John 11:43 "Lazarus, come out!"—the dead man walked out. His words have the ultimate power to "give life."

**"Your Word Is a Lamp"—Christ Is the Light of the World**—John 8:12: "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness." Psalm 119:105's "light" became personal in Christ—not just textual revelation but that true Light Himself descending among us. Reading Psalm 119 to the end—the Word it loves stepped off the page and became Jesus of Nazareth.
