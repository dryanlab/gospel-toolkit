## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第一百三十九篇是我对神的全知、全在、全能最深的默想——当我安静下来想到神对我了解的深度，我既感到敬畏又感到安慰。

**无所不知（第1-6节）**

> "耶和华啊，你已经鉴察我，认识我。我坐下，我起来，你都晓得；你从远处知道我的意念。我行路，我躺卧，你都细察；你也深知我一切所行的。耶和华啊，我舌头上的话，你没有一句不知道的。你在我前后环绕我，按手在我身上。这样的知识奇妙，是我不能测的，至高，是我不能及的。"

弟兄姐妹，"你已经鉴察我认识我"——不是表面的了解，而是"鉴察"——深入的、彻底的、无死角的认识。弟兄姐妹，你最亲密的人对你了解多少？百分之二十？百分之五十？没有人能百分之百了解你——连你自己都不完全了解自己。但神"已经鉴察你认识你"——百分之百。

"我坐下我起来你都晓得"——弟兄姐妹，这些是最普通的动作——坐下和起来——但连这些神都知道。你以为只有"大事"才值得神注意？不——你的坐下和起来、你的来往和休息——神全都知道。

"你从远处知道我的意念"——弟兄姐妹，不是等你说出来才知道——而是"从远处"就知道了。你的意念还在形成的时候——神已经知道了。弟兄姐妹，这对你是安慰还是恐惧？如果你心里有隐藏的罪——这是恐惧。但如果你知道自己的心向着神——这是安慰。你不需要解释——神已经知道了。

"我舌头上的话你没有一句不知道的"——弟兄姐妹，在你说出来之前——神就知道了。你说的每一句话——甚至你想说但没说出来的话——神都知道。

**无所不在（第7-12节）**

> "我往哪里去躲避你的灵？我往哪里逃、躲避你的面？我若升到天上，你在那里；我若在阴间下榻，你也在那里。我若展开清晨的翅膀，飞到海极居住，就是在那里，你的手也必引导我；你的右手也必扶持我。"

弟兄姐妹，我问了一个修辞性的问题——"我往哪里去躲避你的灵？"答案是：哪里都不能。天上——你在那里。阴间——你也在那里。海极——你的手也在那里。弟兄姐妹，没有一个地方是你能躲避神的。

弟兄姐妹，这对约拿来说是噩耗——他试图逃到他施去躲避神。但他发现即使在大鱼的肚子里——神也在那里。这对你来说呢？如果你在逃避神——这是最可怕的消息：你无处可逃。但如果你在寻求神——这是最安慰的消息：你不可能走到一个神不在的地方。

"我若说：黑暗必定遮蔽我，我周围的亮光必成为黑夜——黑暗也不能遮蔽我使你不见，黑夜却如白昼发亮。黑暗和光明，在你看来都是一样。"弟兄姐妹，黑暗不能把你藏起来——因为对神来说"黑暗和光明都是一样"。你以为你可以在黑暗中做没人看见的事？神看得见。

**奇妙的被造（第13-16节）**

> "我的肺腑是你所造的；我在母腹中，你已覆庇我。我要称谢你，因我受造奇妙可畏；你的作为奇妙，这是我心深知道的。我在暗中受造，在地的深处被联络；那时，我的形体并不向你隐藏。我未成形的体质，你的眼早已看见了；你所定的日子，我尚未度一日，你都写在你的册上了。"

弟兄姐妹，"受造奇妙可畏"——弟兄姐妹，你不是偶然的产物——你是被"奇妙可畏"地造出来的。你的DNA有三十亿个碱基对——你的大脑有八百六十亿个神经元——你的心脏每天跳动十万次——这一切是"奇妙可畏"的。

"你所定的日子，我尚未度一日，你都写在你的册上了"——弟兄姐妹，你生命中的每一天——在你还没有出生之前——就已经被神写在册子上了。这不是宿命论——这是神的主权。你的生命不是随机的——而是被计划的。每一天都有意义——因为每一天都在神的计划中。

**鉴察我的心（第23-24节）**

> "神啊，求你鉴察我，知道我的心思，试炼我，知道我的意念，看在我里面有什么恶行没有，引导我走永生的道路。"

弟兄姐妹，这首诗以"你已经鉴察我"开始——以"求你鉴察我"结束。从事实的陈述到主动的邀请。弟兄姐妹，开始的时候我在描述——"你已经认识我了"。但到了结尾——他主动邀请——"求你鉴察我"。弟兄姐妹，你敢邀请神来鉴察你的心吗？不是被动地被看——而是主动地邀请？这个邀请意味着你渴望被纠正——你知道自己可能有看不见的恶行——你请求神来检查。

"引导我走永生的道路"——弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗的终点——鉴察的目的不是定罪，而是引导。神看透你的一切——不是为了审判你——而是为了引导你走"永生的道路"。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 139 is my deepest meditation on God's omniscience, omnipresence, and omnipotence—when I quietly contemplated how deeply God knows me, I felt both awe and comfort.

**Omniscience (vv. 1-6)**

> "O LORD, you have searched me and known me! You know when I sit down and when I rise up; you discern my thoughts from afar. You search out my path and my lying down and are acquainted with all my ways. Even before a word is on my tongue, behold, O LORD, you know it altogether."

Brothers and sisters, "you have searched me and known me"—not surface acquaintance but "searched"—thorough, complete, without blind spots. How well does your closest person know you? Twenty percent? Fifty? No one can know you one hundred percent—you don't even fully know yourself. But God "has searched and known you"—one hundred percent.

"You discern my thoughts from afar"—not waiting until you speak, but knowing "from afar." While your thoughts are still forming—God already knows. Is this comfort or terror for you? If you harbor hidden sin—it's terrifying. But if your heart is toward God—it's comforting. You don't need to explain—God already knows.

**Omnipresence (vv. 7-12)**

> "Where shall I go from your Spirit? Or where shall I flee from your presence? If I ascend to heaven, you are there! If I make my bed in Sheol, you are there! If I take the wings of the morning and dwell in the uttermost parts of the sea, even there your hand shall lead me."

Brothers and sisters, a rhetorical question—"Where shall I flee?" Answer: nowhere. Heaven—you're there. Sheol—you're there too. The sea's far shore—your hand is there. For Jonah this was terrible news—he tried fleeing to Tarshish. But he found God even in the great fish's belly. For you? If you're fleeing God—most terrifying news: there's no escape. But if you're seeking God—most comforting news: you cannot go where God is not.

**Wonderfully Made (vv. 13-16)**

> "For you formed my inward parts; you knitted me together in my mother's womb. I praise you, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made...My frame was not hidden from you, when I was being made in secret...Your eyes saw my unformed substance; in your book were written, every one of them, the days that were formed for me, when as yet there was none of them."

Brothers and sisters, "fearfully and wonderfully made"—you are no accident. Your DNA has three billion base pairs—your brain eighty-six billion neurons—your heart beats a hundred thousand times daily—all "fearfully and wonderfully made."

"In your book were written, every one of them, the days that were formed for me"—every day of your life was written in God's book before you were born. Not fatalism—but divine sovereignty. Your life is not random—but planned. Every day has meaning—because every day is in God's plan.

**Search My Heart (vv. 23-24)**

> "Search me, O God, and know my heart! Try me and know my thoughts! And see if there be any grievous way in me, and lead me in the way everlasting!"

Brothers and sisters, the psalm begins "you have searched me"—ends "search me." From factual statement to active invitation. Do you dare invite God to search your heart? Not passively being seen—but actively inviting? This invitation means you desire correction—you know you may have unseen wickedness—you ask God to examine. "Lead me in the way everlasting"—searching's purpose is not condemnation but guidance.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇139的标题是"大卫的诗，交与伶长"。这首诗在希伯来文中有若干罕见的亚兰语词汇，有些学者因此认为它的写作年代较晚（被掳后时期），但也有学者指出这可能反映了大卫时代北方方言的影响。这首诗在犹太传统中非常珍贵——米大示（Midrash）称诗篇139为大卫最深的祷告。第13-16节"受造奇妙可畏"在现代基督教中常被用来论证胎儿的人格尊严和生命权——"我未成形的体质你的眼早已看见了"被解读为神从受孕之时就认识每一个人。加尔文在注释此诗时特别强调了神的全知作为信徒安慰的根基——不是威胁性的监视，而是慈爱的看顾。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 139's superscription: "To the choirmaster. A Psalm of David." The Hebrew contains several rare Aramaic words, leading some scholars to date it later (post-exilic), though others note this may reflect northern dialect influence from David's era. The Midrash calls Psalm 139 David's deepest prayer. Verses 13-16 "fearfully and wonderfully made" are frequently used in modern Christianity to argue for the unborn's personal dignity and right to life—"your eyes saw my unformed substance" interpreted as God knowing each person from conception. Calvin's commentary on this psalm especially emphasized God's omniscience as the believer's comfort foundation—not threatening surveillance but loving care.

## 经文结构（中文）

**无所不在（7-12节）**——往哪里躲→天上/阴间/海极都在→黑暗如光明。

## Structure (English)

**Omniscience (vv. 1-6)**—Searched and known → sit/rise known → thoughts discerned afar → words known before spoken → knowledge too wonderful.
**Omnipresence (vv. 7-12)**—Where to flee → heaven/Sheol/sea's end: present → darkness like light.
**Wonderfully Made (vv. 13-16)**—Formed inward parts → knitted in womb → fearfully wonderfully made → unformed substance seen → days written in book.
**Righteous Indignation (vv. 17-22)**—God's thoughts precious → wicked should be destroyed → hating those who hate you.
**Final Prayer (vv. 23-24)**—Search me → know my heart → see grievous ways → lead in way everlasting.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神的全知——无所不知的安慰**
改革宗传统教导神无所不知。韦敏斯德信条第二章教导：只有一位又活又真的神，祂是无限的，有无限的知识。诗篇139:1-6描述的全知不是冷漠的监控——而是亲密的认识。"你从远处知道我的意念"——神的全知是个人性的，不是抽象的。

**神的全在——无所不在的同在**
改革宗传统教导神无所不在。韦敏斯德信条第2章："他是无处不在的。"诗篇139:7-12的"往哪里去躲避你的灵"教导的不是神的弥漫性（如泛神论），而是神的位格性同在——在天上、阴间、海极，神都是以位格与你同在。

**"受造奇妙可畏"——人的尊严来自创造**
改革宗传统教导人的尊严来自被神创造。韦敏斯德小要理问答第10问："神照着自己的形像……造人。"诗篇139:13-16强调每一个人从受孕起就被神认识和计划——这是人生命尊严的神学基础。

## Theology (English)

**God's Omniscience—The Comfort of Being Known**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's omniscience. Westminster Confession chapter 2: There is but one only, living, and true God...infinite...having infinite knowledge. Psalm 139:1-6's omniscience is not cold surveillance—but intimate knowledge. God's omniscience is personal, not abstract.

**God's Omnipresence—Presence Everywhere**
Westminster Confession chapter 2: He is everywhere present. Psalm 139:7-12's "where shall I flee" teaches not God's diffusion (pantheism) but His personal presence—in heaven, Sheol, sea's end, God is personally present with you.

**"Fearfully and Wonderfully Made"—Dignity from Creation**
The Reformed tradition teaches human dignity comes from being God-created. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q10: "God created man in his own image." Psalm 139:13-16 emphasizes each person is known and planned by God from conception—the theological foundation of human life's dignity.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督的全知——"主啊，你知道万事"**——约翰福音21:17彼得对耶稣说："主啊，你是无所不知的；你知道我爱你。"诗篇139的全知之神在基督身上完全彰显——约翰福音2:25"因为他知道人心里所存的。"基督"知道"你——不是监控式的知道——而是如牧人知道自己的羊。

**"在母腹中你已覆庇我"——基督的道成肉身**——路加福音1:35"圣灵要临到你身上，至高者的能力要荫庇你。"诗篇139:13的"在母腹中你已覆庇我"在基督道成肉身时得到了最奇妙的应验——圣子在马利亚腹中被"覆庇"，成为真正的人。

**"引导我走永生的道路"——基督是道路**——约翰福音14:6"我就是道路、真理、生命。"诗篇139:24"引导我走永生的道路"——基督自己就是那条"永生的道路"。认识祂就是走在永生的路上。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ's Omniscience—"Lord, You Know Everything"**—John 21:17: Peter said, "Lord, you know everything; you know that I love you." Psalm 139's all-knowing God is fully manifested in Christ—John 2:25: "he himself knew what was in man." Christ "knows" you—not surveillance-style knowing—but as a shepherd knows his sheep.

**"You Knitted Me in My Mother's Womb"—Christ's Incarnation**—Luke 1:35: "The Holy Spirit will come upon you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you." Psalm 139:13's "you knitted me in my mother's womb" found its most wondrous fulfillment in Christ's incarnation—the Son "knitted" in Mary's womb, becoming truly human.

**"Lead Me in the Way Everlasting"—Christ Is the Way**—John 14:6: "I am the way, and the truth, and the life." Psalm 139:24 "lead me in the way everlasting"—Christ Himself is that "way everlasting." Knowing Him is walking the everlasting path.
