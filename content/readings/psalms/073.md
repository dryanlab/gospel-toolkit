## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是亚萨。诗篇第七十三篇是我一生中最诚实的一首诗——关于一个看见恶人亨通而几乎跌倒的人，关于他如何在神的圣所里找到答案。弟兄姐妹，这首诗把你不敢说出口的问题大声说出来了。

**正确的神学开场（第1节）**

> "神实在恩待以色列那些清心的人。"

弟兄姐妹，这是诗的开篇——也是诗人最后得到的结论。他把结论放在开头——好让你知道：即使他说了很多"几乎跌倒"的话，他最终到达的地方是"神实在恩待清心的人"。

**几乎跌倒（第2-3节）**

> "至于我，我的脚几乎滑跤；我的步伐几乎失闪。我所见恶人的亨通，就嫉妒狂傲的人。"

弟兄姐妹，亚萨是圣殿诗班的领袖——是蒙神拣选的人——但他"几乎跌倒"。原因是"嫉妒狂傲的人"——他看见恶人活得好，就嫉妒了。弟兄姐妹，请不要批评亚萨——因为你我都有过同样的想法。"为什么不信神的人过得那么好？""为什么做坏事的人没有受报应？"这不是不信——这是诚实。

**恶人的描述（第4-12节）**

> "他们死的时候没有痛苦；他们的力气却是壮实。他们不受人的苦难，也不像别人遭受灾害。……他们说：神怎能晓得？至高者有什么知识呢？看哪，这些恶人常享安逸，钱财加增。"

弟兄姐妹，亚萨用了九节经文来描述恶人——他们身体健康、财富增加、不受苦难、嘲弄神。弟兄姐妹，这不是旧约时代才有的现象——今天你也看到这种情况。那些完全不理神的人，有时候生活似乎比基督徒还顺。这个问题如果你从来没有想过——说明你还没有认真面对现实。

**内心的挣扎（第13-14节）**

> "我实在徒然洁净了我的心，徒然洗手表明无辜；因为我终日遭灾难，每早晨受惩罚。"

弟兄姐妹，这是亚萨内心最诚实的话——"徒然洁净了我的心"。我跟随神、保持洁净——但结果是遭灾难。而不跟随神的人——却安然无恙。弟兄姐妹，你有没有这种想法？如果有——你正在和亚萨经历同样的挣扎。

这就是"神义论"（theodicy）的核心问题——如果神是公义的，为什么义人受苦而恶人亨通？亚萨没有回避这个问题——他把它大声说出来了。

**顾念神的会众（第15节）**

> "我若说我要这样讲，这就是以奸诈待你的众子。"

弟兄姐妹，但亚萨没有说出口——他忍住了。为什么？"这就是以奸诈待你的众子"——如果我把这些怀疑公开说出来，我会伤害神的会众。弟兄姐妹，信心的挣扎不是不能有——但有些话你不能说给所有人听。有些疑惑需要在神面前独自处理，而不是广而告之使弟兄跌倒。

**圣所中的答案（第16-17节）**

> "我思索这事，为我所难，及至我进了神的圣所，思想他们的结局。"

弟兄姐妹，转折点来了——"及至我进了神的圣所"。不是靠哲学思考、不是靠逻辑辩论——而是进入神的同在。在圣所里，视野改变了。弟兄姐妹，你有多久没有真正"进了神的圣所"了？不是参加礼拜的动作——而是真正进入神的同在？很多信仰的疑惑——不是靠更多的讨论解决的——而是靠真正遇见神。

**恶人的结局（第18-20节）**

> "你实在把他们安在滑地，使他们掉在沉沦之中。他们转眼之间成了何等的荒凉！他们被惊恐灭尽了。他们像人睡醒，主啊，你醒了以后必轻看他们的影像。"

弟兄姐妹，从神的视角看——恶人站在"滑地"上。他们的亨通是暂时的——"转眼之间成了何等的荒凉"。弟兄姐妹，永恒的视角改变了一切。你看到的是今生的几十年——神看到的是永恒。在永恒的框架里，恶人今生的亨通算什么？

**神与我同在（第23-25节）**

> "然而我常与你同在；你搀着我的右手。你要以你的训言引导我，以后必接我到荣耀里。除你以外，在天上我有谁呢？除你以外，在地上我也没有所爱慕的。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗的高峰——从"几乎跌倒"到"除你以外在天上我有谁呢"。视野完全改变了。不是恶人有多好的问题——而是"除你以外，在地上我也没有所爱慕的"。弟兄姐妹，当你到达这个地方——神自己成了你的盼望和满足——"恶人亨通"的问题就变小了。不是问题消失了——而是神变大了。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Asaph. Psalm 73 is my life's most honest poem—about a man who nearly stumbled seeing the wicked prosper, who found the answer in God's sanctuary. Brothers and sisters, this psalm says aloud the question you dare not voice.

**The Correct Theological Opening (v. 1)**

> "Truly God is good to Israel, to those who are pure in heart."

Brothers and sisters, this is the poem's opening—also the psalmist's final conclusion. He placed the conclusion at the beginning—so you know: even though he said much about "almost stumbling," where he ultimately arrived is "God is truly good to the pure in heart."

**Almost Stumbling (vv. 2-3)**

> "But as for me, my feet had almost stumbled, my steps had nearly slipped. For I was envious of the arrogant when I saw the prosperity of the wicked."

Brothers and sisters, Asaph was the temple choir leader—chosen by God—yet he "almost stumbled." The cause: "envious of the arrogant"—seeing the wicked thrive, he envied them. Brothers and sisters, don't criticize Asaph—because you and I have had the same thoughts. "Why do people who don't believe in God live so well?" "Why do wrongdoers go unpunished?" This is not unbelief—this is honesty.

**The Wicked Described (vv. 4-12)**

> "For they have no pangs until death; their bodies are fat and sleek...They say, 'How can God know? Is there knowledge in the Most High?' Behold, these are the wicked; always at ease, they increase in riches."

Brothers and sisters, Asaph spent nine verses describing the wicked—healthy, wealthy, unafflicted, mocking God. This is not unique to Old Testament times. Have you seen people who completely ignore God apparently living better than believers? If you've never thought about this—you haven't honestly faced reality.

**The Inward Struggle (vv. 13-14)**

> "All in vain have I kept my heart clean and washed my hands in innocence. For all the day long I have been stricken and rebuked every morning."

Brothers and sisters, Asaph's most honest words—"all in vain have I kept my heart clean." I followed God, maintained purity—but the result was affliction. Those who don't follow God—perfectly fine. Have you had this thought? If so—you're experiencing the same struggle as Asaph.

This is theodicy's core question—if God is just, why do the righteous suffer and the wicked prosper? Asaph didn't dodge this—he said it aloud.

**The Answer in the Sanctuary (vv. 16-17)**

> "But when I thought how to understand this, it seemed to me a wearisome task, until I went into the sanctuary of God; then I discerned their end."

Brothers and sisters, the turning point—"until I went into the sanctuary." Not through philosophical reasoning, not logical debate—but entering God's presence. In the sanctuary, perspective changed. Brothers and sisters, how long since you truly "went into God's sanctuary"? Not the action of attending worship—but truly entering God's presence? Many faith doubts are resolved not through more discussion—but through truly encountering God.

**The Wicked's End (vv. 18-20)**

> "Truly you set them in slippery places; you make them fall to ruin. How they are destroyed in a moment, swept away utterly by terrors!"

Brothers and sisters, from God's perspective—the wicked stand on "slippery ground." Their prosperity is temporary—"destroyed in a moment." Eternal perspective changes everything. What you see is this life's few decades—what God sees is eternity.

**God Is With Me (vv. 23-25)**

> "Nevertheless, I am continually with you; you hold my right hand. You guide me with your counsel, and afterward you will receive me to glory. Whom have I in heaven but you? And there is nothing on earth that I desire besides you."

Brothers and sisters, the psalm's climax—from "almost stumbling" to "there is nothing on earth I desire besides you." Perspective completely transformed. Not how good the wicked have it—but "there is nothing on earth I desire besides you." When you reach this place—God Himself becomes your hope and satisfaction—the "wicked prospering" problem shrinks. Not that the problem disappeared—but God grew larger.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇73是诗篇第三卷（诗篇73-89）的开篇，全部冠以"亚萨的诗"或"可拉后裔的诗"。亚萨（Asaph）是大卫时代圣殿诗班的领袖（代上6:39），负责带领利未人在约柜面前敬拜。有十二篇诗篇冠名亚萨（诗50, 73-83）。诗篇73的主题——义人受苦、恶人亨通——是旧约智慧文学的核心问题，也是约伯记的主题。但诗篇73与约伯记的结构不同：约伯记是长篇叙事的挣扎，诗篇73是在圣所中突然的顿悟。路加福音16:19-31财主与拉撒路的故事也触及同样的主题——此生的苦乐不是永恒结局的预兆。诗篇73:25"除你以外……在地上我也没有所爱慕的"是诗篇中论"以神为乐"最直接的表达之一。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 73 opens the third book of the Psalter (Psalms 73-89), all attributed to "Asaph" or "sons of Korah." Asaph was the leader of David's temple choir (1 Chron 6:39), responsible for leading Levites in worship before the ark. Twelve psalms bear Asaph's name (Pss 50, 73-83). Psalm 73's theme—the righteous suffering while the wicked prosper—is the core question of Old Testament wisdom literature and the theme of Job. But Psalm 73 differs from Job structurally: Job is a lengthy narrative of struggle; Psalm 73 is a sudden insight in the sanctuary. Luke 16:19-31, the Rich Man and Lazarus, touches the same theme—earthly prosperity or suffering is not a preview of eternal outcome. Psalm 73:25's "there is nothing on earth that I desire besides you" is one of the Psalter's most direct expressions of "enjoying God."

## 经文结构（中文）

**结论先行（1节）**——"神实在恩待清心的人"。

**几乎跌倒的原因（2-3节）**——脚几乎滑跤→嫉妒恶人亨通。

**恶人的描述（4-12节）**——无痛苦→身体健康→不受苦难→嘲弄神→钱财加增。

**内心的挣扎（13-15节）**——"徒然洁净了我的心"→但不说出口怕伤害众子。

**圣所中的顿悟（16-17节）**——"及至我进了神的圣所，思想他们的结局"。

**恶人的结局（18-22节）**——滑地→沉沦→转眼荒凉→被惊恐灭尽→像梦中的影像。

**神与我同在（23-26节）**——常与你同在→搀着右手→引导→接到荣耀→肉体心肠衰残，神是我心里的力量。

**最后的结论（27-28节）**——远离神的必灭亡→亲近神是我的益处。

## Structure (English)

**Conclusion First (v. 1)**—"Truly God is good to those who are pure in heart."

**Cause of Near-Stumbling (vv. 2-3)**—Feet almost slipped → envious of wicked's prosperity.

**The Wicked Described (vv. 4-12)**—No pangs → fat and sleek → unafflicted → mock God → riches increase.

**Inward Struggle (vv. 13-15)**—"All in vain have I kept my heart clean" → but not voicing it for fear of harming God's children.

**Sanctuary Insight (vv. 16-17)**—"Until I went into the sanctuary of God; then I discerned their end."

**Wicked's End (vv. 18-22)**—Slippery places → ruin → destroyed in a moment → swept away by terrors → like a dream.

**God Is With Me (vv. 23-26)**—Continually with you → holding right hand → guiding → received to glory → flesh and heart failing, but God is the strength of my heart.

**Final Conclusion (vv. 27-28)**—Those far from God perish → drawing near to God is my good.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神义论——圣经的回答**
诗篇73给出了神义论（the problem of evil and suffering）的一个关键回答：永恒的视角。改革宗传统教导神的主权超越人的有限理解。韦敏斯德信条第5章：神在他全能的护理中……知道祂在作什么。今生的苦乐不是永恒公义的全部显示——最终的公义在末日的审判中彰显。

**"进了神的圣所"——礼拜的功效**
改革宗传统强调公共礼拜的重要性。韦敏斯德信条第21章论宗教崇拜。进入神的同在改变视野——这不是神秘主义，而是认识论（epistemology）——你在神的同在中得到新的认识框架。很多信仰的疑惑只能在礼拜中解决，不能在辩论中解决。

**"除你以外在地上我也没有所爱慕的"——独一的满足**
改革宗传统教导神是唯一终极的满足。韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问："以祂为乐"。诗篇73:25是"以祂为乐"的最高表达——不是"神很好，加上其他很多好的东西"——而是"除你以外没有所爱慕的"。

## Theology (English)

**Theodicy—Scripture's Answer**
Psalm 73 gives one key answer to theodicy: the eternal perspective. The Reformed tradition teaches God's sovereignty surpasses human limited understanding. Westminster Confession chapter 5. This life's prosperity or suffering is not the complete display of eternal justice—final justice is revealed in the last judgment.

**"Going into the Sanctuary"—The Effect of Worship**
The Reformed tradition emphasizes public worship's importance. Westminster Confession chapter 21 on religious worship. Entering God's presence changes perspective—not mysticism but epistemology—you receive a new cognitive framework in God's presence. Many faith doubts can only be resolved in worship, not in debate.

**"Nothing on Earth I Desire Besides You"—The Singular Satisfaction**
The Reformed tradition teaches God is the only ultimate satisfaction. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "to enjoy him forever." Psalm 73:25 is the highest expression of "enjoying God"—not "God is great plus many other great things"—but "there is nothing on earth I desire besides you."

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"接我到荣耀里"——复活和升天的预表**
诗篇73:24"以后必接我到荣耀里"——这个"接到荣耀"的盼望在基督的复活和升天中得到应验。希伯来书12:2"仰望为我们信心创始成终的耶稣。他因那摆在前面的喜乐，就轻看羞辱，忍受了十字架的苦难，便坐在神宝座的右边。"基督先被"接到荣耀"——祂的荣耀是信徒将来之荣耀的保证。

**恶人"结局"——基督是末日的审判者**
诗篇73:17-18"思想他们的结局……使他们掉在沉沦之中"——基督是末日的审判者（约5:22"父不审判什么人，乃将审判的事全交与子"）。恶人的"结局"由基督来执行——这是诗篇73的神义论问题在基督身上的终极回答。

**"除你以外在天上我有谁"——基督是天上的中保**
约翰一书2:1"若有人犯罪，在父那里我们有一位中保，就是那义者耶稣基督。"诗篇73:25"在天上我有谁"——在天上，信徒有基督在父右边作中保，不断为我们代求（来7:25）。弟兄姐妹，"在天上我有谁"——你有基督。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Afterward You Will Receive Me to Glory"—Resurrection and Ascension Foreshadowed**
Psalm 73:24: "and afterward you will receive me to glory"—this hope of being "received to glory" is fulfilled in Christ's resurrection and ascension. Hebrews 12:2: "looking to Jesus, the founder and perfecter of our faith, who for the joy that was set before him endured the cross, despising the shame, and is seated at the right hand of the throne of God." Christ was first "received to glory"—His glory is the guarantee of believers' future glory.

**The Wicked's "End"—Christ Is the Last Judgment's Judge**
Psalm 73:17-18: "discerned their end...you set them in slippery places"—Christ is the final judge (John 5:22: "The Father judges no one, but has given all judgment to the Son"). The wicked's "end" is executed by Christ—the ultimate answer to Psalm 73's theodicy question.

**"Whom Have I in Heaven"—Christ Is the Heavenly Mediator**
1 John 2:1: "we have an advocate with the Father, Jesus Christ the righteous." Psalm 73:25's "whom have I in heaven"—in heaven, believers have Christ interceding at the Father's right hand (Heb 7:25). Brothers and sisters, "whom have I in heaven"—you have Christ.
