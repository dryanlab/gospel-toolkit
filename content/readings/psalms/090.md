## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西，神的仆人。诗篇第九十篇是我在旷野四十年中写下的唯一一首诗——也是整本诗篇中最古老的一首。当我看见一代又一代的以色列人在旷野中倒毙的时候，我写下了这首关于时间、永恒和神的忿怒的诗。

**永恒的神（第1-2节）**

> "主啊，你世世代代作我们的居所。诸山未曾生出，地与世界你未曾造成，从亘古到永远，你是神。"

弟兄姐妹，在谈到人的短暂之前，摩西先确立了神的永恒。"从亘古到永远你是神"——没有开始、没有结束。诸山——地上最古老、最稳固的事物——但在神面前，诸山有开始的时刻。神没有。弟兄姐妹，时间是受造物——神是时间的创造者。祂不在时间里面——时间在祂里面。

"你世世代代作我们的居所"——弟兄姐妹，摩西说这话的时候，以色列人是没有居所的流浪者——四十年在旷野中住帐棚。但摩西说：我们的居所不是帐棚——而是神自己。弟兄姐妹，你的安全感建立在什么上面？房子？工作？银行账户？这些都是帐棚——终有一天要拆掉。只有神是"世世代代的居所"。

**人的短暂（第3-6节）**

> "你使人归于尘土，说：你们世人要归回。在你看来，千年如已过的昨日，又如夜间的一更。你叫他们如水冲去；他们如睡一觉。早晨，他们如生长的草，早晨发芽生长，晚上割下枯干。"

弟兄姐妹，"千年如已过的昨日"——对你来说一千年是漫长的——对神来说只是昨天。彼得后书3:8引用了这节经文："主看一日如千年，千年如一日。"弟兄姐妹，你在地上活七十年——在神的永恒中连一瞬间都算不上。

"如生长的草"——早晨发芽、晚上枯干。弟兄姐妹，人生就是这么短。你觉得你还有很多时间——但在永恒的框架里，你的一生就像一棵草的一天。早上看起来那么翠绿——晚上就枯干了。弟兄姐妹，你还在把最宝贵的时间花在最不重要的事情上吗？

**神的忿怒与人的罪（第7-9节）**

> "我们因你的怒气而消灭，因你的忿怒而惊惶。你将我们的罪孽摆在你面前，将我们的隐恶摆在你面光之中。我们经过的日子都在你震怒之下；我们度尽的年岁好像一声叹息。"

弟兄姐妹，摩西在旷野四十年——他看见民数记14章中神因百姓不信的怒气——那一代人被判在旷野倒毙。他看见可拉、大坍、亚比兰被地吞灭。他看见火蛇咬死抱怨的人。摩西亲眼见证了"在你震怒之下"度过日子是什么意思。

"我们度尽的年岁好像一声叹息"——弟兄姐妹，一生就是一声叹息。你深吸一口气——然后呼出来——那一声叹息就是你的一生。这么短。弟兄姐妹，当你真正意识到这一点的时候——你对人生的优先次序应该彻底改变。

**七十岁的数算（第10节）**

> "我们一生的年日是七十岁，若是强壮可到八十岁；但其中所矜夸的不过是劳苦愁烦，转眼成空，我们便如飞而去。"

弟兄姐妹，三千五百年前摩西说人生七十岁——今天人均寿命也差不多。医学进步了——但人生的长度并没有根本改变。"其中所矜夸的不过是劳苦愁烦"——弟兄姐妹，你引以为傲的成就——在永恒中"不过是劳苦愁烦"。不是说成就没有价值——而是说你"矜夸"的东西最终都会"转眼成空"。

**数算日子的祷告（第12节）**

> "求你指教我们怎样数算自己的日子，好叫我们得着智慧的心。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗的核心祈求——也是整本圣经中最实际的祷告之一。"数算日子"——不是数你还能活多少天——而是学会把每一天当作有重量的、有意义的、有限的来过。弟兄姐妹，当你知道你的日子是有限的，你就不会浪费。当你知道每一天都是恩典，你就会珍惜。

"智慧的心"——不是智慧的头脑——而是智慧的"心"。知识是头脑的事——智慧是心的事。你可以知道很多神学知识但没有智慧——因为你的心没有真正面对自己的有限。数算日子带来的不是恐惧——而是智慧。

**最后的祷告（第13-17节）**

> "耶和华啊，我们要等到几时呢？求你转回，怜恤你的仆人！求你使我们早早饱得你的慈爱，好叫我们一生一世欢呼喜乐。求你照着你使我们受苦的日子和我们遭难的年岁，叫我们喜乐。愿你的作为向你仆人显现；愿你的荣耀向他们子孙显明。愿主我们神的荣美归于我们身上。愿你坚立我们手所做的工；我们手所做的工，愿你坚立。"

弟兄姐妹，"早早饱得你的慈爱"——不是晚年、不是退休以后、不是"等我有空了"——而是"早早"。弟兄姐妹，现在就饱得神的慈爱。趁还有"今天"的时候——数算你的日子——求智慧的心——早早饱得神的慈爱。

"愿你坚立我们手所做的工"——这是最后的祷告。弟兄姐妹，人的工作在永恒中有什么价值？只有当神"坚立"的时候。你做的事情——如果不被神坚立——就像草一样"晚上割下枯干"。但如果被神坚立——就有永恒的重量。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses, servant of God. Psalm 90 is the only psalm I wrote during forty years in the wilderness—also the most ancient in the entire Psalter. As I watched generation after generation of Israelites fall in the wilderness, I wrote this psalm about time, eternity, and God's wrath.

**The Eternal God (vv. 1-2)**

> "Lord, you have been our dwelling place in all generations. Before the mountains were brought forth, or ever you had formed the earth and the world, from everlasting to everlasting you are God."

Brothers and sisters, before discussing human brevity, Moses first establishes God's eternity. "From everlasting to everlasting you are God"—no beginning, no end. Mountains—earth's oldest, most stable things—but before God, mountains had a starting moment. God did not. Time is a created thing—God is time's Creator.

> "You have been our dwelling place"—when Moses said this, Israel was homeless wanderers—forty years in wilderness tents. But Moses said: our dwelling is not tents—but God Himself. What do you build your security on? Houses? Jobs? Bank accounts? These are all tents—eventually dismantled. Only God is "our dwelling place in all generations."

**Human Brevity (vv. 3-6)**

> "You return man to dust...For a thousand years in your sight are but as yesterday when it is past, or as a watch in the night. You sweep them away as with a flood; they are like a dream, like grass that is renewed in the morning: in the morning it flourishes and is renewed; in the evening it fades and withers."

Brothers and sisters, "a thousand years like yesterday"—2 Peter 3:8 quotes this: "with the Lord one day is as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day." Your seventy years on earth are not even a moment in God's eternity.

**God's Wrath and Human Sin (vv. 7-9)**

> "For we are brought to an end by your anger...You have set our iniquities before you, our secret sins in the light of your presence. For all our days pass away under your wrath; we bring our years to an end like a sigh."

Brothers and sisters, Moses spent forty years in the wilderness—he witnessed God's anger in Numbers 14. "We bring our years to an end like a sigh"—a lifetime is one sigh. You inhale deeply—then exhale—that sigh is your whole life. When you truly realize this—your life's priorities should fundamentally change.

**Numbering Our Days (v. 12)**

> "So teach us to number our days that we may get a heart of wisdom."

Brothers and sisters, the psalm's core petition—one of Scripture's most practical prayers. "Number our days"—not counting how many days you have left—but learning to live each day as weighty, meaningful, finite. "A heart of wisdom"—not a wise mind but a wise "heart." Knowledge is the mind's business—wisdom is the heart's. You can know much theology without wisdom—because your heart hasn't truly faced your own finitude.

**The Final Prayer (vv. 13-17)**

> "Satisfy us in the morning with your steadfast love, that we may rejoice and be glad all our days. Let the favor of the Lord our God be upon us, and establish the work of our hands upon us; yes, establish the work of our hands!"

Brothers and sisters, "satisfy us in the morning"—not in old age, not after retirement—but "in the morning." Now—be satisfied with God's steadfast love. While there is still "today"—number your days—seek a wise heart—be satisfied early with God's love.

"Establish the work of our hands"—the final prayer. Your work—if not established by God—withers like grass "in the evening." But if established by God—it carries eternal weight.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇90的标题是"神人摩西的祈祷"——这是整本诗篇中唯一归于摩西的诗篇。"神人"这个称号在圣经中很少使用（参申33:1, 书14:6），表明摩西特殊的先知地位。如果这确实是摩西所写，那么这是整本诗篇中最古老的一首——比大卫的诗篇早了约五百年。"人生七十岁"的说法在三千五百年后仍然大致准确——全球平均寿命约72-73岁。"千年如已过的昨日"被彼得后书3:8引用来论证神的时间观念与人不同——特别是关于基督再来的等待。诗篇90在犹太传统中在安息日晚间崇拜中诵读。教会传统中这首诗常在葬礼中使用——特别是第12节"求你指教我们怎样数算自己的日子"。以撒·瓦特（Isaac Watts）基于这首诗写了著名圣诗"Our God, Our Help in Ages Past"。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 90's superscription: "A Prayer of Moses, the man of God"—the only psalm in the Psalter attributed to Moses. "Man of God" is rarely used (cf. Deut 33:1, Josh 14:6), indicating Moses' special prophetic status. If genuinely Mosaic, this is the Psalter's most ancient psalm—about five hundred years before David's psalms. "Seventy years" remains roughly accurate 3,500 years later—global average life expectancy is about 72-73. "A thousand years like yesterday" is quoted in 2 Peter 3:8 regarding God's time perspective—especially concerning waiting for Christ's return. Isaac Watts wrote the famous hymn "Our God, Our Help in Ages Past" based on this psalm.

## Structure (English)

**God's Eternity (vv. 1-2)**—Dwelling place through all generations → before mountains → everlasting to everlasting you are God.

**Human Brevity (vv. 3-6)**—Return man to dust → thousand years like yesterday → swept away → grass, morning blooming, evening withering.

**God's Wrath (vv. 7-9)**—Consumed by anger → sins in the light of your presence → years end like a sigh.

**Seventy Years (v. 10)**—Seventy years, if strong eighty → boast is but toil and trouble → soon gone, we fly away.

**Core Prayer (vv. 11-12)**—Who considers the power of your anger → teach us to number our days → get a heart of wisdom.

**Final Petition (vv. 13-17)**—Return, have pity → satisfy us with steadfast love morning → rejoice all our days → establish the work of our hands.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神的永恒与人的有限——创造者与受造物的区别**
改革宗传统强调创造者与受造物之间的本质区别。韦敏斯德信条第二章教导：只有一位又活又真的神，祂是无限的、永恒的、不可测度的。诗篇90用"千年如昨日"来强调这个区别——人的时间感受和神的永恒之间有无限的鸿沟。

**"数算日子"——时间的管家职分**
改革宗传统教导信徒要做时间的好管家。以弗所书5:16"要爱惜光阴，因为现今的世代邪恶。""数算日子"不是消极的倒数——而是积极的管理：每一天都是神的恩赐，每一天都要为神的荣耀而活。

**"坚立我手所做的工"——天职与呼召**
改革宗传统教导所有合法的工作（vocation）都是神的呼召。"坚立我手所做的工"——不只是属灵的事工——而是一切在神面前做的工。你的日常工作——当它在信心中做、为神的荣耀做的时候——神会"坚立"它。

## Theology (English)

**God's Eternity and Human Finitude—Creator-Creature Distinction**
The Reformed tradition emphasizes the essential distinction between Creator and creature. Westminster Confession chapter 2: There is but one only living and true God...infinite...eternal, incomprehensible. Psalm 90 uses "a thousand years like yesterday" to emphasize this distinction.

**"Number Our Days"—Stewardship of Time**
The Reformed tradition teaches believers to be good stewards of time. Ephesians 5:16: "making the best use of the time, because the days are evil." "Numbering days" is not passive countdown—but active management: every day is God's gift, every day is to be lived for God's glory.

**"Establish the Work of Our Hands"—Vocation and Calling**
The Reformed tradition teaches all legitimate work (vocation) is God's calling. "Establish the work of our hands"—not only spiritual ministry—but all work done before God. Your daily work—when done in faith, for God's glory—God will "establish" it.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督是永恒的"居所"**——约翰福音14:2"在我父的家里有许多住处……我去原是为你们预备地方去。"诗篇90:1"你世世代代作我们的居所"——基督为我们预备了永恒的居所。在基督里，信徒有了真正"世世代代"不改变的居所。

**基督"数算"了祂的日子**——耶稣清楚地知道自己在地上的日子是有限的——"我的时候还没有到"（约7:6），"我的时候近了"（太26:18），"时候到了"（约17:1）。基督完美地"数算"了祂的日子——每一天都为父的旨意而活——最终在十字架上"成了"（约19:30）。

**"早早饱得你的慈爱"——基督满足我们的渴望**——约翰福音6:35"我就是生命的粮。到我这里来的，必定不饿；信我的，永远不渴。"诗篇90:14"求你使我们早早饱得你的慈爱"——基督自己就是那慈爱的彰显——在祂里面我们"饱得"永恒的满足。

**"坚立我手所做的工"——基督坚立信徒的工作**——哥林多前书15:58"你们务要坚固，不可摇动，常常竭力多做主工；因为知道，你们的劳苦在主里面不是徒然的。"在基督里的工作被神"坚立"——不会像草一样枯干——而是有永恒的果效。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Is the Eternal "Dwelling Place"**—John 14:2: "In my Father's house are many rooms...I go to prepare a place for you." Psalm 90:1 "you have been our dwelling place"—Christ prepared an eternal dwelling for us. In Christ, believers have a truly unchanging "dwelling place through all generations."

**Christ "Numbered" His Days**—Jesus clearly knew His earthly days were limited—"Then Jesus said unto them, My time is not yet come: but your time is alway ready." (John 7:6), "And he said, Go into the city to such a man, and say unto him, The Master saith, My time is at hand; I will keep the passover at thy house with my disciples." (Matt 26:18), "These words spake Jesus, and lifted up his eyes to heaven, and said, Father, the hour is come; glorify thy Son, that thy Son also may glorify thee:" (John 17:1). Christ perfectly "numbered" His days—each day lived for the Father's will—finally on the cross "When Jesus therefore had received the vinegar, he said, It is finished: and he bowed his head, and gave up the ghost." (John 19:30).

**"Satisfy Us with Your Steadfast Love"—Christ Satisfies Our Longing**—John 6:35: "I am the bread of life; whoever comes to me shall not hunger, and whoever believes in me shall never thirst." Psalm 90:14 "satisfy us in the morning with your steadfast love"—Christ Himself is that love's manifestation—in Him we are "satisfied" with eternal fullness.

**"Establish the Work of Our Hands"—Christ Establishes Believers' Work**—1 Corinthians 15:58: "Be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that in the Lord your labor is not in vain." Work in Christ is "established" by God—not withering like grass—but bearing eternal fruit.
