## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第八篇是一首仰望星空时写下的赞美诗——从无限的宇宙到渺小的人类，从婴孩的口中到万物的管理，这首诗问了一个震撼灵魂的问题："人算什么？"

**宇宙的主宰（第1节）**

> "耶和华我们的主啊，你的名在全地何其美！你将你的荣耀彰显于天。"

弟兄姐妹，这首诗以赞美开始也以赞美结束——第1节和第9节完全相同。这是一个包围结构——整首诗被赞美包裹着。中间所有的思考、惊叹、疑问，都在赞美的框架之内。弟兄姐妹，真正的思考不会离开赞美——越深入思考，越发现值得赞美。

"你的名在全地何其美"——不是"在以色列何其美"——是"在全地"。耶和华不是以色列的地方神——祂是全地的主。

**婴孩的赞美（第2节）**

> "你因敌人的缘故，从婴孩和吃奶的口中建立了能力，使仇敌和报仇的闭口无言。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整本圣经最奇妙的悖论之一——神用最软弱的器皿（婴孩和吃奶的）来彰显最大的能力。不是用军队、不是用智者、不是用勇士——而是用婴孩。为什么？"因敌人的缘故"——为了让仇敌"闭口无言"。弟兄姐妹，当全能的神选择通过婴孩来做工的时候——所有反对神的力量都无话可说。你怎么跟一个婴孩争辩？你怎么对抗一个吃奶的孩子的赞美？

主耶稣在圣殿里被法利赛人质疑时，引用了这节经文（太21:16）。当孩童们在圣殿里喊"和散那"的时候——法利赛人很恼怒。耶稣说："经上说'你从婴孩和吃奶的口中完全了赞美'的话，你们没有念过吗？"弟兄姐妹，神常常通过我们觉得"不够格"的人来成就祂的旨意。

**仰望星空的震撼（第3-4节）**

> "我观看你指头所造的天，并你所陈设的月亮星宿，便说：人算什么，你竟顾念他？世人算什么，你竟眷顾他？"

弟兄姐妹，我在某个夜晚仰望星空——没有光污染的古代夜空，银河横跨天际，繁星密布——我看到了神"指头"所造的天。弟兄姐妹，"指头"——不是"手臂"、不是"全力"——只是"指头"轻轻一点。以赛亚书40:12说神"用手虎口量天"——创造宇宙对神来说，就像你用手指量一段距离那么随意。

然后我问了那个千古之问："人算什么？"——面对无限的宇宙，人类何等渺小。现代天文学告诉我们地球只是银河系数千亿颗恒星中一颗普通恒星的一颗行星——而银河系只是可观测宇宙中数千亿个星系之一。人——连宇宙的一粒灰尘都算不上。

但我的问题不是绝望的——而是惊叹的。"你竟顾念他"——"竟"字包含了全部的惊奇。创造宇宙的神"竟然"顾念这个在宇宙中微不足道的物种？这不是理所当然的——这是恩典。

**人的尊荣（第5-8节）**

> "你叫他比天使微小一点，并赐他荣耀尊贵为冠冕。你派他管理你手所造的，使万物，就是一切的牛羊、田野的兽、空中的鸟、海里的鱼，凡经行海道的，都服在他的脚下。"

弟兄姐妹，从"人算什么"的渺小感——一转成了"比天使微小一点"的尊荣。人不是宇宙的垃圾——人是神按自己形像造的、被"赐荣耀尊贵为冠冕"的受造物。弟兄姐妹，你的价值不取决于你在宇宙中的物理大小——而取决于造你的那位对你的看法。在神眼中——你戴着荣耀和尊贵的冠冕。

"管理你手所造的"——这回到了创世记1:28的文化使命。神造人不是为了让人渺小地活着——而是要人代表神管理受造界。牛羊、野兽、飞鸟、海鱼——都在人的管理之下。这不是人的自大——这是神的委任。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇第八篇的信息是：你同时是渺小的和尊贵的。面对宇宙你是微尘——但面对神你是戴冠冕的管理者。不要因为渺小而自卑——也不要因为尊贵而骄傲。你的渺小让你需要神——你的尊贵是神给你的。这两个真相必须同时持守——丢掉任何一个，你就偏离了真理。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 8 is a hymn of praise written while gazing at the stars—from the infinite universe to tiny humanity, from infants' mouths to dominion over creation, this psalm asks a soul-shaking question: "What is man?"

**The Sovereign of the Universe (v. 1)**

> "O LORD, our Lord, how majestic is your name in all the earth! You have set your glory above the heavens."

Brothers and sisters, this psalm begins and ends with praise—verses 1 and 9 are identical. An envelope structure—the entire psalm wrapped in praise. All the thinking, wonder, and questioning in between remain within praise's framework. Brothers and sisters, true contemplation never leaves praise—the deeper you think, the more you find worthy of praise.

**Infants' Praise (v. 2)**

> "Out of the mouth of babies and infants, you have established strength because of your foes, to still the enemy and the avenger."

Brothers and sisters, one of Scripture's most wondrous paradoxes—God uses the weakest vessels (babies and nursing infants) to display the greatest power. Not armies, not sages, not warriors—but babies. Why? "Because of your foes"—to silence the enemy. When Almighty God chooses to work through infants—all opposing forces are speechless.

Jesus quoted this verse in the temple when Pharisees challenged Him (Matt 21:16). When children cried "Hosanna" in the temple—the Pharisees were indignant. Jesus responded: "Have you never read, 'Out of the mouth of infants and nursing babies you have prepared praise'?"

**The Stargazing Wonder (vv. 3-4)**

> "When I look at your heavens, the work of your fingers, the moon and the stars, which you have set in place, what is man that you are mindful of him, and the son of man that you care for him?"

Brothers and sisters, On some night I gazed at the stars—the ancient sky without light pollution, the Milky Way spanning the heavens, stars densely scattered—I saw heavens made by God's "fingers." Brothers and sisters, "fingers"—not "arms," not "full strength"—just a casual touch of fingers. Isaiah 40:12 says God "marked off the heavens with a span"—creating the universe was as casual as measuring a distance with your fingers.

Then I asked the eternal question: "What is man?"—against the infinite universe, how infinitely small humanity is. Modern astronomy tells us Earth is one planet of one ordinary star among hundreds of billions in the Milky Way—and the Milky Way is one of hundreds of billions of galaxies. Humanity—not even a speck of cosmic dust.

But my question was not despair—it was wonder. "That you are mindful of him"—the marvel is total. The God who created the universe is "actually" mindful of this cosmically insignificant species? This is not entitlement—this is grace.

**Human Dignity (vv. 5-8)**

> "Yet you have made him a little lower than the heavenly beings and crowned him with glory and honor. You have given him dominion over the works of your hands; you have put all things under his feet."

Brothers and sisters, from "what is man's" smallness—a sudden turn to "a little lower than the angels'" dignity. Humanity is not cosmic refuse—humanity is created in God's image, "crowned with glory and honor." Brothers and sisters, your value is not determined by your physical size in the universe—but by your Creator's view of you. In God's eyes—you wear a crown of glory and honor.

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 8's message: you are simultaneously tiny and dignified. Before the universe you are dust—but before God you are a crowned steward. Do not be defeated by smallness—nor proud because of dignity. Your smallness makes you need God—your dignity is God's gift. Both truths must be held simultaneously—lose either one and you depart from truth.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇第八篇的标题是"大卫的诗，交与伶长。用迦特乐器。""迦特乐器"（gittith）可能指一种来自非利士城市迦特的乐器，或与收割葡萄（gat=酒醡）有关的欢乐曲调。"伶长"表明这是圣殿崇拜中使用的诗歌。"人算什么"的问题在旧约中多次出现——约伯记7:17"人算什么，你竟看他为大"（带有控诉的语气）和诗篇144:3"人算什么，你竟认识他"。但诗篇8的语气是赞美和敬畏，不是抱怨。"比天使微小一点"的希伯来原文是"比elohim微小一点"——elohim可以指神、天使或审判者。七十士译本翻译为"比天使微小"，希伯来书2:7引用了这个译法。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 8's superscription reads "A Psalm of David. To the choirmaster: according to The Gittith." "Gittith" may refer to an instrument from the Philistine city of Gath, or a joyful tune related to grape harvest (gat = winepress). "Choirmaster" indicates this was used in temple worship. "What is man" appears multiple times in the Old Testament—Job 7:17 "What is man, that you make so much of him" (with accusatory tone) and Psalm 144:3 "What is man that you regard him." But Psalm 8's tone is praise and awe, not complaint. "A little lower than the heavenly beings" in Hebrew is "a little lower than elohim"—elohim can mean God, angels, or judges. The Septuagint translated it as "lower than the angels," which Hebrews 2:7 follows.

## 经文结构（中文）

**开头赞美（第1节）**——"耶和华我们的主啊，你的名在全地何其美"：全地的主。

**婴孩的能力（第2节）**——从最软弱者口中建立能力→使仇敌闭口。

**仰望星空的惊叹（第3-4节）**——神指头所造的天→"人算什么你竟顾念他"。

**人的尊荣和使命（第5-8节）**——比天使微小一点→荣耀尊贵为冠冕→管理万物。

**结尾赞美（第9节）**——与第1节完全相同：包围结构。

## Structure (English)

**Opening Praise (v. 1)**—"O LORD, our Lord, how majestic is your name in all the earth": Lord of all.

**Infants' Strength (v. 2)**—Strength established from the weakest mouths → enemies silenced.

**Stargazing Wonder (vv. 3-4)**—Heavens, work of God's fingers → "What is man that you are mindful of him."

**Human Dignity and Mission (vv. 5-8)**—A little lower than angels → crowned with glory and honor → dominion over all.

**Closing Praise (v. 9)**—Identical to verse 1: envelope structure.

## 神学要点（中文）

**人的渺小与尊贵——形像神学**
人同时渺小又尊贵——改革宗传统教导人按神的形像被造。韦敏斯德小要理问答第10问："神照着自己的形像造男造女，有知识、仁义、圣洁，并赋予他们管理、支配一切受造之物的权柄。"人的价值不在大小——而在形像。

**"比天使微小一点"——道成肉身的预备**
希伯来书2:6-9将诗篇8应用于基督——基督"暂时比天使小"，然后"因为受死的苦，就得了荣耀尊贵为冠冕"。改革宗传统教导基督的降卑与升高。韦敏斯德小要理问答第27-28问。

**管理受造界——文化使命**
"派他管理"——改革宗传统教导文化使命。创世记1:28的"治理这地"不因堕落而取消。人仍有管理受造界的责任——农业、科学、艺术都是文化使命的一部分。但管理不是掠夺——是代表神的管家职分。

## Theology (English)

**Human Smallness and Dignity—Image Theology**
Humanity is simultaneously small and dignified—the Reformed tradition teaches humans were created in God's image. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q10: "God created man in his own image, in knowledge, righteousness, and holiness, with dominion over the creatures." Human value lies not in size—but in image.

**"A Little Lower than Angels"—Preparation for Incarnation**
Hebrews 2:6-9 applies Psalm 8 to Christ—Christ was "for a little while made lower than the angels," then "crowned with glory and honor because of the suffering of death." The Reformed tradition teaches Christ's humiliation and exaltation. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q27-28.

**Dominion over Creation—The Cultural Mandate**
"Given dominion"—the Reformed tradition teaches the cultural mandate. Genesis 1:28's "have dominion" was not canceled by the fall. Humanity still bears responsibility for creation—agriculture, science, arts are all part of the cultural mandate. But dominion is not exploitation—it is God-representing stewardship.

## 基督的影子（中文）

诗篇第八篇在新约中被明确应用于基督。

**希伯来书2:6-9——基督是"人子"**
"人算什么"的"人"在希伯来书中被解释为基督——"那成为比天使小一点的耶稣，因为受死的苦，就得了荣耀尊贵为冠冕。"弟兄姐妹，基督降卑为人（比天使微小一点），然后因受死得荣耀（荣耀尊贵为冠冕）。诗篇8的"人"最终指向的是那位完美的人——耶稣基督。

**哥林多前书15:27——万物服在基督脚下**
"使万物都服在他的脚下"——保罗引用诗篇8:6应用于基督的最终得胜。"既叫万物都服他，就没有剩下一样不服他的。"弟兄姐妹，亚当失去了对万物的管理权——基督恢复了。在基督里，诗篇8的应许得到了终极的成全。

**马太福音21:16——婴孩的赞美**
耶稣在圣殿引用"从婴孩和吃奶的口中完全了赞美"——指向祂自己接受孩童的"和散那"赞美。弟兄姐妹，如果诗篇8:2是关于赞美耶和华——而耶稣将它应用于自己——那么耶稣就是在宣告：祂就是耶和华。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Psalm 8 is explicitly applied to Christ in the New Testament.

**Hebrews 2:6-9—Christ Is the "Son of Man"**
"What is man" in Hebrews is interpreted as Christ—"But we see him who for a little while was made lower than the angels, namely Jesus, crowned with glory and honor because of the suffering of death." Brothers and sisters, Christ humbled Himself as man (a little lower than angels), then was glorified through death (crowned with glory and honor). Psalm 8's "man" ultimately points to the perfect man—Jesus Christ.

**1 Corinthians 15:27—All Things Under Christ's Feet**
"Putting everything in subjection under his feet"—Paul quotes Psalm 8:6 applied to Christ's ultimate victory. "When it says 'all things are put in subjection,' it is plain that he is excepted who put all things in subjection under him." Brothers and sisters, Adam lost dominion over creation—Christ restored it.

**Matthew 21:16—Infants' Praise**
Jesus quoted "Out of the mouth of infants you have prepared praise" in the temple—pointing to Himself receiving children's "Hosanna" praise. Brothers and sisters, if Psalm 8:2 is about praising Yahweh—and Jesus applied it to Himself—then Jesus was declaring: He is Yahweh.
