## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第一百一十篇是整本旧约中最重要的弥赛亚预言之一——它被新约引用或提及超过二十次，比任何其他旧约经文都多。在这首诗里，我得到了一个关于我后裔的异象——一位既是君王又是祭司的弥赛亚。

**坐在右边的命令（第1节）**

> "耶和华对我主说：你坐在我的右边，等我使你仇敌作你的脚凳。"

弟兄姐妹，"耶和华对我主说"——这是两位之间的对话——"耶和华"（YHWH，圣父）对"我主"（Adonai，弥赛亚）说话。弟兄姐妹，我称弥赛亚为"我主"——但弥赛亚是我的后裔。后裔怎么会是祖先的"主"呢？耶稣用这个问题让法利赛人无言以对（太22:41-45）——"大卫既称他为主，他怎么又是大卫的子孙呢？"答案是：弥赛亚既是大卫的子孙（人性）又是大卫的主（神性）——这就是道成肉身的奥秘。

> "坐在我的右边"

古代王庭中，坐在王的右边是最高的荣耀。弟兄姐妹，弥赛亚被邀请坐在耶和华的右边——这意味着祂与耶和华同等尊荣。使徒行传2:34-35彼得在五旬节引用了这节经文证明耶稣的复活和升天——"大卫并没有升到天上，但自己说：主对我主说：你坐在我的右边。"

"等我使你仇敌作你的脚凳"——弟兄姐妹，基督现在坐在天父的右边——但祂的仇敌还没有全部被征服。保罗在哥林多前书15:25说："因为基督必要做王，等神把一切仇敌都放在他的脚下。"弟兄姐妹，我们活在"已然和未然"之间——基督已经坐在右边了（已然），但仇敌还没有全部作脚凳（未然）。

**锡安的权杖（第2-3节）**

> "耶和华必使你从锡安伸出能力的杖来；你要在你仇敌中掌权。当你掌权的日子，你的民要以圣洁的妆饰为衣，甘心牺牲自己；你的民多如清晨的甘露。"

弟兄姐妹，"你的民要甘心牺牲自己"——弥赛亚的子民不是被迫服从的奴隶——而是"甘心"献上的战士。弟兄姐妹，跟随基督不是强制的——而是甘心的。你的奉献不是被迫的——而是因为看见了王的荣耀而甘心摆上自己。

**永远的祭司（第4节）**

> "耶和华起了誓，决不后悔，说：你是照着麦基洗德的等次永远为祭司。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗——也是整本旧约——最震撼的一句话。弥赛亚不只是君王——祂还是"祭司"。而且不是亚伦等次的祭司——而是"麦基洗德等次"的祭司。

弟兄姐妹，为什么是麦基洗德而不是亚伦？希伯来书7章详细解释了这一点。亚伦的祭司职是暂时的——祭司会死，需要继任者。但麦基洗德在创世记14章突然出现、没有记载家谱、没有记载出生和死亡——他是"永远"做祭司的象征。弟兄姐妹，基督的祭司职是永远的——"他是长远活着，替他们祈求"（来7:25）。

而且——在以色列，君王和祭司是严格分开的。犹大支派出君王，利未支派出祭司。乌西雅王想进圣殿烧香就长了大麻风（代下26:16-21）。但弥赛亚打破了这个分隔——祂既是犹大支派的王，又是麦基洗德等次的永远祭司。弟兄姐妹，只有一位能同时是王和祭司——就是耶稣基督。

**审判列国（第5-7节）**

> "在你右边的主，当他发怒的日子，必打伤列王。他要在列邦中刑罚恶人，尸首就遍满各处；他要在许多国中打破仇敌的头。他要喝路旁的河水，因此必抬起头来。"

弟兄姐妹，这首诗的结尾描述了弥赛亚审判列国的景象。"打破仇敌的头"——这回到了创世记3:15"他要伤你的头"——女人的后裔要打碎蛇的头。弟兄姐妹，从创世记3:15到诗篇110再到十字架上的最终得胜——这是一条贯穿圣经的线索。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇110在七节之内宣告了弥赛亚的身份——祂坐在神的右边（神性）、从锡安掌权（君王职分）、永远做祭司（祭司职分）、审判列国（先知审判职分）。弟兄姐妹，基督的三重职分——先知、祭司、君王——全在这七节经文里。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 110 is one of the most important messianic prophecies in the entire Old Testament—quoted or referenced in the New Testament over twenty times, more than any other Old Testament text. In this psalm I received a vision of my descendant—a Messiah who is both King and Priest.

**The Command to Sit at the Right Hand (v. 1)**

> "The LORD says to my Lord: 'Sit at my right hand, until I make your enemies your footstool.'"

Brothers and sisters, "the LORD says to my Lord"—a conversation between two—"the LORD" (YHWH, the Father) speaks to "my Lord" (Adonai, the Messiah). I call the Messiah "my Lord"—but the Messiah is David's descendant. How can a descendant be an ancestor's "Lord"? Jesus used this question to silence the Pharisees (Matt 22:41-45). The answer: the Messiah is both David's son (humanity) and David's Lord (deity)—the mystery of the incarnation.

"Sit at my right hand"—in ancient royal courts, sitting at the king's right hand was the highest honor. The Messiah is invited to sit at the LORD's right hand—meaning He shares equal honor with the LORD. Acts 2:34-35: Peter quoted this at Pentecost proving Jesus' resurrection and ascension.

> "Until I make your enemies your footstool"—Christ now sits at the Father's right hand—but His enemies are not yet fully conquered. 1 Corinthians 15:25: "For he must reign until he has put all his enemies under his feet." We live between "already and not yet."

**A Priest Forever (v. 4)**

> "The LORD has sworn and will not change his mind, 'You are a priest forever after the order of Melchizedek.'"

Brothers and sisters, the psalm's—and the Old Testament's—most staggering statement. The Messiah is not only King—He is also "Priest." Not in Aaron's order—but in "Melchizedek's order."

Why Melchizedek, not Aaron? Hebrews 7 explains in detail. Aaron's priesthood was temporary—priests died, needing successors. But Melchizedek in Genesis 14 appears suddenly with no recorded genealogy, birth, or death—symbolizing "forever" priesthood. Christ's priesthood is eternal—"Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them." (Heb 7:25).

Moreover—in Israel, kingship and priesthood were strictly separated. Judah's tribe produced kings; Levi's tribe produced priests. King Uzziah was struck with leprosy for attempting to burn incense in the temple (2 Chron 26:16-21). But the Messiah breaks this separation—He is both Judah's King and Melchizedek's eternal Priest. Only one person can be both King and Priest—Jesus Christ.

**Judging the Nations (vv. 5-7)**

> "He will shatter kings on the day of his wrath. He will execute judgment among the nations...crushing heads over the wide earth."

Brothers and sisters, "crushing heads"—returning to Genesis 3:15 "he shall bruise your head"—the woman's offspring crushing the serpent's head. From Genesis 3:15 to Psalm 110 to the cross's final victory—one thread running through all Scripture.

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 110 declares in seven verses the Messiah's identity—sitting at God's right hand (deity), ruling from Zion (kingly office), eternal priest (priestly office), judging nations (prophetic/judicial office). Christ's threefold office—Prophet, Priest, King—all contained in these seven verses.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇110是新约中被引用最多的旧约经文——超过二十次直接引用或间接提及。耶稣自己在马太福音22:41-45引用第1节来挑战法利赛人对弥赛亚的理解。彼得在五旬节讲道中引用（徒2:34-35），保罗多次引用（林前15:25, 弗1:20-22），希伯来书大量引用第4节论麦基洗德（来5:6, 7:17, 7:21）。"麦基洗德"在创世记14:18-20首次出现——他是撒冷王又是至高神的祭司，亚伯拉罕向他献了十分之一。在以色列历史中，只有耶路撒冷的"麦基洗德等次"才能将王权和祭司职合一——因为正统以色列制度中这两个职分是严格分开的。犹太传统对此篇有不同解读——一些拉比将其解释为关于亚伯拉罕或大卫，但基督教传统从使徒时代起就一致将其理解为弥赛亚预言。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 110 is the most-cited Old Testament text in the New Testament—over twenty direct quotes or allusions. Jesus Himself quoted verse 1 in Matthew 22:41-45 to challenge Pharisees' understanding of the Messiah. Peter quoted it at Pentecost (Acts 2:34-35), Paul multiple times (1 Cor 15:25, Eph 1:20-22), and Hebrews extensively quotes verse 4 on Melchizedek (Heb 5:6, 7:17, 7:21). "Melchizedek" first appears in Genesis 14:18-20—king of Salem and priest of God Most High, to whom Abraham gave a tithe. In Israelite history, only Jerusalem's "Melchizedekian order" could unite kingship and priesthood—since in orthodox Israelite polity these offices were strictly separated. Jewish tradition varies in interpretation—some rabbis apply it to Abraham or David, but Christian tradition since the apostolic age unanimously understands it as messianic prophecy.

## 神学要点（中文）

**基督的三重职分——先知、祭司、君王**
韦敏斯德小要理问答第23-26问详细论述了基督的三重职分。诗篇110在七节之内包含了全部三个职分：君王（1-3节"坐在右边""从锡安掌权"）、祭司（4节"照麦基洗德等次永远为祭司"）、先知/审判者（5-7节"审判列国"）。

**基督的神人二性——大卫的主又是大卫的子孙**
韦敏斯德信条第8章论中保基督：主耶稣基督……是真实的神和真实的人……两个完全的本性……合于一个位格中。诗篇110:1的"我主"揭示了弥赛亚的神性——祂是大卫的后裔（人性）却又是大卫的主（神性）。

**基督坐在右边——现在的统治**
改革宗传统教导基督现在正在统治。韦敏斯德小要理问答第28问："基督的升高，在于第三日从死里复活，升上高天，坐在父神的右边，并要在末日来审判世界。"诗篇110:1的"坐在我的右边"不是等待——而是掌权。基督现在就在掌权——直到一切仇敌都被放在祂脚下。

## Theology (English)

**Christ's Threefold Office—Prophet, Priest, King**
Westminster Shorter Catechism Q23-26 details Christ's threefold office. Psalm 110 contains all three in seven verses: King (vv. 1-3), Priest (v. 4), Prophet/Judge (vv. 5-7).

**Christ's Two Natures—David's Lord and David's Son**
Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ the mediator: "The Lord Jesus Christ...being very God and very man...two whole natures...joined together in one person." Psalm 110:1's "my Lord" reveals the Messiah's deity—David's descendant (humanity) yet David's Lord (deity).

**Christ Seated at the Right Hand—Present Reign**
The Reformed tradition teaches Christ now reigns. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q28: "Christ's exaltation consists in his rising again...ascending...sitting at the right hand of God the Father." Psalm 110:1's "sit at my right hand" is not waiting—but reigning. Christ reigns now—until all enemies are put under His feet.

## 基督的影子（中文）

诗篇110是旧约中最全面的基督预言。

**第1节——基督的升天和坐在右边**——使徒行传2:34-36彼得宣告："这位耶稣，神已经立他为主、为基督了。"以弗所书1:20-21"叫基督从死里复活，叫他在天上坐在自己的右边，远超过一切执政的、掌权的、有能的、主治的。"

**第4节——基督的永远祭司职**——希伯来书7:23-25"那些祭司的数目本来多，是因为有死阻隔，不能长久。这位既是永远常存的，他祭司的职任就长久不更换。凡靠着他进到神面前的人，他都能拯救到底；因为他是长远活着，替他们祈求。"基督不像亚伦的祭司需要每天献祭——祂一次献祭成就了永远的赎罪（来10:12）。

**第1节——"已然未然"的框架**——基督"已经"坐在右边（复活升天），但祂的仇敌"尚未"全部被征服。这个"已然未然"（already-not yet）的框架定义了教会时代——我们活在基督第一次来（已然）和第二次来（未然）之间。哥林多前书15:24-26"再后，末期到了……最后所毁灭的仇敌就是死。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Psalm 110 is the Old Testament's most comprehensive prophecy of Christ.

**Verse 1—Christ's Ascension and Sitting at the Right Hand**—Acts 2:34-36: Peter declared "God has made him both Lord and Christ, this Jesus." Ephesians 1:20-21: "He raised him from the dead and seated him at his right hand in the heavenly places, far above all rule and authority."

**Verse 4—Christ's Eternal Priesthood**—Hebrews 7:23-25: "The former priests were many in number, because they were prevented by death from continuing in office, but he holds his priesthood permanently, because he continues forever. Consequently, he is able to save to the uttermost those who draw near to God through him, since he always lives to make intercession for them." Christ doesn't need daily sacrifices like Aaronic priests—He made one offering that achieved eternal atonement (Heb 10:12).

**Verse 1—The "Already-Not Yet" Framework**—Christ "already" sits at the right hand (resurrection-ascension), but His enemies are "not yet" fully conquered. This "already-not yet" framework defines the church age—we live between Christ's first coming (already) and second coming (not yet). 1 Corinthians 15:24-26: "Then comes the end...The last enemy to be destroyed is death."
