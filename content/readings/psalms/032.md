## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第三十二篇是我在深深的认罪之后写下的——这首诗不是关于罪的痛苦，而是关于认罪之后的释放。如果诗篇51是认罪的呼喊，那么诗篇32就是赦免之后的欢歌。

**赦免的福乐（第1-2节）**

> "得赦免其过的，遮盖其罪的，这人是有福的！凡心里没有诡诈、耶和华不算为有罪的，这人是有福的！"

弟兄姐妹，这首诗一开始就给了你结论——"有福的"。三个平行表达描述同一件事：赦免其过、遮盖其罪、不算为有罪。弟兄姐妹，"赦免"是把重担卸下——你不再背着它了。"遮盖"是把罪遮住——神不再看它了。"不算为有罪"是法庭的宣告——你被判无罪了。

弟兄姐妹，保罗在罗马书4:6-8引用了这段经文来论证"因信称义"——"正如大卫称那在行为以外蒙神算为义的人是有福的，说：得赦免其过、遮盖其罪的，这人是有福的。主不算为有罪的，这人是有福的。"弟兄姐妹，我在一千年前就唱出了因信称义的福音！

**隐藏罪的痛苦（第3-4节）**

> "我闭口不认罪的时候，因终日唉哼而骨头枯干。黑夜白日，你的手在我身上沉重；我的精液耗尽，如同夏天的干旱。"

弟兄姐妹，我在这里描述了不认罪的代价——不是神在惩罚他，而是他自己在受苦。"骨头枯干"——不认罪的人从里面枯萎。"精液耗尽如同夏天的干旱"——生命力被榨干了。弟兄姐妹，隐藏的罪不会自己消失——它会从里面吃掉你。

"你的手在我身上沉重"——这是神的管教之手。弟兄姐妹，当你隐藏罪不认的时候——你有没有感觉到一种沉重？那不是焦虑症——那是圣灵在你良心上施加的压力。这个压力是恩典——因为神在逼你走向认罪。不被管教的人才应该担心——"因为主所爱的他必管教"（来12:6）。

**认罪的突破（第5节）**

> "我向你陈明我的罪，不隐瞒我的恶。我说：我要向耶和华承认我的过犯，你就赦免我的罪恶。"

弟兄姐妹，这一节是整首诗的转折点——从痛苦到释放。三个动词："陈明""不隐瞒""承认"——三种方式说同一件事：完全坦白。弟兄姐妹，认罪不是说"我有些不好的地方"——而是把罪名指出来："我犯了这个罪。"

"你就赦免我的罪恶"——弟兄姐妹，注意速度。不是"你慢慢考虑"、不是"你先看看我表现"——而是"你就赦免"。认罪和赦免之间没有间隔。约翰一书1:9"我们若认自己的罪，神是信实的，是公义的，必要赦免我们的罪，洗净我们一切的不义。"弟兄姐妹，神赦免你不是因为你认罪认得够好——而是因为基督的血够用。

**神的引导与保护（第6-8节）**

> "为此，凡虔诚人都当趁你可寻找的时候祷告你；大水泛溢的时候，必不能到他那里。你是我藏身之处；你必保佑我脱离苦难，以得救的乐歌四面环绕我。我要教导你，指示你当行的路；我要定睛在你身上劝戒你。"

弟兄姐妹，"趁你可寻找的时候"——认罪有时间窗口。以赛亚书55:6"当趁耶和华可寻找的时候寻找他。"弟兄姐妹，不要拖延认罪——今天就认。明天你的心可能更硬了。

第8节是神的直接回应——"我要教导你指示你当行的路。"弟兄姐妹，认罪不是终点——而是新路的起点。神赦免你之后不是把你扔在原地——祂接着"指示你当行的路"。

**不要像骡马（第9节）**

> "你不可像那无知的骡马，必用嚼环辔头勒住他；不然就不能驯近你。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗最直白的劝勉——不要像骡马。骡马需要嚼环辔头——外力的强制——才肯走正路。弟兄姐妹，神不想用嚼环勒你——祂想用话语引导你。但如果你硬着脖子不听——神就不得不用更强的手段来引导你。管教越来越重——直到你肯回转。弟兄姐妹，不要做骡马——要做听话的羊。

**结论：义人的欢呼（第10-11节）**

> "恶人必多受苦楚；惟独倚靠耶和华的必有慈爱四面环绕他。你们义人应当靠耶和华欢喜快乐；你们心里正直的人都当欢呼。"

弟兄姐妹，诗篇32从"有福的"开始，到"欢呼"结束。从赦免的福乐到义人的欢呼——整首诗洋溢着得赦免之后的喜乐。弟兄姐妹，基督徒的生活不应该是阴沉的——它应该是充满了得赦免之后的欢呼。你知道你的罪已经被赦免了吗？那就欢呼吧！

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 32 was written after deep confession—this psalm is not about sin's agony but about the release that follows confession. If Psalm 51 is the cry of confession, Psalm 32 is the song of joy after forgiveness.

**The Blessedness of Forgiveness (vv. 1-2)**

> "Blessed is the one whose transgression is forgiven, whose sin is covered. Blessed is the man against whom the LORD counts no iniquity, and in whose spirit there is no deceit."

Brothers and sisters, three parallel expressions for the same reality: transgression forgiven, sin covered, iniquity not counted. Paul quoted this passage in Romans 4:6-8 to argue for justification by faith—I sang the gospel of justification by faith a thousand years in advance!

**The Pain of Hidden Sin (vv. 3-4)**

> "For when I kept silent, my bones wasted away through my groaning all day long. For day and night your hand was heavy upon me; my strength was dried up as by the heat of summer."

Brothers and sisters, I describe the cost of unconfessed sin. "Bones wasted away"—the unconfessing person withers from within. "Your hand was heavy upon me"—God's disciplining hand. When you hide sin—have you felt heaviness? That's not anxiety—that's the Holy Spirit pressing your conscience. This pressure is grace—God driving you toward confession.

**The Breakthrough of Confession (v. 5)**

> "I acknowledged my sin to you, and I did not cover my iniquity; I said, 'I will confess my transgressions to the LORD,' and you forgave the iniquity of my sin."

Brothers and sisters, this verse is the turning point—from agony to release. "You forgave"—notice the speed. Not "you'll think about it," not "let's see your performance"—but "you forgave." No gap between confession and forgiveness. 1 John 1:9: "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness."

**God's Guidance (vv. 6-8)**

> "Therefore let everyone who is godly offer prayer to you at a time when you may be found. I will instruct you and teach you in the way you should go; I will counsel you with my eye upon you."

Brothers and sisters, "at a time when you may be found"—confession has a time window. Isaiah 55:6: "Seek the LORD while he may be found." Don't delay confession—confess today.

**Don't Be Like a Mule (v. 9)**

> "Be not like a horse or a mule, without understanding, which must be curbed with bit and bridle, or it will not stay near you."

Brothers and sisters, the psalm's bluntest exhortation—don't be like a mule. Mules need bits and bridles—external force—to walk the right path. God doesn't want to bridle you—He wants to guide you with words. But if you stiffen your neck—God must use stronger means.

**Conclusion: The Righteous Shout for Joy (vv. 10-11)**

> "Many are the sorrows of the wicked, but steadfast love surrounds the one who trusts in the LORD. Be glad in the LORD, and rejoice, O righteous, and shout for joy, all you upright in heart."

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 32 begins with "blessed" and ends with "shout for joy." The Christian life should not be somber—it should overflow with the joy of forgiveness. Do you know your sins are forgiven? Then shout for joy!

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇32的标题是"大卫的训诲诗"（maskil）——这是诗篇中第一首被称为"训诲诗"的。"训诲"暗示这首诗的目的不仅是个人表达，更是要教导他人。传统认为这首诗是大卫在犯了拔示巴的罪之后写的——与诗篇51的背景相同，但诗篇51侧重认罪的过程，而诗篇32侧重赦免后的喜乐。奥古斯丁特别喜爱这首诗——他把它列为七首"悔罪诗篇"之一（诗6, 32, 38, 51, 102, 130, 143），据说在临终前让人把诗篇32写在墙上以便阅读。保罗在罗马书4:6-8引用了第1-2节来论证"因信称义"的教义，使这首诗成为新约神学中极为重要的旧约经文。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 32's superscription is "A Maskil of David"—the first psalm called "maskil" (instruction). "Instruction" suggests this psalm's purpose is not merely personal expression but teaching others. Tradition holds it was written after David's sin with Bathsheba—sharing Psalm 51's background, but while Psalm 51 focuses on the confession process, Psalm 32 focuses on post-forgiveness joy. Augustine particularly loved this psalm—he listed it among seven "penitential psalms" (Pss 6, 32, 38, 51, 102, 130, 143) and reportedly had Psalm 32 written on his wall near death. Paul quoted verses 1-2 in Romans 4:6-8 to argue for justification by faith, making this psalm extremely important in New Testament theology.

**The Blessedness of Forgiveness (vv. 1-2)**—Forgiven: blessed → covered: blessed → not counted: blessed.

**The Pain of Hidden Sin (vv. 3-4)**—Kept silent → bones wasted → God's hand heavy → strength dried up.

**The Breakthrough of Confession (v. 5)**—Acknowledged + did not cover + confessed → "you forgave."

**Exhortation: Don't Be Like a Mule (v. 9)**—Mules need bit and bridle → don't stiffen your neck.

**The Righteous Shout for Joy (vv. 10-11)**—Steadfast love surrounds the trusting → righteous rejoice → upright shout for joy.

## 神学要点（中文）

**因信称义——保罗的引用**
罗马书4:6-8引用诗篇32:1-2论证因信称义——"在行为以外蒙神算为义的人是有福的。"改革宗传统教导称义唯独因信。韦敏斯德信条第11章：神白白赐恩，使罪人称义……不是因为在他们里面注入了什么，或因他们所做的什么……乃是单靠基督和他的义。

**认罪与赦免——不可分割**
改革宗传统教导认罪是信徒持续的操练。韦敏斯德信条第15章：人不应当满足于笼统的认罪，每一个人都当努力具体地认自己各别的罪。诗篇32:5的三个动词——陈明、不隐瞒、承认——强调具体而彻底的认罪。

**神的管教——恩典的手段**
韦敏斯德信条第五章教导：对于那些犯罪的神的子民，祂使他们经历各种对付、艰难和管教。诗篇32:3-4描述的痛苦不是刑罚——而是父亲对儿女的管教（来12:5-11）。管教的目的是带人回到神面前。

## Theology (English)

**Justification by Faith—Paul's Citation**
Romans 4:6-8 cites Psalm 32:1-2 to argue for justification by faith—"the one to whom God counts righteousness apart from works." The Reformed tradition teaches justification by faith alone. Westminster Confession chapter 11: God freely justifies...not by infusing righteousness into them...but by imputing the obedience and satisfaction of Christ unto them.

**Confession and Forgiveness—Inseparable**
The Reformed tradition teaches confession is a continual practice. Westminster Confession chapter 15: men ought not to content themselves with a general repentance, but it is every man's duty to endeavour to repent of his particular sins particularly. Psalm 32:5's three verbs—acknowledged, did not cover, confessed—emphasize specific and thorough confession.

**God's Discipline—A Means of Grace**
Westminster Confession chapter 5: for those who sin...He visits them with various afflictions, hardships, and disciplines. Psalm 32:3-4's pain is not punishment—but a father's discipline of his child (Heb 12:5-11). Discipline's purpose is to bring one back to God.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"不算为有罪"——基督的义归算给信徒**
诗篇32:2"耶和华不算为有罪的"——保罗在罗马书4章将此解释为基督的义被"算"在信徒账上。弟兄姐妹，你的罪不被"算"——不是因为你的罪不存在——而是因为它已经被算在了基督的账上。基督在十字架上承担了你的罪——你在信心中领受了基督的义。这是"双重归算"（double imputation）——你的罪归给基督，基督的义归给你。

**"遮盖其罪"——基督的血遮盖**
旧约的赎罪日（Yom Kippur）大祭司用血洒在施恩座上"遮盖"百姓的罪——但那只是影子。希伯来书9:12"乃用自己的血，只一次进入圣所，成了永远赎罪的事。"基督的血不是暂时遮盖——而是永远遮盖。

**认罪后的喜乐——复活的喜乐**
诗篇32从罪的痛苦到赦免的欢呼——预表了从十字架到复活的转变。罪的重担（3-4节）如同十字架的痛苦——赦免的释放（5节）如同复活的得胜——义人的欢呼（11节）如同五旬节的喜乐。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Counts No Iniquity"—Christ's Righteousness Imputed to Believers**
Psalm 32:2: "the LORD counts no iniquity"—Paul in Romans 4 interprets this as Christ's righteousness being "counted" to believers. Your sin is not "counted"—not because it doesn't exist—but because it was counted to Christ's account. Christ bore your sin on the cross—you receive Christ's righteousness by faith. This is "double imputation"—your sin imputed to Christ, Christ's righteousness imputed to you.

**"Sin Is Covered"—Covered by Christ's Blood**
The Old Testament Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur) saw the high priest sprinkle blood on the mercy seat to "cover" the people's sin—but that was shadow. Hebrews 9:12: "he entered once for all into the holy places...by means of his own blood, thus securing an eternal redemption." Christ's blood doesn't temporarily cover—it eternally covers.

**Post-Confession Joy—Resurrection Joy**
Psalm 32 moves from sin's agony to forgiveness's shout—foreshadowing the transformation from cross to resurrection. Sin's burden (vv. 3-4) parallels the cross's suffering—forgiveness's release (v. 5) parallels resurrection's victory—the righteous shout (v. 11) parallels Pentecost's joy.
