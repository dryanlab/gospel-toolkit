## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第十九篇分为两个部分——前半部分仰望天空，后半部分打开圣经。天上的启示和书上的启示，都指向同一位神。这首诗告诉你：神用两种方式向你说话——创造和律法。

**诸天的见证（第1-6节）**

> "诸天述说神的荣耀；穹苍传扬他的手段。这日到那日发出言语；这夜到那夜传出知识。无言无语，也无声音可听。他的量带通遍天下，他的言语传到地极。"

弟兄姐妹，"述说"和"传扬"——天空在说话。不是沉默的天空——而是不停地说话的天空。"这日到那日"——每一天都在说。"这夜到那夜"——每一夜都在传。天空没有一刻停止它的见证。

但最奇妙的是第3节："无言无语，也无声音可听。"天空的述说不是用人听得到的语言——而是一种超越语言的宣告。弟兄姐妹，你有没有在一个晴朗的夜晚仰望星空——那一刻你什么都没听到，但你的心里知道：有一位创造者。这就是"无言无语"的述说。

保罗在罗马书1:19-20引用了这个思想："自从造天地以来，神的永能和神性是明明可知的，虽是眼不能见，但藉着所造之物就可以晓得，叫人无可推诿。"弟兄姐妹，没有人能站在星空下说"我不知道有神"——创造本身就是最响亮的见证。

> "神在其间为太阳安设帐幕。太阳如同新郎出洞房，又如勇士欢然奔路。它从天这边出来，绕到天那边，没有一物被隐藏不得它的热气。"

弟兄姐妹，我用两个比喻来描述太阳——"新郎"和"勇士"。新郎从洞房出来——满面荣光、充满喜乐。勇士欢然奔路——充满力量、不知疲倦。太阳每天升起都像一个新郎的婚礼——那么新鲜、那么荣耀。太阳每天运行都像勇士的奔跑——那么有力、那么确定。弟兄姐妹，你有多久没有被日出感动了？当你看到太阳升起的时候——你看到的不只是一颗恒星——你看到的是造物主的作品在"欢然奔路"。

**律法的完美（第7-11节）**

> "耶和华的律法全备，能苏醒人心；耶和华的法度确定，能使愚人有智慧。耶和华的训词正直，能快活人的心；耶和华的命令清洁，能明亮人的眼目。耶和华的道理洁净，存到永远；耶和华的典章真实，全然公义。都比金子可羡慕，且比极多的精金可羡慕；比蜜甘甜，且比蜂房下滴的蜜甘甜。"

弟兄姐妹，从天空转到了律法——从自然启示转到了特殊启示。我用了六个名称来形容神的话语：律法、法度、训词、命令、道理、典章。六个名称，六个特性，六个功效。

注意功效：苏醒人心、使愚人有智慧、快活人的心、明亮人的眼目。弟兄姐妹，天空能让你知道有神——但只有律法能苏醒你的心。天空能让你敬畏——但只有神的话语能给你智慧。自然启示告诉你"有一位神"——特殊启示告诉你"这位神要什么、做了什么、应许了什么"。

"比金子可羡慕……比蜜甘甜"——弟兄姐妹，我认为神的话语比世上最贵重的东西（金子）更值得追求，比世上最甜美的东西（蜂蜜）更令人满足。你读圣经的时候有这种感觉吗？如果没有——不是圣经的问题，是你的味觉出了问题。一个生病的人尝不出蜜的甜——一个灵性枯干的人读不出神话语的甘甜。

**罪人的祷告（第12-14节）**

> "谁能知道自己的错失呢？愿你赦免我隐而未现的过错。求你拦阻仆人不犯任意妄为的罪，不容这罪辖制我，我便完全，免犯大罪。耶和华我的磐石，我的救赎主啊，愿我口中的言语、心里的意念在你面前蒙悦纳。"

弟兄姐妹，从天空的荣耀到律法的完美——然后是什么？是人的罪。当你真正看见神的荣耀、真正明白律法的圣洁的时候——你不会骄傲，你会谦卑。"谁能知道自己的错失呢？"——面对完美的律法，没有人能说自己无罪。

"隐而未现的过错"——你自己都不知道的罪。弟兄姐妹，这是最可怕的——不是你知道的罪，而是你不知道的罪。你以为你已经认了所有的罪——但还有"隐而未现的"。所以我祷告：求你赦免我连自己都不知道的罪。

最后一节是整本圣经中最美的祷告之一——"愿我口中的言语、心里的意念在你面前蒙悦纳。"弟兄姐妹，不只是行为——连"言语"和"意念"都要在神面前蒙悦纳。这个标准高到没有人能靠自己达到——这正是为什么我们需要那位"磐石"和"救赎主"。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 19 falls into two parts—the first half gazes at the sky, the second half opens Scripture. Revelation from above and revelation from the Book both point to the same God. This psalm tells you: God speaks to you in two ways—creation and law.

**The Heavens' Testimony (vv. 1-6)**

> "The heavens declare the glory of God, and the sky above proclaims his handiwork. Day to day pours out speech, and night to night reveals knowledge. There is no speech, nor are there words, whose voice is not heard. Their voice goes out through all the earth, and their words to the end of the world."

Brothers and sisters, "declare" and "proclaim"—the sky is speaking. Not silent heavens—but ceaselessly speaking heavens. "Day to day"—every day speaks. "Night to night"—every night reveals. The sky never ceases its testimony.

But most wondrous is verse 3: "There is no speech, nor are there words." The sky's declaration uses no humanly audible language—but a proclamation transcending language. Brothers and sisters, have you ever gazed at a clear night sky—hearing nothing, yet knowing in your heart: there is a Creator? That is the "speechless" declaration.

Paul drew on this thought in Romans 1:19-20: "For his invisible attributes, namely, his eternal power and divine nature, have been clearly perceived, ever since the creation of the world, in the things that have been made. So they are without excuse."

> "In them he has set a tent for the sun, which comes out like a bridegroom leaving his chamber, and, like a strong man, runs its course with joy. Its rising is from the end of the heavens, and its circuit to the end of them, and there is nothing hidden from its heat."

Brothers and sisters, I used two metaphors for the sun—"bridegroom" and "strong man." A bridegroom emerging from his chamber—radiant, joyful. A strong man running his course—powerful, tireless. How long since you were moved by sunrise? When you see the sun rise—you see not merely a star but the Creator's masterwork "running with joy."

**The Law's Perfection (vv. 7-11)**

"The law of the LORD is perfect, reviving the soul; the testimony of the LORD is sure, making wise the simple; the precepts of the LORD are right, rejoicing the heart; the commandment of the LORD is pure, enlightening the eyes; the fear of the LORD is clean, enduring forever; the rules of the LORD are true, and righteous altogether. More to be desired are they than gold, even much fine gold; sweeter also than honey and drippings of the honeycomb."

Brothers and sisters, from sky to law—from general revelation to special revelation. I used six names for God's Word: law, testimony, precepts, commandment, fear, rules. Six names, six attributes, six effects.

Note the effects: reviving the soul, making the simple wise, rejoicing the heart, enlightening the eyes. The sky shows you God exists—but only the law revives your soul. Natural revelation says "there is a God"—special revelation tells you "what this God wants, has done, and has promised."

**The Sinner's Prayer (vv. 12-14)**

> "Who can discern his errors? Declare me innocent from hidden faults. Keep back your servant also from presumptuous sins; let them not have dominion over me! Then I shall be blameless, and innocent of great transgression. Let the words of my mouth and the meditation of my heart be acceptable in your sight, O LORD, my rock and my redeemer."

Brothers and sisters, from heaven's glory to the law's perfection—then what? Human sin. When you truly see God's glory and truly understand the law's holiness—you won't grow proud; you'll grow humble. "Who can discern his errors?"—before the perfect law, no one can claim innocence.

The final verse is one of Scripture's most beautiful prayers—"Let the words of my mouth and the meditation of my heart be acceptable in your sight, O LORD, my rock and my redeemer." Not just actions—even "words" and "meditations" must be acceptable before God. This standard is so high no one can meet it alone—which is precisely why we need that "rock" and "redeemer."

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇第十九篇的标题是"大卫的诗，交与伶长"，表明这是圣殿崇拜用的诗歌。这首诗常被认为是诗篇中最优美的一首——C.S.路易斯称之为"现存最伟大的诗歌之一，也是世界上最伟大的抒情诗之一。"这首诗的结构分为两个明显的部分——第1-6节论自然启示（用的是"El"这个通称的神名），第7-14节论特殊启示（用的是"耶和华/YHWH"这个约的名字）。神学家称此为"一般启示"和"特殊启示"的区别。保罗在罗马书1:18-20和10:18都引用了本篇的思想。康德的名言"有两件事物我越思考就越觉得敬畏——头上的星空和心中的道德律"与本篇的结构高度吻合。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 19's superscription "A Psalm of David. To the choirmaster" indicates temple worship use. This psalm is often considered the Psalter's most beautiful—C.S. Lewis called it "the greatest poem in the Psalter and one of the greatest lyrics in the world." The psalm's structure clearly divides into two parts—verses 1-6 on natural revelation (using "El," the generic divine name), verses 7-14 on special revelation (using "Yahweh/LORD," the covenantal name). Theologians call this the distinction between "general revelation" and "special revelation." Paul drew on this psalm's thought in Romans 1:18-20 and 10:18. Kant's famous saying "Two things fill me with ever-increasing awe—the starry heavens above me and the moral law within me" closely parallels this psalm's structure.

## 经文结构（中文）

**自然启示：诸天述说（1-6节）**——诸天述说荣耀→日夜不停→无言无语的见证→量带通遍天下→太阳如新郎如勇士。

**特殊启示：律法的完美（7-11节）**——六个名称（律法/法度/训词/命令/道理/典章）×六个特性×六个功效→比金子可羡慕→比蜜甘甜。

**人的回应：认罪与祷告（12-14节）**——谁能知道自己的错失→隐而未现的过错→任意妄为的罪→愿口中言语心里意念蒙悦纳。

## Structure (English)

**Natural Revelation: The Heavens Declare (vv. 1-6)**—Heavens declare glory → day and night ceaselessly → speechless testimony → voice to earth's end → sun like bridegroom and strong man.

**Special Revelation: The Law's Perfection (vv. 7-11)**—Six names (law/testimony/precepts/commandment/fear/rules) × six attributes × six effects → more desired than gold → sweeter than honey.

**Human Response: Confession and Prayer (vv. 12-14)**—Who can discern errors → hidden faults → presumptuous sins → may words and meditations be acceptable.

## 神学要点（中文）

**一般启示与特殊启示——双重启示论**
改革宗传统教导神通过两种方式启示自己。韦敏斯德信条第1章：虽然自然之光和创造护理之工在某种程度上彰显了神的善良、智慧和权能……但不足以给人得救所必需的对神及其旨意的知识。自然启示让人"无可推诿"（罗1:20）——但得救需要特殊启示。

**律法的第三种用途——信徒的指南**
改革宗传统教导律法有三种用途：约束罪（第一用途）、显明罪（第二用途）、指导信徒生活（第三用途）。诗篇19:7-11描述的是第三用途——律法"苏醒人心""快活人的心"——对信徒而言律法不是重担而是喜乐。

**"隐而未现的过错"——全然败坏**
韦敏斯德信条第六章论原罪教导：人的本性因此全然败坏。连我们自己都不知道的罪——说明人的罪比我们以为的更深。这不是让人绝望——而是让人更深地依靠恩典。

## Theology (English)

**General and Special Revelation—Dual Revelation**
The Reformed tradition teaches God reveals Himself in two ways. Westminster Confession chapter 1: "Although the light of nature and the works of creation and providence do so far manifest the goodness, wisdom, and power of God...yet they are not sufficient to give that knowledge of God and of his will which is necessary unto salvation." Natural revelation leaves humanity "For the invisible things of him from the creation of the world are clearly seen, being understood by the things that are made, even his eternal power and Godhead; so that they are without excuse:" (Rom 1:20)—but salvation requires special revelation.

**The Law's Third Use—The Believer's Guide**
The Reformed tradition teaches three uses of the law: restraining sin (first use), revealing sin (second use), guiding believers (third use). Psalm 19:7-11 describes the third use—the law "revives the soul" and "rejoices the heart"—for believers, the law is not burden but joy.

**"Hidden Faults"—Total Depravity**
Westminster Confession chapter 6 on original sin: human nature was thereby wholly defiled. Even sins we ourselves don't know about—indicating human sin runs deeper than we suppose. This doesn't cause despair—but deeper dependence on grace.

## 基督的影子（中文）

诗篇第十九篇在多个层面指向基督。

**基督是终极的"太阳"**——玛拉基书4:2"公义的日头必出现，其光线有医治之能。"太阳如新郎出洞房——基督是教会的新郎（弗5:25-27），祂从天降临如太阳照亮世界（约1:9"那光是真光，照亮一切生在世上的人"）。

**基督是律法的成全**——罗马书10:4"律法的总结就是基督，使凡信他的都得着义。"诗篇19描述的完美律法——基督是唯一完美遵守律法的人。律法"能苏醒人心"——但最终苏醒人心的是基督自己。

**基督是"磐石"和"救赎主"**——第14节"耶和华我的磐石、我的救赎主"——哥林多前书10:4"那磐石就是基督"。基督既是我们站立的磐石——也是我们的救赎主，赎我们脱离律法的咒诅（加3:13"基督既为我们受了咒诅，就赎出我们脱离律法的咒诅"）。

**"隐而未现的过错"需要基督的赎罪**——人连自己不知道的罪都需要赦免。这指向基督在十字架上"一次献祭，便叫那得以成圣的人永远完全"（来10:14）——祂的赎罪覆盖了我们知道的和不知道的一切罪。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Psalm 19 points to Christ on multiple levels.

**Christ Is the Ultimate "Sun"**—Malachi 4:2: "the sun of righteousness shall rise with healing in its wings." The sun like a bridegroom—Christ is the church's bridegroom (Eph 5:25-27), descending from heaven like the sun illuminating the world (John 1:9: "The true light, which gives light to everyone, was coming into the world").

**Christ Is the Law's Fulfillment**—Romans 10:4: "Christ is the end of the law for righteousness to everyone who believes." The perfect law Psalm 19 describes—Christ is the only one who perfectly kept it. The law "revives the soul"—but ultimately, Christ Himself revives the soul.

**Christ Is "Rock" and "Redeemer"**—Verse 14's "O LORD, my rock and my redeemer"—1 Corinthians 10:4: "the Rock was Christ." Christ is both the rock on which we stand and our redeemer, redeeming us from the law's curse (Gal 3:13: "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us").

**"Hidden Faults" Need Christ's Atonement**—Even sins we don't know about need forgiveness. This points to Christ on the cross: "For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified." (Heb 10:14)—His atonement covers all sins, known and unknown.
