## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，诗篇第一百二十一篇是"上行之诗"之一——朝圣者在前往耶路撒冷的路上所唱的诗歌。这首诗简短而有力——只有八节——但每一节都是一颗金子般的确据。

**仰望群山（第1-2节）**

> "我要向山举目；我的帮助从何而来？我的帮助从造天地的耶和华而来。"

弟兄姐妹，"向山举目"——朝圣者在旅途中看到远处的群山——那是耶路撒冷所在的锡安山。但这些山也可能代表旅途中的危险——强盗出没的山地。弟兄姐妹，问题来了："我的帮助从何而来？"当你面对前方的困难——你的帮助在哪里？

答案简洁有力："从造天地的耶和华而来。"不是从山来——而是从造山的那位来。弟兄姐妹，你的帮助不在环境中——而在创造环境的那位神手中。创造天地的神——还有什么是祂做不了的？

**不打盹的保护者（第3-4节）**

> "他必不叫你的脚摇动；保护你的必不打盹。保护以色列的，也不打盹也不睡觉。"

弟兄姐妹，"必不打盹也不睡觉"——这是与异教偶像的对比。列王纪上18章以利亚在迦密山嘲笑巴力的先知："大声求告吧，因为他是神……或者他睡着了，你们当叫醒他。"异教的神会"睡觉"——但以色列的神"不打盹也不睡觉"。弟兄姐妹，当你在半夜醒来、充满焦虑的时候——你的神没有睡着。祂在看着你。二十四小时、七天、全年无休——"保护以色列的也不打盹也不睡觉。"

**全方位的保护（第5-8节）**

> "保护你的是耶和华；耶和华在你右边荫庇你。白日，太阳必不伤你；夜间，月亮必不害你。耶和华要保护你免受一切的灾害；他要保护你的性命。你出你入，耶和华要保护你，从今时直到永远。"

弟兄姐妹，六次说"保护"——在八节诗中说了六次！反复强调——因为你需要被反复提醒。"白日太阳必不伤你"——古代旅行者在中东的烈日下行走，中暑是真实的危险。"夜间月亮必不害你"——古代人相信月光会导致疾病（"lunatic"一词就来自"luna"月亮）。弟兄姐妹，白天和夜晚都被覆盖了——没有保护的空白。

"你出你入"——弟兄姐妹，出门和回家——生活的全部就在这两个动作里。你出去工作——神保护你。你回到家里——神保护你。出和入——就是生活的全部——而神的保护覆盖了全部。

"从今时直到永远"——弟兄姐妹，保护不仅是空间上全方位的——还是时间上永恒的。不只是今天——而是"从今时直到永远"。死亡不能中断这个保护——因为保护延伸到"永远"。

弟兄姐妹，让我们来数一数这首诗中的"保护"——希伯来文"shamar"在八节诗中出现了六次（第3、4、5、7上、7下、8节）。弟兄姐妹，这不是诗人词穷——这是刻意的重复——因为你需要被反复提醒：神在保护你。你的恐惧告诉你"没有人保护你"——但诗篇121六次说"祂保护你"。弟兄姐妹，你信谁？信你的恐惧还是信神的话语？

弟兄姐妹，"耶和华在你右边荫庇你"——"右边"在古代是最脆弱的一面——因为右手拿武器，右边就没有盾牌的保护。弟兄姐妹，神站在你最脆弱的一面——覆盖你的弱点——在你最容易被攻击的地方做你的"荫庇"。你不需要假装自己没有弱点——因为神已经站在那里了。

弟兄姐妹，这首诗从一个问题开始——"我的帮助从何而来？"——然后用六节经文、六次"保护"来回答这个问题。弟兄姐妹，如果你今天正在问"谁来帮助我"——答案不在山上、不在人那里、不在你自己的能力里——而在"造天地的耶和华"那里。祂不打盹、祂在你右边、祂保护你出入、祂的保护从今时直到永远。弟兄姐妹，你还需要什么别的保证？

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 121 is one of the "Songs of Ascent"—sung by pilgrims on the road to Jerusalem. This psalm is brief yet powerful—only eight verses—but every verse is a golden assurance.

**Looking to the Mountains (vv. 1-2)**

> "I lift up my eyes to the hills. From where does my help come? My help comes from the LORD, who made heaven and earth."

Brothers and sisters, "lift up my eyes to the hills"—the pilgrim on the journey sees distant mountains—Mount Zion where Jerusalem sits. But the question arises: "From where does my help come?" The answer is concise and powerful: "From the LORD, who made heaven and earth." Not from the mountains—but from the One who made them. The God who created heaven and earth—what is beyond His ability?

**The Sleepless Protector (vv. 3-4)**

> "He will not let your foot be moved; he who keeps you will not slumber. Behold, he who keeps Israel will neither slumber nor sleep."

Brothers and sisters, "neither slumber nor sleep"—contrast with pagan idols. 1 Kings 18: Elijah on Mount Carmel mocked Baal's prophets: "Cry aloud, for he is a god...perhaps he is asleep and must be awakened." Pagan gods "sleep"—but Israel's God "neither slumbers nor sleeps." When you wake at midnight filled with anxiety—your God has not fallen asleep. Twenty-four hours, seven days, all year—"he who keeps Israel will neither slumber nor sleep."

**Comprehensive Protection (vv. 5-8)**

> "The LORD is your keeper; the LORD is your shade on your right hand. The sun shall not strike you by day, nor the moon by night. The LORD will keep you from all evil; he will keep your life. The LORD will keep your going out and your coming in from this time forth and forevermore."

Brothers and sisters, six times "keep/protect" in eight verses! Repeated—because you need repeated reminding. "The sun shall not strike you by day"—real danger for ancient travelers in the Middle Eastern heat. "Nor the moon by night"—ancients believed moonlight caused illness ("lunatic" comes from "luna," moon). Day and night covered—no gap in protection.

"Your going out and your coming in"—leaving and returning home—all of life in two movements. You go out to work—God protects. You come home—God protects. Going out and coming in—all of life—and God's protection covers all of it.

> "From this time forth and forevermore"—protection is not only spatially comprehensive but temporally eternal. Not just today—but "from this time forth and forevermore." Death cannot interrupt this protection—because protection extends to "forevermore."

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 121 has only eight verses—but answers life's most fundamental question: "From where does my help come?" The answer: from the LORD who made heaven and earth. He doesn't slumber, He's at your right hand, He protects your going and coming, His protection is from now to forever. What other assurance do you need?

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇121是"上行之诗"（Songs of Ascent，诗120-134）之一——这组十五首诗是以色列人每年三次（逾越节、七七节、住棚节）上耶路撒冷朝圣时所唱的。"上行"指的是从以色列各地"上"到海拔约760米的耶路撒冷——无论从哪个方向来，都需要"上行"。朝圣之路充满危险——强盗、野兽、酷热、迷路——因此诗人寻求神的保护。"太阳必不伤你"反映了中东烈日下旅行的真实危险——中暑和脱水。"月亮必不害你"反映了古代普遍的信仰——月亮会导致疾病（英文"lunatic"=疯狂的，源于拉丁文"luna"=月亮）。六次使用"保护/保守"（shamar）一词——是诗篇中罕见的密集重复，强调保护的全面性。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 121 is one of the "Songs of Ascent" (Psalms 120-134)—fifteen psalms sung by Israelites during three annual pilgrimages (Passover, Weeks, Booths) to Jerusalem. "Ascent" refers to going "up" to Jerusalem at ~760m elevation—requiring uphill travel from any direction. Pilgrimage roads were dangerous—bandits, wild animals, extreme heat, getting lost—hence the psalmist's plea for protection. "The sun shall not strike you" reflects real Middle Eastern travel danger—heatstroke and dehydration. "Nor the moon by night" reflects ancient widespread belief that moonlight caused illness (English "lunatic" from Latin "luna" = moon). The word "keep/protect" (shamar) appears six times—a rarely dense repetition emphasizing protection's comprehensiveness.

## Structure (English)

**Question and Answer (vv. 1-2)**—Lift eyes to hills → where does help come → from the LORD who made heaven and earth.
**The Sleepless Protector (vv. 3-4)**—Foot not be moved → keeper won't slumber → neither slumber nor sleep.
**Comprehensive Protection (vv. 5-8)**—LORD your shade at right hand → sun won't strike by day → moon won't harm by night → keep from all evil → keep your life → keep going out and coming in → from this time forth forevermore.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神的护理——全方位与永恒的保护**
改革宗传统教导神持续地护理祂的子民。韦敏斯德小要理问答第11问："神的护理之工，就是用祂至高的圣洁、智慧、权能，保护祂所造的万物，并且管理他们一切的动作。"诗篇121的六次"保护"描述了护理的全面性——空间上（出入）、时间上（白日夜间）、永恒（从今时直到永远）。

**"造天地的耶和华"——创造者的能力**
改革宗传统教导帮助和保护来自创造者。韦敏斯德信条第4章论创造。"造天地的"不是装饰性的修饰语——而是信心的根基：能造天地的神——什么都能做。你的问题比造天地更难吗？

**"不打盹也不睡觉"——神的持续看顾**
改革宗传统教导神的护理是不间断的。韦敏斯德信条第五章教导：神持续不断地保守。与自然神论（deism）不同——神不是造了世界就离开——祂"不打盹也不睡觉"地持续看顾。

## Theology (English)

**God's Providence—Comprehensive and Eternal Protection**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's continual providence. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q11. Psalm 121's six-fold "keep/protect" describes providence's comprehensiveness—spatially (going out/coming in), temporally (day/night), eternally (from now forevermore).

**"The LORD Who Made Heaven and Earth"—The Creator's Power**
The Reformed tradition teaches help and protection come from the Creator. Westminster Confession chapter 4 on creation.: Who made heaven and earth is not decorative—but faith's foundation: the God who made heaven and earth—can do anything. Is your problem harder than creating heaven and earth?

**"Neither Slumber nor Sleep"—God's Continuous Watch**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's providence is uninterrupted. Westminster Confession chapter 5. Unlike deism—God didn't create the world then leave—He "neither slumbers nor sleeps" in continuous watch.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督是信徒的"保护者"**——约翰福音17:12"我与他们同在的时候，因你所赐给我的名保守了他们，我也护卫了他们。"耶稣在大祭司祷告中宣告祂保护了门徒——诗篇121的"保护"在基督身上得到了人格化的应验。

**"造天地的耶和华"——基督参与了创造**——约翰福音1:3"万物是藉着他造的；凡被造的没有一样不是藉着他造的。"诗篇121:2的"造天地的耶和华"包括了圣子——基督参与了创造，祂的能力是保护的根基。

**"从今时直到永远"——基督永远的同在**——马太福音28:20"我就常与你们同在，直到世界的末了。"诗篇121:8"从今时直到永远"的保护应许在基督的同在中得到了终极的成全——"我总不撇下你，也不丢弃你"（来13:5）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Is the Believer's "Keeper"**—John 17:12: "While I was with them, I kept them in your name...I have guarded them." Jesus in His High Priestly Prayer declared He protected His disciples—Psalm 121's "keeping" finds personal fulfillment in Christ.

**"The LORD Who Made Heaven and Earth"—Christ Participated in Creation**—John 1:3: "All things were made through him, and without him was not any thing made that was made." Psalm 121:2's "the LORD who made heaven and earth" includes the Son—Christ participated in creation; His power is protection's foundation.

**"From This Time Forth and Forevermore"—Christ's Eternal Presence**—Matthew 28:20: "I am with you always, to the end of the age." Psalm 121:8's "from this time forth and forevermore" protection finds ultimate fulfillment in Christ's presence—"Let your conversation be without covetousness; and be content with such things as ye have: for he hath said, I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee." (Heb 13:5).
