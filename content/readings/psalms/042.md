## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是可拉后裔的诗人。诗篇第四十二篇是旧约中最深刻的灵魂渴慕之诗——写在神似乎远离的时候，写在仇敌嘲讽的时候，写在灵魂干渴却得不到安慰的时候。但它也是信心在黑暗中的呼喊。

**灵魂的渴慕（第1-2节）**

> "神啊，我的心切慕你，如鹿切慕溪水。我的心渴想神，就是永生神；我几时得朝见神呢？"

弟兄姐妹，"切慕"这个词在希伯来文中是非常强烈的——是饥渴的渴望、是极度的向往。鹿在旷野中奔跑，汗流浃背，渴到极点——那种对水的饥渴是生死攸关的。弟兄姐妹，诗人用这个比喻形容对神的渴慕——不是文雅的宗教情感——而是生死攸关的渴望。

弟兄姐妹，你有没有这种渴望？不是对宗教活动的习惯，不是对道德的认同——而是对神自己的渴望？你有没有经历过那种"我几时得朝见神"的干渴？如果有——你正在经历最深的属灵操练之一。如果没有——那你需要问自己：我的灵魂是否已经麻木了？

**眼泪当食物（第3-5节）**

> "我白日流泪，黑夜也流泪，是我的饮食；人不住地对我说：你的神在哪里呢？"

弟兄姐妹，"眼泪当食物"——昼夜哭泣。弟兄姐妹，诗人不是在演戏——他真的在哭。而且旁边还有人在嘲讽："你的神在哪里呢？"这是最深的伤——不是外来的逼迫，而是在你最软弱的时候，有人质疑你所信的神。

"我从前与众人同往，用欢呼称赞的声音，领他们到神的殿里；我追想这些事，我的心就在我里面忧郁。"弟兄姐妹，诗人记得从前——那时他在人群中，满心欢喜地去神的殿，领人敬拜。但现在呢？他远离了圣殿，只有眼泪和嘲讽。弟兄姐妹，属灵的干旱季节最难受的地方是——你记得从前的甜蜜，但现在感受不到了。

**对自己说（第5节、第11节）**

> "我的心哪，你为何忧闷？为何在我里面烦躁？应当仰望神，因他笑脸帮助我；我还要称赞他。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗最关键的时刻——诗人开始对自己的灵魂"说话"。不是询问——而是命令："应当仰望神。"弟兄姐妹，你的情绪不一定是真相。你的感受可能在说"神不在"——但信心在说"应当仰望神"。诗人选择相信他的神学而不是他的感受。

这个模式在诗篇42和43中出现了三次（第5、11、43:5节）——像一首歌的副歌重复出现。弟兄姐妹，重复说明这很难。他必须一遍一遍地说服自己的灵魂。你有没有经历过这种——你知道神是可信的，但你的感受在说别的，你必须不停地对自己说"应当仰望神"？

**约旦河与黑门山的回忆（第6-7节）**

> "我的神啊，我的心在我里面忧闷，所以我从约旦地，从黑门岭，从米撒山记念你。你的瀑布发声，深渊就与深渊相应；你的波浪洪涛漫过我身。"

弟兄姐妹，诗人在北方的约旦河源头——那是以色列最北的地方，远离耶路撒冷的圣殿。"深渊与深渊相应"——不是平静的水，而是汹涌的激流、轰鸣的瀑布。弟兄姐妹，有时候苦难不是一件一件来的——而是一浪接一浪，"波浪洪涛漫过我身"。你有没有经历过那种感觉——刚刚缓过来，又来了一波？

**白日的慈爱，夜间的歌（第8节）**

> "白日，耶和华必向我施慈爱；黑夜，我要歌颂他——祷告我生命之神。"

弟兄姐妹，这一节是诗中的亮光——在所有的忧郁和干渴中，有一句确据："白日耶和华必向我施慈爱。"不是"也许"不是"如果我表现好"——而是"必"。白日有慈爱，黑夜有歌。弟兄姐妹，灵魂干渴的夜晚——那时候你还能唱歌吗？诗人说他要在夜间歌颂。

**最终的信靠**

弟兄姐妹，诗篇42不是一首"解决了问题"的诗——它没有说到最后诗人的情况改变了、仇敌离开了。它只说：我选择仰望神。这是最真实的信心——不是等问题解决了才信，而是在问题仍在的时候选择仰望。

弟兄姐妹，如果你现在正在经历灵魂的干旱——你的心正在问"你的神在哪里"——这首诗是为你写的。诗篇42告诉你：你的感受不是最终的现实。"应当仰望神，因他笑脸帮助我；我还要称赞他。"

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am a son of Korah. Psalm 42 is the Old Testament's deepest poem of soul-thirst—written when God seemed distant, when enemies mocked, when the soul parched and found no comfort. But it is also faith's cry in the darkness.

**The Soul's Longing (vv. 1-2)**

> "As a deer pants for flowing streams, so pants my soul for you, O God. My soul thirsts for God, for the living God. When shall I come and appear before God?"

Brothers and sisters, "pants" in Hebrew is extremely intense—a desperate, extreme yearning. A deer racing through wilderness, drenched in sweat, desperately thirsty—that thirst for water is life-or-death. This is how the psalmist describes longing for God—not refined religious sentiment—but life-or-death desire.

Do you have such desire? Not habit of religious activity, not moral agreement—but longing for God Himself? Have you experienced the parched "when shall I come and appear before God"? If yes—you're experiencing one of the deepest spiritual disciplines. If not—you need to ask: has my soul grown numb?

**Tears as Food (vv. 3-5)**

> "My tears have been my food day and night, while they say to me all the day long, 'Where is your God?'"

Brothers and sisters, "tears as food"—weeping day and night. And nearby people are mocking: "Where is your God?" This is the deepest wound—not external oppression but, in your weakest moment, someone questioning the God you believe in.

"These things I remember, as I pour out my soul: how I would go with the throng and lead them in procession to the house of God with glad shouts and songs of praise." The psalmist remembers former days—joyfully going to God's house with the crowd. But now? Far from the sanctuary, only tears and mockery. The hardest part of spiritual drought is—you remember former sweetness but cannot feel it now.

**Speaking to Oneself (vv. 5, 11)**

> "Why are you cast down, O my soul, and why are you in turmoil within me? Hope in God; for I shall again praise him, my salvation and my God."

Brothers and sisters, this is the psalm's crucial moment—the psalmist begins "speaking" to his own soul. Not asking—commanding: "Hope in God." Your emotions may not be telling the truth. Your feelings may say "God isn't here"—but faith says "Hope in God." The psalmist chooses to trust his theology over his feelings.

This pattern appears three times in Psalms 42-43 (vv. 5, 11, 43:5)—like a song's refrain repeated. Repetition shows this is hard. He must convince his soul again and again. Have you experienced this—knowing God is trustworthy but your feelings say otherwise, having to keep telling yourself "Hope in God"?

**By Day and in the Night (v. 8)**

> "By day the LORD commands his steadfast love, and at night his song is with me, a prayer to the God of my life."

Brothers and sisters, amid all the gloom and thirst, one certainty: "By day the LORD commands his steadfast love." Not "perhaps," not "if you perform well"—but a command. By day: steadfast love. At night: a song. Can you sing on parched spiritual nights? The psalmist says he sings at night.

**Ultimately Trusting**

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 42 is not a "problem solved" poem—it doesn't say the psalmist's situation changed or enemies left at the end. It only says: I choose to hope in God. This is the truest faith—not waiting until problems are solved to believe, but choosing to look up while problems remain.

If you're now experiencing soul-drought—your heart asking "where is your God"—this psalm was written for you. Psalm 42 tells you: your feelings are not the final reality. "Hope in God; for I shall again praise him."

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇42-43被许多学者认为是一首诗被分成两篇——因为两篇的副歌完全相同（42:5, 42:11, 43:5），而且43篇没有标题（诗篇中没有标题通常意味着它是前一篇的延续）。标题"可拉后裔的训诲诗"——可拉后裔是利未人中负责圣殿音乐的家族（代上6:31-37）。从诗篇42:6可以看出诗人在约旦河的上游、黑门山（现代叙利亚/黎巴嫩边界一带）——距离耶路撒冷很远。他可能是被掳或被逐，无法回到圣殿。有人认为这是诗篇中时间最早的一批，写于大卫之前；也有人认为写于被巴比伦掳走之后。无论何时，"你的神在哪里"的嘲讽反映了以色列在国家危亡时期所受到的讥诮。

## Historical Context (English)

Many scholars consider Psalms 42-43 to be one poem divided into two—because the refrain is identical in both (42:5, 42:11, 43:5), and Psalm 43 has no superscription (absence of title often means continuation of the previous). The superscription "A Maskil of the Sons of Korah"—the sons of Korah were the Levitical family responsible for temple music (1 Chron 6:31-37). Psalm 42:6 reveals the psalmist in the upper Jordan region near Mount Hermon (modern Syria/Lebanon border area)—far from Jerusalem. He may have been exiled or driven away, unable to return to the sanctuary. The mocking "Where is your God?" reflects the ridicule Israel faced during national crisis.

## 经文结构（中文）

**灵魂的渴慕（1-2节）**——鹿渴溪水→灵魂渴想永生神→"我几时得朝见神"。

**眼泪与嘲讽（3-4节）**——泪水当食物→"你的神在哪里"→记念从前欢喜去圣殿。

**第一副歌（5节）**——"我的心为何忧闷"→"应当仰望神"。

**约旦河的忧思（6-7节）**——在北方记念神→深渊与深渊相应→波浪漫过我身。

**白日慈爱夜间歌（8节）**——白日必施慈爱→夜间歌颂祷告。

**再次的困境与信靠（9-11节）**——"神啊你为何忘记我"→仇敌嘲讽骨头痛苦。

**第二副歌（11节）**——重复第5节："应当仰望神"。

## Structure (English)

**Soul's Longing (vv. 1-2)**—Deer pants for streams → soul thirsts for living God → "When shall I appear before God."

**Tears and Mockery (vv. 3-4)**—Tears as food → "Where is your God" → remembering former joyful temple processions.

**First Refrain (v. 5)**—"Why cast down, O soul" → "Hope in God."

**Troubled Thoughts by Jordan (vv. 6-7)**—Remembering God from the north → deep calls to deep → waves overwhelm.

**Steadfast Love by Day, Song at Night (v. 8)**—By day: steadfast love commanded → at night: his song, a prayer.

**Again Troubled and Trusting (vv. 9-11)**—"O God, why have you forgotten me" → enemies taunt, bones suffer.

**Second Refrain (v. 11)**—Repeating v. 5: "Hope in God."

## 神学要点（中文）

**灵魂的渴慕——神是人最深的需要**
改革宗传统教导神是人灵魂的真正满足。奥古斯丁名言："你为自己造了我们，我们的心不得安息，直到在你里面得安息。"诗篇42的"渴慕"揭示了人心的本质需要——人不是被设计来以物质为满足的，而是被设计来以神为满足的。当人离开了神，就经历这种干渴。

**信心中的哀歌——向神呼喊是被允许的**
改革宗传统承认诗篇中的哀歌传统。"你的神在哪里"的痛苦呼问不是不信——而是诚实的信心。韦敏斯德信条第十八章论得救的确据教导：真信徒可能在各种情形下感觉自己好像被神遗弃了。这种感受是真实的，但不是最终的现实。

**对自己的灵魂说话——信心管理情绪**
改革宗传统教导理性与情感的关系——信心的真理高于情感的起伏。诗人命令自己的灵魂："应当仰望神"——不是等感受好了再信，而是用真理对抗感受。这是真实的信心操练。

## Theology (English)

**The Soul's Longing—God Is Humanity's Deepest Need**
The Reformed tradition teaches God is the soul's true satisfaction. Augustine's famous words: "You made us for yourself, and our heart is restless, until it repose in Thee." Psalm 42's "thirst" reveals the heart's essential need—humanity is designed not for material satisfaction but for God. Apart from God, this thirst is experienced.

**Lament in Faith—Crying Out to God Is Permitted**
The Reformed tradition affirms the Psalter's lament tradition. "Where is your God?" is not unbelief—but honest faith. Westminster Confession chapter 18: True believers may have the assurance of their salvation divers ways shaken...feel that they are forsaken of God. Such feelings are real but not final reality.

**Speaking to One's Soul—Faith Managing Emotions**
The Reformed tradition teaches the relationship between reason and emotion—faith's truth supersedes emotional fluctuation. The psalmist commands his own soul: "Hope in God"—not waiting for good feelings before believing, but using truth to counter feelings.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督经历了最深的"灵魂干渴"**——在十字架上，基督喊出了诗篇22:1"我的神为什么离弃我"——这是比诗篇42更深的干渴——被神真实地离弃。约翰福音19:28"我渴了"——基督在十字架上的渴望同时是肉身的渴和灵魂的渴——祂代替我们承受了与神分离的干渴。

**"深渊与深渊相应"——基督降入阴间**——诗篇42:7的深渊预表基督的降死。彼得前书3:19基督"曾去传道给那些在监狱里的灵听"——基督亲自经过了最深的"深渊"——死亡——并且从中复活。

**活水满足灵魂的渴**——约翰福音4:13-14"凡喝这水的还要再渴；人若喝我所赐的水，就永远不渴。"基督是那道活水——诗篇42渴慕"永生神"的灵魂，在基督里找到了永远的满足。约翰福音7:37"人若渴了，可以到我这里来喝。"诗篇42的渴慕在基督里得到了终极的回应。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Experienced the Deepest "Soul-Thirst"**—On the cross, Christ cried out Psalm 22:1 "My God, why have you forsaken me?"—a deeper thirst than Psalm 42—truly forsaken by God. John 19:28 "I thirst"—Christ's thirst on the cross was simultaneously physical thirst and soul-thirst—He bore the thirst of separation from God in our place.

**"Deep Calls to Deep"—Christ's Descent into Death**—Psalm 42:7's deep foreshadows Christ's descent into death. 1 Peter 3:19 Christ "went and proclaimed to the spirits in prison"—Christ personally passed through the deepest "deep"—death—and rose from it.

**Living Water Satisfies Soul-Thirst**—John 4:13-14: "Everyone who drinks of this water will be thirsty again, but whoever drinks of the water that I will give him will never be thirsty again." Christ is that living water—the soul in Psalm 42 thirsting for "the living God" finds eternal satisfaction in Christ. John 7:37: "If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink." Psalm 42's longing finds ultimate answer in Christ.
