## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第二十七篇是一首在恐惧中宣告信心的诗——一边是敌人的围攻，一边是对神同在的深切渴慕。这首诗问了一个关键问题：在一切威胁面前，你最想要的是什么？

**无所畏惧的信心（第1-3节）**

> "耶和华是我的亮光，是我的拯救，我还怕谁呢？耶和华是我性命的保障，我还惧谁呢？那作恶的就是我的仇敌，前来吃我肉的时候就绊跌仆倒。虽有军兵安营攻击我，我的心也不害怕；虽然兴起刀兵攻击我，我必仍旧安稳。"

弟兄姐妹，三个身份——亮光、拯救、保障。亮光驱散黑暗中的恐惧——当你看不见路的时候，亮光让你不再害怕。拯救应对危险——有人来救你了。保障保护生命——你的性命有一座坚固的堡垒。弟兄姐妹，当你拥有这三样的时候——"我还怕谁呢？"这不是鲁莽的勇敢——这是建基于神的属性之上的安稳。

"虽有军兵安营攻击我，我的心也不害怕"——弟兄姐妹，我不是没有敌人。军兵确实来了、刀兵确实举起来了。但他说"我的心也不害怕"——不是因为没有危险，而是因为他知道"耶和华是我性命的保障"。信心不是否认危险的存在——而是在危险面前有更大的确据。

**一件事（第4节）**

> "有一件事，我曾求耶和华，我仍要寻求：就是一生一世住在耶和华的殿中，瞻仰他的荣美，在他的殿里求问。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗的核心——也是我一生最深的渴望。"有一件事"——不是十件、不是五件——一件。弟兄姐妹，当你只能向神求一件事的时候，你会求什么？健康？财富？平安？大卫的选择是——住在耶和华的殿中。

"瞻仰他的荣美"——不是为了得什么好处——而是为了"瞻仰"。弟兄姐妹，你渴望看见神吗？不是为了从祂那里得东西——而是纯粹为了看见祂？这就是爱的本质——爱一个人不是为了得到什么——而是渴望在他面前、看见他的面。

"在他的殿里求问"——瞻仰之后是求问。先看见神的荣美——然后在祂的荣美中寻求祂的旨意。弟兄姐妹，次序很重要——不是先求问再瞻仰——而是先瞻仰再求问。当你真正看见了神的荣美，你求问的方向就改变了。

**隐藏与保护（第5-6节）**

> "因为我遭遇患难，他必暗暗地保守我；在他亭子里，把我藏在他帐幕的隐密处，将我高举在磐石上。现在我得以昂首，高过四面的仇敌。我要在他的帐幕里欢然献祭；我要唱诗歌颂耶和华。"

弟兄姐妹，"暗暗地保守"——你不知道神在保护你。有多少次你不知道自己差点遭遇危险——但神暗暗地把你保守了？"藏在隐密处"——这是至近距离的保护。不是远远地看着你——而是把你藏起来。

**恳切的祷告（第7-12节）**

> "耶和华啊，我用声音呼吁的时候，求你垂听；并求你怜恤我，应允我。你说：你们当寻求我的面。那时我心向你说：耶和华啊，你的面我正要寻求。不要向我掩面。"

弟兄姐妹，前半部分是信心的宣告——后半部分是恳切的祷告。这不矛盾——而是真实。信心不排斥恳求——信心中有挣扎、有呼求、有"不要向我掩面"的迫切。弟兄姐妹，如果你的信心从来没有挣扎过——那可能不是真实的信心。真实的信心既能宣告"我还怕谁"，也能呼求"不要向我掩面"。

"我父母离弃我，耶和华必收留我。"弟兄姐妹，人间最亲密的关系——父母——也可能会离弃你。但耶和华"必收留"。这个"必"字是确定的——不是"也许"、不是"如果你表现好"——而是"必"。

**等候的信心（第13-14节）**

> "我若不信在活人之地得见耶和华的恩惠，就早已丧胆了。要等候耶和华！当壮胆，坚固你的心！我再说，要等候耶和华！"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗的结尾——也是整首诗最实际的劝勉。"等候耶和华"——不是什么都不做——而是在行动中信靠、在期待中坚持。"当壮胆坚固你的心"——等候需要勇气。弟兄姐妹，等候是最难的信心操练——因为你看不到结果、不知道还要等多久。但我说了两次"要等候耶和华"——重复是为了强调：不要放弃。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇27从"我还怕谁"开始，到"要等候耶和华"结束。从无所畏惧的信心到耐心的等候——这是完整的信心生活。信心不只是勇敢——信心也是等候。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 27 is a declaration of faith amid fear—enemies surrounding on one side, deep longing for God's presence on the other. This psalm asks a key question: in the face of all threats, what do you want most?

**Fearless Faith (vv. 1-3)**

> "The LORD is my light and my salvation; whom shall I fear? The LORD is the stronghold of my life; of whom shall I be afraid? When evildoers assail me to eat up my flesh, my adversaries and foes, it is they who stumble and fall. Though an army encamp against me, my heart shall not fear; though war arise against me, yet I will be confident."

Brothers and sisters, three identities—light, salvation, stronghold. When you possess these three—"whom shall I fear?" This is not reckless bravery—this is security built on God's attributes.

**One Thing (v. 4)**

> "One thing have I asked of the LORD, that will I seek after: that I may dwell in the house of the LORD all the days of my life, to gaze upon the beauty of the LORD and to inquire in his temple."

Brothers and sisters, this is the psalm's core—David's deepest lifelong desire. "One thing"—not ten, not five—one. When you can ask God for only one thing, what would you ask? David's choice—to dwell in the LORD's house.

"To gaze upon the beauty of the LORD"—not to get something—but to "gaze." Brothers and sisters, do you desire to see God? Not to obtain things from Him—but purely to see Him? This is love's essence—loving someone not for gain but longing to be in their presence, to see their face.

**Hiding and Protection (vv. 5-6)**

> "For he will hide me in his shelter in the day of trouble; he will conceal me under the cover of his tent; he will lift me high upon a rock."

Brothers and sisters, "hide me in his shelter"—how many times has God protected you from danger you never knew about? "Conceal me"—this is close-range protection. Not watching from afar—but hiding you.

**Earnest Prayer (vv. 7-12)**

> "Hear, O LORD, when I cry aloud; be gracious to me and answer me! You have said, 'Seek my face.' My heart says to you, 'Your face, LORD, do I seek.' Hide not your face from me."

Brothers and sisters, the first half is faith's declaration—the second half is earnest prayer. This is not contradiction—it is reality. Faith does not exclude pleading—faith contains struggle, crying out, the urgency of "hide not your face."

"Though my father and my mother have forsaken me, the LORD will take me in." The most intimate human relationship—parents—may forsake you. But the LORD "will take you in." That "will" is certain.

**The Faith of Waiting (vv. 13-14)**

> "I believe that I shall look upon the goodness of the LORD in the land of the living! Wait for the LORD; be strong, and let your heart take courage; wait for the LORD!"

Brothers and sisters, the psalm's closing—its most practical exhortation. "Wait for the LORD"—said twice for emphasis: don't give up. Psalm 27 begins with "whom shall I fear" and ends with "wait for the LORD." From fearless faith to patient waiting—this is the complete life of faith. Faith is not only courage—faith is also waiting.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇27没有明确的历史背景注释——标题只是"大卫的诗"。学者们对其写作时期有不同意见：有人认为是大卫逃避扫罗追杀时期，有人认为是押沙龙叛乱时期。这首诗的结构在学术上有争议——一些学者认为第1-6节和第7-14节原本是两首独立的诗歌后来合并，因为前者是信心的宣告而后者是恳切的祷告，语气差异明显。但正统解读认为这恰恰反映了信心的真实面貌——既有确信又有挣扎。犹太传统中，诗篇27在以禄月（Elul，犹太新年前一个月）的每天早晚崇拜中诵读，从以禄月初一直到住棚节结束——作为悔改和回归神的预备。"瞻仰他的荣美"的"荣美"（no'am）这个词在旧约中很少用来形容神，表达的是一种令人愉悦的美好。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 27 has no explicit historical background in its superscription—only "Of David." Scholars disagree on its period: some suggest David's flight from Saul, others Absalom's rebellion. The psalm's structure is academically debated—some scholars believe verses 1-6 and 7-14 were originally two separate poems later merged, since the former is faith's declaration and the latter earnest prayer. But orthodox interpretation sees this as reflecting faith's true reality—both confidence and struggle. In Jewish tradition, Psalm 27 is recited in daily morning and evening worship throughout the month of Elul (the month before Rosh Hashanah) through the end of Sukkot—as preparation for repentance and returning to God. "The beauty of the LORD" (no'am) is rarely used to describe God in the OT, expressing a pleasing loveliness.

## 经文结构（中文）

**无所畏惧的宣告（1-3节）**——亮光+拯救+保障→"我还怕谁"→军兵刀兵也不怕。

**一件事的渴慕（4节）**——一生住在耶和华殿→瞻仰荣美→殿里求问。

**隐藏与高举（5-6节）**——患难中暗暗保守→藏在帐幕隐密处→高举磐石上→欢然献祭。

**恳切的祷告（7-12节）**——求垂听(7)→寻求你的面(8)→不要掩面(9)→父母离弃耶和华收留(10)→求指教(11)→求拯救(12)。

**等候的信心（13-14节）**——"我若不信就早已丧胆"→要等候耶和华→壮胆坚固心→再说要等候耶和华。

## Structure (English)

**Fearless Declaration (vv. 1-3)**—Light + salvation + stronghold → "whom shall I fear" → not afraid even of armies.

**One Thing Desired (v. 4)**—Dwell in LORD's house all life → gaze upon beauty → inquire in temple.

**Hiding and Exaltation (vv. 5-6)**—Sheltered in trouble → concealed in tent → lifted on rock → joyful sacrifice.

**Earnest Prayer (vv. 7-12)**—Hear me (7) → seek your face (8) → hide not your face (9) → parents forsake, LORD takes in (10) → teach me (11) → deliver me (12).

**The Faith of Waiting (vv. 13-14)**—"I would have lost heart if not believing" → wait for the LORD → be strong, take courage → again, wait for the LORD.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"耶和华是我的亮光"——基督是世界的光**
改革宗传统教导基督是真光。韦敏斯德信条第8章论中保基督。约翰福音8:12"我是世界的光；跟从我的就不在黑暗里走"——诗篇27的"亮光"在基督身上完全应验。

**"有一件事"——以神为乐的首要目的**
韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问："（一）荣耀神；（二）以祂为乐，直到永远。""瞻仰他的荣美"就是"以祂为乐"的具体表达——不是为了从神得好处而寻求神，而是为了神自己。改革宗传统教导：神本身就是信徒最大的赏赐。

**等候与神的主权**
改革宗传统教导神的时间是完美的。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理：万事都按照他的预旨……必然发生。等候不是被动的无奈——而是主动地信靠神的时间表。"壮胆坚固你的心"——等候中需要刚强，这刚强来自圣灵（弗3:16）。

## Theology (English)

**"The LORD Is My Light"—Christ Is the Light of the World**
The Reformed tradition teaches Christ is the true light. Westminster Confession chapter 8 on Christ the mediator. John 8:12: "I am the light of the world"—Psalm 27's "light" is fully realized in Christ.

**"One Thing"—The Chief End of Enjoying God**
Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "Man's chief end is to glorify God, and to enjoy him forever." "Gazing upon his beauty" is the concrete expression of "enjoying God"—seeking God not for benefits but for Himself. The Reformed tradition teaches: God Himself is the believer's greatest reward.

**Waiting and God's Sovereignty**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's timing is perfect. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence: all things come to pass...according to his decree. Waiting is not passive resignation—but active trust in God's timetable.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督是真正的"亮光"和"拯救"**——约翰福音1:9"那光是真光，照亮一切生在世上的人。"诗篇27:1"耶和华是我的亮光是我的拯救"在基督道成肉身时完全应验。祂来到黑暗的世界——成为亮光；祂走向十字架——成为拯救。

**基督是真正的"殿"**——约翰福音2:19-21"你们拆毁这殿，我三日内要再建立起来……但耶稣这话是以他的身体为殿。"大卫渴望"住在耶和华殿中"——信徒在基督里就住在真正的"殿"中。不再需要物质的圣殿——基督自己就是我们与神同在的所在。

**"父母离弃我耶和华必收留我"——基督收纳被弃之人**——马太福音11:28"凡劳苦担重担的人可以到我这里来，我就使你们得安息。"基督是那位收纳所有被离弃之人的主——孤儿、寡妇、被社会抛弃的人——在基督里都找到"收留"。

**"要等候耶和华"——等候基督再来**——信徒等候的终极目标是基督的再来。帖前1:10"等候他儿子从天降临。"诗篇27的等候在教会历史中成为等候基督再来的信心宣言。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Is the True "Light" and "Salvation"**—John 1:9: "The true light, which gives light to everyone, was coming into the world." Psalm 27:1's "The LORD is my light and my salvation" was fully realized when Christ became incarnate. He came into a dark world—becoming light; He went to the cross—becoming salvation.

**Christ Is the True "Temple"**—John 2:19-21: "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up...But he was speaking about the temple of his body." David longed to "dwell in the LORD's house"—believers in Christ dwell in the true "temple." No physical sanctuary needed—Christ Himself is where we are with God.

**"Though Father and Mother Forsake Me, the LORD Will Take Me In"—Christ Receives the Forsaken**—Matthew 11:28: "Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest." Christ receives all the forsaken—orphans, widows, the socially rejected—all find "shelter" in Christ.

**"Wait for the LORD"—Waiting for Christ's Return**—The ultimate object of believers' waiting is Christ's return. 1 Thessalonians 1:10: "to wait for his Son from heaven." Psalm 27's waiting becomes the church's confession of faith while awaiting Christ's return.
