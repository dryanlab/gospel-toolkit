## 正文（中文）

我是大卫，耶西的儿子。这首诗，是我在伯利恒的旷野放羊的岁月里写下的。那时候我还很年轻，天天带着父亲的羊群出去，走过犹大旷野那些干燥的山谷和碎石遍地的荒坡。我不知道我将来会做王，不知道我会杀死歌利亚，不知道我会被扫罗追杀得走投无路。我只知道——我是一个牧羊人，而耶和华是我的牧者。

> **"耶和华是我的牧者，我必不至缺乏。"**

你们知道做牧羊人意味着什么吗？不是田园诗歌里那种浪漫的画面。旷野里有狮子、有熊。我亲手从狮子口中夺回过羊羔——我的手臂上至今还有那些伤疤。夜里气温骤降，你裹着粗毛毯蜷在岩石底下，羊群在你四围躺卧，你竖着耳朵听——每一声风吹草动都可能是野兽来了。

但就是在那些夜晚——仰望满天的星辰，听着羊群安静的呼吸——我真实地经历了耶和华是我的牧者。我照顾这些羊，而祂照顾我。我知道每一只羊的名字、每一只羊的脾性——有贪吃的、有爱跑的、有胆小的。而耶和华知道我。祂比我认识我的羊更深地认识我。

"我必不至缺乏"——不是说我什么都有。后来我逃避扫罗追杀的时候，我躲在隐基底的洞穴里，饥寒交迫，身边只有几个和我一样走投无路的人。那时我什么都缺——缺食物、缺安全、缺盼望。但我不至缺乏。因为耶和华在那里。

> **"他使我躺卧在青草地上，领我在可安歇的水边。"**

你们不了解犹大的旷野——大部分地方是黄土和碎石，草地是极其稀罕的。一个好牧人知道哪里有青草——他要走很远的路，翻过好几座山头，才能找到一片可以让羊群吃饱的草地。而水——犹大旷野里的溪流很多是急流，从山上冲下来的。羊怕急流。它们的毛一旦浸湿了水就极其沉重，会把它们拖下去淹死。所以牧人要找的是安静的水——池塘、水潭、缓缓流动的小溪。

耶和华就是这样领我的。祂不把我扔进急流里叫我自己挣扎。祂把我领到可安歇的水边。后来我做了王，面对战争、背叛、宫廷的勾心斗角——我常常想起旷野里那些安静的水边。那是我灵魂唯一能安歇的地方——不是王宫，不是宝座——而是耶和华使我躺卧的青草地。

> **"他使我的灵魂苏醒，为自己的名引导我走义路。"**

"为自己的名"——这一点我后来才真正明白。祂引导我，不只是因为爱我——更是因为祂自己的名。祂的名声和我的道路绑在一起。我是合神心意的人——如果我走偏了，人们会说耶和华的拣选出了错。所以祂引导我走义路，部分是为了保全祂自己的荣耀。这反而让我更安心——因为神的名永不落空，所以祂对我的引导也不会落空。

但我也走偏过。拔示巴的事——那是我一生最大的羞辱。我从义路上偏离了。可是"他使我的灵魂苏醒"——先知拿单来找我，一个比喻就把我击碎了。我的灵魂苏醒过来——不是靠我自己，是神差遣拿单来唤醒我。诗篇51就是那次苏醒后写的。

> **"我虽然行过死荫的幽谷，也不怕遭害，因为你与我同在；你的杖，你的竿，都安慰我。"**

弟兄姐妹，你们注意到了吗——从这一节开始，我不再说"他"，我说"你"。"你与我同在"。为什么？因为在青草地上，你可以用第三人称谈论神——"他是我的牧者"。但在死荫的幽谷里，距离消失了。你不再"谈论"神——你直接"对"神说话。苦难把我和神之间的距离缩短了。

死荫的幽谷——我走过太多了。在旷野里真的有那样的山谷——两侧是高耸的岩壁，谷底几乎没有阳光，你不知道前方有什么在等着你。扫罗带三千精兵追杀我的时候，我就是走在这样的幽谷里。后来，我的儿子押沙龙——我最爱的儿子——叛变了，要杀我。我赤脚从耶路撒冷逃出来，一路哭着上橄榄山。那是我一生中最黑暗的幽谷。

而当约押杀了押沙龙的消息传来——"我儿押沙龙啊！我儿，我儿押沙龙啊！我恨不得替你死！押沙龙啊，我儿！我儿！"——那一刻，死荫的幽谷不是比喻，那就是我站的地方。

但即便在那里——"你与我同在"。我不怕。不是不痛——我痛得要死。但我不怕。因为祂在。

杖和竿——杖是短棍，打狼用的。竿是长杆，顶端有弯钩，用来把走偏的羊勾回来。保护和管教。两样都安慰我。是的，管教也安慰我——因为管教证明祂还在牧养我，祂没有丢弃我。

> **"在我敌人面前，你为我摆设筵席；你用油膏了我的头，使我的福杯满溢。"**

"在敌人面前"——不是敌人消灭之后，而是敌人还在看着的时候。我一生有太多敌人——扫罗、非利士人、押沙龙、亚希多弗——但神没有等敌人全部消灭才赐福给我。祂在敌人面前就为我摆设筵席。你不需要等所有问题都解决了才感恩——神现在就供应你，哪怕你的敌人就坐在对面。

膏油——中东的牧人会检查羊回来时头上有没有伤口和虫子，然后用油涂抹治疗。膏油代表医治和照料。福杯满溢——不是刚刚够，而是溢出来的丰盛。

> **"我一生一世必有恩惠慈爱随着我；我且要住在耶和华的殿中，直到永远。"**

"随着我"——希伯来文原文更准确的意思是"追赶我"。恩惠慈爱不是慢悠悠地跟在后面——而是"追赶"。你以为你在逃离神的恩典？你跑不掉。你以为你走得太远了？慈爱追上来了。我一生都在经历这种追赶——我跌倒，恩典追上来。我偏离，慈爱追上来。我在最黑暗的幽谷里，恩惠追上来了。

"住在耶和华的殿中，直到永远"——从旷野的牧羊人开始，到住在神殿中结束。从"我必不至缺乏"到"直到永远"——从今世到永恒。牧者的看顾不止于今生。

弟兄姐妹，我写这首诗的时候还是旷野里的少年。但后来我经历了一切——王位、战争、犯罪、悔改、丧子、年老——回头看，这六节诗一个字都不需要改。耶和华是我的牧者——从伯利恒的旷野到耶路撒冷的王宫——从来如此，永远如此。

## Content (English)

I am David, son of Jesse. I wrote this psalm in the wilderness of Bethlehem, during my years as a shepherd. I was young then, leading my father's flock out every day across the dry valleys and rocky slopes of the Judean desert. I didn't know I would become king, didn't know I would kill Goliath, didn't know I would be hunted by Saul until I had nowhere left to run. All I knew was this—I was a shepherd, and the LORD was mine.

> **"The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want."**

Do you know what it means to be a shepherd? It's not the romantic picture from pastoral poetry. There are lions in the wilderness. There are bears. I once tore a lamb from a lion's jaws with my bare hands—I still carry those scars on my arms. At night the temperature plummets, and you curl up under a rough blanket beneath the rocks, the flock lying around you, your ears straining at every sound—any rustling could mean a predator.

But it was on those very nights—gazing up at the vast canopy of stars, listening to the quiet breathing of the sheep—that I truly experienced the LORD as my shepherd. I tended these sheep, and He tended me. I knew every sheep by name, every one's temperament—the greedy ones, the wanderers, the timid ones. And the LORD knew me. He knew me more deeply than I ever knew my flock.

"I shall not want"—that doesn't mean I had everything. Later, when I was fleeing from Saul, I hid in the cave at En Gedi, cold and hungry, with only a handful of desperate men by my side. I lacked everything then—food, safety, hope. But I did not want. Because the LORD was there.

> **"He makes me lie down in green pastures. He leads me beside still waters."**

You don't understand the Judean wilderness—most of it is dry earth and broken rock. Green pasture is extremely rare. A good shepherd knows where the grass grows—he must walk great distances, over ridge after ridge, to find a patch where his flock can eat their fill. And water—many streams in the Judean wilderness are torrents, rushing down from the mountains. Sheep fear rushing water. Once their wool gets soaked, it becomes impossibly heavy and drags them under. So the shepherd must find still water—ponds, pools, gently flowing streams.

That is how the LORD led me. He didn't throw me into rapids and tell me to swim. He led me beside still waters. Later, when I was king, facing wars, betrayals, the palace intrigues—I often thought of those quiet waters in the wilderness. That was the only place my soul could truly rest—not the palace, not the throne—but the green pasture where the LORD made me lie down.

> **"He restores my soul. He leads me in paths of righteousness for his name's sake."**

"For his name's sake"—I only truly understood this later. He guides me not only because He loves me—but because of His own name. His reputation is bound to my path. I am a man after God's own heart—if I stray, people will question the LORD's choice. So He leads me in righteousness partly to preserve His own glory. This actually gives me greater confidence—because God's name never fails, His guidance of me will not fail either.

But I did stray. The matter of Bathsheba—the deepest shame of my life. I wandered far from the path of righteousness. Yet "He restores my soul"—God sent the prophet Nathan to me, and a single parable shattered me completely. My soul was restored—not by my own effort, but because God sent Nathan to awaken me. Psalm 51 was written in the aftermath of that restoration.

> **"Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for you are with me; your rod and your staff, they comfort me."**

Brothers and sisters, did you notice—from this verse onward, I no longer say "he." I say "you." "You are with me." Why? Because on green pastures, you can speak about God in the third person—"He is my shepherd." But in the valley of the shadow of death, the distance vanishes. You no longer speak about God—you speak to God. Suffering collapsed the distance between us.

The valley of death's shadow—I've walked through so many. In the wilderness there really are valleys like that—sheer cliffs on both sides, almost no sunlight reaching the bottom, and you have no idea what awaits ahead. When Saul hunted me with three thousand soldiers, I walked through valleys like those. Later, my son Absalom—my most beloved son—rebelled against me, intent on taking my life. I fled Jerusalem barefoot, weeping all the way up the Mount of Olives. That was the darkest valley of my life.

And when the news came that Joab had killed Absalom—"O my son Absalom! My son, my son Absalom! Would I had died instead of you, O Absalom, my son! My son!"—in that moment, the valley of the shadow of death was not a metaphor. It was where I stood.

But even there—"you are with me." I was not afraid. Not that I wasn't in agony—I was dying inside. But I was not afraid. Because He was there.

The rod and the staff—the rod is a short club, used to fight off wolves. The staff is a long pole with a crook at the end, used to pull wandering sheep back. Protection and discipline. Both comfort me. Yes, discipline also comforts me—because discipline proves He is still shepherding me, that He has not abandoned me.

> **"You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies; you anoint my head with oil; my cup overflows."**

"In the presence of my enemies"—not after the enemies are gone, but while they are still watching. I had so many enemies in my lifetime—Saul, the Philistines, Absalom, Ahithophel—but God didn't wait until every enemy was destroyed before blessing me. He prepared a table before me in their very presence. You don't need to wait for all your problems to be solved before giving thanks—God provides for you right now, even with your enemies sitting across from you.

The anointing oil—shepherds in the Middle East inspect their sheep when they return, checking for wounds and insects on their heads, then applying oil to heal and soothe. Oil represents healing and tender care. The overflowing cup—not just enough, but abundance spilling over.

> **"Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life, and I shall dwell in the house of the LORD forever."**

"Follow me"—the Hebrew more accurately means "pursue me." Goodness and mercy don't drift casually behind—they chase you. You think you're outrunning God's grace? You can't. You think you've wandered too far? Mercy caught up. All my life I've experienced this pursuit—I fell, and grace ran me down. I strayed, and mercy overtook me. I stood in the darkest valley, and goodness found me there.

"Dwell in the house of the LORD forever"—it begins with a shepherd boy in the wilderness and ends with dwelling in God's house. From "I shall not want" to "forever"—from this life to eternity. The shepherd's care does not end with this life.

Brothers and sisters, I wrote this psalm as a young man in the wilderness. But after I had lived through everything—the throne, the wars, the sin, the repentance, the loss of my son, old age—I looked back, and not a single word of these six verses needed changing. The LORD is my shepherd—from the wilderness of Bethlehem to the palace of Jerusalem—always has been, always will be.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇23是全世界最广为人知的诗篇，也被称为"牧人之歌"。传统认为大卫在牧羊少年时期或晚年回忆牧羊经历时写下此篇。"牧者"的意象在古近东文化中也指君王——亚述和巴比伦的国王常被称为"人民的牧者"。但大卫翻转了这个意象——不是人的君王做牧者，而是耶和华自己做牧者。以西结书34章中神斥责以色列的假牧人，并宣告"我必亲自做我群羊的牧人"——与诗篇23的主题一脉相承。"死荫的幽谷"的希伯来文是"tsalmaveth"——可能是两个词的组合："tsel"（影子）+"maveth"（死亡），也可能是一个独立的词意为"深暗"。犹太传统中这首诗常在葬礼中诵读。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 23 is the world's best-known psalm, also called "The Shepherd's Song." Tradition holds David wrote it during his youth as a shepherd or in later years recalling that experience. The "shepherd" imagery in the ancient Near East also referred to kings—Assyrian and Babylonian kings were called "shepherds of the people." But David inverted this imagery—not a human king as shepherd but the LORD Himself. Ezekiel 34 denounces Israel's false shepherds and declares "I myself will be the shepherd of my sheep"—consistent with Psalm 23. "The valley of the shadow of death" in Hebrew is "tsalmaveth"—possibly combining "tsel" (shadow) + "maveth" (death), or an independent word meaning "deep darkness." In Jewish tradition this psalm is often recited at funerals.

## 经文结构（中文）

**牧者的宣告（第1节）**——"耶和华是我的牧者"→"我必不至缺乏"：全诗根基。

**牧者的供应与引导（第2-3节）**——青草地→安歇水边→灵魂苏醒→为己名引导走义路。

**幽谷中的同在（第4节）**——行过死荫幽谷→不怕遭害→"你与我同在"→杖（保护）和竿（管教）→人称从"他"转为"你"。

**筵席的供应（第5节）**——敌人面前摆筵席→油膏头→福杯满溢。

**永恒的确据（第6节）**——恩惠慈爱一生"追赶"我→住在耶和华殿中直到永远。

## Structure (English)

**The Shepherd's Declaration (v. 1)**—"The LORD is my shepherd" → "I shall not want": the psalm's foundation.

**The Shepherd's Provision and Guidance (vv. 2-3)**—Green pastures → still waters → soul restored → led in righteousness for His name's sake.

**Presence in the Valley (v. 4)**—Walking through death's valley → fearing no evil → "you are with me" → rod (protection) and staff (discipline) → person shifts from "he" to "you."

**The Feast's Provision (v. 5)**—Table before enemies → head anointed → cup overflows.

**Eternal Assurance (v. 6)**—Goodness and mercy "pursue" all life → dwell in the LORD's house forever.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"耶和华是我的牧者"——神的护理**
改革宗传统教导神持续地护理祂的子民。韦敏斯德小要理问答第11问："神的护理之工，就是用祂至高的圣洁、智慧、权能，保护祂所造的万物，并且管理他们一切的动作。"牧者不是造了羊就离开——而是持续地喂养、保护、引导。

**"死荫的幽谷"——苦难中的信心**
改革宗传统不应许信徒免于苦难。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理：神……甚至也允许他们（信徒）暂时遭受各种苦难和试探。但苦难中有同在——"你与我同在"。信心不是没有苦难——而是苦难中不惧怕。

**"住在耶和华殿中直到永远"——永恒的盼望**
改革宗传统教导信徒的永恒盼望。韦敏斯德小要理问答第37问："信徒离世时，灵魂就得以完全圣洁，立刻进入荣耀里。"诗篇23的"直到永远"指向死后与神永远的同在。

## Theology (English)

**"The LORD Is My Shepherd"—God's Providence**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's continual providence over His people. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q11: "God's works of providence are his most holy, wise, and powerful preserving and governing all his creatures, and all their actions." The shepherd doesn't create sheep then leave—He continually feeds, protects, guides.

**"The Valley of the Shadow of Death"—Faith in Suffering**
The Reformed tradition doesn't promise believers freedom from suffering. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence: God: even permits them [believers] to fall into various afflictions and temptations. But in suffering there is presence—"you are with me." Faith is not absence of suffering—but fearlessness in suffering.

**"Dwell in the LORD's House Forever"—Eternal Hope**
The Reformed tradition teaches believers' eternal hope. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q37: "The souls of believers are at their death made perfect in holiness, and do immediately pass into glory." Psalm 23's "forever" points to eternal presence with God after death.

## 基督的影子（中文）

诗篇第二十三篇完美指向基督。

**基督是好牧人**——约翰福音10:11"我是好牧人；好牧人为羊舍命。"诗篇23的牧者在基督身上完全应验——祂不仅供应青草地和安歇水边，祂为羊舍了自己的命。祂不仅在幽谷中同行——祂亲自经过了死亡的幽谷并得胜。

**"死荫的幽谷"预表基督的受死**——基督行过了真正的"死荫幽谷"——十字架。祂不是经过影子——祂经过了死亡本身。但第三天祂复活了——证明死亡不是终点。因此信徒经过死荫的幽谷时"不怕遭害"——因为牧者已经先走过了，而且得胜了。

**"在敌人面前摆设筵席"预表圣餐**——基督在最后的晚餐中设立了圣餐——在犹大的背叛面前、在十字架的阴影下、在魔鬼的攻击中——祂"摆设了筵席"。每次领圣餐都是在"敌人面前"享受牧者的供应。

**"恩惠慈爱追赶我"——基督追赶失丧的羊**——路加福音15:4"你们中间谁有一百只羊失去一只，不把这九十九只撇在旷野，去找那失去的羊，直到找着呢？"基督的恩典不等你来找祂——祂来追赶你。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Psalm 23 perfectly points to Christ.

**Christ Is the Good Shepherd**—John 10:11: "I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep." Psalm 23's shepherd is fully realized in Christ—He not only provides pastures and waters, He gave His own life for the sheep. He not only walks with us through the valley—He personally passed through death's valley and conquered it.

**"The Valley of the Shadow of Death" Foreshadows Christ's Death**—Christ walked through the real "valley of death's shadow"—the cross. He didn't pass through shadow—He passed through death itself. But on the third day He rose—proving death is not the end.

**"A Table Before Enemies" Foreshadows the Lord's Supper**—Christ at the Last Supper established communion—before Judas' betrayal, under the cross's shadow, amid Satan's assault—He "prepared a table." Every communion is enjoying the shepherd's provision "before enemies."

**"Goodness and Mercy Pursue Me"—Christ Pursues the Lost Sheep**—Luke 15:4: "What man of you, having a hundred sheep, if he has lost one of them, does not leave the ninety-nine in the open country, and go after the one that is lost, until he finds it?" Christ's grace doesn't wait for you to find Him—He comes chasing you.
