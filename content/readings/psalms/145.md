## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第一百四十五篇是我所有诗篇中最完整的一首赞美诗——它是字母诗（acrostic poem），希伯来文每一节以字母表顺序开头——从字母"阿列夫"到字母"塔夫"，把整个字母表用来赞美神。这也是整本诗篇中唯一被大卫命名为"赞美"（tehillah）的诗。

**永远颂扬（第1-3节）**

> "我的神我的王啊，我要尊崇你；我要永永远远称颂你的名。我每天要称颂你，也要永远赞美你的名。耶和华本为大，该受大赞美；其大无法测度。"

弟兄姐妹，"每天"——不是偶尔——不是有感觉的时候——而是每天。赞美不是情绪的产物——而是信念的选择。"永永远远"——赞美的时间是没有终点的。弟兄姐妹，如果你以为赞美会在你死后结束——你想错了。启示录5章告诉我们天上有无休止的赞美："圣哉！圣哉！圣哉！"永恒的赞美在此生就开始了。

"其大无法测度"——弟兄姐妹，你可以赞美神一万年——还是没有赞美完祂。因为祂的伟大是"无法测度"的。你赞美了祂的慈爱——还有祂的公义。你赞美了祂的能力——还有祂的智慧。你赞美了祂的创造——还有祂的救赎。弟兄姐妹，赞美是永远完不了的功课——因为被赞美的那位是无限的。

**代代传扬（第4-7节）**

> "这代要对那代颂赞你的作为，也要传说你的大能。我要默念你威严的尊荣和你奇妙的作为。人要传说你可畏之事的能力；我也要述说你的大德。他们记念你丰盛的美善，就欢呼歌唱；他们因你的公义，就欢呼喜乐。"

弟兄姐妹，"这代要对那代颂赞"——赞美是世代相传的。你的赞美不只是你自己的——它是传给下一代的。弟兄姐妹，你有没有把神的大作为告诉你的孩子？你有没有让他们知道神在你生命中做了什么？赞美是信仰最自然的传承方式。

**恩慈与怜悯（第8-9节）**

> "耶和华有恩惠，有怜悯，不轻易发怒，大有慈爱。耶和华善待万民；他的慈悲覆庇他一切所造的。"

弟兄姐妹，第8节再次出现了出埃及记34:6的自我宣告——"不轻易发怒，大有慈爱"。这个神的自我定义在诗篇中反复出现——这不是偶然。神要你记住这个——祂是怜悯的、恩慈的。弟兄姐妹，"慈悲覆庇他一切所造的"——不只是以色列——是一切所造的。神对祂创造的万物都有慈悲。

**神的国度（第11-13节）**

> "他们要讲说你国的荣耀，谈论你的大能，好叫世人知道你大能的作为，并你国度威严的荣耀。你的国是万世的国；你执掌的权柄存到万代。"

弟兄姐妹，赞美神的伟大不可避免地走向赞美祂的国度。"你的国是万世的国"——弟兄姐妹，世上的国都有终结——罗马帝国灭亡了、大英帝国衰落了、苏联解体了。但神的国"是万世的国"——没有终结。弟兄姐妹，你属于哪个国度？如果你属于神的国度——你属于唯一永恒的国度。

**神扶持跌倒的（第14节）**

> "凡跌倒的，耶和华将他们扶持；凡被压下的，将他们扶起。"

弟兄姐妹，在这首宏大的宇宙性赞美诗中间——有这么一节关于跌倒之人的经文。宇宙之主——俯下身来——扶持跌倒的、扶起被压下的。弟兄姐妹，你正在跌倒吗？你正在被压下吗？这位赞美的对象——这位无限伟大的神——关注你的跌倒。祂不嫌你太小、太弱、太不值得关注。

**开手喂饱万有（第15-16节）**

> "万民都举目仰望你；你随时给他们食物。你张开手，使有生气的都随愿饱足。"

弟兄姐妹，"随时给他们食物"——每天的供应。神不只是在特殊时刻显明祂的大能——祂每天在喂饱万有。弟兄姐妹，你今天吃了饭吗？那是神的供应。"你张开手，使有生气的都随愿饱足"——所有生物的每一顿饭——都来自神张开的手。

**公义近于寻求祂的（第17-20节）**

> "耶和华在他一切所行的事上为义；在他一切所做的事上有慈爱。凡呼求耶和华的，就是诚心呼求他的，耶和华便与他们相近。敬畏他的，他必成就他们的心愿，也必听他们的呼救，拯救他们。"

弟兄姐妹，"诚心呼求他的耶和华便与他们相近"——弟兄姐妹，神不是遥远的、不可接近的。"诚心呼求"——就这么简单。不需要特别的仪式、不需要特别的地点、不需要特别的状态——只要诚心呼求——神就相近。

**结论：一切都赞美祂（第21节）**

> "我的口要说出赞美耶和华的话；愿凡有血气的，都永永远远颂赞他的圣名！"

弟兄姐妹，诗篇145从"我"的赞美开始——"我要尊崇你"——到"凡有血气的"的赞美结束。从一个人的赞美到万有的赞美。弟兄姐妹，你的赞美将加入所有被造之物的赞美——天使、万民、全地——"永永远远颂赞他的圣名"。弟兄姐妹，赞美不是束缚——赞美是自由——因为你终于承认了现实：神是配受赞美的那位，而你是被造来赞美祂的那个。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 145 is the most complete hymn of praise among all my psalms—an acrostic poem in Hebrew, each verse beginning with successive letters of the alphabet—from Aleph to Tav, using the entire alphabet to praise God. This is also the only psalm David himself titled "Praise" (tehillah).

**Eternal Praise (vv. 1-3)**

> "I will extol you, my God and King, and bless your name forever and ever. Every day I will bless you and praise your name forever and ever. Great is the LORD, and greatly to be praised, and his greatness is unsearchable."

Brothers and sisters, "every day"—not occasionally—not when you feel like it—but every day. Praise is not emotion's product—but conviction's choice. "His greatness is unsearchable"—you could praise God for ten thousand years—and still not exhaust it. The One being praised is infinite.

**Generational Declaration (vv. 4-7)**

> "One generation shall commend your works to another, and shall declare your mighty acts."

Brothers and sisters, "one generation to another"—praise is generationally transmitted. Have you told your children about God's great works? Have you let them know what God did in your life? Praise is faith's most natural inheritance.

**Gracious and Merciful (vv. 8-9)**

> "The LORD is gracious and merciful, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love. The LORD is good to all, and his mercy is over all that he has made."

Brothers and sisters, verse 8 again features Exodus 34:6's self-declaration. God wants you to remember this—He is merciful, gracious. "His mercy is over all that he has made"—not just Israel—all He has made.

**God's Kingdom (vv. 11-13)**

> "They shall speak of the glory of your kingdom and tell of your power...Your kingdom is an everlasting kingdom, and your dominion endures throughout all generations."

Brothers and sisters, "your kingdom is an everlasting kingdom"—worldly kingdoms all end—Rome fell, the British Empire declined, the Soviet Union dissolved. But God's kingdom has no end. Which kingdom do you belong to?

**He Upholds the Fallen (v. 14)**

> "The LORD upholds all who are falling and raises up all who are bowed down."

Brothers and sisters, in this grand cosmic praise psalm—a verse about the fallen. The Lord of the universe bows down to uphold the fallen, raise the bowed down. Are you falling? Are you bowed down? This One being praised—this infinitely great God—notices your fall. He doesn't consider you too small.

**Opening His Hand (vv. 15-16)**

> "The eyes of all look to you, and you give them their food in due season. You open your hand; you satisfy the desire of every living thing."

Brothers and sisters, "you give them food in due season"—daily provision. Did you eat today? That is God's provision. "You open your hand; you satisfy the desire of every living thing"—every creature's every meal comes from God's opened hand.

**Near to Those Who Call (vv. 17-19)**

> "The LORD is near to all who call on him, to all who call on him in truth."

Brothers and sisters, "near to all who call on him in truth"—God is not distant, unapproachable. "Call in truth"—that simple. No special ritual, no special place, no special condition—just call in truth—and God is near.

**Conclusion: All Praise Him (v. 21)**

> "My mouth will speak the praise of the LORD, and let all flesh bless his holy name forever and ever."

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 145 begins with "I" praising—"I will extol you"—and ends with "all flesh" praising. From one person's praise to all creation's praise. Your praise will join all created things—angels, all peoples, all the earth—"bless his holy name forever and ever." Praise is not constraint—praise is freedom—because you finally acknowledge reality: God is worthy of praise, and you were created to praise Him.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇145的标题是"大卫的赞美诗"——是诗篇中唯一以"tehillah"（赞美）命名的诗，也是整本诗篇书名《tehillim》（赞美之书）的来源词根。这是一首字母诗（acrostic poem）——希伯来文22个字母，每个字母开头一节，但在马索拉本中，字母"nun"对应的节缺失（第13节之后应有以"nun"开头的节，但没有）。然而死海古卷中保存了完整的22节，"nun"节的内容是"神对于所有所言都是可信的，在他所有的作为上都是圣洁的。"犹太传统中，每天三次公共祷告都包含诗篇145——它是犹太礼仪中最重要的诗篇之一。塔木德（Berakhot 4b）说："凡每天三次诵读阿什雷（诗145）的人，必蒙保证是在世界之善中的。"

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 145's superscription: "A Song of Praise. Of David"—the only psalm in the Psalter specifically titled "tehillah" (praise), the root word for the entire book's name "Tehillim" (Book of Praises). This is an acrostic poem—Hebrew's 22 letters, each beginning one verse, but in the Masoretic Text the letter "nun" verse is missing (should follow v. 13). However, the Dead Sea Scrolls preserve all 22 verses; the "nun" verse reads: "God is faithful in all his words and gracious in all his deeds." In Jewish tradition, Psalm 145 appears in daily public prayer three times—one of Judaism's most important liturgical psalms. The Talmud (Berakhot 4b) states: "Whoever recites the Ashrei [Ps 145] three times daily is assured a share in the world to come."

## 经文结构（中文）

**决志赞美（1-3节）**——尊崇你→每天称颂→永远赞美→其大无法测度。
**代代传扬（4-7节）**——这代对那代颂赞→传大能→默念威严→述说大德→记念美善。
**神的属性（8-9节）**——恩惠怜悯→不轻易发怒→大有慈爱→善待万民→慈悲覆庇一切。
**国度的荣耀（10-13节）**——你所造的都称谢你→万圣颂你→讲国的荣耀→你国是万世的国。
**神的具体作为（14-20节）**——扶持跌倒→扶起被压→给食物→张开手→近于诚心呼求→成就心愿→拯救→保护。
**万有的赞美（21节）**——我口称赞→凡有血气的永永远远颂赞圣名。

## Structure (English)

**Resolve to Praise (vv. 1-3)**—Extol you → bless every day → praise forever → greatness unsearchable.
**Generational Declaration (vv. 4-7)**—Generation to generation → declare mighty acts → meditate on majesty → tell abundant goodness.
**God's Character (vv. 8-9)**—Gracious, merciful → slow to anger → abounding love → good to all → mercy over all creation.
**Kingdom's Glory (vv. 10-13)**—All creation gives thanks → saints bless → speak kingdom's glory → kingdom everlasting.
**God's Specific Works (vv. 14-20)**—Upholds fallen → raises bowed → gives food → opens hand → near to sincere callers → fulfills desires → saves → preserves.
**All Flesh Praises (v. 21)**—My mouth praises → all flesh bless forever and ever.

## 神学要点（中文）

**赞美是人的首要目的**
改革宗传统第一条——韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问："（一）荣耀神；（二）以祂为乐，直到永远。"诗篇145完美表达了这个目的——从"我"的赞美到"万有"的赞美——这是人被造的终极目的的实现。

**"代代传扬"——信仰的传承**
改革宗传统教导信仰的代际传承。韦敏斯德信条第25章论教会。诗篇145:4"这代要对那代颂赞"——父母有责任把对神的认识和赞美传给下一代。申命记6:7"也要殷勤教训你的儿女。"赞美是最自然的信仰传承——比教义课更活泼。

**"你的国是万世的国"——基督国度的神学**
改革宗传统教导基督国度的永恒性。韦敏斯德信条第35章论末后的国度。诗篇145:13的"万世的国"在启示录11:15"世上的国，成了我主和主基督的国；他要作王，直到永永远远"中得到终极应验。

## Theology (English)

**Praise Is Humanity's Chief End**
The Reformed tradition's first confession—Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "Man's chief end is to glorify God, and to enjoy him forever." Psalm 145 perfectly expresses this purpose—from "I" praising to "all flesh" praising—this is the realization of humanity's ultimate created purpose.

**"Generation to Generation"—Transmission of Faith**
The Reformed tradition teaches intergenerational faith transmission. Westminster Confession chapter 25 on the church. Psalm 145:4: one generation to another—parents bear responsibility to transmit knowledge of and praise for God to the next generation. Deuteronomy 6:7: "You shall teach them diligently to your children." Praise is faith's most natural inheritance.

**"Your Kingdom Is an Everlasting Kingdom"—Theology of Christ's Kingdom**
The Reformed tradition teaches Christ's kingdom's eternality. Westminster Confession chapter 35. Psalm 145:13's: everlasting kingdom finds ultimate fulfillment in Revelation 11:15: "The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord and of his Christ, and he shall reign forever and ever."

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督是最完全的赞美者和赞美对象**——希伯来书2:12"我要将你的名传与我的弟兄，在会中我要颂扬你。"基督在复活后领着众弟兄赞美父神——祂是最完全的赞美者。同时，约翰启示录5:12"他们大声说：曾被杀的羔羊是配得权柄、丰富、智慧、能力、尊贵、荣耀、颂赞的。"基督也是赞美的终极对象。

**"扶持跌倒的"——基督的怜悯**——马太福音12:20"压伤的芦苇，他不折断；将残的灯火，他不吹灭。"诗篇145:14"凡跌倒的耶和华将他们扶持"在基督的事工中得到了最生动的表达——祂接触麻风病人、与税吏同席、赦免淫乱的妇人、恢复否认祂的彼得。

**"你张开手"——基督是生命的粮**——约翰福音6:35"我就是生命的粮；到我这里来的必定不饿，信我的永远不渴。"诗篇145:15-16"你张开手使有生气的都随愿饱足"——基督的手在十字架上被张开——成为万人真正的食物（约6:51"我所要赐的粮是我的肉，为世人之生命所赐的"）。

**"你的国是万世的国"——基督的国度**——路加福音1:33"他要作雅各家的王，直到永远；他的国也没有穷尽。"诗篇145:13的终极应验是基督的国——不是地上的君王国度——而是神的永恒国度，基督在其中作王直到永远。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Is the Perfect Praiser and Object of Praise**—Hebrews 2:12: "I will tell of your name to my brothers; in the midst of the congregation I will sing your praise." Christ after resurrection leads brothers in praising the Father—He is the perfect praiser. Simultaneously, Revelation 5:12: "Worthy is the Lamb who was slain, to receive power and wealth and wisdom and might and honor and glory and blessing!" Christ is also praise's ultimate object.

**"Upholds the Fallen"—Christ's Compassion**—Matthew 12:20: "a bruised reed he will not break, and a smoldering wick he will not quench." Psalm 145:14's "the LORD upholds all who are falling" found its most vivid expression in Christ's ministry—touching lepers, eating with tax collectors, forgiving an adulteress, restoring the denying Peter.

**"You Open Your Hand"—Christ Is the Bread of Life**—John 6:35: "I am the bread of life; whoever comes to me shall not hunger." Psalm 145:15-16 "you open your hand and satisfy the desire of every living thing"—Christ's hands were opened on the cross—becoming true food for all (John 6:51).

**"Your Kingdom Is an Everlasting Kingdom"—Christ's Kingdom**—Luke 1:33: "he will reign over the house of Jacob forever, and of his kingdom there will be no end." Psalm 145:13's ultimate fulfillment is Christ's kingdom—not earthly monarchy—but God's eternal kingdom where Christ reigns forever.
