## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是可拉后裔的诗人。诗篇第八十四篇是朝圣者的诗歌——是那些长途跋涉、穿越旱地去往耶路撒冷的人所唱的。这首诗的核心问题是：你渴望去神的居所吗？

**对神居所的渴慕（第1-2节）**

> "万军之耶和华啊，你的居所何等可爱！我羡慕渴想耶和华的院宇；我的心肠，我的肉体向永生神呼吁。"

弟兄姐妹，"居所何等可爱"——不是"居所何等壮观"，不是"礼拜何等精彩"——而是"可爱"。圣殿不是展示神权威的地方——而是神与祂子民相遇的可爱的所在。弟兄姐妹，你有没有觉得去礼拜是"可爱"的事情？不是义务、不是习惯——而是真正让你心里渴望的地方？

"我羡慕渴想耶和华的院宇"——弟兄姐妹，诗人不在圣殿里——他正在远处渴望着。羡慕和渴想——两个强烈的词——表达的是一种真实的向往。弟兄姐妹，当你离神的会众很远的时候——你有这种渴慕吗？还是你觉得轻松了——终于可以"休息"了？你对神居所的态度显示了你与神关系的真实状态。

**麻雀和燕子（第3节）**

> "万军之耶和华、我的王、我的神啊，在你祭坛那里，麻雀为自己找着房屋，燕子找着安窝，在那里放雏。"

弟兄姐妹，麻雀和燕子——最普通的小鸟——在圣殿的祭坛旁安了家。诗人用这个意象表达：连最小的鸟都知道神的居所是安全的家。弟兄姐妹，你有没有在神的殿里找到"家"的感觉？会众聚在一起的地方——那不是一个建筑物——那是一个家。

**旱谷变泉源（第5-7节）**

> "靠你有力量、心中想往锡安大道的，这人便为有福！他们经过流泪谷，叫这谷变为泉源之地；秋雨之福，且盖满了全谷。他们行走，力上加力，各人到锡安朝见神。"

弟兄姐妹，朝圣之路不是容易的路——要穿越"流泪谷"，那是旱旱的、充满眼泪的山谷。但奇妙的事发生了——"叫这谷变为泉源之地"。弟兄姐妹，当你怀着渴慕去寻求神的时候——你走过的苦难之谷会变成泉源。这不是说苦难消失了——而是说苦难在朝圣的旅程中被转化了。

"力上加力"——弟兄姐妹，走朝圣的路是越走越有力的——不是越走越疲惫。因为你每走一步都更靠近神——离目的地更近——这给你新的力量。

**一天胜过千天（第10节）**

> "在你院宇住一日，胜似在别处住千日；宁可在我神殿门口看守，不愿住在恶人的帐棚里。"

弟兄姐妹，"在你院宇住一日，胜似在别处住千日"——这是世界上最大胆的代数。一日等于千日。弟兄姐妹，在神同在中的一天比在神以外的一千天更有价值。这不是夸张——这是信仰的现实。在神面前的一天有永恒的意义——在神以外的一千天最终都归于虚无。

"宁可在我神殿门口看守，不愿住在恶人的帐棚里"——诗人宁可做圣殿门口最低微的守门人——只要能在神的居所里——也不愿意在恶人豪华的帐棚里做贵客。弟兄姐妹，你有没有这种价值观？神的同在比世界上最好的位置更值得追求。

**日头与盾牌（第11节）**

> "因为耶和华神是日头，是盾牌，他要赐下恩惠和荣耀；他未尝留下一样好处不给那些行动正直的人。"

弟兄姐妹，"日头"——给光和热。"盾牌"——保护。神既是光的源头、又是保护的盾牌。"他未尝留下一样好处不给那些行动正直的人"——弟兄姐妹，注意这句话。不是"他给那些完美的人"——而是"行动正直的人"。不是道德上的绝对完美——而是方向的正直——心朝向神的人。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇84从"居所何等可爱"开始，到"靠你有力量的人，这人便为有福"结束。渴慕神的居所——这是最深的福气的起点。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am a son of Korah. Psalm 84 is a pilgrim's song—sung by those who traveled long roads through dry lands toward Jerusalem. The psalm's central question: do you long for God's dwelling place?

**Longing for God's Dwelling (vv. 1-2)**

> "How lovely is your dwelling place, O LORD of hosts! My soul longs, yes, faints for the courts of the LORD; my heart and flesh sing for joy to the living God."

Brothers and sisters, "how lovely"—not "how magnificent," not "how impressive the worship"—but "lovely." The temple is not where God displays authority—but the lovely place where God meets His people. Do you find going to worship "lovely"? Not obligation, not habit—but somewhere your heart truly longs for?

**The Sparrow and the Swallow (v. 3)**

> "Even the sparrow finds a home, and the swallow a nest for herself, where she may lay her young, at your altars, O LORD of hosts, my King and my God."

Brothers and sisters, sparrows and swallows—the most ordinary birds—made their homes by the temple altar. Even the smallest birds know God's dwelling is safe home. Have you found "home" feeling in God's house?

**The Valley of Weeping Becomes Springs (vv. 5-7)**

> "Blessed are those whose strength is in you, in whose heart are the highways to Zion. As they go through the Valley of Baca they make it a place of springs."

Brothers and sisters, the pilgrim road is not easy—crossing the "Valley of Baca," that dry, tear-filled valley. But something wondrous happens—it becomes "a place of springs." When you seek God with longing—the valleys of suffering you traverse are transformed. Not that suffering disappears—but suffering is transformed on the pilgrimage. "They go from strength to strength"—the pilgrim road grows stronger with each step, not more exhausting.

**One Day Better Than a Thousand (v. 10)**

> "For a day in your courts is better than a thousand elsewhere. I would rather be a doorkeeper in the house of my God than dwell in the tents of wickedness."

Brothers and sisters, "a day in your courts better than a thousand elsewhere"—the world's boldest algebra. One day equals a thousand. A day in God's presence has eternal meaning—a thousand days outside God ultimately amount to nothing.

"Rather be a doorkeeper"—the psalmist would rather be the temple's most humble doorkeeper—if only he could be in God's dwelling—than honored guest in the wicked's luxurious tent.

**Sun and Shield (v. 11)**

> "For the LORD God is a sun and shield; the LORD bestows favor and honor. No good thing does he withhold from those who walk uprightly."

Brothers and sisters, "sun"—gives light and warmth. "Shield"—protection. God is both the source of light and the protecting shield. "No good thing does he withhold from those who walk uprightly"—not "those who are morally perfect" but "those who walk uprightly"—whose direction is rightward, whose heart is toward God.

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 84 begins with "how lovely is your dwelling place" and ends with "blessed is the one who trusts in you." Longing for God's dwelling—this is the deepest blessedness's starting point.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇84的标题是"可拉后裔的诗，交与伶长。用迦特乐器。"这是一首朝圣诗（pilgrim psalm），与诗篇120-134的"上行之诗"（Songs of Ascent）有相似的主题——以色列人去耶路撒冷朝圣时所唱的诗。"流泪谷"（Valley of Baca，希伯来文baka=白杨树，在旱地生长）的具体位置不确定，但它是朝圣路上一个著名的旱旱山谷。"住一日胜似别处千日"这个比喻成为教会历史中形容礼拜甘甜的经典语句。约翰·加尔文在逃亡日内瓦的艰难时期特别喜爱这首诗——他在那段时期写下了大量关于诗篇的注释。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 84's superscription: "A Psalm of the Sons of Korah. To the choirmaster: according to The Gittith." A pilgrim psalm sharing themes with Psalms 120-134 (Songs of Ascent)—sung by Israelites on pilgrimage to Jerusalem. "The Valley of Baca" (Hebrew baka = balsam/mulberry tree, growing in dry places) is uncertain in location but was a notoriously dry valley on the pilgrimage route. "A day in your courts better than a thousand elsewhere" became a classic phrase in church history describing worship's sweetness. John Calvin particularly loved this psalm during his arduous exile in Geneva—during that period he wrote extensive Psalms commentary.

## 经文结构（中文）

**对居所的渴慕（1-4节）**——居所何等可爱→心肠肉体呼吁→麻雀燕子安家→住在你殿中的有福。

**朝圣之路（5-8节）**——靠你有力量→心想往锡安→流泪谷变泉源→秋雨祝福→力上加力→朝见神。

**代祷与誓言（8-9节）**——垂听我祷告→你的受膏者。

## Structure (English)

**Longing for the Dwelling (vv. 1-4)**—How lovely is your dwelling → heart and flesh cry out → sparrow and swallow find home → blessed are those who dwell in your house.

**The Pilgrim Road (vv. 5-8)**—Strength in you → heart on Zion's highways → Valley of Baca becomes springs → autumn rain blessing → strength to strength → appearing before God.

**Intercession and Vow (vv. 8-9)**—Hear my prayer → your anointed one.

**One Day Better Than a Thousand (v. 10)**—A day in your courts better than a thousand → rather be doorkeeper → not dwelling in tents of wickedness.

**Sun and Shield (vv. 11-12)**—LORD is sun and shield → bestows favor and honor → withholds no good → blessed is one who trusts.

## 神学要点（中文）

**对神居所的渴慕——公共礼拜的神学**
改革宗传统特别强调公共礼拜的重要性。韦敏斯德信条第21章：神是灵，他必须被以灵和真理来敬拜……在公共礼拜中……定期且严肃地对神的圣名进行神圣的崇拜，是所有人的义务。诗篇84的渴慕不只是个人与神的关系——而是对有形的会众、对神圣的聚集的渴慕。

**"靠你有力量"——恩典的根基**
改革宗传统教导属灵力量来自神。韦敏斯德信条第16章：被基督呼召归向神的人……靠着神和他的话，以真实的方式行善。朝圣者的力量不来自自己的意志力——而是"靠你（神）"。"力上加力"是圣灵的工作，不是人的努力。

**"住一日胜千日"——永恒与时间的对比**
改革宗传统教导在神面前时间有特殊的性质。诗篇90:4"在你看来，千年如已过的昨日"——神面前的时间有不同的价值。在神同在中的"一日"有永恒的重量，超过在神以外的"千日"的积累。

## Theology (English)

**Longing for God's Dwelling—The Theology of Public Worship**
The Reformed tradition especially emphasizes public worship's importance. Westminster Confession chapter 21: God is to be worshiped everywhere in spirit and in truth...the solemn worship...publicly in the congregation is all persons' duty. Psalm 84's longing is not just individual relationship with God—but longing for the visible congregation, for the holy assembly.

**"Strength in You"—The Foundation of Grace**
The Reformed tradition teaches spiritual strength comes from God. Westminster Confession chapter 16: those who are effectually called...do truly and spiritually act...by the power of Christ and his Spirit. The pilgrim's strength comes not from willpower—but "in you (God)." "Strength to strength" is the Holy Spirit's work, not human effort.

**"One Day Better Than a Thousand"—Eternity vs. Time**
The Reformed tradition teaches time has special quality before God. Psalm 90:4: "a thousand years in your sight are but as yesterday when it is past"—time before God has different value. "One day" in God's presence has eternal weight, surpassing accumulation of "a thousand days" outside God.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督是真正的"居所"**——约翰福音2:21"但耶稣这话是以他的身体为殿。"弟兄姐妹，诗篇84渴慕去耶路撒冷的圣殿——新约告诉我们基督自己就是那真正的圣殿。在基督里，我们随时可以"进入神的居所"——不需要长途朝圣——因为基督就在我们里面。以弗所书2:21-22"在他里面，各房联络得合式，渐渐成为主的圣殿。"

**"流泪谷变泉源"——基督的苦难变成生命**——约翰福音12:24"一粒麦子不落在地里死了，仍旧是一粒；若是死了，就结出许多子粒来。"基督经过了最深的"流泪谷"——十字架——然后那个谷"变为泉源"——复活带来了生命的泉源。信徒在基督里经过苦难，苦难被基督的复活转化为属灵的滋养。

**"日头"——基督是公义的日头**——玛拉基书4:2"但向你们敬畏我名的人必有公义的日头出现，其光线有医治之能。"诗篇84:11"耶和华神是日头"在基督身上完全应验——约翰福音8:12"我是世界的光；跟从我的，就不在黑暗里走，必要得着生命的光。"

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Is the True "Dwelling"**—John 2:21: "But he was speaking about the temple of his body." Brothers and sisters, Psalm 84 longs to go to Jerusalem's temple—the New Testament tells us Christ Himself is that true temple. In Christ, we can "enter God's dwelling" at any time—no long pilgrimage needed—because Christ dwells within us. Ephesians 2:21-22: "in him the whole structure...grows into a holy temple in the Lord."

**"Valley of Baca Becomes Springs"—Christ's Suffering Becomes Life**—John 12:24: "unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit." Christ passed through the deepest "valley of Baca"—the cross—then that valley "became springs"—resurrection brought the spring of life. Believers passing through suffering in Christ find suffering transformed by resurrection into spiritual nourishment.

**"Sun"—Christ Is the Sun of Righteousness**—Malachi 4:2: "But for you who fear my name, the sun of righteousness shall rise with healing in its wings." Psalm 84:11's "the LORD God is a sun" is fully realized in Christ—John 8:12: "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness, but will have the light of life."
