## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第十六篇是一首在死亡面前宣告信心的诗——我写这首诗的时候，或许正面对生命的威胁，但我的心里有一个确据：即使死亡临近，神也不会把我撇在阴间。

**投靠的宣告（第1-2节）**

> "神啊，求你保佑我，因为我投靠你。我的心哪，你曾对耶和华说：你是我的主；我的好处不在你以外。"

弟兄姐妹，"投靠"这个词在诗篇中出现了超过二十次——它是一个军事术语，意思是在城墙或堡垒中寻求庇护。我不是说"我靠自己"——而是"我投靠你"。投靠意味着承认自己无能为力——然后把自己交给一个比你更强大的保护者。

"我的好处不在你以外"——弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗的神学基础。我的一切好处——健康、平安、喜乐、盼望——都在神里面，不在神以外。你以为你在神以外找到的好处——金钱、地位、关系——它们最终都会证明自己是暂时的。唯有在神里面的好处是永远的。

**拒绝偶像（第3-4节）**

> "论到世上的圣民，他们又美又善，是我最喜悦的。以别神代替耶和华的，他们的愁苦必加增；他们所浇奠的血我不献上；我嘴唇也不提别神的名号。"

弟兄姐妹，我在这里做了一个清楚的划分——与"圣民"为伍、拒绝拜偶像。"以别神代替耶和华的，他们的愁苦必加增"——偶像不能减少你的痛苦，它只能增加。每一个偶像最终都让你失望——因为它承诺了它无法兑现的东西。

**满足的产业（第5-6节）**

> "耶和华是我的产业，是我杯中的分；我所得的，你为我持守。用绳量给我的地界，坐落在佳美之处；我的产业实在美好。"

弟兄姐妹，利未人在以色列中没有土地产业——"耶和华是他们的产业"（申10:9）。我虽然是犹大支派的王，但我在这里宣告自己像利未人一样——不以土地为产业，而以耶和华自己为产业。弟兄姐妹，当你说"耶和华是我的产业"的时候——你是在说：即使失去一切物质的东西，我仍然富足，因为我拥有神自己。

**夜间的指教（第7节）**

> "我必称颂那指教我的耶和华；我的心肠在夜间也警戒我。"

弟兄姐妹，"夜间"——当白天的忙碌停下来、当你独自面对黑暗和安静的时候——你的心在想什么？我说"我的心肠在夜间也警戒我"——我的内心深处在夜间不是焦虑的、不是恐惧的——而是被神的指教所充满的。弟兄姐妹，夜间是你属灵状态的真实反映——白天你可以用忙碌掩盖；夜间你无处可藏。

**不被撇在阴间的确据（第8-11节）**

> "我将耶和华常摆在我面前，因他在我右边，我便不至摇动。因此，我的心欢喜，我的灵快乐；我的肉身也要安然居住。因为你必不将我的灵魂撇在阴间，也不叫你的圣者见朽坏。你必将生命的道路指示我。在你面前有满足的喜乐；在你右边有永远的福乐。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗的高潮——也是全诗篇中最重要的复活预言之一。

"你必不将我的灵魂撇在阴间"——阴间（Sheol）是死人所去的地方。我说：你不会把我留在那里。"不叫你的圣者见朽坏"——肉身不会永远腐烂。弟兄姐妹，我在说什么？我在说：死亡不是终点。

使徒彼得在五旬节的讲道中引用了这段经文（徒2:25-31），他说我是"先知"——预见了基督的复活。我自己后来死了，坟墓至今还在——所以这句话不是指我自己，而是指那位从我后裔而出的基督。"你必不将我的灵魂撇在阴间"——耶稣死了，下到阴间，但第三天复活了。祂的肉身没有见朽坏。

"在你面前有满足的喜乐；在你右边有永远的福乐。"弟兄姐妹，这是诗篇中最美的一句——"满足的喜乐"和"永远的福乐"。不是暂时的、不是部分的——而是满足的、永远的。你在世上经历过"满足"吗？弟兄姐妹，地上的喜乐总是有缺的——但在神面前的喜乐是"满足"的。你在世上经历过"永远"吗？地上的快乐总是会过去的——但在神右边的福乐是"永远"的。

弟兄姐妹，这首诗从"求你保佑我"开始——从人的软弱出发。但它以"永远的福乐"结束——在神的右边。从软弱到荣耀——这就是信心的旅程。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 16 is a declaration of faith in the face of death—when I wrote this psalm, perhaps facing mortal danger, my heart held one certainty: even with death approaching, God would not abandon me to Sheol.

**The Declaration of Refuge (vv. 1-2)**

> "Preserve me, O God, for in you I take refuge. I say to the LORD, 'You are my Lord; I have no good apart from you.'"

Brothers and sisters, "take refuge" appears over twenty times in the Psalms—a military term meaning seeking shelter within walls or a fortress. I did not say "I rely on myself"—but "in you I take refuge." Taking refuge means admitting powerlessness—then entrusting yourself to a protector stronger than you.

"I have no good apart from you"—brothers and sisters, this is the entire psalm's theological foundation. All my good things—health, peace, joy, hope—are in God, not outside Him.

**Rejecting Idols (vv. 3-4)**

> "As for the saints in the land, they are the excellent ones, in whom is all my delight. The sorrows of those who run after another god shall multiply."

Brothers and sisters, I drew a clear division—siding with "saints," rejecting idol worship. "Sorrows of those who run after another god shall multiply"—idols cannot reduce your pain; they only increase it.

**The Satisfying Inheritance (vv. 5-6)**

> "The LORD is my chosen portion and my cup; you hold my lot. The lines have fallen for me in pleasant places; indeed, I have a beautiful inheritance."

Brothers and sisters, Levites had no land inheritance in Israel—"Wherefore Levi hath no part nor inheritance with his brethren; the LORD is his inheritance, according as the LORD thy God promised him." (Deut 10:9). Though I was Judah's king, I declared himself like a Levite—not taking land as inheritance but the LORD Himself.

**Night Instruction (v. 7)**

> "I bless the LORD who gives me counsel; in the night also my heart instructs me."

Brothers and sisters, "in the night"—when daytime busyness stops, when you face darkness and silence alone—what does your heart think? I said my inner being instructed him at night—not anxious, not fearful, but filled with God's counsel.

**The Assurance of Not Being Abandoned to Sheol (vv. 8-11)**

> "I have set the LORD always before me; because he is at my right hand, I shall not be shaken. Therefore my heart is glad, and my whole being rejoices; my flesh also dwells secure. For you will not abandon my soul to Sheol, or let your holy one see corruption. You make known to me the path of life; in your presence there is fullness of joy; at your right hand are pleasures forevermore."

Brothers and sisters, this is the psalm's climax—and one of the Psalter's most important resurrection prophecies.

"You will not abandon my soul to Sheol"—Sheol is where the dead go. I said: you won't leave me there. "Not let your holy one see corruption"—the body will not decay forever.

The Apostle Peter quoted this passage in his Pentecost sermon (Acts 2:25-31), calling me a "prophet"—I foresaw Christ's resurrection. I myself died; my tomb remains to this day—so these words referred not to me but to the Christ descended from me. "You will not abandon my soul to Sheol"—Jesus died, descended to Sheol, but rose on the third day. His body did not see corruption.

"In your presence there is fullness of joy; at your right hand are pleasures forevermore." Brothers and sisters, the Psalter's most beautiful line—"fullness of joy" and "pleasures forevermore." Not temporary, not partial—but full and forever. Have you experienced "fullness" on earth? Brothers and sisters, earthly joy always lacks—but joy in God's presence is "full." Have you experienced "forever" on earth? Earthly pleasure always passes—but pleasures at God's right hand are "forevermore."

Brothers and sisters, this psalm began with "Preserve me"—from human weakness. But it ends with "pleasures forevermore"—at God's right hand. From weakness to glory—that is faith's journey.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇16的标题是"大卫的金诗"（miktam）——"金诗"的含义不确定，可能指"铭刻"或"覆盖/遮蔽"，暗示这是一首特别珍贵的诗。共有6首金诗（诗16, 56-60），都与大卫面临危险的情境有关。这首诗在新约中极为重要——使徒行传2:25-31（彼得五旬节讲道）和使徒行传13:35-37（保罗在彼西底安提阿的讲道）都引用了第10节作为基督复活的旧约预言。"阴间"（Sheol）在旧约中是死者的居所——不完全等同于新约的"地狱"（Gehenna），而是一个所有死人暂时停留的地方。"你的圣者"（hasid）指蒙神特别恩爱的人——在弥赛亚预言的解读中，这指向基督。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 16's superscription is "A Miktam of David"—"miktam" is uncertain in meaning, possibly "inscription" or "covering," suggesting a specially precious poem. There are six miktam psalms (Pss 16, 56-60), all related to David facing danger. This psalm is extremely important in the New Testament—Acts 2:25-31 (Peter's Pentecost sermon) and Acts 13:35-37 (Paul's sermon at Pisidian Antioch) both quote verse 10 as an Old Testament prophecy of Christ's resurrection. "Sheol" in the Old Testament is the abode of the dead—not fully equivalent to the New Testament's "Gehenna" (hell), but a temporary dwelling for all the dead. "Your holy one" (hasid) refers to one specially beloved by God—in messianic interpretation, this points to Christ.

## Structure (English)

**Refuge Declaration (vv. 1-2)**—"Preserve me" → "In you I take refuge" → "No good apart from you."

**Siding with Saints, Rejecting Idols (vv. 3-4)**—Delight in saints → those running after other gods see multiplied sorrows.

**Satisfying Inheritance (vv. 5-6)**—The LORD is my portion → pleasant places → beautiful inheritance.

**Night Counsel (v. 7)**—Blessing the LORD who counsels → heart instructs at night.

**Assurance Against Sheol (vv. 8-11)**—LORD at right hand → heart glad, being rejoices → not abandoned to Sheol → not see corruption → path of life → fullness of joy → pleasures forevermore.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"我的好处不在你以外"——神的充足性**
改革宗传统教导神是人灵魂的唯一满足。韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问："（一）荣耀神；（二）以祂为乐，直到永远。""以神为乐"——因为祂是我们一切好处的源头。在神以外没有真正的好处。

**"你必不将我撇在阴间"——复活的盼望**
改革宗传统教导身体的复活。韦敏斯德信条第32章：在末日，所有已死的人都要复活。诗篇16:10是旧约中最清楚的复活预言之一——使徒们将其直接应用于基督。

**"满足的喜乐……永远的福乐"——永恒的喜乐**
改革宗传统教导永生的实质是"以神为乐"。韦敏斯德小要理问答第38问："在复活的时候，信徒又从基督得着什么益处？"答："在复活的时候，信徒要在荣耀中复活。并在审判的日子，在众人面前被主承认；又被断定无罪，全然蒙福，以神为乐，直到永远。"终极的福乐不在天堂的环境——而在神的"面前"和"右边"。

## Theology (English)

**"No Good Apart from You"—God's Sufficiency**
The Reformed tradition teaches God is the soul's only satisfaction. Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "Man's chief end is to glorify God, and to enjoy him forever." "To enjoy Him"—because He is the source of all good. Apart from God there is no true good.

**"You Will Not Abandon My Soul to Sheol"—The Hope of Resurrection**
The Reformed tradition teaches bodily resurrection. Westminster Confession chapter 32: At the last day, all the dead shall be raised up. Psalm 16:10 is one of the Old Testament's clearest resurrection prophecies—the Apostles applied it directly to Christ.

**"Fullness of Joy...Pleasures Forevermore"—Eternal Joy**
The Reformed tradition teaches eternal life's substance is "enjoying God." Westminster Shorter Catechism Q38: ultimate blessedness is not in heaven's environment—but in God's "presence" and at His "right hand."

## 基督的影子（中文）

诗篇第十六篇是旧约中最直接的复活预言。

**使徒行传2:25-31——彼得的五旬节讲道**
彼得直接引用诗篇16:8-11论证基督的复活："大卫……就预先看明这事，讲论基督复活说：他的灵魂不撇在阴间；他的肉身也不见朽坏。这耶稣，神已经叫他复活了，我们都为这事作见证。"弟兄姐妹，大卫的坟墓还在耶路撒冷——他的肉身确实见了朽坏。所以这个预言不是指大卫——而是指那位从大卫后裔而来、死后第三天复活、肉身没有见朽坏的耶稣基督。

**使徒行传13:35-37——保罗在安提阿的讲道**
保罗同样引用这节经文："你必不叫你的圣者见朽坏。大卫……已经睡了……他的身体也已见了朽坏；惟独神所复活的，他并未见朽坏。"两位最重要的使徒都用同一节经文证明同一件事——基督的复活是诗篇16的终极应验。

**"在你面前有满足的喜乐"——基督复活后的荣耀**
基督复活升天后"坐在神的右边"（来1:3）——诗篇16:11"在你右边有永远的福乐"在基督身上完全应验。而因着基督，信徒也将在"你面前"经历"满足的喜乐"——这是天国的应许。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Psalm 16 is the Old Testament's most direct resurrection prophecy.

**Acts 2:25-31—Peter's Pentecost Sermon**
Peter directly quoted Psalm 16:8-11 to prove Christ's resurrection: "David...foresaw and spoke about the resurrection of the Christ, that he was not abandoned to Hades, nor did his flesh see corruption. This Jesus God raised up, and of that we all are witnesses." Brothers and sisters, David's tomb still exists in Jerusalem—his body did see corruption. So this prophecy pointed not to David—but to Jesus Christ, descended from David, who rose on the third day with a body that did not see corruption.

**Acts 13:35-37—Paul's Sermon at Antioch**
Paul likewise quoted this verse: "You will not let your Holy One see corruption. For David...fell asleep...and his body saw corruption, but he whom God raised up did not see corruption." Both chief apostles used the same verse to prove the same truth—Christ's resurrection is Psalm 16's ultimate fulfillment.

**"In Your Presence Fullness of Joy"—Christ's Post-Resurrection Glory**
After resurrection and ascension, Christ "sat down at the right hand" of God (Heb 1:3)—Psalm 16:11's "at your right hand are pleasures forevermore" was perfectly fulfilled in Christ. And through Christ, believers too will experience "fullness of joy" in God's "presence"—the promise of the kingdom.
