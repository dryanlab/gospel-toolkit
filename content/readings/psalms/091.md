## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，诗篇第九十一篇是一首关于神保护的诗——它描述了一个住在神"隐密处"的人所拥有的全方位安全。这首诗的应许何等宝贵——但也需要正确地理解。

**隐密处的宣告（第1-2节）**

> "住在至高者隐密处的，必住在全能者的荫下。我要论到耶和华说：他是我的避难所，是我的山寨，是我的神，是我所倚靠的。"

弟兄姐妹，"隐密处"——不是每个人都在那里——而是"住在"那里的人。这是一个主动的选择——你选择住在至高者的隐密处。弟兄姐妹，"隐密处"意味着不是所有人都能看到的地方——那是一个只有与神亲密的人才能到达的地方。不是在人群中——而是在"隐密处"。

四个名称——避难所、山寨、神、倚靠。"避难所"是你逃避危险时去的地方——"山寨"是高处的堡垒——"神"是一切的主宰——"倚靠"是你把全部重量放上去的那个支撑。弟兄姐妹，这四个名称从不同角度描述同一件事：你的安全完完全全在神那里。

**脱离网罗和瘟疫（第3-6节）**

> "他必救你脱离捕鸟人的网罗和毒害的瘟疫。他必用自己的翎毛遮蔽你；你要投靠在他的翅膀底下；他的诚实是大小的盾牌。你必不怕黑夜的惊骇，或是白日飞的箭，也不怕黑夜行的瘟疫，或是午间灭人的毒病。"

弟兄姐妹，保护是全方位的——黑夜和白日、瘟疫和箭矢、可见的和不可见的。"翎毛遮蔽"——又是母鸡护雏的温暖意象。"盾牌"——又是军事的坚固意象。弟兄姐妹，神的保护既是温柔的（翎毛）又是坚固的（盾牌）。

**千人仆倒（第7-8节）**

> "虽有千人仆倒在你旁边，万人仆倒在你右边，这灾却不得临近你。你惟亲眼观看，见恶人遭报。"

弟兄姐妹，"千人""万人"——数字越来越大——但"这灾却不得临近你"。弟兄姐妹，这不是说信徒从来不会遭遇灾难——而是说在神的保护下，即使周围的世界都在崩塌，住在至高者隐密处的人仍然安全。这个"安全"不一定是肉身的——而是灵魂的。

**脚踩狮子（第11-13节）**

> "因他要为你吩咐他的使者，在你行的一切道路上保护你。他们要用手托着你，免得你的脚碰在石头上。你要踹在狮子和毒蛇的身上，践踏猛狮和大蛇。"

弟兄姐妹，魔鬼在试探耶稣时引用了第11-12节（太4:6）——要耶稣从殿顶跳下来："因他要为你吩咐他的使者……免得你的脚碰在石头上。"但耶稣拒绝了——因为这是对经文的错误使用。弟兄姐妹，诗篇91的应许不是让你故意跳进危险然后期待神来救你——而是在你"行的一切道路上"（正常的、顺服的道路上）神会保护你。信心不是试探神——信心是信靠神。

**神亲口的应许（第14-16节）**

> "神说：因为他专心爱我，我就搭救他；因为他知道我的名，我就把他安置在高处。他若求告我，我就应允他；他在急难中，我要与他同在；我要搭救他，使他尊贵。我要使他足享长寿，将我的救恩显明给他。"

弟兄姐妹，整首诗在这里转到了神的第一人称——神自己在说话。"因为他专心爱我"——保护的条件是什么？"专心爱我"。不是完美的行为——而是专心的爱。弟兄姐妹，你专心爱神吗？不是三心二意的宗教——而是专心的爱。

"他在急难中，我要与他同在"——弟兄姐妹，注意：神没有说"我不让急难来"——祂说"在急难中我与他同在"。保护不等于免除——保护等于同在。弟兄姐妹，你最需要的不是没有急难——而是在急难中有神同在。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇91是最甜美的保护应许之一——但它不是魔法的护身符。它是给那些"住在至高者隐密处"的人的——给那些"专心爱神"的人的——给那些在正常信靠的道路上行走的人的。在这条路上，神的保护是全方位的、是温柔又坚固的、是从今生直到永远的。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 91 is a psalm about God's protection—describing the comprehensive safety of one who dwells in God's "shelter." This psalm's promises are precious—but must be rightly understood.

**The Shelter Declaration (vv. 1-2)**

> "He who dwells in the shelter of the Most High will abide in the shadow of the Almighty. I will say to the LORD, 'My refuge and my fortress, my God, in whom I trust.'"

Brothers and sisters, "shelter"—not everyone is there—but those who "dwell" there. This is an active choice. "Shelter" means a place not visible to everyone—a place reached only by those intimate with God.

Four names—refuge, fortress, God, trust. These four names describe the same thing from different angles: your safety is entirely in God.

**Deliverance from Snare and Pestilence (vv. 3-6)**

> "For he will deliver you from the snare of the fowler and from the deadly pestilence. He will cover you with his pinions, and under his wings you will find refuge; his faithfulness is a shield and buckler. You will not fear the terror of the night, nor the arrow that flies by day, nor the pestilence that stalks in darkness, nor the destruction that wastes at noonday."

Brothers and sisters, protection is comprehensive—night and day, pestilence and arrows, visible and invisible. God's protection is both tender (pinions) and strong (shield).

**A Thousand May Fall (vv. 7-8)**

> "A thousand may fall at your side, ten thousand at your right hand, but it will not come near you."

Brothers and sisters, this does not mean believers never face disaster—but that under God's protection, even as the world crumbles around them, those dwelling in the Most High's shelter remain safe. This "safety" is not necessarily physical—but spiritual.

**Treading on Lions (vv. 11-13)**

> "For he will command his angels concerning you to guard you in all your ways. On their hands they will bear you up, lest you strike your foot against a stone."

Brothers and sisters, the devil quoted verses 11-12 when tempting Jesus (Matt 4:6)—urging Him to throw Himself from the temple pinnacle. But Jesus refused—because this was Scripture misused. Psalm 91's promises are not for deliberately jumping into danger expecting rescue—but for protection on "all your ways" (normal, obedient paths). Faith is not testing God—faith is trusting God.

**God's Own Promise (vv. 14-16)**

> "Because he holds fast to me in love, I will deliver him; I will protect him, because he knows my name. When he calls to me, I will answer him; I will be with him in trouble; I will rescue him and honor him."

Brothers and sisters, the psalm shifts to God's first person—God Himself speaks. "Because he holds fast to me in love"—protection's condition: devoted love. "I will be with him in trouble"—notice: God didn't say "I won't let trouble come"—He said "in trouble I will be with him." Protection doesn't equal exemption—protection equals presence.

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 91 is one of the sweetest protection promises—but it is not a magic amulet. It is for those who "dwell in the Most High's shelter"—who "hold fast in love"—who walk the normal path of trust.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇91没有标题标注作者——但犹太传统和部分基督教传统将其归于摩西。七十士译本将其标注为"大卫之诗"。这首诗紧接在诗篇90（摩西的祈祷）之后——可能是同一背景下的另一首诗。在新约中，这首诗因为魔鬼在旷野试探中引用第11-12节（太4:6, 路4:10-11）而特别有名。路德注意到魔鬼也能"引用圣经"——但会扭曲其含义。诗篇91在犹太传统中被视为"驱魔诗篇"——有些犹太人在睡前诵读以求保护。教会历史中，这首诗在瘟疫时期特别受重视——比如14世纪黑死病和后来的各种流行病中，信徒常以诗篇91为安慰。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 91 has no authorship superscription—but Jewish tradition and some Christian tradition attribute it to Moses. The Septuagint labels it "A Psalm of David." It immediately follows Psalm 90 (Moses' prayer)—possibly from the same context. In the New Testament, this psalm is especially famous because the devil quoted verses 11-12 during the wilderness temptation (Matt 4:6, Luke 4:10-11). Luther noted the devil can also "quote Scripture"—but twists its meaning. In Jewish tradition, Psalm 91 is viewed as an "exorcism psalm"—some Jews recite it before sleep for protection. In church history, this psalm was especially cherished during plagues—such as the 14th-century Black Death.

## Structure (English)

**The Shelter's Safety (vv. 1-2)**—Dwelling in the Most High's shelter → Almighty's shadow → refuge, fortress, God, trust.

**Comprehensive Protection (vv. 3-8)**—Delivered from snare and pestilence → covered with pinions, under wings → shield → fear neither night nor day → thousands fall but it won't come near.

**Angelic Protection (vv. 9-13)**—Made the LORD your dwelling → commands angels → bear you up → tread on lion and serpent.

**God's Own Promise (vv. 14-16)**—Holds fast in love: I will deliver → knows my name: set on high → calls: I will answer → in trouble: I am with him → honor, long life → show my salvation.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神的护理——全方位的保护**
改革宗传统教导神对信徒有特别的护理。韦敏斯德信条第5章：就他对他教会的特别看顾而言，万事都为教会的好处而有效运作。诗篇91的全方位保护反映了神对祂子民特别的看顾——不是让他们免于一切苦难，而是在一切苦难中保守他们的灵魂。

**试探与信心的区别**
魔鬼引用诗篇91:11-12试探耶稣——改革宗传统教导信心与试探的根本区别。韦敏斯德信条第14章论得救的信心：凡得救的信心……以基督为唯一根基……绝不使用那些与信心相悖的方式。故意跳进危险然后期待神来救——不是信心，是试探神（申6:16"你们不可试探耶和华你们的神"）。

**"急难中同在"——苦难神学**
改革宗传统不应许信徒免除苦难。韦敏斯德信条第十八章教导：真信徒可能灵性受到各种摇动。但在苦难中有神的同在——"我要与他同在"——这是比免除苦难更深的应许。

## Theology (English)

**God's Providence—Comprehensive Protection**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's special providence for believers. Westminster Confession chapter 5: as to his special care of his church, all things work together for the good thereof. Psalm 91's comprehensive protection reflects God's special care for His people—not exempting them from all suffering but preserving their souls through all suffering.

**The Difference Between Temptation and Faith**
The devil quoted Psalm 91:11-12 to tempt Jesus—the Reformed tradition teaches the fundamental distinction between faith and testing God. Westminster Confession chapter 14 on saving faith. Deliberately jumping into danger expecting God's rescue is not faith—it is testing God (Deut 6:16: "You shall not put the LORD your God to the test").

**"With Him in Trouble"—Suffering Theology**
The Reformed tradition doesn't promise believers freedom from suffering. Westminster Confession chapter 18. But in suffering there is God's presence—"I will be with him"—a promise deeper than exemption from suffering.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督拒绝错用诗篇91——正确使用圣经**
马太福音4:5-7"魔鬼就带他进了圣城，叫他站在殿顶上，对他说：你若是神的儿子，可以跳下去，因为经上记着说：主要为你吩咐他的使者用手托着你。耶稣对他说：经上又记着说：不可试探主你的神。"弟兄姐妹，基督面对魔鬼对诗篇91的滥用——用申命记6:16回应。这教导我们：圣经的应许需要在完整的圣经框架中理解，不能断章取义。

**基督是真正的"隐密处"**——歌罗西书3:3"你们的生命与基督一同藏在神里面。"诗篇91:1"住在至高者隐密处"——在基督里，信徒被"藏在神里面"。基督自己就是那个"隐密处"——在祂里面我们找到了终极的安全。

**"踹在狮子和毒蛇身上"——基督战胜撒但**——路加福音10:19"我已经给你们权柄可以践踏蛇和蝎子，又胜过仇敌一切的能力。"基督在十字架上"踹"了那条古蛇（创3:15"女人的后裔要伤你的头"），这是诗篇91:13的终极应验。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ Refused Psalm 91's Misuse—Proper Use of Scripture**
Matthew 4:5-7: the devil took Jesus to the pinnacle of the temple and quoted Psalm 91:11-12. Jesus responded with Deuteronomy 6:16: "You shall not put the Lord your God to the test." Brothers and sisters, Christ faced Satan's abuse of Psalm 91—and responded with fuller Scripture. This teaches: Scripture's promises must be understood within the complete biblical framework, not taken out of context.

**Christ Is the True "Shelter"**—Colossians 3:3: "your life is hidden with Christ in God." Psalm 91:1 "dwells in the shelter of the Most High"—in Christ, believers are "hidden in God." Christ Himself is that "shelter"—in Him we find ultimate safety.

**"Tread on Lions and Serpents"—Christ Defeats Satan**—Luke 10:19: "I have given you authority to tread on serpents and scorpions." Christ on the cross "trod on" that ancient serpent (Gen 3:15: "he shall bruise your head")—the ultimate fulfillment of Psalm 91:13.
