## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第二十二篇是我一生中写过的最痛苦、也最荣耀的一首诗。当我写下这首诗的时候，我正经历极深的苦难——但圣灵带领我写出的，远远超过了我个人的经历。一千年后，我的后裔耶稣在十字架上引用了这首诗的第一句。

**被神离弃的呼喊（第1-2节）**

> "我的神，我的神！为什么离弃我？为什么远离不救我？不听我唉哼的言语？我的神啊，我白日呼求，你不应允；夜间呼求，并不住声。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整本圣经中最令人震惊的呼喊之一。不是"神啊你为什么让恶人攻击我"——而是"你为什么离弃我"。不是外在的苦难——而是神的"离弃"。弟兄姐妹，一切苦难中最可怕的不是身体的疼痛、不是人的背叛——而是感觉被神离弃。

但注意——"我的神，我的神"——即使在呼喊"你为什么离弃我"的时候，我仍然说"我的神"。这不是不信的控告——而是信心的挣扎。他没有说"你不是我的神"——他说"你是我的神，但你为什么离弃我？"弟兄姐妹，信心最深的时刻不是你感受到神同在的时候——而是你感受不到神，却仍然说"我的神"的时候。

**信心的根基——列祖的见证（第3-5节）**

> "但你是圣洁的，是用以色列的赞美为宝座的。我们的祖宗倚靠你；他们倚靠你，你便解救他们。他们哀求你便蒙解救；他们倚靠你就不羞愧。"

弟兄姐妹，在极度痛苦中，我做了什么？他回到了历史——祖宗们的见证。他们倚靠你，你解救他们。弟兄姐妹，当你觉得神离弃你的时候——回到见证。想想神过去的作为——不只是你自己的经历，还有教会历史中千千万万圣徒的见证。

**虫而非人（第6-8节）**

> "但我是虫，不是人，被众人羞辱，被百姓藐视。凡看见我的都嗤笑我；他们撇嘴摇头，说：他把自己交托耶和华，耶和华可以救他吧！耶和华既喜悦他，可以搭救他吧！"

弟兄姐妹，"我是虫不是人"——被羞辱到了连人都算不上的地步。"他把自己交托耶和华，耶和华可以救他吧"——这是嘲讽。他们不是质疑神的能力——而是嘲笑这个人跟神的关系。弟兄姐妹，这段经文在马太福音27:39-43中几乎逐字应验："从那里经过的人讥诮他……他倚靠神，神若喜悦他，现在可以救他。"

**身体的痛苦——十字架的预写（第14-18节）**

> "我如水被倒出来；我的骨头都脱了节；我的心在我里面如蜡融化。我的精力枯干，如同瓦片；我的舌头贴在我牙床上。你将我安置在死地的尘土中。犬类围着我，恶党环绕我；他们扎了我的手、我的脚。我的骨头我都能数过。他们瞪着眼看我。他们分我的外衣，为我的里衣拈阄。"

弟兄姐妹，读到这里你是否感到震惊？我写这首诗的时候——十字架刑还不存在。十字架是罗马人后来发明的。但我描述的——骨头脱节、舌头干渴、手脚被扎、衣服被分——完完全全就是十字架上的耶稣。约翰福音19:23-24记载士兵们分耶稣的外衣、为里衣拈阄——逐字应验了第18节。

弟兄姐妹，这不是巧合——这是圣灵在一千年前就写下的预言。我用自己的苦难为笔——圣灵用基督的十字架为墨——写出了人类历史上最伟大的救赎篇章。

**从苦难到赞美的转折（第22-24节）**

> "我要将你的名传与我的弟兄，在会中我要赞美你。你们敬畏耶和华的人要赞美他！雅各的后裔都要荣耀他！以色列的后裔都要惧怕他！因为他没有藐视憎恶受苦的人，也没有向他掩面；那受苦之人呼吁的时候，他就垂听。"

弟兄姐妹，注意第21节到第22节之间的巨大转折——从"救我脱离狮子的口"直接跳到"我要将你的名传与我的弟兄"。没有过渡——只有转折。从极度的绝望到爆发的赞美——中间发生了什么？答案就是：复活。弟兄姐妹，如果诗篇22的前半部分是十字架——后半部分就是复活后的赞美。希伯来书2:12直接引用第22节应用于基督："我要将你的名传与我的弟兄，在会中我要颂扬你。"

**万国的敬拜（第27-31节）**

> "地的四极都要想念耶和华，并且归顺他；列国的万族都要在你面前敬拜。"

弟兄姐妹，这首诗从一个人的苦难开始——到万国的敬拜结束。从"我的神为什么离弃我"——到"地的四极都要归顺他"。十字架的苦难不是终点——万国的救恩才是目的。弟兄姐妹，每一次你觉得苦难没有意义的时候——想想十字架：那是人类历史上最大的苦难，却带来了最大的救恩。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 22 is the most agonizing yet most glorious poem I ever wrote. When I penned it, I was experiencing profound suffering—but what the Holy Spirit led me to write far surpassed my personal experience. A thousand years later, my descendant Jesus quoted its first line from the cross.

**The Cry of God-Forsakenness (vv. 1-2)**

> "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me? Why are you so far from saving me, from the words of my groaning? O my God, I cry by day, but you do not answer, and by night, but I find no rest."

Brothers and sisters, one of Scripture's most shocking cries. Not "God, why do you let the wicked attack me"—but "why have you forsaken me?" Not external suffering—but God's "forsaking." Brothers and sisters, the most terrible suffering is not bodily pain or human betrayal—but feeling forsaken by God.

But notice—"My God, my God"—even while crying "why have you forsaken me," I still said "my God." This is not unbelief's accusation—but faith's struggle. Faith's deepest moment is not when you feel God's presence—but when you cannot feel God yet still say "my God."

**Faith's Foundation—The Fathers' Testimony (vv. 3-5)**

> "Yet you are holy, enthroned on the praises of Israel. In you our fathers trusted; they trusted, and you delivered them."

Brothers and sisters, in extreme agony, what did I do? I returned to history—the fathers' testimony. When you feel forsaken—return to testimonies.

**A Worm, Not a Man (vv. 6-8)**

> "But I am a worm and not a man, scorned by mankind and despised by the people. All who see me mock me... 'He trusts in the LORD; let him deliver him; let him rescue him, for he delights in him!'"

Brothers and sisters, this passage was fulfilled almost verbatim in Matthew 27:39-43: "those who passed by derided him... He trusts in God; let God deliver him now, if he desires him."

**Bodily Agony—The Cross Pre-Written (vv. 14-18)**

> "I am poured out like water, and all my bones are out of joint; my heart is like wax; it is melted within my breast. For dogs encompass me; a company of evildoers encircles me; they have pierced my hands and feet—I can count all my bones. They divide my garments among them, and for my clothing they cast lots."

Brothers and sisters, when I wrote this—crucifixion did not yet exist. Crucifixion was a later Roman invention. But I described exactly what happened to Jesus on the cross—bones disjointed, tongue parched, hands and feet pierced, garments divided. John 19:23-24 records soldiers dividing Jesus' garments and casting lots for His tunic—fulfilling verse 18 word by word.

This is not coincidence—this is prophecy written by the Holy Spirit a thousand years in advance.

**The Turn from Suffering to Praise (vv. 22-24)**

> "I will tell of your name to my brothers; in the midst of the congregation I will praise you. For he has not despised or abhorred the affliction of the afflicted, and he has not hidden his face from him, but has heard, when he cried to him."

Brothers and sisters, notice the massive turn between verses 21 and 22—from "save me from the lion's mouth" directly to "I will tell of your name to my brothers." No transition—only reversal. From utter despair to erupting praise—what happened in between? The answer: resurrection. If Psalm 22's first half is the cross—its second half is post-resurrection praise. Hebrews 2:12 directly quotes verse 22 applied to Christ.

**The Nations' Worship (vv. 27-31)**

> "All the ends of the earth shall remember and turn to the LORD, and all the families of the nations shall worship before you."

Brothers and sisters, this psalm began with one person's suffering—and ends with all nations worshiping. From "my God, why have you forsaken me" to "all the ends of the earth shall turn to the LORD." The cross's suffering is not the destination—the nations' salvation is the purpose.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇22的标题是"大卫的诗，交与伶长。调用朝鹿。""朝鹿"（Aijeleth Shahar，意为"晨曦的母鹿"）可能是一种曲调名称。犹太传统和基督教传统都高度重视这首诗——犹太拉比文献中有将其解释为以斯帖的苦难或以色列整体苦难的传统，而基督教传统从最早期就将其视为弥赛亚诗篇。马太福音27:46和马可福音15:34记载耶稣在十字架上引用了第1节（用亚兰文："以利以利，拉马撒巴各大尼"）。第16节"他们扎了我的手我的脚"是旧约中最直接描述钉十字架的经文——尽管希伯来文的文本变体存在争议（MT读作ka'ari"如狮子"，但七十士译本和死海古卷读作ka'aru"他们扎了"）。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 22's superscription reads "A Psalm of David. To the choirmaster: according to The Doe of the Dawn" (Aijeleth Shahar). Jewish tradition and Christian tradition both highly value this psalm—rabbinic literature interprets it as Esther's suffering or Israel's collective anguish, while Christian tradition from the earliest period viewed it as a messianic psalm. Matthew 27:46 and Mark 15:34 record Jesus quoting verse 1 from the cross (in Aramaic: "Eli, Eli, lema sabachthani"). Verse 16's "they have pierced my hands and feet" is the Old Testament's most direct description of crucifixion—though Hebrew textual variants are debated (MT reads ka'ari "like a lion," but the Septuagint and Dead Sea Scrolls read ka'aru "they pierced").

## 经文结构（中文）

**被离弃的呼喊（1-2节）**——"我的神为什么离弃我"→白日夜间不住呼求。

**信心的挣扎：回顾与现实（3-11节）**——祖宗倚靠蒙拯救(3-5)→但我是虫不是人(6-8)→你从母腹使我有依靠(9-11)。

**苦难的描述：十字架预写（12-18节）**——公牛环绕(12-13)→如水倒出骨头脱节(14-15)→犬类围绕手脚被扎(16-17)→分外衣拈阄(18)。

**求救（19-21节）**——求你不要远离→救我脱离刀剑狮子。

**大转折：赞美与敬拜（22-31节）**——向弟兄传名(22)→敬畏者赞美(23-24)→大会中赞美(25)→万国归顺(27-28)→后裔侍奉(30-31)。

## Structure (English)

**The Cry of Forsakenness (vv. 1-2)**—"My God, why have you forsaken me" → crying day and night.

**Faith's Struggle: Memory and Reality (vv. 3-11)**—Fathers trusted and were delivered (3-5) → but I am a worm (6-8) → you made me trust from the womb (9-11).

**Suffering Described: The Cross Pre-Written (vv. 12-18)**—Bulls surround (12-13) → poured out like water, bones disjointed (14-15) → dogs encompass, hands and feet pierced (16-17) → garments divided, lots cast (18).

**Plea for Help (vv. 19-21)**—Do not be far → save from sword and lion.

**The Great Turn: Praise and Worship (vv. 22-31)**—Declaring name to brothers (22) → fearers praise (23-24) → praise in the assembly (25) → nations turn (27-28) → posterity serves (30-31).

## 神学要点（中文）

**基督的代赎——被神离弃的意义**
"我的神为什么离弃我"——改革宗传统教导基督在十字架上承担了父神对罪的忿怒。韦敏斯德信条第8章：基督……承受了神最可怕的忿怒。基督不是象征性地被离弃——而是真实地承受了与父分离的痛苦——这是地狱的本质。祂代替我们被离弃——好让我们永远不被离弃。

**苦难中的信心——呼喊不等于不信**
诗篇22教导我们：向神呼喊"为什么"不是不信。改革宗传统承认哀歌是信心的合法表达。韦敏斯德信条第十八章论得救的确据教导：真信徒可能在各种情形下灵性受到摇动，但他们永远不会完全失去信心的种子。

**十字架的宇宙意义——从个人到万国**
诗篇22从一个人的苦难到万国的敬拜——改革宗传统教导十字架具有宇宙性的救赎意义。韦敏斯德信条第8章：主耶稣……买赎了一群人。十字架不只救一个人——它改变了历史的方向。

## Theology (English)

**Christ's Substitutionary Atonement—The Meaning of God-Forsakenness**
"My God, why have you forsaken me"—the Reformed tradition teaches Christ bore the Father's wrath against sin on the cross. Westminster Confession chapter 8: Christ: underwent the most grievous torments. Christ was not symbolically forsaken—He truly bore the agony of separation from the Father—the essence of hell. He was forsaken in our place—so we might never be forsaken.

**Faith in Suffering—Crying Out Is Not Unbelief**
Psalm 22 teaches: crying "why" to God is not unbelief. The Reformed tradition acknowledges lament as legitimate faith expression. Westminster Confession chapter 18: True believers may have the assurance of their salvation divers ways shaken...yet they are never utterly destitute of the seed of faith.

**The Cross's Cosmic Significance—From Individual to Nations**
Psalm 22 moves from one person's suffering to all nations worshiping—the Reformed tradition teaches the cross's universal redemptive significance. Westminster Confession chapter 8: "The Lord Jesus...purchased a people." The cross saves not just one person—it changed history's direction.

## 基督的影子（中文）

诗篇第二十二篇是旧约中最完整的十字架预言。

**耶稣亲口引用**——马太福音27:46"约在申初，耶稣大声喊着说：以利！以利！拉马撒巴各大尼？就是说：我的神！我的神！为什么离弃我？"耶稣选择在十字架上最痛苦的时刻引用这首诗的第一句——祂在告诉所有听见的人：诗篇22正在应验。

**逐项应验**：(1)被嗤笑："他倚靠神，神若喜悦他，现在可以救他"（太27:43=诗22:8）；(2)手脚被扎：钉十字架（约20:25）；(3)骨头可以数过：十字架上身体被拉伸；(4)分外衣拈阄："兵丁……拿他的衣服分为四分……又拿他的里衣……就拈阄"（约19:23-24=诗22:18）；(5)口渴：约19:28"我渴了"。

**希伯来书2:11-12的应用**——"所以他称他们为弟兄也不以为耻，说：我要将你的名传与我的弟兄。"基督复活后对门徒说"你们去告诉我的弟兄"（太28:10）——诗篇22:22的"弟兄"在基督身上完全应验。

**从十字架到万国**——诗篇22:27-28"地的四极都要想念耶和华"——基督的大使命"你们要去使万民作我的门徒"（太28:19）正是这个预言的应验。十字架的苦难开启了万国的救恩。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Psalm 22 is the Old Testament's most complete prophecy of the cross.

**Jesus Himself Quoted It**—Matthew 27:46: "About the ninth hour Jesus cried out with a loud voice, saying, 'Eli, Eli, lema sabachthani?' that is, 'My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?'" Jesus chose to quote this psalm's first line at the cross's most agonizing moment—telling all who heard: Psalm 22 is being fulfilled.

**Point-by-Point Fulfillment**: (1) Mocked: "He trusts in God; let God deliver him now" (Matt 27:43 = Ps 22:8); (2) Hands and feet pierced: crucifixion (John 20:25); (3) Bones counted: body stretched on the cross; (4) Garments divided, lots cast: "the soldiers...took his garments and divided them into four parts...for his tunic...they cast lots" (John 19:23-24 = Ps 22:18); (5) Thirst: John 19:28 "I thirst."

**Hebrews 2:11-12's Application**—"He is not ashamed to call them brothers, saying, 'I will tell of your name to my brothers.'" After resurrection, Christ told the disciples "Then said Jesus unto them, Be not afraid: go tell my brethren that they go into Galilee, and there shall they see me." (Matt 28:10)—Psalm 22:22's "brothers" perfectly fulfilled in Christ.

**From Cross to Nations**—Psalm 22:27-28 "All the ends of the earth shall remember and turn to the LORD"—Christ's Great Commission "Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost:" (Matt 28:19) is this prophecy's fulfillment. The cross's suffering opened salvation to all nations.
