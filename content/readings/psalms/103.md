## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第一百零三篇是我写过的最喜乐的一首赞美诗——从头到尾都是赞美。没有哀求、没有抱怨、没有仇敌——只有纯粹的赞美。

**对自己灵魂说话（第1-2节）**

> "我的心哪，你要称颂耶和华！凡在我里面的，也要称颂他的圣名！我的心哪，你要称颂耶和华！不可忘记他的一切恩惠！"

弟兄姐妹，我又一次对自己的灵魂说话——不是等灵魂自己要赞美，而是命令它赞美。"我的心哪，你要称颂"——弟兄姐妹，赞美有时候需要命令自己开始。你的心可能在说"我不想赞美"——但你可以命令它："你要称颂耶和华！""不可忘记"——弟兄姐妹，人最容易忘记的就是神的恩惠。你很容易记得自己的苦——但神的恩惠呢？

**五重恩惠（第3-5节）**

> "他赦免你的一切罪孽，医治你的一切疾病。他救赎你的命脱离死亡，以仁爱慈悲为你的冠冕。他用美物使你所愿的得以知足，以致你如鹰返老还童。"

弟兄姐妹，五样恩惠——赦免、医治、救赎、冠冕、更新。注意次序：先赦免罪——然后医治疾病。弟兄姐妹，罪是最根本的"病"——身体的疾病是其次的。先解决罪的问题——然后一切的医治才有根基。

"以仁爱慈悲为你的冠冕"——弟兄姐妹，冠冕是王戴的——但我说神用"仁爱慈悲"做你的冠冕。你的荣耀不是金子——而是神的仁爱慈悲。弟兄姐妹，当你最软弱、最不值得被爱的时候——神仍然用仁爱慈悲给你戴上冠冕。这不是你赚来的——这是恩典。

**神公义的属性（第6-10节）**

> "耶和华施行公义，为一切受屈的人伸冤。他使摩西知道他的法则，叫以色列人晓得他的作为。耶和华有怜悯，有恩典，不轻易发怒，且有丰盛的慈爱。他不长久责备，也不永远怀怒。他没有按我们的罪过待我们，也没有照我们的罪孽报应我们。"

弟兄姐妹，"不轻易发怒且有丰盛的慈爱"——这是出埃及记34:6耶和华向摩西宣告的自己的名字。弟兄姐妹，这不是我对神的想象——这是神对自己的定义。神说：我是怜悯的、恩典的、不轻易发怒的、有丰盛慈爱的。

"他没有按我们的罪过待我们"——弟兄姐妹，如果神按我们的罪过待我们——我们早就灭亡了。但祂没有。这就是恩典——你得到的不是你应得的。你应得的是审判——你得到的是慈爱。弟兄姐妹，这个真相应该让你一辈子不停地赞美。

**天地之间的距离（第11-12节）**

> "天离地何等的高，他的慈爱向敬畏他的人也是何等的大！东离西有多远，他叫我们的过犯离我们也有多远！"

弟兄姐妹，我用了两个最大的距离来形容——天地之间的距离代表慈爱的大——东西之间的距离代表赦免的彻底。弟兄姐妹，"东离西有多远"——东和西永远不会相遇。你往东走永远到不了西——你往西走永远到不了东。弟兄姐妹，你的罪被移到了那么远的地方——远到再也找不回来。

**父亲的怜恤（第13-14节）**

> "父亲怎样怜恤他的儿女，耶和华也怎样怜恤敬畏他的人！因为他知道我们的本体，思念我们不过是尘土。"

弟兄姐妹，"思念我们不过是尘土"——神不是不知道你的软弱——祂比你自己更清楚。祂不是对你有不切实际的期望然后因为你达不到就失望——祂从一开始就知道你"不过是尘土"。弟兄姐妹，这是何等的安慰——神了解你的材质，祂知道你的极限，祂根据你的本体来怜恤你。

**草与花的比喻（第15-18节）**

> "至于世人，他的年日如草一样。他发旺如野地的花，经风一吹便归无有；他的原处也不再认识他。但耶和华的慈爱归于敬畏他的人，从亘古到永远；他的公义也归于子子孙孙。"

弟兄姐妹，人像草——经风一吹就没了——"他的原处也不再认识他"——你曾经住过的地方不再认识你了。弟兄姐妹，你走了以后这个世界会继续运转——你的位置会被别人填上——你的"原处"不再认识你。但是——"耶和华的慈爱从亘古到永远"。人是短暂的——但神的慈爱是永恒的。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇103从"你要称颂耶和华"开始——到"你们要称颂耶和华"结束。从个人的赞美到万有的赞美——天使、天军、万物、全地都在赞美。弟兄姐妹，你的赞美不是孤独的——你是在加入宇宙的大合唱。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 103 is the most joyful praise hymn I ever wrote—praise from beginning to end. No lament, no complaint, no enemies—only pure praise.

**Speaking to One's Soul (vv. 1-2)**

> "Bless the LORD, O my soul, and all that is within me, bless his holy name! Bless the LORD, O my soul, and forget not all his benefits."

Brothers and sisters, I again speak to my own soul—not waiting for the soul to want to praise, but commanding it. "Bless the LORD, O my soul"—praise sometimes needs commanding yourself to begin. "Forget not"—what humans most easily forget is God's benefits.

**Five Benefits (vv. 3-5)**

> "Who forgives all your iniquity, who heals all your diseases, who redeems your life from the pit, who crowns you with steadfast love and mercy, who satisfies you with good so that your youth is renewed like the eagle's."

Brothers and sisters, five benefits—forgiveness, healing, redemption, crowning, renewal. Note the order: first forgiveness of sin—then healing of disease. Sin is the fundamental "disease." "Who crowns you with steadfast love and mercy"—your glory is not gold—but God's steadfast love and mercy. When you are weakest, most undeserving of love—God still crowns you with steadfast love.

**God's Righteous Character (vv. 6-10)**

> "The LORD is merciful and gracious, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love. He will not always chide, nor will he keep his anger forever. He does not deal with us according to our sins, nor repay us according to our iniquities."

Brothers and sisters, "slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love"—this is Exodus 34:6, Yahweh's self-declaration to Moses. If God dealt with us according to our sins—we would have perished long ago. But He did not. This is grace—you receive not what you deserve.

**The Distance Between Heaven and Earth (vv. 11-12)**

> "For as high as the heavens are above the earth, so great is his steadfast love toward those who fear him; as far as the east is from the west, so far does he remove our transgressions from us."

Brothers and sisters, two greatest distances—heaven-to-earth represents love's greatness; east-to-west represents forgiveness's thoroughness. "As far as east from west"—east and west never meet. Your sins have been moved so far away—they can never be found again.

**A Father's Compassion (vv. 13-14)**

> "As a father shows compassion to his children, so the LORD shows compassion to those who fear him. For he knows our frame; he remembers that we are dust."

Brothers and sisters, "he remembers that we are dust"—God doesn't have unrealistic expectations that lead to disappointment when you fail. He knew from the start that you are "but dust."

**Grass and Flower (vv. 15-18)**

> "As for man, his days are like grass; he flourishes like a flower of the field; for the wind passes over it, and it is gone, and its place knows it no more. But the steadfast love of the LORD is from everlasting to everlasting on those who fear him."

Brothers and sisters, humans like grass—gone when the wind blows—"its place knows it no more." But "the LORD's steadfast love is from everlasting to everlasting." Humans are temporary—but God's love is eternal.

Psalm 103 begins with "Bless the LORD, O my soul" and ends with "Bless the LORD"—from personal praise to universal praise—angels, hosts, all His works, all places. Your praise is not lonely—you join the cosmic chorus.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇103的标题是"大卫的诗"。这是诗篇中最纯粹的赞美诗之一——没有祈求的元素，全篇都是感恩和赞美。第8节"耶和华有怜悯有恩典不轻易发怒且有丰盛的慈爱"几乎逐字引用了出埃及记34:6——耶和华在西奈山向摩西宣告自己名字时的话——这被称为"耶和华的十三属性"（Thirteen Attributes of Mercy）在犹太传统中是赎罪日的核心经文。诗篇103:15-16"他的年日如草一样"与以赛亚书40:6-8的"草必枯干花必凋残"高度平行。彼得前书1:24-25引用了以赛亚书的这段经文。"如鹰返老还童"可能指鹰每年换羽后看起来焕然一新的现象。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 103's superscription: "Of David." One of the Psalter's purest praise psalms—no petition elements, entirely thanksgiving and praise. Verse 8 "The LORD is merciful and gracious, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love" nearly verbatim quotes Exodus 34:6—Yahweh's self-declaration to Moses at Sinai—called the "Thirteen Attributes of Mercy" in Jewish tradition, central to Yom Kippur liturgy. Psalm 103:15-16 "his days are like grass" closely parallels Isaiah 40:6-8 "the grass withers, the flower fades." 1 Peter 1:24-25 quotes Isaiah's passage. "Youth renewed like the eagle's" may refer to the eagle's annual molting that gives it a renewed appearance.

## 经文结构（中文）

**对灵魂的命令（1-2节）**——"你要称颂耶和华"→不可忘记恩惠。
**五重恩惠（3-5节）**——赦免→医治→救赎→冠冕（仁爱慈悲）→更新（如鹰返老还童）。
**神的属性（6-10节）**——公义→怜悯→恩典→不轻易发怒→丰盛慈爱→没有按罪待我们。
**慈爱与赦免的距离（11-12节）**——天离地=慈爱之大；东离西=赦免之远。
**父亲的怜恤（13-14节）**——如父怜子→知道我们是尘土。
**人的短暂vs神的永恒（15-18节）**——人如草花→耶和华慈爱从亘古到永远。
**宇宙的赞美（19-22节）**——宝座在天→天使称颂→天军称颂→万物称颂→我的心称颂。

## Structure (English)

**Command to the Soul (vv. 1-2)**—"Bless the LORD" → forget not all his benefits.
**Five Benefits (vv. 3-5)**—Forgives → heals → redeems → crowns (steadfast love) → renews (like eagle).
**God's Character (vv. 6-10)**—Righteous → merciful → gracious → slow to anger → abounding love → not dealing according to sins.
**Distances of Love and Forgiveness (vv. 11-12)**—Heavens above earth = love's greatness; east from west = forgiveness's thoroughness.
**A Father's Compassion (vv. 13-14)**—Father compassionate to children → knows we are dust.
**Human Brevity vs. God's Eternity (vv. 15-18)**—Man like grass/flower → LORD's love everlasting to everlasting.
**Universal Praise (vv. 19-22)**—Throne in heavens → angels bless → hosts bless → all works bless → my soul blesses.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"没有按我们的罪过待我们"——恩典vs功德**
改革宗传统的核心教义之一——唯独恩典（Sola Gratia）。韦敏斯德信条第11章论称义："不是因为在他们里面注入了什么，或因他们所做的什么……乃是单靠基督和他的义。"诗篇103:10是旧约中最清楚的恩典教导——你得到的不是你应得的。

**"思念我们不过是尘土"——人的软弱与神的耐心**
改革宗传统教导神按照人的本体来怜恤人。韦敏斯德信条第6章论人的堕落和全然败坏。神不是因为我们表现好才爱我们——祂知道我们"不过是尘土"——但祂仍然以仁爱慈悲为我们的冠冕。

**"从亘古到永远"——永恒的约**
改革宗传统教导恩典之约是永恒的。韦敏斯德信条第7章论神与人的约。诗篇103:17"从亘古到永远"——神的慈爱不是暂时的情绪——而是永恒的约定。

## Theology (English)

**"Not Dealing According to Our Sins"—Grace vs. Merit**
A core Reformed doctrine—Sola Gratia. Westminster Confession chapter 11 on justification: not by infusing righteousness into them...but by imputing the obedience and satisfaction of Christ. Psalm 103:10 is the OT's clearest grace teaching—you receive not what you deserve.

**"Remembers We Are Dust"—Human Weakness and Divine Patience**
The Reformed tradition teaches God shows compassion according to human nature. Westminster Confession chapter 6 on the fall and total depravity. God doesn't love us because we perform well—He knows we are "but dust"—yet still crowns us with steadfast love.

**"From Everlasting to Everlasting"—The Eternal Covenant**
The Reformed tradition teaches the covenant of grace is eternal. Westminster Confession chapter 7 on God's covenant with man. Psalm 103:17 "from everlasting to everlasting"—God's steadfast love is not temporary emotion—but eternal covenant commitment.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"赦免你的一切罪孽"——基督的赎罪**——以弗所书1:7"我们藉这爱子的血得蒙救赎，过犯得以赦免，乃是照他丰富的恩典。"诗篇103:3的"赦免一切罪孽"在基督的十字架上得到了终极的成就——不是暂时的遮盖，而是永远的赦免。

**"东离西有多远"——基督挪去了罪**——希伯来书10:17-18"我不再记念他们的罪愆和他们的过犯。这些罪过既已赦免，就不用再为罪献祭了。"基督一次献祭使罪"东离西那么远"——永远不再记念。

**"以仁爱慈悲为冠冕"——基督的爱加冕信徒**——罗马书8:37-39"然而，靠着爱我们的主，在这一切的事上已经得胜有余了。因为我深信无论是死，是生……都不能叫我们与神的爱隔绝。"基督的爱就是那顶"仁爱慈悲"的冠冕——信徒在基督里永远戴着。

**"如父怜子"——基督启示天父的心**——约翰福音14:9"人看见了我就是看见了父。"诗篇103:13的"父亲怜恤儿女"——基督启示了天父的真实面目——祂不是遥远的法官，而是怜恤儿女的父亲。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Forgives All Your Iniquity"—Christ's Atonement**—Ephesians 1:7: "In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of our trespasses, according to the riches of his grace." Psalm 103:3's "forgives all iniquity" achieved ultimate fulfillment on Christ's cross—not temporary covering but eternal forgiveness.

**"As Far as East from West"—Christ Removed Sin**—Hebrews 10:17-18: "I will remember their sins and their lawless deeds no more. Where there is forgiveness of these, there is no longer any offering for sin." Christ's one sacrifice moved sin "as far as east from west"—never again remembered.

**"Crowned with Steadfast Love"—Christ's Love Crowns Believers**—Romans 8:37-39: "in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us. For I am sure that neither death nor life...will be able to separate us from the love of God." Christ's love is that crown of "steadfast love and mercy"—believers in Christ wear it forever.

**"As a Father Shows Compassion"—Christ Reveals the Father's Heart**—John 14:9: "Whoever has seen me has seen the Father." Psalm 103:13's "father compassionate to children"—Christ revealed the Father's true face—not a distant judge but a father compassionate toward his children.
