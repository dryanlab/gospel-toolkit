## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第五十一篇是我一生中最痛苦的祷告——在先知拿单指出我的罪之后，在拔示巴和乌利亚的事之后，我跪下来写了这首诗。这不是一首悲观的诗——而是一首关于神的怜悯有多大的诗。

**哀求怜悯（第1-2节）**

> "神啊，求你按你的慈爱怜恤我！按你丰盛的慈悲涂抹我的过犯！求你将我的罪孽洗除净尽，并洁除我的罪。"

弟兄姐妹，注意我如何开口——不是靠自己的行为、不是靠自己的悔改程度——而是"按你的慈爱""按你丰盛的慈悲"。我没有资格来到神面前——我知道。唯一的依据是神自己的性情：祂是慈爱的、祂的慈悲是丰盛的。弟兄姐妹，你来到神面前认罪的时候，你依靠的是什么？是自己的泪水流得够多吗？是悔改的感情够真诚吗？不——唯一的依据是神的慈爱。

三个并行动词——怜恤、涂抹、洗除。"涂抹"是把记录罪的文件上的墨水抹去——罪的记录被清除。"洗除"是洗涤——把污点彻底洗干净。"洁除"是宗教洁净仪式——使不洁净的人变成洁净。弟兄姐妹，神对你的罪是全面处理的——不只是表面清洗，而是彻底的洁净。

**真实的认罪（第3-4节）**

> "因为我知道我的过犯；我的罪常在我面前。我向你犯罪，惟独得罪了你；在你眼前行了这恶，以致你责备我的时候显为公义，判断我的时候显为清正。"

弟兄姐妹，"惟独得罪了你"——这是最令人震惊的一句。我明明伤害了拔示巴、杀害了乌利亚——我怎么能说"惟独得罪了你"？弟兄姐妹，这是真实认罪的本质——最终你的罪不只是对人的伤害，而是对神的冒犯。因为神是一切道德的基础——所有的罪首先是对祂的背叛。

"在你责备我的时候显为公义"——弟兄姐妹，这是真正悔改的标志：你承认神对你的审判是公义的。你不辩解、不推卸责任、不说"但是……"——你说：你审判我，你是公义的。当你能这样说的时候——你的悔改是真实的。

**原罪的承认（第5节）**

> "我是在罪孽里生的，在我母亲怀胎的时候就有了罪。"

弟兄姐妹，我在这里不是找借口——而是承认罪的根源比这一件事更深。我犯的这个罪不是偶然的——它来自我本性的败坏。"在罪孽里生"——从出生就有罪性。弟兄姐妹，这不是说婴儿有罪行——而是说人的本性在出生时已经是败坏的。这是改革宗传统所说的"原罪"。

**内里的真实（第6节）**

> "你所喜爱的是内里诚实；你在我隐密处，必使我得智慧。"

弟兄姐妹，神要的不是外表的仪式——而是"内里诚实"。我犯的罪之所以是罪，是因为我心里有诡诈——表面上我是王，表面上我是"合神心意的人"——但内里有隐藏的情欲和自私。神看穿了我的内里。弟兄姐妹，你有没有经历过这种——外表在敬拜，内里却是假的？神要的是"内里诚实"。

**重新创造的祷告（第10-12节）**

> "神啊，求你为我造清洁的心，使我里面重新有正直的灵。不要丢弃我，使我离开你的面；不要从我收回你的圣灵。求你使我仍得救恩之乐，赐我乐意的灵扶持我。"

弟兄姐妹，"造清洁的心"——"造"这个词在创世记1:1用于创造天地——是从无到有的创造。我没有能力改变自己的心——我需要神"造"一颗新心。弟兄姐妹，你有没有试过靠意志力改变自己？你是否发现那颗旧心怎么改都改不过来？是的——你需要的不是"改心"，而是"新心"——神的重新创造。

"不要丢弃我……不要从我收回你的圣灵"——弟兄姐妹，大卫的祷告让我们看到圣灵在旧约时代的工作——圣灵可以"离开"人（比较扫罗王，撒上16:14）。但在新约时代，耶稣应许："我要求父，父就另外赐给你们一位保惠师，叫他永远与你们同在"（约14:16）。弟兄姐妹，你不用祷告"不要从我收回圣灵"——因为在基督里，圣灵永远与你同在。

**忧伤痛悔的心（第17节）**

> "神所要的祭，就是忧伤的灵；神啊，忧伤痛悔的心，你必不轻看。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗的高峰——"神所要的祭"不是公牛、不是燔祭——而是"忧伤的灵"和"忧伤痛悔的心"。弟兄姐妹，你能给神的最好的事不是你的成就，不是你的奉献，不是你的眼泪有多多——而是一颗真正在神面前破碎的心。"你必不轻看"——神不会小看它。带着忧伤痛悔的心来到神面前——祂必不轻看。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 51 is the most agonizing prayer of my life—after the prophet Nathan confronted my sin, after the matter of Bathsheba and Uriah, I knelt and wrote this psalm. This is not a despairing poem—but a poem about how great God's mercy is.

**Plea for Mercy (vv. 1-2)**

> "Have mercy on me, O God, according to your steadfast love; according to your abundant mercy blot out my transgressions. Wash me thoroughly from my iniquity, and cleanse me from my sin."

Brothers and sisters, notice how I begin—not by my deeds, not by the depth of my repentance—but "according to your steadfast love," "according to your abundant mercy." I have no standing before God—I know that. The only basis is God's own nature: He is loving, His mercy is abundant.

Three parallel verbs—mercy, blot out, wash. "Blot out"—erasing ink from the document recording sin. "Wash"—thoroughly cleansing away stains. "Cleanse"—ritual purification, making the unclean clean. God's treatment of your sin is comprehensive—not surface cleaning but thorough purification.

**Genuine Confession (vv. 3-4)**

> "For I know my transgressions, and my sin is ever before me. Against you, you only, have I sinned and done what is evil in your sight, so that you may be justified in your words and blameless in your judgment."

Brothers and sisters, "against you only"—most shocking line. I clearly harmed Bathsheba, killed Uriah—how can I say "against you only"? This is authentic confession's essence—ultimately your sin is not just harm to people but offense against God. Because God is the foundation of all morality—all sin is first betrayal against Him.

"So that you may be justified"—this is genuine repentance's mark: you acknowledge God's judgment against you is just. No excuses, no deflection, no "but..."—you say: you judge me and you are right.

**The Acknowledgment of Original Sin (v. 5)**

> "Behold, I was brought forth in iniquity, and in sin did my mother conceive me."

Brothers and sisters, I am not making excuses—but acknowledging sin's root is deeper than this one act. "Brought forth in iniquity"—the sinful nature from birth. Not that infants commit sins—but human nature at birth is already corrupt. This is what the Reformed tradition calls "original sin."

**The Prayer for Re-creation (vv. 10-12)**

> "Create in me a clean heart, O God, and renew a right spirit within me. Cast me not away from your presence, and take not your Holy Spirit from me. Restore to me the joy of your salvation, and uphold me with a willing spirit."

Brothers and sisters, "create a clean heart"—"create" is the word used in Genesis 1:1 for creation from nothing. I cannot change my own heart—I need God to "create" a new heart. Have you tried changing yourself by willpower? You don't need a "changed heart"—you need a "new heart"—God's re-creation.

**A Contrite Heart (v. 17)**

> "The sacrifices of God are a broken spirit; a broken and contrite heart, O God, you will not despise."

Brothers and sisters, this is the psalm's climax—"the sacrifices of God" are not bulls or burnt offerings—but "a broken spirit" and "a broken and contrite heart." The best thing you can bring God is not your achievements, your offerings, your tears—but a heart truly broken before Him. "You will not despise"—God will not look down on it. Come before God with a broken and contrite heart—He will not despise it.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇51的标题给出了明确的历史背景："大卫的诗，交与伶长。是他与拔示巴同寝、先知拿单来见他以后作的。"这是诗篇中少见的有明确历史事件背景的诗篇。撒母耳记下11-12章记载了这个故事——大卫与拔示巴通奸、谋杀了她的丈夫赫人乌利亚、然后先知拿单用"有一个穷人只有一只小母羊"的比喻揭发大卫的罪。"忧伤的灵"和"忧伤痛悔的心"在整个教会历史中成为了真实悔改的最经典描述。路德改革的核心之一就是真实的悔改——不是外在的功德，而是内心在神面前的破碎。奥古斯丁、约翰·加尔文都对此诗有大量注释。"为我造清洁的心"（创造一颗新心）在基督教神学中成为重生的预表。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 51's superscription provides explicit historical context: "A Psalm of David, when Nathan the prophet went to him, after he had gone in to Bathsheba." This is one of the few psalms with a clearly identified historical event. 2 Samuel 11-12 records the story—David's adultery with Bathsheba, the murder of her husband Uriah the Hittite, then the prophet Nathan exposing David's sin through the parable of the poor man's one little lamb. "A broken spirit" and "a broken and contrite heart" became throughout church history the classic description of genuine repentance. One of Luther's reforms' cores was genuine repentance—not external merit but inner brokenness before God. Augustine, John Calvin both extensively commented on this psalm. "Create in me a clean heart" became in Christian theology a foreshadowing of regeneration.

**Plea for Mercy (vv. 1-2)**—According to steadfast love → according to abundant mercy → have mercy / blot out / wash / cleanse.

**Genuine Confession (vv. 3-5)**—I know my transgressions → "against you only" → evil in your sight → brought forth in iniquity.

**Longing for Inner Truth (v. 6)**—God desires inner truth → wisdom in the hidden part.

**Re-creation (vv. 10-12)**—Create a clean heart → do not cast away → take not the Spirit → restore joy of salvation.

**The True Sacrifice (vv. 16-17)**—God does not delight in burnt offering → sacrifices of God: broken spirit → contrite heart not despised.

## 神学要点（中文）

**原罪——全然败坏**
诗篇51:5"在罪孽里生"——改革宗传统教导原罪的教义。韦敏斯德信条第6章：由于这个原罪，人的本性完全败坏，与一切良善完全无能为力……并且完全倾向于一切恶事。大卫不是把责任推给环境——而是承认他的罪根植于他的本性。

**重生——神的创造**
"为我造清洁的心"——改革宗传统教导重生是圣灵的工作。韦敏斯德信条第十章论有效恩召教导：圣灵在选民里面创造新的心，更新他们的意志，有效地呼召他们出黑暗进入奇妙光明。人不能改变自己的心——必须被神重新创造。以西结书36:26"我也要赐给你们一个新心"——这个应许在新约时代通过基督的灵成为现实。

**真实的悔改——不只是感情**
改革宗传统教导悔改不只是情感的流露。韦敏斯德信条第15章：悔改是一种救恩的恩赐……悔改者因认识到罪的污秽和可恶……就为自己的罪忧伤……并从此转向神。诗篇51展示了真实悔改的各个方面：认识罪（3-5节）→哀痛（3节）→认罪（4节）→悔改（10-13节）。

## Theology (English)

**Original Sin—Total Depravity**
Psalm 51:5 "brought forth in iniquity"—the Reformed tradition teaches the doctrine of original sin. Westminster Confession chapter 6: "From this original corruption...we are utterly indisposed, disabled, and made opposite to all good, and wholly inclined to all evil." David is not blaming environment—he acknowledges his sin rooted in his nature.

**Regeneration—God's Creation**
"Create in me a clean heart"—the Reformed tradition teaches regeneration is the Holy Spirit's work. Westminster Confession chapter 10 on effectual calling: The Spirit...creates a new heart in him...effectually calling him out of darkness into marvelous light. Humans cannot change their own heart—they must be re-created by God. Ezekiel 36:26 "I will give you a new heart"—this promise becomes reality through Christ's Spirit in the New Testament.

**Genuine Repentance—More Than Emotion**
The Reformed tradition teaches repentance is more than emotional expression. Westminster Confession chapter 15: "Repentance unto life is a saving grace...the penitent, being ashamed of his sins...so grieves for them...and turns from them unto God." Psalm 51 displays all aspects of genuine repentance: knowing sin (vv. 3-5) → grief (v. 3) → confession (v. 4) → turning (vv. 10-13).

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"惟独得罪了你"——基督代替我们承担这罪**
基督自己没有罪——但祂代替我们站在神面前认罪。哥林多后书5:21"神使那无罪的，替我们成为罪"——基督承担了我们"惟独得罪了你"的罪，使我们得以无罪站立在神面前。

**"为我造清洁的心"——圣灵的重生**
约翰福音3:5"人若不是从水和圣灵生的，就不能进神的国。"诗篇51:10的"为我造清洁的心"在新约中通过基督的救恩成为现实——"若有人在基督里，他就是新造的人"（林后5:17）。基督带来了诗篇51所渴望的彻底更新。

**"忧伤痛悔的心你必不轻看"——基督接纳破碎的人**
路加福音18:13-14那个税吏祷告"神啊，开恩可怜我这个罪人"——他下去的时候比法利赛人更算为义了。这正是诗篇51的应验——"忧伤痛悔的心你必不轻看"。基督接纳那些破碎的人——"我来本不是召义人，乃是召罪人悔改"（路5:32）。

**"不要从我收回你的圣灵"——新约应许的超越**
大卫的这个祷告揭示了旧约信徒的担忧。但约翰福音14:16耶稣应许圣灵"永远与你们同在"——信徒不需要再祷告"不要从我收回圣灵"。这是新约恩约远超旧约的地方——内住的圣灵永不离开信徒。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Against You Only"—Christ Bears This Sin for Us**
Christ Himself had no sin—but He stood before God bearing our sin. 2 Corinthians 5:21: "For our sake he made him to be sin who knew no sin." Christ bore our sin of "against you only," enabling us to stand before God without sin.

**"Create in Me a Clean Heart"—The Spirit's Regeneration**
John 3:5: "Unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God." Psalm 51:10's "create in me a clean heart" becomes reality in the New Testament through Christ's salvation—"Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new." (2 Cor 5:17). Christ brings the thorough renewal Psalm 51 longed for.

**"A Contrite Heart You Will Not Despise"—Christ Receives the Broken**
Luke 18:13-14: the tax collector prayed "God, be merciful to me, a sinner"—he went down justified rather than the Pharisee. This is Psalm 51's fulfillment—"a broken and contrite heart you will not despise." Christ receives the broken: "I came not to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance." (Luke 5:32).

**"Take Not Your Holy Spirit"—New Covenant's Surpassing Promise**
David's prayer reveals Old Testament believers' concern. But John 14:16 Jesus promised the Spirit "will be with you forever"—believers need no longer pray "take not your Holy Spirit." This is where the new covenant far surpasses the old—the indwelling Spirit never leaves believers.
