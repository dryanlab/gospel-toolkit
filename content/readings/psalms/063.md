## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第六十三篇是我在犹大旷野时写下的——可能是押沙龙叛变、我被迫逃离耶路撒冷的那段日子。旷野是干旱的地方，没有水，没有遮荫，没有安慰。但在旷野里，我写下了对神最深的渴慕。

**干旱之地的渴慕（第1节）**

> "神啊，你是我的神，我要切切地寻求你。在干旱疲乏无水之地，我的心渴想你；我的肉体切慕你。"

弟兄姐妹，"你是我的神"——即使在旷野中，即使逃亡在外，这个关系没有改变。祂仍然是"我的神"。弟兄姐妹，环境可以改变——但"你是我的神"这个事实不会因为你在旷野就改变了。

"干旱疲乏无水之地"——我在旷野里——那里真的没有水，真的干旱。但这句话同时也是属灵状态的描述。弟兄姐妹，你有没有经历过属灵的旷野？那种干燥、疲乏、感觉神很远的状态？我在物质旷野中经历属灵的渴慕——我的外在旷野成了我内心旷野的镜子。

"我的肉体切慕你"——弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗最令人惊讶的地方。我说连我的"肉体"都在渴慕神——不只是灵魂，连身体都在寻求神。这表达了一种全人的渴慕——灵魂与身体一起向神倾伏。

**圣所中见过的荣美（第2节）**

> "我在圣所中曾如此瞻仰你，为要见你的能力和你的荣耀。"

弟兄姐妹，我现在在旷野——但他记得在圣所中曾经"瞻仰"神的荣耀。这个记忆是旷野中的粮食——他吃的是记忆。弟兄姐妹，你有没有属灵的记忆——神在某个时刻向你显示的荣耀，你一辈子都忘不了？那个记忆是旷野中的粮食。它在说：神是真实的，我曾见过祂，我会再见到祂。

**慈爱比生命更好（第3节）**

> "因你的慈爱比生命更好，我的嘴唇要颂赞你。"

弟兄姐妹，这是整首诗——可以说是整本诗篇中——最大胆的一句话。"慈爱比生命更好"。弟兄姐妹，生命是人最宝贝的东西——你把什么都愿意放下，但你不会轻易放下生命。但我说：神的慈爱比生命更好。

弟兄姐妹，你相信这句话吗？如果你有机会得到神的慈爱但要失去生命——你会换吗？大卫的答案是：会。因为生命是暂时的，但神的慈爱是永恒的。得了生命而没有神的慈爱——你最终失去一切。得了神的慈爱而失去生命——你最终得到一切。

**一生的颂赞（第4-5节）**

> "我还活的时候，要这样称颂你；我要奉你的名举手。我的心得到满足，如同吃了丰盛的食物；我的口要以欢乐的嘴唇颂赞你。"

弟兄姐妹，"我还活的时候"——不是等到我离开旷野、不是等到我得胜凯旋——而是现在，在旷野里，"还活的时候"就要颂赞。信心不等待好环境——信心在坏环境中就开始感谢。

"我的心得到满足如同吃了丰盛的食物"——弟兄姐妹，我在旷野里——我的肚子可能真的饿着——但我的心"得到满足"。属灵的饱足和物质的丰富不是同一件事。你可以物质丰富而属灵饥渴——也可以物质贫乏而属灵饱足。我此刻选择了后者。

**夜间的思念（第6-7节）**

> "我在床上记念你，在夜更的时候思想你。因为你曾帮助我，我就在你翅膀的荫下欢呼。"

弟兄姐妹，"在床上记念你"——夜晚是思念神的好时候。白天有太多干扰——但夜晚，在黑暗的旷野里，当外面的声音安静了，我思想神。弟兄姐妹，你有没有在夜里思念神？不是辗转反侧的焦虑——而是平静地思想祂。

"在你翅膀的荫下欢呼"——弟兄姐妹，这是一个母鸡护雏的意象。母鸡把翅膀张开，把小鸡都遮在翅膀下——那个空间是安全的、温暖的、被遮蔽的。我在旷野中经历这种庇护。弟兄姐妹，你有没有在患难中感到被神的翅膀遮盖？不是问题消失了——而是在问题中被遮蔽。

**信靠的终点（第8节）**

> "我心紧跟你；你的右手扶持我。"

弟兄姐妹，"紧跟"——不是远远地跟——而是"紧"跟。靠得很近、不放松、不滑落。弟兄姐妹，跟随神的生活需要"紧"——一旦松懈，就容易滑落。"你的右手扶持我"——紧跟的另一面是：神的右手在扶持你。你能紧跟，是因为神在扶持你。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇63从旷野的渴慕——到神的慈爱比生命更好——到床上的思念——到紧跟神的决心——这是旷野中信心的完整图画。你现在在旷野吗？这首诗是为你写的。在旷野里，你可以渴慕、可以记念、可以在翅膀的荫下欢呼、可以紧跟神。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 63 was written during my time in the Judean wilderness—probably when Absalom's rebellion forced me to flee Jerusalem. The wilderness was parched, without water, without shade, without comfort. But in the wilderness, I wrote my deepest longing for God.

**Longing in the Dry Land (v. 1)**

> "O God, you are my God; earnestly I seek you; my soul thirsts for you; my flesh faints for you, as in a dry and weary land where there is no water."

Brothers and sisters, "you are my God"—even in the wilderness, even in exile, this relationship is unchanged. Brothers and sisters, circumstances change—but "you are my God" is not altered by your being in a wilderness.

"My flesh faints for you"—the psalm's most surprising statement. Even his "flesh" longs for God—not just soul, but body. This expresses whole-person longing—soul and body together inclining toward God.

**The Glory Seen in the Sanctuary (v. 2)**

> "So I have looked upon you in the sanctuary, beholding your power and glory."

Brothers and sisters, I am now in the wilderness—but he remembers having "beheld" God's glory in the sanctuary. This memory is food in the wilderness. Do you have a spiritual memory—a moment when God revealed His glory to you, unforgettable for life? That memory is wilderness food. It says: God is real, I have seen Him, I will see Him again.

**Steadfast Love Better Than Life (v. 3)**

> "Because your steadfast love is better than life, my lips will praise you."

Brothers and sisters, this may be the boldest sentence in the entire Psalter. "Steadfast love is better than life." Life is what people treasure most—you'd surrender anything before surrendering life. But I say: God's steadfast love is better than life.

Do you believe this? If you could receive God's steadfast love but lose your life—would you exchange? David's answer: yes. Because life is temporary but God's steadfast love is eternal.

**Lifelong Praise (vv. 4-5)**

> "So I will bless you as long as I live; in your name I will lift up my hands. My soul will be satisfied as with fat and rich food, and my mouth will praise you with joyful lips."

Brothers and sisters, "as long as I live"—not waiting to leave the wilderness, not waiting for victory—right now, in the wilderness, while still alive, praising. Faith doesn't wait for good circumstances—faith gives thanks in bad ones.

"My soul satisfied as with fat and rich food"—I am in the wilderness, possibly literally hungry—yet his soul is "satisfied." Spiritual fullness and material abundance are not the same thing.

**Night Meditation (vv. 6-7)**

> "When I remember you upon my bed, and meditate on you in the watches of the night...In the shadow of your wings I will sing for joy."

Brothers and sisters, "in the shadow of your wings"—a mother hen protecting chicks, spreading wings wide, gathering all her chicks beneath them—that space is safe, warm, sheltered. I experienced this shelter in the wilderness. Have you felt covered by God's wings in trouble? Not that the problem disappeared—but being sheltered within it.

**The End of Trust (v. 8)**

> "My soul clings to you; your right hand upholds me."

Brothers and sisters, "clings"—not following from afar—but "clinging." Close, without loosening, without sliding. Following God requires "clinging"—once you relax, you easily slip. "Your right hand upholds me"—you can cling because God upholds you.

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 63 moves from wilderness longing—to steadfast love better than life—to nighttime meditation—to clinging to God—this is the complete picture of faith in the wilderness. Are you in a wilderness now? This psalm was written for you.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇63的标题是"大卫的诗，他在犹大旷野的时候。"大多数注释家认为这指的是押沙龙叛变时大卫逃往旷野的那段时期（撒下15-17章）——大卫被迫离开耶路撒冷，渡过汲沦溪，进入旷野。还有人认为是年轻时逃避扫罗时期。犹大旷野（Judean wilderness）是耶路撒冷以东到死海之间的干旱地区——极度干旱、荒凉、炎热。"夜更"（watches of the night）指古代以色列把夜间分成三段，每段约四小时。"翅膀的荫下"是诗篇中常见的庇护意象（参诗17:8, 36:7, 57:1, 91:4），可能来自会幕中基路伯张开翅膀遮掩约柜的意象（出25:20）。

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 63's superscription: "A Psalm of David, when he was in the wilderness of Judah." Most commentators believe this refers to David's flight into the wilderness during Absalom's rebellion (2 Sam 15-17)—David forced from Jerusalem, crossing the Kidron Valley, entering the wilderness. Others suggest his earlier flight from Saul. The Judean wilderness is the arid region east of Jerusalem toward the Dead Sea—extremely dry, desolate, hot. "Watches of the night" refers to the ancient Israelite division of night into three periods of about four hours each. "Shadow of your wings" is a common Psalter sheltering image (cf. Pss 17:8, 36:7, 57:1, 91:4), possibly from the cherubim spreading wings over the ark in the tabernacle (Exod 25:20).

## 经文结构（中文）

**干旱之地的渴慕（1节）**——"你是我的神"→切切寻求→心渴想→肉体切慕。

**圣所的记忆（2节）**——曾在圣所瞻仰→见能力和荣耀。

**慈爱比生命更好（3-5节）**——慈爱比生命好→称颂你→举手→心得满足如丰盛食物→欢乐颂赞。

**夜间的思念（6-7节）**——床上记念你→夜更思想你→曾帮助我→翅膀荫下欢呼。

**紧跟的决心（8-11节）**——我心紧跟你→右手扶持→仇敌被灭→王要靠神欢喜→说谎者的口必被塞住。

## Structure (English)

**Longing in Dry Land (v. 1)**—"You are my God" → earnestly seeking → soul thirsts → flesh faints.

**Sanctuary Memory (v. 2)**—Beheld you in sanctuary → saw power and glory.

**Love Better Than Life (vv. 3-5)**—Steadfast love better than life → bless you → lift up hands → soul satisfied like rich food → joyful praise.

**Night Meditation (vv. 6-7)**—Remember you on bed → meditate in night watches → you have helped me → sing joy in wing-shadow.

**Clinging Resolve (vv. 8-11)**—Soul clings to you → right hand upholds → enemies destroyed → king rejoices in God → liars' mouths stopped.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"慈爱比生命更好"——神是最高善**
改革宗传统教导神是最高善（summum bonum）。韦敏斯德小要理问答第1问："（一）荣耀神；（二）以祂为乐，直到永远。""慈爱比生命更好"是"以祂为乐"的极致表达——神自己比生命更有价值。奥古斯丁："我们的心不得安息，直到在你里面得安息。"

**"我心紧跟你"——perseverance坚忍**
改革宗传统教导圣徒的坚忍。韦敏斯德信条第17章：神所接受的人……不会完全堕落，反而必然坚持到底，直到得救。"紧跟"是主动的努力——但能"紧跟"的根基是"你的右手扶持我"——神的保守。

**旷野的价值——神使用苦难**
改革宗传统教导神使用苦难成就祂的目的。韦敏斯德信条第5章论神的护理：神在他的护理中，经常使用一些手段……甚至也允许他们暂时遭受各种苦难。旷野不是神遗忘了大卫——而是神在旷野中深化大卫对祂的渴慕。

## Theology (English)

**"Steadfast Love Better Than Life"—God Is the Supreme Good**
The Reformed tradition teaches God is the summum bonum (highest good). Westminster Shorter Catechism Q1: "Man's chief end is to glorify God, and to enjoy him forever." "Steadfast love better than life" is the supreme expression of "enjoying God"—God Himself is more valuable than life. Augustine: "Our heart is restless until it repose in Thee."

**"My Soul Clings to You"—Perseverance**
The Reformed tradition teaches the perseverance of the saints. Westminster Confession chapter 17: "those whom God has accepted...shall certainly persevere therein to the end, and be eternally saved." "Clinging" is active effort—but the foundation enabling "clinging" is "your right hand upholds me"—God's preservation.

**The Value of Wilderness—God Uses Suffering**
The Reformed tradition teaches God uses suffering for His purposes. Westminster Confession chapter 5: God in his ordinary providence makes use of means...yet sometimes permit them to fall into...various afflictions. The wilderness is not God forgetting David—but God deepening David's longing for Him in the wilderness.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**基督在旷野四十天**——马太福音4:1-11记载耶稣被圣灵引到旷野受魔鬼试探四十天。耶稣经历了比大卫更深的旷野——饥饿、干渴、孤独。但祂"靠着神的话语"胜过了试探——"人活着不是单靠食物，乃是靠神口里所出的一切话。"诗篇63的渴慕("我的肉体切慕你")在耶稣的旷野经历中得到了完全的表达。

**"慈爱比生命更好"——基督为我们舍命**——基督在十字架上证明了"慈爱比生命更好"——祂真的用自己的生命换取了对我们的慈爱。腓立比书2:8"既有人的样子，就自己卑微，存心顺服，以至于死，且死在十字架上。"基督舍了生命——彰显了神慈爱的极致。

**"翅膀的荫下"——基督的庇护**——马太福音23:37"耶路撒冷啊……我多次愿意聚集你的儿女，好像母鸡把小鸡聚集在翅膀底下，只是你们不愿意。"耶稣用了与诗篇63:7完全相同的"翅膀"意象——祂自己就是那双张开翅膀来庇护我们的。信徒在基督里得到"翅膀的荫下"的庇护。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Christ in the Wilderness for Forty Days**—Matthew 4:1-11 records Jesus being led by the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted forty days. Jesus experienced a deeper wilderness than David—hunger, thirst, loneliness. But He overcame temptation "by the Word of God"—"Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of God." Psalm 63's longing ("my flesh faints for you") found complete expression in Jesus' wilderness experience.

**"Steadfast Love Better Than Life"—Christ Gave His Life for Us**—Christ on the cross proved "steadfast love is better than life"—He truly exchanged His life for love toward us. Philippians 2:8: "Being found in human form, he humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross." Christ gave His life—displaying the ultimate of God's steadfast love.

**"Shadow of Wings"—Christ's Shelter**—Matthew 23:37: "O Jerusalem...How often would I have gathered your children together as a hen gathers her brood under her wings, and you were not willing!" Jesus used exactly the same "wings" image as Psalm 63:7—He Himself is the one who spreads wings to shelter us. Believers receive shelter "in the shadow of wings" in Christ.
