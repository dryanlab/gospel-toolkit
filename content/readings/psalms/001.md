## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是大卫。诗篇第一篇是整本诗篇的大门——你打开这扇门，就决定了你走哪条路。这首诗没有标题、没有署名——因为它不属于某一个人，它属于每一个站在十字路口的人。

**义人的三个"不"（第1节）**

> "不从恶人的计谋，不站罪人的道路，不坐亵慢人的座位。"

弟兄姐妹，注意这三个动词的递进：从→站→坐。"从"是路过——你只是经过恶人的想法；"站"是停留——你开始在罪的道路上驻足；"坐"是定居——你已经在亵慢的座位上安家了。罪的进展就是这样——从偶然的接触到习惯性的停留到舒适的定居。没有人一开始就"坐在亵慢人的座位上"——他们都是先"从"恶人的计谋经过，觉得没什么大不了，然后就"站"住了。

弟兄姐妹，"亵慢人"是什么人？不是公然犯罪的人——而是嘲笑敬虔、讥讽信仰的人。箴言21:24说"心骄气傲的人名叫亵慢。"亵慢人的特征是：他们自以为高于一切神圣的事物。你身边有没有这样的声音——"信什么信""别那么认真""都什么年代了还守这些规矩"？这就是亵慢人的座位。

**义人的喜乐——律法的默想（第2节）**

> "惟喜爱耶和华的律法，昼夜思想，这人便为有福。"

弟兄姐妹，义人的生命不只是"不做什么"——而是积极地"做什么"。他"喜爱"耶和华的律法——不是被迫遵守，而是从心里喜欢。"昼夜思想"——希伯来文是"hagah"，意思是低声默念、反复咀嚼，就像牛反刍一样。弟兄姐妹，你有没有一节经文在你心里反复回响、白天想了晚上还在想？那就是"昼夜思想"。

注意——不是"喜爱神学知识"，而是"喜爱耶和华的律法"。律法不是抽象的学问——它是耶和华亲口说的话。你喜爱一个人的话，是因为你喜爱说话的那个人。喜爱律法的根基是喜爱耶和华自己。

**义人的比喻——溪水旁的树（第3节）**

> "他要像一棵树栽在溪水旁，按时候结果子，叶子也不枯干。凡他所做的尽都顺利。"

弟兄姐妹，"栽在溪水旁"——不是野生的、不是自己长的、而是被人"栽"在那里的。你能成为溪水旁的树，不是因为你自己选了个好位置——而是因为神把你栽在那里。你的属灵生命是恩典的栽培，不是自我奋斗的结果。

"按时候结果子"——不是每天都结果子。弟兄姐妹，有些季节你看不到果子——冬天的树光秃秃的，但根还在吸水。不要因为你现在看不到果子就怀疑自己的根。义人的果子是"按时候"的——神的时间表不是你的时间表。

"叶子也不枯干"——即使在不结果子的季节，叶子也是绿的。生命力不因环境改变而消失。弟兄姐妹，你可能正在灵性的冬天——没有明显的果子、没有激动人心的经历——但如果你的根还在溪水旁，你的叶子就不会枯干。

**恶人的比喻——被风吹散的糠秕（第4节）**

> "恶人并不是这样，乃像糠秕被风吹散。"

弟兄姐妹，对比何等鲜明！树——有根、有深度、有生命力、经得起风暴。糠秕——没有根、没有重量、没有生命力、一阵风就散了。弟兄姐妹，糠秕看起来和麦子很像——在打谷场上它们混在一起，你分不清。但风一吹——真假立判。试炼就是那阵风——它不改变你的本质，它只是显露你的本质。

**审判的结局（第5-6节）**

> "因此，当审判的时候，恶人必站立不住；罪人在义人的会中也是如此。因为耶和华知道义人的道路；恶人的道路却必灭亡。"

弟兄姐妹，"耶和华知道义人的道路"——"知道"在希伯来文中不只是"知晓信息"，而是"亲密地认识、关怀、看顾"。神"知道"你的道路——意思是祂在看着你、保护你、带领你。而恶人的道路"必灭亡"——不是说恶人不存在了，而是他们的道路通向虚无——没有终点、没有意义、没有永恒。

弟兄姐妹，诗篇第一篇把整本诗篇的核心问题摆在你面前：你走哪条路？溪水旁的树还是被风吹散的糠秕？这不是一次性的选择——而是每天早上醒来时的选择。你今天选择"昼夜思想"耶和华的律法，还是"从恶人的计谋"经过？两条路，两个结局——中间没有第三条路。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am David. Psalm 1 is the gateway to the entire Psalter—when you open this door, you decide which road you walk. This psalm has no title, no attribution—because it belongs not to one person, but to everyone standing at a crossroads.

**The Righteous Person's Three "Nots" (v. 1)**

> "Blessed is the man who walks not in the counsel of the wicked, nor stands in the way of sinners, nor sits in the seat of scoffers."

Brothers and sisters, notice the progression of three verbs: walks → stands → sits. "Walking" is passing through—you merely encounter wicked thinking; "standing" is lingering—you begin to pause on sin's path; "sitting" is settling—you've made yourself at home in the scoffer's seat. Sin's progression works exactly this way—from casual contact to habitual lingering to comfortable settling. No one begins by "sitting in the seat of scoffers"—they all first "walked" past wicked counsel, thought it harmless, then "stood" still.

**The Righteous Person's Delight—Meditating on the Law (v. 2)**

> "But his delight is in the law of the LORD, and on his law he meditates day and night."

Brothers and sisters, the righteous life is not merely about "not doing"—but actively "doing." He "delights" in the LORD's law—not forced compliance, but heartfelt love. "Meditates day and night"—the Hebrew "hagah" means to murmur softly, to chew repeatedly, like a cow chewing cud. Brothers and sisters, has a verse ever echoed in your heart repeatedly, thought about by day and still resonating at night? That is "meditating day and night."

**The Righteous Person's Metaphor—A Tree by Streams (v. 3)**

> "He is like a tree planted by streams of water that yields its fruit in its season, and its leaf does not wither. In all that he does, he prospers."

Brothers and sisters, "planted by streams"—not wild, not self-grown, but deliberately "planted" there by someone. Your becoming a tree by streams is not because you chose a good spot—but because God planted you there. Your spiritual life is grace's cultivation, not self-effort's result.

"Yields fruit in its season"—not every day. Brothers and sisters, some seasons show no fruit—a winter tree stands bare, but its roots still drink water. Don't doubt your roots because you see no fruit now. The righteous person's fruit comes "in season"—God's timetable is not yours.

**The Wicked's Metaphor—Chaff Blown by Wind (v. 4)**

> "The wicked are not so, but are like chaff that the wind drives away."

Brothers and sisters, what a contrast! A tree—rooted, deep, alive, able to withstand storms. Chaff—rootless, weightless, lifeless, scattered by one gust. Chaff looks like wheat—on the threshing floor they're mixed together, indistinguishable. But when the wind blows—truth is revealed. Trials are that wind—they don't change your nature; they reveal it.

**The Judgment's Conclusion (vv. 5-6)**

> "For the LORD knows the way of the righteous, but the way of the wicked will perish."

Brothers and sisters, "the LORD knows the way of the righteous"—"knows" in Hebrew means not just "possesses information" but "intimately recognizes, cares for, watches over." God "knows" your way—meaning He watches, protects, and leads you. The wicked's way "will perish"—not that the wicked cease to exist, but their path leads to nothing—no destination, no meaning, no eternity.

Brothers and sisters, Psalm 1 places the Psalter's core question before you: which road do you walk? A tree by streams or chaff blown by wind? This is not a one-time choice—but a choice made every morning when you wake. Do you today choose to "meditate day and night" on the LORD's law, or "walk in the counsel of the wicked"? Two roads, two destinations—there is no third way.

## 历史背景（中文）

诗篇第一篇被认为是整本诗篇的序言——它没有标题、没有作者署名，这在诗篇中是非常少见的。犹太传统认为这首诗与第二篇一起构成诗篇的"双序言"——第一篇论个人的义，第二篇论弥赛亚的国度。塔木德（Berakhot 9b-10a）甚至有意见认为第一和第二篇原本是一首诗。"昼夜思想"的希伯来文"hagah"也出现在约书亚记1:8"这律法书不可离开你的口，总要昼夜思想"——这是约书亚进迦南前神的命令。"栽在溪水旁的树"的意象在耶利米书17:7-8有几乎完全相同的平行："倚靠耶和华、以耶和华为可靠的，那人有福了。他必像树栽于水旁。"诗篇第一篇的"两条路"主题贯穿整本圣经——从申命记30:19"生死祸福陈明在你面前"到马太福音7:13-14"引到灭亡那门是宽的……引到永生那门是窄的。"

## Historical Context (English)

Psalm 1 is considered the preface to the entire Psalter—it has no title and no author attribution, extremely rare among the Psalms. Jewish tradition views this psalm together with Psalm 2 as a "double preface"—Psalm 1 on individual righteousness, Psalm 2 on the Messiah's kingdom. The Talmud (Berakhot 9b-10a) even contains the opinion that Psalms 1 and 2 were originally one poem. The Hebrew "hagah" for "meditate" also appears in Joshua 1:8: "This Book of the Law shall not depart from your mouth, but you shall meditate on it day and night"—God's command before Joshua entered Canaan. The "tree planted by streams" imagery has a nearly identical parallel in Jeremiah 17:7-8: "Blessed is the man who trusts in the LORD...He is like a tree planted by water." Psalm 1's "two ways" theme pervades all of Scripture—from Deuteronomy 30:19 "life and death I have set before you" to Matthew 7:13-14 "the gate is wide that leads to destruction...the gate is narrow that leads to life."

## 经文结构（中文）

**义人的三个"不"（第1节）**——不从恶人计谋→不站罪人道路→不坐亵慢座位：罪的递进（从→站→坐）。

**义人的积极生命（第2节）**——喜爱耶和华律法→昼夜思想(hagah)：默想是反刍式的沉浸。

**义人的比喻（第3节）**——栽在溪水旁的树→按时结果→叶不枯干→凡所做尽都顺利。

**恶人的比喻（第4节）**——糠秕→被风吹散：没有根、没有重量、没有生命力。

**审判的两个结局（第5-6节）**——恶人站立不住→耶和华知道义人的道路→恶人的道路必灭亡。

## Structure (English)

**The Righteous Person's Three "Nots" (v. 1)**—Not walking in wicked counsel → not standing in sinners' way → not sitting in scoffers' seat: sin's progression (walk → stand → sit).

**The Righteous Person's Active Life (v. 2)**—Delighting in the LORD's law → meditating day and night (hagah): meditation as ruminative immersion.

**The Righteous Person's Metaphor (v. 3)**—Tree planted by streams → yields fruit in season → leaf does not wither → prospers in all.

**The Wicked's Metaphor (v. 4)**—Chaff → driven away by wind: no root, no weight, no life.

**Judgment's Two Outcomes (vv. 5-6)**—Wicked cannot stand → the LORD knows the righteous way → the wicked's way perishes.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"两条路"——没有中间地带**
诗篇第一篇只有两条路——改革宗传统教导没有道德的中间地带。韦敏斯德信条第6章论堕落。你要么在义人的道路上，要么在恶人的道路上——没有第三条"还行"的路。马太福音12:30"不与我相合的就是敌我的。"

**"昼夜思想"——默想的神学**
默想神的话语是灵命成长的核心操练——改革宗传统教导圣经的充足性和首要性。韦敏斯德信条第1章论圣经。不是"偶尔翻翻"而是"昼夜思想"——属灵生命需要持续的喂养。

**"栽在溪水旁"——恩典的主权**
树是被"栽"的不是自己长的——改革宗传统教导恩典的主权。韦敏斯德信条第10章论有效恩召。你能成为义人不是因为你的选择——而是因为神把你栽在溪水旁。以弗所书2:8"你们得救是本乎恩，也因着信；这并不是出于自己，乃是神所赐的。"

## Theology (English)

**"Two Ways"—No Middle Ground**
Psalm 1 presents only two paths—the Reformed tradition teaches no moral middle ground. Westminster Confession chapter 6 on the fall. You are either on the righteous path or the wicked path—there is no third "okay" road. Matthew 12:30: "Whoever is not with me is against me."

**"Meditates Day and Night"—The Theology of Meditation**
Meditating on God's Word is the core discipline of spiritual growth—the Reformed tradition teaches Scripture's sufficiency and primacy. Westminster Confession chapter 1 on Scripture. Not: occasional browsing but "day and night meditation"—spiritual life requires continuous nourishment.

**"Planted by Streams"—Sovereign Grace**
The tree is "planted," not self-grown—the Reformed tradition teaches grace's sovereignty. Westminster Confession chapter 10 on effectual calling. Your becoming righteous is not your choice—but God planting you by streams. Ephesians 2:8: "For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God."

## 基督的影子（中文）

诗篇第一篇指向基督在三个方面。

**基督是那棵完美的树**——诗篇第一篇描述的"有福之人"完美地应验在基督身上。祂从不"从恶人的计谋"——在旷野受试探四十天，拒绝了魔鬼的每一个提议。祂"昼夜思想"耶和华的律法——祂就是道成肉身的律法（约1:14）。祂"按时候结果子"——十字架是最大的果子，结在神所定的时候（加4:4"及至时候满足，神就差遣他的儿子"）。

**基督承受了糠秕的审判**——在十字架上，基督经历了"被风吹散"的审判——被神离弃（太27:46"我的神，我的神，为什么离弃我"）。祂代替我们承受了恶人道路的结局——"灭亡"——好让我们这些本该被吹散的糠秕，被栽成溪水旁的树。

**"耶和华知道义人的道路"——基督使我们被"知道"**——因着基督的义归算给我们（罗4:6），我们从"恶人的道路"被转移到"义人的道路"上。耶和华"知道"我们——不是因为我们自己的义，而是因为基督的义覆盖了我们。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Psalm 1 points to Christ in three aspects.

**Christ Is the Perfect Tree**—Psalm 1's "blessed man" is perfectly fulfilled in Christ. He never "walked in the counsel of the wicked"—tempted forty days in the wilderness, He rejected every satanic proposal. He "meditated day and night" on the LORD's law—He was the incarnate Word (John 1:14). He "yielded fruit in season"—the cross was the greatest fruit, borne at God's appointed time (Gal 4:4: "when the fullness of time had come, God sent forth his Son").

**Christ Bore the Chaff's Judgment**—On the cross, Christ experienced the judgment of being "blown away"—forsaken by God (Matt 27:46: "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?"). He bore the wicked path's destination—"perishing"—in our place, so that we, who should have been scattered chaff, might be planted as trees by streams.

**"The LORD Knows the Righteous Way"—Christ Causes Us to Be "Known"**—Through Christ's righteousness imputed to us (Rom 4:6), we are transferred from "the wicked's way" to "the righteous way." The LORD "knows" us—not because of our own righteousness, but because Christ's righteousness covers us.
