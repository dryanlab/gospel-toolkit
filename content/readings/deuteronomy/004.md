## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第四章是我对以色列人最热切的呼唤之一——这一章包含了旧约中最深刻的一神论宣告、对偶像崇拜最严厉的警告，以及对神怜悯最温柔的应许。

**不可加添也不可删减（第1-4节）**

> "以色列人哪，现在我所教训你们的律例典章，你们要听从遵行，好叫你们存活……所吩咐你们的话，你们不可加添，也不可删减。"

弟兄姐妹，"不可加添也不可删减"——弟兄姐妹，这是圣经对自身权威的根本性宣告之一。神的话是完整的——不需要你加什么上去——也不允许你减什么下来。弟兄姐妹，法利赛人的错误在于"加添"——他们在神的律法上加了无数的人为传统。自由派的错误在于"删减"——他们把不喜欢的经文删掉或重新解释。弟兄姐妹，我们的责任是"听从遵行"——不多也不少。

**哪一大国的人有神与他们相近（第7-8节）**

> "哪一大国的人有神与他们相近，像耶和华我们的神在我们求告他的时候与我们相近呢？又哪一大国有这样公义的律例典章？"

弟兄姐妹，摩西发出了两个修辞性的问题——两个"哪一大国"。第一个问题：哪个国家的神像耶和华这样"相近"？弟兄姐妹，异教的神是遥远的、需要复杂的仪式才能接近的。但耶和华——"在我们求告他的时候与我们相近"。弟兄姐妹，你不需要爬上高山、不需要穿过沙漠、不需要复杂的仪式——"求告"——就这么简单——祂就与你相近。

第二个问题：哪个国家有这样"公义"的律法？弟兄姐妹，古代近东有许多法典（汉谟拉比法典等）——但以色列的律法独特在于它的公义性——保护穷人、寡妇、孤儿、寄居者的权利——在三千年前就有这样的社会公义观念——这是独一无二的。

**你只听见声音却没看见形像（第12-20节）**

> "耶和华从火焰中对你们说话，你们只听见声音，却没有看见形像。"

弟兄姐妹，这是申命记反对偶像崇拜的神学根基——你在何烈山"没有看见形像"——所以你不能给神做任何形像。弟兄姐妹，偶像崇拜的根本错误不是用什么材料——而是试图把无限的神缩小到一个有限的形像中。弟兄姐妹，任何试图用人手造的东西来"代表"神的行为——都是对神无限性的贬低。

"因为耶和华你的神乃是烈火，是忌邪的神"——弟兄姐妹，"烈火"——不是温暖的篝火——而是烈火。弟兄姐妹，神的圣洁如火一样不能容忍不洁。"忌邪的"——弟兄姐妹，"忌邪"（jealous）不是人类的嫉妒——而是丈夫对妻子的排他性的爱——如果你去拜别神——就好像妻子去找别的男人——神会"忌邪"。

**你若归回就必寻见（第29-31节）**

> "但你们在那里必寻求耶和华你的神。你若尽心尽性寻求他，就必寻见。日后你遭遇一切患难的时候，你必归回耶和华你的神，听从他的话。耶和华你的神是有怜悯的神，他总不撇下你，不灭绝你，也不忘记他起誓与你列祖所立的约。"

弟兄姐妹，"他总不撇下你"——弟兄姐妹，在严厉的警告之后——突然转向了怜悯。弟兄姐妹，这就是我们的神——祂严厉到令你害怕——然后温柔到令你哭泣。"不撇下、不灭绝、不忘记"——三个"不"——三重的保证。弟兄姐妹，无论你走得多远、跌得多深——"你若尽心尽性寻求他就必寻见"。

**惟有耶和华他是神（第35、39节）**

> "惟有耶和华他是神，除他以外再无别神。""所以，今日你要知道，也要记在心上，天上地下惟有耶和华他是神，除他以外再无别神。"

弟兄姐妹，这是旧约中最明确的一神论宣告之一——"除他以外再无别神"。不是"祂比别的神更大"——而是"除祂以外再无别神"。弟兄姐妹，在多元主义的时代，这话听起来可能很"排他"——但真理本来就是排他的——二加二等于四排斥了所有其他答案。弟兄姐妹，惟有耶和华是神——这不是偏见——这是事实。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 4 is one of my most passionate appeals to Israel—containing the Old Testament's most profound monotheistic declarations, the sternest warnings against idolatry, and the tenderest promises of God's mercy.

**Do Not Add or Take Away (vv. 1-4)**

> "And now, O Israel, listen to the statutes and the rules that I am teaching you...You shall not add to the word that I command you, nor take from it."

Brothers and sisters, "do not add or take from it"—one of Scripture's fundamental declarations about its own authority. God's word is complete—needing nothing added, allowing nothing removed. The Pharisees' error was "adding"—countless human traditions atop God's law. Liberalism's error is "taking away"—removing or reinterpreting unwanted texts. Our responsibility is "listen and obey"—no more, no less.

**What Great Nation Has a God So Near? (vv. 7-8)**

> "For what great nation is there that has a god so near to it as the LORD our God is to us, whenever we call upon him? And what great nation is there, that has statutes and rules so righteous?"

Brothers and sisters, two rhetorical questions. Pagan gods are distant, requiring elaborate rituals to approach. But the LORD—"whenever we call upon him, is near to us." You don't need to climb mountains, cross deserts, perform complex rituals—"call"—that simply—and He is near.

**You Heard the Voice but Saw No Form (vv. 12-20)**

> "Then the LORD spoke to you out of the midst of the fire. You heard the sound of words, but saw no form; there was only a voice."

Brothers and sisters, Deuteronomy's theological foundation against idolatry—at Horeb you "saw no form"—so you cannot make any image for God. Idolatry's fundamental error isn't the material used—but attempting to shrink the infinite God into a finite image.

"For the LORD your God is a consuming fire, a jealous God"—not a warm campfire—but consuming fire. "Jealous"—not human envy—but a husband's exclusive love for his wife.

**If You Seek Him You Will Find Him (vv. 29-31)**

> "But from there you will seek the LORD your God and you will find him, if you search after him with all your heart and with all your soul...For the LORD your God is a merciful God. He will not leave you or destroy you or forget the covenant with your fathers."

Brothers and sisters, "he will not leave you"—after severe warnings—suddenly turning to mercy. This is our God—severe enough to make you afraid—then tender enough to make you weep. "Will not leave, not destroy, not forget"—three "nots"—triple assurance. No matter how far you've gone—"if you search with all your heart you will find him."

**The LORD Alone Is God (vv. 35, 39)**

> "The LORD is God; there is no other besides him." "Know therefore today...that the LORD is God in heaven above and on the earth beneath; there is no other."

Brothers and sisters, one of the OT's clearest monotheistic declarations—"there is no other besides him." Not "He is greater than other gods"—but "there is no other." In an age of pluralism, this sounds "exclusive"—but truth is inherently exclusive—two plus two equals four excludes all other answers. The LORD alone is God—not prejudice—but fact.

## 历史背景（中文）

申命记第四章是摩西第一篇讲论（1-4章）的高潮和总结。这一章的一神论宣告（第35、39节）在学术界被广泛认为是以色列明确一神论（explicit monotheism）的最早和最清晰的表达之一——相比之下，较早的经文（如出20:3"不可有别的神"）可能只是一神崇拜（monolatry，承认别的神存在但只敬拜一位），而申4:35明确宣告"除他以外再无别神"——这是本体论上的一神论。"不可加添也不可删减"（第2节）后来被启示录22:18-19呼应——形成了圣经首尾的呼应。第12节"你们只听见声音却没有看见形像"是以色列反偶像崇拜神学的核心——被第二诫（出20:4"不可为自己雕刻偶像"）具体实施。

## Historical Context (English)

Deuteronomy 4 is the climax and summary of Moses' first address (chs. 1-4). This chapter's monotheistic declarations (vv. 35, 39) are widely regarded academically as among the earliest and clearest expressions of explicit monotheism in Israel—compared to earlier texts (like Ex 20:3 "no other gods before me") which may only be monolatry (acknowledging other gods' existence while worshiping only one), Deut 4:35 explicitly declares "there is no other besides him"—ontological monotheism. "Do not add or take away" (v. 2) is later echoed by Revelation 22:18-19—forming a scriptural inclusio. Verse 12 "you heard the voice but saw no form" is the core of Israel's anti-idolatry theology—concretely implemented by the second commandment (Ex 20:4).

## 经文结构（中文）

**律法的权威（1-8节）**——听从遵行→不可加添不可删减→巴力毗珥的教训→哪一大国有神相近→哪一大国有公义律法。
**何烈山的启示（9-14节）**——谨慎保守你的心→传给子子孙孙→何烈山前火焰→只听声音没看形像→十条诫写石版。
**偶像崇拜的警告（15-28节）**——没看见形像所以不可雕像→神是烈火是忌邪的→你们必从那地灭亡。
**归回与怜悯（29-31节）**——你若尽心寻求就必寻见→归回耶和华→他不撇下不灭绝不忘约。
**独一神宣告（32-40节）**——从天这边到那边→惟有耶和华是神→除他以外再无别神→你要遵守。

## Structure (English)

**The Law's Authority (vv. 1-8)**—Listen and obey → don't add or take away → lesson of Baal-Peor → what nation has God so near → what nation has righteous statutes.
**Horeb's Revelation (vv. 9-14)**—Guard your soul carefully → teach your children → fire at Horeb → heard voice saw no form → Ten Commandments on stone.
**Idolatry Warning (vv. 15-28)**—Saw no form so make no image → God is consuming fire, jealous → you will perish from the land.
**Return and Mercy (vv. 29-31)**—If you seek with all heart you'll find → return to LORD → He won't leave, destroy, or forget covenant.
**Monotheistic Declaration (vv. 32-40)**—From one end of heaven to other → LORD alone is God → no other besides Him → obey.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"除他以外再无别神"——一神论**
改革宗传统教导严格的一神论。韦敏斯德信条第二章教导：只有一位又活又真的神，除了祂之外再无别神。申命记4:35、39的宣告是改革宗一神论的旧约根基。这不是文化相对的——不是"对以色列人来说只有一位神"——而是本体论的宣告：在天上地下惟有耶和华是神。

**"不可加添也不可删减"——唯独圣经（Sola Scriptura）**
改革宗传统教导圣经的充足性和权威性。韦敏斯德信条第1章：圣经的全部旨意……已充分明确地载于圣经之中……不可在任何时候增添。申命记4:2是"唯独圣经"原则的旧约先驱——神的话不需要人为的补充。

**"烈火"与"怜悯"——神的两面**
改革宗传统教导神同时是圣洁公义的和怜悯恩慈的。韦敏斯德信条第2章。申命记4章在烈火的警告（24节）和怜悯的应许（31节）之间转换——展示了神的完整属性：祂的公义使祂成为"烈火"——祂的慈爱使祂"不撇下你"。两者同样真实——不可偏废。

## Theology (English)

**"No Other Besides Him"—Monotheism**
The Reformed tradition teaches strict monotheism. Westminster Confession chapter 2: There is but one only, living, and true God...besides him there is no God. Deuteronomy 4:35, 39 is the OT foundation of Reformed monotheism—not culturally relative but ontological declaration.

**"Do Not Add or Take Away"—Sola Scriptura**
The Reformed tradition teaches Scripture's sufficiency and authority. Westminster Confession chapter 1. Deuteronomy 4:2 is the OT precursor of Sola Scriptura—God's word needs no human supplement.

**"Consuming Fire" and "Merciful"—God's Two Aspects**
The Reformed tradition teaches God is simultaneously holy-just and merciful-gracious. Westminster Confession chapter 2. Deuteronomy 4 alternates between fire's warning (v. 24) and mercy's promise (v. 31)—displaying God's complete character: His justice makes Him "consuming fire"—His love makes Him "not leave you." Both equally real—neither dispensable.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"你们只听见声音却没看见形像"——基督是神的形像**——歌罗西书1:15"他是那不能看见之神的像。"以色列在何烈山"没看见形像"——因为看见神的形像需要等到道成肉身。约翰福音1:18"从来没有人看见神，只有在父怀里的独生子将他表明出来。"基督是那"没看见的形像"——不是人手所造的偶像——而是永生神的真实彰显。

**"尽心尽性寻求就必寻见"——基督的应许**——马太福音7:7"你们祈求，就给你们；寻找，就寻见。"申命记4:29的寻求应许在基督里得到了最完美的应验——"寻找就寻见"——基督就是那位被寻见的神。

**"他总不撇下你不灭绝你"——基督永不离开的应许**——希伯来书13:5"主曾说：我总不撇下你，也不丢弃你。"马太福音28:20"我就常与你们同在，直到世界的末了。"申命记4:31"不撇下不灭绝不忘记"的三重应许在基督里得到了永恒的保证。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"You Heard But Saw No Form"—Christ Is God's Image**—Colossians 1:15: "He is the image of the invisible God." Israel at Horeb "saw no form"—because seeing God's form required waiting for the incarnation. John 1:18: "No one has ever seen God; the only God, who is at the Father's side, he has made him known." Christ is that "unseen form"—not a man-made idol but the living God's true manifestation.

**"Seek with All Heart and Find"—Christ's Promise**—Matthew 7:7: "Ask, and it will be given to you; seek, and you will find." Deuteronomy 4:29's seeking promise finds perfect fulfillment in Christ—"seek and you will find"—Christ is the God who is found.

**"He Will Not Leave or Destroy You"—Christ's Promise Never to Leave**—Hebrews 13:5: "I will never leave you nor forsake you." Matthew 28:20: "I am with you always, to the end of the age." Deuteronomy 4:31's triple promise "not leave, not destroy, not forget" receives eternal guarantee in Christ.
