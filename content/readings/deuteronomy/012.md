## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第十二章开始了律法的核心部分（12-26章）——第一条也是最重要的一条：敬拜必须在耶和华所选择的地方进行——不可各人随自己的意思。

**拆毁与不可效法（第1-4节）**

> "你们存活于世的日子在耶和华你们列祖的神所赐你们为业的地上要谨守遵行的律例典章乃是这些：你们要将所赶出的国民侍奉他们神的各地方——无论是在高山上、小山上、各青翠树下——尽都毁坏。也要拆毁他们的祭坛，打碎他们的柱像，用火焚烧他们的木偶，砍下他们雕刻的神像，并将其名从那地方除灭。你们不可照他们那样侍奉耶和华你们的神。"

弟兄姐妹，"尽都毁坏"——弟兄姐妹，为什么要彻底毁坏？因为偶像崇拜的"场所"本身就是诱惑。弟兄姐妹，如果你把酒瘾者放在酒吧里——即使你告诉他"不要喝"——环境本身就是试探。弟兄姐妹，迦南人的"高处"（high places）后来一直是以色列人的属灵绊脚石——列王纪反复记载"只是高处还没有废去"。

"你们不可照他们那样侍奉耶和华"——弟兄姐妹，这句话非常关键。弟兄姐妹，问题不只是拜别的神——还包括用错误的方式拜真神。弟兄姐妹，你不能说"我心里是拜耶和华的只是用迦南人的方式"——方式也重要。弟兄姐妹，敬拜不只是"心意"——还包括"方式"——你用什么方式敬拜必须符合神的吩咐——不是你自己觉得好的方式。

**耶和华所选择的地方（第5-14节）**

> "但耶和华你们的神从你们各支派中选择何处为立他名的居所，你们就当往那里去求问，将你们的燔祭、平安祭、十分取一之物和手中的举祭，并还愿祭、甘心祭，以及牛群羊群中头生的都奉到那里。"

弟兄姐妹，"耶和华所选择的地方"——这个短语在申命记中出现了超过二十次——是整本书的核心短语之一。弟兄姐妹，神没有具体说是哪里——只说"我选择的地方"——后来被确认为耶路撒冷。弟兄姐妹，敬拜的地点不是人选的——是神选的。弟兄姐妹，这个原则在新约中发生了转变——耶稣对撒玛利亚妇人说："时候将到，你们拜父也不在这山上也不在耶路撒冷……用心灵和诚实拜他"（约4:21-23）。

弟兄姐妹，"在那里你们和儿女仆婢并住在你们城里无分无业的利未人以及寄居的要在耶和华你们的神面前欢乐。"弟兄姐妹，注意——敬拜是"欢乐"的。弟兄姐妹，敬拜不是沉重的义务——而是"在耶和华面前欢乐"。而且这个欢乐是包容的——"儿女仆婢利未人寄居的"——所有人都参与——没有人被排除在外。

**不可各人行自己眼中看为正的事（第8节）**

> "你们将来不可照我们今日在这里所行的，各人行自己眼中看为正的事。"

弟兄姐妹，"各人行自己眼中看为正的事"——这句话在士师记中反复出现（士17:6, 21:25）——描述了以色列最黑暗的时代。弟兄姐妹，当每个人都按自己的标准行事——社会就崩溃了。弟兄姐妹，"自己眼中看为正的"听起来很"自由"——但自由如果没有真理的约束就成了混乱。弟兄姐妹，后现代社会推崇"你的真理"和"我的真理"——但申命记说：不可这样——真理只有一个——敬拜只在神选择的地方。

**不可吃血（第15-28节）**

弟兄姐妹，这一章也规定了日常宰杀牲畜的规则——你可以在任何地方宰杀吃肉——但不可吃血。"只是你要心意坚定不可吃血，因为血是生命，你不可将血与肉同吃。"弟兄姐妹，"血是生命"——血代表生命——生命属于神——所以血要倒在地上归给神。弟兄姐妹，这个禁令从挪亚之约就开始了（创9:4），贯穿整个旧约——在使徒行传15:20的耶路撒冷会议中仍然被保留——说明这不只是礼仪律——而有更深的神学意义。

弟兄姐妹，申命记第十二章的核心信息：敬拜不是你想怎么来就怎么来——而是按照神的方式在神选择的地方。自由不是"各人行自己眼中看为正的"——真正的自由是在真理的框架内。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 12 begins the law's core section (chs. 12-26)—the first and most important rule: worship must occur at the place the LORD chooses—not as each person sees fit.

**Destroy and Do Not Imitate (vv. 1-4)**

> "You shall surely destroy all the places where the nations whom you shall dispossess served their gods...You shall not worship the LORD your God in that way."

Brothers and sisters, why destroy completely? Because idolatry's "places" themselves are temptation. The Canaanite "high places" remained Israel's spiritual stumbling block—Kings repeatedly records "the high places were not removed."

"You shall not worship the LORD your God in that way"—the problem isn't only worshiping other gods—it includes worshiping the true God wrongly. You can't say "my heart worships the LORD just using Canaanite methods"—method matters too.

**The Place the LORD Will Choose (vv. 5-14)**

"But you shall seek the place that the LORD your God will choose...to put his name and make his habitation there." This phrase appears over twenty times in Deuteronomy. God didn't specify where—only "the place I choose"—later identified as Jerusalem. The worship location isn't human-chosen—it's God-chosen. This principle transforms in the NT—Jesus told the Samaritan woman: "Jesus saith unto her, Woman, believe me, the hour cometh, when ye shall neither in this mountain, nor yet at Jerusalem, worship the Father. Ye worship ye know not what: we know what we worship: for salvation is of the Jews. But the hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth: for the Father seeketh such to worship him." (John 4:21-23).

**Not As Each Sees Fit (v. 8)**

"You shall not do according to all that we are doing here today, everyone doing whatever is right in his own eyes." This phrase recurs in Judges (17:6, 21:25)—describing Israel's darkest era. When everyone acts by their own standard—society collapses.

**Do Not Eat Blood (vv. 15-28)**

"Only you must not eat the blood, for the blood is the life." Blood represents life—life belongs to God—so blood must be poured on the ground. This prohibition from Noah's covenant (Gen 9:4) persists through the OT and was retained at the Jerusalem Council (Acts 15:20).

Deuteronomy 12's core: worship isn't however you want—but God's way in God's place. True freedom operates within truth's framework.

## 历史背景（中文）

"耶和华所选择的地方"这个短语在申命记中出现约21次——后来被确认为耶路撒冷（代下6:6"但选择耶路撒冷为我名的居所"）。在大卫之前，约柜曾在示罗、挪伯、基列耶琳等地。"高处"（bamot）是迦南人和以色列人在山丘顶上设立的敬拜场所——考古发现了许多这样的遗址。列王纪对每位国王的评价几乎都会提到"高处"是否被废除——即使是好王如约沙法、约阿施也被批评"只是高处还没有废去"。"不可吃血"的禁令从创世记9:4（挪亚之约）延续到利未记17:10-14，再到使徒行传15:20——是旧约中少数跨越到新约教会的禁令之一。

## Historical Context (English)

"The place the LORD will choose" appears about 21 times in Deuteronomy—later identified as Jerusalem (2 Chr 6:6). Before David, the ark resided at Shiloh, Nob, Kiriath-jearim. "High places" (bamot) were hilltop worship sites—many archaeological remains found. Kings evaluates each monarch partly by whether "high places" were removed. "Do not eat blood" extends from Genesis 9:4 (Noahic covenant) through Leviticus 17:10-14 to Acts 15:20—one of few OT prohibitions carried into NT church practice.

## 经文结构（中文）

**毁坏偶像崇拜场所（1-4节）**——高山小山青翠树下→拆毁祭坛打碎柱像→焚烧木偶→除灭其名→不可照他们那样。
**集中敬拜（5-14节）**——耶和华选择的地方→带燔祭平安祭十一奉献→在那里欢乐→不可各人行自己看为正的→到安息地后更要谨守。
**日常宰杀与不可吃血（15-28节）**——可以随意宰杀吃肉→不洁净的人也可以吃→不可吃血→血是生命→倒在地上如倒水一样→行耶和华眼中看为正的。
**不可效法列国（29-32节）**——赶出列国后不可效法→他们用火焚烧儿女献给他们的神→不可加添不可删减。

## Structure (English)

**Destroy Idolatrous Sites (vv. 1-4)**—Mountains, hills, green trees → tear down altars, smash pillars → burn Asherah poles → destroy names → don't worship LORD that way.
**Centralized Worship (vv. 5-14)**—Place LORD chooses → bring offerings, tithes → rejoice there → don't each do as sees fit → more careful in rest-place.
**Daily Slaughter and No Blood (vv. 15-28)**—May slaughter anywhere → unclean may eat → don't eat blood → blood is life → pour out like water → do what's right in LORD's eyes.
**Don't Imitate Nations (vv. 29-32)**—After driving out, don't imitate → they burn children for their gods → don't add or take away.

## 神学要点（中文）

**限定性原则（Regulative Principle of Worship）**
改革宗传统独特地教导"限定性原则"——敬拜中只可做神所吩咐的。韦敏斯德信条第21章：可蒙悦纳的敬拜神的方式乃是神自己所设立的，且在圣经中有其限定。申命记12章是这个原则的旧约根基——"不可照他们那样侍奉耶和华"——敬拜的方式必须由神决定，不是由人的喜好决定。

**敬拜的集中性——合一的表达**
改革宗传统教导教会的合一。韦敏斯德信条第25章。"耶和华所选择的地方"要求全以色列人到同一个地方敬拜——这强调了神子民的合一。各地各样的"高处"代表分裂——一个圣所代表合一。

**"各人行自己眼中看为正的"——人类自治的失败**
改革宗传统教导人的堕落使人的判断不可靠。韦敏斯德信条第6章。"各人行自己眼中看为正的"是人类罪性最简洁的描述之一——人以自己为标准——而不是以神为标准。这是后现代相对主义的旧约版本——结果是士师记中的混乱和暴力。

## Theology (English)

**Regulative Principle of Worship**
The Reformed tradition uniquely teaches the "Regulative Principle"—only what God commands in worship is permissible. Westminster Confession chapter 21. Deuteronomy 12 is this principle's OT foundation.

**Centralized Worship—Expression of Unity**
The Reformed tradition teaches church unity. Westminster Confession chapter 25.: The place LORD chooses requires all Israel to worship at one place—emphasizing God's people's unity.

**"Everyone Doing What's Right in His Own Eyes"—Human Autonomy's Failure**
The Reformed tradition teaches the fall makes human judgment unreliable. Westminster Confession chapter 6. This is one of human sinfulness's most concise descriptions—the postmodern relativism of the OT—resulting in Judges' chaos.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"耶和华所选择的地方"——基督是真正的圣殿**——约翰福音2:19-21"耶稣回答说：你们拆毁这殿，我三日内要再建立起来……但耶稣这话是以他的身体为殿。"旧约中神选择耶路撒冷圣殿为"立祂名的居所"——新约中基督的身体就是真正的圣殿。敬拜不再局限于一个地理位置——因为基督就是神与人相遇的地方。

**"用心灵和诚实拜他"——敬拜的转变**——约翰福音4:21-24"时候将到，你们拜父也不在这山上也不在耶路撒冷……那真正拜父的要用心灵和诚实拜他。"申命记12章要求一个地方的集中敬拜——基督将敬拜从外在的地方转向了内在的心灵——真正的"耶和华所选择的地方"是你的心。

**"血是生命"——基督的血是永恒的生命**——希伯来书9:22"若不流血罪就不得赦免了。"旧约禁止吃血因为"血是生命"——基督却说"喝我血的人有永生"（约6:54）——因为基督的血不只是"生命"——而是赐予"永生"的血。旧约的血倒在地上归给神——基督的血倒出来归给我们。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"The Place the LORD Chooses"—Christ Is the True Temple**—John 2:19-21: "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up...But he was speaking about the temple of his body." The OT's chosen Jerusalem temple as God's dwelling—in the NT, Christ's body is the true temple.

**"Worship in Spirit and Truth"—Worship Transformed**—John 4:21-24. Deuteronomy 12 requires centralized worship at one place—Christ transforms worship from external location to internal spirit—the true "place the LORD chooses" is your heart.

**"The Blood Is the Life"—Christ's Blood Is Eternal Life**—Hebrews 9:22: "Without the shedding of blood there is no forgiveness." OT prohibited eating blood because "blood is life"—Christ says "Whoso eateth my flesh, and drinketh my blood, hath eternal life; and I will raise him up at the last day." (John 6:54)—Christ's blood isn't just "life" but gives "eternal life." OT blood poured on ground for God—Christ's blood poured out for us.
