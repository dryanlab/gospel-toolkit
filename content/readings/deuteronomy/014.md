## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第十四章涵盖了两个看似不相关的主题——饮食律例和十一奉献。但它们其实被一条线串在一起——"你们是耶和华你们神的儿女"——因为你是神的儿女，所以你的生活方式——吃什么、花什么——都必须不同。

**你们是神的儿女（第1-2节）**

> "你们是耶和华你们神的儿女。不可为死人用刀划身，也不可将额上剃光；因为你归耶和华你神为圣洁的民。耶和华从地上的万民中拣选你特作自己的子民。"

弟兄姐妹，"你们是神的儿女"——弟兄姐妹，在旧约中以色列人被称为神的"儿女"——这是极其亲密的关系。弟兄姐妹，"不可为死人用刀划身"——这是迦南人和周围民族丧葬仪式中的做法——用自残的方式表达悲伤或安抚死者的灵魂。弟兄姐妹，为什么不可以？因为你是"圣洁的民"——你的身体属于神——不可以用异教的方式处理它。

**洁净与不洁净的食物（第3-21节）**

弟兄姐妹，这一段列出了可以吃和不可以吃的动物——与利未记11章基本相同。分蹄且倒嚼的陆地动物可以吃——有翅有鳞的水中动物可以吃——许多飞禽（主要是食肉的猛禽和食腐鸟）不可以吃。弟兄姐妹，关于这些律例的意义——改革宗传统认为它们属于"礼仪律"——在新约中已经被废除（可7:19"耶稣这是说各样的食物都是洁净的"）。

弟兄姐妹，但这些律例在旧约时代有重要的功能——它们让以色列人在最日常的活动——吃饭——中也记得自己的身份。弟兄姐妹，你每天要吃三顿饭——每顿饭都在提醒你："你是分别出来的。"弟兄姐妹，饮食律例不是关于卫生（虽然有些确实有卫生功能）——而是关于身份——每一餐都在说："你不一样。"

"不可吃任何自死的物"——弟兄姐妹，自死的动物没有经过合法的宰杀和放血——血没有被倒出来——所以不洁净。"不可用山羊羔母的奶煮山羊羔"——弟兄姐妹，这条律例被犹太人扩展为肉奶不同食的规则。原本的意思可能是禁止一种迦南人的宗教仪式——乌加里特文献中有用母奶煮小羊的仪式记录。

**十一奉献（第22-29节）**

> "你要把你撒种所产的按岁十分取一。你可以在耶和华你神所选择要立为他名的居所在耶和华你神面前吃那五谷、新酒和油的十分之一，并牛群羊群中头生的；这样你可以学习时常敬畏耶和华你的神。"

弟兄姐妹，"这样你可以学习时常敬畏耶和华你的神"——弟兄姐妹，十一奉献的目的不是给神钱——神不缺钱。目的是"学习敬畏"。弟兄姐妹，你怎么学习敬畏？通过把你最实在的东西——收入的十分之一——交出去。弟兄姐妹，钱包是人最敏感的地方——动你的钱包就是动你的心——所以十一奉献是操练信心最直接的方式。

> "你要照耶和华你神所赐你的全然蒙福的份量来给利未人。每逢三年的末一年你要将本年的土产十分之一都取出来积存在你的城中——使你城里的利未人以及寄居的与孤儿寡妇都来吃得饱足——好叫耶和华你的神在你手里所办的一切事上赐福与你。"

弟兄姐妹，第三年的十一奉献专门用于慈善——利未人、寄居的、孤儿、寡妇。弟兄姐妹，十一奉献不只是"宗教义务"——还包含了社会公义。弟兄姐妹，你的奉献在养活没有产业的利未人的同时——也在照顾社会中最脆弱的人。

弟兄姐妹，申命记第十四章告诉我们：因为你是神的儿女——你的生活方式在每一个细节中都应该反映你的身份——从你吃什么到你怎样使用金钱。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 14 covers two seemingly unrelated topics—dietary laws and tithing. But they're linked by one thread—"You are the sons of the LORD your God"—because you're God's children, your lifestyle—what you eat, how you spend—must be different.

**You Are God's Children (vv. 1-2)**

> "You are the sons of the LORD your God. You shall not cut yourselves or make any baldness on your foreheads for the dead. For you are a people holy to the LORD your God."

"You shall not cut yourselves"—a Canaanite mourning practice—expressing grief through self-mutilation. Why forbidden? Because you're "holy people"—your body belongs to God.

**Clean and Unclean Foods (vv. 3-21)**

This section lists clean and unclean animals—largely matching Leviticus 11. The Reformed tradition considers these "ceremonial law"—abolished in the NT (Mark 7:19). But in the OT era they served a crucial function—in the most daily activity—eating—reminding Israel of their identity. Three meals a day saying: "You are set apart."

"You shall not boil a young goat in its mother's milk"—this possibly prohibits a Canaanite religious ritual—Ugaritic texts record cooking a kid in mother's milk.

**Tithing (vv. 22-29)**

"You shall tithe all the yield of your seed...so that you may learn to fear the LORD your God always." Tithing's purpose isn't giving God money—God doesn't need money. The purpose is "learning to fear." How do you learn reverence? By giving away your most tangible thing—a tenth of income. The wallet is humanity's most sensitive spot—touching your wallet touches your heart.

Every third year's tithe goes to charity—Levites, sojourners, orphans, widows. Tithing isn't just "religious duty"—it includes social justice.

Deuteronomy 14: because you're God's children, your lifestyle should reflect your identity in every detail—from what you eat to how you use money.

## 历史背景（中文）

"用刀划身"和"剃光额头"是古代近东丧葬仪式的常见做法——考古证据表明迦南人、腓尼基人和周围民族都有这种做法。列王纪上18:28记载巴力的先知"用刀枪自割自刺直到身体流血"——虽然那是祭祀仪式而非丧葬，但显示了自残在异教崇拜中的普遍性。饮食律例与利未记11章大致平行——学术界对其起源有多种解释：（1）卫生说，（2）象征说（洁净动物代表以色列应有的特质），（3）文化分离说（与迦南人的饮食区分），（4）反偶像崇拜说。"不可用母奶煮小羊"——乌加里特文献（KTU 1.23:14）中确实有类似的仪式描述。十一奉献制度——"每逢三年"的特殊奉献用于社会福利——被学者认为是古代世界最早的系统性社会保障制度之一。

## Historical Context (English)

"Cutting yourselves" and "shaving foreheads" were common ancient Near Eastern mourning practices—archaeological evidence confirms Canaanite, Phoenician and neighboring peoples practiced these. 1 Kings 18:28 records Baal prophets cutting themselves. Dietary laws largely parallel Leviticus 11—scholars offer multiple explanations: (1) hygiene, (2) symbolic (clean animals represent qualities Israel should have), (3) cultural separation, (4) anti-idolatry. "Do not boil a kid in mother's milk"—Ugaritic text KTU 1.23:14 contains a similar ritual description. Every third year's special tithe for social welfare—considered one of the ancient world's earliest systematic social security systems.

## 经文结构（中文）

**圣洁的身份（1-2节）**——你们是神的儿女→不可划身剃额→你是圣洁的民→被拣选的子民。
**可吃的陆地动物（3-8节）**——分蹄倒嚼可吃→骆驼兔子不可吃→猪不倒嚼不可吃。
**水中与空中（9-20节）**——有翅有鳞可吃→特定飞禽不可吃→有翅膀爬行的不洁净。

**十一奉献（22-29节）**——按岁十分取一→在耶和华面前吃→学习敬畏→路远可换银子→每三年存城中→利未人孤儿寡妇寄居的吃饱。

## Structure (English)

**Holy Identity (vv. 1-2)**—You are God's children → don't cut/shave → holy people → chosen.
**Land Animals (vv. 3-8)**—Split hoof and chew cud: clean → camel, rabbit: unclean → pig: unclean.
**Water and Air (vv. 9-20)**—Fins and scales: clean → certain birds: unclean → winged insects: unclean.
**Other Dietary Rules (v. 21)**—Don't eat what dies naturally → don't boil kid in mother's milk.
**Tithing (vv. 22-29)**—Annual tenth → eat before the LORD → learn to fear → if far, convert to silver → every third year: store in town → Levites/orphans/widows/sojourners eat their fill.

## 神学要点（中文）

**礼仪律的废除与道德原则的延续**
改革宗传统区分道德律、礼仪律和民事律。韦敏斯德信条第19章：礼仪律……在新约之下全部废除了。饮食律例属于礼仪律——在新约中被废除（可7:19, 徒10:15"神所洁净的你不可当作俗物"）。但其背后的道德原则——分别为圣、作神圣洁子民的意识——在新约中延续。

**十一奉献与基督徒的慷慨**
改革宗传统教导十一奉献的原则在新约中延续但形式改变。韦敏斯德信条第21章。旧约的十分之一是最低标准——新约的标准是"捐得乐意"（林后9:7）和"照你们所蒙的福分给"。原则没变：你的金钱反映你的心——你的奉献反映你的信心。

**身份决定行为**
改革宗传统教导称义在先成圣在后。韦敏斯德信条第13章。"你们是神的儿女"是身份（称义）——"不可划身""十一奉献"是行为（成圣）。你不是通过好行为成为神的儿女——而是因为你已经是神的儿女所以你的行为要改变。

## Theology (English)

**Ceremonial Law's Abolition, Moral Principle's Continuation**
The Reformed tradition distinguishes moral, ceremonial, and civil law. Westminster Confession chapter 19. Dietary laws are ceremonial—abolished in NT (Mark 7:19, Acts 10:15). But the underlying moral principle—being set apart—continues.

**Tithing and Christian Generosity**
The Reformed tradition teaches tithing's principle continues in the NT but form changes. Westminster Confession chapter 21. OT tenth was the minimum—NT standard is "Every man according as he purposeth in his heart, so let him give; not grudgingly, or of necessity: for God loveth a cheerful giver." (2 Cor 9:7). Principle unchanged: your money reflects your heart.

**Identity Determines Behavior**
The Reformed tradition teaches justification precedes sanctification. Westminster Confession chapter 13.: You are God's children is identity (justification)—"don't cut yourselves" and "tithe" are behavior (sanctification).

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"你们是神的儿女"——基督使我们成为神的儿女**——加拉太书3:26"所以，你们因信基督耶稣都是神的儿子。"旧约中以色列是神的儿女——新约中所有信基督的人都是神的儿女——不分种族、不分背景——"因信基督耶稣都是神的儿子"。

**饮食律例的废除——基督洁净了一切**——使徒行传10:15"神所洁净的你不可当作俗物。"旧约的饮食律例划分了洁净与不洁净——基督洁净了一切——在基督里"各样的食物都是洁净的"（可7:19）。这不只是关于食物——而是关于外邦人——神也洁净了外邦人——使他们与犹太人一同成为神的子民。

**十一奉献——基督的完全奉献**——哥林多后书8:9"你们知道我们主耶稣基督的恩典：他本来富足，却为你们成了贫穷，叫你们因他的贫穷可以成为富足。"我们奉献十分之一——基督奉献了祂的一切。我们从丰富中拿出一部分——基督从至富变为至贫。我们的奉献学习敬畏——基督的奉献成就救赎。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"You Are God's Children"—Christ Makes Us God's Children**—Galatians 3:26: "For in Christ Jesus you are all sons of God, through faith." OT: Israel is God's children—NT: all who believe in Christ are God's children—regardless of race or background.

**Dietary Laws Abolished—Christ Cleansed All**—Acts 10:15: "What God has made clean, do not call common." OT dietary laws divided clean and unclean—Christ cleansed all—"Because it entereth not into his heart, but into the belly, and goeth out into the draught, purging all meats?" (Mark 7:19). This isn't just about food—but about Gentiles too.

**Tithing—Christ's Complete Offering**—2 Corinthians 8:9: "Though he was rich, yet for your sake he became poor, so that you by his poverty might become rich." We give a tenth—Christ gave everything. Our giving learns reverence—Christ's giving accomplished redemption.
