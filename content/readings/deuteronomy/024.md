## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第二十四章是一章充满怜悯的律法——从离婚的规定到保护穷人、寡妇、孤儿和寄居者的条例。这一章让我们看到神的律法不只是冷冰冰的规矩——而是充满温度的保护。

**离婚的规定（第1-4节）**

> "人若娶妻以后见她有什么不合理的事不喜悦她，就可以写休书交在她手中打发她离开夫家。"

弟兄姐妹，这段经文在马太福音19章中被法利赛人引用来试探耶稣——"摩西为什么吩咐给妻子休书就可以休她呢？"耶稣的回答是关键的："摩西因为你们的心硬，所以许你们休妻，但起初并不是这样。"弟兄姐妹，"许你们"——不是"命令你们"。休书不是神的心意——而是因为人心的刚硬而设立的保护机制。弟兄姐妹，在当时的文化中，丈夫可以随意抛弃妻子——休书至少给了妻子一个正式的法律文件——证明她是被合法离婚的——她可以再嫁。弟兄姐妹，律法不是认可离婚——而是在人性的罪中为弱者提供最低限度的保护。

**新婚的免役（第5节）**

> "新娶妻之人不可从军出征也不可托他办理什么公事，可以在家清闲一年使他所娶的妻快活。"

弟兄姐妹，"在家清闲一年"——弟兄姐妹，神给新婚夫妇一整年的保护期！弟兄姐妹，不用打仗、不用办公事——就是在家陪妻子。弟兄姐妹，婚姻关系的建立需要时间——神认为这比国家的军事需求更重要。弟兄姐妹，今天的社会中新婚夫妇可能连一周蜜月都没有——但神给了一年。弟兄姐妹，这显示了神对婚姻的看重——婚姻的根基比一切事务都优先。

**不可拿磨石作当头（第6节）**

> "不可拿人的全套磨石或是上磨石作当头，因为这是拿人的命作当头。"

弟兄姐妹，磨石是用来磨面粉的——没有磨石就不能做饼——不能做饼就没有食物。弟兄姐妹，"拿人的命作当头"——你可以抵押别的东西——但不可以拿走一个人维生的基本工具。弟兄姐妹，这就像今天说"你可以要他还钱——但不能拿走他的饭碗"。弟兄姐妹，债权是合法的——但债权不能压过人的生存权。

**留给穷人的（第19-22节）**

> "你在田间收割庄稼若忘下一捆不可回去再取，要留给寄居的与孤儿寡妇。你打橄榄树不可再打——要留给寄居的与孤儿寡妇。你摘葡萄园的葡萄不可再摘——要留给寄居的与孤儿寡妇。你也要记念你在埃及地作过奴仆，所以我吩咐你这样行。"

弟兄姐妹，"忘下一捆不可回去再取"——弟兄姐妹，这就是"拾穗权"——路得记中路得在波阿斯田间拾穗就是基于这条律法。弟兄姐妹，神不只是说"你可以施舍给穷人"——祂立了律法保证穷人有权利自己去捡拾。弟兄姐妹，这保留了穷人的尊严——他们不是在"乞讨"——而是在行使律法赋予的权利。弟兄姐妹，施舍可以让人觉得羞耻——但"拾穗"让人在劳动中保持尊严。

弟兄姐妹，"你也要记念你在埃及地作过奴仆"——弟兄姐妹，又是这句话！弟兄姐妹，怜悯的动力始终是记忆——记念你曾经也是弱者。弟兄姐妹，你今天有田有地有丰收——但不要忘记你曾经一无所有。弟兄姐妹，神不是用道德义务来驱动怜悯——而是用记忆来驱动——"记念你曾经也在那里"。

弟兄姐妹，申命记第二十四章的怜悯律法展示了神律法的温度——保护被弃的妻子、保护新婚的夫妇、保护穷人的生计、保护弱者的尊严。弟兄姐妹，律法不是冷冰冰的规则——律法的核心是爱。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 24 is a chapter full of compassionate laws—from divorce regulations to protecting the poor, widows, orphans, and sojourners. This chapter shows God's law isn't cold rules—but warm protection.

**Divorce Regulation (vv. 1-4)**

Pharisees cited this passage testing Jesus (Matt 19). Jesus' answer was key: "Because of your hardness of heart Moses allowed you to divorce your wives, but from the beginning it was not so." "Allowed"—not "commanded." Divorce certificate wasn't God's desire—but a protective mechanism given human sinfulness. In that culture, husbands could abandon wives freely—at least the certificate gave wives a legal document proving lawful divorce—enabling remarriage. The law doesn't approve divorce—but provides minimum protection for the vulnerable amid human sin.

**Newlywed Exemption (v. 5)**

"When a man is newly married, he shall not go out with the army...He shall be free at home one year to be happy with his wife." God gives newlyweds an entire year's protection! No military, no public duties—just home with his wife. Marriage relationship building needs time—God considers this more important than national military needs.

**Don't Take Millstones as Pledge (v. 6)**

"No one shall take a mill or an upper millstone in pledge, for that would be taking a life in pledge." Without millstones, no flour; no flour, no bread; no bread, no food. "Taking a life in pledge"—you may demand repayment—but can't take away someone's basic survival tool. Debt rights are legitimate—but can't override survival rights.

**Leave for the Poor (vv. 19-22)**

"When you reap...and have forgotten a sheaf in the field, you shall not go back to get it. It shall be for the sojourner, the fatherless, and the widow." The "gleaning right"—Ruth gleaned in Boaz's field based on this law. God didn't just say "you may give to the poor"—He legislated the poor's right to gather for themselves. This preserves dignity—they're not "begging" but exercising legal rights. Charity can shame—but gleaning maintains dignity through labor.

"You shall remember that you were a slave in Egypt"—mercy's motivation is always memory—remembering you too were once vulnerable.

## 历史背景（中文）

离婚律法中的"不合理的事"（ervat davar）在犹太传统中引发了著名的学派之争——沙买学派（Shammai）认为这只指"淫乱"——希勒尔学派（Hillel）认为包括任何让丈夫不满的事（甚至烧焦了食物）。马太福音19:3法利赛人用这个争议来试探耶稣。"拾穗权"（leket, shikchah, peah）在路得记中有最美丽的实践——路得记2:2-3路得去波阿斯田间拾穗。犹太传统将"拾穗权"发展为完整的社会福利系统——包括角落的庄稼（peah）、遗漏的（shikchah）、散落的（leket）。"不可拿磨石作当头"的规定也出现在出埃及记22:25-26中关于外衣的类似规定——"日落的时候你总要把当头还他因为只有这件当被盖的他拿什么睡觉呢"。

## Historical Context (English)

"Some indecency" (ervat davar) in the divorce law sparked the famous Shammai vs. Hillel debate—Shammai said it means only "sexual immorality"—Hillel included anything displeasing (even burnt food). Pharisees used this controversy to test Jesus (Matt 19:3). "Gleaning rights" (leket, shikchah, peah) have their most beautiful practice in Ruth 2:2-3. Jewish tradition developed gleaning into a complete social welfare system. The millstone pledge prohibition parallels Exodus 22:25-26's cloak regulation—"you shall restore it before the sun goes down...in what else shall he sleep?"

## Structure (English)

**Divorce Law (vv. 1-4)**—If displeased, write certificate → after remarriage, can't take her back → she has been defiled.
**Newlywed Exemption (v. 5)**—One year no army/public duty.
**Protecting Livelihood (vv. 6-7)**—No millstones as pledge → kidnapping punishable by death.
**Skin Disease (vv. 8-9)**—Follow priest's instructions → remember Miriam.
**Compassionate Lending (vv. 10-13)**—Don't enter house for pledge → return poor person's cloak by sunset → he'll bless you before the LORD.
**Workers' Rights (vv. 14-15)**—Don't oppress poor hired worker → pay same day → lest he cry to the LORD.
**Individual Responsibility (v. 16)**—Fathers not put to death for children or vice versa → each for own sin.
**Protect Sojourners/Orphans/Widows (vv. 17-18)**—Don't pervert justice → don't take widow's garment.
**Gleaning Rights (vv. 19-22)**—Forgotten sheaf: leave it → olive trees: don't beat again → vineyard: don't strip again → for sojourner/orphan/widow → remember Egypt slavery.

## 神学要点（中文）

**休书——神对罪中弱者的保护**
改革宗传统教导婚姻是不可轻易破裂的约。韦敏斯德信条第24章论婚姻：婚约不可以任何借口解除。但同时承认在某些情况下（奸淫、恶意离弃）可以离婚。申命记的休书不是认可离婚——而是在罪的现实中为被弃者提供最低保护。律法有时不是展示理想——而是管理堕落。

**拾穗权——结构性的公义**
改革宗传统教导社会公义是律法的重要目的。韦敏斯德大要理问答第141问。拾穗权不是可选的慈善——而是结构性的社会保障。弟兄姐妹，这不是说"如果你想帮助穷人就留一些"——而是说"你必须留"。神把穷人的权利写进了律法——不依赖富人的善意。

**"记念你在埃及作过奴仆"——恩典驱动的伦理**
改革宗传统教导恩典产生感恩和顺服。韦敏斯德信条第16章论善行。怜悯的动力不是律法的强制——而是恩典的记忆。"我曾经也在那里"——这是怜悯最强的动力。

## Theology (English)

**Divorce Certificate—God's Protection for Sin's Vulnerable**
The Reformed tradition teaches marriage is an unbreakable covenant. Westminster Confession chapter 24. Deuteronomy's certificate doesn't approve divorce—but provides minimum protection for the abandoned amid sin's reality. Law sometimes doesn't display the ideal—but manages the fallen.

**Gleaning Rights—Structural Justice**
The Reformed tradition teaches social justice is law's important purpose. WLC Q141. Gleaning isn't optional charity—but structural social security. "You must leave"—God wrote the poor's rights into law—not dependent on the rich's goodwill.

**"Remember You Were a Slave"—Grace-Driven Ethics**
The Reformed tradition teaches grace produces gratitude and obedience. Westminster Confession chapter 16. Mercy's motivation isn't legal compulsion—but grace's memory. "I was once there too"—mercy's strongest motivation.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**休书——基督永不离弃祂的新妇**——以弗所书5:25"你们作丈夫的要爱你们的妻子正如基督爱教会为教会舍己。"旧约允许休书——但基督永远不会给教会写"休书"。以赛亚书54:5-7"因为造你的是你的丈夫……耶和华召你如被离弃心中忧伤的妻。"即使以色列不忠——神仍然不离弃。

**拾穗权——基督是穷人的份**——路得记2:12波阿斯对路得说"愿你从耶和华以色列神的翅膀下得着满足的赏赐。"路得在波阿斯田间拾穗——预表了外邦人在基督的恩典中拾取救恩。路得是穷人、寡妇、外邦人——三重弱势——但在恩典中她得到了比拾穗更多的——她得到了波阿斯本人——成为大卫的曾祖母。弟兄姐妹，在基督里你得到的不只是"拾穗"——而是"全部丰收"。

**"当日给他工价"——基督即时的恩典**——路加福音23:43"我实在告诉你今日你要同我在乐园里了。"申命记要求当日付工价——基督的恩典也是即时的——十字架上的强盗"今日"就与基督同在乐园。恩典不迟延。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Divorce Certificate—Christ Never Abandons His Bride**—Ephesians 5:25: "Husbands, love your wives, as Christ loved the church." The OT allowed divorce certificates—but Christ will never write the church a "divorce certificate." Isaiah 54:5-7: "Your Maker is your husband."

**Gleaning Rights—Christ Is the Poor's Portion**—Ruth 2:12: Boaz to Ruth: "May the LORD repay you...under whose wings you have come to take refuge." Ruth gleaning in Boaz's field foreshadows Gentiles gathering salvation in Christ's grace. Ruth was poor, widow, foreigner—triple-vulnerable—but in grace she received not just gleanings—she received Boaz himself—becoming David's great-grandmother. In Christ you don't just get "gleanings"—you get "the entire harvest."

**"Pay Wages Same Day"—Christ's Immediate Grace**—Luke 23:43: "Truly, I say to you, today you will be with me in paradise." Deuteronomy requires same-day wages—Christ's grace is also immediate—the cross's thief was "today" in paradise. Grace doesn't delay.
