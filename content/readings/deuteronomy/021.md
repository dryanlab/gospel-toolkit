## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第二十一章处理几个不同的主题——未破案的谋杀、战俘妇女的婚姻、长子的权利、悖逆子女的惩罚，以及挂在树上的尸体。这些看似杂乱的规定被一个主题串联：生命的神圣性和社会秩序的维护。

**未破案的谋杀（第1-9节）**

> "在耶和华你神所赐你为业的地上，若遇见被杀的人倒在田野，不知道是谁杀的，你的长老和审判官就要出去，量明那被杀的人离哪一座城较近。那城的长老就要取一头未曾耕地未曾负轭的母牛犊……在那里折断母牛犊的颈项。祭司要祷告说：耶和华啊，愿你赦免你的百姓以色列；不要叫流无辜人血的罪归在你百姓以色列中间。"

弟兄姐妹，"不知道是谁杀的"——弟兄姐妹，现代社会也有许多悬案——永远找不到凶手。弟兄姐妹，神在律法中提供了处理这种情况的程序——不是装作没发生——而是要承认、认罪、寻求赦免。弟兄姐妹，"我们的手没有流这人的血"——附近城市的长老要公开宣告无罪——这是一种集体的道德洁净仪式。

弟兄姐妹，这个律法背后的神学是：流无辜人的血会污染土地——即使凶手不知道是谁——那血仍然在那里。弟兄姐妹，神的土地不容无辜人的血被忽视——必须有正式的处理程序来恢复社会的洁净。

**战俘妇女（第10-14节）**

弟兄姐妹，这段经文在今天看来令人不安——以色列男子可以娶战俘妇女。弟兄姐妹，必须在古代背景中理解：当时的战争惯例是把女战俘当作奴隶或随意处置。申命记的律法实际上是对这种惯例的限制——给战俘妇女提供了尊严和法律保护——"她已经成了你的妻子"——她有妻子的地位，不只是奴隶。弟兄姐妹，这是神律法的渐进性——不是一步到位的理想——而是在当时文化背景下对弱势者的保护。

**长子的权利（第15-17节）**

> "若某人有两个妻子，一个所爱，一个所恶，所爱的和所恶的都给他生了儿子，所生的长子是那所恶的妻所生的，那人把产业给他儿子的日子不可将那所爱之妻的儿子立为长子，以代替那所恶之妻的儿子——那所恶的妻所生的是长子，必将两份的产业归他，因为他是父亲力量初出的，长子的名分是他的。"

弟兄姐妹，"力量初出的"——弟兄姐妹，长子有双份产业——但这个权利不能因为父亲的偏爱而被剥夺。弟兄姐妹，雅各最爱拉结——所以他想把长子名分给约瑟（拉结的儿子）——但按照这个律法，以西结是合法的长子。弟兄姐妹，感情不能凌驾于法律之上——父亲的偏爱不能剥夺长子合法的权利。

**挂在树上（第22-23节）**

> "人若犯了该死的罪被治死了，你将他挂在木头上，他的尸首不可留在木头上过夜，必当天将他葬埋，因为被挂的人是在神咒诅之下的；你不可玷污耶和华你神赐你为业的地。"

弟兄姐妹，"挂在木头上的是在神咒诅之下的"——弟兄姐妹，这短短一句话是整个基督受难神学的旧约根基。使徒保罗在加拉太书3:13直接引用这节经文：基督为我们受了咒诅——因为祂被挂在十字架（木头）上——承担了我们所当受的神的咒诅。弟兄姐妹，十字架不是偶然的——而是神在律法中预先写下的救赎计划的实现。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 21 handles several topics—unsolved murder, captive women, firstborn rights, rebellious children, and hanging on a tree. A common thread: the sanctity of life and maintenance of social order.

**Unsolved Murder (vv. 1-9)**

> "If someone is found slain...and it is not known who killed him...the elders of the city that is nearest to the slain man shall...break the heifer's neck...and they shall testify, 'Our hands did not shed this blood.'"

"Unknown killer"—modern society also has many cold cases. God provided procedures for this situation—not ignoring it—but acknowledging, confessing, seeking forgiveness. The theology: innocent blood pollutes the land—even without knowing the murderer—the blood remains. God's land cannot ignore innocent blood—formal procedures must restore societal purity.

**Firstborn Rights (vv. 15-17)**

If a man has two wives and the firstborn is from the unloved wife—"he shall acknowledge the firstborn...by giving him a double portion of all that he has." Favoritism cannot strip the firstborn's legal rights. Jacob loved Rachel—wanted to give Joseph the birthright—but the law protects the legitimate firstborn's right. Emotion cannot override law.

**Hanging on a Tree (vv. 22-23)**

> "If a man has committed a crime punishable by death and he is put to death, and you hang him on a tree...for a hanged man is cursed by God."

"Cursed by God"—this brief phrase is the entire NT theology of Christ's crucifixion's OT foundation. Paul directly quotes this in Galatians 3:13: Christ became a curse for us—hung on the cross (tree)—bearing God's curse we deserved. The cross wasn't accidental—but the fulfillment of the redemptive plan God wrote into the law beforehand.

## 历史背景（中文）

"折断母牛犊的颈项"仪式是旧约中处理"血染之地"的特殊程序——流无辜人的血会在神学意义上污染土地（民35:33"血是污秽那地的；除了杀那流血之人的血，别的不能赎那地所流的血"）。战俘妇女的规定——给她一个月的时间哀悼父母——体现了神律法的人性关怀。"挂在木头上的人"——希伯来文"木头"（ets）与"十字架"的概念在希腊文和拉丁文中是相连的——彼得前书2:24"他被挂在木头上亲身担当了我们的罪"；使徒行传5:30"他是挂在木头上杀害了的那位，我们祖宗的神已经叫他复活"。

## Historical Context (English)

The "heifer's neck broken" ceremony was a special procedure for handling "blood-polluted land"—innocent bloodshed theologically polluted the land (Num 35:33). The captive woman regulation—giving her a month to mourn her parents—reflects the law's humane care. "Hanged on a tree"—Hebrew "tree" (ets) connects to "cross" concepts in Greek and Latin—1 Peter 2:24: "He himself bore our sins in his body on the tree"; Acts 5:30: "The God of our fathers raised Jesus, whom you killed by hanging him on a tree."

## 经文结构（中文）

**未破案的谋杀（1-9节）**——发现倒在田野的被杀者→量明距离最近的城→长老取母牛犊→折断颈项→洗手宣誓→祭司祈求赦免。
**战俘女子（10-14节）**——可娶战俘中美丽的女子→带她回家→给她一月哀悼→然后成为妻子→不可以奴婢待她。
**长子的名分（15-17节）**——两妻各生儿子→长子是所恶之妻所生→不可将爱妻之子立为长子→长子得双份产业。
**悖逆的儿子（18-21节）**——顽梗悖逆的儿子→父母管教不听→带到城门口→城里的人用石头打死→除灭恶人。
**挂在木头上的人（22-23节）**——犯该死罪被挂→不可留尸过夜→必当天葬埋→被挂的人是神咒诅之下的。

## Structure (English)

**Unsolved Murder (vv. 1-9)**—Find slain in field → measure nearest city → elders take heifer → break neck → wash hands, testify → priest seeks forgiveness.
**Captive Women (vv. 10-14)**—May marry captive beautiful woman → bring her home → one month mourning → then marry → don't sell her as slave.
**Firstborn Rights (vv. 15-17)**—Two wives each with sons → firstborn from unloved wife → can't transfer birthright to loved wife's son → firstborn gets double portion.
**Rebellious Son (vv. 18-21)**—Stubborn, rebellious son → parents discipline but doesn't listen → bring to city gate → people stone him → purge evil.
**Hanging on a Tree (vv. 22-23)**—Criminal hanged → don't leave body overnight → bury same day → hanged person is under God's curse.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"挂在木头上是神咒诅之下"——代赎神学**
改革宗传统教导基督承担了我们当受的咒诅。韦敏斯德信条第8章论中保基督。"被挂的人是在神咒诅之下"——基督被钉在十字架上——就是"被挂在木头上"——承担了罪人当受的咒诅。这是"代替救赎"（penal substitution）最清晰的旧约预言之一。

**流无辜人的血——生命的神圣**
改革宗传统教导人是照神形像所造的，因此生命是神圣的。韦敏斯德信条第4章。"流无辜人的血"污染土地——土地的洁净需要仪式恢复——这反映了神对每一条生命的珍视。没有无辜的死亡是"可以忽视的"——每一条无辜的生命都值得追究。

**感情与正义——不可偏心**
改革宗传统教导公义高于感情。韦敏斯德信条第11章。父亲的偏爱不能剥夺长子的合法权利——感情是真实的，但不能凌驾于法律和正义之上。神的公义是不以貌取人的——也不因感情而偏颇。

## Theology (English)

**"Hanged Is Under God's Curse"—Substitutionary Atonement Theology**
The Reformed tradition teaches Christ bore the curse we deserved. Westminster Confession chapter 8.: Hanged is under God's curse—Christ on the cross—"hung on a tree"—bore the curse sinners deserved. One of substitutionary atonement's clearest OT prophecies.

**Shedding Innocent Blood—Sanctity of Life**
The Reformed tradition teaches humans made in God's image, thus life is sacred. Westminster Confession chapter 4.: Shedding innocent blood pollutes land—no innocent death is "ignorable"—every innocent life deserves accountability.

**Emotion vs. Justice—No Favoritism**
The Reformed tradition teaches justice transcends emotion. Westminster Confession chapter 11. A father's favoritism can't strip the firstborn's legal rights. God's justice shows no favoritism—not swayed by emotion.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"挂在木头上的人是神咒诅之下的"——基督为我们成了咒诅**——加拉太书3:13"基督既为我们受了咒诅，就赎出我们脱离律法的咒诅；因为经上记着'凡挂在木头上都是被咒诅的。'"这是申命记21:23在新约中最直接的应用——十字架不是偶然事件——而是神在西奈山的律法中就预先写下的救赎计划。弟兄姐妹，律法书中已经写着基督将要承受咒诅的方式。

**"尸首不可留过夜"——基督被取下葬埋**——约翰福音19:31"那日是预备日，又因那安息日是个大日，犹太人就求彼拉多叫人打断他们的腿，把他们取下来。"申命记规定挂在木头上的人当天必须埋葬——约翰记载犹太人要求当天取下被钉的人——这正是因为要遵守这条律法（逾越节前的准备日）。律法预先为基督的埋葬作了安排。

**流无辜人的血——基督是无辜的受害者**——使徒行传3:14"你们弃绝了那圣洁公义者，反求着释放一个凶手给你们。"彼拉多说"这人没有罪"——基督是无辜的——但祂的血被流了。这血不会被神忽视——它呼求赦免——不是审判（来12:24"所撒的血所说的比亚伯的血所说的更美"）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Hanged Is Under God's Curse"—Christ Became a Curse for Us**—Galatians 3:13: "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us—for it is written, 'Cursed is everyone who is hanged on a tree.'" This is Deuteronomy 21:23's most direct NT application—the cross wasn't accidental—but the redemptive plan God wrote into Sinai's law beforehand.

**"Don't Leave Body Overnight"—Christ Was Taken Down and Buried**—John 19:31: "The Jews asked Pilate that their legs might be broken and that they might be taken away." Deuteronomy required same-day burial—John records Jews requesting removal—precisely to observe this law (preparation day before Passover). The law pre-arranged Christ's burial.

**Shedding Innocent Blood—Christ Is the Innocent Victim**—Acts 3:14: "You denied the Holy and Righteous One." Pilate said "I find no guilt in him"—Christ was innocent—yet His blood was shed. This blood God won't ignore—it cries for forgiveness—not judgment (Heb 12:24 "the sprinkled blood that speaks a better word than Abel's blood").
