## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。从今天起，我们开始申命记——我最后的讲道、我的临终遗言。我一百二十岁了，站在摩押平原，面对约旦河对面的应许之地——我自己不能进去，但我要把一切都告诉这新的一代。

**开场：时间与地点（1-5节）**

> "以下所记的是摩西在约旦河东的旷野向以色列众人所说的话。"

弟兄姐妹，申命记的希伯来名字是"Devarim"——"话语"。这整本书就是"话语"——我在死前对以色列人讲的话。中文名"申命记"来自希腊文Deuteronomion——"第二次的律法"——不是新的律法，而是重申、解释、应用那在西奈山已经颁布的律法。

第2节有一个令人心碎的括号："从何烈山经过西珥山到加低斯巴尼亚有十一天的路程。"弟兄姐妹——十一天的路程，我们走了四十年。这不是路途遥远——而是不信的代价。

"出埃及第四十年十一月初一日"——距离进入迦南只有大约两个月了。我在生命的最后两个月讲了这些话。弟兄姐妹，这是一个将死之人的紧迫——我没有时间闲聊了——我要把最重要的事情说清楚。

**回顾：从何烈山出发（6-8节）**

> "你们在这山上住的日子够了。要起行转到亚摩利人的山地。"

弟兄姐妹，四十年前神在何烈山（西奈山）对以色列说"够了"——不要再停留了，出发吧。西奈山是领受律法的地方——但不是最终目的地。有些人在属灵经历中停留太久——他们喜欢西奈山的安全感——但神说"够了"。属灵的高峰经验是为了装备你出发——不是为了让你安营不走。

"你们要进去得这地，就是耶和华向你们列祖亚伯拉罕、以撒、雅各起誓应许赐给他们和他们后裔为业之地。"弟兄姐妹——"起誓应许"——这不是一个建议——是一个起了誓的盟约。迦南之地不是你争取来的奖赏——是神发了誓要给你的产业。

**设立领袖（9-18节）**

"管理你们的重任，我独自担当不起。"弟兄姐妹，我在回顾我最初承认自己能力有限的那一刻。以色列人像天上的星那样多——一个人管不了。于是我请各支派选出智慧人、有见识的人、有名望的人作千夫长、百夫长、五十夫长、十夫长。

弟兄姐妹，连摩西都需要分担领导——这不是软弱的标志——而是智慧。一个人试图做所有的事——不是忠心，是骄傲。教会需要多层次的治理结构——长老、执事、各种分工——不是因为牧师不行，而是因为神的百姓需要被照顾到每一个角落。

**加低斯巴尼亚的灾难（19-46节）**

弟兄姐妹，这是第一章最长也最沉痛的部分——我重新讲述了加低斯巴尼亚的探子事件。

"看哪，耶和华你的神已将那地摆在你面前……不要惧怕，也不要惊惶。"弟兄姐妹——神说"不要怕"——以色列人说"先派人去看看"。看起来合理——但这是信心的退步。神已经说"摆在你面前"了——你还要"先看看"？

十二个探子回来——"耶和华我们的神所赐给我们的是美地"——承认地好，但随即说"住那里的民比我们强大"。你们就在帐棚里发怨言，说"耶和华因为恨我们，所以将我们从埃及地领出来"。弟兄姐妹——"恨我们"！把出埃及的拯救恩典说成了仇恨！这是不信最丑陋的面目——把神的爱说成恨。

"你们在旷野所行的路上，也曾见耶和华你们的神抚养你们，如同人抚养儿子一般。"弟兄姐妹——"如同人抚养儿子一般"——神不是一个冷漠的管理者——祂像父亲抱着儿子一样带你们走过旷野。你们亲眼见过——却仍然不信。

结果——那一代人被禁止进入迦南。只有迦勒和约书亚可以进去。甚至我——"耶和华为你们的缘故也向我发怒，说：你必不得进入那地。"弟兄姐妹，我也不能进去。

然后——最讽刺的一幕——以色列人听到审判后突然"悔改"了："我们情愿上去争战。"但神说"不要上去，因我不在你们中间。"弟兄姐妹——迟来的顺服不是顺服——是悖逆的另一种形式。神说去的时候不去——神说不去的时候偏要去。他们擅自上山——被亚摩利人打得落花流水。

弟兄姐妹，申命记第一章是整本书的序曲——它用回顾的方式告诉新一代：你们的父辈因为不信而失去了应许之地。现在轮到你们了——你们会重蹈覆辙，还是凭信心过河？

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. From today we begin Deuteronomy—my final sermon, my deathbed testament. I am 120 years old, standing on the plains of Moab, facing the promised land across the Jordan—I myself cannot enter, but I will tell this new generation everything.

**Opening: Time and Place (vv. 1-5)**

> "These are the words that Moses spoke to all Israel beyond the Jordan in the wilderness."

Brothers and sisters, Deuteronomy's Hebrew name is "Devarim"—"Words." This entire book is "words"—my words to Israel before death. The English name "Deuteronomy" comes from Greek Deuteronomion—"second law"—not new law, but restating, explaining, and applying what was already given at Sinai.

Verse 2 contains a heartbreaking parenthetical: "It is eleven days' journey from Horeb by the way of Mount Seir to Kadesh-barnea." Brothers and sisters—an eleven-day journey that took forty years. Not because the road was long—but because unbelief is costly.

"In the fortieth year, on the first day of the eleventh month"—approximately two months before entering Canaan. I spoke these words in my final two months. Brothers and sisters, this was a dying man's urgency—no time for small talk—I had to make the most important things clear.

**Review: Departing from Horeb (vv. 6-8)**

> "You have stayed long enough at this mountain. Turn and take your journey."

Brothers and sisters, forty years ago God told Israel at Horeb (Sinai) "enough"—stop lingering, depart. Sinai was where law was received—but not the final destination. Some people linger too long in spiritual experiences—they love Sinai's security—but God says "enough." Mountain-top spiritual experiences equip you to depart—not to camp permanently.

"Go in and take possession of the land that the LORD swore to your fathers, to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob, to give to them and to their offspring after them." Brothers and sisters—"swore"—this was not a suggestion—it was a sworn covenant. Canaan was not a prize you earned—it was inheritance God swore to give.

**Appointing Leaders (vv. 9-18)**

"I am not able to bear you by myself." Brothers and sisters, I was recalling when I first admitted my limited capacity. Israel was numerous as stars—one person could not manage them. So I asked each tribe to choose wise, understanding, experienced men as commanders of thousands, hundreds, fifties, and tens.

Brothers and sisters, even Moses needed to share leadership—not a sign of weakness, but wisdom. One person trying to do everything is not faithfulness but pride. Churches need multi-layered governance—elders, deacons, various roles—not because pastors are inadequate, but because God's people need care reaching every corner.

**The Kadesh-barnea Disaster (vv. 19-46)**

Brothers and sisters, this was chapter 1's longest and most painful section—I retold the spy incident at Kadesh-barnea.

"See, the LORD your God has set the land before you...Do not fear or be dismayed." Brothers and sisters—God said "don't fear"—Israel said "let us send men first to spy." Seemingly reasonable—but a retreat from faith. God already said "set before you"—and you still needed to "look first"?

Twelve spies returned—"It is a good land that the LORD our God is giving us"—acknowledging the land's goodness, but then "the people who dwell there are stronger than we." You grumbled in your tents, saying "Because the LORD hated us he has brought us out of Egypt." Brothers and sisters—"hated us"! Reframing the Exodus's saving grace as hatred! This is unbelief's ugliest face—calling God's love hatred.

"In the wilderness...you have seen how the LORD your God carried you, as a man carries his son." Brothers and sisters—"as a man carries his son"—God was not a cold administrator—He carried you through the wilderness like a father holding his son. You saw it with your own eyes—yet still did not believe.

The result—that generation was banned from Canaan. Only Caleb and Joshua could enter. Even me—"The LORD was angry with me also on your account and said, 'You also shall not go in there.'"

Then—the most ironic scene—after hearing judgment, Israel suddenly "repented": "We are willing to go up and fight." But God said "Do not go up, for I am not among you." Brothers and sisters—belated obedience is not obedience—it is rebellion in another form. When God says go, they won't; when God says don't go, they insist. They presumed to go up—and the Amorites routed them completely.

Brothers and sisters, Deuteronomy chapter 1 is the entire book's overture—using retrospection to tell the new generation: your fathers lost the promised land through unbelief. Now it's your turn—will you repeat their failure, or cross by faith?

## 历史背景（中文）

申命记的写作背景是古代近东的宗主条约（suzerainty treaty）——学者们发现申命记的结构与赫人帝国（Hittite）的条约格式高度相似：序言（宗主自我介绍）→历史回顾（宗主的恩惠）→律法条款→祝福与咒诅→见证与保管。这不是说申命记抄袭了赫人条约——而是说神使用了当时人们熟悉的法律形式来表达祂与以色列之间的盟约关系。"从何烈山到加低斯巴尼亚有十一天路程"——这个距离大约是280-300公里（取决于路线），一支大型民族迁徙队伍每天行进约25-30公里是合理的。加低斯巴尼亚（Kadesh-barnea）被认为是今天以色列-埃及边境附近的Ain el-Qudeirat绿洲。申命记的讲道日期"出埃及第四十年十一月初一日"——以色列人在五月十日（正月十日）过约旦河（书4:19），所以摩西在死前约五个月开始讲道。

## Historical Context (English)

Deuteronomy's background aligns with ancient Near Eastern suzerainty treaties—scholars discovered its structure closely parallels Hittite treaty format: preamble (suzerain self-introduction) → historical review (suzerain's beneficence) → stipulations → blessings and curses → witnesses and document deposit. This does not mean Deuteronomy copied Hittite treaties—but that God used a familiar legal format to express His covenant relationship with Israel. "Eleven days' journey from Horeb to Kadesh-barnea"—approximately 280-300 kilometers (depending on route); a large national migration caravan traveling 25-30 kilometers daily is reasonable. Kadesh-barnea is identified as the Ain el-Qudeirat oasis near today's Israel-Egypt border. Deuteronomy's sermon date "first day of the eleventh month in the fortieth year"—Israel crossed the Jordan on the tenth of the first month (Josh 4:19), so Moses began preaching about five months before his death.

## 经文结构（中文）

**序言——时间地点和背景（1-5节）**——摩押平原、出埃及第40年11月、"十一天的路程走了四十年"。

**回顾：从何烈山出发（6-8节）**——"住的够了要起行"→进迦南地得列祖应许为业。

**设立领袖（9-18节）**——摩西"独自担当不起"→千夫长百夫长五十夫长十夫长→公正审判不偏待人。

**加低斯巴尼亚灾难（19-33节）**——"不要惧怕"→"先打发人窥探"→好地报告→百姓发怨言"耶和华恨我们"→不信→审判→一代人被禁。

**迟来的悖逆（34-46节）**——百姓假悔改要上去→"我不在你们中间"→擅自上山→被打败→回来哭号→住加低斯许多日子。

## Structure (English)

**Preamble—Time, Place, and Setting (vv. 1-5)**—Plains of Moab, 40th year 11th month, "eleven days' journey took forty years."

**Review: Departing Horeb (vv. 6-8)**—"Stayed long enough, take your journey" → enter Canaan, take inheritance promised to fathers.

**Appointing Leaders (vv. 9-18)**—Moses "cannot bear alone" → commanders of thousands, hundreds, fifties, tens → just judgment, no partiality.

**Kadesh-barnea Disaster (vv. 19-33)**—"Don't fear" → "send men to spy first" → good land report → people grumble "the LORD hated us" → unbelief → judgment → generation banned.

**Belated Rebellion (vv. 34-46)**—People falsely repent, want to go up → "I am not among you" → presume to go up → defeated → return weeping → stayed at Kadesh many days.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"十一天走了四十年"——不信的代价**
从何烈山到加低斯巴尼亚只需十一天——改革宗传统教导不信的严重后果。韦敏斯德信条第6章论人的堕落。希伯来书3:19"这样看来，他们不能进入安息是因为不信的缘故了。"不信不是无害的态度——它有具体的、可计算的代价：三十九年。你的不信让你多绕了多少路？

**"如同人抚养儿子一般"——神的父性**
神像父亲一样带领以色列——改革宗传统教导神的父性和护理。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理。申命记1:31是旧约中最温柔的父子关系描述之一——但以色列人"在这事上却不信"。认识神的爱却仍然不信——这是罪最深的层面。

**迟来的顺服不是顺服——时机的重要性**
神说去的时候不去，神说不去的时候偏要去——改革宗传统教导顺服的时机性。韦敏斯德信条第16章论善功。传道书3:1"凡事都有定期，天下万务都有定时。"迟到的顺服可能比初始的悖逆更危险——因为它包含了对神话语的再次违抗。

## Theology (English)

**"Eleven Days Took Forty Years"—The Cost of Unbelief**
Horeb to Kadesh-barnea needed only eleven days—the Reformed tradition teaches unbelief's severe consequences. Westminster Confession chapter 6 on the fall. Hebrews 3:19: So we see that they were unable to enter because of unbelief. Unbelief is not a harmless attitude—it has concrete, calculable costs: thirty-nine years. How many extra years has your unbelief cost you?

**"As a Man Carries His Son"—God's Fatherhood**
God led Israel like a father—the Reformed tradition teaches God's paternal nature and providence. Westminster Confession chapter 5 on providence. Deuteronomy 1:31 is one of the Old Testament's most tender father-son descriptions—yet Israel "did not believe." Knowing God's love yet still not believing—this is sin's deepest layer.

**Belated Obedience Is Not Obedience—Timing Matters**
When God says go, they won't; when God says don't go, they insist—the Reformed tradition teaches obedience's timeliness. Westminster Confession chapter 16 on good works. Ecclesiastes 3:1: "For everything there is a season, and a time for every matter under heaven." Belated obedience may be more dangerous than initial rebellion—because it contains another violation of God's word.

## 基督的影子（中文）

申命记第一章指向基督在两个方面。

**"如同人抚养儿子"——基督是好牧人**
神在旷野抚养以色列"如同人抚养儿子"——约翰福音10:11"我是好牧人；好牧人为羊舍命。"神不只是在旷野里供应食物和水——祂像父亲一样抱着以色列走。基督不只是给我们救恩——祂每天抱着我们走过人生的旷野。以赛亚书40:11"他必像牧人牧养自己的羊群，用膀臂聚集羊羔抱在怀中。"

**摩西不能进迦南——但约书亚（耶稣）带百姓过河**
"你必不得进入那地"——律法（摩西代表）不能带人进入安息。"伺候你的嫩的儿子约书亚，他必得进入那地"——约书亚（Yeshua/耶稣）带百姓进去。弟兄姐妹，希伯来书4:8"若是约书亚已叫他们享了安息，后来神就不再提别的日子了。"旧约的约书亚只是预表——真正带我们进入永远安息的，是耶稣基督。律法指出罪——但不能拯救你。只有恩典——只有耶稣——才能带你过约旦河。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

Deuteronomy 1 points to Christ in two aspects.

**"As a Man Carries His Son"—Christ the Good Shepherd**
God carried Israel in the wilderness "as a man carries his son"—John 10:11: "I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep." God did not merely provide food and water in the wilderness—He carried Israel like a father. Christ does not merely give us salvation—He carries us daily through life's wilderness. Isaiah 40:11: "He will tend his flock like a shepherd; he will gather the lambs in his arms; he will carry them in his bosom."

**Moses Cannot Enter Canaan—But Joshua (Jesus) Leads the People Across**
"You also shall not go in there"—the Law (represented by Moses) cannot bring people into rest. "Joshua the son of Nun, who stands before you, he shall enter"—Joshua (Yeshua/Jesus) leads the people in. Brothers and sisters, Hebrews 4:8: "For if Joshua had given them rest, God would not have spoken of another day later on." The Old Testament's Joshua was only a prefiguration—the One who truly brings us into eternal rest is Jesus Christ. The Law reveals sin—but cannot save you. Only grace—only Jesus—can carry you across the Jordan.
