## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第九章是一把利剑——直刺人心中最深的骄傲。摩西用整整一章来打碎以色列人可能有的任何自义——"你进去得这地并不是因你的义"——然后用何烈山金牛犊的历史来证明：你们不是义人，你们是硬着颈项的百姓。

**不是因你的义（第1-6节）**

> "以色列啊，你当听！你今日要过约旦河，进去赶出比你强大的国民。你进去得他们的地，并不是因你的义，也不是因你心里正直，乃是因这些国民的恶，耶和华你的神将他们从你面前赶出去，又因耶和华要坚定他向你列祖亚伯拉罕、以撒、雅各起誓所应许的话。你当知道，耶和华你神将这美地赐你为业，并不是因你的义，你本是硬着颈项的百姓。"

弟兄姐妹，"不是因你的义"——在短短几节中摩西说了三次！弟兄姐妹，为什么要说三次？因为人太容易相信自己配得。弟兄姐妹，当你成功的时候——你的第一反应是什么？"我努力了所以配得"——不是的！"不是因你的义"——你进入应许之地有两个原因：（一）迦南人的罪恶（他们的审判到期了）；（二）神对列祖的应许（神守约）。注意——两个原因都跟你的"义"没有关系。

弟兄姐妹，"你本是硬着颈项的百姓"——弟兄姐妹，"硬着颈项"——就是牛不肯转头——你拉它它不动——它把脖子绷紧了拒绝被引导。弟兄姐妹，这就是以色列人——也是我们。我们不是听话的羊——我们是硬着颈项的牛。

**何烈山的悖逆——金牛犊（第7-21节）**

> "你当记念不忘，你在旷野怎样惹耶和华你神发怒。自从你出了埃及地的那日直到你们来到这地方，你们时常悖逆耶和华。你们在何烈山又惹耶和华发怒，他恼怒你们要灭绝你们。"

弟兄姐妹，"时常悖逆"——不是偶尔——是"时常"。弟兄姐妹，摩西把以色列从出埃及以来的历史总结为一个词——"悖逆"。弟兄姐妹，如果你诚实地回顾自己的信仰历程——你也只能用同样的词来描述自己——"时常悖逆"。

弟兄姐妹，何烈山的金牛犊事件（出32章）——摩西在山上与神面对面四十天——山下的百姓就造了金牛犊来拜。弟兄姐妹，四十天！他们等不了四十天！弟兄姐妹，"我就把那两块版从我手中扔下去在你们眼前摔碎了"——弟兄姐妹，两块石版——神亲手写的——摩西摔碎了。弟兄姐妹，约还没有正式开始就被打破了——这就是以色列人。约还没有交到他们手里就已经不配了。

**摩西的代祷（第25-29节）**

> "我因耶和华说要灭绝你们，就在耶和华面前照旧俯伏四十昼夜。我祈祷耶和华说：主耶和华啊，求你不要灭绝你的百姓。他们是你的产业，是你用大力救赎的。求你记念你的仆人亚伯拉罕、以撒、雅各，不要想念这百姓的顽梗、邪恶、罪过。"

弟兄姐妹，"俯伏四十昼夜"——弟兄姐妹，摩西禁食四十天四十夜——为了这些硬着颈项的百姓代祷。弟兄姐妹，摩西的代祷有三个论点：（一）"他们是你的产业"——他们属于你；（二）"你用大力救赎的"——你已经投入了这么多，不要放弃；（三）"记念你的仆人亚伯拉罕以撒雅各"——你对列祖有应许。弟兄姐妹，注意——摩西没有说"他们其实没那么坏"——他没有为百姓的罪辩护——他求的是神的怜悯和神自己的信实。

弟兄姐妹，这就是代祷的本质——不是告诉神"这个人值得被怜悯"——而是告诉神"你是有怜悯的神"。代祷不是基于被代祷者的配得——而是基于代祷者对神性情的认识。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 9 is a sword—piercing the deepest pride in the human heart. Moses uses an entire chapter to shatter any self-righteousness Israel might harbor—"not because of your righteousness"—then uses the Horeb golden calf to prove: you are not righteous; you are a stiff-necked people.

**Not Because of Your Righteousness (vv. 1-6)**

> "Hear, O Israel...Not because of your righteousness or the uprightness of your heart are you going in to possess their land, but because of the wickedness of these nations the LORD your God is driving them out...Know, therefore, that the LORD your God is not giving you this good land to possess because of your righteousness, for you are a stubborn people."

Brothers and sisters, "not because of your righteousness"—Moses says it three times in just a few verses! Why three times? Because humans too easily believe they deserve things. Two reasons you enter the Promised Land: (1) the Canaanites' sin (their judgment was due); (2) God's promise to the fathers (God keeps covenant). Notice—neither reason relates to your "righteousness."

**The Golden Calf at Horeb (vv. 7-21)**

> "Remember and do not forget how you provoked the LORD your God to wrath in the wilderness. From the day you came out of Egypt until you came to this place, you have been rebellious against the LORD."

"Continually rebellious"—not occasionally—"continually." If you honestly review your faith journey—you can only describe yourself with the same word.

Moses on the mountain forty days with God face to face—the people below made a golden calf. Forty days! They couldn't wait forty days! "I took the two tablets and threw them out of my hands, breaking them before your eyes"—the covenant was broken before it was even fully delivered.

**Moses' Intercession (vv. 25-29)**

> "So I lay prostrate before the LORD for these forty days and forty nights...And I prayed to the LORD, 'O Lord GOD, do not destroy your people...Remember your servants, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. Do not regard the stubbornness...wickedness...sin of this people.'"

Moses' intercession has three arguments: (1) "they are your heritage"—they belong to you; (2) "you redeemed with great power"—you've invested so much; (3) "remember Abraham, Isaac, Jacob"—you made promises. Notice—Moses never said "they're actually not that bad"—he didn't defend their sin—he appealed to God's mercy and God's own faithfulness.

This is intercession's essence—not telling God "this person deserves mercy"—but telling God "you are a merciful God." Intercession isn't based on the interceded-for's merit—but on the intercessor's knowledge of God's character.

## 历史背景（中文）

申命记第九章回顾的金牛犊事件详细记载在出埃及记32章。"四十昼夜"的禁食在出埃及记中记载了两次（出24:18, 34:28）——第一次是接受十诫时，第二次是为金牛犊事件代祷后重新刻版时。"硬着颈项"（stiff-necked）是旧约中对以色列悖逆的标志性描述——出现在出33:3, 33:5, 34:9等处。金牛犊很可能模仿了埃及的哈比斯（Apis）牛崇拜——以色列人在埃及生活了四百年，深受其文化影响。摩西摔碎石版的行为不只是愤怒的表达——在古代近东，摔碎条约文本象征着条约的终止。第25-29节的代祷模式后来成为犹太传统中代祷祷告的典范。

## Historical Context (English)

Deuteronomy 9's golden calf account is detailed in Exodus 32. "Forty days and nights" fasting appears twice in Exodus (24:18, 34:28)—first receiving the Ten Commandments, second after interceding for the calf incident. "Stiff-necked" is the OT's signature description of Israel's rebellion—appearing in Ex 33:3, 33:5, 34:9. The golden calf likely imitated Egyptian Apis bull worship—Israel lived in Egypt four hundred years, deeply influenced by its culture. Moses breaking the tablets wasn't just anger—in ancient Near East, breaking treaty documents symbolized treaty termination. The intercession pattern (vv. 25-29) became the model for intercessory prayer in Jewish tradition.

## 经文结构（中文）

**不是因你的义（1-6节）**——要过约旦河→进去赶出强大国民→不是因你的义(x3)→因他们的恶和神的应许→你是硬着颈项的。
**何烈山的悖逆（7-17节）**——时常悖逆→何烈山惹怒→四十昼夜不吃不喝→神要灭绝→摩西摔碎石版。
**其他悖逆事件（22节）**——他备拉、玛撒、基博罗哈他瓦也惹怒。
**加低斯巴尼亚的不信（23-24节）**——从加低斯差遣→你们违背不信不听。
**摩西的代祷（25-29节）**——俯伏四十昼夜→不要灭绝你的百姓→他们是你的产业→记念亚伯拉罕以撒雅各→不要想念他们的罪。

## Structure (English)

**Not Your Righteousness (vv. 1-6)**—Cross Jordan → drive out stronger nations → not your righteousness (x3) → because their wickedness and God's oath → you are stubborn.
**Horeb Rebellion (vv. 7-17)**—Continually rebellious → provoked at Horeb → forty days/nights fasting → God wanted to destroy → Moses broke tablets.
**Other Rebellions (v. 22)**—Taberah, Massah, Kibroth-hattaavah provoked.
**Kadesh Barnea Unbelief (vv. 23-24)**—Sent from Kadesh → you rebelled, didn't believe or listen.
**Moses' Intercession (vv. 25-29)**—Prostrate forty days/nights → don't destroy your people → your heritage → remember Abraham Isaac Jacob → don't regard their sin.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"不是因你的义"——唯独恩典（Sola Gratia）**
改革宗传统教导救恩完全出于恩典。韦敏斯德信条第十一章论称义教导：不是因为他们里面有什么或他们所做的什么，完全是因为基督的缘故。申命记9:4-6的"不是因你的义"是旧约中"唯独恩典"最清晰的表达之一——你得到应许之地不是因为你配——而是因为神的应许和祂对列祖的约。

**人的全然败坏（Total Depravity）**
改革宗五要点的"T"在本章中有充分的证据。"你本是硬着颈项的百姓""你们时常悖逆""你们向来悖逆耶和华"——人的本性不是偶尔犯错——而是"时常"悖逆。这不是说人不能做任何好事——而是说人的每一个部分都被罪影响——没有一个部分是完全纯洁的。

**代祷——基于神的信实而非人的功德**
改革宗传统教导代祷的根基是神的应许。韦敏斯德信条第21章。摩西的代祷不是基于以色列人的好行为——而是基于"记念你的仆人亚伯拉罕以撒雅各"——神自己的应许和约。有效的代祷不是给神讲被代祷者有多好——而是提醒神祂自己的应许有多信实。

## Theology (English)

**"Not Your Righteousness"—Sola Gratia**
The Reformed tradition teaches salvation is entirely by grace. Westminster Confession chapter 11 on justification: "not for anything wrought in them, or done by them...but for Christ's obedience and satisfaction." Deuteronomy 9:4-6 is among the OT's clearest expressions of Sola Gratia.

**Total Depravity**
TULIP's "T" has ample evidence here. "You are a stubborn people," "continually rebellious"—human nature isn't occasional error but "continual" rebellion.

**Intercession—Based on God's Faithfulness, Not Human Merit**
The Reformed tradition teaches intercession's foundation is God's promises. Westminster Confession chapter 21. Moses' intercession wasn't based on Israel's good behavior—but on "remember Abraham, Isaac, Jacob"—God's own promises. Effective intercession isn't telling God how good the person is—but reminding God how faithful His promises are.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**摩西代祷四十昼夜——基督永远的代祷**——希伯来书7:25"凡靠着他进到神面前的人，他都能拯救到底；因为他是长远活着，替他们祈求。"摩西为硬着颈项的百姓俯伏代祷四十天——基督为我们这些罪人永远地代祷。摩西的代祷是暂时的——基督的代祷是"长远活着替他们祈求"。

**"不是因你的义"——因信称义**——罗马书3:21-22"但如今，神的义在律法以外已经显明出来……就是神的义，因信耶稣基督加给一切相信的人。"以色列进入应许之地"不是因他们的义"——我们进入天国也"不是因我们的义"——而是因基督的义加给了我们。

**摩西摔碎石版——旧约的终结与新约的开始**——耶利米书31:31-33"耶和华说：日子将到，我要与以色列家和犹大家另立新约……我要将我的律法放在他们里面，写在他们心上。"摩西摔碎的石版象征着旧约的脆弱——人不能守住外在的律法——需要神将律法"写在心上"。这就是新约的应许——在基督里成就的应许。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Moses' Forty-Day Intercession—Christ's Eternal Intercession**—Hebrews 7:25: "He is able to save to the uttermost those who draw near to God through him, since he always lives to make intercession for them." Moses prostrate forty days for stiff-necked people—Christ intercedes eternally for sinners. Moses' intercession was temporary—Christ's is "always lives to intercede."

**"Not Your Righteousness"—Justification by Faith**—Romans 3:21-22: "But now the righteousness of God has been manifested apart from the law...the righteousness of God through faith in Jesus Christ for all who believe." Israel entered the Promised Land "not by their righteousness"—we enter heaven "not by our righteousness"—but by Christ's righteousness credited to us.

**Moses Breaking Tablets—Old Covenant's End, New Covenant's Beginning**—Jeremiah 31:31-33: "I will make a new covenant...I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts." The broken tablets symbolize the old covenant's fragility—humans cannot keep external law—needing God to "write it on hearts." This is the new covenant's promise—fulfilled in Christ.
