## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第五章重述了十条诫命——这不是简单的重复，而是在进入应许之地的门口，对新一代以色列人重新宣告神的约的核心条款。

**面对面说话（第1-5节）**

> "摩西将以色列众人召了来，对他们说：以色列人哪，我今日晓谕你们的律例典章，你们要听，可以学习，谨守遵行。耶和华我们的神在何烈山与我们立约。这约不是与我们列祖立的，乃是与我们今日在这里存活之人立的。耶和华在山上，从火中，面对面与你们说话。"

弟兄姐妹，"不是与我们列祖立的，乃是与我们今日在这里存活之人立的"——弟兄姐妹，新一代的以色列人大多不在场——他们要么还是孩子，要么还没有出生。但摩西说"与我们立的"——不是"与他们立的"。弟兄姐妹，神的约跨越世代——你的父母信主的那一刻，你就在约里面了。

**十条诫命（第6-21节）**

弟兄姐妹，十诫分为两组——前四条关于人与神的关系（第一块石版），后六条关于人与人的关系（第二块石版）。

"除了我以外，你不可有别的神"——弟兄姐妹，第一条也是最根本的。所有其他的诫命都建立在这一条上。如果你有"别的神"——后面的诫命都没有意义了。弟兄姐妹，你的"别的神"是什么？金钱？事业？感情？任何你放在耶和华之上的——就是"别的神"。

"不可为自己雕刻偶像"——弟兄姐妹，第二条延伸了第一条——不只是不可拜别的神，连独一真神也不可用偶像来代表。因为任何人手造的形像都会贬低神。

"不可妄称耶和华你神的名"——弟兄姐妹，"妄称"不只是用神的名起誓——而是以任何轻率的方式使用神的名——包括用祂的名为自己的私利服务。

"当照耶和华你神所吩咐的守安息日为圣日"——弟兄姐妹，申命记版本的安息日诫命有一个独特之处——它给出的理由不是创造（如出埃及记20:11），而是救赎："你也要记念你在埃及地作过奴仆；耶和华你神用大能的手和伸出来的膀臂将你从那里领出来。因此耶和华你的神吩咐你守安息日。"弟兄姐妹，安息日不只是因为神创造后休息了——更因为神把你从奴役中拯救出来了。你休息——是因为你不再是奴隶了。

"当孝敬父母"——弟兄姐妹，这是第一条带应许的诫命。

"不可杀人。不可奸淫。不可偷盗。不可作假见证。不可贪恋。"——弟兄姐妹，后六条保护人的生命、家庭、财产、名誉和心灵的纯洁。弟兄姐妹，这些不是任意的规则——每一条都在保护一个根本性的人类价值。

**百姓的恐惧（第22-33节）**

> "耶和华在山上，从火中、云中、幽暗中，大声晓谕你们全会众；此外并没有添别的话。他就把这话写在两块石版上，交给我。你们听见从黑暗中发出的声音，山被火烧着，你们中间所有支派的首领和长老都来就近我，说：'耶和华我们的神已将他的荣光和大能显给我们看……只恐怕我们死亡……我们何必冒死呢？若再听见耶和华我们神的声音就必死亡。'"

弟兄姐妹，百姓因为恐惧而不敢直接听神的声音——所以他们请摩西做中保。弟兄姐妹，这是旧约中保制度的起源——人无法直接面对圣洁的神——需要一个中间人。弟兄姐妹，这为什么指向基督？因为基督是唯一完美的中保——"在神和人中间只有一位中保，乃是降世为人的基督耶稣"（提前2:5）。

弟兄姐妹，申命记第五章教导我们：十诫是神的约的核心——它们保护人与神和人与人的关系。安息日是救赎的标记——不只是创造的纪念。我们需要中保——因为我们无法直接面对圣洁的神。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 5 restates the Ten Commandments—not simple repetition, but at the threshold of the Promised Land, re-declaring the covenant's core terms to a new generation.

**Face to Face (vv. 1-5)**

> "The LORD our God made a covenant with us in Horeb. Not with our fathers did the LORD make this covenant, but with us, who are all of us here alive today. The LORD spoke with you face to face at the mountain, out of the midst of the fire."

Brothers and sisters, "not with our fathers but with us"—the new generation mostly wasn't present. But Moses says "with us"—not "with them." God's covenant spans generations—when your parents believed, you were included in the covenant.

**The Ten Commandments (vv. 6-21)**

Brothers and sisters, the Ten are divided into two groups—first four on the human-God relationship (first tablet), last six on human-human relationships (second tablet).

"You shall have no other gods before me"—the first and most fundamental. All other commandments build on this. What is your "other god"? Money? Career? Romance?

"You shall not make for yourself a carved image"—extending the first—even the true God cannot be represented by idols.

The Sabbath command in Deuteronomy is unique—its reason is not creation (as in Exodus 20:11) but redemption: "You shall remember that you were a slave in the land of Egypt, and the LORD your God brought you out." The Sabbath is not only because God rested after creation—but because God rescued you from slavery. You rest—because you're no longer a slave.

**The People's Fear (vv. 22-33)**

> "The LORD spoke these words to all your assembly at the mountain out of the midst of the fire...Then you said, 'Behold, the LORD our God has shown us his glory...let us not die...If we hear the voice of the LORD our God any more, we shall die.'"

Brothers and sisters, the people's fear of hearing God's voice directly—so they asked Moses to mediate. This is the origin of the OT mediation system—humans cannot directly face the holy God—they need a mediator. This points to Christ—"For there is one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus;" (1 Tim 2:5).

Brothers and sisters, Deuteronomy 5 teaches: the Ten Commandments are the covenant's core—protecting God-human and human-human relationships. The Sabbath marks redemption, not just creation. We need a mediator—because we cannot directly face the holy God.

## 历史背景（中文）

申命记第五章的十诫与出埃及记20:1-17的十诫有细微但重要的差异。最显著的是安息日诫命的理由——出埃及记20:11以创造为根据（"因为六日之内耶和华造天地海……第七日便安息"），而申命记5:15以救赎为根据（"你也要记念你在埃及地作过奴仆"）。这两个不同的理由并不矛盾——而是互补——安息日同时纪念创造和救赎。另一个差异是第21节将"妻子"从"房屋"中分离出来单独列为"不可贪恋"的对象——出埃及记版本将两者合在一起。犹太传统将十诫视为整个律法的总纲——613条诫命都可以追溯到十条诫命中的某一条。

## Historical Context (English)

Deuteronomy 5's Ten Commandments have subtle but important differences from Exodus 20:1-17. Most notably, the Sabbath command's rationale—Exodus 20:11 bases it on creation ("in six days the LORD made heaven and earth...and rested the seventh day"), while Deuteronomy 5:15 bases it on redemption ("you shall remember that you were a slave in the land of Egypt"). These two rationales don't contradict—they complement—the Sabbath simultaneously commemorates creation and redemption. Another difference: verse 21 separates "wife" from "house" as a distinct "do not covet" object—Exodus combines them. Jewish tradition views the Ten Commandments as the entire law's summary—all 613 commandments can be traced to one of the Ten.

## 经文结构（中文）

**约的重申（1-5节）**——召集以色列人→何烈山立约→不是与列祖立的→面对面说话。
**十条诫命（6-21节）**——第一条:不可有别神→第二条:不可雕像→第三条:不可妄称→第四条:守安息日(救赎理由)→第五条:孝敬父母→第六至十条:不杀不淫不偷不作假见证不贪。
**百姓的恐惧和摩西的中保（22-33节）**——火中大声晓谕→百姓恐惧求摩西代听→耶和华嘉许→愿他们常存敬畏的心→谨守遵行使你得福。

## Structure (English)

**Covenant Restatement (vv. 1-5)**—Assembled Israel → Horeb covenant → not with fathers but with us → face to face.
**Ten Commandments (vv. 6-21)**—1st: no other gods → 2nd: no images → 3rd: no vain use of name → 4th: Sabbath (redemption rationale) → 5th: honor parents → 6th-10th: no murder, adultery, stealing, false witness, coveting.
**People's Fear and Moses' Mediation (vv. 22-33)**—Loud voice from fire → people feared, asked Moses to mediate → LORD approved → wish they always had such hearts → observe carefully to prosper.

## 神学要点（中文）

**十诫——神的道德律**
改革宗传统教导十诫是"道德律"——不同于礼仪律和民事律——道德律是永恒有效的。韦敏斯德信条第19章：此道德律永远约束一切人。十诫不因基督的来到而废去——而是在基督里得到了更深的诠释（太5:17-48）。

**安息日的双重根基——创造与救赎**
改革宗传统教导安息日（主日）同时纪念创造和救赎。韦敏斯德信条第21章论安息日。出埃及记版以创造为根基——申命记版以救赎为根基——两者结合给出了安息日的完整神学：我们休息因为神创造后休息（创造的秩序），也因为我们从奴役中被释放（救赎的恩典）。

**中保的必要——人无法直接面对神**
改革宗传统教导基督作为唯一中保的必要性。韦敏斯德信条第8章论中保基督。百姓害怕直接听神的声音——这暴露了罪人面对圣洁之神的根本困境。这个困境需要中保来解决——旧约的摩西是中保的影子，基督是中保的实体。

## Theology (English)

**The Ten Commandments—God's Moral Law**
The Reformed tradition teaches the Ten Commandments are "moral law"—distinct from ceremonial and civil law—moral law is eternally binding. Westminster Confession chapter 19: This moral law binds all people forever. The Decalogue wasn't abolished by Christ's coming—but received deeper interpretation in Christ (Matt 5:17-48).

**Sabbath's Dual Foundation—Creation and Redemption**
The Reformed tradition teaches the Sabbath (Lord's Day) commemorates both creation and redemption. Westminster Confession chapter 21. Exodus bases it on creation; Deuteronomy on redemption—together providing complete Sabbath theology.

**The Mediator's Necessity—Humans Cannot Directly Face God**
The Reformed tradition teaches the necessity of Christ as sole mediator. Westminster Confession chapter 8. The people feared hearing God's voice directly—exposing sinners' fundamental predicament before the holy God. Moses was the mediator's shadow; Christ is the mediator's substance.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**摩西作中保——基督是唯一中保**——提摩太前书2:5"因为只有一位神，在神和人中间只有一位中保，乃是降世为人的基督耶稣。"以色列人请求摩西做中保——因为他们不能直接面对神。基督是摩西所预表的最终中保——祂不只传达神的话——祂自己就是神的话（约1:1）。

**安息日的救赎根基——基督是我们的安息**——希伯来书4:9-10"这样看来，必另有一安息日的安息为神的子民存留。因为那进入安息的，乃是歇了自己的工。"申命记版安息日的救赎根据（"你在埃及作过奴仆"）在基督里得到了终极应验——基督把我们从罪的奴役中释放出来，进入真正的安息。

**十诫在基督里的成全**——马太福音5:17"莫想我来要废掉律法和先知；我来不是要废掉，乃是要成全。"基督没有废除十诫——而是成全了它——不只是外在的遵守，而是内心的顺服。"不可杀人"变成了"不可怒"（太5:22）；"不可奸淫"变成了"不可动淫念"（太5:28）。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Moses as Mediator—Christ Is the Only Mediator**—1 Timothy 2:5: "For there is one God, and there is one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus." Israelites requested Moses as mediator—unable to face God directly. Christ is the ultimate mediator Moses foreshadowed—He doesn't just convey God's word—He is God's Word (John 1:1).

**Sabbath's Redemption Foundation—Christ Is Our Rest**—Hebrews 4:9-10: "So then, there remains a Sabbath rest for the people of God. For whoever has entered God's rest has also rested from his works." Deuteronomy's Sabbath rationale ("you were a slave in Egypt") finds ultimate fulfillment in Christ—He freed us from sin's slavery into true rest.

**The Commandments Fulfilled in Christ**—Matthew 5:17: "Do not think that I have come to abolish the Law...I have not come to abolish them but to fulfill them." Christ didn't abolish the Ten Commandments—He fulfilled them—not just outward compliance but inward obedience. "Do not murder" becomes "But I say unto you, That whosoever is angry with his brother without a cause shall be in danger of the judgment: and whosoever shall say to his brother, Raca, shall be in danger of the council: but whosoever shall say, Thou fool, shall be in danger of hell fire." (Matt 5:22); "do not commit adultery" becomes "But I say unto you, That whosoever looketh on a woman to lust after her hath committed adultery with her already in his heart." (Matt 5:28).
