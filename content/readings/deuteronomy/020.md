## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第二十章是关于战争的规矩——以色列人怎样出去打仗。这一章在今天读来令人不安——涉及战争和杀戮。但在神的救赎历史的框架内理解，它揭示了神的公义、怜悯和对生命的珍视。

**不要怕（第1-4节）**

> "你出去与仇敌争战的时候，看见马匹、车辆并有比你多的人民，不要怕他们，因为领你出埃及地的耶和华你的神与你同在。"

弟兄姐妹，战前的第一句话不是战术——是神学："不要怕，因为神与你同在。"弟兄姐妹，多少雄壮的军队以比你多的马匹和车辆出战——但如果神不与你同在——你必失败。多少弱小的军队以少数人出战——但如果神与你同在——你必得胜。弟兄姐妹，争战的胜负不取决于兵力对比——而取决于神是否与你同在。

弟兄姐妹，"领你出埃及地的耶和华你的神与你同在"——弟兄姐妹，祭司在战前对百姓说的话也是这个：记念出埃及。弟兄姐妹，当你面对巨大的挑战时——最有力量的不是壮胆的话——而是"记念"——记念神过去已经为你做了什么。

**可以回家的人（第5-9节）**

弟兄姐妹，这一段令人意外——神让很多人在战前可以回家：建了新房子但还没有点用的、种了葡萄园还没有吃果子的、已经聘了妻还没有迎娶的，以及"心中胆怯的"。

弟兄姐妹，"心中胆怯的"——弟兄姐妹，神允许胆怯的人回家！弟兄姐妹，为什么？"恐怕他使弟兄的心消化，和他一样"——弟兄姐妹，一个心中恐惧的人会传染整个军队的士气。弟兄姐妹，这对那些留下来打仗的人是公平的——他们有权利和甘心的人一起作战——不被迫和胆怯者一同作战。

弟兄姐妹，但更深的信息是：神不需要靠人数来打胜仗——"因为领你出埃及的神与你同在"。弟兄姐妹，少数精兵的信心比多数懦夫的胆怯更有价值。弟兄姐妹，士师记7章基甸的三百人打败十三万五千米甸人——就是这个原则的完美展示。

**攻城的规矩（第10-18节）**

> "你临近一座城攻打它，要先向城里的人宣告和睦的话。若城里的人回答说'愿意和睦'，向你开门，城里所有的人都要给你做苦工，服侍你。若不肯与你和好，要与你打仗，你就要围困那城。"

弟兄姐妹，"先向城里宣告和睦"——弟兄姐妹，即使在战争中——神的律法首先要求和平的机会。弟兄姐妹，进攻前必须先谈判——给对方机会投降。这显示了神对和平的偏爱——战争是最后的手段，不是第一选择。

弟兄姐妹，第16-18节的规定更严格——对迦南七族"必不能存留任何有气息的"——弟兄姐妹，这是圣经中最难理解的命令之一。弟兄姐妹，在神的历史框架中——这是神对迦南人罪恶达到顶点时的审判（创15:16"亚摩利人的罪孽还没有满盈"）——同时也是保护以色列人不被迦南宗教腐化。

**不可砍果树（第19-20节）**

> "你若围困一座城，打仗许多日子，就不可举斧砍坏那里的树木，因为你可以吃那树上的果子，不可砍伐，难道树木是人使你来受围困吗？"

弟兄姐妹，这是古代战争规范中最独特的一条——不可砍果树！弟兄姐妹，当时的惯例是"焦土政策"——把一切都摧毁。但神说：不可砍结果实的树——因为树是无辜的——树不是你的敌人。弟兄姐妹，这个命令显示了神对创造物的珍视——即使在战争中——生命（树的生产力）都是要被保护的。弟兄姐妹，这是圣经环境伦理的早期表达——创造物有其内在的价值——不可随意毁坏。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 20 regulates warfare—how Israel goes to battle. Disturbing to modern readers—involving war and killing. But understood within God's redemptive history, it reveals His justice, mercy, and valuing of life.

**Don't Fear (vv. 1-4)**

> "When you go out to war against your enemies and see horses and chariots and an army larger than your own, you shall not be afraid of them, for the LORD your God is with you, who brought you up out of the land of Egypt."

The battle's first word isn't tactics—it's theology: "Don't fear, for God is with you." Many mighty armies with more horses and chariots fail if God isn't with them. Many small armies with few fighters win if God is with them. Victory doesn't depend on force comparison—but on whether God is with you. "Remember the Exodus"—when facing huge challenges, the most powerful thing isn't encouraging words—but "remembering" what God has already done.

**Who May Go Home (vv. 5-9)**

Before battle, God allows many to go home: those with new houses not yet dedicated, new vineyards not yet enjoyed, betrothed but not yet married—and "whoever is fearful and fainthearted."

God allows the fearful to go home! Why? "Lest his brothers' heart melt as his heart." One fearful person infects an entire army's morale. More deeply: God doesn't need numbers to win. Gideon's 300 defeating 135,000 Midianites (Judg 7) is the perfect illustration.

**Rules of Siege (vv. 10-18)**

"When you draw near to a city to fight against it, offer terms of peace to it first." Even in war—peace must first be offered. Attack only after negotiation fails. God prefers peace—war is the last resort.

**Don't Cut Fruit Trees (vv. 19-20)**

"When you besiege a city...you shall not destroy its trees by wielding an ax against them...for the tree of the field is not a man that it should be besieged by you." The most unique ancient warfare rule—don't cut fruit trees! The period's norm was "scorched earth." But God says: don't cut productive trees—trees are innocent—not your enemy. This shows God's valuing of creation—even in war—life's productivity must be protected. An early expression of biblical environmental ethics.

## 历史背景（中文）

申命记第二十章的战争规范在古代近东背景下是独特的。"宣告和睦"的规定——给敌城和平投降的机会——在其他古代法典中罕见。"心中胆怯的可以回家"——这个规定在古代战争文化中也是独特的——通常军队会用各种强制手段迫使士兵战斗。"不可砍果树"——与亚述和巴比伦的"焦土政策"形成鲜明对比——亚述人以系统性地摧毁被占领地区的农业基础著称。迦南七族的全灭命令（herem，意为"圣战禁令"或"完全毁灭"）——在学术界和神学界有广泛讨论——改革宗传统通常将其理解为神对迦南人罪孽达到顶点的审判，同时也是圣战的独特语境——不适用于一般战争。

## Historical Context (English)

Deuteronomy 20's warfare regulations are unique in the ancient Near East context. "Offer peace terms"—giving enemy cities peaceful surrender options—rare in other ancient codes. "Fearful may go home"—unique in ancient military culture—armies typically used compulsion to force soldiers to fight. "Don't cut fruit trees"—contrasts sharply with Assyrian and Babylonian "scorched earth" policies—Assyrians were known for systematically destroying captured areas' agricultural infrastructure. The Canaanite herem (complete destruction) command has generated extensive academic and theological discussion—Reformed tradition generally understands it as God's judgment on Canaanite sin's peak, within the unique holy war context—not applicable to general warfare.

## 经文结构（中文）

**战前的鼓励（1-4节）**——看见马匹车辆多人不要怕→领你出埃及的神与你同在→祭司告知百姓。
**可以回家的人（5-9节）**——建新房未点用者→种葡萄园未吃果者→已聘妻未迎娶者→心中胆怯者→免得使人心消化。
**攻城规矩（10-18节）**——先宣告和睦→愿和睦就服役→不愿和睦就围困→杀所有男丁→妇女儿童牲畜为掠物→迦南七族必须全灭→免得他们使以色列犯罪。
**不可砍果树（19-20节）**——不可举斧砍果树→可以吃果实→可以砍不结果实的用来筑壁垒。

## Structure (English)

**Pre-battle Encouragement (vv. 1-4)**—Don't fear horses/chariots/many people → God who brought you from Egypt is with you → priests address the people.
**Who May Go Home (vv. 5-9)**—New house not dedicated → new vineyard not enjoyed → betrothed not yet married → fearful and fainthearted → lest he infect others.
**Rules of Siege (vv. 10-18)**—First offer peace → if peaceful: compulsory labor → if not: besiege and defeat → kill all males → women/children/livestock as plunder → Canaanite seven nations: complete destruction → lest they teach abominations.
**No Cutting Fruit Trees (vv. 19-20)**—Don't use ax on fruit trees → can eat their fruit → non-fruit trees may be cut for siege works.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"神与你同在"——圣战神学**
改革宗传统教导神是历史的主宰。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理。"圣战"（herem）不是人类的侵略——而是神通过以色列执行对罪恶民族的审判。改革宗传统警告不可把圣战的范畴扩展到一般战争——圣战是救赎历史中特定时期的特殊命令，不是普遍适用的战争原则。

**少数的精兵——神不靠人数取胜**
改革宗传统教导神不需要人的力量来成就祂的旨意。韦敏斯德信条第3章。"心中胆怯的可以回家"——神的战争靠的是神自己的能力——不是军队的数量。这个原则在整个救赎历史中一再重复：基甸三百人、大卫一人对哥利亚、一个十字架对抗整个世界的罪。

**创造物的保护——圣经环境伦理**
改革宗传统教导人对创造物有管理的责任。韦敏斯德信条第4章论创造。"不可砍果树"是圣经环境伦理的早期表达——创造物不是人类任意处置的工具——它有神赋予的价值。这与现代基督徒对环境保护的责任相连。

## Theology (English)

**"God Is With You"—Holy War Theology**
The Reformed tradition teaches God is history's sovereign. Westminster Confession chapter 5. "Holy war" (herem) isn't human aggression—but God executing judgment through Israel on sinful nations. Reformed tradition warns against extending holy war scope to general warfare.

**Few Soldiers—God Doesn't Win by Numbers**
The Reformed tradition teaches God doesn't need human strength. Westminster Confession chapter 3.: Fearful may go home—God's war relies on His own power. Repeated throughout redemptive history: Gideon's 300, David vs. Goliath, one cross against all the world's sin.

**Protection of Creation—Biblical Environmental Ethics**
The Reformed tradition teaches humans have stewardship responsibility for creation. Westminster Confession chapter 4.: Don't cut fruit trees—early expression of biblical environmental ethics—creation isn't humanity's tool to dispose of arbitrarily.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"不要怕因为神与你同在"——基督是以马内利**——马太福音1:23"'以马内利'翻出来就是'神与我们同在'。"申命记说战前"神与你同在"——基督的名字就是"神与我们同在"。弟兄姐妹，你面对的每一个"战场"——疾病、软弱、试探——你可以说"以马内利"——神在这里。

**基甸三百人与教会——神靠少数人成就大事**——哥林多前书1:27-28"神拣选了世上愚拙的……软弱的……卑贱的和被人厌恶的，以及那无有的，为要废掉那有的。""心中胆怯的可以回家"——在神国的争战中，力量不在数量——而在神。教会在历史上多次以弱胜强——不是靠政治权力——而是靠神同在的力量。

**先宣告和睦——基督是和平之君**——以赛亚书9:6"他名称为……和平的君。"申命记要求攻城前先宣告和睦——基督在救赎中也是先宣告和平——"他来传和平的福音给你们"（弗2:17）。神的战争最终目的是和平——不是消灭——而是和好。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Don't Fear for God Is With You"—Christ Is Immanuel**—Matthew 1:23: "'Immanuel' (which means, God with us)." Deuteronomy says in battle "God is with you"—Christ's name is "God with us." In every "battlefield" you face—illness, weakness, temptation—you can say "Immanuel"—God is here.

**Gideon's 300 and the Church—God Does Great Things with Few**—1 Corinthians 1:27-28: "God chose what is foolish...weak...low and despised...to bring to nothing things that are." "Fearful may go home"—in God's kingdom warfare, strength isn't in numbers—but in God. The church has repeatedly overcome by weakness—not political power—but the power of God's presence.

**First Offer Peace—Christ Is the Prince of Peace**—Isaiah 9:6: "His name shall be called...Prince of Peace." Deuteronomy requires offering peace before attacking—Christ in redemption first proclaimed peace—"And came and preached peace to you which were afar off, and to them that were nigh." (Eph 2:17). God's war's ultimate goal is peace—not destruction—but reconciliation.
