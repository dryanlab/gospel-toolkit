## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第十章从重新刻版的恩典开始——然后引向全书中最美的经文之一："以色列啊，现在耶和华你神向你所要的是什么呢？"

**重新刻版——恩典的重新开始（第1-5节）**

> "那时耶和华吩咐我说：你要凿出两块石版，和先前的一样，上山到我这里来，又要做一木柜。你先前摔碎的那版，其上的字我要写在这版上。"

弟兄姐妹，"和先前的一样"——弟兄姐妹，第一次的石版是神亲手凿、亲手写的。这一次——摩西凿，神来写。弟兄姐妹，第一套石版摔碎了——代表着以色列人破坏了约。但神没有说"约废了，完了"——祂说"再凿一套，我再写一遍"。弟兄姐妹，这就是恩典——你打碎了——神重新来过。你失败了——神给你第二次机会。弟兄姐妹，恩典不是说"没关系你打碎了也无所谓"——恩典是说"你打碎了你要承受后果——但我仍然愿意重新开始"。

弟兄姐妹，石版内容是一样的——但过程不同了。第一次是全部出于神——第二次摩西参与了（他自己凿版）。弟兄姐妹，在恩典的重新开始中——神让你参与——不是因为祂需要你——而是因为参与的过程本身就是恢复的一部分。

**神向你所要的是什么（第12-13节）**

> "以色列啊，现在耶和华你神向你所要的是什么呢？只要你敬畏耶和华你的神，遵行他的道，爱他，尽心尽性侍奉他，遵守他的诫命律例，就是我今日所吩咐你的，为要叫你得福。"

弟兄姐妹，"向你所要的是什么"——弟兄姐妹，这个问题把信仰浓缩到了本质。在律法的复杂性中——在613条诫命中——核心是什么？弟兄姐妹，摩西给了五个动词：敬畏、遵行、爱、侍奉、遵守。弟兄姐妹，注意顺序——先"敬畏"——然后"遵行"——然后"爱"——然后"侍奉"——最后"遵守"。弟兄姐妹，敬畏是开始——因为"敬畏耶和华是智慧的开端"（箴9:10）。没有敬畏就没有真正的遵行——没有遵行就不会真正地爱——没有爱就不会甘心侍奉——没有侍奉就不会持久遵守。

弟兄姐妹，"为要叫你得福"——弟兄姐妹，神所要的不是为了祂自己——祂什么都不缺。祂所要的是"为了叫你得福"。弟兄姐妹，你遵守诫命不是在给神好处——而是在给自己好处。神的诫命就像使用说明书——你按照说明书来——机器运转良好——你不按——机器坏了。弟兄姐妹，你得福还是受损——取决于你是否按照创造者的说明书生活。

**万神之神万主之主（第14-22节）**

> "看哪，天和天上的天，地和地上所有的，都属耶和华你的神。耶和华但喜悦你的列祖，爱他们，从万民中拣选他们的后裔就是你们。"

弟兄姐妹，"天和天上的天都属耶和华"——弟兄姐妹，天已经无限大了——但还有"天上的天"——弟兄姐妹，这是旧约对神超越性最壮丽的描述之一。一切都属于祂——但祂"喜悦你的列祖爱他们"——弟兄姐妹，这是何等的降卑！拥有天和天上的天的那位——喜悦你、爱你。

> "因为耶和华你们的神，他是万神之神，万主之主，至大的神，大有能力，大而可畏，不以貌取人，也不受贿赂。他为孤儿寡妇伸冤，又怜爱寄居的，赐给他衣食。所以你们要怜爱寄居的，因为你们在埃及地也作过寄居的。"

弟兄姐妹，"万神之神万主之主"——紧接着就是"为孤儿寡妇伸冤"。弟兄姐妹，全宇宙最大的那位——祂关心的是谁？不是王宫里的君王——而是"孤儿""寡妇""寄居的"。弟兄姐妹，这是以色列律法中社会公义的神学根基——我们为什么要照顾弱势群体？因为"万神之神"关心他们。弟兄姐妹，如果宇宙的主宰关心孤儿寡妇——你有什么理由不关心？

"你们要怜爱寄居的，因为你们在埃及地也作过寄居的"——弟兄姐妹，你要怜悯外来者——因为你自己曾经也是外来者。你要体恤软弱的——因为你自己也曾经软弱过。弟兄姐妹，怜悯不是居高临下的施舍——而是同理心——"我也曾经在那里"。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 10 begins with the grace of re-carved tablets—then leads to one of the book's most beautiful verses: "What does the LORD your God require of you?"

**Re-Carved Tablets—Grace's Fresh Start (vv. 1-5)**

> "At that time the LORD said to me, 'Cut for yourself two tablets of stone like the first...and I will write on the tablets the words that were on the first tablets that you broke.'"

Brothers and sisters, "like the first"—the first tablets were carved and written by God Himself. This time—Moses carves, God writes. The first set was shattered—representing Israel's broken covenant. But God didn't say "covenant's over, done"—He said "cut another set, I'll write again." This is grace—you broke it—God starts over. Grace doesn't mean "it doesn't matter that you broke it"—grace means "you broke it, there are consequences—but I'm still willing to start again."

**What Does God Require? (vv. 12-13)**

> "And now, Israel, what does the LORD your God require of you, but to fear the LORD your God, to walk in all his ways, to love him, to serve the LORD your God with all your heart and with all your soul, and to keep the commandments?"

Brothers and sisters, "what does he require"—this question distills faith to its essence. Among the complexity of 613 commandments—what's the core? Moses gives five verbs: fear, walk, love, serve, keep. Notice the order—first "fear"—then "walk"—then "love"—then "serve"—finally "keep." Fear is the beginning—"The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom: and the knowledge of the holy is understanding." (Prov 9:10).

"For your good"—what God requires isn't for His benefit—He lacks nothing. It's "for your good." Obeying commandments isn't doing God a favor—it's doing yourself a favor. God's commands are like an owner's manual—follow the manual—the machine runs well.

**God of Gods, Lord of Lords (vv. 14-22)**

> "Behold, to the LORD your God belong heaven and the heaven of heavens, the earth with all that is in it. Yet the LORD set his heart in love on your fathers and chose their offspring after you."

> "He is God of gods and Lord of lords, the great, the mighty, and the awesome God, who is not partial and takes no bribe. He executes justice for the fatherless and the widow, and loves the sojourner, giving him food and clothing. Love the sojourner, therefore, for you were sojourners in the land of Egypt."

Brothers and sisters, "God of gods, Lord of lords"—immediately followed by "executes justice for fatherless and widow." The universe's greatest One—who does He care about? Not palace kings—but "fatherless," "widow," "sojourner." This is the theological foundation for social justice in Israel's law. If the universe's Sovereign cares for orphans and widows—what excuse do you have not to?

"Love the sojourner, for you were sojourners in Egypt"—show mercy to outsiders—because you were once an outsider. Mercy isn't top-down charity—it's empathy—"I was once there too."

## 历史背景（中文）

申命记10:12-13与弥迦书6:8形成了旧约中对"神向人所要的是什么"最著名的两个回答。弥迦版本："世人哪，耶和华已指示你何为善。他向你所要的是什么呢？只要你行公义，好怜悯，存谦卑的心，与你的神同行。"两者互补——申命记强调敬畏和爱神——弥迦强调公义和怜悯。犹太传统将申10:12-13视为律法的总纲性经文之一。"万神之神万主之主"（第17节）的用语后来在但以理书2:47和启示录17:14, 19:16中被呼应。"为孤儿寡妇伸冤怜爱寄居的"——在古代近东社会中，孤儿、寡妇和寄居者是最脆弱的社会群体——以色列律法中有大量保护他们的条例。

## Historical Context (English)

Deuteronomy 10:12-13 and Micah 6:8 form the OT's two most famous answers to "what does God require?" Micah's version: "What does the LORD require of you but to do justice, and to love kindness, and to walk humbly with your God?" Both complement each other—Deuteronomy emphasizes fearing and loving God—Micah emphasizes justice and mercy. Jewish tradition views Deut 10:12-13 as one of the law's summary texts. "God of gods, Lord of lords" (v. 17) is echoed in Daniel 2:47 and Revelation 17:14, 19:16. "Executes justice for fatherless and widow, loves the sojourner"—orphans, widows, and sojourners were ancient Near Eastern society's most vulnerable—Israel's law contains extensive protections for them.

## Structure (English)

**Re-Carved Tablets (vv. 1-5)**—Cut two tablets → go up mountain → God writes again → placed in ark.
**Levites' Role (vv. 6-9)**—Aaron died at Moserah → Eleazar succeeded → Levites set apart → carry ark, stand before LORD.
**God's Requirements (vv. 10-13)**—Moses again forty days → God unwilling to destroy → what does He require → fear/walk/love/serve/keep → for your good.
**God of Gods (vv. 14-19)**—Heaven of heavens belong to LORD → loved the fathers → chose offspring → circumcise hearts → God of gods → impartial → justice for orphan/widow → loves sojourner.
**Reverence and Praise (vv. 20-22)**—Fear/serve/hold fast → He is your praise → seventy went to Egypt, now like stars.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"神向你所要的"——律法的总纲**
改革宗传统教导律法可以总结为爱神和爱人。韦敏斯德小要理问答第42-44问将十诫总结为"尽心尽性尽意爱主你的神"和"爱人如己"。申命记10:12-13给出了旧约版本的"总纲"——五个动词覆盖了信仰生活的全部：敬畏（态度）、遵行（行为）、爱（情感）、侍奉（奉献）、遵守（持续）。

**"不以貌取人也不受贿赂"——神的公义**
改革宗传统教导神的公义是绝对公平的。韦敏斯德信条第2章论神的属性。"不以貌取人"——神不看你的社会地位、种族、财富——祂看的是你的心。这是旧约社会公义的神学根基——如果神不以貌取人，祂的子民也不可以以貌取人（雅2:1-9）。

**"将心里的污秽除掉"——心的割礼**
改革宗传统教导真正的割礼是心灵的。韦敏斯德信条第7章论与人所立的约。"将心里的污秽除掉"（直译"割你的心"）预示了新约中的重生——外在的割礼不够——需要内在的更新。罗马书2:29"真割礼也是心里的，在乎灵不在乎仪文。"

## Theology (English)

**"What God Requires"—The Law's Summary**
The Reformed tradition teaches the law summarizes as loving God and neighbor. WSC Q42-44. Deuteronomy 10:12-13 gives the OT "summary"—five verbs covering all of faith life: fear (attitude), walk (behavior), love (emotion), serve (dedication), keep (persistence).

**"Not Partial, Takes No Bribe"—God's Justice**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's justice is absolutely fair. Westminster Confession chapter 2.: Not partial—God doesn't consider social status, race, wealth—He sees the heart. This is OT social justice's theological foundation (James 2:1-9).

**"Circumcise Your Heart"—Heart's Circumcision**
The Reformed tradition teaches true circumcision is spiritual. Westminster Confession chapter 7.: Circumcise your heart foreshadows NT regeneration—external circumcision insufficient—internal renewal needed. Romans 2:29.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**重新刻版——基督里的新约**——耶利米书31:33"我要将我的律法放在他们里面，写在他们心上。"摔碎的石版→重新刻的石版，这个过程预示了旧约→新约的转变。但新约更彻底——不是写在石版上——而是写在心上。基督带来的不只是"再写一遍"——而是全新的写法——从外在到内在。

**"将心里的污秽除掉"——基督的灵使我们心灵受割礼**——歌罗西书2:11"你们在他里面也受了不是人手所行的割礼，乃是基督使你们脱去肉体情欲的割礼。"摩西说"割你的心"——但人无法自己割自己的心——这需要基督——"不是人手所行的割礼"。

**"万神之神为孤儿寡妇伸冤"——基督怜悯弱者**——马太福音25:40"这些事你们既做在我这弟兄中一个最小的身上，就是做在我身上了。"万神之神关心孤儿寡妇——基督认同"最小的弟兄"——照顾弱者就是服侍基督本人。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Re-Carved Tablets—The New Covenant in Christ**—Jeremiah 31:33: "I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts." Broken tablets → re-carved tablets foreshadows old covenant → new covenant. But the new covenant is more radical—not on stone but on hearts. Christ brings not just "writing again"—but an entirely new method—from external to internal.

**"Circumcise Your Heart"—Christ's Spirit Circumcises Our Hearts**—Colossians 2:11: "In him also you were circumcised with a circumcision made without hands, by putting off the body of the flesh, by the circumcision of Christ." Moses says "circumcise your heart"—but humans can't circumcise their own hearts—this requires Christ—"circumcision made without hands."

**"God of Gods Executes Justice for Orphans"—Christ's Compassion for the Weak**—Matthew 25:40: "As you did it to one of the least of these my brothers, you did it to me." God of gods cares for orphans and widows—Christ identifies with "the least"—caring for the weak is serving Christ Himself.
