## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第三章继续回顾约旦河东的征服——击败巴珊王噩，分配约旦河东的土地，以及我个人被拒绝进入应许之地的痛苦经历。

**不要怕他（第1-7节）**

> "以后，我们转回，向巴珊去。巴珊王噩和他的众民都出来，在以得来与我们交战。耶和华对我说：'不要怕他！因我已将他和他的众民并他的地都交在你手中。'"

弟兄姐妹，"不要怕他"——巴珊王噩是令人恐惧的对手。第11节告诉我们他的铁床长九肘、宽四肘——按现代换算约四米长、两米宽！弟兄姐妹，噩是利乏音人的后裔——就是巨人族。在人的眼里，这是不可能赢的仗。但神说："不要怕他！因我已将他交在你手中。"

弟兄姐妹，你面对的"巨人"是什么？你的疾病？你的债务？你的困境？弟兄姐妹，在你看来不可能战胜的"巨人"——神说"不要怕"。因为争战不在于你的力量——而在于神是否"已将他交在你手中"。弟兄姐妹，当神说"我已交给你"——巨人的身高就不重要了。

"我们杀了他们，没有留下一个。那时我们夺了他所有的城，共有六十座。"弟兄姐妹，六十座城——完全的胜利。弟兄姐妹，当神把敌人交在你手中的时候——不是部分的胜利——而是"没有留下一个"的完全胜利。

**约旦河东的分配（第12-20节）**

> "那时，我们得了这地。从亚嫩谷边的亚罗珥起，我将基列山地的一半并其中的城邑，都给了流便支派和迦得支派。其余的基列地和巴珊全地就是噩王的国，我给了玛拿西半支派。"

弟兄姐妹，流便、迦得和玛拿西半支派选择了约旦河东的土地——因为那里草地丰美，适合放牧。摩西允许了——但有一个条件："你们要带着兵器在你们弟兄以色列人前面过去……等到耶和华使你们的弟兄得享平安……然后你们才可以回到我所赐给你们为业之地。"

弟兄姐妹，你可以在自己先得到安顿之后就忘记弟兄——但摩西说不行。你得先帮助弟兄们也得到他们的份——然后才能回来享受你的。弟兄姐妹，基督徒的生活不是"各扫自家门前雪"——而是"先帮助弟兄，再享受自己的"。

**摩西被拒（第23-29节）**

> "那时，我恳求耶和华说：'主耶和华啊，你已将你的大力大能显给仆人看。在天上，在地下，有什么神能像你行事、像你有大能的作为呢？求你容我过去，看约旦河那边的美地。'"

弟兄姐妹，这是申命记中最动人的段落之一——摩西恳求神让他进入应许之地。弟兄姐妹，想想——他在旷野带领以色列人四十年——受了多少委屈、忍了多少抱怨——现在到了门口——他想进去看看。弟兄姐妹，摩西的请求是真诚的、是合情理的——他只想"过去看看那美地"。

> "但耶和华因你们的缘故向我发怒，不应允我。耶和华对我说：'罢了！你不要向我再提这事。你且上毗斯迦山顶去，向东、西、南、北举目观望，因为你必不能过这约旦河。'"

弟兄姐妹，"罢了"——多么沉重的一个词。弟兄姐妹，神说"罢了"——不是不爱摩西——而是摩西在加低斯击打磐石的时候没有在百姓面前尊神为圣（民20:12）。弟兄姐妹，后果是真实的——即使是摩西这样伟大的仆人——也必须承担行为的后果。

弟兄姐妹，但神给了摩西一个安慰——"你且上毗斯迦山顶去举目观望"——你不能进去，但你可以看到。弟兄姐妹，有些事情你在今生不会亲身经历——但神让你远远地看到那美地。弟兄姐妹，摩西最终在变像山上见到了耶稣（太17:3）——他不只是看到了应许之地——他见到了应许之地的主人。

"你要嘱咐约书亚，勉励他，使他胆壮；因为他必在这百姓前面过去，使他们承受你所要观看之地。"弟兄姐妹，即使不能自己进去——摩西要"嘱咐约书亚、勉励他"。弟兄姐妹，你完不成的使命——交给下一代。你的失败不是终点——因为神已经预备了继承人。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 3 continues reviewing the Transjordan conquests—defeating Og king of Bashan, distributing Transjordan lands, and my personal painful experience of being denied entry into the Promised Land.

**Do Not Fear Him (vv. 1-7)**

> "Then we turned and went up the way to Bashan. And Og the king of Bashan came out against us...But the LORD said to me, 'Do not fear him, for I have given him and all his people and his land into your hand.'"

Brothers and sisters, "do not fear him"—Og was a terrifying adversary. Verse 11 tells us his iron bed was nine cubits long and four cubits wide—about four meters by two meters! Og was a descendant of the Rephaim—giants. Humanly, this battle was impossible to win. But God says: "Do not fear him, for I have given him into your hand."

What "giants" do you face? Your illness? Debt? Predicament? God says "do not fear." Because the battle doesn't depend on your strength—but on whether God has "given him into your hand." When God says "I have given"—the giant's height no longer matters.

**Moses Denied Entry (vv. 23-29)**

> "And I pleaded with the LORD...Please let me go over and see the good land beyond the Jordan.' But the LORD was angry with me because of you and would not listen to me. And the LORD said to me, 'Enough from you; do not speak to me of this matter again. Go up to the top of Pisgah and lift up your eyes...for you shall not go over this Jordan.'"

Brothers and sisters, "enough"—what a heavy word. God saying "enough" wasn't from lack of love—but because Moses at Kadesh struck the rock without honoring God before the people (Num 20:12). Consequences are real—even for a servant as great as Moses.

But God gave Moses comfort—"go up to Pisgah's top and look." You can't enter, but you can see. Some things you won't personally experience in this life—but God lets you see that good land from afar. Moses ultimately met Jesus on the Mount of Transfiguration (Matt 17:3)—he didn't just see the Promised Land—he met its Owner.

"But charge Joshua, and encourage and strengthen him, for he shall go over at the head of this people." Even unable to enter himself—Moses must encourage Joshua. Your unfinished mission—pass it to the next generation. Your failure isn't the end—God has prepared a successor.

## 历史背景（中文）

巴珊王噩是利乏音人（Rephaim）的最后遗裔之一——利乏音人是旧约中提到的巨人族（创14:5, 15:20）。第11节的"铁床"——有学者认为这可能是一个玄武岩石棺（巴珊地区以玄武岩著称），而不是睡觉用的床。九肘约4.1米——无论噩本人是否真有这么高，这个记录强调了他在当时人眼中的恐怖形象。巴珊地区在旧约中以肥美著称（参诗22:12"巴珊大力的公牛"；摩4:1"巴珊的母牛"），现代的戈兰高地就是古代巴珊的一部分。摩西被拒进入迦南的原因在民数记20:12——他在米利巴击打磐石两次，没有在百姓面前"尊耶和华为圣"。

## Historical Context (English)

Og king of Bashan was one of the last survivors of the Rephaim—a giant race mentioned in the OT (Gen 14:5, 15:20). Verse 11's "iron bed"—some scholars suggest this may be a basalt sarcophagus (Bashan was known for basalt) rather than a sleeping bed. Nine cubits is about 4.1 meters—whether Og was truly that tall, the record emphasizes his terrifying image. Bashan was known in the OT for its fertility (Ps 22:12 "strong bulls of Bashan"; Amos 4:1 "cows of Bashan"). The modern Golan Heights is part of ancient Bashan. Moses was denied entry to Canaan because at Meribah he struck the rock twice, failing to "uphold God as holy" before the people (Num 20:12).

## 经文结构（中文）

**击败巴珊王噩（1-11节）**——向巴珊去→不要怕他→杀了他们没留一个→夺了六十座城→噩的铁床。
**约旦河东分地（12-20节）**——亚嫩谷到基列→流便迦得得基列半地→玛拿西半支派得巴珊→带兵器帮弟兄。
**对约书亚的嘱咐（21-22节）**——你亲眼看见耶和华所行的→耶和华也必在前面为你争战→不要怕。
**摩西被拒进应许之地（23-29节）**——摩西恳求过约旦河→耶和华发怒不允→上毗斯迦山观望→嘱咐勉励约书亚。

## Structure (English)

**Defeating Og (vv. 1-11)**—Went to Bashan → don't fear → killed all, none remaining → captured sixty cities → Og's iron bed.
**Transjordan Land Division (vv. 12-20)**—Arnon Valley to Gilead → Reuben/Gad got half Gilead → half Manasseh got Bashan → armed help for brothers.
**Charge to Joshua (vv. 21-22)**—Your eyes have seen what LORD did → LORD will fight for you → don't fear.
**Moses Denied Entry (vv. 23-29)**—Moses pleaded to cross Jordan → LORD angry, refused → go up Pisgah, look → charge and encourage Joshua.

## 神学要点（中文）

**"不要怕他"——神的应许消除恐惧**
改革宗传统教导信心的核心是信靠神的应许。韦敏斯德信条第14章论得救的信心。"不要怕他因我已将他交在你手中"——恐惧的对立面不是勇气，而是信靠神的应许。当神说"我已交给你"——争战的结果已经决定了——你的"不怕"是基于神的话语，不是基于你的能力。

**摩西被拒——后果的真实性**
改革宗传统教导行为有真实的后果。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理。即使是摩西——神最亲密的仆人——也必须承担他在米利巴的行为后果。这不是说神不爱他——而是说神的公义是真实的。加拉太书6:7"不要自欺，神是轻慢不得的。人种的是什么，收的也是什么。"

**代际传承——"嘱咐约书亚"**
改革宗传统教导信仰的代际传承。韦敏斯德信条第25章论教会。"嘱咐约书亚勉励他"——摩西自己不能进去，但他的责任是预备下一代的领袖。教会的使命不是一代完成的——每一代都要"嘱咐"下一代，把使命传递下去。

## Theology (English)

**"Do Not Fear Him"—God's Promise Eliminates Fear**
The Reformed tradition teaches faith's core is trusting God's promises. Westminster Confession chapter 14.: Do not fear, for I have given him into your hand—fear's opposite is not courage but trusting God's promise. When God says "I have given"—the battle's outcome is already decided.

**Moses Denied—Consequences Are Real**
The Reformed tradition teaches actions have real consequences. Westminster Confession chapter 5. Even Moses must bear consequences for his actions at Meribah. Not that God didn't love him—but God's justice is real. Galatians 6:7: "Do not be deceived: God is not mocked, for whatever one sows, that will he also reap."

**Generational Succession—"Charge Joshua"**
The Reformed tradition teaches intergenerational faith transmission. Westminster Confession chapter 25. Moses can't enter himself but must prepare the next generation's leader. The church's mission isn't completed in one generation—each must "charge" the next.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**摩西不能进去，约书亚引领进入——律法与恩典**——约翰福音1:17"律法本是藉着摩西传的；恩典和真理都是由耶稣基督来的。"摩西代表律法——律法不能把你带进应许之地。约书亚（"耶稣"的希伯来原名）代表恩典——是恩典引领你进入安息。希伯来书4:8"若是约书亚已叫他们享了安息，后来神就不再提别的日子了。"旧约的约书亚只是影子——真正引领我们进入永恒安息的是耶稣基督。

**"不要怕巨人"——基督已经胜了世界**——约翰福音16:33"在世上你们有苦难，但你们可以放心，我已经胜了世界。"巴珊王噩多么强大——但神说"不要怕"。世界的苦难多么真实——但基督说"我已经胜了"。弟兄姐妹，你的"巨人"在基督已经得胜的事实面前——都不算什么。

**摩西在变像山上——不只是远望而是与主同在**——马太福音17:3"忽然，有摩西、以利亚向他们显现，同耶稣说话。"摩西在今生没有进入应许之地——但在变像山上，他不只是看到了那地——他见到了那地的主人耶稣基督。弟兄姐妹，你在今生得不到的——在永恒中会超过你所求所想。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Moses Can't Enter, Joshua Leads In—Law and Grace**—John 1:17: "For the law was given through Moses; grace and truth came through Jesus Christ." Moses represents the law—law cannot bring you into the Promised Land. Joshua ("Jesus" in Hebrew) represents grace—grace leads you into rest. Hebrews 4:8. The OT Joshua was only a shadow—the One truly leading us into eternal rest is Jesus Christ.

**"Don't Fear the Giant"—Christ Has Overcome the World**—John 16:33: "In the world you will have tribulation. But take heart; I have overcome the world." However powerful Og was—God said "don't fear." However real the world's suffering—Christ says "I have overcome." Your "giants" are nothing before Christ's victory.

**Moses on the Mount of Transfiguration**—Matthew 17:3: "And behold, there appeared to them Moses and Elijah, talking with him." Moses in this life never entered the Promised Land—but on the Transfiguration mount, he didn't just see the land—he met its Owner, Jesus Christ. What you don't receive in this life—in eternity will surpass all you ask or imagine.
