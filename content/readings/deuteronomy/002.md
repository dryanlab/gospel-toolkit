## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第二章记载了以色列人在旷野三十八年之后终于再次启程的故事——从绕行西珥山到击败希实本王西宏，这一章教导我们什么时候该等待、什么时候该前进。

**绕行的日子够了（第1-7节）**

> "此后，我们转回，从红海的路往旷野去，是照耶和华所吩咐我的。我们在西珥山绕行了许多日子。耶和华对我说：'你们绕行这山的日子够了，要转向北去。'"

弟兄姐妹，"绕行了许多日子"——三十八年！因为加低斯巴尼亚的不信，整整一代人在旷野绕行——不是前进，不是后退，而是"绕行"。弟兄姐妹，你有没有经历过人生中的"绕行"期——你似乎没有前进也没有后退——只是在原地打转？弟兄姐妹，绕行不是没有意义——旧的一代在绕行中死去，新的一代在绕行中成长。神在"绕行"中预备你——即使你感觉什么都没有发生。

"你们绕行这山的日子够了"——弟兄姐妹，注意："够了"——不是你自己决定什么时候结束绕行——而是神说"够了"。弟兄姐妹，你的等待季节什么时候结束？当神说"够了"的时候。不早也不晚。

"这四十年耶和华你的神常与你同在，故此你一无所缺"——弟兄姐妹，即使在绕行中——即使在看似浪费的三十八年中——"耶和华常与你同在"。绕行不等于被遗弃。旷野不等于没有神的同在。弟兄姐妹，你的"旷野"中，神也在。

**不要惹的三个民族（第4-23节）**

> "你们弟兄以扫的子孙住在西珥，你们要经过他们的境界……不可与他们争战；他们的地连脚掌可踏之处我都不给你们，因我已将西珥山赐给以扫为业。"

弟兄姐妹，神命令以色列人不可与三个民族争战——以东人（以扫的后裔，第4-8节）、摩押人（罗得的后裔，第9-12节）、亚扪人（罗得的后裔，第19-23节）。为什么？因为神把那些地已经赐给了他们。弟兄姐妹，不是所有的"地"都是你的——神分给别人的你不能去抢。

弟兄姐妹，这个原则在今天也适用——不是所有的机会都属于你。神给你的你要全力争取——但神没有给你的你不要去抢。弟兄姐妹，知道什么是你的、什么不是你的——这需要智慧和谦卑。嫉妒别人拥有的——就是不信任神给你分配的。

**击败希实本王西宏（第24-37节）**

> "你们起来前往，过亚嫩谷；我已将亚摩利人希实本王西宏和他的地交在你手中，你要与他争战，得他的地为业。"

弟兄姐妹，前面神说"不要争战"——现在神说"你要与他争战"。弟兄姐妹，神的命令根据情况不同——有时候要退让，有时候要前进。"不要争战"和"你要争战"都是从同一位神来的——区别在于时间和对象。弟兄姐妹，你要分辨——什么时候该退让、什么时候该前进——这取决于神的引导，不是你的情绪。

"但希实本王西宏不容我们从他那里经过；因为耶和华你的神使他心中刚硬"——弟兄姐妹，"耶和华使他心中刚硬"——这是神主权的一个深奥彰显。正如出埃及记中耶和华使法老的心刚硬一样——神的主权甚至运行在敌人的心中。弟兄姐妹，这不是说西宏没有责任——他的刚硬也是他自己的选择。但神的主权在人的选择之上运行——使一切都服务于祂的计划。

弟兄姐妹，申命记第二章教导我们：绕行的日子有神的时间——"够了"的时候祂会告诉你。不是所有的地都属于你——尊重神给别人的分配。当神说前进的时候就前进——祂已经把敌人交在你手中了。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 2 records Israel's journey after thirty-eight years in the wilderness—from circling Mount Seir to defeating King Sihon of Heshbon. This chapter teaches us when to wait and when to advance.

**Enough Circling (vv. 1-7)**

> "Then we turned and journeyed into the wilderness in the direction of the Red Sea...And we traveled around Mount Seir for many days. Then the LORD said to me, 'You have been traveling around this mountain country long enough. Turn northward.'"

Brothers and sisters, "many days" of circling—thirty-eight years! Because of unbelief at Kadesh Barnea, an entire generation circled in the wilderness—not advancing, not retreating, just "circling." Have you experienced life's "circling" season—seeming neither to advance nor retreat—just going in circles? Circling is not meaningless—the old generation died in circling; the new generation grew in circling. God prepares you in "circling"—even when you feel nothing is happening.

"You have been traveling around this mountain long enough"—"enough"—not you deciding when circling ends—but God saying "enough." When does your waiting season end? When God says "enough." Not sooner, not later.

"These forty years the LORD your God has been with you. You have lacked nothing"—even in circling—even in the seemingly wasted thirty-eight years—"the LORD has been with you." Circling doesn't mean abandoned. Wilderness doesn't mean absence of God's presence.

**Three Nations Not to Provoke (vv. 4-23)**

God commanded Israel not to fight three nations—Edomites (Esau's descendants), Moabites (Lot's descendants), Ammonites (Lot's descendants). Why? Because God had given those lands to them. Not all "land" is yours—what God assigned to others you cannot seize.

This principle applies today—not all opportunities belong to you. What God gives you, pursue fully—but what God hasn't given you, don't seize. Knowing what is yours and what isn't requires wisdom and humility. Envying what others have means distrusting God's allocation for you.

**Defeating King Sihon (vv. 24-37)**

> "Rise up, set out on your journey...Behold, I have given into your hand Sihon the Amorite, king of Heshbon, and his land. Begin to take possession, and contend with him in battle."

Brothers and sisters, earlier God said "don't fight"—now God says "fight." God's commands vary by situation—sometimes retreat, sometimes advance. "Don't fight" and "fight" both come from the same God—the difference is timing and target. Discern—when to yield, when to advance—depends on God's leading, not your emotions.

"But Sihon king of Heshbon would not let us pass by him, for the LORD your God hardened his spirit"—God's sovereignty operating even in the enemy's heart. As with Pharaoh in Exodus—God's sovereignty works above human choices—making everything serve His plan.

Brothers and sisters, Deuteronomy 2 teaches: circling has God's timing—He'll tell you when "enough." Not all land belongs to you—respect God's allocations to others. When God says advance, advance—He has already delivered the enemy into your hand.

## 历史背景（中文）

申命记第二章覆盖了以色列人从加低斯巴尼亚的失败（民13-14章）到约旦河东征服的过渡期——约三十八年的旷野漂流浓缩在这一章中。"以扫的子孙住在西珥"——以东人是以扫的后裔，住在死海南部的山地（今约旦南部）。摩押人和亚扪人是罗得的后裔（创19:30-38）——他们分别住在死海东岸和约旦河东北。"希实本王西宏"是亚摩利人的王——亚摩利人是迦南地原住民之一——他们在约旦河东建立了王国。民数记21:21-30已记载了以色列人击败西宏的事件——申命记第二章是摩西的回顾和诠释。"耶和华使他心中刚硬"与出埃及记中法老"心中刚硬"的神学主题一致——神的主权与人的责任并行不悖。

## Historical Context (English)

Deuteronomy 2 covers the transition from Israel's failure at Kadesh Barnea (Num 13-14) to the Transjordan conquests—approximately thirty-eight years of wilderness wandering compressed into one chapter. "Esau's descendants dwelling in Seir"—Edomites were Esau's descendants, dwelling in mountains south of the Dead Sea (modern southern Jordan). Moabites and Ammonites were Lot's descendants (Gen 19:30-38). "Sihon king of Heshbon" was an Amorite king—Amorites were Canaan's original inhabitants who established kingdoms in Transjordan. Numbers 21:21-30 already records Israel's defeat of Sihon—Deuteronomy 2 is Moses' retrospective interpretation. "The LORD hardened his spirit" shares the theological theme of Pharaoh's "hardened heart" in Exodus—divine sovereignty and human responsibility running in parallel.

## 经文结构（中文）

**绕行西珥山（1-7节）**——转回旷野→绕行许多日子→"够了，转向北去"→四十年一无所缺。
**经过以东（4-8节）**——以扫子孙在西珥→不可争战→用钱买粮买水→离开西珥。
**经过摩押（9-16节）**——不可扰害摩押→以米人先住那地→三十八年兵丁灭尽。
**经过亚扪（17-23节）**——不可与亚扪争战→散送冥人先住→迦斐托人除灭亚卫人。
**击败西宏（24-37节）**——起来过亚嫩谷→西宏心中刚硬→在雅杂交战→击杀众民→夺取城邑→不挨近亚扪地。

## Structure (English)

**Circling Mount Seir (vv. 1-7)**—Turned back to wilderness → circled many days → "enough, turn northward" → forty years lacking nothing.
**Passing Through Edom (vv. 4-8)**—Esau's descendants in Seir → don't fight → buy food and water → left Seir.
**Passing Through Moab (vv. 9-16)**—Don't disturb Moab → Emim lived there before → thirty-eight years until warriors perished.
**Passing Through Ammon (vv. 17-23)**—Don't fight Ammon → Zamzummim lived before → Caphtorim destroyed Avvim.
**Defeating Sihon (vv. 24-37)**—Rise, cross Arnon Valley → Sihon's spirit hardened → battle at Jahaz → struck down all → captured cities → didn't approach Ammonite land.

## 神学要点（中文）

**神的主权与人的刚硬**
改革宗传统教导神的主权甚至运行在恶人的心中。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理：神……甚至安排了罪的发生。申命记2:30"耶和华你的神使他心中刚硬"与出埃及记中法老的心刚硬一致——神的主权不免除人的责任，但确保了一切都服务于祂的计划。

**等待的智慧——"够了"的时间**
改革宗传统教导神有祂自己的时间表。韦敏斯德信条第3章论神的旨意。"绕行这山的日子够了"是神定时的启示——不是人的焦急可以加速的，也不是人的懒惰可以拖延的。传道书3:1"凡事都有定期，天下万务都有定时。"

**尊重神对别国的安排**
改革宗传统教导神是万国的主。韦敏斯德信条第5章。神把西珥赐给以东、亚珥赐给摩押、亚扪地赐给亚扪——这表明耶和华不只是以色列的神，祂安排了所有民族的居所（参徒17:26"他从一本造出万族的人，住在全地上，并且预先定准他们的年限和所住的疆界"）。

## Theology (English)

**God's Sovereignty and Human Hardness**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's sovereignty operates even in the wicked's hearts. Westminster Confession chapter 5: God...even ordains the occurrence of sin. Deuteronomy 2:30 "the LORD your God hardened his spirit" is consistent with Pharaoh's hardened heart in Exodus—God's sovereignty doesn't exempt human responsibility but ensures everything serves His plan.

**The Wisdom of Waiting—God's "Enough" Timing**
The Reformed tradition teaches God has His own timetable. Westminster Confession chapter 3.: You have been traveling around this mountain long enough is God's timed revelation—neither accelerated by human anxiety nor delayed by human laziness. Ecclesiastes 3:1.

**Respecting God's Arrangements for Other Nations**
The Reformed tradition teaches God is Lord of all nations. Westminster Confession chapter 5. God gave Seir to Edom, Ar to Moab, Ammon's land to Ammon—showing the LORD is not just Israel's God; He arranged all nations' dwelling places (Acts 17:26).

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"绕行够了"——基督结束了我们的旷野漂流**——希伯来书4:9-10"这样看来，必另有一安息日的安息为神的子民存留。因为那进入安息的，乃是歇了自己的工，正如神歇了他的工一样。"以色列绕行三十八年终于前进——基督为我们结束了属灵的旷野漂流，引领我们进入真正的安息。

**"耶和华使他心中刚硬"——基督是被交出去的**——使徒行传2:23"他既按着神的定旨先见被交与人，你们就藉着无法之人的手，把他钉在十字架上杀了。"正如神使西宏心中刚硬为了将他交在以色列手中——基督也是按神的定旨被交出去、被钉十字架。神的主权在人类最大的恶行（杀害基督）中运行，成就了最大的善（救赎世界）。

**"四十年一无所缺"——基督在旷野的供应**——约翰福音6:35"我就是生命的粮；到我这里来的必定不饿，信我的永远不渴。"以色列在旷野四十年"一无所缺"——基督是永远的供应者，在我们属灵的旷野中使我们"一无所缺"。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Enough Circling"—Christ Ends Our Wilderness Wandering**—Hebrews 4:9-10: "So then, there remains a Sabbath rest for the people of God, for whoever has entered God's rest has also rested from his works." Israel circled thirty-eight years then finally advanced—Christ ends our spiritual wilderness wandering, leading us into true rest.

**"The LORD Hardened His Spirit"—Christ Was Delivered Up**—Acts 2:23: "this Jesus, delivered up according to the definite plan and foreknowledge of God, you crucified and killed by the hands of lawless men." As God hardened Sihon's spirit to deliver him to Israel—Christ was delivered up according to God's plan, crucified. God's sovereignty operated in humanity's greatest evil (killing Christ) to accomplish the greatest good (redeeming the world).

**"Forty Years Lacking Nothing"—Christ's Provision in the Wilderness**—John 6:35: "I am the bread of life; whoever comes to me shall not hunger." Israel in the wilderness forty years "lacked nothing"—Christ is the eternal provider, making us "lack nothing" in our spiritual wilderness.
