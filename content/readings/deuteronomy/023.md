## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第二十三章是关于谁可以"入耶和华的会"——就是参与以色列的敬拜和社会生活。这一章同时也涉及战营的洁净、逃跑奴仆的保护、禁止取利以及许愿的信实。

**谁可以入耶和华的会（第1-8节）**

弟兄姐妹，这一段列出了几类不可入会的人——包括身体残损者、非婚生子女及其后裔到十代、亚扪人和摩押人永远不可入会。弟兄姐妹，这些规定在新约的光中必须被重新理解——因为在基督里"不分犹太人希腊人自主的为奴的或男或女因为你们在基督耶稣里都成为一了"（加3:28）。

弟兄姐妹，但即使在旧约时代——这些规定也有例外：路得是摩押女子——但她因为信靠以色列的神而被接纳——成为大卫的曾祖母——最终出现在耶稣的家谱中（太1:5）。弟兄姐妹，信心超越了民族的界限——恩典打破了律法的限制。

弟兄姐妹，以东人和埃及人受到相对温和的对待——"他们第三代子孙可以入耶和华的会"。为什么？"因为以东人是你的弟兄"（以扫的后裔）——"你在埃及寄居过"（埃及曾经收容了以色列人）。弟兄姐妹，血缘关系和历史恩情都被考虑在内——神的公义是有层次的、是考虑具体情况的。

**战营的洁净（第9-14节）**

> "你出兵攻打仇敌，就要远避诸恶。你们中间若有人夜间偶然梦遗就是不洁净的，应当出到营外……你在营外也该定出一个地方作为便所。在你器械之中当预备一把锹，你出营外便溺以后用以铲土，转身掩盖。因为耶和华你的神常在你营中行走，要救护你将仇敌交给你，所以你的营理当圣洁，免得他见你那里有污秽就离开你。"

弟兄姐妹，"免得他见你那里有污秽就离开你"——弟兄姐妹，即使是军营的卫生问题——也被赋予了神学意义。弟兄姐妹，神"在你营中行走"——所以你的环境必须圣洁。弟兄姐妹，这里有一个深刻的原则：神的同在要求圣洁——不只是心灵的圣洁——连物理环境也包括在内。弟兄姐妹，你的生活空间是否反映了你对神同在的尊重？

**保护逃跑的奴仆（第15-16节）**

> "若有奴仆脱了主人的手逃到你那里，你不可将他交付他的主人。他必在你那里与你同住，在你的城邑中自择一个所喜悦的地方居住；你不可欺负他。"

弟兄姐妹，这条律法在古代近东是革命性的。弟兄姐妹，汉谟拉比法典规定收容逃跑奴仆者处死——但以色列的律法说"你不可将他交付主人"！弟兄姐妹，为什么？因为以色列人自己曾是埃及的奴隶——神把他们从奴役中拯救出来——所以他们没有资格把另一个逃跑的奴仆交回去。弟兄姐妹，这是旧约中最接近"废奴"精神的律法之一。

**不可向弟兄取利（第19-20节）**

> "你借给你弟兄的不可取利：无论是钱财的利、粮食的利或其他任何东西的利。借给外邦人可以取利，借给你弟兄却不可取利。"

弟兄姐妹，"不可向弟兄取利"——弟兄姐妹，在以色列社会内部，借贷是为了帮助困难中的弟兄——不是赚钱的手段。弟兄姐妹，高利贷剥削穷人是旧约先知反复谴责的罪行（结22:12，尼5:7-10）。弟兄姐妹，这条律法的核心是：你的弟兄有困难——你帮助他——不可从他的困境中获利。

**许愿的信实（第21-23节）**

> "你向耶和华你的神许愿偿还不可迟延；因为耶和华你的神必向你追讨，你不偿还就有罪。你若不许愿倒无罪。你嘴里所出的要谨守遵行。"

弟兄姐妹，"不可迟延"——弟兄姐妹，在神面前许的愿是严肃的——不是说了就忘。弟兄姐妹，"不许愿倒无罪"——不许愿没关系——但许了就必须还。弟兄姐妹，传道书5:5"你许愿不还不如不许"。弟兄姐妹，话语有重量——在神面前说的话更有永恒的重量。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 23 addresses who may "enter the LORD's assembly"—participating in Israel's worship and social life. It also covers camp cleanliness, protecting runaway slaves, prohibiting interest, and vow faithfulness.

**Who May Enter the Assembly (vv. 1-8)**

Several categories are restricted. These regulations must be reinterpreted in NT light—in Christ "There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither bond nor free, there is neither male nor female: for ye are all one in Christ Jesus." (Gal 3:28). Even in the OT—Ruth the Moabitess was accepted through faith—becoming David's great-grandmother and appearing in Jesus' genealogy (Matt 1:5). Faith transcends ethnic boundaries—grace breaks through legal restrictions.

**Camp Cleanliness (vv. 9-14)**

> "Because the LORD your God walks in the midst of your camp...therefore your camp must be holy, so that he may not see anything indecent among you and turn away."

Even military camp hygiene receives theological significance. God "walks in your camp"—so your environment must be holy. A profound principle: God's presence demands holiness—not just spiritual but even physical environment.

**Protecting Runaway Slaves (vv. 15-16)**

> "You shall not give up to his master a slave who has escaped from his master to you. He shall dwell with you...You shall not oppress him."

Revolutionary in the ancient Near East. Hammurabi's Code prescribed death for harboring runaway slaves—but Israel's law says "don't return him"! Because Israel was once Egypt's slaves—God freed them—so they have no right to return another fugitive to bondage. One of the OT's closest laws to the spirit of abolition.

**No Interest from Brothers (vv. 19-20)**

"You shall not charge interest on loans to your brother." Within Israel, lending helps struggling brothers—not a profit mechanism. Exploitative lending was repeatedly condemned by prophets.

**Vow Faithfulness (vv. 21-23)**

"If you make a vow to the LORD your God, you shall not delay fulfilling it." "If you refrain from vowing, you will not be guilty." Ecclesiastes 5:5: "It is better that you should not vow than that you should vow and not pay." Words carry weight—words before God carry eternal weight.

## 历史背景（中文）

"不可入耶和华的会"的限制中，亚扪人和摩押人被永远排斥——原因是"因为你们出埃及的时候他们没有拿食物和水在路上迎接你们，又因他们雇了巴兰来咒诅你们"（第4节）。路得记是这个律法最著名的例外——路得记2:12波阿斯对路得说"你来投靠耶和华以色列神的翅膀下"——路得的信心打破了摩押人的禁令。逃跑奴仆的保护律法（15-16节）在古代近东法律中是独一无二的——汉谟拉比法典§16规定收容逃跑奴仆者处死，§17规定帮助奴仆逃跑者处死。以色列律法的立场是对古代奴隶制度的根本性挑战。禁止取利的律法后来影响了中世纪教会对高利贷的禁令。

## Historical Context (English)

Ammonites and Moabites permanently excluded because "they did not meet you with bread and water when you came out of Egypt, and because they hired Balaam to curse you" (v. 4). Ruth is this law's most famous exception—her faith broke the Moabite prohibition. The runaway slave protection (vv. 15-16) is unique in ANE law—Hammurabi §16 prescribes death for harboring fugitive slaves, §17 for helping them escape. Israel's stance fundamentally challenges ancient slavery. The interest prohibition later influenced medieval church prohibition of usury.

## 经文结构（中文）

**入会限制（1-8节）**——受伤或阉割者不可入→非婚生子女十代不可入→亚扪人摩押人永不可入→以东人第三代可入→埃及人第三代可入。
**战营洁净（9-14节）**——出兵远避诸恶→梦遗出营外→定便所用锹掩盖→耶和华在营中行走→营当圣洁。
**逃跑奴仆（15-16节）**——不可交付主人→与你同住→自择居处→不可欺负。
**禁止庙妓（17-18节）**——以色列不可有庙妓→妓女的钱不可入神殿。
**借贷与取利（19-20节）**——不可向弟兄取利→外邦人可取利。
**许愿（21-23节）**——许愿不可迟延→不许愿无罪→嘴里出的要遵行。
**邻居地里摘取（24-25节）**——可用手摘但不可用镰刀割。

## Structure (English)

**Assembly Restrictions (vv. 1-8)**—Emasculated excluded → illegitimate to tenth generation → Ammonite/Moabite permanently → Edomite third generation → Egyptian third generation.
**Camp Cleanliness (vv. 9-14)**—Avoid wickedness → emissions: go outside camp → designate latrine area → LORD walks in camp → camp must be holy.
**Runaway Slaves (vv. 15-16)**—Don't return to master → dwell with you → choose own place → don't oppress.
**Cult Prostitution Forbidden (vv. 17-18)**—No cult prostitutes → prostitute's wages not in temple.
**Lending and Interest (vv. 19-20)**—No interest to brothers → may charge foreigners.
**Vows (vv. 21-23)**—Don't delay fulfilling → not vowing is no sin → keep what you said.
**Neighbor's Field (vv. 24-25)**—May pick by hand → may not use sickle.

## 神学要点（中文）

**恩典超越律法——路得的故事**
改革宗传统教导恩典运行在律法的架构之上。韦敏斯德信条第7章。律法禁止摩押人入会——但路得因信而被接纳——说明恩典不被律法的字面所限制。神的主权和慈爱可以打破律法的界限——而不违背律法的精神。

**圣洁的环境——神的同在要求洁净**
改革宗传统教导神的圣洁要求敬拜者的洁净。韦敏斯德信条第21章。"耶和华在你营中行走"——神的同在是实际的——因此环境的洁净也是实际的要求。属灵的洁净不脱离物质的洁净——两者在旧约中是统一的。

**保护弱者——逃跑奴仆**
改革宗传统教导保护弱者是公义的核心。韦敏斯德大要理问答第141-142问。"不可将逃跑的奴仆交付主人"——这是旧约中神对被压迫者站立的最清晰表达之一。以色列人曾是奴隶——所以他们有义务保护逃离奴役的人。

## Theology (English)

**Grace Transcends Law—Ruth's Story**
The Reformed tradition teaches grace operates above the law's framework. Westminster Confession chapter 7. Law prohibits Moabites entering assembly—but Ruth was accepted by faith—showing grace isn't limited by law's letter. God's sovereignty and love can break through legal boundaries without violating the law's spirit.

**Holy Environment—God's Presence Demands Cleanliness**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's holiness requires worshippers' purity. Westminster Confession chapter 21.: The LORD walks in your camp—God's presence is actual—thus environmental cleanliness is an actual requirement.

**Protecting the Vulnerable—Runaway Slaves**
The Reformed tradition teaches protecting the vulnerable is justice's core. WLC Q141-142. "Don't return the runaway slave"—one of the OT's clearest expressions of God standing with the oppressed.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**路得——恩典打破界限的预表**——马太福音1:5"波阿斯从路得氏生俄备得。"路得是摩押女子——按律法永远不可入耶和华的会——但她因信被接纳进了弥赛亚的家谱。弟兄姐妹，在基督里，没有任何背景可以把你排除在神的恩典之外——"凡劳苦担重担的人可以到我这里来"（太11:28）。

**保护逃跑奴仆——基督释放被压迫者**——路加福音4:18"主的灵在我身上，因为他用膏膏我，叫我传福音给贫穷的人；差遣我报告被掳的得释放。"申命记保护逃跑奴仆——基督来"报告被掳的得释放"。基督不把我们交回罪的"主人"——而是说"你与我同住你自由了"。

**"不可向弟兄取利"——基督白白的恩典**——罗马书3:24"如今却蒙神的恩典，因基督耶稣的救赎，就白白地称义。"以色列人不可从弟兄的困境中获利——基督更是"白白地"把救恩给了我们——不收取任何"利息"。恩典是免费的——因为代价已经由基督付了。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Ruth—Grace Breaking Boundaries Foreshadowed**—Matthew 1:5: "Boaz the father of Obed by Ruth." Ruth the Moabitess—legally permanently excluded—yet accepted by faith into the Messiah's genealogy. In Christ, no background can exclude you from God's grace—"Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest." (Matt 11:28).

**Protecting Runaway Slaves—Christ Liberates the Oppressed**—Luke 4:18: "The Spirit of the Lord is upon me...to proclaim liberty to the captives." Deuteronomy protects fugitive slaves—Christ proclaims "liberty to the captives." Christ doesn't return us to sin's "master"—but says "dwell with me, you're free."

**"No Interest from Brothers"—Christ's Free Grace**—Romans 3:24: "justified by his grace as a gift, through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus." Israelites can't profit from brothers' hardship—Christ "freely" gave us salvation—charging no "interest." Grace is free—because Christ already paid the price.
