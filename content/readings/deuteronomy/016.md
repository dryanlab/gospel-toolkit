## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第十六章规定了以色列人每年必须守的三大节期——逾越节、七七节和住棚节——以及对公义审判的要求。这三个节期不只是宗教仪式——而是用身体来记忆神的大能作为。

**逾越节与除酵节（第1-8节）**

> "你要注意亚笔月，向耶和华你的神守逾越节，因为耶和华你的神在亚笔月夜间领你出了埃及。你要在耶和华所选择要立为他名的居所，从牛群羊群中将逾越节的祭牲献给耶和华你的神。你吃这祭牲不可吃有酵的饼；七日之内要吃无酵饼就是困苦饼。"

弟兄姐妹，"亚笔月"——春天的月份——正是出埃及的时候。弟兄姐妹，逾越节纪念的是出埃及之夜——灭命天使"越过"门楣上有血的以色列家庭（出12章）。弟兄姐妹，"无酵饼就是困苦饼"——弟兄姐妹，为什么叫"困苦饼"？因为那一夜他们匆忙离开——面饼来不及发酵——所以无酵饼代表出埃及的紧迫和困苦。弟兄姐妹，每年吃这个没有味道的扁饼——就是用味觉来记忆你曾经是奴隶。

弟兄姐妹，"七日之内你的四境不可见到面酵"——弟兄姐妹，不只是不吃——连"见到"都不行。弟兄姐妹，这个严格程度提醒我们——除掉罪必须彻底——不只是行为上远离——连环境中都不能有。保罗引用这个原则说："你们既是无酵的面应当把旧酵除净"（林前5:7）。

**七七节（第9-12节）**

> "你要计算七七日，从你开镰收割禾稼时算起共计七七日。你要照耶和华你神所赐你的福在耶和华你神面前守七七节——随你力量甘心奉献。你和你儿女仆婢并住在你城里的利未人以及在你们中间寄居的与孤儿寡妇都要在耶和华你神所选择立为他名的居所在耶和华你的神面前欢乐。"

弟兄姐妹，七七节——收割的节期——从逾越节数七个七日就是四十九天——第五十天守节——后来的希腊文叫"五旬节"（Pentecost）。弟兄姐妹，这是感恩的节期——庆祝收割——但注意参与的人："儿女仆婢利未人寄居的孤儿寡妇"——所有人都被包括在内。弟兄姐妹，丰收的欢乐不是只属于地主的——而是要与所有人分享——尤其是没有产业的人。

**住棚节（第13-15节）**

> "你把禾场的谷酒榨的酒收藏以后就要守住棚节七日。守节的时候你和你儿女仆婢并住在你城里的利未人以及寄居的与孤儿寡妇都要欢乐。"

弟兄姐妹，住棚节——秋天的收割之后——以色列人要住在临时搭的棚子里七天——纪念旷野四十年住帐篷的日子。弟兄姐妹，当你住在舒适的房子里——你容易忘记你曾经住在帐篷里。住棚节就是提醒你——你的舒适不是理所当然的——曾经你什么都没有——是神把你带到这里。弟兄姐妹，三个节期——逾越节记念救赎（春天）、七七节感恩丰收（初夏）、住棚节记念旷野路程（秋天）——构成了以色列信仰生活的年度循环。

**公义的审判（第18-20节）**

> "你要在耶和华你神所赐的各城里按着各支派设立审判官和官长。他们必按公义的审判判断百姓。不可屈枉正直，不可看人的外貌，也不可受贿赂，因为贿赂能叫智慧人的眼变瞎了，又能颠倒义人的话。你要追求至公至义，好叫你存活承受耶和华你神所赐你的地。"

弟兄姐妹，"你要追求至公至义"——弟兄姐妹，"追求"（tzedek tzedek tirdof）——原文重复了两次"公义"——"公义公义你要追求"。弟兄姐妹，为什么重复？弟兄姐妹，因为公义极其重要——重要到要说两遍。弟兄姐妹，"不可受贿赂因为贿赂能叫智慧人的眼变瞎了"——弟兄姐妹，贿赂不只是腐蚀愚蠢的人——连"智慧人"也会被贿赂蒙蔽。弟兄姐妹，没有人能免疫于贿赂的诱惑——所以必须有制度性的防范。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 16 prescribes Israel's three annual festivals—Passover, Feast of Weeks, and Feast of Booths—plus requirements for just judgment. These festivals aren't mere rituals—they use the body to remember God's mighty acts.

**Passover and Unleavened Bread (vv. 1-8)**

> "Observe the month of Abib and keep the Passover...You shall eat no leavened bread with it. Seven days you shall eat it with unleavened bread, the bread of affliction."

"Bread of affliction"—that night they left in haste—bread had no time to rise—unleavened bread represents the Exodus's urgency and hardship. Eating this flat, tasteless bread yearly—using taste to remember you were once a slave.

"No leaven shall be seen in all your territory"—not just don't eat—don't even "see" it. This strictness reminds us: removing sin must be thorough. Paul applies: "Purge out therefore the old leaven, that ye may be a new lump, as ye are unleavened. For even Christ our passover is sacrificed for us:" (1 Cor 5:7).

**Feast of Weeks (vv. 9-12)**

Seven weeks from Passover—forty-nine days—the fiftieth day is the feast—later called "Pentecost." A thanksgiving harvest feast—but note the participants: "sons, daughters, servants, Levites, sojourners, orphans, widows"—everyone included. Harvest joy isn't just for landowners—it's shared with all—especially the landless.

**Feast of Booths (vv. 13-15)**

After autumn harvest—Israel lived in temporary shelters for seven days—remembering forty wilderness years in tents. When you live in comfortable houses—you forget you once lived in tents. Booths remind you—your comfort isn't guaranteed.

Three festivals: Passover remembers redemption (spring), Weeks celebrates harvest (early summer), Booths remembers wilderness journey (autumn)—forming the annual cycle of Israel's faith life.

**Just Judgment (vv. 18-20)**

"Justice, justice, you shall pursue." Original Hebrew repeats "tzedek" twice—justice so important it must be said twice. "A bribe blinds the eyes of the wise"—bribery doesn't just corrupt the foolish—even the "wise" are blinded. No one is immune—hence the need for institutional safeguards.

## 历史背景（中文）

三大节期（shalosh regalim）是以色列男丁每年必须"在耶和华面前出现"的三次朝圣节期（出23:14-17）。逾越节（Pesach）纪念出埃及——"亚笔月"后来在巴比伦历法影响下改称"尼散月"。七七节（Shavuot）后来被犹太传统与西奈山赐律法联系起来——五旬节（Pentecost，希腊文"第五十"）在新约中是圣灵降临的日子（徒2章）。住棚节（Sukkot）是犹太历法中最欢乐的节期——被称为"节期"（ha-chag）——在约翰福音7章中耶稣在住棚节最后一天大声说"人若渴了可以到我这里来喝"。"公义公义你要追求"（tzedek tzedek tirdof）——双重重复在希伯来语中表示最高级的强调——这句话后来成为犹太法律传统的座右铭。

## Historical Context (English)

The three pilgrimage festivals (shalosh regalim) required all Israelite males to "Three times thou shalt keep a feast unto me in the year. Thou shalt keep the feast of unleavened bread: (thou shalt eat unleavened bread seven days, as I commanded thee, in the time appointed of the month Abib; for in it thou camest out from Egypt: and none shall appear before me empty:) And the feast of harvest, the firstfruits of thy labours, which thou hast sown in the field: and the feast of ingathering, which is in the end of the year, when thou hast gathered in thy labours out of the field. Three times in the year all thy males shall appear before the LORD God." (Ex 23:14-17). Passover (Pesach) commemorates the Exodus—"Abib" later renamed "Nisan." Feast of Weeks (Shavuot) was later linked to Sinai law-giving—Pentecost (Greek "fiftieth") is the Spirit's descent day in the NT (Acts 2). Feast of Booths (Sukkot) is the most joyous Jewish festival—called simply "the Festival" (ha-chag)—in John 7, Jesus cried out on its last day "If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink." "Justice, justice, you shall pursue" (tzedek tzedek tirdof)—double repetition is Hebrew's superlative emphasis—this phrase became Jewish legal tradition's motto.

## 经文结构（中文）

**逾越节与除酵节（1-8节）**——亚笔月守逾越节→在选择的地方献祭→无酵饼七日→困苦饼→四境不见面酵→第七日严肃会。
**七七节（9-12节）**——数七七日→甘心奉献→儿女仆婢利未人寄居者孤儿寡妇一同欢乐→记念你在埃及作过奴仆。
**住棚节（13-15节）**——收藏后守住棚节七日→在选择的地方→尽都欢乐。
**三次朝圣（16-17节）**——一年三次在选择的地方→逾越七七住棚→各人按自己的力量奉献。
**公义审判（18-20节）**——各城设审判官→按公义判断→不可屈枉正直→不可看人外貌→不可受贿→公义公义你要追求。
**禁止木偶柱像（21-22节）**——不可栽什么树木作木偶→不可立柱像。

## Structure (English)

**Passover and Unleavened Bread (vv. 1-8)**—Observe Abib, keep Passover → sacrifice at chosen place → unleavened bread seven days → bread of affliction → no leaven in territory → solemn assembly day seven.
**Feast of Weeks (vv. 9-12)**—Count seven weeks → freewill offering → children/servants/Levites/sojourners/orphans/widows rejoice together → remember Egypt slavery.
**Feast of Booths (vv. 13-15)**—After gathering, keep Booths seven days → at chosen place → altogether joyful.
**Three Pilgrimages (vv. 16-17)**—Three times yearly at chosen place → Passover/Weeks/Booths → each gives as able.
**Just Judgment (vv. 18-20)**—Appoint judges in every city → judge righteously → don't pervert justice → no partiality → no bribes → justice, justice pursue.
**No Asherah or Pillars (vv. 21-22)**—No trees as Asherah → no pillars.

## 神学要点（中文）

**节期的神学——身体的记忆**
改革宗传统教导圣礼是"可见的话语"。韦敏斯德信条第27章论圣礼。旧约的节期用身体行为来记忆神的作为——吃无酵饼记念出埃及、住在棚子里记念旷野。新约的圣餐也是身体的记忆——"你们应当如此行为的是记念我"（路22:19）。信仰不只是头脑的——还是身体的——用吃、住、行来记念。

**"公义公义你要追求"——社会公义的神学根基**
改革宗传统教导公义是神的核心属性。韦敏斯德信条第2章。"公义"说两遍——因为这是最高优先级。审判官的公义直接反映神的公义——腐败的审判等于否认神的性情。以赛亚书1:17"学习行善，寻求公平，解救受欺压的"。

**包容性的欢乐——所有人都参与**
改革宗传统教导神的恩典面向所有人。韦敏斯德信条第25章。三大节期都强调"儿女仆婢利未人寄居的孤儿寡妇"同来欢乐——没有人被排除在外。这个包容性预示了新约教会——不分犹太人希腊人为奴的自主的——"在基督里都成为一了"（加3:28）。

## Theology (English)

**Festival Theology—Bodily Memory**
The Reformed tradition teaches sacraments are "visible words." Westminster Confession chapter 27. OT festivals use bodily actions to remember God's acts—eating unleavened bread, dwelling in booths. NT communion is also bodily memory. Faith is not only mental but physical.

**"Justice, Justice Pursue"—Social Justice's Theological Foundation**
The Reformed tradition teaches justice is God's core attribute. Westminster Confession chapter 2. "Justice" said twice—highest priority. Judges' justice directly reflects God's justice. Isaiah 1:17.

**Inclusive Joy—Everyone Participates**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's grace is for all. Westminster Confession chapter 25. All three festivals emphasize everyone's inclusion. This inclusivity foreshadows the NT church—"There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither bond nor free, there is neither male nor female: for ye are all one in Christ Jesus." (Gal 3:28).

## 基督的影子（中文）

**逾越节——基督是逾越节的羔羊**——哥林多前书5:7"因为我们逾越节的羔羊基督已经被杀献祭了。"逾越节的羔羊被杀→血涂在门楣上→灭命天使越过。基督是终极的逾越节羔羊——祂的血涂在我们心上——使审判越过我们。

**七七节——五旬节圣灵降临**——使徒行传2:1-4"五旬节到了……他们就都被圣灵充满。"旧约的七七节是收割的节期——五旬节是属灵收割的开始——三千人归主（徒2:41）。旧约献初熟的果子——五旬节是教会的"初熟果子"。

**住棚节——基督"支搭帐幕在我们中间"**——约翰福音1:14"道成了肉身，住在我们中间。""住"的原文是"支搭帐幕"（eskenosen）——基督在地上的生活就是一个"住棚节"——祂暂时住在人间的帐幕中。约翰福音7:37-38耶稣在住棚节最后一天喊："人若渴了可以到我这里来喝。信我的人就如经上所说从他腹中要流出活水的江河来。"

**"公义公义你要追求"——基督是我们的义**——哥林多前书1:30"神又使他成为我们的智慧、公义、圣洁、救赎。"我们追求的公义——最终在基督里找到——因为基督本身就是我们的义。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**Passover—Christ Is the Passover Lamb**—1 Corinthians 5:7: "Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed." The Passover lamb killed → blood on doorposts → angel passed over. Christ is the ultimate Passover lamb—His blood on our hearts—judgment passes over us.

**Feast of Weeks—Pentecost Spirit Descent**—Acts 2:1-4: "When the day of Pentecost arrived...they were all filled with the Holy Spirit." OT Weeks was harvest festival—Pentecost began the spiritual harvest—three thousand converted (Acts 2:41).

**Feast of Booths—Christ "Tabernacled Among Us"**—John 1:14: "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us." "Dwelt" (eskenosen) means "tabernacled"—Christ's earthly life was a "Feast of Booths." John 7:37-38: Jesus on Booths' last day: "If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink."

**"Justice, Justice Pursue"—Christ Is Our Righteousness**—1 Corinthians 1:30: "God made him to be our wisdom and our righteousness." The justice we pursue—ultimately found in Christ—because Christ Himself is our righteousness.
