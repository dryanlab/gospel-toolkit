## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第八章是旷野四十年的属灵总结——神为什么让你经历旷野？不是要毁灭你——而是要"苦炼你、试验你、叫你终久享福"。这一章也包含了耶稣在旷野受试探时引用的那句名言。

**旷野四十年的意义（第1-5节）**

> "你也要记念耶和华你的神在旷野引导你这四十年，是要苦炼你，试验你，要知道你心内如何，肯守他的诫命不肯。"

弟兄姐妹，"苦炼你试验你"——弟兄姐妹，旷野不是惩罚——是训练。弟兄姐妹，你经历的困难——失业、疾病、失望——可能不是惩罚——而是"苦炼"。苦炼的目的不是折磨你——而是"要知道你心内如何"。弟兄姐妹，神当然知道你的心——祂无所不知。但"要知道"的意思是——让你自己知道你的心如何。弟兄姐妹，你不知道自己的心——你以为你信——直到考验来了你才知道你信不信。旷野就是那个考验——让你看见自己心里真正有什么。

> "他苦炼你，任你饥饿，将你和你列祖所不认识的吗哪赐给你吃，使你知道人活着不是单靠食物，乃是靠耶和华口里所出的一切话。"

弟兄姐妹，"人活着不是单靠食物乃是靠耶和华口里所出的一切话"——这句话耶稣在旷野受撒但试探时引用了（太4:4）。弟兄姐妹，为什么神"任你饥饿"然后"赐吗哪"？为什么不直接给？因为神要你先经历"没有"——然后再经历"神给"——这样你才知道一切都是从祂来的。弟兄姐妹，如果你从来没有饥饿过——你不会知道食物是恩典。如果你从来没有缺乏过——你不会知道供应是恩典。弟兄姐妹，旷野的饥饿是为了让你认识恩典的吗哪。

"这四十年你的衣服没有穿破，你的脚也没有肿。"弟兄姐妹，四十年衣服不破、脚不肿——这是神奇迹性的供应。弟兄姐妹，你可能没有华衣美食——但你有"不穿破的衣服"——够用的恩典。弟兄姐妹，保罗说"我知道怎样处卑贱也知道怎样处丰富"（腓4:12）——不破的衣服教会你满足于够用的。

"你当心里思想，耶和华你神管教你好像人管教儿子一样。"弟兄姐妹，"管教"——不是仇敌对俘虏的虐待——而是父亲对儿子的训练。弟兄姐妹，管教证明你是儿子——"因为主所爱的他必管教，又鞭打凡所收纳的儿子"（来12:6）。

**富足中的危险（第10-18节）**

> "你吃得饱足就要称颂耶和华你的神……你要谨慎，免得忘记耶和华你的神。恐怕你吃得饱足，建造美好的房屋居住，你的牛羊加多，你的金银增添……你就心高气傲忘记耶和华你的神。"

弟兄姐妹，第六章警告过——第八章再次警告——弟兄姐妹，反复警告说明这个问题有多严重。"吃得饱足→建造美好房屋→牛羊加多→金银增添→心高气傲→忘记耶和华"——弟兄姐妹，注意这个下坡路——从饱足开始——经过富裕——最终到达骄傲和忘记神。弟兄姐妹，富足不是罪——但富足是通往忘记神的最短路径之一。

> "恐怕你心里说：这货财是我力量我能力得来的。你要记念耶和华你的神，因为得货财的力量是他给你的。"

弟兄姐妹，"这货财是我力量我能力得来的"——弟兄姐妹，这是富人最常犯的罪——以为成功是自己的功劳。弟兄姐妹，你的智慧——是谁给的？你的健康——是谁维持的？你出生的国家和时代——是你选的吗？弟兄姐妹，连"得货财的力量"本身都是神给的——不只是货财是神给的——连你赚钱的能力都是祂给的。弟兄姐妹，你有什么不是领受的呢？

弟兄姐妹，申命记第八章的信息可以总结为两个"记念"——在旷野中记念神的训练（第2节）——在富足中记念神的供应（第18节）。无论在贫穷还是在富足——关键都是"记念耶和华你的神"。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 8 is the spiritual summary of forty wilderness years—why did God let you experience the wilderness? Not to destroy you—but to "humble you, test you, that in the end it might go well with you." This chapter also contains the famous verse Jesus quoted when tempted in the wilderness.

**The Meaning of Forty Years (vv. 1-5)**

> "You shall remember that the LORD your God led you all the way these forty years in the wilderness, to humble you, testing you to know what was in your heart, whether you would keep his commandments or not."

Brothers and sisters, "humble and test you"—the wilderness is not punishment—it's training. Your hardships—unemployment, illness, disappointment—may not be punishment—but "humbling." The purpose isn't to torment—but "to know what was in your heart." God of course knows your heart—He is omniscient. But "to know" means—to let you yourself see what's truly in your heart. You don't know your own heart—you think you believe—until the test comes and you discover whether you truly do.

> "He humbled you and let you hunger and fed you with manna...that he might make you know that man does not live by bread alone, but man lives by every word that comes from the mouth of the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, Jesus quoted this when tempted by Satan (Matt 4:4). Why did God "let you hunger" then "give manna"? Why not give directly? Because God wants you to first experience "having nothing"—then experience "God providing"—so you know everything comes from Him. If you've never hungered—you won't know food is grace.

"These forty years your clothing did not wear out and your foot did not swell." Four decades, clothes not worn out, feet not swollen—God's miraculous provision. You may not have fine clothes and feasts—but you have "clothing that doesn't wear out"—sufficient grace.

**The Danger of Prosperity (vv. 10-18)**

> "When you have eaten and are full...take care lest you forget...lest your heart be lifted up and you forget the LORD."

Brothers and sisters, "eat and are full → build fine houses → herds multiply → silver and gold multiply → heart lifted up → forget the LORD"—notice the downhill slide—from satisfaction to prosperity to pride to forgetting God. Prosperity isn't sin—but prosperity is one of the shortest paths to forgetting God.

> "Beware lest you say in your heart, 'My power and the might of my hand have gotten me this wealth.' You shall remember the LORD your God, for it is he who gives you power to get wealth."

Brothers and sisters, "my power got me this wealth"—the rich person's most common sin—believing success is self-made. Your intelligence—who gave it? Your health—who maintains it? Even "the power to get wealth" itself is God-given—not just the wealth but even your ability to earn it. "For who maketh thee to differ from another? and what hast thou that thou didst not receive? now if thou didst receive it, why dost thou glory, as if thou hadst not received it?" (1 Cor 4:7).

Deuteronomy 8's message: two "remembers"—in wilderness, remember God's training (v. 2)—in prosperity, remember God's provision (v. 18). Whether poor or rich—the key is "remember the LORD your God."

## 历史背景（中文）

申命记8:3"人活着不是单靠食物乃是靠耶和华口里所出的一切话"是旧约中被新约引用最著名的经文之一——耶稣在旷野禁食四十天后，撒但引诱祂把石头变成饼时，耶稣引用了这节经文（太4:4，路4:4）。"吗哪"是神在旷野四十年间每天供应的食物（出16章）——除安息日外每天收取，安息日前一天收双倍。"衣服没有穿破脚也没有肿"（第4节）——尼希米记9:21也记载了这个神迹。犹太传统认为这是旷野中最隐秘的神迹之一——不像红海分开那么壮观——但每天持续四十年。"得货财的力量是他给你的"（第18节）后来成为犹太商业伦理的核心——在塔木德中被反复引用——提醒富人他们的财富来自神的赐予。

## Historical Context (English)

Deuteronomy 8:3 "man does not live by bread alone" is one of the OT's most famously quoted verses in the NT—Jesus quoted it when Satan tempted Him to turn stones to bread after forty days' fasting (Matt 4:4, Luke 4:4). "Manna" was God's daily provision for forty wilderness years (Exodus 16). "Clothing did not wear out, foot did not swell" (v. 4)—Nehemiah 9:21 also records this miracle. Jewish tradition considers this one of the wilderness's most hidden miracles—not as spectacular as the Red Sea parting—but continuing daily for forty years. "He who gives you power to get wealth" (v. 18) became core to Jewish business ethics—frequently cited in the Talmud—reminding the wealthy their wealth comes from God's gift.

## 经文结构（中文）

**旷野的目的（1-5节）**——记念四十年→苦炼试验→要知道你心内如何→任你饥饿赐吗哪→人活着不单靠食物→衣服不破脚不肿→如父管教儿子。
**美地的描述（6-10节）**——美地有水泉溪流→有小麦大麦葡萄树→有石油有铜→吃得饱足→称颂耶和华。
**富足中的危险（11-17节）**——谨慎免得忘记→饱足后美好房屋→牛羊金银增添→心高气傲→忘记出埃及→忘记旷野供应。
**记念的命令（18-20节）**——记念耶和华你的神→得货财力量是他给的→若忘记必灭亡。

## Structure (English)

**Purpose of Wilderness (vv. 1-5)**—Remember forty years → humbled and tested → to know your heart → let hunger, gave manna → not by bread alone → clothes didn't wear, feet didn't swell → as father disciplines son.
**Description of Good Land (vv. 6-10)**—Land with streams and springs → wheat, barley, vines → iron, copper → eat and be full → bless the LORD.
**Danger of Prosperity (vv. 11-17)**—Take care lest forget → full, fine houses → herds, gold multiply → heart lifted up → forget the Exodus → forget wilderness provision.
**Command to Remember (vv. 18-20)**—Remember the LORD → power to get wealth is His → if forget, surely perish.

## 神学要点（中文）

**旷野的神学——试炼作为训练**
改革宗传统教导苦难有神的目的。韦敏斯德信条第5章论护理："最智慧、最公义、最恩慈的神，确实常常让祂的儿女暂时受各种试探。""苦炼你试验你"——神使用旷野作为训练场——不是因为祂不爱你——而是"好像人管教儿子一样"。希伯来书12:11"凡管教的事当时不觉得快乐反觉得愁苦，后来却为那经练过的人结出平安的果子就是义。"

**"人活着不是单靠食物"——话语的优先性**
改革宗传统教导神的话语高于一切物质需要。韦敏斯德信条第1章论圣经。"靠耶和华口里所出的一切话"——人的终极需要不是面包——而是神的话。这是唯独圣经（Sola Scriptura）的存在性根基——不只是教义的来源——而是生命的来源。

**"得货财的力量是他给你的"——普遍恩典与主权**
改革宗传统教导神的主权覆盖一切——包括人获取财富的能力。韦敏斯德信条第5章。你的能力、机遇、健康都是神的赐予——人的一切"功劳"归根到底都是在使用神赐的资源。这是"唯独荣耀归给神"（Soli Deo Gloria）的实际应用。

## Theology (English)

**Wilderness Theology—Trials as Training**
The Reformed tradition teaches suffering has divine purpose. Westminster Confession chapter 5.: Humble and test—God uses wilderness as training ground—not from lack of love—but "as a man disciplines his son." Hebrews 12:11.

**"Not by Bread Alone"—The Primacy of the Word**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's word surpasses all material needs. Westminster Confession chapter 1.: By every word from the mouth of the LORD—humanity's ultimate need isn't bread—but God's word. This is Sola Scriptura's existential foundation—not just doctrine's source but life's source.

**"He Gives You Power to Get Wealth"—Common Grace and Sovereignty**
The Reformed tradition teaches God's sovereignty covers everything—including the ability to acquire wealth. Westminster Confession chapter 5. Your abilities, opportunities, health are all God's gifts. This is Soli Deo Gloria's practical application.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"人活着不是单靠食物"——基督在旷野的得胜**——马太福音4:3-4"那试探人的进前来，对他说：你若是神的儿子，可以吩咐这些石头变成食物。耶稣却回答说：经上记着说'人活着不是单靠食物，乃是靠神口里所出的一切话。'"以色列在旷野四十年学了四十年还没有完全学会的功课——基督在旷野四十天就完美地彰显了：真正的生命在于神的话语——不在于面包。

**吗哪——基督是生命的粮**——约翰福音6:31-35众人说"我们的祖宗在旷野吃过吗哪"。耶稣回答："我实实在在地告诉你们，那从天上来的粮不是摩西赐给你们的，乃是我父将天上来的真粮赐给你们。因为神的粮就是那从天上降下来赐生命给世界的。……我就是生命的粮；到我这里来的必定不饿，信我的永远不渴。"弟兄姐妹，旷野的吗哪只是影子——基督是真正的天粮。

**"如父管教儿子"——基督使我们成为神的儿女**——希伯来书12:5-7"你们又忘了那劝你们如同劝儿子的话说：我儿，你不可轻看主的管教……因为主所爱的他必管教。"旷野的管教证明以色列是神的儿子——基督使我们也成为神的儿女——能够承受父亲的管教。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Not by Bread Alone"—Christ's Victory in the Wilderness**—Matthew 4:3-4: "The tempter came and said, 'If you are the Son of God, command these stones to become loaves of bread.' But he answered, 'It is written, Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of God.'" What Israel took forty years to imperfectly learn—Christ perfectly demonstrated in forty days: true life is in God's word—not in bread.

**Manna—Christ Is the Bread of Life**—John 6:31-35: The crowd said "Our fathers ate the manna." Jesus answered: "It was not Moses who gave you the bread from heaven, but my Father gives you the true bread from heaven...I am the bread of life; whoever comes to me shall not hunger." Wilderness manna was only a shadow—Christ is the true bread from heaven.

**"As a Father Disciplines His Son"—Christ Makes Us God's Children**—Hebrews 12:5-7: "My son, do not regard lightly the discipline of the Lord...For the Lord disciplines the one he loves." Wilderness discipline proved Israel was God's son—Christ makes us also God's children—able to bear the Father's discipline.
