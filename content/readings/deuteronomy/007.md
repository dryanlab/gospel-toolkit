## 正文（中文）

弟兄姐妹，我是摩西。申命记第七章是关于拣选——神为什么选择以色列？不是因为他们多、不是因为他们好——而是因为神的爱和祂对列祖的应许。

**圣洁的分别（第1-6节）**

> "耶和华你神领你进入要得为业之地，从你面前赶出许多国民……共七国的民，都比你强大。耶和华你神将他们交给你击杀，那时你要把他们灭绝净尽，不可与他们立约，也不可怜恤他们。不可与他们结亲。"

弟兄姐妹，这段经文在现代人看来非常困难——"灭绝净尽""不可怜恤"。弟兄姐妹，要理解这个命令，必须放在当时的处境中——迦南七族的宗教包含人祭（把孩子献给摩洛）、庙妓、各种令人发指的偶像崇拜。弟兄姐妹，神的审判不是任意的暴力——而是对极度邪恶的公义审判——就像洪水审判挪亚时代的世界一样。

"不可与他们结亲"——弟兄姐妹，为什么？"因为他必使你儿子转离不跟从主，去事奉别神。"弟兄姐妹，这不是种族歧视——而是信仰保护。弟兄姐妹，所罗门王就是活生生的反面教材——他的外邦妻子"诱惑他的心去随从别神"（王上11:4）。弟兄姐妹，保罗也教导"你们和不信的原不相配，不要同负一轭"（林后6:14）。

"因为你归耶和华你神为圣洁的民。"弟兄姐妹，"圣洁"的原意就是"分别出来"——以色列被分别出来归给神——这个"分别"要求他们在生活的方方面面都与周围的民族不同。

**拣选的原因——不是因为你好（第7-11节）**

> "耶和华专爱你们拣选你们，并非因你们的人数多于别民，原来你们的人数在万民中是最少的。只因耶和华爱你们，又因要守他向你们列祖所起的誓。"

弟兄姐妹，"并非因你们的人数多"——弟兄姐妹，拣选的原因不在被拣选的人身上——而在拣选者的爱里。你被爱不是因为你可爱——而是因为神选择爱你。弟兄姐妹，这是改革宗神学"无条件拣选"的旧约根基——以弗所书1:4-5"就如神从创立世界以前在基督里拣选了我们……又因爱我们就按着自己的意旨所喜悦的，预定我们藉着耶稣基督得儿子的名分。"

弟兄姐妹，"原来你们在万民中是最少的"——弟兄姐妹，神专门选了最小的。不是埃及——当时最强大的帝国。不是巴比伦——文明的中心。而是以色列——一个小小的游牧民族。弟兄姐妹，"神拣选了世上愚拙的叫有智慧的羞愧"（林前1:27）。

"只因耶和华爱你们"——弟兄姐妹，为什么神爱他们？"只因耶和华爱你们"——这是一个循环！弟兄姐妹，爱的终极原因就是爱本身——神爱你因为祂选择爱你——没有更深的原因了。弟兄姐妹，如果拣选有原因——那就是条件——就不是恩典了。恩典之所以是恩典——就是因为它没有原因。

**不要惧怕（第17-26节）**

> "你若心里说，这些国的民比我更多，我怎能赶出他们呢？你不要惧怕他们，要牢牢记念耶和华你神向法老和埃及全地所行的事。"

弟兄姐妹，"你若心里说……比我更多怎能赶出他们呢？"弟兄姐妹，摩西预见了以色列人的恐惧——面对比自己强大的敌人时的自然反应。弟兄姐妹，解决恐惧的方法是什么？不是"鼓起勇气"——而是"记念"——记念神过去为你做过什么。你忘记了出埃及吗？你忘记了红海分开吗？弟兄姐妹，过去的恩典是未来信心的根基。

"耶和华你神必将这些国的民从你面前渐渐赶出；你不可把他们速速灭尽，恐怕野地的兽多起来害你。"弟兄姐妹，"渐渐赶出"——不是一次性的，而是"渐渐的"。弟兄姐妹，为什么？"恐怕野地的兽多起来害你"——弟兄姐妹，如果敌人一下子全消灭——空出来的土地会被野兽占据——你还没有足够的人口去管理那么大的地方。弟兄姐妹，神的计划是渐进的——不是因为祂没有能力一次完成——而是因为你还没有预备好承受一次性的大祝福。弟兄姐妹，很多时候神"渐渐地"赐福给你——不是吝啬——而是保护你不被你还无法承受的祝福压垮。

## Content (English)

Brothers and sisters, I am Moses. Deuteronomy 7 is about election—why did God choose Israel? Not because they were many, not because they were good—but because of God's love and His oath to the fathers.

**Holy Separation (vv. 1-6)**

> "When the LORD your God brings you into the land...and clears away many nations before you...you must devote them to complete destruction...You shall not intermarry with them."

Brothers and sisters, this passage is difficult for modern readers—"complete destruction," "show no mercy." Understanding requires the historical context—Canaanite religion included child sacrifice (to Molech), temple prostitution, and horrific idolatry. God's judgment wasn't arbitrary violence—but righteous judgment against extreme evil—like the flood judging Noah's world.

"You shall not intermarry"—why? "For they would turn away your sons from following me, to serve other gods." Not racial discrimination—but faith protection. Solomon was a living counter-example—his foreign wives "For it came to pass, when Solomon was old, that his wives turned away his heart after other gods: and his heart was not perfect with the LORD his God, as was the heart of David his father." (1 Kings 11:4).

**Why Chosen—Not Because You're Great (vv. 7-11)**

> "It was not because you were more in number than any other people that the LORD set his love on you and chose you, for you were the fewest of all peoples. But it is because the LORD loves you and is keeping the oath that he swore to your fathers."

Brothers and sisters, election's reason isn't in the elected—but in the Elector's love. You aren't loved because you're lovable—but because God chose to love you. This is the OT foundation of Reformed "unconditional election"—Ephesians 1:4-5.

"You were the fewest"—God deliberately chose the smallest. Not Egypt—the mightiest empire. Not Babylon—civilization's center. But Israel—a small nomadic people. "But God hath chosen the foolish things of the world to confound the wise; and God hath chosen the weak things of the world to confound the things which are mighty;" (1 Cor 1:27).

"Because the LORD loves you"—why does God love them? "Because the LORD loves you"—circular! Love's ultimate reason is love itself. If election had a reason—it would be conditional—and no longer grace. Grace is grace precisely because it has no cause.

**Don't Be Afraid (vv. 17-26)**

> "If you say in your heart, 'These nations are greater than I. How can I dispossess them?' you shall not be afraid...remember what the LORD your God did to Pharaoh."

The antidote to fear is not "summon courage"—but "remember"—remember what God did for you before. Past grace is future faith's foundation.

"The LORD your God will clear away these nations before you little by little. You may not make an end of them at once, lest the wild beasts grow too numerous." "Little by little"—not all at once. Why? If enemies vanished instantly—wild beasts would fill the empty land. God's plan is gradual—not because He lacks power to finish at once—but because you aren't ready to handle an all-at-once mega-blessing. Often God blesses "little by little"—not stinginess—but protecting you from being crushed by blessings you can't yet bear.

## 历史背景（中文）

申命记第七章提到的"七国"——赫人、革迦撒人、亚摩利人、迦南人、比利洗人、希未人、耶布斯人——是当时巴勒斯坦地区的主要居民。考古发现证实了迦南宗教的残酷性——乌加里特文献（约公元前1400-1200年）描述了巴力和亚舍拉崇拜的细节。迦南人的宗教仪式包括把婴儿献给摩洛（利18:21, 20:2-5），在耶路撒冷附近的欣嫩子谷（Gehenna）进行。"渐渐赶出"（第22节）是对实际军事和定居策略的务实指导——以色列人需要时间来占领和管理这片土地。约书亚记的征服记录表明——即使在约书亚的征服之后，许多迦南人仍然留在了这片土地上（士1-2章），最终成为以色列人的网罗。

## Historical Context (English)

The "seven nations" in Deuteronomy 7—Hittites, Girgashites, Amorites, Canaanites, Perizzites, Hivites, Jebusites—were Palestine's main inhabitants. Archaeology confirms Canaanite religion's cruelty—Ugaritic texts (c. 1400-1200 BC) detail Baal and Asherah worship. Canaanite rituals included infant sacrifice to Molech (Lev 18:21, 20:2-5) in the Valley of Hinnom (Gehenna) near Jerusalem. "Little by little" (v. 22) was pragmatic guidance for military and settlement strategy. Joshua's conquest records show many Canaanites remained after conquest (Judg 1-2), ultimately becoming a snare for Israel.

## 经文结构（中文）

**灭绝与分别（1-6节）**——七国比你强大→灭绝净尽不立约→不可结亲→免得儿子转离→你是圣洁的民。
**拣选的恩典（7-11节）**——拣选非因人多→你们是最少的→只因耶和华爱你们→守列祖的誓→他是信实的神→守约施慈爱直到千代。
**顺从的福（12-16节）**——谨守遵行→神必爱你赐福→使你人数增多→牲畜增多→胜过万民→不可事奉他们的神。
**不要惧怕（17-26节）**——怎能赶出？→记念出埃及→神必赶出他们→渐渐赶出→偶像金银不可贪→可憎物不可带进家。

## Structure (English)

**Destruction and Separation (vv. 1-6)**—Seven nations stronger → destroy completely, no treaties → no intermarriage → lest sons turn away → you are a holy people.
**Grace of Election (vv. 7-11)**—Chosen not for numbers → you were fewest → because LORD loves you → keeping oath to fathers → faithful God → covenant love to a thousand generations.
**Blessings of Obedience (vv. 12-16)**—Keep and observe → God will love and bless → multiply people and livestock → above all peoples → don't serve their gods.
**Don't Fear (vv. 17-26)**—How can I dispossess? → remember Egypt → God will drive them out → little by little → don't covet idol gold/silver → don't bring abominations home.

## 神学要点（中文）

**无条件拣选（Unconditional Election）**
改革宗五要点（TULIP）中的"U"在申命记7:7-8有清晰的旧约表达。韦敏斯德信条第3章论神的永远旨意："神从万古以先，本着祂自己的旨意，按着祂最智慧、最圣洁的计划，全然自主的、绝不改变的决定一切将要成的事。"拣选的原因不在人——而在神自己的爱和旨意。"并非因你们的人数多"排除了人的功劳——"只因耶和华爱你们"确立了恩典的绝对性。

**圣洁的呼召——分别为圣**
改革宗传统教导信徒是"分别为圣"的。韦敏斯德信条第13章论成圣。"你归耶和华你神为圣洁的民"——圣洁不是道德完美——而是被"分别出来"归给神。这个分别既是地位上的（称义的时刻就已经分别）——也是生活上的（成圣的过程中不断分别）。

**渐进的得胜**
改革宗传统教导成圣是渐进的。韦敏斯德信条第十三章教导："在这场争战中，那残存的败坏虽然可能暂时佔有优势，但是基督成圣之灵不断加力量给人，那蒙神重生的新人靠这力量终必得胜。""渐渐赶出"——属灵的得胜不是一次性的——而是终身的渐进过程。

## Theology (English)

**Unconditional Election**
The "U" in Reformed TULIP finds clear OT expression in Deuteronomy 7:7-8. Westminster Confession chapter 3. Election's cause is not in humans—but in God's own love and will. "Not because you were more" excludes human merit—"because the LORD loves you" establishes grace's absoluteness.

**The Call to Holiness—Set Apart**
The Reformed tradition teaches believers are "set apart." Westminster Confession chapter 13.: You are a people holy to the LORD—holiness isn't moral perfection but being "set apart" for God.

**Gradual Victory**
The Reformed tradition teaches progressive sanctification. Westminster Confession chapter 13.: Little by little—spiritual victory is not one-time but a lifelong gradual process.

## 基督的影子（中文）

**"只因耶和华爱你们"——基督里无条件的爱**——以弗所书1:4-5"就如神从创立世界以前在基督里拣选了我们……又因爱我们就按着自己的意旨所喜悦的预定我们藉着耶稣基督得儿子的名分。"申命记7章的无条件拣选在基督里得到了终极的实现——神拣选我们不是因为我们好——而是"按着自己的意旨所喜悦的"。

**"你归耶和华为圣洁的民"——基督使我们成圣**——希伯来书10:10"我们凭这旨意，靠耶稣基督只一次献上他的身体，就得以成圣。"以色列是旧约中"圣洁的民"——教会是新约中"圣洁的国度"（彼前2:9）——这个圣洁不是靠我们自己——而是靠基督一次献上的身体。

**"渐渐赶出"——基督里渐进的成圣**——腓立比书1:6"我深信那在你们心里动了善工的必成全这工，直到耶稣基督的日子。"迦南人被"渐渐赶出"——我们的罪也是被"渐渐"对付的——成圣是一生的过程——但神"必成全这工"。

## Christ's Shadow (English)

**"Because the LORD Loves You"—Unconditional Love in Christ**—Ephesians 1:4-5: "He chose us in him before the foundation of the world...In love he predestined us for adoption to himself as sons through Jesus Christ." Deuteronomy 7's unconditional election finds ultimate realization in Christ.

**"A People Holy to the LORD"—Christ Makes Us Holy**—Hebrews 10:10: "We have been sanctified through the offering of the body of Jesus Christ once for all." Israel was the OT's "holy people"—the church is the NT's "But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people; that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of darkness into his marvellous light:" (1 Pet 2:9)—holiness achieved not by ourselves but through Christ's once-for-all offering.

**"Little by Little"—Progressive Sanctification in Christ**—Philippians 1:6: "He who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ." Canaanites were driven out "little by little"—our sins are also dealt with "gradually"—sanctification is lifelong—but God "will bring it to completion."
